What is this article about?

This article describes the Microsoft Advertising editorial process.

As part of the editorial review process, each ad and keyword is checked against Microsoft Advertising Policies to ensure that advertising content is in compliance with each country’s local laws, and that the ads are relevant not misleading to our users.

The Editorial team is the team that builds the Microsoft Advertising policies and manages the following work:

  • Manual Review (PMA)
  • Appeals (both ticketed and inline)
  • Bypass Requests
  • PMA Expedite Requests
  • QBR Penalty Application & QBR Appeals
  • Ad Pull Requests
  • Trademark Reviews
  • Policy Inquiries
  • Site Reviews
  • Adult Enablement (Detailed in KB 3169233 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Adult Advertising)

2. Editorial statuses ﻿﻿

Submitted ad groups will undergo a review to make sure that they meet Microsoft Advertising policies. The ad and keyword editorial review is a quality check to make sure that ads are well written and relevant to users' searches.

After new ads or keywords are saved, advertisers can see details of the review process in the Delivery column. In most cases, ads and keywords are reviewed in a timely manner and are live soon after they are submitted. The review could take longer if the ad or keywords do not meet Microsoft Advertising Policies. In these situations, additional review of ads and keywords could take several hours or as long as several business days.

3. Finding rejected/pending content

In the UI:

  1. Select the Campaigns page, and then the Ads tab or the Keywords tab.
  2. If you have any disapproved ads or keywords, there will be a yellow alert at the top of the page with a hyperlink to the list of disapproved ads/keywords.
  3. Select the hyperlink and it will take you to a page with all of the disapproved ads/keywords.
  4. To see a list of disapproved ads, select download and you will receive an excel export of all of the disapprovals.

In Editor:

In the View drop down, select Editorial disapprovals. If you only want to see a specific subset of disapprovals, you can select one of the options under Editorial disapprovals.

4. Ad/Keyword appeals

4.1 Standard appeal

When a customer is impacted by disapprovals, an inline appeal must always be submitted first as the default process. Appeals tickets should only be escalated if an inline appeal is unavailable or if there are issues with the process. See section 4.2 below for more details.

  1. Complete the issue specific details (issue category = Editorial Escalations, issue type = Appeal for disapprovals), and submit to UCMT Tier 2 Receiving Team = Network Quality Services (NQS) > Sub-Team = Appeals.
  2. Escalate to UCMT for processing.
  3. If the appeals team comes back and says that changes need to be made to the advertiser's account in order to approve the rejected ads/kws, communicate this to the advertiser and close the ticket. Do not keep the ticket open while the advertiser makes changes. The ticket can be reopened once the changes are made. These can be coded as 'not overturned' until the changes are made

Note: Delivery suppression repeals related to financial services rejections should be sent to a separate team. For more information on the escalation process, visit the Payday Loan Policy Change FAQ article.

4.2 Inline appeals

An inline exception is also known as an inline appeal, it gives advertisers the ability to appeal a rejected ad or keyword without the need to contact a support advocate. The advertiser can click on a rejected ad or keyword and simply request an appeal.

  • Inline appeals are now also allowed at the Ad Group level. Ad Groups with same ID can be appealed inline only once.
  • In most cases, advertisers will not need to contact support for requests to create an inline appeal.
  • If an advertiser is inquiring about the disapproved keywords and has not submitted the inline appeal yet, educate the advertiser about the submission steps in the user interface or Editor (Editor) as well as how they will be alerted on a decision.

Microsoft Advertising UI:

  1. Users can access inline appeals directly from both the Ad and Keyword tabs in the Microsoft Advertising user interface by clicking on the ellipsis (...).
    Bulk inline appeals are available in the UI. To do a bulk appeal, select the rows you wish to appeal, select Edit and then select Request exception.
  2. To submit the appeal, the user must provide justification as to why they feel Microsoft Advertising should consider an exception and click the Request exception button.

    Note: Advertisers should avoid using general or vague justifications in their request for an exception. For example, "Approve my ad" instead of focusing on the justification/need.
     
  3. Once justification has been given the user then clicks the Request exception button.
  4. When the inline appeal is submitted, the Delivery status changes to "Exception under review" and it is sent for manual review.
  5. After the manual review has taken place, the status will automatically update in the UI to reflect the decision made by the editor.
  6. An email is also sent to the submitter, advising them of the decision on their inline appeal.


Editor (Editor):
The inline appeals feature is also available in the Editor (Editor).

Note: Editor does not allow for in tool exception requests for upfront blocks when creating new content.

  • Only disapproved content can be appealed using Editor.
  • Advertisers can use this tool to view disapprovals by editorial category and appeal disapproved items in bulk.
  1. Advertiser will access the account and be notified that they have Disapproved Ads or Keywords and should be aware of the following Editor status definitions:
    • Active-Appealable: disapproval can be appealed
    • Not Appealable: disapproval cannot be appealed (for example: if the entity has already been appealed)
    • Exception Under Review: appeal is being reviewed
  2. An advertiser can highlight all ads and keywords they would like to appeal and submit the request in bulk.
    • Once a selection has been made, the Reasons for appealing editorial disapprovals field will appear at the bottom of the tool screen.
    • They will need to input a justification for their appeal which will be applied to all selected items.
  3. Once this has been done, they'll need to select the Sync button in Editor in order to submit their request. Doing so will change the status of the content to "Appeal Pending." The sync functionality must be used to retrieve the latest updates and monitor the status of the appeal.
  4. After the manual review has taken place, the status will automatically update in the UI to reflect the decision made by the editor.
  5. An email is also sent to the submitter, advising them of the decision on their inline appeal.

4.3 Inline appeals issues requiring ticket escalation

If an advertiser has already submitted an inline appeal and the ad or keyword has been in “Exception Under Review” status for over two days, submit a ticketed appeal following the standard process. Make sure to mention that an exception was requested and is out of service level agreement (SLA).

If you disagree with the decision of an inline appeal, submit a ticketed appeal following the standard process. Mention that an inline appeal was already submitted, and explain why you disagree with the decision, using policy to support your statement.

Inline appeals ticket coding:

Ensure you are using one of the following Solution Delivered codes whenever advising an advertiser on inline appeals.

  • Educated customer on inline appeals process - Inline appeal submitted by customer: Use this code when you are walking the customer through the process and they successfully submitted their appeal.
  • Educated customer on inline appeals process - Inline appeal to be submitted by customer after Ad/KW corrections: Use this code if you have walked the customer through the process and they are unable to submit any changes at that time, but will later.
  • Educated customer on inline appeals process - Inline appeal submitted by advocate: Use this code if you have submitted the inline appeal on behalf of the customer.

5. QBR penalty

﻿Quality Based Ranking (QBR) is a classification with an associated score that can be assigned to an account at the account ID level. QBR blocks the delivery of all ads and keywords within that account. There is no override capability for specific listings within an impacted account.

Internal terminology vs. external terminology:
Never ever use the terms QBR Penalty or Policy when speaking with an advertiser! These are internal terms only. Instead, reference the acceptable external terms below:

Internal Term

External Term

QBR

Quality/User Experience

Penalty

Concerns

When discussing the delivery issues possibly caused by the QBR score, discuss relevancy and landing page/site quality with advertisers and how improving both would improve the delivery. Use the talking points in this article specific to the QBR Classifications.

Be sure to refer the advertiser to our Relevance and Quality Guidelines whenever discussing any information related to quality and/or relevance. One of the key components to provide an effective experience for advertisers and a relevant experience for consumers is to help ensure our team reviews the quality of keywords, ads and landing pages.

QBR is an assessment of quality, relevance and risk, and includes an assessment of:

  • Bidded keywords
  • Ad copy: titles, text and display URLs
  • Landing page
  • User experience

Scores vary from 1,000 to 500,000.

  • Scores of 1000, 1300 and 2000 have no impact on delivery
  • 480,000 is an old auto penalty score that completely suppresses delivery.
  • 490,000 is a score that suppresses delivery temporarily on some new accounts
  • 500,000 is associated with most manually applied penalties and completely suppresses delivery.

Resolution steps

  1. Check the advertiser's account in UCM or Ad Inquiry to determine if the advertiser is impacted by QBR. If the advertiser has a QBR penalty, it will show as a "UPM Score: 500000" in Ad Inquiry. In UCM, the QBR penalty and classification will be displayed in the Alerts tab.
  2. Check the reason for the penalty against the guidance in KB 2917523 QBR classifications and talking points.

If the scenario is appealable, and the advertiser’s site and Account is fully in compliance

If the scenario is not appealable (as defined in KB 2917523), or the advertiser’s site is still in violation (as shown by policy guidance in KB 2917523)

Proceed with step 3

Use the talking points referenced in the article, and DO NOT send up an appeal until the advertiser has made the changes needed in order to be in compliance
  1. For scenarios that are appealable, submit an appeal to UCMT Tier 2 Receiving Team = Network Quality Services (NQS) > Sub-Team = Appeals.
  2. Use the talking points or suggested content changes provided by the QBR team to talk about next steps with the advertiser.

6. PMA expedite

﻿The manual review process occurs when ads are “Pending.” Most ads and keywords that enter our system go live immediately. A small percentage need evaluation and might (PMA Live) or might not (PMA Not Live) serve while they wait for an editor to assess them.

For example, the word “caliber” is most usually associated with guns, but an advertiser might assert “our customers get the highest caliber of service,” in their ad copy. Although we don’t allow guns in most markets, there are non-weapon uses of the term that can be compliant with policy. Whenever there’s ambiguity about or risk associated with a term, it goes to PMA.

6.1 PMA expedite process

If ads/keywords are found by the requestor to be pending editorial review, there is an option to send an expedited request for immediate processing. If an advertiser asks about the status of an ad, the L1 advocate should immediately verify how long ad has been pending by asking the advertiser. If it has been longer than the typical PMA SLA or if the advertiser expresses urgency about getting ads or keywords live (such as for a large sale), submit a PMA Expedite request.

How to Submit a PMA Expedite request

  1. Complete the issue specific details (issue category = Editorial Escalations, issue type = PMA Expedite), and submit to UCMT Tier 2 Receiving Team = Network Quality Services (NQS) > Sub-Team = Appeals.
  2. Escalate to UCMT for processing – see KB 3170756 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Escalating to Partners using UCMT, COSMIC, and CAP.

Note: The Editorial team is not customer facing. They will correspond with you but not directly with the customer. If additional details are required, the Editorial team will request additional information via the ticket. All the information you need to update the advertiser will be included in or attached to the UCMT ticket. It is your responsibility to frequently check Qview for status updates from UCMT.

Note: Check the status of the service request (SR) frequently (at least once each business day). Set the expectations with advertisers regarding the SLA accordingly and escalate any situations that require immediate attention to your manager. If more details are required, send the request via the UCMT ticket.

Once the pending items have been reviewed, ensure that you do not have any questions before going back to the advertiser. Provide resolution to the advertiser through the parent ticket that you own. Potential resolutions include:

  1. The ads/keywords going live, with no further action for the advertiser
  2. The ads/keywords are reviewed and rejected
  3. The ads/keywords are compliant, but in order to start delivering, the advertiser must make a small change to each affected entity (such as adding a space) and then resubmit.

7. Bypass/exceptions requests

Note: “Bypass” is an internal term only. Use terms like “exception” or “system update” when communicating with advertisers.

Important: Remember that bypasses only work for future submissions and have no effect on content has already been submitted.


Bypass scenarios:
If your advertiser is experiencing any of the following issues, consider requesting a bypass:

  • Cannot load term into campaign: The advertiser is experiencing an error message and/or is unable to save an ad/keyword that contains a particular term.
  • Recurring PMA or long term PMA:
    • The advertiser is experiencing additional review time for the same term(s), which result in approvals most of the time.
    • The advertiser experiences a long review period with certain terms, which eventually result in approvals most of the time.


Bypass criteria:

  • Customer level: Bypasses are granted at the customer level; all accounts under a given customer must be eligible for the requested bypass for it to be granted. To be approved for bypass, the term must be something that will always be approved for that particular advertiser across all of their accounts.
  • Risk assessment: Note the potential for risk by allowing a bypass for terms that fall into editorial high risk areas: Adult, Pharma, Trademark and Gambling. Pay special attention to bypass requests that fall into these categories and review the Microsoft Advertising Policies to help inform.
  • Relevance and Quality: This is a main component that is evaluated against all bypass requests. Although a term might not present a high risk business area (for example: adult, pharma), it is critical that the term is highly relevant to the advertiser’s ad content, business model and user’s search query. For example, if an auto insurance or software download advertiser is bidding on terms like “Obama” or “Justin Bieber,” this bypass would not be granted because there is not a direct relevance. In addition, sites must be of good quality to be eligible for a bypass. Any low quality indicators that you may see behind QBR penalties or Spam rejections would prevent a bypass from being granted. For more information, review the Relevance and Quality Guidelines.
  • Provide as much context and background as possible:
    • What is the advertiser’s justification for needing a bypass?
    • Be sure to provide details on the advertiser’s business model and how it works.
    • How many times has the term in question gone through PMA? Have they experienced any delays?
    • Is the advertiser having issues loading the term initially? Describe the steps they took and what errors they are encountering.

7.1 Submitting a bypass/exception request

  1. Complete the issue specific details (issue category = Editorial Escalations, issue type = Bypass), and submit to UCMT Tier 2 Receiving Team = Network Quality Services (NQS) > Sub-Team = Appeals.
  2. Escalate to UCMT for processing.

SLA for bypass requests is 1 to 2 business days.

Notes: Advertisers are also able to submit a request for exception in the UI similar to an inline appeal.

  • The Editorial team will resolve the request in the child ticket without replying to the advertiser.
  • If additional details are required, the Editorial team will add comments in the ticket. All the information you need to update the advertiser will be included in or attached to the child ticket. It is your responsibility to frequently check the child ticket for updates or resolution.
  • Check the status of the SR frequently (at least once a business day). If more details are requested, update the child ticket.
  • Once the resolution is provided, review the information to ensure that you do not have any questions before going back to the advertiser. If you do not agree with resolution, or need clarification on any decisions, comment in the child ticket immediately. If the resolution is clear, then comment in the child ticket that you have reviewed resolution and the child ticket can be closed. Provide resolution to the advertiser through the parent ticket that you own.

8. Request for ad pull 

Advertisers may contact support requesting a specific ad or ads be pulled from serving. The process for these requests is to direct advertisers to the topic specific webform. We should not escalate any ad pull requests directly to the Ad Quality team.

The Editorial Ad Quality Team will review all ad pull requests submitted through the forms but will only investigate the specific ads provided in the escalation. A specialist will review each ad pull request against Microsoft Advertising Policies; not every ad-pull request result in action. The Editorial Ad Quality Team cannot proactively pull certain categories of ads or monitor an individual advertiser in response to an ad-pull request.

The SLA for ad pull requests is 3 to 5 days. Due to escalation volume, the Ad Quality team is not able to respond to advertiser submissions through the Low Quality ad form. Brand owner submissions to the Intellectual Property Concern form will receive a response indicating when any appropriate actions have been taken.

If you receive an inquiry from a customer who already submitted a request via the webform over 3 to 5 days ago and the ad is still serving, request the ad r-link and confirm that the ad is in violation of our policies. If it is, escalate to UCMT Tier 2 Receiving Team = Network Quality Services (NQS) > Sub-Team = Complaints.

8.1 Trademark, copyright & counterfeit ad pull requests 

Advertisers are responsible for ensuring that their use of keywords and ad content, including trademarks, does not violate the intellectual property rights of others. Trademark owners are responsible for contacting advertisers directly if they believe that their trademark is being used improperly.

If a trademark owner believes his/her trademark is being used improperly, instruct them to do the following:

  1. Contact the advertiser directly and ask them to stop using their trademark term in any way that violates Microsoft Advertising’ Policy.
  2. Submit an ad pull request via the Trademark misuse form on the Intellectual Property concern page. As of March 27, 2017 Microsoft Advertising no longer investigates trademark complaints at the keyword level for any market apart from Hong Kong and Taiwan.


Talking points:

  • “Microsoft Advertising strives to bring high quality relevant ads to its users, and it employs robust systems to ensure that ads on the Microsoft Advertising Network are compliant. However, we welcome feedback regarding ads that violate our policies. Feedback can be submitted only through the ad pull request form”.
  • “We take these complaints very seriously. If the ad is in violation of our polices, it will be removed from the network.”


If the advertiser says they have already filled out a complaint form and no action was taken, look at the ad in question.

  • If it is within policy guidelines, take the time to explain the policy to the advertiser and discuss why this ad meets policy guidance.
  • If it is not within policy guidelines, encourage the advertiser to submit another ad pull request form, and provide the following talking point:

    “It looks like this ad is in violation of our policies. To alert the Editorial team, please fill out the ad pull request form and include all required information. Sometimes one ad is pulled, and the advertiser loads a similar ad to the network. The Editorial Ad Quality Team is not at liberty to proactively pull certain categories of ads or monitor an individual advertiser in response to an ad pull request.”

9. General policy inquiry

The ad and keyword editorial review is a quality check to ensure that ads are well written and relevant to customers. After submitting an ad group, the content goes through an initial review to ensure that it meets the Microsoft Advertising Policies. The editorial status of ads and keywords can be found in the Delivery column on the Ads or Keywords tab of the Campaigns page.

To help ads and keywords pass the editorial review, see the Best practices to avoid disapprovals and Disallowed products and services blog posts.

In most cases, ads and keywords are reviewed promptly and should be available online soon after being submitted. The review takes longer if:

  • The ad or keyword list contains questionable words.
  • The advertised website is inaccessible. The website contains information that is unrelated or not allowed.

In these instances, additional review of ads and keywords could take a few hours or several business days.

To expedite resolution, submit an escalation to UCMT Tier 2 Receiving Team = Network Quality Services (NQS) > Sub-Team = Appeals.

The SLA for standard priority can be up to five business days, so set expectations with the advertiser before escalating.

12. FAQ

Q: What determines how soon ads are live? Why is my content taking so long to review? How come questionable content can be approved for one advertiser, but not another?
A: All new content that is loaded into advertiser’s accounts goes through a series of checks before it goes live. The system can detect that some content is low risk, and therefore it goes live right away. Other content that may pose a risk is sent to a team of editors for manual review. During this time, the status of the ads or keywords will show as “Pending Editorial Review” until a decision is made by the editors. On average, the manual review will take 24 to 48 hours. Can you please tell me when you created your ads/keywords?

Note: If the content has been in Pending status for over 48 hours, submit a PMA Expedite request on the customer’s behalf.

Q: I am losing customers and revenue during the review process. How can you justify this?
A: Microsoft Advertising policies contribute to high quality advertising. These policies are important for delivering the results that search users want and ensuring a positive, safe experience on our network. The editorial review process helps promote a high quality, low risk user experience that leads to higher click through and conversion rates.

Q: Do you offer credits or refunds for the revenue that I am losing?
A: No. We do not offer credits or refunds for editorial review concerns. However, during a review period, ads and keywords that are not serving are not billed. We know that your time is valuable, and we strive to minimize editorial or system delays and get your ads serving as quickly as possible.

Q: Why am I unable to load my ads or keywords? Is there anything that you can do to help me?
A: If you cannot load an ad or keyword and it meets our technical requirements (for example, length is less than 100 character, and no prohibited special characters), one or more of the terms you’re using could be considered high risk by the system based on our Microsoft Advertising policies. All content must comply with our guidelines to ensure a positive, safe user experience on our network. If you feel your content is compliant with our policies, we want to help you get everything live. Let me take a look at your account. Can you please tell me the error message you receive when you try to upload the ads and/or keywords?

Q: Will my ads be reviewed every time that I make a change?
A: If you make any changes to an ad’s title, description, display web address or destination address, the ad again undergoes the editorial review process. If the content is compliant with Microsoft Advertising policies, it will go live immediately.

Q: My ads were previously live on Bing, but now they are disapproved. What is the reason for this?
A: The content serving on our network is continuously reviewed to ensure a positive, safe user experience. Additionally, our Microsoft Advertising policies do change. Content that was previously compliant with our guidelines can be determined to pose a risk. If you disagree with the decision, you can submit an exception request to have the content reviewed again.

Q: I have two ads that are very similar, however one is approved, and one is not. Also, the ads lead to the same landing page. How can this be?
A: We have a variety of systems in place that proactively monitor our network for quality, relevance, and user safety. Some of those checks occur after ads and keywords have been initially approved. Keywords and ads individually and in combination may undergo different types of review; it’s possible for similar keywords and/or ads within the same ad group to have different statuses. Ensure that all keywords and ads align with Microsoft Advertising policies, including our Quality and Relevance Guidelines.
If the ads are almost identical, the reason for the disapproval on one ad could be due to our duplicate ads policy. This policy was put in place to prevent duplicate ads from the same advertiser serving on the same term to ensure variety in the marketplace.

Q: Why do I still see other ads showing for XYZ when my ads have been rejected?
A: “User safety and network quality are top priorities, and we will review any ad quality or policy complaints reported through the low quality ad submission form against our policies. We encourage you to submit a complaint form if you feel that other advertisers are violating our policies.”
