Procedure: Warranty End of Life (EOL) for devices and accessories

Who should use this article?

What is this article about?

This article provides handling instructions for customers needing to service their devices and accessories that are now discontinued. Use this article when MST prompts you to review this article, or anytime you need more information on devices that have reached End of Life (EOL).

1. Overview

This article explains handling and alternatives to offer when a customer has a device or accessory that can no longer be serviced because it was discontinued.

Also, when you try to write an order, your Service Order Management (SOM) tool will display the following text under Messages:

"Coverage on this device has expired. Because this model has been discontinued, out of warranty support and service for such models is no longer available. For more information on handling, review KB 3073426."

2. Scenarios and messaging

2.1 Out of warranty scenarios

Review the table below for expected actions:

Scenario

Details and advocate action

Surface advocate: See section 2.3.

 

This means an order can no longer be written for the item because the coverage is now expired.

Take the following actions:

  1. Thank the customer for their contact and inform them that the item is no longer covered under Microsoft’s Standard Limited Warranty or our Microsoft Complete Extended Service Plan for repairs and service based on expiration date.
  2. OOW support or service is not available because the model has been discontinued.
  3. Inform the customer that if they are interested, there are often promotions running that may make it attractive to upgrade to a brand-new Surface.
  4. If the customer wants to learn more, there are a few options available:
  • Warm transfer the customer to Sales support. If the customer wants to try later, provide the link to www.microsoftstore.com.
  • If this is a chat session provide link to www.microsoftstore.com. If the customer wants to continue with chat, they will need to be reminded to reinitiate contact through the website link provided.
  • Look up a retailer using www.surface.com/buy and relay as appropriate. You can also provide the link so the customer can choose a retailer of their choice.
  1. Thank the customer for being a Surface fan.

Xbox advocate: See section 2.3.

This means an order can no longer be written for the item because the coverage is now expired.

Take the following actions:

  1. Thank the customer for their contact and inform them that the item is no longer covered under Xbox Standard Limited Warranty for repairs and service based on expiration date.
  2. OOW support or service is not available because the model has been discontinued.
  3. Inform the customer that if they are interested, there are often promotions running that may make it attractive to upgrade to a brand-new Xbox.
  4. If the customer wants to learn more, there are a few options available:
  • Warm transfer the customer to Sales support. If the customer wants to try later, provide the link to www.xbox.com/deals.
  • If this is a chat session provide link to www.microsoftstore.com. If the customer wants to continue with chat, they will need to be reminded to reinitiate contact through the website link provided.
  • Look up a retailer using www.xbox.com/deals (shop other retailers) and relay as appropriate. You can also provide the link so the customer can choose a retailer of their choice.
  1. Thank the customer for being an Xbox fan.

Customer is from Norway or Sweden and claims additional entitlement.

Review KB 4131707 Procedure: Special warranty handling for customers in Norway and Sweden for additional handling considerations.

2.2 In warranty scenarios

Scenario

Details and advocate actions

Surface and Xbox advocates: See section 2.3.

This means an order can no longer be written for the item because the device or accessory is EOL.

Take the following actions:

  1. Validate that the device or accessory is in warranty by reviewing the warranty end date or checking the proof of purchase.
  2. Advise the customer that their device or accessory is discontinued and no longer available.
  3. Inform the customer that because there is no upgrade path for their device or accessory, we will have to offer them a refund.
  4. Follow the guidelines outlined by KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios to escalate the refund request to the appropriate team. Escalation Topic > In Policy Refund Request Repair or Fulfillment Order, Escalation Scenario > End of Life Refunds for in warranty devices where exchange is not possible.

The customer is from Norway or Sweden and claims additional entitlement.

Review KB 4131707 Procedure: Special warranty handling for customers in Norway and Sweden for additional handling considerations.

3. FAQ/Suggested messaging

Q: Why can’t I service my OOW device?

A: “We’re sorry, but the coverage on your device has expired. We can no longer provide out of warranty device servicing as your model has been discontinued.”

Q: My warranty just ran out? And now I have to go buy another device? Why?

A: “We’re sorry, but the coverage on your device has expired. We can no longer provide out of warranty device servicing as your model has been discontinued."

Q: Why should I buy another Microsoft product to replace the one I have now?

A: “Microsoft continues to invest and evolve the [Surface/Xbox] family to bring innovations and improved performance to market on the newer generation of devices.”

Q: Are you in any violation of the warranty statement by not providing me any servicing options?

A: “No, we are compliant with all local requirements.”
