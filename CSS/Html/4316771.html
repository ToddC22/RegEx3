Procedure: Commercial: Remote Assistance

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor
 

What is this article about?

The purpose of this policy is to define standards for connecting to a customer's network for troubleshooting. These standards are designed to minimize the potential exposure to our customer from damages that may result from inappropriate use of resources and limit liability for Microsoft Customer Services and Support. This policy applies to all Microsoft owned or personally owned equipment used to connect.

Statement

Where there is a desire to utilize screen sharing or remote assistance tools during troubleshooting:

Support engineers shall:

  • Use only Microsoft corporate approved remote access tools to perform troubleshooting.*
    [Asset 4590452]
  • Approved by TrIP for limited use such as LogMeIn outages or when explicitly requested by customer. Any other use requires separate approval by submitting an engagement with IPG here. Note that Skype for Business or Microsoft Teams for may be used for communications with customers by non-contact center personnel at any time.
  • Provided that use of the requested tool is not prohibited by your local business group. If your local business group establishes more restrictive requirements regarding customer-supplied 3rd party tools, you must follow your local business group’s requirements.
  • Follow the CSS Remote Assistance Implementation Rules, which contains the following information:
  • A list of Microsoft approved remote assistance tools.
  • Acceptable Use protocols that govern the use of all remote assistance tools.
  • The data protection requirements that must be followed by all support engineers.
  • How to request an exception to the Services Remote Assistance Policy.

Support engineers shall not:

  • Use remote access tools that are not approved for use in the Services Remote Assistance Policy unless an exception has been requested and granted according to the CSS Remote Assistance Implementation Rules.
  • Use a remote assistance connection for any purpose other than to provide service to the customer.
  • Request, view, change, or record customer passwords.​

Note:

  • Remote connection access, over ​the internet, is subject to the customer's company policies.

Exceptions

  • For Commercial Support (FTE/Contact Center Suppliers) , if a customer insists on the use of a particular technology such as WebEx or Microsoft Teams, then it is acceptable to use those tools without seeking an exception from TrIP, provided the use of the requested tool is not prohibited by the engineers local business group. Commercial support cannot compromise customer confidentiality when using these tools, for example:
  • Do not add unauthorized parties to the conversations.
  • File sharing via Teams is expressly prohibited. Files classified as Highly Confidential must be transferred via DTM due to transport, storage and retention requirements.
