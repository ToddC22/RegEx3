1. Overview

As Microsoft looks to modernize the sales experience for its partners, the legacy platform, Partner Sales Connect (PSC), will be retiring. Partner Center (PC) is the “one-stop-shop" where partners engage with Microsoft, thus the referral sharing experience will also be done within Partner Center. Setting up the new platform could represent complexity for partners, which is why CED specialists have been asked to support this workstream.

Note: This is not a migration of active deals. Specialists must advise partners to close out active deals within Partner Sales Connect and use Partner Center for new deal sharing.

Note: This workstream will be subject to change as partner volumes increase and the initiative evolves. If at any point you run into questions, concerns, or issues that you are unable to address, please inform the partner that you will do some research and then get back to them. These questions/concerns should then be captured real time and immediately flagged via the Teams site dedicated to this initiative. CSS will flag these to OCP and provide you with the correct way to proceed.

Note: White Glove is often focused on OCP-managed partners but is not limited to OCP-managed partners. The priority partners included in white glove have been prioritized because Microsoft wants to keep co-selling with them, co-sell more with them, or wants to begin co-selling with them.

Note: There is no firm retirement date for Partner Sales Connect. Retirement of the platform will occur when a set of success criteria, defined by the co-sell program, are met. That said, migration of partners actively using PSC is targeted to complete by December 1, so that active co-sell partners are using the modern sales experience for the remainder of FY21. More importantly, there is only funding for white glove migration through Q1, so should partners choose not to migrate at this time, they will have to “self-serve” their migration at a later date this year, and will not have dedicated help in doing so.

If the deferral is beyond Q1, we should treat this as a decline.

1.1 Field engagement model

If at any point the PDM contacts you about something out of scope please follow up with the CSS leadership team who can escalate to OCP

  • In scope: Meeting updates/discussion, process related questions
  • Out of scope: Reporting requests, process changes, 1:1 sessions outside of cadence

1.2 Completion criteria

  • Business profile has been set up and validated.
  • All relevant MPN IDs are whitelisted & confirmed by partner.
  • All relevant solutions are whitelisted & confirmed by partner.
  • Optional: validated 1 opportunity received from MSFT (Inbound)
  • Optional: validated opportunity shared with MSFT (Outbound)
  • Optional: The partner has been connected with the PTC team for API integration

1.3 Whitelist status definitions

  • Active: PartnerOne has each OCPCRM Account in Active state
  • Eligible Partial: PartnerOne has at-least one OCPCRM Account in Eligible state and at-least one OCPCRM Account in Not Eligible state
  • Eligible: PartnerOne has each OCPCRM Account in Eligible state
  • Whitelisted Partial: PartnerOne has at-least one OCPCRM Account in Whitelisted state and at-least one OCPCRM Account in either Eligible or Not Eligible state
  • Whitelisted: PartnerOne has each OCPCRM Account in Whitelisted state
  • Active Partial: PartnerOne has at-least one OCPCRM Account in Active state and at-least one OCPCRM Account in non-Active state.
  • Not Eligible: PartnerOne has each OCPCRM Account in Not Eligible state

4.2 Initial contact

Initial outreach to the Partner and PDM should happen in the same day back to back.

PDM outreach

When contacting the PDM the object is to inform them of the migration and give them the opportunity to engage as much as they want to. This migration does not revolve around the PDM and an unresponsive PDM should not be a blocker to the success of this initiative.

Send the PDM this email template socializing the workstream with them

When contacting the PDM it is important that all relevant PDM contacts attached to account are included in the TO: line UNLESS the account has Global PDM (PDM at PGA level) in which case you would only include Global PDM.

PDM OOF: If you receive an Out of Office message from a PDM, please continue to include them on all communications/meeting invitations with the partner unless they reach out and decline.

Follow-up process/Strikes: Not required

PDM involvement

For managed (MPL) partners, who are managed globally, White Glove specialists should engage the Global PDM to coordinate migration. The Global PDM is expected to reach out to local PDMs as needed so that

  • Global PDM - MPN vOrg IDs are valid for all OCP CRM accounts.
  • Global PDM - All solutions to be used for referral sharing are active and not duplicated in OCP tooling.
  • Specialist - A primary Referral Admin has access to the module. This partner contact is expected to set up others in their organization who also need access.
  • Specialist - Demo the referral module & answer questions on it. The primary Referral Admin is expected to inform and train other individuals in their organization on the module with common Partner Center Referral module resources.

For managed (MPL) partners who are managed locally, White Glove specialists should engage the United States PDM - or best local PDM based on white glove team capacity - and this local PDM will be expected to

  • Local PDM - Coordinate with other local PDMs to ensure MPN vOrg IDs are valid for all OCP CRM accounts.
  • Local PDM - All solutions to be used for referral sharing are active and not duplicated in OCP tooling.
  • Specialist - A primary Referral Admin has access to the module. This partner contact is expected to set up others in their organization who also need access.
  • Specialist - Demo the referral module & answer questions on it. The primary Referral Admin is expected to inform and train other individuals in their organization on the module with common Partner Center Referral module resources.

For unmanaged (non-MPL) partners, White Glove specialists will work directly with the partner contact(s). Specialists own all the below:

  • Ensure all OCP CRM accounts have valid MPN vOrg IDs.
  • All solutions to be used for referral sharing are active and not duplicated in OCP tooling.
  • A primary Referral Admin has access to the module. This partner contact is expected to set up others in their organization who also need access.
  • Demo the referral module & answer questions on it. The primary Referral Admin is expected to inform and train other individuals in their organization on the module with common Partner Center Referral module resources.

Account Whitelisting:

The first step in the whitelisting process is to ensure all accounts associated with the partner have been whitelisted or deactivated. This template is used to request whitelisting for specific accounts for a partner.

When sending requests to OCPSUP@Microsoft.com for white listing accounts or solutions, please follow below format for subject when you send a request.

“White Glove – Account/Solution Change Request <mm/dd> - <requestor alias>”

E.g. “White Glove – Account/Solution Change Request <08/31> - <v-vamar>”

If an account is whitelisted, then its associated CRM solutions will be whitelisted automatically

Without account being whitelisted if you send request for solutions whitelisting then it may delay and lead to further mail discussions.

So please make sure to verify account level whitelisting status, if it’s not whitelisted (i.e “Whitelist for PC Co-Sell” is not “Yes”) then send the request to white list Account too.

Note: PC Minted solutions (i.e. where solution ID is a GUID (25 characters), not SOL-1234-XXX) are not automatically whitelisted when an account is whitelisted.

So you need to send an explicit whitelisting request for such PC minted solutions

 

To complete this process follow the below steps:

  1. Prior to submitting a request for whitelisting, you must ensure the partner’s membership is managed or migrated to PC. View the Details section of reporting for the below columns:
     

    Is PC Born

    (Yes/No), whether the partner’s membership began in Partner Center, as opposed to Partner Membership Center—the legacy membership system. Newer partners will typically be “Yes.” This attribute is based upon PartnerOne ID, sometimes called “Vorg” ID.

    Is PC Invited

    (Yes/No), for partners who have been with Microsoft for longer periods of time, they likely managed their membership from PMC. Therefore, their membership had to be migrated to Partner Center. This binary flag indicates whether they were invited to migrate. It is OK if this value is “No,” and in most cases it will be “Yes.”

    Is PC Migrated

    (Yes/No), for partners who used the legacy PMC to manage their membership, these partners need to be migrated to Partner Center before a sales migration (PSC to PC) can occur.

    If “Is PC Migrated” = No, direct the partner to either:

    A) Login to Partner Center at partner.microsoft.com and ensure their MPN membership is renewed; or

8. FAQ

What happens if the partner is not interested in migrating from PSC to PC at this time?

We mark the solution as partner declined per the guidance and stop pursuing the partner. This migration is fully at the partner's discretion at this time.

What would be the recommendations for managing existing deals in PSC?

All existing deals in PSC will not be migrated over and they can continue to manage them through PSC.

Who whitelists the partner accounts in OCP CRM?

The local PDM, if they are unreachable then the global PDM. When a PDM is not involved, please complete the process in section: Account Whitelisting.

What happens if the partner is working out of an old version of Partner Center?

All partners are in the new version of Partner Center.

What is the impact of not moving to Partner Center?

They won’t get to use the modern day functionality of PC.

If a PDM/partner replies in another language how do I proceed?

Please keep the conversation in English and if it causes issues please flag this in Teams, this is an English only work effort

If a partner has no Co-sell ready solutions how do I proceed?

Complete all parts of the migration that we can, i.e. ensure they are set up in Partner Center, have a business profile set-up, and understand the tool.

Once the partner transitions to PC, do all the MS sellers have to accept the new opportunities again?

Only those Partner Deals that a partner deactivates in Partner Sales Connect and re-enters in Partner Center for active co-sell help would need to be accepted by the MSFT sales team. However, we recommend that a partner just "drain" PSC by closing out existing deals there, and simply submit any net-new deals in Partner Center.

If an account has no PDMs how do I proceed?
If a PDM asks to postpone a migration how do I proceed?

If the PDM/Partner does not have the bandwidth at this time and if the PDM/Partner provides you a timeline to re-engage - Please perform the following actions:

1- Ask the PDM /Partner when would it be a better time? Ask for a month to reengage.

2- Place the partner on Deferred status and make a reminder to recontact the partner.

3- If the PDM says reengage in July, then place a reminder to send your first email contact to the PDM.

Where can I find the deal ID?

It's only visible in the URL of the referral https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/psc-to-pc.

If a deal is accepted but a few days or a week goes by and anyone reaches out to us, is there a way for us to follow up with someone or via the partner portal?

Does the partner mean "a week goes by and NO ONE reached out to us"? Yes, once the Referral is accepted by the MSFT sales team, you can go back into the Referral and see who has accepted it on the MSFT side, including their email address. Keep in mind MSFT sellers only have to accept deals where the partner has requested some specific co-sell help (not for deals where the partner has selected the "no help needed at this time" value).

Follow up question to #1, how long should we wait before we follow up with anyone if we could?

You can follow up as early and as frequently whenever you want, depending on the urgency of the need the customer has.

If we need to edit some of the main aspects of the deal such as the location, for example, how do we do that?

The customer details are not editable after the Referral is submitted. You would need to close the existing deal and submit a new one to change who the base customer is... as this will trigger a new routing to the new MSFT sales resource.
[Asset 4586139]

We see that there is an "accepted" label on the deal and then on the timeline shows as won/lost stage, is there any other way to track this deal after it has reached this stage?

There is intentionally no normalized sales stage tracking in the Referral object in Partner Center, as not all partners will align exactly with MSFT's sales stages, and this value was rarely updated in Partner Sales Connect. Rather, [the partner should] use the notes to call out specific next steps desired from the MSFT sales resource, or to update on what the latest customer needs are... and keep the estimated value and close date updated.

Can we deal share with the MS reps? If so, how?

"Deal share with MS reps"? That's what sharing an "outbound" deal is - sharing with a specific MS sales rep... need more context to understand what this question is trying to convey... that's what co-sell is - sharing deals with MS sellers or other partners.

What are the parameters for a deal to get declined on the Microsoft side? Anything we can do there? Resubmit?

Did this partner have a deal declined? There should be a decline "reason" in all of these deals. Most common is when a partner today is submitting deals for SMB customers (for which we have no managed customer account rep to "route" to)... though these deals will stop being declined and start being routed to the TCM-SMB resources by the end of month [September 2020].

What happens to partners that are already migrating and happen to have these SAM/IoT deals? Can you finish the migration process normally?

The specialist should check the deal volume in the report. If less than 2-5% of the total volume a partner has shared is related to SAM or IoT, typically that is simply done in error and is not representative of a business outcome they are trying to drive. In this case, the specialist can reach out to the partner and ask what kind of co-selling they are trying to do
