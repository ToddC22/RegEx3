1. What's new

As part of the CSP compliance requirement, CSP hard blocking feature is live in production!

About the feature:

This is an Anti-Corruption requirement from CELA where Partner Center is non-compliant today in terms of offboarding illicit partners from the system, and increasing partner offboarding backlog is adding significant risk to Microsoft. 

The Non-Severe Hard Block feature provides protection to Microsoft with the ability to temporarily turn off a CSP / MPN partner account that is in a probationary state, without any financial impacts (the financial requirements for severe hard-blocks will be proposed for all CSP partners in Phase II). 

The Suspension is reversible to cater the false positives.

This was automated and it is done in PROSE.

For more information on this, please refer to the Partner Center offboarding article.

2. Summary

All partners invited to migrate to Partner Center or have already migrated (applicable for all partners, not only net new), are being run through the vetting process. In account settings, the partner can see the detailed progress of the vetting process.

The Vetting process is the process of verifying a partner who migrates from the old Partner Membership Center Platform to the new one called Partner Dashboard based on the information they insert in the system in the moment of the migration (Email, Domain, Business Profile). Also, after the migration process is completed, in case Partner wants to update account info – this will automatically trigger a new round of vetting to be completed.
 

Important: MPN and CSP vetting cases are not in scope for Frontline advocates anymore. If partners select the correct vetting support topic from Partner Center, their tickets will be directly sent to OneVet team.

Email ownership- Pending

Instruct the user that an email was sent to the address listed on the account. They need to locate the email and complete the verification step, also to check their junk/spam folder as well.

 

User hasn’t received the email in the inbox yet

Ask the user to click on ‘resend email’ on Partner Center dashboard. A new email will get sent out within minutes. Please have them locate the email and complete the verification step by clicking ‘verify me’.

 

User clicked on ‘verify me’ but still didn’t get verified

There could be some delays; please ask partner to refresh verification/subway line page (the browser) and if issue persists transfer the case to OneVet without creating a collaboration task, informing the team with regards to the workaround followed by partner.

Instruct the user to log into their account and update the account information. This will cause a new vetting to be triggered.

Specific anti-corruption process for CSP partners and Indirect Resellers:

Microsoft has recently enabled a CSP anti-corruption holds and notification process for vetting failures with the attached notification going to more than 3000 CSP partners (including indirect resellers). 

The current plan is to provide 30 days’ notice (to be confirmed) from the time the emails go out until these accounts are put into suspended state.

3. Vetting steps

Email ownership: (same as Email Verification in OneVet tool)

This check verifies whether partner owns the email address that was provided for registration. User should click on the verification email to verify the email.

Employment verification: (same as EmailDomainValidation in OneVet tool)

This check verifies that domain of the email address tprovided by partner during registration. This check could take 1-3 days to complete.

Business verification: Validates an entity's official name and address. This helps determine if a business is legitimate and still in business. Verification is done through an automated check against OneVet service provider's databases and human investigation.

Please note that none of the above actions is in scope for Frontline team. They’ve been described for a better visibility and understanding.

4. Vetting process for Net new partners

Net new MPN partners will be blocked and are not allowed to perform any actions in Partner Center until the vetting status is moved to Approved. For rejected status, partners should be able to fix it using profile page changes.

  • Block Net new MPN partners and don't activate their enrollment till the vetting status is moved to Approved.
  • Show pending page to partners till vetting is completed.

Range as 2-3 business days
