Summary


Who should follow this procedure?
  • Only those who have passed the Office 365 Data Protection IW Elevation Submitter Training, and have been provisioned with proper security permissions.  
What is this procedure about?
  • This article describes how to elevate an O365 user account, or an admin account in another admin role, to the role of Company (Global) Administrator. Note: Global Admins (GA) are called Company Admins (CA) in Modern ViewPoint, so that role is referred to as GA/CA in the rest of this document.
When should this procedure be followed?
  • When a customer’s Office 365 tenant no longer has an available GA/CA, and the caller has provided a user account that is eligible for elevation. Only UPNs within the tenant can be elevated.

Assumptions

  • This article describes only the approved procedure of elevating an existing user or existing administrator on the Office 365 tenant to the GA/CA role.
  • It is the customer’s responsibility to identify the user to be elevated.
  • Microsoft Support does not create new user accounts. Only customer admins can create new accounts. 
  • Unmanaged tenants are not eligible for IW elevation. For information about unmanaged tenants, please refer to: 2995721: How to troubleshoot Commercial Self Service Offers in Office 365, 3068485: Job aid for Commercial Self Service for Office 365 and How to manage a domain already in use by people in your organization who signed up with their work or school email address. Unmanaged tenants are not eligible for IW elevation.
  • Elevation requests made by a Technical Contact must follow the same procedure as for an O365 user.
  • If there is a listed partner, ask the customer to get support directly from the DAP partner and provide the partner’s contact information listed on this tab.
    • If the customer does not want to contact the partner and refers to a dispute, stop providing support and explain the terms and conditions when adding a DAP partner, you can refer to the link.
    • If there is no dispute, but the customer does not want to contact the partner, assist the customer with the request as you normally would.

Procedure

  • There is a valid user to elevate (part of the tenant).
  • One of the following is true:
    • Confirmation via phone and/or UPN/alt from listed GA/CA(s) about not being part of the company.
    • No response from the listed GA/CA(s) email communication after 24 business hours (1 business day).
    • Bounce-back received from UPN and alternate email (for all listed GA/CA(s).
  • One point (approval or verification) received in the following order:
    • Authorized Administrator approval (if different from the requestor)
    • Technical Contact approval (if different from the requestor)
    • DNS TXT verification

Step 1: Validate that no GA/CAs are available

Inform the requester that Microsoft must independently verify that none of the listed GA/CAs are available to perform this task, (the process may take up to 48 hours). The request for elevation cannot proceed without this first step.

Step 2: Obtain Elevation permission from authorized administrators

If there are no authorized listed administrators (other than GA/CA), proceed to Step 3: Obtain Elevation permission from Technical Contact.

Inform the customer that Microsoft will send an email message to the authorized listed administrators email addresses to request elevation permission.

Step 3: Obtain Elevation permission from Technical Contact

If the GA/CA main or alternate UPN is the same as the Technical Contact UPN listed, proceed to Step 4: DNS TXT Record Confirmation.

Inform the customer that Microsoft will send an email message to the Technical Contact (TC) email address to request elevation permission.

Step 4: DNS TXT Record Confirmation

Request that the customer add a TXT record at their domain hosting service to prove domain ownership.

Unable to validate customer

To be used when:

  • There is no form of validation obtained.
  • The verified domain was purchased through Microsoft.
  • There is no valid user to be elevated and there is a valid reason to access the account.

If one of the above is true, escalate to the Technical Advisor (O365DPTA@microsoft.com) using User Elevation Template: 2714453: Office 365 How to get account ownership validation.

  • Regional TAs are to follow the same process to notify the DPET by sending an email to O365DPTA@microsoft.com for approval in Modern ViewPoint after the Identity Validation has been completed.

Step 5: Request account elevation

  1. Provide the info below in Non-Customer facing note in ticketing system:
    • Provide the primary email address of user to be elevated:
    • Name of user or non-GA/CA who is to be elevated:
    • Form or validation (choose from the following):
      1. Authorized Administrator approval
      2. Technical Contact approval
      3. DNS TXT verification
    • Clear justification for elevation to GA/CA:
  2. Submit the request in the system.
  3. Once approved, execute the elevation in the system.

Step 6: Follow up with customer

  1. In the system, confirm the request status as “Approved”. Once elevation is approved, click on “Execute”, look up the tenant and verify that the user is listed as GA/CA. Follow up to ensure the customer can log on as a GA/CA.
    • In-hoop submissions will be reviewed within 2 hours (Sunday at 10pm PT through Friday at 10pm). If you do not receive an approval/denial within 2 hours, please email for an update at O365dpta@microsoft.com.
    • Out-of-hoop submissions will be reviewed during normal business hours.
  2. If the new admin is unable to log on with their current password, or cannot remember their password, reset the password of the new GA/CA by using the procedure in article. If the customer needs a password reset, please follow the process: Procedure: M365 TS: Data protection - M365 password reset.
  3. Document the Elevation case and confirm it is associated with the newly elevated GA/CA.
  4. Direct the customer to this support article Assigning admin roles in Office 365:
    • Update the mobile phone and alternate email address so that they can use the "Forgot your password?" link for self-service resets in the future. Both are required for the service to work.
    • Advise the new administrator to update the administrator(s) listed on file to reflect only active members, as well as the technical contact on file.
    • Advise that they consider enabling Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for security purposes.

  5. Send closure email and close the case.

How to validate GA/CAs availability
  • Call all the listed GA/CAs for the company using the phone numbers listed for each GA/CA.
  • If you are unable to reach any GA/CAs by phone, or if anyone other than listed GA/CA answers the phone, use Email template 1: Independent Verification to Listed GA to send a separate email to each of the listed GA/CAs, in the CC include the alternate email.
  • If no response is received within 24 hours, or the GA/CA responds and confirms they are no longer with the organization, the GA/CA can be considered permanently unavailable, and you can proceed to the next step.
  • If the GA/CA responds and denies the elevation request, escalate to the O365DPTA@microsoft.com for further review. Include the region code, “Declined Elevation Request” and the SR number in the subject line and the following details:
    • Tenant Name
    • Requestor Name / Relation to the company
    • Reason that the admin declined the elevation (if applicable)
How to Obtain Elevation permission from authorized administrators
  1. Only the following authorized administrators can provide permission to elevate a user:
    • Billing Administrator
    • License Administrator
    • Security and compliance administrator
    • User management administrator
    • SharePoint Administrator
    • Exchange Administrator
  2. Send a separate email message to each of the listed authorized administrators’ UPNs and alternate email addresses using Email Template 2: Request Permission from the Authorized administrator. Any replies to your verification email messages that appear to have been forwarded or responded to from a DL/alias must include the original email message in the response. If it does not, have a TA review and notate approval in the ticketing system before submitting through Modern ViewPoint. All necessary security requirements must be met prior to escalation.
  3. If no response is received from the authorized administrator after 1 business day (24 business hours), proceed to Step 3: Obtain Elevation permission from Technical Contact.
  4. If the authorized administrator declines the request via email, call to inform the person requesting elevation that the request was declined. Never share any personally identifiable information (PII). Decline elevation request and close case as non-entitled.
How to Obtain Elevation permission from Technical Contact
  1. In the SR ticketing system, send an email message to the Technical Contact UPN using Email Template 3: Request Permission from the TC. Any replies to your verification email messages that appear to have been forwarded or responded to from a DL/alias must include the original email message in the response. If it does not, have a TA review and notate approval in the ticketing system before submitting through Modern ViewPoint. All necessary security requirements must be met prior to escalation.
  2. If no response is received from the Technical Contact after 1 business day (24 business hours), proceed with steps outlined under Step 4: DNS TXT Record Confirmation. If the Technical Contact declines the request via email, escalate to the O365DPTA@microsoft.com for further review. Include the region code, “Declined Elevation Request” and the SR number in the subject line and the following details:
    • Tenant Name
    • Requestor Name / Relation to the company
    • Reason the TC declined the elevation (if applicable)
  3. If permission is obtained and the requestor is the Technical Contact, proceed to Step 4: DNS TXT Record Confirmation.
How to verify DNS TXT Ownership
  1. Customer must meet the following requirements to use this verification method:
    1. Tenant must have a custom domain. Look up the domain in Modern ViewPoint. On the left navigation menu, click Troubleshooting. In the Troubleshooting pane, click the Domains tab. Look for a custom domain using the Select Domains drop down list.
      • If the tenant does not have a custom domain, proceed to Unable to validate customer.
      • If the tenant has more than 1 domain, customer will need to proof ownership on at least 3 of the verified domains.
    1. Confirm that the custom domain(s) was not purchased through Microsoft.
    2. The customer must have access to the DNS records for their custom domain(s) as an indicator of their connection to the tenant.
  1. Verbally provide a code (Use any code generator tool) for the customer to enter a Text (TXT) Record on the domains associated to the Office 365 tenant within the Domain Name System (DNS).
  2. Use nslookup to verify that the customer has added the TXT record. If the TXT record is entered, record the TXT code in the case notes and confirm that domain ownership has been proven on the domain.
  3. If customer experiences a technical blocker (domain purchased from Office 365) preventing them from adding the DNS TXT record, proceed with steps outlined under Unable to validate customer.
  4. If customer states that they do not have access to the DNS, politely inform them that they must resolve the access issue before continuing with the request.

More information

Security codes

  • Must be created by the SE or DPET.
  • Must be 9 characters (letters and numbers) in 3 groups of 3 (###-###-###).
  • Must be unique for each case (Engineers should not use the same format/pattern of letter vs. numbers).
  • Must be recorded in case notes.
