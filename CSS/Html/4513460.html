What is this article about?

This article outlines what the Microsoft Advertising Shopify app is, how it integrates with Shopify, and how you should support Shopify customers using the app to create/manage Microsoft Advertising Shopping campaigns.

You will need to be familiar with Bing Shopping and Bing Merchant Center in order to support this feature successfully.

For more information on Shopify see the About Microsoft Advertising and Shopify help article.

For advocates who are not trained on supporting Shopify app issues:

Phone advocates:
If you receive a call about an issue with the Shopify app:

  1. Advise the customer we have a team of specialists to handle that issue.
  2. Then warm transfer the call to the Shopping Campaign team.
    • If you are unable to complete a warm transfer, create a ticket and use the push/pull process to arrange a call back from the relevant team.

Chat advocates:
When you determine that the chat is related to a Shopify campaign:

Advise the customer we have a team of specialists to handle that issue, and ask whether they would like to call the team directly, or if they would prefer for us to arrange a call back:

  • If the customer would like a call back - Create a ticket and use the push/pull process to arrange a call back from the relevant team.
  • If the customer would like to call directly - Ask them to dial 1-800-518-5689 and select option 2 to connect with a Shopping Campaign specialist.

1. What is Shopify? 

Shopify is a third-party e-commerce platform for online stores and retail point-of-sale systems. They offer online retailers a suite of services including payments, marketing, shipping, and customer engagement tools to simplify the process of running an online store.

Customers pay a monthly subscription fee to Shopify for hosting their business, and this is payed directly to Shopify. 

In addition, advertisers incur advertising costs from Shopify’s partners, (for example, Microsoft Advertising or Google Ads) and these payments are made to the partner directly.

2. What is the Microsoft Advertising Shopify app

The Microsoft Advertising Shopify app is an API plug-in that enables merchants to sign-up and manage their Microsoft Advertising Shopping campaigns directly from the Shopify platform. It will run within Shopify, on any platforms that Shopify currently supports.

The application is downloadable from the Shopify platform and uses a built-in API key to access only certain features and functionalities within the Accounts & Billing and the Shopping Campaigns sections of the Microsoft Advertising platform. The Microsoft Advertising Shopify app only accesses the Accounts & Billing sections of the UI to help customers add an initial payment method. All other billing functionality must be completed in the Microsoft Advertising UI.

Users can manage their shopping campaigns directly from the app without having to log into the Microsoft Advertising UI.

Payment for clicks on the Microsoft (Bing) network is made directly to Microsoft and not to Shopify. Merchants using the Shopify app pay for clicks the same way all our customers do and can be managed via the Accounts & Billing page.

3. What can users do with the Microsoft Advertising Shopify app?

The Microsoft Advertising Shopify app is designed to create and manage Microsoft Shopping campaigns. For more information on Shopping campaign functionality, see KB 3173658 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Bing Shopping & Bing Merchant Center. The product catalogue is taken directly from the customer’s product list in Shopify.

Advertisers manage their Microsoft Shopping campaigns directly from within the app itself, though they can sign-in to the Microsoft Advertising UI directly if they wish, using their Microsoft account, and manage their campaigns from within the UI directly.

By using the Microsoft Advertising app, advertisers can do the following to save time and effort:

  • Easily create and/or link their Microsoft Advertising account (aka ‘CID’) and their Microsoft Merchant Center Store (MMC) account to a Shopify account.
  • Add an initial payment method to their Microsoft Advertising UI.
  • Use Shopify as a source for their Product Feed/Catalogue in Microsoft Shopping Campaigns.
  • Use Shopify to create and manage their Microsoft Shopping campaigns directly in the Shopify app. Note that only the following Shopping campaign settings can be edited in Shopify:
    • Campaign Budget
    • Campaign Bid
    • Product Filters
  • You can see if a Shopping campaign was created in Shopify by checking either the Campaign Name, Store Name or Feed File Name. If any of these contain .myshopify.com_ShoppingCampaign, it means they were created in the app. 
  • Post-GA, the Activity Name (taken from Shopify) will also be added to the campaign name.
  • Post-GA you can also see Shopify customer’s within UCM under Tech Partner.
    [Asset 4517045]

Within the Microsoft Advertising UI, a standard Shopping campaign will be created, using the Shopify store as a replacement for the usual Product Catalogue.

Although Shopify users will normally manage their campaigns within the Shopify app, the full suite of Bing Shopping features will be available to customers within Microsoft Advertising’s UI (for example, Adding Promotional Text or Campaign Priority).

Changes made in Shopify will automatically update into Microsoft Advertising UI, and vice versa. Advertisers will be able to update Shopify by making the changes within the campaigns in the Microsoft Advertising UI.

If a Shopify store is closed, paused, or the subscription fee is not paid, then Shopify’s API will automatically pause the customer’s campaigns in Microsoft Advertising (and other partner apps) to avoid unexpected charges being added.

Note: Changes made by the Shopify app will be shown within the Change History Report, for ease of troubleshooting.

If Microsoft Advertising encounters fraud, fraud pending, or other hold scenarios, the customer will get the usual email notifications, and their Microsoft Advertising account will experience the usual interruption in performance until the issue is resolved. The Microsoft Advertising app will not display these alerts to the customer in the app interface. Non-Microsoft Shopping campaigns within Shopify will continue to run as normal.

Note: Because the Shopify app relies on an API link to be established on the back-end during campaign creation, Shopify cannot be used to connect with existing Shopping Campaigns. Existing advertisers will need to continue managing their Bing Merchant Store and Product Catalogues for non-Shopify Shopping Campaigns.

4. Support scenarios

The following Shopify-driven scenarios are in-scope for CSS Support:

  • Educating customers on how the Shopify app integrates with Microsoft Advertising (for example, “how does the partnership work?” “what can I do in Shopify to manage my Shopping campaigns?”).
  • Helping customers create and/or link their Microsoft Advertising account to Shopify.
  • Troubleshooting errors encountered using the Shopify app (for example, “I can’t click ‘publish’ to make my Shopping campaign changes”).
  • Post-setup management/troubleshooting of Shopping campaigns created through Shopify (for example, “My changes aren’t being reflected in the Microsoft Advertising UI”).
  • Troubleshooting editorial issues encountered with Shopping Campaigns.
  • Billing/payment inquiries related to Microsoft Advertising accounts created via the Shopify app.
  • Coupon code inquiries relating to Performance based coupons (PBC) received through the Microsoft Advertising Shopify app setup (only applies to new Microsoft Advertising customers).
  • Account access Requests for Microsoft account or Microsoft Advertising.

The following Shopify-driven scenarios are out-of-scope for CSS Support:

  • How to use Shopify for Store/Product creation/management (for example, “How do I set up my business in Shopify?” “How can I add new products to my Shopify Store?” “How do I delete my Google campaign in Shopify”).
  • Resolving technical issues with Shopify that are not related to Microsoft Advertising (for example, “When I click ‘Marketing’ the page is giving me an error” “I am getting latency trying to search for apps”).
  • Resolving billing issues related to Shopify subscriptions. 
  • Resolving login issues related to the Shopify platform.

For all out of scope requests, ask the customer to reach out to Shopify directly via their Contact Support Page.

4.1 Educating customers on how the Shopify app integrates with Microsoft Advertising

The Shopify app can be used to link Shopify store information to a new/existing Microsoft Advertising account. New Shopping campaigns will then be created, which use the product catalogue information from Shopify to power the ads.

This will allow advertisers to run PPC advertising on Bing’s search network via Product Ads, using rich data from Shopify to generate Product Ads based on search user inquiries.

The Shopify app also makes it easy to create a Microsoft Account if needed, and link this to a Microsoft Advertising account.

As the customers may be new to Microsoft Advertising, note the following when discussing new account creation:

  • A Microsoft account will be required to create a Microsoft Advertising account.
  • Once created, the Shopify-driven campaigns can be accessed either through Shopify or directly from the Microsoft Advertising homepage. 
  • Our advertising uses a ‘cost per click’ model, so advertisers will be charged for the clicks they receive on their Product Ads, and billed as outlined in KB 3175877 Process: Microsoft Advertising: How To Pay.
  • Shopify subscriptions are paid for separately.
  • Additional ad types and search audiences are available through our platform via non-Shopping campaigns.

4.5 Troubleshooting errors encountered using the Shopify app

If the customer encounters errors during import/publish of Shopify campaigns, confirm the following with the customer:

  • The customer has the correct CID and account associated to their Shopify Campaign.
  • The customer has successfully created and/or linked their Bing Merchant Center store to their Shopify account. 
    • Note:  There is a known issue that can prevent a Store from being visible in the Shopify app. Advertisers must make sure the URL they entered when creating a Microsoft Advertising account matches the URL they registered for their store.
  • The customer’s Shopify product catalogue is set up correctly (they will need to confirm this to us. Any setup errors here should be resolved with Shopify, as this is out of scope for CSS. Only editorial issues relating to product approvals would be solved by CSS).
  • Login to Microsoft Advertising, using the Microsoft account provided by the customer (after Fortification is complete) and check for any possible fraud or hold issues that might be preventing delivery.
    Note: If the account is less than 3 days old, remember that Shopping campaigns can take up to 3 days before delivery begins.

If the above checks have been completed, collect the following and raise an escalation to Global Support:

  • CID
  • AID account name and number
  • Campaign name
  • Shopify store name
  • Screenshot(s) of the error message and issue encountered

The team will review the back-end information and attempt to understand why the errors are being encountered. 

Unexpectedly paused campaigns (Microsoft Advertising UI)

If a customer is using the Microsoft Advertising UI to monitor their Shopify campaigns, and notices that their campaigns have been unexpectedly paused, advise them to log into Shopify and ask them to confirm there are no account issues within their larger Shopify account that might be impacting their app’s ability to serve.

Shopify will automatically pause campaigns in app services if the Shopify account is not kept up to date with subscription charges and compliance with Shopify policies.

Customers should resolve any Shopify issues with the Shopify support team first, before we continue to troubleshoot.

4.7 Coupon code inquiries relating to PBC acquired through the Shopify setup

If a customer is asking whether a coupon was applied, or when a PBC coupon will be redeemed, treat these as standard coupon inquiries and use the Microsoft Advertising UI to resolve the query.

If a customer claims to have been a new advertiser who did not receive a PBC coupon as part of their Shopify signup experience, (or if you find that a coupon was not applied to a newly created account during the Shopify creation process) take these steps:

  1. Verify that their account was newly created.
  2. Collect the customer’s XID and Shopify Store name.
  3. Share this with your SME, who can escalate to the TA. 
  4. The TA will provide you/your SME with the coupon you can use to apply on the customer’s behalf.

Note: Shopify coupons are specially minted to provide Shopify with a cashback reward when a customer register with us. For this reason, we are unable to use standard PBC coupons to resolve missing coupon inquiries. Do not apply standard PBC coupons as a replacement in this scenario, as it will directly damage our relationship with this client.

Email outreach: CSS best practice

When attempting to contact a customer who’s reached out via phone, the follow-up logic will depend on whether a CID was available:

  • With CID: 
    • Call + email on same day as the customer email was received.
    • Call + closing email if no contact with customer is made. (Use Standard non-responsive logic.)
  • Without CID:
    • Email on same day as the customer email was received
    • Closing email if no contact with customer is made. (Use Standard non-responsive logic.)

6. FAQ

Q:  I’m being told I must create a new Microsoft Merchant Center account (MMC) – does this mean I have to set up my products/catalogue all over again?
A:  Not at all! Once you’ve created your MMC in Shopify, your product catalogue will automatically be applied to your new Shopping campaigns in Microsoft Advertising. There may be a delay in delivery whilst editorial checks are run, and we’ll be able to help with any editorial concerns that might arise once the checks are completed.

Q:  I imported Shopify campaigns into my existing Microsoft Advertising shell, now I’m finding out I should have been eligible for a coupon? Where’s my coupon?
A:  Only net-new Microsoft Advertising customers are eligible for a performance-based coupon, to allow them time to familiarize themselves with how our platform works. 

Q:  Can I connect my Shopify campaigns with existing Microsoft Shopping campaigns?
A:  Currently only campaigns created in Shopify can be linked with the Shopify platform. For existing Microsoft Shopping campaigns, continue to use your Microsoft Merchant Center and Product Catalogue to manage your advertising. You can create new Shopping Campaigns via Shopify; however, be aware of any possible duplication in products.

Q:  Can I import my Shopping Campaign setup from Google?
A:  The Microsoft Shopping Shopify app does not currently support this workflow. 

Q:  I have existing Shopping Campaigns promoting the same products as my Shopify Campaigns. Do these compete against one another?  
A:  Yes – if you are promoting the same products via Shopify and standard Shopping campaigns, Bing will treat these ads as separate entities, which can lead to direct competition. To avoid unnecessary bid competition, we recommend reviewing any existing Shopping campaigns to ensure you are not duplicating your products.

Q:  I created Shopping campaigns with Shopify but now I want to switch them to a different Product Catalogue. How do I do this?
A:  Shopping Campaigns created with Shopify cannot be unlinked due to back-end API setup. If you would like to run Shopping campaigns via a non-Shopify Product Catalogue, create new Shopping campaigns in the Microsoft Advertising UI account, and pause/delete your Shopify-created Shopping campaigns as needed.

Q: How can CSS tell if a customer is a Shopify advertiser?
A: The customer will be listed as Tech Partner: Shopify in UCM. The customer’s campaign name will also contain .myshopify.com_ShoppingCampaign as part of the name.

Q:  How soon will campaigns created in Shopify begin delivery on Bing?
A:  Shopping campaigns created in Shopify will need to pass through all the same editorial checks as a standard Shopping campaign. For this reason, it can take up to three days for newly created campaigns to begin delivery.

Q:  Can we use Log Me into (LMI) view Shopify campaigns/setup?
A:  Yes, we can use LMI to walk customers through the signup/management process. Always get the customer’s permission to use LMI before you create a session.

Q:  I see a few other Microsoft Advertising related apps in the Shopify Store. Do you support these as well?
A:  Only the official ‘Microsoft Advertising’ app is supported by CSS. Other apps offering support for Microsoft Advertising are created by third-party developer teams and not in scope for our assistance.  

Q:  I want to use Promotional Text and/or Campaign Priority for my Shopify campaigns. Where do I do this?
A:  These aspects of Shopping functionality are not supported in Shopify; however, advertisers can still use these features by logging into the Microsoft Advertising UI and enabling them from within the account. By default, the Campaign Priority is set as High.

Q:  Is the pilot URL invite link specific to a single user?
A:  No – this link isn’t unique, however the pilots chosen to be included in this test are trusted sources and we don’t expect this URL to have been ‘shared around’.

Q:  Do I need to do anything else to set up UET or track conversions?
A:  No. By downloading the Microsoft Advertising app for Shopify, your UET tag is automatically created and the tag is added to every page of your website. There's nothing more for you to do.
