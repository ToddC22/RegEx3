PSTN Calling

(applies only to countries where PSTN Calling is available):
 
Setting up PSTN Calling:
  1. Buy an Office 365 license and assign it to a user. (See table for prerequisites.)
  2. Buy a Skype for Business Phone System license (unless you are using Office 365 E5, which includes Phone System) and a calling plan and assign them to a user.
    1. SfB Domestic Calling and SfB Domestic and International Calling licenses cannot be assigned to the same user – they are mutually exclusive. A tenant can purchase and use both types of licenses but can assign only one of the two to an individual user.
  3. Search for and reserve phone numbers for users. You can get new numbers or transfer existing phone numbers over to Skype for Business Online. See section on Phone number porting.
  4. Add emergency addresses and locations for your organization. For more information about emergency addresses and locations, see What are emergency locations, addresses, and call routing?
  5. Assign a phone number to a user. You will get an error message if you haven’t purchased the correct number of licenses. If you need to buy more licenses to enable a user for PSTN calling, click Buy more.
Note: Because of the latency between Office 365 and Skype for Business Online, it can take up to 24 hours for the user to be enabled for PSTN calling.
 
911 (or local equivalent emergency number) emergency calling:
  • Emergency calling capability will not be suspended if a tenant reaches their monthly calling minute cap.
  • 911 (or local equivalent emergency number) emergency calling operates differently with PSTN Calling services than on traditional telephone services. For more information, direct the customer to the following resources:
    • 911 Calling terms and conditions
    • Skype for Business Online: 911 Calling disclaimer label
 
Phone number porting:
Porting existing phone numbers to PSTN Calling:

With PSTN calling, you can either assign new phone numbers to users or take existing numbers from your phone carrier and port (transfer) them over to Skype for Business or Teams.
  • If you need fewer than 999 phone numbers for your users, you can do the porting yourself using the New Local Number Port Order wizard in the Skype for Business admin center. You can provide this link to the customer; however, if they have questions about the process, refer or transfer them to Technical Support.
  • If you have more than 999 numbers, you manually submit a port order request.
Porting PSTN Calling phone numbers to another carrier:

If a customer with PSTN calling is changing to another carrier and is porting their phone numbers out (from Microsoft to the new carrier), the carrier may ask the customer for their Microsoft account number. The customer should tell the carrier that Microsoft does not require them to have the Microsoft account number to transfer the phone numbers from Microsoft.
What happens to unused monthly, pooled minutes?
If the tenant has not used all of their calling minutes by the end of the calendar month, none of the remaining minutes carry over to the next month (i.e. no rollover). No credit will be provided for unused minutes.
What happens when PSTN calling usage exceeds the monthly, pooled minute cap?
The tenant administrator(s) will be sent emails advising them when they are approaching the cap and when they have reached the cap.

If the customer sets up a PSTN Consumption fund, calls for the monthly, pooled minute cap will be deducted from the Consumption balance.

If the customer has NOT set up a PSTN Consumption fund or if their PSTN Consumption fund falls below zero:
  • For PSTN Domestic Calling, once a tenant reaches the monthly, pooled minute cap, all PSTN Services (except for emergency calling) for numbers assigned to that tenant will be suspended for the remainder of the calendar month. Service will resume automatically on the first day of the next calendar month following suspension. On the 1 st day of the new calendar month, the pool will be refreshed with new minutes.
  • For PSTN Domestic and International Calling, service will be suspended upon exhaustion of the domestic or international monthly, pooled minute cap, whichever occurs first. Service will resume automatically on the first day of the next calendar month following suspension. On the 1st day of the new calendar month, the pool will be refreshed with new minutes.

Audio Conferencing:

Setting up Audio Conferencing:

Customer-facing information about Audio Conferencing licensing, set up, and providers can be found here.

  • Dial-in conferencing is used when users want to use a phone and call into a meeting. In Office 365, you can sign up for Skype for Business Online with dial-in conferencing that allows your users to dial-in to Skype for Business meetings using a phone instead of using a mobile device or PC.
  • Important: If users are calling into a meeting, they will only get audio. If they want instant messages (IMs), shared desktops or access to files that are being shared in the meeting, they will need to join the meeting using a mobile device or PC with the Skype for Business application installed.
  • You only need to set up dial-in conferencing for users who plan to schedule or lead meetings. Unless the organizer has locked the meeting, anyone who has the dial-in number and conference ID can join the meeting. For details, see Getting started with dial-in conferencing and Dial-in numbers available for dial-in conferencing.

    To set up dial-in conferencing with Microsoft as your dial-in conferencing provider (for support boundaries between Commerce and Technical Support, consult the Support boundary KBs listed at the end of this article)
    1. Purchase and assign an Enterprise E1, E3, E4 or E5 license to your users. See Assign or unassign licenses for Office 365 for business to assign your Office 365 licenses.

      Important: You can use Skype for Business Online Standalone Plan 2* instead of an E1, E3, E4 or E5 license but you can't use Skype for Business Online Standalone Plan 3.
    2. Purchase and assign SfB Audio Conferencing add-on licenses to users who will be scheduling or leading meetings.

      If you are using an:
      • Enterprise E5 license for your users, you don't need to purchase and assign the Audio Conferencing add-on. It is included in the Enterprise E5 license.
      • Enterprise E1, E3, E4 or SfB Online Plan 2* license for your users, you will need to purchase the Audio Conferencing add-on and assign it to users.

        Note: You should know, if you are using the plan Office 365 Enterprise E5 without Audio Conferencing, you can still use a third-party audio conferencing provider (ACP) for dial-in conferencing.
    3. Use the Skype for Business admin center > Dial-in conferencing > Dial-in users to view the user or users that were enabled to verify that they appear in the list of users and are using Microsoft as the dial-in conferencing provider.

      [Asset 4544213]

      The dashboard will show the number of users enabled for dial-in conferencing.

      [Asset 4544220]
    4. Design your meeting invitations, if you want. The dial-in numbers that are set for the user will be automatically added to the meeting invitations that are sent to attendees.
    5. You can select a different default phone number for each user if you need to. This phone number that will show up in the meeting that is scheduled by the user.

Note: For Audio Conferencing Pay-Per-Minute and Communications Credits (both part of the Audio Conferencing service), you can use your Enterprise Agreement (EA) to pay as long as the currency on the EA and the currency of the tenant are the same. In other words, if one of those Audio Conferencing subscriptions are provisioned through an EA that is set up with a different currency as the tenant, then the option to use the EA as a payment method will not be available.   6. Your cart opens. If your cart disappears from the screen, select Cart to reopen it, review the order and then choose Check Out.
  7. Complete the details from Step 1 and click Next.

Audio Conferencing Complimentary Dial Out EOL
 

The Audio Conferencing promotion allowed for complimentary dial out (with an excessive use limit of 900 minutes per user per month) effective through November 30, 2019. After that date, the steady-state subscription limit is 60 minutes per user per month (pooled at the tenant level) that can be applied to dial out to Zone A countries. Dial out to Zone A countries in excess of this monthly allotment of minutes, (and dial out to non Zone-A/Pay as you go) countries are billed on a per minute basis using Communications Credits. For further details, refer to this FAQ.

Due to mixed messaging, some customers may have been under the impression that this dial out promotion applied only to international minutes, and that unlimited domestic dial out would always be included. For customers who present this specific reason, it is the intent to allow them to have dial out of 900 minutes/user/month until the end of their contract.
