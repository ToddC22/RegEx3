Procedure: Account verification scenarios

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer

What is this article about?

This article describes the procedure for different scenarios you may face when performing account verification.

Table of contents

  1. Cannot verify account

  2. Help customers even if they have failed verification

  3. Account verification is not required

When to verify account: If you are accessing customer information, or potentially modifying customer information, you’ll need to verify the account.

Note: This does not apply for webform requests, see KB 4458819 Procedure: Xbox: Web-form Refunds.

1. Cannot verify account

Scenario

Advocate handling

Customer calls in wanting to use a Microsoft account that they don’t own.

Only assist a customer who owns the account they’re calling about. A customer calling in for a different account isn’t seen as the owner, even if they have access to it.

You cannot assist the customer using someone else's Microsoft account.

Customer wants to use a credit card that has a different name and different billing address from the shipping address.

You are authorized to use the credit card that the customer is providing as long as the customer can provide the full information required for us to place an order (CC#, expiration date, billing address, CVV code).

Note: If the card is already on the customer’s account, then we’ll just use the established payment method, and the customer can provide the last four digits of the card and the CVV code. CVV is always required.

HUP customer has access to the business email account associated with the order but doesn’t remember the last 4 digits of the credit card used to place it.

Provide the download link to the customer’s email address associated with the order. You cannot provide any details regarding the order since the customer’s account cannot be verified or pass manual Personal Data Verification.

KB 4337656 Informational: Office: Home Use Program

Customer calls in stating that their card was stolen, and they see charges from Microsoft Store.

Instruct the customer to contact their financial institution and refute the charge. We cannot assist the customer in a post-sale scenario since we cannot determine that they are the true card holder.

KB 2896711 Procedure: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops

Customer provides a Microsoft account that’s different than the one associated to the order.

Inform the customer that we cannot assist the customer with this Microsoft account. The customer must provide the account used to place the order. All Microsoft Store Online orders require a Microsoft account for them to be placed, and thus the proper account and verification is required.

Exception: HUP orders are often placed using a work email that’s not a Microsoft account. You can assist HUP orders by using manual Personal Data Verification.

KB 4337656 Informational: Office: Home Use Program

Customer is contacting Microsoft through a translator via Phone (Customer is deaf) to help manage their account.

Continue the call with the interpreter and allow them to verify account but proceed with providing support.

If the customer has any further questions, they can be referred to the Disability Answer Desk for either Chat support or American Sign Language Support (US only). We have a team of advocates who provide Video Phone support for customers who are Deaf and speak American Sign Language (ASL). This offering is available from 8am – 5pm PT.

You can find details for contacting the Disability Answer Desk, including ASL at this site: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/answerdesk/accessibility.

2. Help customers even if they have failed verification

We must verify all customer contacts in order to protect customer's personal data and follow Microsoft privacy and security requirements. Scenarios where verification may be challenging, but where we can still help customers, and exceptions to the account verification requirement, are detailed below.

Scenario

Advocate handling

RRT

RRT (product safety) scenarios must always be completed, even if a customer cannot be verified. For more information on handling RRT calls, see KB 2764515 Procedure: Surface & Xbox: Handling Product Safety (RRT) Contacts.

Connectivity issues – out of the box experience

New customers might not have a Microsoft account yet when they call us for help. In this case you provide connectivity and account creation support. Look up the Microsoft account the customer created and verify at the end of the call to make sure they have their Microsoft account, password, and security proofs setup correctly. Customers who already have a Microsoft account that they wish to use during account sign up, or who have the ability to sign up for an account with another device should be verified at the beginning of the call as normal. If they are unable to be verified, resolve their connectivity issues in addition to provide account access assistance.

Unauthorized access (UA)

If a customer is experiencing a UA issue and cannot pass be verified because they do not have control of the account, do not code the call as "Customer cannot pass verification." If you must escalate a customer to Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA), they do not have to be verified. For additional details, see KB 928204 Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges.

Customers who only know the payment instrument information

We can provide some limited help to customers who are only able provide the payment instrument. For additional details see KB 2885545 Procedure: Handling a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner. This scenario includes subscription cancellation requests for deceased Xbox account owners.

Hardware customers who have no internet access/Microsoft account who request hardware service

If a customer does not wish to disclose any account information, we can still create a warranty repair for that customer. If the customer has an account and cannot be verified, you should help the customer regain access to their account before submitting the repair order. The follow-up support options for repair orders created for “offline” customers is extremely limited, which is why we should always attempt to create repair orders with a verified account. For more information about how to handle "offline customers" calls, see KB 2904202 Procedure: Xbox: Creating repair or fulfillment orders for offline" customers".

People’s Republic of China note: All hardware repair customers must be verified in PRC. Customers who fail verification should not have an offline repair order created.

3. Account verification is not required

The following scenarios do not require you to try to authenticate the customer before providing assisted support. For all the following scenarios where a Service Request (SR) is created, do not code the root cause as "Customer failed authentication."

Scenario

Advocate handling

Xbox One out of the box experience (OOBE)
 

Customers calling with an Xbox One out of the box experience should not be verified. Associate their Microsoft account, if they have one, and proceed to troubleshooting the case. If there is time during the interaction (they are downloading a system update, or some other time intensive procedure) and have access to a computer, we should offer to assist them with creating a Microsoft account.

Microsoft account password/proofs issues

Microsoft account status tool should be leveraged to understand the customer’s scenario. Be aware that just because a customer presents with proof or password issues, we should not assume that they have exhausted all proof/password opportunities to prove account validity.

Misroute transfers

If a customer reaches you with an issue that is out of that your scope of support (that is a customer who has a billing issue reaches a hardware advocate), that customer will have to be transferred to the correct line of business. You cannot transfer the verification status together with the customer, and we do not want the customer to have to be verified twice. For more information about how to transfer a customer see KB 3163896 Procedure: All-up landing page for Phone and Chat Transfer.

Prank contacts/abusive/threatening

Customers who are clearly contacting support only to prank call, abuse or threaten you and can be identified as such before the verification step do not have to be verified. For Xbox See KB 2626175 Procedure: Handling abusive customers.

Ghost calls/quick disconnect

Calls that end before you can locate a customer account will not result in an SR and therefore will not require verification. If a caller can be identified through Click2Call or Click2Chat information and is not verified, and your call back attempt fails, an SR must be created, but verification is not required. For more information about how to handle a ghost call, see KB 3099069 Procedure: Call Handling: Holds, disconnects, callbacks and ghost calls.

Law enforcement inquiries around stolen consoles or compromised accounts

Law enforcement officials requesting information about a stolen console or compromised account are expected to provide their information but are not required to be verified before the you take appropriate action. For more details, see KB 950232 Procedure: Legal: How to respond to information requests from law enforcement.

Customers who are not Xbox customers and have fraudulent charges

Non-Xbox customers that call in regarding charges on their payment instrument and have not enabled their payment instrument to be used by someone who is an Xbox customer will not have to pass verification. For more information see KB 928204 Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges.
