Procedure: Surface: CSV offer for Surface Pro 4 (SP4) scramble issues

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Surface Consumer

What is this article about?

This article explains how to handle the Currency Stored Value (CSV) offer for customers with Surface Pro 4 (SP4) screen scramble issues. This procedure is for customers outside the three-year replacement policy for SP4 with scramble issues. All Surface advocates can extend a CSV offer or repair fee discount for specific countries so that affected customers can receive this discount. This article should be followed when a customer is experiencing SP4 scramble issues and is outside of the three-year replacement program. See section 1 for devices and countries valid for this offer.

Note: This is published to the Store portal as FYI in case of escalations due to issues with POBO or issuing the CSV. Store advocates should transfer new cases to Surface for validation of the technical issue before any offer can be made.

2. Identifying and confirming the offer

Notes:

  • The fraud team monitors this procedure. Abuse of this procedure will be handled appropriately.
  • If the customer is calling back to take advantage of this offer, review case notes and verify all steps have been taken, then proceed with step 5, Customer agrees to the CSV offer.

1. Confirm that the customer is experiencing a Surface Pro 4 screen scramble issue and troubleshooting steps have failed. See Wiki page Surface-Pro-4 Known Issues for the proper procedure.

2. Confirm that the customer has not previously received this offer on this device.

3. Ensure the device with the scramble issue is linked to their account. If not, ask the customer to register their device.

4. Confirm that the device is outside of the three-year offered exchange period. If it is, follow the messaging below.

Hong Kong, Taiwan, or Korea

  • We may complete an out of warranty order for your device for a 20% discount on the standard out of warranty price.

All other countries from table in section 1.

“It does appear that your device is outside of the three-year exchange period that Microsoft is offering for this issue. We are unable to exchange the device for you for free at this time. However, we would like to offer you two options.

  • The first option is a [enter local currency amount] credit towards only the purchase of a new [list the valid devices from section 1] from our Microsoft Online Store.
  • The second option is that we may also complete an out of warranty order for your device for the standard out of warranty price.

If you would like to take advantage of the credit offer, I can help you with the selection and purchase of a new device from our online store and provide the [enter local currency amount] credit immediately.”

5. The customer will make one of three choices. If the customer asks to get the offer but make the selection through the Microsoft Online Store, inform them that this offer is not currently available using that method. Then, follow the directions based on the choice made:

  • The customer agrees to the CSV offer
  1. Update the case notes using the following template:
  • Customer called in with SP4 screen scramble issue on device. Customer is outside of the three-year exchange period for this issue and troubleshooting steps did not resolve the issue. Customer did accept the [discount or CSV offer].
  1. Code the case.
  • Scenario: Screen
  • Issue: Scramble
  • How did I resolve the customer issue?: OOW - $200 CSV Offer – Accepted
  • Achieve More: Yes
  • Achieve More Product: Surface
  • Achieve More Campaign: Get Current
  • Customer Interested: Yes
  1. Follow the appropriate steps based on the customer’s country.
  • Hong Kong, Taiwan, or Korea
  • Process an OOW order in SOM tool setting the Problem Type to Display Scramble to ensure the discount is applied.
  • All other countries from table in section 1
  • Proceed to step 6.
  • The customer agrees to the standard out of warranty option
  1. Update the case notes using the following template:
  • Customer called in with SP4 screen scramble issue on device. Customer is outside of the three-year exchange period for this issue and troubleshooting steps did not resolve the issue. The Customer did not accept the [discount or CSV offer]. Following normal out of warranty option.
  1. Code the case.
  • Scenario: Screen
  • Issue: Scramble
  • How did I resolve the customer issue?: OOW - $200 CSV Offer – Rejected
  • Achieve More: Yes
  • Achieve More Product: Surface
  • Achieve More Campaign: Get Current
  • Customer Interested: No
  1. Proceed to process an out of warranty request, see KB 4039212 Procedure: Surface: Out of Warranty Support.
  •  The customer wants to delay their decision, or rejects all options
  1. Update the case notes using the following template:
  • Customer called in with SP4 screen scramble issue on device. Customer is outside of the three-year exchange period for this issue and troubleshooting steps did not resolve the issue. Customer did not accept the [discount or CSV offer] at this time and [fill in what the customer decided to do].
  1. Code the case.
  • Scenario: Screen
  • Issue: Scramble
  • How did I resolve the customer issue?: OOW - $200 CSV Offer – Rejected
  • Achieve More: Yes
  • Achieve More Product: Surface
  • Achieve More Campaign: Get Current
  • Customer Interested: No
  1. Use the messaging and email template in section 3.
  2. Proceed to close the case.

6. Determine if the customer already knows which device they’d like to purchase and use the CSV credit.

  • If they do not know what device they want, point to the Surface Product landing page and have them look at the valid devices from section 1, if available locally.
  • https://www.microsoft.com/store/collections/surfacelist?headerid=department-surface
  • If the customer knows which of the allowed devices they want to purchase, proceed to section 3 to help the customer with their purchase and adding the CSV.

3. Customer delays options

If they would like some time to consider the offer, inform them that this is a limited time offer and that we cannot guarantee that it will be available outside of 15 days from today.

Messaging:

“I will send you a follow up email containing this information. Please call back in under 15 days to take advantage of this offer.”

Continue with the interaction and send the below email template to the customer.

Email template:

“Hello,

Thank you for taking the time to talk to me today. As discussed, I am sending this email to confirm that you would like some time to consider the offer of [enter local currency amount] towards the purchase of a new Surface Pro 6, Surface Pro 7, Surface Pro X, Surface Book 3 or Surface Laptop 3 device. This offer is good for a limited time, so please take advantage of it as soon as possible. Please be sure to call back and reference this case number [#####] to take advantage of this offer.

If you have any further questions, please feel free to reply to this email.

(Signature)”

4. Adding CSV

Notes: 

  • The customer must be verified prior to beginning this process. Follow proper account verification process prior to adding the CSV to the customer’s account.
  • The credit can only be given if an actual purchase is being made.
  • Surface advocates should only proceed with this if the customer knows which Surface device (not accessory) they want to buy, and they are buying it now.
  • At any time in the process of adding CSV, if you get an error, get disconnected during this process or experience poor internet connectivity after or during submitting, do not attempt to reapply the CSV. Inform a Tech Lead and inform the customer there is a tool issue and that you will finish the order and contact them within next business day.
  1. If necessary, point the customer to the Surface Product landing page and have them look at the valid devices (found in section 1), if available locally.
    • https://www.microsoft.com/store/collections/surfacelist?headerid=department-surface
  2. Ensure the customer has added a payment method to their account. The payment method must be added before Surface advocates can proceed. The payment method must contain the current billing address before proceeding.
    1. Direct the customer to Change the payment method or credit card linked to your Microsoft account for instructions on adding or updating a payment method to their account.
    2. You may have to refresh your SOM tool to see the payment method was added by the customer.
  3. Add the valid device the customer wants to purchase to their order. If the customer would like to add to the order they may.
    • Remember, they must purchase at least one of the valid devices required for this CSV offer to be valid. See section 1 for valid devices.

5. FAQ

Q: Why am I limited to only certain devices?

A: Currently, this offer only extends to the devices mentioned as it is to replace your current Surface Pro with a newer device.

Q: What should an advocate do if they encounter an error or system failure while adding CSV, in order to avoid multiple CSV submissions?

A: You should wait one business day to see whether the CSV was successfully added/applied to the Microsoft account. Do not attempt to reapply CSV without waiting to see if it was processed. If needed, reach out to your Tech Lead.

Q: Are customers eligible for the SP4 Screen Scramble Offer if they are still within the extended policy period of three years within device purchase?

A: No, customers are only eligible for this offer if they are outside of the extended policy period. If an exception is needed, escalate following the standard escalation process.

Q: Can a customer receive an additional discount on top of the $200USD (or equivalent) CSV offered?

A: Yes, just as a gift card, customers are able to apply the CSV to any device that is already discounted. Military or student discounts on top of SP4 CSV offer is possible/acceptable.

Q: What should an advocate do if the customer is requesting a receipt for their purchase?

A: Microsoft Store receipts can be found at https://account.microsoft.com/ under Order History.

Q: Can advocates re-direct customers to the Sales Team for any SP4 Screen Scramble CSV purchase?

A: No. The Sales team now has the guidance to direct customer back to CSS if they claim a discount has been offered by CSS. Sales advocates should no longer provide CSV for this program.

Q: What if a customer claims they have seen the device they want to purchase at a better price at a third-party store?

A: Unfortunately, we can’t match third-party prices. If the customer finds a better deal, they can purchase a device with the other partner without the SP4 discount.

Q: What if a customer claims they just saw the device at a better price at the MS Store, but now the price has increased?

A: If the customer decided to purchase a device after a price increase, then we can’t really help them. However, if we weren’t able to finish the transaction because of an issue within CSS and the price increased, then we should help the customer get the original lower price that was quoted; this is why you should always document the quoted price at time offered to customer. Recommend that the customer contacts CSS as soon as they see a deal they want to take at the MS Store, before it changes.

Q: Can a customer purchase more than one item as the advocate creates the POBO for the SP4 Screen Scramble Offer?

A: As long as the customer is purchasing one eligible Surface Device with offered CSV, then the customer is free to purchase additional items together, as needed.

Q: What is the return policy for a device purchased using CSV?

A: The standard return policy applies for any device purchased through the MS Store. The CSV will be credited back to the same Microsoft account used during the original purchase.

Q: What are the shipping options and SLA for a device purchased using CSV?

A: Standard for any device purchased through the MS Store for their country.
