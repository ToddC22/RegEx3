Procedure: Documenting Case Notes

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor

What is this article about?

Documenting case detail allows for clear knowledge of the case. It allows any engineer visibility into the resolution activities in progress and any activities that have already taken place. Documenting case notes is the responsibility of the case owner and any other support personnel that collaborate on the customer's incident. It is important to document the support incident in detail and the steps taken to resolve it. Up to date and accurate documenting case note plays a critical role in our understanding of a customer's incident and its status. Documenting case note is also important because it may be used by others to drive product improvement or solve other cases.

Note: Due to feature differences in the ticketing systems, some guidance in this article may not be applicable.

Steps

Keep all notes up to date

  • Any technical progress must be documented in the case notes, not just in communications.

Customer or Partner interactions

  • Document every customer or partner interaction as soon as possible.
  • Add case notes, using an Action Plan Template or Summary Template every day there is customer or partner contact. Action Plan Templates or Summary Templates should be used even where there is no current technical action plan in place, to document when Microsoft will next reach out to the customer or partner and what the next actions will be. Create a new Follow-up task to document when Microsoft will next reach out to the customer or partner. 

Case Transfers and Escalation

  • If transferring or escalating a Case:
    • Follow the proper transfer procedure and document in case notes.
    • If escalating to a known person (direct escalation) paste in and complete a Summary template.
    • If escalating to a queue paste in and complete a Transfer template.
    • Complete the templates in English (except Japan and Korea).

Collaboration

  • If collaborating on a case first:
    • Create a Request Assistance or a Collaboration task.
    • Paste in the summary template.
    • Complete the summary template in English (except Japan and Korea).

Case Titles

  • All cases must have a clear, accurate technical description of the issue in the case title if the case title allows for modification.
  • Case coding and contact dates can be used in the Internal SR title if required and available. 

Case Coding

  • Keep the support topic, Support Area and product coding accurate.
  • Keep the "Disposition"/"Status" updated to reflect the current status of the case.
  • Set the symptom(s), cause(s) and resolution coding accurately before closure.

Case Language

  • Complete Summary and case Transfer templates in English (except Japan and Korea).
  • Ensure the solution/resolution is in English (exception Japan and Korea) as the solution/resolution is customer viewable.
  • If you are working with the customer in local language, provide customer with local language email on the solution.

Customer or Partner Data, PII, and Microsoft Confidential Information

  • Use notes for documenting proprietary information that must not be shared with the customer/partner
  • Handle all customer/partner Data and PII according to 4457137 | Policy: Customer Data
