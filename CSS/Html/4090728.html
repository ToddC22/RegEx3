How To Create or Request Internal Content

DO NOT EDIT THIS ARTICLE WITHOUT CONSULTING WITH THE CXP KNOWLEDGE MANAGEMENT TEAM. Please email ContentIdea Triage Owners - citriageowners@microsoft.com to request an update.

This article discusses how to create or request internal content. The content is intended for support agents, customer service agents, and other internal Microsoft Support roles.

This article covers the following audiences:

  • Commercial
  • Consumer
  • PPP

PPP & Tech TS differences

Internal policy, process & procedure articles

  • Contains info for case decision-making or internal tool handling.
  • Usually multi-products (may have exceptions).
  • Info usually can’t be shared with customers.

Technical troubleshooting articles

  • Contains product specifications or technical troubleshooting steps for a product fix.
  • Usually product-specific (may have exceptions).
  • Info can be shared, or steps can be walked through with customers.

Use Content Idea Auto-publish to create internal Portico articles

This Solution Creation Guidelines document can help you decide when content is needed, and provide steps to create clear, complete content that can be searched in Service Desk Knowledge.

1. Getting access to Content Idea

IMPORTANT!! Go to http://contentidea . This auto-enrolls you in our database. You can close this window once you see the screen below. NOTE: You only have to do this once. If you get “Access Denied” using the template links below, you probably did not perform this step.

[Asset 4459496]

2. Click the orange tile to open the CI bug template.

You can create a content auto-publish article  in two ways:

  1. Create a template from an active or closed work item (recommended )
    [Asset 4586930]
  2. Complete the work item from scratch each time
  3. Create a template from scratch (not recommended)
    [Asset 4586931]

3. Complete the following CI fields as shown below so that Content Idea Auto Publish can process your request:

  • Purpose of Request = “Create new content for me”
  • Requested Content Type = “*KB article or /Help article”
  • Confidentiality = “Internal Content”
  • Assigned To = “Active”
  • Article Type = <Choose Break-fix or Informational. Informational limits you to the Summary and More Information fields only>
  • Portico Team = <choose one>

4. Fill out the Title (IMPORTANT: THIS IS THE TITLE OF YOUR ARTICLE!)

5. Complete any remaining required fields for Business Area, Primary Product, Support Topic, as applicable.

How do I keep from publishing while I edit? (I want to save my work but publish later).

To work on an internal article without publishing it until you are ready, you can assign the bug to yourself. (The assigned field is right below the Title and is usually assigned to 'Active') When you are ready to publish, change the value back to ‘Active’ and save. Note that Assigned To=Active is one of the required fields to auto-publish.

6. Add your content:

a) Download the Document template for best results. You can find the link in the Content Idea work item in the "Content Idea Auto Publish" tab:

[Asset 4477022]

b) Create a draft of the issue summary containing any additional information from the case logs, such as:

  • error messages or event IDs, logs, stack traces     
  • related bugs
  • related content links
  • related cases

IMPORTANT: Check screenshots and text, and remove all customer PII info, including IP addresses, Server names, Domain names and so on. If using a demo account, only use company and individual names as directed in CELA’s Approved fictitious names & guidelines site.

c) Save your Word draft onto your computer.

d) Attach your content in a Word doc to the CI request. The attachments tab is the furthest right, below the header.

7. Save. Your article will be created in 10 to 20 minutes. You will receive a confirmation email with the link.

8. Note : If you want to confirm that the content is being extracted, wait a half minute or so, then refresh the work item while watching the fields in the Internal Technical Content' tab. You should see the content appear after about a minute. (This can take longer if you have a lot of formatting or images in the doc). This step is optional, but you should do it at least for one article to make sure you are following the correct process.

9. In 10 to 20 minutes, refresh the CI request and you should see a Content Link and KB Article Number in the respective fields below the title. Test the link and make sure your content is in the article as you intended.

How to update an existing internal article

You can easily edit an internal article by clicking the 'Content Idea ID' link below the title to open the request, browse to the “Internal Technical Content' tab, and edit the content fields.

Get notified of new or updated articles daily

  1. Join a Channel for your product on Microsoft Teams
    • Go to the CSS Content Notification team and view the channels below it.
    • Click the star by the channel(s) you want to follow, to Favorite it.
    • Click the “…” by your product or area channel and choose “Follow this channel.”
  2. You will get a daily summary of published articles for your channel either in email (if you are logged out of Teams) or a Teams notification if you are logged into Teams. Simply check your channel in Teams for the latest conversation.

CONSUMER

Procedure for Technical Troubleshooting articles:

WINDOWS

Submit a request to the Content Partnership Council (CPC) by filling out the template at: http://aka.ms/windowscpc.

Mandatory fields:

  • Title
  • Description – Please fill out all sections included in the Description field to the best of your abilities, including the business justification. Submissions without a business justification are likely to be declined by the triage team.
  • Release (If it is new or emerging issue for a specific release of Windows, such as RS4, select the release version. If it is an ongoing technical issue, select “Continuous Publishing”.)
  • PM Owner (located on the “Team” tab) – CSS members are recommended to add the SPM that owns the issue to this field.

OFFICE

For Office Consumer Internal content requests, the link to use is https://aka.ms/cssofficeinternalci.

OTHER PRODUCTS

PPP

To gain permission to access the Content Tracking template:

Follow these steps to get permissions to the Consumer\Consumer instance on IRT

  • In IDWeb, search for IRT_CS3_Contrib.
  • Click on IRT_CS3_Contrib
  • Click OK to submit your request
  • Alternatively, JOIN the CS3 IRT instance (grants access to Consumer\Consumer)

To connect to the Azure DevOps (ADO) server:

You can connect to ADO several different ways.  

  • Visual Studio: This application provides all of the features of ADO. Please visit https://aka.ms/DogfoodVisualStudio for guidance on installing all supported Visual Studio versions, product lines, and tools.
    • Setup the Server
      • Launch Visual Studio
      • From the View menu select View Team Explorer
      • Click on the Manage Connections icon, then Connect to a Project> Add Azure DevOps Server
      • Select the target server, iteration, and area path
      • Select Connect from the multifunction button

Consumer Content Tracking template instructions:

  • Apply the Advocate Content Triage template or fill in all required fields with pertinent information.
  • Title: {Affected Product}: {~10-word description of proposed change}
  • Area Path: CSS IRT\Consumer\Consumer
  • Assigned to: (blank)
  • Triage Status:
    • Pre-triage - Work in progress, not ready for intake review
    • Triage - (Default) Ready for initial review and assignment to an Enablement team member
    • Info Needed - Additional information needed from requestor to proceed
    • In Progress - Assigned to an Enablement team member, not ready for governance review
    • On Hold - Work on hold due to internal or external factors; Required - note describing roadblock and expected next action date
    • Technical review - Work complete, ready for governance and publication
  • Content Type: KB - Agent Facing, or other option as appropriate
  • Change requested:
    • Add: New Content
    • Change: Editorial Revisions Only (minor word choice and typography)
    • Change: Technical Content (process changes, adding sections, changes to troubleshooting)
  • Priority:
    • For critical Pri 1 requests, you must email the Delivery Enablement Support Team using the priority 1 request template. Allow 24 hour SLA
    • Per chart in 4464195 Consumer: Service Level & Agreement (SLA), set to 3 by default
  • Product Family
    • Multi-Products, if applicable to more than one product line
    • Select specific product family, if narrowly applicable
  • Primary Product
    • Multi-Products, if applicable to more than one product model
    • Select specific product model if only one applicable
