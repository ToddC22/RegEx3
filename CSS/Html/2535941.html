1. Local listings and claiming the business

1.1 What are local listings and how does Bing Places work?

  • Local listings are free business listings which appear on Bing Local. They contain basic business information such as name, address, phone number, and additional insights collected from a variety of online data sources (known as feeds). These feeds can be third-party websites such as TripAdvisor, Yellow Pages, or Yext.  
  • Because Bing pulls information from these feeds and merges (collates) the information to form a business listing, (known as a YPID) many advertisers will be listed on Bing Local before they’ve registered with Bing Places. 
  • Bing Places for Business is the platform which advertisers can use to either claim or create a business listing on Bing Local. This gives advertisers more control over the accuracy of their YPID listing and allows them to add additional information such as business name, contact information, hours of operation, or images.
  • Bing Places is currently available for:

    • The United States (US-EN)

    • Canada (CA-EN, CA-FR)

    • The United Kingdom (UK)

    • India (IN)

    • Austria (AU)

    • Germany (DE)

    • Italy (IT)

    • Spain (ES)

    • Brazil (BR)

    • Mexico (MX)

    • AT 

    • CH-DE

    • Hong Kong (KK)

    • Taiwan (TW)

    • CN

1.2 Claiming the business 

  1. Search: The advertiser searches for their business, either using Bing or the Bing Places platform.
  2. Claim or Create: See KB 2964669 Procedure: Bing Places: How to claim, edit, or submit a listing for more information.
    • Claim: If a YPID listing already exists on Bing, the advertiser can claim this directly.
    • Create: If a business does not already exist on Bing, the advertiser creates a new business listing in Bing Places and enters information like the business name and address.
    • SMB users can also import directly from Google My Business. See KB 4337642 Process: Bing Places: Account Imports for more information.
  3. Verify: Before Bing Places publishes any changes, the advertiser must prove they own (or are affiliated with) the business.
  4. Publish: Once a user has verified their affiliation with a business, Bing Places will submit their changes to the data pipeline for publish on Bing’s local listings. See KB 3138298 Process: Bing Places: ​Publishing Cycle for more information.

3. Getting started ﻿ 

3.1 How to sign into Bing Places ﻿ 

Unlike Microsoft Advertising, the Bing Places system allows customers to use a variety of sign in options. See KB 4021987 Bing Places: Login Options & Issue Resolution for more information.   

To begin managing their Bing Local listings through the Bing Places platform:

  1. Navigate to http://www.bingplaces.com.
  2. Select either New User or Existing User to begin the process:
    • Existing User redirects to the sign-in page. Once the user has signed in with a valid Provider ID, they are taken straight to the Bing Places dashboard homepage. Any claimed listings they already have will be visible here.
    • Selecting New User will take the customer to a new page where they are prompted to either import from Google My Business (KB 4337642 Process: Bing Places: Account Imports) or claim or add your business manually (KB 2964669 Procedure: Bing Places: How to claim, edit, or submit a listing).

Note: The Microsoft account used to sign into Bing Places is displayed at the top right corner or the webpage.

  1. Selecting the Help link in the upper right corner will open in-product help content.

3.3 Preferences page and notifications options

Once a user is logged in to Bing Places, they can update their personal details via the Preferences page (located in the drop-down menu at the top-right of the screen, after selecting their User ID).

The Preferences page has two tabs: Profile and Notifications.

Profile tab:

The Profile tab contains basic advertiser information such as name, phone, and email.

  • If an advertiser has many unread emails, a callout on this page will note the number of emails that were unread and invite the advertiser to update their preferred Primary Email Address.
  • Advertisers will also have the option to set up Facebook Messenger notifications on this page, so that they can receive account alerts and activity updates via the Facebook Messenger app.

    [Asset 4497422]

Note: Unread email alerts, and Facebook Messenger prompts, will also be displayed within the Bing Places Dashboard, on the upper left of the page within the Contact Information section.

Notifications tab:

  • The Notifications tab contains a list of email alert types, and the option to opt-out of these individually. Advertisers can use these options to control the flow of information sent to them in relation to Bing Places.
  • Selecting Unsubscribe on any Bing Places email communication will also take the advertiser to this page of the web UI.

4. Managing a business listing

Customers should always search for their business first, before creating a new listing. Often a YPID listing will already be on Bing and this can simply be claimed and updated. 

To begin this process:

  1. Sign into Bing Places, as described earlier.
  2. Select New User and then select Claim or add your business manually.

Note: You can also select Add New Business on the Bing Places dashboard homepage to begin this process.

  1. Search for the business. Depending on the market, customers may either search by phone number or business name + location.
  2. If they can see their business, select Claim and Edit Details.
  3. If they can’t see their business, they should select Create New Business.
    • Both steps four and five will take the customer to a new page where they can review/edit their business information. If they are claiming a listing, then the fields will be pre-populated, but should be checked for accuracy prior to selecting Submit.
    • Note: Customers can also claim listings directly from Bing’s local listing results. Selecting the Is this your business? hyperlink (located beneath the local listing) will prompt the user to sign in and take them to the editing page. See KB 2964669 Procedure: Bing Places: How to claim, edit, or submit a listing for detailed information on this process.

5. Verification process

Listing Verification is the process of verifying the ownership of a business listing. Newly created listings are published on Bing only when verification and publication are complete.

Bing Places provides users with different verification options. The option provided is based on whether the business is new or if it already exists in Bing.

  • Postal Mail
  • Email
  • Phone
  • Text (SMS)
  • Online (only available in EN-US)

For complete information on the verification options, process, and support scenarios, see KB 2964704 Process: Bing Places: Business listing verification.

After a business listing has been verified it is submitted for publishing.

7. Third-party feed information and publishing ﻿

  • During the publishing process, external data feed sources will be Conflated (merged) together to create a single consolidated YPID listing for the customer.
  • If a customer chooses not to enter their address information on third-party sites, or to hide their address information, the address data will not be sent to Bing. This can lead to difficulties in matching a YPID listing created in Bing Places against a YPID listing generated by third-party feeds. This is often the cause of duplicate listings on Bing or missing third-party reviews for listings created in Bing Places.
  • The best option for customers is to claim an existing YPID listing to ensure the third-party feeds are already matched against the YPID.

8. Insights and tips (UI performance data)

Once a listing has been claimed in Bing Places, advertisers will be able to see data for how their listing is performing on Bing. This is accessible via the My Listing page, after an advertiser chooses a listing to view:

[Asset 4538769]

Selecting View Detailed Insights or View Insights and Tips will take the advertiser to a new page in the UI, where they can see performance insights for their listings.

Advertisers will also be able to choose several viewing options:

  • Your Business
  • Average Similar Businesses
  • Popular Similar Businesses

For Similar Businesses, advertisers can sort down by zip code, city, state, or country depending on how locally they operate.

Advertisers can also switch timeframe view between Daily or Weekly views, and choose the lookback period for either Last 4, 8, or 12 weeks, and apply filters between All, Search Page, Map, Desktop, or Mobile performance.

[Asset 4555942]

On the right of the page, advertisers will also see suggestions on how to improve the listing data and grow their online presence, such as adding deals/discounts or trying Microsoft Advertising. (Suggestions vary per customer).

Advertisers will also be able to see a Top Query list, showing the frequency of impressions, clicks, and average position of their listing.

Note: The recommendations offered are intended only to help improve the listing quality. These changes will not improve ranking position on Bing’s local listing results.

﻿9. Disallowed business models

Although Bing Places allows advertisers to promote themselves on Bing’s Local Listings results page, certain business models are not allowed to display on Bing Local. 

These are:

  • Real estate companies attempting to list individual realty properties (for example, for sale or rent) as an independent local listing.

Note: They can still list their office locations.

  • Online-only businesses, who do not have a physical local presence.
  • Although an advertiser may initially be able to get a Bing Places listing for such disallowed models to display on Bing Local, our automated crawlers and manual editorial reviewers will pick these up over time and the listings will be removed from the Local Listings results.
  • If you are asked why we do not allow such listings, use the following messaging:

    “    The intent of our Bing Local Listings is to return relevant local results to a search user, so that they can view or visit the location of a business in their area. After reviewing your business in detail, it seems that your business model does not fit this scenario.

To drive the most customers to your business via Bing, I would like to recommend using both our free Webmaster resources (to get the most out of our organic search results), and the Microsoft Advertising platform, which will allow you to create and customize sponsored results on Bing.”

Disallowed content

  1. In addition to the above business models, advertisers may also be disallowed due to failure to comply with our Microsoft Services Agreement Code of Conduct.
    • Don’t do anything illegal.
    • Don’t engage in any activity that exploits, harms or threatens to harm children.
    • Don’t send spam. Spam is unwanted or unsolicited bulk email, postings, contact requests, SMS (text messages) or instant messages.
    • Don’t publicly display or use the Services to share any inappropriate content or other material (involving, for example, nudity, bestiality, pornography, offensive language, graphic violence, or criminal activity).
    • Don’t engage in activity that is fraudulent, false, or misleading (for example, asking for money under false pretences, impersonating someone else, manipulating the Services to increase play count, or affect rankings, ratings or comments).
    • Don’t wilfully circumvent any restrictions on access to or availability of the services.
    • Don’t engage in activity that is harmful to you, the services, or others (for example, transmitting viruses, stalking, posting terrorist content, communicating hate speech, or advocating violence against others).
    • Don’t infringe upon the rights of others (for example, unauthorised sharing of copyrighted music or other copyrighted material, or the resale or other distribution of Bing maps or photographs).
    • Don’t engage in activity that violates the privacy of others.
    • Don’t help others break these rules.
  2. In these scenarios, do not attempt to assist the advertiser in resolving their support query. Instead, use the following messaging: "After reviewing your business in detail, it seems you are not compliant with the terms and conditions of our Microsoft Services Agreement Code of Conduct. As a result, we are unable to offer support in this scenario."
  3. Use the issue coding User Education > Disallowed Business Model to close your ticket in ASD.

11. Locked accounts

In some circumstances, Bing Places accounts may be locked by engineering. Locked accounts will no longer submit data to the pipeline for publish.

  • Accounts are locked if the listings are not updated after one year. After this time, engineering will send an email to the customer, to ask if they still wish to advertise with us. Customers that confirm that they are no longer advertising can request engineering to lock their account.

Note: Currently, we will never lock an account without the customer’s permission.

  • Advertisers who previously locked their accounts may contact you to ask for their account to be unlocked. In this scenario, ask them to first refresh their listings and confirm the accuracy of any current data.

Once this is done, create an escalation to Global Support, and share the Provider ID of the account which the customer would like to have unlocked.

  • Engineering will unlock the account within 24 hours and listing changes will then publish in one publish cycle.
  • If you are contacted by a customer who has received an engineering email to lock their account, advise: 

    • If they want to keep advertising: Resubmit their data in Bing Places to confirm listing accuracy.

    • If they want to lock their account: Reply to the engineering email, asking them to apply the lock.

12. Microsoft Advertising hotel ads (awareness)

  • Some advertisers may notice that hotel listings in SERP contain additional information, such as room pricing or additional images.  
  • These are part of the Microsoft Advertising Hotel Ads pilot program, which monetizes enhanced hotel ads on Bing, to drive customer traffic and encourage same-day bookings on hotels. 
  • This is currently a closed pilot for selected advertisers in Microsoft Advertising – inform the customer that they should continue to claim/create hotel listings in Bing Places in order to manage the accuracy of the data that appears in our local listing results.  
  • Think of the hotel ads data from Microsoft Advertising as a data feed, providing additional information to the final YPID.  
  • For comparison, this is how the Microsoft Advertising Hotel Ads program works alongside Bing Places: 

[Asset 4092586]

Q: What should I tell a Bing Places customer who wants to show their hotel pricing on SERP? 

A: Explain that this pricing is being supplied through a pilot program in Microsoft Advertising known as Hotel Ads, which is currently available to UK/US advertisers. If the advertiser has (or would like to sign up for) a Microsoft Advertising account for these markets, we can offer to create a nomination request and transfer to a member of Microsoft Advertising support, who can reach out to discuss this with the advertiser in more detail.  

Explain that pricing information is monetized, and clicks will be charged to the advertiser’s Microsoft Advertising account using normal PPC bidding logic. 

If the advertisers agree, you can transfer your ticket to the appropriate Microsoft Advertising team for them to make an outreach attempt to discuss pilot participation.

13. FAQ

Q: The customer wants to update the ‘Questions & Answers’ section, displayed against their YPID listing in Bing SERP. How can they do this?

A:  If the customer has a published BPID associated to this listing entity, you can escalate to Global Support to have the information updated.  For users unable/unwilling to claim the listing, advise them to use the Suggest an Edit link on Bing.

Q:  Why do I see a ‘Category’ displayed beneath the business name for some YPID listings on SERP?

A: For some hospitality and retail areas, this information may be shown on a YPID. It is not taken from Bing Places, but from data mining website and feed information. For any changes required to this information, use Global Support.

Q: In some YPIDs on SERP I see a Description. Is this pulled from Bing Places?

A: Yes, however this is not something we publish as standard. The Describe Your Business field in Bing Places is there for advertisers to add additional information relevant to their business listings. In some cases, we experiment with publishing this information as part of improvement testing, however the standard behaviour is that will not be published against a Local listing.

Q: I have seen the 'Book It' feature for my travel/hotel listing on Bing, which takes me to a third-party booking site such as TripAdvisor. I'm not happy about having a third-party site promoted because my business cannot be booked through site, and/or I do not want to pay commission fees to a third-party. Can this be controlled through Bing Places?

A: This functionality exists to support our Bing trusted partner sites.  If a customer's business cannot be booked via the partner site, or if the customer complains that they are unhappy about the use of a third-party aggregator site to book their business, then on these occasions we can raise an escalation to Global Support, to ask them to switch off the 'Book It' feature for that customer. (Not that we cannot update the button itself to link to the customer's own website.)  

Q: Can a doctor have a Bing Places listing?

A: Doctors can create Bing Places business listings to represent themselves. However, there is a special process to note if the doctor resides in the United States. See KB 3159579 Process: Bing Places: Professional Profiles Listings for instructions on handling these.

Q: I have a FR-FR customer wanting to run a listing. Is this possible?

A: We do not currently support the FR-FR market. French advertisers should be directed to http://boutique.pagesjaunes.fr/index.php/fiches/inscription-gratuite/ in order to run their advertising through our third-party partner.

Q: Why does Bing Places not include my UK customer's preferred UK County address? 

A: Bing Places' address system is based off the UK Post Office's own structure, and adheres to the International Organisation for Standardisation (ISO) compliance rules.  

We recognize that some customers prefer to list their address by Metropolitan County, however due to ISO compliance requirements Bing returns results based on Metropolitan District. Customers within a regional County will all have a specific Metropolitan District they can select when filling in their address. (A search on Bing, using their post-code, should return this result.)

The following Metropolitan Counties are therefore not available for selection in Bing Places:

  • County Durham
  • East Yorkshire
  • Gwent
  • Middlesex
  • Lanarkshire
  • Berkshire
  • West Midlands
  • Merseyside
  • South Glamorgan
  • South Yorkshire
  • Tyne and Wear

Select the county for your business based on the Metropolitan District assigned to your post-code.

We understand this may not be the preferred address option, and if customers would like to provide feedback on this to our engineering teams, do so via placesfeedback@microsoft.com.

Q:  Why aren’t my Facebook/Twitter details showing for my published business listing?

A:  Only certain Categories of business will display their Social Media information on Bing. These are known as ‘Go Big’ segments, where customer engagement is critical to their success. Currently we support the following Go Big Categories:

  • Restaurants

  • Hotels

  • Retail

  • Healthcare

  • Education

Internal only: We are looking to surface Social Media for other categories of business, however due to the way Bing is configured we are unable to make exceptions or bypass this at the current time.
