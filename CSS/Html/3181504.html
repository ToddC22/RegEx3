2.1 Change History report

Advertisers can use the Change History report to see which changes were made in their advertising campaigns, who made them, how they made (by adding the ‘Tools’ column to the report) and undo changes, even undoing deleted campaigns.

Combining Change History with performance reports can also help see which changes led to an increase/decrease in performance.

2.2 Share of Voice report

The Share of Voice report gives you an estimate on where you are losing to competitors in the marketplace. It is available at keyword/match-type level for advertisers, making it easier for you to help advertisers quantify missed impressions and prioritize optimization efforts.

You can also view what percentage of clicks an advertiser's ads are receiving compared to competing ads (click share). For example, if you find that 80% of an advertiser's impression share has been lost to budget, you can easily help him or her adjust to capture more relevant traffic.

Reporting data is kept for specified periods of time. If you want to learn how far back hourly, daily, weekly, monthly, yearly and summary aggregated data can be retrieved for a report.

Note: Share of Voice reports require enough impression share data for Microsoft Advertising to accurately calculate an advertiser’s Share of Voice. If the advertiser has a very low number of impressions, this report may not be valuable to them.

For more information see the Find out your Impression Share help article.

2.3 Search Term report

The impressions, clicks, click-through rate, and average position for search terms that have triggered your ads. Use this Help link to Run a search term report.

You can add keywords and negative keywords directly to an account from a Search term report in one of three ways:

  1. Within the Keywords page of the Campaign tab.
  2. From the Dimensions tab
  3. From the Search Term Report itself.

See Add the words people are searching for as keywords for information on how to do this.

In the Search Term report, you may see keywords that begin with "*RN," such as *RNJD, *RNDC, *RNHC, etc. *RN is a code that stands for *REALNAME, so Bing won't show people's real names in search query reports. So *RNJD could mean REALNAME*JohnDoe. However, sometimes Bing assumes words are people's names even with brand names, so *RNLV could potentially be REALNAME*LouisVuitton.

2.5 Auction insights

Allows advertisers to compare their performance with their competitors so they can make strategic decisions on keywords, bids and budgets. Auction insights is available in Intelligence and on the Campaigns page in Microsoft Advertising online.

Notes:

  • Auction Insights data is only kept for 180 days.
  • Auction Insights will only run if there are enough impressions. New campaigns, and campaigns with too few impressions, will not generate Auction Insights.

The metrics used in Auction Insights are:

  • Impression share % – The number of impressions you received divided by the estimated number of impressions you were eligible to receive.
  • Average position – The average rank of the ad in the auctions, which determines the order of the ads on the search results page. For example, an ad position of 4 means that, on average, your ad will be in 4th place on search results pages.
  • Overlap rate – How often another participant’s ad received an impression when your ad also received an impression. For example, if one of the other participants in your auction insights report is showing “19%” in the overlap rate column, this means that in 19 out of every 100 times your ad showed, an ad from this participant showed as well.
  • Position above rate – How often the other participant’s ad was shown in a higher position than yours was, when both of your ads were shown at the same time. For example, if one of the other advertisers is showing “85%” in the position above rate column, this means that the other participant’s ad showed in a position above yours in 85 out of every 100 times your ads showed at the same time.
  • Top of page rate – How often your ad (or the ad of another participant) was shown at the top of the page, which is the mainline position above the organic search results.
  • Outranking share – The percentage of time your ad showed up higher on the search results pages than your competitors, or your ad showed when theirs did not. If the rate is high, you are getting a more desirable ad position than other advertisers.

Advertisers can also view/download their Auction Insights with the following Segment data:

  • Unit of Time
  • Device Type

For more information, see help article See how you compete with the auction insights report.

2.7 Top mover

Top Mover is a report that helps user to quickly diagnose account performance variation issues by identifying campaigns and ad groups that had biggest performance change and providing corresponding possible causes.

It allows customers to compare account performance by selecting two comparison time periods, successive 7,14, 28 days against its previous adjacent period of the same length of time.

It identifies up to 10 campaigns or ad groups whose performance has changed the most over a specified time. It also provides possible causes for these changes, considering the following types of changes:

  • Account changes
  • Search traffic changes
  • Competitor changes

2.7.1 How are Top Movers generated?

Top Mover report is generated when the account’s clicks (in total) are 100 or more during the report time periods. Top movers are (up to 10) ad groups or campaigns that saw the largest change by size, not percent.

There is no duplication among an ad group and its parent campaign, by following the rules:

  • If a campaign has only one ad group, or an ad group’s change is bigger than its parent campaign, only the ad group will be selected.
  • If a campaign’s change is larger than each of its child ad group, only the campaign will be selected.

3.2 Inline reporting

In addition to reports created via the Reports tab, you can also run in-line performance reporting from many of the sub-tabs on the Campaigns page within the Microsoft Advertising UI, (for example, Campaigns, Ad Groups, Ads, or Keywords) within the performance grid section of each sub-tab.

These in-line reports can be used to create, schedule, and share reports with additional users.

Advertisers will see a performance chart that shows the details of the change and includes a summary of what we believe caused this and what actions the advertiser can take.

5. Troubleshooting

5.1 Ad duplication tracking

Currently, mainline (ML) position ads can be duplicated to also show in bottom of page (BOP) position during the same search query. This is by design, though the frequency of duplication varies between Bing and our partner networks (such as, Bing and Yahoo).

To ensure we are tracking the number of impressions accurately, Bing will record ML and BOP as two separate impressions and assign each impression a separate ‘token id’. For example:

  • Search user enters keyword ‘Contoso Laptops’.
  • An ad for Contoso is shown in ML, and the same advert is duplicates in BOP.
  • Customer clicks Contoso’s ML advert.
  • When Contoso’s account manager checks reporting, they will see two ad impressions, and one click.

Ad duplication FAQ:

Q: Why do you use this logic?

A: To avoid incorrectly inflating CTR, we track an impression any time an instance of this ad is shown in Bing search results. This is an industry standard logic that we have adopted to help advertisers make more accurate performance assessments.

Q: Do ML and BOP ads compete with one another for performance?

A: No. BOP ads are a duplication of ML, and do not inflate CPC. We assign a separate ‘token id’ purely so that we can accurately report impressions.

Q: A customer wants to know why his CTR has dropped and/or why we are tracking separately for when the same advert shows to the same customer in the same search.  What should we tell them?  (Note: this question will only apply in the first few weeks following GA on July 31st – after metrics have stabilized, no ‘sudden’ drop in CTR due to this behavior is expected.)

A: Depending on the publishing website, adverts that show in the Mainline positions may also be duplicated at the bottom of the page – this helps increase your chances or receiving a click from a customer.  To help track impressions accurately, we use the industry-standard practice of treating duplicated ads as separate impressions. Rest assured these will not be competing against each other for performance, and you will only be charged once per click as normal regardless of which ad the customer clicks.

Q: Can I turn off duplication?

A: Because Mainline/Bottom of Page duplicate is controlled by the website publisher, this is not a feature that can be controlled in Microsoft Advertising’s UI. 

Q: What reports are impacted by ad duplication tracking?

A: Only reports that rely on impressions as a part of their calculation will be impacted. As this is a global change, all advertisers will be impacted equally, and so no damage to performance or competitiveness will be experienced.

5.2 Time zone settings

When you create a report request, specify the date range based on the time zone of the campaign. The report data is stored in the time zone of the campaign. For example, if the time zone of the campaign is Pacific Time and a click occurs at 12 PM Eastern Time, the time of the click is recorded as 9 AM. If you request a report for the campaign using hourly aggregation, the report data will include a click that occurred at 9 AM (the report data is not adjusted based on the local time zone of your campaign).

For more information see the following help articles:

  • How does account time zone impact data?
  • Changing your account time zone

Note: Changes to account time zones should take effect within a few minutes.

5.3 Data shown in UI performance data grid is missing or inaccurate

If advertisers cannot see certain user interface content (for example, they see blank campaigns or ad group lists), instruct them to do the following:

  1. Check Windows Update at http://update.microsoft.com to ensure they have the latest security patches installed.
  2. Verify that the customer is using Internet Explorer.
  3. If the customer is using Internet Explorer, have the customer try an alternative browser (for example, Firefox).
  4. Ensure the customer has selected ‘Performance Data For’ to reflect active performance time.

If there’s still no resolution, gather information for and escalate to Global Support.

5.4 UI performance data grid has inconsistent data

If the advertiser notes a discrepancy between reporting and the Home user interface data, follow these steps:

  1. Select Reports.
  2. If not already expanded in the left pane, select Standard reports, then select Performance.
  3. Select the relevant report to the Home user interface view and filter columns/campaigns as required. Make sure the date ranges are identical.
  4. Compare the results. If data discrepancies exist, escalate to Global Support for further investigation.

Note: It’s possible that the Data Grid can be impacted by what is known as “BI out of SLA,” which will not impact the Microsoft Advertising Reports. See KB 2743346 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Support Global Contact Information or escalate to Ad Support for further investigation if this is a concern.

5.8 Data from reports does not match reporting from third-party

Discrepancies can occur due to differences in how each tool (including Microsoft Advertising) accounts for certain metrics algorithmically, such as impressions, clicks and conversions. Also, certain settings, such as de-dup parameter values in conversion tracking, can be modified by advertisers and cause discrepancies with third-party reporting.

In general, the guidance when comparing Microsoft Advertising reports to a third-party report is to ensure that they are trending consistently, and that generally “large” variances do not exist (some variations are typically expected).

If advertisers are having trouble with inaccurate third-party reporting tools, suggest they use the Microsoft Advertising Reports for more accurate and convenient information.

The advertiser can find training on how to use Microsoft Advertising Reports using the following URL: Detailed reporting in Microsoft Advertising.

5.9 Report doesn’t contain desired information

Microsoft Advertising Reporting allows advertisers to select various reports and customize them by specifying attributes. If any given report is missing the desired information (or attribute) needed, have the advertiser run a new report after checking and confirming all filter options.

Note: The following instructions are for Performance reports, but the same process can be used for the other types of reporting such as Change History and Targeting.

  1. Select Reports.
  2. If not already expanded in the left pane, select Standard reports, then select Performance. Select the performance report you want.
  3. Select the unit of time and the download format you want.
  4. Use the Choose your Columns and Filter options to add/remove report fields.
  5. Select Run to run the report or Download to download the report as CSV, TSV or XLSX (Microsoft Office Excel) file.

5.10 More clicks than impressions in the reports

Though rare, there are a few reasons for why an advertiser might see this phenomenon. See these troubleshooting steps and insights:

  1. Remember that reporting can have latency; impressions data may not have finished compiling before click data has been counted. Check the data on the following day, to see if this has updated. (As a best practice, checking a day’s data on the following day is always preferable for a complete report.
  2. Run a Keyword Performance Report and add ‘Low Quality’ as a column. You may find that impressions were deemed ‘low quality’ and discounted from reporting, but the click was still registered because a search user clicked the ad.
  3. Note that some browsers may cache search results (including ads) for speed of retrieval. Cached data isn’t triggered by Microsoft Advertising, so a new impression isn’t registered, but a click on cached data will still register as a new click.
  4. Advertisers may see additional clicks in weblogs, due to allowed Search user behaviour. For example, Search users may be comparison shopping or returning to a site (via a sponsored result) for more information.
    Additionally, if a Search user clicks an ad multiple times, with a sufficient delay between clicks, these will be registered as valid. (Some browsers and search experiences may open sponsored results in a new tab, for example, and leave the search page open…if the customer navigates back and clicks again on the same ad, it will be charged again but only a single impression will have been registered.)
  5. Some IP providers assign a single IP to multiple users at a location. It is therefore important to note that multiple clicks from the same IP may still be valid as they originate from different users.

If the data is still showing inaccurately after 24 hours and the advertiser remains concerns, please escalate to Global Support for analysis.
