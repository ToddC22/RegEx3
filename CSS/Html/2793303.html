ASMS – Out of Office (OOF) Guidance and Checklist

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.  

Overview  

This article provides guidance on how Support Engineers (SEs) should submit out of office requests (OOF) and how SEs should handle their cases during their OOF periods.

Note: This process only applies to FTEs and internal vendors. If you are an external vendor, your vendor company should provide you with the processes and tools you need to log OOF periods. 

Secondary Note: Ultimately, the handling of this process is at the discretion of the respective pod lead, but best practices are provided below. 

Policy 

  • Planned OOF periods should be discussed with your Pod Lead/Manager as far in advance as possible, at a minimum 4 weeks prior to the planned leave. 

  • OOF automatic email responses should only be set to send during weekends and planned OOF periods, not during the standard work week. 

Guidelines 

  • For Planned OOF periods > 3  business days: The original SE should transfer all active cases to a stand-in SE with a warm handover.  

  • For Planned OOF periods < 3  business days: The original SE should create tasks for the stand-in SEs to manage their cases in their absence.  

  • When returning from OOF, you are not required to take back any of the cases in which you handed off before going OOF. If you do choose to take your cases back, it is at your own discretion but it is not required.

  • For guidance on how to handoff a PPP case, follow TSG 4464499. 

Support Engineer Processes 

Planned OOF Checklist 

Use the following checklist for planned OOF periods, such as vacations or other time off. 

  1. Get your manager’s approval for your requested OOF period. 

  2. Sync with your Technical Advisors (TAs) prior to going OOF to check the workload of your bin. They will help you define and review the action plan for your remaining cases. 

  3. Add your Planned OOF request into the Workforce Management (WFM) Tool. 
    See this documentation for more information on the WFM tool.

  1. Before going OOF, sync with your TA to determine how to manage the remaining cases in your bin. 

    1. Refer to the Guidelines section to determine if a case should be transferred or if tasks should be created. 
    2. Ensure that all tasks and/or case transfers are complete before you go OOF.
  1. If your cases are being transferred, contact your customer and let them know that another Support Engineer will be following up on their support request.  

  2. Set an OOF message in Outlook.  

    • See the OOF Message (Automatic Reply) section for guidance.

Unplanned OOF Checklist 

Use the following checklist for unplanned OOF, such as illness, family emergencies, etc. This process applies to FTEs only. 

  1. Inform your Pod Lead/Manager, and TA of your situation as soon as possible.  

  2. Email the asmsuxoof@microsoft.com DL using the form in the Unplanned OOF Message section, citing your reasons for being OOF. Be sure to add your Pod Lead/Manager and TA to the Cc line.

    Note: This DL is to be used by FTEs only. The DL includes all ASMS Managers and Duty Managers. 

  3. If you have commitments (such as calling a customer) that are scheduled to happen while you are off, be sure to raise awareness of that commitment with your team so that an available SE can follow up on your behalf. 

  4. Add your Unplanned OOF request into the Workforce Management Tool.  See this documentation for more information on the WFM tool.

    Note: If you are unable to do so, ask your Pod Lead/Manager to add the OOF request to the tool for you.

  5. If you return from your unplanned OOF period sooner than your email in Step 2 indicated, email asmsuxoof@microsoft.com (include your Pod Lead/Manager, and TA on Cc,) letting them know that you are available to be placed back into the queue. 

  • Modify your Unplanned OOF entry in the Workforce Management Tool to indicate that you have returned from your unplanned OOF period.  See this documentation for more information on the WFM tool.

Unplanned OOF Message 

Use the following form to email your Pod Lead/Manager, TA, and the WADM team during an unplanned OOF. 

Hello WADM,  

Please remove <your full name and Microsoft alias> from the queue due to <Reason, such as: Illness, Research, Computer Issues, etc.> for the following dates given below. My pod lead/manager, and TA are on CC for visibility. 

Starting: <Start Date> 
Ending: <Anticipated End Date>  

Regards,  

<Signature> 

OOF Message (Automatic Reply) 

Use the following form or similar messaging for an automatic reply while you are OOF. 

Hello,  

Thank you for your message. I am currently out of the office.  I will be returning on <Month><Day> at <Time in UTC>.  

If you need immediate assistance regarding an existing service request, please send an email to <insert your podDL@microsoft.com>.  

Include the Service Request number so that one of my colleagues can reach out to you to assist you with your request.  

If your concern is not related to an existing service request, please create a new request using the Management portal and a Support Engineer will reach out to you shortly.  

 Regards,  

<Signature> 

Customer OOF Process

If the customer informs us of their OOF

  1. Request an alternate contact for customers going out of office (OOF).
  2. Verify with the customer whether or not there will be an alternate contact to continue working the case.
  3. If an alternate is provided and not already included under contacts, add the new name and continue working the case as appropriate.
  4. Follow steps below if customer does not have an alternate contact.
    1. Customer OOF / Restricted Change Access Log: Enter a Customer Contact Labor entry. 
    2. Customer<enter name> OOF
    3. Dates OOF / Change Access- mm/dd/yy through mm/dd/yy (date format optional as long as it includes month day and year)
    4. Adjust Severity: Request permission from the customer to lower Severity Level to C. If customer refuses, note in log entry and notify your Manager
    5. Schedule a Callback: Schedule an agreed upon callback time with the customer. If no confirmation of callback is made, schedule a callback within 48 business hours of the customer's return. Include the mm/dd/yy- time + time zone (date format optional as long as it includes month day and year)

OOF auto-response from a customer:

In the event you receive an out of office response from the customer, do the following:

  1. If an alternate contact is identified in the customer’s OOF message, attempt to engage that individual to continue working the case.
  2. If the alternate contact does not respond (or is not equipped to work on the case), follow the next best action below.
    1. If a return date is specified and no alternate contact is identified/available, leave the case open and use the Commitment feature to set a reminder to contact the customer on the return date specified in their OOF message.
    2. If no return date is cited in the customer’s OOF message, keep the case open and set the proper expectations with the customer. Using the guidance below, use the Commitment feature to set a reminder to follow-up.
  • Inform the customer that you received their Out-of-Office message, and will follow back up with them in one week. 
  • If no return date is available, ask the customer to provide a date.
  • Suggest to the customer that if they plan to be back sooner, to provide us with the best time to contact them or if they need longer than a week before we follow up to let us know.  
  • Ask the customer if there is an alternate contact we can work with in the meantime (if no alternate contact was previously provided.  
  1.  If you receive a response with either a return date or an alternate contact, proceed as described above. If not:
  • Follow up with the customer when prompted by your Commitment reminder. If the customer does not respond to your follow-up, initiate standard 3-strike process. The follow-up itself does not count as Strike 1.
  • Use your best judgment in customer-OOF scenarios. Leverage details from the customer’s OOF message to inform your approach.
