1. Uploads (Bulk Campaign Management) overview

An upload is a change or assignment. Changes or assignments can be done manually via the Microsoft Advertising UI or in bulk via Editor.

BOS advocates upload campaigns when a requestor asks for new campaigns to be created, or to update existing ones. The Uploads service is also used to modify account settings such as targeting and budget options. The BOS advocate will:

  • Download the account and create a backup.
  • Review the upload in Excel.
  • Import it into Editor.
  • Upload to Microsoft Advertising.
  • Troubleshoot any issues prior to sending the output back to the requestor.

This document aims to educate readers about the Uploads service utilizing Editor and Microsoft Advertising UI, as executed by Back Office advocates. This module also includes step-by-step instructions of the processes required to execute the Uploads service for our BOS advocates. Additional resources are available at the end of this module.

3. Scope of service (support boundaries)

In scope

If the request falls outside the uploads scenarios below (which our advocates are most familiar with), requestors are asked to clearly define the process for advocates to follow in the Microsoft Advertising UI or Editor as part of their request.

  • Ad group language.
  • Adding uploads to existing campaigns.
  • Adding/modifying/deleting all levels of the hierarchy and all properties of an entity type (targeting – all types).
  • Association of existing remarketing lists and bid adjustments, and in-market audience segments.
  • Audience ads.
  • Audience Associations.
  • Creating negative shared list through either UI or Editor.
  • Dynamic Search Ads (DSA Uploads only: DSA ads proposals are not in-scope currently).
  • Editorial issue correction (delete and re-upload campaigns that needed editorial bypass).
  • Google Import/Uploading Google import bulk sheet.
  • IP address exclusions.
  • Keywords (which is inclusive of bid adjustments).
  • Labels
  • Match types
  • Network settings
  • Pausing/Resuming all levels of the hierarchy.
  • Shared Negative/Library Upload & Association.
  • Targeting settings.
  • Time Zone changes (delete and re-upload).
  • Upload conversion goals.
  • Uploading new campaigns, ad groups, ad copy and extensions (for those supported in the AES menu), and keywords.
  • URL changes (for example, final URL to destination URL and vice versa) including modifying URLs using information provided by the requestor.
  • Website exclusions

Note: All new upload items will be imported in a pause state, and the requestor is responsible to activate them, unless otherwise directed. For all new items that inherit their associated parent status such as targeting settings and ad extension, these will be posted as is, unless otherwise directed.

Out of scope:

  • Creating new campaigns and ad groups where advocate is required to create content and/or configure account settings without explicit instructions provided by the requestor.
  • Manually editing custom parameters within destination URLs.
  • Google Merchant Center Import (as the format of the Google file is not the same, many additional fields are not supported).
  • Logging into the Google Ads account to sync the account data.

Out of scope for Back Office support (BOS) and Advertiser Enablement Services (AES)

  • Adult advertisers.
  • Language translation and localization.
  • Work requiring advocates to log into non-Microsoft products and services (for example, Google Ads) due to privacy concerns.
  • System/tool limitations inherent to each service.
  • Making any modifications directly to advertiser accounts without explicit direction and/or approval from requestor.
  • Providing service for accounts who are currently in bad standing with Microsoft Advertising (such as, credit hold, and quality based ranking (QBR) penalties)
  • Any services that are not listed on the AES Menu of Services; however, out-of-scope requests are considered on a case-by-case basis and may be handled with a stated turnaround time that is outside the norm.
  • Any customers in non-supported AES Markets.

4. Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Typically, uploads requests are completed within two business days.

The following are the service level agreements for uploads requests:

  • Under 200k line items – One business day.
  • 200k - 500k – Two business days.
  • 500,001 - 1M – Three business days.
  • Over 1M – Four business days.

Note: For requests greater than 1M, advocates will work with the requestor to agree on the estimated time needed to complete the request.  For requests with multiple accounts, advocates will update the requestors on the ETA by reviewing the account size (line items for all the accounts).
