1. Overview

The Family Safety App is a feature set for families within the app which allows them to view and manage their settings. Safe driving is a part of this app which allows families to monitor driving behavior and alert them about unsafe driving habits.

2. Features

2.1 Features overview

Messaging

Description  

Features

Develop healthy digital habits

Get information about your family’s activity so you can have a conversation about what your kids are doing online and on their devices. View screen time, top websites visited, and terms kids are searching for online. Receive an email summary each week to help facilitate a dialogue on healthy digital choices and begin developing good habits from a young age.
  • Activity summaries
  • Weekly email reports

Find screen time balance

Set screen time limits across Xbox and Windows. Or if your kids will be on devices longer for things like online learning, you can set limits on specific apps or games instead. Now when you say only one hour of a certain game, that really means one hour of that game—whether that’s being played on a Windows PC, Xbox, or Android phone. And if they run out of screen time, they can ask for more. You have the choice to add more time or not based on what is right for your family.
  • App and game limits (Xbox, Windows, Android)
  • Screen time limits (Xbox, Windows)
  • Screen time requests

Choose the right content

Create a safe space for your kids to explore online. Set healthy boundaries with web and search filters to block mature content and set browsing to kid-friendly websites on Microsoft Edge. Get notified when your kids want to download a more mature app or game from the Microsoft Stores with age limits, keeping you in the know and helping to avoid surprises. Family Safety provides you with the tools to begin talking about the type of content that is right for your kids.
  • App and game filters
  • Web and search filters
  • Content filter requests

Locate your loved ones

Stay connected even when you’re apart. See loved ones on a map with location sharing. Plus, save places they visit the most, like home, work, or school, to know where family members are at glance. Now, there’s no need to text Mom about when she’ll be home from work, you can find her on your map view in Microsoft Family Safety! Plus, more features coming soon! Build better habits behind the wheel with drive safety to get insights on how your family is doing on the road and location alerts to get notified when someone arrives or departs a location.
  • Location sharing
  • Saved places

2.3 Premium features

For Microsoft 365 subscribers, premium features Drive Safety and Location based alerts are available for Android users. These features will be added for the iOS version of the app soon.

Feature

Benefit

Safe driving reports

(Australia, Canada, the United Kingdom, and the United States only)

Allows families to monitor driving behaviors of those within their family or loved ones, by providing alerts and history about unsafe driving habits such as:

  • Rapid acceleration
  • Phone usage during driving
  • Top speeds
  • Sudden braking, “Hard stops”

Important to note that Microsoft never shares this information with insurance companies or data brokers. This feature is meant for subscribers to have meaningful discussions with their loved ones, about safe driving behaviors.

Location based alerts

Free features that will be available to all users:

  • Location sharing – seeing the last known location of your loved ones on a map.
  • Add Saved Places such as home, schools, airports, work or bus stops that family members frequently visit.​​​

Premium features for Microsoft 365 Family subscribers

  • Automatic notifications when loved ones leave or arrive at these locations (Location based alerts).

This premium feature gives families peace of mind that family members make it to their desired locations or show you when they are on their way.

FAQ

Q: Do I have to have a subscription to Microsoft 365 Family to use the Safe Driving and Location Alerts features?

A: Yes. These are premium features meaning they you have to be a paid subscriber to take advantage of these features. It is a great value-add and feature for why to be a Microsoft 365 Family subscriber.

Q: Can Microsoft 365 Personal subscribers also get access to these feature?

A: No. These features are meant for subscribers who share their subscription and have Family Members that they wish to use Safe Driving and Location based alerts.

Q: What does the disclaimer mean that “availability varies by platform and market?”

A: This disclaimer is referring to the fact that the app at launch (Sept 28, 2020) will only be available to customers on the Android app and will be available in the Apple iOS Store in the near future. It also refers to the fact that customers must be within US, CA, UK, or AU to take advantage of these features.

Q: What is this new icon [Asset 4588928] I see across Microsoft 365 Family PDP pages, SKU Choosers and other websites?

A: This is the app icon for Family Safety. You will notice this icon being used next to Word/Excel/PowerPoint/Teams to indicate that the Microsoft 365 Family subscription will include Family Safety premium features.

Q: How do you get the Family Safety app, and how do they access these premium features if they are a subscriber?

A: You can download the app from the web here or download from your Android app store, or Apple iOS Store. If you are a Microsoft 365 Family subscriber, signing into your app with your Microsoft account associated with your subscription will unlock these premium features.

Q: How do I setup my family members?

A:

  • If you have a Microsoft 365 Family subscription and have shared it already with others they will automatically be included as part of your Family.
  • If you want to add others:
    •  Go to https://account.microsoft.com/family/.
    •  Sign in with your with Microsoft account used for your Microsoft 365 subscription.
    •  Select Create Family Group.

Note: The purchase flow to get the premium features for non-subscribers may not be easily discoverable for customers from within the app. This article Get premium Family Safety features with Microsoft 365 Family should help.

You can also help facilitate the purchase by POBO if that is easier for the customer. Tell the customer that it can take up to 30 minutes after purchase to be able to start using the premium features within the Family Safety app. Or, they can try signing out/in to the app if they want to access the features sooner.

4. Permissions needed to use the Family Safety app

For every feature in the Family Safety app to work, certain device permissions need to be accepted or allowed. Even if Family Safety app settings are set the way you want, device-level permissions can still block the features from working if they aren’t turned on properly. These permissions vary by device and are listed based on which Family Safety features you want for each family member.

Android

The names and steps for permissions may be slightly different, depending on the make and model of each device used.

Family Safety feature

Permission

Content filters

Screen time: App and game limits

 

 

 

Accessibility service:

This allows Family Safety to enforce app limits and content filters and provides an added layer of accuracy to screen time data.

How to set it for Family Safety:

Settings > Accessibility > turn on Use service > Allow

Battery optimization:

This allows the app to send screen time data regularly and gives the family group the most accurate and up to date information possible.

How to set it for Family Safety:

Settings > Battery > menu icon [Asset 4562819] > Battery optimization > Not optimized > Family Safety > Don’t optimize

Usage access:

This allows the app to access screen use data.

How to set it for Family Safety:

Settings > General > Security > Apps with usage access option > Family Safety > Allow access

Device admin:

This stops the app by being uninstalled without approval from an organizer in the family group. This fosters communication around when members want more screen time than what their limits are.

How to set it for Family Safety:

Settings > Biometrics and security > Other security settings > Device admin apps > Family Safety > Activate

Location sharing

This lets your family know where you are. They can see you on the map and the address of your current location.

Location access:

This allows the app to access your current location.

How to set it for Family Safety:

Settings > Location > App permission > Family Safety > Allow all the time

Notifications

Notifications will let you know about things like screen time, content filters, and family location.

Notifications:

This allows the app to send you notifications for things like when someone joins the family or when a member asks for more screen time.

How to set it for Family Safety:

This is usually on by default on Android devices. To check: Settings > Notifications > Family Safety > make sure Allow Notifications is on

 

iPhone

The steps may be slightly different, depending on the iPhone model used.

Family Safety feature

Permission

Notifications

Notifications:

This allows the app to send you notifications for things like when someone joins the family or when a member asks for more screen time.

How to set it for Family Safety:

Settings > Notifications > Family Safety > turn on Allow Notifications

Location sharing

This lets your family know where you are. They can see you on the map and the address of your current location.

Allow location access:

This allows the app to access your current location.

How to set it for Family Safety:

Settings > Family Safety > Location > Always
