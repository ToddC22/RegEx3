Process: Microsoft Advertising: No or low impressions

[Asset 4509089]

Who should use this article?

  • Microsoft Advertising

What is this article about?

One of the most common customer inquiries is: “Why aren’t my ads delivering?” Customers may use the term ‘delivery’ or ‘delivering’ to describe several scenarios:

  • No impressions – The ads and keywords are in the UI, but the advertiser isn’t seeing any impression data.
  • No Clicks – The ads are receiving impressions, but they are receiving low or no clicks from potential customers.
  • No Conversions – The advertiser is receiving impressions and clicks, but no conversions or sales.

1. Overview ﻿

Impressions are the number of times an ad is displayed. There are a variety of reasons that a campaign is not driving impressions or driving very low impressions. In order to accurately discover what is inhibiting the campaign from delivering, follow the series of troubleshooting steps below.

3. Troubleshooting in Ad Inquiry

Utilize Ad Inquiry to do deeper troubleshooting. Checks which fail will be displayed in Red. Red items usually indicate something that is impacting delivery.

Note: All these checks can be later exported if you end up needing to escalate to Global Support.

Campaigns Items – Campaign sub tab 

  1. Enter the Account id/number of the account you are investigating, and select the checkbox show ad groups.
    1. Note: If the advertiser is concerned about delivery in a particular campaign, not across the entire account, drill down to the campaign view by inputting the campaign ID into the designated field. To retrieve the Campaign ID, navigate into the Campaign within the Microsoft Advertising UI and take note of the campaign id listed in the URL address bar. https://ads.microsoft.com/Campaign/Campaigns?cid=6003090&aid=0#customer/6003090/account/913023/campaign/61555876/adgroup
  2. Look through the results and identify anything that may be affecting delivery:
    • Financial – Any status other than ‘No Hold’ indicates an issue with billing that will prevent delivery
    • Status: Inactive – Customer has been deleted. This will prevent delivery.
    • Status: Paused – Account/campaign is paused. This will prevent delivery.
    • Fraud status: Impacted by Fraud – Account was closed for fraud. This will prevent delivery. See Fraud status KB for more information.
    • Fraud status: Fraud pending – If the customer is new, they'll need to add payment information before a Fraud review can be completed.
      • Note: If the payment method is present, this status may have been triggered by other factors. (See KB 4096230 Editorial: Guidelines for campaign or ad group editorial frauds and holds for full breakdown.) Fraud reviews will be completed with 72 hours, however CSS should escalate immediately to save customer effort.
    • UPM Score/QBR Penalty – Any number higher than 1,000 will prevent delivery.
    • Relevance override – Suspended, or Relevance Override Score of one. This means that the delivery engine is overriding the account due to very low relevance and the ads will not deliver.
    • Budget daily cap – If the budget cap is set to daily, and the budget is very small this will limit delivery. For more information see ‘bid to budget ratio’ in the glossary below.
    • CBS Internal pause status – This will tell you if the campaign budgeting system paused the campaign. The status may show as paused even if the status in the UI shows as eligible. Ad Inquiry is often one to two hours ahead of the status shown in the UI. Advertisers with this status should be escalated to Global Support for further investigation.

Check the advertiser’s account to make sure they have a valid PI and campaign setup before escalating to Global Support. 

Note: Customers may find it difficult to understand why their campaign is on budget pause on the back end when their status says eligible and no data is showing in the campaign performance grid within the UI. Although this may be a difficult conversation to have, inform them that there are various delays in our system that are in place to ensure that the data that is shown in the grid is accurate and only reflective of valid traffic. This may mean that impressions and clicks that happen at 10 AM PST, will not show up on the grid until 11 AM PST, and that the spend associated with that performance will not trigger a budget paused status in the UI for up to two hours.

Proceed with troubleshooting in the UI.

Confirming Keyword Delivery
Choose a keyword that was performing well (or would be likely to perform well) from the UI and find its Keyword ID and Ad ID. Then proceed with these steps:

  1. Search in AdInquiry, setting ‘Results Filter’ to ‘All’ and limiting the results to the account number (or accountID) to get the filter reason, if there is one.
  2. If no ad from the account delivers (or the ad/keyword you were expecting isn’t in the results), double check that you are correctly mirroring their targeting settings in adInquiry (or use AdPreview).
  3. Check to see whether the Ad/Keyword is listed as ‘Adult’. Both the keyword and Ad will need to be ‘Adult’ to trigger an impression.

4. Troubleshooting in the UI ﻿

  1. Verify that there are ads and keywords within the ad groups, and that they have a delivery status of ‘eligible’. If the status is anything other than ‘eligible,’ this is what is limiting delivery. 
  • Note: Selecting the ellipsis (three dots) next to Eligible will show a pop-up which explains whether ads are delivering right now or not. (Some keywords may be eligible but still not delivering due to pending reviews.)

[Asset 4538598]

  1. Verify that the account, campaigns, and ad groups are active. A status of paused, not started, or expired will prevent delivery. Check the start Date of Ad Groups to ensure these are valid.
  2. Check advertiser’s targeting. If it is too limited (by zip code, one small city, or a country with very little traffic), this will drastically impact delivery.
  3. Check the advertiser’s bid to budget ratio. If the advertiser has high bids (for example: $10) and a low daily budget (for example: $15), this will limit delivery.
  4. Check to see if the advertiser is using the ad scheduler. The ad scheduler schedules delivery for certain days or times, and this will limit overall delivery.
  5. Check the advertiser’s targeting and ad distribution settings. 
    • If the advertiser has ad distribution to only include O&O sites this will limit the overall delivery and volume of impressions. 
    • Bid Modifiers may also impact delivery.
    • Location targeting and other settings may also block delivery depending on the search user.
  6. Check the advertiser’s keyword selection. If they have less than 10, or all their keywords are long-tail keywords set to exact match type, this will limit delivery.
  7. Check the advertiser’s match types. If everything is set to ‘exact’ this may impact delivery. Look out for negative keyword conflicts that may also impact delivery.
  8. If the are using dynamic text or param insertion, verify that this is set up correctly. Navigate to the keywords tab. If the Param1, Param2 and Param3 columns are not already shown, add them by selecting the Columns drop down and selecting Modify Columns. In the exposed window next to Attributes select Param1 and then select Add. Do this for Param2 and Param3 as well. Then select apply.
  9. Pull a Change History report, and look for the following things in recent changes:
    • Change in Bids, or adding bid adjustments.
    • Adding negative keywords.
    • Deleting high-performing ads or keywords.
    • Deleting keywords that had a low CTR, but very high impressions.
    • Lowering Campaign Budget.
    • Change in Targeting.
    • Opting out of a publisher site.
  10. Review the account against the full list of things below that can impact delivery. If none of them are the reason for the low delivery, escalate to Global Support and include the troubleshooting steps that you took in the escalation.

Steps to resolve
Newly Created Entity (for example, New Ads, Keywords and Sitelinks) Variable

Confirm if the customer is reporting non-delivery for newly created creative content. Note that the 24 hour editorial review is only for new creative content that will be visible on Bing – for example, ad copy, keywords (which can be dynamically inserted into ad copy), and ad extensions.

Paused campaigns or Ad Groups

None

To resume a campaign, edit the row and alter the Status column from “paused” to “active."

Fraud closure

None

The 'Fraud' status, formerly known as "dot-fraud," indicates an account that has been closed for fraudulent behaviour as defined by automated rules that Microsoft Advertising uses to review accounts. You will see this 'Fraud' status in the Status field within advertiser search results and account details status summary. 

Fraud pending status

None

Add a payment method to the account so it can go through the initial onboarding reviews. Once these are complete, the status will be removed, and the ads will deliver.
Non-delivery fraud check applied None

Non-delivery holds may be applied to all newly created/edited items (regardless of age) and lasts up to 24 hours before the temporary hold expires.

Multiple hold-checks may need to be completed before approval, and so we should set a customer expectation of 72 hours to delivery.

However, escalations can be raised after 48 hours have passed since the item was created/edited.

See section 8 for troubleshooting guidance.

Budget Pause

None

If an ad campaign runs out of budget, delivery status will say “campaign out of budget” and Microsoft Advertising will automatically pause the campaign. Ad display will resume either at the start of the next day or the next month, depending on the campaign budget settings.

To resume their ads now, advertisers can increase their budget.

Note: If the advertiser adds funds, the campaigns will go active within 30 minutes.

  1. Select the Campaigns page.
  2. On the Campaigns tab, in the table of campaigns, find the campaign that has the delivery status of Campaign out of budget.
  3. Select the budget for the campaign, and then enter a new budget amount.
  4. Select Save.

Budget is directly affected by keyword bid amounts. Since keyword bids can vary, be sure to monitor the bid amounts and frequency of clicks. If the advertiser notices unfavourable bid amounts and click frequencies, change the budget settings. To make edits:

  1. Go to the Campaigns page.
  2. Hover over the Budget column of the Campaign row you want to update. 
  3. Select the pen icon to proceed.
    Note: You can only change the budget amount this way. To change the budget type and amount,     select the campaign and choose the settings tab. 

Under campaign settings, alter the Campaign budget information.

Low bid to budget ratio

Limited

If the Budget is $10.00 and the bids are $2.50, or the Budget is $5.00 and the bids are $1.00, then the Microsoft Advertising platform will be very careful about how many times it enters the ad into the auction as it doesn’t want to exceed the budget, and the bids are pretty high.

  • While still on this page, look near the top of the page, beneath the header Campaign and you can see what the budget is for that campaign and whether it’s set to daily or monthly.

Bids too low for first page

Limited

Increase bids

 To target a page position:

  1. From the Campaigns tab, select Keywords.
  2. Check the box next to the keyword you wish to change.
  3. Select Estimation drop-down.
  4. Select one of the four pages estimation choices. Each one means the bid amount in the Current Bid column will change to a close approximation of what it costs for that keyword to achieve a:
    • First Page Estimation means the ad will usually run anywhere on the first page.
    • Traffic Estimation means the amount of traffic for a given set of keywords.
    • Best Position Estimation means expected costs and traffic by bidding for position 1.
    • Mainline Estimation means that the ad positions will be one to four rather than ad positions five to eight of the search results page (which would place it at Bottom of Page (BOP) or Sidebar (in markets still supporting this feature).
  5. Select Save.

Multiple bid adjustments

Limited

If more than one of the advertiser’s target criterion is met and the ad is clicked, the advertiser pays for each of the bid adjustments that match (but not for those that don't match). For example, let's say they have set up the following bids and targeting:

  • Keyword bid = $3.00
  • Target location = Chicago, Bid adjustment = Bid + 20%
  • Target days = Saturday, Bid adjustment = Bid + 10%

Target device = Tablet, Bid adjustment = Bid - 20%

Limited distribution

Limited

Opt into additional search networks within the Ad Distribution settings

By default, Microsoft Advertising distributes your ads to both All Search Networks (Bing, AOL and Yahoo! Search and Syndicated Search Partners) and content networks. If a customer has changed their Search Network distribution settings to only deliver ads to Bing, AOL, and Yahoo Search (Owned and Operated) only search network, they may see a substantial decline in impressions. Encourage them to opt back into syndicated search partners by selecting All search networks (Bing, AOL, and Yahoo search and syndicated search partners) and then optimizing results by excluding poor performing sites.

Handling non-delivery fraud check issues

Although most Fraud checks are resolved within 24 hours, multiple automated rules may be running at once, and these can extend the time it takes before delivery resumes. For this reason, advise customers it can take up to 72 hours, from the time at which an entity was last created/edited, before they should expect to see their performance.

In the meantime, you can escalate to Global Support for help with non-delivery issues after 48 hours.

For customer talking points, use the following:

“Thanks for your patience whilst I investigated this for you. Your ads are currently part of a seasonal review process, which will be resolved by [insert date here]. We use these checks to help improve the overall user experience on our platform, and thank you in advance for your understanding.”

When closing your ticket, use the appropriate coding in ASD to categorize your ticket. (Under the ‘Poor/No Performance’ Scenario.)
