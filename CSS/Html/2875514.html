Procedure: VLS: Authenticating VL Administrator

[Asset 4489629]

Who should follow this process?

  • VL Advocates

What is this process about?

This article provides a guideline of the

  • Information VL Advocate must confirm from the callers to authenticate them.
  • Supporting / Authenticating customers who don’t know their licensing ID (Agreement Numbers or Open License Numbers)
  • Guidance on how to support / Authenticate Resellers who don’t know their licensing ID (Agreement Numbers or Open License Numbers)

When should this process be followed?

  • When supporting inquiries relating to Account Specific volume licensing information.

Note

General “How Do I questions”, queries that can be answered by directing callers to public content, etc. do not require entitlement.

Applies to

Legacy Volume Licensing – Enterprise, Select, Open, Open Value, Campus

Policy

The Microsoft verification and entitlement policy protects customers and Microsoft against the fraudulent or unauthorized activity.

Account Specific support entitlement is limited to:

  • VL Administrators, as verifiable in VLSC,
  • Partners of Record, as verifiable in Explore.MS.

Under no circumstance should any Volume Licensing Agreement or personal identification information (PII) be proactively provided over the phone.

VLSC User Verification

  1. Gather the caller’s First Name, Last Name, and Work Email Address.
  • If the caller’s email address does not contain the company domain that is tied to the agreement, then ask if they have an alternative business email address.
  1. Gather the license ID License and Authorization Number for Open, Donation numbers (for TechSoup donations / Open Charity), PCN for Select, Enrolment or Agreement Number for Enterprise, Open Value or Campus.
    • If caller does not know the license ID see <Supporting Customers Who Do Not Know Their Agreement Number>.
  2. Authenticate the following in VLSC CSR Tool Box
    1. If the caller is a registered VLSC user and is associated to the license ID the inquiry is about, the caller is verified.
    2. If the caller is not a registered VLSC user but is associated to the license ID in question, assist the caller to register the license before proceeding with any account specific support.
    3. If the caller is not associated to the license ID the inquiry relates to, Advocates should try to establish a relationship between the customer’s email domain and a License ID verify more than one of the following:
      1. The company name provided by the customer matches the company name on the agreement on VLSC or Explore.ms.
      2. The customer’s email domain matches the company name or the email address of the VL administrator listed on the agreement.
      3. The customer’s email matches the email domain of existing VLSC Administrators.

 

Scripting for Unverified Callers

If the agent cannot locate agreement details or the customer’s email domain does not match the email domain on record or of a prior VLSC Admin, the customer is unverified, advise the customer that

"We are unable to confirm licensing details based on the information you have provided. It is possible that your organization’s purchasing department or finance department may be able to confirm the licensing information that you need or be able to identity of your Microsoft Reseller from your organization’s purchasing records."

Supporting Customers Who Do Not Know Their Agreement Number or where user is not listed in VLSC

I Search

Tool Tip - How to verify a caller who does not know their License ID or who is not associated to the license ID

  1. In VLSC CSR search by the email address provided by the customer

  2. If a search by email does not reveal any license ID, search by Company Name to locate agreement information (Authorization number and License ID),

  3. In CMAT search for the user’s email domain to find the Enterprise agreement number. (See KB 2870042)

 

II Verify if customer is from the same domain or not

Caller is not from the same domain – either when the customer already provided a different domain which is listed in VLSC or customer reports not receiving the email which support sent to the customers provided email address or does not respond, the customer is unverified.

  • Provide the unverified message and don’t give any admin data to the customer
  • Close the case as “Un-resolved” with AIRC: Root Cause - Volume Licensing\Misroute/Redirect\External\Administrator

 

Customer is from the same domain:

To verify if the customer is from the same domain  and that email is not a consumer domain send an email with the case number to the email address the customer provided and ask the customer to reply from that email so that we can verify access to the email account.

If the domain match there are different scenarios to consider:

  1. Email address provided by the customer has the same email domain as the email address listed in VLSC – and that email is not a consumer domain.
    1. Email address provided by the customer matches the email address listed in VLSC, but customer doesn’t recall which specific email was used. (User may have multiple email accounts linked to different license IDs).
    2. Email address provided by the customer does NOT match the email address listed in VLSC, but that VLSC email looks like a shared mailbox in the same domain.      
      example: User is JohnDoe@contoso.com but VLSC email is admin@contoso.com, ITDept@contoso.com, licensing@contoso.com)

 

Solution:

  1. Verify that the customer’s email response is from the same domain (see section verify same domain.
  • Advocate can confirm the specific email address associated with the license ID during the phone call.
  • If the customer has no access to the shared email account but is on the same domain, Advocates should refer to admin : Advocate are allowed to provide the first name Last name from the VL Admin via email.

Set Expectations: Inform the customer we will keep the case open for two days and wait their feedback, if we don’t receive a reply, we will close the case. No reply from the customer after two days, sent closure email and close the case as

Unresolved: AIRC …, AIRC: Root Cause - Volume Licensing\Misroute/Redirect\External\Administrator

If customer says their admin is no longer with the company – follow the Add Admin process – #2531298 Add administrator functionality in CSR tool box

  1. Customer is from the same domain, but has no access to the listed email address
    1. Slightly different email address is listed in VLSC (one character is missing or a “.” Missing) but domain name is an exact match*     
      *where email is JohnDoe@Contoso.com – a typo in JohnDoe would be in scope as the domain contoso.com is the same.     
      A domain name discrepancy (contoso.uk.com) would NOT be in scope     
      A consumer domain (gmail.com / outlook.com would be out of scope!
    2. Completely different email is listed in VLSC, but email is from the same domain as the email address the customer provided you.
 

Solution:

  • Advise the customer that their email is not a match for the email listed in VLSC and they should contact their VLSC Admin to change the email address on VLSC if necessary. Don’t provide the email address which is listed in VLSC!
  • Send an email from SD to the email address the customer is providing e.g. johnd@contoso.com with the case number you created in SD and ask the customer to reply to your email from the same address.
  • If the customer does not know the admin, advocates may identify the Admin so long as the customer has exactly same email domain name and provide the first name and last name to the customers email address
  • Set expectations: Inform the customer we will keep the case open for two days and await their feedback, if we don’t receive a reply, we will close the case.
  • No reply from the customer after one day – send closure email and close the case as Un-resolved, AIRC: Root Cause - Volume Licensing\Misroute/Redirect\External\Administrator

If customer says their admin is no longer with the company – follow the Add Admin process – #2531298 Add administrator functionality in CSR tool box

If you are unsure how to proceed, reach out to a coach or trainer for further assistance.

Guidance how to support Reseller:

Authenticating Resellers who don’t know their licensing ID (Agreement Numbers or Open License Numbers)

  1. Unless the reseller is named on the license and Admin in VLSC or they are the Partner of Record (POR) in Explore.ms, NO information should be provided.
  2. Direct Resellers to request the customer VLSC Admin add them as an Admin to any licenses as needed in the VLSC Portal.

Direct Reseller calls in for assistance on multiple customers and has the credentials for the customers’ accounts requesting agreement information.

  1. Reseller is an Admin in VLSC or Point of Record (PORPOR) in Explore.ms and can be verified on an agreement = Proceed on agreements ONLY that the Reseller is an Admin or PORPOR on.
  2. Reseller is not an Admin on the agreement(s) in VLSC or a POR in Explore.ms = No information can be provided. Only general assistance

Reseller indicates they have the customers credentials, but are not an Admin on the agreement(s) in VLSC nor a POR in Explore.ms

  1. No information can be provided. Only general assistance
  2. Microsoft does not support the sharing of credentials. It is against MS policy.

Reseller requests to open a ticket on behalf of a customer.

  1. Reseller is an Admin in VLSC or POR in Explore.ms and can be verified = Proceed to open ticket as an Admin
  2. Reseller is not an Admin in VLSC or a POR in Explore.ms = As long as the RSC is not exposing / communicating any customer information back to the Reseller and all communication goes directly back to customer, the Reseller can open up a ticket.

Reseller calls in for information on a customer agreement

  1. Reseller is an Admin in VLSC or POR in Explore.ms and can be verified = Proceed
  2. Reseller is not an Admin in VLSC or a POR in Explore.ms = General information on VL can be answered, but no customer agreement information can be provided.

For a Reseller to work on behalf of the customer and for any PII information to be exposed to them, they must be named as an Admin on the customers agreement in VLSC or a Partner or Record in Explore.ms and able to pass the verification process. No Exceptions.
