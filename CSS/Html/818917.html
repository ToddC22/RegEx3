Procedure: Xbox: How to respond to a customer who wants to cancel an Xbox Live Gold membership

[Asset 4049349]

Xbox Live Billing article

Use this article as a reference when taking cancellation calls.

This article describes what to do if a customer wants to cancel an Xbox Live Gold membership.

Who should follow this process? (Affected agent types)

  • Accounts & Billing
  • Xbox

What is this process about?

  • This process provides agent scripting and recommended resources for agents to help customers understand the value of the Xbox Live, so that agents can make the right recommendation for customers who want to cancel their memberships.

When should this process be followed?

  • Whenever a customer wants to cancel their Xbox Live Gold membership by either turning off auto-renewal, removing the payment instrument associated to the Xbox Live subscription, or cancelling the subscription immediately.
  • Applies-to markets: All markets.

Policy

  • If the payment instrument owner calls to cancel the membership, you must still first try to verify the customer. Always use the process in KB 2885545 Policy: How to handle a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner.

Process

When a customer calls to cancel, we want them to know that we value their business. We also like to learn why someone wants to cancel their membership and try to keep them as an Xbox Live customer by explaining the benefits and value of the Xbox Live service. This will be unique for every customer based on how the customer is currently using the service and why they want to leave.

Steps for the People’s Republic of China

If you are presented with the saves and retention workflow, select the following options:

  • I don't use Xbox Live anymore

  • I am too busy to use the service

Steps for the rest of world

Follow the steps below to help understand why the customer wants to leave Xbox Live, how to use MST to gain insight on how a customer is using Xbox Live, and identify resources based on the customer’s reason for cancelling.

  1. Ask the customer what they like about the Xbox Live service.

  2. Navigate to the Recent Activity section of MST. This is where you will find information specific to the apps and content the customer uses that you can leverage to connect with the customer and ask probing questions.

    [Asset 4095680]

  3. Ask the customer why they want to cancel their membership. Based on the customer’s response, use the resources in the Cancellation reason categories (below) to help the customer see the value of the Xbox Live service and to identify possible recommendations that will help keep the customer as an Xbox Live Gold member.

I have been banned for TOU violations.

  • My Xbox Live account was banned.
    • Do not refund or cancel the customer’s subscription – since they violated the terms of use, they are not eligible for a refund, and the subscription will be cancelled on the back-end.

    • If the customer has a specific question about an Xbox account suspension or console ban, please refer them to https://enforcement.xbox.com.

      Note If the customer was banned and was charged for a subscription after the date of the ban, refund the erroneous charge and cancel the subscription. To find the date of the enforcement action, review the Alerts section of MST:

      If the customer has a suspended account alert, it will present as a lock icon as shown below.




      Select the lock icon, and the customer’s enforcement date will be displayed. 

  • My console has been banned.

    Customers who have had the console banned may or may not also have their account banned. If the Xbox Live account has not been banned, the customer has several options to continue to use Xbox Live:
    • The customer can use their existing hard drive in another console.
    • The customer can download their profile onto a friend’s console and play at a friend’s house.
    • Customers who have a Windows 8 device or a Windows Phone can use Xbox Games to still keep up with their friends and play certain games.
      http://www.xbox.com/en-US/windows/games?xr=shellnav
    • If the customer has questions about why we ban consoles, refer to:
      http://support.xbox.com/en-US/billing-and-subscriptions/account-management/xbox-live-console-ban
    • If the customer has questions about the reason the console was banned, direct the customer to the enforcement website for support:
      https://enforcement.xbox.com

I can't use my Xbox console.

  • My Xbox was stolen.
    • Educate the customer on how to remotely password protect their Xbox Live profile. This will protect the account from being accessed by anyone other than the account holder:
      http://support.xbox.com/en-US/billing-and-subscriptions/account-security/password-protect-account
    • Customers who have a Windows 8/Windows 10 device or a Windows Phone can use Xbox Games to still keep up with their friends and play certain games.
      http://www.xbox.com/en-US/windows/games?xr=shellnav
    • Customers can download their profile onto a friend’s console and play while they are at a friend’s house. This would be a good option if they are planning on purchasing a new console shortly.
  • My console is OOW and I cannot afford a repair.
    • Customers who have a Windows 8 device or a Windows Phone can use Xbox Games to still keep up with their friends and play certain games.
    • Customers can download their profile onto a friend’s console and play while they are at a friend’s house. This would be a good option if they are planning on purchasing a new console shortly.
  • My console is in warranty but will repair it later.
    • Educate the customer on the online repair process – customers can quickly create a repair order at a reduced cost so they do not have to be without their console. If customer wants to cancel because of the repair process of lost time, refer to KB 2229086, Section 2.5, Customer lost time on subscription due to service order. Customer should be provided the one month one the repair or exchange process has been completed.

I am dissatisfied with the Xbox Live service.

  • I had a poor customer service experience.

    We should be doing everything we can to fix the underlying issue that has caused the customer to want to leave Xbox Live.
    • What happened that led the customer to determine they need to cancel? Review all case notes and ask probing questions to get a clear picture of what happened.
    • Do everything you can to make it right – check with a supervisor if you need help or are unsure of the steps needed to resolve the customer's issue.
    • If the issue the customer needs help with is not in your scope of support, transfer to a specialist. If needed, set up a callback to ensure the customer’s issue was resolved.
  • I have recurring broadband/connectivity problems.
    • Has the customer contacted support before on this issue?
    • Were we able to resolve but the issue returned?
    • Let the customer know you can transfer them to a connectivity specialist. If needed, set up a callback to ensure the customer’s issue was resolved.
  • I had charges I was not aware of on the account.
    • Check the refund policy in KB 3083551 Policy: One Microsoft common refund/return policies; the customer may be eligible for a one-time refund on the items purchased.
    • Educate the customer on how to update their contact information so that they will always receive account communications.

      https://account.microsoft.com/profile/contact-info
    • Educate customers on how to manage their account online and find charges.

      https://account.microsoft.com
  • I did not know my free trial would auto-renew.
    • Educate the customer on some of the benefits of auto-renewal:
      • Uninterrupted access to Xbox Live
    • Educate the customer on how to update their contact information so that they will always receive account communications.

      https://account.microsoft.com/profile/contact-info
    • Educate customer on how to find their renewal date online and on the console.

      https://account.microsoft.com/services
    • I prefer a competitor (Wii, PS4) because they are free.

      Use the link below to learn about the features and benefits of Xbox Live Gold:

      https://www.xbox.com/live/gold

    • Xbox Live Rewards–earn credits by having an Xbox Live membership.

I found a better price for the subscription.

  • I received an email or Dash offer.
    • Some promotional offers are only available to new Xbox Live customers.
    • The customer may be able to take advantage of the offer by "stacking" it onto their current subscription–this depends on what the subscription offer was.
    • Are they on the best plan that meets their needs? Moving to a different full price subscription might offer similar benefits.
  • I received a retail promotional offer.
    • Some promotional offers are only available to new Xbox Live customers.
    • Some retail offers cannot be used if the customer has already had a trial subscription before–have the customer check the fine print.
    • The customer may be able to take advantage of the offer by "stacking" it onto their current subscription–this depends on what the subscription offer was.
    • Are they on the best plan that meets their needs? Moving to a different full price subscription might offer similar benefits.
  • Retail pre-paid code (PPC) is cheaper
    Online PPC is cheaper

    Customers do not need to cancel their existing subscription to use a PPC; they can add the PPC to the existing subscription. This will allow them to keep their tenure and avoid service interruption.
    • Educate the customer on how to redeem the PPC online or on the console.

      https://support.xbox.com/browse/billing/prepaid-codes
    • Educate the customer on the next charge/renewal date on the subscription so the customer understands what to expect.

I have a problem with my Microsoft account and cannot access Live.

  • I cannot accept the Terms of Use (TOU).
    • If the customer needs help troubleshooting TOU acceptance issues, resolve the issue with their Microsoft account. If necessary, schedule a follow-up to make sure the customer’s issue is resolved.
    • If the customer does not know the Microsoft account for the Xbox account, refer to xbox.com/forgot.
    • If the customer does not know the password for their Microsoft account, refer the customer to:

      https://account.microsoft.com
  • I cannot get my password reset.
    • If the customer does not know the password for their Microsoft account, refer the customer to:

      https://account.microsoft.com
  • I cannot access the proofs on my account.
    • Customers can still access Xbox Live and use their subscription even if they do not have access to their proofs. However, they will not be able to perform certain account management functions online that require the customer to authenticate using a code sent to the security proofs. If the customer still needs help with proofs, refer to xbox.com/proofs.
  • I cannot access the parent or child account.
    • If the customer does not know the Microsoft account for the parent or child Xbox account, refer to xbox.com/forgot.
    • If the customer does not know the password for the parent or child Microsoft account, refer the customer to:

      https://account.microsoft.com

I don't use Xbox Live anymore.

  • I am on military deployment.
    I am moving.
    I am going away to school.
    • Educate the customer on how to take your Xbox Live profile with you by using a USB drive to store your profile.

      http://support.xbox.com/xbox-360/accessories/usb-flash-drive
    • Educate the customer that they can download their profile onto any console.
    • Educate the customer on how to save their games using cloud storage in the cloud so they can pick up where they left off–no matter what console they last used:

      https://support.xbox.com/games/game-setup/cloud-game-saves-faq
    • Customers who have a Windows 8 device or a Windows Phone can use Xbox Games to still keep up with their friends and play certain games.

      https://support.xbox.com/browse/xbox-on-windows
  • I am not sure if I like the service.

    Use the usage indicators to determine which features best would resonate with the customer:
    • Apps – are there apps the customer has not yet tried?

      https://www.microsoft.com/store
    • Games – are there any new titles coming out that the customer has played before? e.g., Halo. Call of Duty, Forza, etc.

      http://www.xbox.com/xbox-one/xbox-one-games?xr=shellnav
    • Kinect & Voice Control – has the customer tried this feature?

      http://www.xbox.com/kinect/entertainment
    • Cloud Storage – does the customer know about this feature?

      https://support.xbox.com/games/game-setup/cloud-save-games
    • Games for Gold – customers can download two free games per month.
    • Xbox Live Rewards – earn points by having an Xbox Live membership.
  • I am too busy to use the service.

    Most customers still think Xbox Live is only for playing games and don’t take advantage of the great entertainment apps. Use the usage indicators to determine which feature best would resonate with the customer:
    • Movies and TV–what movie and TV apps does the customer like? What movie and TV apps has the customer not yet tried?

      https://www.microsoft.com/store/movies-and-tv
    • Games–are there any new titles coming out that the customers have played before? i.e., Halo. Call of Duty, Forza, etc.

      https://www.xbox.com/games?xr=shellnav

I already have a subscription.

  • I have another gamertag with a subscription.
    • If the customer has a friend or family member who wants to use this account, the customer can change the Microsoft account associated with the account to the friend or family member’s Microsoft account. They may also want to change the payment instrument as well to avoid future subscription charges.
  • I accidentally purchased another subscription.
    • If the customer has a friend or family member who wants to use this account, the customer can change the Microsoft account associated with the account to the friend or family member’s Microsoft account. They may also want to change the payment instrument as well to avoid future subscription charges.

My subscription is in decline or suspended.

  • I do not have another payment method to use.
    • The customer will either need to add a new payment instrument or use a PPC. If the customer only has a PPC, they may need to bring the balance current before they can add the PPC to avoid the subscription cancelling.
  • I cannot afford the renewal fee.
    • Are they on the best plan that meets their needs? Moving to a different full price subscription plan might make it more affordable by getting rid of one of their cable boxes.
    • Do they rent a lot of movies? Crackle also allows free movie and TV series rentals–price is included in the subscription price.
    • Do they have other subscription services such as Amazon Prime, HBO GO, MLB? Make sure they know the additional features available for those apps in Xbox Live.
    • Many of the video apps offer movie rentals at great prices, making their entertainment dollar go further.
    • Xbox Live Rewards–earn points by having an Xbox Live membership.

I no longer want to pay for my child's subscription.

  • I had charges I was not aware of on the account.
    I have an overdraft fee because of a recent charge.
    • Check the refund policy in KB 3083551 Policy: One Microsoft common refund/return policies; the customer may be eligible for a one-time refund on the items purchased.
    • Educate the customer on how to update their contact information so that they will always receive account communications.

      https://account.microsoft.com/profile/contact-info
    • Educate the customer on how to find their renewal date online and on the console.

      https://account.microsoft.com/services
    • Educate the customer on how to password protect the console.

      http://support.xbox.com/billing-and-subscriptions/account-security/xbox-live-pass-codes
  • I want to limit the time my child use Xbox Live.
    • Set parental controls for the console including the Family Timer.

      https://support.xbox.com/xbox-360/security/parental-safeguards
    • Set online safety and privacy settings.

      https://www.microsoft.com/digital-skills/online-safety?rtc=1&activetab=protect-whats-important:primaryr3
  • I want to use a PPC.

    Customers do not need to cancel their existing subscription to use a PPC; they can add the PPC to the existing subscription.
    • Educate the customer on how to redeem the PPC online or on the console.

      https://support.xbox.com/billing/prepaid-codes/redeem-prepaid-codes
    • Educate the customer on the next charge/renewal date on the subscription so the customer understands what to expect.
  • My child is no longer living in the household.
    • If the child still wants to use the Xbox Live subscription, changing the payment instrument from the parent’s to another payment instrument (PI) will allow the current card holder to no longer pay for the subscription and the child to still use the subscription. If you use this option, be sure to first add the new PI and then remove the parent’s PI to avoid future charges.
  • I want to change the payment instrument on the subscription.
    • Educate customers that they do not need to cancel the existing subscription to change the card used to pay for the subscription. Update the payment option on the subscription. You may need to change the contact information for billing information as well.
