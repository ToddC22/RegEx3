What is this article about?

This article details how to address customer claims of a stolen/lost device, including Surface, Xbox console, mobile, and augmented/virtual reality devices. This should be followed when a customer claims their Microsoft device has been lost or stolen and voices concerns about the security of the information stored on it.

Note: For customer scenarios involving compromised Microsoft accounts, see KB 930018 Procedure: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account.

﻿﻿1. Procedure

When a customer reports their device, sensor, or accessories stolen, our policy is to advise customers to report the theft to their local police force and insurance company.

Customers can self-service device information by signing in to account.microsoft.com and reviewing all the devices listed in the “Devices” section and use the Find and lock a lost Windows device feature if enabled.

Use the following procedure when a customer reports that their device has been stolen.

Messaging:

"I'm sorry to hear that your device was stolen. Please report the theft to your local police. You might want to report the theft to your insurance company as well."

Create a service request for the customer and record that the customer has called to report a theft, including the type of device or accessory.

Authenticate customer’s account. See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

If the customer is the registered device owner and you can authenticate the customer successfully, you can tell them the device serial number. The customer may need the serial number for insurance reporting purposes.

If the customer is not able to verify ownership of the account, do not provide any information about the device or the device’s registration.

1.1 Recommend changing account password and removal of trusted devices

Advise the customer to change the password and remove trusted devices linked with their account by signing into account.microsoft.com and doing the following:

  • Reset password: Select the Security tab. From there, select Change your password and follow the steps to complete the process.
  • Update security info: Select the Security tab and then select Update your security info to see all security options. Under “Trusted devices,” select Remove all the trusted devices associated with my account.

1.2 Manage payment options

Customers can also manage the payment options and billing information associated with the account by selecting Payment & billing and then selecting Payment options.

1.3 Additional options – Commercial customers

  1. Ask the customer if the stolen device has remaining Extended Warranty (EHS or Complete for business).
    • If yes – Send an email to devicesAPEsc@microsoft.com and provide the serial number (SN) of the stolen device in order to remove Autopilot.
      1. Update all case notes.
      2. Create a collaboration task.
      3. Set the Support Topic to Warranty Eligibility and Registration.
    • If not – there is no further action.
  2. The customer should contact their IT group to determine whether Absolute Computrace software was purchased and enabled on the device.
  • Computrace, which locates, locks, deletes, and recovers a device, is a third-party software and support for Computrace is not provided by Microsoft.
  • For more information and support for Absolute Computrace, the customer can contact Absolute Computrace at http://www.absolute.com/en/support/contact.
  1. Microsoft Complete and Extended Service Plans for Commercial are non-refundable and non-transferable per the terms and conditions of the plans.

﻿2. Law enforcement (police) inquiries

If a caller identifies themselves as a law enforcement or police officer to obtain information about a registered device owner, do not provide any information.

See KB 950232 Procedure: Legal: How to respond to information requests from law enforcement for the procedure to handle these contacts.
