1. Verifying the business location

1.1 Overview

All Microsoft Advertising customers will see options to verify their business location within the UI:

  • Every customer will receive a prompt upon login to update their business location.
    • Though it can be dismissed by selecting the Remind Me Later button, it will continue to show each time the customer logs in and until they take the steps to verify their business location.
  • Some customers will also receive email reminders prompting them to verify this information.

1.2 What is the business location and why is it update important?

The business location is the country or region that an advertiser’s business is legally registered. If the customer is an agency, then it is the region where their client is legally registered.

Based on the business location, the customer might be required to pay tax on their advertising charges. Although this applies mostly to customers in Europe, all customers need to verify their business location, for each of their accounts. This is so that we can ensure that customers aren’t erroneously taxed.

1.3 Update the business location

The customer can verify their business location from either the Account details page or in the business location column. After they have done this, they shouldn’t be prompted anymore.

Single account:

Verify from the Account details page:

  1. In the business location and tax information section, select the pencil icon.
  2. Select the appropriate business location (country) from the drop down:
    • If the customer selects a taxable business location, they will be prompted to enter full address details and VAT registration number (optional).
    • If the customer selects a non-taxable business location, no additional detail fields will appear.
  3. Select Save.

Verify in the business location column:

  1. Select set location.
  2. Enter the business location and, if required, the full address and VAT registration number (optional).
  3. Select Save.

Multiple accounts:

Note: To edit in bulk, all accounts must have the same business location.

  1. From the Accounts tab within Accounts & Billing, select the accounts that need to be updated.
  2. In the business location column, select set location.
  3. Enter the business location and, if required, the tax information.
  4. Select Save.

Customers from Mexico will need their RFC tax number on their invoices. Follow the process below to help the advertiser add this number.

4. Brazil

4.1 Overview

In Brazil, billing is managed by Microsoft do Brasil Importação e Comércio de Software e Games LTDA.

  • As of February 13th, 2018, Microsoft Advertising is required to charge Imposto sobre Serviços (ISS) at 2.9% on advertising costs in Brazil (tax rate is subject to change).
  • As of January 1st, 2019, customers must have complete Account Business Location (ABL) details in their details and a tax ID, or risk deactivation of their account due to eNF failure.
    • For customers with a business account located in Sao Paulo city, a CCM registration number (Inscrição Municipal) is also required.

For more details including how this tax is applied per payment setting, see: Tax or VAT Information (select Brazil in the drop-down).

In Brazil, Microsoft pays the following taxes (these taxes are not passed on/charged to Microsoft Advertising advertisers):

  • Programa de Integração Social (PIS): This is a social contribution tax, payable by corporations, targeted to finance the payment of unemployment insurance and allowance for low paid workers. The current rate is 1.65%.
  • Contribuição para o Financiamento da Seguridade Social (COFINS): This is a federal contribution tax, based on gross revenues of business sales. The current rate is 7.6%.

4.2 Brazil ISS tax for personal and business customers

There are two unique tax statuses in Brazil which are self-proclaimed by our Microsoft Advertising Advertisers: Business and Personal.

Regardless of tax status, all Brazilian customers will be charged ISS tax on all invoice transactions (prepay, threshold and monthly invoice) as of the effective date. To successfully generate an eNF, we must have complete and accurate address details for all customers.

The difference is whether a valid tax ID must be provided.

  • Business customers are required to provide a registered business tax ID (CNPJ).
  • Personal customers may provide their personal tax ID (CPF). CPF is optional.

Microsoft will ship invoice transaction details to City Hall daily and eNFs are expected to be returned and linked to the Microsoft Advertising Billing grid within about 48 hours, although there is no published SLA with City Hall.

City Hall will perform three standard validation checks when data is shipped through:

  1. If this is a Business customer, is there a CNPJ? If no, they will reject the eNF.
  2. Is the CNPJ valid? If no, they will reject the eNF.
  3. Do the UF (state code) and CEP (postal code) match? If no, they will reject the eNF.

The tax ID type applies at the account (XID) level, and the advertiser may elect to have different tax IDs for each account; the tax ID type cannot be changed after the account is created.

  • São Paulo registered customers: As of February 2nd, 2018, São Paulo City Hall has introduced new functionality to their system when a São Paulo registered tax ID (CNPJ and/or CPF) is provided. When the tax ID provided is registered in São Paulo, City Hall will override any Microsoft Advertising data (customer name, address) with the data on record with City Hall. Should customers need to update the information on their eNF because of this, they must work with City Hall directly.
  • New for São Paulo customers as of January 2019 (exact date TBD): CCM tax field will be added to the business location and tax information section of Account Details for São Paulo customers. This field will be required for new customers and existing customers who make a change to their business location. This additional tax requirement is validated against the São Paulo government details. If customers receive a failed eNF notification, it could be that they are missing necessary CCM details or that their company details are not matching to São Paulo city record requirement.
  • For technical assistance with failed eNF's email Global Support.

4.3 eNF | Customer specific or Microsoft General eNF

The Brazilian government introduced new legislation at the end of January 2018 to assist service providers in the transition period to the ISS law for online advertising.

The new legislation Instrução Normativa 22/17 [IN 22/17] allows service providers to submit one monthly general eNF to City Hall for all invoice transactions from February 13th, 2018 through December 31st, 2018. Brazil Tax and Compliance agree that Microsoft Advertising should proceed in a hybrid manner.

Customer Specific eNF: Microsoft Advertising will respect the original legislation where possible and generate the Customer specific eNF when complete and accurate address and tax ID details are provided.

Microsoft General eNF: For those accounts with incomplete or inaccurate customer details, Microsoft Advertising will send those invoice transactions to City Hall on a General Monthly MSFT eNF.

  • Customers invoices that are covered in the General Monthly MSFT eNF will not receive an individual eNF and this is supported through the above legislation IN22/17. If a customer specific eNF is requested by the customer, we will not supply one to them. Based on the IN22/17, the individual eNFs will be replaced by the monthly general eNF.
  • Business customers are more likely to request the customer specific eNF as they are traditionally required to present their eNF to their tax authorities. Since the government published legislation IN 22/17, they are aware that the customers may have a reconciliation gap. Customers may choose to supply the Microsoft Advertising commercial invoice as IN 22/17 is the legal basis to support this.
  • Personal customers are less likely to request and require the customer specific eNF as they are not required to present their eNF to their tax authorities. Brazil does provide a unique opportunity to Personal customers where if they are registered with City Hall, they are entered in a lottery, and if they win the lottery they can reclaim their taxes paid from City Hall.

4.4 eNF with inaccurate details

Once rendered, eNFs can be electronically cancelled if a full adjustment is processed within the grace period. Partial adjustments do not affect eNFs, nor will any other tax document be generated. Advertisers should use the original eNF generated for all tax and accounting purposes and consult with their local tax authority with further questions.

  • SAP Monthly invoice grace period is through the final calendar day of the month that the Microsoft Advertising invoice was generated. For example, an invoice that was generated on March 1st, 2018 must have the cancellation requested and full credit processed in Microsoft Advertising by March 31st, 2018.
  • Prepay and Threshold invoice grace period is the 5th calendar day of the month following the invoice generation. For example, a prepay invoice that was generated on March 29th, 2018 must have the cancellation requested and full credit processed in Microsoft Advertising by April 5th, 2018.

Manual cancellation of an eNF is only possible outside of the grace period once the full value is credited. Send the following information to brzfisca@microsoft.com: customer-specified cancellation reason, eNF#, customer name, customer address (ABL), and taxID (CPF/CNPJ). Subject line of the email must read "eNF CANCELATION REQUEST – BING (SOLICITACAO DE CANCELAMENTO DE eNF – BING)".

Notes:

  • It may take up to one year for São Paulo City Hall to review and approve a manual eNF cancellation request.
  • There is no guarantee the request will be approved.
  • Brazil Tax will provide a protocol number and government website for the customer to track progress once the request has been submitted.

An eNF should generate and be available to download in the Microsoft Advertising Billing grid for all invoice transactions (prepay, threshold and monthly invoice). Note: If a tax ID update is made and the customer is SAP, inform GFS (also known as WOCS) so that they can update their records.

4.6 CPOM

This information was sourced: http://thebrazilbusiness.com/article/introduction-to-cpom.

CPOM, short for Cadastro de Empresas Fora do Município, which is Portuguese for Register of Companies from Other Municipalities, is a mandatory registration for legal entities that provide services and issue invoices or equivalent documents in a different city from where the company is legally established. These companies must be registered with CPOM, which is organized by the Municipal Secretariat of Finances of major cities.

Microsoft must be CPOM registered in municipalities when we contract with and provide services to a company legally registered outside of São Paulo. We can only apply for CPOM registration when we can prove that we have a customer located in that specific municipality. There are nearly 5,000 municipalities across Brazil and each has a unique CPOM registration process or set of requirements.

6.2 India e-invoice

For businesses located in India, changes to billing documents are occurring as of October 1st, 2020, as required by the Government of India for e-invoicing.

  • QR code: Each billing document will contain a QR (Quick Response) code that can be scanned to display the details of a billing document on a separate mobile or tablet device.
  • IRN: If you provide a Goods and Services Tax Identification Number (GSTIN), we’ll supply a government issued Invoice Reference Number (IRN) on your billing document.

All customers in India will encounter system validation to ensure their business address in Microsoft Advertising matches the registered address on their GSTIN. The first two digits of the customers GSTIN should also match the first two digits of the state code. If there is a mismatch between their complete address (including PIN) and the registered address on their GSTIN, then Microsoft Advertising won’t be able to issue a government approved tax invoice.

Important:

These official tax documents (called an e-invoice) are possible with the help of India government records validation. The validated IRN (tax number) and QR code make the tax document unique and only available when validation successfully occurs between the customer entered data points in the Microsoft Ads platform (customers GSTIN tax ID and business entity) and what is on file with the India government. Once validation is successful, an e-invoice is successfully rendered in the tax documents section of the billing grid. If an e-invoice fails, the validation was not successful (for example, a mismatch between what’s entered in the UI and what is on file with the India government) and the customer will have an option to update the UI details to fix it.

There are four potential statuses in the UI for tax invoices:

  1. Available – e-invoice generated successfully.
  2. Pending – e-invoice is awaiting generation and should be available soon.
  3. Failed – occurs when an update needs to occur in the UI via the ‘fix it’ link (due to missing or incorrect tax details).
  4. Failed – this status can remain following completion of the ‘fix it’ workflow, while system is awaiting new invoice generation.

How to identify and resolve failures due to incorrect tax ID’s or ABL details:

Depending on the advertiser’s payment method, the success, failure, and resolution of failed e-invoices will differ. Always check the customer payment method to determine how to assist with resolution:

Postpay threshold:

  • When a billing document is issued a incorrect or mismatched tax ID or business location details, it will result in failed status with a ‘fix it’ link.
  • The ‘fix it’ link will take the client to update their tax ID or ABL details. Once updated, the system will automatically do a credit and rebill.

Note: The tax document will still show as ‘failed’, but the ‘fix it’ link will disappear.

  • Once complete, a credit memo will be generated, along with a rebilled billing document and tax document.

Prepay (online payment method):

  • Tax validation occurs when a client attempts to add their funds.
  • If an account has an incorrect tax ID or business location, they will not be able to add funds until the mismatch is fixed.
  • If the tax ID or business location does not match, auto-recharge will be disabled.
  • There will be no failed tax documents for online prepay customers due to these preventative measures.

Prepay (offline payment method):

  • With offline payments (such as, check or wire), there is no online tax validation.
  • Once the offline payment is received and posted to the account, tax validation takes place.
  • If the tax ID or business location does not match, the tax document will have a failed status and ‘fix it’ link.
  • The ‘fix it’ link will take the client to update the tax ID or ABL.
  • Once updated, the message will change, telling the client to ‘Contact Support for assistance’.
  • CSS will need to manually initiate a credit for offline payment methods, resulting in a credit memo for the offline payment.
  • Once the credit memo has generated, CSS should request that CASHAPPS reapply funds (as identified by the credit memo). As an alternate, the customer can obtain an offline refund (via SANOPS).
  • Reminder: To request reapplication of funds from CASHAPPS, use UCMT. This team does not get a lot of requests of this nature, so be clear in your request by:
    • Clearly stating your subject line: Reapply offline payment to Microsoft Advertising customer account.
    • Always include the customer’s account number, amount, credit memo, and note the original form of payment (check/wire).
    • Explain that a refund occurred but the customer desires to reapply the prepaid funds to their account.
    • Understand that this may require special approvals and take a few days to complete.
    • Escalate to your TA if there are issues or complications with this process beyond four day SLA.

Monthly invoice:

  • Monthly invoice customers will be validated similarly to postpay threshold.
  • When a billing document is issued, incorrect or mismatched tax ID or business location details will result in failed status with a ‘fix it’ link.
  • The ‘fix it’ link will take the client to update their tax ID or ABL details. Once updated, the fix it link will disappear.
  • The credit memo and rebilled document will not appear until the 1st or 2nd day of the following billing month (just like standard invoice billing).
  • From the adjustment tab, internal users will see that the invoice has a credit and rebill pending.

Note: The tax document will still show as ‘failed’, but the ‘fix it’ link will disappear.

  • Once complete, a credit memo will be generated, along with a rebilled billing document, and tax document.

6.3 Request for tax reimbursement

There are special circumstances for India where customers may request tax reimbursement from Microsoft. Validation of such requests must be done via SANOPS. This can require extensive SLA’s given nuances in market and lack of systematic process to validate and provide such refunds. Never promise tax refunds in advance of validation and authorization. Once a tax refund has been authorized and approved, determine how the advertiser prefers to receive the refund. It could be in the form of a credit in UI or require a credit/rebill. For questions or concerns with how to issue such refunds, refer to the tax authority (via SANOPS) or your TA: Pre/post-pay customers – Full credit and rebill required.

  • To claim the new tax back from the Indian government when the customer's post-pay threshold or prepay billing documents generated July 1st, and forward do not show the new tax types and rates (for example, CGST and SGST or IGST, and going from 15% to 18%).
    Or
  • If the customer wants their post-pay threshold or prepay billing documents to show their PAN or GSTIN beginning July 1st, but they had not defined these on their account prior to the July statements being generated.

India Invoice customers – full credit and rebill required:

  • To claim the new tax back from the Indian government when the customer's SAP invoice documents generated August 1st for July activity, and forward do not show the new tax types and rates (for example, CGST and SGST or IGST, and going from 15% to 18%).
    Or
  • If the customer wants their SAP invoice documents to show their PAN or GSTIN beginning July 1st, but they had not defined these on their account prior to the August statements being generated.

    Note: For invoices generated July 2    nd-3rd (for June activity), these will be compliant with the new Indian tax regulations, but not yet introducing the new tax logic since these will have a workaround of June 30th, 2017 printed on the invoice instead of a July date.

Complete the rebill by following the process on the KB 3173012 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Credits, Refunds, & Rebills.

6.4 FAQ

Q: What is India GST?

A: India Goods and Service Tax (GST) is a new tax regime in India which applies to Microsoft Advertising. This new tax regime replaces an existing service tax in India.

Q: What's changing?

A: Customers in India (with their account business location in India) will receive three different tax line items on their invoice. Depending on their location, different tax rates will apply. Combined, all tax rates will be 18% vs. 15% which is applied prior to GST. Furthermore, customers will need to provide their GSTN and PAN numbers (local tax numbers). These numbers will be surfaced on the customers’ invoice.

Q: What is the impact on my customer?

A: We are asking all customers with ABL = India who have not provided their full account business location (ABL) address details, their PAN (required) and GSTIN (optional) number to provide this. This address is collected at the account level and can be updated from the All Accounts grid or the single account view.

Q: Why is my customer seeing a lightbox?

A: The lightbox is triggered if a customer in India has not provided their PAN number or the full ABL address yet. Non-SAP accounts are required to provide this information as it is vital for us to gather this data from customers.

Q: Why are we doing this enforcement?

A: We are doing this enforcement because we want to have the legally registered business address to help us identify, and understand the companies we are doing business with. This address is also used to determine if and how much tax should be applied to the customer.

Q: From whom do we need the address if my customer is an agency?

A: The address information which we are collecting at account level is based on the Sold-to, this means we must collect the address of the end customer, not of the agency.

Q: What about SAP customers?

A: For SAP customers, we leverage the Sold-to SAP information of the customer to determine the legally registered business address. All information is stored in SAP and must be manually updated by SAP. We should already have all information, but help collect the PAN & GSTIN Number reactively and send it to GFS (also known as WOCS) per your standard operating procedure so that it can be added to SAP.

Q: What happens if my customer does not provide this information now?

9.5 VAT disputes and exemptions (invoice/non-invoice)

VAT exemption certificate:

EMEA customers are not required to pay VAT if they provide Microsoft with a VAT Exemption Certificate. This certificate cannot be provided into the Microsoft Advertising UI and must be provided by the customer via email. Only banks and non-profits qualify for VAT exemption. Insurance Companies, Financial Services Companies, charities are not vat exempt. These organizations may not be obliged to charge VAT on their supplies or goods/services others purchase from them, but they are required to pay VAT on advertising.

The following are acceptable forms of proof of VAT exemption:

  • VAT Exemption Certificate.
  • Valid VAT registration number (if they have an exempt status on their business).
  • A written reference to a local legislative provision on the company header paper that can be verified.
  • VAT 56B Form (Irish customers only).

If a​​​​​ customer does not have one of the above proofs of VAT exemption, Bing will charge VAT. There are no exceptions to this rule. If an advertiser provides a proof of exemption, escalate the proof the appropriate team per the processes below.

Customers may provide proof of VAT registration or Exemption to be considered for a full rebill of incorrectly taxed documents.

FAQ

Q: How should we treat customers with an agency hybrid shell?

A: If the agency is linked to an advertiser shell, and the advertiser is the bill-to and sold-to party, then the advertiser is the end customer and should therefore have to enter their ABL and VAT.

Q: What is a SoldTo SAP and how is it changing?

A: Think of it as the payment method that is associated to an invoiced account. This payment method (just like a credit card) contains crucial customer information that is utilized to run checks against a customer’s financial history for us to extend them with a credit line that they can use to accrue charges. A SoldTo SAP is specifically created and used to find out the end customer for which we are delivering ads. The party who ultimately is getting the return out of the advertising.

In SoldTo deprecation, we will only perform checks against the BillTo SAP that is associated to an invoiced account. The SoldTo details are being replaced with Full Account Business Location, allowing us to store this information for taxation and sequential liability purposes.

Q: Why use Full Account Business Location?

A: Full Account Business Location will start being enforced in the product. With this effort we will be collecting the same type of information that we have for SoldTo SAPs at the account level. This will allow us to get the information upfront and remove the dependency on SoldTo SAPs to know who the end customer for an account is.

The information available for Full ABL contains the same required for a SoldTo SAP. The plan is to utilize it to collect the required information that can be passed to WOCS for validation of who the end customer is for a managed invoiced account.

Q: Who is benefited by this change of using Full Account Business Location instead of SoldTo SAPs?

A: Most importantly, the managed customers who are linked to another party responsible for billing. Customers will not have to wait for offline processes to complete to get their accounts eligible for delivery and spend. If they are linked to another party who becomes responsible for billing, they will spend against the credit limit already assigned for that party. Important: Our direct advertisers or accounts who are managed by another party that is not taking over billing will go through the same process of needing an SAP ID.

Q: What about existing accounts that are set up with a SoldTo SAP?

A: For accounts in the system that currently have a SoldTo SAP that is different than the BillTo SAP we will start grabbing the corresponding information from the SAP and migrate it to Full ABL. We will migrate incrementally by market groups.

Q: What happens after SoldTo SAPs get deprecated and migrated to Full ABL?

A: Upon being migrated, an invoiced account does not have a dependency to have a SoldTo SAP anymore. From that point onwards all workflows where the SoldTo SAP was being surfaced will now surface the Full ABL.

All workflows or rules that relied on SoldTo SAP should start only utilizing Full ABL. Allowing the end customer to enter the information inline, which will be immediately sent for WOCS Risk review in the form of a system triggered UCM-T ticket.

AMs and support advocates do not need to do this on behalf of the customer through an offline ticket. The customer can perform the action or if hand holding is needed this can be done by internal users in the product. Full ABL will be required for invoiced accounts when taking over billing, agency linking with billing takeover and payment option switching to invoice. We are now enforcing Full ABL everywhere. No more accounts without it.

Q: What is the process if any follow up is required?

A: An account will be placed on manual hold while the clarifying information is provided. If customer outreach is required, WOCS will reach out to CSS (or AM for managed advertisers) to request customer outreach for necessary information.

Q: What does this mean to your customers?

A: All customers who are managed by agencies (with linked accounts) where the agency is responsible for billing will be required to provide a Full Account Business Location.

  • The business location should be that of the end customer as called out in the help pop up in the product.
    • Business location
      • What it is: This is the country/region where your business is legally registered. (If you are an agency, this is the country/region where your client is legally registered.)
      • Why is it important: Business location determines your tax accounting obligations.

Q: There will be accounts that will transition from MOI to MIOL (Microsoft Ireland Operations Limited) based on the country submitted in the business location. We had instances where a lot of customers had NULL SoldTo information and now is our opportunity to get their real location.

A: We look at the Account Business Location (ABL) on file for each individual account. If the country is in an in-tax scope market, the account will be taxed unless a TAX VAT ID/Certificate is provided.

  • If the ABL=blank on the account, the customers will be forced through several workflows to provide this information. In this scenario accounts whose country falls into MIOL will transition to that company code.
    • When this is the case two billing documents will be generated. All remittance instructions and the reason for the change are being exposed in the invoices for customers to understand how to proceed moving forward.
  • The customer may be prompted to enter their TAX VAT ID, if it is not already on the account. In such cases, the ensuing billing cycle will generate only one billing document for the month, regardless of when the TAX VAT ID was added to the account.  It will reflect the TAX/VAT amount and apply it for the entirety of the month.

Q: How will this impact my customers account activity? Is there a chance they will go dark?

A: Accounts will continue to deliver during this transition. There is no expected downtime.

Q: Will this introduce any new tax treatment for our customers?

A: Yes, this change introduces taxing if the Full Business Location country falls under a taxable country/company code.

Q: How does the deprecation impact monthly invoice customers?

A: Monthly invoice customers who transition to Microsoft Ireland Operations Ltd., will need to update their payment remittance instructions for all invoices issued by MIOL. This is explained in the billing documents. Impacted accounts have been transitioning since Mid-April, as this is mainly for EMEA. The United States and France are scheduled to transition in July. The customer may receive two invoices for the month when their SoldTo SAP ID is deprecated and if they move to MIOL.

Q: Why will my customer possibly see two invoices for the month they transition to MIOL?

A: Depending on the exact date and time the account SoldTo SAP ID deprecates, the system will generate two invoices. One invoice will reflect ad delivery and revenue associated with Microsoft Online Inc., and the other invoice will be from the transition date onward reflecting ad delivery and revenue associated with Microsoft Ireland Operations Ltd.

Q: Can my customer pay for two invoices with one single payment?

A: Yes. We are aware that some customers may only be able to issue a single payment to a vendor for a billing period. Our accounts receivable team can accept and acknowledge payment for two invoices with one payment if the following requirements are provided with the payment:

  • Customer name
  • Billing document numbers

Q: Where will my customer find more information about the changes to monthly invoice remittance changes?

A: Customers will be notified within their monthly invoice documents issued starting in March. The Microsoft Advertising Help files will also be updated to include this information.
