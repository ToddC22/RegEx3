Procedure: Handling abusive customers

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Microsoft support employees, supplier partners and advocates/engineers (consumer & commercial).  

What is this article about?

This article describes handling instructions for customers who exhibit abusive behaviors such as harassment, including abusive language and tone.

Note:

This process is not meant to address customers who abuse their entitlements to support (for example, fraudulent activities) or those who exhibit non-legal threatening behaviors. For non-legal threats, refer to KB 4508928 Procedure: Legal: Handling threats of imminent bodily harm. If the customer is requesting for a legal contact or arbitration, refer to KB 2629445 Procedure: Legal: Assisting customers requesting for a legal contact or arbitration for additional guidance.

In this procedure Premier Account Representative equals Technical Account manager (TAM), Support Delivery manager (SDM), Incident manager (IM) or Support Account Coordinator (SAC).

1.  Overview

You may receive contacts from customers where the conversation turns abusive due to frustration during a support incident OR those who contact support with the intent to abuse.

These situations may include the following:

Profanity/vulgar language used Personal insults/name calling Irate, yelling or screaming Sexual advances/ harassment
Excessive/repeated contacts Racial, ethnic or religious slurs used    

 

While we should not tolerate abusive customer behavior, if circumstances during the support contact (for example, customer does not agree with outcome) led the customer to lose sight and lose track, this procedure provides guidance on how to drive recovery.

It is important to differentiate those who contact support with the intent to abuse. In this instance, you likely do not know who the caller is and there is no known support issue. There is zero tolerance for this type of behavior.

Review the proceeding sections for handling instructions.

1.1  Things to do and not do

Do:

  • Maintain your professionalism.
  • Keep your patience.
  • Use active listening.
  • Always be aware of your tone.
  • Be sincere.
  • Use call control.
  • Attempt to resolve the core problem.
  • Set proper expectations.
  • Personalize the conversation.
  • Declare your intent and boundaries.

Do not:

  • Take anything personally.
  • Match the tone.
  • Yell or scream.
  • Turn off active listening.
  • Let the person intimidate you.

Return to top

1.2  Requests to speak with a manager or supervisor

If the customer asks for a manager, have your supervisor or manager take over the contact. If one is not available at time of the customer contact, set appropriate expectations for a call back. Never refuse to have your supervisor or manager take over the contact.

2.  Procedures

2.1  Telephone and chat

This section applies to all Consumer and Commercial advocates and engineers.

1. Attempt to calm the customer. Suggested talking points are provided for reference only. Select options based on the context of the discussion.

Scenario

Suggested scripting
  • Irate, yelling or screaming
  • “[Insert customer name], I am going to do everything I can to help you.”
  • “[Insert customer name], I understand that you are frustrated. I am going to do everything that I can to help you.”
  • “[Insert customer name], I can see that you had a bad experience up to this point. However, I am here to help you get to a solution.”
  • Profanity/vulgar language used
  • Personal insults/name calling
  • Sexual advances/ harassment
  • Racial, ethnic or religious slurs used     
  • “[Insert customer name], to help you, we need to keep this conversation professional.”
  • “[Insert customer name], I find your comment offensive. For us to best help you, I ask that we keep this conversation professional.”
  • “[Insert customer name], please do not swear at me. I understand you are frustrated, but we need to keep this contact professional for us to be able to assist you.”
  • Excessive repeated contacts
  • “[Insert customer name], I see that you contacted us multiple times. Is there anything else I can assist you with?”

 

2. Use the decision point below:

Scenario

Your action

There is an underlying support issue

Proceed to Step 3.

Caller is just being abusive

  • You do not know who the caller is (for example, no authenticated account)
  • There is no true support issue

Relay suggested talking point below and end contact. Report this experience to your supervisor or manager.

Suggested talking point:

“I have to end this contact. Please contact us back when we can have a conversation at a professional level. Thanks for contacting [insert department]. Goodbye.”

Repeated contacts by likely the same customer: If there are no underlying support issues and you or others already relayed the suggested talking point above at least once, you may just hang up the contact.

 

3. Use the table below to determine next actions depending on severity of infraction.

Note: If you are at any point uncomfortable with handling the situation, skip below and proceed to section 2.1.1 (consumer) or section 2.1.2 (commercial) for additional guidance.

Scenario

Your action

Aimed at you:

  • Profanity / vulgar language used
  • Personal insults / name calling
  • Sexual advances / harassment
  • Racial, ethnic or religious slurs used        

Repeat scripts from step 1 – up to 1 time only

Consumer: Proceed to section 2.1.1

Commercial: Proceed to section 2.1.2

  • Irate, yelling or screaming
  • Excessive repeated contacts

Not aimed at you:

  • Profanity/vulgar language used
  • Racial, ethnic or religious slurs used

Repeat scripts from step 1 – up to 3 times only

Consumer: Proceed to section 2.1.1

Commercial: Proceed to section 2.1.2

Return to top

2.1.1  Consumer

This section is a continuation from section 2.1 above. Ensure you followed steps there first before continuing.

  1. If you are at any point uncomfortable with handling the customer, you can have your supervisor or manager take over the contact by relaying the following.

Note: If your supervisor or manager is not taking over the contact, skip this step and proceed to the next step.

Suggested talking points:

“Since we are unable to work towards a resolution together, I would like to have my supervisor (or manager) take over this contact. Please kindly hold.”

Note: The customer may not agree to be put on hold. Since you cannot assist the customer, relay the message and place them on hold while your supervisor or manager takes over.

Supervisor or manager: Repeat steps in section 2.1 before proceeding to next step.

  1. Inform the customer that you are disconnecting the contact. Relay the following to the customer.

Suggested talking points:

“I want to help you resolve your problem, but we need to keep our communication professional. If you continue, I will have to end this contact.”

  1. If the customer does not stop, disconnect the contact after relaying below.

Suggested talking points:

“I have to end this contact. Please contact us when we can have a professional conversation. Thanks for contacting [insert department]. Goodbye.”

Return to top

2.1.2  Commercial

This section is a continuation from section 2.1 above. Ensure you followed that guidance before continuing.

Note: If at any point the customer recovers composure, even after the threshold is reached, you may assist the customer. But, if you remain uncomfortable with the situation, you may have a peer take over, or continue to escalate.

  1. Inform the customer that you need to set up a call back. Relay the following to the customer.

Suggested talking points:

“Since we are unable to work towards a resolution together, I would like to have [your Premier Account Representative /  Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers] OR [my supervisor / manager] contact you back for next steps. Is there a preferred time?”

  1. If the customer provides a preferred call back time, set appropriate expectations.
  1. Disconnect the contact after relaying below.

Suggested talking points:

“I have to end this contact now. But please expect a call back from [your Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers] OR [my supervisor / manager] for next steps. Goodbye.”

  1. Engage the customer’s Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers OR your supervisor or manager. Provide context on the escalation, referencing this KB 2626175.
  2. Await feedback from the customer’s Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers OR your supervisor or manager before taking any further actions.
  3. When you receive an update to engage the customer again, call the customer back. If you are uncomfortable, you can have another engineer engage the customer. 
  1. If for whatever reason the customer is abusive again even after the recent escalation, relay to the customer the following.

Suggested talking points:

“I have to end this contact now. I will inform [your Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers] OR [my supervisor / manager] that we are closing the case for now. Please contact us back when we can have a conversation at a professional level. Thanks for contacting [insert department]. Goodbye.”

  1. Inform the customer’s Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers OR your supervisor or manager that you closed the case.

Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) / supervisor or manager instructions:

  1. Attempt to calm the customer. When the customer is calm and ready to be assisted with the core issue, have the engineer call the customer back.
  2. If the customer continues to be abusive, close the case.

Return to top

2.2  Email

This section applies to all Consumer and Commercial advocates and engineers.

If you encounter inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior in an email communication, please notify your supervisor or manager for review.

Not replying to an email is not an option. However, we should always remind the customer that you are available to assist if professional communication can be maintained.

Return to top

2.2.1  Consumer

This section is a continuation from section 2.2 above. Ensure you followed steps there first before continuing.

If abusive behavior persists, seek approval from your supervisor or manager to relay a response that includes below.

START

We are committed to providing the best customer support experience possible. However, this can only be accomplished if the conversation remains professional. We will stop replying beyond this point. Please contact us back when you are ready to resume the conversation at a professional level. Thank you.

END

Return to top

2.2.2  Commercial

This section is a continuation from section 2.2 above. Ensure you followed steps there first before continuing.

  1. If abusive behavior persists, relay a response that includes below.

START

We are committed to providing the best customer support experience possible. However, this can only be accomplished if the conversation remains professional. At this point, let me engage [your Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers] OR [my supervisor / manager] for next steps. Thank you.

END

  1. Engage the customer’s Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) – managed Dynamics customers OR your supervisor / manager. Provide context on the escalation, referencing this KB 2626175.

Premier Account Representative / Support Account manager (SAM) / supervisor or manager instructions:

  1. Attempt to calm the customer. When the customer is calm and ready to be assisted with the core issue, have the engineer reengage.
  2. If the customer continues to be abusive, close the case.
