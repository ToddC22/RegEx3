1. What is personal data?

Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes verified and non-verified data, linked or linkable to a PUID, Device ID, ANID, or any similar ID that singles out a user.

An identifiable individual can be directly or indirectly identified by:

  1. A name, an identification number, location data, or online identifier.
  2. One or more factors specific to the physical, psychological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity of that individual.

See KB 3064766 Procedure: Privacy: Handling Microsoft customer personal data for more information.

2. What is a data protection incident?

A data incident is the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized release of, or access to personal data that was transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed.

Common examples of data protection incidents within CSS include:

  • An engineer is working on two cases. The engineer sends an analysis to customer A that was meant for customer B. Customer A now has personal information and possible environmental information about customer B.
  • Any full (16 digit) credit card/CVV number that was entered into a chat window.*
  • The customer provides a credit card number before the advocate freezes the call recording.
  • The engineer uploads the customer files to the wrong file share.
  • The customer sends sensitive or HBI data, including log files, or environmental information containing end-user information through systems outside of Data Transfer Management (DTM) or a privacy-approved alternative.
  • The supplier team sends 5X5 product keys, log files, and other highly confidential personal data in an unsecured way, such as by email, to Microsoft internal teams for review.
  • Remote assistance support incidents where an advocate inadvertently views, opens, deletes, copies, captures screen images, or edits any files or documents which are not related to the issue. All actions dealing with files and documents must be performed by the customer.

While these are some common scenarios, this is not a complete list. If you have any concerns regarding whether an incident has occurred, be cautious and report it.

* What isn’t considered a data protection incident:

  • Partial credit card information, name on a credit card, or expiry date (for example, only the last four digits or only the full name inserted in the chat window or email).

3. Procedure for handling data protection incidents

It is important that you report all data protection incidents immediately. Failure to do so could have legal and regulatory implications for Microsoft and our processors.

If you suspect that an incident has occurred, follow the steps below:

Internal (access to CorpNet)
  • Report the incident to aka.ms/servicesincident.

Outside of CorpNet
  • Report incidents by email to IPGIRTeam@microsoft.com, or escalate to your Technical Advisor/Service Delivery Manager immediately to report the incident on aka.ms/servicesincident:

1. Fill out the site questions.

2. Select Submit.

3. Select Report an incident.

4. Provide the following information:

  • Time and time zone incident occurred.
  • Cause of reported event – customer or support advocate.
  • Time incident was reported (start of this item created).
  • Name (username as displayed) and location (if possible, to understand) of customer whose information is exposed.
  • Geography (country) where the incident occurred.
  • Full name and location of customer who received exposed information.
  • Description of what happened or was exposed.
  • Case (SR) number involved.
    • Include required details such as chat ID, visit ID, or engagement ID (as applicable).
  • Supplier/Contact center name.
  • Supplier/Contact center site name.

Note: Ensure that copying relevant stakeholders is kept to a minimum when reporting incidents. For example, IPGIRT contact, team lead, or relevant Ranger (Xbox) only. Do not copy multiple distribution lists.

Protecting customer data is the responsibility of all of us and powers our company mission of trust and empowering every person and every organization on the planet to achieve more.
