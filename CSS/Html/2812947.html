1. Applies to

The following services: Office 365, Dynamics 365, Microsoft Intune
The following advocate roles: All roles
The following markets: All markets (For detailed information about product-specific availability, see 2748142: Markets where Microsoft Online Services are available .)
 

Scenario

This document describes the periods of time for a subscription between Active, In grace period, Disabled, De-provisioned, and tenant deletion.

Guidance

Lifecycle information pertaining to a tenant and its subscriptions can only be shared with the Global or Billing Administrators of the tenant.

2. Description of information

Direct and Prepaid Subscription status:

Status in CMAT Reason in CMAT Term end date column in the Microsoft 365 admin center Description of Status
Active Other Displays subscription end date Customer’s subscription is Active.
In grace period Lifecycle Canceled (reduced functionality)

Billing stops if user's subscription status is In grace period with reason codes such as "Lifecycle" or "UserRequest". Otherwise, if the subscription status is In grace period with reason code "Payment", the customer will be billed for this time and can make a payment to re-enable the subscription.

NOTE: The "In grace period" status will cause Power BI Premium subscriptions to lose access.
Disabled Lifecycle Canceled (reduced functionality) Users no longer have access to their services except for Exchange and SharePoint. Exchange may continue to allow users to send emails, and SharePoint remains active. All admin functions remain viable except for license management. Admins can still add or delete users and purchase new subscriptions, but they cannot revoke or assign licenses. The service can be restored by re-enabling the subscription, except for prepaid subscriptions.

Prepaid subscriptions, once in the Disabled status, cannot be renewed by the customer or re-enabled by Support.
Disabled LifecycleLockout Canceled (Locked out) All services are shut down for this subscription. A subscription can be re-enabled from this status. Permissions to re-enable a subscription in this status are currently only held by Tier 3 Commerce Engineering. Refer to 2772686: Enabling expired or disabled subscriptions for Tier 1 instructions to escalate to Tier 3.
De-provisioned Lifecycle Canceled (data deleted) or subscription removed entirely Customer’s subscription and all data are deleted. It can occur as soon as 90 days or as long as 135 days after customer receives the cancellation request. There is no recovery from this status.

a. Trial
1. Active to In grace period.
  1. The majority of trials are active for 30 days. To determine the date the trial will move from Active to In grace period, refer to the Current Status End Date field in the Subscription details of the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT). When this date is reached, the trial will move from Active to In grace period. 

    Notes: By design, Dynamics trials do not have a grace period. These trials will be de-provisioned 7 days after they are disabled.For details of specific SKU, you may also visit Offer Tool.  
2. In grace period to Disabled.
  1. The time that a subscription remains in the In grace period status varies from offer to offer but is generally 30 days. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from In grace period to its next status, Disabled.
     
3. Disabled to Disabled LifecycleLockout.
  1. The time that a subscription remains in the Disabled status varies from offer to offer but is generally 30 days. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from Disabled to its next status, Disabled with Current Reason Code: LifecycleLockout.

    Note: CMAT will not reflect the Next Status as Disabled:LifecycleLockout. It will show Next Status as De-provisioned. When the subscription is moved to Disabled:LifecycleLockout, the Current Status End Date will be moved out 7 more days. Therefore, you may notice that the Current Status End Date for Next Status: De-provisioned changes by 7 days.
     
4. Disabled LifecycleLockout to De-provisioned.
  1. This is a variation of the disabled status prior to de-provisioning. The timeframe a subscription remains in this status can vary. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from Disabled LifecycleLockout to De-provisioned.

    Note: When an Office 365 subscription is de-provisioned, all mailboxes and subscription data is deleted and becomes unrecoverable. Administrators can still log on to the Microsoft 365 admin center and buy additional or new subscriptions on the existing tenant as well as manage domains and users.
5. All subscriptions de-provisioned to tenant deletion.
  1. Administrators will be able to log on to their tenant, perform administrative functions and make purchases.

    If all subscriptions are de-provisioned and the customer is able to log in, but they do NOT have the option to purchase, the customer will need to create a new tenant. If they would like to use the same tenant, please let them know that the tenant is queued for deletion and it cannot be stopped or expedited. The customer can wait for the tenant to be deleted, but it could take a day to months to complete the deletion and free up the tenant for re-creation.

    When the tenant is deleted from Commerce it will still be visible in CMAT, but the customer will not be able to make purchases on the tenant.

    When the tenant is completely deleted, the admin will no longer be able to log on and the tenant will not be visible in CMAT. Timeframes for these deletion actions are variable but generally take months.

b. Paid-Credit Card
1. Active to In grace period.
  1. Dunning:
    i. The billing system tries to charge the customer. If the customer’s credit card is declined, the customer goes into dunning. The customer is sent a notification via email.

    ii. As soon as the customer is in dunning, the system will try to charge the customer five additional times at an interval of 3 days between each try.

    The first dunning retry begins 3 days after the credit card charge failed for the first time. A notification is sent to the customer via email for each failed attempt.

    iii. The day after the fifth try (16th day of dunning), the subscription will be put into In grace period, and administrators will see a warning message in the Microsoft 365 admin center. Along with the decline notification, a status change notification is emailed to the customer.
    If the subscription is re-enabled, the account will not be charged until the next billing cycle.

    iv. On the 18th day, there will be one last try to charge the customer’s card. If this charge is successful, the subscription will be re-enabled. The customer is sent a status change notification upon being re-enabled.

    v. If the next try on the 18th day is unsuccessful, no more tries will be made on the customer’s card until the next account anniversary day. One last decline notification is sent to the customer during this attempt.
    NOTE: The billing platform checks for unpaid balances every month on the account anniversary day for both monthly- and annually-billed customers. If an annually-billed credit card customer has an unpaid balance after the dunning cycle, the billing platform will retry the charge to the credit card on the next account anniversary day the following month.
  2. Auto-renewal:
    Paid subscriptions are generally active for 1 year from the date of purchase. If the customer has auto-renewal turned off, the subscription will move to In grace period when the end date is reached. If the end date is reached and auto-renewal is turned on, the subscription will be renewed for another year.
2. In grace period to Disabled.
  1. The time that a subscription remains in the In grace period status varies by the offer but is generally 30 days. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from In grace period to its next status, Disabled.
3. Disabled to Disabled LifecycleLockout.
  1. The time that a subscription remains in the Disabled status varies from offer to offer but is generally 90 days. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from Disabled to its next status, Disabled with Current Reason Code: LifecycleLockout.

    Note: CMAT will not reflect the Next Status as Disabled:LifecycleLockout. It will show Next Status as De-provisioned. When the subscription is moved to Disabled:LifecycleLockout, the Current Status End Date will be moved out 7 more days. Therefore, you may notice that the Current Status End Date for Next Status: De-provisioned changes by 7 days.
4. Disabled LifecycleLockout to De-provisioned.
  1. This is a variation of the disabled status prior to de-provisioning. The timeframe a subscription remains in this status can vary. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from Disabled LifecycleLockout to De-provisioned.

    Note: When an Office 365 subscription is de-provisioned, all mailboxes and subscription data is deleted and becomes unrecoverable. Administrators can still log on to the Microsoft 365 admin center and buy additional or new subscriptions on the existing tenant as well as manage domains and users.
5. All subscriptions de-provisioned to tenant deletion.
  1. Administrators will be able to log on to their tenant, perform administrative functions and make purchases.

    If all subscriptions are de-provisioned and the customer is able to log in, but they do NOT have the option to purchase, the customer will need to create a new tenant. If they would like to use the same tenant, please let them know that the tenant is queued for deletion and it cannot be stopped or expedited. The customer can wait for the tenant to be deleted, but it could take a day to months to complete the deletion and free up the tenant for re-creation.

    When the tenant is deleted from Commerce it will still be visible in CMAT, but the customer will not be able to make purchases on the tenant.

    When the tenant is completely deleted, the admin will no longer be able to log on and the tenant will not be visible in CMAT. Timeframes for these deletion actions are variable but generally take months.

c. Paid-Invoice
1. Active to In grace period.
  1. Auto-renewal:
    i. If the customer has auto-renewal turned off, the subscription will move to In grace period when the end date is reached.
    ii. If auto-renewal is turned on, the subscription will be renewed for another year, when the end date is reached.
    Dunning:

    Invoice Pay customers who are being dunned due to an overdue balance will be put into In grace period before being disabled. If the customer requests to reactivate their subscription, follow the procedure for Invoice Pay customers in 2772686: Enabling expired or disabled subscription. Billing stops for Invoice Pay customers once their subscription is in In grace period or Disabled status. If the subscription is reactivated, billing will resume from the reactivation date. This is different behavior from credit card subscriptions in non-active states.
2. In grace period to Disabled
The time that a subscription remains in the In grace period status varies by the offer but is generally 30 days. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from In grace period to its next status, Disabled.

3. Disabled to Disabled LifecycleLockout
  1. The time that a subscription remains in the Disabled status varies from offer to offer but is generally 90 days. Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from Disabled to its next status, Disabled with Current Reason Code: LifecycleLockout.

When the subscription is moved to Disabled:LifecycleLockout, the Current Status End Date will be moved out 7 more days. Therefore, you may notice that the Current Status End Date for Next Status De-provisioned changes by 7 days.

4. Disabled LifecycleLockout to De-provisioned.

d. Volume Licensing (VL) Subscriptions

For the procedure to handle a request to extend a customer’s VL subscription, refer to 2820242: Supporting Volume Licensing customers (Scenario 12).

1. Active to In grace period.
  1. A VL subscription will move from Active to In grace period due to the end date being reached, and the VL subscription status will be changed to HAR or END immediately. The status on the VL side can be viewed by searching CMAT by VL Information and entering the Agreement Number.

    VL Status in CMAT:
    ACT = Active
    HAR (Held Awaiting Reactivation) = In grace period or disabled or de-provisioned
    END = In grace period or disabled or de-provisioned

    [Asset 4086184]
2. In grace period to Disabled.
  1. The grace period, (from subscription end date to when the subscription is disabled, is 90 days for all O365 service subscriptions purchased through volume license programs.
    Microsoft is working to also align for other services like Dynamics, EMS as they have different life cycle.
    The advocate can tell customers the specifics of the subscriptions that they currently own.
    Use the Current Status End Date field to determine when the next status change will take place. Users continue to have access to their service when the subscription is in the In grace period status.
    Advocates can point customers to the publicly available content:
    -->     What happens to my data and access when my Office 365 for business subscription ends?     
    1. During this time, there is no change to the service for the end users
    2. In the Microsoft 365 admin center, the subscription will show as ‘expired’.
    3. To avoid a service disruption to their end users, the customer’s EA renewal order must be processed 48 hours prior to the end of the grace period.
    4. Email notifications are sent to all global and billing administrators on the tenant. No notification will be sent to VL admins(like primary noticing contact or OSM- online service managers) on the day a subscription expires, 14 days from when the subscription will be disabled, and 7 days from when the subscription will be disabled.

      NOTES:
      - The email notifications are not visible in CMAT or ExploreMS       For more info see below email example. For more details check this example notification.
      - Prior to September 2017, online service subscription grace periods and disabled periods could vary by the offer, but this was standardized for simplicity.
3. Disabled to Disabled Lifecyclelockout.
  1. The disabled period, from the grace period end date to when the subscription is de-provisioned, is 30 days.  Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from Disabled to its next status.

    Note: CMAT will not reflect the Next Status as Disabled:LifecycleLockout. It will show Next Status as De-provisioned. When the subscription is moved to Disabled:LifecycleLockout, the Current Status End Date will be moved out 7 more days. Therefore, you may notice that the Current Status End Date for Next Status: De-provisioned changes by 7 days. 
4. Disabled Lifecyclelockout to De-provisioned.
  1. After the 30-day disabled period, the subscription data will be irrevocably deleted, if the customer has no other active subscription for the same product. If the customer does not want their data to be deleted, they need to renew or call support for an extension.
  2. IMPORTANT: If the customer has purchased a different product in the same family (e.g. they used to own O365 E1 and now they own O365 E3), data for users with the new licenses will not be deleted and the global admin has to reassign the licenses.
    Use the Current Status End Date field in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move from Disabled Lifecyclelockout to De-provisioned.
5. All subscriptions de-provisioned to tenant deletion.
  1. A tenant with a VL subscription (even when de-provisioned) will never be deleted.

    Note: Lifecycle information pertaining to a tenant and its subscriptions can only be shared with the Global or Billing Administrators of the tenant.
