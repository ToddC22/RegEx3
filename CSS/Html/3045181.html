Procedure: Windows: Product Key Replacement

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Windows

What is this article about?

This article provides instructions on how to determine when a replacement product key should be provided, as well as the steps that are necessary to process the appropriate charge (if any), and generate the key to the customer.

  • This article applies to Windows product keys only
For information on handling non-Windows replacement key requests, see KB 3147392 Procedure: MPA: Handling Escalations for Blocked, or to Block, Product Keys and KB 2967104 Procedure: Office: Product key replacement.

1. Process trigger

Use this process in the following circumstances:

  • The customer did not receive a product key with the software.
  • An error occurs when the customer tries to use the product key that the customer received.
  • The customer has misplaced, discarded, or destroyed the product key.
  • A support professional needs a product key to resolve a customer's issue.

Note: If the caller is a Microsoft employee or vendor calling for a product key on a work computer, refer the employee or vendor to the caller's IT department for any work-related product key issues.

2. Product key privacy

A customer's product key is considered private information. Never enter a customer's product key into tools that are visible to other advocates, such as service request notes.

3. Determine the distribution channel of the customer’s copy of Windows

Before generating a product key for a customer, determine the product and distribution channel, and make an effort to help the customer locate the product key or determine whether the key is valid. For more information, see KB 2967104 Procedure: Office: Product key replacement.

4. Scenarios when you can provide a product key (PK) to a customer at no charge

All other scenarios customers are charged for a replacement product key.

4.1 OEM Windows Replacement Key Process

The general rule is to refer customer to OEM for a replacement PK.

Exceptions when you can provide a PK to an OEM customer at no charge:

  • Product key was injected into the BIOS at time of manufacturing

  • Replaced the motherboard with non-OA 3.0

  • HDD replaced or reimaged using OEM copy of Window from recovery media.
  • OEM went out of business – Make it right scenario
  • OEM refuses to provide replacement PK: If the OEM was to provide the key and they did not, collect the OEM name, model of the device, O/S version and escalate the information to Microsoft and then provide the customer PK at no charge.
    Note: One of the conditions for supporting this scenario is that the details need to be collected and the customer reach out to Microsoft Support.

Return to top

4.2 Retail Windows Replacement Key Process (FPP)

  1. Retail product key is damaged and/or unreadable - Requires proof of purchase*
  2. Retail PK is not accepted, and all standard troubleshooting is unsuccessful - Requires proof of purchase*
  3. Upgrade purchased from Microsoft (requires proof of purchase*), CSS provides media or FPP PK as Make it right – All versions of O/S
  4. Customers in countries where Service Package is not available

*What is a valid proof of purchase? How we should confirm?

The customer is not required to send a copy or image of the proof of purchase. The customer is only required to read out details for any one of the following options: (Advocates have to document the proof of purchase information the customer provided in PID Plus as part of key generation process).

  • Invoice
  • CD Rom or disk nr. 1
  • Certificate of Authenticity
  • Front page of manual
  • End-User License Agreement
  • Electronic purchase confirmation

Note: If the customer cannot provide a valid proof of purchase, you would need to charge for the replacement PK.

Return to top

4.3 Non-genuine Windows

If the customer is using Retail or OEM software, and you are unable to help the customer locate the key, if the key is damaged/unreadable, or if the key registers as invalid, the next step would be to generate a new product key for the customer. Generating a new product key may require a charge to the customer. See the product key pricing section of KB 2967104 Procedure: Office: Product key replacement.

Refer customers to their seller/shop/OEM to clarify the situation and to get a different product. Alternatively, the customer can buy a new product from Microsoft. Refer the customer to www.howtotell.com.

Steps on how to charge the customer and generate a new key in PIDPlus are available in KB 2967104 Procedure: Office: Product key replacement.
