About

Speak Up! is a SharePoint-based platform that enables worldwide support agents and engineers, who serve our customers daily, to provide Microsoft with direct, closed loop feedback on all aspects of the their experience.

Speak Up! is a feedback tool that allows advocates, agents, and engineers the opportunity to provide direct feedback, vote on the feedback of others, and see the status of their suggestions. Speak Up! is designed to provide a closed-loop feedback process, which means Microsoft will respond to (and provide status on) submissions.

1. What is Speak Up!?

Speak Up! is a forum that enables support advocates and engineers worldwide to provide Microsoft with direct, closed-loop feedback on all aspects of their experience, including support guidelines, procedures, content, tools, products, and training.

Speak Up! can be accessed by visiting aka.ms/speakup or the full address: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/SpeakUp

  • Speak Up! relies on your experienced use of Microsoft content, procedures, guidelines, and products to suggest ways to make them better. You are empowered to provide direct feedback and engage in a two-way discussion on suggested changes and improvements.

What happened to Speak Up! Express?

In March 2020, Speak Up! Express was deprecated along with the previous Speak Up! site. It has been replaced by https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/SpeakUp. You can still send us all your great feedback about our content, just be sure to include the KB number!

2. Speak Up! process

Consumer advocates can submit any type of feedback from an error or a gap, or if they have a new idea to improve content, guidelines, procedures, products, or training.

Commercial advocates can submit feedback regarding internal Microsoft content. Additionally, some sites and Lines of Business have begun routing feedback for other topics, such as Commercial Escalations or Windows Technical Support. If you are unsure what you can submit feedback about, simply check the New Item form to see if your topic is listed as a Category for you to choose (if you don’t see it, that means that topic is not in scope for you yet).

  1. Search the Speak Up! site for similar items. Vote these up and add your comment to increase their impact (to do this, see section 3.2).
  2. If you don’t find a similar item, create a new one!
  3. Microsoft moderators, including our content owners, review and respond to new submissions, providing clarification if the information already exists or identifying items needing more work.
  4. If more information is needed from you, the item’s status will be set to Open - Additional details required from advocate. If there is no response from the submitter within about two weeks, the item will be closed but can be reactivated with the new information at any time.
  5. When an item is answered or a change is complete, the status will be updated to best reflect the action that was taken, and you will receive an email to notify you of this change.
  6. Some items may be redirected to a more appropriate path when necessary, such as engaging team leads for access issues or tool failures.
  7. You can still comment and vote on resolved items, and these items can be reopened at any time, as needed.

2.1 Which Category should I use?

Categories will become available based on how you fill out the previous form fields. This is to make sure you only see what is relevant to your Line of Business (LOB). If you can’t find what you need based on the table below, it is possible that LOB is not prepared to intake feedback on that topic yet.

Consumer:

​Category

When to use?

Advise Me CXF Priority

When you have feedback about the Advise Me CXF Priority and associated programs under the Advise Me umbrella.

Content – Advocate Facing

When you have feedback or find an issue with internal advocate content. This could include KB articles, blog posts, or SharePoint pages.

Content – Customer Facing

When you have feedback or find an issue with external customer content. This could include websites from microsoft.com, support.microsoft.com, support.office.com, support.xbox.com, Virtual Agent, or anything similar.

Customer Experience & Feedback

When you are reporting an issue or feedback to improve an experience the customer goes through. This can relate to any number of topics, such as signing up online, making a purchase, or activating a product key.

ELP

This should only be used by the Executive, Legal, and Press (ELP) escalations team, which is based out of Bucharest, Romania.

Emerging Issue

For only select scenarios when you are reporting a new, high-impact issue. This might come from a call with your site TAs or Rangers. If you do not see this listed as an option, your team or LOB is not tracking Emerging Issues in Speak Up!.

Game On

When you have feedback about Game On gamification, which rolled out May 2020.

Microsoft Rewards

When you have feedback regarding a feature of Microsoft Rewards. This can include Bing quizzes or other point-related promotions, in addition to Rewards as they work with a Microsoft account.

Process and Procedure

When you have questions or feedback about one of your internal advocate procedures.

Product

When you have feedback about a Microsoft product specifically. This could include feedback for Windows, Office, Surface, or Xbox devices.

Note that if you’re reporting an issue with a product feature (such as a mistranslation), there is usually an in-product feedback hub that is the best method to send this feedback to Microsoft. User Voice is accessible from all Office apps, and most Windows 10 apps/settings have a Feedback Hub.

Windows Apps & Games (pre-installed only)

When you have an issue with or feedback regarding an app or game that came pre-installed with the customer’s Windows suite.

 

This would not be in-scope for any additional apps or games the customer downloaded on their own.

Tools

When you have an issue or feature suggestion for an internal Microsoft tool. This could be regarding ASD, RAVE, MST, or any other tool you interact with during your work as an advocate.

Note: If a tool is broken, down, or otherwise not working as it should, please raise a ticket with CSS Command Center (CSS CC) or RaveDesk.

Training

When you have feedback about a training course you have taken as an advocate. It is always helpful to provide the course ID, so we can research your issue faster.

3.3 Creating a new Speak Up! item

  1. Select the orange New Item button in the top right corner of the Speak Up! page.

[Asset 4550585]

  1. Fill out the fields as prompted; your selections will determine the options offered in later fields. 
  •  ​​​​​Notes:
    • Be sure to not include any Personal Data!
    • You can include links in the item description.
    • Always include the KB number when providing feedback on our content.
    • If you previously used Speak Up! Express only, this form may seem very lengthy. We promise that all this data helps us research your feedback better and faster; you’ll get used to it in no time!

[Asset 4550586]

  1. Add any attachments as needed, such as screenshots or a Word document with tracked changes for KB feedback. Additionally, you can always add attachments after submitting the Speak Up! item. You can add multiple attachments with a total limit of 10 MB.
  • Note: Be sure to review your attachments to confirm they don’t include any Personal Data!
  • Attached files may be visible to other advocates once reviewed by the moderation team.
  1. After selecting Submit, select the blue checkmark icon to start a new item, if you have more feedback.
  2. If you are prompted to take a survey about Speak Up! after submitting, please do so! We love to hear all feedback, even if you’ve completed the survey in previous months.
  3. Refresh your browser page to see your new item show up in the My Dashboard list.
  4. You will receive an email confirming that your item has been successfully submitted and whenever a Moderator comments on or changes the item.

3.4 Speak Up! item statuses

Item status is a quick reference to identify the stage of your item.

  • Open - New – The item has recently been submitted and is awaiting moderator review.
  • Open - Additional Details Required from Advocate –The moderator has requested more information to determine next steps, as detailed in the moderator comments.
  • Open - In Progress – The moderator has requested additional internal review, or the work is in progress.
  • Open - Change Pending – We have requested the change be made, but it is taking time to complete.
  • Closed - Change Implemented – The team has made the suggested changes. See the moderator comments for details.
  • Closed - Clarification Provided – The moderator has clarified the issue in their comments.
  • Closed - As Designed – The subject of the item is as it was designed. This may also result from lack of response when more information is needed. See the moderator comments for details.
  • Closed - Submitted for Future Consideration – The suggestion cannot be implemented currently but has been included for later feature sets. Items that require significant engineering resources or affect future tool features are more likely to be closed with this reason.
  • Closed - Additional information not received – We have requested additional details from the submitter/site, but no response was received within two weeks or more.
  • Declined - Duplicate – Another version of this feedback already exists. Vote up the existing item to maximize its impact!
  • Declined - Redirect to CSS CC – The item involves a tool break or other situation that the moderators can’t affect. You will be directed to engage your team or technical lead.
  • Declined - Redirect to Team Lead – Work with your Team or Site Lead for assistance with this specific issue.

5. FAQ

Q: What is Speak Up!?

A: Speak Up! is a SharePoint Online-based platform that enables support advocates and engineers to provide Microsoft with direct feedback on content, guidelines, procedure, tools, and training.

Q: If I enter a feedback at the wrong location, what happens?

A: Our moderation team will respond or re-categorize as needed.

Q: Who is looking at my Speak Up! item once submitted?

A: A Microsoft moderation team reviews and responds to all submissions. Site leads may also review items from their site for training purposes.

Q: How long does it take to look at a submission?

A: The moderation team has an initial response goal of 48 hours.

Q: Can advocates comment or request additional information once an item has been resolved?

A: Yes! You can always provide additional feedback, even after a submission has been resolved. You can do this by leaving a comment on the Speak Up! item itself, or you can email SpeakUpModerator_ProgramManagement@service.microsoft.com (if you do this, include the Speak Up! ID number as a reference).

Q: I remember the New Item form being a lot shorter. What happened?

A: You are likely remembering using Speak Up! Express; this was a “light” version of Speak Up!, and was previously available in our prior toolset. This has been replaced with our current tool, which rolls Speak Up! and Speak Up! Express all into one location, making it even easier to keep track of your items. You can still use the form the same way you would before; be sure to include your KB numbers, and feel free to attach any images or documents that might help.
