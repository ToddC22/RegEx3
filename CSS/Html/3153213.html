Procedure: Legal: Assisting customers wanting to report concerns of integrity, antitrust and fair competition

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer
  • All Commercial

What is this article about?

This article describes how to respond to customers wanting to report concerns of integrity, antitrust, and fair competition.

Note: This process is not meant to address support issues.

This process is not meant to address support issues such as billing concerns including refunds, technical issues or warranty concerns around device failures. If the customer has a support issue, address her or his issue directly. If the customer is requesting for a legal contact or arbitration, refer to KB 2629445 Process: Legal: Assisting customers requesting for a legal contact or arbitration for additional guidance.

1. Overview

Microsoft is committed to the highest standards of business ethics, including fair competition and compliance with antitrust laws. Employees, partners, customers or others outside the company who have concerns about compliance or Microsoft business practices can report their concerns to the Office of Legal Compliance (OLC).

2. Procedures

2.1 Identifying the customer’s concern

The OLC handles concerns around Microsoft’s business conduct and compliance with antitrust laws. The OLC is meant for reporting up allegations of:

  • Breaches of standards of business conduct.
  • Bribery.
  • Conflicts of interest.
  • Anticompetitive activities.
  • Integrity risks.

The OLC does NOT handle:

  • Mediation of unresolved support issues such as billing concerns including refunds, technical issues or warranty concerns around device failures.
  • Customer complaints
  • Commercial issue complaints

To ensure the customer’s concerns are addressed effectively, it is important to clearly understand the customer’s core concerns. You must only refer customers to the OLC for matters they handle.

Scenario

Advocate action
  1. Customer:
    • Makes ANY references to antitrust or fair competition.
    • Threatens to sue or request to initiate claim regarding antitrust or fair competition.
    • Requests communication regarding antitrust or fair competition with the OLC.

Customers may mention the following key words (but not limited to): 

Antitrust, Anti Trust, Anti-Trust, Anticompetitive, Browser, Bundling, Competition Commission, Compliance, DOJ, Department of Justice, European Commission, European Union, Exclusive Dealing, Exclusivity, FTC, Fair Trade Commission, Interoperability, Interoperability Principles, KFTC, Korea Fair Trade Commission, Media Player, MIP, Microsoft Interoperability Program, Monopolist, Monopoly, N Edition, N-SKU, N Version, Regulatory, Tying, Warranty Agreement, WSPP, Workgroup  Server Protocol Program

Follow these steps:

  1. Identify the scenario. It may sound like (but not limited to):
    • “I think Microsoft partners are coordinating pricing. They are all quoting the same price. This seems illegal.”
    • “I told my account rep that I want to switch to a competing product and he offered to pay me not to. It felt like a bribe.”
    • “I’m concerned Microsoft is competing unfairly.”
    • “I don’t think Microsoft is complying with its EU antitrust settlements.”
  2. Proceed to section 2.2
  1. Customer has a support issue, resolved or unresolved

Follow these steps:

  1. Show empathy and offer help, focusing on the customer’s issue. Suggested scripting:

“As a support advocate/engineer, I am here to assist you with support issues/questions. Please let me know how I can help resolve your support related concerns.”

  1. Address the customer’s issue.
  1. If the customer is requesting for a legal contact or arbitration, refer to KB 2629445 Process: Legal: Assisting customers requesting for a legal contact or arbitration.

2.2 Referring the customer to the Office of Legal Compliance (OLC)

Ensure you followed section 2.1 before proceeding with the steps in this section.

  1. If you identify the scenario as appropriate, let the customer know that you can assist with support issues, but will need refer the customer to the OLC.

Suggested scripting:
"I can continue to assist you with any support concerns or issues you may have. Concerns of integrity, antitrust and fair competition are handled by the Office of Legal Compliance. I will provide information now on how you can engage that office directly.”

  1. Provide the link to the Microsoft Standards of Business Conduct site. From there, the customer can report her or his concerns.
  2. In your case notes, document the following as clearly as possible. Do not ask additional clarifying questions – just document as best as possible based on what you already heard from the customer.
    • A summary of the customer’s concern, and that you referred her or him to the OLC.
    • Include KB number 3153213 and any keywords that may have been mentioned.
