What is this article about?

The Microsoft Business Applications ISV (independent software vendor) Connect Program has been designed to better align with mutual success of Partner’s company and Microsoft, including coordinated sales efforts within enterprise and mid-market field teams to enable joint selling in select scenarios. The new program is built around a revenue sharing model to help offset the cost of running ISV SaaS offerings on the Dynamics 365 platform and to enable continued investment in ISVs through new technical, marketing, and sales enablement benefits.
One of the goals of the new program is to ensure only certified applications, that meet a certain quality bar, are part of the ISV ecosystem available through AppSource. This will give customers confidence that any ISV app on AppSource is a Microsoft certified app.

All new and existing Dynamics 365 and PowerApps applications must enroll in the program, be certified (or recertified), and listed on AppSource.

Eligibility

The program applies to apps that use or extend the following Business Application workloads: Dynamics 365 for Customer Service, Field Service, Sales, Talent, Marketing, Retail, Finance and Operations and PowerApps. All apps that build on, extend to, or connect with Dynamics 365 applications, PowerApps or CDS by deploying in the customer’s tenant through a package/solution or API equivalent must participate in the program.

More information about apps covered by the program can be found here: https://aka.ms/BizAppsISVAddendum

Currently, Dynamics 365 Business Central, AI applications, mixed reality apps, Power BI, and on-premises solutions are not included, but some of these may be added in future versions of the program.

ISVs will be prompted to join the program New and existing ISVs with applicable Dynamics 365 apps and PowerApps will be presented with and prompted to accept the program’s terms as part of the app publishing workflow. Every ISV that wishes to publish and transact on AppSource will be required to participate in the program prior to submitting applications for certification or recertification. Once enrolled in the program, the primary program contact will receive an e-mail notification from the Microsoft Partner Go-to-Market (GTM) team. This team will be charged with taking the necessary steps to help Partner organizations to access the marketing benefits available for their business.

The Deal Registration tab is created for the ISV Connect program to register their deals to initiate invoicing directly, whether cooperative selling occurred with Microsoft sellers or not, whereas the “Co-sell opportunities” tab is where co-selling with Microsoft sellers begins and also for any solution that is incentive eligible and marked as won meaning IP Co-sell as this is the only incentive today.

Deal registration

This project tracks App enrollment and deal registration in Partner Center. All existing Dynamics CE and Operation offers must enroll in the standard tier of BAG ISV program. ISVs can choose to enroll in the premium tier if they meet all the criteria and participate in the co-sell.

Standard tier: 10% rev share with MS. Existing D365 CE and FO offers will be re-certified and enrolled into the ISV Connect Standard tier by default.

Premium tier: 20% rev share with MS. ISVs will get benefits of GTM and co-sell in return.

[Asset 4560336]

The Deal Registration tab in Partner Center is created for the ISV Connect program to register their deals to initiate invoicing directly, whether cooperative selling occurred with Microsoft sellers or not. The Co-sell opportunities tab is where co-selling with Microsoft sellers begins and also where any solution that is incentive eligible and is marked as won, (for example, IP Cosell, as this is the only incentive today)

There are two paths for deal registration in Partner Center:

  • IP co-sell path (using Co-sell opportunities tab - +New Deal)
  • ISV connect path (using Deal Registration tab - +New Deal registration)

For Azure and Biz Apps which are eligible for IP Co-sell, they can create and close deals in Partner Center (Co-sell opportunities -> +New deal created). When they close the deal, this is automatically registered - details are described in 4558187 Informational: Manage Co-sell opportunities in Partner Center.

[Asset 4584658]

Partners that are enrolled in the ISV program can register deals they've won in Partner Center by providing additional information about the contract.

For certain eligible solutions, after they select “Won” in the referral response process, they'll be asked to provide additional information in order to register their deal (pop-up message). Microsoft will review the info they have provided and may ask for additional details during the review process.

They can also register new deals they have won that use one of their solutions, even if they didn't originate through a referral.

Register a new deal

To register a new deal, go to Partner Center account > click on Referrals section > Deal registrations tab > Action required folder and select +New deal registration option.

In the New deal page, provide the following information.

  • Customer details: Enter the Company name for your customer and select their Country/Region. Then enter their City and State/Province.
  • Solution: Select the solution that will be used for the deal. If you don't see the right solution listed, contact support.
  • Contract type: Specify whether this is a New contract or a Renewal of a previous contract.
  • Total contract value: The total expected value for the engagement. This should include all software and service fees, but not hardware costs. Be sure to select the appropriate currency.
  • Solution value: The total value of the cloud solution that will be used for the deal. Be sure to include all costs associated with software and maintenance fees, but don't include reimbursable items, non-recurring customization fees, or directly associated CSP license fees paid by Microsoft.
  • Deployed on: Select either Azure or Other.
  • Where will the solution be deployed? Select either the Customer tenant or the Partner tenant.
  • Contract start date: The date on which the contract will begin.
  • Contract end date: If the contract will end on a specific date, select Has an end date and provide that date. If not, select Perpetual.
  • Contract signed date: The date on which the final contract was signed by your organization and by the customer (when the partner registers the deal, the contract signed date should be in the past).
  • Registration contact: The First name, Last name, Phone number, and Email for a person in your organization who we can contact if we need more details about any of the information provided here.

When partner completed all sections of the page, they should select Register. The deal registration will then be moved to the ‘In progress’ page. Microsoft will then review the information they have provided.

If a deal originated from a referral and they haven't registered it yet, they will see the deal listed in the Action required page. Deal registrations that were created and registered but require further updates are also shown here. They can use the filters to show only Incomplete or Update required deal registrations.

[Asset 4586025]

FOR IP CO-SELL DEALS: A deal will be eligible for deal registration only if it meets all the below criteria:

1. Microsoft is invited to the deal.

2. Microsoft has either accepted the invitation or marked the deal as won. You can understand the Microsoft status by looking at the Microsoft card below your deal details.

3. There is an incentive eligible solution in the deal.

If the deal is eligible for deal registration, there will be additional milestone added to the lifecycle of the deal called "Deal registration" as shown below.

[Asset 4584659]

You can choose to register the deal immediately after marking the deal as won or at a later point in time through the deal lifecycle Register now button. Once the deal is registered you can view the progress of the deal validation from the same lifecycle. If there is any action required from your company, appropriate errors are shown in the deal lifecycle view. The deal goes into the closed state when the deal validation is complete.

Viewing active and closed deal registrations

The Partner can review new, active, and closed deal registrations in the different pages under Referrals > Deal registration.

  • Action required
    • In the Referrals > Deal registrations > Action required page, the Partner will see new registrations that haven't yet been registered or need an update. They can use the filters to show only Incomplete or Update required deal registrations. Incomplete deal registrations are those which originated from referrals but haven't been registered yet. Update required deal registrations are ones which were created and registered, but which require an update.
  • In progress
    • In the Referrals > Deal registrations > In progress page, they will see the deal registrations that Microsoft is reviewing. If we need further information, we'll use the Registration contact information that they provided.
    • If any information needs to be updated, we may move the deal registration into the Action required page.
  • Closed
    • In the Referrals > Deal registrations > Closed page, they will see the deals which were successfully registered and reviewed by Microsoft. You can use the filters to show only Approved or Declined deal registrations.

FAQ on ISV

How do I enroll and publish my app?

1. Login to Partner Center and go to your Commercial Marketplace dashboard (https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/dashboard/commercial-marketplace/overview).​

          a. Your existing Microsoft AppSource offers will be shown in the middle of the page.​

          b. Or, to create a new offer, select the “+ New offer” button at top of page. Then select either Dynamics 365 Customer Engagement or Operations (depending which type of app you are creating). Provide requested information about the offer on each screen (i.e., Offer Setup, Properties, Offer Listing, Co-sell, etc). While you are entering this information, your offer will be in “Draft” status.​

2. Once you create (or begin creating) a Dynamics 365 CE or Operations offer, the ISV Connect Addendum (i.e., agreement) should be available. To accept it, choose Settings (gear icon in upper right corner of screen) > Developer Settings > Agreements (or go to https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/dashboard/account/agreements), and accept the Microsoft Business Applications ISV Addendum. Only certain user roles are able to review and accept agreements.​

3. Setup billing information by going to Settings (gear icon) > Developer Settings > Billing setup (or go to https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/dashboard/account/v3/accountsettings/billingprofile). Only certain user roles can access this functionality.​

4. Lastly, to submit the offer in step 1 above, be sure to click, “save”, “review and publish” and then “publish”. Doing so will move your offer from “Draft” to “In Review” status. Once you are notified by email that that your offer has been approved (in few days/weeks), please return to the offer in Partner Center (at link in step 1 above) and click “Go live” to move the offer from “Preview” to “Live” status.​

Note: These instructions apply to existing partners with MPN / Partner Center credentials and setup for Microsoft’s Commercial Marketplace. Additional steps are required for new partners.

How do I request Premium TIER?

By default, all offers are placed in the Standard (10%) tier upon enrollment. Publishers may request Premium (20%) tier for offers meeting eligibility requirements.​

To do so, please follow these steps:​

1. Login to Partner Center and go to your Commercial Marketplace dashboard (https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/dashboard/commercial-marketplace/overview). Your existing Microsoft AppSource offers will be shown in the middle of the page.​

2. Click on the desired offer within Partner Center, and then go to its “Offer setup” screen. ​

TIP: For new offers, return to this page after offer is enrolled in Standard tier and live on Microsoft AppSource. Since Premium tier is for offers with a proven record working with Microsoft Sellers, the box shown below is not visible when initially publishing a (new) offer.​

3. Look for the following checkbox towards the bottom of this page to request Premium tier. ​

[Asset 4586032]

4. After checking the box, select “save”, “review and publish”, and then “publish” (to submit it). This creates a review notification for the Microsoft Cosell Desk. The Cosell Desk will consult internally and then mark the request as approved or not approved in our publishing system. If approved, the offer is automatically re-tagged in Partner Center as Premium tier and its internal incentive and readiness tags are manually updated. ​

How do I register completed sales?

1. To enter sales (aka deal registration), you will need the “Referrals Admin” permissions in Partner Center. If you see the “Referrals” tab in the left nav of Partner Center (when logged-in at https://partner.microsoft.com/dashboard), then you have access. ​

        *If not, please ask ask your global admin to provide “Referrals Admin” access. To grant access, the admin will need to login to Partner Center, click settings (gear icon in upper right hand corner), select “Partner Settings”, pick “User management”, and assign “Referrals Admin” role to you (and, if you are not an existing user, to add you as a user).​

2. For sales resulting from co-selling with Microsoft (Opportunity shared via Partner Center), when you mark the Opportunity within Referrals as “Won”, a draft Deal Registration record is created for you under Referrals > Deal Registration. You should go to Referrals > Deal Registration to complete entering the information and submit the record.

3. For all other sales (i.e., not cosold with Microsoft, or a legacy PSC-shared deal), create a new Deal Registration record by selecting Referrals > Deal Registration > Needs Attention > +New (or go to https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/dashboard/deals/actionrequired and select “+New”). (See sample screenshot on next slide).​

        *Note: The “+New” button is in the middle of the Needs Attention page and is easy to overlook. ​

How do I access the agreement?

1. Accepted and pending agreements are available within Partner Center under settings (the gear icon in upper right hand corner of screen) > Developer Settings > Agreements (or go to https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/dashboard/account/agreements). Look for Microsoft Business Applications ISV Addendum and Microsoft Azure Marketplace Publisher Agmt (which is the Microsoft Publisher Agmt).​

2. A PDF of the current version of this Addendum is also available on the web at https://aka.ms/bizappsisvAddendum. And additional program reference information is available at https://aka.ms/bizappsisvPolicies and https://aka.ms/bizappsisvWeb ​

How do I change my billing details?

Go to Settings (gear icon) > Developer Settings > Billing setup (or go to https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/dashboard/account/v3/accountsettings/billingprofile). Only individuals with certain user security roles can access this functionality.​
