Scenario

A CSP Partner contacts Partner frontline support requesting a CPE credit for one of their customer’s their online service. CPE (Customer and Partner Experience) relates to the goal of becoming more customer-focused and connecting with customers and partners. A CPE credit can be used to help retain a customer who is dissatisfied with a service or with their support experience.

Return to top

Policy

CPE credits can be used to help retain a CSP customer who is dissatisfied with the service or with their support experience. CSP support team is authorized to approve CPE credits according to the thresholds and approved scenarios listed in this document.

This policy and process are for CSP subscriptions sold to Direct CSP partners or Indirect Provider CSP partners only. It is therefore very important that the credit request is submitted by the CSP partner (not by the end customer or the Indirect Reseller), along with all the required information, such as the CSP credit refund form downloadable from Partner Center.

If the CPE credit is approved, Microsoft will process the credit onto the CSP Partner’s account (the credit is normally visible on next month’s invoice). It is the partner’s responsibility to provide credit to their customer(s).

Scenario

Policy

Evidence

1. Did not understand the price/bill:

  • Customer did not understand the pricing or billing model for a service.
  • Customer is requesting for a credit in arrears to receive non-profit or Edu pricing
  • First Time/One Time exception of 100% credits for the first 2 months of the subscription from purchase,
    • For qualified non-profits Microsoft will provide a 100% credit back to the creation of non-profit pricing for the amount that the customer was charged above the non-profit price for appropriate skus. Anticipate exception retirement.
    • For Qualified EDU customers Microsoft may provide 100% Credit for the current calendar/school year with appropriate evidence.
  • Standard empowerment applies.
  • CSP credit refund form fully filled out which needs to contain an explanation of the terms that were not understood, and from what documentation the misunderstanding arose.
  • Documentation showing that this was reported within 120 days from first usage.
    • EXCEPTION: Non-profits may request this correction at any time. EDUs may request within current school year.

2. Misinformation or promise from Microsoft

  • Customer places reliance on incorrect or unclear documentation that results in surprise charges.
  • Microsoft representative incorrectly provides information on services or pricing that results in surprise charges
  • Standard guideline: credit for this scenario should not exceed 1-month subscription charge.
  • It is possible to ask for more credit with a solid business justification
  • We are unable to credit a customer for more than what they have been billed.
  • Standard empowerment applies.
  • CSP credit refund form fully filled out which needs to contain a link or attachment to the documentation and or the documentation of the incorrect Microsoft representative information that was provided that resulted in the charges.

3. Technical reason (non-SLA)

A CPE credit can be used to help retain a customer who is dissatisfied with a service or with their support experience with a confirmed technical issue (bug) not covered by SLA. Some examples:

  • Customers may receive a CPE credit for bugs that result in data loss.
  • Customers may receive a CPE credit for systemic issues resulting in the inability to use a portion of their service over time that did not breach SLA in a given month.
  • Multiple SIEs over the course of many months eroding customer confidence in a product
  • Customers may potentially receive a CPE credit when a portion of a Suite Sku is unavailable in a sovereign cloud or provisioned into the incorrect service instance.
  • A Service is not correctly provisioned in GCC (in the standard Service by error) and has been in use by the customer.
  • Microsoft committed to a service availability date in a Go-Local or Sovereign cloud and the availability was not met. Where the customer purchased in anticipation of that date.
  • A service, feature or capability is deprecated by Microsoft which will result in significant effort on behalf of the customer and proper notice was not given for the deprecation.
  • Standard guideline: credit for this scenario should not exceed 1-month subscription charge.
  • It is possible to ask for more credit with a solid business justification
  • We are unable to credit a customer for more than what they have been billed.
  • Standard empowerment applies.
  • CSP credit refund form fully filled out which needs to contain documentation (bug, etc.) that shows the technical issues.

4. Special/Exceptional Circumstances. Some examples:

  • Customer is forced to transition between offers, and current offer is no longer valid
  • Partner manual Transition from Direct CSP to indirect resellers and some offers are no longer valid
  • Promotional pricing that must be honored.
  • Agents should seek approval to offer a credit and a discount on the new subscriptions to honor the original subscription price until the original subscription end date

Example 1: customer transitioning to another partner and a discounted offer (like D365 SMB) is no longer available.

New reseller will provision the existing offer at full price.

Once new subscription is active, a discount can be applied to align to old pricing (= SMB offer price) but only until the original subscription end date

Example 2: customer might decide to move all their subscriptions to a new tenant. Since MSFT doesn’t offer any subscription transition capability for license-based offers, the customer will have to repurchase all the services. If customer had a retired offer on the old tenant (like D365 SMB), they will have to buy an existing offer at full price. Once new subscription is active, a discount can be applied to align to the old pricing but only until the original subscription end date.

  • Standard empowerment applies
  • CSP credit refund form fully filled out

5. System error. Example:

  • Partner Center catalog pricing error
  • Agents should raise the pricing issue to the appropriate team. Once confirmation is received, agents should seek approval to offer a credit and a discount to honor the correct/original pricing.

Example 1: partner might have agreed on a special pricing approved by Partner Business Desk. If partner gets charged differently, we must honor whatever pricing is stated in the DAF. See more details in article id 4038716, section Discounts / Special pricing.

Example 2: if Price list says that offer xyz is priced at $10 per unit/month, but then the same offer is priced at $12 per unit/month in the PC catalog, MSFT has a contractual obligation to give partners a 30-day notice period before introducing any price changes. In this situation, we must honor the price list price even if it was incorrect.

  • Special empowerment applies
  • Price List at time of purchase.
  • Appropriate data to properly calculate credit
  • No need to request CSP credit refund form

 

Standard Empowerment:

Frontline Agent and EMEA Accenture CSP team boundaries:

  • If ≤ $650 USD (or local currency equivalent), frontline agents can apply the approved credit in CMAT following the process in 4023103 Procedure: Partner: Cloud: Applying a credit/discount using CMAT;
  • If > $650 USD (or local currency equivalent),
    • Frontline agents should create a work item for your Escalation team for approval. Once approval is obtained, apply the credit in CMAT. If the credit is >$650, CMAT will notify approvers of the pending credit request. Upon approval, the credit will be automatically applied to the customer’s account.
    • EMEA Accenture CSP team, create a collaboration task for your EMEA MSFT CSP team to obtain approval. Once approval is obtained, apply the credit in CMAT. If the credit is >$650, CMAT will notify approvers of the pending credit request. Upon approval, the credit will be automatically applied to the customer’s account.

Escalation specialists and EMEA MSFT CSP team boundaries:

  • If ≤ $2500 USD (or local currency equivalent), Escalation agents can apply the approved credit in CMAT following the process in 4023103 Procedure: Partner: Cloud: Applying a credit/discount using CMAT;
  • If > $2500 USD (or local currency equivalent), seek approval from MPN Global support Once approval is obtained, escalate to PayOps Redmond by using this templated link and filling in the requested details to apply the credit in CMAT/CST (SLA 3 business days). Do NOT apply the credit in the tools.

IMPORTANT: for scenario 4.Special/Exceptional Circumstances, the calculation to determine the empowerment level must include *both* the credit for incorrect past charges AND the discount amount estimate for future charges.
Ex.: if credit for past charges amounts to $2,000 and discount estimate amounts to $3,600, the total credit value will be $5,600, therefore outside of Escalation agent boundaries.

*Special empowerment:

  • Outside of escalation agent boundaries (>$2500 USD) up to $10,000 USD, seek approval from Cindy Urlaub (email to curlaub@microsoft.com). Once approval is obtained, escalate to PayOps Redmond by using this templated link and filling in the requested details to apply the credit in CMAT/CST (SLA 3 business days). Do NOT apply the credit in the tools.
  • If >$10,000 USD, seek approval from MPN Global support. Once approval is obtained, escalate to PayOps Redmond by using this templated link and filling in the requested details to apply the credit in CMAT/CST (SLA 3 business days). Do NOT apply the credit in the tools.

IMPORTANT: for scenario 5.System error, the calculation to determine the empowerment level must include *both* the credit for incorrect past charges AND the discount amount estimate for future charges.
Ex.: if credit for past charges amounts to $2,300 and discount estimate amounts to $8,500, the total credit value will be $10,800, therefore outside of Cindy Urlaub’s empowerment.

The following scenarios are prohibited for CPE credits:

Note: This is not a complete list, however it represents many potential requests that are not authorized for a CPE credit.

  • Customer seeks credit for the unused portion of the service.
  • SLA claim is miscalculated or input incorrectly resulting in an incorrect credit to the customer.
  • The customer has received compensation from an SLA claim, but the amount received does not match the financial loss experienced by the customer.
  • A customer received an SLA credit but is seeking additional CPE credit funding to cover the financial loss of the outage.
  • CPE credits are *not* to be used as an alternative for a denied Service Level Agreement (SLA) claim.
  • Customer requests funds to implement disaster recovery architecture after a severe outage.
  • Customer requests credit for performance issues when not all technical issues have been resolved.
  • Tenant mapping issues cause customers to be unable to use the service, or they spent time and money to remediate the issue. The customer requests reimbursement.
  • Customer asks for credit or compensation due to deployment blockers or delays.
  • Auto-update or product release severely impacts the customer’s tenant or customer’s environment/business. Despite advanced notifications about the update/release, the customer would like a credit for the impact.
  • The country law requires Microsoft to change product specification for which advanced warning was given; however, the changes were not made which resulted in the customer paying for services not rendered.
  • Customer issue requires Microsoft Support to work with the 3rd-party support team. Due to communication or partnership breakdown, the customer suffers either with product performance, staffing, IT, etc. which causes additional costs to the customer.
  • Compensation for after-the-fact 3rd party services required to remediate a Microsoft-fault issue.

Process

CSP Partner should submit a credit request via Partner Center following these steps:

  1. From your Partner Center dashboard, select Support from the left nav.
  2. Select Partner support requests and then select CSP - billing and payments / Credit requests and refunds
  3. Download the Credit request and refund form and complete the information
  4. Ensure to attach the form when creating your service request

IMPORTANT: If the partner omits to attach the form, agents should request the partner to send a fully filled out form. This is important to ensure consistency of the information requested from partners and to back up all processed credit requests with appropriate documentation.

Remember:

  • Do not share the credit amount with the partner until it has been applied in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT).
  • Use the pre-tax amount for credits applied in CMAT. The partner’s account will receive the approved credit amount plus any taxes paid.
