Guideline: Customer feedback survey

[Asset 4049349]

 

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer
  • All Commercial

What is this article about?

This article provides guidelines around mentioning the survey during customer interactions.

Interim guidance 

Consumer:

If you use Rave as your primary tool, continue to follow KB 4514518 Procedure: Case management in Rave section 9.

We published new guidelines on May 16, 2020, around mentioning the survey during customer interactions.

Effective immediately, we are temporarily reversing the new guidelines while we review feedback from stakeholders and partners. For the time being, we are reverting to the previous guidelines. See below.

Consumer:

Mentioning the survey to customers is considered tampering with results and is not permitted.

If a customer asks about the survey experience, you may inform the customer that we do survey customers to learn about their experience with Microsoft Support and they may receive a survey by email within the next few days (phone) or at the completion of the chat (chat).

Discussing the details of the survey, such as the type of questions they may receive, or attempting to provide guidance on how to fill in the survey forms are prohibited.

Commercial:

When communicating with a customer, Microsoft employees shall not:

  • Make any attempt to manipulate customer survey results verbally or in writing.
  • Use words like “quality of service”, “quality of support”, or “satisfaction” in email taglines, signatures, the body of the email text, or in conversation with customers as an attempt to influence how a customer may respond to a survey.
  • Contact the customer about any poor survey rating received following the closure of a service request.
  • Note: Post-survey follow-up can be performed by managers based on specific line of business needs.

 

FAQ

Use the FAQ below to answer questions from customers about the feedback process.

Q: Is there a feedback process?

A: “Yes. You will be given an opportunity to provide feedback immediately after the call (if available) or chat, or through a link provided in an automated email. We appreciate and take seriously all feedback.”

Q: Who is eligible to provide feedback?

A: “Everyone is eligible to provide feedback on their experience with Microsoft. We appreciate and take seriously all feedback.”

Q: What kind of questions are asked?

A: “I unfortunately cannot discuss the specifics but appreciate it if you can take a few moments.”

Q: Is there some kind of scale that indicates good or bad?

A: “I unfortunately cannot discuss the specifics but appreciate it if you can take a few moments.”
