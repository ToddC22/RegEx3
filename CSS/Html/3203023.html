1.2 Issuing a Goodwill token

We offer Goodwill tokens on select scenarios. These scenarios, where applicable, applies to Office technical support advocates.

Do not tell the customer you are giving them a “token of appreciation” or “Goodwill token”. Keep your messaging kind, personal, and heartfelt like “I appreciate the time you spent with me today and am going to be adding an extra month to your subscription as my way of saying thank you!”.

How to issue a Goodwill token

Goodwill process:

  • MST: Send the Office White Glove Pilot – Microsoft Rewards outtake email – and quickly explain the brand change since the ORST tokens still have the previous brand name (Office 365 Home, Personal, Solo).
  • Tell the customer they will receive separate emails from Microsoft Online Customer Service support and orst2@microsoft.com.
  • In ORST, select MTLU 1 and select the appropriate product.

Reminder: There are rules in place on how and when to use the Goodwill process. See section 3 Solve Goodwill.

1.5 Proof of purchase (PoP)

Instructions on how to obtain proof of purchase

Reminder: You must use DTM to collect proof of purchase or any customer data as dictated in this article. The needs of DTM criteria from this article may differ slightly from KB 4135034 Tool User Guide: Data Transfer & Management (DTM), the information needed has been approved and must be collected for solve solutions called out in certain solve scenarios.

The customer must provide valid proof of purchase (PoP) for many Office Solve scenarios. Use the DTM tool to capture these items.

Do not share the required proof of purchase criteria with the customer. This information is internal and is to help you determine if the receipt is valid. If you are unable to determine if the receipt is valid, get help from your Team Lead (or equivalent). If an image in DTM has poor quality (blurry, unclear, or only part of the receipt is visible), inform the customer we need a clearer quality picture of the full proof of purchase, and request a new image.

Screenshot of the paper receipt

  • Ask the customer to write down their service request (SR) number on both the receipt and on the back of the 5x5 card.
  • Check that the serial number (SN) on both the receipt and the PPC/POSA card match.
    • If the SN does not match on both the receipt and the PPC/POSA card, or you believe PoP has been edited/photoshopped, escalate to CSS Phishing Alerts (CSSPALERT@microsoft.com) and decline further assistance.

We cannot accept unclear images. Make sure the image is clear. If the image is blurry, fuzzy, or unclear in any way, ask the customer to upload the image again.

Email receipt or PDF attachment from the retailer

  • Ask the customer to provide a copy of the original email order summary. This ensures the product is not coming from a random seller or individual.
  • Upload to DTM.

Photo scan of Office or Microsoft 365 product key card

If you need proof of purchase for a damaged Office/Microsoft 365 product 5x5 card issue, you will need a photo scan of the damaged card. We cannot accept unclear images. Make sure the image is clear. If the image is blurry, fuzzy, or unclear in any way, ask the customer to upload the image again.

In addition to the above, the proof of purchase receipts (both paper and email receipt, and product key card) must have the following information:

Paper receipt

  • Name of the retailer
  • Invoice or bill number
  • Purchase amount
  • Date of sale
  • Product description
  • Serial number

Email receipt or PDF

  • Retailer's name
  • Retailer's email address
  • Date and time the email (look top right or left of email)

Damaged Office/Microsoft 365 Product Key Card

  • Back of the card including view of damaged 5x5.
  • Serial Number below 5x5 scratch off.
    • There may also be multiple serial numbers listed in the top and bottom corners of the card.
  • Current Service Request number written on the back of the card.

Japanese advocates:

For PIPC Office: Get the PoP or invoice from the PC.

  • Include the OEM PC model name.
  • If no OEM PC name, get a picture of the label located on the device. This may have the information needed.

Check that the PC model comes with Office PIPC.

Damaged cards: No serial look-up needed for Office PIPC.

2. Download, install, and activate issues

2.1 Damaged Office or Microsoft 365 product key card

Scenario description

Solve process

The 5x5 numbers on the product key card are damaged.
  • The customer must provide valid Proof of Purchase (PoP). See section 1.5.
  • Look up the serial number using the Token Lookup tool. See KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool for more details.
  • If you can successfully see the associated 5x5 code, help the customer redeem the product, and read it out while they are on the setup page. Do not provide any Solve tokens if you can give the code back to the customer.
  • If you are unable to look up the token, ask your Team Lead to contact the TA team at ocsta@microsoft.com. Provide complete screenshots of the back of the POSA card.
  • T2 see the ACC blog post [T3] Token Look-up using DARSy if unable to collect the 5x5 from the Token Lookup tool.

2.4 Customers having issues with OEM-installed Office or Microsoft 365 product

Scenario description

Solve process

The customer has purchased a computer with Office pre-installed (Consumer Microsoft 365 or perpetual).

Example of issues where troubleshooting will not help.

  • Purchased Microsoft 365 Family, and instead Microsoft 365 Personal came with OEM PC
  • Purchased Office, but no Office installed on the OEM PC
  • Office came installed on the OEM PC as per purchase, but it is only a trial, no option to activate.

The following example needs troubleshooting.

  • Correct version of Office did not come activated.
    • Guide the customer through the activation process.
    • If the correct version of Office successfully activated after troubleshooting, do not offer ORST token.

Try to resolve the customer’s issue with general troubleshooting as mentioned in KB 4012605 Procedure: Office: Activation for Office and Office preinstall kit 2004 (Errors and known issues section). Follow the guidance in the article first or CAA notices will be issued. If the only way to resolve the issue is by providing a new key, provide a replacement token for the Office product that came with the machine and help the customer activate Office.

The customer must provide proof of purchase (PoP) with mention of Office, for the solve process to apply. See section 1.5 about PoP.

Information needed for Solve and to add in case notes:

  • Any error messages.
  • Proof of purchase verifying the device was new at purchase.
    • If purchased second hand, no solve applies.
    • If purchased used/refurbished, no solve applies.
  • Recent repairs or exchanges done on the device.
    • Was Office redeemed and activated before repairs?
      • If after, Solve doesn’t apply.
      • If before, Solve applies.

If everything checks out, provide a token using ORST.

ORST

  • Select Make It Right in ORST tool.
  • In MIR SKU box, select Solve 6.
  • Enter the name of the Retailer/Store Purchased as it appears on the PoP. If it was a gift, enter Gifted.
  • Pin Generation Reason select Other.
  • Comment: Issue with OEM installed Office Product

SRID: Provide ticket number.

3. Solve Goodwill

3.1 Microsoft 365 Goodwill for technical support issue (Office TS only)

Scenario description

Solve process

You may choose to provide a one-month Goodwill token using MTLU1 if you helped a Microsoft 365 customer with challenging technical support issues like:

  • Download, install, activate – DIA didn’t go as expected.
  • Unable to open and use Office apps.
  • Sending or receiving email, setting up Outlook.

Non-approved reason:

  • The customer bought a device that does not support Office.
  • The customer is using trial version of Microsoft 365.
  • The customer is going to be transferred to Tier 2 for additional support (T2 will provide token if needed).
  • The customer has very easy how-to questions where it is obvious that they are trying to get a free token, like:
    • How do I change fonts?
    • How do I underline a word?
    • How do I delete an email?

Important:

Qualifying customers shouldn’t receive more than one token per six-month period. This is to reduce customers from calling in with non-approved scenarios hoping to get more free months. If the customer assumes, they will get a one-month token and expects one, simply thank them for being a Microsoft 365 customer and ask if they need further assistance.

 ORST

  • MIR SKU MTLU1.
  • In Pin Generation Reason select Other.
  • Comment: #goodwill
  • SRID: Provide Ticket Number
  • Add notes in MST (#goodwill) for every issuance of MTLU1, using the same case number from RAVE.
    • Example: Comment in MST: 1000899999 #goodwill
      • To add notes in MST, remember to verify the customer’s Microsoft account.

The MTLU1 SKU is strictly monitored to make sure the process is being strictly adhered to.  If you are unsure if a customer qualifies for a token, reach out to your team lead.

4. Legacy Office product

4.1 Legacy product not functioning (2007, 2010, 2011)

Scenario description

Solve process

The customer’s Office (2007, 2010, 2011) product is no longer functional. The customer lost product key and cannot reinstall it.

 

 

Customer caused the problem:

The customer uninstalled Office and needs to reinstall it. 

  • We cannot provide a replacement product key. 
  • Offer Microsoft 365 Family or Microsoft 365 Personal first, explaining the benefits. 
    • The customer pays for one-year subscription product.
    • We provide three months more, free.
  • Office 2019 Perpetual at Get Current discounted price.
    • KB 4024479 Procedure: Office: Get Current.

Microsoft update or advocate support caused the problem:

An update or upgrade of the operating system caused Office to stop functioning or removed Office. This occurred during troubleshooting.

  • Advocate removed Office.
  • Windows Update removed Office.

You, with supervisor or manager’s approval, can offer the customer a product replacement token of Microsoft 365 or 2019 Perpetual.

Managers will write down the actions in case notes:

  • If caused by advocate, what steps did the advocate take?
  • If caused my Windows update, which update? 
  • If you see trends, report them to TA.

Manager to provide the replacement token.

  • In ORST, use Solve 10 if you need to provide replacement token, or PID Plus if needed.
  • If the customer wants a Microsoft 365, then partner with your TA and use a Tier 3 token for the full year of Microsoft 365.

ORST

  • If the customer chooses to Get Current: follow section 4.1 KB 4024479.

4.2 Legacy product not functioning (2013, 2016)

Scenario description

Solve process

1. If an update by Microsoft or an action committed by an advocate caused Office to no longer function or removal from the customer’s OS, then follow the below steps:

  • Help the customer go to account.microsoft.com and login with their Microsoft account, and install Office.
  • If the customer has forgotten their Microsoft account, use the procedure for recovering the information by using MSA tab in ORST.
  • If the customer has forgotten the Microsoft account password, help them select Forgot password link, and follow the steps to recover it.
  • If the customer cannot recover their Microsoft account/Password, then Solve applies.

2. If the customer lost Office or did something to lose Office and cannot recover the Microsoft account to login to account.microsoft.com and get Office, then offer Get Current. See KB 4024479.

 

In all cases:

  • Offer Get Current.
    • Benefits of a subscription.
    • Benefits of latest Office version.
  • Get manager approval. 
  • Manager must leave case notes documenting:
    • Actions advocates used that caused issue.
    • Windows update that caused issue.
      • Report to TA team and investigate the update.
    • Any other cause.

Replace product based on the customer’s recommendation.

  • Use Solve 10 for same Office replacement.
  • Use Solve 16 for Microsoft 365 subscription
    • Offer three months free.

India Team Lead (or equivalent):

  • If the customer wants same product, no transfer.
  • If the customer wants to buy Microsoft 365 yearly subscription and get three months free, transfer.

5.1 Transfer to another Microsoft account (Microsoft 365 Family or Personal only)

The customer owns the Microsoft account

Scenario description

Solve process

The customer wants to transfer their current Microsoft 365 Family or Microsoft 365 Personal subscription to another Microsoft account they own.

  • Not a stacking scenario (see section 7.2 Microsoft 365 Subscription Stacking).
  • We cannot action partner billed accounts (such as Apple and Best Buy).
  • Microsoft should not be in a suspended or blocked state.

Note: If the customer purchased from Microsoft and refunds the product within the refund policy window, do not provide a token.

Using MST:

  • Verify the customer’s existing Microsoft account. If not verified:
    • No subscription cancellation.
    • No tokens provided.
  • Once verified, determine how many months remain on the subscription. You can round up to the nearest month.
    • See ORST steps after performing the following.
  • MST: Cancel the subscription from existing Microsoft account.
  • Include the Rave SR number in the case comments.
  • Generate the tokens using ORST.
  • No need to capture the email or new Microsoft account.
  • You might need to send multiple tokens based on how much time the customer has left on the current subscription. (For example, seven months = one six-month token and one one-month token.)
    • You can select multiple options by using the CTRL key during selection.

ORST

  • Select the Make It Right tab in ORST tool.
  • In MIR SKU box, select Solve 1.
  • Select the appropriate product.
  • Send multiple tokens based on remaining time on the customer’s current subscription.
    • (Seven months = one six-month token plus one one-month token).
    • Use the CTRL key for selecting multiple SKU’s.
  • In Pin Generation Reason select Other.
    • Comment: (Use these comments only): Move subscription to another Microsoft account.

SRID: Provide ticket number.

  • Office technical support located outside of India.
  • Accounts & Billing

5.4 Error in ORST: Microsoft account has purged due to inactivity error

Scenario description

Solve process

The customer cannot access the Microsoft account they used to redeem Office.

The error message: MSA has been purged due to inactivity can sometimes occur from the MSA tab in ORST.

This can happen if:

The customer purchased Office 2013 before January 1st, 2014:

The customer purchased, redeemed, and associated a Microsoft account to Office 2013 before January 1st, 2014. The customer then never signed back into the Microsoft account and Microsoft eventually closed the account due to inactivity.

This scenario is very rare, especially now since Microsoft has not ‘purged’ an inactive Microsoft account in five plus years.

The customer deleted the Microsoft account used to redeem Office.

  • If the customer tried to recreate the Microsoft account using the same username and domain, the PUID will be different from the original Microsoft they created and won’t be recognized as the account used to redeem Office.
  • Occurs on and after January 1st, 2014.

For Solve to apply, you will need:

  • The 5x5 key,), and the Office Proof of Purchase (see section 1.5).
  • In ORST: Enter the 5x5 key in the geo-block tab and review the redemption date.
    • If redemption date is before January 1st, 2014, and you see the error in the MSA tab, this Solve applies.
    • If redemption date is on or after January 1st, 2014, this Solve does not apply.
  • Check the PoP date against the redemption date. T2 can reach out to TA team as needed.

ORST

  • Select Make It Right in ORST tool.
  • In MIR SKU box, select MIR 10 MSA Purged Due to Inactivity Error.
  • Select the appropriate product.
  • In Pin Generation Reason select Other.
  • Comment: (enter only this comment): Microsoft account/MSA purged due to inactivity error.

SRID: Provide ticket number.

5.5 No proofs associated to Microsoft account

Scenario description

Solve process

The customer lost access to Office.

  • The customer does not remember the Microsoft account password and is unable to recover the Microsoft account:
    • The customer account does not have security proofs.

Note: Signing up for new a Microsoft account when Redeeming Office does not require security proofs. Security proofs include phone number, and an alternative email.

Lost access to the Microsoft account (forgot password), with no security proof associated.

If the customer lost access to their Microsoft account, but meets conditions outlined below, replace the product using the Solve process.

Solve applies for purchase with the original credit card. Solve does not apply to other payment methods.

Solve does not apply if:

  • Security proofs exist:
    • Check the Microsoft account for security proofs using MST (alerts tab).
  • Microsoft account is in a blocked or suspended state.
  • Account shows signs of compromise of possibility of social engineering:
    • See KB 2991467 Procedure: Privacy: Handling social engineering, fraud, and phishing incidents.

Tier 1 steps:

  1. Get the customer's permission for blocking the current product before replacing:
    • If they don’t agree, Solve doesn’t apply.
