1. Data sources used to measure cloud performance

All of the Microsoft systems/data sources and that are recognized by the cloud performance requirement are listed in this section.

  • Digital Partner of Record (DPOR) – The Digital Partner of Record (DPOR) is the partner who helps the customer design, builds, deploys or manages a solution that they have built on the service. (It is not the partner who sold the subscription.) Partners that are associated by their customers as the DPOR are recognized toward the competency performance requirement for eligible activities.
  • After the partner assigned themselves as the DPOR for their clients. It will still take around 10 business days for this to reflect/recognize in the partner dashboard and be counted towards partner's sales performance to attain a competency. https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/membership/digital-partner-of-record
  • Partner action: There is no action needed if the partner is already the DPOR for each of their customers. Partners that fulfill this role who are not yet the DPOR should encourage their customers to complete that designation as soon as possible. Partners can be directed to Digital Partner of Record and Partner Association page (aka.ms/por) for instructions on how they can be attached by their customers as the DPOR. The page also includes Frequently Asked Questions, video demonstrations and other information. There are no changes to the existing DPOR association process.
  • Partner action: The process is automated. No action is needed by partners that are the transactional partner in Microsoft internal sales systems.
  • Delegated Admin Privileges (DAP) – Delegated Admin Privileges (DAP) allow the partner to administer an account on behalf of a customer. Partners that have this privilege are recognized toward the competency performance requirement for customer’s usage and consumption of Office 365, Intune, Enterprise Mobility Suite, or CRM Online subscriptions.

    Partner action: The process is automated. No action is needed by partners that are already the delegated administrator for their customers. Partners that are not can request their customer to authorize them as a delegated administrator. If they are not already, partners will first need to be certified to offer Delegated Admin Privileges (DAP). They will then send the customer an offer for delegated administration. This can be included with a trial invitation or purchase offer, or can be offered to the customer at a later time. 
  • Reseller of Record on Commerce Platform (for CSP subscriptions) – Partners that are associated as a Reseller of Record on CSP subscriptions are recognized toward the competency performance requirement.

    Partner action: The process is automated. No action is needed by partners that are already the Reseller of Record on a CSP subscription. If they are not, in the Partner Admin Center, the Distributor can enter the MPN ID of a partner reseller. This will designate the partner as the Reseller of Record on a CSP subscription. Afterward, all subsequent orders for that customer will automatically default to include the partner as the Reseller of Record. If the partner was not added as the Reseller of Record to previous transactions, those subscriptions will need to be updated to include a Reseller of Record. Partners can be directed to the document Adding Reseller-of-record information to CSP subscriptions.

2. Guidelines

The following are basic business rules regarding the cloud performance requirement.

  • The partner must be associated to an eligible customer subscription.
  • There are multiple ways that a partner can be associated to a subscription and be recognized towards the performance requirement. (For details, refer to the section of this article named Data sources used to measure cloud performance.)
  • Sales must be sold/assigned within the previous 12 months.
  • Customers are counted based on Tenant ID.
  • Qualifying products are limited to specific products/services/SKUs.
  • Trial and free subscriptions do not qualify.
  • Some cloud performance competencies have lower sales performance requirement for developing and emerging markets.
  • The required performance varies by the enrolment option (silver/gold).
  • Some cloud performance competencies require new customers in addition to a total number of subscription or seats.

3. Partner Center Insights 

Dashboard description

This new Partner Center feature should not be mistaken with OCP Insights (internal validation tool) or Analyze Tab or MyInsights (partner facing). For more details, including escalation process, check article Partner Center Insights.

Insights is a unified reporting dashboard in Partner Center for Microsoft’s Commercial partners who are enrolled in the Microsoft Partner Network (MPN) program. The Insights dashboard provides 360-degree view of your key performance indicators (KPI) across Cloud products such as Office, Azure, Dynamics, and licensing models such as CSP and EA. It exposes a rich set of KPI reports that can help you make data driven decisions for your organization.

There are two new roles in Partner Center designed specifically for access to Insights: Report Viewer and Executive Report Viewer. Users in the Executive Report Viewer role have access to all reporting data sets, while users in the Report Viewer role will not have access to sensitive data sets such as revenue and customer/employee personal data. The Global admin or the Account admin can assign users these roles and are assigned either for the entire company or for a specific MPN location.

For a detailed description check public article - Partner Center Insights - a dashboard that shows how a Microsoft commercial partner is doing

5. How can Frontline team check if a partner was associated as DPOR

There are two methods:

Method 1

Frontline team must use CMAT tool, and check by customer’s tenant domain. In case they don’t have it, they should use the subscription GUID, subscription name, number of seats, etc.

[Asset 4487416]

After that, they need to go to “Subscriptions” tab, select correct subscription, expand it and scroll a bit down.

[Asset 4487417]

In this way, they can see who is the current Partner of Record for this particular subscription and the date when they’ve been added. They may also click on the “clock” icon to check the history of all associated partners. 

Advocates have to open "Billing” and then leave CMAT open on "Transactions". If they select "Charges" as per the screenshot in the article, they won't be able to search by the Subscription GUID.

[Asset 4487418]

Method 2

Another check can be done in OCP Insights tool to see exactly when the report was refreshed and if the latest DPOR association is reflected, by clicking on refresh info ([Asset 4487443]button).

6. How can Frontline team check if a partner was associated as ROR

In order to check who is attached as Reseller of Record (ROR), Frontline team must look after a “short” subscription id. They can find it by using subscription GUID only.

Steps to follow:

1. Once they search by a CSP subscription GUID in CMAT, they will see who is a CSP indirect / direct provider and they need to copy the “Reseller tenant GUID”. Please do not open the tenant by clicking on black right arrow.

[Asset 4487419]

2. If they found the reseller Tenant GUID, they need to open it in CMAT by inserting the tenant guid manually.

[Asset 4487421]

3. After that, they can open the tenant and go to: billing tab -> select reseller -> select subscription GUID -> provide subscription GUID -> expand subscription and they will be able to see the “Short” Subscription ID.

[Asset 4487423]

4. Using this “Short” Subscription ID, please search from the beginning in CMAT and open reseller’s tenant one more time.

[Asset 4487424]

5. Go to subscriptions tab, provide a short subscription id and click on search, then please expand subscription, scroll a bit down and you will see partner’s information and the date when they were assigned.

[Asset 4487426]
