1. Overview

Some customers may proactively validate the state of their warranty by contacting support or by signing into device management online. Other customers may find the state of their warranty at the time of contact when they encounter an issue and need help. This article describes what to do in the situation where the customer requests to have their warranty adjusted. Follow the steps in the scenario appropriate for the customer’s situation. If the customer is also needing a repair/exchange follow KB 4041348 Procedure: Microsoft Complete not applied to device.

Note for Manufacturer Warranty:

Review KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios for country-specific warranty lengths.

Note:

For Australia, consumers have the right to repair, replace, or refund in certain situations where their product may be out of warranty (OOW). See KB 4100081 Guideline: Microsoft and the Australian and New Zealand Consumer Law. Where a consumer has that right, you must follow the Australian and New Zealand Consumer Law (ACL).

2. Scenarios

2.1 Standard warranty is not what the customer expects

When the customer is disputing the status of their warranty, device management online, or the Service Order Management (SOM) tool shows the customer's standard warranty is shorter than what the customer expects resulting in a dispute use the SOM tool to verify the warranty indicator for the device in question. If the warranty indicator is not there, it means the device while displayed, is not registered. Register the device as needed.

  1. Expand to see an image of a registered device in warranty.
  2. Note status of “In Warranty” in the image.
Expand for image

[Asset 4092295]

  1. Double-click on the registered device to verify the Start/End dates.
  2. Expand to see image warranty status.
Expand for image

[Asset 4092294]

  1. If the dates don’t agree with what the customer expects, ask the customer to send a copy of their proof of purchase (PoP) in an email message (they can take a snapshot with a smartphone or scan the receipt) ideally, while the customer is still on the line with you.

Note: If real time is not possible, you can decide to set up a callback with the customer.

  1. Send the customer an email message from the case handling tool requesting the PoP.
  2. The customer attaches the PoP to their reply.
  3. Validate the PoP once it has been provided. See section 3.
  4. Verify the start date in MST is different based on the PoP.
  5. Validate the warranty length for the customer’s country by reviewing the exchange policy information specific to that country. See KB 4514977 Procedure: Surface: Services Exchange and Repair Offers Overview for country and region specific information.
  6. Once you have verified the warranty status or if the device is from a country that has a longer warranty period, explain to the customer that you will have the case reviewed by a specialist who can adjust the warranty.
  7. Consult a Repair Specialist. Your Repair Specialist continues with the next actions and informs you of any necessary steps.
    • Repair specialists, see KB 4054825 Procedure: Incorrect warranty dates for how to correct the warranty data.

2.2 Extended warranty coverage period is not what the customer expects (Microsoft Complete)

Be aware that Microsoft Complete is not offered in all markets. Third-party retailer specific offers must be referred to the third-party retailer.

Note for China:

PerformanceGuard is not available for sale.

If the extended warranty status (Microsoft Complete) is being disputed by a customer:

  1. Use the Service Order Management (SOM) tool to verify the warranty indicator for the device in question.
    • If the warranty indicator is not there, it means the device while displayed, is not registered. Register the device as needed.
  2. Expand to see image of a registered device in warranty.

  3. Note status of “In Warranty” in the image.

  2. A device with Microsoft Complete associated will display reference to Extended Service Plan and Accidental Damage Protection as shown below.
  3. Expand to see warranty status.

  1. If this is not shown and the customer claims to have purchased the plan, proceed further.
  2. Verify where the customer purchased their Microsoft Complete by asking them to send a copy of the proof of purchase (PoP) in an email message (they can take a snapshot with a smartphone or scan the receipt) ideally, while the customer is still on the line with you.

Note: If real time is not possible you can set up a callback with the customer.

2.3 Customer has a C-SKU device

A C-SKU is an unsupported working device sold without a warranty. Microsoft is not obligated to provide any support, including Software Tech Support. For more information on handling these review KB 4098515 Procedure: Surface: Membership Support Tool (MST) displays non-supported device in Series or Messages.

2.4 Requests for leniency or grace

The customer may know the status of the warranty and is requesting to have the warranty fees waived. Microsoft understands that some issues may span the expiration of the warranty, which may create a situation where the customer needs an exchange just outside of the warranty period. All manufacturer warranties include a built in 30-day grace period, that is not displayed in the device warranty period. Your tools will provide all options for which that device is eligible, including any courtesy exchanges.

  • For more information on 30-day grace period, see KB 3089272 Procedure: Surface: Device warranty - 30-day grace period.

3. Validating proof of purchase (PoP)

3.1 General PoP validation

Note: For China region legacy (EOL) devices (Surface only), refer to the following for validation, PoP process for WICs PDF.

  1. Make sure the PoP received is not one of the following, as they are not valid PoP.
  • Bank or credit card statements. It is not possible to determine based off these statements whether it is a hold or a purchase.
  • Hand-written receipts. With the exception of APJ and any regions with different legal requirements.
  • Statements from eBay, Amazon Marketplace, or other non-retail forms of evidence. Amazon Marketplace receipts may be valid, if it states item was sold new.
  • Pre-order receipts, Pre-order receipts cannot be accepted because they can be cancelled prior to processing and Microsoft Complete cannot be purchased until the device is actually purchased. Therefore, ask the customer to provide the Microsoft Online Store order.
  1. If the customer has provided a PoP that appears valid (based on passing step 1):

1. Note these in the Service Request (SR) if they are present on the PoP:

Note: This information is not required for escalation but will allow for a faster resolution for the customer and should be captured if possible.

  • Retailer's name
  • Date of purchase
  • Description of product
  • Retailer’s product number (this can be handwritten on top of the receipt)

2. Review the PoP to confirm it is a valid, unmodified receipt.

[Asset 4481798]

3. Confirm the device is not a refurbished or used unit.

  • If the device is a refurbished device, then it carries a full market specific warranty from the date of purchase. A refurbished unit may contain a label or sticker on the packaging stating Refurbished. 
  • Note: This differs from a service center processed repair/replacement refurbish device which may carry a 90-day warranty from the time of repair or the remaining warranty, whichever is longer. For Surface, see KB 4514977 Procedure: Surface: Services Exchange and Repair Offers Overview under the section Length of Warranty for complete details and differences in length for China, Korea, and Thailand.

4. Confirm the marketplace and country of purchase/country origin match.

  • If marketplace and country of purchase/country origin do not match, follow one the corresponding scenarios below: 
    • Scenario 1 – The customer lives in country “A”, visits country “B” and purchases Surface devices while in country “B”. The customer then moves back to country “A” and would like their warranty updated according to the country they live in. The warranty options link in accordance to country “A”. If the customer also has a repair/exchange issue, see KB 2764525 Procedure: Surface: Out of Region device exchange and fulfillment.
    • Scenario 2 – The customer lives in country “A” and purchases a device from a local/online retailer. Customer receives a device configured for country “B” from the retailer. The customer needs to go back to the retailer to receive support.

4. FAQ

4.1 The customer is disputing the warranty status of their device

Scenario

Solution

Customer has purchased the device from another person.

The manufacturer's warranty follows the device and not the owner. If the warranty status in MST shows expired, advise the new owner that the device is out of warranty. Provide out of warranty options.

Customer has purchased a brand-new device from an authorized retailer.

Follow the dispute process and consult a Repair Specialist at this point. Your Repair Specialist will continue with the next actions and inform you of any necessary steps.

4.2 China only - Post-Sales support claiming Microsoft Complete benefits

Customer Scenario

Customer Action/Support Touch Point

Guidance

Escalation Process

Resolution

The customer wants to use Microsoft Complete warranty benefits

Option one: Call Surface Devices Customer Service and Support (CSS) team

If calling CSS Product:

1. Surface Devices CSS team will locate the Microsoft Complete Serial Number (SN) via MST to file a claim.

2. The customer will be advised on deductible charge for Microsoft Complete.

3. The customer will pay deductible via Collect on Delivery (COD) process.

Escalation one: If damage is not covered by Accidental Damage Protection (ADP), the customer will be escalated to Tier 3 support.

Escalation two: If the serial number (SN) is not visible or Complete did not provision correctly, the customer will provide PoP.

Surface Devices CSS advocate will escalate as per existing procedure for escalations around selling warranty through an incident ticket.

Claims Resolution – Same Unit Repair Process

CSS Product advocate will facilitate FedEx to collect the device and submit to iQor to repair via JDM1 and deliver to the customer via FedEx.

Repair Service Level Agreement (SLA): 10-15 business days

Escalation Resolutions

Escalation one: As per Tier 3 policy and SLA

Escalation two: Microsoft Complete will be successfully re-provisioned within four to five days of ticket.

The customer wants to use Microsoft Complete warranty benefits

Option two: Visit a Walk-in Center (WIC)

1. Customer brings device to the WIC as per Same Unit Repair policy.

2. WIC advocate locates Microsoft Complete SN in Repair on Behalf of/Bulk Support for Services (ROBO/BSFS).

3. WIC advocate collects deductible to process claim if Microsoft Complete.

If Microsoft Complete failed to provision, the WIC advocate will be advised to escalate to Tier 3 support.

Same Unit Repair Process:

WIC will submit the device to iQor to repair via JDM1 and deliver back to the customer via FedEx

Repair SLA: 10-15 business days

The customer wants to use Microsoft Complete warranty benefits

Option three: Visit an Authorized Retail (AR) Center

1. The customer brings the device to the AR center. The AR center advocate uses the existing ERP system to input: serial number, phone number, name, address, etc. via online store link: https://www.microsoftstore.com.cn/msscncategory/microsoft-commodity-out-of-warranty-service/p/micjs0519

AR Center advocate selects the customer’s correct warranty.

2. The ERP system performs an eligibility check to validate Microsoft Complete warranty benefits. If there is a Microsoft Complete plan attached for Surface, the ERP system will display a requirement to pay 329 Chinese Yuan (CNY) deductible fee. The ERP system will leverage existing payment collection process through Baozun to process the deductible fee.

3. AR center follows Same Unit Repair procedure for China.

4. The AR center advocate contacts the customer to return to the AR center to collect the repaired device.

If the AR center advocate cannot locate a Microsoft Complete warranty offer against the customer serial number, the AR center advocate will escalate to Tier 3 support.

Same Unit Repair Process:

The AR center will submit the device to iQor to repair via JDM1 and inform the customer to return to the AR center to collect their repaired device.

Repair SLA: 10-15 business days

Escalation Resolutions

Escalation one: As per Tier 3 policy and SLA

Escalation two: Microsoft Complete will be successfully re-provisioned within four to five days of ticket.

Customer has questions about their ADP claim or technical questions about their device warranty offer

Call Surface Devices CSS team

A Surface advocate leverages internal Service Desk Knowledge resources to answer questions.

If the Surface Devices CSS advocate is unable to resolve the question, they will escalate per existing procedures.
