2. Description of information 

A. Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT)


i. Key concepts
  • The subscription anniversary is:
    • The date the customer purchases a subscription
    • The date the recurring charge for a subscription is calculated in the system (but no invoice or billing statement is created until the account anniversary)
    • The date shown in the Anniversary Date field on the Subscription Details page of the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT)
  • The account anniversary is:
    • The date the customer purchases the first subscription under the tenant
    • The day of the month on which the billing system determines whether any subscription charges have been calculated and, if there are, creates the customer’s billing statement within 24 hours
    • The date on which a billing notification is emailed to the customer telling them to log in to view their billing statement online
    • The date shown in the Anniversary Day field on the Account page of CMAT

    Note:  For all subsequent subscriptions purchased under the same tenant (except the first bill for subsequent purchases), the customer will be billed a day after the account anniversary date and will receive a separate bill for each order placed. The service dates on the invoice will reflect the billing period for the subscription. For example, if the customer's initial purchase is on August 27, 2016 for a monthly plan, the invoice will be generated on August 28, 2016. Succeeding invoices will generate a day after the account anniversary date. On this purchase, the invoice will be generated every 28th of each month.
  • Multiple orders = multiple billing statements
    Customers who purchase multiple subscriptions in one order receive one invoice for all subscriptions purchased during that order. Customers who purchase multiple subscriptions with different orders receive separate invoices on the account anniversary date. If a customer requests that their subscriptions be combined into one billing statement, they can take to repurchase all services under 1 cart/order. The process is listed below:
  • Log in to Office 365 portal
  • Purchase services
  • Located subscriptions wanting to buy and add to cart
  • Add all desired subscriptions to same cart
  • Proceed to check out
  • Complete purchase

Once purchase is done and new subscriptions are visible, proceed to cancel individual subscriptions that were active prior to this date.

  • Change-of-billing-date requests
    Billing dates are based on the date of purchase and cannot be changed. If a customer requests that their billing date be changed, tell the customer that it is not possible to change the billing date of a subscription, and apologize for the inconvenience.

ii. Billing cycle for customers billed in CMAT 
Note: For invoice customers, payment is due 30 days from the invoice date.

For credit/debit card customers, the card is charged the day after the invoice date.

B. Additional licenses purchased 


For details about how a license count change is calculated and reflected on the customer’s bill, see 2900712: How to help customers understand their invoice .

Customers who are billed monthly will see the changes reflected on the next bill that is generated after the license change.

Customers who are billed annually will be billed for the license change on the next account anniversary day plus 1 day after the license change.
  • For example, if an annually-billed customer is billed on January 17, 2014 (account Anniversary Day is 16) and adds licenses on July 31, 2014, a pro-rated invoice for the additional licenses will be in the portal on August 17, 2014.
Customers who purchase and cancel additional licenses in the same day may or may not be billed for the additional licenses purchased. Assume that the customer will be billed and advise the customer to contact us if their next bill shows a charge for the additional licenses purchased. If the customer was charged for the licenses, we can submit a credit request at that time.

C. Value-added tax ID added after purchase

Note: For customers in the US and Canada who are claiming exemption from sales or use tax, follow the process in 2807966: Processing a Sales and Use Tax Exemption Certificate for customers in the United States, Canada and Puerto Rico .

If the customer adds a value-added tax ID (VAT ID) after a subscription is purchased, billing for the VAT will stop, and a credit request for the VAT paid must be submitted.

Example:
  • Customer purchases a monthly subscription on 05/01/2012 but does not enter a VAT ID. VAT is charged (if the customer is in a country that charges VAT) on each monthly invoice.
  • The customer enters a VAT ID on 06/15/2012. Subsequent bills will not have VAT added.
  • A request for PayOps should be created to request credit for the VAT taxes paid to date. In the work item, note the VAT ID, the VAT ID effective date, the date to credit back to, and the VAT paid to date. See 2849736: Supporting Value-Added Tax and Tax Exemption . The credit will be calculated and applied for the VAT charged since the subscription start date or the VAT ID effective date, whichever is more recent:
    • If the VAT ID effective date is 04/01/2012, credit will be calculated from 05/01/2012 (the subscription start date).
    • If the VAT ID effective date is 06/01/2012, credit will be calculated from 06/01/2012 (the VAT ID effective date).
 

D. Billing notification

All Global and Billing Administrators (up to 25 administrators) on the account will receive a billing notification email when a bill has been generated. They are directed to sign in to the Microsoft 365 admin center or the Intune Account Portal (IAP) to view their bill. This notification is sent to the Office 365 email address and the alternate email address of the Global and Billing administrators. 

 


 

E. Payment confirmation

Customers will see their payment reflected in the Microsoft 365 admin center, or on their next invoice (for users whose country is still using old invoice format). Bills are not generated for accounts with a zero balance.

Note: If an annually-billed customer pays their bill in full, they will not receive another invoice until there is a charge or a credit on their account. Annually-billed customers with a zero balance will not receive another bill reflecting their payment until a) they add or remove licenses or b) their subscription is billed for the new annual term.

For example, if an annually-billed customer is billed $500 on February 1, 2014 and pays $500 on February 11, 2014 (resulting in a zero balance), they will not receive another invoice to show the payment and the zero balance. The next time they will receive an invoice will be at renewal, on February 1, 2015, unless they make changes to the subscription in the interim.
