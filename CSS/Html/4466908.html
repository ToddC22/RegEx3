Procedure: Accounts & Billing ERA team: Engagement scenarios

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Accounts & Billing ERA team
  • Athens Store CSC team (pilot for direct escalation to PayOps)

What is this article about?

This article describes the engagement scenarios and steps for Digital Goods issues for Accounts & Billing ERA (A&B Empowered Resolution Advocates) when engaged by Sales and A&B Tier 1 advocates.

Important:

Follow up daily on the PayOps bug that you’ve submitted, as PayOps will close a bug if you have not responded to any requests from them within two days. Use this link to check all active bugs: https://aka.ms/era-PayOpsQ.

Note: If A&B ERA team still does not have access to PayOps escalation form, escalate to your SDM, keep engaging those scenarios to Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) until you acquire the access.

  • For scenarios regarding physical goods, see KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical products or software returns and exchange.
  • For scenarios landing with Sales advocates not covered by any previously mentioned article that requires PayOps escalation, the local CSC team will directly engage PayOps.
    • Tier 1 advocates are escalating to CSC as per KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing Scenarios.
  • For scenarios regarding replacements for office, and goodwill (MIR), ERA advocates may utilize Tier 3 Use Only SKU in ORST, see section 2.13 Subscription Issues.

2.1 Billing account violation

Valid engagement scenarios

KB article/reference must be followed

ERA (Empowered Resolution Advocates) team action

The account lock remains after ACA completed Account Takeover (ATO) or fraud investigation.

KB 931798 Procedure: How to handle billing account violations (formerly account locks)

Proceed with the following based on the scenario:

Scenario 1: If Billing account violation says "Account Locked for Terms of Service (TOS) Violation or Suspicious/Fraudulent Behavior": 

  • The Service Request (SR) must be transferred to the Risk Ops team for review as a Child SR (do not engage ACA).

Scenario 2: If Risk Ops' resolution is "Account lock not removed due to suspicious behavior":

  • Contact the customer and inform them that the lock cannot be removed.

Scenario 3: If Risk Ops' resolution is "Account lock not removed due to fraudulent behavior":

  • Contact the customer and inform them that the lock cannot be removed.

Scenario 4: If Risk Ops' resolution is "Account lock has been removed":

  • Ensure the account lock has been removed, then contact the customer and inform them that the lock has been removed.
  • Educate the customer to wait 48 hours before attempting to make a purchase.

*PayOps Bug Submission

KB 4466270 Procedure: A&B: ERA team for PayOps escalation

PayOps may advise that a check will need to be issued. If so gather the customers first and last name, as well as their best mailing address (In DTM) and submit manual check request to ACA here.

Once submitted, the check request will be reviewed by the ACA team leads, and if approved, a Microsoft FTE will approve the check.

Note: If the customer claims that it's been more than 45 days without having received the manual check, escalate to ACA. They will first contact ProHelp to locate the invoice and then FARGO to confirm if the check was cashed or not. At this point, if there was an issue with the shipping or an error with the information previously provided, ACA can request ProHelp and FARGO to re-issue the check.

Example of Child SR escalation note: The customer had a refund issued over a week ago but has not received it. When reviewing the transaction in MST, it shows refund failed.

2.6 Adding/Removing payment instrument issues

Valid engagement scenarios

KB article/reference must be followed

ERA team action

The customer or advocate cannot add or remove the PI.

Adding Payment Instruments:

  • The customer sees error: 25055 “We cannot add this payment method because it already exists in your account”, however there is no such payment instrument (PI) on the account.
  • The customer/you see a risk rejection: “This account has exceeded the maximum number of payment instruments that can be added to an account”, however there only a few if any active PI’s on the account.
  • The customer/you see error: 8017 “Incorrect personal profile information that won't allow PI validation”. If the customer confirms with the bank that their details are valid.
  • The customer has a payment instrument stuck in the Pending state in MST. This is more common with direct debit and related PI’s commonly used in Europe (SEPA).

Removing Payment Instruments:

  • You receive an error “Payment instrument has an outstanding balance that is not paid”, or “Payment instrument cannot be removed, there are subscriptions or pre-orders referencing it”. If no outstanding balance exists, and there are no pre-orders or subscriptions attached to the PI.
  • You see an error "The customer needs to dissociate any subscription from this payment instrument. This only applies to Mint-managed subscription”.

KB 4053337 TUG: MST: Payment instrument section

KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST)

Sometimes a payment instrument cannot be removed using MST but can be removed using CMAT. See KB 3014934 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to remove a credit card in CMAT. For all other situations, engage PayOps with the last four digits of the PI (if applicable) and screenshots of the error.

KB 3065764

​How to check the license associated to a Microsoft account​:

1. In MST, select Xbox Live under Services. ​

2. Select Windows Store and Xbox One Entitlements.​

3. Select View all.​

4. Filter the results by date or Order ID to locate the license you are searching.​

5. Check if the Status is Active or Revoked. ​

  • If you are unable to locate the license on the customer’s account, we will need to verify that the purchase was made on this Microsoft account. If we can verify that the purchase was made on this account, but the license did not apply, we can provide the customer with the funds to purchase the game from the Xbox marketplace.​

6. Engage PayOps with a precise description of the issue as well as troubleshooting that has been attempted.

Include the following if applicable: ​

  • A summary of the issue/request​.
  • An expected time frame for completion​.
  • The customer/partner impact of not completing this request within the expected timeframe​.
  • Example CVs, Fiddler Traces, and relevant identifiers (for example, Microsoft account/AAD, Device ID).

Also include screenshots and specific repro steps, and request that they engage the Microsoft team for further review.

2.15 Non-recurring issues requires CSS Command Center (CSS CC) escalation

Valid engagement scenarios

KB article/reference must be followed

ERA team action

Unknown Issue

  • Unknown or undocumented issues with no established engagement type that cannot be resolved at Tier 1.

Required information:

  • Customer account ID
  • Microsoft account having issues.
  • Error code messages seen by customer.
  • Error code messages seen by Tier 1 (include which tool).

Duplicate charge for single order

Try to locate a second order and validate that the customer did not receive two orders. If no second order exists,

Required information:

  • Orders impacted.
  • Transaction amount for both charges.

Authorization charge remains on account

Required information:

  • Ten business days must have passed since authorization was placed onto customer account.

Note: For scenarios regarding Physical Goods only, including but not limited to those within the Microsoft Store, see KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical products or software returns and exchange.
