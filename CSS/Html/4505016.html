The performance thresholds are part of the requirements partners need to meet to qualify for one of the competencies requiring performance goals:

  • Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)
  • Cloud Platform
  • Cloud Productivity
  • Data Analytics
  • Enterprise Mobility Management (EMM)
  • Small and Midmarket Cloud Solutions (SMMCS)
  • Windows and Devices

Scenario: Partner claims there are missing net new customers from the net new acquisition report

Guidance for Frontline advocates

First question must be if partner already paid the subscription.

  • If no, then this is the reason why the customer does not reflect and partner should be encouraged to pay the subscription.
  • If yes, Frontline advocate must inform that the net new customer will be displayed under the competency report in 4 weeks after the payment is confirmed.
  • If there are less than 4 weeks after payment, partner must be informed to check after 4 weeks.
  • If more than 4 weeks have passed, and the customers are still missing, partner should provide necessary proofs if he states that the subscriptions are already paid, Frontline advocates must escalate to MPN Global Support including the following details: Tenant id, Subscription GUID for the same, Customer name, transaction identifiers: (Enrollment Number and Microsoft Invoice proof). Cases that are escalated without all the above-mentioned details will be dispatched back by MPN Global support to the owning team.

Frontline advocate should explain to partner how “Net new customer” is counted.

Status 1: Customer is newly added in the last 365 days and doesn’t have any association with partner prior to 365 days

Status 0: Customer is having subscription start date > 365 days meaning associated from more than 12 months

Status -1: Customer has the subscription in deprovisioned status, hence system adds as “-1” to this customer from overall count

EMM

Scenario: Partner claims he is missing net new customers from the Relevant new customers acquired and Relevant active entitlements reports

Guidance for Frontline advocates

The Net new customers are Customers who have subscription start date within previous 12 months and have Active usage. Hence if a new tenant acquired by partner has no active entitlement he will not be considered for competency, nor will appear in the New customer breakup report.

Whenever the partner calls for these kinds of queries, Frontline advocates must investigate the following:

  • Seats must be paid licenses to count towards seat requirements. Trial or free seats are not applicable. Please allow 4 weeks from the payment date for an active customer to be displayed as New.
  • Check if the tenant claimed by the partner is coming up in the active entitlement breakup report or not.
  • If No, then inform the partner that we will consider this tenant only when this tenant has active entitlement.
  • If the partner doesn’t claim that the tenant has active entitlement, then the issue is resolved.
  • Active Entitlement: End user performs an intentional action on Enabled Entitlement on the workload in last 28 days. Hence the AE value might vary from one month to another, being considered only the data available for latest snapshot. 
  • If the partner claims that the tenant has active entitlement, Frontline advocates must escalate to MPN Global Support including the following details: Tenant id, Subscription GUID for the same, Customer name, transaction identifiers: (Enrollment Number and Microsoft Invoice proof) and also proof of active usage.
  • Cases that are escalated without all the above-mentioned details will be dispatched back by MPN Global support to the owning team.

Scenario: Partner claims he is missing active usage from the PC Insights performance report.

Guidance for Frontline advocates

First question must be: when was the last activity on this Microsoft 365 Active usage (Non-proplusWorkloads)?
Monthly Active Usage (MAU): Monthly active usage is calculated on a rolling 28-day basis and represents the unique number of users taking an intentional action on a specific service.

  • Depending on partner’s answer, please inform that he should allow 4 weeks from that date for an active usage to be displayed under the competency report.
  • If 4 weeks already passed since the last activity of that usage, Frontline advocate should ask partner to provide the details of the customer he thinks that should be recognized and is not.
  • Please note that charity tenants (non-profit organizations) are not considered for calculation and as per policy, no exemptions are provided for this type of tenants. (See article FAQs for Competencies in Partner Center)
  • Very often, partners are not aware of the difference between Qualified Usage & Active Usage. For example, partner might ask why Active usage is only 1 while qualified usage is 1200? Frontline advocates must explain the differences as per below:

*Qualified Usage: Customer organization owns or has purchase the license that gives the rights to use a workload.

*Enabled/Assigned Usage: License in assigned to end user in O365 admin portal to use a workload.

*Active Usage: End user performs an intentional action on Enabled Usage on the workload in last 28 days.

*Active Usage = 1 means that active usage is counted for only one Active User

  • In case the active usage data is not displayed on the Active usage report under the competency details, Frontline advocates must escalate to MPN Global Support including Tenant ID, Subscription GUID and tenant name of the missing Customers, so that engineers be able to perform a manual verification.
  • Partner should check first if the SKU is part of competency evaluation. Click here to know the qualified SKUs. If yes, partner must also provide any proof they might have showing the active usage of the impacted customers (for example: Microsoft 365 admin center portal >>Reports > Usage > filter by 30 days).

Association Type

Definition

Digital Partner of Record (DPOR)


DPOR is the partner servicing the customer on a Microsoft cloud subscription.
 

- The end customer must designate the partner as DPOR each time a subscription is sold (new or existing subscription).

- The DPOR association must remain current for the duration of the incentive.

- The Partner only earns incentive/Azure consumption from month assigned or month of competency attainment, whichever is later.

More information is available in the public article Digital Partner of Record (DPOR).

Tracked Templates (same as CUA)

The partner associates their MPN ID with the application deployment template; use case is IP installed in customer tenant (ISV) – also called Customer Usage Attribution.

Claim Partner of Record (CPOR)

CPOR connects a partner to a customer's Microsoft cloud subscription/workload via a claims process. The CPOR can only associate with customers at the subscription or workload level and only one partner can be associated per subscription/workload.

More information is available in the public article OSA Claim Model and Process.

Transacting Partner of Record (TPOR)

TPOR is the partner on a subscription (Azure, Modern Workplace, Business Applications) at the time of the transaction. On Azure consumption, in cases where the partner is the DPOR on a subscription, they will not earn as the TPOR on the same subscription but will earn for any other subscriptions under the enrollment where they are not DPOR.

Partner as End Customer

Consumption of Azure occurs on the partner’s own subscription; primarily SaaS or host scenario.

Partner Admin Link

PAL association allows partners to focus on services they offer to customer to drive consumption via partner network ID with the credentials used for service delivery. Using PAL, partners gain the required access to customer's environment. The use case is services engagements (MSP/SI).

More information is available in article 4495301 - Informational: Azure Partner Admin Link (PAL).

Admin on Behalf of (AOBO)

Partners with a Microsoft billing relationship will, by default, be granted admin rights to the customer's Azure subscription.

Marketplace

Partner solution is purchased through marketplace.

CSP Tier 1

Direct providers who sell to end customers.

CSP Tier 2

Major distributors who resell through tier 1 CSP providers.

Windows and Devices performance discrepancies

For OEM partner option (applicable for Named OEM and MAR partners), Frontline advocates should escalate the case to MPN Global Support providing the following details for analyzing the request: TPID (Top partner ID), proof sustaining the relevant devices sold, MAR agreement reference number. Microsoft Authorized OEM Distributors are top-level distribution partners whose business model and size qualify them for a direct contractual relationship with Microsoft. Microsoft engages in a high level of business, sales, and marketing activities with these distributors. Check here if partner is an OEM Distributor.

For Surface Reseller option, Frontline advocates should escalate the case to MPN Global Support providing the following details for analyzing the request: revenue proof of Surface Commercial devices and accessories across in the TTM (trailing twelve months) period, TPID (Top partner ID), proof of Authorized Surface ADR or Disti Managed Partner (DMP) issued by Microsoft.

For Surface Hub option, Frontline advocates should escalate the case to MPN Global Support providing the following details for analyzing the request: proof of Relevant devices shipped in TTM (trailing twelve months) period, TPID (Top partner ID), proof of Authorized Surface Hub Device Reseller issued by Microsoft.

 

SPLA revenue missing under Hosting Option

The reason leading to this discrepancy might be that the SPLA id is not mapped to the MPN id. Frontline advocates should raise a new mapping request as described in article 4520665. If this process fails or the mapping request is rejected, please document the case and escalate to Global Support as described in article 2865698.

 

Microsoft Authorized Distributor or CSP Indirect Provider status not recognized under Distributor Option

Microsoft Authorized Distributor or CSP Indirect Provider status not recognized under Distributor Option: see article 4342461 Partner Center: Distributor Option for more details.

 

Missing KPIs under Learning Option

Missing KPIs under Learning Option: see article 2717776 Enrollment into the Learning Option at Action Pack, Silver or Gold levels in Partner Membership Center and Partner Center for more details.

4. Escalation path

  • For discrepancies under a particular competency in Competencies Tab, Frontline advocates should refer to article 4033695 PMC to Partner Center Migration: How to escalate cases. Cases dispatched without respecting the escalation template will be dispatched back by MPN Global Support team to the owning team.

  • For partners concerns regarding ANALYZE TAB/My Insights or reported discrepancies, Frontline advocates must redirect partners to INSIGHTS tab in PC, as starting September 15, 2020, all data under Analyzed tab moved under Insights tab (depreciation happening September 30, 2020), hence OCP support engineers are not supporting existing/ new potential tickets.

    Call to action: for the existing cases raised previously with OCP support, redirect partners to use Insights Tab and if under that tab they notice any issues, document the case, collect PSR and raise the case to MPN Global Support team as per process described in article 4536289 Informational: Partner: Partner Center Insights.
    For potential new cases questioning about Analyze Tab/My Insights, please redirect partner to Insights tab.

  • For raising a parenting request, Frontline advocates should raise a parenting ticket through OCP Insight tool following 4520665 Process: Partner: OCP Insights new parenting (mapping) request
