Procedure: Legal: Assisting customers requesting for a legal contact or arbitration

 ﻿[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer
  • All Commercial

What is this article about?

This article describes how to respond to customers seeking a legal point of contact within Microsoft or requesting legal arbitration due to Microsoft being unable to resolve their concerns through customer support channels.

Note:
This process is not meant to address support issues such as billing concerns including refunds, technical issues, or warranty concerns around device failures. If the customer has a support issue, address her or his issue directly

1. Overview

As an Advocate/Engineer, you are expected to address product and service related support issues such as billing concerns including refunds, technical issues, or warranty concerns around device failures. You are expected to address all underlying issues prompting the customer to want to engage Microsoft’s legal department. Keep in mind that the customer’s preference to engage legal is not the issue itself. But the underlying issue prompting the customer to want to engage legal is the true issue to resolve.

At times, customers may not agree with the outcome of the support session and request for information on how to contact Microsoft’s legal department for arbitration purposes.

Microsoft’s legal department does not handle complaints from customers directly. It is critical that you understand their concerns and address them adequately. This includes consulting Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) where available in consumer support for handling extenuating circumstances prompting customers to want to contact Microsoft’s legal department directly.

Note:
For customers located in the European Union (EU), review scenario 3 in section 2 for additional information.

2. Procedures

Microsoft’s legal department does not handle complaints from customers directly. They do not mediate unresolved support issues, such as billing concerns including refunds, technical issues or warranty concerns around device failures. To ensure the customer’s concerns are addressed effectively, it is important to clearly understand the customer’s underlying issues.

Scenario

Advocate/engineer action
  1. Customer has a support issue that is unresolved (from the customer’s perspective)

Follow these steps:

1. Show empathy and offer help, focusing on the customer’s issue. Suggested scripting:

“As a support advocate/engineer, I am here to assist you with support issues/questions. Please let me know how I can help resolve your support related concerns.”

2. Address the customer’s underlying issue.
  1. Worldwide EXCEPT Europe: Customer insists on contacting Microsoft legal for arbitration or other reasons such as threatening legal action for an unresolved support issue

Follow these steps:

1. Ensure steps from scenario 1 above are followed.

2. If the customer does not agree with the previous resolution delivered AND your current support attempt does not change the previous resolution delivered, listen for the customer’s extenuating circumstances.

3. Consult your Supervisor/Manager to determine if these circumstances require further review or consideration.

Supervisor: In consumer support, consult Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) as needed to address the underlying issue. Do NOT engage ACA to report a legal threat.

4. If the customer insists on a legal point of contact or contacting legal, use the suggested scripting below:

“Microsoft’s legal department does not mediate unresolved support issues directly. Customer Support also does not have access to direct contact details for the legal department.”

5. If pressed, use the suggested scripting below:

“Addresses of local Microsoft offices nearest to you are available online by searching on https://aka.ms/WWoffices. Please understand however that if you choose to write the local offices, they will route all support related requests back to Customer Support to handle.”

Note: For U.S. office locations , customers should select the “Corporate Sales Office” locations.

 
  1. Europe only: Customer insists on contacting Microsoft legal for arbitration or other reasons such as threatening legal action for an unresolved support issue

Follow these steps:

1. Ensure steps from scenario 1 above are followed.

2. If the customer does not agree with the previous resolution delivered AND your current support attempt does not change the previous resolution delivered, listen for the customer’s extenuating circumstances.

3. Consult your Supervisor/Manager to determine if these circumstances require further review or consideration.

Supervisor: In consumer support, consult Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) as needed to address the underlying issue. Do not engage ACA to report a legal threat.

4. If the customer insists on a legal point of contact, use the suggested scripting below:

“Microsoft’s legal department does not mediate unresolved support issues directly. Customer Support also does not have access to direct contact details for the legal department.

There is an option to submit a complaint on the European Commission Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) platform by visiting https://aka.ms/EU-ODR. Whether Microsoft participates in online dispute resolution may depend on circumstances and country."
