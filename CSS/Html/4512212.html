1. Smart campaigns

1.1 Overview

The latest AI-powered feature for small businesses from Microsoft Advertising, smart campaigns, continually improve ad performance (auto-optimize), allowing small and medium businesses (SMB) to spend more time running their business. They provide automated ad campaign optimization, quick set up, minimal ongoing management, and customer reach across Bing, Yahoo!, and partner websites.

Customers should sign up in smart mode if they:

  • Have a functioning website that is ready to use.
  • Do not have a lot of time on their hands.
  • Do not intend on using advanced ad features.

Customers should sign up in expert mode if they:

  • Intend on using advanced ad features.
  • Are agencies or large direct advertisers with complex business models.

Talking points

  • "Whether you’re new to search engine marketing or already a pro, I can offer you personalized assistance with whatever you need to get your campaigns up and running successfully in no time."
  • "I can give you a tour of the Smart UI and show you some exciting features that will save you time with managing your campaigns and help to boost your ad performance."
  • "Smart campaigns help cover the basics to help you understand how Microsoft Advertising works. I can also give you information on the more advanced campaign management tools, expert mode, and transfer you to a team that can review the benefits and teach you how to use it."
  • "Regardless of whether you choose smart or expert mode, Microsoft Advertising ensures you have the right support to help get your ad campaigns set up for better performance so that you get the most from your investment with us."

For more details see Smart Campaigns: Is switching to Expert Mode right for you? and Smart vs. Expert mode for the decision-making matrix to advise on the most appropriate account mode and section 3 if needed.

1.2 Smart features timeline

Smart mode/Campaigns are currently in ramp to general availability (GA) to become the default mode for all NA-EN and EU-EN sign-ups. You can see the Releases website for feature and timeline details.

Smart Campaign feature notes:

  • Customers can use PBCs with Smart Campaigns and may receive targeted onboarding auto-PBC offers.
  • Smart accounts cannot yet target ads globally (US and UK only until further notice).
  • Expert mode agencies can link to Smart clients.
  • Smart mode agencies must convert to expert to link to clients.
  • Converted Expert mode customers cannot yet create Smart or Expert mode accounts under their existing CID.
  • Smart mode customers cannot yet create Smart mode accounts under their existing CID.
  • Expert mode customers cannot yet create Smart mode accounts under their existing CID.
  • Expert mode accounts cannot yet create Smart Campaigns under their existing account.
  • Smart and converted Expert mode accounts can create Smart Campaigns under their existing account.
  • Ads and Keyword Themes undergo regular editorial reviews with inline appeals.
  • Smart mode customers can see notifications in the new unified queue.
  • Internal users can download editorial reports for Smart Campaigns.

1.3 Getting started with Smart Campaigns

New customers who might benefit from Smart Campaigns may sign up for a new smart mode account at https://aka.ms/smartstart. Existing advertisers can use this link to leverage multi-user access and create a new Smart mode account accessible with their existing credentials.

Marketing efforts to acquire new customers through Smart Sign up are underway and using automated performance-based coupons.

Notes:

  • Sign-up steps cannot be skipped. If the sign-up flow is abandoned, advertisers can resume where it was abandoned on next login.
  • Import from Google AdWords Smart Campaigns is not supported.

For more help see All about Smart Campaigns for details on the sign-up process and feature overview deck for customer onboarding steps.

2. Smart campaign settings and management

UCM Opportunities: UCM opportunities are not currently relevant to smart campaigns and should be ignored.

Note: Smart accounts converted to expert may still have relevant opportunities for their full-feature campaigns. Be sure to check all campaign types before dismissing opportunities as irrelevant. 

Because smart campaigns are automatically optimized, there are limited optimization capabilities so it’s good to ensure all settings are current. For more information, see Smart campaigns: Monitor your campaign's performance.

To determine how a smart campaign is performing, focus on the goals defined, then review metrics on impressions, clicks and number of verified phone calls received. Smart campaigns optimization capabilities are limited to the settings outlined below:

  • Create a new campaign: Create a new campaign to target a new radius location, test additional promotions, and use distinct landing pages in ads.
  • Modify campaign goals: The campaign goal that customers select will help guide them through campaign setup but cannot be changed once set. For example, if a customer chooses Get more phone calls to my business, customers will be required to enter in a phone number while it is optional if other goals are selected.
  • Keyword Themes: Advertisers should choose keyword themes that best reflect their offerings. Selections help the system target relevant search phrases so be sure to select at least four-five. Keyword Themes are subject to normal editorial review. See KB 4521657 Ad Quality SOP: Smart campaigns editorial support for details. For more information, see Smart campaigns: How your settings for locations, products, and services affect who sees your ads.
  • Refine Ad copy: The landing page and other signals are used to automatically generate the first ad unless the keyword themes are entered manually. Not all advertisers will see proposed ad copy. For more information, see Smart campaigns: About effective ads. Smart campaigns may contain up to 100 ads. Ad copy is subject to normal editorial review. See KB 4521657 Ad Quality SOP: Smart campaigns editorial support for details.
  • Manage search phrases: Smart campaigns will automatically determine when an ad delivers. Advertisers will see a sample of search phrases, but advertisers can disable search phrases to prevent their ad from delivering on terms they deem irrelevant. On rare occasions, advertisers may see Bing advanced search operators which tailor organic results, these do not affect paid ad delivery. Smart campaigns: How your settings for locations, products, and services affect who sees your ads.
  • Refine locations/targeting: Target multiple locations using within a specific location or a single location using radius. For more info, see Smart campaigns: Where and when will my ads appear?
  • Adjust Budget: A daily budget (standard) will be recommended based on other signals. Advertisers can use the performance estimates to help set a budget for their desired reach. For more information, see Smart Campaigns: About budgets and what you pay for.
    • Note regarding Bids: There are no keyword bids within smart campaigns. The system will automatically determine how much to bid for a search term based on your budget and other indicators to maximize your campaign’s value. Advertisers can manage their search phrases and expand keyword themes.

Reporting

Supported reports:

  • Account and campaign performance reports.
  • Change history.
  • Budget and billing statement.

All other reports will be blocked from rendering details for smart campaigns, even if visible in the UI.

Internal users can download editorial report (Ads, AdID, editorial status) for campaigns in smart mode.

Google Ads smart campaigns import is not supported.

3. Switch to expert mode

Before conversion:

See Smart Campaigns: Is switching to Expert mode right for you? and Smart vs Expert – Best Fit decision-making matrix to advise on the best path for the customer. Ensure the customer is aware that Expert Mode requires regular maintenance to ensure optimal performance and competitiveness. To switch a Smart mode account to Expert mode, access the Gears menu and select Switch to Expert Mode. Accounts cannot be switched back to Smart.

Note: Agencies must switch their CID to expert mode to enable client account linking. Expert mode agencies can manage Smart mode client accounts.

After conversion:

Ensure the customer understands the account will require ongoing management to remain competitive and consider referring them to Onboarding Consultation (see KB 4510981 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Support & Services Cross-Team Phone Transfers) or providing guidance/walkthrough with resources relevant to their reason for switching to expert (for example, give them a tour and share both How to get your campaigns up and running in Microsoft Advertising and How to target my customers by adjusting bids if their reason for conversion was a desire for sophisticated bid management.)

Existing Smart Campaigns will appear in the campaigns list and will be labelled smart under Campaign type. Smart Campaigns will remain live and spending. Users cannot see any campaign details from the ad group, kw, and ads tabs and must select the campaign name to modify smart campaign settings and can return to view full-feature campaigns using the top-level Campaigns page link. Advanced features cannot be adopted in Smart Campaigns, but new campaigns can be created after switching to expert to take full advantage of advanced features.

[Asset 4550164]

Until the ability to create a new Smart mode account within the existing customer shell is available, existing advertisers can create a separate Smart Mode customer shell via https://aka.ms/smartstart with their existing login credentials (multi-user access). Because it is a completely separate customer shell, payment information will need to be added during the sign-up flow.

4. Achieve More 

To Achieve More with a Smart Campaign, focus on ensuring complete setup for new customers and ensuring awareness of Smart Campaign capabilities (outlined above) and advocating other resources, like User Voice.

For smart mode manager accounts, conversion to Expert mode should not be pitched unless the customer expresses willingness to invest more time in managing their accounts and has interest in or need for more complex features and functionality. Refer to section 3 and the Smart verses Expert – Best Fit decision-making matrix to advise on the best path for the customer.

If conversion to expert is appropriate for the customer, ensure the customer is prepared to self-manage their account by referring them to Onboarding Consultation (see KB 4510981 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Support & Services Cross-Team Phone Transfers) or providing guidance/walkthrough with resources relevant to their reason for switching to expert (for example, give them a tour and share both How to get your campaigns up and running in Microsoft Advertising and How to target my customers by adjusting bids if their reason for conversion was a desire for sophisticated bid management.)

5. Ticket handling

Existing call flow, ticket handling, and quality requirements apply, per KB 3171493 Process: Microsoft Advertising & Bing Places: Reactive Support Ticket Handling Policy. Focus on scoping existing customer inquiries prior to authentication (which requires ticket creation) since customers may require immediate redirection to Standard Support for full feature campaign support. Microsoft Advertising Sample Language may be leveraged as needed.

Ticket creation & coding

All inquiries originating through a specialized Smart Support channel (as determined by the IVR whisper or virtual chat/phone queue) must be handled in the most appropriate regional Smart ticket queue in ASD, regardless of team or issue type.

Note: If the customer is following up on an existing ticket in another queue, standard ticket handling guidelines apply, and the existing ticket should be updated/pulled rather than a new ticket created.

If the MSA mode is smart or the customer’s inquiry is specific to Smart Campaigns, be sure to set Campaign Type = Smart Campaign. Inversely, if the MSA mode is expert and is not a smart campaign inquiry, be sure to set Campaign Type to the most appropriate value (such as, Not Applicable or Search). For presales inquiries, set Campaign Type = Not applicable unless/until an account is created, then select the most appropriate value per the chart below.

7. Manual CPC – Bid Strategy Deprecation (Pilot)

Starting 26th August, 2020, we will be testing the removal of the Manual CPC bid strategy for new signup customers who choose to create SMART campaigns.

After this date, if customers switch from SMART to Expert campaigns, they will no longer have the option to run manual CPC bids.

Instead, customers will be able to use Enhanced CPC, which provides them with the ability to choose a desired CPC average that the system will target, whilst still modifying bids to try and get the advertiser the best chance for a conversion.

Opting-out

Although opt-out is possible, we encourage advocates not to provide this as their primary solution if a customer asks to switch back to Manual CPC.

Instead, use the FAQ talking points below to educate the advertiser and reassure them that Enhanced CPC will continue to provide them with the effective bid management solution they desire.

If an advertiser continues to insist on returning to Manual CPC, you can escalate to Global Support, who will contact engineering to make this request.

FAQ

Q: Does Microsoft Advertising still allow manual bidding?

A: With advertisers increasingly relying on platform automation, Microsoft Advertising is currently testing automated bid only campaigns. We’ve seen multitudes of success stories where customers are seeing improved ROI with auto bidding, and we are confident that this change will benefit our users.

Q: I still want to manage ad group and keyword level bids. Is this possible?

A: Yes! You can continue to set ad group and keyword level bids by selecting the Enhanced CPC bid strategy (default). Enhanced CPC automatically adjusts your bids at auction time so that you bid up on users who are more likely to convert and bid down on users less likely to convert. Your CPC will not exceed your bid, on average.

Q: How can I control my ROI?

A: Microsoft Advertising offers a variety of auto bidding strategies to meet your needs. Refer to this auto bidding documentation page for help deciding which strategy is best for you. If granular control is desired, you can continue to manage keyword level bids through Enhanced CPC, which allows for this and at the same time takes advantage of auction time enhancements.
