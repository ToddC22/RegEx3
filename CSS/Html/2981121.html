What is the Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP) program?


The Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP) program is a programmatic approach to enable partners to sell Microsoft cloud products directly to customers and to own the full customer cloud lifecycle. The CSP program enables partners to sell cloud services in an integrated way by adding reselling, provisioning, billing, and support to their portfolio. In the CSP program:

  • The partner is adding their own managed services to Microsoft cloud services and selling this on a subscription basis to their customers.
  • Invoicing of individual customer usages is done by the partner. The customer gets an invoice for Microsoft services AND partner managed services by the partner, and they no longer receive any invoice from Microsoft.
  • The customer gets all support through the partner, and Microsoft only provides support to the partner.

How is the process for becoming a Microsoft Cloud Solutions (CSP) provider changing?

Direct CSP Onboarding – Change from invitation only to open application

In the past, partners could only become a Cloud Service Provider (CSP) by invitation. Starting July 7, 2016, any partner will be able to complete the onboarding process without the need for an invitation e-mail from Microsoft. This will greatly expand the accessibility of the CSP program to partners and may drive greater call volume from partners with questions about the CSP program or onboarding process.

What is the difference between the Direct and Indirect Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP) business models?

Microsoft offers two business models for partners to leverage as a CSP. The Direct model requires a robust infrastructure to enable end-to-end ownership of the customer relationship. The Indirect model offers Resellers the opportunity to work with Distributors who can provide the tools and resources necessary to manage customer relationships. See below for more details about each model.

Direct CSP Model
In the Direct CSP model, partners provide cloud services directly to customers and are responsible for all aspects of delivery, support, and billing.The Direct CSP model is appropriate for partners who have:
  • Strong services offerings in their existing portfolio.
  • A dedicated technical cloud support team, which is the first and only point of contact for customers according to customer needs.
  • Tools (a billing engine) and processes to invoice all CSP customers.
Some advantages of being a Direct CSP are:
  • A strong relationship with Microsoft.
  • Increased licensing margins by scaling efforts and providing strong standardized managed services.
For more information about the Direct CSP model, see Explore the Direct model.
Go to How do I become a Direct Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP)? for instructions on enrolling as a Direct CSP.
 

Useful sample questions that help a partner understand what is involved in being a Direct CSP:

  1. Do you have the capability to bill customers for usage based billing?
  2. If so, what is the average cost per customer related to this activity?
  3. If so, how do you manage your pricing strategy to accommodate the liability risk of rebilling?
  4. Can you support front line support queries relative to the Microsoft products you are selling?
  5. If so, what is the average cost per customer related to this support and do you have Advanced Support for Partners or Premier Support to back up these resources?
  6. Do you use a portion of the margin provided by your CSP provider to adjust pricing in competitive situations?

Indirect CSP Model

In the Indirect CSP model, partners work with a Distributor to re-sell services to customers. Within this model a partner can either be a Distributor or a Reseller. The Reseller sells cloud service to customers, and the Distributor manages delivery, support, and billing aspects of the relationship. Distributors have been verified by Microsoft to have the systems and infrastructures in place to provide this robust level of support, so Resellers can focus on their customer offerings.

The key requirements for being a Direct CSP are high and most partners do not fulfill them, or only fulfill some parts. These partners will not have enough revenue to profit as a Direct CSP. It is more beneficial and provides a better profit margin to start as a Reseller in the Indirect business model. The partner can always become a Direct CSP in the future once they build their services, billing, and support practices.

Some advantages of being a Reseller in the Indirect CSP model are:
  • A strong relationship with the Distributor.
  • All key requirements (technical support, billing infrastructure, service offerings) of a Direct CSP are provided by the Distributor. The Reseller does not need to fulfill these requirements. The Reseller simply has to enroll with the Distributor of their choice and they can begin transacting.
  • Cloud Distributors offer cloud platforms with a variety of additional services, and the Reseller can use and bundle these services with their own offerings.
For more information about the Indirect CSP model, see Explore the Indirect model.

Once I become a Direct Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP), can I sell CSP licenses in other countries?

With regional authorization, partners with international businesses can more easily manage customers in different regions and countries around the world. This reduces the number of tenants that partners need to manage, reduces the billing dates that partners have to handle, and gives partners a more consolidated view of their sales.

The list of Sales Territories can be found in this public article (section Country/region information): https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/regional-authorization-overview

If a partner wants to resell CSP licenses outside of their Sales Territory, this is possible by creating a CSP partner tenant located in the desired country/region. But note that the partner must have a legal address in the country they are trying to onboard to. This is required for vetting and Credit Check. They will then need to onboard to CSP again using that tenant and the MPN ID corresponding to that location ( If the partner doesn’t have this yet, they will need to add that location in Partner Center, please refer to article 4512647 . Once showing as onboarded on that tenant, they can use it to sell to customers in that country/region.

Important: If the partner wants to sell in multiple currencies, they will need to onboard a different tenant to CSP for each additional currency.

How do I become a Reseller in the Indirect Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP) business model?

If a partner determines that becoming a Reseller in the Indirect CSP model is the best fit for their business, the RSC agent should direct the partner to the Microsoft Cloud Solution Provider Program website. From here, partners can navigate to a directory of Distributors, and the partner can contact each Distributor for more details and decide which is the best fit for their business needs. Once a partner has signed up with a Distributor, they can work with the Distributor directly to set up their processes, answer questions, and resolve issues.

Important: RSC agents should not make any recommendation for a Distributor. Each Distributor has a different business portfolio, and it is the responsibility of the partner to assess each Distributor and make the right decision for their business.
 

How do I become a Distributor in the Indirect Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP) business model?

Indirect Provider status – in which you manage CSP Indirect resellers and support and bill the customers those resellers bring to you -- is available to only a select few Microsoft partners in each global region. To become an Indirect Provider, you would need to be nominated by a Microsoft Field executive in the country in which you want to be approved for Indirect Provider status. The nomination then goes through an extensive review process by Microsoft to ensure that your company meets the requirements.

If you’re currently a Microsoft managed partner, please express your interest in Indirect Provider status to your account manager, who can take the next step in the process and with consult with you on your company’s candidacy.

What do I do if my application was declined?

After an application to become a Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider (CSP) is submitted it must go through an approval process. If the partner is declined, they may call the RSC for an explanation.

When an application is submitted the following criteria are validated :

  • A credit review (a payment history review for pre-existing partners or a credit check for new partners)
  • Name and address verification

Step 1: agents check CMAT for the current status

Step 2: agents will further verify the status in PCST (Prose) if under credit check or vetting

Below issue applies if it takes more than 12 business days:

Vetting Pending / Declined – ask the partner to check Partner Center > Account Settings if there is a need to update their company information. Agent then should ask if the partner received an email, respond to the email if possible. Escalate to WOCS if no update is received.

For Credit Check Pending or if the Partner wants to understand why the Credit Check has been Declined, refer the partner to ucmwrcsp@microsoft.com and escalate to WOCS if credit team is unresponsive.

If a Microsoft Cloud Solutions Provider Program (CSP) partner application is declined, the partner will receive an email with a link to resubmit the application.

How do I offboard my account?

If a partner wants to replace a CSP tenant due to an organization name change, or if a partner mistakenly maps the wrong tenant with CSP, and would like to change the activation of the CSP account with the correct tenant, the RSC should escalate according to the instructions for your region listed in Escalations to cancel the current CSP tenant relationship and redo the sign up process with the correct tenant.
 

Can a partner re-apply for CSP using a tenant that was previously rejected? (In other words: is it possible to switch a “CSP Disqualified” tenant into “CSP Active”?)

It is NOT possible to reinstate enrollments that were rejected prior to June 2016 -> therefore partners will have to re-apply with a different tenant. No exceptions in this scenario.

It might be possible to reinstate enrolments that were rejected after June 2016 -> in this case RSC agents should still advise the partner that they have to re-apply with a different tenant, as their first answer. If there is a valid business case, agents can escalate to Operations (CAP queue: CBO_CSP) requesting if an exception can be made.

Background info: Reinstating will depend on the reason for rejection. If partner was rejected due to vetting issues, then Operations (CAP queue: CBO_CSP) can reinstate and retrigger the process. If they were rejected due to credit failure, then the partner will need to contact credit team to determine if their tenant can be reinstated. Operations (CAP queue: CBO_CSP) can only reinstate failed credit entries IF they receive communication from the credit team that the tenant is credit approved.

CMAT “Partner State” Status

Definition

Pending Qualification

Partner has submitted an enrollment form, but has not yet been approved; i.e. vetting in progress.

Pending Onboarding

Pending Onboarding is a status that the partners gets in for an interim period after they are qualified and then sign the agreement to get activated. Pending Onboarding is a state between these but the partner shouldn’t be stuck in this state.

Qualified

Enrollment has been approved and is ready for partner to accept the MCRA in Partner Center.

Active

Partner has accepted the MCRA in Partner Center and can transact.

Disqualified

The partner’s enrollment has been rejected and they will no longer be able to submit an enrollment form for the same tenant.

Email verification

Same as email ownership in admin tool. This check verifies whether you own the email address that you provided for registration. User has to click on the verification email to verify the email.

Employment verification

Same as domain validation in admin tool. This check verifies that domain of the email address you provided during registration is owned by your company. This check could take 1-3 days to complete.

Business verification (Combination of BusinessMarketProfile and BusinessInvestigation) BusinessMarketProfile

This checks entity name against D&B’s existing list of validated business. If business entity is unknown ‘Business Investigation’ is spawned. If business entity is known in D&B’ database then this check is authorized and DUNs number and other relevant details are returned for this company.

BusinessInvestigation

This check is for accounts that scored low confidence or for those with no match in D&B’s database during MarketProfile check. For this check: D&B does a detail investigation using third party sources and this process could take 1-2 weeks depending upon where the company is located.
