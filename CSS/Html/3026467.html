Guidance

Office 365 A1 Plus is an A1 Offer that includes Office 365 ProPlus, and allows students, faculty and staff to install the latest version of Office on up to five PC or Mac computers and on other mobile devices, including Windows tablets and the iPad.

Click Link for “Office 365 Education Self-Sign up: Technical FAQ”.

Eligibility requirements for students, faculty, and staff to receive (qualify for) Office 365 A1 Plus:

  • EDU tagged (for more information, please refer to article 2818467 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Customer does not have the option to purchase academic offers or wants to remove EDU tag in the Microsoft 365 admin center.)
  • Customer license Office institution-wide for faculty and staff through Enrollment for Education Solutions, Open Value Subscription Education Solutions, Cloud Solution Provider (CSP), or a school contract. (Check the Details here.)

For more information about Office 365 Education, please see article: 4465600 PSIA (Product Support and Information Article) Office 365 Education

Students, faculty, and staff can now sign up for the Office 365 A1 Plus for Students or Faculty offer using an email address with their academic institution’s domain. All existing and eligible new customers who take advantage of the self-service sign up process for Office 365 A1 Plus will get 5,000 licenses that can be used by their students/faculty. There is no cost for these licenses. These will increase incrementally by 300 each time the customer bumps up against that limit while assigning licenses. These limits can be expanded but will require an escalation.
 
If the school domain is not already associated with a tenant, one will be created when a student, faculty member or staff signs up for this offer. This tenant is known as an IW or unmanaged tenant and will not be visible in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT).

O365 General EDU

Scenarios and advocate steps for general EDU

Scenario:

Advocate steps:

Scenario 1: An Admin sees a new subscription on their account that they don’t recognize.

a. If the tenant has an Office 365 A1 Plus for faculty or students subscription with 5,000 licenses, advise the customer that this is the Office 365 A1 Plus offer which is available to all schools who have the Office Student Advantage Program. Information Worker (IW) sign-up allows students, faculty, and staff to take advantage of Office 365 services without the need to have their administrator set up an account for them.

b. Provide the following links to provide information about the offer: 
http://www.microsoft.com/education/ww/StudentAdvantage/Pages/hed.aspx.

c. If the administrators wants to know how to turn this option on/off for their tenant, they can use this article: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/education/deploy/office-365-education-self-sign-up#how-can-i-prevent-students-from-joining-my-existing-office-365-tenant,
If not working, please direct the admin to Technical Support.

Scenario 2: An Admin is trying to become the admin of an IW/unmanaged domain.

It is possible for the school’s administrator to become the admin for the tenant by following the admin takeover process. The administrator will then have full admin privileges and will be able to perform all administrative functions. Please check here for more details.

Scenario 3: A student, faculty member or staff of an academic institution is having issues signing up for the offer.

Refer the customer to the Microsoft Community site: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us.

Scenario 4: A small number of customers need more than 50,000 licenses.

Follow this article 3210277 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to handle customer requests about increasing Edu viral licenses.

A1 Plus

Procedure for A1 Plus Support

a. Contacting AVE Support

When the previous requirements are met and the customer has one of the agreements mentioned, engineers can submit a validation for Academic Institution following the steps with AVE Support team.

  1. Click on New Request
  2. In the Select a tenant for your request section, choose from drop sown list Microsoft 365
  3.  On the New Vetting Request page please fill all the fields and enable the toggle for SA Entitlement.
    Note: For Domain we highly recommend adding the vanity domain. (Vanity domain is mandatory for EDU customers)
  4. To send the request click on Submit and a request summary will be shown when it's submitted successfully.
    On the Import Request page, you can find the record of your requests and the progress.

The validation ETA is 3 business days. If you don't have a resolution within 3 days or if any additional details are needed (ex. rejection), click on Escalate to engage with AVE team.

If the vetting team has any questions or need more information / evidence on any request, they will reach you via an email from:

Microsoft Internal Support Services <snwprod@itsm.microsoft.com>
Subject: CREATED: INCxxxxxxxx AVE - .domain.com

[In the content you will be informed that an Incident ticket INCxxxxxxxx was created to track your issue. You will need to access it and provide the needed details.]

b. How to add A1 Plus on tenant

Customer can get the plan directly from Microsoft at Office 365 Education if they meet all three of the following requirements:

  1. They have a school-specific email address provided by the school (for example, sara@contoso.edu) that can receive external email.
  2. They are of legal age to sign up for an online offer individually (13 years old).
  3. They have Internet access.

Then, a user will see the option to Sign up as a Student or a Teacher. If this is the first user doing the self-sign-up, depending on this choice, the system will add A1 Plus for Students or A1 Plus for Faculty on customer’s tenant.

If the user was not created in Admin Center and Automatic Tenant Join option is True, the system will create the user and assign license. (check the Details here.)

Note: A1 Plus is a viral subscription and customer will see it in Admin Center -> Billing -> Licenses.

Scenarios and advocate steps for A1 Plus

Scenario:

Advocate steps:

Scenario 1: Customers ask about how to determine whether an organization is eligible for Office 365 A1 Plus for Students, Faculty or Staff.

Support can provide this link to the customer: Office 365 Education Self-Sign up: Technical FAQ.

Scenario 2: Existing customers contacted support to ask why the licenses for their Office 365 A1 Plus went down from unlimited to 5,000.

For more information, refer to article 3210277 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to handle customer requests about increasing Edu viral licenses.

Scenario 3: Existing customer who had unlimited licenses for Office 365 A1 Plus now only sees 5,000 licenses on their M365 Admin Portal and needs to assign more licenses to their students/faculty.

Please share with customer that

“Through the self-service license assignment flow, the number of licenses will increase by 300 every time the license max is reached.

For example:

After the initial 5000 license max is reached, the next student or teacher from the tenant that goes to the self-service license assignment flow, will be assigned a license and total licenses under the tenant will increase to 5300.”

By default, viral Edu offers come with 5,000 licenses for Faculty and 5,000 licenses for Students. An additional 300 licenses will be added to any eligible EDU tenant (approved by AVE) once they have exhausted their available (unused) licenses. However, if the school or institution is attempting to assign licenses to students (in bulk) and need more than 300 new licenses, their request must be escalated to engineering. For more information, refer to article 3210277 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to handle customer requests about increasing Edu viral licenses.

In the event that the customer does not want to go through the self-service sign up process to update their licenses, Tier 1 Ambassadors need to reach out to their respective TL/SME for assistance on the escalation process.

*Note: Please advise the customer that we will submit their request for validation. Do not confirm that their request will be fulfilled unless EDU support confirmed that the request has been approved and the licenses has been added on the customer’s account.

Frontline Engineers need to ask customer and collect below information for each request:

  • Number of licenses that customer currently need
  • Will the customer need to add more licenses in the future?
  • If Yes: How many licenses would be needed?
  • If No: Proceed to the next step.

After the information is acquired, send an email to EDU Help O365 (a1pluslicenserequest@microsoft.com) in Rave (excluding customer email address) with the following details:

  • Tenant GUID:
  • Company Name:
  • Number of existing unassigned users:
  • Number of additional licenses needed:
  • Will they need to add more licenses in the future? Yes/No
    (If yes, indicate the total number of licenses that they would need in the future.)

*Note: The standard turnaround time is about a week, please set proper expectation with the customer. Upon sending the email, EDU Help O365 may ask for additional details regarding the customer’s request.

If the request is approved, an email confirmation will be sent by EDU Help O365 and the licenses will be added on the back end and visible in Rave.

Scenario 4: A customer insists that they should be entitled to the Office 365 A1 Plus for Students or Faculty offer since they work for an academic institution.

a. Verify the organization's background as outlined in the process from this article 2818467 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Customer does not have the option to purchase academic offers or wants to remove EDU tag in the Microsoft 365 admin center (only check in http://www.microsoftvolumelicensing.com/DocumentSearch.aspx?Mode=3&DocumentTypeId=7).

b. If it's not a qualified academic institution, advise that only actual institutions can avail of the said offer and not entities working on their behalf.

c. If it's a qualified academic institution and still did not receive the offer, refer to this Article. If customer is EDU Approved, he will have to meet also the requirements for A1 Plus benefit.

If they meet the benefit, please check the Procedure for A1 Plus Support.

Scenario 5: Move A1 Plus subscription to another tenant.

This is not possible, customer needs to self-sign-up on the new tenant, after the domain is verified on the new tenant. A new escalation to AVE Support might be needed.

Scenario 6: Delete A1 Plus subscription.

This can be done by customer from Azure Portal, following this official article “Delete a directory in Azure Active Directory”.

Scenario 7: Instead of A1 Plus, users are receiving A1.
  • Engineers need to verify that domain used for self-sign-up was approved for A1 Plus.
  • Check in Student Advantage Site if the domain above was added to be approved and is Completed.
  • Escalate to AVESUP@Microsoft.com if needed. They can confirm this.
  • If yes, engineers can start collaboration with Technical Support (Identity Team) for further investigation.

Scenario 8: A1 Plus for Students or Faculty is already on tenant, but customer can’t add the other one via self-sign-up.
  • Check the domain in this list https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/ave/Lists/Student Advantage Domain List/AllItems.aspx.
  • Escalate to AVESUP@Microsoft.com if domain is not there to verify if domain is approved for Student Advantage.
    • If not, add it to the list above to be approved.
    • If yes, please check again if the domain used for self-sign up is approved for A1 Plus as well.

Scenario 9: A1 Plus for Students and/or Faculty are already on tenant but when using self-sign-up, A1 is assigned instead of A1 Plus.

This happens when the domain used for self-sign up is not approved for A1 Plus or customer is no longer approved for A1 Plus. Follow steps indicated in Scenario 8.
