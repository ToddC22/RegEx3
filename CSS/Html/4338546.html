Procedure: Surface: Device and accessory upgrades and substitutions for End of Life (EOL)

Who should use this article?

What is this article about?

This article provides handling instructions for when a customer needs to service a discontinued device or accessory, and when the Service Order Management (SOM) tool prompts you to review KB 4338546 for more information.

1. Overview

Certain devices and accessories can no longer be replaced because the item was discontinued. When a regional service center can no longer replace with the same model, we ship an alternative, this article explains these alternatives.

When you try to write an order, the SOM tool may display the following text.

Message:
"Because this model has been discontinued, we may not be able to provide like for like replacements. For more information on what is expected to ship, review KB 4338546."

5. FAQ

Q: Customer has a Special Edition Xbox controller but I can’t find the replacement offer in my SOM tool?

A: If this is not a newly released Xbox controller, the controller has been discontinued. Offer the customer a different Special Edition Xbox controller as a substitution.

Q: Where is this happening?

A: In all countries where [customer’s current device] launched officially. See Countries/Regions In-Scope column in tables above for specific country limitations.

Q: Will the upgrade/substitution include any accessories?

A: Accessories will be bundled if the previous generation of device accessories are not compatible with the newer generation such as Surface 3 to Surface Go or Xbox 360 to Xbox One.

Q: What is the warranty?

A: Same as all exchanges - 90 days or remainder of warranty, whichever is longer (or longer if customer country requires longer).

Q: What if I have USB-A accessories that aren’t compatible with the Surface Go?

A: We ask that you purchase a USB-A to USB-C adapter. (If the customer presses on the topic and is in warranty, consult a Tech Lead.)

Q: Customers who knew the previous out of warranty price are asking Microsoft why the price is increasing?

A: Due inventory constraints, Microsoft is upgrading customers to the next available version (see above table). To cover the cost of the upgrade, the out of warranty price is increased.

Q: What if the customer’s environment is unable to support an upgrade to the next version?

A: Microsoft understands that some business customers may not be able to support an upgrade to the next available version due to specific OS or hardware requirements however due to inventory constraints, Microsoft can no longer provide like for like replacements.
