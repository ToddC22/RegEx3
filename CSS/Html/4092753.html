Overview

Azure for Students (170p) is a special offer that will be available only for students that are 18 years of age or older and attend an accredited, degree-granting four-year educational institution as a full-time student or faculty in a STEM-related field. It is an addition (not a replacement) to Azure for Students Starter (formerly Imagine) subscription. This offer consists of a $100 USD credit (provided via Sponsorship benefit) that can be used for 12 months and Free Services equal to the ones available on Free Account.

Policy

Data Migration/SOT

  • There are no limitations on data migration in/out of the Azure for Student subscription. Use the process defined in TSG 2856526. 

  • Subscription Ownership Transfers(SOT) are not allowed for this offer type.

Quota Increase

Quota cannot be increased for 170p. However is the customer attaches a Payment Instrument (PI) and requests the offer to be converted to Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) or another supported offer, then a quota request may be considered. 

Offer Conversion

  • Conversions to 170p are not supported.

  • Self-serve conversions to a different Azure offer are not yet available for this offer. Customers must add a PI through the accounts portal and contact ASMS to convert the offer on their behalf. 

Pricing Details

  • This offer consists on a $100 USD credit (provided via Sponsorship) that can be used for 12 months and includes Free Services equal to the ones available on Free Account.

    • The offer is only rated in USD. No other currencies are supported.

Suspension

  • If a subscription consumes the $100 USD at any point during the 12 months, the subscription will automatically be suspended including the Free Services.

  • Once suspended, subscription can be converted to PAYG, but only with Support help. There are plans for a self-serve conversion, but no ETA.

  • If subscription has been suspended, ASMS must check for registration status in AIRS. Only if active may the subscription be re-activated. If subscription was previously connected to that registration, (meaning no SOT or offer conversion to 170P) then it will re-connect and Sponsorship team does not need to be engaged.  Only if there appears to be zero consumption of sponsorship value does Sponsorship team need to be engaged to evaluate a disconnect scenario.

  • When Azure for Students subscription is disabled, please check the activity log in CMAT and AIRS account status to get details. If the user has been disabled for Monetary Cap or End of Entitlement they should be educated and converted to Pay As You Go subscription (this can be done only by ASMS support).

  • Only if customer has moved from one registration to another, you must work with the Azure Sponsorship team to get the sub connected in AIRS prior to re-enabling the sub. In this scenario please do not re-enable without Azure Sponsorship team approval.

Subscription Disabled

If a 170P subscription is disabled and no longer wishes to use Azure, but would like a copy of their services, follow the process below.

  1. Engage your TA/PTA to review and for approval.

  2. TA/PTA will review and if required enable the subscription, and keep the Task to monitor.

  3. SE will inform the customer that their subscription will be temporarily enabled for 2 business days and on 3rd business day it will be disabled by system.

  4. If customer is unresponsive, on 3rd day TA/PTA will disable the Subscription and complete the Task.

Note: If the customer requests to be enabled for additional days in order to download their data, they must first add a PI through the accounts portal and then contact ASMS to convert the offer on their behalf.

Monthly Usage ​

  • Since this offer has a $100 USD credit provided via Sponsorship benefit as normal with Sponsorship subscriptions.

  • TA's can look up AIRS usage. 

Customer Experience and Provisioning

The customer signup experience can be found here. However, you should also note the following:  

  • This offer is available on Microsoft Account (MSA) and OrgID

  • No Credit Card needed for signup, but SMS challenge may apply​

  • To continue using Azure at the end of 12 months or after the customer has exhausted their $100 credit, whichever occurs first, customer must upgrade to a Pay-As-You-Go subscription by contacting Azure Support
