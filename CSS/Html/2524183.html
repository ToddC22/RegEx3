Summary

Who should follow this procedure?

  • Office 365 Frontline support engineers (SEs).

What is this procedure about?

  • The goal of case notes is to provide a historical record, help us organize our thoughts and notes on the issue. This will help us better understand the issue, communicate it, and bring the technical issue to resolution sooner.
  • This procedure defines the method of documenting a case’s issue, troubleshooting steps taken, and planning next steps.
  • The procedure helps SEs comply with the procedure in Case Documentation.

When should this procedure be followed?

  • This procedure must always be followed when SEs are documenting cases.

Assumptions

  • There is one term introduced by this article that all SEs should become familiar with:
    • Engineer Signature: SEs will need to sign some of their entries with a personalized signature when they update a case notes. The Procedure section provides more detail.
  • Individual policy, process and procedure (PPP) [Asset 4346155] articles such as escalation articles may instruct SEs to include in their case notes details about that issue or action in addition to what is detailed in this article’s Procedure section.

Procedure

O365 SEs should use the 4346609-Template: Summary for case notes to comply with the 4457092: Procedure: Documenting Case Notes procedure in the 4346835: Process: Commercial Case Management Standards.

Summary Template Usage Guidelines

Follow these guidelines when you use the 4346609-Template: Summary:

  • Avoid all abbreviations unless they were explained earlier in the same case notes and are considered customer friendly by both the vendor and Microsoft. When it is unclear how to continue, try to use what will be most easily read by another member of the support team.
  • Always use complete sentences.
  • Use of Bullet Point format is strongly recommended for troubleshooting notes. Use hyphens rather than special characters to distinguish each new line.
  • Every time case notes are added, the SE should copy the previous case notes and paste it into a new non-customer facing log, after which the SE will update based on the procedure that is described later in this document.
  • An Engineer Signature contains both the date the notes were added (in month/day) followed by the engineer's alias. For example:

    "12/01 v-aaa. This format maintains consistency with the customer relationship management (CRM) systems used by support."

    An Engineer Signature is required for all notes added to the Case Status / Summary and Action Plan sections.

Note: 4457149-Template: Action Plan section notes always must contain the Engineer’s Signature. These notes must be ordered with oldest notes at the top to the newest notes at the bottom of the section. These guidelines apply to any other section if notes must be added to those sections for any reason. If there are any exceptions to these guidelines, they will be documented in the corresponding terms and definitions entry later in this article.

  • If the pre-existing case notes are no longer valid or they are not reasonably comprehensible, create a new 4346609-Template: Summary as a separate note in the case. Document why the prior case notes no longer applies, and reference the date stamp for the old notes so that they can be easily referenced.
  • If a case requires creating a bug ID or an escalation to a different team, keep the 4346609-Template: Summary as the latest record in the case.

Summary Template Terms and Definitions

CUSTOMER CONTACT INFORMATION

This first section on Customer Contact Information stays at the top of the Summary Template and includes information on any administrators the SE interacts with over the life of the case. This information must also be listed in the Summary Template even when the customer contact information is already listed in the taxonomy of the case. This section contains no Engineer Signature line or header, to keep the template simple.

  • All contacts listed in this section must be entitled administrators.
  • Do not manage, enter or alter customer contact information in any ticketing system without customer consent. For auditing purpose, all contact changes in the ticketing system require case notes documentation of fully entitled administrator’s verbal or written consent.
  • If customer consent is granted, add to the Customer Contact Information section the names and, if available, the preferred telephone number and email address of each additional contact as they become involved in the case. (Information about admins letting non-admins continue phone calls with SEs should be detailed in the Engineer Assessment section of the Summary Template.)
  • In a case involving multiple administrators, use multiple lines.

Note: The format of [Administrator’s First and Last Name, email address, and telephone number] helps keep contact information for each administrator in a single line.

  • The customer’s Microsoft Online Distinguished Routing Domain (MODRD) should be spelled out completely, and will serve as a reference to the tenant being worked on for this case.  

SCOPE

  • Under a heading of Business Impact: If it is possible, try to collect and then document how the issue affects the customer’s business or operations.
  • Under a heading of Incident Statement: use a short and clear problem statement that gives a detailed description of either the specific symptom the customer is encountering, or the customer’s specific request. For example, typing "A problem with the calendar" is unacceptable. However, typing: "User Joe@Contoso.com is unable to share his default calendar to external contact using Outlook 2013 due to error: <Exact error message>" is acceptable.
    • If the customer has provided a written description of the issue as part of a Web Submission case, or in some other manner, copy this under a heading of CUSTOMER DESCRIPTION OF ISSUE.
    • Update the Incident Statement to refine the scope to be more detailed, but do not re-scope the case unless the original scope is wrong.
  • Under a heading of Scope Agreement: copy the Scope Agreement with the customer.
  • This section must be brief and to the point so that anyone looking at this template can identify the customer’s main issue.
  • This section does not require an Engineer Signature.

CASE STATUS / SUMMARY

  •  Under a heading of Completed Action(s) and Result(s):
    • This section requires documentation of all contact attempts, – if no call attempt was made on a specific date the reason should be documented. For example:
      "Was not able to contact customer during normal business hours due to call volume."
      • Include the name of the contact, and contact method i.e. phone number or Email address used.
    • Any troubleshooting notes should detail what actions were tried and what results were observed. Document if the SE asked for data and the customer could or would not provide it, or if the SE performed an action and could not collect any good data from it.
    • Pertinent [Asset 4346155] articles, including the article number, title, and link, that were found during research or used during troubleshooting, or provided to the customer.
  • Under a heading of Engineer Assessment: This is the most important part of the case. This section serves as a record to describe every customer and SE actions and the results of those actions in brief, concise and accurate notes.
    • Include Opinion, Opinion / Possible Diagnosis / key Error Messages etc. to date
    • Include all troubleshooting steps, tests run and their results, and any PowerShell cmdlets used
    • Include all solutions provided to the customer including links to [Asset 4346155] articles
    • If there are any error messages, include a brief description of the message(s).

      Note: Always collect a screen shot of any error message the customer is receiving and include exact message in writing under your Issue statement. If the exact error is not available on first contact, it should be added as soon as possible.  Once we have the exact error, it should always be typed out under the ISSUE heading, to allow us to easily search on the error. Lengthy details, such as logs and full exception error reports, should go in a separate case note entry, or the customer should provide an attachment.
    • If the customer is able to reproduce the issue on a Windows machine, use PSR.EXE (Steps Recorder) to collect screen shots of the repro steps.
    • Document any potential Risk of Data Loss
    • Include a reference to any screenshots in the case (Screenshots should be appropriately titled to make the easily located)
    • Document any contact attempts including unresponsive customers, strikes, OOFs
    • Root cause when closing case:
      • Non-Sev A/1: When you close an case, clearly and succinctly detail in a complete sentence or two what you determine is the most likely or the root cause of the issue. This statement would look like this, for example: "User did not update to latest version of Outlook."
      • Sev A/1: If you are closing a Sev A or Sev 1 case, do not try to determine the most likely or root cause. Instead, consult with your available resources TL, TM, or SME for assistance, as well as any standard processes that your support team uses for Post Incident Review (PIR), Root Cause Analysis (RCA) or post-mortems.
    • This section should include all information that is not included in any other sections
    • This section should be updated as additional relevant information is collected about the issue.
  • Under a heading of Environment:
    • Include Product / Versions / Cloud / On-Prem / Tenant
    • The names and email addresses of affected users.
    • The number of affected users.
    • Machine and client specs.
    • The time that the issue first occurred for the affected user(s).
    • Where the issue occurred. For example, Microsoft 365 admin center, Outlook Web Access (OWA), FOPE AC, or Outlook.
    • Whether the issue can be reproduced. If it can be reproduced, detail the exact steps that you must follow to do this.
    • Whether the issue occurs intermittently. If it does occur intermittently, detail the time or circumstance when it seems most likely to occur.
    • This section should be updated as additional relevant information is collected about the issue.
  • Under a heading of Logs:
    • Include Name and Location

CSS ACTION PLAN

  • The 4457149-Template: Action Plan section should cover the actions of Microsoft and the customer in the next scheduled contact.
  • Note any outstanding actions or questions for the customer. Keep including these in every written communication until they are addressed by the customer in some manner.

4457149-Template: Action Plan information should be documented by using the following template: 

·         Under the heading of Action on Microsoft:

o    Concisely detail all actions that Support will perform next in order to keep moving the case forward to resolution. For example, “Contacting the customer” is unacceptable. An example of an acceptable entry is, “Will contact the customer to deliver additional mail flow troubleshooting.” If you are waiting for the customer to respond, make sure that you detail what you are waiting for and why. For example, “Will contact customer to check status of steps I provided on November 1.”

·         Under the heading of Action on Customer:  

o    Concisely detail all actions that the customer will perform next in order to keep the case moving forward technically and narrow the technical scope of the issue. This should include clear specific detailed questions, and/or actions for the customer to respond to, in bullet point format, that will be emailed to the customer.

For example, “Will contact customer tomorrow” provides incomplete information and does nothing to communicate with the customer in a way that moves the case forward technically to narrow the scope of the issue. 

An example of an acceptable entry is:

-Does your Outlook connectivity issue occur for all users?

-If it only impacts specific users, please provide their UPN.

-Does your connectivity issue only impact specific client machines?

-Does the issue only impact internal users, or does it impact external users as well?

-Please run ExRCA from a client experiencing the issue and include the results in your reply. 

As these questions, or actions, are confirmed by the customer they should be added under the Completed Action(s) and Result(s) section of the case, and dropped from Action on Customer section of the case.

·         Under the heading of Next Contact: 

o    List the date and time of the agreed-upon next interaction between support and the customer.  This should be within the case's SLA based on severity. If the case is deferred, include the reason for deferral and when the deferral period ends.

Note:

  • The CSS Action Plan section should not be historical:
    • When you copy the Action Plan template into the latest non-customer facing log to continue documentation, make sure that there is only one Action Plan and not a history of all previous Action Plans. 
    • Any completed actions including previously recorded actions in the Action Plan should be moved to the Case Status / Summary > Completed Action(s) and Result(s) sections.
    • If you own an case or are updating an existing case that has a prior owner, make sure that you do both of the following:
      • Add a new Action Plan according to this section’s guidelines.
      • Make sure that the outcomes of the prior Action Plan have been documented in the Completed Action(s) and Result(s) section. The prior documented Action Plan section information can then be removed from the template.
