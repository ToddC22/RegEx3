Procedure: Legal: Request to access an account that belongs to the deceased

[Asset 4049349]

 

[Asset 4557567]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer

What is this article about?

This article provides instructions on how to handle requests from customers who want to gain access to an account that belongs to a next-of-kin that is deceased. The information provided here applies globally, EXCEPT for customers in Germany. For customers in Germany, follow requirements on KB 4504663 Procedure: Legal: Request to access an account that belongs to the deceased (Germany).

 

1. Overview

Some customers may contact us for assistance in hopes of gaining access to or acting against an account that belongs to a next-of-kin that is now deceased. Some examples include:

  • Accessing the Outlook.com/Hotmail.com email account.
  • Accessing the OneDrive account for photos, videos or other stored files.
  • Closing out the account or terminate subscriptions to “stop” recurring billing of subscription services such as Xbox Live, OneDrive, Skype and Microsoft 365.
  • Taking down publicly available content.
  • Accessing purchased content, such as games or movies OR transferring license rights.

For privacy and other legal reasons, we are unable to provide any information about the account in question. For more information on handling, follow the Procedures section.

2. Procedure

Note: This procedure applies to ALL Microsoft accounts.

Customer location

Advocate action

Global (excluding Germany)

Refer the customer to Accessing Outlook.com, OneDrive and other Microsoft services when someone has died.

Germany

Follow requirements on KB 4504663 Procedure: Legal: Request to access an account that belongs to the deceased (Germany).
