Guideline: Microsoft Store: Shipping and customer expectations

 [Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer

What is this article about?

This article provides instructions on setting customer expectations for shipping guidelines related to the following:

  • Product delivery status after an order has been placed through the Microsoft Store, or sales that are made from advocate purchase on behalf of (POBO) in the Membership Support Tool (MST).
  • Shipping timelines before placing an order.
  • Change a ship-to address.
    • Note: The ship-to address cannot be updated on the order after it is placed. If a wrong shipping address was selected, the order should be cancelled and a new one placed.
    • Note: A shipment can be stopped in-transit and returned to MSFT (Intercept), but the process is only used for wrong address scenarios and HOTs. For any other scenario, inform the customer that they must refuse or return the item for a refund.
  • Request an UPS shipment to be held for pick up.

1. Shipping overview

Online orders placed at the United States Microsoft Store may ship via UPS, USPS, or FedEx. All shipments are subject to the courier’s terms and conditions.

Important delivery notes:

  • What is the courier’s liability when the customer signs for a delivery? When the customer signs for delivery, they have accepted the delivery – advise the customer to not take their signature lightly. They should reject deliveries if there’s concern that contents may be damaged or missing.
    • The customer should examine the box for any signs of tampering (such as cut tape, re-taped packages, broken seals) or damage (tearing, crushing, holes, water damage, flattened corners), and feel the weight of the box. If the box is excessively light or shows signs of the above, the customer should refuse the delivery if they are concerned.
       
  • Is the customer allowed to open the box to check the contents before signing? Usually, the delivery driver does not wait for the customer to open the box and thoroughly check contents before signing as the courier needs to make other deliveries. We can request that the courier perform an investigation if contents are missing or damaged but once the customer has signed, there are some scenarios where it may be less likely the courier will close an investigation in the customer’s favor. Encourage customers to follow the above practices of inspecting the outer box for issues before offering their signature.

Where we ship:

  • The United States Microsoft Store can only ship to U.S. locations. For sales in other countries, visit one of the international online Microsoft Stores.
  • Shipping to a PO box is discouraged but may be possible if the customer follows these instructions:

Note:

US ONLY: We do not ship to personal mailboxes, or US Territories other than Puerto Rico, PO boxes in Puerto Rico or for Xbox Design Lab, even if the below conditions are met.

 

  • The Address 1 or Address 2 lines must contain one of the following variations: POBOX #, POSTALBOX #, POSTBOX #, POSTOFFICEBOX #, or BOX # (# is a placeholder for the PO box number).
  • Letters in the address may be uppercase or lowercase.
  • Any of the following separator characters between letters are allowed: . , ; : # < > or a space.
  • A full street address is required for delivery, and rural route numbers may result in a longer delivery time.
  • Microsoft Store does not permit a carrier to redirect a package to a new address after it has been shipped (this helps fight online fraud).
  • Starting on October 29, 2020, US customers may ship to an APO/FPO address. For more detail, refer to KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on Behalf of (POBO) and KB 4578369 Procedure: Purchase on behalf of (POBO).

2.2 When will customers receive their product

Physical products are shipped Monday through Friday during normal business hours. The shipping timeline is based on the shipping method selected for the purchase. For a full list of shipping timelines, see Shipping options, costs and delivery times.

Most preorders ship to arrive on the general availability (GA) date. If the GA is unknown, check the Microsoft Store website for updates. The following statuses helps to identify when the customer will receive their preorder:

  • Ships by GA: The product will begin shipping on the date of general availability and will be received based on the shipping method selected by the customer.
  • Delivers by GA: The product will ship in advance and will be delivered on the date of general availability.
  • Call Handling - Received order later than delivery estimate: The customer states that the delivery was made outside the estimated timeframe for the shipment method selected. Review the latest handling instructions in KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical Products/Software Products: Returns & Exchange.

2.4 Delivery information

The carrier will deliver only to the address provided in the order (ship to address). Address changes are not allowed, and the address provided by the customer is the address used for delivery. No other addresses will be accepted.

What this means to customers:

Note:

The carrier will not contact the customer to validate the correct address.

  • If the customer omits key address details (for example, city or postal code), the order will not be delivered. The shipment is returned to Microsoft and a refund will be issued per standard process.
  • If the customer makes a mistake on the address (for example, mix up of postal code and city) the shipment is returned to Microsoft and a refund will be issued per standard process.
  • If the postal code does not conform to the country standards (for example, space between Sweden postal code or, used in Luxembourg postal code) the shipment is returned to Microsoft and a refund will be issued per standard process.
  • If the customer is not at home, the shipment will not be handed to a third party (for example, a neighbor). This is particularly relevant if the customer’s address is in an apartment block.
    • In EMEA, UPS holds unsuccessful deliveries at an access point. Reference KB 4045781 Informational: EMEA UPS Access Point for Microsoft Store order pickups.
    • In other regions, the courier may leave the package on the customer’s porch or in the vicinity of the delivery address.
  • The CSS support team cannot change a delivery address. If the customer is not able to accept the delivery at the address on the order, refer to KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical Products/Software Products: Returns & Exchange to determine whether the shipment is eligible for intercept. If it is eligible, the shipment will be returned to Microsoft and a refund issued per standard process.
  • If the warehouse identifies an invalid address on an order, they will flag it to the Microsoft supply chain team. A block is applied to the order and the supply chain team raises the order to customer service support (CSS). The CSS team contacts the customer, informs them of the issue, cancels the order and assists in placing a new order with the correct address details. In this scenario, the customer is not provided with a make-good.

What options are available if a customer provides an incorrect address?

Option one: Check the order in MST. If the cancel button is available, cancel the order and assist the consumer in placing a new order with the correct delivery address details. The ship-to address cannot be updated on the order after it is placed and this cannot be circumvented even if the product is out of stock and not re-orderable.

Option two: If the order has already been shipped, determine whether the customer is capable of receiving the shipment at the address listed. If not, review the Package Intercept Request section of KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical Products/Software Products: Returns & Exchange.

Option three: Request a hold on the package with the carrier at the closest pickup location to the customer’s address. The customer can then visit the location at their convenience to retrieve the order. Reference public article 4000644 for instructions on how to sign up for membership services with each courier – the customer can utilize these services to access a variety of delivery options for their package.

Note:

The customer may need to arrange a hold and pick up with the carrier directly and will need to present photo ID to collect the package.

2.5 Free shipping offers

These limited time offers are valid only on online purchases from Microsoft Store for qualifying products shipped in the fifty US States and in Washington DC.

  • Free shipping offers do not apply to APO/FPO addresses or to PO boxes.
  • When free shipping is offered, it will automatically be applied at checkout and be reflected in the shopping cart.
  • Free shipping offers do not apply to previous purchases, are non-transferable, and are not redeemable for cash.

3. Order status

3.1 Order status overview

If the customer has placed an order, advocates:

  • Must verify the customer before providing any order details
  • Look up the order for the customer
  • Provide the customer with an order status
  • Educate the customer on how to view their order status online:
    • Go to store.microsoft.com
    • Go to account page
    • Locate order placed
    • Select Track Package

Return to top 

3.2 Cancellation request 

If the customer requests a physical order cancellation:

  1. Verify the customer information before providing any order details or status.
  2. Look up the order in MST.
  3. Provide the customer with the status of the order:
    • If the cancellation button is present, you can select it to initiate cancellation.
      • Inform the customer that a cancelation email will be sent shortly.
    • If the cancellation button is not present, the order cannot be cancelled.
      • Advise the customer that we are unable to cancel the order and it is being processed.
      • Inform the customer that they will get an email when their order has been shipped.
      • The customer can contact Microsoft Store support after receiving the shipping email for assistance in creating a return request for the shipment.

Note: Advocates or sites may escalate escalation requests under certain conditions, see KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical Products/Software Products: Returns & Exchange.

3.3 Tracking number not in order 

The customer may contact Microsoft support because they have not received a tracking number for their order, or the tracking number appears unused. If the customer’s carrier is UPS, you can attempt locating an alternate tracking:

  1. Access the UPS tracking tool: https://www.ups.com/track#/
  2. Under Track by Reference Number on the right side, complete the following information:
    • Shipment Reference: Enter the order number.
    • Shipment Range: Adjust the Shipment Range to cover the predicted ship date.
    • Shipper Account: Leave blank.
    • Destination Country: United States/Canada/etc.
    • Destination Zip Code: Access the order and find the zip code located under Ship to Address.
  3. Once the information has been entered, select Track to search for alternate tracking numbers.
    • If you find a new tracking number: Check the tracking number and the details to ensure it is valid. Provide the tracking number to the customer and close the case.
    • If there is no alternate tracking number, or the alternate tracking number shows no movement: Escalate to FT4 for investigation. Follow the instructions in KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical Products/Software Products: Returns & Exchange.

4. Return to sender

A shipment in transit may be refused by the customer or redirected back to the sender (Microsoft) if any issues are identified with the shipment or the order. Return to sender orders that have not been received by the customer are considered:

  • Return to sender (Standard): The order may retain the same shipping information or have alternate tracking details attached to the initial shipment and will be returned to the distribution warehouse. These order can be refunded prior to Microsoft receiving the return shipment as long the customer was never in possession of the package.

    Note: The Shipment Progress in the courier’s tracking details will state “Service disruption occurred/The package will be returned to the sender”, “The customer refused the shipment/The package will be returned to the sender” or similar language.
  • Return to sender (Sender initiated): Microsoft may request that a shipment be intercepted in transit if an issue is detected with the order, or with the customer’s account. The order may retain the same shipping information or have alternate tracking details attached to the initial shipment and will be returned to the distribution warehouse. These orders can be refunded prior to Microsoft receiving the return shipment as long as the customer was never in possession of the package.

    Note: Intercept requests are only processed by Supply Chain, CP Fraud, or by FT4 as escalations. Escalations are only due to:
    • Order placed with the incorrect shipping address.
    • EXEC HOT/Quick Assist Case.

      Note: The Shipment Progress in the courier’s tracking details will state “Sender Requested return/The package will be returned to the sender” or similar language.
  • Return to sender (Customer received package): The recipient has five business days to refuse the package only if the package is unopened and not tampered with. The customer can call the carrier to request a pickup or drop the package off at the carrier’s location for a return without charge. The package must be received at the Regional Return Operations Center and be processed prior to a refund being issued.
    • Note: Opened packages must be returned through the standard return process.
