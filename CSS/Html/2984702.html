Customer receives the following email is received after a security info replacement is requested:

Microsoft account
Security info replacement

Someone started a process to replace all of the security info for the Microsoft account <account name>.

[Asset 4521635]

Or, the following message appears when a customer tries to access Security and Password in their Microsoft account settings:
 
Your security info change is still pending

[Asset 4521636]

2. Why is this happening

When a customer believes they have lost access to all their security info, or if that info is not valid, there is an option available to replace all security info associated with an account. However, to deter someone else from taking over an account and locking customers out completely, this replacement process takes 30 days to complete.

3. Customer scenarios

3.1 I don't remember requesting this

If the customer does not remember requesting to have their security info replaced it may be a sign that someone is trying to take over their account. Here's what to do:
 
1. Make sure the customer didn't request a security info change.

Sometimes customers forget doing this or don't associate the process of changing security info with this notification.

Ask if they recently had problems receiving a security code, were asked to verify their account info, or changed phone numbers/email addresses.

If they did then they may have changed their security info.
2. Make sure the notification is for the customer's account. If the account mentioned does not belong to the customer, it might have been accidentally added by someone else as an alternate contact for their account.

If the account is the customers and:

  • They received an email, skip to step 4.a.
  • They received a text message, skip to step 4.b.
  • They are at a prompt after sign-in, skip to step 4.c.

If it is not the customer's account and:

  • They received an email, continue with step 3.a.
  • They received a text message, skip to step 3.b.
3.a. If the customer is looking at an email message, have them look at the bottom of the email and select where it says "If you don't recognize the Microsoft account <account name> you can click here to remove your email address from that account".

This will remove their email address from that accounts security info and you should be able to end the call.		 [Asset 4521637]
3.b. If the customer received a text message about an account that they don't own.

Inform the customer that the owner of that account probably entered their phone number by mistake, and they can safely ignore the messages.

Once the replacement is complete, the messages will likely end. If the customer has no other questions, you should be able to end the call.
4.a. If the customer is responding to an email notification, have the customer follow the instructions near the bottom of the email to "Click here and we'll help you protect this account". [Asset 4521638]
4.b. If the customer is responding to a text message on their phone, have the customer select the link at the bottom of the message to https://account.live.com/Proofs/Manage. [Asset 4521639]
4.c. If the customer is responding to a prompt in Windows or on the web, they can do one of three things:
  1. If a compromise is suspected, select let us know. This will cancel the request and prompt the customer to change their password.
  2. If the customer regained access to their original security info, select cancel this request and the request will be cancelled without prompting for a password change.
  3. If the customer did request a security info replacement, select Next, which will usually put them back where they were before the attempt to access secure info and you can stop here.
 [Asset 4521640]
5. The customer will be prompted to verify their account by selecting one of their masked pieces of original security info (phone or email) and then filling in the masks with the correct info.

Once the info is filled in, select Next. (Phone example shown here.)		 [Asset 4521641]
6. The customer will receive a security code to the email address or phone number they selected above (phone shown here). [Asset 4521642]
7. Ask the customer to copy the security code from the email or text message to the Enter your security code prompt. Once finished, select Next. [Asset 4521643]
8. (Optional) If the customer had a recovery code, they’ll receive a notice that their recovery code has changed. Customers should copy their recovery code to a safe place before selecting Next.

Note: This prompt will not appear if the customer never had a recovery code configured for their account.		 [Asset 4521644]
9. If the customer indicated that they suspect this was an attempt at unauthorized access by someone else, they will be prompted to change their password to secure their account. [Asset 4482320]
10. The customer will be informed that the cancellation is completed, and they should be able to access their account normally. [Asset 4482321]
11. The customer will also receive a notification via email that the security info replacement was cancelled.

3.2 Why does this take 30 days

The 30-day waiting period keeps someone else from changing an account’s security information to completely take over that account in case the change was made by someone other than the real account owner. This waiting period gives the real account owner enough time to be alerted of the change and, if necessary, to cancel it.

  • Public content: Why does resetting my security information take 30 days?

If the customer requested security info replacement and has waited 30 days, but the new one after the wait period did not replace the old security information, engage ACA. For more details, refer to section 2.2 in KB 817557 Guidelines: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement.

Return to top

3.3 Will I be locked out for 30 days

No, the customer should be able to access most of their Microsoft services as usual during the 30-day waiting period. However, secured info that would normally require a security code will be unavailable during this time. These restricted activities include:

  • Accessing and changing security info and passwords at account.microsoft.com.

Return to top

3.4 Can you bypass this for me

No, once the process to replace security info has begun, the customer must wait 30-days for it to complete unless the customer regains access to their original security info and cancels it themselves. There is no other way to bypass this waiting period.

If the customer is absolutely certain that they have regained access to their original security info, they can cancel the security info replacement process by following the steps outlined in section 3.1.
