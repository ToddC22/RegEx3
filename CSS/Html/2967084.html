What is this article about?

When a customer is having a computer problem, you might want to connect to their machine using Remote Assist Tools, such as Quick Assist and LogMeIn (LMI). In this article, you'll find the Remote Assist Login guidelines and steps to reconnect customers back to chat after a reboot.

1. What is the Remote Assistance Tool?

This is a tool that enables you to remotely access and take control over your customer’s PC. This remote access is used when the customer is unable to troubleshoot to resolve technical issue.

There are two ways to access customers' PCs remotely, selecting the appropriate one depends on the customer’s Windows version:

Note: Quick Assist is the tool of choice when using Remote Assistance for Windows 10 RS 1 or later.

2. Quick Assist and LogMeIn

The remote assistance tool can be very useful when you are assisting a customer. However, it’s important that you only use the tool when it’s appropriate to do so. Use your judgement to decide if a remote assistance tool will save you and the customer time and effort.

  • Never view, open, delete, add, copy, or edit passwords, files or documents during remote assistance which are not specifically related to fixing the customer’s issue.
  • Never use Remote Assistance when the customer's personal information could be exposed, specifically during a purchase experience where payment information could be exposed.
  • Do not use Remote Assistance to manipulate the chat survey or to submit input to the survey form. This is a zero-tolerance policy, and unethical. The survey form is design for customers to share their feedback about their support experience. Never interact with this survey form.
  • Do not use Remote Assistance if the customer is no longer connected via phone or chat. Offline usage of LMI or remote assistance is prohibited if you are not communicating with the customer via phone or chat tool (Communication Console). In addition, always communicate what you are doing on the customer PC while you are in a remote session.
  • Don't transfer files during Remote Assistance. If a large file must be downloaded, help the customer download it from the internet, not from your computer or Remote Assistance Tool.
  • If a customer is not at their machine, immediately pause the sharing session to ensure you are only accessing areas of the file system required to troubleshoot the issue at hand.
  • If a customer moves away from their machine for an extended period close the sharing session completely. This minimizes legal risks to Microsoft and assures our customers that we are not making any unauthorized changes to the system nor accessing any sensitive materials without the customer’s knowledge.
  • Immediately end the sharing session once the troubleshooting has concluded regardless of tasks still pending to resolve the case.
  • Remote assistance tool is not permitted for the following:
    • Paid offer declined: For a scenario where a paid service is required, we don’t expect any troubleshooting before the offer has been accepted. Therefore, remote assistance is not expected or permitted.
    • Redirect​ Windows and Office Technical Support only (excludes Surface): Quick Solution.

 

  1. Consider using a remote assistance tool for the following scenario types:
  • Paid scenarios (for example, one time paid, Assure, or Microsoft 365)
  • Download, install, and activate.
    • Do not use a remote assistance tool for other case types where troubleshooting or demonstrations are not required.
  1. Set expectations and educate the customer. Take the time to explain what Remote Assistance is and how you can use it to help resolve their issue. Do not start using Remote Assistance tool without explaining. Take the time to explain what you are about to do and how this tool can help the customer.
  • “Log me in is a secured remote channel used by Microsoft. This will allow us to view and control your computer for troubleshooting.”
  1. Confirm customers know that if they ever feel uncomfortable about what you are seeing or doing on their computer, they can stop sharing or close the program immediately.
  • “At any point if you feel uncomfortable, you can end the remote session by selecting the red X button.”
  • "Once we've started our session, I will have the ability to view your desktop. Please close any windows that may contain sensitive data."
  1. Confirm the customer has read and agreed to receive remote assistance support from Microsoft at support.microsoft.com/help before proceeding. Tell the customer:
  • "Please take a moment to review the terms on this page. If you accept the terms, please select I accept."
  1. Ask the customer to close any open applications that they don't want you to see.
  2. Use the Pause button if there is a possibility that a customer's personal data may become viewable.
  3. Continuously educate and engage the customer, ask for confirmation before taking each action on a customer's device. If you must obtain system information, ask the customer for approval before collecting any data.
  4. When ending a remote session, make sure to inform the customer the remote session is ending at that moment and that you no longer have remote access to the customer’s device after the remote session has ended.
  • Problems using Remote Assistance Tool: If you are having problems launching either Quick Assist or LogMeIn tool on a customer's computer, do not use it and go back to troubleshooting without it.
  • If at any point you discover content that is not appropriate, follow the instructions in KB 2626212 Procedure: Legal: Discovery of what may depict child pornography during remote assistance.
