1. General information

1.1 What are AFO and AFOLB?

Activation for Office (AFO) is a mechanism that digitally entitles a Windows computer with a Microsoft 365 subscription service. AFO offers generally apply to a trial which is essentially a one month offer to try Microsoft 365 Family. Customers can also purchase Office if they choose.

Activation for Office late binding (AFOLB) allows OEMs to bind or link Microsoft services like Office to a Windows key anytime from the point fulfillment to after user activation. AFOLB can include an offer for a one-year subscription to Microsoft Family or Personal, or a perpetual SKU license like Home & Student.

In either case, the customer's experience is that they see the Office application icons in the Windows Start screen. This may lead them to think that they received a full copy of Office with their purchase of a new PC. This is correct if the PC has AFO late binding but incorrect if it has AFO trial.

﻿1.2 How to identify an AFO device

  1. A quick way to identify if the user has an AFO device with or without an offering is to have the customer open one of the pre-installed Office apps for the first time.
  2. If the customer sees the You’ve got Office message, Office comes installed as either a one-year Family or Personal subscription. 
  3. After the customer signs-in to or creates a Microsoft account, the product attaches to the Microsoft account. This allows Office to be reinstalled and managed without a product key.

  4. If the customer has the Let’s get started message, the pre-installed Office on the device is a trial version. It has not been associated with their Microsoft account.

  • Note: Once the customer redeems Office perpetual or a Microsoft 365 subscription, the procedure for installing on a different PC is the same. They go to their account.microsoft.com (AMC) page and follow the instructions to Download and install or reinstall Microsoft 365 or Office 2019 on a PC or Mac. For perpetual products, follow the guidance in Activate Office using the Activation wizard.

1.5 Customer support tips for AFO issues

Use your troubleshooting skills to determine the best way to support the customer’s Office issue.

Do

  • Provide correct troubleshooting, Fix-it solutions, and known issue solutions.
  • Provide correct solutions for Solve scenarios as well as the Office session ID located in File>Account>About Word and escalate to Tier 2 as needed.
  • Provide Microsoft account troubleshooting as a part of the Out of box experience (OOBE) setup.
  • Check that the customer activated Windows.
  • Make sure to re-open the Office app if the customer hasn’t done so recently.

Don’t

  • Ask the customer about product Key or PIN information on AFO devices. No physical MPI (Microsoft product identifiers) card is bundled, or PIN is entitled with Windows DPK (digital product keys) on system board.
  • Redirect customers to OEMs (PC Manufacturers). Provide the right troubleshooting and escalate the issue if needed.
  • Once confirmed that Office is pre-installed on the device - Don't navigate to Apps & Features in Settings and select Modify. The Modify button is to troubleshoot Office application-and not for installing it.

1.6 Verifying proof of purchase

When a customer contacts support for help with pre-installed Office on an OEM machine, CSS advocates need to check the proof of purchase (PoP) before issuing any ORST replacement token.

See the proof of purchase in section KB 3203023 Procedure: Office: Office Solve scenarios (Goodwill version) (section 1.5). If you have any questions/difficulties in verifying the PoP/invoice shared by the customer, reach out to your respective TA.

  • If the customer is in the US
    • The device’s invoice must contain the Office purchase information.
    • If Office isn’t on the invoice, have the customer check if there’s an Office label on the device. Have them take a photo and upload using DTM.
  • If the customer is outside the US
    • The device’s invoice must contain the Office purchase information.
    • If Office isn’t on the invoice, have the customer check if there’s an Office label on the device. Have them take a photo and upload using DTM.
    • If neither invoice nor device label exist, ask the customer whether there’s Office information on the device package.
      • Only Lenovo China pastes the Office label on external packaging and device.
  • Dell Support Assist Service Tag Verification

Customers who purchased Dell can get receive an invoice:

  1. Visit the Dell Support webpage: https://support.dell.com.
  2. Choose Order Support > Access your Order Status Online – Enter the order number to download the invoice.
    Expand to see image

    [Asset 4573909]

  3. Once they have the invoice from the Dell Support page, verify it with the PoP provided by the customer. You need to specifically check for an Office /SKU listed in the invoice/PoP.
  4. Initiate a remote session on the customer’s PC.
  5. Download the Detect my Dell PC/Support Assist app from the Dell support page: https://www.dell.com/support/home/us/en/19/Eula/scan?sourcePage=J&scanType=TMC&loadSection=N&AppName=drivers&app=drivers.
  6. Run the app and verify the service tag.
  7. Also, verify the Service Tag from Command prompt – As an admin, see https://www.dell.com/support/article/us/en/19/sln268357/how-to-locate-system-service-tag-using-command-prompt?lang=en for more information.
  8. Once the PoP and service tag are verified, you may proceed in processing a Solve. See KB 3203023 Procedure: Office: Office Solve scenarios (Goodwill version).
  • Lenovo China
  1. Validate the manufacturing date on the serial number (SN) tag. 
  2. Provide an email alias (bpocuars@wicresoft.com) to Lenovo for them to submit a token replacement request with PoP.
  3. For the devices that Lenovo can look up in their system, Lenovo provides system screen capture and the customer’s Microsoft account.
  4. If the devices aren’t detected in Lenovo’s system, Lenovo provides the devices’ windows PKID and the customer’s Microsoft account. You send an email to msoemops@microsoft.com to check bundling status. This alias is our operation helpdesk, they can check in the backend system with Windows PKID to provide office bundling status (office version, bundling date, redeemed or not). Normally it is subject to a one business day resolution cycle.
  5. If the customer contacts Microsoft directly but can’t provide PoP, ask for the customer’s device serial number and send an email to Lenovo: Bin Lu (lubin4@lenovo.com) and Wei He (heweic@lenovo.com). The wait time is one business day.
    • Note on Chinese invoice title: A personal title isn’t required for customers if we have verified the device bundling status using the device serial number with operations following the process above. Otherwise check the device labels and external packaging for validations.

2. Errors and known issues

The customer purchases an Office license with their OEM PC and is unable to activate the Office product on the OEM device, the first time, immediately after purchase.
  1. Verify the proof of purchase to confirm the customer has purchased Office as part of their OEM PC.
  2. Determine if Office is installed on the PC, when you open the application it should show an Activate window.
  3. If the Try, Buy or Enter Product Key window is displayed instead:
    1. Run the Activation Fix-It troubleshooter and try opening the Office application again.
    2. If it still shows the same window, there could be a delay in the binding data on the OEM PC.
    3. Ask the customer to wait for two to three days and try again.

If you see the customer has Office 365 trial installed on the PC during troubleshooting by an OEM tech or by the customer, the Try option was selected:

  1. Uninstall the trial, install Office using the direct link, run the troubleshooter and try opening the Office application again.
  2. If the Try, Buy, or Enter product key window is shown ask the customer to try again in two to three days.

If Office still doesn't show the Activate option after waiting for two to three days, provide a replacement per KB 3203023 Procedure: Office: Office Solve scenarios (Goodwill version).

  • Add detailed notes to the case including the issue description and error message.

The customer can activate their Office product on the device the first time after purchase, but it’s a different version of Office than they ordered.
  1. Verify the proof of purchase to confirm customer has purchased Office as part of the OEM PC.
  2. Determine if the version of Office is different than the version mentioned in the proof or purchase versus accounts.microsoft.com.
  3. Determine if the OEM PC shows Office associated with the same Microsoft account.
  4. Update Office using Update now to ensure all important updates are applied. Run the activation troubleshooter. Check if the SKU has changed.
  5. If the issue persists, proceed with a Solve scenario per KB 3203023 Procedure: Office: Office Solve scenarios (Goodwill version).
    • Add detailed notes to the case capturing Issue description and error message.

Known Issue (by design): SKU Conversion where customers see Activation required (OPK prior to 2004) or Download Pending (OPK 2004) when they see the licensed product does not match the customers’ purchased product.

This is by design refer to the SKU conversion section above for more information.

Sorry, couldn't set up Office error with code 0xc004f200 when activating Office.

See An error occurs when you activate pre-installed Office on a new PC (0xC004F200).

Note: This is the most common AFO issue. It occurs if customers have not activated Windows then try to activate Office. The error dialog the customer receives links to this content (include Fix-it ﻿﻿﻿Troubleshooter tool﻿﻿﻿).

OPK 16.2.1 Pre-installed image doesn't include Outlook, Access, or Publisher.

See Can't find Outlook, Access, or Publisher apps on a new PC that included Office.

Note: These apps are missing from some images. Once customers activate AFO, an update runs when they start any app (or let Office update in the background) and the remaining apps are installed.

Apps missing after Push Button Reset (PBR).

See Can’t find Outlook, Access, or Publisher apps on a new PC that included Office direct the customer to their My Account page to reinstall.

The customer chose the wrong option, such as selecting Continue but had AFO binding or closed the Try, Buy, or Activate window without choosing an option.

The solution is to close the app and restart it.

See Office apps stop working if you close "Let's get started" screen immediately.

Office apps immediately stopped working after closing the Let's get started screen.

The solution is to close the app and restart it.

See Office apps stop working if you close "Let's get started" screen immediately.

The flow hangs when the customer attempts to redeem Office.

The solution is to close all browsers and the office app, then restart setup.

See Your new computer freezes while trying to redeem Office 365 or Office 2019.

Cannot start Office apps after Microsoft 365 or Office 2019 activation.

The solution is to re-install Office from account.microsoft.com.

See Cannot start Office apps after Office 365 or Office 2019 activation.

The Let's get started screen disappears on the first start of PowerPoint.

The solution is to open a different Office app (such as Word or Excel), and select one of the options on the Let's get started screen.

Office displays in English instead of Chinese or Serbian.

The solution is to change the display language.

See Office displays in English instead of Chinese or Serbian.

The customer purchased Office with a PC (AFO), but when they boot an Office app, they see the Try, Buy, or Activate window instead of just Activate.

The binding report that ties the Windows key to the Office key has not been sent to Microsoft by the OEM, or Microsoft has not yet processed the binding.

Solution: Ask the customer to try redeeming again later.

To escalate: Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) need to gather some information for the product group.

Use KIT to gather:

  • Identify Windows PKID (13 digits) by Product Key (5x5) or PID
    PKID = PKGROUPID (4 digits) + CHANNELSEQUENCE (9 digits).
  • Identify Windows key status and activation history.

Use PIN tool:

  • Identify office Binding information by Windows PKID (13 digits).
  • Identify both Windows and Office activation/redeem status.
  • Tips: It is possible to input multiple PKIDs by using a comma at one query and saving as XSL.

Alternately: Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) can work with OEM partners (support/service) to get this information, then work with OEM ops PM and OPS support (MSOEMops):

  • Unit serial numbers (service tags)
  • Product Key or Product ID
  • PKID (if possible, from their Operation or Factory team)
  • PBR submission status (if possible, from their Operation or Factory team)

The Let’s try that again screen populates when trying to set up Office.

Capture the screenshot and the customer’s Microsoft account for escalation, as well as the Office session ID located in File > Account > About Word.
