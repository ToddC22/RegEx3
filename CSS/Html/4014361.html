﻿1. What's New?﻿

Academic License Sales

Heads-up - As a part of AEP retirement, Microsoft will introduce an automated validation of all new Education customer account submissions to verify customer eligibility, starting December 20, 2019. Submissions for new Education customer accounts will be verified automatically, informing the partner of whether the submission was accepted or rejected.

New functionality notice: Partners now can correct customer account information and resubmit for vetting for cases where the customer was denied. For full details see step-by-step instructions on how to do this in the updated "Creating an education customer in Partner Center guide".

This was also communicated to partners via Partner Center Announcement. Please see it below:

As part of our continued commitment to compliant business practices for customers and partners, Microsoft introduced an automated validation of all new Education customer account submissions to verify customer eligibility. Since we launched the automated validation capability, we have noticed an increasing number of vetting issues because incorrect or invalid customers data was entered. To help streamline the process and minimize any delays in the vetting process, please follow the instructions below:

  • To reduce any delays in the investigation of vetting failures for legitimate entries, please ensure to enter customer data that is as accurate and as complete as possible. Do not enter in test/dummy data.
  • Review the Creating an education customer in Partner Center guide and Add new customer page documenting what is required when creating a new education customer in Partner Center. Both be found in the resource gallery below.

NOTE: The Creating an education customer in Partner Center guide now includes step-by-step instructions for how to correct customer information and resubmit if the customer has failed vetting.

  • For more details, please review the Partner Center announcement and the Partner FAQ attached.

The Authorized Education Partner (AEP) program, where partners get certified via training to transact Academic licenses to education customers, is discontinued as of October 1, 2019. This coincides with the distribution of the updated Microsoft Partner Agreements (MPA) being sent to partners and distributors for signature on the same day.

As a result of this retirement:

  • The AEP certification is no longer required for resellers
  • Distributors will no longer need to check for reseller AEP credentials.

All resellers and distributors are still required to confirm customer eligibility as defined by the Microsoft Qualified Education Users documentation, for academic purchases. They may ONLY transact academically-priced licenses to academic institutions as defined in the documentation.

For more details on the AEP retirement and for an update on the fundamentals of reselling academic licenses, please refer to this deck.

Information and Support

If frontline receive cases where the CUSTOMER needs to be tagged for Education and they were not approved, please follow the below steps:

NOTE: For LATAM Frontline advocates where Tier 1.5 exists, frontline advocate should escalate to Tier 1.5 to complete.

1. Advocate requests proof from Partner that their customer is entitled to EDU pricing.

Note: In script include link to QEU ( Qualified Education User) - (https://www.microsoftvolumelicensing.com/DocumentSearch.aspx?Mode=3&DocumentTypeId=7)

2. Upon receipt of proof the advocate should attach it to the case in CAP.

3. Advocate escalates to OneVet Support Requests - AVESUP@microsoft.com via email, using the below template:

Subject line:

Partner Center CATS: Tenant: ENTER TENANT ID. Customer: ENTER ENTITY NAME EDU validation

Content of email:

Hello,

Could you validate if the following customer is registered as an EDU?

Customer Name: ENTER ENTITY NAME

Customer Address: ENTER ENTITY MAILING ADDRESS

Segment: K-12 OR Higher Education

Entity website url: (optional but Important)

Customer contact email: (optional)

<Please let us know if you know this school or if you have done some research to make sure this is a school>

Thank you,

In addition we need to add <submit any documentation as proof that the customer is eligible for Education based on the eligibility definition for your region: https://www.microsoftvolumelicensing.com/DocumentSearch.aspx?Mode=3&DocumentTypeId=7>

Feel free to add any other information in the content of the email you think is relevant, or to help you track the request.

4. AVESUP will confirm that Customer should either be EDU or Not back to Advocate.

Note: The AVESUP SLA to reply is 2 business days.

5. If AVESUP confirms Customer is indeed EDU, advocate should update CMAT (steps below).

6. If AVESUP confirms Customer is NOT EDU, advocate should inform Partner that customer has failed secondary review and is not EDU.

Tagging customers for EDU in CMAT:

1. Search for the customer in CMAT. Under the “Account” tab, scroll down to TAGS (QUALIFICATIONS) and click on “Manage”.

[Asset 4538861]

2. The below Pop-up window will appear. Click on “Add a qualification”, then select EDU and Approved from the drop-down lists. Add the SR# in the comments, and click on next page ([Asset 4538862] ).

[Asset 4538863]

3. Fill in the SR number and click Ok.

[Asset 4538864]

4. Once the agent clicks on OK, it should be visible in CMAT.

[Asset 4538865]

End of process.

Call to action for advocates - For Sandbox accounts advocates must manually add the EDU tagging in CMAT as described in the steps above.
