ASMS – Azure Administrator Roles – Adding and Managing

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This article provides an overview of each of the Administrator types, and their roles and responsibilities. This article also provides instructions on how to change an Account Administrator (for both interior and exterior customers), and directions on how to add an Account Delegate.
Applies To: All Azure Microsoft Online Service Plan (MSOP) accounts but does not include Enterprise Agreement (EA) accounts.

Policy

Account Administrator: Account Center

  • There can be only one Account Administrator.
    • This role is established during account setup.
  • They can manage the following:
    • Subscriptions.
    • Payment instruments.
    • Offer types.
    • Adding new subscription purchases.
    • Adding Service Administrators and Co-Administrators.
  • They work in the Account Center.
  • They can see the subscription’s billing details in the Account Portal but cannot see details about any running services in the Ibiza Portal.

Account Delegate: Commerce Portal

Note: Only applicable to MINT accounts.

  • Only available for Microsoft Accounts.
  • There can be only one Account Delegate, which is established by the Account Administrator.
  • They work in the Commerce Portal.
  • They have the same capabilities as the Account Administrator, except that they cannot change the Account Administrator.

Service Administrator: Ibiza Portal

  • There can be only one Service Administrator, which is established by the Account Administrator.
  • They can manage all running services internal to the subscription.
    • Example: Adding or deleting virtual machines, websites, SQL databases, etc.
  • They work in the Ibiza Portal.
  • They can see details about any running services in the Ibiza Portal but cannot see the subscription’s billing details in the Account Portal.

Subscription Co-administrator: Ibiza Portal

  • There can be multiple Subscription Co-administrators (up to 200.) They are established by the Service Administrator.
  • They work in the Ibiza Portal.
  • They have the same capabilities as the Service Administrator.

Note: Azure in Open (AIO) and Customer Solution Provider (CSP) accounts do not support co-administrators. Users can be added to role-based access control (RBAC) but cannot have their roles set to be co-administrators.

Support Scenarios

Change the Service Administrator or Co-administrator

The Service Administrator (SA) can only change Co-administrators in the Ibiza Portal. 
ASMS Engineers do not have the ability to update or change these roles. 

In some cases, the Account Administrator (AA) can change both roles in the Ibiza Portal. In cases where the AA does not have the proper permissions to do this, the AA will need to add themself as a SA in order to change these roles. To do so, the AA should perform the following steps:

  1. Sign in to Account Center Portal as the AA.
  2. Select a subscription.
  3. On the right side, click Edit subscription details.
  4. In the SERVICE ADMINISTRATOR field, enter the email address of the new Service Administrator to be added (in this case, the AA).
  5. Click the checkmark to save the change.

If your customer needs additional guidance on how to change these roles, you can link them to the (public facing) Add or change Azure subscription administrators documentation.

Update the Service Administrator or Co-Administrator for Internal (AIRS) Subscriptions

This request is a self-serve action and can be completed by either the Service Administrator or the Co-administrator, using the steps below:

  1. Log into the Azure Portal.
  2. Click on Cost Management + Billing in the left blade.
  3. Click on the line item with your subscription.
    (This will open the Overview for your subscription.)
  4. On the Subscription blade, click on Properties.
  5. Click the [Asset 4342580] Service Admin button.
  6. Enter the email of the user that you want to set as a Service Administrator.
  7. Click OK.

Change the Account Administrator

  • Customers with OMS accounts will need to complete a Subscription Ownership Transfer. Refer to TSG 3040383 for instructions on how to complete the transfer.
  • For customers with MINT accounts, you can reassign the Account Administrator as follows:

Note: After the migration from MINT to OMS is completed and all accounts are in OMS this functionality will no longer be available.
Warning: The original Account Administrator will automatically get demoted to an Account Delegate.

    1. In CMAT, pull up the account by the Account Administrator’s email address.
    2. From the Home > Customer Profile page, click the Account Admins Manage hyperlink.
    3. The New Account Admin field will be pre-populated.
    4. Enter the new account Administrator ID (Microsoft Account (MSA) or Organization ID) and enter any relevant comments in the provided Comments field.
    5. Click the next arrow button.
    6. Review your changes and click the checkmark button to save your changes.
