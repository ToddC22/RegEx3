What is this article about?

This article details how to pay for Microsoft Advertisings including accepted payment methods, payment types (prepay and postpay), and how to set up a payment method on an account.

Payment methods are entered at the advertiser level, and then individually associated with each account. The number of accounts that can be entered at the advertiser level depends on the shell type, but typically, one payment instrument can be associated to up to five active accounts at a time. To add and associate more cards to accounts, qualify the customer for a Service level/Shell Type upgrade as needed.

Accounts are only eligible to go live once a valid payment instrument is associated with the account.

1.1 Direct debit payment options

SEPA

SEPA is a payment integration initiative of the European Union for the simplification of bank transfers denominated in Euro. SEPA enables customers to make cashless Euro payments using a single bank account and a single set of payment instruments. SEPA is available as a payment instrument (PI) for advertisers in Microsoft Advertising with a German billing address. Customers can reference directions on how to add SEPA as a payment instrument in the help content: Paying with SEPA Direct Debit.

To add SEPA as a payment instrument:

  1. From the Payment Methods tab, select ADD SEPA Direct Debit. 
  2. Enter the SEPA direct debit account name.
  3. Enter the bank account details (advertisers will be informed on this page that they are agreeing to Microsoft to debiting the account).
  4. With the migration from ELV direct debit to SEPA, the Account Number is replaced by IMicrosoft AdvertisingN and the Sort Code is replaced by BIC.
  5. Complete the Billing Address information.
  6. Select Next to continue.
  7. Review the bank account verification instructions.
  8. SEPA PI remains in Pending Validation status until the verification steps are complete.
  9. Complete the bank account verification steps (confirm the deposit amount or three-digit code on the customer’s bank statement and verify in the Microsoft Advertising Payment method tab). If the micro deposit does not appear in the customer’s bank account within the specified timeframe*, the customer will be required to remove the SEPA PI and re-add it in order to initiate another PICV challenge. If an advertiser fails many times within a short period, their SEPA PI may be blocked from being added back. If this occurs, escalate to Ad Support.

Note: SEPA will only be available for association to prepay EUR accounts; otherwise, SEPA will be greyed out. Confirm the payment option (prepay or postpay) in the Account List as needed.

SEPA must have made at least one successful payment on the first associated account to be added to other accounts in the customer shell.

*SEPA payments and refunds will be processed similar to credit card payments; however, the settlement period will be slightly longer (up to five business days for initial payments, up to two business days for subsequent payments). Payments submitted after 8 PM Central European Time (UTC+1) will be processed starting the following business day. Payments cannot be canceled once initiated. Payments cannot be canceled once initiated. For questions or problems related to SEPA direct debit transactions, the customer will need to contact their banking institution.

The prepaid balance on the account will not increase until after the payment has settled. Advertisers using the prepay billing option with credit cards or direct debit (SEPA) have the option of automatically recharging their payment instrument when it's used 80 percent of its budget. The auto-recharge feature helps protect advertisers' campaigns from unplanned pauses due to depleted budget. To set up this functionality, advertisers can select the Accounts & Billing page and then the Details tab. Select the link to set up Auto-Recharge and select the recharge amount.

Note: There is one automated re-try for failed SEPA payment attempts.

1.2 New agency customers trying to add/set up a payment method

Agency-managed advertisers are unable to add a payment method to new accounts if they are set up as Sold-To only. The following error message is displayed when the advertiser attempts to create a new account.

"Microsoft Advertising has encountered an error and is unable to continue with your selected action. Select Support at the bottom of any Microsoft Advertising page and provide the Microsoft Advertising Support Team with the details of the problem."

For a Sold-To advertiser to add a payment method to their account, a request to change their SAP from Sold-To to No-Hold should be submitted to WW Online Credit Services>WOCS Credit Services (UCMT). Note: Global Financial Services (GFS) is also known as Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS).

Boleto

  • Advertisers can add a Boleto Bancário payment method at any time, but you can only use Boleto Bancário if your billing address is in Brazil and the account currency is the Brazilian Real. This payment option will only show up in the UI for customers is already set to a Brazilian billing address and the currency is set to Brazilian Real.
  • Each Boleto Bancário payment slip can only be used to make a single payment to your Microsoft Advertising account. Every time you want to fund your account with a Boleto, you'll need to create a new one.
  • Boleto Bancário payments can be made at a bank, post office, lottery agent, and some supermarkets.
  • Payments can take up to three banking days to reach Microsoft Advertising. If the payment is entered over the weekend, it will take longer than three days.
  • When the customer selects make a payment, they will be prompted to confirm payment information. In Payment and Estimated Taxes, enter the desired amount, then select the ‘Pay Now button to finish the transaction. Note that the Estimate Taxes can be disregarded.
  • Funding the account begins by returning to the Available Payment Methods screen and selecting the option to add funds associated with this payment instrument. Note that the same add funds link will be available for all other payment methods within the Available Payment Methods section.
  • There is a limit to how much an advertiser can apply to their account. See section 2 for more details.

1.3 Conversion fees and authorization charges

Conversion fees: The currency in which the customer is billed is the currency in which Microsoft will send the request for payment to the advertiser's bank. Advertisers should ensure that their financial institutions accept international currency transactions; most banks in the US will charge anywhere from 1-5% of the transaction amount in conversion fees. Advertisers should always confer with their banks to confirm acceptance of charges in foreign currencies, and to assess if they will be charged any conversion fees. Conversion fees are assessed by the advertiser’s bank, and Microsoft does not have insight into these charges. We can refund these charges if the advertiser contacts us with this request.

For a quick reference on currency conversions, search Bing for currency converter and the calculation tool is built right into the search engine results page.

Important: You cannot change the currency settings of an account after you set it up; however, you can create a new account that uses a different currency setting. Microsoft Advertising cannot bill in a different currency than the currency that was selected for the account.

For an external list of accepted currencies see Choosing your account currency.

Authorization charges: If the advertiser selects their language as English-United States (EN-US) during signup and inputs a payment method associated with an Indian billing address, there will be an authorization swipe of $1 (about 45 rupees, based on the current exchange rate), followed by a credit for that same amount. This is a known issue and a fix is expected to be deployed.

If the advertiser selects their language as English-India (EN-IN) during signup and inputs a payment method associated with an Indian billing address, there will be a 2 rupee charge that is reversed quickly (usually within a matter of hours but could take a few days, depending on the financial institution). The authorization charge is used only to confirm the payment method is active.

Note: Before escalating to Global Support to confirm whether an authorization transaction was either released or settled by Microsoft, first have the customer speak to their financial institution about the time to remove the hold. If the standard lead time for hold removal on the bank's side has passed, escalate the transaction ID (from the statement), date, time, and amount of the hold to Global Support.

Messaging for authorization charges (aka Authorization holds)

"Non-PIN based credit and debit transactions are a two-step process, consisting of an authorization and settlement. Authorization charges are holds on funds and are standard practice in banking industries. The purpose is to verify the account is valid and that sufficient funds are available. Hold durations depend on the type of card used and are subject to the policies of the card issuer."

"I can get this to a specialist to ensure the authorization did not settle on our side. Once we get confirmation, you may need to follow up with your financial institution to understand their policy for releasing authorization holds on unsettled charges."

"The time to release authorization holds varies by financial institution. We unfortunately cannot speak to the internal processes of each institution with which we conduct business; however, we typically see funds held for authorization released within a few hours—sometimes holds on credit cards take a few days to fall off and it’s not unheard of for holds on credit cards to last as long as thirty days. I would recommend following up with your financial institution to understand what their policy is for releasing authorization holds."

1.4 Messaging for unsupported payment methods

If a payment method is not supported: “We strive to provide the most effective and efficient means of payment for customers around the world. We appreciate your feedback and will continue to advocate on your behalf, but at this time we do not support your first choice of a payment method. However, we do accept several other payment methods. Let’s talk through options and find the best one for you.”

If a payment method is not supported based on the advertiser's business location/billing center: “We strive to provide the most effective and efficient means of payment for customers around the world. We appreciate your feedback and will continue to advocate on your behalf, but at this time we do not support your first choice of a payment method based on your business location. However, we do accept a few other payment methods. Let’s talk through options and find the best one for you.”

Not supporting debit cards in India: “We do not accept debit cards as a payment method for advertisers in India. Threshold billing allows advertisers to set up a recurring payment to a debit or credit card. In India, there is a limitation on the merchant code offers by some banks, which blocks recurring charges. As a result, advertisers who signed up using debit cards would see a decline on the payment. Because the blocked payments caused a significant amount of customer dissatisfaction and calls to support, we decided to stop accepting debit cards for India advertisers at this time. Advertisers in India can still use other payment methods, such as a credit card.”

2.2 Offline payment methods

Offline payments are payments made by check or wire transfer. Offline payment methods are available for both prepay and invoiced accounts. Offline payment will ONLY be accepted for a postpay threshold account if the account is in write-off status.

  • Once the offline payment is received and deposited by Microsoft, the XID details included by the customer in the payment will be used to identify the Microsoft Advertising account to which the payment should be applied. If the XID is missing or is not a prepay account, the payment will be returned to the advertiser.
  • Advertisers should expect that payments including a prepay XID, once received by Microsoft, will be applied to their account within three to four business days (up to five days including weekends).
  • Instructions on how to submit an offline payment for a prepay account are available in the user interface after an advertiser has added Check/Bank Transfer as a payment method under their CID. The add funds link will direct the advertiser to payment submission instructions. All prepay offline payments must include the XID to which the payment should be applied.
  • For accounts billed by monthly invoice, the payment instructions are available on the invoice. The payment destination (recipient address and bank details) may vary by market.

3. Ways to pay

Microsoft Advertising offers advertisers three ways to pay, either before or after clicks are accrued. Prepay accounts allow advertisers to pay in advance for clicks while postpay accounts allow ads to deliver before the advertiser is charged.

Because postpay payment option allows advertisers to accrue clicks before they are charged, the term postpay can be used to refer to either threshold or monthly invoice.

3.1 Billing threshold

Standard billing threshold policies

  • The standard billing threshold starts at $50 and can then be raised to $100, $500, $1,000, and $2,000. (In Euros, the amounts are €50, €100, €500, €1,000, and €2,000.)
  • If an account reaches its billing threshold three times in a billing month (that is, by the billing cycle date, or BCD), the billing threshold is automatically raised to the next higher level. (If the account does not reach the billing threshold three times by the BCD, the count is reset to one at the start of the next billing month.)
  • If payment is declined three times in succession, the billing threshold is automatically reduced to the next lower level. For example, a billing threshold of $100 would be lowered to $50.
  • Four consecutive declines will result in the threshold being reset to the lowest limit.
  • The billing threshold is increased automatically; it cannot be increased manually.
  • The billing threshold is not automatically increased after a certain time has passed.
  • An advertiser is billed as soon as the billing threshold is reached, regardless of the BCD.

It is possible to have multiple accounts that reach billing thresholds at the same time or intermittently throughout the month. An advertiser may also have differing billing thresholds for different accounts, depending on each account's history.

Advertisers are charged one time per month on their billing date, or when spend reaches their billing threshold, whichever comes first.

Note: Microsoft Advertising has a minimum charge amount of $1 USD. If the customer does not reach $1 before their anniversary or threshold swipe, they will be charged as soon as their spend exceeds the $1 minimum.

Messaging:

  • “The day that you create an account is your monthly billing date. For example, if you create an account on October 21st, your billing date will be the 21st of each month. You cannot change the billing date. If you signed up on a day that does not appear in every month, you will be billed on the last day of the month. For example, if you signed up on March 31st, you will be billed on April 30th, May 31st and so on.”
  • “The amount at which Microsoft Advertising bills your account is called the billing threshold. Over time, as you make timely payments, we will increase your billing threshold.”

﻿3.2 Bill me now/Force bill

Advertisers may pay the full outstanding balance at any time. This is known as the Bill Me Now or force bill option. This option is available on the Balance and Billing tabs.

Note: For advertisers with a billing address in India and currency set to Indian rupees (INR), only the advertiser can process a Bill Me Now payment; it is disabled for service and support teams for accounts based in India.

When Bill Me Now is selected, there may be some clicks that have been received but aren't yet propagated into the billing system. On rare occasions, this may mean the advertiser receives a charge for slightly more than what was the outstanding balance at the time Bill Me Now was selected. Usually, this discrepancy is less than $5, depending on how well the account is delivering.

  • Select the account for which you want to use the Bill Me Now option, and then select Go.
  • Select the Bill Me Now button. (If there is no balance on the account, the button will not be visible.)
  • The advertiser needs to enter their Mastercard SecureCode or Verified by Visa code when adding a payment method and when choosing the Bill Me Now option. (Regular threshold billing does not require verification each time.)

3.3 Switching payment option (Prepay to Postpay)

Advertisers can switch payment option settings depending on their business needs.

Notes:

  • If a postpay advertiser wants to switch to invoice billing, the system will attempt a swipe for the amount owed before switching the account to invoice billing. If the swipe is unsuccessful, the system will not allow the switch. The error message will let the user know the payment failed.
  • SEPA customers cannot deactivate or switch payment methods unless their account balance is $0. They must either spend the funds, submit a chargeback, or contact support to issue a manual adjustment to switch payment option. If circumstances require a manual adjustment to payments made within the past eight weeks, request a fraud review and gain TA approval before processing the adjustment to minimize the risk that the customer will also pursue a chargeback. See KB 3173012 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Credits, Refunds, & Rebills for further details on SEPA payment refunds.

4.1 Advertiser is unable to add/update payment method

Make sure the advertiser has the correct role assigned to their username. Not all users can edit this. The ability to do so depends on their permissions.

  1. If the role is incorrect, ask them to reach out to their super admin to upgrade their permissions. If the super admin is no longer available, assist the user by fortifying their account, and then inviting them as a new user with the appropriate user role.
  2. If the user is the super admin and is the bill-to party, assist the user in an in private browsing session.
    1. Ask them to clear their cache and log out of any other Microsoft account they may be logged into.
    2. Try a different browser.
  3. Look up the payment method in CMAT to identify a possible decline reason.
  4. If there is no decline reason, escalate the issue to Global Support with a screenshot of the error message and set expectations with the advertiser on the five to seven business day SLA.

Note: A new Modern Payment Experience supporting credit/debit card payment method only is currently ramping for specific existing customers across these markets: US, CA, UK, FR, DE, ES, and AU.

Changes include:

  • Clearer error messaging (see the list of revised error messages below-errors that were not changed are not listed here).
  • The ability to select a previously used address when setting up a new card.
  • Auto detection of the card type based on the card number entered.
  • Faster loading of the form.
  • Experience only visible to customers logged in with a Microsoft account. The legacy Microsoft Advertising username credential type is not exposed to this modern experience.

4.5 Offline payment has been cashed, but not applied to the account

If funds have been transferred from the customer's bank to Microsoft's bank but not applied to the prepay account within 5 days, it is possible that payment was not received by the Cash Applications team (Cashapps), or there was a failure when adding the payment to the user interface (for example, the Microsoft Advertising account details were not specified in their payment or payment could not be located). In these cases, an offline payment status request (template below) should be submitted to CashApps.

  1. Complete the ASD SR Template Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS)>Offline Payment Inquiry - Payment Not Applied.
  2. Escalate the ticket via transfer to WW Online Credit Services>WOCS Accounts Receivable.

Note: With SEPA the initial payment settlement time is up to five business days and up to two business days for subsequent payments.

If the advertiser would like to stop the payment from being applied to the account, you can inform CashApps. However, before any offline payment can be refunded, the payment must first be located and posted in the UI. If payment is not yet rendering, request an offline payment status from CashApps first. Assuming the payment is located, the payment should be reversed in the UI by CSS, before going to SANOPS (aka AdOps)  for processing an offline refund to the customer. If payment cannot be posted in the UI by CashApps, but they confirm payment was received, CashApps may be able to directly initiate a refund to AP (sending a customer link to complete MPC details as part of the offline refund process). Alternatively, CashApps can also assist with 'reapplying' the funds to another account if this is the preferred course of action (instead of a refund).

Funds not found: There may be instances where CashApps cannot locate customer payments. Often this is the result of customers not supplying adequate proof of the original payment transaction from their bank. If the customer is unable or unwilling to provide sufficient documentation or proof to CashApps for payment investigation, the customer should be advised to resolve the payment dispute with their banking institution.

If an online payment processed by Boleto or SEPA has not been applied to a Microsoft Advertising account, Global Support should investigate the payment status. If Global Support confirms that the payment is traceable, and the account is not affected by a fraud closure, a request for offline payment application can then be submitted to the Cash Application team using the steps and template above. Be sure to include the confirmation from Global Support that the payment was received and must be applied.

4.6 Changing a payment method

Advertisers can associate a new payment method to active accounts, or delete payment methods not associated to active accounts, at any time.

If an advertiser currently uses invoice billing, they can use the Change Payment Instrument feature to choose to change to credit card billing in Microsoft Advertising. To change from credit card to invoice billing, Support must handle that change.

As soon as the advertiser associates a new payment method with an account, all pending charges will be transferred to it. Microsoft Advertising authorizes the card each time we attempt to charge it, resulting in a $1 authorization fee appearing on the billing statement. We will not actually charge $1, as Microsoft Advertising does not ever settle the $1 charge. The authorization should drop off the account after a few days, depending on the issuing bank for that card.

Advertisers cannot change the currency settings for an account after it is set up. However, users can create a new account that has a different currency setting.

Charges cannot be transferred to a different payment method after a transaction is completed.

4.8 Expired credit cards

If a card expires, the card issuer (bank or other financial institution) may decline payment for the account charges. If payment is declined, ads for the account may be paused.

The account may continue to accrue charges even though the card is expired. Advertisers can stop additional charges to their accounts by pausing campaigns on the Campaigns page. After the card is updated or the advertiser switches to a different payment method, they will need to resume the campaign to display ads again. For more information, see Pause or enable your campaigns.

7. Paperless billing

The option to enroll in delivery via email, also known as paperless invoicing, can be done only if the following scenarios apply:

  1. The user is a super admin.
  2. The customer is on invoice billing (not available for non-invoice customers currently).

Paperless invoicing may not be available in the following situations:

- The Bill To address is in a country location that does not support our current paperless options. For example, only France invoice customers with a France Sold To location are eligible (due Microsoft Advertising system limitations Bill To France locations and non-invoice customers will always receive hard copy via postal mail).

- Per local regulations, postal mail invoices are required for billing addresses. Currently, the following countries/region require hard copy invoices: China, Colombia, India, Korea, Panama, Peru, Russia, Taiwan, and Venezuela.

See Billing settings for monthly invoice accounts for additional information.
