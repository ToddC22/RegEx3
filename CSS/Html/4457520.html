Procedure: Escalation Engineer Engagement (Windows - WDG)

Description

The procedure listed below applies to all products within Windows - WDG Commercial in CSS Business delivery. The following steps outline the procedure for Support Engineers (SEs) and Support escalation Engineers (SEEs) to engage Escalation Engineers (EEs).

1. Steps

Engagement Criteria

  • If you are looking for a highly experienced subject matter expert engage a Support Escalation Engineer (SEE) first. They have a focus on comprehensive product knowledge, troubleshooting, configuration and in-depth analysis of traces & logs.
  • Escalation Engineers (EEs) must only be engaged for:
    • Debug analysis.
    • Source code review questions.
    • Issues where SME (SEE/TA) knowledge has been completely exhausted.
    • Product Group and/or Servicing team engagement.
  • All Support Engineers must have a pre-escalation review of their case completed by a SEE or Technical Advisor (TA) before engaging an EE:
    • All other action plans and steps should be investigated before engaging the Windows Escalation Team.
    • SEEs may engage with Escalation Engineers without a pre-escalation review.

How to engage an EE:

If you have a case you believe requires EE engagement check the engagement criteria listed above first to ensure that you are engaging EEs at the right time for the right kind of work:

  • Open KB 4457327 | Template: Summary (WDG EE Engagement) template and complete with all relevant information. 
  • Please take the time to complete the template with as much information as possible to help us engage the most appropriately skilled Escalation engineers.
  • The problem description must clearly describe the issue and the circumstances under which data was collected. Refer to http://aka.ms/problemdescription for more information on how to describe an issue correctly.
  • Please ensure the title starts with the POD name that owns the technology for which you are looking for assistance (ADS / NET / DND / SHA / PRF / UEX).
  • Create a Service Desk escalation task and paste the template into the Description Field.
  • If you are using a tool that enables you, please copy the title from the template to the “internal task title” field in Service Desk Case.
  • If you are a Support Engineer:
    1. Request a pre-escalation review of your case with a SEE or TA.
    2. The SEE or TA will add a pre-escalation review a to your case and advise if EE engagement is suitable.
    3. Note: Once the review has been completed you must engage the SEE or TA again separately if additional help is needed.
  • Transfer the Service Desk Escalation task to the appropriate Service Desk queue:
    1. MSaaS Windows EE Premier
    2. MSaaS Windows EE Professional
  • For Critsits, send a notification email to "csspodwineeall" and cc "pgesmgr":
    • Set the Subject to:
      • CRITSIT <Case ID> <Escalation task Title used in Template>
    • In the Body:
      • Include the Summary (WDG EE Engagement) template.
      • Please be clear on the action(s) requested from the EE team.
  • EE request response times:
    • Critsit – An EE will attempt to respond within 1 hour.
    • Non Critsit - An EE will attempt to respond within 2 business days, but depending on bandwidth, it could take longer.
  • If you have a situation that requires a quicker response, EMail pgesmgr DL with the Case ID, Title and a completed Summary (WDG EE Engagement) template.
  • Remain responsive and available to the EE Team to discuss your case.
  • Work to understand EE findings, and make sure that you are in sync with them on what will be communicated to customer:
    • Do Not cut and paste conversations or EE case notes and send them to the customer without discussing with the EE first.
  • Remain engaged with customer:
    • Continue troubleshooting.
    • Own follow-up and communication with the customer throughout the life of the case.

How SEEs and TAs are to complete a pre-escalation review

  • Act quickly to review the case confirming if EE engagement is necessary:
    • Focus on the summary problem statement and ensure it accurately describes the ask of the EE team.
  • Create a Collaboration task, add your findings in a succinct but complete Technical review and then mark the task as completed.
    • If the case is not appropriate for EE engagement:
      • List the reasons why.
      • Provide an action plan.
    • Note, the SEE or TA who completes the review is not responsible for additional help on the case after completing the review. The SE must engage the SEE or TA again separately if additional help is needed.

How EEs are to work engagements

  • Accept the task and begin review in a timely manner.
  • Be available to discuss findings with the case owner.
  • Provide detailed steps to engineer to gather necessary data if requested.
  • Respond back to the case owner with customer-ready communication detailing:
    • Your analysis.
    • Next steps.
  • The Escalation Engineer will discuss case ownership with the case owner and the escalation engineer may decide to take ownership of the case if appropriate.
