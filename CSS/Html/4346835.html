Process: Commercial Case Management Standards

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor | Duty Manager | Team Manager

What is this article about?

This process covers the remote delivery of technical solutions for break-fix and advisory cases raised by customers and partners using On-premises and Cloud solutions. It is also referred to as the Case Management Standards(CMS).

1. Overview

​​​​​To deliver a consistently great customer experience, follow all the non-negotiable actions shown in the table below:

Non-Negotiables

ASSIGNMENT

SCOPING

SOLUTION DEVELOPMENT

SOLUTION VALIDATION

CLOSURE

  • SLA pending and SLA complete work are prioritized equally
  • Customer and Partner cases and collaboration requests are prioritized equally
  •  Contact the customer or partner as soon as possible after case creation to support speed of scoping
  • Scope case as soon as possible after case creation
  • Be as detailed as possible
  • If available use standard workflow scoping questions
  • Set clear closure conditions
  • Set support boundaries and customer or partner expectations
  • If available use standard workflows
  • Provide a summary of your calls with the customer or partner and confirm any actions
  • Use the 4464163 | Procedure: Swarming to collaborate as appropriate
  • Use 4346078 | Template: Transfer, if transferring or escalating to a queue
  • For collaborations or direct escalations use 4346609 | Template: Summary
  • Ensure solution is confirmed by customer or partner
  • Use closure template (see  4459286 | Case Management Standard (CMS) Templates)
  • Ensure closure emails have detailed closure summary
  • Send closure email as soon as possible
  • Don’t delay closure email while engaging recovery
  • Follow 4457094 | Procedure: Unresponsive Customer 

GENERAL

  • Use phone first and frequently, unless the customer or partner specifically asks to be contacted by email only or service level requires Web Response only
  • Accurately maintain case status or disposition at all stages of the case
  • Always set a next contact time with the customer or partner and meet your commitments
  • Use 4457149 | Template: Action Plan or 4346609 | Template: Summary every day there is customer or partner contact to document next contact and next actions
  • Engage recovery early in case if any CSat risk
  • Include all emails and phone calls into the case.
  • Use email signature template (CSS MW Signature Guidelines)
  • For quick close scenarios including duplicate, unsupported or customer resolved, see the exception section

2. Steps

ASSIGNMENT

  1. Engineers should check and set their status in their tools appropriately at all times (Skype, Teams, Rave).   
  2. ​​Carefully review the case information. Do not miss:
    • Attachments, Customer or Partner files and Flashes.
    • Automated Reports filed in customer communications (for example, Azure Subscription Management BillingHelper report).
    • Other requirements (for example, looping in their team on email communication).
  3. Make yourself aware of:
    • Current case severity
    • Service Offering: be conscious if this is a customer or partner case
      • Including any special handling requirements, for example Microsoft Unified Support or US National Support Offering).
      • For Partner cases whether this is a Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) scenario.
      • Azure support plan (see offerings - https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/support/plans/)
        Note: For unsupported customers (out of support boundaries) see exceptions point 2.
    • Service Offering SLA:
      • If this is a Partner case  make sure you understand the SLA (see 4343153 | Informational: Partner support offerings).
    • Any entitlement to 24x7 support
    • Any other open cases belonging to the customer or partner to identify duplicates or related cases
    • If this is a Partner case, refer to the Partner as "Partner" in correspondence and notes.
    • If this is a Partner on behalf of customer case, refer to the Partner as "Partner" and the Partner's end customer as "Partner's customer" or "Partner's end customer" in correspondence and notes.
  4. Take ownership of the case or follow the misroute procedure.
  5. Make initial response (IR) as early as possible by following the Initial Response procedure:
    • Only complete initial response if you are the technical resource who:
      • Is immediately available to begin working the case.
      • Intends to deliver a technical resolution to the customer.
      • Will contact the customer to begin scoping.
    • Use the telephone first and frequently, unless the customer or partner specifically asks to be contacted by email only or their service level requires Web Response only.
    • If customer or partner is not available, leave a message and send an email with a timescale when you will try again.
  6. Where possible during first contact:
    • Combine IR with scoping and first action plan and arrange a remote troubleshooting session if necessary, following 4464166 | Guideline: First Quality Response (FQR).
    • Send the IR email while you have customer or partner on telephone or for CAP meet IR by completing a phone log. Rave and Service Desk Case IR is measured based upon engineers' first call or email to the customer.
  7. If the customer or partner specifically asks for email communications, request the necessary information to begin scoping and whatever logs are needed to start troubleshooting:
    • Ask whether future contact can be made by telephone to speed resolution.
  8. Exchange contact information including:
    • Hours of availability.
    • Best contact time.
    • ​Preferred contact method (encourage telephone).

SCOPING

  • Always scope by telephone and on the first call when you can, unless customer or partner specifically asks to use email only or their service level requires Web Response only. Confirm the following:
    • Primary and secondary (if available) contact details and preferred contact method.
    • Impact.
    • Severity (agree a severity that is appropriate and reflects the impact).
    • Environment information.
    • Closure expectations.
    • Technical detail.
  • Ask probing questions:
    • If 4466241 | Standard Workflows: Index are available check for any specific scoping questions to ask based on the customer or partner verbatim.
    • Start with open questioning and confirm back to verify your understanding.
    • Be as detailed as possible when talking with the customer or partner, don't make any assumptions.
    • For Partner 'Cloud Solution Provider' (CSP) cases make sure you:
      • Understand:
        • Whether the Partner is acting on behalf of their end customer.
        • What specific solution they are working on with the customer.
      • ​Verify what specific help is needed from Microsoft.
      • Confirm what diagnostics and troubleshooting have been completed and what the results were.
  • Consider support boundaries:
    • Set customer or partner expectations during scoping if there are any likely concerns such as 3rd party issues or unsupported scenarios.
      • ​See the Third-Party Issues procedure for additional information.
    • Make sure that scope covers only one issue per case.
    • For Rave engineers working on O365 cloud cases, multiple issues are allowed in a case.
      • If the issue needs extensive troubleshooting, and it's within support agent's technical capability in the same workload, continue to work with the customer. You may create new case on behalf of the customer for case management purpose if needed.
      • If additional issue is regarding a different technology/workload, then make use of Swarming to bring in the correct skills to help resolve the customer issue.
    • Check the incident type is correct (except Rave). If incident is not break/fix follow advisory procedures.
  • Complete the scoping email:
    • Include as much technical detail as possible.
    • Describe business impact in nontechnical words.     
    • Describe specific closure conditions without repeating the issue definition.
    • Send the scoping email as soon as possible after the scoping conversation.
  • Make sure that case title accurately reflects the technical issue. Rave case title is not editable.
  • Check the following are set or added correctly:
    • Product.
    • Support Path.
  • Update case coding if it is required:
    • CAP users must set the Disposition to Scope Confirmed.

SOLUTION DEVELOPMENT

  1. ​Take advantage of tools and peers during research and troubleshooting, especially when you are at a knowledge block or CSAT is at risk:
    • First check for existing known solutions and, if available, 4466241 | Standard Workflows: Index.
    • For cloud-based products check service availability dashboards for known service interruptions.
    • Use approved internal resources or tools for data collection, analysis, remote assistance and troubleshooting.
    • Consider using lab resources to reproduce the issue, troubleshoot further or test solutions.
  2. Develop a troubleshooting plan and keep all documentation up to date according to the case documentation procedure.
  3. Collaborate across technology early if required:
    • Use swarming procedures to collaborate in real time.
    • Notify managers or technical advisors of collaboration blockers.
    • When dispatching a collaboration request that does not require real-time response, use summary template to ensure efficient collaboration.
      • Complete the summary template in English (except Japan and Korea).
  4. Request escalation according to the criteria in the escalation procedure:
    • Use swarming procedures to collaborate in real time.
    • Use summary template for direct escalations to ensure effective escalation:
      • Complete the summary template in English (except Japan and Korea).
    • When dispatching a collaboration request that does not require real-time response, use transfer template if escalating to a queue:
      • Complete the transfer template in English (except Japan and Korea).
  5. Request transfer to other technologies according to the criteria in the 4457521 | Procedure: Transfer Ownership. .
    • Always attempt swarming before transferring to a different technology.
    • When dispatching a case transfer request that does not require real time response, use transfer template:
      • Complete the transfer in English (except Japan and Korea).   
  6. Manage customer or partner expectations for next callback and follow-ups:
    • Provide next action plans and call summaries to the customer.
    • Make sure that you confirm any actions that are the responsibility of the customer to verify that they know how to execute on them.     
  7. Don't allow cases to go idle, follow Call-back Commitment guidance.
  8. Keep the following current:
    • ​Dispositions and Case Status.
    • Case Severity.

SOLUTION VALIDATION

  1. ​​If you provide a solution, do not wait for confirmation from the customer or partner, document the case as solution delivered:
    • Send the customer or partner the solution by email even if you have communicated it over the telephone
    • Set the Disposition (CAP), Case Status (Rave), or Status (Service Desk Case) to:
      • SD - Pending Confirmation (CAP)
      • Pending Customer Verification (Rave)
      • Waiting for Confirmation (Service Desk Case)
    • Complete a Customer Facing Comment (CAP), Email Communication (Rave), or Notes (Service Desk Case) for every solution offered:
      • Create them in English Language (except for Japan/Korea) and ensure they are Customer viewable
    • If at any time the customer or partner confirms the solution does not work:
      • Update the Disposition (CAP), Case Status (Rave), or Status (Service Desk Case) to an appropriate status, after resetting it to:
        • Waiting on Support (CAP)
        • Troubleshooting (Rave)
        • Troubleshooting (Service Desk Case)
  2. Validate with the customer or partner that the solution addresses the scoped problem:
    • If the solution is confirmed, or you have initial confirmation of the solution but closure will be delayed allowing for monitoring:
      • Set the case Disposition (CAP) to:
        • SD - Solution Confirmed (CAP)
    • If validation was obtained over the telephone, document it in a phone log.
    • For Partner cases:
      • Check with the Partner whether the end customer is also in agreement with the solution, to avoid closing the case too early and resulting in a re-open.
      • ​Ensure the partner understands how to avoid a similar problem(s) in the future.
    • If you do not have immediate validation of the solution, ensure you have a commitment on the next communication to receive validation.

CLOSURE:

  1. ​​Obtain customer or partner approval to close the case if possible:
    • If they are not responding to your attempts to communicate, follow the 4457094 | Procedure: Unresponsive Customer.
  2. If the customer or partner wants to discuss additional issues:
    • Provide advise if the question is quick to answer.
    • If the issue needs detailed troubleshooting, request they open a separate support case or open one for them if possible.
    • For Rave engineers working on O365 cloud cases:
      • If the issue needs extensive troubleshooting, and it's within support agent's technical capability in the same workload, continue to work with the customer. You may create new case on behalf of the customer for case management purpose if needed.
      • If the issue is regarding a different technology/workload or can be supported better by a different SME, create a new case on behalf of the customer and transfer accordingly.
  3. Check the following are set or added correctly:
    • Product.
    • Support Path.
    • Resolution classification.
    • Bug information if any.
  4. Send closure summary email to the customer or partner using the appropriate template based on case status:
    • ​Do not delay sending the closure email, send it out very soon after closure confirmation received.
    • Ensure closures emails contain a detailed summary.
  5. Close case:
    • Case should be closed by the technical resource who communicated resolution.
  6. Share Knowledge as appropriate (for example by creating and publishing new internal or public Help articles through http://contentidea).

3. Deviations or workarounds

If at any time, you determine the case falls into one of the following categories follow the Quick Close scenarios steps below:

Duplicate
  1. Confirm the duplication with the customer or partner and get agreement to close the newest case:
    • If they are not responding to your attempts to communicate, follow the unresponsive customer procedure.
  2. Document the open duplicate case number and add any additional information if missing from the original case:
    • For CAP use a non-customer Task log or Comment and in the Duplicate of SR field, if available for your team.
    • For Rave use Internal Notes section.
    • For Service Desk Case use Notes section.
  3. Ensure the Severity is set to C or Non-critical.
  4. Proceed to close the newest request:
    • Send a closure summary email to the customer or partner.
    • For CAP:
      • Set the final Disposition to Closed-Duplicate.
    • For Rave:
      • Set the Resolution Code to Duplicated.
    • For Service Desk Case:
      • Set the Status to Duplicate.
Unsupported (out of support boundaries)
  1. If the request is being closed as unsupported, ensure the customer or partner understands the reason the request is outside of support boundaries:
    • Engage the TAM for Premier or Manager for non-Premier, if necessary.
    • If they are not responding to your attempts to communicate, follow the unresponsive customer procedure.
  2. Document the reason the request is outside of support boundaries referencing (if available – for example TechNet or KB article, Microsoft Engineering Group confirmation:
    • For CAP use a non-customer-facing Task log or Comment.
    • For Rave use Internal Notes section.
    • For Service Desk Case use Notes section.
  3. Proceed to closure the service request:
    • Send a closure summary email to the customer or partner including the reason the request is outside of support boundaries.
    • For CAP:
      • Set the Disposition to Closed-Rejected
    • For Rave:
      • Set the Resolution Code to Unresolved
    • For Service Desk Case:
      • Set the Status to Un-Resolved.
Customer Resolved
  1. Document that the customer resolved the issue themselves, including solution if known:
    • For CAP use a non-customer Task log or Comment.
    • For Rave use Internal Notes.
    • For Service Desk Case use Notes section.
  2. Proceed to close the service request:
    • Send a closure summary email to the customer or partner documenting they resolved the problem and how they resolved it if known.
    • For CAP:
      • Set the Disposition to Closed-Cancel
    • For Rave:
      • Set Resolution Code to Resolved
    • For Service Desk Case:
      • Set the Status to Resolved by Customer.

4. Supporting documents or information

Call-back Commitment

  • Be proactive in contacting the customer or partner, even if the next action is on the them. You should always have a next date and time that you will follow-up with a call if you don't hear from them sooner.
  • Document your next contact time in an action plan template.
  • Microsoft should always be driving case progression, a case without a call-back commitment is not progressing and may be considered idle.

Recovery

  • Engage recovery as soon as any CSat risk is identified:
    • Engaging recovery after solution is confirmed is too late, identify risks early
  • Engage your Technical Advisor, Manager, or the Incident Manager (IM) to help with recovery:
  • In all situations requiring recovery ensure the customer or partner has been responded to within 72 hours and ideally the same day.
    • Complete recovery as quickly as possible and do not allow the cases to stay in recovery for extended periods of time (no more than 1 week recommended).

Dispositions and Case Status

  • Keep the Disposition and Case Status updated at all stages of the case. This helps identify who has the next action on the case.

Communication

  • Use the telephone first and frequently, unless the customer or partner specifically asks to use email only or the customer's service level requires Web Response only.
  • Include all emails and telephone calls into the case to enable other roles to follow case progression.
    • For Service Desk Case:
      Include email address       support@mail.support.microsoft.com.
    • For Rave:
      Ensure all emails are sent by using the built-in email interface in Rave, and all emails should be corresponded WITHIN Rave. For any email communication happened outside of Rave, it can be attached to the internal notes section of the case
  • If the customer or partner replies just to you, forward the communication to include the email address support@mail.support.microsoft.com (for Service Desk Case) and use the built – in email interface (in Rave)   for complete tracking of the communication flow.
  • Use the official email signature template (CSS MW Signature Guidelines)
    • Teams may also add information or contact details specific to the technology or segment they support.
  • If at any time the customer or partner cannot be reached by phone and does not respond to email, follow the Unresponsive Customer Procedure.

Case Documentation

  • Do not rely on emails to update status, keep tasks updated.  
  • Use an action plan template or summary template every day there is customer or partner contact to document next action and next customer or partner contact.

Out of Office

  • Every engineer should have a backup or team manager contact or partner in their signature, so the customer or partner can always receive support if engineer is unavailable.
  • For planned OOF handover any cases with a customer or partner commitment due during your absence.
  • If there is an unexpected OOF:
    • Keep your case updated with a next contact commitment and action plan. 
    • Ensure contact details provided to the customer or partner includes an alternative contact.

5. Frequently asked questions

Q: Is it important to set the Pending Confirmation Wait State/Disposition if the customer confirms the solution immediately?

​​​​​A: Yes! All Wait States (referred to as Dispositions in CAP) should be set correctly as you work the case. Failure to set the Wait State, skipping Wait States that are valid for the scenario, or setting them out of normal case workflow order (for example, setting Solution Confirmed, then Pending Confirmation and then closing the case), could cause non-compliance.​
