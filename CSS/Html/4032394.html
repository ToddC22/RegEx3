What is this article about?

This article covers known issues for Surface Pen (all). Use this article to verify if a customer’s symptom is associated with a known issue.

Before you begin

Check for updates or Run Surface Diagnostic Toolkit (SDT).

  • For information on SDT, see KB 4014334 Procedure: Surface: Using the Microsoft Surface Diagnostic Toolkit.

  • To check for updates, go to Settings > Update & security > Windows Update and select Check for updates. Install any necessary update and restart the Surface.

1.1 Fixed August 8th, 2018 Surface Pen with No Clip (V4) features are not be compatible with all Surface devices

Symptom:
Customers may contact support saying that features such as Tilt or 4096 points of pressure are not present on their Surface device while using Surface Pen with No Clip (V4).

Resolution:

  • This issue was resolved in an update released August 8, 2018. Check for updates to resolve.

4. Inking

4.1 Surface Pen with No Clip (V4): Inking is inaccurate or offset from the tip while inking on Surface

Symptom:
Customers using Surface Pen with No Clip to ink on a Surface Pro 1796, or Surface Pro 1807 may experience an issue where the pen tip and inking line do not match, and may be offset by a small distance.

To see a visualization of the issue, you can review this Answers.Microsoft.com post.

Resolution:
There is no solution available for this issue currently. Deliver the below messaging to the customer. You can also share out the forum post as an additional reference point.

Messaging:
"Thank you for your feedback and the great information you’ve shared about your Pen inking experience. We understand the concern, and are currently investigating a possible root cause and solution. Please continue to check for Surface and Windows Updates to ensure your device has the most current firmware and software available."

Return to top

4.2 Fixed – 12/7/2018: Pen no longer inks, but Single Touch works after device wakes from sleep

Symptom:
Some customers may experience an issue where their Surface Pen no longer inks, but single touch functionality still works after their device wakes from sleep. It is likely also that multi-touch (pinch to zoom, etc.) also does not work.

Resolution:
Run SDT or WU to install the latest updates. If this does not resolve the issue, follow standard troubleshooting, as there is likely a different root cause.

5. Typing, clicking, or mousing

5.1 Unexpected panning or scrolling in certain apps while using the pen after applying KB 4089848

Symptom:
After applying KB 4089848, some users may experience unexpected panning or scrolling in certain apps (Adobe Photoshop, Clip Studio Paint) while using the pen.

Resolution:
You can resolve this issue using the following steps:

  1. From Start, search for cmd.
  2. Right-click Command Prompt in the search results, and choose Run as administrator.
  3. At the command prompt, type or paste:

    reg add HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Pen /v LegacyPenInteractionModel /t REG_DWORD /d 1 /f
  4. Press Enter.

After the command executes, the issue with panning and scrolling should be resolved.

Messaging:
"Microsoft is working on a resolution and will provide us an update in an upcoming release.

For more information refer to:

  • https://support.microsoft.com/help/4089848/windows-10-update-kb4089848 
  • https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_10-hardware/unexpected-panning-or-scrolling-in-some-apps-with/e8db3f51-91db-4a99-ba24-572d59686250?tm=1523650087221"
