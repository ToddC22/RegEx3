1. Overview

1.1 Bing Shopping campaigns

Bing Shopping campaigns let advertisers run Product Ads on Bing and Yahoo in the US and Bing for other markets.

Product Ads showcase retail advertisers’ products by letting them display an image, price, merchant name, product details, and shipping costs (DE only). Product Ads from Bing Shopping campaigns are displayed when a searcher enters terms in Bing that are relevant to products in an advertiser’s Bing Merchant Center store.

[Asset 4012919]

  • Product ads can display in the sidebar and mainline of the search result page. Up to eight Product Ads can be displayed.
    • Can show a Text Ad and a Product Ads from the same merchant
    • Can show two or more Product Ads from the same merchant
  • Promotional text is displayed when search users roll their curser over the ad.
  • When a product ad is clicked on, search users are linked to the advertiser’s website where they can purchase the product.
  • Clicks on product ads are charged on a cost per click basis.
Advertisers can set up dedicated shopping campaigns for specific types of products by targeting certain product attributes, as defined by Product Groups.
 
The more specific the product targets are, the more control the advertiser has on which product serves.

1.2 Bing Merchant Center (BMC)

BMC allows you to create a store and upload product catalogs, which include images and other information about your products, so that your products can display on Bing. Advertisers can list their products through Bing Shopping campaigns. BMC is only available to advertisers in the following markets:

  • United States for PCs, tablets, and smartphones.
  • United Kingdom, France, Germany, Canada, and Australia for PCs and tablets

4. Bing Shopping 

4.1 Requirements

Available markets: US, UK, CA, FR, DE, AUS.

Advertisers must:

  • Be able to verify their domain ownership with Bing Webmaster Tools or with a UET tag.
    • A website domain, sometimes just called a domain, is the name of the website or URL, and is sometimes called the host name.
    • Domain ownership is the act of claiming a website and validating ownership. It is used to verify that you indeed own your uploaded product offers. Product offers with URLs that are not sub-paths of this website destination URL will be rejected.
    • See section 6 or https://help.ads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/50888/1​ for more details.
  • Offer retail products or a subscription and have at least one SKU.
  • Be a non-adult advertiser.
  • Have available markets setting in Microsoft Advertising.
  • Sell products online with a secure online checkout option (either internal or external).
  • Sign up/sign in pages must have SSL or https encryption
  • Have a valid destination URL with no errors or redirects.
  • Use currency of target market, and ship to target market.

4.2 Campaign management features

Bing Shopping campaigns offer several features that streamline campaign management and give better access to campaign insights:

  • Campaign priority - Set your campaign priority to give a specific, timely campaign preference despite bid amounts. This only compares campaign priorities of the same store and won’t help regarding competitors’ campaigns.
    • Example: If one of the products you sell is flip flops, you can set the priority for the campaign that features your flips flops to “High” during the summer to boost the likelihood that it will show compared to your other campaigns. Once fall hits, you can keep running the campaign, but shift its priority back to its default (low). 
  • One ad within an ad group - Write your promotional copy once and use it for a whole ad group.
  • Product groups replace product targets - A streamlined hierarchal structure organizes automatic Product Groups, so each group has a unique set of offers — meaning complete product coverage without overlaps.
  • Target individual offer values/attributes - Define narrower Product Groups with up to seven filters — as opposed to the three filters that can be used with Product Targets.
  • Insights into Number of Products Matching - View how many products are being targeted by your BSC campaigns with respect to your overall Products in the Bing Merchant Store. Under the Product Groups tab, four columns can be manually added to the Product Group grid:
    • Total number of products submitted - This is what the advertiser submitted in BMC​.
    • Total number of products ready to serve - This is what is received from Product Ads offline (before campaign filters are applied).
    • Number of products targeted - The number of products matching the Product Group condition​.
    • Percent of products targeted - Percent of products matching the Product Group condition.
  • New feed elements - New custom labels, plus Bing category, and merchant-defined Product Type.
  • Google Shopping feed data - Can be easily imported to use with Bing Shopping campaigns.

5.1 Requirements 

  • Be able to verify their domain ownership with Bing Webmaster Tools or with a UET tag.
    • See section 6 or https://help.ads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/50888/1 for more details.

  • Sell and ship products to available markets.
  • Pay for their ads in available market currency.
  • Products on website are sold in available markets currency.
    • If merchant is not physically located in an available market, the merchant can request a manual review and still qualify for “having a presence in an available market” as long as they meet all of the following criteria. The SLA for these manual reviews is five business days. 
  • Products on website are sold in available market language.
  • Merchant’s ads are targeting available markets – check the campaigns tab and find the Product Ads campaign. If you select Settings, you will see the locations that the ads are targeting. If there are no campaigns created, and they pass the rest of the requirements, then you can approve the store. 
  • Merchant has an available market Microsoft Advertising shell – this will be evident if the Product Ads checkbox is available as an option in the BMC store under the Store Settings tab.
  • Merchant is not located in mainland China.

5.5 $0 priced products

Merchants offer customers with goods at $0 provided customers pay the actual value of the item in installments or customers sign-up for a contract with them. Product Ads will start supporting these merchants and unlock products that are priced at zero for the customers.
 
Requirements:
  • Product title should contain the terms, “with contract” or “with payment plan” or “with installment plan” or variations of these.
  • Product category can be either “Tablet computers[ID 4745]” or “Mobile phones[ID 267].”

To submit products with “0” price, merchants need to add below details to their product feed: 

  • Feed attribute “title” should contain one of the below options:
    • “with installment plan”
    • “with payment plan”
    • “with contract” 
    • “with lease”
  • Feed attribute “product_category” should contain one of the below options:
    • Electronics > Communications > Telephony > Mobile Phones (Same as Google product category)

      OR

      Bing category ID – 267 (Same as Google category ID)
    • Electronics > Computers > Tablet Computers (Same as Google product category)

      OR

      Bing category ID – 4745 (Same as Google category ID)

5.6 Agencies and resellers

Agencies can create and manage Bing Merchant Center stores on behalf of their clients under a single Microsoft account.

To manage client stores, the agency must ensure that:

  • The client’s domain has been claimed in Bing Webmaster Tools (the agency can do this on behalf of the client). 
  • The agency has a Microsoft account that’s listed as a user for the client’s domain in Bing Webmaster Tools.

To set up a client store, the agency must first log in to its own Microsoft Advertising agency shell, and then navigate to the appropriate sub-account for the client whose store it would like to create or manage. 

Notes: 

  • Agencies will be able to see all of a client’s Bing Merchant Center stores that have been created at any point, even those that they do not have permission to manage and those that were previously created under a different Microsoft account.
  • Agencies cannot see which client account they are working in from within Bing Merchant Center. To verify the sub-account, the agency must return to the Microsoft Advertising UI.
  • If an agency creates a store for a client with a non-available market billing address, it will not trigger manual review because the system checks the agency’s country and billing address, not the client’s. Agencies should not be creating stores for non-available market clients at this time. 
  • A store created by an agency is still owned by the client.

5.7 API functionality

Advertisers should consider using the Content API if the feed includes many products and are able to integrate the Content API with the rest of their inventory management systems. Customers who are currently using the Google Content API will be able to adapt their code to use the Microsoft Advertising Content API easily.

Customers with smaller product feeds, or a limited ability to integrate with their inventory systems, may be better off using the simpler FTP method.

Customers will not be able to use FTP/automatic download/manual upload and the content API simultaneously. Once a merchant account has been migrated to use the Content API, the advertiser will not be able to submit to their product feed via any of the three available methods in the UPI.

For information on Product Ads in the API, see Content API Overview.

5.8 Content API for Product Ads catalog management

For advertisers who want to manage their Bing Shopping ads to reflect real time market conditions, Content API provides a programmatic way to manage product offers (insert, update, delete and read) and provide a mechanism for the advertiser to provide faster pricing and availability information to reflect relevant and up-to-date product information in the ad.

Key points and value propositions:

  • Allows merchants/retailers to programmatically insert, update, read, and delete product offers instead of managing data feeds through FTP.
  • Provides support for faster availability and pricing updates for product offers to reflect real time market conditions.
  • Allows advertiser to update product details incrementally rather than uploading entire data feed.
  • Accepts Google PLA format which minimizes engineering friction for merchants/retailers to switch from competition.
  • Is a REST service that supports both legacy creds (username, pwd) and OAuth (MS account) for authentication. 
  • Supports E2E SLA of ≤ 36 hours for new offers and ≤ 3 hours for pricing and availability updates.
  • Is a RESTful API (not SOAP based), that uses standard HTTP GET, POST, PUT, and DELETE operations to access data on the server. Note that a default catalog cannot be deleted.
  • Supports batching multiple operations into a single HTTP request.
  • Entries in dry-run mode do not affect live data so you can use dry-run mode to verify that your feed will not generate any unexpected warnings or errors before modifying your live feed data.

Content API requests should be sent to https://{prefix}.content.api.bingads.microsoft.com and {prefix} should be replaced with the prefix value you were issued during API provisioning.

6.1 Create BMC store with UET tag

Microsoft Advertising customers can create a BMC store by adding UET tags to their website.

Criteria:

  • At least 50 UET events must be fired within the last 30 days. 
  • In the Create a Store workflow, under Store Info, the advertiser must check the “Use UET tag to populate the above destination URL” box (see screenshot below). If a new UET tag was added and 50 events were fired from the website, it can take up to 48 hours for the domain destination URL to appear in BMC. 
  • Automated checks will be performed to verify the UET tags are associated with the customer registered in the system. The domain(s) will only show up in BMC if there is a match.

  • XML Authentication
  1. Select BingSiteAuth.xml to save the custom XML file, which contains your customized ownership verification code, to your computer, and then upload the file to the root directory of the registered site.
  2. Using the FTP site type FTP here and hit enter. It would prompt for a username and password and then you can just copy and paste the feed file into File Explorer
  3. Place an XML file on your web server.
  4. Download BingSiteAuth.xml and save to your desktop. Upload that file to the root directory of their website.
  5. Upload the file, and select add.
  6. Verify the ownership.
  7. Select the file, save it, find it saved as BingSiteAuth(1) web browsers like Firefox and Chrome and IE will sometimes add parentheses or an X to the filename, if there is already a file on there.

  1. Copy the displayed <meta> tag with your custom ownership verification code to the clipboard. Then open your registered site’s default page in your web development environment editor and paste the code at the end of the <head> section. Make sure the <head> section is followed by a <body> tag. Lastly, upload the revised default page file containing the new <meta> tag to your site.
  2. Adding a meta tag to the header is useful for users that are unable to upload files, but they can modify the HTML. You must be able to access some sort of UI or platform to add an HTML tag, and there are many different service providers.

6.3 Google Import

Importing your campaigns is recommended for advertisers currently using Google Shopping campaigns.

Things to remember:
  • Set up your Bing Merchant Center first. Uploading your product feed to the Bing Merchant Center can take up to 48 hours for the feed to process. Use the same feed as you’re using for Google Shopping campaigns. No changes are needed to your Bing Merchant Center store. 
  • Importing your Google Shopping campaigns into Microsoft Advertising only imports your campaign structure – not your product feed itself, which you upload to the Bing Merchant Center (see above).
  • Be sure to set your campaign priority. Set the priority of your imported campaign to medium or high priority. 
Import your Google Shopping campaign:

https://help.bingads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/51050/0

After import, go to Campaign Settings to ensure the campaign is linked correctly to your Bing Merchant Center store.  Also remember to check your import summary log for any errors.

Recommendation: Start with a few Bing Shopping campaigns. Once you’re satisfied with your set up and have started seeing performance data, import your remaining shopping campaigns from Google.

Note: Extensions must be added manually.

When a Bing Shopping advertiser is impacted by store rejections, evaluate whether they are an aggregator by using the criteria outlined below. If they are an aggregator, follow the process below to escalate the appeal and provide guidance.

7.1 How to identify Shopping Aggregators

A “true” aggregator/shopping comparison website shows products from many different website stores. The website allows the user to search for products and returns curated products based on the criteria they’ve set. A website that has fewer than three stores, or one that does not really let a user search for products is not an aggregator and should not be approved to be an aggregator.

Here are some examples of aggregator websites:

http://www.bizrate.com/womens-shoes/blue/1162-1586/buy
http://www.shopzilla.com/
http://www.shopping.com/Hush-Puppies-shoes/products?CLT=RSH&KW=Hush+Puppies+shoes
http://www.leguide.com/
http://www.bhg.com/shop/houzz-m.html

If you review these websites, you can see how they let users search, sort, and filter based on their criteria. The information displayed on the sites should be true, display the actual seller, and redirect to the seller’s website if a user clicks on the product. It should not create poor user experiences such as excessive popups

Aggregators will most likely contact support because their store is rejected (not offers) due to no valid products or product redirection.

Ask the customer the following questions to determine whether they are an aggregator or not:

“Do you sell products directly on your website?”

  • If YES, then they are not an aggregator. Get an example product URL, verify, and submit a store rejection appeal as usual.
  • If NO, then they might be an aggregator. Ask the follow-up question: “Do you run a shopping comparison site?”
    • If the answer to this question is YES, then they are an aggregator. Follow the process below to submit an aggregator appeal.

If you’re not sure, escalate to Ad Platform & Tools (refer to the Escalation Guide for Sub-Team details) for an appeal and request guidance as to whether the customer is an aggregator or not.

7.2 Aggregator appeal escalation process

  1. Escalate an aggregator store appeal to Ad Platform & Tools. Refer to the Escalation Guide for Sub-Team details.
  2. In the store review escalation template, answer “Yes” to the field “Is this advertiser an Aggregator?”.
  3. The review team will respond letting you know whether the store has been approved as an aggregator store.
    • If approved, inform the customer that they must adhere to the aggregator specific feed rules.
      • Seller_name must be passed for every product they sell.
      • The link field must be a link to the actual product page.
      • The bingads_redirect link must be used for their third-party redirect/click tracking.
    • If rejected, inform the customer that our Product Ads policies only allow websites with valid products, that can be purchased at checkout on the same site. (There are exceptions to this rule since some stores will use other payment platforms to complete the purchase such as PayPal, Shopify, or Yahoo commerce).

8.1 Advertiser is unable to upload or publish their catalog 

Note: 40% of all Bing Shopping campaign and BMC incidents are about the feed file.

Assistance with BMC errors: Troubleshooting Bing Merchant Center (EN-US | DE | FR | ES | IT | EN-UK | PT-BR).

Issue: The store is not properly enabled.

Advertiser may be receiving one of the following errors:

  1. Publishing catalog files is not available for this store.
  2. Publishing catalog files using the FTP server is not available for this store.
  3. Customer is getting an error when trying to upload a feed file to BMC through FTP.

Determine if the store is fully enabled

  1. Access BMC>Store Management.
  2. Confirm if both the "Publish to the index" and "FTP Account" boxes are checked.
  3. If either box is unchecked, escalate to BMC Support to request enablement. [AR1] 
Issue: The catalog is not enabled.

Enable the catalog:

  1. Access BMC>Catalog Management>Catalog Settings.
  2. Check the "Enable publishing” box.
  3. Advise the advertiser to resubmit their catalog.
  4. If the catalog does not show up in the Recent files table within 15 minutes, contact BMC support.
Issue: The advertiser is unable to upload their catalog through FTP.

The catalog is enabled for API management under Catalog management > Catalog settings. Having this checkbox enabled will prevent all methods (automatic, FTP, or manual) from working.

  1. Access BMC>Catalog management>Catalog Settings.
  2. Create a new catalog and make sure the API management box is not checked. 
  3. If the catalog does not show up in the Recent files table within 15 minutes, contact BMC support.
Issue: The feed file is malformed.

Confirm the type of malformation:

  1. If the advertiser has selected to automatically download the file from a URL (from within their Catalog Settings page), ensure the URL entered directs to an already existing file. The URL cannot be a script that generates a file. The URL might be a script if it contains parameters (as indicated by the presence of a '?' and '&') or the file extension is ".aspx" or ".php".
  2. If the URL directs to an XML file, ensure it is a Google-formatted XML file. Refer the advertiser to Can I use the Google feed file?
  3. Advise them to fix and resubmit their catalog feed.
     
  4. If the issue persists, escalate to the Ad Platform & Tools Team.
Issue: The feed file name uploaded through FTP does not exactly match what is specified in the catalog management page.

Ensure the name of the uploaded feed file exactly matches what is specified in the catalog management page.

  1. Access BMC>Store Summary and check the Store report. A store report will only generate if the feed file name uploaded through FTP does not exactly match what is specified in the catalog management page.
  2. The advertiser should either change the name of the file being uploaded or access BMC>Catalog Management>Catalog Settings to update the catalog feed file name.
  3. Advise them to resubmit their catalog.
  4. If this does not resolve the issue, submit a request for assistance to the Ad Platform & Tools Team. 
Issue: Errors in Google Import file.
  • Check to make sure the columns are the same. For more information visit: https://help.ads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/51050/0.
  • Escalate any persisting issues to the Global Support team.
Issue: Error message: The Store is not enabled for API.

This means that the store was not migrated to uBMC or the migration wasn’t successful. To enable the store, navigate to the BMC Admin tool (only accessible through RAS access) and select enableUbmc. This should resolve the error and force the store migration.

Messaging:

"There seems to be a technical issue preventing your catalog from publishing. I'll need to have a specialist look at it. It may take up to 5 business days to resolve. What day and time works best for me to follow up with you?"

Customer is seeing a status of Rejected on Store Summary page.

Issue: Customers in markets where Product Ads are not supported

The only countries where Product Ads are supported are the United States, the United Kingdom, France, Australia, and Germany. Check the advertiser's account to make sure they meet all the following market requirements:

  1. The Microsoft Advertising shell is based in a country where product ads are supported.
  2. The products ship to a country where product ads are supported.
  3. The products are sold in the market currency for the supported country.
  4. Advertiser is not located in Mainland China.

If the advertiser is actually located in the approved market or meets the conditions for having a business presence in that market and believes the store application was rejected incorrectly, request the advertiser provide information verifying their eligibility (such as, verifiable address or business license) and re-submit the store for review by requesting assistance from the NQS team.

If the advertiser does not meet the market requirements above, inform them that the store application was denied because the business is located outside of a country that is enabled for Product Ads.

Issue: Page is not SSL compliant

Any page with either password or credit card information must be Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) protected. Inform the advertiser the store was denied because personal information can’t be entered on a page which is not SSL compliant.

Issue: 404 Errors

The most common reason for 404 errors is that the advertiser is blocking crawlers from viewing their website. Ask the advertiser to allow our crawler. For more information, see KB 3168545 Bing Crawlers & Bots.

Issue: Invalid checkout steps

Inform the advertiser the store was denied due to invalid checkout or payment collection steps. Stores must include a valid customer checkout or payment collection mechanism.

See https://help.ads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/52023/1 for more information.

Issue: Website URL redirect

Inform the advertiser the store has been denied because the URL specified for the Destination URL in the Store Settings tab of BMC redirects to a different URL. The Destination URL must point to the actual URL of the store.

For more information regarding Microsoft Advertising URL policy see https://about.ads.microsoft.com/en-us/resources/policies/ad-content-and-style-policies#AC15%C2%A0.

Inform the advertiser the store has been denied due to violation of Microsoft Advertising product URL redirection policy.

Issue: Product redirect - the product link is set to redirect to a different site.

Product URL redirection is currently not allowed or supported. Products on the site cannot redirect to other retailers for purchase.

For more information regarding the Microsoft Advertising URL policy see https://about.ads.microsoft.com/en-us/resources/policies/ad-content-and-style-policies#AC15%C2%A0.

Issue: No Products

Inform the customer that their store has been denied due to having no products, or invalid products, listed for sale.

Examples of invalid products include services (such as marketing, advertising, or advising); estimates (instead of providing a solid price); and memberships (gift cards/certificates for memberships are acceptable).

See https://help.ads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/52023/1 for more information.

Issue: URL is already in use - duplicate store - two or more stores are registered with the same domain.

Inform the customer that their store has been denied because there is already a store registered for the domain name, or there is an existing store registered with their account ID.

Currently we do not allow duplicate named or owned stores to be active simultaneously. If the advertiser has access to another Microsoft Advertising account containing a Bing Merchant Center store in which the URL is already being used, they can access the other account to deactivate the store if they choose.

Either the customer or advocate can delete the duplicate store.

Issue: Professionalism - the site does not meet our Microsoft Advertising policies.

Inform the customer that their store has been denied because the content of their site does not meet our Microsoft Advertising Policies requirements.

Common reasons for denial include:

  1. Website is mostly advertisements or is difficult to determine which products are being sold and which are being advertised for other companies.
  2. Website contains broken, missing, or unrelated links to other webpages.
  3. Website exceeds 30 second per page load time limit. All pages must load in under 30 seconds.

Issue: Adult content

Inform the customer that their store has been denied because their products contain humor, imagery, text, video, or audio that is not appropriate for non-adults.

Microsoft classifies advertising as adult content if the target website features sexually explicit content, including but not limited to:

  1. Pornography
  2. Sites that promote porn stars and their personal brand.
  3. Sites where sex toys are the prominent feature of the site.
  4. Dating sites with the clear intent for sexual encounters, where the website content features graphic images on the landing page, or the language is graphic or explicit in nature.

Refer the advertiser to our Adult content policies for more information.

Next steps:

Once the required changes have been made, submit the store for review by requesting assistance from the NQS team. For more details, see section 3.

8.4 Delivery and offer matching issues 

Issue: A customer is noticing low or no ad delivery for Shopping campaigns.

Escalate to Tier 2 support if:

  • You have verified Product Offers are matching to the Product Group in the Campaign UI.
  1. Campaign level matching:
    • Feeds must be renewed every 30 days.
      • Check products tab within a campaign: If empty, open campaign in BMC, look at graph for campaign, and if it changes from green to orange, the campaign has expired. Have the customer reupload the feed.
    • Check products tab within a campaign – If it is empty, but you see active products within BMC, it could be one of three scenarios include:
      • Campaign settings’ filters: Campaign level filter can block everything that is showing in the products tab because it has been filtered out. Download the feed or download published reports to double-check.
      • Country/region of sale: Having a country/region of sale which does not match the catalog’s location will affect the listings. Make sure it’s set correctly for the campaign. This is not changeable after the campaign is created.
      • Customer with multiple stores: Make sure campaign is set to the right store. This is not changeable after the campaign is created.
  2. Checking product groups
    • Product groups listed for a campaign: Select the ellipses next to a listed product group.
      • If delivery changed on a certain day, a feed change could have affected the product group. Go to BMC and download the published feed report. The customer may have changed image URLs or some other aspect of the product in the campaign.
      • If it shows that the group doesn’t contain any products, this usually suggests an import issue where a campaign has been directly imported from Google. Have the customer select the edit option next to the group and move the correct listings from left to right column.
  3. Only for newly matching products (for example, product offers that are newly matching to any existing product group): The Product Offer has been matching to the Product Group for at least one day. 
  4. You are testing a relevant search query – most times, the offer title is useable.
  5. Ad Inquiry and Ad Preview have not returned any filtration results
  6. Escalation template is complete

Issue: Customer notices that some or all their offers are not showing up in the products tab or matching to product groups. This usually happens when a customer either has no offers published yet or have set their campaign up incorrectly.

Escalate to Tier 2 support if:

Offers are active in BMC:

  • The campaign’s country/region of sale value matches the desired catalog’s country location.
  • The campaign has correct campaign filter settings.
  • The escalation template is filled out.

﻿9. Product ads optimizations & best practices  

The best way to optimize Product Ads is through optimizing the data feed. Below are the best ways to do this.

  1. Data validation - Ensure your product feed file is properly formatted. Our crawling advocate, bingbot, must be allowed to crawl your site or your products will not display.
  2. Product discoverability - Categorize your products so customers can search for and find them. Use unique identifiers to help differentiate product titles and descriptions that may only differ in size or color. They should be consistent between data feeds.
  3. Conversion - Ensure your product’s stock status is up to date (in stock or out of stock). 
    • Use a relevant product description and title.
    • Use variant attributes – colors or sizes. 
    • Use the GTIN column to differentiate your items in terms of color and size.

Return to top

9.1 Best practices: Product feed

  • Go beyond the required attributes. Populate your feed with as many recommended attributes as possible for each product offer.
  • The more information you make available, the more likely your Product Ads will be shown for relevant searches.
  • Use the custom labels for Bing Shopping campaigns. These will help you group your products and better target. For example, by best sellers, high margin products, items with high/low return or spend, or even by seasonal event. 
Use a descriptive title for your product.
  • Include all relevant information possible in your title.
  • One – 255-character limit.
  • Add relevant search terms that will trigger your product queries.
  • Include brand and product type in the title. If applicable, add the gender, size, and color. 

Include “Product Type,” a recommended attribute.

  • Include your own product type or category for product. This will help you break out your campaigns more granularly.

Include “Microsoft Advertising Redirect,” a recommended attribute.

  • Include your destination URL for dynamic tracking parameters.
  • This link redirect attribute will allow the advertiser to add third-party tracking and ensure that tracking parameters won’t fall off when links are redirected.

Include “Conditions,” a recommended attribute.

  • Include the product’s condition: New, used, or refurbished. This can help you show a wider range of products that match searcher intent.

Use a high-resolution image for the image link (product image URL).

  • Use images with a white background to make them pop.
  • Do not include any text within your images (for example., promotional messaging like “save now for spring”).
  • Image size must be at least 220x220.

Test different images of the same product to determine which image performs best.

  • Include your own product type or category for product. This will help you break out your campaigns more granularly.

Refresh your feed weekly.

  • Your feed must be uploaded at least once every 30 days or the feed will expire.
  • We recommend a weekly refresh at the minimum, but daily refresh is optimal.
  • Refresh your feed when there are any changes with your inventory. For example, items become out of stock or are back in stock, prices change, discounts or sales are in effect, or you offer new items.

9.2 Best practices: Campaigns

Create new campaigns for your Bing Shopping campaigns.

  • Instead of adding Bing Shopping Campaigns to existing campaigns, create new campaigns to ensure that performance statistics for each can be isolated.

Create at least one campaign that targets all products in the feed.

  • This gives advertisers the ability to continually gain insights about products in the feed that may deserve attention but aren’t currently captured by other product groups.

Test different titles, images, and the granularity of product targets.

  • Include all relevant information possible in your title (255-character limit).
  • Add relevant search terms that will trigger your product queries.
  • Include brand and product type in the title. If applicable, add the gender, size, and color.

Use the product tracking destination URL for dynamic tracking.

Target specific products in your feed.

  • Beyond the ‘all-inclusive product’ target, include specific brands, product types, or individual products that align with your business goals.

Use negative keywords

  • Use negative keywords to prevent your products from showing in response to irrelevant or poor-performing searches.

Get as granular as possible with the category levels – categorize products at the lowest categorical level, and adjust your bids based on the item.

  • For example, Apparel & Accessories > Clothing > Pants > Jeans> Item ID.
  • First broken out by top category level (Apparel & Accessories), then drilled to the 4th category level (Jeans), then broken out by ID level to target top selling items and increase their bids to achieve top positioning.
  • Excluded “All Products” as this campaign focuses only on jeans. This “All Products” target should live in your main Bing Shopping Campaign, but always have an All Products group active. The more granular you get, the higher you should bid.

Use custom labels within your campaign to better target.

  • If you use custom labels in your product feed for best sellers, high margin products, items with high/low return on ad spend, or even by seasonal event, you can then target by these in your campaign.

Use campaign priority settings to indicate which of your campaigns should serve with priority over others.

  • Use campaign priority settings to have your top campaigns serve ahead of your non-performing campaigns by setting them to “High” priority.
  • If a Bing Shopping campaign is set at “Low” priority, the campaign with the highest bid will serve.
    • Low – the default setting for regular Bing Product Ads.
    • Medium – Will serve Bing Shopping campaigns over standard Product Ads campaigns.
    • High – Will serve over your standard Product Ads campaigns and any Bing Shopping campaigns that are set at medium.

9.3 Best practices: Performance tracking 

Routinely monitor performance.

  • Regularly monitor Bing Shopping campaign performance through the UI, campaign report, and product group reports.

Use the Shopping report to monitor performance of individual items.

  • This report found in the Dimensions tab can prove useful in optimizations – identifying which IDs are costing you without converting or converting well with a positive return.

Use Share of Voice (SOV) reporting for CPC, CTR, and impressions.

  • The SOV report is designed to help you identify the source and impact of lost impression share compared to competing ads in the marketplace and to provide only estimated impressions and click shares to help optimize your campaigns. It doesn't show exact delivery performance, but it does show records data if there is significant marketplace participation. The insights can be useful for bidding and positioning.

Use the search term report to negate and search terms you may deem irrelevant to your Bing Shopping campaigns.

  • Look at corresponding search negative terms within your search campaigns and use them for your BSC negatives.

Initially, allocate a similar budget for your Bing Shopping campaigns as you would to keywords for the same products.

  • Some advertisers have found success allocating 30% of their total budget to Product Ads is a good starting point.

Set your initial Product Group bids equal to or greater than similar Text Ad bids.

  • When new keywords are introduced into the marketplace for Text Ads, they need to have a high enough cost-per-click to be shown. Product Ads need the same.

Set bid adjustments for smartphone and tablet audiences.

  • To reach mobile audiences, advertisers should modify their bid boosts for smartphone and tablet devices (from -100% to +100%), from within the campaign UI
  • Product Ads are only available in the United States for PCs, tablets, and smartphones, and in the United Kingdom and Australia for PCs and tablets

﻿10. FAQ

Q: Could a Text Ad and a Product Ad for the same product, and from the same advertiser, serve at the same time?
A: Relevance and bid will determine what ads we show for Text or Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns. It’s possible that both ad types, from the same advertiser, for the same product, will be displayed at the same time.

Q: Can Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns be associated at the ad-group level?
A: Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns can be associated at the campaign and ad group level. Users will associate their store at campaign level, then any ad groups created under that campaign will be associated with that same store. The hierarchy is just like Text Ads – for example, ad group settings trump campaign settings.

Q: What are the differences between Search Ads and Product Ads?
A: Search Ads use keywords for ads to be triggered while Product Ads don’t.

4012926 
Product Ads from Bing Shopping campaigns are displayed when a searcher enters terms in Bing that are relevant to products in an advertiser’s Bing Merchant Center store.

Q: Is there a way to move a Bing Shopping campaign to the top position?
A:

  • All ads will have a starting of sidebar 4 – that is, position 4, but this is the best spot.
  • Average position is no longer a modifiable setting in the UI grids (was driving support volume), but this may still be listed in the reports.
    • If asked, let a customer know that this position isn’t an issue.
    • Unlike Text Ads which do better at the top, studies show that the position in sidebars deliver similar click-through rates for product ads.
    • What makes a difference for click-through rates are: Price, seller name, and title. For example, a known store or name brand will do better than unknown brands.

Q: Are Bing Shopping campaigns similar to what other search advertising platforms offer?
A:They are similar to the Google Shopping campaigns with a few variances in feed files, but they are not like Amazon’s Product Ads.

Q: Can I use my Google- import file?
A: Yes. Bing Merchant Center supports the use of Google data feeds for catalog feed files. This way merchants won’t have to redo the work already done for Google’s product listing ads.

Q: We are a small business and use an external checkout option. Is this OK?
A: Yes. It’s common for small businesses to use external checkouts, and sometimes this is the safer option for shoppers. If the checkout is secure, and all personal consumer information entered on the site (such as account creation and billing information) is SSL protected, this should be OK.

Q: Are Bing Shopping campaigns available to non-retail advertisers?
A: Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns will only serve for queries that have “commercial intent” (such as, “Canon Rebel”). Thus, only real ads from retail advertisers with product offers will serve.

Q: What is the cost for Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: Product Ads works on a pay per click model. While you bid on keywords for Text Ads, for Product Ads, you will instead bid on specific products and their attributes within a Bing Shopping campaign.

Q: Should we reduce spend on Text Ads (for products) if we use Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: The two will have different costs per click, different click-through rates and different conversion rates. We provide reporting that enables advertisers to make the appropriate decisions about where to assign their advertising dollars.

Q: Is geo-targeting or device targeting available?
A: Geo-targeting within the US is available. Desktop, tablet, and mobile targeting are also currently available through bid adjustments.

Q: What’s the mobile experience like for Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: Product Ads are now showing up on hi-fi mobile phones (Android and iOS only) in the US to increase advertiser clicks and their marketing reach across devices. Customers cannot choose to opt in or out of this, but they can modify their bid boost for mobile device (from -100% to +100%) from within the campaign UI. Three Product Ads will serve at the top of the page with or without Text Ads.

Q: Could a Text Ad and a Product Ad for the same product, and from the same advertiser, serve at the same time?
A: It’s possible that a Text Ad and a Product Ad from the same advertiser for the same product will be displayed at the same time. Relevance and bid will determine what ads we show for Text or Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns.

Q: Can I set different bids on different product groups?
A: You can set and assign bids for different product groups. Groups can be defined using various elements/filters, such as brand, product category, or SKU.

Q: What information will be displayed in the links/images?
A: For Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns, the following elements will be displayed as a part of the ad copy: Product title or description, a product image, promotion text (45 characters, via hover-over) and price.

Q: Can I export Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: Yes.

Q: Will I be able to set different bids on the Product Groups (formerly Product Targets)?
A: You can set bids on different product groups. Product groups can be defined using various elements or filters, such as brand, product category, or SKU.

Q: Can you filter by SKU or MPID (For example SKUs 0-100 or MPID’s 100-200)?
A: You can filter at the Item ID level, not SKUs. To filter for a group of item IDs, you can use a custom filtering field.

Q: Is there a way to exclude products using negative keywords?
A: Yes. Advertisers should use negative keywords to exclude products.

Q: Do I need to enter promotional text to create an ad?
A: Promotional text is not required. You can leave the field blank and move on to create your product groups. When you select Save, a Product Ad will be created without promotional text.

Q: Is there a way to have better keyword targeting in our feeds?
A: This is located within the UI as the Search Term report. If you have a Bing Shopping campaign, specify this and you will see search queries and the product groups they are matching to.

Q: Can we exclude products/categories within an "all products" group?
A: Yes, you can exclude product groups or sub-categories in the UI.

Q: What if we have over 20,000 products? Would you still suggest targeting Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns at the product level?
A: With the way product groups are structured in Bing Shopping campaigns, offers not covered in specific product group filters are still covered in each of the main hierarchical product sections unless they are specifically excluded. For example, if an “All Products” product group was created, and specific subgroups were created (for example, Apparel & Accessories), other products not included within the specifically bidded subgroups will be included in an “Everything else” grouping, which ensures 100% coverage of all offers (unless specifically excluded).

Q: Does accelerated delivery work with Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: Yes. Bing Shopping campaigns follow the same budget settings as regular search campaigns. In the campaign settings, you can also set priority (low/medium/high) for campaigns to appear, regardless of your bid amount.

Q: When will we be able to use comma separated values for product labels?
A: This is not available in the current Bing Shopping campaign experience. Custom labels are single value fields both on Google and on Bing.

Q: Will Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns appear in certain ad positions only (for example, mainline or sidebar)?
A: Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns may appear in the mainline or sidebar positions alongside other Product Ads.

Q: Google won’t serve a PLA unless there are three advertisers who are offering the product. Does this happen with Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: No, not at this time, but this is being considered for the future.

Q: How do Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns interact with other search ads? For example, what’s the impact on Rich Ads in search or ads with Sitelink Extensions?
A: Product Ads coexist with other ad formats on the search results page and thereby generate incremental visibility for advertisers.

Q: Will advertisers be able to bid on brand or item ID with Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: Yes, advertisers can continue to bid on brand and item ID.

Q: If a merchant is not physically in an available market, but sells products or has a presence in the US, can they use Product Ads or Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: If merchant is not physically located in the United States, the merchant can still qualify for “having a presence in an available market” as long as they meet all of the following criteria:

  • Merchant has an available market Microsoft Advertising shell – this will be evident if the Product Ads checkbox is available as an option in the BMC store under the Store Settings tab.
  • Merchant is paying for clicks with available market currency – check the Accounts & Billing tab in Microsoft Advertising to find the payment method.
  • Products on website are sold in available market currency.
  • Website content is in available market language.
  • Merchant’s ads are targeting available markets – check the campaigns tab in Microsoft Advertising and find the Bing Shopping campaign. If you select Settings, you will see the locations that the ads are targeting. If there are no campaigns created, and they pass the rest of the requirements, then you can approve the store.
  • Merchant is not located in mainland China.

Q: If a catalog's feed setting is set to automatic, and the advertiser uploads another feed before the first feed goes live, will the first feed be preempted by the newly-loaded feed?
A: No. Needs are processed as they come. Both feeds would be processed in the order they were received.


Reporting and tracking FAQ

Q: Can I see how many products are being targeted by my Bing Shopping campaigns with respect to my overall products in the Bing Merchant Store?
A: Yes, the Product Match Count report will provide this information. Columns supported in the report are: Campaign ID, Campaign Name, Ad Group ID, Ad Group Name, Number of Products Matched (one row per campaign and ad group), and Total Number of Products Submitted at the Store Level.

Q: How will the KW performance report work with Bing Shopping campaigns data?
A: The KW performance report is not applicable to Bing Shopping campaigns. The Product Target Report (which is similar to the KW performance report) is specifically intended to provide Bing Shopping campaign data at the start of the Beta.

Q: What performance reports are available for Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: Performance reporting is available at the product group and offer-level under Microsoft Advertising standard reports. The reports will reflect changes of the performance of the offers/products currently within each product group.

Q: Is there a report that tells me how many offers are displaying?
A: Yes, the Publishing Report is available under the Catalog Management tab for your store. You can also check this in the Products tab within the shopping campaigns. This will give you an amount of products associated with the campaign.

Q: How do I assess average position metrics for Product Ads in Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: We currently do not have average position reporting capabilities.

Q: Where are the Product Ads-specific search query report located for Bing Shopping campaigns?
A: You can use the standard search query report, and just filter by your Bing Shopping campaigns. If you are having trouble, reach out to your Account Manager.

Q: Is there a way to add revenue to the reports or the UI?
A: We’re investigating how to accommodate this once this product releases.

Q: Can we view conversion data at the Product Group level?
A: You can add the conversion data columns when you’re defining your Product Group report.
