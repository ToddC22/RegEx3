Scenario
 

  • Customers may contact Commerce Support saying that they cannot complete a purchase or that they received the following message when purchasing: The invoice order will require a credit check, for which we will need to contact you to get additional information. By selecting this check box, you agree to be contacted by us for the credit check.”
  • Customers in the Store for Business contact support saying that when they try to purchase offline licenses, even when using a credit card, they receive the following message:  Your Organization is not Eligible to purchase offline licenses this time.
  • A customer who has recently undergone a credit check adds more licenses to their subscription which puts them over their credit limit.  The customer contacts Support asking how to avoid the second credit check.

2. Guidance

Advocates are not authorized to unlock the shopping cart. See the Procedure section for steps to handle customers who contact Commerce Support when their cart has been locked.

a. Invoice pay threshold

Customers must meet a purchase threshold to be eligible for invoice pay as a payment method. The Total of the purchase in the shopping Cart (including tax) is the amount used to determine if invoice pay will be offered as a payment method.  The monthly or annual cost of other active orders on the tenant are not used to calculate the invoice threshold for the current purchase. (For invoice thresholds by country and by billing system, see 3091140: Helping Invoice Pay customers with issues and questions .)

b. Preapproved credit limit

The purchase threshold for most customers before a credit check is required is $5,000 U.S. Dollars (USD) or local currency equivalent outside the United States. See the Default Credit Limits for all countries here.

Note: If customers pay for subscription by credit card or bank account, any new licenses that they buy are available immediately after they receive an order confirmation. If customers pay by invoice, they might have to wait for a credit check before their new licenses are available to use.

Note: Customers who pay by invoice and later wish to change to payment by credit card will need to purchase a new subscription (using a credit card) and cancel the old (invoice) subscription. See 2765235: How to change a subscription between monthly and annual billing as well as credit card and invoice .

c. Verify the customer’s threshold balance

The Invoice Credit Check Threshold balance is the amount (the sum of open invoices and credits) that a customer can carry before the customer’s cart is locked.

To check the customer’s threshold balance in CMAT:

  1. Load the customer / tenant in CMAT.
  2. Go to the PAYMENT INSTRUMENTS tab.
  3. Expand Invoice Pay.
    • If the customer has multiple Invoice Pay purchases, there will be multiple Invoice Pay PI listings (one for each order).
    • If Invoice Pay is not listed, the customer has not completed an Invoice Pay purchase.
  4. Look for the Current Invoice Credit Check Threshold amount within the Details section.  The amount indicated is the remaining amount the customer can purchase before the cart is locked.

3. Procedure

Emails sent to customers regarding credit checks:

1. Request for Financial Statements and Accounts Payable contact:
Customer’s shopping cart is locked (i.e. their Invoice Pay order is over the credit limit and must be reviewed and approved by GFS Risk):

a. Tier 1 steps for online cases

NOTE: Locked carts (e.g. Credit checks) typically take up to 24 hours to be processed. If the purchase has been made within the past 24 hours, refrain from escalating to GFS (Global Financial Services) Risk.
Instead, reaffirm with the customer that their order is being reviewed and thank them for their patience.

If the cart has been locked for 24 hours or more continue with steps below:

  1. Create an assistance request for GFS Risk.
  2. Include below mandatory details in the assistance request:
  • Case#:
  • Company Name:
  • Tenant ID:
  • Country:
  • AP Contact Name:
  • AP Contact Email Address:
  • Phone Number:
  • Subscription ID/Name:
  • Total Order Value (In local currency):
  • Number of Licenses Requested:(from & to)
  • Requested Credit Limit (USD): 
  1. Information for the Subject line: Subscription Type/Credit Check/Customer Type/Severity/Country/Type of Escalation.
  2. Transfer the assistance request to GFS Risk.
  3. Close the case after GFS Risk closes the assistance request. 


b. Steps for GFS Risk

  1. GFS Risk will send the credit application to the customer.
  2. Once the credit application is received and processed, GFS Risk will either unlock the shopping cart or decline the order.
  3. GFS Risk will email the Global and/or Billing administrator(s) that their order is either approved or denied.
  4. GFS Risk will then close the assistance request.


c. Tier 1 steps for phone cases:

For phone cases, follow the same steps as for an online case. Suggested scripting:

“We apologize for the inconvenience. We would need to engage another team to investigate further. Please allow us up to 2 business days to get an update on the unlock request. As soon as we get an update or if we may need additional time, we will immediately contact you within that period.” 

SLA for GFS Risk to unlock the shopping cart and contact the customer:

If an existing customer has a 30+ day unpaid invoice, GFS Risk will not unlock the cart until payment has been received, even if the current order plus any existing recurring charges is less than $15,000 (USD or local currency equivalent). A representative from GFS Risk will contact the customer to discuss the past due balance.

For all locked cart scenarios, GFS Risk will either unlock the cart or decline the order and email the global or billing administrator with their decision, based on the following SLAs:
 
  • For orders less than 25,000 USD or local currency equivalent (for emerging markets): 2 business days
  • For orders greater than 25,000 USD or local currency equivalent (for emerging markets): 5 business days.
  • For orders less than 50,000 USD or local currency equivalent (for mature markets): 2 business days
  • For orders greater than 50,000 USD or local currency equivalent (for mature markets): 5 business days.

If the customer had exceeded their country’s credit market threshold, the customer will be required by GFS Risk to provide the latest two years Audited Financial Statement of their account. Please see the sample email that GFS Risk will send to the customer here. Please see the following country credit market threshold:

  • APAC – 25K USD (except Japan & Singapore, Australia, New Zealand)
  • India – 15K USD
  • North America – 50K USD
  • EMEA (Western Europe) - 50K USD
  • MEA (Middle East Asia)/CEE (Central and Eastern Europe) – 25K USD
  • LATAM – 25K USD

    For EMEA countries, please refer to here for details.

Note: Audited Financial Statements are reviewed by a Certified Public Accountant and notarized, which is different from traditional financial statements.

Customers in the Store for Business contact support saying they receive a message stating their organization is not eligible to purchase offline licenses even if using a credit card.

If the customer contacts support with this issue, a credit check is required before the customer can purchase.  Send a request to GFS Risk to valid credit eligibility. Once the credit limit is raised, the customer will be able to purchase.

If the customer wants more information about this scenario, the suggested  advocate scripting below can be used: Since offline licenses are self-licensing, we require a review with Microsoft credit services before a customer is authorized to purchase them. The minimum credit limit for offline license purchasing is US$30,000.

A customer who has recently undergone a credit check adds more licenses to their subscription which puts them over their recently-approved credit limit. The customer contacts Support asking how to avoid the second credit check.
  • If the customer is contacting support to avoid this new credit check, create an assistance request  for GFS Risk asking for the credit line to be increased. GFS Risk still has to review and approve the credit limit increase request. Their SLAs are the same as for the “Cart Locked” scenario documented in this article. The following information is required by GFS Risk:
