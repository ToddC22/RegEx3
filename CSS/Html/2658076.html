ASMS – Wire Transfer Accounts, Payment Addresses, and W-9s

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides guidance on helping customers obtain payment instructions or W-9 tax forms that may be needed to make payments via wire transfer, also known as electronic funds transfer (EFT).

Note: The guidance in this TSG is for invoice customers, not credit card customers. The only exception is for credit card customers doing a one-time wire transfer payment.

Support Scenarios

Customers may contact support for assistance making wire transfer payments. In these instances, leverage the following guidance.

Payment Instructions

Customers paying via wire transfer/EFT must route their payments to specific Microsoft accounts and addresses based on the customer’s “bill-to” country or region. These payment instructions are available on the customer’s invoice and in the Pay for your subscription public-facing documentation.

A customer might contact support for the instructions to send their wire transfer payment under the following circumstances:

  • The payment instructions on their invoice are unavailable.
  • They are a credit card customer making a one-time wire transfer.
  • The information on the Pay for your subscription website is unavailable.

If you receive a case requesting payment instructions for a wire transfer payment from Brazil, India, or Japan, provide the customer with the appropriate payment instructions from the following table based on their “bill-to” country.

If you are unable to find the address listed on the public-facing page referenced, or the customer is unable to see it on their invoice, reach out to your technical advisor (TA) using the guidance in TSG 2929022. In return, your TA will work with the GFS-aligned TAs to obtain the address.

Microsoft’s W-9 Tax Form

Customers may contact support requesting Microsoft’s W-9 tax form to make their wire transfer payment.

If you receive such a case, provide the customer with the form for the year requested:

  • 2020 W9
  • 2019 W9
  • 2018 W9
  • 2017 W9
