Broad Commercial / Pro Customers

These customers do not have a TAM that can follow up on the status of their DCR. The preferred method is for them to leave feedback per 2529942 instead of filing a DCR. If they would like to proceed with a change request anyway, contact a member of IET to determine if a DCR is warranted.

Premier Customers

When a Premier customer requires a change to the service/product that is not there today they have the ability to request the change.  However, the customer will need to work with their TAM to fill out a Business Impact Statement that goes into full detail on what they want change, why they want it changed, and what the impact to the business is without the change being implemented.

After the Business Impact Statement has been obtained then engage the Intune escalation team as per KB 4482666. Be sure to also fill out the appropriate escalation template

After the DCR has been submitted, we cannot commit to any timelines for when the change will go into place or even if the Product Group will accept the change request.

After the bug has been filed then provide the ICM link and bug # to the TAM and close out the service request. DCR's are generally evaluated by the PG every 90 days so the TAM will need to follow up on status after then.

The TAM can obtain status by navigating to https://icm.ad.msft.net/imp/v3/incidents/search/advanced , searching for the bug # to see the latest status or by contacting the ICM owner (currently Myra Lopez mylopez@microsoft.com)

IMPORTANT Filing a DCR does not guarantee that any change will be implemented. Support tickets should be closed once DCR is raised.

Business Impact Statement 

Part 1 – To be completed by the Microsoft engineer

1. Case Number

2. Company Name

3. Issue Description (Please enter the problem details of the customers design change request and why current design is limiting)

Part 2 – To be completed by the Customer and/or Technical Account Manager

Note: Please answer all questions and provide as much information as possible.  The information provided in this section is used by the product development team to gauge the severity of the issue.

1. What is the total Intune licenses count and number users and/or devices that are affected by this issue?

2. What is the frequency of the issue?

3. How does this issue impact the end users?

Please provide a brief description of how the issue affects the users or the scenario Eg 1 : "Unable to access website using Client auth cert via Managed Browser". Eg: 2 "IT Pro can't identify retired devices inn Intune on Azure console"

4. How would you rate the impact that this issue has on your business?  Use the following guidance when choosing your answer and provide supporting detail:

Customer Priority:

    P1 - Deployment blocker

    P2 - Will continue to use service without this but High Priority

    P3 - Nice to have

Please include specific details such as potential financial impact, project delays, lost productivity hours from users etc.


5. Will this issue have any impact on subscription renewal?

6. In a few sentences, please describe how Intune is being used in your organization in general and how the functionality affected by this problem is being used?

7. How is the problem keeping you from performing specific business functions? Please provide details around how the problem that the case was opened for affects the user’s ability to perform certain business functions (e.g. Users can't access internal website via Managed Browser)

8. Are there alternate solutions or workarounds available? (If yes, please include the details of the workaround)

9. If one or more workarounds have been suggested, please explain why the workarounds are not acceptable.
