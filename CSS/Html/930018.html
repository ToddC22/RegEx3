What is this article about?

This article details how to handle account takeover claims on a customer's Microsoft account. Follow it when a customer suspects their Microsoft account has been compromised.

​​1. Account Takeover on a Microsoft account

Note: If the customer receives the error message "Help us secure your account" at sign in, follow the process in KB 2980022 Procedure: Microsoft account: Help us secure your account.

Any time a customer states their account has been compromised, the customer must secure their account. Common scenarios may be:

  • "I noticed some charges on my bank account I do not recognize."
  • “I am getting a message on my console or on my PC saying, ‘Help us secure your account.’”
  • "I think someone changed my gamertag."
  • "I am getting a message when I sign into my profile saying my profile was last signed in on another console."

2. Securing the customer’s account

If the customer can access their account, use the following process to help secure their account and identify possible risks to their account security.

  1. If the customer has a compromised account message on their console, guide the customer through the compromise workflow on their Xbox One console, following the process in KB 2980022 Procedure: Microsoft account: Help us secure your account.
  2. Advise the customer to change their password by signing in to their Microsoft account at Security & privacy and then going to the https://account.microsoft.com/ section. 

Explain that it’s a good idea to choose a password that they do not use elsewhere.

  • If the customer is not able to sign into their account, follow the steps for password reset account recovery in KB 2984712: Procedure: Microsoft account: Password reset.
  • If the customer can regain access to the account: Follow step 3 to verify if there is any unfamiliar activity on the account. Review the Microsoft account status in ASD for any recent security lock changes.
  • If the customer receives a denial from the Account Recovery form or the form did not recognize the customer’s Microsoft account, review the Microsoft account's status in ASD for any recent security lock changes. Ask the customer to visit Signup.live.com to register for a new Microsoft account using a different/new email address:
  1. Make sure no Xbox profile is associated with this new Microsoft account.
  2. All resolution emails are sent to the email address used to register for the new Microsoft account.
  3. Obtain the ACSR number from the customer.
  4. Ask the customer if they use a Microsoft domain for email (@outlook, @MSN, @Live, @Hotmail):
  • Yes, ask the customer for at least three names of any email folders created and record them in the case notes.
  • Yes, ask the customer for at least three email contacts from the account’s address book.
  • Yes, ask the customer for at least three recent subjects of emails sent from the account.
  1. Ask the customer if they have any payment instruments on the account.
  • Yes, ask the customer to provide the last four digits of the payment instrument and the expiration date.
  1. Check their account for XUID or ask the customer if they have an Xbox:
  • Yes, ask the customer for their Xbox console ID/device ID. Follow the steps in the articles below:
  • Find the Xbox One console ID, device ID, Xbox One serial number, and Kinect serial number
  • Find the Xbox 360 serial number, Kinect sensor serial number, and Xbox 360 console ID
  1. Engage the case as an account takeover using the engagement process found in section 3, Engagement, below.

Note: Do not lock the billing account or cancel the subscription, even at the customer’s request.

  1. Ask the customer to review recent activity on their account by selecting See my recent activity in the Security & privacy section of https://account.microsoft.com/. If the customer finds any unfamiliar activity or still believes the account is compromised, continue to step 4.

Note: Locations on this page are based on IP address and are approximate in order to protect the customer’s privacy. Ask the customer to look for consistency rather than exactness of location, as some Internet providers might route the IP address through a different location.

  1. Ask the customer to review their account information in the Your info section of https://account.microsoft.com/. Ask the customer to edit their personal info and billing info to ensure there have been no changes, additions, or deletions of account information.
  2. Ask the customer to review their account alias in the Your info section of https://account.microsoft.com/ and select Manage how you sign into Microsoft to make sure that no changes, additions, or deletions have been made to their account alias.
  3. Ask the customer to review their security info in the Security & privacy section of https://account.microsoft.com/ and select More security settings to make sure that no changes, additions, or deletions have been made to their security information.
  4. Ask the customer to sign in to https://OneDrive.live.com and select Shared to review the folders and files that the customer is currently sharing to make sure they’re not sharing anything with people that they don’t know or that the shares they previously made weren’t deleted. (Refer to How to share file in OneDrive to guide the customer).
  5. If the account is an Outlook or Hotmail email account, ask the customer to sign in to https://outlook.com, select the settings icon and then Options. Check the following settings and restore any that might have been removed or changed:
  • Mail > Automatic Processing > Automatic Replies
  • Mail > Automatic Processing > Inbox and Sweep Rules
  • Mail > Accounts > Connected accounts
  • Mail > Accounts > Forwarding
  • Mail > Junk Mail > Blocked senders
  • Mail > Junk Mail > Safe mailing list
  • Mail > Junk Mail > Safe senders
  • Mail > Junk Mail > Filters and reporting
  • Mail > Layout > Email signature
  1. Ask the customer to review their purchase and order history in the Payment & billing section of https://account.microsoft.com/. Ask the customer to review all recent orders since the unfamiliar account activity.
  • If the customer finds any unrecognized charges, ask the customer if they have shared their account with other family members. If so, ask the customer to check with those family members if they made any of the purchases.
  • If the customer confirms a family member made the purchases and is requesting a refund, follow KB 4338531 Procedure: Return/Refund: Unauthorized purchase by a family member/known person to find if an exception can be made for a refund.
  • If the customer has a question about a charge on their financial institution statement that they do not find in the Payment & billing: Order history here are some common reasons that could occur:
  • The customer may have a subscription that has renewed. There may be a delay from the time the subscription renewed to the time the renewal charge appears on the customer’s credit card statement.
  • The customer may have another Microsoft account with the payment instrument on it. Phone Support advocates can attempt to search for other accounts found with a payment instrument on them and attempt to verify the customer. See KB 2885545 Procedure: Handling a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner.
  • Charges from multiple purchases may be grouped together as one charge on a statement.
  • There may have been a retry of a previous charge that was declined.
  • A bank may have placed an authorization hold because of a pending order, or the customer added, changed or removed a credit card from their financial account.
  • If the customer has identified unrecognized charges or transactions on the account and they have verified the transactions were not made by them or any member of their household; follow the instructions in section 3.

Note: Do not lock the billing account or cancel the subscription, even at the customer’s request.

3. Engagement

3.1 When should I engage with the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates?

Ensure the customer has been walked through securing their account. Troubleshoot any issues on the customer’s account.

Only engage as an account takeover case for escalation if:

  • The customer cannot recover the account (due to the Microsoft account password being or security proofs changing).

If the customer can successfully recover the account, only engage as an account takeover case in any of these situations:

  • The unauthorized charges amount to more than $500 from a console the customer has never owned.
  • To investigate the account, as there is a previous request with UA/ATO or Unauthorized Access/Account Takeover on the same account.
  • License transfer occurred without the customer’s knowledge.
  • A region migration occurred without the customer’s knowledge.

If none of the conditions above have been met and the customer insists on further investigation due to loss of a special gamertag, engage with Advanced Capability Advocates team, include the reason why this is a special request. Otherwise, the engagement may be declined when no special request is mentioned.

3.1.1 Scenarios you should not engage Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates 

Do not engage with Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates if:

  • The customer secured the account and unauthorized charges amount to less than $500, with no previous refunds provided on the account. See KB 928204 Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges.
  • An Xbox gamertag change occurred on the customer’s account.
  • The account password has not been updated in the last 60 days and the customer cannot recover the account:
  1. Refund all disputed transactions (should be less than $500).

    Note: If further investigation with the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates is requested, the refund should not be issued by Frontline.
  2. If a gamertag change occurred, issue CSV in the amount of a gamertag change for the customer’s region ($10 plus tax for customers in the US). For more information, see KB 928204 Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges.
  3. Code the SR with the following taxonomy:
    • Why the customer called: I have a question or issue with my Microsoft account.
    • Root cause: Account Compromise
    • Solution Delivered: Educated customer
    • Subject line: Add ATO to the front of the subject line.
      • Note: Only issue $10 CSV if you do not engage with Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates.
  • Banned Accounts
    The customer's Xbox Live profile is banned, see KB     981729 Procedure: Xbox Live: Enforcement activity (bans).
  • Error code present on the customer’s account
    Use the Error Code Lookup tool in ASD; if it is an unknown error, engage as an unknown issue – billing and account. See KB     817557 Guideline: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement.
  • Fraudulent account creation
    If the customer claims fraudulent account creation, see KB     928204 Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges.

3.2 How to engage with Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates

Before engaging, make sure to follow the process in section 2, Securing the customer’s account if you have not already done so. Use the steps below to protect the customer’s account from further access:

  • Xbox One customers: Ask the customer to create a passkey: support.xbox.com/xbox-one/security/create-xbox-one-passkey.
  • Xbox 360 customers: Use the Trusted Console tool in ASD to require the Microsoft account credentials to be entered for all consoles.

Document all troubleshooting and code the SR with the following taxonomy to activate the ATO engagement form. You must have all the following information in the SR for the engagement to be valid:

  • Lifecycle: Account & Billing
  • Line of Business: Microsoft account
  • Engagement Type: Account Takeover
  • Advanced Capability Engagement Form (ATO taxonomy):

Note: When the customer creates a Microsoft account, ensure they use an alternate email address that is not listed on their current Microsoft account. The resolution is sent to this email. Once this is done, the customer signs into account.live.com and selects Your Info to update the Name, Birthdate, and Country. If the customer is going to use a third-party for their Microsoft account, ensure the customer signs into their third-party email and validates the email address.

(If any of the above steps are missed, the customer’s resolution will be delayed.)

  1. Can the customer recover the account after ACSR process?
    • Yes, the following fields populate:
      • ACSR number: Provide number if possible or reason it can’t be given.
      • Unauthorized charges amount (in USD)
  1. Were there security proof(s) the changed that the customer does not recognize?
    • No, the following fields populate:
      • ACSR number: Provide a number if possible or the reason it can’t be given.
  1. Send an email to the customer requesting the following information using the Data Transfer and Management tool process. See KB 4135034 Tool User Guide: Data Transfer & Management (DTM).
    • Name
    • Birth date
    • Country/Region
    • Region/State
    • Postal/ZIP code
    • Contact phone
    • Alternate email address (cannot be listed on an account, register for a new Microsoft account at http://account.microsoft.com. The resolution will be sent to this email account.)
      When you create a new Microsoft account, make sure to sign into account.live.com and select Your Info to update the following information: Name, Birthdate, Country. If you are using a third-party for your Microsoft account, sign into your third-party email and validate the email address.
    • The email addresses of several contacts from address book (at least three emails)
    • Recent subjects of email sent from the account (at least three subjects)
    • The names of any email folders created.
    • Billing information used for recent purchases or subscription.
    • IP address where the account is often used? Use a computer or a mobile device connected to the same network as the primary console and search for “IP Address” at bing.com. The Xbox web browser app available on the console may not have the features to correctly display the webpages from the results and may provide inaccurate information.
  1. Xbox customers provide the console serial number(s) and Device ID (Xbox One)/Console ID (Xbox 360) where the profile has been recovered/downloaded. See https://support.xbox.com/en-US/my-account/warranty-and-service/find-xbox-one-kinect-serial-number and https://support.xbox.com/en-US/my-account/warranty-and-service/serial-number.
  2. Is this an Xbox customer:
    • Yes, the following fields populate:
      • Primary console ID (360)/Device ID (One) (Use N/A)
        • Primary Console Serial Number (Use N/A)
        • Can customer recover the account?
    • No, the following fields will pop up:
      • Unauthorized charges amount (in USD)
        • Did the customer create a new Microsoft account? (Yes/No)
        • Was there recent password change? (Yes/No)
        • Was there a Microsoft account or security proof(s) changed? (Yes/No)
        • Was there a region migration? (Yes/No)
        • Was there a license transfer? (Yes/No)
        • Is there a previous SR containing "Unauthorized Access”/“Account Takeover” or “UA”/“ATO” in subject line? (Yes/No)
        • Have you discarded or sold your console in the past year? (Yes/No)
  1. Is the primary account user an adult or a child? (Adult/Child)

How to obtain Device ID (Xbox One) or Console ID (Xbox 360) information

  • Console ID - Xbox 360
  • Device ID - Xbox One

To find the Device ID on the Xbox One:

  1. On the Xbox Dashboard, go to Settings and select System.
  2. Select Console Info.
  3. The Xbox Live Device ID is displayed on the screen. (See Xbox One system settings.)

To find the console ID on the Xbox 360:

  1. In the Xbox Dashboard, go to Settings and select System.
  2. Select Console Settings.
  3. Select System Info.
  4. The console ID is displayed on the screen, below the console serial number. (See Find the Xbox 360 serial number, Kinect sensor serial number, and Xbox 360 console ID.)
Note: Console/device ID is different than console serial number. Ensure the customer understands which number they are collecting.

Note: If the customer cannot provide the CID or DID, see KB 928204 Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges.

4. Account Takeover – FAQ

4.1 Console

Q: Can I get my gamertag back?

A: A thorough investigation will need to be performed, but if we discover that your gamertag was taken from you we will assign it back to your account.

Q: Why is my Xbox live Gold not working?

A: Allow 72 hours for all updates to complete after the Microsoft account swap.

Q: Can I change my gamertag?

A: If your gamertag was created for you when you initially signed up for Xbox (meaning you did not choose it yourself), you can change it one time for free. Additional Xbox gamertag changes cost extra. (fee varies by region and currency).

Q: Can you give me a free month of Xbox live?

A: If there was not delay on our end we will not; however, as a courtesy we can always consider the compensation.

Q: How do you know the purchases I claimed were UA originated from my console?

A: Purchases leave a digital footprint which include the IP, Console ID (CID) and geo location of where the purchases originated from.

Q: Why did you cancel my subscription?

A: To move your profile over to a new Microsoft account, your subscription was cancelled. You will be provided with tokens for any unused time you had remaining on your sub.

4.2 Billing

Q: When will I get my refund?

A: Refunds provided in ASD by line item can take three to five business days up to 30 days. Manual Checks can take up to eight weeks.

Q: The attacker sold/deleted/changed/traded-in my game players/points, how do I get them back?

A: You need to contact the developer.

Q: Can you provide me with a list and date of the purchases made on my account?

A: We are unable to provide that information via email; however, you can review your order at https://account.microsoft.com/billing/orders.

Q: Where are the options to remove a payment instrument for the account?

A: Sign into the Microsoft account you want to change. Select Payment & billing, then select Payment options. Select the payment option you want to delete and then follow the instructions to remove your payment instrument. Note: If the payment instrument is used to pay for a subscription, you'll need to first cancel your subscription.

Q: How did my card get added to the account after I removed it?

A: Payment instruments can only be added by someone having access to it and the CVV number on the back of the card.

Q: After this ATO, I don't want to put my payment instrument on the account, what other options do I have?

A: You may use prepaid cards that can be bought at a local retailer.

Q: Why does Microsoft want to give me a check if my Bank Card was charged?

A: By law we can only issue refunds back to the payment instrument that made the charges. Should the payment instrument change we will need to issue a check for the refund.

4.3 Account

Q: How can I get my original email account back?

A: We can begin an account recovery for you if Two-Step Verification is not turned on. The process can take up to 60 to 90 days.

Q: Why do I need to wait 30 days to get my account back?

A: Our security feature does not allow us to override any attempts to update changes to the account.

Q: How do I recover deleted content from my account (for example, friends list, email, or OneDrive)?

A: Unfortunately, we cannot recover deleted items.

Q: How was my account compromised?

A: Social engineering, phishing, credential sharing.

Q: Why am I missing some of my DLC after you moved/swapped my account?

A: After a recovery on an account, it can take up to 72 hours for the account information and details to reflect on the new Microsoft account.

Q: Can you delete/remove my account?

A: I can assist you with the steps to delete/remove accounts. However, I cannot do it for you.

Q: Why did Microsoft allow someone to steal my account?

A: Account sharing, and third-party emails are the most common ways that accounts are compromised.

Q: Why do you need an alternate email address?

A: The email address that is currently on the account can potentially be under attack and accessed by an unknown user. With the new email we can ensure that you are the only one accessing the account.

Q: How do I change my email myself?

A: Refer to: http://support.xbox.com/my-account/microsoft-account/change-email-sign-in-for-microsoft-account.

Q: Are you going to prosecute the people that hacked my account?

A: Customers wanting to pursue the compromisers actions may do so by filing a police report with their local authorities.

Q: Did the attacker delete anything from my account?

A: We do not have insight to that.

Q: Why are you asking me to validate ownership of my account when the previous agent told me that they can see someone may have accessed my account?

A: Before we can begin an investigation, we first need to verify that we are speaking with the account owner.

4.4 Account security

Q: What is two step verification?

A: An additional security feature that allows only the user to verify the code based on the alt email/phone number you choose.

Q: What more can I do to protect my account?

A: Follow the tips detailed here to help keep your Microsoft account safer and make it easier to recover if it's compromised.

Q: Can I access my email during the 30 to 60 day wait? (Microsoft account customers)

A: Keeping your account locked during this time will prevent the compromiser from vetoing any changes and prevent them from turning on two step verification that would make recovering the account impossible.

Q: Has the attacker accessed my emails or OneDrive files?

A: They may have been due to the breach on the account. We cannot say for sure. We can only secure the account or suspend the account.

Q: Can I use the same password as before?

A: Changing your password and adding strong proofs may prevent your account from further compromise.

Q: Can the attacker who used my credit card view the full card number or just the last four digits?

A: For account security, your credit card number is encrypted except for the last four digits.

Q: Does Microsoft offer up to date alerts for when someone tries to access my account?

A: Yes, you’ll receive notifications. By default, this option is enabled on your Microsoft account security settings.

Q: How can I add parental controls?

A: To add parental controls, you need to set up a Microsoft family. Microsoft family is one of the many benefits of having a Microsoft account. It’s a free service that helps families stay connected and keep kids safer on Windows 10 and Xbox One devices, along with Android devices running Microsoft Launcher. You’ll find settings like activity reporting, screen time limits, location sharing, and content restrictions on family.microsoft.com, where you can also track kids’ spending and add money to their Microsoft accounts. Learn more here.

Q: What is the location of the person who stole my account?

A: For the safety and security of all Microsoft account owners, we are unable to disclose that information.

Q: Why did you change my Microsoft account?

A: To secure your Xbox profile and prevent further access to the compromiser, your account had to be moved over to a new Microsoft account. This ensure that only you are in control of the account.

Q: Why does it take so long to get my email back?

A: Due to security protocols changes can only be made once every 30 days.

4.5 Account suspension/banned

Q: How can I contact/speak with the Enforcement team?

A: They can be contacted only via request on Xbox.com https://enforcement.xbox.com.

Q: How can I report someone for sending threatening or inappropriate messages on Xbox Live?

A: Refer to the https://www.xbox.com/live/abuse/ page.

Q: What will happen to the person who took my account?

A: Our enforcement team reviews all claims of confirmed ATO and takes action against those individuals.

Q: Why is my account banned?

A: If you have any questions about a suspension, visit the Enforcement website https://enforcement.xbox.com/

Q: Can you remove the suspension/ban from my account?

A: Those issues are handled by our enforcement teams.

4.6 Miscellaneous

Q: Can you tell me who the attackers are?

A: Unfortunately, we are unable to provide that information, as it is proprietary to our investigation.

Q: Can I speak to your supervisor?

A: I can request a supervisor. It can take up to 24 hours for the contact to be made.

Q: What’s your direct phone number/email?

A: As we are an outbound call center, we do not have a direct number or email address. The number you see on your caller ID will call into frontline support if you need further assistance.

Q: What is an ACSR?

A: Automated Customer Service Representative, used to assist a customer in resetting access to their account.

Q: Can you provide me with the evidence you found during your investigation?

A: Due to Microsoft proprietary information we are unable to provide our processes of the investigation.

Q: Are you going to prosecute the people that hacked my account?

A: Customers wanting to pursue the compromisers actions may do so by filing a police report with their local authorities.

Q: Can you speed up the 30-day wait time?

A: No, the 30-day update process cannot be sped up due to security protocols.

Q: Why can’t I use the Microsoft account that I created with the Frontline advocates?

A: The Microsoft account created with the Frontline agent may not be completed or is invalid.
