Procedure: Case Closure

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Team Manager | Business Process Manager | Technical Account Manager

What is this article about?

The purpose of this procedure is to ensure a consistent experience for the customer/partner and ensure no unnecessary delay in closure.

Steps

Steps

  1. Obtain Customer or Partner approval to close the case if possible:
    • Document the response.
    • If they are not responding to your attempts to communicate, follow the KB 4457094 | Procedure: Unresponsive Customer.
  2. If the Customer or Partner wants to discuss additional issues:
    • Provide advise if the question is quick to answer.
    • If the issue needs detailed troubleshooting, request they open a separate support case or open one for them if possible.
    • For Rave engineers working on O365 cloud cases:
      • If the issue needs extensive troubleshooting, and it's within support agent's technical capability in the same workload, continue to work with the customer. You may create new case on behalf of the customer for case management purpose if needed.
      • If the issue is regarding a different technology/workload or can be supported better by a different SME, create a new case on behalf of the customer and transfer accordingly.
  3. If the Customer or Partner does not agree to close the case:
    • If they are dissatisfied follow the KB 4457522 | Procedure: Recovery.
    • If they request to monitor the incident beyond a reasonable timeframe for confirming the initial scope is resolved:
      • For Premier Customers and Partners, discuss with the Incident Manager (IM) for help setting customer expectation on case resolution.
      • For Non-Premier cases, discuss with your Manager the request for monitoring.
      • Set a Call Back Commitment depending on how long they would like to monitor.
  4. If a Customer or Partner closes their support request prior to troubleshooting or they request a full or partial refund or non-decrement, for:
    • Premier see the KB 4457427 | Premier Non-Decrement.
    • Non-premier see the KB 4455856 | Procedure: Professional Support and Broad Commercial Refund.
    • Unified Support as support is provided "as-needed" and not billed hourly, there is no need for a refund or non-decrement.
  5. Once the agreement to close the case has been received check the following are set or added correctly:
    • Customer's or Partner's contact details.
    • Product
    • Support Topic down to the lowest level possible:
      • Note: coding accurately to the lowest level of support topic gives serviceability the best insight into technologies that may be driving incident rate.
    • Symptom classification(s).
    • Cause classification(s) if known.
    • Bug information if applicable.
    • Solution Task if scoping has been completed.
  6. Select an appropriate closure email template (see KB 4459286 | Case Management Standards (CMS) Templates)
    • If the Customer, Partner or Incident Manager (IM) has requested case closure before Initial Response of Scoping is completed it is important they still receive a closure email:
      • Use Un-Scoped
    • If the resolution was provided during the initial scoping of the case:
      • Use Scope and Close
    • If the Customer or Partner requests temporary closure of the case (for example, if they will be on vacation):
      • Use Standard Break Fix Resolved or Not Resolved.
      • Do not push them into temporary closure of the case, this will lead to early survey of the case and possibly multiple surveys on the same case.
    • If the case is being closed as non-decrement (e.g. a bug has been found in a mainstream product) on a contract that would usually require a cost to the Customer or Partner:
      • Use Non-Decrement On-Premise.
    • If the case is Advisory:
      • Use Standard Advisory.
    • For any other closure:
      • Use the Standard Break Fix Resolved or Not Resolved.
      • If during closure you find it easiest to summarize your case using the Advisory format, consider if the case coding should be changed before closure.
  7. Complete and send your closure summary email to the Customer or Partner:
    • Do not delay sending the closure email, send it out very soon after closure confirmation received.
    • Consider copying the Incident Manager (IM), BCC. the additional contacts if more appropriate.
  8. Close the case:
    • The Service Request should be closed by the technical resource who communicated resolution:
      • Exception: If a translator assists the case owner to communicate the resolution, the Service Request should be closed by the case owner.
    • Set the Problem Type and the Problem and SR Resolution accurately:
      • For more information on Malware or Security Bulletins problem or case type usage see the KB 4456926 | Procedure: Malware and Security Bulletin Support.
      • See the KB 4456524 | Procedure: Resolution Codes for what resolution codes to use.
    • For Rave users, Resolution steps entered in Rave Summary Tab will be visible to some Premier customers reading from Admin Center, these steps should be concise and not simply a copy-paste of the full notes from the final email. Support will see a confirmation pop up before publishing resolution steps to the admin center.
      For more Q&A, screenshots, and best practices, please see Yammer conversation in the Rave Announcements Group: microsoft.com accounts | office365support.com accounts)
      • Detailed guidance on how to fill out Issue Description and Resolution Steps:
        • Issue Description guidance:
          • Describe the customer’s issue in the form of Product Feature Scenario. Examples:
            • SharePoint site will not be deleted.
            • Exchange mailbox is not created.
          • Describe between 5 – 15 words.
          • If applicable, include As Needed details.
          • Do Not fully copy/paste the customer’s description.
          • Describe what the scenario is, do Not say, for example: This is not a SharePoint issue.
          • If multiple issues, label them with #Issue1, #Issue2.
        • Resolution Steps guidance:
          • Make bullet list of the actions that solved the customer’s issue.
          • Note what got you to the solution.
          • If applicable, include link to help articles.
          • Do NOT include the troubleshooting steps that did not resolve the issue.
          • Do NOT fully copy/paste your notes.
          • If multiple issues, label them with #Resolution1, #Resolution2.
    • Also see below guidance on Verification Dropdown requirement in the Summary Tab.

      Verification Dropdown

      Definition

      Verified by Customer

      Select when technical solution has been provided and verified/confirmed by the customer.

      Verified by Agent

      Select when solution was sent by support or explanation was offered (OOB or by design), however customer did not confirm or agree.

      Not verified

      Select when customer was not at all reachable such as unresponsive (no solution was provided).
      Note: For Transferred and Duplicated cases: Set case verification status based on the case development status at transferring or closing. See article 2023806 for additional scenarios on duplicated cases.
  9. Share Knowledge as appropriate (for example by creating and publishing new internal or public Help articles through http://contentidea).

Frequently asked questions

Who should close a recently transferred case?

The case should be closed by the technical resource who communicated resolution to the customer or partner.

In the event that the customer or partner has resolved the issue themselves before the receiving engineer has started work, the receiving engineer should close the case as long as they have sufficient knowledge from the transfer template to complete the closure email.

If insufficient details were provided during transfer the receiving engineer should engage with the engineer who last worked the case to return ownership, so they can provide a satisfactory closure email to the customer and take ownership of closing the case.
