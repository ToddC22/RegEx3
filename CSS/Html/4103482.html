What are keywords?

These are unique terms that authors use as hidden flags on an article to allow for more refined filtering. 

When applied these keywords will be applied to every search you do. You can add or remove these at any time by navigating to Search Preferences. 

Note: Portals are often one or multiple keywords applied. Eventually portals will be removed and only keywords will be used. 

I entered vkb152 as a keyword but when I search I'm getting different results than I did searching in portal in VKB.

VKB portals often have more than 1 keyword applied to them which is why you will get different results. We have provided a link to get you the portal to keyword mapping.

Why do I not see the most relevant product at the top of the Product filters after searching?

To make your search more accurate use quotes "" around the specific items in your search you want to be looked at.

 

For example:  I want to search Windows 10 Support Boundaries, I would enter my search as follows: "Windows 10" support boundaries

 

Why is Knowledge defaulting to AND with searches?

We use a default AND when searching. When a user enters windows xbox  in knowledge the results returned must have both windows and xbox in each result returned.

If a user wishes to change this they can use the operators to do OR and NOT. See the "What search operators are available in Knowledge?" section.

What determines what Products I see initially in the list of options?

 The initial set of Products you see under the Product Filter are the top 10 products (those with the largest number of content associated to your search). The see more /see less expands this list to the top 100 products

Note: Products are a metadata point on article creation. Not all articles have Products mapped to them while being authored. Hence on seaching for "Window 10" you may not see that as the top Porduct filter. 

What search operators are available in Knowledge?

Operator

Intended Use

+

The AND operator is a plus sign. For example, wifi +surface will search for documents containing both wifi and surface.

|

The OR operator is a vertical bar or pipe character. For example, wifi | surface will search for documents containing either wifi or surface or both.

-

The NOT operator is a minus sign. For example, wifi -surface will search for documents that have the wifi term and do not have surface.

Note: wifi - surface will not remove surface due to the space between the operator.

*

The suffix operator is an asterisk. For example, sur* will search for documents that have a term that starts with sur, ignoring case.

" "

The phrase operator encloses a phrase in quotation marks. For example, Surface wifi (without quotes) would search for documents containing Surface and/or wifi anywhere in any order, "Surface wifi" (with quotes) will only match documents that contains that whole phrase together and in that order (text analysis still applies).

()

The precedence operator encloses the string in parentheses. For example, surface +(wifi | bluetooth) will search for documents containing the surface term and either wifi or bluetooth (or both).
How does the search result ranking work?

We have implemented the following scoring profile (weighting) when searching against Portico content:

Article ID x 5

Article Title x 1.5

Keywords x2

In essence meaning if you search with an article ID number the article with that ID will always be at the top and articles that reference that ID or contain that number will be lower in the rank. If you search with a term and that term exists in the Title or Keyword of the article will rank higher than an article

Question Details
How do I find case history in Service Desk Knowledge? Migrated case data will exist as private support articles with specific keywords. For our support engineers, you will be able use these keywords to refine or limit all searches. With no filtering, case history will be returned alongside other support articles based on ranking. Case history will not be returned for users configured as part of a team that does not use case history.
What are the keywords applied to case history?

KMSolutions VKBRetirement

 Applied to all case history published as support articles. Includes MSSolve, MBS, older cases with only Solution Tasks, and a rolling seven years of newer cases with full case history. These keywords are exclusive to articles published as part of the migration of case history from VKB.
CaseNotes Applied to a rolling seven years of newer cases with full case history This keyword also applies to some articles manually created from case history.
MSSolve

Applied to all case history from MSSolve.

 This keyword also applies to some existing MSSolve articles.
MBS Applied to all case history from MBS. This keyword also applies to some existing MBS articles.

How do I use these keywords?

Keywords can be used as part of your search query along with operators described here. Keywords can also be added as Keyword Filters in your Search Preferences, which will apply them to all searches until removed. Examples follow.

  • Refine an existing query to case history with full notes: "add "+CaseNotes" to the query.
  • Refine an existing query to migrated case history: add "+KMSolutions" and/or "+VKBRetirement" to your query.
  • Limit all searches to migrated case history: in your Search Preferences, add "KMSolutions" and/or "VKBRetirement".
  • Limit all searches to migrated case history with full notes: in your Search Preferences, add "CaseNotes" and "KMSolutions" and/or "VKBRetirement".
  • Expand all searches to include MBS case history: if in your Search Preferences you have a portal/team mapping in use or other personal keywords, adding "MBS" will expand your searches to include MBS results.
