Procedure: M365 TS: How to request an increase in daily email recipient limit

[Asset 4462485]


Who should use this article?

What is this article about?

This procedure describes how to communicate with customers requesting an increase in the daily email recipient limit and includes guidelines for rejecting increase requests.

Assumptions

  • The default maximum number of email recipients per 24-hour period (rolling window) for a single Exchange Online mailbox is now 10,000. Previously customers were limited to 1,500 and could request an increase to 6,000.
  • Currently there is no provision for an increase beyond the new default limit of 10,000.
  • Most requests for an increase will be rejected.
  • For particular situations, when a customer needs a recipient limit that’s more than 10,000, follow the escalation process. Examples of such situations are the Dean of a University sending messages to all students, or a CEO of a company sending messages to all employees.
  • All Exchange Online mailboxes have been updated to accommodate the increased default limit of 10,000.
  • A recipient is defined as any entry on the TO, CC or BCC lines. A distribution group in the shared address book is counted as a single item. A distribution group stored in the Contacts folder of a mailbox is expanded so that members of the group are counted individually.
  • This procedure is not used to request an increase in the individualemail recipient limit of 500 per email message. Do not use this procedure when the customer wants to increase the individual email limit of 500 recipients per individual email ("total recipients" include addresses in the To, CC, BCC, and distribution lists and each address in the distribution list is also counted if the addresses are stored as a local Contact instead of in the Office 365 shared address book). A request to increase the 500 recipient limit per individual email will always be denied.
  • The customer cannot use distribution groups or dynamic distribution groups as a workaround to this problem.

Procedure

Rejecting customer requests to increase the daily email recipient limit

  1. Inform customers that Exchange Online is not intended to be used for Bulk Mail scenarios.
  2. Refer customers to the current Exchange Online Service Description, which states the following: “Exchange Online customers who need to send legitimate bulk commercial email (for example, customer newsletters) should use third-party providers that specialize in these services.”

For more information about recipient limits and alternatives, see Recipient and sender limits.

Escalation process for particular situations

  1. Frontline support engineers (FL SEs) use the following template to send the customers a mail about the corresponding policy and questions to verify that the customers are compliant with these policies.
  2. FL SEs work with customers to complete the questions before escalating.
  3. Escalation tier validates and sanity-checks the data and then escalate to EOP:AntiSpam Engineering Group.

To customer request for mail recipient increase

Email Template:

Hi <customer first name>,

Thanks for contacting Microsoft 365 subscription support. I understand you’d like to increase the daily email recipient limit.

Here are the about increasing limits:

  • Microsoft 365 is not intended to send mass unsolicited emails.
  • This policy applies to single and multiple accounts used for sending emails.
  • With an appropriate business justification, an exception for rate limit increase can be granted. (Examples of such situations are the Dean of a University sending messages to all students, or a CEO of a company sending messages to all employees.)
If you would like to request an exception, please respond to my mail by answering the questions below:
  1. What is the business justification for such a large rate increase? Provide a short overview of what you are looking to accomplish?
  2. Is this a one-time increase or will you need a higher limit eventually?
  3. Have you considered to use a Distribution List (DL) to send the messages?
  4. What is the nature of email content you need to send? 
    • Transactional mail related to business (e.g. purchase orders, invoices, receipts, Terms and conditions change notification).
    • Recurring newsletters or notifications to customers
    • Marketing mail to potential customers who have not opted in to receive these messages
    • Message within a single tenant organization or subsidiaries under a parent company
    • Other - please specify
  5. Confirm that the intended recipients of these messages have either:
    • Directly opted in to receive these messages from your company (e.g. via an online subscription)
    • Indirectly opted in to receive these messages from your company (e.g. via 3rd party subscription)
    • An existing business relationship with your company
    • Employees of the company
    • Other - Please specify
  6. Confirm that you have mechanisms in-place to keep your email address distribution lists accurate and up-to-date, specifically:
    • Verifying email addresses that are being added to your distribution lists - to ensure that a recipient isn’t being added without their consent? A typical way to do this is with a double opt-in.
    • Removing email addresses that no longer wish to receive their messages. Recommended ways to do this are to ensure a straightforward unsubscribe mechanism, and also to process bounce messages that you get in response to your messages.
    • Articulating a method in your mail to opt-out from receiving further messages.

Once I receive this information, I continue working on your request.

 

Thanks,

First and Last Name
