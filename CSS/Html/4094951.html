1. Personal data

1.1 What is personal data?

Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes authenticated and unauthenticated data, linked or linkable to a PUID, Device ID, ANID, or any similar ID that singles out a user.

An identifiable individual can be directly or indirectly identified by:

  1. A name, an identification number, location data, or online identifier.
  2. One or more factors specific to the physical, psychological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity of that individual.

1.2 What is a data subject rights request?

Customers may contact Microsoft to inquire about their data privacy rights. Microsoft must comply with data subject rights requests for access, deletion, requests to stop processing or any other privacy related question regardless of where the data resides. For example, downstream repositories, such as OneDrive, SharePoint, and email. It is critical that we do not put customer personal data in these locations.

A data subject rights request may come in the form of any of the following scenarios:

Request type

Scenario

Deletion request

“I would like Microsoft to delete my data.”

Access request

“I would like to know what data Microsoft has about me.”

General question

“How do you protect the data that you have about me?”

“How long do you retain the data that you collect from me?”

“What do you use my data for?”

“Who do you share my data with?”

“How do you comply with GDPR, HIPPA, CASL, PIPEDA?”

Data Protection Authority

“I want to know how you protect my data, or I will contact my DPA.”

“What data does Microsoft collect from me or from my device(s)?”

2. Procedure for handling data subject rights requests

  1. In any of the above scenarios, do not try to answer questions on behalf of Microsoft. Instead, walk the customer through the online, public-facing webform at https://aka.ms/privacyresponse.
  2. Inform the customer that a privacy professional will contact them to answer their questions.

Commercial

A Microsoft account user with no Azure subscription goes to the Privacy Blade in Azure Portal with a delete request and is directed to the PRC form.

Direct the customer to the PRC form or to account.microsoft.com to delete.

Commercial

An authorized representative of a customer contacts support sand requests deletion of a specific SR or list of SRs.

The engineer submits an incident at: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/servicesincident/Lists/Submit_Incident_Form/Submit%20Form.aspx.

The title should be: Customer requested SR deletion and the request should list all requested SR Numbers.

Note: If a customer contact asks to delete all SRs for an organization without reference to specific SR Numbers, raise an IPG Engagement and wait for direction before proceeding.

Commercial

An authorized representative of a customer contacts support and requests deletion of  files in DTM.
  1. If the SR is still open, the engineer should advise the customer that they can self-delete the files in the portal. If the customer is an authorized representative of the company and is not a contact on the SR, the support engineer may delete the files on their behalf.
  2. If the SR has been closed for more than 90 days, the engineer should advise the customer that the files have already been purged.
  3. If the SR has been closed for less than 90 days, the engineer should submit a Helpdesk ticket with a request to delete the files in workspaces attached to particular SRs. You must also delete any files you have downloaded and any other known copies of the file.

3.4 Reporting a privacy incident

Line of business

Scenarios

Scenarios outcome

All

I see the customer has entered their credit card information on Chat. What do I do?

Inform the customer that Microsoft never asks for a full credit card number within a chat scenario. Ask the customer not to provide this information in any chat in the future. Report the incident immediately. See KB 4094950 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data protection incidents for more information.

  • Internal (access to Microsoft CorpNet) - report the incident to aka.ms/servicesincident.
  • Outside of Microsoft CorpNet - report incidents by email to ServicesIncidents@microsoft.com or escalate to your manager immediately.

All

I have a concern about a Microsoft support tool collecting personal information in a non-secure way.

Report the incident immediately. See KB 4094950 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data protection incidents for more information.

  • Internal (access to Microsoft CorpNet) - report the incident to aka.ms/servicesincident.
  • Outside of Microsoft CorpNet - report incidents by email to ServicesIncidents@microsoft.com or escalate to your manager immediately.

All

A customer calls in and provides a credit card and CVV or log in and password, but the advocate doesn't stop the call recording.

Report the incident immediately. See KB 4094950 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data protection incidents for more information.

  • Internal (access to Microsoft CorpNet) - report the incident to aka.ms/servicesincident.
  • Outside of Microsoft CorpNet - report incidents by email to ServicesIncidents@microsoft.com or escalate to your manager immediately.

All

In a chat conversation with support - Hi, I have trouble getting into my account, my login and password are: <insert login>:<insert password> can you please help me?

Inform the customer that Microsoft will never ask for your login and password. Ask the customer to not provide this information in the future.

Report the incident immediately. See KB 4094950 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data protection incidents for more information.

  • Internal (access to Microsoft CorpNet) - report the incident to aka.ms/servicesincident.
  • Outside of Microsoft CorpNet - report incidents by email to ServicesIncidents@microsoft.com or escalate to your manager immediately.

3.6 Commercial additional scenarios

Line of business

Scenarios

Scenarios outcome

Commercial

A customer or partner reaches out to get guidance on GDPR such as,

  • “How do I get GDPR compliant?”
  • “What is GDPR?”
  • “What is Microsoft doing to help me as customer get GDPR Compliant?”

Engineers/advocates can point the customer to:

  • Service Trust Portal: https://servicetrust.microsoft.com
  • Service Trust Center: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/TrustCenter/Privacy/gdpr/default.aspx
  • Find more customer and partner content here: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/sites/Infopedia_G03/officeonramp/SitePages/gdpr.aspx
  • GDPR Partner Specific link: https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/asset/collection/gdpr#/
  • Compliance Manager: https://servicetrust.microsoft.com/ComplianceManager

Commercial

The customer contacts support and requests assistance in the eDiscovery space. The customer wants to make an adjustment to an eDiscovery setting believing it will help achieve GDPR compliance.

The engineer should assist the customer with the eDiscovery setting, or engage the appropriate resource to assist. However, do not provide confirmation of GDPR compliance. Suggest that the customer or their legal team review Microsoft's public GDPR information found in the resources below.

Commercial

The customer would like help reviewing their Office 365 Security and Compliance settings to confirm they have achieved GDPR compliance.

Engineers should assure customers that Office 365 provides the granularity and flexibility to achieve GDPR compliance, but we cannot confirm GDPR compliance. Suggest that the customer or their legal team review Microsoft's public GDPR information found in the resources below. (The customer can review the GDPR section of the Compliance Manager.)

Commercial

The customer contacts support asking if a particular DLP rule / MRM policy lends to GDPR compliance or needs adjustment.

Engineers should tell the customer that Microsoft cannot provide any confirmation of GDPR compliance. Suggest that the customer or their legal team review Microsoft's public GDPR information found in the resources below.
