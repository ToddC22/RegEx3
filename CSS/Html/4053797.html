1. Digital Gifting

1.1. Overview 

Customers can purchase digital gifts, such as, Xbox One games, game durables, Xbox subscriptions, and digital gift cards from the Microsoft Store. These can be purchased for anyone with an e-mail address. The recipient will receive an email with instructions to redeem the 5x5 code. Once a digital gift is redeemed the customer is able to start using it immediately.

Digital gift cards can be added to the customers Microsoft account as soon as the code is redeemed and can be used to purchase digital or physical goods on Microsoft store.

On the Xbox One console, the customer can purchase a digital gift and have the option to pick the recipient from their Xbox Friends list. The friend will receive the gift message on Xbox in addition to an email, and they can redeem the 5x5 code directly on Xbox.

Content that can be purchased as a digital gift: 

  • Xbox One games
  • PC games
  • Game durables
  • Xbox Live Gold non-renewing subscriptions
  • Xbox Game Pass non-renewing subscriptions
  • Gift cards

Excluded content from digital gifting: 

  • Apps (Coming soon)
  • Avatar items (Coming soon)
  • Consumables
  • EA Play, ESO subscriptions
  • Free/trial app/games
  • Discounted/Promo prepaid subscriptions, subscriptions with recurring billing
  • Pre-order games
  • Original Xbox and Xbox 360 back-compat games

  • Other Store content: Music (album/track) and video (TV/movie), books, physical goods, Office

1.2. Gifting limiations 

  • Recipient must meet any age rating requirements to redeem.
  • No limits on gift purchases while product is at full price.
  • Customers can purchase 2 gifts of a particular product at discount, and 10 total gifts at discount every 14 days.
  • Customers need to wait 14 days to gift a product once the discount limit is reached.
  • Free items cannot be gifted, including items that are free due to a pass or subscription.
  • Customers can only gift full-price subscriptions.
  • Customers can only redeem tokens gifted to them in the country/region where they were purchased.
  • China region is excluded from gifting through Windows 10 Store App and Store on Xbox.

  • Only the sender/purchaser can request a refund. Recipients cannot get a refund or CSV for a gift they cannot use for any reason. 

Learn more about the Microsoft gift terms and conditions.

[Asset 4054211]

  1. Choose Buy as a gift on product description page (PDP)
  2. Enter the email of the gift recipient
  3. Enter the sender's name
  4. Confirm the purchase

Return to top

2.1.3 Purchasing digital gift cards online
  1. Customers access https://www.microsoft.com/gift-cards and select if you want to purchase for Xbox or Microsoft store.
  2. Customers determine the gift card amount, fill out the order form with the recipient’s name, senders name, and any message they would like to include
  3. Customers review their order prior to finalizing their purchase, then click Purchase
  4. Once the purchase is completed, customers receive confirmation. Customers have now the option to print the gift card by clicking Print
  5. In the Order History tab, customers can click Print to print the card
  6. Once printed, the card can be either redeemed on redeem.microsoft.com or by scanning the QR code with their mobile device

Please note the behavior and attributes of the print to paper Digital Gift Card are the same as the send by email option.

  • When a purchase has been made, the customer may see a message that their order is processing. The customer usually will be able to print within a few minutes after the purchase, but sometimes, while rare, this may take up to 30 minutes. Explain to the customer that this is normal behavior based on network performance and backend processing. Please ask the customer to retry in a few minutes.
  • Print can be done multiple times.
  • Once redeemed, print and cancel order will no longer be available for the customer.

2.2 Redeeming experience

2.2.1 Managing a gift on account.microsoft.com
  • Purchasers can see the gift purchase on https://account.microsoft.com/billing/orders. The gift recipient email or gamertag will be located under the item name.
  • To see the gift code sent to the recipient, select View Gift Code. If the customer needs to resend the gift code to the recipient, they can copy the 5X5 and give it to the recipient.
2.2.2 Redeeming a gift from email

To redeem the gift select Redeem Code on the e-mail message received and follow prompts.

2.2.3 Redeeming a gift from Xbox message

To redeem the gift select Redeem Code in the Xbox message and follow prompts.

3. Scenarios

3.1 Sender scenarios

Important: When handling issues with digital gifts you must action from the purchaser’s account. The only scenario that does not require handling from the purchaser’s account are issues redeeming a 5x5 token.

Scenario

Steps for resolution

When to escalate/engage

I want to gift a game/subscription, but I don’t see an option to buy as a gift.

 

Confirm that none of the limitations on section 1.2 apply to this content.

Check if content is eligible to be gifted.

Confirm the country the customer is purchasing from. Gifting is not available in the People’s Republic of China .

  • If the content cannot be gifted, explain to the customer that content type is not eligible for gifting, and recommend they purchase a Microsoft gift card instead.
 

If you confirm content is available for gifting and none of the limitations on section 1.2 apply: engage ACA for further investigation.

I am getting an error message saying “you’ve gifted this as many times as you can during this sale” when purchasing a gift.

 

Confirm that none of the limitations on section 1.2 apply to this case (You can only gift the same discounted game twice).

Confirm the customer’s order history to determine how many times the customer has gifted the discounted content.
 

Do not escalate this scenario. The customer will need to wait to gift this content when the sale is over and the content is at full price.

I am getting an error message saying “Try gifting this in a few days” when purchasing a gift.

 

You can only gift up to 10 discounted items in a 14-day period.

Confirm the customer’s order history to determine how many discounted games the customer has gifted and when.

Do not engage this scenario. The customer will need to wait 14 days from the date they gifted the first discounted game. 

I want to make a purchase, but my purchase doesn’t go through.

 

Understand the error message the customer is receiving. Ask customer to retry purchase by using another Microsoft account and/or device.

  • If you determined that the error is systemgenerated, engage the Advanced Capability Advocates Team.
For additional troubleshooting see KB 2896711 Process: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops for how to handle declined transactions and risk rejections.
 

 

 

Engage ACA as an Ordering issue if the following conditions are met:

  • Issue is not caused by authorization failure or risk block.
  • Customer has attempted purchase by using another account and device.
  • Obtained error message if any given.
I got an email from Microsoft saying my order is processing.

Gift orders can sometimes be under a manual review process to validate the gift order.

Follow the steps below to resolve the issue:

  1. Load the customer and the order details in MST.
  2. Look at the state of the Order, verify if it’s in a Pending state. Some orders may be in a Risk Review state as well.
  3. Check how long the order has been in this state since the order was created. If less than 12 hours in the Pending or Risk Review state, inform the customer that their order is still processing and that they will need to wait.
  4. If more than 12 hours in Pending or Risk Review state, you will need to escalate to Risk Ops for further review. Before escalating, check with your Ranger or Supervisor to determine if there is an SLA issue with Risk Ops.

Only escalate if the case is in Risk Review for more than 12 hours and you have confirmed with your Supervisor or Ranger than there is not an SLA issue with Risk/Fraud Ops.

For specific LOB escalation steps see KB 2896711 Process: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops.

I want to see if a charge in my order history was a gift.

Customers can view purchases on https://account.microsoft.com/billing/orders. If the item is a gift, it will say that it is a gift and indicate the purchaser’s name.
[Asset 4054228]

In MST you can check to see if it is a gift by looking at the details tab and looking for the Giftee gamertag or email.
[Asset 4054229]

Do not escalate.
I sent my gift to the wrong email or gamertag.

Confirm to which email or gamertag the gift was sent to. The sender/purchaser can cancel the gift at any time as long as the recipient has not redeemed the 5x5 code. If the gift was redeemed, it can no longer be refunded.

See KB 4338530 Policy: Digital Order Return/Refund for more information.

Check if gift is still eligible for refund according to the refund policy. Purchaser can repurchase the gift at any time in Microsoft store. If the customer already resent a second gift, you may refund the original gift for the customer.
 
 Do not engage.
The gift I sent was already redeemed, but I want a refund. Once the code has been redeemed, the purchase cannot be refunded. Do not engage.

The recipient said they never got my gift, but I see the status as “Gift code sent” on account.microsoft.com.

 

Confirm the email or gamertag the gift was sent to. If the customer sent it to the wrong email or gamertag, follow instructions above.

If the email/gamertag is correct, check to see the status of the gift in MST by looking at Product Key details. If the token status shows as active (not redeemed), inform that the customer can see the 5X5 in the account.microsoft.com order history. You can also share the 5X5 to the customer, so they can send it to the gift recipient. 
[Asset 4054230]
[Asset 4054226]

 

If the giftee email or gamertag is correct but customers are not receiving the email or message that they have a gift, resolve the customers issue by providing the 5x5 to the purchase.

Escalate to your Ranger or TA to inform that customers are not receiving gift alerts, so the issue can be investigated.

The recipient said they did not get my gift, but I see the status as “Gift redeemed” on account.microsoft.com. 

 

  1. Confirm the email or gamertag the gift was sent to. If the customer sent it to the wrong email or gamertag, follow instructions above.
  2. If the email/gamertag is correct, check to see the status of the gift in MST by looking at Product Key details. If the token status shows as redeemed, and the customer confirms the recipient did not redeem the gift, return and refund the gift purchase.

Do not escalate.

I sent a gift to a person living in a different country and now they can’t redeem it.

 

Confirm if the limitations on section 1.2 apply to this content. Restrictions apply to gifts being sent to users in different regions, due to content and currency mismatch.

Recipient will be unable to redeem the gift on another country. Purchaser should cancel their order to receive a full refund.

Canceling an order will occur if the 5x5 code has not been redeemed and is within refund policy window . If it is outside of refund policy window, guide customer to redeem and they need to spend in the market Store which the gift card is purchased.

 

Do not escalate.
The recipient said they could not redeem the gift I sent them.

Confirm if the limitations on section 1.2 apply to this content.

If the recipient cannot redeem the gift for any reason, refund the gift purchase. You can only refund the gift from the purchaser’s account .
 
 
 Do not engage for refund scenarios.

I got an e-mail notification saying my gift was not delivered.

 

Note: gifts that are not delivered are not automatically cancelled.

If the customer receives an e-mail notification informing that the message for the gift was not delivered, customer has to resend the gift or cancel and repurchase. Follow next scenario for this.

Do not escalate.

I need to resend my gift to the recipient.

 

For digital content: Currently there is no way to resend a gift email or Xbox message. There are two ways a customer can provide the gift token to the recipient if the original notification is lost:

  1. The customer can see the gift token 5X5 sent to the customer in their order history on account.microsoft.com.
  2. You can provide the 5X5 that was gifted for the purchaser, view it under the Product Key tab in MST.

For digital gift cards: Customers can resend the gift via e-mail address.

  1. Confirm if the address of the recipient is correct and if the e-mail sent was stored in the junk folder. If the recipient didn’t receive the e-mail, either because it was incorrect or inbox malfunction, follow the next step.
  2. Direct the customer to resend the e-mail or update recipient’s e-mail address under https://www.microsoft.com/store/b/gift-cards, then go to Gift Card History and resend the order.
Alternatively, purchaser can cancel the order and repurchase gift.

Do not escalate for refund scenarios.

3.2 Recipient scenarios

Important: When troubleshooting issues with digital gifts you must troubleshooting from the purchaser’s account. The only scenario that does not require troubleshooting from the purchaser’s account are issues redeeming a token. 

Scenario

Steps for resolution

When to escalate/engage

Someone said they sent me a gift, but I did not receive it.

 

Ask the customer to check their email, junk folders, and Xbox Live accounts for messages about a gift. If they cannot locate the gift email or message, the recipient will need to contact the purchaser. Purchaser can contact Microsoft Support for assistance.

Only the gift purchaser can check the status of the gift or provide the gift token to the customer or resend the gift to the appropriate e-mail address. Alternatively, the purchaser can cancel the order and repurchase the gift.

Do not escalate.

I received an email/Xbox message about my gift but I deleted it.

Ask the customer to follow up with gift purchaser so they can confirm the status of the gift and/or provide the gift recipient with the gift token or resend the e-mail.

Do not escalate.

Can I re-gift the digital gift card code I received?

 

Only in instances where the recipient received a digital gift card code via email can they forward it to any e-mail address and it can then be redeemed by any Microsoft account.

The code can be forwarded as many times, so long as it hasn’t been redeemed.

Do not escalate.

 

I do not want the gift I received.

Or

I already own the content gifted to me.

 

No gift recipient can exchange a gift, or receive a refund, or get a credit (CSV) for the value of the gift in their Microsoft account. This applies to content a user may already own, and to content they don’t want. Only the gift purchaser can request a refund for a gift.

The recipient can contact the sender/purchaser who can request a refund. Do not offer the recipient any sort of compensation for the gift.

Do not escalate.

I do not have a Microsoft account to redeem the digital gift card I received.

 

Only the gift purchaser can request a refund for a gift.

You may inform the customer/recipient that, in cases where the customer received a digital gift card for Microsoft Store, the gift card does not expire, so once they have a Microsoft account, then they can use the gift card.

The recipient can contact the sender/purchaser who can request a refund.

Do not engage.

I am getting an error saying I need a parent’s permission to redeem a gift I received.

This error appears because the maturity level on the customer’s account will not allow them to view or play the content. They can request that a parent to change the settings on family.microsoft.com, or they can make a content exception on the console. If the parent does not want to approve the content exception, the can contact the gift purchaser, so the purchaser can get a refund.

Do not engage. Do not replace the token or grant CSV to the customer.

I am getting an error saying I can’t redeem my gift in my country.

 

Gifts can only be redeemed in the country or region the gift was purchased in. See section 1.2 for details.

If the customer is unable to redeem, they can contact the gift purchaser, so the purchaser can get a refund.
 

Do not engage. Do not replace the token or grant CSV to the customer.
I am getting an error message when I try to redeem my gift.

Use KB 818928 Troubleshooting: Prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription redemption issues for troubleshooting assistance.

If the customer is still unable to redeem a token after following all the troubleshooting steps, they can contact the purchaser who can request a refund.
 

Do not replace the token or grant CSV to the customer.

Follow existing escalation policies for issue redeeming tokens.
I’m getting an invalid error while redeeming the digital gift card code.

Agent will look up and verify the status of the 5x5 code using MST:

  • If already redeemed, no action as it has already been consumed.
  • If the code was blacklisted/canceled, agent will educate the customer that the code was canceled and redirect the recipient to the sender.
  • If token code gives Inactive or Created status, engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates.
If the code is not found in Token Lookup, obtain image of Token code. Review code in image. If reviewed code still cannot be found in Token Lookup, engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates.		 Engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates as Ordering issue for Created/Inactivestatus or if code not found in Token Lookup with image verification. Description of status required for Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates engagement.

5. Taxonomy coding for Xbox

Depending on the nature of the customer's issue, the following two problem statements should be used for taxonomy coding:

  • I cannot complete a purchase

  • I need help with a code or token

These problem statements are triggered when Xbox One Account Management or Xbox 360 Account Management Call type is selected.

5.1 For Xbox customers who need help with a digital gift card:

  1. Verify the customer with the Microsoft account that was used to purchase

  2. Select Xbox One Account Management or Xbox 360 Account Management, depending on the customer's primary or main console

  3. In the Platform field, select Other Platforms

  4. Select Microsoft Digital Gifting from the Other Platforms dropdown

  5. Fill other relevant fields as needed

  6. Select the Problem Statement from one of those listed in the previous section

  7. Select the appropriate Root Cause and Solution Delivered

  8. Close the case as you usually would

Note: Agents should help Xbox customers by using MST.

Return to top

5.2 For Non-Xbox customers (with a Microsoft account) who need help with a digital gift card:

  1. Verify the customer with the Microsoft account that was used to purchase

  2. Select one of the following call types: Xbox One Account Management or Xbox 360 Account Management

  3. In the Platform field, select Other Platforms

  4. Select Microsoft Digital Gifting from the Other Platforms dropdown

  5. Fill other relevant fields as needed

  6. Select the Problem Statement from one of those listed in the previous section

  7. Select the appropriate Root Cause and Solution Delivered

  8. Close the case as you usually would

Note: a Microsoft account is required to help with digital gift card transactions.
