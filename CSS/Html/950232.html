Procedure: Legal: How to respond to information requests from law enforcement

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer
  • All Commercial

What is this article about?

This article describes how to respond to requests for information about Microsoft or one of its customers from a person who states they are a law enforcement official.

Note: This article addresses requests from law enforcement officials only. For customers requesting a Microsoft legal contact point, refer to KB 2629445 Procedure: Legal: Assisting customers requesting for a legal contact or arbitration.

Overview

Law enforcement officials include the following people:

  • FBI/Secret Service agents
  • Police officers/sheriffs
  • Postal inspectors

Do not give any information about Microsoft or any of its customers to law enforcement officials.

  • Only provide contact details for the Microsoft Online Legal Services Request Line to law enforcement officials.
  • Law-enforcement officials must use the appropriate channels to request information when they are investigating legal issues.
  • If a law-enforcement official calls following up on a previous call, follow the process below as if it were the first call. Do not attempt to view or retrieve the service request (SR).

Procedure

  • Refer all calls from law-enforcement officials to the Microsoft Online Services Legal Request Hotline at the following numbers:
    • United States and Canada: (425) 722-1299
    • Other countries (including LATAM): +1(425) 722-1299

EMEA/ANZ/GCR/Asia/India/Japan

Suggested advocate scripting

"I am forwarding this request to our internal team, who will be able to help you with your questions. You can expect to be contacted within 10 working days. If you need a faster response, please telephone the Microsoft Online Services Legal Request Hotline at 001 425 722 1299. The hotline is based in the US and is available to help you with your request."

Note Non-English) language support may be limited.

  1. Create an email-only account using the email address msnwwcc@microsoft.com.
  2. In the SR notes, collect and document the following information:
    • Law enforcement official’s name:
    • Contact telephone number:
    • Contact email address:
    • Address/location of the official's office and team:
    • Case description:
    • Console serial number and ID (if applicable):
    • Xbox Live gamertag (if available):
  3. Update the SR notes with the following template and close:

    Last action/update: This case has been escalated internally to our Criminal Compliance team. We have no visibility of action taken after escalation, so please assume the officer will be contacted by their local Microsoft subsidiary. There will be no further updates provided in this SR.

    Next step: None - case closed.

    All law enforcement requests should carry the above data; if they do not, the SR may be returned to the originating agent, as legally we should not be contacting police officers from corporate offices until Criminal Compliance has ownership of the case.
  4. Xbox advocates only: Use the following ASD taxonomy:
    • Call Type: Xbox 360 / Xbox One Hardware
    • Why the Customer Called: My console was stolen – investigating a stolen console.
    • Root Cause: Law enforcement official wants information.
    • Solution Delivered: Referred customer to Microsoft Legal Hotline.

Tier 1: After obtaining and recording this information, send an email from ASD with the above data to the Criminal Compliance team.

Example:

To: msnwwcc@microsoft.com

Subject: – Law Enforcement Request –

Hello,

Please find below a law enforcement request received by our call center:

  • Police officer name:
  • Contact telephone number:
  • Contact email address:
  • The address/location of the official's office and team:
  • Case description:
  • Console serial number and ID (if applicable):
  • Xbox Live gamertag (if applicable):

Regards,

<Advocate name>

<Call center>
