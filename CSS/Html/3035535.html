Broad Commercial Support Guidelines for customers that had issues with in-app support

If a customer has an issue with the in-app support experience and opens a support case with Microsoft, the customer must provide the email alias that they used to create the in-app support ticket. Make sure the customer created an in-app support ticket with in the last 24 hours. Make sure the customer turned on notifications for Outlook within the device operating system so they know when the in-app support team responds to their case. 
‎ ‎ 
‎ ‎ A support engineer can email the support Escalation Outlook App iOS Android (  Outlook-iOS-Android  ) alias with the customer’s email alias and the question that the customer had. In-app support will check to see whether the customer registered a case with them and reply through the in-app support experience. Notice that the escalation alias will not connect you with the in-app support agent who is working on the ticket, nor can a support agent be requested through the escalation alias.

NOTE:  The   Outlook-iOS-Android   alias should not be used for non-Premier escalations, except for the scenarios mentioned above.

Connectivity issues

Common questions or things to try:

  Is the user's Intune managed? Do they have the Company Portal app installed on the device?

  Does Outlook Web Access work to their Exchange Server?

  Does their corporate firewall allow access to the Outlook Mobile service? If this is on a device with cellular capabilities, can they try connecting via cellular? See   Outlook for iOS and Android in Exchange Online  for additional information.

  Does Outlook for Windows or Mac connect to Exchange Server?

  (On-Premise Only) Have the customer use the native Mail app on their device to connect to their Exchange Server to determine if there are any problems with EAS.

  (O365 Only) Does the customer have their own ADFS login page set up for accessing O365? If so, are they able to login/access their data from other MS apps on the same device running Outlook?

Escalation process to Product Group

Note: Filing a bug within the Outlook Mobile ADO directly is strictly prohibited. Any escalations that are filed outside the process below will be rejected.

Use the following process to file a bug on behalf of a Premier customer.

1. Reproduce the issue.

2. Ask the customer to follow the instructions outlined in the "  In-app log gathering workflow for Premier cases  " section.

3. Before escalating, ensure that you have gathered all the required information and completed a thorough log analysis. You can find additional information regarding PG guidelines and best practices here: Outlook Mobile Escalation Best Practices

3. File an escalation request by filling out the form at this link:   https://aka.ms/cssbug  .    (Note: If you don’t have access to this link, ask a member on your team who has access to assist you. Please don’t send these details over email. If you require access and you are part of the Outlook support team or are part of CSS and have a legitimate business need, you may send a separate request to Dave Vespa at    dvespa@microsoft.com   .) After submitting, you will receive an automated email with a link to your escalation.

3. The PG will review the escalation to ensure it is accurate and complete. Escalations that are deemed incomplete or require additional information will be rejected.

4. Once accepted, engineering will evaluate the bug based upon the logs and your repro steps. All notes associated with the PG’s review will be included within the ADO ticket.

5. If requested, obtain a Business Impact (BI) statement from the customer for the bug submission and send the statement to the product team.

6. Once a bug has been ported to engineering, the associated bug can be found in the escalation ticket. Do not leave comments on the engineering bug. The product team will reach out to you through the escalation ticket if more information is necessary. If you have additional questions, please leave them as a comment in the escalation ticket.

7. The engineering bug will be marked as resolved once the issue has been fixed. You can find out when a fix has been released by searching for the bug on the Release Notes History page.

8. Confirm with your customer that the issue has been resolved. Once confirmed, close the escalation ticket as resolved in ADO. If the issue is unresolved, please let the product team know by leaving a comment in the escalation ticket.

Note : This process should only be used when an investigation has already been completed by CSS and all support measures have been exhausted. Any escalation request that has not gone through a full investigation by CSS will be rejected by an EE or the product team. For assistance with filing an escalation, please review this article: Outlook Mobile Escalation Best Practices .

Status of bugs filed with the Product Group

The status of a bug or escalation can be found within the Outlook Mobile ADO:  https://office.visualstudio.com/Outlook%20Mobile/_queries  . All enterprise escalations can be found under the  Outlook Mobile\Support\Enterprise  area. Ported bugs can be found in their respective area outside of  Support . If you need to determine the status of a bug and don't have access to ADO, please email   Outlook-iOS-Android   with the SR and bug number. 

If a previously opened escalation is transferred from one CSS engineer to another, please let the product team know by leaving a note in the ADO ticket. This helps ensure the escalation progresses in a timely manner.

CritSit process for Premier support

During business hours:

1. Do your best to ensure this is an issue with the app itself and not something else (for example, the Office 365 service, Exchange, Azure AD, Intune, and so on).

2. Have the customer submit logs from Outlook by following the instructions outlined in the "  In-app log gathering workflow for Premier cases  " section. Be sure to include the Powerlift incidents in your escalation.

3. If you have access to the product team's bug database, file an escalation at this link and mark it as a CritSit:  https://aka.ms/cssbug  . NOTE:  Escalations that are missing information (such as log analysis, reproduction steps, or customer environment data) are subject to rejection by the PG.

4. Follow the guidance that's given within the ADO ticket.

During non-business hours:

1. Before escalating, please ensure that the request matches all of the following requirements: 
‎ ‎ 
‎ ‎ The customer is experiencing a critical functionality loss (CFL) that is affecting a majority of users within an organization. This can be broken down to just all users on one particular OS (iOS, Android) or across both OSes. Critical functionality losses are considered as:

  Inability to synchronize more than half of their messages and/or calendar events. Allow up to a half hour for sync to occur once an account has been added.   Note: the Outlook app only syncs messages received over the last 30 days and/or the last 1,000 emails residing in the user’s Inbox folder, whichever comes first. Calendar events up to 1 year ahead and 3 weeks behind the current date are synced.

  Inability to send messages.  

  The application is crashing or hanging upon launch or during normal usage.

  Inability to add work accounts (O365, Exchange, or OneDrive for Business) to the app.   Note: Issues with the AutoDetect service are not considered an Outlook Mobile app/service escalation. Information on filing AutoDetect escalations can be found here:    https://internal.support.services.microsoft.com/en-us/help/4052171  .   Users who are being sent to the incorrect account type due to an AutoDetect issue can add their account by manually selecting it from the “Add Account” screen in Outlook.

  Inability to view or open more than half of the messages within a user’s inbox. Issues viewing one or just a few messages are not accepted.

  Mail and/or Calendar data corruption  (Note: Issues where a specific email or calendar event is showing as corrupted does not constitute a CFL.)

  Data leakage and/or a security issue residing within the Outlook app (  Note: Issues related to MAM support should first be verified by the Intune team.)

2. Have the customer submit logs from Outlook by following the instructions outlined in the " In-app log gathering workflow for Premier cases " section. Be sure to include the Powerlift incidents in your escalation.

3. Review the Outlook logs and ensure this is an issue with the app and not something else (for example, the Office 365 service, Exchange, Azure AD, Intune, and so on). If the user is Intune enrolled, engineers  must  be able to reproduce the issue without Intune enrollment to ensure the problem is with the Outlook client. If the issue is only reproducible with Intune enrollment, a problem should be sent to the Intune team prior to escalating to the Outlook PG. An Intune-related security issue can be escalated only after Intune has verified the issue is due to a flaw within the Outlook client.  Cases/logs that have not been fully reviewed by CSS prior to escalation are subject to rejection by the PG.   Information regarding log reviews and troubleshooting information for Outlook Mobile can be found on the following pages:

   https://aka.ms/olmobilelogging

   https://aka.ms/omsteam

4. File an escalation request here  https://aka.ms/cssbug  .  (Note: If you don't have access to this link, ask a member on your team who has access to assist you. Please don't send these details over email. If you require access and you are part of the Outlook support team or are part of CSS and have a legitimate business need, you may send a separate request to Dave Vespa at    dvespa@microsoft.com  .)   The following information  must  be included when filing the support request:

a. MSSolve/Rave Case Number and Customer Name

b. Detailed description of the issue, including related log information from the Outlook client, expected behavior and actual outcome, description of customer’s environment, and notes related to the investigation completed by CSS. More information on required information for CritSits can be found in the “CritSit Process for Premier Support” section of this article.

c. Test accounts (if any) (Note: If no test accounts are available and the issue cannot be reproduced outside of the customer’s environment, the product team holds the right to reject the escalation until the next business day or until a test account becomes available from the customer.)

d. Related videos and/or screenshots of the issue

e. Detailed reproduction steps

e. Links to associated HelpShift tickets and/or PowerLift logs 

f. Once your escalation has been accepted by an EE, email the Support Escalation Outlook App iOS Android alias at   Outlook-iOS-Android@microsoft.com   and include a link to the filed ticket.

g. Wait 1 hour for a response from the alias. If no response, contact one of the following on-call PG contacts:

Filing a Design Change Request

Customers interested in filing a Design Change Request must first fill out a DCR form, which can be downloaded  here.  The form must be filled out in full before a DCR is considered.

Prior to submitting a new DCR, please check first to see if an existing request has been submitted within the Outlook Mobile ADO:  https://aka.ms/omdcrquery  . All requests can be found under the  Outlook Mobile/Support/Enterprise  area path within ADO.

If you do find an existing request that matches your customer’s DCR, perform the following:

1. Add the customer-filled DCR form to the existing ticket as an attachment.

2. Notify the PG about the new request by leaving a comment for the PM assigned to the ticket in the Discussion section. Include a brief explanation of your customer’s business impact and a link to your form.

New DCRs can be submitted at the following link:  https://aka.ms/omdcr  .

After the form has been submitted, a ticket will automatically be filed in the Outlook Mobile repository and sent to the appropriate resource. You will receive an email that includes a link to your filed DCR.

NOTE:  Filing a DCR does not guarantee that a request will be accepted; acceptance of a DCR is at the product group’s discretion. Requests that include detailed descriptions regarding the need for a request have a higher chance of acceptance than those which don’t. Specifically, business impact statements that detail the organization and individual user perspectives allows the product team to understand the impact and experience. This empowers the product team to accurately prioritize each request.
