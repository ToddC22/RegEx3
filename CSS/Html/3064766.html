1. Personal data overview

Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual. This includes authenticated and unauthenticated data, linked or linkable to a Persistent Unique Identifier (PUID), Device ID, ANID, or any similar ID that singles out a user.

An identifiable individual can be directly or indirectly identified by:

  1. A name, an identification number, location data, or online identifier.
  2. One or more factors specific to the physical, psychological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity of that individual.

To ensure correct handling of personal data it's important to understand Microsoft's privacy and data protection requirements and incorporate strong privacy practices in your day-to-day work.

Here are definitions of frequently used terms regarding customer data:

Term

Definition/Notes

Privacy

Privacy is about being accountable for and respecting customer choices over the collection, control, use, and distribution of personal data, as well as providing ways to manage those preferences. This is critical to maintaining customer trust and loyalty. Personal information must never be disclosed in unauthorised ways.

Security

Security is about protecting data from unauthorized access. The personal information we collect is only used to facilitate requests and authorized services in compliance with Microsoft privacy and security requirements.

  • Note: For information on Phishing and Social Engineering, see KB 2991467 Procedure: Privacy: Handling social engineering, fraud, and phishing incidents.

Data protection

Data protection is about ensuring appropriate use of personal data. This means managing support incidents properly, and appropriately handing customers data and any privacy related enquiry they may have.

1.1 Identifying sensitive (highly confidential) personal data

Sensitive or Highly Confidential Data is information that could be associated with personal data and used to:

  1. Discriminate (for example, race, ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, political opinions, trade union memberships, sexual orientation, physical, or mental health).
  2. Facilitate identity theft (for example, mother's maiden name).
  3. Permit access to a user's account (for example, passwords or PINs). It also includes data that is not technically personal data but has historically made users cautious (such as a user's precise location) and data that has a reasonable expectation of embarrassing the user.
  4. Data that could be associated with enterprise or commercial data and used to negatively impact a business, such as confidential business information that should not be disclosed to competitors or made public.

2.1 Handling scenarios

Scenario

Instructions

Secure communication methods
  • Data that can only be shared verbally: Full credit card numbers (16 digits) or CVV should never be shared or stored in written format under any circumstances even via DTM.
  • Data that can only be handled via DTM: Highly Confidential/Sensitive information can only be handled through the DTM tool.
  • Data that can be handled via Microsoft provided incident management tools: Confidential only information can be handled via Microsoft tools with embedded email and chat functionality.

Customer account verification 

Do not provide customer personal data proactively and unnecessarily to customers.

  • If the customer has provided a Microsoft account, you can provide the email domain (for example, @contoso.com) and the last two digits of the contact phone.
  • If customers are not sure what Microsoft account they used, you can help them by using a general question like “Is there a different email you may have used or potentially did it have a different provider name such as hotmail.com or live.com?” or guiding them to utilize the user name hint flow. See KB 4479728 Procedure: Microsoft account: Forgot my username.
  • Once the account has been verified, you may confirm that the information they provided matches that in the profile.
    • If a customer can pass account verification using a phone number as an account alias and has forgotten their Microsoft account, you can provide it at their request.
  • See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

Case notes usage
  • Do not record highly confidential/sensitive data in case notes.
  • Highly confidential/sensitive personal data should only be handled through the DTM tool when indicated in this table as approved (for example, full Credit Card numbers or CVV should never be requested even in DTM).
  • Confidential data only exception:

Payment instrument last four digits, expiry date or name on card cannot be recorded in case notes, even if shared separately.

Payment instruments handling
  • Full payment instrument information (full digits of credit card numbers and associated CVV numbers) should never be sent or stored under any circumstances including with the DTM tool (never request images of the customer’s credit/debit cards).
  • Limited payment instrument information (last four digits of CC, expiry date or name on card for example), can be sent by the customer via Microsoft tools with embedded email and chat functionality.

Chat:

  • Chat advocates cannot handle full payment instrument information (16 digit or CVV) and must transfer the customer to a phone advocate.

Phone:

  • Phone advocates must pause call recording when payment information (for example, full 16-digit credit card numbers or CVV) is being collected, and resume after collecting that information.
  • See KB 3102066 Tool User Guide: OneCloud for Advocates for more details on how to pause/resume.
 

Troubleshooting technical issues

To minimize the risk of accessing sensitive or confidential customer information unnecessarily, do not troubleshoot technical issues using customer-provided data.

  • When a customer has a problem applying specific functionality to their data, provide links to online content that explains how to achieve the customer’s goal, such as Excel formulae or mail merge.
  • You may also help the customer using a new, blank file or workbook and dummy data to rule out file corruption as a factor, rather than the customer’s file with their data.

Storing personal data
  • Do not store customer personal data outside of approved Microsoft tools.

Handling passwords/PIN numbers

Phone:
Never request this information from customers:

  • Microsoft account password or PIN.
  • Email password from customers.

Chat:
Never request this information from customers:

  • Microsoft account password/PIN.
  • Email password.
  • Credit card number (full).
  • Credit card CVV number (security code).
  • Credit card PIN.
  • Other payment instrument information (for example, PayPal password).
    • Note: Requesting an account number is permitted.

2.2 Examples of inappropriate personal data handling

  • Storing highly confidential/sensitive data in unsecured areas of Microsoft tools, such as case notes or escalation templates.
  • Storing customer personal data outside of Microsoft authorized tools.
  • Releasing personal data to unauthorized recipients (for example, sending emails with customer information to different customers or to other advocates).
    • Exception: Transferring or escalating cases to other advocates or Microsoft, who are considered authorized recipients.
  • Requesting a customer to provide full credit card number or CVV image via DTM or chat.
  • During a remote assistance session, viewing, opening, deleting, copying, or editing any passwords, files, or documents which are not related to the issue.
  • Using Remote Assistance if the customer is no longer connected via phone or chat. Offline usage of LMI or remote assistance is prohibited.

2.3 Consumer advocate personal data

Do not provide any of the following information if requested to by a customer:

  • Your full name (however, you may provide either your first name, last name or your nickname).
  • Your gamertag.
  • Your personal email address (primary or alternate).
  • Your personal phone numbers.
  • Your social media account usernames or passwords.
  • Your work schedule or a requested Store Associates work schedule.
  • Your exact location or specific address.

Note: For your privacy and security, it is important not to give out personal details or the details of your location. You may state a generic location like “I am located in Manila or the Philippines”.

3. Handling requests for call recording or chat transcript 

Scenario

Instructions

Phone recording

If a customer requests phone recordings of their support contact, direct them to https://privacy.microsoft.com/privacy-questions, where they can submit a request under I have a privacy question or concern.

Chat transcript

A customer may directly copy chat transcripts from the chat window before ending the chat session with the advocate.

The Live Engage chat platform used by our Consumer Sales team also has an option for the customer to receive the chat transcript at the end of the interaction by email.

For all Microsoft lines of business, when a customer contacts us at a later stage, advocates may pull the specific chat transcript as requested by the customer after account verification and email the chat transcript to the customer using RAVE or ASD.

If the chat transcripts cannot be located, direct them to https://privacy.microsoft.com/privacy-questions, where they can submit a request under I have a privacy question or concern.

Transcripts across multiple channels

When a customer wants to obtain their transcripts across multiple channels such as phone, chat and email, direct them to https://privacy.microsoft.com/privacy-questions, where they can submit a request under I have a privacy question or concern.
