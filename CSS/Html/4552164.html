What is this article about?

This article defines expected behaviors Consumer advocates should exhibit throughout the lifecycle of a support engagement. Below are the five core steps to be followed by all advocates when a customer engages with support.

1. Introduction

The Case Management Framework (CMF) provides steps for advocates that enable the delivery of business defined customer experiences.

It is tools agnostic, consistent, and easily discoverable and understood by all advocates and engineers across LOB’s.

Greet and establish communication
  • The step where a customer reaches a Microsoft advocate for help and where you greet our customer accordingly.

Identify customer need
  • The step where the issue/customer request is framed and where you mutually agree on what needs to be addressed.

Solution discovery/develop solution
  • The step where a solution/value propositions is built based on the information gathered and matching the customer’s need, using Service Desk Knowledge (SDK) or other LOB approved resources.

Deliver solution/verify solution
  • The step where the troubleshooting is performed/the solution is provided.
  • The step where the resolution is verified, and the customer confirmed the solution addressed the need. Aim to deliver a solution during first contact.

Close
  • The step where case closing conditions are met and actions are taken to close the case with the customer’s permission.

2. CARE principles

All core behaviors should match to our CARE principles: Communicate effectively, take Accountability, be Resourceful, and show Empathy. Use these behaviors as appropriate.

Communicate effectively

Take Accountability
  • Set appropriate expectations with the customer.
  • Actively listen and frame the customer’s issue.
  • Engage quickly with the customer and ask for assistance when needed.
  • Build trusting relationships.
  • Identify the issue with effective probing questions.
  • Ensure customer understands given solution.
  • Take ownership for the customer experience.
  • Assign equal priority to problems, tasks, and cases to deliver great customer experiences.
  • Take ownership and partner to resolve the customer’s issue.
  • Be accountable for the problem or tasks and follow-through.

Be Resourceful

Show Empathy
  • Do whatever it takes to make the customer successful.
  • Proactively mitigate future risks.
  • Quickly bring in the relevant expert to help solve my customer’s issue.
  • Make it easy for the customer.
  • Deliver simple and clear access to the right support solution.
  • Understand the issue from the customer’s perspective.
  • Quickly know the customer information and preferences.
  • Anticipate the customer’s needs.
  • Treat customers holistically, beyond the immediate issue to deliver a solution.

3. Behaviors to exhibit - specific to each core steps

Core steps

Behaviors to exhibit

Greet and establish communication
  • Accept the support request (chat/phone/email/web) within established timelines.
  • Greet the customer using Microsoft branding before they greet you.
  • Greet the customer with a personalized greeting based on authentication status.
  • Get to know your customer and build rapport:
    • If time allows, prepare yourself to engage with the customer by reviewing case notes, files, or documents if provided by the customer.
    • When receiving a transfer, review case notes to understand the customer issue.
  • Recognize and understand the customer’s effort in reaching you.
  • Always customize your response towards resolving the customer’s issue.
  • Reassure the customer you will help them and confirm your understanding of the request.
  • Always set correct expectations: Be clear, say what you mean and do what you say.

Identify customer need
  • Ask probing questions including reproducing steps to have clarity on the customer issue.
  • Be clear on what is feasible based on boundaries and policies.
  • Be as detailed as possible in reviewing the case:
    • Review the customer support request history and profile.
    • Use the available tools/resources to get additional context.

Solution discovery/develop solution 

Note: Plan and structure your solution prior to presenting it to the customer.

  • Demonstrate confidence. Have the customer feeling they are in good hands.
  • Leverage standard workflows for known issues and top call drivers by using reference materials like SDK or other approved resources.
  • Formulate the solution.
  • Propose the solution to the customer.
  • Ensure the customer agrees with your proposed solution and expectations on time to complete.

Deliver solution/verify solution
  • Deliver detailed resolution steps.
  • Validate that the solution provided resolves the customer’s issue.
  • If you cannot resolve the issue, seek help from others.

Close
  • Verify if there is anything else that needs addressing.
  • Follow up on commitments made during the interaction. Make sure you deliver on anything you promised the customer.
  • Recognize the customer’s efforts and thank them for their time.
  • Obtain the customer’s permission to close the case.
  • Show a willingness to help the customer if a new issue is raised at the end of the contact.
