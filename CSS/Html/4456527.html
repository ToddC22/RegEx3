Procedure: Root Cause Analysis

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer (CSS) | Escalation Engineer (CSS) | Duty Manager | Team Manager | Technical Advisor 

What is this article about?

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) is the process of identifying the cause of a technical issue after the issue has been remediated. It should only be performed at customer request and with clear boundaries set in advance to the extent both parties will invest in identifying cause. Where cause is required to be found before an issue can be resolved, that is considered troubleshooting and is out of scope for this procedure.

Scope

  • Customers who have purchased Microsoft Unified Support or Premier Support, and the customer reported issue is related to on-premise technology or a hybrid deployment of Cloud technology and on-premise technology. (Excluding Azure Stack)
  • If the customer reported cloud related issues is part of a Service Incident, a root cause analysis of a major service incident will be published on the relevant service portal. Post-Service Incident RCA for O365/M365 is called a Post Incident Review (PIR).
  • RCA related to Support for Mission Critical cases is out of scope for this procedure and continues to have access to unlimited RCA delivered by DTU and CSS engineers.
  • RCA related to Support for  Mission Critical CritSit cases should follow RCA guidance in the KB 4456542 | Procedure: SMC CritSit (formerly PMC CritSit).
  • Customers who have purchased O365 Engineering Direct (Support++) or O365 Dedicated as part of their Unified Performance Support Agreement are entitled to only request a Post Incident Review (PIR) for core Office 365 products, EXO, SPO, Skype, Teams, and OneDrive for Business. All other workloads and services are out of scope and a PIR cannot be requested. See 4456938 | Procedure: O365 Engineering Direct (Support++) and https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/o365engineeringdirect.

Details

  1. When completing troubleshooting on a Premier/ Unified case, If the customer requests further effort to identify root cause then:
    • If you identify that RCA is not possible based on the scenario and technology involved, communicate to the customer and proceed with closure of the break-fix support case.

      For Premier customers only, inform the customer's IM of their request to proceed with additional Root Cause Analysis investigation and check that the customer has enough Advisory hours available to continue.
    • Create a new support case against the Advisory line item on the contract. 
      • If you are unable to create the case, contact the support phone line (with the customer on hold if necessary) for them to create the case then take ownership of it.
      • Root Cause Analysis cases can be Severity B/C/Standard only and not raised to Severity 1/A/Critical.
      • For cases where root cause may potentially be related to multiple separate issues it is valid to create separate cases to investigate each possible cause. Case numbers for all related RCA cases should be captured in the related break-fix case where possible, or in each RCA case.
    • Close the related break-fix case after documenting the request for an RCA and the case number of the RCA case. Provide the RCA case number to the customer in the closing email.
  2. Meet initial response and scope all new RCA cases following standard procedures. RCA scoping should include the case number of the break-fix case and an estimate of hours Microsoft will contribute to the RCA effort. Unified Support cases should not exceed 10 hours or 25% of the labor on the related break-fix support case (whichever is greater):
    • One (1) hour prior to the scoped limit being reached, ensure the customer is aware of the upcoming limit.
    • When the labor limit is reached, discuss with the customer the conclusion of any RCA work completed and whether they would like to rescope to proceed with further hours or to close the case.
    • If re-scoping to increase scoped hours, contact the IM before continuing to confirm the customer has enough Advisory hours available to continue.

Guidance

  • RCA work must always be carried out on a separate case from reactive troubleshooting, except where root cause is required as part of troubleshooting to resolve a break-fix support case.
  • RCA is differentiated from break-fix troubleshooting based on whether the scoped break-fix issue is considered resolved. Where the scoped issue is not currently present, but you believe is likely to reoccur in the short term or mitigated by a workaround that cannot be used permanently, the case should be considered still in the troubleshooting phase.
  • When working across technologies to service a request for root cause analysis:
    • CSS Engineers should be aware that a customer’s eligibility for RCA for one product or technology area does not extend to all products or technology areas, for example Broad Commercial Azure (RCA eligible) and Broad Commercial Windows (RCA ineligible), refer to the RCA Eligibility Table for guidance.
    • CSS Engineers are permitted to request collaboration with another team if the customer is eligible for RCA for the owning team’s technology. 
      • Collaborating engineers should contribute to RCA activities wherever possible, however there may be times where this may not be possible for example, direction from the Product Group.  Under these circumstances, collaborating engineers must work with the owning engineer to set appropriate customer expectations around the RCA restrictions for the product or technology area.
    • Cases are not permitted to be transferred to another team if the customer isn’t eligible for RCA based on the other team’s product or technology area.
