1. Overview

Co-sell publishing in Microsoft’s commercial marketplace enables partners to supply and manage solution-level, seller-facing taxonomy, collateral and sales contacts (collectively referred to as “co-sell data”) from the same interface (Partner Center) that they manage all data for their offer/listing in commercial marketplace.

Partner benefits

  • Simplifies partner experience for publishing and maintaining co-sell data in the same place they manage other offer/listing data.
  • Unifies offer/listing management capabilities across all go-to-market channels from a single interface, Partner Center.

Schedule

Partner solutions will be enabled for co-sell publishing by offer type according to the schedule below. Solutions with offer types not available for publishing in commercial marketplace will continue to be co-sell managed in OCP GTM.

Dynamics F&O, CE and Power Apps SaaS, Dynamics BC, Azure Applications  All other offer types supported in commercial marketplace 
December 2019 April 27, 2020 TBD

NOTES:

  • Starting on April 27, 2020, partners will see a call-to-action message in OCP GTM notifying them if their co-sell solution data is eligible to migrate to Partner Center. If the solution doesn’t have a valid offer type in OCP GTM, the call-to-action message will prompt the partner to supply an offer type.
  • At this time, a partner is not required to move co-sell data to commercial marketplace. Partners can continue to separately maintain co-sell data in OCP GTM without disruption. At a future date, it will be required for partners to move their data to commercial marketplace to maintain visibility for their offers/listings in seller-facing catalogs and interfaces.

2. Offer migration scenarios

When a publisher has an offer type that is eligible for migration, users should select the Click to start link on the OCP GTM page.

[Asset 4558696]

After selecting the Click to start link on the OCP GTM page, users will be notified via a call-to-action pop-up which asks them to migrate the solution data to Partner Center.

[Asset 4561025]

Following migration, the offer type and publisher account associated with the newly created draft can be viewed in the lower right-hand corner of the Solution detail page.

[Asset 4561026]

Partner scenario A partner does​ not have a published marketplace offer/listing and wishes to migrate an offer to Partner Center.

Partner solution A partner can trigger the creation of a draft commercial marketplace offer from OCP GTM. The draft will copy basic solution attributes and co-sell data from OCP GTM to Partner Center. Partners must complete the commercial marketplace publishing process in Partner Center to make changes to their co-sell data and maintain seller discovery of their solution.

NOTE: The creation of draft commercial marketplace offers is not exclusive to OCP GTM. New draft marketplace offers may also be created in Partner Center, but may become duplicated if a draft of the same offer is created in both OCP GTM and Partner Center.

Partner scenario A partner has a published marketplace offer/listing and would like to match their existing OCP GTM co-sell data with their offer listed on Partner Center.

Partner solution A partner may tie their co-sell data with their existing offer/listing in commercial marketplace. This is an engineering-led action and will require an escalation. Review the Escalation section for details. Once the action is complete, the partner will view and manage their co-sell data from Partner Center.

3. Offer type considerations

For solutions without a valid offer type in OCP GTM, users should select the specify offer type link to add their offer type to their solution.

[Asset 4558701]

After selecting the specify offer type on the OCP GTM page publishers will be notified to supply a valid offer type via a call-to-action pop-up message. (See Publishing guide by offer type for guidance on offer type selection). 

[Asset 4561031]

NOTES:

  • Publishers are not required to select an offer type and may continue to maintain and view co-sell data in OCP GTM.
  • Publishers who select an offer type will receive a call-to-action to migrate the offer type to Partner Center immediately upon selecting the offer type or later when the offer type is eligible to migrate.

4. Escalation

  • General issues within the Co-Selling with Microsoft tab in Partner Center which cannot be resolved should escalate to Tier 2 by creating a VSO ticket. For instructions to create a VSO ticket please review the Guideline: Partner: AMPS – Creating VSO tickets for Tier 2 escalations article.
  • Marketplace Publisher Support should escalate to engineering teams via OCPSUP@microsoft.com for the following support scenarios:
  • Migrated co-sell metadata is missing or incomplete
  • Migrated co-sell metadata cannot be viewed or downloaded
  • Merged co-sell metadata is associated with the wrong offer
  • The wrong offer type is associated with the migrated/merged offer
  • The wrong publisher account is associated with the merged offer

NOTE: Please collect the following information and include in each escalation, SLA for response should be 1 business day:

  • OCP Solution ID
  • URL for the direct marketplace listing
  • Offer Type
  • Marketplace Publisher Support should refer partners to business support resources for these scenarios.
  • Partner has questions about the Co-sell program or qualification > https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/membership/sell-with-microsoft#become-ready
  • Partner has a question about a specific co-sell referral / deal > Refer partner to cosell@microsoft.com

5. FAQ

Q: How does the recent change impact how I maintain my co-sell solution collateral and metadata?

A: Previously, you published and maintained your commercial marketplace offer data in Partner Center and separately maintained your co-sell solution collateral and metadata in OCP GTM systems. Now you will maintain all your co-sell data in one place: Partner Center.

Q: How is the co-sell data in Partner Center used?

A: The co-sell collateral and metadata you provide is used by Microsoft sales teams to evaluate and recommend your solution to customers. Co-sell data is not published in customer-facing storefronts such as Azure Marketplace or Microsoft AppSource.

Q: I have other published offers in marketplace. Will I maintain co-sell collateral and metadata in Partner Center for all my solutions?

A: We are moving co-sell solution collateral and metadata to Partner Center by offer type. If your organization has multiple co-sell solutions with different offer types it may be necessary for you to continue to maintain your data for some co-sell solutions in OCP GTM until the migration is complete.

Q: Why is this change necessary?

A: We are streamlining our commerce systems and business processes to bring all our GTM channels onto a single platform. Over time, these changes will simplify how you manage your commerce business with Microsoft and provide you with self-service access to our global customer base through customer direct marketplaces, Microsoft sales teams and our global reseller channel.

Q: How do I publish co-sell data in Partner Center?

A: When you create or migrate an offer to Partner Center, click on your offer to view the Co-selling with Microsoft tab. Select the option to “Review and Publish”.

[Asset 4561032]

Q: How do I find my co-sell data in Partner Center?

A: To view co-sell data in Partner Center, login to Partner Center. From the Dashboard page, navigate to Commercial Marketplace > Overview page to see a list of your offers. Click on your offer to view the Co-selling with Microsoft tab.

Q: What is a draft migration?

A: If you have created a draft offer in OCP GTM that is not a published listing today, you can migrate that offer type into Partner Center to publish as the future state commerce system for your co-sell data.

Q: I migrated my solution in OCP GTM but can’t see it in the marketplace

A: Ensure you have completed the publishing process in Partner Center. The migration from OCP GTM only puts it in a draft status, it must be reviewed and submitted and go through the certification/validation processes before it will be listed in marketplace and for you to benefit from referrals/opportunities.

Q: I migrated my solution from OCP GTM to Partner Center, but it’s now listed as the wrong offer type / publisher name.

A: Once a solution has been migrated to Partner Center, the offer type / publisher name cannot be updated by the publisher. You need to create a support ticket and Tier 1 will need to contact OCPSUP as per escalation guidance.

Q: My solution / offer is not showing up in the Co-sell Solution Finder?

A: This is separate from Partner Center and should not be visible to partners as it's an internal view for PDMs and Microsoft sellers. Therefore, this is out of scope for Marketplace Publisher Support team. The PDM should escalate this directly to ocpsup@microsoft.com.

Q: How do I delete a draft if it is duplicate or unwanted?

A: Login to Partner Center. From the Dashboard page, navigate to Commercial Marketplace > Overview page to see a list of your offers. Click on the offer you wish to delete and click ‘Delete Draft’ at the top of the page.

Note: Partners cannot delete in OCP GTM.

Q: What is the difference between merge and draft?

A: Merge references an offer that will have metadata migrated to Partner Center but is already a published listing and therefore requires engineering work to migrate. A draft offer doesn’t have any listing data associated to it and it has not yet been published.

Q: The tool is prompting me to select an offer type to retain co-sell publishing status. How do I know which offer type to choose?

A: See Publishing guide by offer type for guidance on offer type selection.

Q: I am logged into Partner Center but do not see a Commercial Marketplace tab.

A: Access to Commercial Marketplace needs to be provided by a manager for your organization’s publisher(s). Identify your organization’s publisher(s) and contacts here to request access.

Q: How can my organization’s publisher give me permissions to access the offers or listings they manage?

A: A manager user on your organization‘s publisher(s) will need to give you permissions. Guidance regarding permission types for Commercial Marketplace can be found to access the offers/listings they manage.

Q: I completed the steps to migrate, but I don’t see my offer in PC? (escalation scenario)

A: Please supply the following details and we’ll look into this for you:

  • OCP Solution ID
  • URL for the direct marketplace listing
  • Offer Type
