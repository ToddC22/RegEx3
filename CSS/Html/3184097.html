1. Overview

Microsoft Audience Network (MSAN) is the name we use to describe the process by which Microsoft empowers our advertisers to target customers using native placement (for example, ads contained within website content) through non-search experiences on O&O and syndicated partner websites. These ads are integrated into the user experience of the webpage on which they’re displayed, and function across device.

We are able to do this using our Microsoft Graph (the data Microsoft stores on customers, obtained through products/services operated by Microsoft to determine things such as demographics, location, and LinkedIn info) and AI logic to provide smart ad-selection, relevancy matching and pricing/click/conversion predictions, to deliver the best return on investment.

The Audience Network aims to provide an alternative to traditional search marketing campaigns, by offering advertisers the ability to target people through things such as Audience Ads, In-Market Audience lists, or Custom Audiences.

See the following articles for more insights on this topic:

  • Welcome to the Microsoft Audience Network.
  • About Audience Marketing and the Microsoft Audience Network.

Availability note:

  • Dedicated Audience Ad campaigns are currently GA in US only. Advertisers exclusively targeting these markets will be eligible to create dedicated Audience Ads campaigns.
  • Audience ads in Search campaigns are available in AU, CA, DE, FR, NZ, UK, and US.

1.1 MSAN vs. Microsoft search network 

  1. MSAN does not rely on keywords to get an advertiser’s ads in front of customers. Instead, we use Audience Signals to determine when an ad should be shown. (Audience Signals = one or more unique attributes, such as age/gender/LinkedIn-info/remarketing data.)
  1. MSAN ads do not appear on search engine results pages. Instead, they appear in native placements on MSN, Outlook, Edge, Syndicated Partners. These are known as Audience Ads.

2. Audience Ads

Audience ads do not have ranking positions and show exclusively on MSAN. Page placement is pre-determined by the publisher and ads will show in the slots the publisher has available for Audience Ads. For example, on the MSN homepage, as the publisher, MSN has decided Audience Ads will show in the infopane at the top of the page, as well as dedicated slots in both the middle and bottom of the page.

[Asset 4493002]

Where will Audience Ads show?

Audience ads will show on the Microsoft Audience Network on sites like MSN, Microsoft Outlook, Microsoft Edge, and other partners (with more to come over time).

What are the benefits of using Microsoft Audience Ads?
Along with being the only native advertising solution optimized for search advertisers, Audience Ads can be a powerful tool for advertisers.

  • Highly relevant audience. A variety of signals, such as search history, site remarketing and page content, help deliver ads that are highly relevant to customer intent. Advertisers can take advantage of these consumer intent signals to target their audience through our premium publishing partners.
  • Boosted volume and more conversions. Advertisers can scale ads to a larger audience and increase chances for conversions.
  • Ease of use. Advertisers can use familiar setup and campaign management tools that are fully integrated with Microsoft Advertising.

The Microsoft Audience Ads page can be shared with advertisers interested in learning more about this functionality.

Audience Ads support the following Microsoft Advertising features:

  • Text ads, image ads, and product ads.
  • All extensions (both manual and automated) that are available for standard Microsoft Advertising text ads.
  • Bing Shopping campaigns.
  • Remarketing - If the customer has a Microsoft user ID associated with a remarketing campaign, and the user is identified as engaging with related content on MSN, Bing will serve the relevant Audience Ad.
  • Available on all devices; PC, tablet, and mobile.

3. Creating Audience Ads

Advertisers can create Audience Ads in one of two ways:

  • Audience Ads in search (Ads will be created and run alongside standard Search Ads).
  • Audience Ads in dedicated MSAN campaigns.

Return to top

3.1 Audience Ads in search

To create an Audience Ads in Search campaign, see the About Microsoft Audience Ads article for additional information.

Advertisers should remember these steps:

  1. Optimise campaigns for Audience Ads by choosing bid modifiers (+900% to primarily target Audience Ads, or -100% to exclude Audience Ads.) This is done via the campaign or Ad Group Settings page, under Other Settings > Audience Ads.
  2. Indicate a preference that specific expanded text ads should be displayed in the Audience Ads format (on the Ad creation page).
  3. Create image extensions to support the ads, see KB 2686702 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Ad Extensions. Although images aren’t required, Audience Ads may not receive premium placement without them. Automated image extensions may be used to add image content if no image is provided, so a best practice is always to provide at least two images via Image Extensions.
  4. If they exist, advertisers can link to existing Remarketing/Audience lists via the Create Association option, on the Audiences sub-tab.
  5. Test and optimise, based on performance data in the campaign's grids and via reports.

Notes:

  • Audience Ads metrics (for example, CTR and Quality Score) will not impact search campaign performance.
  • Advertisers can create separate ad copy within a search campaign which will show only for Audience Ads placements. By using Custom Ad Text for Audience Ads, ad copy can be tailored specifically for native placements.
  • Every ad group requires at least one search ad copy in addition to custom ad copy for Audience Ads. If the ad group has only one ad and that ad is marked as preferred for Audience, then that flag will be ignored, and the ad will be served for both Audience Ads and search ads.
  • We recommend using custom ad text and applying bid modifiers to optimize Audience Ads being run through existing search campaigns.

3.2 Audience Ads in dedicated MSAN campaigns

To create an Audience Ads campaign, advertisers should follow the process outlined in How to create an audience campaign.

Things to note:

  • For dedicated MSAN campaigns, images are required, not optional. (Feeds will pull Images from the Shopping feed.)
  • The delivery SLA for new dedicated MSAN campaigns is 48 hours.

There are two type of Audience Ads that can be created in a dedicated MSAN campaign:

  1. Image-based Audience Ads:
    • Advertisers need to provide two images, a wide and a square image, and the accompanying image text.
    • Audience Ads will automatically resize the images to suit the native ads they’re delivered in.
    • Advertisers can also crop the image by moving the aspect-ratio window to display the desired imagery.
    • Advertisers can use the same creative assets as they use in Google Display Network, Facebook Audience Network, and Yahoo Gemini.
    • Advertisers can also select images from existing image extensions.
    • Stock Images are also available – See KB 2686702 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Ad Extensions for more information.
  1. Product Audience Ads (based on ‘Product Audience’ remarketing feed) – US only during pilot:
    • Images and targeting attributes are linked to one/more remarketing feeds, known as Product Audience Feeds.
    • Only available for Product (Shopping) Ads.
    • Customers who want to use Feed Based Ads must take the following steps first:
      1. Implement UET Tags on their website.
      2. Create a Bing Merchant Center store, see KB 3173658 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Bing Shopping & Bing Merchant Center.
      3. Create a Shopping Campaign.
      4. Create a Product Audience list, see KB 3184201 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Remarketing & Audience Targeting.

3.3 Audience Targeting explained

When an advertiser creates either type of Audience Ad campaign, they will need to select which audiences they wish to target (for example, Device/Age/Gender/Feed). Within each audience, they will be able to add/exclude segments, for example by country/region, age, gender, or desktop/tablet. See example below.

[Asset 4507689]

After they have selected the audiences to target, the advertiser will need to choose a Target Setting from one of two bid options:

  • Bid Only
  • Target and Bid

These determine how/when the Audience Ads will be eligible for delivery, as follows:

Bid only:

Ads will show to all customers in the Audience Network, (unless specifically excluded) and Bid Boosts will be applied to any audience segments that have been selected for a bid increase.

Target and bid:

Ads will only be shown to customers if they are within at least one of EACH audience that has been targeted. For example, in the screenshot above, the customer would need to be in the US and 18-24 and Female and on a desktop/tablet.

As a best practice, we recommend using Target and Bid first, and expanding to Bid Only if targeting seems too narrow.

3.4  Audience Network Planner and Personas

The Audience Network Planner works similarly to the Keyword Planner – customers can use the Audience Network Planner to research the size and makeup of audience segments – and combinations of segments – before creating an Audience campaign.

See the Research audience segments with the Audience Network Planner Help article for more information.

Persona management

Personas make it easier to save and apply a specific set of audience targeting to future campaigns. (For example, a “Young Americans” Persona could be used to target the 18-25 Age range with Location in United States.)

Personas can be created and edited within the Audience Network Planner. 

See the Research audience segments with the Audience Network Planner Help article for more information.

4. Image extensions, editorial reviews, and appeals 

Image extensions are recommended (but not required) to ensure Audience Ads will display in the best possible location on Bing. Note: Without image extensions, Bing will select an appropriate image using automated image extensions, which may not best reflect the advertiser’s marketing goals for the ad.

For this reason, it is recommended that advertisers manually add image extensions, to give them more control over their images and how their brand is showcased.

Images will be reviewed through the editorial system prior to going live. Review the help content to learn more about setup and image specification guidelines. Advertisers need to adhere to the current image policies regarding functionality and quality, see KB 2970603 Policy: Image, Audio, and Video Ad Policies (2970603) . Images will be stored in the DAM system and will be uploaded only when they have passed editorial review.

If an advertiser is meeting the editorial guidelines and was erroneously rejected, an appeal can be submitted to the editorial team following the normal appeals process.

5. Opting out of Audience Ads (Search campaigns only)

To stop receiving traffic from Audience Ads, you must set the Audience Ads Bid Modifier to -100% for all campaigns.

  1. From the Campaigns or Ad Groups tab, select the name of the campaign or ad group you want to adjust.
  2. Select Settings.
  3. Under Advanced settings, select Audience Ads.
  4. Adjust the bid:
    1. Select Decrease by if you want to invest less. To opt out of Audience Ads completely, decrease your bid by 100%.
    2. Select Increase by if you want to opt back in or invest more in Audience Ads for this campaign or ad group. You can adjust the bid up to 900% for maximum participation.

Note: These settings will only apply to existing campaigns/ad groups. Any new campaigns that are created/imported later will also need their Audience Ads Bid Adjustment value set to -100% (for opt-out).

What is the high-priority ad takedown process?
If an Audience Ad is displaying explicit/offensive content and needs to be removed ASAP, please send an email to the Audience Ads Engineering team (nativeads-dri@microsoft.com) with the R-Link or the Account ID and image details (for example, screenshot and image URL). Engineering will take the ad down within 24 hours (but likely much faster than that).

Note: Create a child ticket and email the team from the child, so that this is registered as an escalation in internal reporting.

Block-listed customers
Some specific advertisers, such as pharmaceutical and other sensitive business types, are block-listed from serving on the Audience Ad network. You will be advised if a customer is block-listed after escalating to Global Support for lack of impressions, we do not expose this information in the UI.

This is to prevent the risk of DSAT and poor end-user experiences by exposing sensitive products/services to customers which may not be relevant, or should not be exposed in this fashion due to industry/legal requirements.

6. Troubleshooting Audience Ad issues

Use the following guidance to solve for common Audience Ad setup issues:

Problem: Image uploads are generating errors (for example, height/width errors).

Solution:

  1. Ask customer for the image they want to upload.
  2. Check it meets Microsoft Advertising Image Requirements.
  3. Work with the customer to crop/swap the image for one that meets the guidelines and re-upload.

Problem: Non-Delivery or ‘Ad Group Privacy Pending’ error message.

Solution:

  1. Locate the Ad Group with this message.
  2. Check Ad Group Delivery Status. If the status is Ad group targeting too narrow, see KB 4514255 Delivery: Troubleshoot MSAN Audience campaigns with Privacy status TargetingTooNarrow.
  3. If this is not the issue, check the Audience List (such as Remarketing and Custom Audience) to see when these were created. There is a 48-hour delivery delay between adding a new list and delivery.
  4. If it has been more than 48 hours since the Audience List was added to the campaign, escalate to Global Support for assistance.

Problem: Ad headlines are being cut off when viewing them in the Audience Ads Ad Preview display.

Solution:

N/A – Headlines may be truncated based on the way various publishers show them. Advertisers can ensure ad headlines will show on all publisher sites by shortening their ad copy. Note: This is called out in the UI preview guidance: “These are examples of how your audience ads could appear. Depending on placement, the actual ad shown may appear differently -- for example, some text may be shortened.”

7. FAQ

Q: I don’t see Audience Ad impressions even though my bid modifiers are optimized for this service. Why is this? (To be used only with block-listed customers.)

A: Not all business types can be shown as Audience Ads, due to the sensitive nature of the product/service being offered by the business. For example, businesses providing services/products which require industry and legal regulation, may not be shown for this reason.

Q: Do Audience Ads include LinkedIn?

A: Currently no. For now, Audience Ads will only show on MSN and Outlook but as we continue to integrate LinkedIn into the Microsoft family, we will explore this possibility.

Q: Will Microsoft Advertising be the only ad provider for MSN and Outlook?

A: Microsoft Advertising is one of the many ad providers that MSN and Outlook use. In addition to Microsoft Advertising, some of the other companies powering ads are AppNexus, TripleLift, Taboola, and Outbrain.

Q: Is there a filtering process in place to avoid Audience Ads from serving next to unflattering news articles? For example: A Delta Audience Ad shows next to an article about a plane crash?

A: Audience Ads, like normal Microsoft Advertising, are set up to detect negative sentiment documents/topics algorithmically. When such an MSN page is detected, it will not render an Audience Ad. However, in the rare case Audience Ads miss it, you can escalate to Global Support and this will then be addressed with utmost priority.

Q: Should I anticipate traffic spikes with Audience Ads?

A: There can be times when an article gets very popular and receives abnormally high traffic. At the same time, that content is very relevant to an advertiser. This can lead to a situation where an advertiser can get much higher traffic than normal. Any traffic increase received by the advertiser is allowed if it is like the historical traffic an advertiser had previously received in their account.

Q: Do Audience Ads in MSAN campaigns have a dedicated r-link compatible with AdInquiry?

A: Yes. Can be captured in the same way by right clicking the ad. Some URLs may begin with https://www.bing.com/api/v1/. In this case, you will need to use the EXCELTOOLMACRO to generate the r-link.
Note: For Edge browser use, you may need to follow these steps to obtain the r-link:

  1. Press F12, navigate to the Elements tab, and search for the domain (for example, jcpenney) in the search bar.
  2. The r -link is included in the href=" " parameter.

Q: Do Audience Ads compete with other providers such as AppNexus or Taboola on websites like MSN or Outlook for bids/relevance?

A: Internal only: We share a percentage of traffic with other providers, yes. Certain areas of the page will be reserved exclusively for Audience Ad content from Search/MSAN campaigns.

Q: How should we optimise Audience Ads in MSAN campaigns?

A: You can use Bid Landscape settings to see how impression estimated would change, based on bid adjustments. Advertisers may want to have ad groups with lower bids set to target a broad range of dimensions, and a more specialised ad group set to target focused dimensions at a higher bid. The ads should also be tailored to suit the segments they’re targeting, and that there is enough budget to allow delivery.

Q: Can we import Audience Campaigns from Google Ads?

A: Yes.

Q: Can we manage Audience Campaigns using Bing Ads Editor?

A: Not at the current time.

Q: Can we check ad delivery using Ad Preview or a similar tool?

A: Not at the current time.

Q: Is there a full list of syndicated partners comprising the Audience Network available to share to advertisers?

A: We do not share a full list of partners, but we continue to expand. (For example, Verizon Media Properties.)
