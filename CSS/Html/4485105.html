2. Customer experience

2.1 How can customers access Xbox Refund Webform process?

All customers in regions in scope countries will go through the Xbox Refund Webform process to assist them to gather all the information needed to complete the form. For subscriptions only, frontline advocates should help these customers directly according to KB 4338529 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Exception based cancel and return for recurring subscriptions.

Customers sometimes bypass the Refund Request Solution by not selecting a refund as there intent when going through Contact Us to get to the phone and chat options.

Return to top

2.2 What is the customer experience after Xbox Refund Webform process?

  1. The customer goes through the Xbox Refund Webform process.
    • The customer, after determining the best refund request reason, is informed of any prerequisite actions and then lands on the webform. The webform has drop-downs and an optional open text field to allow customers to provide specifics on their request.
    • A customer who can self-serve a cancellation are sent to AMC to complete the cancellation.
  2. Customers receives an on-screen confirmation with an SR number and email confirmation. The SLA for response to the request is 72 hours.

3. Customer scenarios and contact handling

3.1 The customer is asking for a refund

Redirect the customer to one of these public articles:

  • CSS assist: Request an Xbox digital product refund: http://xbox.com/refundshelp.
  • Webform: Xbox Refund Webform process: http://xbox.com/refunds.

Important: Subscriptions exception.

Note: Non-Xbox team should transfer this scenario to Xbox team.

  • Subscription refund requests should be handled by frontline advocates without referring to the refund team. See KB 4338529 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Exception based cancel and return for recurring subscriptions.
  • Non-subscription refund requests during the same contact should follow procedures in this article.

Messaging:
“Refund requests can only be made through the form at http://xbox.com/refunds. Our dedicated team is ready to assist you.

Before you go, make sure you sign into the account with the purchase and have your order number. Order numbers can be found by accessing your purchase history on your Microsoft Account at https://account.microsoft.com/billing/orders.”

Exception scenario: If the customer is looking to change the version of a game, they can repurchase it and then you as an advocate can process a refund for the original version in a call or chat contact. This is a case where the refund is used as a troubleshooting step. Note: Non-Xbox team should transfer this scenario to Xbox team.

3.2 The customer is asking about the status of a refund request

Advise the customer that their request will be responded to within 72-hours and the financial institution may take up to five days to process the refund to their account. Customers can easily check the status of their refund at https://account.microsoft.com/billing/orders.

Note: Customers can also be reminded to check our homepage for notifications regarding delays in refund processing.

  • Report fraud on any attempts with suspicious verbiage or usage.

3.7 Subscription refund history

Refunds listed in the Alert section and comments on MST may not include all past subscription refunds. To confirm past refunds for subscriptions for the lifetime of the account:

  1. Go to account Status and determine when the account was created (for example, eight years ago).
  2. Go to Order History and select view all orders.
  3. Change the start date to the year the account was created (for example, 01/01/2011).
  4. Change Status to all Refunded.
  5. Count any refunds for the subscription type being requested.

4. FAQ

Q: What if the customer needs to cancel the subscription first before getting the refund?

A: Customers should be using the self-serve cancellation option on account.microsoft.com. If the customer is eligible for a refund, it will be applied automatically. Otherwise, they will be given the option to disable auto-renew to prevent future charges. Advocates can assist with cancellation.

Q: What if a customer insisted on getting the refund through us, because they don’t know the email nor the order number as it is an unknown charge the customer sees on their bank or CC statement that either doesn’t appear on their order history, or they don’t have a Microsoft account. Can we refer them to their bank?

A: Yes, refer the customer to their financial institution which can investigate the charge and issue a chargeback if warranted. Otherwise, the customer can get the order number to use in submitting their webform request from their order history.

See details in KB 2885545 Procedure: Handling a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner.

Q: What if they don’t have access on the associated email? Do they have an option to provide an alternate email address?

A: They need the email address (Microsoft account) used for the original purchase to submit a refund request via the webform, to verify their account. However, after signing in, they have the option of providing an alternative email address for future communication.

7. Escalation

Customers:

For scenarios where customers land in our queues after filling out the webform (complaints), review the SR and advise the customer to reply to the decision email.

Advocates:

If you, as an advocate, feel there was an error in the resolution provided, or that the refund advocates did not provide enough information, escalate the case immediately via email to: Refund Escalations Concentrix_Xbox_All@concentrix.com. This email alias is internal only and not to be shared with customers. Make sure to have your site SME/Team Lead or Ranger review the escalation prior to sending the email.

After the Xbox refund team receives the escalation, they will contact the customer directly if there is a change in the case (for example, provided refund) and follow up with escalated advocate to share what was found upon review for info sharing purposes. If there is no change in the case, the customer will not receive a response.

If the customer replies to the refund decision email to request more info or dispute (instead of advocate escalating to above team), they will receive a response when the reopened case is processed.
