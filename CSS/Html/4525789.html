ASMS – Modern Commerce – Partner Led (CSP)

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

Modern Commerce – Partner Led is the third phase of the Modern Commerce platform rollout. This phase is targeted at existing and new Cloud Solution Providers (CSPs) and Partners.

Partners will be required to sign a new Microsoft Partner Agreement (MPA). More information on the agreement is available in the Microsoft Partner Agreement for Cloud Solution Provider Partners document.

After signing the new MPA, Partners can transition their customers to the Modern platform using Partner Center and APIs. Partners can also onboard new customers to the Modern Commerce platform using Partner Center. Partners will receive monthly invoices for their customers in their Billing Profile.

In Modern Partner Led, there are two types of Partners:

  • Tier One (T1) – T1 Partners who directly interact with customers to provide billing, technical support, and service administration.
  • Tier Two (T2) – Partners who purchase services from T1 Partners which they resell to end customers.

Partners are supported using the Commerce Support Tool (CST). More information is available in the CST user guide (TSG 4487922).

For more information on distinguishing Partners in CST, see the How to Distinguish Partners in CST scenario.

Policy

Offer Limitations

  • Standard (17G) Modern subscriptions are available during this phase.
  • Dev/Test (148G) Modern subscriptions are not available during this phase.

Pricing

  • Partners have an incentive program coupled with the roll out of Modern to CSP.
    • More information is available on the Partner Incentives page (requires a Partner login).
  • Prices are not published for the end customers of this phase.
    • Partners can show their customers standard Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) rates in the Azure portal. They can do so by navigating to the Cost Management + Billing page, clicking on the Policies tab, and enabling the Users in tenant with access to an Azure subscription can view charges at retail price option.

Steps for CSP to CSP Billing Ownership Transfers (High Level)

Note: This workflow is like a Billing Ownership Transfer request.

  1. The new partner creates a relationship with a customer in Partner Center following the steps outlined in the customer-facing How to add a new customer record in Partner Center page.
    • This operation creates an Azure Plan for that customer and provides the Partner access to the end customer’s tenant.
  2. The new partner initiates a Billing Ownership Transfer request in Partner Center with the information of the source partner provided by the end customer.
    • The request is active for 30 days.
    • The new partner can check the status of transfer requests in Partner Center while in the context of their end customer.
  3. The source partner will receive a Billing Ownership Transfer request email.
    • This email includes a link to Partner Center and provides the source partner a list of eligible subscriptions to transfer to the destination partner.
  4. The source partner selects the subscriptions to transfer with inputs from the end customer. After the transfer request is approved, the transfer of Billing Ownership of selected subscriptions is immediately initiated.
    • The source partner is responsible for all purchases up to the point of transfer.
    • The destination partner is responsible for all purchases after the point of transfer.
  5. After the transfer is complete, the destination partner will see the transferred subscriptions in Partner Center. With the proper level of access, they can administer and purchase services under those subscriptions.

Billing Support

All billing support is done through CST.
 

Quota Increase

Subscriptions will remain in place as 17G subscriptions. However, Partner Led quota requests will follow the same process as 145P Legacy CSP subscriptions.

Follow existing guidance for quota in Legacy 003P offers. See TSG 4086154 (Quota Increases) for additional information.

Note: When searching in CMAT or CST, the subscription will show as a 17G subscription. However, when using the ARM endpoint in Jarvis, the subscription will show the Offer ID as 145P.
 

Credits and Refunds

  • All refunds can be processed by ASMS using the steps outlined in TSG 4487934.
  • Credits can only be provided by the Commerce Engineering Escalations (CEE) Partner Led team.
     

Payment Instrument (PI) Management

Customers must manage their PI by using the Billing Profile tab in the Cost Management + Billing blade. Support on behalf of customers for PI management is not currently available in CST.

Customers with PI management issues can be escalated to the ACE team via ASE.

Note: ACE should only be contacted through the ASE escalation path. ASE will engage ACE and get back to you with an appropriate response. For more information, see TSG 3093263.
 

Customer Led Subscriptions Joining Partner Led Subscriptions​

Partners will be unable to pull in a Customer Led subscription. The customer must perform a resource migration to a newly set up subscription by their Partner Led Partner. More information is available on the customer-facing Move resources to a new resource group or subscription page.
 

Direct Enterprise Agreement (EA) to Partner Led Billing Ownership Transfer

Only EA subscriptions are supported with this functionality.

Partner Led Partners can transfer billing ownership of subscription billing responsibilities from other accounts (specifically 17P subscriptions) by following the steps outlined on the customer-facing Get billing ownership of Azure subscriptions to your MPA account page.

For customers that are having issues with migration, submit an IcM to the CEE Partner Led team. Details required for this increase can be found in the IcM template.

Requirements:

  • The Partner must be an Azure Expert Managed Services Provider (MSP).
    • More information is available on the customer-facing Azure Expert Managed Services Provider (MSP) page.
  • The source subscription must be an EA subscription.
  • This transfer is self-serve only.
  • Until Partner Led Dev/Test subscriptions are introduced, EA Dev/Test (148P) subscriptions will be converted to a standard (17G) subscription and will not benefit from Dev/Test pricing.
  • The Partner must have a destination Azure Access Pass set up.
  • SaaS, Reserved Instance (RI), and Marketplace Subscription assets will not transfer and must be cancelled in the source then rebought in the destination.

Partner Led partners use self-service functionality documented at Billing Ownership request functionality.

Microsoft has restricted access to the Direct EA to CSP Transfer tool for all S500 (strategic) customers and customers consuming more than $1M of Azure Container Registry (ACR) per year. This was done to ensure that EA customers fully understand the implications of losing their EA benefits if they transfer their Direct EA Azure subscriptions to CSP.

When a Partner attempts to invite a customer in this category to transition to CSP through the tool, the Partner receives an error message requesting additional approvals. While we advise Partners against having these customers transfer to CSP, Pro Direct Managers can initiate a process on behalf of Partners and customers to secure approval for CSP transfers through the tool.

The approval process is as follows:

  1. The end customer, Partner, and end customer Account Team must discuss the limitations described in the Customer Checklist slide.
  2. The end customer, Partner, and end customer Account Team must provide written confirmation of their agreement to move to CSP despite the known limitations.
  3. The Partner must submit an approval form to the Partner Business Desk conveying the decision to transfer to CSP, or engage the Partner Business Desk for an EA Outsourcer Enrollment alternative.
    1. Email cspmgr@microsoft.com and ask for a copy of the request form.
    2. Submit the completed form with e-mail approvals to the Partner Business Desk on the Business Desk 360 CRM Tool page.

Issues with migration – Ensure CSPs meet the pre-requisites in the public documentation, then escalate through IcM to the Azure Commercial Experiences team.

Migration Tooling – Review the Transfer from Direct EA to CSP documentation to learn more.

Guidance for Azure Expert MSP Partners

If an Azure Expert MSP partner needs help evaluating whether it is appropriate to transition an EA customer subscription to a CSP follow these steps:

  1. Are you trying to transfer to CSP?
    1. If Yes, move on to question 2.
  2. Are you an Azure Expert MSP?
    1. If Yes, move on to question 3.
    2. If No, provide Resource Migration public documentation.
  3. Are you trying to transfer an EA Direct subscription to CSP?
    1. If Yes, provide MPA Request Ownership public documentation.
    2. If No, provide Resource Migration public documentation.

Note: You can also provide the partner with the Partner Center link to access the consultation help form a Partner Technical Consultant: Technical Presales and Deployment Assistance.

  • Ensure the Request Title contains: <Direct EA to CSP TRANSITION> Partner Company Name

To provide a single combined invoice for managed services and Azure consumption, a CSP can take over billing ownership of Azure subscriptions from their customers with Direct EAs. Additional guidance can be found in the public-facing documentation.

Note: Currently, Azure does not support Indirect EA enrollments  for transferring Billing Ownership to CSP.
 

CSP to CSP Billing Ownership Transfers

Limitations

  • Support is not able to perform transfers on behalf of the customer.
  • Reservations and Marketplace SaaS products will not transfer.
    • The source partner and end customer must coordinate to cancel these products and the destination partner can rebuy.

Additionally, you should note the following about these transfers:

  • Customers are expected to communicate with their current and future partners.
    • We cannot communicate on their behalf.
  • The burden of accuracy of the information required to perform a Billing Ownership Transfer is on the end customer.
  • Do not provide any contact information, including tenant details, to the source or destination partner.
  • Partners can coordinate with their customers to transfer subscriptions from one Partner Led CSP to a new Partner Led CSP. This functionality is self-serve through the Partner Center for the CSP  Global Admin or Admin Agent:
    1. In the Partner Center, click Customers,
    2. Select the customer name for the transfer request.
    3. Click Subscriptions.
    4. Click Transfer Requests.
  • ASMS does not have a support scope beyond providing links to the public documentation.
    • For Partners who are having issues with CSP to CSP billing ownership transfers, submit an IcM to the Partner Center Engineering team. Details required for this increase can be found in the IcM template.

Partner Led to Partner Led Escalations

As part of Partner Led to Partner Led Transition, Microsoft is enabling a capability within Partner Center to transfer Azure plans between partners. For the context of this scenario, the target partner is the one who is gaining business from the customer and source partner is the one losing business.

Note: Microsoft is not an active party to the agreement between the customer and the source partner. Microsoft also, has no knowledge of the contents of the agreement between the customer and the source partner. It is not appropriate for Microsoft to get involved in any dispute between the two parties of the agreement. Instead the following should occur:

  • The customer should refer to the relevant cancellation terms in their agreement and work with the source partner to cancel their agreement in alignment with those terms.
  • If for whatever reason the source partner is not cooperative or responsive, and the customer feels the source partner is not honoring the (cancellation) terms of their agreement, they should leverage any relevant dispute resolution options available to them per the terms of their agreement.

Should a target partner escalate a situation where the source partner is not acknowledging the customer transfer request, do the following:

  1. Provide the details mentioned above to the target partner.
  2. Send an "FYI" email to Azure CSP Migration alias (OCP) in response to the escalation. Be sure to provide the following in the email:
    1. Summarize the guidance provided to the partner.
    2. Clearly marked the email as “FYI only”, to make it clear that no further action is required at this time.
    3. Be sure the customer nor the case email tag is on the To or Cc line.

Note: OCP will centrally track and monitor these escalations and provide visibility to the source and/or target partner PDMs as applicable. If needed, corrective measures (by the PDM) can be taken if specific source partners are found to repeatedly be the subject of escalation.

  1. Close the case.
     

How to Distinguish Partners in CST

  1. Open CST.
  2. Search for the subscription or account.
  3. Select the desired account from the results.
  4. See the corresponding Purchase Context field under the Billing Account Details heading.
    • Indirect – Indirect Customer is a CSP Customer
    • PartnerIndirect – Indirect Partner is a T2 Partner
    • PartnerReseller – Partner Led is a T1 Partner
