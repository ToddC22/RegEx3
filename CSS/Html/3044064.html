ASMS – Subscription Disabled and Dunning Process

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to assist customers that are experiencing dunning, disablement, and deprovisioning of services due to payment issues.

This TSG applies to all web direct customers that are:

  • Using expired credit cards.
  • Having Azure charges declined because of credit card limits.
  • Disputing charges on their invoices and withholding payment.

Definitions

  • Dunning: This is the process of communicating with customers who have not paid for their services. These communications happen through portal notifications and emails to the Account Administrator (AA).
  • Canceled: This subscription state occurs when a customer shuts off their account (either through self-serve or through a support ticket).
  • Soft disabled: This is an intermediate subscription state between Active and Disabled in which billing is stopped, and the customer’s services are switched to a read-only state. Services will continue to run until the subscription is fully disabled.
  • Disabled: This is a subscription state in which all services are stopped, and all storage is switched to a read-only state . All services will need to be redeployed if the balance is settled on an account that is in this state. An account will be deprovisioned if it is left in this state for 90 days.
  • Deprovisioned: When an account is in this state, all data related to the subscription is deleted and cannot be recovered.
    • Unless specifically expedited by the customer or changed by an extenuating circumstance, this occurs 90 days after a subscription has been disabled.
  • Banned payment instrument: This occurs when the dunning system has banned a credit card. These cards should not have charge attempts made against them.
    • Shows as BanPI in our tools.

Dunning and Account Status Context

The following sections detail various processes and statuses related to dunning and deprovisioning.
Note the following about this section:

  • For credit cards, no charge attempts are made on days 28 – 31 of any given months.
  • There are differences in case handling between Legacy and Modern accounts. Be sure to verify which type of account you’re supporting before referencing the following sections.

Invoice Dunning Cycle
The invoice dunning cycle uses five emails to warn a customer and then disables their account if they fail to pay. Acting on the Disputes:

  • Global Financial Services (GFS) will work with the SE to determine:
    1. If the current action is on the customer side to make a payment:
      • With the instructions from GFS, you will inform the customer that their subscription will disable in seven days if they do not provide proof of payment.
    2. If Microsoft is still investigating the usage validation internally:
      • GFS will work with you to ensure the investigation completes in a timely manner.

Action When a Customer Refuses to Pay:

  1. If a customer fails to provide payment, GFS will create a document outlining a request to disable their account for non-payment and will include all relevant details.
    • The information in the document includes customer name, company name, outstanding balance, duration of the billing dispute, account manager (if applicable), and the justification for suspending the subscription.
  2. If applicable, GFS will engage the customer’s account manager to notify them of the outstanding balance as a final effort to collect payment.
  3. If there is no payment after engaging the account manager, GFS will inform the account manager that the customer subscription must disable to avoid any future charges.
  4. Azure Operations (AO) will give you the customer detail document to inform account managers of disablement for non-payment, in the event of an executive escalation.
  5. After all parties are aware of the pending disablement, GFS proceeds in suspending the customer’s subscription.
    • If the customer pushes to activate the subscription again, you may activate the subscription for a 48-hour window and engage GFS for further advice. Azure Operations will review the extension request and advise you accordingly.

Support Scenarios

Invoice Customer Is Disputing Their Bill (Modern and Legacy)

If the customer is disputing an invoice while in dunning, do the following:

  1. Engage Global Financial Services (GFS, formerly GCS/WOCS) through a task.
    • Include the invoice number that the customer is disputing.
    • See TSG 2647567 for more information about GFS.
  2. GFS will work with you to determine next steps for the customer’s subscription.
  3. If the customer refuses to settle their balance, the following actions will occur (on GFS’s side).
    1. If a customer fails to provide payment, GFS will create a document outlining a request to disable the customer’s account for non-payment and will include all relevant details.
      • The information in this document includes customer name, company name, outstanding balance, duration of the billing dispute, account manager (if applicable), and the justification for suspending the subscription.
    2. If applicable, GFS will engage the customer’s account manager to notify them of the outstanding balance as a final effort to collect payment.
      • If there is no payment after engaging the account manager, GFS will inform the account manager that the customer subscription will be disabled to avoid any further charges.
      • In the event of an executive escalation, Enterprise Azure Operations (EAO) will provide a detailed document to inform account managers of disablement for non-payment.

Note: If the customer pushes for re-activation, you may re-activate the subscription for 48 hours and engage GFS for further guidance. EAO will review the extension request and advise you accordingly.

Reinstate Disabled Subscriptions

Customers must contact support to re-enable a disabled subscription. Support engineers (SEs) may be able to re-enable the subscription depending on the disablement reason. To assist a customer with a disabled subscription, do the following:

  1. Determine the reason for the disabled status.
    • Examples: Non-payment, monetary cap, customer self-cancellation.
  2. Follow the process outlined in TSG 2651536.
  3. After completing the process in the above TSG, verify that the subscription is fully active with the following steps.
    1. If the customer has a Modern account:
      1. Open the customer’s account in CST.
      2. Navigate to the Assets + Subscriptions tab.
      3. Verify that the State column for the customer’s account is listed as Active.
    2. If the customer has a Legacy account:
      1. Open the customer’s account in CMAT.
      2. Verify that all the following statuses are Active:
        • Subscriptions Status
        • Entitlement Status
        • Fulfillment Status
    3. In Jarvis, navigate to AzureRT/Subscription Management/Get Subscription with Details.
      • Check for SubscriptionStatus:Active in the Red Dog Front End (RDFE) status field.
    4. In Jarvis, navigate to Azure Resource Manager/Subscription State Management/Get subscription.
      • Check for State: “Enabled” in the Azure Resource Manager (ARM) status field.

Helping Customers Recover from a Subscription Disablement

  • After disablement, a customer may need technical support to get their services and data re-deployed.
    • For example, the customer may need assistance re-deploying their cloud services (which get deleted when the subscription becomes disabled) or recovering their IP addresses (which are deleted unless the customer configured Reserved IPs).
  • In these scenarios, open a new case for the customer and transfer it to the correct Azure Technical Support team.

Pause Dunning Process

In exceptional circumstances, we can pause the dunning process for customers or delay their payment dates.
Reference the following sections for instructions on how to do this based on the customer’s type.

Invoice and Enterprise Agreement (EA) Customers
For Invoice and Enterprise Agreement (EA) customers, we can pause dunning until the 20th day of the following month.
For example, invoices due in March can have dunning paused until April 20, April invoices can have dunning paused until May 20, and so on.

To assist Invoice and EA customers, do the following:

  1. Send a task to the GCS Direct CAT queue using the following template:

Note: If possible, have the customer provide an official request document from their company’s senior executive, such as the CEO or CFO.

  1. GFS will reply to your task with a decision in the following time frame:
    • If the amount to be delayed is less than $100K, GFS will reply within 7 business days.
    • If the amount to be delayed is more than $100K, GFS will reply within 10 business days.

Credit Card Customers
For credit card customers, we can shift a bill’s due date to 30 days into the future. The support actions for Legacy versus Modern accounts differ. Verify the customer’s account type before using the following sections.

To assist Legacy credit card customers, do the following:
Note: You will need to engage your TA/PTA to complete this action in CMAT. If this process fails, engage PayOps for further assistance.

  1. Customers can only have this type of deferral done once. Verify that the customer has not had this deferment performed previously.
    • Check the Support Actions tab in CMAT to determine if the customer has already had a deferral applied.
  2. In CMAT, click on the Billing tab.
  3. Click on the Charges sub-tab.
  4. Check the customer’s balance status.
    • We are looking for the balances in question to be in a Requested or Failed status, and a balance type of ScheduleSettle.
    • If the balance the customer wants to pause is in an Open status, run the Process Balance function to change the balance status to Requested.
  5. For balances that meet the above criteria, engage your TA/PTA so they can update the billing due date to be 30 days after the current billing date.
    • If CMAT asks for comments when you update the due date, put “N/A” in the comments field.
    • If the balance is in a Failed status prior to your update, update the status to Requested.

To assist Modern credit card customers, do the following:

  1. Customers can only have this type of deferral done once. Verify that the customer has not had this deferment performed previously.
    • Check the Activities tab of CST to determine if the customer has already had a deferral applied.
  2. Escalate to your TA/PTA, they will extend dunning using the Extend Due Date option in CST for up to 30 days. 
    • TA/PTAs - follow TSG 4487934 section 8.4 for process. 

Cloud Solution Provider Customers
To assist Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) customers, transfer the entire case to the Partner Center team.
