1. Overview

Customers may run into issues when completing their transactions if they are declined.
The reasons for a transaction to be declined include:

  • Scenario 1: Microsoft flags the transaction as a potential risk and blocks the purchase, order, or device registration as “Risk Rejected.”
  • Scenario 2: The customer’s financial institution declines the transaction.
  • Scenario 3: There is an account violation on the Microsoft account.

This article covers the first two scenarios (1 & 2). For scenario 3, check the Additional resources section 3 of this article.

Additionally, to determine if the customer is experiencing issues related to either risk rejections or payment instrument failures, review the Order section/Risk Status tab in the tool and check the Reason Code.

These are two specific error messages that are in-scope for this article. If the customer reports any other error messages or the tool shows other errors, review KB 2846008 Process: Billing Errors.

Note: Never tell a customer that their transaction did not work due to “fraud” or “risk”. Instead, you can tell the customer that the transaction has been declined. Scripting guidance is provided within this article.

2. Procedures

2.1 Review the order for Risk Reasons

In determining if a transaction requires further review by Risk Ops, follow the steps below to review the customer’s purchase or order for Risk Reasons.

If you encounter a Risk Reject error when attempting to register a customer’s device serial number (Xbox, Surface) against a Microsoft account, skip to section 2.2.

1. Ensure the correct Microsoft account used to purchase a product or service is verified following KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

2. You will find information regarding Risk alerts in two areas within MST:

a. Alerts > Risks: Expand the section. This will display some high-level account risks that have occurred on the account.

 [Asset 4468916]

b. Orders > Risk Status: This has risk information specifically related to an order or line item. If inquiring about an order more than 90 days old, you will need to search for the order using a date range by selecting view all.

 [Asset 4468915]

3. Go to the Orders section. Under Order State, look for RiskRejected or CheckingOut. Click on the down arrow to expand the entry.

Note: If a RiskRejected or CheckingOut entry does not exist, review section 2.3 scenarios 1 or 2. Proceed to next step as appropriate.

[Asset 4468914][Asset 4468913]

4. Review the Risk Status tab of the specific transaction. Check the Reason Code for any additional instructions. 

 [Asset 4468912] [Asset 4468911] [Asset 4497750]

5. Follow section 2.1, section 2.2 and section 2.3 to determine if an escalation to Risk Ops is appropriate. If the transaction displays Risk Rejected, it is generally appropriate to engage Risk Ops.
The Reason Code provides next steps guidance and may indicate “Follow normal procedures for review” or “If risk declined, please escalate to RiskOps for investigation” when appropriate to engage RiskOps. Make sure not to miss any conditional statements (for example, must meet time requirements in some instances before escalating).

6. Relay to the customer that you are having a specialist review the transaction and that you will provide a resolution back to the customer once a resolution is available. Do NOT disclose to the customer their transaction was rejected by the RiskOps team.

Suggested script:
“Our systems identified something a little out of the ordinary with the payment for [insert product or service paid] and for your protection, we stopped the transaction. I will need to have a specialist further review your transaction. Before I can do this, let me gather additional details from you.”

7. Proceed to the section 2.4.

2.2 Device registration triggers Risk Reject error

Customers who attempted to register their devices online OR through a call center advocate may be prompted that their device serial number cannot be registered due to Risk Reject.

Advocate facing message: Will prompt to use this article for resolution. [Asset 4468910]

Customer facing message: Will indicate the device could not be registered and prompt to contact Customer Support. Note: You should still attempt to register the device in MST to confirm that the error was due to Risk Reject.

[Asset 4471370]

When you encounter this scenario, follow the steps below.

  1. On approval from a Tech Lead or SME, relay to the customer that you are having a specialist review the transaction and that you will provide a resolution back to the customer once a resolution is available. Do NOT disclose to the customer their transaction was rejected by the Risk Ops team.

Suggested script:
“Our systems identified something a little out of the ordinary with your device and for your protection, we stopped the registration. I will need to have a specialist further review this. Before I can do this, let me gather additional details from you.”

  1. Proceed to the section 2.4.

2.3 Additional scenarios and advocate actions

Scenario

Advocate action

1. Order State indicates Pending AND Risk Status indicates Risk Review

Check how long the order has been in this state. Unless the Reason Code indicates alternative timeline requirements, determine next actions based on below:

1. Digital orders

  • Less than 8 hours in Pending or Risk Review state, inform customer that their order is still processing, and we appreciate their patience. Do NOT engage Risk Ops.
  • More than 8 hours Pending or Risk Review state, consult your Tech Lead or SME to engage Risk Ops.

2. Physical orders

  • Less than 48 hours in Pending or Risk Review state, inform customer that their order is still processing and we appreciate their patience. Do NOT engage Risk Ops.
  • More than 48 hours Pending or Risk Review state, consult your Tech Lead or SME to engage Risk Ops.

3. Repair / fulfillment orders for devices

  • Less than 6 hours in Pending or Risk Review state, inform customer that their order is still processing, and we appreciate their patience. Do NOT engage Risk Ops.
  • More than 6 hours Pending or Risk Review state, consult your Tech Lead or SME to engage Risk Ops.

Tech Leads or SME: If you received consults for orders being stuck in Pending or Risk Review for periods longer than listed above and this amounts to 15% of total call volume (or greater than 10 unique cases), from that point forward, escalate to CSS Command Center (CSS CC) as a high volume incident instead of a Risk Ops escalation. Follow KB 3038858 Process: How and when to engage CSS Command Center (CSS CC) – all regions.

2. Order State indicates Declined OR Risk Status indicates transaction was declined by payment provider or bank

Refer the customer to their financial institution to determine the reason for the decline. The customer’s financial institution will need to resolve this.

Suggested script:
“It looks as though your financial institution has declined the transaction. You will need to contact them to resolve the issue, and then try your purchase again.”

3. Reason Code indicates DO NOT ENGAGE: Customer payment information does not match account information. Customer should ensure that payment and account information matches.

Have the customer double check and update their information on account.microsoft.com.

Do NOT engage Risk Ops even if purchase is not successful.

4. Reason Code indicates DO NOT ESCALATE: Ask customer to try a payment option that matches the Store region or change the Store region to match the region that issued the payment option.

Follow guidance provided online.

If the customer’s payment instrument is valid for the region (matching), alert your Tech Lead or SME.

Tech Leads or SME: If you received consults for this error and this amounts to 15% of total call volume (or greater than 10 unique cases), from that point forward, escalate to CSS Command Center (CSS CC) as a high-volume incident. Follow KB 3038858 Process: How and when to engage CSS Command Center (CSS CC) – all regions.

5. Reason Code indicates Customer has exceeded spending limits. Customer should wait before attempting another purchase or use a gift card. Follow normal procedures for review.

Engage Risk Ops on approval from Tech Lead or SME and only if customer:

  • Acknowledges recent spending activity.
  • Is not willing to use a gift card.
  • Does not want to wait to make their purchase (minimum 2 hours, preferably 24 hours) AND all error messages related to a given payment instrument indicate escalation.

6. Reason Code indicates DO NOT ENGAGE: We have received one or more notifications from your bank disputing purchases made by this account. To protect you and Microsoft from future fraud or abuse, future purchases have been suspended for this account.

Inform customer to use a gift card from retailer to make purchases from Microsoft.

Do NOT engage Risk Ops.

Suggested script: text in Reason code.

7. Other Reason codes not listed

Follow the instructions provided in the Reason Code. If clarification on action is needed, consult your Tech Lead for assistance.

Tech Leads or SME: Consult Microsoft as needed.

2.4 How to escalate to Risk Ops

Follow section 2.1, section 2.2 and section 2.3 to determine if an escalation to Risk Ops is appropriate. If the transaction displays Risk Rejected, it is generally appropriate to engage Risk Ops. The Reason Code provides next steps guidance and may indicate “Follow normal procedures for review” or “If risk declined, please escalate to RiskOps for investigation” when appropriate to engage Risk Ops. Make sure not to miss any conditional statements (for example, must meet time requirements in some instances before escalating).

1. Ensure you gather and document the customer’s circumstances. These details will assist in the review process and investigation by Risk Ops. First two bullet points are mandatory.

  • Summary of the conversation.
  • Clear indication of the specific issue the customer is facing, and steps taken so far by the customer.
  • Additional details that may help a risk investigation (provide if appropriate to the customer’s situation):
    • Outside of the transaction in question, did the customer attempt other orders?
    • Did the customer create other Microsoft accounts to try and get around the issue they are facing? If so, how many accounts?
    • Is the customer currently traveling?
    • Is the customer using someone else’s credit card?
    • If any shipping is involved, is the customer shipping to someone or somewhere else?
    • If this is a repair/fulfillment order for Xbox or Surface, the serial number of the device.
    • If discount was applied, why was it applied?
    • Any verifiable non Personal Data the customer is willing to provide (examples below):
      • If the caller is from a school, but did not use the school email address for their purchase, the domain of the school (for example, @nameofschool.edu);
      • If the caller is from a company/corporation, but did not use their work email address for their purchase, the domain of the company/corporation (for example, @nameofcompany.com);
      • If shipping to a school or company/corporation, the name of the school or company/corporation at the ship to address.

2. In case notes of the incident form, ensure you document what are listed under the details needed column.

Details needed in case notes

Instructions

Parent SR#

Document the original SR# (not the SR# being escalated to Risk Ops as a Child SR)

Risk Tracking ID

Expand the transaction in the Orders section of MST. Tracking ID is listed under Risk Status. [Asset 4468909]

Order State

Document the Order State in MST for the transaction in question. [Asset 4468908]

Date / Time

Expand the transaction in the Orders section of MST. Date and Time of order was placed is listed under Details. [Asset 4468907]

Product(s) / Service(s) ordered

List the product(s) and/or service(s) ordered, including any special offers or discounts applied on the order.

Summary of customer situation

Document the details collected from step 1 above.

3. Follow section 1.2 on KB 2981105 Process: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement to invoke the Advanced Capability Engagement Form.

Note: While the Advanced Capability Engagement Form is used, you will escalate the case to Risk Ops directly. The ACA team will not be directly involved, so do not engage them.

4. On the engagement form, ensure the following values are set. Regardless of the line of business you support, you must select these values.

  • Lifecycle: Accounts & Billing
  • Line of Business: Billing
  • Engagement Type: Risk rejection

 [Asset 4468906]

Note: Alternatively, Sales advocate can only choose the following values. Step 5 must be followed to ensure the Child SR reaches the Risk Ops team.

  • Lifecycle: Sales & Devices
  • Line of Business: Sales
  • Engagement Type: Risk rejection

[Asset 4497944]

5. If you are a Surface or Xbox advocate, proceed to step 6. If you are an Accounts & Billing or Sales advocate, follow these steps before proceeding to step 7.

  • Surface or Xbox advocate: Your Child SR automatically escalated to Risk Ops. Proceed to step 6.
  • Accounts & Billing or Sales advocate: To ensure your Child SR reaches the Risk Ops team, manually transfer the Child SR. When done, proceed to step 7.
    • On saving the Child SR, select Transfer.

[Asset 4471868]

  • Look for Transfer Type = MS Store Fraud Review Escalations. Ensure the Transfer Destination = Microsoft Store CP Fraud Operations Escalation, then complete the manual transfer.

Note: The exact Transfer Type may NOT exist at all call center locations. Look for a Transfer Type where Transfer Destination = Microsoft Store CP Fraud Operations Escalation.

[Asset 4471869]

  • You can alternatively use Qview to manually transfer the Child SR to Risk Ops. If you prefer to do this instead, follow KB 4050317 Tool User Guide: Assisted Support Desktop (ASD) – Transfers . Look for the same Transfer Destination as above.

6. If the request is to review a repair/fulfillment order for Xbox or Surface, ensure the Subject of the incident form on the Child SR contains “Device Repair”.

 [Asset 4468905]

7. Set expectations with the customer on next steps. The Parent SR will get updated by the Risk Ops team with guidance within 24 hours.

Note: You are expected to provide a final response back to the customer based on the input from Risk Ops even if on occasion, they reach out to the customer directly for more information.

2.5 Reviewing status/outcome of recent escalation

For cases submitted to the Risk Ops team for review, you can expect an update from them to be visible under comments within the incident. It provides an outcome and messaging to relay to the customer.

[Asset 4505142]

The table below explains what you should expect to see from the Risk Ops team, as well as any additional instructions.

Scenario

In Comments within the Incident

Additional Advocate instructions

**UNDER INVESTIGATION**

Case is still under review

We are currently looking into this and will provide an update as soon as possible.

 

**REACTIVATED**

Risk Ops removed risk blocks

This order was cancelled in error. Please assist in placing a new order for the customer. All account holds or locks have been removed. We advise customers and advocates to wait at least 30 minutes- 1 hour after the response is received to attempt to purchasing again due to the risk of order running in Risk status again. Suggested messaging for the customer:

“Thank you for your patience while we investigated your issue. We have finished our investigation and found your order was improperly cancelled. Unfortunately, I’m unable to save your previous order, but am happy to assist you in placing a new order.”

If the purchase still fails, restart the process following section 2.1 of this article. If an escalation is necessary, remember to reference the original SR.

 

If escalating a new child SR, make sure to gather as much detail about the purchase, or unique circumstances like using a new address for billing or shipping information. This insight helps inform the Risk Ops team of information not available on the customer account.

**DECISION UPHELD**

Transaction remains blocked due to suspicious activity

We have reviewed this account and confirmed that due to suspicious activity, this customer will not be permitted to make purchases on the Microsoft platform.  Please terminate all communication with this customer, and do not attempt to assist in placing additional orders for this customer.  

 

Please provide the following message to the user and end all communication: 

 

“We have performed a thorough analysis of the account.  Due to suspicious activity associated with this account, we will not be reinstating this account and supporting further purchases.  I understand the inconvenience this places on you, but I will be unable to provide further assistance regarding this account or the transactions.”

 

**DECISION UPHELD**

Transaction remains blocked due to chargebacks

We will not be able to resolve this issue.  Going forward the customer is only able to purchase using prepaid methods.  Please give the customer the following message:

 

“We have received one or more notifications from your bank disputing purchases made by this account.  To protect you and Microsoft from future fraud or abuse, future purchases have been suspended for this account.    I understand that this is not an ideal solution and I apologize that we are not able to resolve this issue as you would like.  Given the nature of this issue, there is no further support that we can provide.”

 

**DECISION UPHELD**

Spending limit reached

We will not be able to resolve this issue, the customer has reached the spending limit for their account. Please ask them to wait up to one week before attempting to make purchases. During that time, they may continue to use gift cards or prepaid tokens with their account. 

 

Give the customer the following message:

 

"Our escalations review team has completed their investigation and determined that you have reached the spending limit for your account.   Please wait up to one week before attempting to make purchases. During that time, you may continue to use gift cards or prepaid tokens with your account.  We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience that you have experienced, and I appreciate your understanding and patience while this problem was investigated."

2.6 Customer disagrees with outcome of recent escalation / discussing review logic with the customer

If a customer was recently escalated for risk rejection but disagrees with the outcome of the escalation (for example, Risk Rejection upheld), they may seek additional information.

Due to the sensitive nature of methods used to review financial transactions, it is important to avoid speculation when discussing why any given transaction or payment instrument was not authorized.

Follow these rules:

  • Any information not clearly indicated by Risk Ops for communicating with the customer should be considered restricted information and should not be discussed.
  • If you are unsure whether a certain piece of information can be discussed, review the resolution provided by Risk Ops carefully. If you need further clarity, consult your Tech Lead or SME.
