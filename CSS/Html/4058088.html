1. Adding payment instruments

Guide the customer through self-service for adding a payment instrument.

1. On account.microsoft.com, under the Payment & billing menu.

The customer selects Payment Options.

On a Windows 10 Phone, customers need to navigate to the Store app. Then through the menu at the top left corner choose Payment Options which prompts them to sign into their Microsoft account and provides a mobile version of account.microsoft.com/billing/payments.

[Asset 4058089]

2. Customers verify the Payment Instrument they wish to add if it is not already present.

Then select Add a payment option.

[Asset 4058090]

3. The customer needs to select the appropriate payment method type.


Then the customer needs to verify the region where the payment instrument will be used.

Note: If the customer selects the wrong region, they may be subject to geo-fencing. Not all payment methods are available in all regions.

Select Next.

[Asset 4058091]

4. The customer fills in the appropriate information that must match the information required by their financial institution for authorization.

Select Next.

  • On the Xbox One console, see the online support content for managing the payment options at: https://support.xbox.com/billing/payment-and-purchases/manage-payment-options-on-xbox-one.
  • On the Xbox 360 console, see the online support content for managing the payment options at: https://support.xbox.com/billing/payment-and-purchases/manage-payment-options-on-xbox-360.

3. Removing payment instruments2

When removing a payment instrument, there may be conditions that prevent it from being removed.

Examples:

  • There is an outstanding or pending balance on the payment instrument.
  • There is a renewing subscription on the payment instrument.

Educate the customer about the impact of removing a payment instrument on active subscriptions tied to the account. When removing the payment instrument, select Cancel renewing subscriptions at the end of cycle and do not renew [No Refund] (Do not select Cancel renew subscriptions immediately).

  • Note: PayPal: select Cancel the subscription immediately to remove it, otherwise system limitations won't allow PayPal to be removed even if Auto Renew is turned off.

3.1 Steps to resolve payment instrument removal prevention

If there is an outstanding balance

Review MST Payment Instruments and Orders section to identify the charge causing the outstanding balance.

Inform the customer and follow appropriate policies and processes regarding the product or content to resolve the outstanding balance, typically by:

  • Transferring the balance to a different valid payment method.
  • Offsetting the transaction and canceling the product order.

If there is a pending refund

Use the balance function to resolve the pending refund.

If the payment instrument is tied to a renewing subscription

Advice the customer of the subscription, then:

  • Switch the subscription to a different payment method.
  • Turn off auto renewal on the subscription.
  • If the subscription is in a delayed cancel or auto renewal is turned off, but the PI is still tied to the subscription. Check the subscription to determine if it is a type a replacement token can be issued for. If it is, cancel the subscription and provide a replacement for the time remaining

Accounts & Billing or Sales: For an Xbox subscription transfer to Xbox Support for replacement.

Xbox: For an Office subscription Accounts & Billing.

See KB 3065764 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool for additional steps.

4.3 Payment instrument troubleshooting

If a payment instrument is failing when attempting to add or update it or if purchase attempts are failing, use the following steps to troubleshoot the issue:

  1. Confirm the billing address on record matches the address used by the customer's financial institution:
  • Make sure the billing address for customer’s Microsoft account matches the billing address used by customer’s financial institution. Sample advocate messaging: "In order to charge your payment instrument, I need to make sure that billing address we have on record matches the address used by your financial institution. May I have the last four digits of the payment instrument you are using?"
  • Confirm the billing address, expiration date, and name on the payment instrument are correct.
  • A common mistake is customer’s providing their mailing address with their financial institution rather than the billing address the payment instrument is set to which may be the same address but written differently.
  • The addresses may be to the same location but have different abbreviations such as “DR” instead of Drive or “Ave” instead of “Avenue”.
  • Having the exact information is critical to the authorization process.
  • Verify the customer has provided the correct CVV code.
  • American Express: “The 4-digit security code located on the front right of your payment instrument. The code is printed on the card and is not raised like the account number. It is typically located above the account number."
  • Visa, MasterCard, or Discover: “The 3-digit security code located on the back of your payment instrument. The code is printed on the card and is not raised. It is located on or near the signature panel.” Note: If the Microsoft account address matches the billing address used by the financial institution, try the next solution.
  1. Determine if the payment instrument is newly issued by the customer’s financial institution:
    • Ask if the customer is using a newly issued payment instrument. If the payment instrument is new, confirm the customer has activated it. Sample advocate messaging: "Is this a new payment instrument?"
    • If the answer is yes, use the following messaging: "Have you activated the payment instrument with your financial institution?"
  2. Check for a Risk Alert in MST:
    • Review the Alerts section of MST, see details in KB 2896711 Procedure: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops.
    • If the rejection is for the payment instrument the customer is trying to add, view the details and engage A&B ERA/Xbox offline team for PayOps escalation, or RiskOps if the reason code instructs you to.
    • If there is no declined transaction present, the customer is being declined by their bank, or the payment instrument information entered is invalid or incorrect.
  3. Insufficient funds and Outstanding Balance:
    • Check all payment instruments in MST to see if there are any in Decline status.
    • Ask the customer if the outstanding balance on the Microsoft account is more than the available credit on the payment instrument, if applicable. Sample advocate messaging: "Do you know if the available credit balance on your payment instrument is enough to pay for the service? If you are unsure if funds are available, please contact your financial institution."
    • If the customer is sure they have sufficient funds, ask the customer if we may settle the balance for the outstanding transaction. – If yes, process the balance.
    • If the balance will not settle for that payment instrument, check if there is another payment instrument the customer will agree to transfer the balance on to.
    • If the balance transfer fails to settle or the customer does not have another payment instrument to use take the following steps:

1. Verify the type of content the transaction was for.

2. If the content is digital, offset the transaction.

  1. Advise the customer they need to repurchase the content.
  2. If the digital content is a subscription, verify if any active time on the subscription came from another source such as a PPC/Token.
  3. Cancel the subscription and provide a replacement token for any time that was active, and was not part of the transaction that was offset.
  4. Reach out to a supervisor to provide the case as an example to the Technical Advisors for issue tracking.

3. If the content is for a pre-order, the customer needs to wait for the cycle to cancel on its own.

  1. Determine if the payment instrument is added on another account.
    • Go to Advanced Search in MST.
    • Search the customer’s PI with one of the following options:
      • Partial PI details
      • Full PI details
      • PayPal email
  2. Verify the payment instrument is set up to accept online purchases.
    • Ask the customer if the payment instrument is set up to accept online purchases or automatic withdrawals. Sample advocate messaging: "Do you know if this payment instrument can be used for online purchases or automatic withdrawals? Some debit cards or prepaid payment instruments cannot be used for online transactions. If you are unsure, contact your financial institution to confirm that your payment instrument can be used for online purchases."
  3. Customer’s region
  1. Ask the customer if they are currently located in the country associated with their account. Sample advocate messaging: "Are you currently located in [read country name]?"
  2. If the customer answers no, use the following messaging "You will not be able to update your payment instrument information on Microsoft account while you are outside of the country where your account is registered”
    • Use existing processes to help the customer with switching the country if the location difference is not temporary.
  3. Some location changes can’t be changed again until a certain period of time has passed, for example a region migration for an Xbox Billing account. If the customer is actively traveling advise the customer of possible future effects, such as any lock out timers that may affect them.
  4. Contact the financial institution regarding the authorization failure.
    • There are situations in which a financial institution may prevent authorization that Microsoft cannot foresee.
    • Advise the customer to contact their financial institution regarding the authorization failure if all troubleshooting to this point has not resolved the issue.
  5. If these steps do not resolve the issue or if there are other issues, ask the customer try one of these methods (if customer is willing):
    • Try again in 48 to 72 hours.
    • Use a different credit card.
    • Note: If the customer is in a chat help the customer by directing them to account.microsoft.com and walking them through the process; However, if the situation requires support to add the payment instrument, advise the customer that adding a new payment instrument can only be done over the phone or you will need to use our Data Transfer & Management (DTM) tool to collect such data. (Refer to KB 4135034 Tools User Guide: Data Transfer & Management to learn how to use DTM). To start a phone session, direct the customer to the contact us website (http://support.microsoft.com/contactus) to be connected to a phone advocate.
    • Purchase a prepaid subscription or points card from a retailer.
  6. If the customer has performed all troubleshooting through step 8.
    • Obtain any error codes the customer receives when attempting to update or add the payment instrument.
    • Engage with A&B ERA or Xbox Offline team for PayOps escalation.

Note: If the customer is calling with a bank representative on the line, use the following sample advocate messaging: "The credit card number that you are using cannot be authorized through the billing system. The authorization process does not give me the specific reason for the authorization failure. As an online retailer, we are unable to force a credit card to work when it fails. We have ruled out the most common reasons that may cause your card to fail. Right now, the only solution is to use a different credit card or try again later in 24 to 48 hours.”

Note: Microsoft cannot force a credit card to work. If a customer's card is being declined, ask the customer to verify that their information is accurate, use a different payment option, or try again in 24 to 48 hours.

If the customer's account has been locked, see KB 930018 Procedure: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account

5. Other payment instrument information and scenarios

5.1 How to handle a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner

Customers who are the payment instrument owners can receive assisted support if they are unable to pass verification, but you must first determine if they can pass verification. If a customer cannot pass verification, it is important to understand that the assisted support options will be limited to information about transactions. If the customer wants to perform other account management activities, such as updating the account information, wants a refund, or wants account details, the customer must pass verification.

See KB 2885545 Procedure: How to handle a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner for additional details.

5.2 Determining if a payment instrument is banned

To determine if a customer’s PI has been banned, follow the below steps:

  1. Go to view all Orders History in MST.
  2. Select a wide time range, such as the past five years.
  3. Filter by Failed status.
  4. Select Search.
  5. Open the Transactions tab for each order, and if the PI is banned the Reason Code will be PGW_E_BANNED_PAYMENT_METHOD.

[Asset 4490366]

  1. If the PI is banned, advocates using the Place Order on Behalf Of tool (POBO) will see the following error: PI-BannedPaymentInstrument.

[Asset 4490367]

  1. For more information about banned PI’s, see KB 2896711 Procedure: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops.

5.3 Tier 1 - Banning payment instruments

Note: When a payment instrument is banned in MST, it is banned across all Microsoft entities.

Who can ban a payment instrument?

  • The Commerce system may automatically ban the customer's payment instrument if the system finds fraud, billing issues (including a chargeback), or non-payment.
  • If the customer requests we ban their PI to prevent the PI from being used on the customer’s Microsoft account or added to any other Microsoft accounts engage A&B ERA or Xbox Offline Team for PayOps escalation.
  • Regarding banned payment methods identified as banned by the Commerce system follow messaging on risk message

5.4 LATAM Debit Card Rejection

  1. Customers trying to use a debit card as their payment instrument in the LATAM region will receive the following error: "We cannot authorize this payment method. Make sure the information is correct or use another payment method. If you keep getting this message, you may contact your financial institution."
  2. Inform the customer that the card issuer or local operating rules may not allow us to accept specific debit cards.
  3. Recommended messaging: "Bank accounts have different rules and some debit cards seem to be accepted while others are not. We do not have an influence on it. It is not a Microsoft decision to block a debit card, but it is likely the issuer or our acquiring bank due to operating rules that will not allow it."

5.5 Customers get charged on credit cards for which they did not provide us any details or customers get charged on the wrong credit card

  • If a customer’s credit card is replaced due to expiration or loss, some banks will enable recurring purchases to continue to be charged to the new replacement card.
  • Customers should contact their banks for more details since each bank handles it differently.

6. Refund timelines

Rough timelines to process refunds from Microsoft to each of the below types payment instruments:

  • Credit/debit card: 3-5 business days​
  • PayPal: Up to 30 days based on PayPal’s terms
  • Mobile number: Next Billing Cycle​
  • Check: 4-6 weeks​
