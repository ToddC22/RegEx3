ASMS – How to Edit and Re-enable a Subscription

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to re-enable both invoice and credit card subscriptions, when the customer is unable to do so via the self-serve option in the portal.

Policy

  • Subscriptions suspended by ART/Risk/HIT/ Fraud can only be re-enabled by ART. ASMS does not have permissions to re-enable, please escalate to ART for these re-enablement’s.
  • When a subscription is disabled, the customer’s running applications are affected.
    • Azure Cloud Services will be removed.
    • VMs will be put into a stopped state.
    • SQL services will be read only.

However, when a subscription is re-enabled, the subscription will continue to be consumed at the same location. Customers will have to redeploy their cloud services as they would have been deleted and restart their VMs.

Note: Storage services are untouched when a subscription is disabled.

  • A new Subscription Anniversary (SA) date will be assigned to subscriptions that have been re-enabled. The number of days (interval) in which the subscription was suspended, will be added to the original SA date. Any Anniversary date falling on the 29th, 30th, or 31st will result in the SA date being set to the 1st of the following month. 

Example:

  • The customer's original Subscription Anniversary is the 25th.
  • The subscription was suspended on 10/3 and re-enabled on 10/9. 
  • The subscription was disabled for 6 days (interval of 6).
  • The interval is then added to the original SA, and the sum becomes the new SA date (25+6=31).

Note: In this example, since the SA date is now greater than 28, the new SA date will be the 1st of the following month.

Helping Customers Recover from a Subscription Disablement

When a customer's subscription has been re-enabled, the customer may need technical assistance. For example, the customer may need assistance re-deploying their Cloud Services (which is deleted when the subscription is disabled) or recovering their IP addresses (which is deleted unless the customer is using Reserved IPs).

In these instances, Support Engineers (SEs) should open a new case on behalf of the customer and route it to the correct Azure Technical Support team queue. ASMS is not equipped or trained to provide technical support to customers, so we should not attempt to assist the customer beyond this point.

Customer Experience and Provisioning

Customers’ subscriptions are disabled when the following have occurred:

  • Payment has not been received and they are disabled for non-payment.
    • Missing Payment for Invoice Customers: Customer states the payment has been made but is not showing for subscription or was misapplied. Refer to TSG - How to handle misapplied payments.
  • Customer has abused the Terms of Use.
  • Customer’s subscription expired at the end of term (Auto-renewal was not set).
  • Customer has exhausted their subscription monetary credits.
  • Customer accidently canceled their subscription.

The following methods of payment are how customers can settle their outstanding account balance (The method of payment is based on the account type settings and location).

  • Credit Card Customers:  To update or add a new credit card, customers will need to do so via the Azure Portal.  Public facing guidance can be found here.
  • Invoice Customers: Mail in a check – Refer the customer to the payment instructions on their invoice as checks aren’t accepted in every country.
  • Invoice Customers: Send in a wire transfer - Refer the customer to the information as noted on their invoice. See TSG 2658076 for more details.

    5. Send a task to the Web Direct team for their awareness that the credit was processed because the customer had paid the amount through an invoice. Refer them to the payment proof provided by the customer.
    6. Engage GFS to apply the amount paid through wire or check to SAP.
    7. GFS will track the payment and provide the document number to ASMS.

 

Split Payment Option

Split Payment is a one-time courtesy option provided by PayOps, when a customer is unable to settle a balance using a single transaction. There are two options for splitting a payment:

  • Split the payment using two credit cards.
  • Split the payment over 2-3 transactions using one credit card.  
    • Splitting the charges multiple times tampers with the invoice, so set the customer's expectations accordingly.
    • Do not accept requests where the customer wants to split the charges to more than three transactions (e.g. split $10,000 in 10 transactions of $1000 each) unless there is a strong business justification. Always consult with your TA in these instances, so they can add their approval to the PayOps ticket to avoid denied requests.

Write Off (WO) process for credit card customers only.

Customer is in write off AND wants to settle the outstanding balance, AND continue using Azure.

Note: Wire transfer of additional or reduced payment cannot be applied using a CC as the PI unless the customer has Invoice PI enabled. Therefore, please make sure the customer pays the exact amount of their oustanding invoice balance.

 

Note: If the customer has more than one WO charge, check the activities section in CMAT to see if the customer has paid the previous WO charge, if they have not paid the previous WO ask if they would like to pay those charges in this transaction as well. If they say no, then proceed to process the most current WO charge.

If Write off value is under $5000, follow the below steps:

  1. Enable the subscription. 
  2. Once you enable the subscription, have the customer add a new credit card.
  3. Inform the customer of the following:
  • They will not get the WriteOff amount as a Charge on their new added credit card.
  • Only for this unique occurrence, Microsoft will not claim the WriteOff amount.
  • They will be able to continue using Azure normally. However, alert the customer that this is a unique occurence and that we will keep the subscription disabled if any WriteOffs occur in the future.

If Write off value is over $5000, follow the below steps:

  1. The subscription should remain suspended
  2. Inform the customer of the exact amount of the write off balance, and that they must clearly specify where to apply the wire/check payment for the past due CC invoice ID and the amount that they intend to pay. For guidance on wire transfer accounts and payment addresses, see TSG 2658076.
  3. After the charges are moved to write off state, wait for billing cycle completion so that the charges reflect in SAP as credit and so that CashApps can help move the payment to Misc GL.
  4. Engage GFS to apply the amount paid via wire/check to in SAP accordingly (GFS will track the payment and use approval to apply it to in SAP accordingly).
  5. GFS will track the payment and apply it to in SAP accordingly, and provide the document number to ASMS.
  6. After the payment is recieved/applied you can re-enable the subscription. 

    Support should make note of the transaction within the activities section of the account in CMAT. This should include, at a minimum:

  • Document #
  • SR #
  • Value of payment
  • Date of payment received

Note: The customer's subscription has been disabled for a while now and this engagement process could take 5-7 business days. Be sure to set the proper expectations with the customer.

Note: This process does not require any payment instrument conversion. If there are ANY cases where GFS pushes back, please engage your TA. Your TA will start an e-mail thread with the web direct escalation queue/team.

Customer is in write off and does not want to continue using Azure. Subscription disabled for non-payment.

1. Inform the customer that as a one-time courtesy, the written off charges will be credited, and their subscription will remain disabled.

2. Engage the Azure Web Direct Support team via [Asset 4498193], to record the Write off value, and include the below information:

a. Account ID

b. SubGUID

c. Amount (post tax)

d. Currency

e. Invoice Number(s)

Customer’s subscription is disabled and they no longer wish to use Azure, but would like a copy of their services.

1. Enable the subscription, and inform the customer that their subscription will be temporarily enabled for 2 business days.

2. If the customer is requesting to be enabled for more days to download the data, engage your TA for approval.

3. Ask the customer to add a new CC to pay for the data transfer out charges.

4. Disable the subscription once the data has been successfully downloaded by customer.

Note: For Azure for Students – 170P cases, see TSG 4092753 for more details.

Customer submits a Sev- A request to re-enable their subscription after being disabled due to non-payment.  

In the following instances, Support may re-enable the subscription as a one-time courtesy if both of these statements are true:

1. The outstanding charge is not in DelayWriteOff or WriteOff state.

2. There are no statements in CMAT from GFS instructing us to not re-enable.

  • If Support re-enables a subscription without GFS approval because the customer is contacting us via a Sev A, support must engage GFS via a Task within 24 hours of us re-enabling the subscription (TSG 2647567).
  • Inform the customer that unless payment is made in full for all outstanding invoices, the subscriptions will only be enabled for a 30-day period; after which the subscriptions will return to a disabled state.

Note: Support can reenable the subscription if the charges are in delaywriteoff by waiving off the outstanding charges if the below points are met. The outstanding amount should be within your empowerment, and there should not be any other delaywriteoff in the past.

Invoice Subscriptions Only - Disabled by GFS.

1. Review comments in CMAT, to verify if the subscription has been disabled by GFS.

  • If disabled by GFS, read comments and follow appropriate actions.
  • If the disabled account is tagged as “Fraud” or “No re-enablement without GFS approval” – Do not re-enable. 

    • However, if there is a strong business justification as to why the subscription should be re-enabled, escalate to your TA. They will review the situation and decide whether to re-enable the subscription or keep it disabled while GFS investigates. 

    • Note: GFS has the ability to overrule a TA, and can come back post approval from a TA and tell you to cancel the subscription again.  

2. Gather customer’s information and details then engage GFS via a Task following TSG 2647567.

  • If the disabled account is, tagged as “Bad debt/test/never paid” or “Delinquency”, you can consider re-enablement only if customer can provide one of the following and GFS approves:
    • The check number of the check sent in; or Copy of Check that was sent in; or
    • Proof of Remittance (wire transfer receipt, etc.); or
    • Promised to Pay – date/type of payment (Must be within 2 weeks of SR date)
Customer caught in billing loop cannot move to Invoice PI.

The GFS team will not approve a customer for Invoice PI if they have outstanding payments so you may receive a case from a customer who wants to move to Invoice PI but can only pay by wire pay or check or bank transfer.

By the time their payment arrives and is applied to their Microsoft account, another bill has already been issued due to the timeframes involved.

In order to prevent the customer from getting stuck in a cycle of trying to clear their outstanding payments, which prevents them from moving to Invoice PI, use the following process:

  • Once you have Proof of Payment from the customer, open the following two Collab tasks to GFS:
  1. Cash Apps team – Open a task to them with proof of payment and indicating the customer intends to move to Invoice PI.
  2. Credit Risk team – At the same time, open a task to validate if customer is eligible for Invoice PI and clearing stating that a task has been sent to Case Apps team for customer outstanding payment.

Note: This scenario only applies to customer wanting to move to Invoice PI and not for customers who just do a one-time payment with check or wire and wish to stay on credit card PI.
Block non-EA offers.

Some customers may request the ability to block non-EA signups in their tenant due to security concerns.

To accommodate these customers, use the process in the following link: https://aka.ms/BlockNonEa

 

To better understand the Settle Balance feature, review the terms and defintions below: 

  • Open Balance: A customer's current unbilled charges that incurred based on the daily consumption of the services deployed.
  • Ready Balance: The amount that is currently invoiced. This will include any requested amounts that are already being declined on the PI or in Delayed Writeoff.
  • Process Balance: The act of processing a balance, in which the Open Balance amount moves to Ready Balance.
  • Settle Balance: The act of settling the current outstanding amount that shows up under Ready Balance on the customer’s desired PI.

Note: Settle Balance will settle ALL charges associated with the credit card, including O365 and any other Microsoft services the customer may have running. We currently do not have visibility into where the charges are from. So be sure to let the customer know if you see other charges that aren't in line with Azure and get permission from the customer prior to settling the balance.

Process Exceptions

If the customer is an EA customer and they have an old charge associated with their MOSP subscription (that is in the same account as their EA subscription account) and are in good standing, re-enable the customer and assist them with refunding the charges (if within your empowerment, TSG 3084129) or assist with a latent onboarding credit (TSG 2909478).

Azure Internal offer subscription re-instate process

If you receive any subscription re-enable request for Azure Internal Consumption offer, please ensure to check in AIRS registration site (https://aka.ms/airs) to verify that the Account Admin has an active AIRS registration. Only then re-instate.

If no active registration found or if you have any doubts to proceed with re-instating the subscription, please reach out to AIRS escalation "Azure Internal Offer (MSFT only)" per TSG 2927087.

How to Re-enable a Subscription

  • The subscription status will be noted as Disabled in CMAT (it will show as Canceled on the customer’s Account Portal on azure.microsoft.com)
  • After 90 days, the subscription status will be noted as Deprovisioned. this state indicates the data is permanently deleted and cannot be recovered. (It will no longer show at all on the customer’s Account Portal). At this point, subscriptions cannot be re-enabled.

Step 1: Select the subscription that you want to re-enable in CMAT.

Step 2: Select Reinstate Subscription.

Step 3: Enter comments and select arrow to proceed to next screen.

Step 4: Enter the Service Request (SR) number and select the check box to proceed to the next screen.

Step 5: Confirm that the status has been changed to Active.

Step 6: Follow the steps below to verify that the customer’s subscription is fully active:

  1. In CMAT, check that Subscriptions Status, Entitlement Status, and Fulfillment Status are all set to Active.
  2. In Jarvis, run AzureRT / Subscription Management / Get Subscription With Details to check for SubscriptionStatus:Active

Note 1: All four statuses in CMAT and Jarvis must show Active. If not, the subscription should not be considered fully active and you should engage PayOps (TSG 2651579, 3030324).

Note 2:  Keep in mind that once the subscription has been fully re-enabled, a new Subscription Anniversary date will be assigned to the subscription as outlined in the Policy section of this TSG.
