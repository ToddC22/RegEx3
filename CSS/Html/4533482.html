Technical Guide: Surface: Thermal issues

[Asset 4049349]

 

Who should use this article?

  • Surface

What is this article about?

This article will help you address thermal issues and should be used any time you work with a customer to resolve their thermal issue.

Table of contents

  1. Device is warm to the touch/customer thinks device is overheating

  2. Device is overheating and shuts down

  3. Fan is running excessively

  4. Fan has abnormal noises

Note: For any issue where the customer reports that they have been injured or scalded by a device refer to KB 2764515 Procedure: Surface & Xbox: Handling Product Safety (RRT) Contacts. 

1. Device is warm to the touch/customer thinks device is overheating

Confirming the issue

When the customer is using the Surface device, they mention words like overheating or warm to the touch. Temperature is subjective, so customers may say things like overheating when their real concern is that they believe the device is too warm.  They may also reference that the fan is running all the time.

Confirmation/paraphrase

"Just to make sure I understand your issue… While you were using your Surface, the device is overheating or warm to the touch or the fan is running excessively."

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

Surface Device>Thermal / Cooling>Thermal / Cooling>Customer perceives Surface is too hot

Resolution steps

Follow the steps in the order below until the issue is resolved or until all the steps have been completed. While following the steps, use the "Why" column to inform the customer why you are performing the step and, if successful, to better understand what the cause may have been. As you follow the steps, document the result of each one in your notes. Then proceed to the "Is the issue resolved?" section at the end of the table for next steps.

This ensures a consistent format for capturing and reviewing case information. Performing this standard procedure helps prevent instances where a step was neglected to be performed.

2. Check known issues.

From KB 4072657 Informational: Surface: Technical guides and known issues landing page, select the customer’s Surface model and check for the issue in the “Windows OS performance and maintenance” section.

There may be a current known issue that the customer is experiencing, with different troubleshooting steps.

Check known issues regularly.

3. Force the device to shut down and restart.

(Varies by new vs. old generation.)

Hold the power button for 10 seconds to force Windows to quit. Press once again start Windows. If that does not work, follow these steps by model:

Studio, Laptop, Pro 1796, Book 2, and Go: Press and hold the power button for 20 seconds.

Other devices: Press and hold the volume-up button and the power button at the same time for at least 15 seconds and then release both.

The Surface may boot to UEFI. This is normal. Have the customer exit UEFI.

Video available at Force a shut down and restart your Surface.

The device may be in a “hung” state. Force the Surface to completely turn off all power and restart.

Performing a forced shutdown is like removing the battery from a laptop or unplugging a desktop PC. It resets the hardware and clears all the cache and memory for your hardware.

  4. Check Task Manager.

From the Startup tab in Task Manager, disable unnecessary programs and then restart.

After the restart, from the Processes tab in Task Manager, view any items with abnormally high CPU usage. Select the CPU column heading to sort by it.

Check Windows stops responding on Surface for more info.

If a Windows application or service uses lots of CPU resources for an extended period, the processor will get warm. That is expected and normal, regardless of what brand of computer you're using. Also, the faster the processor, the warmer it can become. When an i7 processor is under high utilization, it will become warmer than an i5 processor, which in turn will become warmer than an i3. 

5. Run SDT or Windows Updates (WU).

For SDT: Have your customer go to http://aka.ms/surfacedt.

For WU: Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Updates > Check for updates

Note: Restart the Surface after installing updates.

Updates have fixes to common problems, or issues. Ensuring the customer has all the latest updates on their Surface will ensure they have the best possible experience.

Note: If a device is installing many updates (cumulative updates or OS upgrades) the device fan may run and be warm to the touch until all the updates are installed.

6. Check for environmental conditions.

Check with the customer to see if any of the following scenarios match:

  • Set up your device on a stable work surface that allows for adequate air circulation under and around it. 
    Use caution when operating your device on a pillow, blanket, or other soft material, because the material can block the airflow, and this may cause device overheating.
  • If your device is on your lap and it gets uncomfortably warm, remove it from your lap and place it on a stable work surface.
  • If the device is being used in a warm environment or in the sun move device to cooler environment.

The device may become very warm during normal use. The device complies with the user accessible surface temperature limits defined by the International Standard for Safety of Information Technology Equipment (IEC 60950-1).

7. Reset.

Good: Fresh Start

Go to Start > Settings > Update & security > Recovery.

Under More recovery options, select “Learn how to start fresh with a clean installation of Windows”. Follow the on-screen instructions.

Better: Reset from within Windows

Go to Start > Settings > Update & security > Recovery.

Under Reset this PC, select Get Started. Follow on screen instructions.

Best: USB recovery

The customer will need to download and set up a recovery image on a USB drive. Send the USB recovery link to the customer and help them get started. Information on what size and type of drive is provided at the destination page.

-OR-

Set up an order to have a USB recovery drive sent to the customer.

 

Fresh Start (good):

  • Fresh Start is the least intrusive form of reset.
  • Customers will not lose any files or personal information.
  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop programs.
  • Fresh Start also ensures the customer has all available updates once complete.
  • If there is an issue with Windows itself, the issue may still occur after a reset.

Reset from within Windows (better):

  • This option uses the devices Hard Drive Partition to reimage the device.
  • This option can delete personal data (Customer can choose Keep my files or Remove everything).
  • The customer will need to reinstall desktop apps.
  • The customer will need to run updates upon completion.
  • If there is an issue with the partition, the issue may still occur after the reset.

USB recovery (best):

  • This option reimages the entire device, including the partition on the hard drive.
  • It is the most complete form of reset.
  • Customers will need to back up their personal data.
  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop apps. Customers will need to run updates after completion.

If the issue occurs after a reset, then the root cause is very likely hardware related.

Is the issue resolved?

 

 

Yes, close the case: Once resolved, have the customer check for updates.

For SDT enabled sites, let the customer know about the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit.

For all other sites, check Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update for updates.

KB 4014334 Procedure: Surface: Using the Microsoft Surface Diagnostic Toolkit

Making sure the Surface and Windows are up to date may help avoid this problem in the future.

No, escalate the case: Work with a Tech Lead to make sure all necessary information is captured prior to escalation.

2. Device is overheating and shuts down

Confirming the issue

When the customer is using the Surface device the device will shut down.  On the next bootup it may display the thermometer icon.  It may take some time before the device is able to boot up.

Confirmation/paraphrase

"Just to make sure I understand your issue… While you were using your Surface, the device shut down and when you turned it back on the thermometer icon is displayed?"

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

Surface Device>Thermal / Cooling>Thermal / Cooling>Surface Overheats and shuts down

3. Fan is running excessively

Confirming the issue

When the customer is using the Surface device, they say the fan is running excessively. This could be running all the time or the customer may perceive that the time it is running is not normal. They may also report that the noise coming from the fan is too loud or noisy. Note: If the fan is making abnormal noises then see section 4 Fan has abnormal noises issue instead.

Confirmation/paraphrase

"Just to make sure I understand your issue… While you were using your Surface device the fan runs excessively or all the time or is very noisy."

4. Fan has abnormal noise

Confirming the issue

When the customer is using the Surface device, they say the fan is making abnormal noises.  These noises would be different from the normal sound of the fan. It could be described as clicking, clunky, or other abnormal sounds. 

Confirmation/paraphrase

"Just to make sure I understand your issue… While you were using your Surface the device the fan is making abnormal noises."

Surface Device>Thermal / Cooling>Thermal / Cooling>fan – Abnormal fan noise
