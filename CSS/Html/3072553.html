1. Overview

As a Tier 1 advocate do your best to assist customers by using available resources, including this article. This article provides guidance on customer assistance for Tier 1 advocates in specific scenarios.

If you cannot resolve the customer’s issue and engagement/escalation is needed, set the expectations for the customer’s accordingly based on the paths outlined below.

For Sales advocates who do not have direct engagement path to Accounts and Billing (A&B) Empowered Resolution Advocates (ERA) team, when A&B ERA team engagement is needed, transfer the customer to A&B (or guide them to contact billing support via support.microsoft.com/contactus).

For Microsoft Store team in Athens – engage with your Tier 2 (T2) (CSC team).

  • ERA (Empowered Resolution Advocates): One business day (Not available in China/Japan/Korea)
  • Advanced Capability Advocate (ACA): Two business days
  • PayOps via Tier 2: Seven to ten business days
  • CSS Command Center (CSS CC) via Tier 2: Dependent on the severity

For customer communication guidance after consulting with ERA/ACA team, see KB 4049320 Billing support templates.

Tier 1 process

Issue remains unresolved after standard process

Required information for engagement
  • If the gamertag contains offensive information, refer the customer to enforcement.xbox.com.
  • Forced name changes due to Terms of Use violations do not qualify for a refund or a free gamertag change. KB 981729 Procedure: Xbox Live: Enforcement activity (bans).

  • The customer’s email address.

The customer received a charge for their first gamertag change on their account.

  • If the customer has no history of a gamertag change since the account was created that is an indicator that the customer has not utilized the free gamertag change.

3.10  Adding/Removing payment instrument issue

Tier 1 process

Issue remains unresolved after standard process

Required information for engagement

For most scenarios regarding PI see KB 4053337 TUG: MST: Payment instrument section. Rare scenarios may need to be escalated to Tier 2 or PayOps for resolution, if the customer or advocate cannot add or remove the PI.

Adding payment instruments:

  • Customer sees error: 25055 “We cannot add this payment method because it already exists in your account”; however, there is no such PI on the account. Escalate parent service request to Tier 2.
  • Customer/advocate sees a risk rejection: “This account has exceeded the maximum number of payment instruments that can be added to an account”; however, there only a few if any active PI’s on the account. Escalate parent service request to Tier 2.
  • Customer/advocate sees error: 8017 “Incorrect personal profile information that won't allow PI validation”. If the customer confirms with the bank that their details are valid. Escalate parent service request to Tier 2.
  • Customer has a payment instrument stuck in the ‘Pending’ state in MST. This is more common with direct debit and related PI’s commonly used in Europe (SEPA). Escalate parent service request to Tier 2.

Removing payment instruments:

  • The advocate receives an error “Payment instrument has an outstanding balance that is not paid”, or “Payment instrument cannot be removed, there are subscriptions or pre-orders referencing it”. If no outstanding balance exists, and there are no pre-orders or subscriptions attached to the PI, escalate parent service request to Tier 2.
  • The advocate sees an error "The customer needs to dissociate any subscription from this payment instrument. This only applies to Mint-managed subscription”. Escalate parent service request to Tier 2.

ASD coding:

Customer need: Manage Payment Options.

Customer question: (Most applicable to reason provided by the customer).

Symptom/cause: (Most applicable to reason provided by the customer).

Root cause: (Most applicable to reason provided by the customer).

If issues, such as the ones listed below remain, engage A&B ERA team. This may involve PayOps escalation.

Payment instrument issues

  • Troubleshooting must be completed.
  • Mismatched PI (order and invoice do not match).
  • Customer has contacted their financial institution in cases of authorization failure.

For Japan/Korea/China: Engage ACA.

3.12 Prepaid card/token issues

Tier 1 process

Issue remains unresolved after standard process

Required information for engagement

Review the following articles, if issue remains unsolved, follow guidance in next column.

KB 818928 Procedure: Xbox: Code redemption issues (prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription)

KB 2531998 Procedure: Xbox: A retail bundle is missing an item (Xbox bundles only)

KB 3044887 Procedure: Currency stored value (CSV).

Name Your Game Bundle tokens: Engage Tier 2 if tokens from this program are missing from the box or shows up as: inactive, invalid, expired, or if a customer has damaged their token. Customer is required to provide proof of purchase (front and back) and token card images (for all scenarios except missing tokens) before engagement.

After checking the token status in MST token tool and Prepaid Card Lookup Tool, ensure one of the following scenarios is true, based on the token status:

Active

Troubleshooting per KB 818928 Procedure: Xbox: Code redemption issues (prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription) failed to resolve.

The region of the token matches the customer’s account.

Damaged

The customer lives in one of the following regions:

  • Australia
  • Brazil
  • Hong Kong SAR
  • India
  • Korea
  • New Zealand
  • Singapore
  • Taiwan
  • Japan
  • People’s Republic of China

In other regions:

  • A physical prepaid card came in a boxed product published by Microsoft.


Redeemed/consumed

  • Token has been redeemed on the customer’s account, but not applied (license/subscription/csv).
  • If a customer redeemed a token from another region to their account, do not engage.

Customers are only able to redeem out-of-region tokens on their account if they have changed their region settings on the website or on the Xbox One console.

Refunds or replacement are not offered for intentional redemption of an out-of-region token.

Invalid

  • Token contains only valid characters.
  • Images of the proof of purchase, front, and back of the card have been provided prior to engagement.

Created/inactive

  • 24 hours has passed, and the customer still cannot redeem.
  • The retailer has refused refund/replacement.
  • Images of the proof of purchase, front, and back of the card have been provided prior to engagement.

Deactivated/blacklisted

  • Do not provide any concessions to the customer. Inform the customer we are unable to assist them further with their token, and refer them to the retailer where they purchased the token.

    Do not engage.

    Note: Tokens redeemed to another account are not a valid engagement.

3.14 Refund not received - Non-LATAM regions

Tier 1 process

Issue remains unresolved after standard process

Required information for engagement

Issues in scope for this scenario include:

  • Issues when a refund was processed but not received.

Issues not in scope for this scenario:

  • Refund eligibility disputes.
  • Refund requests.

Before seven days of refund:

If it has not been seven business days since the refund was processed, ask the customer for their patience, and advise them that it can take some time for some financial intuitions to post refunds. Advise that the customer will need to reach out to their financial intuition for additional information.

After seven days of refund:

If it has been over seven days since the refund was processed, you will need to attempt to process the refund again.

  • Using MST, view all orders to locate the transaction.
  • Using the events tab, confirm that the transaction is not in a chargeback state.
  • If the transaction is in a chargeback state, the customer will need to reach out to her or his financial institution for information regarding the refund.
  • If not, attempt to process the refund. If it does not go through an error message will be displayed immediately.
  • Attempt to process the refund up to three times.
  • Advise that the customer can remove any non-pertinent information to protect their privacy prior to sending that information.

Set proper expectations with the customer and escalate your parent SR to Tier 2 as a Refund Issue.

Review the following articles, and if issue remains unsolved, use guidance in next column:

KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical products or software returns and exchange

KB 840536 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Digital content return/refund

Tier 1 process

Issue remains unresolved after standard process

Required information for engagement

For Xbox Refund Request Solution available countries, see the following KB for available countries and process:

KB 4485105 Procedure: Xbox: Refund Webform Process

For Xbox other languages or non-Xbox:

  1. Determine if the customer has received a refund within 365 days. If not, proceed to Step 2.
  2. Identify the refund amount:
    • Refund amount ≦ 500 USD (less than or equal to 500 USD or local currency equivalent) per account, proceed to step 3.
    • Refund amount > 500 USD (more than 500 USD or local currency equivalent) per account
      • For non-Xbox, engage with Empowered Resolution Advocates (ERA) team (for Japan/Korea/China, engage ACA).

  6. If the refund currency is not in USD, use Bing search (bing.com) to validate the local currency equivalent value (for example, “500 USD to euro”).
  7. Help the customer understand how to limit or control purchases in their scenario. This may require moving the child’s account into a proper family structure (Microsoft Family), utilizing payment controls, or removing a credit card from an account.

3.21 Modern subscription status/Order lock issue

Tier 1 process

Issue remains unresolved after standard process

Required information for engagement

Issue:

This occurs on modern active subscriptions with recurring billing on, but the expiry date is in the past. The subscription cannot be cancelled via MST if it has this error message.

  • OrderLockedException
    • Exception Message: Order cannot be modified until manually fixed.
    • Resolution: The customer issue must be escalated, as manual intervention with a finance teams is required.
  • PreviousRequestInProgressException
    • Exception Message: Order cannot be modified until previous processing finishes.
    • Resolution: Wait 30 hours and retry the action that produced the error. During those 30 hours the system will be automatically cleaning up whatever is generating the error. Customers should be shown how they can self-service their request whenever possible and informed of the time they can try again.

ASD coding:

Customer need: Manage My Subscriptions.

Customer question: I want to change/renew my subscription.

Symptom/cause: Option not listed.

Brief description of why the customer called: Order Locked.

Engage A&B ERA for PayOps escalation.

  • The Microsoft account is having issues.

3.22 Unable to redeem RUB CSV – Russia Only

Tier 1 process

Issue remains unresolved after standard process

Required information for engagement

Issue: Customers may contact support when encountering an error with redeeming Russian Ruble (RUB) CSV tokens via https://redeem.Microsoft.com. The error will appear as follows:

“Unable to redeem, please call customer service”

This error is due to a shift in Microsoft business practices from using RUB to USD in the Microsoft Store for Russia.

Collect 5x5 RUB CSV code and customer’s shipping address via DTM for investigation and engage Advance Capability Advocates (ACA).
