Summary


Who should follow this procedure?
  • Data Protection Escalation Team (DPET – Global EN) and Frontline Support Engineers who handle Microsoft 365 Data Protection workflows (non-Global EN) and have been provisioned with the proper security permissions. All cases should be directed accordingly by using KB 2500396: Office 365 redirect resource sheet.
What is this procedure about?
  • When a Company Admin (GA/CA) tries to verify a domain in Microsoft 365, and receives one of the following error messages:
    <Domain_Name> has already been verified for your account, or for another Microsoft Online Services account.

    Can't verify the domain
    <Domain Name> was already added to a different Microsoft 365 account XXXX.onmicrosoft.com.
    Sign in to that account as an admin, and remove domain <Domain Name>. Then come back here and try adding <Domain Name> to this account again {select this text and the curly braces to enter content}
When should this procedure be followed?
  • When a Company Admin (GA/CA) tries to verify a domain in Microsoft 365, and it exists in another Microsoft 365 tenant.
  • DO NOT follow this procedure when a customer is looking to verify the domain into a GoDaddy account. Redirect the customer (requestor) to Advance Technical Support (ATS) with GoDaddy. Do not offer to create a trial account to process a domain removal.

Assumptions


This article describes scenarios where a Company Admin (GA/CA) receives an error when trying to verify a domain to their Microsoft 365 account. This could mean that the GA/CA may or may not have awareness of the domain associated to another tenant.
  • Unmanaged tenant: This kind of tenant is created when customers sign up for a variety of Microsoft 365 offers using a business email address. These new tenants carry no Admin account, no Technical Contact. For more information, see 2995721: How to troubleshoot Commercial Self Service Offers in Office 365, 3068485: Job aid for Commercial Self Service for Office 365 and How to manage a domain already in use by people in your organization who signed up with their work or school email address.

Only a full entitled Admin (GA/CA) can request a domain removal. Entitle the caller via 3023334: DPET How to perform entitlement in Office 365.


Only an Admin (GA/CA) has the ability to remove a domain from within the Admin Portal.

Note: Removing a domain association alters mail flow, can affect tenant access, and may result in data loss. Requests need to be a carefully managed event.

Procedure


Ask the Admin (GA/CA) whether they are aware of the Microsoft 365 account that holds the vanity domain and if they have access to it.
  1. If the Admin (GA/CA) is the Administrator on the M365 tenant where the domain is located, they must perform the following steps for domain removal:
    1. Sign in to the Microsoft 365 portal (https://portal.office.com) as a Global Admin.
    2. In the left navigation pane, click domains.
    3. Select the domain that you want to remove, click Remove, and then follow the instructions on the page.
  2. Research both the target tenant and the holding tenant in Rave.
    1. Is the holding tenant unmanaged? Refer to:
      • 2995721: How to troubleshoot Commercial Self Service Offers in Office 365
      • 3068485: Job aid for Commercial Self Service for Office 365
      • How to manage a domain already in use by people in your organization who signed up with their work or school email address
    2. Is the holding tenant a Student Advantage, Power BI, or Microsoft 365 Basic tenant or other IW Signup tenant scenario?
      • If yes, please advise the customer that the resolution is self-service and follow the steps in 3068485: Job aid for Commercial Self Service for Office 365.
      • If no, go to step 3B.
    3. Are there similarities that point to the holding tenant being related to the target tenant, such as Company Name, Company Address, Admin Names, Technical Contact, Phone numbers, and so on?
      • If there are similarities, document them. Continue by informing the requesting GA that we may have identified an “intra-company connection” between the tenants.
      • Request permission from the requesting Company Admin (GA/CA) to make an intra-company connection by sharing their contact information with the holding tenant Administrators. If permission is granted, call or/and email the holding tenant Admins and advise them that someone from their organization is requesting domain removal. If you can’t reach the GAs on the phone, or no reply after 24 hours, go to step 4.
  3. If the Admin (GA/CA) doesn't know or doesn't have control over the Microsoft 365 tenant where the domain is located, follow these steps:
    1. Review the target tenant:
      • The requestor must be a GA/CA on the target tenant.
      • The GA/CA needs to fully entitle the account.
    2. Confirm that the requesting Admin (GA/CA) has control over the vanity domain:
      • Go to the domain tab in the Rave and select the domain.
      • Status should be: Unverified.
      • Click on Check DNS, look for “verification records” to get the TXT-MS=XXXXX code.
      • Check against public records to confirm that the code propagated correctly.
      • If the code is not visible through public records, provide the code to the customer and ask for it to be entered as a DNS TXT.
      • Document the Microsoft code in the case notes.
      • If the domain is not found in the target tenant, this means that the customer has not followed the instructions to verify the domain; ask the customer to do so using the Microsoft code. 
        Note: Do not disclose any information about the holding tenant to the requestor.
    3. Verify if there is a root-child domain relationship in Rave: if there is a child domain, inform the customer that we need to first remove the child domain before removing the root domain. Before proceeding, customer must provide written approval to remove both root and child domains.
    4. Verify that the domain is not federated. If the domain is federated: Inform the customer that we will need to remove the federation and we will need written authorization. Use Email template 1: Reverse Federation | GA Confirmation.
    5. If there are no similarities between the tenants, the domain is not federated and no child domains exist or the child domains are approved for removal, proceed to step 4.
  4. Explain to the caller that we need written confirmation from listed admin on the target account in order to proceed with the domain removal: Using the ticketing system, send an email to the GA making the request (both UPN and alt email) by using the Email template 2: Domain Removal | GA Confirmation. 
    • If no response is received in 24 business hours, contact the GA (requestor) and explain that Microsoft is not able to proceed without the written confirmation.
    • If a positive response is received in less than 24 business hours, proceed to step 5.
    • If a positive response is received more than 24 business hours later, consult the case with your TA.
  5. Update the case with the following template:
    The following information needs to be added in the case notes:
    • Customer must be fully entitled on the target tenant, and the entitlement must be completed over the phone.
    • Customer needs to be fully aware of risk of data lost and service interruption.
