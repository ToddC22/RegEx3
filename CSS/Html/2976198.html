ASMS – Local Language Cases in the Global English Queue

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.  

Overview 

The global English queue periodically receives local language cases both within and outside the business hours for local language teams. This TSG tells you how to deal with the cases, and contains initial response (IR) templates in local languages to send to these customers. 

Policy 

Note: Japanese cases should be sent to the Japanese queue as they work 24 hours a day, seven days a week, and Global English does not work those cases. 

Local language cases will be sent to one of the global English queues for two reasons: 

  • SLA guidelines require a response outside of local language business hours. 

  • The case is a Severity A (Sev A) or Sev B with enhanced support entitlement (e.g., Microsoft Unified Support, Premier, or ProDirect) that came in after normal business hours, and needs to be worked on now. 

  • The case is a Sev A case with standard entitlement that came in after normal business hours and needs to be worked on now. 

  • The ASMS Central Europe or the ASMS Ibero-Americas local language team has requested backup assistance. 

  • A team is temporarily experiencing higher volume and needs to have some cases worked by the Global English team. 

  • An engineer may be out of the office or a local holiday may impact coverage. 

  • In this case, an agreement will be made between the local language manager and the TAs that may include specific case-handling information and instructions. Your TA will have more information about when and how to transfer cases back to the local language team. 

  • If a customer selects English as the support language while creating the case but submits the customer verbatim in a language besides English, send your IR in the language that the verbatim is in.

Support Scenarios  

Case Handling 

If you are assigned a local language case in the Global English queue, reference the below notes and then follow the numbered steps to assist the customer. 

Notes 

  • If the case requires special handling due to support entitlement, send it to the ASMS Premier queue. For more information on properly identifying and routing these cases, see TSG 2786284.
  • PPP engineers may be assigned LL cases by the Duty Managers, in which the customer may not be Premier, Pro-Direct or a Partner. In thoses instances, continue to work the case by following the guidance in this TSG. Do not push back on the Duty Managers for case re-assignment.
  • If the Global English team is providing backup for one of the local language teams, your TA may have instructions on case handling that take precedence over the normal process. 
  • Guidance for Delivery Partners who receive local language cases can be found in TSG 2965727.

 

  1. Send the local language IR in the user’s preferred language (as selected in [Asset 4497459]). If the case verbatim is in English and the resolution to the issue is immediately apparent, perform an FQR.

  2. If the customer is unresponsive, adjust the severity of the case as needed. Before routing the case back to the local language team, ensure that you have waited long enough for a customer response (guidelines below).

  • If the case is a Sev A Premier case: 

    • Wait one hour and follow up with the customer 

    • Follow the Premier process for adjusting severity (2786284)

    • Hand off the case as necessary to the next shift 

    • If the customer has still not responded (and the Technical Account Manager (TAM) is unavailable to approve a severity change) by the time the local language team comes back on shift, transfer the case back to the local language team and notify anton@microsoft.com or feliro@microsoft.com. 

  • If the case is a Sev B Premier case: 

    • Wait two hours and follow up with the customer 

    • Follow the Premier process for adjusting severity (2786284)

    • Hand off the case, as necessary, to the next shift 

    • If the customer has still not responded (and the TAM is unavailable to approve a severity change) by the time the local language team comes back on shift, transfer the case back to the local language team and notify anton@microsoft.com or feliro@microsoft.com.

  • If the case is a Sev A Non-premier case: 

    • Follow up with the customer after one hour.

    • Hand off the case as necessary to the next shift.

    • If the customer has not responded within two hours, adjust the case severity to Sev B.

    • Transfer the case to the local language queue and inform the customer that a local language team member will be following up with them to provide support. 

  1. Once the customer makes their choice to either work with you in English or in their local language using the translation services (TSG 2658542), follow up and work the case as normal. 

  1. If the customer prefers to work with the local language team during queue business hours, do the following:

    1. Downgrade the case to Sev B.

    2. Record the severity downgrade in the case notes.

    3. Put the case back in the proper local language queue. 

  • Send the customer written confirmation that they have chosen to wait for the local language team, and that you will be transferring the case to the proper team for further action. 

  • Inform the customer that while they are waiting, English support is still available if they change their mind, or if the issue becomes more serious. 

Note: Do not transfer cases to the local language queue without performing the IR.
