2.5 Tips for transferring to in-app support

Outlook.com technical support phone and chat advocates should instruct customers who are able to sign to Outlook.com to use in-app support for technical support. Please treat the following as sample guidelines:

  • “I’m sorry to hear that you’re experiencing a problem with your Outlook.com email. Microsoft provides email support via Outlook.com and we have a team of support advocates that are dedicated to supporting Outlook.com. For free technical support, login to Outlook.com from any browser, select the Help icon on the top banner of the page, enter your question, and follow the prompts to request support."
  • “I’m sorry to hear that you’re experiencing a problem with your Outlook.com email. Microsoft now provides email support via Outlook.com and we have a team of agents that are dedicated to supporting Outlook.com. For free technical support, go to https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/p/?linkid=853938 to open the Help window. Enter your question and follow the prompts to request support."

If the customer insists that they want to use assisted support, free-of-charge technical support is only available for the following customers:

  • Non-subscribers may incur charges for support. If a customer does not wish to pay, you should again direct the customer to in-app support, or they can ask their question in the Outlook.com community forum (answers.microsoft.com).

In-app support via direct weblink

  • Click https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/p/?linkid=853938 to open a new browser window (with sign-in if required) and start in-app support.

Note: Mobile and desktop client users also need to go online to access in-app support. Customers must use a browser on a PC or Mac to see the most modern version of the Outlook.com web UX, which will offer the Help pane and Contact Support experience.
