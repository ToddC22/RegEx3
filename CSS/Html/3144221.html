1. Overview

Defines when and how to escalate to RiskOps team (the fraud team) for verification and removal of hold on account or subscription.

2. Description of information

If an escalation is received and the customer reports they are unable to perform an action in MAC due to one of the following errors:
Sorry, we can't process your request.
The verification checks on your payment method have failed.
Please select another payment method and try again.
If the problem doesn't go away, please submit a Service Request.
Close Support Information
Correlation ID: scu#128462f3-6dcf-4e06-8954-14164fb79b7a
Error code: 0
These errors may be an indication of one of the following scenarios:
  1. Risk has disabled a subscription.
  2. Risk has rejected an attempt to make a purchase.
  3. Risk has rejected an attempt to add or change a payment method.
Do not mention the Risk Team or “fraud” to the customer. If you need to escalate to the Risk team, simply explain that further investigation is required and you have escalated the issue for review.

Step 1: When an escalation is received or a call is received after-hours to re-enable subscriptions, first check CMAT to verify that the event or subscription was rejected or disabled by the Risk team. If the subscription was disabled by the Risk team, do not reactivate the subscription. Instead, escalate to RiskOps for review and verification. RiskOps will decide whether to reactivate the subscription as appropriate.
Scenario 1: Customer subscription is disabled
Check the “Subscriptions” page in CMAT. If the Subscription status is “Disabled” and the Reason Code is “Fraud”, do not re-enable the subscription. Instead, escalate directly to the RiskOps team per Step 2 below.

[Asset 4582444]

Scenario 2: Customer is unable to complete a purchase or to add a payment method.
Check the “Declines” page in CMAT and confirm that the Risk Decision states “Rejected” and that the Reason Code does not state “Do Not Escalate”. If it says “Do Not Escalate”, then follow the notes in the Reason Code. Otherwise, if it only says “Follow normal risk escalation procedures”, then proceed to Step 2.

[Asset 4293671]
 
NOTE: If you are unable to confirm the issue is the result of a Risk block, then transfer the case to RiskOps in Rave support area.

Step 2: Transfer the case to RiskOps

Complete the RiskOps Escalation Template below, then copy/paste it into the Rave case internal notes section. Important: Include best way to contact customer (email, phone or SMS).

Step 3: Inform the customer (caller/submitter) that further investigation is required before we can reactivate the subscription. Do not mention the Risk Team or “fraud” to the customer. If the case will be held by commerce then state that further investigation is required, and you have escalated the issue for review. If the case is being transferred to RiskOps to handle and close, then simply state that an escalation resource will contact them once the investigation is complete. Do not reactivate a customer who is blocked by Risk. RiskOps must review the request and will reactivate if appropriate.
Note: If Commerce retains ownership of the case or receives a secondary case from the customer, please provide any updated details to the Risk Case as soon as possible. Not doing so will delay resolution and can cause customer to be upset if asked for information twice.

Step 4: After reviewing the escalation, RiskOps will communicate the resolution to the customer directly and close the case in Rave. In case RiskOps cannot support the language the customer requires, RiskOps will add a note with resolution steps and information that should be shared with the customer in the Rave ticket notes and transfer the ticket to a support area that can support the language.

Note: If Incident Managers(IMs) want to get in touch with RiskOps support, send an email to hitleads@microsoft.com, email tile should include “Premier Customer Request for Rave Case ###”.
