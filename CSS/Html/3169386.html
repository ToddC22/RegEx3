1. Overview

  • The fraud status, formerly known as dot fraud, indicates an account has been closed for fraudulent behavior as defined by Microsoft Advertising policies. Fraud closures happen at the customer level and affects all ads under the customer shell.
  • Internal users can see the fraud status, and closure reason in a variety of tools. Advertisers cannot see this status. Their experience is suddenly being locked out of their Microsoft Advertising account, and having all ads stop delivering.
  • New account creation should never be proactively suggested to get the advertiser back on Microsoft Advertising.
  • Fraud issue codes should always be used to close tickets for fraud accounts if the account is flagged, even if the fraud customer is contacting Support for a different reason.
  • To collect appeal information for a chat or email interaction, create a Data Transfer Management workspace for the ticket (see KB 4056786 Microsoft Advertising: Data Transfer Management) and request the customer complete and upload the Account Review Form. These details can be collected real-time via phone.

2. How to tell if an account is impacted by fraud, and locate the reason for the closure

As of November 1st, 2020, customer accounts impacted by account closure will be notified via email. Internal users can determine and confirm an advertiser has been impacted by fraud, and find the reason for the closure, using several different tools.

Note: Start by verifying the advertiser in Ad Inquiry, because this is the only tool that shows the fraud pending status. Once you have confirmed the status in Ad Inquiry, use either the Microsoft Advertising UI or the UCM Agent Workspace to find the fraud closure reason.

  • Ad Inquiry - The fraud status and closure reason can be determined on the Campaign Items tab. Look up the account using the XID. If the advertiser is impacted by fraud, it will show in red under the fraud status section.

    Ad Inquiry is the only tool that shows the fraud pending status. The other two show the account as active. If the account is impacted by fraud, not fraud pending proceed with finding the closure reason in one of the two tools below.
  • Microsoft Advertising UI - The fraud status and closure reason can be determined from the advertiser search results on the main page. You can look up the customer by name, or account number. If the advertiser is closed for fraud, the status will say fraud, and there will be a reason in the fraud reason column.
  • UCM Agent Workspace - The fraud status and closure reason can be determined on the Alerts tab. Search by the advertiser name or customer ID (CID) in the main search page. If the advertiser is impacted by fraud, the status will show in red next to their customer name, and the closure reason will be displayed in the description column.

Once you locate the fraud closure reason, proceed with the steps below that map to the closure reason. Code the ticket based on the individual fraud closure reason and outcome.

If you see that ads are not delivering but are eligible in Microsoft Advertising UI, and you receive the following error message in the inline preview tool, escalate to Ad Platform & Tools. Refer to the Escalation Guide for sub-team details.

3. Fraud pending status

  • Do not use the words “Fraud” or “Fraud pending” when speaking with the customer.
  • The fraud pending status is different than the regular fraud status. Advertisers impacted by the fraud pending status are new customers that have not yet entered an online payment method and have no ads delivering until our series of rigorous security checks are concluded.
  • Ensure the advertiser has added their preferred payment method and created at least one campaign (with an ad and keywords) to be eligible for delivery (for prepay accounts, ensure funds have been added).
  • If the advertiser has added a payment method/funds and is still under fraud pending status, inform the advertiser that a specialist will review their account and get back to them within 24 hours.
  • Messaging: “It appears your account may be in a pending state on the backend. I just need to gather a few details from you to help get your ads live.”
  • Gather details from the customer to complete the Issue Specific Details for Issue Category = Fraud Escalations, Issue Type = Fraud Pending.
  • Submit the issue specific details to Fraud for review.
  • Advise the advertiser they are on a temporary hold and you’ll get back in touch with them on the status of their account as soon as possible, likely same day. FraudAds SLAs can be found in the Escalation Guide.

4. Fraud closure reason: Bad Debt

4.1 Resolution steps

Use this guidance if the advertiser’s fraud closure reason is Bad Debt as indicated in the UI or the UCM Agent Workspace.

  1. Determine whether the advertiser is prepay or postpay. Prepay advertisers can have this hold overturned if approved by the fraud team, and if they submit a payment. Postpay advertisers cannot. If the advertiser is postpay, do not proceed with step 2. Instead refer to the messaging below.
  2. If the advertiser is prepay, inform them that their account has been placed on hold due to non-payment. Set expectations that you need investigate further and will reply within two business days or less with next steps. Do not use the term fraud or bad debt.

    Messaging: “It appears that there may be an outstanding debt associated with your account. I need to investigate the details to see if this hold can be removed. I will follow up with next steps in two business days or less.”
  3. Escalate to the fraud team to see if the hold can be removed.
    • If the hold can be removed:
      • Prepay customers only:
        • Let them know that once the payment is received, it can take three to four business days to resume delivery on the account. Thank the advertiser for contacting us and proceed with the ticket handling process.
        • Let the customer know that they need to pay the amount owed to have the hold removed from their account. Provide the amount owed and the payment instructions below.
    • If the hold cannot be removed:
      • Provide the permanent closure messaging to the advertiser and close the ticket.

Based on our review, your account [insert account number XID] has been closed. For privacy and security reasons, we cannot provide details around your specific account closure, but the most common reasons that require us to close Microsoft Advertising accounts include:

  • The payment method on file was not authorized by the account owner.

  • The account is related to an account that was previously closed.

Please know that we have taken this matter seriously and have thoroughly investigated the issue. This decision is final, and we consider the issue closed.

Microsoft Advertising Support Team”

“Your account has been closed for non-payment. This is a permanent decision and cannot be overturned.”

If the advertiser asks about creating a new Microsoft Advertising account:

"There are no system limitations preventing you from creating a new Microsoft Advertising account; however please be aware that if the same behavior is exhibited, this could result in the same outcome."

Important: Do not assist the advertiser with setting up a new Microsoft Advertising account. Accounts closed for fraud: bad debt carry a much higher risk than those inactivated for declined payments.

Prepay advertiser says they sent the check/wire transfer, but their account is still on hold:

In some cases, the Microsoft Advertising CashApp team is unable to determine for which account the payment was intended. Assist the advertiser by submitting a status request to the CashApp team.

  1. Collect the following information from the advertiser
    • Microsoft Advertising Customer Name
    • Microsoft Advertising Account Number
    • Check Number (if available)
    • Address to which the check was sent
    • Date of the check
    • Date payment was cashed (if applicable)
    • Documentation. Provide some sort of proof of payment.
  2. Escalate to Global Financial Services (GFS), also known as Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS) > WOCS Accounts Receivable (UCMT) to ensure that the payment is applied to the correct account and the hold is removed. Refer to the Escalation Guide for sub-team details.

7.3 Compromised site failed appeal: Destructive software messaging

“Dear <advertiser name>,

The following is regarding hxxp://www.xxx.com, which is part of your Microsoft Advertising account. Please DO NOT visit this website as it has live malware, which will be executed simply by visiting it. Please remediate the issue following the template below.

Troubleshooting tips for remediating malware infections

There are three primary ways your website might be serving malware. Listed below is a description of each one, along with helpful tips on addressing the problem.

Malware might be directly hosted on your webpages. Malware might also be embedded in images, binaries, documents, and the HTML page itself.
How to fix: Inspecting the files located on the web server is a good start. An antivirus scan might reveal malware embedded in binary, document, or image files. Malware embedded in an HTML page might hide itself from antivirus products within obfuscated JavaScript. Such code is identifiable as it often contains long sequences of characters with a custom de-obfuscation routine. Deletion of any bad JavaScript or corrupted files disinfects your website. If you find such changes to the original webpages saved on your web server, it might indicate that your web server security is vulnerable. Change your passwords, update the server's operating system and your web server applications with the latest security updates, and if applicable, scan your website development computer for malware.

Malware might be pulled into your website by links to untrustworthy third-party content, such as images, user generated content, advertisements, counters, blog templates, web widgets/gadgets, and RSS feeds. If any linked third-party content is malicious, your webpage is also considered malicious, because a user browsing to your webpage from the Bing results webpage might be harmed.
How to fix: Inspect the third-party content of your website and, if necessary, remove it. Only embed content from trusted third parties into your webpages.

Malware might be injected into your webpage as it travels from your web server to the end user. In this case, a direct inspection of the original webpage source code files on your web server would likely reveal no malware infection. However, by revealing and examining the source code within your web browser for the flagged webpages after either browsing directly to your website or by clicking the Cached page link for your webpage on a Bing search results webpage and comparing the results to the original file from the web server, you might detect changes to the webpage code. If you see new or modified webpage controls, modified links, or scripting changes between the original source and the browser source versions of the webpage, your website might be a target of a malicious man-in-the-middle attack.
How to fix: Shared hosting providers have been known to be vulnerable to such attacks. If the source code comparison test shows suspicious differences that appear like the malicious modifications mentioned above, inform your web hosting provider that they might be the victims of a man-in-the-middle attack. If no action is taken by your provider as a result, consider moving your website to a more trusted provider.

Once the problem is resolved, please contact Support and request a re-evaluation of your account.

Sincerely,

<Signature>”

8. Fraud closure reason: Phishing

Use this guidance if the advertiser’s fraud closure reason is Phishing as indicated in the UI or the UCM Agent Workspace.

Note:

Never use the term phishing when talking to an advertiser.

Return to top

8.1 Resolution steps

  1. Determine the customer create date using Ad Inquiry.
    • If the customer was created less than 30 days ago, this closure is not appealable. Deliver the permanent closure messaging below and close the ticket.
    • If the customer was created more than 30 days ago, take down all issue-specific details. Use the initial messaging below to end the call/chat/email.
  2. Submit an appeal to the Traffic Quality team for further review. Traffic quality will investigate the phishing content, and then send the account to fraud to review for account compromise. The SLA for this investigation is seven business days for the TQ investigation plus two days for the fraud investigation (nine total business days). See the Escalation Guide for additional details.  
    Fraud will review the account to confirm whether an unauthorized third-party committed the phishing offense within a legitimate advertiser’s account. In the compromised scenario, the account will be eligible for reinstatement.
  3. Once confirmation on the account compromise is received from fraud, follow the steps below:
    • If the account was compromised – Work with fraud to clean up the account by following the steps in KB 3169740 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Compromised Account/Unauthorized Access.
    • If the account was not compromised – Provide the permanent closure messaging below.

12. Reporting an advertiser for fraudulent behavior

12.1 Report an advertiser safety issue (If you suspect fraudulent activity, follow the steps below):

  1. Collect the following information and transfer the ticket to an L2/SME.
    • Suspected advertiser’s customer name, CID, and XID.
    • Suspected advertiser’s landing page.
  2. L2s/SMEs: Escalate to FraudAds.
  3. If reported by a user, thank the user and let them know that our team of investigators will take a look and take action if the advertiser is in violation of Microsoft Advertising policies and close the ticket.
  4. The FraudAds team will follow up with action taken. There is no need to report this to the user who reported the ad.

Return to top

12.2 Report a user safety issue (for example, malicious ad or phishing ad)

  1. Gather any information available from the user (for example, keyword query used, display URL, and ad text).
  2. If you can render the ad from a search result, do not click the ad.
  3. Hover over the ad, right-click and copy the r-link.
  4. Escalate all the above information to Network Quality Services (NQS). See the Escalation Guide for sub-team details. Use subject line: P0 Safety Issue.

13. Statement inquiries from .Fraud account holders

We are not required to provide invoices to advertisers closed for fraud unless the customer is located in a VAT or GST country (for more information see KB 3176436 Informational: Microsoft Advertising: Taxes). We are required to provide these invoices to customers located in markets that collect VAT or GST. Customers are not eligible for invoices that have been credited as part of the closure process.

Messaging for customer not located in a VAT or GST country:

"Microsoft Advertising will no longer be able to provide documents for accounts that are closed. Record keeping is the responsibility of the account holder. Any documents needed for tax purposes should be maintained by the account owner or obtained from the account owner's banking statements."

Important notes regarding .Fraud refunds and refund eligibility:

  • Treatment of remaining prepay balances at time of account closure vary by payment method, for example:

14.2 Declined online credit memos

Updated fraud process for declined (online) credit memos:

  1. Always advise customers with a failed online credit memo or customers claiming online credits were not refunded as expected, to first contact their financial institution.
  2. Await customer response before proceeding with any further investigation.
  3. If a customer states that they have validated with their financial institution that funds were not received, but the UI shows it as successful, send a request for investigation to Global Support with UST/PayOps:

Request Global Support investigate where the funds reside (with Microsoft or within the customer’s bank) and request that they provide a customer-facing tracking number for reference, so that the customer can follow-up with their bank:

  • Tracking number provided. Yes - Provide tracking to customer and advise them to contact their bank to investigate.
  • Tracking number provided. No - Ask customer for bank statement or communication confirming funds were not received.
  1. Await customer response in step 3 before proceeding to next step.
  2. If the customer comes back with documentation from their bank confirming that funds have not been received (written confirmation, bank statement), let them know you will provide this evidence for consideration.
  3. Submit a request to SANOPS for an offline refund due to funds not received. Include bank provided documentation via DTM and the reason why an exception to the process should be considered.
  4. Once SANOPS confirms approval to proceed, let the customer know to expect a link to MPC.
  5. Make sure that offline refunds are never be promised in advance of AP approval. This is especially important for .Fraud customers given the complexity.
  6. Once SAFE Approval is complete and you've received notice that the refund has been approved, follow standard SLA guidance to set appropriate customer expectations around SLA.
