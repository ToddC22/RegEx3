Background Information

Microsoft is now offering new subscriptions to purchase a domain. Previously an Office 365 or Dynamics 365 customer could buy a domain through a link to GoDaddy.com from the Domains page of the Microsoft 365 admin center. Now, some customers will see these new offers while in the Domain page in Microsoft 365 admin center or during the Office 365 Sign Up wizard.

Note: Starting in March 2017, Power BI subscribers in the United States can purchase a domain from Microsoft during the initial sign-up procedure. This experience will be rolled out more broadly to users in the United States – first to users signing-up for Power BI and then more broadly to users signing-up for Office 365.

The subscription term for these domain subscriptions is one year.

Payment for domain subscriptions is a one-time payment. The customer can pay by invoice or by credit card.  

The price varies, depending on the type of domain purchased. Domains sold by Microsoft are:

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How does an advocate find the domain subscription in CMAT?

    Domain Subscription will be on the Subscriptions page and the subscription details page has a field called Context. This field shows the purchased domain with “GoDaddy” next to it.

  • How does the domain subscription appear in the Microsoft 365 admin center?

    The subscription can be found on the Subscriptions page (under BILLING). It is called Domain Subscription: domainname.com. The Microsoft logo will appear next to the domain purchased from Microsoft to distinguish it from domains purchased elsewhere.

     
  • Why do two domain subscriptions sometimes appear?
    • If the customer chooses private, the information about the owner of the domain is not available to the public. This option creates another free subscription called Domain Privacy Subscription. There is no charge associated with the Domain Privacy Subscription.
    • If the customer chooses public, the information about the owner of the domain will be made publically available per Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN) regulations.

Important things to know

  • Customers cannot purchase the domain for more than one year at a time. To ensure their domain is renewed, customers should check that Auto-renew is turned ON in the Microsoft 365 admin center.
  • The seat count for these subscriptions is one and cannot be changed.
  • Cancellation of domain subscriptions is not allowed. If a customer wants to cancel their domain subscription, their only option is to turn off Auto-renew in the Microsoft 365 admin center. This will expire the subscription on the renewal date. Customers do not have the option to cancel their domain subscription in the Microsoft 365 admin center.

2. Guidance

 

Refunds or credits for domains purchased from Microsoft are not allowed. This includes Service Level Agreement (SLA) credits. Even though the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT) does not restrict the Refund Transaction option for domain subscriptions, do not apply a credit or refund for these subscriptions.

Exception: If the customer’s purchased domain is not shown on the domain list, refund can be approved for customer to disable the current domain and to repurchase it.

How to cancel a domain subscription purchased from Microsoft.
Customers cannot cancel a domain subscription. If the customers ask about canceling, advise them to turn off Auto-renew for the subscription in the Microsoft 365 admin center. There is no refund or credit for domain subscriptions.

Note: If the customer contacts us within 5 days from purchase, there is a possibility to cancel and refund. The canceled domain will be available for purchase again in 5-10 days.
A customer wants to re-enable a domain subscription that was disabled

You should not disable or enable the subscription in CMAT. If an expired domain registration can be recovered, the admin portal will provide the customer with a link or button in domain details page > Overview tab, which will launch a wizard to purchase a new subscription.

A customer wants a Service Level Agreement credit for degraded performance or an outage related to their domain subscription.

See Guidance section.

A customer is having technical difficulty with their domain subscription or has questions about setup, domains, DNS, or their domain status is not Active.

Transfer customer to Technical Support. Refer to 2500396: Office 365 redirect resource sheet.
