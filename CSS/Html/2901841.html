1.  Overview

1.1 What is Microsoft Complete? 

Microsoft Complete is a service plan that provides extended hardware service and accidental damage coverage for users, plus unlimited technical support. Additional public-facing content related to Microsoft Service Offerings can be found here: https://www.microsoft.com/store/b/microsoft-complete. 

Important: Microsoft Complete plans should only be referred to as a Protection Plan or Service Contract. Do not refer to these offers as a “Warranty” or “Extended Warranty”.

2.2.2 Available post device purchase through an advocate

Generally, customers can add Microsoft Complete if within 45 days of purchasing their device with the following exceptions:

  • China: We do not have a way at the call center to directly charge the customer. The only way the customer can add the plan is at the time of device purchase through Microsoft Store online.

Europe: Due to regulatory reasons, customers can only add the plan if their device was purchased from a Microsoft Store, and within 45 days of purchase. Devices purchased from a third-party retailer are not eligible even if within 45 days of purchase. Select the Microsoft Complete SKU that matches the customer’s billing address. For a compliant sale, the billing address (country) must match the Microsoft Complete SKU (country) in cart.

Return to top

2.2.3 Available post device purchase through device’s app store

Customers can add Microsoft Complete to their device directly through the device’s Microsoft Store app on their Surface or Xbox. To do this, the customer must:

  • Initiate the purchase from the device they want to attach the plan.
  • Device must be new and powered on within the last 45 days or shipped within the last 90 days.
  • Both Surface and Xbox must be on RS4 minimum with latest updates for the app store installed.

There are however some exceptions as noted on the table in section 1.2.

  • China: We do not have a way at the call center to directly charge the customer. As such, the only way the customer can add the plan is at the time of device purchase through Microsoft Store online.
  • Europe: Due to regulatory reasons, customers can only add the plan if their device was purchased from a Microsoft Store and within 45 days of purchase. Devices purchased from a third-party retailer are not eligible even if within 45 days of purchase.
  • Microsoft Complete for Elite controller is not available for post device purchase through Xbox or Windows Store app.

2.3.1 Transferability or portability of the plan

Device exchanged by the Service Center during the coverage period

Microsoft Complete follows the device, just as the warranty does. As the device must be registered to place a repair order, the warranty coverage is transferred to the replacement device when the serial number is updated at the Service Center. There are no additional actions required of the customer for this to occur. If the warranty coverage did not transfer properly, follow section 3.5.

Device sold to someone else during the coverage period

If the customer purchased Microsoft Complete for their device then sells that device to another person, the new owner receives the remaining coverage of the plan. There are no additional actions required of the customer for this to occur, other than they must accept the Terms & Conditions when the device is re-registered under their Microsoft account.

Customer owns multiple devices

If the customer owns multiple devices, the plan is not transferable between those devices. The coverage only applies to the device to where the plan was purchased.

2.3.2 Canceling the plan

The customer may cancel Microsoft Complete and receive a refund, if they are within their plan period and have not claimed against the plan, their entitlement will be automatically revoked by the system. See section 3.3 if you need to process a cancellation request.

  • Within 30 days: Full refund.
  • Beyond 30 days: Prorated refund.

3.1.2 Australia requirements

  • Giving advice: When customers are deciding to purchase the plan, do not provide advice or opinion. You need to make sure you only provide the facts of what the plan covers. Additional guidance is provided in the FAQ section on how to address customer questions that seek advice or opinion.
  • Age requirement: Customers must be 18 years old or older to purchase Microsoft Complete. If you are unsure how old your customer is, ask them before finalizing the purchase.
  • Request for Product Disclosure Statement (PDS):
    • Prior to sale of plan: Customers can request to receive the PDS prior to finalizing the purchase to aid their decision. If the customer requests the PDS is provided, send the link (https://support.microsoft.com/en-au/help/4089871) in email.
    • After sale of plan: Within 24 hours, the customer will receive two emails – a purchase confirmation email that lists the amount paid and what they purchased as well as a ‘welcome’ email that contains a link to the PDS. If the customer wants the PDS now, you can send the link (https://support.microsoft.com/en-au/help/4089871) in email.
      • Customer specifically requests a printed copy (reactive, do not volunteer): Let the customer know that automated emails will be delivered within 24 hours, but you will also submit a request for a printed copy to be sent. Do not promise any timelines as it will be mailed from the United States, so it may take time. Consult your Tech Lead/SME then on approval, engage ACA.
  • Other rights: Consumers have the right to repair, replace, or refund in certain situations. See KB 4100081 Guideline: Microsoft and the Australian and New Zealand Consumer Law. Those rights are in addition to any limited warranty provided by Microsoft and the protection offered by Microsoft Complete. You should never say or suggest to a consumer that they would be entitled to under the Australian Consumer Law.

3.1.4 New Zealand – Microsoft Complete End of Life

  • As of December 31st, 2019 Microsoft Complete is no longer sold in New Zealand. We will continue to support plans sold prior to December 31st, 2019 per standard process.
  • This includes both consumer and commercial Microsoft Complete plans for all products.
  • If a customer in New Zealand expresses interest in purchasing Microsoft Complete, respond with the following:
    “Thank you for your interest in purchasing Microsoft Complete. This product is no longer available for sale in this market. We apologize for any inconvenience this may cause you.”
  • Do not speculate on why the product was discontinued in the market.

3.1.5 New Zealand – FAQ for Complete End of Life

Q: Is there any alternative? (Do we have plans to replace MS Complete with a different Product)

A: We are currently exploring alternatives but currently no commitment or roadmap.

Q: If my device needs repair, how much will it cost to repair? Where can I have my device fixed?

A: Customers who are already covered under Complete will continue to be able to process a claim. For non-Complete customers, refer and offer customers a transfer to technical support for the relevant product for further information on the repair process and costs. 

Q: How does this change impact the consumer law in New Zealand?

A: There is no impact to the OEM warranty, only Complete. Nothing changes with the OEM warranty.

Solution/Response

The device went through a repair and was exchanged during the plan coverage period. What happens to the plan?

Microsoft Complete follows the device, just as the remainder of the warranty does. When the Service Center updates the repair records, warranty entitlements including Microsoft Complete coverage are automatically transferred to the replacement device serial number. Customers are not expected to take any additional actions for this to occur.

Follow section 3.5 if there is a warranty dispute due to entitlements not transferring.

The device was replaced with Microsoft Store inventory within the returns period. What happens to the plan?

Unless Microsoft Store accepted the device back for a full refund and the customer repurchased a like device with the plan, the plan will not transfer automatically.

In this situation, the customer will need to provide proof of purchase for Microsoft Complete, as well as evidence that the device was exchanged within the return period.

Devices advocate: Consult a Tech Lead/SME, who will be able to add Microsoft Complete to the new device.

Non-Devices advocate: Transfer the customer to a Devices advocate.

The device with the plan attached was sold to someone else. What happens to the plan?

If the customer has purchased Microsoft Complete for their device and then sells that device to another person, the new owner will receive the remaining protection.

What happens if the device with the plan attached is taken to a region or country where the plan is not officially offered or available?

If the customer purchased Microsoft Complete for their device, and then took the device to a region where Microsoft Complete is not offered or provided, they may not be subject to any of the terms and benefits the plan provides.

The customer may be provided with extended warranty coverage based on the information in MST but is not guaranteed to have all the benefits and options the plan provides (such as accidental damage protection). Set expectations appropriately if the customer is considering pre-purchase of the plan or has already purchased and alluded to taking the device to a region where Microsoft Complete is not offered.

Messaging:

"If you choose to take your device to a region where Microsoft Complete is not offered, please be aware that the benefits and extended warranty may not be assured. The plans Terms & Conditions are intended to apply to the country where it was originally purchased.”

4.4 Purchasing Microsoft Complete from the device’s app store

Scenario/Question

Solution/Response

Can I purchase Microsoft Complete for my Xbox through the Microsoft Store Windows app?

No, you must purchase Microsoft Complete online, through a physical store, or support advocate. For example, a customer must buy Microsoft Complete from the Xbox Store on the Xbox they want to attach Microsoft Complete.

Microsoft Complete for Elite controller is only available for purchase by calling a customer support advocate at the time of device sale. This offer is not currently available for purchase directly from the Xbox Store app.

Can I purchase Microsoft Complete for my Surface though the Microsoft Store Windows app on another Surface or device?

No, you must purchase Microsoft Complete from the device itself.

If the customer recently purchased a Surface device and wants Microsoft Complete for that device, they must purchase through the Microsoft Store Windows app on the device itself. They cannot use a different PC or purchase through their mobile phone.

How does the customer select what device they are purchasing the plan for?

By purchasing Microsoft Complete from the device, itself, the plan attaches to the device that made the transaction.

Microsoft Complete is associated to a device serial number, and the device serial number is automatically recorded during the purchase process.

Can I purchase Microsoft Complete for Elite controller via the Xbox Store app?

As of right now, customers cannot purchase and attach Microsoft Complete for Elite controller on the Xbox Store app.
