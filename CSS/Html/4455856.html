Procedure: Professional Support and Broad Commercial Refund

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Manager

What is this article about?

Customers are eligible for a refund on incident charges when the incident is within policy. This procedure provides guidelines for incident handling when customers request a whole or partial refund of a Professional Support or Broad Commercial case. This includes pay-per-incident (PPI).

Exceptions

Broad Commercial non-Azure Cloud Online Services (O365)

All Reactive Incidents for Broad Commercial customers where the product is a non-Azure (O365) Online Services product, shall be non-decremented as the customer receives unlimited problem resolution support as part of their subscription fee.

Windows 10 upgrade and installation

Windows 10 upgrade and installation issues on a single device (non-deployment scenarios) may qualify for a refund. See the procedure 4522825 Procedure: Windows 10 installation Support.

  • For MBS (APAC), Japan and Latam please also see the deviations / workarounds section below
  • Any request for refund that is not in policy must be approved by the Team Manager (TM) and documented in the incident. Few non-policy scenarios will be approved for non-decrement. If you have spent more than the 60 minutes troubleshooting an issue, the customer should be charged. Please refer to tables below for more information on scenarios eligible for a refund.

SEs handling Professional-level Customers

Support Engineer

  • Never volunteer to provide a refund unless the incident meets the in-policy criteria. Explain the policy in detail.
  • When a customer requests to not be charged and the reason is not "in policy":
    • Ask the customer to send a written request via email.
    • Post the email from the customer in the Service Request (SR).
    • Engage recovery process with the Team Manager. If TM agrees, and the case meets the criteria above for refund, the case can be closed as Non-Decrement.
  • What to do if the customer reports they have not received a refund?
    • Make sure the case was closed as Non-Decrement.
    • Allow 10 business days for the refund to go through. If the customer has still not received their refund, submit an OAS Refund Request to trigger a refund of the customer.

Team Manager

  • TM will engage customer and determine if a refund should be granted and document the SR with appropriate action for SE.

 

What to do in Rave

  • When a customer’s request to not be charged is approved and the reason is not “in policy”:
    • Uncheck Decrement box from the Summary Tab in the Rave case
    • Enter approval’s name (manager) into Rave internal notes section for this action

What to do in Service Desk Case

  • When a customer's request to not be charged is approved and the reason is not "in policy":
    • Set the Grace Case flag.
    • The Grace Case flag is only enabled when “Issue Type = Technical Issue”.
    • The Grace Case flag is only available to users who have elevated permissions.

How does refund work in Service Desk Case/Rave

Typically, Service Requests are charged on open and refunded on non-decrement close. There are exceptions to this behavior:

  • NOAM: If the incident is closed as non-decrement, customer is refunded.
  • Japan: Single PPI incidents created by phone agents on behalf of customers are charged via invoice on incident close as decrement and are paid by bank or wire transfer. This includes all After Hours (24x7) Sev A incidents and a portion of Business Hours Sev B or Sev C incidents.
  • Taiwan: Single PPI incidents can also be created by phone agents on behalf of customers and paid via a local card using a Third party CC processor. Customer is provided a contract and access ID when they purchase. If the incident is closed as non-decrement, the contract and access ID can be used again.
  • China and Hong Kong: Third Party credit card web-based processing tool that charges on open and provides a contract and access ID. If the incident is non-decrement, the contract and access ID can be used again. This is almost the same as Taiwan except Taiwan is order by phone and China and Hong Kong is order by web.
  • LATAM: If the incident is closed as non-decrement, customer is refunded.
  • Korea: Customer initiates transaction on a Third party local website which processes credit card or wire transfer before incident is opened. If incident is closed as non-decrement, the customer is refunded.
    • C​redit card refund: the engineer informs the agent (who is able to make a refund) by email and asks to process a refund.
    • Wire transfer refund: the agent engages local finance team.​

2. Deviations or workarounds

MBS (APAC)

To ensure that customers and partners have an avenue for case review should they feel they have been incorrectly charged. This Guideline is designed to provide guidance on the process required for when customers or partners request the chargeability of their case/s be reviewed.

  • On receipt of the incident dispute e-mail, request a review of the email from the appropriate team manager
  • If the incident is to be made chargeable, inform the partner along with the reason and a copy marked to the Asia Pacific licensing team (mbslques)
  • If the incident is to be made non-chargeable there are 2 options:
    • Option 1 If the incident has been closed for less than 30 days, the support team manager is requested to have the charges reversed and the partner is informed of the same. The Licensing team (mbslques) are to be informed should there be an invoice pending.
    • Option 2 If the incident has been closed for more than 30 days, then request the Asia Pacific licensing team (mbslques) to reverse the charges. The partner would be subsequently informed of the decision by the licensing team.
