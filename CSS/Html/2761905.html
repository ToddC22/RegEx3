Summary

Who should follow this process?  

  • Commercial Post-Sales Agents / Technical Routers (TR) 

What is this process about?

  • This article includes redirect information, in a table format, that is used for all Office 365 customers who are calling about products other than Office 365

When should this process be followed?

  • The IVR currently only routes Office 365 customers to TR agents when the customer chooses Exchange Online as the product for which they need support in the IVR.  If a customer reaches a TR agent and needs support for a different Office 365 product, agents should use this information to correctly to redirect the customer.

If the caller wants to be the one to be elevated, a case must be created and then transferred to the O365 Data Protection Team following standard processes for that team; otherwise the person who will be escalated to Global Admin must first call for assistance with that issue.  After a new Global Admin is elevated, billing assistance from the Commerce Team can proceed.

  2. COMMON MISROUTES
    The following issues are frequent issues that are misrouted to the Commerce Team. Please make sure that the customer’s issue does not require a redirect to another team as this will result in additional frustration.

    • Non-MOP Dynamics 365 Billing Requests
      Commerce agents are only able to assist customers who purchase Dynamics 365 thru the Microsoft Online Portal (MOP), and these customer’s subscriptions will appear in ViewPoint.
      If the customer’s Dynamics 365 subscription does not appear in ViewPoint, it means that they purchased it thru Dynamics CRM rather than thru MOP.
      Do not create a Service Request and cold transfer the customer to the following numbers:

      • US: 877-276-2464 and select option 2 from the menu

      • CA: 866-792-3244 and select option 2 from the menu

    • Cancellation Requests
      If the customer wants to cancel a commercial subscription, do not create a Service Request; instead, follow the redirect instructions in the O365 Cancellation Requests section of the Redirect table below

    • Trial Extensions
      If the customer wants to extend a trial extension, do not create a Service Request; instead, follow the redirect instructions in the O365 Pre-Sales section of the Redirect table below

    • Consumer Billing Inquiries
      If the customer is a Consumer subscribers (O365 Home Premium and O365 University), do not create a Service Request; instead, follow the redirect instructions in the Consumer Billing information in the Redirect table below.

      • The Commerce team only assists Commercial subscribers with billing issues, not Consumer subscribers.

      • For help verifying the customer's subscription type, see Determine whether the customer's O365 subscription is Commercial or Consumer (primarily needed when the subscription does not appear in ViewPoint)

    • Concierge Continuing Case

      • If the case number begins with "30216-XXXXXX", provide the customer with the direct phone number 800-636-7914 and then perform a COLD TRANSFER to the OneCall Transfer code 2014795 – Concierge NA Transfer

    • Partner Billing 
      Listen for cue words that indicate the caller is a Partner (e.g. they say that they are working on behalf of a client or customer), and if they occur explicitly ask the customer if they are a Microsoft Partner.  Alternately, some Partners may self-identify themselves during the call.

      • Syndicated and Cloud Solution Provider Partners calling on behalf of their tenant customer's billing issues are routed per the Partner Billing section below.

      • Any other type of partner with tenant customer billing issues (including Signature Cloud Support Partners) are routed to the Commerce team.  Use the tenant subscription if the calling Partner is a billing or global admin on the subscription.  Otherwise use the Partner's subscription (assuming that the caller is an admin on it).

      • If a Partner is calling about their own subscription(s) rather than a Tenant, then unless the calling Partner is a tenant of a different partner, then they are an end user customer of Microsoft and their billing inquiries should be routed to the Commerce Team (e.g. billing issues for the Partner's tenants might be a Partner Billing issue, but billing issues for the Partner itself would not be).

Determine whether the customer's O365 subscription is Commercial or Consumer

For some issues, it is essential that you determine whether the customer has a consumer or commercial level Office 365 subscription, however the customer may be unable to tell you the specifics on the subscription type. The steps below should be used to determine what type of subscription is involved.

  1. Commercial subscriptions can be identified using the following information:
    1. The customer’s subscription will be seen in ViewPoint, with details about the subscription type
      1. Trial or purchased
      2. SKU type (e.g. E-SKU, P-SKU, A-SKU)
    2. The customer’s Office 365 login will be in the form of <name>@<org>.onmicrosoft.com
    3. When the customer logs into with their account they will be in the Microsoft Online Portal (MOP), and the URL that the customer is accessing for their account should start with https://portal.microsoftonline.com/

TIP:  for additional info, see Where to sign in to Office 365

  1. ​​​​​Commercial subscription customer Admins will either: 
    1. Have an Admin tile in the Office Apps (see About the Office 365 admin center)
    2. or, for Small Business customers, have an Admin option in their toolbar (see Video: Introducing Office 365 Small Business)

TIP:  for more information on determining if a customer has a commercial subscription, see What Office 365 business product or license do I have?

  1. Consumer subscriptions can be identified using the following information:
    1. The customer’s subscription will not be seen in ViewPoint
    2. The customer login will be a Microsoft ID using a typical email address, rather than having the onmicrosoft.com login ID format
    3. When the customer logs into their account, they will be at an Office page rather than the Microsoft Online Portal (MOP).  The URL that the customer is at when logged in will be in the format of https://office.microsoft.com/en-us/MyAccount.aspx

How to Handle Small Business Premium Product Key Requests

Small Business Premium customers obtain Office 365 by purchasing it as a 1 year subscription through either Microsoft Store or a retail store, unlike the other subscriptions which are either purchased through our Office 365 Presales Department or through a Microsoft Partner.

Use the following information to assist customers with Small Business Premium Product Key Requests:

  1. Always collect the basic contact information:
    1. First and Last Name
    2. Phone Number and Alternate Phone Number (if applicable)
    3. Problem Description
  2. Confirm whether or not the customer has a Microsoft ID that they use to sign into the Office 365 Admin Portal.
    1. If they do have a valid Microsoft ID and can be verified in ViewPoint, create a case as normal and route appropriately. (Go to Step 3 for special exemptions)
    2. If they do not have a valid Microsoft ID and cannot be verified in ViewPoint, gather the basic contact information listed in Step 1 and their current email address. Create a Service Request (SR) and send the customer to the Commerce and Billing Department.
  3. If the customer is having any of the following issues, create a case and send the customer to the following department:
    1. Product Key is not working/Activation Issues are routed to Commerce and Billing
    2. Setting up user(s)/administrator(s) issues are routed to Commerce and Billing
    3. Setting up a new Office 365 Account issues are routed to Office 365 Presales.
