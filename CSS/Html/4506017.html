1. What's new

October 2020

Implementation approach:
The MPA will be implemented for indirect resellers in a batch approach starting October 26, 2020, beginning with resellers with the lowest risk to revenue impact and expanding to additional resellers and geos depending on Microsoft exit criteria for each batch being met.

Upon implementation, indirect resellers who have not accepted the MPA will be unable to create new customers or place new orders for existing customers in the Cloud Solution Provider program.

Communication approach:
Microsoft will be contacting indirect providers and indirect resellers directly over the coming weeks, reminding them of the implementation dates above and actions they need to take. Additionally, Microsoft will also contact indirect providers and indirect resellers ahead of their individual deadlines.

2. Overview

Microsoft Partner Agreement provides partners a simple, unified, digitally accepted agreement framework, and also helps Microsoft, partners, and customers support data privacy and security, promote compliance and encourage trusted business practices.

The Cloud Solution Provider program will be the first partner channel to leverage Microsoft Partner Agreement.

Effective September 1, 2019, the Microsoft Partner Agreement (the new version of the Cloud Solution Provider program partner agreement), will be available to all regions on Partner Center.

2.1. Partners and agreements in the scope of September 1, 2019 availability
  • For September 1, 2019 availability, partners transacting in the CSP program are in the scope. These partners include: CSP indirect providers, direct bill partners, and CSP indirect resellers.

  • These partners are currently contracted via following agreements. These will be replaced by the new version of the CSP partner agreement.
    • Microsoft Cloud Reseller agreement (MCRA) for CSP Direct Bill partners
    • Microsoft Cloud Distributor agreement (MCDA) for CSP Indirect Providers
    • Multi-tier terms
    • Indirect reseller terms
    • US Government Cloud terms

Please be noted that as part of this new enhanced process, all CSP indirect resellers will need to accept the Microsoft Partner Agreement on Partner Center.

2.2. Timeline for Microsoft Partner Agreement

  • July 1, 2019: The Microsoft Partner Agreement is available for partner preview on Partner Center.
  • August 31, 2019: All versions of the Microsoft Cloud Reseller Agreement and Microsoft Cloud Distributor Agreement expire on August 31, 2019.
  • September 1, 2019: The Microsoft Partner Agreement is available for signature on Partner Center starting September 1, 2019.
  • November 4, 2019: The Microsoft Partner Agreement Status API for Indirect Provider partners is generally available. Agreement Status API public documentation can be found here.
  • January 31, 2020: All partners in the CSP program are required to accept the Microsoft Partner Agreement to transact in the CSP program.
  • July 1, 2020: MPA is enforced. Read more in section 5 of this article.

6. FAQ

Refer to the FAQ for partner questions and answers.

Revoking/updating an MPA signature (aka Rescinding the MPA agreement acceptance) – is that possible?

No. If the agreement was signed by a representative of the company that was authorized to do so at the time or, as the acceptance language provides, represents to Microsoft that they are so authorized, then the MPA is valid.

CELA has proposed this language as a response to these requests:

Please note that on [ date of acceptance_] a representative of your organization accepted the Microsoft Partner Agreement on behalf of your organization, representing that they were authorized to do so. Microsoft considers this to be legally valid and enforceable acceptance of the Microsoft Partner Agreement on behalf of your organization, which superseded all prior agreements between you and Microsoft regarding the subject matter addressed in the Microsoft Partner Agreement, including your participation in the Cloud Solution Provider Program.

The change in name of a company has no effect as to the validity of the MPA.

The MPA Preview states that a Gold Cloud competency is needed to sell all online services. Are we changing the requirements for Direct CSPs and Indirect providers?

No, confirm to partners that there is no need to have a Gold Cloud Competency to be in the CSP program. The current MPA agreement posted in Partner Center is a preview so changes and corrections might be incorporated prior to publishing the final version.

Is there any difference between the MPA that a Direct CSP and an Indirect Provider have to sign? Or is the MPA the same for all partner types?

There is a difference between the Direct and Indirect Provider agreements, and the difference is the language within the contract and can't be determined by the title.

In the Online Services and Software Channel Authorization portion of the agreement, there will exist either

  • a reference to being able to resell to Customers – present only for Direct Bill MPAs
  • a reference to being able to resell to Indirect Resellers present only for Indirect Provider MPAs
  • a specific section called “Indirect Reseller Authorization” – present only for Indirect Resellers MPAs
If partner had a multi-tier amendment, do they need to sign anything extra? Or will the MPA suffice to cover their multi-tier status?

If a partner is marked as a MultiTier for Microsoft Partner Agreement in Prose, the MPA that they accepted is for Multi-tier partners. The title of the document does not reflect what type they agreement is, however, there is language within the document that covers Multi-tier partners. If the partner has any specific questions regarding the terms or language of the agreement, they should contact their Microsoft account manager or field sales.

What’s the process for negotiated MPAs?

The Partner’s PDM needs to engage Partner Business Desk for a negotiated MPA and the approved amendment will have to be signed by the partner via DocSign.

What’s the escalation path for REST/Rider Country partners, in case partners complain that they did not receive the hardcopy MPA yet?

Advocates need to escalate to Operations (aka GOC) via child SR to CBO_CSP.

Partner is willing to sign the MPA, but they have concern about anti-corruption requirement defined at – “3. Business Conduct Training.” They would like to know if it is equal to the one in the Microsoft Partner Network Agreement for MPN, and who should take this requirement, what is the frequency, can they take this assessment from Partner University, etc.

The training requirements highlighted in the MPA is more inclusive, and more comprehensive than those provided in MPN. We encourage the partner to review their obligations under the MPA to confirm their compliance. The MPA provides that training obligations apply to “Company employees in a position to influence the pricing, terms, or conditions under which Microsoft’s Products are distributed, resold, used, or marketed (but excluding employees engaged solely in distribution of Microsoft’s Products to end consumers).” Training must be provided on a “regular” basis – which would likely be whatever is commercially reasonable – but the Partner must interpret this portion of the agreement themselves. 

Partner is claiming a CPE credit of 10% of an invoice they’ve received with a >10-day delay as per the MPA.

This is not correct. Please note that the agreement does provide this reference with regards to credits:

“If Microsoft determines that Company has overpaid, Microsoft will give Company a credit. Any payment disputes will be treated separately from Company’s obligation to pay invoices and other amounts when due, and Company may not withhold or Offset any amounts due before the dispute is resolved and any related credits are issued (and then only in the amount of such credit).”

How do I know if I have accepted the Microsoft Partner Agreement?

There are several ways to confirm if you have accepted the Microsoft Partner Agreement:

  1. When logged in to Partner Center, navigate to either the “Partner profile” or “Reseller profile” pages within Account settings. “Program info” will list your current agreement, when it was accepted and by whom.
  2. Upon landing on the CSP Overview page in Partner Center, you will be prompted to accept the Microsoft Partner Agreement if you have not done so already. Alternatively, you will notice a green banner at the top of the page confirming it has been accepted and no further action is required.
  3. Upon landing on the Partner Center Home page, if your organization has not accepted the Microsoft Partner Agreement yet an action will appear in your “Pending actions”.
