1. What is the Advise Me Educate program?

The Advise Me Educate program is your chance to share great ideas and experiences with our customers.

The Advise Me program:

  • Teaches customers about the features and benefits of Microsoft products and services so they get the most out of using them.
  • Shows customers all the cool, useful, and relevant things they can do with Microsoft products and services. You become the customer’s trusted advisor!
  • Creates and keeps Microsoft fans because you show customers how to love our products and services.
  • Aligns to Microsoft’s mission “to empower every person and every organization on the planet to Advise Me.”

From experience, we know that Advise Me conversations boost customer satisfaction because you’re teaching customers new things relevant to them and they'll thank you for the extra insights you give them.

2. Why are Advise Me conversations important?

Customers often aren’t aware of all the features and benefits of the Microsoft products and services they’re using. You have a unique opportunity to show them the value of their Microsoft products and services. Using your great listening skills, you can have relevant Advise Me conversations with customers to help them do what’s important to them.

We’re asking you to have Advise Me conversations because often you are the only contact a customer will have directly with Microsoft, and it is a great opportunity for you to help create a Microsoft fan.

  • Your responsibility goes beyond just fixing the customer’s original reason for contact.
  • Use listening skills to help them and teach them. You can make their lives easier and they’ll appreciate the tips you give them.

Show customers how cool, useful, easy, and exciting our products and services are, and build fans.

3. When to have an Advise Me conversation?

When to have an Advise Me conversation
Once you have resolved or are in the process of resolving the customer’s issue, you can have an Advise Me conversation.

Here are some examples when you can have an Advise Me conversation:

  • Right after verifying a customer’s account, for example, if they called about their Office 365 subscription, you could talk to them about OneDrive, which is one of the benefits of their Office 365 subscription.
  • During quiet time while you are waiting for a program or file to download or install.
  • During issue resolution - an Advise Me conversation may be relevant to solving the customer’s issue, for example, using Edge or OneDrive may be the solution to an issue or the answer to a question.
  • After you’ve resolved the issue, near the end of conversation, but before closing the session.

Use your listening skills and have the conversation when it’s natural and most relevant for the customer. Remember, you are helping them. Listen for times when you can seamlessly transition from what you’re working on to talking about the campaign.

When Not to have an Advise Me conversation
Your first responsibility is always to support the customer to resolve their issue. Do not start an Advise Me conversation until you’ve identified the customer’s issue and the customer feels confident that you are helping them resolve their issue.

It will not always be appropriate to have an Advise Me conversation. For example, do not have an Advise Me conversation if:

  • The customer is noticeably unhappy; they’ll probably not respond well.
  • The customer seems rushed; it may not be appropriate if they’re in a hurry.
  • If you’re redirecting a customer to another Line of Business

4. How to have an Advise Me conversation

Your first responsibility is always to support the customer to resolve their issue. Use your listening skills and have an Advise Me conversation when it’s most relevant for the customer.

Engagement framework

The engagement framework for Advise Me provides you with a conversation process flow and should help you talk to the customer:

Conversation process:

[Asset 4133246]

Get to know your customer to understand how the customer is using the technology and services and to find out their level of technical understanding (for example, tech minimalist or tech enthusiast), so that you can cater your approach to their needs.

[Asset 4133249]

Actively listen to establish a relationship and identify opportunities to improve the customer’s Microsoft experience. Identify opportunities and relevant support scenarios to make the Advise Me pitch relevant to the customer.

Listen for times when you can seamlessly transition from what you’re working on to talking about the Advise Me campaign. You are helping the customer. It might be during quiet time, when you’re downloading files, near the end of the case, or in response to something the customer says or asks.

[Asset 4133248]

Use a transition statement to smoothly open the Advise Me conversation and relate back to the customer’s needs and interests, for example:

  • “While we’re waiting…”
  • “By the way…”
  • “Before you go…”
  • “Did you know…”

Discuss how the customer can do more with their Microsoft products and services by using the Advise Me value statement (the pitch) and features/benefits to help answer customer questions.

We have very specific campaigns we want you to talk about.

  • Chat advocates can copy/paste the pitch directly from the campaign content; always share the URL with the customer for them to learn more.
  • Phone advocates can use the pitch and features & benefits as guidance to have a natural conversation.
  • All advocates can use the features & benefits if the customer asks questions or wants more information.

[Asset 4133250]

Assess whether the customer is interested (for instance, positive, neutral, or negative response) and answer any customer questions/objections.

Advise the customer you will send email content in the case email:

  • Copy the email content for the specific campaign you talked about, into your case closure email. You can find your specific campaign by going to the Advise Me hub page. See below for an example of where to put this content (highlighted in grey in this Windows email):

    [Asset 4101846]

Use the tools correctly:

  • Universal campaigns should be coded in ASD, using the instructions in section 5.

Important: For full details of scenarios, talking points, email content, and Q&A, see the information on the Advise Me hub page.

5. Coding Advise Me campaigns in ASD 

  1. If you have an Advise Me conversation, you need to code it in ASD. Start by first coding the main customer issue under Reason for Contact.
  2. Then code the Advise Me conversation – it’s the last question in this section of the ASD form.
    Notes:
  • Office generalist advocates will have a slightly different view of ASD than below as there is more than one incident form.
  • This field will be mandatory for some advocates. This is to help ensure more accurate tracking of Advise Me conversations.

  1. Under Advise Me (Achieve More) Conversation?, select Yes. On selecting Yes, two new drop-down menus will appear. Note: This field defaults to No, so Yes must be selected to see the drop-down menus.

    [Asset 4098698]
  1. In the first drop-down menu, select the Advise Me Product family (for example: Windows).
  2. In the second drop down menu, select the Advise Me Campaign you discussed with your customer (for example: Edge, OneDrive)

  • If the customer is interested, and you select Yes, you must copy the campaign content into your case email to send to the customer. See section 4, for an example of where to put this content.

6. How to code an Advise Me conversation in ASD for Advocates using Rave

If you have an Advise Me conversation with your customer, you need to code the Advise Me conversation in ASD. (You can’t code it in Rave at this time.) You can do this at any time, (for example, during the case if it doesn’t interrupt helping the customer or after you close the case in Rave).

  • Copy the Rave case ID in Rave.
  • Open a new case in ASD.
  • Code the Advise Me details in ASD.
    • Under Ghost / Transfer / Redirect always choose Advise Me Conversation to see the Advise Me fields.
  • Paste the Rave Case ID in the Rave Case Number text box.
  • Under SR Status always choose Closed: Resolved
  • Select the New button to save and close the ASD ticket.

7. Active Advise Me Campaigns

All active Advise Me campaigns are listed on Advise Me hub page.

Once on the hub page, select your advocate group to see Advise Me campaigns for your top support scenarios. The links take you to the content needed to have helpful Advise Me conversations and include the content for the case closure email. If you don't see what you need under your advocate group, go to the All active campaigns tab.
