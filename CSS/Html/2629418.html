Azure Support - EA Differences in Supporting EA Partners

Overview:

In most cases, supporting EA partners have the same processes as supporting Direct EA customers. This article highlights the differences and FAQ. 

 

Policy and Process

Indirect EA customers purchase Azure Enterprise Agreements through a Microsoft Channel Partner (aka EA Partner for our purposes).  EA partners are responsible for providing pricing information and billing support to their indirect EA customers. When EA partners logon to WAPEP, they will able to see information about indirect customers assoicated with them. EA Partners have a slightly different view in the WAPEP and also some restrictions compared to direct EA customers. 

The experience on WAPEP will differ from that of Indirect Customers and Direct Customers in that they will not have any Account or Subscription creation/management abilities. It will be limited to viewing and downloading information for their enrollments. For other issues not highlighted here, please follow the process from existing EA TSGs.

Following is a description of the links in the Left Nav:

  • List Enrollments – takes them to the Enrollment Dashboard and shows all the Direct and Indirect Enrollments under that partner admin user in the main content section of WAPEP
  • Manage Partner Administrators – takes them to the Manage Partner Administrators page and allows them to add additional WLIDs as Partner Admins
  • Manage Partner Notifications – takes them to the Manage Partner Notifications page. Partner Admins can manage the frequency of Periodical Usage notifications they will receive from this page for each Partner Admin listed.
  • View Usage Summary – For a Partner Admin, this page will display the Commitment Balance and Usage summary for an Indirect OR Direct Enrollment according to what was selected by the PA. If this is an indirect enrollment, there is a link on this page which allows the user to download (in Excel) the balance and usage summary data for that particular Indirect Customer for the current quarter and 3 months underlying the quarter.

Following are some limitations for EA Partners at the WAPEP:

  • The EA Partner will not have the ability to create new accounts on behalf of the customer. The ‘Add Account’ button will not appear in the Partner UI.
  • The EA Partner will not have the ability to add subscriptions
  • The EA Partner does not have the ability to add Enterprise Administrators on behalf of the end customer
  • The EA Partner does not have the ability to remove Enterprise Administrators on behalf of the end customer
  • The EA Partner will have access to the Partner Invoicing Report, which is a summary report of usage and pricing for all of their indirect customer enrollments. 
  • The EA Partner will not have the ability to download usage for multiple enrollments at the same time
  • The EA Partner does not have the ability to modify the End Customer notification frequency
  • The EA Partner cannot resend the invitation to activate to the customer. The Partner or customer will have to call FAS to request invitations be re-sent.
  • The partner will also not be utilizing any Azure services themselves 

Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: What are the key WAPEP features for EA Partners?

WAPEP provides daily, weekly, or monthly notifications of Windows Azure platform service usage, including:

  • Ability to download all usage data across all customer accounts and subscriptions
  • View monthly service usage and quarterly commitment and overage balances on individual customer enrollments
  • View partner price list for individual customer enrollments
  • Ability to download Excel summaries, for each end customer, of their commitment balance and overage charges. This can in turn be marked up and provided to end customers to show their commitment balances and overage.

Q: How is EA Partner billing done?

  • Overage billing completed quarterly
  • Overage email notification is sent to the Channel Partner on the 6th day of the month after quarter end
  • Partner is requested to provide an updated PO number to be invoiced against, by the 15th day of the month
  • ROC sends an invoice to the partner on the 16th day of the month
  • Channel Partner pays using standard payment processing
  • Channel Partner follows their own processes for invoicing the End Customer

Q:  Can a partner access an enrollment if the customer has not yet activated it? 

Yes.  It should be available in the Partner List.

Q: An EA Partner contacts support stating they are not able to see one of their indirect customer enrollment or usable information. How do we assist in this scenario?

If this is for a new indirect customer enrollment, it could be a problem with the EA Contract Set up. Please refer to Azure Support - EA Troubleshooting Contract Setup for troubleshooting steps. Indirect EA customers could also have switched their assoicated partner. We called this a Change of Channel Partner event (CoCP). Data past the CoCP effective date will no longer be visible to the orginal partner. The enrollment will only be visible to this partner for 120 days. Please do not disclose any information related to this enrollment to this partner especially who is the new partner assoicated in the record. 

Note: CoCP is done on the backend. There is no FAS involvement.
