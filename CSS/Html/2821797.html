Description of information:

a. General Information

Azure and EMS (VL support only), Office 365, Dynamics 365 and Microsoft Intune have plans that take advantage of a payment instrument called Prepaid. A prepaid plan allows a customer to purchase a 12-month subscription prior to use without future billing. These plans require a product key, which is needed to sign up and activate the service. Product keys are good for 1 year after they are redeemed. Product keys are 25 digits long and contain both alphabetic and numeric characters. Partner-sold Product keys are sold in packs of 1, 5, 25, and 50 user licenses. Partners are also able to create a product key for the exact number of users the customer has. For example, if a customer needs 167 licenses, their partner can provide a single product key that is good for 167 licenses for 1 year rather than providing multiple product keys to add up to the desired seat count.

Prepaid plans (referenced in this article as Partner-sold prepaid plans) are sold only through Partners through the Open License, Open Value programs and Open Value Subscription programs. If an Open License or Open Value customer purchases a prepaid plan, the subscription will expire 1 year from redemption, regardless of the Open License or Open Value agreement end date.
 

To activate their subscription service, customers obtain an OSA (Online Services Activation) key from VLSC and redeem it on the applicable online service portal.

OSA keys are the equivalent of Pre-Paid Tokens which are sold in the Retail channel.

Replacement of OSA Keys may be required in some instances – see article 4566340: Procedure: VLS: Replacement token/key scenarios and rules.

[Asset 4575468]

Prepaid keys cannot be shared between Affiliated regions. Customers and resellers must sign agreements and place orders with resellers within the same Defined Region. For example, a customer with contracts in Middle East/Africa cannot add their Prepaid keys to their tenant in Europe. For more information, view this website: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/licensing/licensing-programs/open-regional.aspx

d. Extend the end date of a prepaid subscription

The customer can extend their prepaid subscription at any time up to a maximum of 2 years from the current date. For example, if a customer's subscription has a start date of 02/01/2013 and end date of 02/01/2014, extending their subscription on 06/01/2013 would move their end date to 02/01/2015.

In order to keep the same amount of licenses, the customer must enter the same amount of prepaid product key licenses when extending. Extending a subscription with fewer licenses will result in reducing the customer’s seat count. Extending a subscription with more licenses will result in increasing the customer's seat count.

The steps to extend the end date of a prepaid subscription are the same as the renewing steps. See the section titled Renew a prepaid subscription.

To extend the subscription and move to a lower number of licenses, follow these steps:

  1. Customer must renew a direct subscription with the same number of licenses as the current prepaid subscription.
  2. Support the de-provision of the source prepaid subscription.
  3. Customer adds the new prepaid subscription (with the desired number of licenses) in the Microsoft 365 admin center and re-assigns users from the direct to the prepaid subscription if the customer is purchasing the exact same subscription.
  4. Customer cancels the direct subscription.
  5. Support checks the Charges tab in CMAT to ensure the direct subscription has been FULLY credited automatically. If yes, no further action is required. If no, then the customer is charged for the first 30 days. Go to the next step.
  6. Support applies a credit for the first 30 days by following the process in 2996655: Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT.
e. Add more seats to a prepaid subscription

A customer will have the option to increase the seats on their prepaid subscription by choosing the Add option in the portal and adding a product key. Doing so results in a new user and an updated end date.

Each product key has a number of users and days for those users associated with it. A product key for one user for 1 year has 365 days associated. A product key with five users for 1 year has 1825 days associated with it.

The end date calculation is performed whenever a customer adds users to an existing prepaid subscription.

The number of days being added to the subscription is added to the days currently on the subscription and divided by the number of users to give the days the subscription will be active.

If the customer uses a credit card to increase the number of seats, this will result in a new subscription with a different end date than their current users. The new anniversary day will be the day the day the customer purchased the additional seats with their credit card.

Example (The customer adds two users to an existing five-user prepaid subscription):
The customer has a prepaid subscription with five users and 300 days remaining on their subscription.
Customer adds two users.
The two new product keys have 365 days.
(5 users * 300 days + 2 users * 365 days) / 7 users = 318.57 rounded up to 319 days for the subscription

In this example, the customer’s subscription’s end date will be moved out 19 days, because the additional time is spread among all users.
Example (The customer renews with a smaller license count product key):
  • The customer has a prepaid five-seat M plan. The subscription end date is March 1, 2014. If the customer renews the subscription on February 1, 2014, using a one-seat product key, what happens?
  • The seat count is reduced to one on the day the customer renews with a one-seat product key, February 1, 2014. The remaining time of the five users is distributed to this one user, pushing the end date out 5 months (~June 21, 2015).
  • (5 * 28 days (time remaining on the subscription) + 365 days) / 1 user

f. Reduce seats for a prepaid subscription
Advocates do not perform seat reductions. If a customer wants to reduce their licenses, their only option is to renew with a key that has the lower seat count. Note: If the tenant has more licenses assigned than licenses purchased, the seat count will increase during this activity. 
The customer must first:
  • Reduce assigned licenses to the desired number of licenses.
  • Then renew or extend with a key that has the lower seat count. This will reduce the licenses and the remaining time will be distributed to the lower number of users.

i. Switching from a prepaid to a direct subscription before the expiration date of the prepaid plan


If the total cost of the new subscription is greater than or equal to the remaining value of the prepaid subscription, the customer will see a credit on the new subscription for an amount equal to the unused time left on the prepaid plan. However, if the total cost of the new subscription is less than the remaining value of the current prepaid subscription, the unused time will be forfeited. The customer will be notified before checkout, and they can wait to switch until closer to your prepaid subscription’s expiration date. 

j. Cancellations

A customer on a prepaid subscription can request a cancellation, but we cannot provide a refund for the unused portion of their service. It is best to suggest to the customer that they leave the subscription active and available for use.

k. Tax exemption for Value-added tax (VAT) and non-VAT

Any customer who is tax exempt will need to receive their tax exemption at the time of purchase from the reseller. Microsoft is unable to process any tax refunds for prepaid cards sold by resellers. If the prepaid plan was purchased at the Microsoft Store, please follow 2500396: Office 365 redirect resource sheet to transfer the customer to the Microsoft Store team.

If VL support gets such request, follow the same instruction above.

l. SLA credits

SLA credits for prepaid plans are fulfilled by extending the end date of the prepaid subscription. Commerce Support will calculate the credit and will perform the extension of the end date. See 3078460: Processing a credit request due to a service outage for Direct and Prepaid customer for the process to calculate the SLA credit and extend the end date of a prepaid subscription. 

If VL support gets such request, follow the same instruction above.

m. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs):

Q: Can a customer who has a prepaid subscription renew with a credit/debit card or with an invoice?
A: Yes, the customer will need to navigate to the "Purchase" section of the Office 365 admin portal near the time their prepaid subscription expires and purchase the desired subscription. The customer will not need to re-assign users to the new subscription as long it is the same type of subscription. When the customer's prepaid subscription expires, the active licenses on the direct purchase subscription will continue the customer's service uninterrupted.

Q: Can a prepaid card customer activate on an existing tenant? (For example, if the customer has a direct trial or if they already have a direct paid subscription and they would like to use to the prepaid model?)
A: Yes.

Q: Will customers who purchase a prepaid plan receive an invoice from Microsoft?
A: No. Microsoft does not generate invoices for prepaid plans.

Q: Can a prepaid plan be purchased in one country and used in another?
A: Prepaid keys cannot be shared between Affiliated regions. Customers and resellers must sign agreements and place orders with resellers within the same Defined Region. For example, a customer with contracts in Middle East/Africa cannot add their PrePaid keys to their tenant in Europe. For more information, view this website: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/licensing/licensing-programs/open-regional.aspx.

Q: Can a prepaid and a direct plan exist on the same tenant?
A: Yes.
