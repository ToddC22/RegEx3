Procedure: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement

[Asset 4049349]

Who should follow this process?

  • Accounts & Billing
  • Surface
  • Xbox

What is this process about? 

This article outlines the process for engaging with an Advanced Capabilities Advocate (ACA) to effectively resolve the customer’s issue. You will maintain contact with the customer throughout the support process and engage with an Advanced Capabilities Advocate as necessary to determine the appropriate resolution to the customer’s issue.

Process

1. Engagement process

This section outlines the process for engaging with an Advanced Capability Advocate at either the site or Microsoft. You will engage with ACA to determine the best resolution for the issue, and you will maintain communication with the customer throughout the support process.

1.1 Site-based Advanced Capability Advocates

Site-based Advanced Capability Advocates do not replace your normal site procedures for engaging with team leads or supervisors when you may not be able to fully resolve a customer issue on your own. Examples include:

  • Approval for refunds
  • Troubleshooting guidance

Site-based Advanced Capability Advocates are groups that have special tools or insight that can help solve specific problems. These teams have several different names:

  • Xbox: Offline Team – Shipping and repair/replace process issues
  • Surface: Super Users - Shipping and repair/replace process issues
  • Sales: Customer Service Champions – Order delivery issues

Each site will have a different process for contacting these teams, so make sure you connect with your supervisor if you are not familiar with how to reach them.

1.2 Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocate engagement

For scenarios that require engagement with a Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocate (MS ACA), a child SR will be created and routed to the appropriate team based off on the coding on the ASD form. For more information on valid engagement, see KB 817557 Policy: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement. For guidance on SR creation, see the following articles:

  • Accounts & Billing, Sales and Xbox: KB 2988026 Process & Policy: SR Creation and Case Ownership

To engage with a Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocate, follow the steps below and see Create Child SR in the "Customer Support" section of KB 3046180 Tools User Guide: Assisted Support Desktop (ASD), for more detail on child SRs:

1. Load the appropriate SR and then complete coding the Incident form; see section 2.1 for guidance.

2.  Prepare for engagement by setting the proper field to “Consult with Advanced Capability Advocate”:

  • Accounts & Billing: Solution Delivered
  • Sales: Solution Delivered
  • Surface: Additional Actions
  • Xbox: Solution Delivered

3. The Is this the Child SR? field will now populate; set this to "No" on the parent SR.

4. Once the parent case is created and the incident form is completed, click New Child SR. The Child SR is created, and opens as a new SR.

Note Look for the Parent SR in the left navigation pane as a confirmation that you are currently working in the child SR. Similarly, if you see Child SR in the left navigation pane, you are currently working in the Parent SR.

5. In the Child SR, click Edit so that it inherits the content from the Parent SR.

Screenshot

[Asset 4051569]

6. Set the Is this the Child SR? field to "Yes" on the child SR only; this will populate the Advanced Capability Engagement Form.

Screenshot

7. Complete all fields of the Advanced Capability Engagement Form; see section 2.2 for guidance.

Screenshot

[Asset 4051571]

8. Cases will be routed automatically for Xbox and Surface agents at this point.

Note Accounts & Billing and Sales agents will need to transfer the case to the Microsoft Advanced Capability Advocate queue as you did previously for escalations:

  • Accounts & Billing: Advanced Capability Advocates – S&S Accounts
  • Sales: Advanced Capability Advocates – CSS Post-Sales

9. On the child SR, click Save and then Complete.

Screenshot

[Asset 4051572]

10. Return to the parent SR and set a follow-up for 24 hours. See KB 4050217 Schedule and reschedule follow up for guidance.

11. After 24 hours, review the parent SR Case Notes for an update from MS ACA. See KB 4049296 TUG: ASD: Case View icon for guidance.

12. If additional information is requested by MS ACA to resolve the issue, collect the specified information and add it to the child SR by clicking Edit Comments Only. See KB 4050422 Edit comments only for all cases for guidance.

13. Once the resolution has been determined, MS ACA will provide an update to the parent SR case notes. MS ACA will immediately close the child SR following resolution.

14. Confirm the resolution with the customer. If additional engagement regarding the issue with MS ACA is needed, and return to step 4.

15. Once resolution has been confirmed, update your case notes, as MS ACA will close the child. You will then close the parent SR accordingly.

2.2 Advanced Capability Engagement Form taxonomy

Please use the table below for guidance in filling out the Advanced Capability Engagement Form:

Actions taken to troubleshoot

Provide a summary of the troubleshooting completed with the customer, provide a request of the Advanced Capability Advocate team.

Symptoms and error code/message seen

Outline any symptoms of an issue, document all error codes and messages seen.

Confirmed account contact information?

Confirm contact information on account with customer and update as necessary:

  • Yes – Account information is accurate for contacting

    All information on the customer’s account is up to date and appropriate for contacting the customer.
  • Yes – Assisted customer in updating account contact information

    Customer’s information was outdated when they called in; and you assisted them in updating contact info on their account
  • No – Unable to update account contact information, see case notes

    Contact information on the account is inaccurate, and you were unable to assist with updating the contact info. Correct contact information should be placed in Case Notes.

Preferred contact channel

Indicate whether the customer would like to be contacted by phone or email.

Customer time zone

Select the time zone that the customer resides in.

Customer preferred contact window (at least 3 hours)

Select a time slot when the customer will be available for contact.

Is contact in English acceptable?

Select appropriate response.

Preferred language
 

Note This field will populate if
"No" is selected in the Is contact in English acceptable? field.

If English is not acceptable, please select the customer’s preferred language for communicating with Support.

Supervisor/Mentor who approved?

Enter the alias of the supervisor or mentor who reviewed the engagement for validity.

Lifecycle

Select as appropriate:

  • Accounts & Billing – select for any billing or Microsoft account-related engagements.
  • Sales & Devices – select for any hardware-related engagements.
  • Technical/Up & Running – select for any technical-related issues.
  • Rapid Response Team (RRT) – select for any product safety events.

Line of business

Select as appropriate:

Accounts & Billing:

  • Billing – select for any billing-related scenarios.
  • Microsoft account – select for any Microsoft account-related scenarios.
  • MSN – select for any MSN service-specific account or billing scenarios.
  • OneDrive – select for any OneDrive service-specific account or billing scenarios.
  • Skype – select for any Skype service-specific account or billing scenarios.

Sales & Devices:

  • Xbox - select for any Xbox hardware-specific scenarios.
  • Surface/Band - select for any Surface or Band hardware-specific scenarios.
  • Sales - select for any Sales-related scenarios.

Engagement type

See the individual Engagement Scenario KBs for guidance:

  • KB 3072553 Policy: Engagement scenarios for Microsoft Store- Accounts & Billing and Sales
  • KB 3072567 Engagement scenarios: Xbox
  • KB 3072561 Engagement scenarios: Surface and Band

Additional details

The fields in the "Additional Details" section of the Advanced Capability Engagement Form will vary by the Engagement Type selected in the form.

Subject

The Subject field must begin with the Engagement Type name; review KBs linked in the "Engagement type" cell above.

Once an Engagement Type is selected in the form, the Subject field will auto-populate with the escalation type name as seen in the image above.
