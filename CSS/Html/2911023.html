1. Overview

1.1 Introduction

Customers in North America can be granted tax exempt status based on government determination. This status can apply to people, companies, or organizations, relieving them from paying sales tax. Microsoft provides sales tax refund after a purchase was made and only upon customer providing valid documentation proving the exemption status.

Note: Microsoft Store does not provide tax exemption when processing an order unless the customer shops in a tax-free state. You can’t remove sales tax from an order before it is placed, including Buy Online, Pickup in Store (BOPIS) for tax-free states. To receive tax exemption, the customer would need to shop only in their local Microsoft Store.

Microsoft accepts tax exemption post purchase when the customer can provide valid required documents. We provide a sales tax refund after an order is placed, under the following conditions with no exceptions:

  1. The customer must provide valid documentation describing their organization’s exempt status from paying sales tax (more information on what documents we accept, see section 2.2).
  2. The Microsoft account name and the organization name on the tax-exempt document must match. 
  3. The billing address must match the physical address of the organization.
  4. The customer’s order must comply with the Microsoft Store’s Terms of Use and Sale.
  • Note: Products for sale at the Microsoft Store are intended for personal and non-commercial use. Resellers are not eligible for purchasing from the Microsoft Store.

1.2 Tax & International Fees questions

If a customer needs help understanding why the tax charged does not match their country:

  1. Review their Microsoft account in MST and determine if it's a system problem or an account problem.
  2. Verify that the country displayed in their Microsoft account profile matches the country where they currently live.
  3. If you determine that the tax was incorrectly applied to the purchase, engage Accounts and Billing Advanced Capability Advocates support.

Note: If the customer's country is incorrect in their Microsoft account when they made a purchase, we cannot provide the refund for that purchase.

A customer might purchase an app and be charged an international fee. For example, when a customer's credit card is from an international bank, the bank might charge a fee for purchases. Note: Microsoft does not refund international fees.

1.3 Types of documentation accepted for Microsoft Store tax exemption

The following forms of documentation are accepted:

  • A current state-issued sales tax exemption certificate.
  • A current state-provided sales tax-exempt purchasing form filled out by the customer.
  • A current letter of tax exemption from the issuing government agency.

Unacceptable forms of documentation include but are not limited to:

  • Resale Certificates
  • Sales Tax Permits
  • W-9 forms

These documents will be reviewed by specialized Microsoft internal billing team before a sales tax refund can be approved (See section 2.2).

2. Procedure

2.1 Basic tax exemption procedure

If a customer contacts us requesting information regarding sales tax exemption on a Microsoft Store online order, follow the guidance below:

If the customer hasn’t placed an order yet:

  • Inform the customer that Microsoft Store online does not allow a tax-exempt sale prior to making the purchase.

If the customer has already made a purchase:

  • Ask them to submit proof of exempt status. Follow section 2.2 for North America customers, or follow the relevant article listed below if the customer is from a different region:
    • For EMEA customers - Refer to KB 2854372 Procedure: EMEA Value Added Tax (VAT).
    • For China customers - Refer to KB 4462484 Procedure: Asia Tax Exemption.

The specialized Microsoft internal billing team will review the documents and if appropriate, the sales tax will be refunded.

Note: If the customer has a previous SR but is contacting support regarding order numbers not mentioned in the original request, please guide the customer to create a new ticket by e-mailing taxexmpt@microsoft.com. Do not reopen and re-escalate previous SRs with new order numbers.

2.2 Submitting tax exemption for North America customers

Note: This is information is required before submitting:

  • The Microsoft account name and the organization name on the tax-exempt document must match. 
  • The billing address must match the physical address of the organization.

 

  1. Instruct the customer to submit their documents in one of the following ways:
    • Email: The customer can send their documents as an email attachment to taxexmpt@microsoft.com.
      • Note: The email address is missing a second “e”. This is the correct spelling.
    • Fax: The customer may fax their documents to 1-425-936-7329
      • Please include a cover letter with the following information:
        Attention: Microsoft Store Tax Exemption
        Order# (s):
        Email Address:
  2. Close the case. There is no need to engage Advanced Capability Advocates for any tax-exempt questions.

Note: If a customer contacts you because they’re not getting a response from us regarding their submitted form, verify the customer is using the correct email address taxexmpt@microsoft.com. If the customer is using the correct email address, you can advise customers to wait for processing.

  1. All further contact about tax exemption will be done between the specialized internal billing team and the customer.
