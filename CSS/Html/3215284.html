1. Applies to

The following services: All services
The following advocate roles: T1
The following markets: All markets (For detailed information about product-specific availability, see 2748142: Markets where Microsoft Online Services are available .)
 

Scenarios approved to flag an account as DNC:
 

  • Customer promises to pay (PTP)
  • Brazil customer or India customer being dunned for withholding taxes
  • EA Migration:
    • EA start date has been confirmed, the direct subscription has been disabled and the customer is entitled for credits.
    • The customer is in the process of migrating to EA and does not want a service interruption.
  • Billing Dispute:
    • Increase/decrease in licenses: Seat number change happened in between billing cycles, therefore billing adjustment will only reflect when the next invoice is generated.
    • User cancellation: Cancellation happened in between billing cycles and the auto-credit will only reflect on the next invoice.
    • VAT/tax issue: the customer has been identified as tax-exempt or with a discrepancy in tax percentage. Credit request for the adjustment has already been requested and/or processed.
    • Incorrect invoice: Monthly billed customer has updated their details in the portal and has to wait until the next invoice for their updated bill.
    • Change of payment instrument: Invoice Pay account is disabled, the customer is waiting for the adjusted invoice that shows the cancellation credit.
    • Change of billing frequency/subscription: Change occurred mid-cycle and customer is waiting for the next invoice to reflect the adjustment.
    • System/Technical issues: TFS bug has been created. The customer cannot use their subscription until the bug is resolved.
    • MOSA Amendment: (These are rare and are usually EDU or GOV customers.) If the customer is in the process of MOSA amendment or if the form has already been completed and signed and the credit request for the amendment has already been requested or processed.
  • The customer is being dunned and requests a Statement of Account, Account Balance or Account Reconciliation
    • The procedure is documented in 3200211: Handling account balance or statement of account/account reconciliation requests for Invoice Pay customers.
  • The customer has paid, but their payment is not reflected and they are being dunned. The customer has provided proof of payment.
    • The procedure is documented in 2864341: How to handle misdirected, misapplied, or missing payments for Invoice Pay customers.

Scenarios not approved for DNC flags:

  • Payment issues are not considered a valid billing dispute. These should be considered an account reconciliation or Statement of Account request and handled according to 3200211: Handling account balance or statement of account/account reconciliation requests for Invoice Pay customers.
  • Withholding tax (WHT) amounts should not be flagged as DNC. Any billing dispute for WHT (except for customers in Brasil and India) should be escalated to the CAICashApps team. For handling procedures for customers in Brasil and India, refer to 2809401: Supporting withholding tax credit requests from customers.
  • Accounts with zero balances should not be flagged as DNC.
  • If the credits are already shown in SAP but not in CMAT, open an assistance request for CAICashApps to complete the credit offset in SAP.

2. Guidance 
 

  • It is not possible to prevent credit card customers from being sent dunning emails. Do not send DNC requests for credit card customers to GFS (Global Financial Services) or to PayOps.

3. Procedure

Promise to pay:

Note: Flagging an invoice as Promise to Pay will suspend dunning for that invoice until the flag expires.
  1. Create a case and obtain the promised payment information from the customer.
     
    Promise to Pay Form Advocate-provided information:
    Company Name  
    Order Number(s)  
    Invoice number(s) being paid  
    Detailed description of PTP reason  
    Payment amount/currency to be sent  
    Form of payment (check or wire transfer)  
    Customer contact name  
    Customer contact email  
    Customer contact phone number  
  2. Place the CPC Promise to Pay Request (region) template in the Rave ticket. Ensure that all fields have complete information.
  3. Once done, submit an assistance request in Rave.

    NOTE: This also needs to be done in the BILLING DISPUTE and EA MIGRATION scenarios
  • Request is approved:

    i. Tier 3 will process the request, provide the PTP flag expiration date to Tier 1, record the information in the Rave ticket and close the request.
    ii. Inform the customer their request is complete and provide the expiration date of the PTP flag.

    NOTE: This also needs to be done in the BILLING DISPUTE and EA MIGRATION scenarios.
  • Request has incomplete information:

    i. Tier 3 will reject the request as incomplete.
    ii. Get the missing information and complete the template in the Rave ticket then create a new assistance request.

    NOTE: This also needs to be done in the BILLING DISPUTE and EA MIGRATION scenarios.

    iii. Tier 3 will evaluate the request and information provided. Follow the procedure based on Tier 3’s decision.
  • The customer’s account already has a PTP flag.

    i. Tier 3 will provide the current PTP expiration date to Tier 1.
    ii. Tier 1 informs the customer their account already has a Promise to Pay flag in effect until <PTP expiration date>.
    iii. If the customer needs more time than the current expiration date, Tier 1 escalates to GFS: AR Support area CAICAT.
  • The request is invalid:

    i. Tier 3 will reject the request as invalid.
    ii. Inform the customer their request cannot be processed.

Billing Dispute:
 
  1. Create a case and obtain the following information from the customer.
    Do Not Contact Request Form Advocate-provided information:
    Company Name  
    Order Number(s)  
    DNC Reason Code Billing Dispute
    Detailed description of DNC reason  
    Customer contact name  
    Customer contact email  
    Customer contact phone number  
  2. Open an assistance request to request a DNC flag on the customer’s account.
  • The request is complete and valid:

    i. Open the assistance request and apply the template CPC Billing Dispute T1 Request (region) to the Rave case notes. If the template cannot be accessed, use the example* to fill out the request. Ensure all fields have complete information. 
    ii. Tier 3 will process the request, provide the DNC flag expiration date to Tier 1, record the information in the case notes, and close the assistance request.
    iii. Inform the customer their request is complete and provide the expiration date of the DNC flag.
  • Request has incomplete information:

    i. Tier 3 will reject the request as incomplete.
    ii. Get the missing information and contact Tier 3 via the POD again.
    iii. Tier 3 will evaluate the request and information provided. Follow the procedure based on Tier 3’s decision.
  • The customer’s account already has a DNC flag.

    i. Tier 3 will provide the current DNC expiration date to Tier 1. 
    ii. Tier 1 will inform the customer their account has already been flagged to stop dunning emails until <DNC expiration date>.
    iii. If the customer needs more time than the current expiration date, Tier 1 escalates to GFS: AR Support area CAICAT.
  • The request is invalid:

    i. Tier 3 will reject the request as invalid.
    ii. Inform the customer their request cannot be processed.

Brazil or India customer being dunned for withholding taxes:
  1. Create an assistance request for GFS. Use this subject line:
    Subject – [GFS to Review] Request DNC of Customer [Namexxxxx]/Order[IDxxxxxx]/Country/[DNC Code]
  2. Use the following queue:
    AR Support area: CAICAT
  3. Use the following template in the assistance request for GFS:
    Do Not Contact Request Form Advocate-provided information:
    Company Name  
    Order Number(s)  
    DNC Reason Code Brazil customer or India customer being dunned for withholding taxes
    Detailed description of DNC reason  
    Customer contact name  
    Customer contact email  
    Customer contact phone number  
    Note: GFS will evaluate the DNC request for the customer and respond within 48 hours. The maximum duration for the DNC order is the following, but not every customer will receive the maximum DNC duration. GFS will specify if there is a different duration for each assistance request.
    • For billing errors or corrections: 60 days
    • For direct subscription to Enterprise Agreement migration: 90 days
