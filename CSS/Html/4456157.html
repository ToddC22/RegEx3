Procedure: Misroute

[Asset 4462485]

Description

This document details what to do when a case has been misrouted. A misroute could be caused by a vague problem description, misalignment of technical skills or because of a lack of qualifications for the specific offering or customer segment. It ensures that the customer/partner will receive a timely response to their support request and a Support Engineer will be ready to assist.

1. Steps

As soon as you identify that the SR was misrouted, act immediately to determine the correct path and follow the information below to determine if it is necessary to re-route the case.

Criteria or scenarios that may trigger re-routing steps:

  • If the case was auto-assigned to you by MSaaS Assignment.
    • Take ownership of the case, do not reject it.
      • Rejecting the case will require the next available engineer to re-route it if they determine it is a misroute.
      • Continue working through this procedure
  • If the case relates to a Third party or unsupported product.
    • Inform the customer as to the supportability of the product and either move forward with the service request or close the service request (see 4457809 | Procedure: Case Closure).
  • If the case notes do not make it clear why the case has been assigned to you or why it was routed to your queue.
    • If your team has been trained to Swarm and you are part of the CSS Cross Technology Swarming team, determine if there is enough information in the case to Swarm with another technology prior to contacting the customer
    • Call the customer and have a conversation about the kind of support they require
      • Leave a voice mail and send a follow-up email if they are not available.
    • Complete Initial Response or First Quality Response (FQR) if your team has been trained in FQR.
    • Record the outcome of the customer contact.
      • By adding notes in "Internal note" section (Rave).
      • In a phone log and Notes (Service Desk Case).
    • Make sure you have recorded your understanding of the customer's issue in the case notes.
    • Either Swarm (if enabled for your team) to bring in the resources needed or complete the re-routing steps below.
  • If there is enough information in the case and it was clearly a misroute caused by the system.
    • Attempt to Swarm to find an owner, if you don’t get a response and you know where to route it complete the re-routing steps below.

Re-routing Steps:

  • Document case notes to explain the transfer and misroute
  • Update the case title with 'Rerouting to <Technology Name>: <SR Description>'.
    • Replace <Technology Name> with the technology the customer requests assistance with.
    • Replace <SR Description> with an accurate description of the customer's issue.
  • Updated the case with correct product and support topic information.
  • Transfer the case:
    • Service Desk Case to Rave:
      1. Change the Product Family, Product, Product Version, Support Topic, and Support Subtopic on the Service Desk case to a product supported in Rave (e.g. Exchange Online, Office 365, etc.). See 4488309 Rave and Service Desk product support list for details. [Asset 4504430]
      2. Transfer the Service Desk case to the MSaaS Rave Transfers queue.
        Note: It will show it is going to a queue in Service Desk MSaaS Rave Transfer, do NOT OVERRIDE that. Manually Overriding and selecting the MSaaS Rave Transfers queue will NOT work. The case must be transferred by updating the Product and Support Topic on the case and then transferring.
        Note:
        • If it is a Restricted Access case, it will route to the appropriate Restricted Access Queue in Service Desk.
          Note: The Modern Workplace teams are still working Restricted Access cases in Service Desk until additional capabilities around collaboration and DTM can be delivered in Rave. So when transferring a Restricted Access case to Modern Workplace teams, the Service Desk user will not see MSaaS Rave Transfers, but ANOTHER MSaaS queue.
        • During the transfer process, do NOT use the Manual Override.
          [Asset 4505737]

          [Asset 4497867]
      3. Once the Service Desk case is transferred:
        • The Service Desk case is automatically assigned to offagent@microsoft.com. Note: Assigning directly to offagent@microsoft.com will NOT cause the case to transfer.
        • A clickable link to the Rave case shows in External uri, located at the bottom of details page.
          [Asset 4524162]
        • The photocopied Rave case will be auto assigned to the proper Support Area in Rave based on the related SAP from Service Desk.
        • In Rave, the Case summary panel on the right side of the case will contain the Service Desk case number in the Secondary ticket field.
          Important: Do NOT touch or manually close the Secondary ticket/Shadow case in Service Desk when Rave case is open (primary case). Changes made to the Service Desk case after completing the transfer to Rave is NOT ‘synced’ to the Rave case.  For example, if you need to update the Severity after the transfer, update the Severity in the Rave case instead.  The Shadow case exists to support backend services needed for interoperability between Rave and Service Desk.
      4. In Rave, the Case title, Customer Statement, Contact, Entitlement, Case Notes and Email Communication will be transferred from the Service Desk case to the photocopied Rave case.  Manually add other necessary information, if needed using Add internal note.
      • In Rave, Service Desk case notes and email communication details will appear by default:​​​​​
      • Service Desk case notes will look similar with Rave notes but without any information on the user that saved it.

      The Rave engineer will accept the case and work the case to closure. When the Rave engineer closes the Rave case (survey sent from Rave), the linked Service Desk case (the Secondary ticket/Shadow case number) is automatically closed (survey suppressed in Service Desk). 

      Note: When a case is transferred from Rave to Service Desk, the Rave case is automatically closed (survey suppressed in Rave). The Service Desk case can be transferred back to Rave if the case turns out to be a misroute to Service Desk. When the Service Desk case is transferred back to Rave, the original Rave case will be Re-opened.  If you see the Rave copy type value of TransferToMSaaS in the View additional case details link at the bottom of the Details section in the Service Desk Case, this means the case was transferred from Rave to Service Desk.

      • Transfer for product that’s NOT on the Rave direct transfer list:
        If a Rave case needs to be transferred to Service Desk Case user/team that’s NOT on the Rave direct transfer list (case ownership change), create and dispatch a NEW Service Desk Case using these steps:
        • Contact TR (Global Customer Service phone numbers or Premier Support contact) to create a Service Desk Case by searching for the customer domain, name or organization. 
        • Include a summary of the customer issue in a troubleshooting task, you may use 4346609 | Template: Summary as a guideline.
        • Document the newly created Service Desk Case number in the internal case notes section in Rave.
        • Close the Rave case as a duplicate.

      See 4339885 | Office 365 Premier Support in Rave; 4491025 On-premise support in Rave for additional details.

    • Within Service Desk Case:
      • Select the Transfer Reason of 'Misroute' and transfer the SR to queue.
      • Do not re-reroute a case to the 'Misroute' queue as it is not monitored.
      • If the target queue is incorrect or shows the Misroute queue, use Manual Override and use MSaaS Support Portal to determine the queue name.
      1. Determine reason for transfer:
        • If transferring or escalating a Case or Task to a queue for continued support within the same time zone:
          • Complete the transfer template in Notes (Service Desk Case) in English (except Japan and Korea).
        • If transferring a Case or Task to a queue at the end of your shift for continued support in a different time zone:
          • Ensure the customer wants to continue working afterhours and understands the language requirement before initiating handover.
          • Complete the transfer template in Notes (Service Desk Case) in English.
        • If transferred because the originating engineer is no longer adding value to solve the problem.
          • Confirm you have the customer’s consent.
      2. Determine if a warm handover is required:
        • For SevA/Critical severity cases, ensure a warm handover is completed by discussing the case with the receiving engineer to confirm their understanding of case information. For SevB/Standard severity cases, warm handover to receiving engineer is optional. Whether a warm handover to receiving engineer is done or not, a transfer template must be completed and placed in the case notes.
        • To perform a warm handover:
          • Create a Follow-up Task (Service Desk Case).
          • In the Task description, indicate how you (the current case owner) would like to be contacted (teams, telephone, email) and if case ownership is needed.
          • Set Task Schedule details for current date and time.
          • Transfer the Task to the appropriate queue and wait to be contacted.
          • Discuss the case with the receiving engineer.
          • Note that customer may potentially be included in warm handover discussions if beneficial for case progression.
      3. For transfers without a warm handover:
        • Create a Follow-up Task (Service Desk Case).
        • In the Task description, indicate reason for handover, troubleshooting done, next steps, and any other important details including if case ownership is needed.
        • Set Task schedule details based on the callback window you promised the customer or partner (verify the time zone is correct for your customer).
      4. Transfer the entity to a queue:
        • Follow Service Desk Case routing rules for selecting queue. If you can see the incorrect queue was selected, then check that Product and Support Topic are correct. If it is necessary, refer to MSaaS Support Portal to identity correct queue or contact.
        • If escalating, create a new Escalation Task and transfer the task to a queue (Service Desk Case). In Service Desk Case, Escalation Tasks will be routed to escalation queue if escalation routing rules are configured.
        • Expected Response time for an escalation request matches the initial response goal by service level and severity.
      5. Follow up on all customer commitments while you wait for a response to the transfer request:
        • If your task is not picked up by an engineer within the expected response time, use MSaaS Support Portal for Support and Contact details and email to request an update.
      6. The receiving engineer:
        • Taking ownership of the case.
        • Reading the transfer template added in the Task description Notes (Service Desk Case).
        • Contacting the previous owner to complete a warm handover, bringing the customer or partner into the conversation as necessary.
        • Meeting any outstanding commitments.
        • Transferring the case back to the originating region at end of shift if the customer's or partner's time zone does not match their time zone.
    • Within Rave:
      • If a Rave case needs to be escalated or transferred to another Rave user or team (case ownership change), you should only perform a case transfer or case reassignment in Rave after consulting with VTL/TL/TA/TM.
      • Ensure support is provided in the preferred language and time zone.

      Reassign a case

      Notes

      • Reassigning a case should only be used for cases within the same technology/support area. For example, when an Exchange engineer is OOF and you need to assign another Exchange engineer to work on it.
      • If the case needs to be re-assigned within your team, but to an engineer from another technology/support area, then the case needs to be first transferred to the correct support area. Afterwards the case can be assigned to the designated engineer.

      Here are the steps if a case needs to be re-assigned from one engineer to another, (you must own the case or be a Rave admin to perform a transfer), Premier flag will carry over for a reassignment:

      1. Select Reassign from the Case action drop-down list in the Actions panel.
      2. The reassign panel will show the skill the target agent needs to have (in this case Portuguese, Partner, Unclassified and Premier).
      3. Search for the desired agent and select Reassign. (only those agents with the skills matching the case skills can be returned)
      4. Additionally, if you want to know which skills the case has, you can expand the Full History and review the Role, skills and Support area.
        [Asset 4340484]
        [Asset 4340485]
    • If appropriate email the Duty Manager to let them know the case was re-directed to the correct queue.
  • If the transferred case is misrouted:
    • Transfer Service Desk to Rave, case was misrouted
      • If proper routing is another Rave team, Rave engineer transfers the case as normal.
      • If proper routing is a Service Desk team, Rave engineer can transfer the case back to Service Desk. When Rave case is transferred back to Service Desk, it will automatically close the original Rave case and the Secondary ticket/Shadow case will then be the primary Service Desk case.
    • Transfer Rave to Service Desk, case was misrouted
      • If proper routing is another Service Desk team, Service Desk engineer transfers the case as normal.
      • If proper routing is a Rave team, the Service Desk engineer can transfer the case back to Rave. When Service Desk case is transferred back to Rave, it will automatically reopen the original Rave case and the Service Desk case will then be the Secondary ticket/Shadow case.

Frequent Misroutes

Communicate these to your manager and/or leads if you are noticing patterns of misroutes so they may address and make corrections in the routing process.

Where teams may not utilize Duty Managers

If further assistance is needed for a misrouted case, engage your Tech Advisor or Team Manager.

2. Deviations or workarounds

MBS

Instructions to transfer a phone call from MBS to Microsoft Office Accounting (MOA):

  • If a MOA call is routed to the MBS support line, the call should be transferred.
  • Transfer the caller to 866-827-1619.
  • Explain to the customer that they are being routed to Customer Central.
    • The Customer Central team entitles customers for support and routes them to the appropriate support team.

NOTE: Customers should not be transferred directly to the MOA support team. All MOA customers must go through Customer Central for entitlement before speaking to the support team
