What is this article about?

Describes the new procedure for Xbox home console switch that is effective as of November 14th, 2018. This article will guide you when handling calls from customers who are requesting a switch but have already exceeded their limit.

1. Home Console Switch background

The home console enables other users on the console to play downloaded games if they were not the primary purchaser or to play online games without having a GOLD subscription. There is a limit of 5 times that a console may switch as the home console per year, which is clearly displayed on the account page and customers can see how many switches remain.

In the past, customers would call Xbox support to get additional switches added to their account by advocates. They could do this up to three times per year. The result was increased contacts and abuse of game sharing outside the home console procedure. For this reason, the Assist team has developed a way for customers to earn one additional home console switch without contacting Microsoft.

2. Procedure

Effective November 14th, 2018, advocates will no longer switch the home console counter in MST for customers who have exceeded their limit and access will be terminated, this is also when we enable an on-console experience for customers. 

The new experience will allow only one additional switch per year, which customers can earn by doing the following:

  • Completing a simple quest
  • Watching a video
  • Doing a survey

Customers still have five home console switches per year in advance of this one earned switch.

2.1 Call handling

If the customer contacts Xbox support with the issue of not being able to switch their console, explain that Xbox now offers just one switch per year and that it can only be earned directly on console by completing a quest (self-serve). This change means zero support for this scenario, see below for more details:

  • The home console feature is never to be used for troubleshooting. If a customer has a log in or game issue do not advise them to change home consoles to see if it works elsewhere.
  • Do Not advise a customer to create an additional account if they forget their log in, then use home console to “share with themselves”.
  • Help customers understand the licenses are not on the console, they are with an account so everywhere they log in they can play with others to share
  • Do not conduct a home console switch.
  • You cannot see customer switch history or details; this is now entirely self-serve.
  • Do not stay on the call or chat to “walk a customer through” finding the extra switch option on console, this is now a self-serve scenario. You should only provide the information on the resource and then end the contact. Only customers who request and require assistance should be guided through the switch process.
  • Do not offer to assist with the quest unless the customer requests it.
  • No home console switch capability is enabled for any advocate of any tier, at any site.

2.2 Code as follows in ASD

  • Root Cause: Home Console Switch Limit Reached
  • Solution Delivered: No More Switches Allowed

This will provide us the data that for this contact, the customer was directed to get one additional switch on console if they have not used this benefit yet. Unless the call or chat is lost, no other Solution Delivered should be used (do not use Educated Customer, contact dropped, or other options).

Scenario Messaging
Customer wants a switch

Error message: “Oops, you’ve reached your maximum number of switches! But don’t worry, we’ve got a quick an easy way for you to earn one extra switch until this same time next year. We’ve listened to your feedback and have made many enhancements to our systems so now you can play all your games with your friends without having to change the home console.”

“If you’ve reached your maximum number of switches, you can go to Settings-> Personalization-> My home Xbox and click on Need one more?

By earning your extra switch, you’ll learn more details about when it is appropriate to switch home Xboxes. For more information please visit http://support.xbox.com/games/game-setup/my-home-xbox.”
Customer wants agent to stay on call/chat while they go earn the switch

“I am confident that you will find everything you need in Settings-> Personalization-> My home Xbox. The information, questions and responses you provide are only accessible to you.

Please utilize this self-serve option at your convenience as our support advocates are no longer able to provide console switches or grant switches.”
Customer wants to know when his/her next switch is OR wants to know how long between switches, etc.

“Everything you need to know is now located on your console - just go to Settings-> Personalization-> My home Xbox.

Since this information is unique to you, I will not be able to see it in my tools.”
Customer wants to know about survey questions “The quick and easy survey will only take a couple of minutes to complete. It will ask you simple questions about how you use your home Xbox.”
Customer wants to know why you can’t give more switches

“Now, with the Home Xbox one extra Switch feature in Settings>Personalization, you can get your one extra switch without ever needing to contact support.

The number of console switches and information about current policies can be found at http://support.xbox.com/games/game-setup/my-home-xbox”
Customer does not see the option in My home Xbox

“You must have the latest system update for this to work: https://support.xbox.com/xbox-one/console/system-update-operating-system.

To locate this on your console, go to Settings>System>Console Info, your operating system version is in the second row.”


Appendix
To learn how to designate an Xbox One console as the home console, customers can follow the directions located at http://support.xbox.com/games/game-setup/my-home-xbox.
