2. Product information

About Microsoft Store for Business

The Microsoft Store for Business (MSfB) is the place where commercial organizations can find, acquire, manage, and distribute Windows 10 applications to users within their organization. The Microsoft Store for Business portal will allow MSfB administrators and purchasers to browse the application catalog and acquire apps.
Apps acquired through the portal can then be added to the organization’s Private Store or assigned to specific people in the organization, who need only to click on a link to install them. This will provide organizations with a controlled distribution channel and customizable store experience for users within their organization.

Prerequisites

MSfB is available only for customers with Windows version 10, build 10568 or higher. To check the version and build of Windows, the customer should follow these steps:

1. Press the Windows key and “r” to get to the Run window.
2. Type “winver” at the Run window and click OK.
3. A screen will appear that shows the Windows version and build.

Refunds
Policy
  • Customers can self-cancel within 30 days and receive a full refund. Please direct the customer to the Order History page to self-cancel if the purchase was within 30 days. Advise the customer to ensure all licenses for the app have been unassigned. Refunds cannot be processed until all licenses for the order are unassigned.
  • After 30 days from purchase, all purchases are final and non-refundable, unless the customer falls within an exception listed below.
  • Unless otherwise indicated, credits or refunds must be requested within 90 days of the date of purchase.
Advocates are authorized to process all refund amounts within CMAT:
  • The customer was unable to self-cancel in first 30 days
  • App no longer available in the store
  • The customer has not already installed/used the content
  • Content does not match Store description
  • Accidental purchase
  • Duplicate Charge
  • If the incorrect price charged – issue credit for the discrepancy

Note: Commerce Support Advocates are authorized to apply a credit for VAT billed on the invoices for the last 6 months if the credit amount is ≤$650 USD.
If the approved credit is ≤$650 USD, CMAT will apply the credit to the customer’s account without triggering the approval workflow.
IMPORTANT: If Tier 1 or 2’s approval limit is lower than the CMAT threshold of $650, the Advocate must obtain approval for the credit before submitting the credit in CMAT.

If it is unclear whether an Advocate can approve a refund, contact Tier 3 via the POD.

Advocate steps to refund a purchase in CMAT:
  1. Ask the customer for the name of the app to refund. The app name will appear under Item Description on the New Orders tab. 
  2. Advocates will not be able to Refund the order if any licenses are still assigned to a user. Check the INVENTORY page to see if there are any assigned licenses. If there are still assigned licenses, ask the customer to un-assign all licenses for that app in order to receive a refund. [Asset 4529912]

    Note: If the app name does not appear, ask the customer for the Order ID. The Order ID is found on their Order History page.
  3. Go to the New Orders page in CMAT. Find the item to refund using the Order ID provided by the customer. The Order ID = the number in the Seller field. Click Refund.

    [Asset 4090993]
  4. Check the box next to the item to refund and enter relevant details into the Comments field. Click the arrow to continue.

    [Asset 4529913]
  5. Review the details, enter the Service Request Number and click the check mark to finalize the refund or the back arrow to return to the previous page. No other action is required for the refund to be pushed to the credit card. Our system will push out the credit to the bank the same day it is processed in CMAT and the refund will show up on the next invoice.
    [Asset 4529914]
Error messages received while trying to refund a customer’s MSfB order in CMAT
  • Refund Transaction Failed (Calling Commerce Restful Service Failure):
    • This error occurs when attempting a refund in CMAT when licenses are still assigned. The customer must un-assign all licenses to the app in order for the refund to succeed.[Asset 4529915]
       
  • Index was out of range. Must be non-negative and less than the size of the collection.
    • This error occurs when the customer has already been refunded.[Asset 4529916]

Tax-exempt is now being handled by Commerce NOAM and EMEA. T1 Advocates need to follow the steps indicated in [Asset 4346155]2807966 Processing a Sales and Use Tax Exemption Certificate for customers in the United States, Canada and Puerto Rico (or 2849736 Supporting Value-Added Tax and Tax Exemption for EMEA), to have the account tagged as tax-exempt and use the recalculate tax feature in CMAT after the account has been tagged.

The Advocate uses the Recalculate Tax feature in CMAT to recalculate the tax by following steps:

  1. Go to the New Orders page in CMAT. Find the item to recalculate the tax using the Order ID provided by the customer. The Order ID = the number in the Seller field. Click Recalculate Tax.
    [Asset 4529917]
     
  2. Review the details and enter relevant details into the Comments field. Click the arrow to continue.[Asset 4529918]
     
  3. Enter the Service Request Number and click the check mark to finalize the recalculation or the back arrow to return to the previous page. Note: This operation is not immediate and therefore it might take 7 to 10 business days for the change to appear on the customer's credit card. This refund will appear on the customer's next invoice.[Asset 4529919]
     
IW Refunds

Information Worker (IW) refund support is available in Store for Business (e.g. Minecraft Education purchases). To refund an IW, an IW search (email search) in CMAT is needed to see the purchases. These purchases will not show for a tenant search.

Note: To refund an IW – you MUST refund in the IW search. This is true for MANAGED and UNMANAGED tenants. The experience is identical for both. A managed tenant is one that is managed by a company admin. Unmanaged tenant is when an IW signs up without an administrator. For more details about managed and unmanaged tenants, please refer to article ﻿3080556: Supporting end user-purchased subscriptions.

4. Supporting documents/Additional info

Technical Support Issues

Windows technical support

Help topics for common administration tasks can be found under the Support tab of Store for Business. Customers who need Technical Support with MSfB should be directed to the Support page where they can submit an online Service Request to Windows Technical Support. This is a pay per incident (PPI) support model, meaning the customer must pay for support. Technical Support handles issues such as application distribution, application installation and use, etc.

NOTE: Technical support scenarios for Minecraft Education Edition should not be raised to Tier 3.
To create technical scenarios use the following linkhttp://support.microsoft.com/oas/default.aspx?prid=16096
