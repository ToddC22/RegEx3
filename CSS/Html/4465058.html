1. Introduction

Issuing CSV or Refunds

If adding CSV or return eligibility is met, select an appropriate reason code and the appropriate item.

  • CSV – Use this option to add CSV back to the customer’s account.
  • Return Line Item – Use this option for standard refund process. This will remove the entitlement of the product or service and refund the customer.
  • Refund Line Item – We should only use this option if the customer needs to keep the product or service, but they require a refund because of circumstances, such as the service is down or the incorrect price was charged.

See KB 4053523 TUG: MST: Orders section for details on the refund process and guidance on reviewing comments.

Select the most appropriate reason code. In the comment field, include (examples):

  • Support case reference number, such as Service request number, Rave ID, or other support tool case reference number (for example, Service Request (SR) 1823831, Rave ID (RaveID), MSX Lead ID).
  • CSV/Refund procedure article number (for example, KB 4465058).
  • Title of the scenario used (for example, Price guarantee (Microsoft Store): Price drop or offer change to include Free Items).

Customer scenarios:

The scenarios within this article are the only times in which you should provide CSV credits to an account. See the correct SKU to use when a CSV credit exception scenario occurs.

2. Pricing issues

Microsoft Terms of Sale (ToS) must be followed. All scenarios listed below are treated as exceptions to the ToS.

Scenario

Advocate handling

CSV SKU and reason code

Price Promise (Microsoft Store):

Price drop or offer change to include free items.

Microsoft Store Online will not price match third-party retailers. The customer must return the product to the third-party and repurchase on the Microsoft Store. We will not honor price match across countries/markets.

Microsoft Store Online will honor a price drop on recently purchased products within the applicable return timeframe with a partial refund. For offers that change to become better than what was already purchased by offering a free item, sales associates can use CSV to bring price of the "free item" to $0.

The holiday extended returns timeframe applies to Price Promise for Microsoft Store. Extended returns will begin for orders placed between October 1st, 2020 - December 31, 2020, and any qualifying purchases can be returned or exchanged through January 31, 2021.

Exception: China’s Price Promise: 30-days price promise for Surface. https://www.microsoftstore.com.cn/support-and-help/coupon-faq

Sales only: We will honor a price drop on recently purchased products within the return timeframe window with a partial refund.

Typically, the timeframe window is 30 days. Microsoft Store may offer extended return timeframes or price drop guarantees as part of a promotional offer. Be aware of these offers and adjust price honoring according to the extended timeframes that come with these offers.
 
For offers that change (within 30 days) to become better than what was already purchased (for example, adding a free sleeve), sales associates can use CSV to bring the price of the product to the correct price and send it to the customer.
 

Notes:

  • All offers must be live on the website to provide a partial refund or a free sleeve. Once an offer ends or goes out of stock, so does the price guarantee.
  • This procedure is internal to CSS only. In some cases, the website may say we do not price match, but what CSS can do for a make it right may be different. This is an approved procedure that may not be posted online.
  • If we lower our price on an item within the return time frame from purchase date, we will honor the lower price.

Requirements before offering a partial refund:

  1. Verify promo is still live and available to purchase: We cannot match promotional pricing if the offer has ended/expired, is no longer live, or if it is out of stock.
  2. Verify order status: Customers must have a Completed: Settled order placed prior to or during the promotion to be eligible. Orders placed after a promotion are ineligible for price match.
  3. Verify purchase date is within return timeframe window: You are authorized to provide a partial refund in the amount of the discount back to the customer, if they call in. Customers must validate the purchase was made within the last 30 days, or equivalent return window in the region.
  4. Verify customer has not already received a partial refund: Look at refunds already applied to the order and ensure a partial refund has not already been made.
  5. Verify customer history: Review SRs associated to the Microsoft account to ensure customers are not abusing the MIR (CSV) procedure.

8. Stocking issues

Scenario

Advocate handling

CSV SKU and reason

Oversell

All orders that are cancelled due to an oversell should already have notes and (potentially) a MIR already provided by the CSS PSS team. The CSS PSS team works with Supply Chain to contact the impacted customers and provide a MIR prior to their order being cancelled.

If you think that there is a case where a customer’s order was cancelled due to an oversell, and the CSS PSS team has not contacted the customer, escalate to CSC so they can create a consult for the CSS PSS team to investigate.

  1. Confirm order was placed successfully, and that customer did not receive CSV or codes either directly deposited or via email (customer checked their junk e-mail) after the stipulated time frame (this detail should be part of the promo Terms & Conditions).

  • CSV SKU - CSS Xbox Store Make it right.

Sales only - missing codes: CSV not deposited into account; Pack in Scenarios (BOGO); Assistance with CSV for Xbox Points.

If the customer placed an order that came with free CSV credit, and they have not received it. We want to make sure that we are validating first that it has not been sent and follow the Service Level Agreement (SLA) guidelines.

First review this checklist:

  • Confirm that the customer placed their order during the qualifying timeframe. Confirm that the customer has waited 10 business days since date of order shipment.
  • Investigate for a history of abuse before providing any support. Check SRs, case notes, and return history. Flag any suspicious activity via MST.

If the customer qualifies for the offer due to date range and confirmation e-mail, provide the customer with a partial refund that is equal to what they should have received.

We are providing a partial refund instead of replacing the bounceback CSV to avoid further customer confusion and potential abuse.

If the customer has not waited 10 business days, inform them to wait the SLA time (no replacement until time has passed).

Bundles that include physical + digital products intended to be gifted: The bundle was purchased with the intention of it being a gift. The digital game/content auto-entitled to the purchaser’s Microsoft account at the time of checkout making the digital game/content unable to be gifted.

If customer requests to gift the digital game:

  1. Identify the customer purchased a bundle that auto entitled the game to the purchaser’s Microsoft account, not allowing the customer to gift to someone else.
    1. In MST got to the Services section and select One Store.
    2. Select dropdown Windows Store and Xbox One Entitlements.
    3. Validate that the digital game is listed in their entitlements.
  2. Do a line-item return for the digital game to revoke the entitlement and give money back to the customer.

Note: Don’t perform a refund. A line-item return must be done to revoke the digital copy of the game.

  1. Provide the customer with CSV.
  2. Instruct customers on how to purchase a digital game through the Buy as gift option.
    1. If the game is still on pre-order:
      • After providing the CSV to the customer’s Microsoft account, let them know that they would need to go through the Buy as gift process once the game has launched. Provide the customer with the launch date.
    2. If the game has launched:
      • After providing the CSV to the customer’s Microsoft account, direct the customer to www.xbox.com/games/xbox-one and have them search for the game. After selecting that game have the customer select the Buy as gift option underneath the Buy box. [Asset 4569721]
      • If the customer does not know the e-mail address or Gamer Tag of the gift recipient, then the customer could put in their own e-mail address and the 5X5 code would be e-mailed to them.
    3. Advocates cannot POBO a gift. Gifting must be done by the customer.

How to calculate the appropriate amount of CSV to provide to the customer:

  1. Go to that original order to see how much the customer for the game.
  2. Create a new order for the digital game, but do not complete the order. The cart just before completing a purchase will have the games cost including tax.
  3. Take the total cost of the game and tax and that amount will be how much CSV you should provide the customers.

  • For customers, K-12, higher education, and military discounts are available through the low bar verification process. The low verification process is applicable to all products except for Microsoft 365 Personal, which will continue to follow the AVE process.

The customer receives Xbox Design Lab Controller different than ordered.

If the customer claims that the Xbox Design Lab controller they received isn’t the one that they ordered or there is something wrong with it, Tier 1 will first need to determine if this is a warranty claim or if the customer did indeed receive the incorrect controller.

If you determine this to be a warranty claim, direct the customer to support.xbox.com to make the claim, or transfer the customer to Xbox CSS.

If the customer confirms that the controller is different than what they ordered:

  1. Tier 1 advocates will send an e-mail to the customer via ASD and ask for the customer to reply to that e-mail by attaching three clear photos of the controller:
    • One photo of the front
    • One photo of the back
    • One photo of the numbers on the sticker inside the battery case (must be readable).
  2. Tier 1 advocate will then transfer the case to the CSC.
  3. CSC will determine if the controller matches the one ordered.
    • If it matches – a hard message will be delivered to the customer regarding our No Returns procedure. No further action.
    • If it is different – CSC will consult PSS via TFS.
  4. PSS to validate if the number inside the battery cover matches SAP Order number for this customer’s order.
    • If it matches - The refund is approved by PSS and TFS bug is reassigned back to CSC. CSC will refund the original order.

If it is different – CSS PSS team will determine if the customer is committing fraud.
