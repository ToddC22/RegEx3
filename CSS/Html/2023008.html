1. BOS and AES overview

The Advertiser Enablement Services program provides billing services, bulk campaign management, reporting and the execution of optimization services on behalf of Microsoft Advertising Service & Support advocates and Microsoft Advertising Sales Teams working directly with Microsoft Advertising customers in high-impact markets. The Back Office Support team is made up of skilled search optimization advocates who perform a variety of tasks. By conducting this work in bulk via a low-cost operations model, we enable frontline service professionals to spend more time engaging with their customers; building relationships, selling Microsoft Advertising accounts and features, driving revenue and increasing customer loyalty.

BOS advocates are not customer-facing. Work is done on behalf of frontline professionals who own all aspects of the customer experience. As this is an internal-only support team, it is advised that you do not discuss the Advertiser Enablement Services program directly with advertisers.

2. Eligibility requirements

Advertiser Enablement Services (AES) is a program performed by the Back Office Support team and offers support to Frontline MAST Services & Support advocates and Microsoft Advertising Sales team members in markets and languages where the service is available. You can request work on behalf of a customer when you are a member of their account management team, directly engaged with the customer for a service offering, or are assisting the customer with monthly invoice inquiries.

5.1 Microsoft Sales Account Team request process

This is a high-level overview of the process for submitting a request to BOS.

  1. Ensure the service you are requesting is on the Menu of Services, see section 4 for the full list.
  2. Navigate to Microsoft Advertising: Advertiser Enablement Services page and select the service you need to request.
  3. Complete the form and make sure that all fields are accurately filled out, including accurate customer information and complete instruction.
  4. Once done, select Submit. You will receive a ticket number reference immediately, and then follow-up emails from a BOS advocate shortly.

Important notes:

  • Always use the AES webform submission process to send requests. Please bookmark this site: Microsoft Advertising Advertiser Enablement Services page.
  • The AES webform attachment is up to 1G of data in up to 5 attachments. When attaching more than 1G, use The Very Large File Share and provide the link to your documents.
  • For implementation, specify if you want the BOS advocate to implement the change in the customer’s account or if you prefer to review the changes in an offline file first; in the latter case, the advocate will await your explicit approval of the offline file before implementing the change in the customer’s account.

Submission exception:

  1. Use the MARKET INITIALS | LANGUAGE | TACTIC NAME | CUSTOMER NAME | XID/CID email request process  for the following scenarios:
    • Multiple service requests in one submission. If different services apply to specific accounts under a customer, provide each impacted account number (XID) by service.
    • Lack of tools access/corpnet (single or multiple service requests)
    • Cross border requests where non-English, multiple-language requests can be submitted with one ticket.
  2. Then, send your request to the correct language-specific alias.  Use AESEnglish@microsoft.com for global English, or use AESOther@microsoft.com for all non-English contacts.
  3. If you have identical work (for example, the exact same services being requested with identical requirements) that is required across multiple CIDs, it is acceptable to send in a single request using the MARKET INITIALS | LANGUAGE | TACTIC NAME | CUSTOMER NAME | XID/CID email request process.

5.2 Frontline MAST Services and Support advocates via ASD Child Ticket

This is a high-level overview for submitting a request to BOS.

  1. Ensure the service you are requesting is on the Menu of Services, see section 4 for the full list.
  2. Create a child ticket for the main tactic you are requesting. Include any further requests in the notes (AES will then create the extra tickets on your behalf for the further tactics).
  3. Fill out the necessary fields on the Incident form.
  4. Select Yes on Issue-Specific Details Required? field in the Case Disposition and Closing section.
  5. In the Issue-Specific Details section, fill out the following fields:
    1. Issue Category: Back Office Support Requests
    2. Issue Type: Select the service you are requesting and fill out ALL the fields. This will ensure that the necessary information is provided and reduce the time of completion.

[Asset 4498380]

  1. Transfer the child ticket(s) to the AES queue:
    1. In Transfer Type, enter Frontline to BOS
    2. In language specific transfer path, enter the appropriate routing based on language:
      1. Global English Language: BOS AES English Routing
      2. Non-English Languages: BOS AES ARV-MUE
        • Dutch
        • French
        • German
        • Portuguese
        • Spanish
  2. Once done, select Apply Transfer. Your ticket will be assigned to the next available BOS advocate shortly, including the service reference number and follow-up emails.

Important notes:

  • A short note will be added in the Parent Ticket case note informing that the AES child ticket is updated.
  • After the work is completed, the BOS advocate will add a closing message in the child ticket confirming that the work is done, which will automatically close the ticket.
  • Be sure to proactively check the child ticket within 24 hours for possible questions and comments from the BOS team.
  • If you initially requested that BOS send you offline files rather than implementing the optimization service, you may open a second child ticket and request implementation.

How to submit Additional Attachment Information to AES:

If BOS requires additional attachments from you in order to process the request with sensitive customer information, please create an internal DTM workspace.

  1. Create an internal workspace at the DTMv2 Engineer Portal using InPrivate browser session. URL: https://client.dtmnebula.microsoft.com
  2. Upload all necessary attachments there.
  3. Share the link to the BOS team and/or to the Frontline Service team, so the advocate can download the attachments through case notes. URL: https://client.dtmnebula.microsoft.com/msa/Home?srNumber=xxx, enter the case number (digits only) where "xxx" is displayed here.

See KB 4056786 Data Transfer Management  for further guidance.

7. Communicating with BOS

When/if clarification is needed on a request, following the process below based on your site:

Frontline MAST Services and Support:

Comments will be added to the child ticket, which makes it very important to check back frequently to see if anything has been updated.

Microsoft Sales Account team:

Back Office advocates will communicate via email. However, if available, BOS advocates may use instant messaging (IM) to ask quick clarifying questions on tickets. To comply with privacy/security policies, the following outlines the approved usage of IM for Microsoft Sales Account team:

Permitted:

  • BOS advocates can share information like a ticket number, customer name and account name.
  • BOS advocates may use IM for quick questions or clarification on a request
  • Both parties must use their MSFT alias or v-account.

Not permitted:

  • Do not send files or share their desktop to share files with PII over IM (including XID)
  • Requesters should not initiate an IM to express urgency or open an AES ticket.

8. Escalations

For a quality issue on a specific ticket, partner with BOS to resolve the issue and fill out the survey after the ticket is closed (link will be provided upon ticket closure).

For issues that cannot be resolved by partnering with BOS, email the ticket number and issue details to: sbosesc@microsoft.com. This is internal alias only - do not disclose to the advertisers. A MAST manager will respond within one business day.

10. Resources

  • Blank Templates (to attach to submissions) – This link contains all AES-related templates to supplement your request, such as, but not limited to: Ad Extension template, Customer Setup Form (CSF), and Google Workbook files.
    • Note: CSF is intended to be filled out by internal users only. Do not send the document to the customer. Instead, ask them the questions over the phone or chat and fill it out on their behalf.

    • Due to the SharePoint size limitations, BOS advocates are required to delete hosted files within 1 week of ticket closure. Additionally, external users (customers) can’t access the Very Large File Share themselves, so internal users must pull necessary reports on behalf of their customers.
