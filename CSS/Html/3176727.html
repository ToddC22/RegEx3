1. Declined payments on threshold account

  1. Confirm the following information in the account and with the advertiser before taking any action:
    • The type of payment instrument (for example, is this a prepaid card that isn’t an allowed form of payment?).
    • Confirm the payment instrument is still valid (not expired or reported lost/stolen by the advertiser). 
    • There are available funds on the payment instrument to accommodate the payment amount. 
    • There is no transaction limit preventing the charge from going through. 
    • If the advertiser pays for additional theft protection services through their financial institution, the advertiser has confirmed with the financial institution that Microsoft Advertising is an approved merchant. AMEX may request a Microsoft Advertising Merchant ID to accept charges from Microsoft Advertising. 
  2. Confirm internally (do not ask the advertiser or mention this check) that the card is not associated with another account market as fraud using CMAT.
  3. Determine why the card was declined using CMAT. 
    • If the decline reason is Hard Bank Decline, this means that the financial institution associated with the card did not authorize the payment. Inform the caller and refer them to their financial institution for more information.

      Messaging: "The authorization appears to have been declined by your financial institution. You will need to follow up with your bank or use a different card to pay the outstanding balance in order to continue advertising.      "
  • If the decline reason was due to Risk or RIME ban, submit a child ticket to fraud. Use the suggested messaging on the fraud KB article depending on the outcome of the investigation.
  • If the decline reason is due to Card Data Errors, assist the customer in double-checking the details in the UI. If they all match the card that the customer is using, escalate to Global Support.
  • If the decline was due to a Payment Gateway (PGW) Ban, Internal Only, this means Microsoft will not process any transactions on the card. Cause for authorization failure may not be shared with customer. Escalate to Global Support for appeal. While reinstatement is unlikely, in rare cases, PGW bans may be lifted.

Messaging for escalation: “I need to send this to a billing specialist for a deeper look. This could take up to two business days.”

Messaging if not overturned: “The credit card cannot be accepted by our system. You will need to use a different card to process payment.”

  • If the decline reason is Bad Card Table, this means that the customer’s payment method cannot be processed by our system and the customer will need to add a new card to their account.

Messaging: “The credit card appears to have been declined too many times and cannot be accepted by our system. You will need to use a different card to process payment.”

  1. If CMAT does not surface the decline reason, escalate to Global Support.

2. Pay an overdue balance on an inactive threshold account

2.1 Decline schedule

As a risk-management measure, Microsoft Advertising follows a strict schedule when an account's credit card is declined that is sometimes referred to as the scope of darkness, since actions are taken on the account as the declines progress. In situations where our platform determines the debt as uncollectable based on the current card on file, a card may encounter fewer than 11 swipe attempts (and notification) before the account goes into write-off status. In all cases, customers are notified of the desire to collect funds and have options to recover their account before it goes into write-off status.

  • Only a failed swipe initiated by Microsoft Advertising will advance the account through the decline schedule.
  • When a user selects the Bill Me Now option, it will not advance the account through the declined payments schedule, even if the swipe fails.
  • If a declined payment occurs within 30 days of the account creation, the account is automatically put on credit hold status (even if the Bill Me Now option triggered it) this measure was put in place for security reasons.
  • Payment attempts made on the backup payment method do not count toward the decline schedule.
  • Any action initiated against the account or card does not impact the backup payment method.
  • This decline schedule applies to system-initiated attempts to collect payment, including attempts because of a manually established threshold limit.
  • Not all failed attempts will result in a retry. Customers will see at least one failure and no more than 11 attempts, before a debt is considered uncollectable and written off.
  • Threshold invoices for declined swipe attempts will still show Amount paid by preauthorized debit at the bottom of the invoice PDF. This does not indicate whether the payment was successful but means that the advertiser’s account type (Self-serve/Self-serve Plus) authorizes Microsoft to charge the payment method on file upon reaching the threshold or anniversary date.
  • Additional payment attempts will be made on the backup payment method do not count toward the decline schedule. Any action initiated against the account or card do not impact the backup payment method.

This decline schedule applies to system-initiated attempts to collect payment from the primary payment method, including attempts because of a manually established threshold limit. At any point throughout this schedule, Microsoft may determine the debt (or card) has an “unretryable” error and decide not to proceed through this schedule. Regardless of the number of attempts made to a card, following six months of uncollectable debt, the account will be written off for bad debt and show with a write-off status. At which point the account is not recoverable (see more information about this detailed under Attempt 11 in the table below).

Attempt on primary payment method

Actions and recovery steps

Attempt 1

  • Timing: Threshold attained or anniversary date.
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined): No action, unless the account was created within the last 30 days.
    • If the account was created in the last 30 days, we will immediately place the account on hold after this first decline. Review the system-initiated actions for the second payment attempt noted below, as these impact this type of account at this stage as well.
  • Recovery steps: Change payment instrument and attempt Bill Me Now or advise advertiser to check with their financial institution.
    • If the account was created in the last 30 days, customer can take the above steps or select the remove the hold link in the red banner to be guided through the credit hold workflow to resolve.

Attempt 2

  • Timing: 24 hours after initial attempt.
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined):
    • The advertiser is sent an email notification to update credit card information.
    • The advertiser can create new accounts.
    • All accounts using the declining payment method are placed on hold status, suspending delivery.
    • Upon sign in, the advertiser will see the red banner indicating there is a hold which links them into the credit hold workflow to resolve.
    • In addition, the PI causing the hold will be marked in red font with On Hold DD/MM/YY in the below workflows, so customers are aware a PI is causing credit holds. Also, a new lightbox will serve if the PI causing holds is selected in the workflow to help make customers aware of the risk when using that PI on another account:
      • Account creation
      • Account update
      • Credit Hold removal workflow [PI selection dropdown]
      • Payment option switching
      • Account linking
      • Global Financial Services (GFS), also known as Global Credit Services (GCS) will have a new SAP PI level hold feature that will be available only to that team.
      • Advocates can find this new field as read only on the Customer Details page.
  • Recovery steps: Select the remove the hold link in the red banner. This will guide customers through the credit hold workflow to resolve. Or, if this is presenting issues for the customer, they can alternately change the payment instrument and attempt Bill Me Now or advise advertiser to check with their financial institution.

Attempt 3

  • Timing: Two days after initial attempt (one day after second attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined):
    • The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails the third attempt.
    • If both primary and backup payment method attempts fail, a second warning email is sent to advertiser.
    • All accounts using the declining primary payment method are placed on hold, regardless of the CID in which they reside.
    • Threshold is decreased to the next lower limit for the advertiser’s service level and currency.

Attempt 4

  • Timing: Nine days after initial attempt (seven days after third attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined):
    • The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.

Attempt 5

  • Timing: 16 days after initial attempt (seven days after fourth attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined):
    • The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.
    • Threshold is decreased to lowest limit for the advertiser’s service level and currency. See Payment Limits for starting thresholds by shell type.
    • If the customer is unmanaged and has no SAP PIs in their wallet, we will check if more than 50% of the PIs are failing. If they are, we will roll up the hold to the customer level.
    • We will not hold SAP accounts if there is nothing wrong with the SAP PI.

Attempt 6

  • Timing: 30 days after the initial attempt (two weeks after fifth attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined): The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.

Attempt 7

  • Timing: 45 days after the initial attempt (two weeks after sixth attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined): The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.

Attempt 8

  • Timing: 60 days after the initial attempt (two weeks after seventh attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined): The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.

Attempt 9

  • Timing: 70 days after the initial attempt (10 days after eighth attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined): The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.

Attempt 10

  • Timing: 80 days after the initial attempt (10 days after ninth attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined):
    • The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.
    • If both primary and backup payment methods fail, the primary payment method is added to the bad card table.
    • The primary payment method cannot be added to any new accounts.
    • Note: The payment method's addition to the bad card table does not prevent the eleventh swipe from occurring on either the primary or backup payment methods.
  • Recovery steps: The action against the primary payment method is irreversible. Advertisers must successfully pay charges if they wish to resume advertising within the same account and CID. The card may no longer be added to any other accounts.

Attempt 11

  • Timing: 180 days after initial attempt (100 days after tenth attempt).
  • System-initiated actions (if attempt is declined):
    • The backup payment method is attempted one minute after the primary method fails.
    • If both primary and backup attempts fail, the advertiser’s CID and all XIDs associated with it are permanently moved to inactive status and will not be eligible for delivery.
    • An email notification is sent to the advertiser that the account has been inactivated.
  • Recovery steps: The account inactivation is irreversible. To continue advertising, the advertiser must create a new CID using a new credit card. If the advertiser is interested in paying the outstanding balance and creating a new account, assist the customer using the process in KB 3175030 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Balance Transfers.

2.2 Payment instructions by market

Payment options by market may change without notice. Always recommend customers check Choosing a payment method for Microsoft Advertising with their preferred market and currency, before choosing their payment option or remitting payment to Microsoft.

Messaging:

Advertiser on credit hold (not to be used if the account has already reached write-off status (six months of uncollected revenue) or has been inactivated)

“It appears that there may be an outstanding debt associated with your account. Follow the credit hold workflow to add another payment option to your account and pay the outstanding balance. Once the payment is received, your account will be reactivated within three days. If the payment options presented here do not meet your needs, please let me know and we can look into transferring the outstanding debt to a new account or payment method."

Account has reached write-off status (six months of uncollected revenue or 11 consecutive declines) and has been inactivated:

“I apologize for not being able to reinstate the existing account. I can assist with migrating your campaign details into a new account; however, you will be unable to use the same payment method in the new account.”

3. Threshold account balance paid, account still on hold

Ensure the account is not being used in any other accounts or customer shells that may be causing holds to occur based on the credit card. Resolve any declined payments in other accounts/customer shells using the card using CMAT. 

If unable to locate any other accounts using the card or all payments are current, escalate to Global Support for assistance- the credit hold may be a technical issue.

  • Complete issue-specific details for Category=Global Support Escalations and Type=Tools Technical Issue.
  • Authorized users can create a ticket with Global Support.

4. Invoice customers on credit hold

4.1 Assist the customer using the credit hold dashboard

This functionality is available in all markets except India as of June 23th, 2016. Deployments in India will be coming soon, and this article will be updated with the timeline and confirmed deployment as it is available.

Customers on Credit Hold will receive a UI notification stating, "This account is on hold or credit hold because there was a problem with your payment." Selecting the link within this notification will direct the customer through the Credit Hold Dashboard to resolve the issue per account or payment method. Except for payment failures with Monthly Invoice accounts or scenarios where the user is not the Bill-To Customer, the customer can self-resolve their credit hold issues.

For payment failures, the customer will be presented with the following resolution options:

  • Update this payment method.
  • Choose one of your existing payment methods to replace this.
  • Add a new payment method to replace the current one.

Any of these options will send the customer through the appropriate workflow and will alert the customer of errors throughout the process, will show success of the changes or additions made, will summarize the total balance across all of the accounts that are impacted by the PI issues with the balance due and allow the customer to pay the due balance. If the payment changes are successful and the payment has been submitted, there is a 20-minute SLA for the changes to take effect and the credit hold status to be updated.

Internal note: If customer is showing a status of Payments not received but a balance due of zero, this indicates this account was put on hold by partner (Yahoo) or the customer's account has been written off. In both cases, the account cannot be rehabilitated. To confirm whether a monthly invoice account is current or delinquent, email the customer service alias on the customer-facing PDF invoices (this alias directs to a market-specific accounts receivable team) to determine how much, if any, balance is outstanding (the customer does not need to be included in this communication). If an outstanding balance is causing the credit hold, inform the advertiser of how much is due and guide them to submit payment using the instructions available on their PDF invoices in the Microsoft Advertising UI.

If the advertiser requires a payment plan, direct the advertiser to contact the alias on their invoice for further assistance. Depending on the length and severity of the delinquency, a payment plan may not be possible and the credit hold may be permanent.

When accounts are behind in the payment of their invoices, Microsoft’s billing department notifies the advertisers associated to these accounts. The following account, account number or name, is past due. You can send in the past due amount now. Let me know once you have sent the check or when you plan to send the check, and I can relay this information to our billing department. If you have a copy of the check, send it to me. I will forward this to the billing department, and they may expedite removal of the hold.

4.2 Payment applied to monthly invoice account, account still on hold

  1. Confirm whether a monthly invoice account is current or delinquent.
  2. From the ASD ticket, email the customer service alias on the customer-facing PDF invoices (this alias directs to a market-specific accounts receivable team) to request how much, if any, balance is outstanding (the customer does not need to be included in this communication). 
  3. If payment has been collected and the account is current, the credit hold should be lifted within two business days of receipt of payment.
  4. If the credit hold has not been lifted after payment is received, email the customer service alias on the customer facing PDF invoices to confirm that the hold has been lifted. If the hold has been lifted by the accounts receivable team but persists in the Microsoft Advertising UI, escalate to Global Support for further assistance.

    Messaging: "Can you send me a copy of the check you sent or the transaction number from your online statement? I will check with our billing department to ensure the payment was received and that your account hold will be lifted."
