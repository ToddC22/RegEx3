What is this article about?

This article provides information on how our service centers process orders and guidelines on how to assist customers who dispute a damage claim made by the service center (SVC). Use this article when a customer disputes a tamper or damage claim.

1. Overview

All returned devices go through an inspection process at the SVC to determine how an order is processed. Physical damage to a device is not covered by the standard warranty. If there is any damage to the device, the SVC technicians follow the Unified Order Disposition Policy flows. These flows determine if the order should proceed as intended or if the order should be cancelled and the device returned as is. This article explains how the Returned Unrepaired (RUR) process works and how to assist customers who dispute the findings of the SVC.

1.1 Surface device returned unrepaired with letter

The SVC will send the following letter when a device is returned as is and the order not completed:

"Dear Surface Customer,

We have received the device you returned requesting service. After careful inspection, we found signs of physical or intentional damage to the device that is unrelated to a defect in materials or workmanship. This type of damage is not eligible for warranty service, as it is expressly excluded from the Limited Warranty you accepted when you purchased the device. Physical or intentional damage may include (but is not limited to) the following:

  • Cracked or scratched touchscreen.
  • Significant cosmetic damage, such as scratches, dents, etc.
  • Kickstand hinge damage.
  • Evidence of unauthorized service or attempted disassembly.
  • Tamper label removed, altered, or otherwise disturbed.
  • Modifications to internal components.
  • Serial number removed, mismatched, altered, or otherwise unreadable.

Physical damage due to the causes above voids the warranty for your Surface device. Because of this, your Surface device is not eligible for in-warranty service and it is being returned to you as-is (not serviced).

Although your device is not eligible for warranty service, you may return your device and pay for out-of-warranty service. Or, if you have purchased Accidental Damage Protection (ADP) for your Surface Device, certain damage may be covered, and you may file an ADP claim (*). Please contact Surface Support or go online to www.surface.com/service to request a new service order for either out-of-warranty service or to file an ADP claim.

Sincerely,


The Surface Support Team

www.surface.com/service

(*) Accidental Damage Protection is available only in certain limited countries. Deductibles and conditions may apply."

1.2 Types of damage

Customer induced damage

This is a catch-all label for any direct or indirect damage to a device caused by the customer.

Note: There may be specific device exceptions, see KB 4540877 Procedure: Surface: Customer induced damage exceptions.

Minor physical damage

This can be any type of minor cosmetic damage, including to the screen (such as a small scratch), device casing, or frame. It is often referred to as wear and tear damage. This type of damage is covered by the standard warranty.

Expand to see example of minor physical damage:

[Asset 4339392]

Cracked screen

The most common customer induced damage scenario is a cracked screen. This is typically caused by impact damage (such as a fall off a counter or dropped on the ground). This type of damage is not covered by the standard warranty. If a customer sends in a device with a cracked screen to the SVC on an in warranty order and there are no signs of damage caused while the device was in transit, it will be returned to the customer unrepaired with the warranty voided. In this scenario the device is eligible for a paid out of warranty (OOW) order.

Expand to see example of cracked screen damage:

[Asset 4339393]

Severe physical damage

Any type of major damage to the screen, broken case or large case openings, abnormal damage to connectors or ports, broken kickstand, damage to Wi-Fi cover, or UBC. This type of damage is not covered by the standard warranty. If a customer sends a device with severe physical damage into the service center on an in warranty order and there are no signs of damage caused while the device was in transit, it will be returned to the customer unrepaired with the warranty voided. In this scenario the device is eligible for a paid OOW order.

Liquid damage

Liquid damage of any kind is not covered by the standard warranty. In the event a customer mentions their device has been submerged or immersed in water or other liquid, frontline support advocates are advised to warn the customer if they send the device in on an in warranty order it will be returned to them as is. They can get support with a paid out of warranty offer.

In-transit damage (Customer to service center)

Microsoft provides instructions on how to properly package a device for exchange in the order confirmation email the customer receives after order creation. Failure to follow the packaging instructions may result in the service center receiving a damaged device. In the event a package is received damaged by the service center and the service center confirms the damage occurred in transit, the service center will create a TFS work item and work with both the courier and the Variance team to process the exchange order.

Example 1 – Damaged in Transit, Courier at Fault - In this scenario, the variance advocate will close the open order and create a one-way exchange order to send the customer a replacement device.
[Asset 4052930]

Example 2 – Damaged in Transit due to Insufficient Packaging/Box used - The customer did not use adequate packaging materials and/or a box to ship their device as listed in the terms and conditions of the service request. This is customer induced damage, which is not covered by the warranty.
[Asset 4052934]

Example 3 – Damaged in Transit due to Neglect/Insufficient Packaging/Box used - Microsoft provides instructions on how to properly package a device for exchange, (for example, sturdy box and materials) when the order is created. The customer did not use adequate packaging materials to ship their device as listed in the terms and conditions of the service request.
[Asset 4052945]

Note: If the customer claims the device was packaged/boxed by the courier prior to shipping, request the customer provide a receipt or other proof showing the courier is at fault. If able to provide proof, an exception should be made for the customer.

In-transit damage (from the service center to the customer) – In the event the customer receives a device which has been damaged in transit from the service center, see section 4 for handling transit claims.

Note: Per Taiwan law, Civil Code; Article 635; The carrier is liable for loss or damage due to apparent defects in packing, if they have accepted the goods for transportation without reservation. Once the courier/carrier accepts a package, any in transit damage is no longer the fault of the customer. If the customer quotes this law, contact leadership for further guidance.

2. Customer disputes tamper or damage claim (Standard exchange)

  1. Obtain the following information from the customer:
  • Standard exchange order number
  • Full name
  • Return address
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Inbound tracking number
  1. Ask the customer if they received the Surface Returned Unrepaired letter in the same package their device was returned in:

Note: If they didn’t receive the letter and the box the device was received in is damaged, follow the guidance provided by section 4.

  1. Navigate to TFS and search for a TFS work item associated with the customer or the order.
  • If there is a TFS Work Item, review it to determine the reason why the customer's device was returned:
  1. If you agree with the findings of the service center, advise the customer the damage to the device is not covered by the warranty so the device was returned as is. Provide the customer with OOW service options if available. Proceed with case closure noting why the device was returned and the customer was provided the appropriate messaging based on policy.
  2. If you disagree with the findings of the service center, proceed to step 4.
  • If there is no TFS Work Item, create a TFS Work Item asking the SCV to provide information/documentation on the order. Specifically, ask for photos of the device.
  1. If you agree with the findings of the service center, advise the customer the damage to the device is not covered by the warranty so the device was returned as is. Provide the customer with OOW service options if available. Proceed with case closure noting why the device was returned and the customer was provided the appropriate messaging based on policy.
  2. If you disagree with the findings of the service center, proceed to step 4.
  1. Review the customer’s service order history to see if they have received an exception for any previous order. This may require a deep dive into the customer’s contact history with support. Any previous contact with Surface Support, up to and including Escalation Support.
  • If the customer has never received an exception in the past, proceed to step 5.
  • If the customer has received an exception in the past, advise the customer they have been provided an Out of Policy Exception in the past and another cannot be granted. Educate the customer on the reason why and when the exception was granted. Proceed with case closure noting why the exception was denied.
  1. In your Service Order Management (SOM) tool, create an In-Warranty Flag Bypass (Standard exchange) order using the serial number of the damaged device as the ticketed device.
  2. Advise the customer they will need to send in the non-functioning device first. Once it’s received, the replacement device will ship from our fulfillment center.
  3. Once the customer has confirmed receipt of the replacement device move toward case closure.
  • If the customer reports that the device they received from the In-Warranty Flag Bypass (Standard exchange) order is also damaged in some manner, follow the Escalation Guidelines outlined by KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios.

3. Customer disputes tamper or damage claim (Advanced exchange)

Note: We do not send the Surface Returned Unrepaired letter in the event the service center receives a damaged ticketed device on an advance exchange order. Per the terms and conditions of the advance exchange service request, if we do not receive the original device back or the device the customer returns to us is not covered under the terms of the warranty, or has indications of tampering or physical damage, the customer’s credit card will be charged a replacement and handling fee equivalent to either the paid out of warranty exchange price or the full retail value of the customer’s Surface device (plus applicable taxes). For commercial customers, the customer/company will receive an invoice for the order with the total amount owed.

  1. Obtain the following information from the customer:
  • Advance exchange order number
  • Full name
  • Return address
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Inbound tracking number
  1. Navigate to TFS and search for a TFS Work item associated with the customer or the order.
  • If there is a TFS Work item, review it to determine the reason why the customer's device was returned.
    1. If you agree with the findings of the service center, advise the customer the device was received by the service center with damage not covered by the OEM Warranty thus the customer was charged/invoiced for the order based on the severity of the damage. Proceed with case closure noting why the device was returned and that the customer has been provided with the appropriate messaging based on policy.
    2. If you disagree with the findings of the service center, proceed to step 3.
  • If there is no TFS Work item associated with the customer or the order; create a TFS Work item asking the SCV to provide information/documentation on the order. Specifically, ask for photos of the device.
    1. If you agree with the findings of the service center, advise the customer the device was received by the Service Center with damage not covered by the OEM Warranty thus the customer was charged/invoiced for the order based on the severity of the damage. Proceed with case closure noting why the device was returned and that the customer has been provided with the appropriate messaging based on policy.
    2. If you disagree with the findings of the service center, proceed to step 3.
  1. Review the customer’s service order history to see if they have received an exception for any previous order. This may require a deep dive into the customer’s contact history with support. Any previous contact with Surface Support, up to and including Escalation Support.
  • If the customer has never received an exception in the past, proceed to step 4.
  • If the customer has received an exception in the past, advise the customer they have been provided an Out of Policy Exception in the past and another cannot be granted. Educate the customer on the reason why and when the exception was granted. Proceed with case closure noting why the exception was denied.
  1. Follow the Escalations Guidelines outlined by KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios to escalate the exception request. See section 7.

4. Handling damaged in transit claims – Outbound to customer

  1. Obtain pictures from the customer that clearly show the damages to the box and or device while in transit from the service center.
  2. Follow the process specific to the region the customer resides in/service center who supports that region:
  • AOC
    1. If the courier was Fed Ex, follow the process outlined by KB 4054957 Procedure: FedEx OneCall to file a damaged in transit claim. Once Fed Ex provides the claim number, proceed to step 3.
    2. If the courier was UPS, follow the process outlined by KB 4054958 Procedure: Devices: UPS trace & intercept handling to file a damaged in transit claim. Once UPS provides the claim number, proceed to step 3.
  • APOC
    1. Create a TFS Work Item actioned to the appropriate service center to report the damaged in transit claim and request investigation with the courier. Include the photos of the device provided by the customer. Once the SCV replies with the results of the investigation and provide the claim number, proceed to step 3.
  • EOC
    1. Create a TFS Work Item actioned to the appropriate service center to report the damaged in transit claim and request investigation with the courier. Include the photos of the device provided by the customer. Once the SCV replies with the results of the investigation and provide the claim number, proceed to step 3.
  1. In your SOM tool, review the warranty status of the device the customer received.
  • If the device is in warranty, select Physical Damage to Case of Kickstand as the Problem Type, and create a Damaged on Delivery (Standard exchange) order using the serial number of the damaged device as the ticketed device.
  • If the device is out of warranty, use the Warranty Update Tool to extend the warranty by 30 days. Once the device is back in warranty, select Physical Damage to Case of Kickstand as the Problem Type, and create a Damaged on Delivery (Standard Exchange) order using the serial number of the damaged device as the ticketed device.

Note: Do not forget to include case notes explaining why the 30-day extension was applied.

  1. Advise the customer they will need to send in the non-functioning device first. Once it’s received, the replacement device will ship from our fulfillment center.
  2. Once the customer has confirmed receipt of the replacement device, proceed towards case closure.
  • If the customer reports that the device they received from the damaged on delivery (Standard exchange) order is also damaged in some manner, follow the Escalation Guidelines outlined by KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios.

5. Cosmetic inspection of a Surface Studio device and serial number verification

The service technician will perform a 10 to 15 second inspection of the Surface Studio device to verify no signs of customer induced damage (CID) or tamper. The service technician will verify the following:

  • Screen is not cracked or damaged in any way.
  • Hinge assembly on screen works normally and no shows no signs of damage.
  • No signs of drop/fall damage (For example, dents to device body or cracks in screen).
  • No signs of water intrusion or spillage on the device itself.
  • If the Surface Studio device has any CID present, the service technician must perform the following steps:
  1. Document each instance of CID found on the device in case notes.
  2. Take photos of each instance of CID and send the photos to SurfaceStudioEsc@microsoft.com. The email header must adhere to the following format: Customer Reference Number – (CID).
  3. Alert the customer to any damage found and determine if the customer’s device is covered by Microsoft Complete with Accidental Damage Protection (ADP).
  • If the device is not covered by an ADP warranty, any replacement device would need to be ordered at cost (if hardware replacement is required).
  • If device is covered by an ADP warranty, device replacement is covered under warranty.
  1. Verify the serial number matches the ticketed device on the order. The service technician can locate the serial number two ways depending on the working state of the device.
  • Device powers on, run the Quick Check Tool to determine serial number.
  • Alternatively, the Surface app will display the serial number (will require photos).
  • The device does not power on or does not boot to Windows, locate the serial number from the base of the Surface Studio.

6. Surface Studio tamper signs and on-site technician visits

Unisys on site techs will not open a Surface Studio during a tech visit. The tech will perform troubleshooting, swap on site, or pick up the device and send the device to the service center for exchange.

7. Escalations

Below are the valid escalations scenarios linked to Customer Disputes Tamper or Damage Claim as outlined by KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios.

  • In-Policy Refund Request (Repair or Fulfillment Order) > Advanced Exchange Hold or Charge Refund
  • In-Policy Refund Request (Repair or Fulfillment Order) > OOW Paid Replacement Refund
  • Problem with Product Delivered > Customer Disputes Tamper or Damage Flags
  • Problem with Product Delivered > Damaged (Severe or Cosmetic) or Non-Functional Device Received
  • Problem with Product Delivered > Damaged in Transit
