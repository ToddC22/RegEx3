ASMS – CSS Pre-Sales and Digital Sales Engagement

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG. 

Overview

This TSG provides guidance on when and how to engage the CSS Pre-Sales and the Digital Sales teams (via the GROW lead generation process).

Policy

The CSS Pre-Sales and the Digital Sales teams support the following for Broad Commercial customers (via the GROW lead generation process):

  • Potential project advice
    • Submit a GROW lead when a customer requires clarity on building out and architecting an Azure solution.
       
  • How to purchase Azure
    • Submit a GROW lead when a customer requires guidance on Azure pricing models outside of Pay-as-you-Go.
       
  • Sales inquiries for Cloud Solution Areas
    • Examples: Dynamics, Microsoft 365 & Security, Power Platform, and Azure (including Azure Information Protection (AIP), Advanced Threat Protection (ATP), Azure Active Directory (AAD)).
       
  • Projected cost questions
    • Submit a GROW lead when a customer needs to identify resource requirements in order to estimate cost.
       
  • Purchaseing Azure with a new Enterprise Agreement (EA) or Microsoft Cloud Agreement (MCA) 
    • These interests may incur additional time (extended SLA) as they need to be routed from Sales to the Customer Success Manager (CSM). CSMs handle all licensing/MCA customer requests.

The following scenarios are not supported by CSS Pre-sales or the Digital Sales teams: 

  • Billing Support
    • Examples: Account is down, billing issues, expired cc, wrong charges.
    • This is part of ASMS R&R as highlighted in TSG 3143315.  
       
  • Technical Support
    • Examples: Lost passwords, server issue, service interruptions.
    • This is to be routed to the respective Technical Support teams highlighted in TSG 3143315.
       
  • Compliance Requests
    • Examples: HIPPA, BAA Requests, SOC 1 and 2, PCI 1 and 2 NDA, GFS, Proofpoint, Intune, IS0-2700, ICSMS certificates, audit certification, Azure Supplier Surveys, etc. 
    • Most of these requests are now self-service or have a different process in which ASMS has no insight into. In these instances, you should refer the customer to the Microsoft Trust Center or to work with their respective Partner for any further survey/compliance needs. 
       
  • Services / Offerings 
    • If the customer is interested in service related support offerings, refer the customer to the following self help resources:
      • Azure specific support offerings, including Pro Direct: https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/support/plans/
      • Enterprise Services, including Unified and Premier: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/msservices/support
  • Sales inquiries for Premier and Unified (Managed) customers
    • Refer the customer to contact their Microsoft Incidnet Manager (IM+) or Customer Success Account Manager (CSAMs, formally known as TAMs).
  • Education (EDU) Inquires
    • For managed EDU customers, suggest that they reach out to their IM+ or CSAM. 
      • As the support engineer you can also reach out to the IM+ or CSAM on the customer's behalf, to alert them of the customer's inquiry. 
    • For unmanaged EDU customers, direct them to Partner Center for more information and assistiance: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/solution-providers/home. 

Support Scenarios

Creating a GROW Lead

If you receive a case where the customer has pre-sales related questions, follow the steps below to create a sales lead:

  1. Submit an Internal Referral Form to the Pre-Sales/Sales team at http://www.aka.ms/GROW.
  2. All fields are required, but note the following about these specific fields:
    • In the Submitter's functional group field, select Support from the dropdown.
    • In the Job Role field enter either the customer's role at his/her company (Ex: Systems Admin) or their role as it relates to Azure (Ex: Account Admin, Service Admin).
    • In the Questions/Comments field be sure to include a brief description of the customer’s needs/ask.

Note: This form can only be completed by Support and is not to be shared directly with the customer. Additional information about the form can be found at CSS Guidance for Advise Me Grow.

  1. Once you have submitted the form, inform the customer that a Solution Specialist will contact them within a week.
    • If it has been longer than a week and the customer states that they have not received further communication, you can escalate by emailing GROW@microsoft.com to have the status checked. 
    • This escalation is for internal use only.
  2. If there are no further actions required of ASMS, proceed with archiving the request, per the normal process.

Note: The Advise Me GROW team reviews all incoming submissions bi-weekly and provides Engineers with updates on the lead progress. These types of communications are only sent to FTEs and are out of scope for our v- alias engineers. More information about the Grow program can be found at http://www.aka.ms/AMG.

Pre-sales for System Integrator (SI) Partners

If a System Integrator (SI) wants technical support for Pre-Sales activities, contact the Partner Enterprise Architect Team (PEAT) at peateam@microsoft.com.

  • The SI must have a “managed partner” status at Microsoft (i.e. there should be a Partner Account Manager assigned to the Partner).
  • Examples of SIs would be Global (Infosys, Accenture), and National (Aditi, Neusdesic).
  • There is a minimum deal size and criteria of $25-30K Annually of new Windows Azure revenue, IaaS and PaaS based solutions.
     

Requests for RDS CAL SAL (licenses):

Customers who contact Azure support regarding the purchase of Windows licenses should be redirected to contact the proper support team, using the phone number 1-877-696-7786 (877MYMSSTORE).

You can also provide the customer with the following purchase links:

  • Device Client Access License
  • User Client Access License

Note: ASMS has no direct path to help the user purchase these licenses.
 

Global Demand Center (Sales Bot)

Customers may be contacted by Global Demand Center’s (GDC) Bot Platform program called Kristin Meyer . The goal of this program/platform is to provide higher quality leads to sales, by initiating a bot-lead conversation over email and determining the customer's initial level of interest through a set of AI-based algorithms.

Note: If you receive a case regarding a customer who was recently contacted, note that the bot engages customers who have recently consumed marketing content (and have not recently been contacted by the bot), which is likely why your customer received an email.

If you are replying to an email thread or creating meeting requests, you should remove “Kristin” from the communication string and/or invite, given its bot-like nature.

To learn more about how to engage customers, leverage the Wiki.
If you have any other questions regarding the Bot, reach out to your TA/PTA.
