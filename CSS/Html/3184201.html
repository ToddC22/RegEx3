1. Overview

Search advertising isn’t just about finding the right search queries, it’s about finding the right people: your target audience. Audience targeting can increase the chance that the customers you’re looking for see your ad. With features that incorporate your own data, and those that take advantage of powerful Microsoft data, you can pinpoint your target audience.

With a full suite of targeting features, you can reach potential customers who meet the criteria you specify, exposing your ad to customers who are interested in your products or services and are more likely to convert. Learn about all our audience targeting options in this article.

Note: See What is audience targeting? for more information.

2. Remarketing

Remarketing in Paid Search lets you improve your return on investment by optimizing your campaigns for specific audiences.

When people look for products and services online, they rarely convert on their first visit. Instead, people visit a site, then they check out competitors, they price compare, and sometimes they just get distracted (in no specific order). About 96% of visitors leave websites without converting. For first time visitors, an average website has a bounce rate of over 60% and a shopping cart abandonment rate of over 70%. That`s where remarketing comes in. With remarketing advertisers can:

  • Appeal to all or a subset of visitors who did not convert and stay top of mind.
  • Exclude, upsell, or cross-sell to existing customers.
  • Drive higher ROI (return on investment).

Steps to set up remarketing:

  1. Add the UET tag to your website.
  2. Create a remarketing list (also called "Audience List').
  3. Associate remarketing list with campaigns/ad groups.
  4. Optimize campaigns/ad groups for remarketing:
    • Set a specific bid amount.
    • Customize ads to your specific audience.
    • Expand keywords for a specific audience.

Note: Remarketing lists are created automatically, based on the total number of customers who visited the advertiser’s website within the last 30 days, and who are also active on the Microsoft Advertising Network or Microsoft audience network (this is in line with Google parity).

For more information, see Remarketing: Target people who have visited your site before or FAQ: Remarketing.

3. Campaign and ad group level associations

With campaign-level associations for audience targeting, you can associate audience lists and bid boosts at the campaign level.

  • Set up and manage easily
    It’s quick and easy to set up and manage your audience associations at the campaign level.
  • Save time
    Associate audiences across your entire campaign with one click, rather than associating individually for each ad group within a campaign.
  • Expand your audience targeting coverage
    Adding audiences at the campaign level makes it faster and easier to extend your audience targeting across all your ad groups within a campaign.

3.1 What are associations?

Associations link your audience targeting list with your campaign or ad group. You can either show the ad to the audience or exclude the audience from seeing your ad.

Which audience targeting features can you associate to your campaigns?

  • In-Market Audiences
  • Remarketing
  • Custom Audiences
  • Product Audiences
  • Similar Audiences

For more information, see Associate audiences with ad groups or campaigns.

3.2 Ad group vs. campaign level associations

Before you start using audience targeting associations, it’s important to determine which association type is best for your campaigns.

When to use ad group-level associations:

  • If you’re using different audiences and bids for various ad groups in the same campaign, you can use ad group-level associations to manage and optimize individually.
  • If your account is segmented at the ad group level or you want to keep precise control (such as, bid modifiers), you should use ad group associations. For example, you want to test a particular audience first before applying it to all other ad groups.

When to use campaign-level associations:

  • If you’re using the same audience and bid for each of your ad groups in your campaign, we recommend campaign-level associations. They’re easy to set up, let you quickly associate to all your ad groups in one click at the campaign level, and eliminate the hassle of creating a new association each time you build a new ad group.

Important notice for customers with existing ad group-level associations: Before adding campaign-level associations, you must remove all existing ad group-level associations. Your Microsoft Advertising account won’t allow you to proceed unless there are no ad group-level associations (this doesn’t apply to exclusions). The same is true for customers with existing campaign-level associations. Before adding ad group-level associations, you must remove all existing campaign-level associations.

3.3 Exclusions

For exclusions, audiences can be excluded from the campaign at both the campaign level and ad group level. If an ad group has exclusions at both levels, ad group exclusions will override campaign-level exclusions.

During the ad delivery time, we’ll honor:

  1. Exclusion over targeting associations.
  2. Ad group level over campaign level for exclusions.

Example:

Let’s say list A is excluded at the campaign level (campaign 1) and list B targets (target and bid) at the ad group level (ad group 1 which belongs to campaign 1).

  • For ad group 1, only users in “list B” but not in “list A” can see the ad. A user present in both lists will be excluded.
  • For all other ad groups in campaign 1, only users in list A will be excluded and everyone else can see the ad.

Creating exclusions:

  1. From the Campaigns page, select the Audiences tab (or from the main menu on the left, select All campaigns and then Audiences).
  2. Select Create association.
  3. In the drop-down menu, select either Campaign or Ad group.
  4. Select the appropriate ad group or campaign.
  5. Select Add exclusions.
  6. In the Select audience drop-down menu, select the appropriate audience type.
  7. Select the appropriate audience.
  8. Select Save.

3.4 Rules of audience association

Note:

  • Audience targeting associations cannot be applied at both the campaign and ad group level at the same time. Advertisers will need to remove targeting associations at one level before switching to the other.

4. In-market audiences

This feature is available in the US, CA, UK, AU, DE, and FR markets. 

Microsoft Advertising uses search-user behavior and intent to pre-define potential customers into In-market audience lists. Advertisers in Microsoft Advertising can associate these In-market audience lists to their ad groups, via the Audience tab in the UI, allowing them to target and modify bids for their desired audience.

The benefits of this are:

  • Increased conversions (Customers are engaged and more likely to convert).
  • Improved bids/targeting.
  • Simple set up (No UET tag is required).

Once set up, advertisers can view their In-market audience targeting and exclusions via the Audiences tab. Advertisers can also apply In-market audience lists in bulk via the Ad Groups tab.

Note: Advertisers from other parts of the world can be enabled for in-market audience functionality, but only if they are targeting markets in which the feature is generally available.

For more information, see In-market audiences: Reach people who are ready to buy.

Best practices for setup

If customers ask for best-practice guidance in setting up in-market audiences, note that Microsoft recommends:

  • Select the in-market segment that closely matches your business vertical.
  • Create associations with existing ad groups, as new ad groups and ads will take time to gain quality score and delivery.
  • Use bid-only targeting with a positive bid boost.
  • Monitor performance over time and optimise as needed. Note: For In-market audiences, the best option to view in-market audience performance is via the Audience segmentation feature in the Campaign/Ad group/Keyword/Ad tabs in UI.
  • Target settings are applicable at ad group level, meaning that an ad group can only have either Bid only or Target & bid association for all audiences. Therefore, be cautious when creating new associations as it may overwrite the existing target settings.
  • If an ad group is associated with multiple in-market segments without a distinguishing bid boost, then the performance may be attributed to any of the in-market segments at random, which will make it hard to optimise in future.

For detailed steps on how to create/manage in-market audience lists, see In-market audiences: Reach people who are ready to buy.

5. Custom audiences 

This feature is available globally; however, advertisers in the EU cannot be enabled or targeted.

Note: Advertisers in the EU who wish to target non-EU markets using custom audiences can be enabled; escalate to Global Support to request this.

A custom audience is a type of remarketing list that is generated by using an advertiser’s own customer data to create richer user segments based on first-party data. (Note: Data must come from the advertiser’s own website. We do not allow third-party provider data that has been bought/leased.)

Custom audiences can be used in conjunction with remarketing lists, usually through a Data Management Provider (DMP). When the DMP connects to our custom audience feature, custom audiences can be imported into Microsoft Advertising for Search/MSAN.

Different DMP providers will have different steps to enable integrations with their audience manager. Advertisers will need to work with them to set up integration successfully once opted into the pilot. (Support for DMP setup is out of scope for CSS.)

We currently support the following DMPs. (Select the hyperlink to access customer-setup guide, which can be shared to customers.)

  • Adobe Audience Manager (AAM)
  • Oracle BlueKai
  • LiveRamp

Note: Advertisers will need to provide their DMP with the Microsoft Advertising customer ID. For LiveRamp, they will also need to provide the user ID, which must be either "SuperAdmin" or "StandardUser" to be valid.

Once set up, the custom audience list will be available in the Microsoft Advertising UI via Shared Library > Audiences.

Note:

  • You can use custom audiences along with remarketing, with no UET required.
  • Custom audience lists can be used with all campaign types (for example, shopping, search, or MSAN).
  • It can take up to 48 hours for new custom audience lists to be available in Microsoft Advertising.
  • Custom audiences are applied at the CID level. They cannot be applied to a single account.
  • DMP setup is out of scope for CSS. Advertisers will need to work with their DMP’s support team.

For more information, see Custom audiences: Create richer user segments with your own data.

Q: Why is the performance data in Campaign tab and Audience tab different?

A: Audience tab is not on Fast BI so there is a two to four-hour lag. Best practice is to compare the two tabs for yesterday or earlier.

Q: Why is List size in Bing greater than List size in DMP?

A: It’s expected for the segment size to be different. Our audience size is dependent on the ID sync from the advertiser site enabled by the DMP pixel. When a user visits the website an ID sync happens. If the advertiser creates an Audience on the DMP and sees a certain size on date X, and enables sharing with Bing on date X, that size may be larger than what gets sent to Bing as the sync with Bing is triggered off a user visiting that site after date X.

Q: What's Addressable Audience Number?

A: A count of overlap of devices that have realized either a rule-based trait or an onboarded trait during the look-back window and devices that we have an ID sync with the chosen destination regardless of the time of syncs.

Q: What's Match rate?

A: This is the share of users on a custom audience that are also active on Bing. Expected to be 30-35%.

Q: Can Audience Id for Custom Audience be passed via URL Tracking parameter?

A: Yes, this is supported for audience targeting. The specific URL parameter to be added to destination URLs is called {TargetId}. Once this is added, we will pass the attributed audience id of the user in the URL. For more information, see What tracking or URL parameters can I use?.

6. Similar audiences (pilot)

This pilot requires an existing remarketing/audience list, with a minimum of 1000 targeted customers.

By choosing to target similar audiences Microsoft Advertising will target search users whose behavior/attributes are like those being used by the existing remarketing/audience lists.

Setup is done in the same way as standard remarketing/audience lists.

Note: Customers in existing lists will not be duplicated in the similar audience targeting lists. Deleting/editing an existing remarketing list will delete/update similar audiences as well.

For more information, see Similar audiences: Reach a wider qualified set of customers.

7. Dynamic remarketing

What is dynamic remarketing?

Dynamic remarketing lists allow advertisers to add their website visitors to a list based on the products they view. This allows Bing to show targeted and personalized ads to these users when they interact with Bing and Yahoo (for search network) and other Microsoft web properties, like MSN, Outlook, or Edge browser (for audience network).

For example, let’s say a customer adds a handbag to their shopping cart but abandons the purchase. A week later, they’re on a Microsoft web property (audience network) or they perform a search for handbags. The advertiser can show their ad for the same handbag to the customer again.

Dynamic remarketing can be used in one of two ways:

  1. For search campaigns, via Bing Shopping.
  2. For audience campaigns on MSAN (not everyone has audience campaigns yet).

We currently offer five types of dynamic remarketing lists, so you don’t need to create a remarketing list for each individual product:

  • General Visitors
  • Product Searchers
  • Product Viewers
  • Shopping Cart Abandoners
  • Past Buyers

See Dynamic remarketing lists: Remarketing for products for more information.

7.5 Setting up dynamic remarketing

A typical setup will involve the following steps:

  1. Create a product store in Bing Merchant Center.
  2. For the audience network, create an audience campaign with your product store. For the search network, create a shopping campaign with your product store.
  3. Create and associate your dynamic remarketing list with the shopping campaign.
  4. Update your UET tag with an additional piece of code to include your page type and product ID.
  5. Ensure the  dynamic remarketing list is populated with at least 300 users for MSAN and search (it won’t be eligible for delivery until this limit is met).

The UI workflow to create a dynamic remarketing list follows the same path in the UI as creating a remarketing list. See the “How do I create a dynamic remarketing list?” section of the help article for details.

2. 'ecomm_prodid': 'REPLACE_WITH_PRODUCT_ID'

This parameter is the core of the dynamic remarketing feature. ProductId is the SKU of products on the customer's website. To serve product ads to a user in the  dynamic remarketing list, it is required that ProductId matches an approved ItemId or the ItemGroupId in the Product Catalog. The expectation is that the customer will have functions on their webpage script to capture the product ID and then pass it to the UET code.


This parameter can accept:

a) A single productid: The value of ecomm_prodid must be within 1 to 50 characters. Hyphen and alphanumeric characters are allowed.

Sample script:
<script>
window.uetq = window.uetq || [];
window.uetq.push('event', 'REPLACE_WITH_EVENT_NAME', {'ecomm_prodid': 'get(prodid)', 'ecomm_pagetype': 'REPLACE_WITH_PAGE_TYPE'});
</script>

b) An array of product Ids: The value of individual productid must be within 1 to 50 characters. Hyphen and alphanumeric characters are allowed. The array size will be limited by the URL length of bat.js which is ~4K. If a page has hundreds of product ids, then in general we recommend passing the top 10 products in the array.

Sample script:
<script>
window.uetq = window.uetq || [];
window.uetq.push('event','REPLACE_WITH_EVENT_NAME', {'ecomm_prodid': ['PRODUCT_ID1', 'PRODUCT_ID2'],'ecomm_pagetype': 'REPLACE_WITH_PAGE_TYPE'});
</script>

7.10 Dynamic remarketing statuses

Possible statuses and actions needed to resolve:

  • Ready: No issues – dynamic remarketing is working fine.
  • Missing tag on the site: Microsoft Advertising hasn't received any user activity data from the UET tag on your website. It can take up to 24 hours for Microsoft Advertising to verify a new tag. After that point, if you see this status, you either have not added the UET tag tracking code to your website or there is an issue with the setup that you need to fix.
  • No activity in the past 24 hours: Microsoft Advertising hasn't received any user activity data from the UET tag in the last 24 hours. Make sure that the UET tag tracking code is still on your website.
  • Missing custom events on the tag: Microsoft Advertising hasn't received any custom event data required for this audience. Make sure that you have updated your UET tag tracking code correctly to report back custom event parameters.
  • Building the audience: It takes 24 hours for an audience to be built after you create a new audience or update an existing audience.
  • Audience too small: The number of people in this audience doesn't meet the minimum number of users required for this audience to be eligible to serve on either the search or audience network. Make sure your list definition is accurate and not too narrow.
  • Ready for search campaigns, but too small for audience campaigns: This audience is eligible to serve on the search network but not on the Microsoft audience network.
  • Recalculating audience size: You have edited your audience, and Microsoft Advertising is in the process of determining its size. This process can take 24 hours.
  • Possible UET tag issue: There are zero people in your dynamic remarketing, so it's likely that there is a problem with your UET tag. We recommend you make sure that:
  • You have modified your UET tag on all pages of your website for this dynamic remarketing.
  • Your modified UET tag refers to the correct page type and product ID.
  • The product ID referred to in your UET tag matches an ID in your Microsoft Merchant Center store.

10. Sharing remarketing lists

Once a Customer Hierarchy is built, remarketing lists can be shared across different accounts and manager accounts within the same hierarchy using the Shared Library functionality.

Sharing can be managed by the remarketing list owner or the owner’s manager Super Admin. Sharing permissions will vary based on the scope of the Super Admin’s user access.

Once shared at the Manager Account level, audiences which are associated with a UET tag can be shared at the individual Ad Account level.

Important: Sharing a remarketing list is dependent upon the associated UET tag being shared. If a UET tag is not shared, then it isn’t possible to use a remarketing list associated with that UET tag.

For more information about Customer Hierarchy or Shared Library, see Hierarchies in Microsoft Advertising. The Shared Library content is at the very bottom of the page.

10.1 Sharing an existing remarketing list

There are two places where you can manage your remarketing lists:

The Used By column displays the accounts that are currently using a shared list. 12. Targeting setting

The targeting setting determines if a campaign/ad group must serve ads only to users in the associated remarketing lists (Target and Bid option) or to all users (Bid only option). It is not logically possible for a campaign/ad group to be associated with one remarketing list using the Target and Bid targeting setting and another using the Bid only setting. Hence, the system enforces the same targeting setting across all the associations for a given campaigns/ad groups. While the status and bid adjustments of associations can be edited in bulk by selecting associations and selecting Edit on the action bar in the Audiences tab, targeting setting cannot. The targeting setting can only be edited one campaign/ad group at a time from the Audiences tab, by selecting Add association and then selecting the relevant campaign/ad group. The campaign’s/ad group’s existing mapping and targeting setting is retrieved by Microsoft Advertising, advertisers can edit the targeting setting at that time. The updated targeting setting is applied to all the associations of that campaign/ad group.

13. Terminology quick reference

  • Tag - Universal Event Tracking (UET) tag that is placed on the advertiser website and enables Microsoft Advertising to identify user actions on the advertiser site. Tags are currently defined at the customer level and shared across accounts.
  • Goal - A way for advertisers to define what constitutes a conversion. Supports destination URL, pages/visit, duration on site and custom event conversions. Goals (like tags) are currently defined at the customer level and shared across accounts.
  • Referrer URL - When a user clicks on a link in a web page, the user's browser moves to the specified link. When the browser requests the new page, it sends along the URL of the previous page (along with many other details). This sent-along URL is called a referrer URL.
  • Custom event - UET allows advertisers to customize the UET tag to report more granular events taken by visitors on the advertiser website. Custom events can be used to define either conversion goals or remarketing lists.
  • Audience - A group comprised of users who visited or performed specific actions on the advertiser website.
  • Remarketing list (Also called ‘Audience List’) - The advertiser website needs to include UET Tags for remarketing to work. Remarketing lists do not require conversion goals to be set up. Remarketing lists will be defined at the customer level and shared across accounts. An advertiser can define up to a maximum of 1,000 remarketing lists at GA (limit is 100 at pilot). The list is generated based on the number of active users who acted on the advertiser’s website within the last 30 days.
  • List size - The number of users that are part of remarketing list. As users visit advertiser sites and take actions, the Microsoft Advertising back-end pipeline places the users into various segments. Note that at GA the list size (also referred to as cookie pool internally) must be at least 1,000 for the ad delivery engine to apply bid adjustment settings.
  • Membership duration - The look-back window for user activity on the site for the purpose of putting users into remarketing lists.
  • Audience association - This refers to the act of associating campaigns/ad groups and audience targeting. Note that this is a many-to-many relationship: one campaign/ad group can be associated with multiple remarketing lists and vice versa.
  • Bid adjustment - Applied at audience<->campaign/ad group association level, this refers to the % increase or decrease in the bid that advertisers want to make for the combination. Advertisers can increase the bid by up to 900% and decrease by up to 90%. A maximum of 100,000 associations can be made for a given account.
