Overview 

This TSG provides a compilation of all the different teams and their engagement paths.

Policy 

  • If you are engaging other teams within the company, use the CSS Known "Hot Spots" site to see if there are any known cross-team delays.
    • Use this to help set expectations at a high level of how long your customer might have to wait before being contacted. 
    • Share only estimates of time with your customer; they do not need to know the specifics of the delays. 
  • SR Wait State: When you engage a partner team for collaboration on a case, be sure to set the case's SR Wait State status appropriately in Service Desk Case.
  • In scenarios where two or more partner teams are pushing back on ASMS stating the task is not theirs to solve, engage your technical advisor (TA). Your TA will initiate communications with all concerned partner teams on the same email or conference call to move the issue forward.
  • When engaging other teams to investigate or resolve issues, be sure to update your customer using verbiage that demonstrates ownership on your part.

    For example: “I am working with our Accounting Team to research the issue…” instead of, “Our Accounting Team is working on the issue…”
  • There are certain internal team names and acronyms that should not be referenced when updating customers. In these instances, use the general business function as the name so that customers can easily understand. Or simply say, "I am working/waiting to hear back from my Engineering Team or my Operations Team."
  • Please eliminate use of the term “backend team” from all customer-facing communications.
  • If you are unsure about which terms to use, contact your TA for guidance.
    • ACE-IPO (formerly MIST, AEO): Azure Commerce Escalations for Internally Paid Offers
    • ASE: Azure Service Engineering
    • Capacity Management: Azure Capacity
    • CXP (formerly WASU): Azure Customer Experience Team

    • GFS: Global Financial Services
    • HIT (formerly ART): Human Intelligence Team

    • PayOps Execution (formerly CBO): Accounting

    • WFM: Workforce Management

Technical Team Engagement

If you cannot find the appropriate team to help resolve the customer’s issue, engage your TA, partner technical advisor (PTA), or team lead (TL).

Do not advise customers to directly contact technical teams if you encounter technical issues after the main problem has been resolved. Adhere to the engagement processes outlined in this TSG.

Azure Commerce Escalations for Internally Paid Offers (ACE-IPO, formerly MIST, EAO)

Depending on the scenario, you can either send a collaboration task to the correct queue, or if it's an issue that requires TA support, work with your TA.

ACE-IPO may take over ownership of the case depending on the scenario and customer type.

Note: This team is not tied to the Enterprise Agreement (EA) team.

Reserved Instance (RI) support is not monitored by this team. RI support is tied to the EA team.

ACE-IPO has five (non-EA) Service Desk Case queues:

  • MSaaS Azure Internal Offer (MSFT only) – Task or SRs that relate to internal accounts or subscriptions

  • MSaaS Azure Sponsorship Offer – Task or SRs that relate to 143p or 36P (MPN, BizSpark Plus, Philanthropies, Academic, etc.)

  • MSaaS Azure Web Direct Escalations – Credit or refund approvals, that are not related to 15P, 36P, or 143P.

  • Azure for Startups (BizSpark, MS4SU, MfS) - Task or SRs related to Azure for Startups.

  • Azure in Open Escalations - Task or SRs related to AIO escalations.

Types of cases that can be escalated:

Note: Not all issues will fit into these categories. Use your best judgement when using this alias for escalations:

  • Threats (i.e. escalating to Scott Guthrie, go to social media, etc.)

  • Any escalation to a GM and above (i.e. Jon Acone, Charlotte Yarkoni, Kim Laursen and her leadership team, etc.)

  • Other high visibility accounts forwarded from CXP

  • Case where production services are impacted (i.e. customer’s sub will be cancelled, etc.) and cannot be resolved by TAs without escalation

Types of cases that should not use this alias:

  • Cases that can be handled quickly and can wait for the weekday:

  • Internal subscription is cancelled erroneously, and production services are down. In this case, just re-enable the sub and send AIO a task with the necessary information. Outstanding tasks sitting with AIO should be escalated to a TA whom will provide guidance on next steps, as TA's may know the “health” of the Internal queue based on weekly engagements.

  • Sponsorship ends on a weekend and the customer doesn’t want to get charged or cancelled on the weekend. Use the on-call for incurring charges.

SLA and workhours: There is no on-call team, and the queue is only managed between 8am-5pm PST (Redmond) Monday – Friday

Note: Internal and Sponsorship escalation teams do not adhere to SLAs
  • For Cyber Grants or Philanthropy activations, Microsoft requires engagement by email to the Philanthropies team.

  • If you are unsure what the customer is requesting, engage your TA first.

  • Engage your TA and they can escalate to Azure Sponsorship Offer via a PTA.

For a faster response time, ensure you include the following information in the TA task:

  • Task number to appropriate ABD queue on the issue

  • Customer information:

  • SubGUID(s)

  • Account Owner ID

  • Commerce Account ID or MINT ID

  • Quick explanation of the issue

  • Justification of escalation

Azure Rapid Response (ARR)

You can engage ARR or any technical team for two reasons:

  • Simple Technical Advisory on a case which has primary scope on ASMS
  • Technical RCA for potential refund/credit consideration under SLA/SIE.

Engage the appropriate Technical Support team via an SDC Collaboration Task. Fill out the template as follows:

  • Select appropriate SME area for which collaboration is needed: ARR VM, ARR Networking, ARR PaaS, ARR App Services, ARR Identity or ARR AKS
  • Detailed issue description
  • Issue date/time (server time)
  • Subscription GUID
  • Resource URI (ResourceInstanceID)
  • Troubleshooting steps/findings by ASMS (please be as detailed as possible)
  • Request from ARR Engineer. If this involves a potential refund (for SLA/SIE), include the following information:
    • Who is at fault: Microsoft or the customer?
    • All links related to the issue (IcM, Product Group, or any other links to support the SLA/SIE Impact)
    • Was the issue resolved? (If yes, how? Share RCA if available.)
    • Start date and time of the issue (to the minute if possible)
    • End date and time of the issue (to the minute if possible)
    • List of services impacted (in addition to the primary service)
 Engage your TA or PTA.
