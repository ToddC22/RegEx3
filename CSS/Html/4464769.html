Who should use this article?

What is this article about?

It provides general information, advocate procedures, and escalation details for handling 715-123XXX errors in OneVet Digital Fingerprinting (OVDF) in:

  • Visual Studio Subscriptions (VSS)
  • Azure Development Tools for Teachers (ADT4T)
  • Volume Licensing Service Center (VLSC) and/or Business Center Portal (BCP)

Overview

This article documents how to handle cases where customers receive 715-XXXXXX errors

Example on VLSC or BCP

  • User is unable to access their VLSC account and sees message: 715-123125
  • When a Volume Licensing user is repeatedly receiving the VLSC or BCP challenge questions, even though they are able to successfully answer the challenge questions.

Examples for MPSA customers in the NGVL platform with distinct messaging for the different NGVL Portals.

NGVL Support Portal: 715-123251

We are unable to validate your information. Please contact us and reference message code: 715-123251 and transaction ID: [insert session ID here].

NGVL Operations Portal: 715-123250

We are unable to validate your information. Please contact us and reference message code: 715-123250 and transaction ID: [insert session ID here].

NGVL Field Portal: 715-123252

We are unable to validate your information. Please contact us and reference message code: 715-123252 and transaction ID: [insert session ID here].

Example on my.visualstudio.com / ADT4T

  • user attempts to access Visual Studio functionality/place an order using Azure Dev Tools for Teaching and receives the 715-123150/715-123170 message code
  • this will alert the advocate that the customer's account has a problem and requires review by OVDF Escalations.

  1. When a user receives error codes, "715-xxxx", this will notify the Advocate that the customer's account was locked and requires review by OVDF Escalation Support (SentEsc).
  2. The advocate specifies which error code it is, collects the initial time of impact from the customer, and all of the data which is needed for the escalation.
    • 715-123150
    • 715-123170
    • 715-123125
    • 715-123251
  3. The advocate is to deliver the following message verbatim:
    Visual Studio

    “Our systems are detecting a problem with your account and we are unable to validate your access to this Visual Studio functionality. We will review the issue further and let you know our findings.”

    Azure Dev Tools for Teaching

    “Our systems are detecting a problem with your account and we are unable to validate your access to place an order via Azure Dev Tools for Teaching. We will review the issue further and let you know our findings.”

    VLSC

    “Our systems are detecting a problem with your account and we are unable to validate your access to Volume Licensing Service Center . We will review the issue further and let you know our findings."

    BCP

    “Our systems are detecting a problem with your account and we are unable to validate your access to BCP. We will review the issue further and let you know our findings."

    IMPORTANT: Don’t provide any workarounds which will enable the customer to get access!

Detailed steps for how and when to re-escalate an issue to SentEsc and cc Bluehawk PS OneVet DF Esc <bhsenesc@microsoft.com>

Error Message Reappears (Scenario 1)

If the customer returns stating that error [715 - XXXXXX] is again being displayed, follow the steps provided above.

If the issue still persists after 24 hours, escalate to the OVDF Escalations team:

Note for VLSC you don’t need to wait 24 hours.

  • Start with a new escalation, but reference the first INC ticket number
  • Add 'Repeat issue' in the Subject line and in the notes
  • Provide the template again

Customer does not accept the answer (Scenario 2)

Second customer contact – Case was already escalated to SentEsc once:

If customer does not accept the first answer and wants to escalate further, or the customer is still facing the issue after waiting 24 hours to see if it resolves the second time, the Advocate should collect the following information from the customer:

For VL provide the following information and for DEV submit the original template again.

  • Are you the administrator for an organization? Yes/No
  • Are you sharing the account credentials with other users in your organization? Yes/No
  • Do you have a proof of purchase that you could send me (applicable only if paid subscription)?

and re-escalate the case to OVDF Escalation Support team:

  • Start with a new escalation, but reference the first INC ticket number and mention this is the second escalation in the Subject line and in the notes
  • Provide the template again
  • Provide the input to the questions above for VL
  • Update the SD Internal title and add the new Inc ticket in the title: e.g. 1. Incxxx, 2: incxxx;

Script for customers: thanks for providing the additional information. I will re-escalate your issue and will come back to you. Please note that Re-Escalations like this sometimes take a number of days to evaluate; we apologize for any inconvenience this may cause.

Note: if the customer does not provide the additional data mentioned above, the advocate should still escalate to SentEsc but, this second escalation to SentEsc will be the last escalation allowed.

NOTE: This will be forwarded by SentEsc to higher tiered teams for further evaluation and will likely take a number of days to evaluate.

Sent the email to SentEsc and cc Bluehawk PS OneVet DF Esc <bhsenesc@microsoft.com>

 

Third contact – Case was already escalated to SentEsc two times and a response of “The customer’s access will not be reactivated” is received or if no additional data was provided in second customer contact:

Advocate should provide the following script and NOT escalate further:

“Thanks for your patience. After checking in our database, we have found that your account, xxxx, does not meet Microsoft requirements and remains blocked.

Please don’t contact Support again as we have deeply reviewed the situation and the outcome will remain the same.

We will now close the service request and we will not reopen the case, even if you contact us again via phone or email.“

Important guidelines for OVDF Escalations

  • The SentEsc email account (SentEsc@microsoft.com) is for internal support only and should not be shared with the customer or persons outside of Microsoft.
  • SentEsc does not communicate directly with customers or close SRs that were originally created by front line teams.
  • All communication to and from SentEsc is English-only.
  • Escalations alias: OVDF Escalations – sentesc@microsoft.com must be in the “TO:” line rather than “CC: or BCC:” line.
  • Ensure the terms AW, RE, FW are NOT included in the subject line (if they are, SentEsc will not get the e-mail).
  • If you had an existing thread with the team and you remove the AW, RE, FW, etc., this will not create a ticket; you need to start a new thread.
  • A ticket cannot be re-opened 7 days after resolution.  A new ticket must be created. You can add the existing thread as attachment to the new e-mail/escalation.
  • When you re-open a case, it is best that you create a new e-mail and refer to the original ticket number.
  • After sending the escalation, you should get an automatic reply along with the SNOW ticket within 30 minutes.
  • The support team follows a 48-business hour SLA (no US night, weekend, or holiday support). Re-escalations may take more time than this, as they are often sent to other teams for more in-depth evaluation.
  • If SLA missed, or the case has broad impact such as a group/company all getting the same error, or there is a question about an unusual scenario, send email to: Bluehawk PS OneVet DF Esc <bhsenesc@microsoft.com>. Note that re-escalation requests are not accepted by this alias; they should be handled per process outlined in this document.

Q: What if a customer inquiry mentions that there are additional users that are being impacted by error?

  1. Customer reports 'others impacted':
    • Ask the customer if all personnel in their organization are impacted, and if so, if that is more than 4-5 people. If not more than 4-5 people impacted, go to step 2.
    • If more than 4-5 people:
      • open a ticket with sentesc@microsoft.com, and cc: bhsenesc@microsoft.com.
      • Include the specific customer’s information as normal
      • Add a comment that all customers in this organization are impacted, and request that they please treat this as a high priority.
  2. If the customers report that others are impacted and can provide the necessary information for advocates to review:
    • Advocates can collect the information, and should create one case per impacted user; otherwise, inform the customer that each impacted customer should contact support to review their specific account.
    • Note that each case will be independent, and advocates must not communicate the results to the individual, but instead only within the case details.

 

Q: I received the 715-123150 but now, I am not able to download, is this the same problem?

A: No, while 715-123150 and Download issues may occur at around the same time, they are not the same.  Please treat each scenario independently, but start with the 715-123150 escalation, followed by troubleshooting download issues.

 

Q:  I am seeing the 715-xxxxxx error after you already confirmed this was resolved?

A: Advocates should review this as a “net new” issue and follow escalation details. Advocates should know that 715 -123xxx can occur again. When escalating, please add a note informing that this happened before and roughly when it happened before. If this issue has been escalated three or more times by this customer, please see instructions under Fourth Contact for how to handle this.

 

Q: What if the customer asks for compensation?

A: No compensation will be offered. Defer to information provided by OVDF Escalation Support on closure.
