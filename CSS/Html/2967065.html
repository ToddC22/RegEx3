2. Entitlement process

  1. Perform Account Verification see, KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification with the Microsoft account the customer used when they purchased subscriptions.
  • It is important to confirm with the customer that they provided the Microsoft account they used to purchase the subscription, or you will not be able to entitle the customer.
  1. In the Services section of MST, select the Services in topic see, KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST ).
     
     
  2. Select Manage Subscriptions.
  3. Look for the Service in topic from the below list and check if the status is Active.
    • Assure
    • Microsoft 365
    • Outlook.com
    • Microsoft Complete
    • PerformanceGuard

After confirming the subscription in topic is active, proceed to provide technical support to the customer.

3. When you cannot find customer subscription

Note: For Assure, confirm that the customer has purchased from the retail Microsoft Store and if so, they might not have redeemed. For details, see KB 2846205 Procedure: Token Redemption and Support Scenarios for Assure subscription.

If you've tried to locate the subscription using the above steps and you're not able to find the customer’s subscription and the customer is saying they have a subscription for a service, then do the following:

  1. Even if you don’t find the customer’s subscription, honor the service to which they say they are subscribed.
  2. Do not tell the customer you could not find their subscription.

Return to top

4. Redos

When a customer contacts Microsoft support within 30 days of purchasing a one-time paid support service for the same issue, you may reopen the case at no additional cost to the customer. This is called Redo.
  • Redo’s are only applicable for the same issue within 30 days of purchase.
  • If the customer is contacting for a different issue, you may not provide a Redo and ask the customer to purchase new support service.
