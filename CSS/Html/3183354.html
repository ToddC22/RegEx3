1. Overview

Targeting allows advertisers to get their ads in front of the customers that align to their target market by adjusting the following targets:

  • Language, physical location, and location intent
  • Days of the week
  • Time of the day
  • Age and gender
  • Device used (desktops/laptops and tablets, or smartphones)
  • Website or IP address exclusions
  • LinkedIn

Targeting is an excellent way to make ads more effective. For example, if an advertiser knows which gender is more likely to be interested in an ad, they can target the ad so that either males or females are more likely to see it.

Similarly, if your product is meant for an audience in a certain geographic location, or for an audience that is online at specific hours of the day, advertisers can use targets to show the ad only in certain places or at certain times. They can also target searchers by location, regardless of the country where the site they visit is published.

Targeting can be set at the campaign or ad group level. Targeting set at the ad group level will override targeting at the campaign level only if the same target type is present at both levels. This will help serve ads to your most relevant audiences.

2. Cost and effect on ad position

2.1 Cost

Advertisers decide how much to bid for targets. If they’re using incremental bidding, each target that they add to a keyword requires an additional (incremental) bid.

Remember that the base bid always applies. For example, if an advertiser places a base bid of 10 cents and bids an additional 100 percent for a certain target, 20 cents is the maximum amount that they would pay for an ad to be shown when the target is matched. The advertiser only pays enough to beat the person who is positioned under them. In this example, the amount an advertiser pays could be any amount between five cents and the full 20 cents, depending on the other factors that affect positioning, including how much other people bid on the keyword and the target, as well as relevance and click-through rate.

Incremental bids are additive. For example, with a base bid of 10 cents and an additional bid of 100% on Tuesdays and 100% on males, if a male search user selects the ad on Tuesday, the advertiser may pay up to 30 cents for that click: 10-cent base bid + 100% Tuesdays (10 cents) + 100% Males (10 cents).

If the ad is shown to users who do not match the target criteria, and they click on the ad, the advertiser does not pay any part of the additional targeting bid. The advertiser only pays the base bid.

Return to top

2.2 Effect on ad position

Adding targets to keywords might improve an ad's position. With incremental bids, they are added to the keyword bid. The total bid amount will be higher with those incremental bids than without. Generally, the higher the total bid amount, the higher the ad's position online.

5. Exclusions

Advertisers can prevent their ads from serving in specific locations, on certain syndication partners, or certain IP addresses. See How to prevent your ads from showing to certain people for more information.

Account-level shared website exclusions

Advertisers can now apply website exclusions across all accounts within a managed shell. This makes it easier to optimize for Syndicated Network delivery and manage exclusions in bulk. See Share website exclusion lists across accounts for more information.

Things to note:

  • Each manager account can create three website exclusion lists in the Shared Library.
  • Each list can contain up to 10,000 excluded URLs.
  • It’s only available in the new web UI.
  • Each URL must specify the domain and can contain one subdomain and two directories.

To create/edit account-level website exclusion lists:

  1. Ensure you’re on the Manager Account view.
  2. Go to Tools.
  3. Select Website Exclusion Lists.
  4. On the page that loads, select Create website exclusion list.
  5. Give the exclusion list a Name, enter the excluded websites as separate line items, and select Save.
  6. From the Website Exclusion Lists page, select the exclusion lists you would like to add using the tick-boxes, and select Add to accounts.
  7. Then select which accounts to apply the lists to, and select Save.

Customers can drill into individual lists via the Website Exclusions Lists page to edit/delete lists, as needed.

6. Geographical location targeting

Geographical (GEO) targeting in Microsoft Advertising helps advertisers expand their reach to find new customers and home in on their perfect customers on Microsoft Advertising.

Advertisers can target campaigns and ad groups to customers by physical location and search query location intent. Advertisers can also choose to bid higher on keywords matched to customers in their targeted geographical area. Geographical targeting is sometimes referred to as regional targeting, location-based targeting, geo-targeting, and demo targeting.

When advertisers target by location, they can choose to show ads to potential customers in specific countries/regions, states/provinces, metro areas (or Nielsen Designated Market Areas (DMAs) in the US), zip codes/post codes, counties, or cities.

Advertisers in any market can target ZIP/post codes in locations offering this targeting option, even if their own market does not support this form of targeting.

  • Zip/Post code targeting is available in: AU, CA, DE, FR, UK, US
  • City level targeting is supported in: AU, CA, FR, UK, US  

Advertisers can set both geo/area and radius targeting entries for the same campaign or ad group for greater control and flexibility.

In addition, advertisers can target campaigns by excluding specific physical locations. This feature is especially useful for advertisers with specific geographic areas to which they don’t ship or provide services. They can also use it to offer targeted special promotions. Advertisers can retain location exclusions when importing campaigns from Google. For example, if they’ve targeted the United States but excluded some states, those exclusions will be supported when they import their campaigns.

Advertisers can also use language and location options to reach users in multiple countries who speak the same language.

Return to top

6.1 How geo-targeting works

  • Microsoft Advertising applies geo-targeting based on a reverse-IP lookup of each user:
  • The IP address of a user is based on how that user accesses the Internet.
    • Geo-targeting may not be accurate in the following situations:
      • Private network: The user accesses the Internet through a private network. The user sees ads that are not targeted by location because Microsoft Advertising cannot apply geo-targeting to an unknown address.
      • Corporate Network: The user accesses the Internet from a different location. For example, corporations frequently map all their IP addresses to a single location. Frequently, the actual location of all the users on a corporate network is not reflected in their IP address.
      • IP mapping: The user's IP address location is based on the ISP and in some cases, the user’s physical location may differ from what our IP lookup indicates. This may be due to rerouted IP pools by the ISP and may result in a user not seeing ads that are targeted to limited geographic areas. IP mapping confidence improves as geographic targeting scope is broadened.
      • Mobile: IP-based geo-targeting may not work accurately for mobile ads due to the nature of mobile technology itself, and Radius Targeting requires end-users to share their location data.

6.2 Editor compatibility

To use enhanced ad scheduling (for example 15-minute increment), you must upgrade to Editor v10.5, which will be available in mid-August. You cannot fetch enhanced ad schedules with previous versions of Editor, and if schedules are set in the UI and updated in Editor 10.4, the schedules will be overwritten and revert to the old format.

If you set schedules using Editor 10.4, they will be shown in the new scheduling format when viewed in the UI. You can continue using Editor 10.4 to manage ad schedules that you do not wish to convert to the new scheduling format.

6.3 API compatibility

Enhanced ad scheduling is supported in the API v9 refresh, which is already available. You must use v9 refresh to modify ad schedules in the new format. If you set schedules in the UI, you can fetch those using API v8 or v9, but you will not be able to modify them. If schedules are set in the UI and updated using v8 or v9, the schedules will be overwritten and revert to the old format.

If you set schedules using API v8 or v9, they will be shown in the new scheduling format when viewed in the UI. You can continue using API v8 or v9 to manage ad schedules that you do not wish to convert to the new scheduling format.

6.4 Physical location

Advertisers can choose to include or exclude locations. Location exclusions are especially useful if advertisers have specific geographic areas to which they don't ship or provide services. They can also use it to offer targeted special promotions. Advertisers can retain their location exclusions when importing campaigns from Google. For example, if they've targeted the United States but excluded some states, those exclusions will be supported when they import their campaigns.

When targeting by Nielsen Designated Market Areas (DMAs) in the US and metro areas in other markets, it’s important to remember that DMAs or metro areas may cross state boundaries. For example, Cincinnati, Ohio, is located on the border with Kentucky. When advertisers target Cincinnati, they also reach residents of Kentucky living in the Cincinnati DMA. DMAs are used in place of metro targeting in the US only but apply to advertisers outside the US who target US DMAs.

If advertisers select a city name that has more than one possible state (for example, Portland, Maine, and Portland, and Oregon), they need to make sure that the correct state is indicated. It is possible that not all cities will be available in the pick list. Try using the search function to find the desired target or select the surrounding metro area or DMA.

For advertisers targeting regions in Canada, selecting all available cities within a province is equivalent to selecting the whole province.

Under Locations in their campaign or ad group settings in Microsoft Advertising online, advertisers can select from these options:

  • All available countries/regions.
  • Selected cities, metro areas (or Nielsen Designated Market Areas (DMAs) in the US), states/provinces and countries/regions.

If they choose Selected cities, metro areas, states/provinces, or countries/regions, they have an option to choose Advanced search. Under Advanced search, advertisers can:

  • Set both geo/area and radius targeting entries for the same campaign or ad group for greater control and flexibility.

6.5 Search query location intent

Microsoft Advertising uses the location information in a searcher's query to determine if an ad is relevant. Advertisers can choose to:

  • Show ads to people in, searching for, or viewing pages about your targeted location.
  • Show ads to people in your targeted location.
  • Show ads to people searching for or viewing pages about your targeted location.

6.6 Radius targeting

With radius targeting, advertisers can target customers within 1 to 500 miles (1 to 800 kilometers) of business locations. They can also avoid unwanted clicks from outside their businesses’ service areas. Under Locations in their campaign or ad group settings, they can enter a specific radius number and enter a zip/postal code, an address, a landmark, or coordinates.

See the How does ad language and location targeting affect who can see my ads? help article for more information.

6.7 Inline location targeting recommendations

Available on the Settings sub-tab within the Campaigns tab, advertisers are now able to see inline location targeting recommendations, which provide recommended locations and the estimated impressions that could be gained by adding them.

Advertisers can select some or all the suggestions and add these to their targeting to improve their audience reach and increase their potential impressions.

From the Settings sub-tab, advertisers can use the All Settings option to view individual campaign recommendations, by selecting the light bulb alert icon. Advertisers can also select Locations to see a list of all available location recommendations and the campaigns they’re being recommended for.

See Inline location targeting recommendations for more information on how to use this feature.

7. Targeting by day and time

Advertisers can target by the day of the week or the time of the day that they’d like an ad to deliver. Advertisers can choose whether to use the searcher’s time zone or their own account’s time zone, when determining whether the ad should deliver.

If using incremental bidding, advertisers place higher bids on their target times to target an ad group and show ads more frequently at those times. Modifications to this targeting go into effect within one hour.

If the advertiser is not using incremental bidding, the ad will be displayed only on the days or at the times selected. Modifications to this targeting do not go into effect until the selected day and time begins.

8. Targeting by age and gender

8.1 Audience segments

When creating a new campaign or ad group, advertisers can target a specific age group or gender. They can target an ad to the following audience segments:

  • All age groups and all genders
  • Specific age groups
  • A specific gender

We recommend using bid-boosting (incremental bidding) to reach an intended audience. Bid boosting means advertisers bid an additional amount beyond their keyword bids to reach their ideal customers.

Return to top 

8.2 Under 18 user safety

To protect minors under the age of 18, and adhere to UK law, Bing will not display ads to minors under the age of 18 if they fall into any of the following sensitive categories:

  • Adult
  • Pharma
  • Gambling
  • Tobacco
  • Weapons
  • Violence
  • Alcohol
  • Drugs
  • Mature
  • Political content
  • Religious/hate speech

In addition, advertisers can manually choose to opt-out of showing their advertising to people under the age of 18. (As determined by their demographic information when signed into Bing.)

To opt out, refer the advertiser to the Age and Gender section of the How can I get my ads in front of my customers? help article.

9. Expanded device targeting

Expanded device targeting gives advertisers more control in their audience targeting strategy by making it possible to set individual bid adjustments by device type (PC, tablet, and mobile), and further their targeting strategy based on unique business needs.

There is a range to +900% for each device type – PC, tablet, and mobile, as well as the ability to completely opt out (set to -100%) from displaying ads on tablet and mobile. With these new controls, advertisers can segment by device to tailor ad messaging specifically for them. When importing campaigns from AdWords, negative PC bid adjustments will be automatically adjusted to 0%, as it’s not supported in Microsoft Advertising today.

10. Language targeting

Your ad language setting determines the language you will use when you write your ads and should be the language of your customers. Advertisers can use language and location options to reach users in multiple countries who speak their target languages.

Campaign language settings can be edited at any time. Advertisers can choose to target All Languages or apply one/several from the available language options.

The ad language setting:

  • Applies to all ads in an ad group. (Best practice: If you want to run your ads in multiple languages, consider setting up an ad group for each language.)
  • Defaults to the ad language you selected for the campaign.
  • Can be edited at any time.

Note: Advertisers interested in reaching Spanish-speaking consumers in the US can serve Spanish keywords and ads within EN-US ad groups. See the How does ad language and location targeting affect who can see my ads? help article for more information.

11. LinkedIn targeting

Advertisers can target potential customers at the campaign/ad group level using three LinkedIn dimensions:

  • Industry (for example, Retail, Banking, or Marketing)
  • Company (for example, Facebook, Microsoft, or Verizon)
  • Function (for example, Engineering, Sales, or Consulting)

Bid boosts can be applied to each value. The targeting can be used for Search, DSA, and BSC campaigns, in addition to Audience campaigns.

LinkedIn data, like existing demographic data, is pulled when a search user is using credentials associated to a LinkedIn profile.

Advertisers can use this feature to target the following markets:

US, UK, CA, AU, FR and DE.

See LinkedIn profile targeting for more information.

Note:

Due to LinkedIn EMEA’s default settings, advertisers will not automatically be opted in to share their LinkedIn profile data with third-party advertising providers. If a customer asks why they see a low volume of EMEA LinkedIn targets (when compared with actual numbers of LinkedIn EMEA profiles), you can explain that due to GDPR the default for all LinkedIn EMEA members is to keep profile information private unless they choose to share it for advertising and job-seeking purposes.

FAQ

Q: Where do I see performance?

A: Dedicated reporting is unavailable, but you can see performance under the Demographics grid in the UI. The Other data in the grid represents LinkedIn profiles that are available, but not currently targeted.

12. Ad Scheduling

With this functionality, advertisers can:

  • Schedule ads in 15-minute intervals.
  • Set up different schedules for each day of the week.
  • Choose whether to use their account’s time zone or the search-user’s time zone to deliver ads.
  • Easily import Google ad schedules.
    • Note: Google uses the advertiser’s location as the basis for establishing time zone; Bing users can choose to use either the search user’s time zone or their account’s time zone as the basis for establishing ad scheduling.

Time-zone scheduling notes:

API users must set AdScheduleUseSearcherTimeZone boolean to false.

The default setting for newly created time zone targeting is as follows:

  • Web UI: Account’s time zone (Customer will have the option to alter)
  • Google Import: Account’s time zone (No option to toggle)
  • API: Search-user’s time zone (Customer will have option to alter)
  • MAE: Search-user’s time zone (No option to toggle yet – due H2 FY20)

12.3 Best practices for ad scheduling

  • If you use both the UI and Editor, be sure to download Editor version 10.5 or later.
  • If you use both the UI and API, switch to the latest version of the API (v9 refresh).
  • If you have day and time targets at both the campaign and ad group level and switch to setting ad schedules at the campaign level, be sure to review your ad group level ad schedules and update those as needed.
    • Note: Ad schedules which have been set to the time zone of users may lead to a small number of impressions seen outside of expected times.

13. Known targeting issues in LATAM & the Nordics 

Issue: LATAM or Nordic Ads appear not to be delivering on LATAM/Nordic Yahoo properties (In Columbia, Chile, Peru, Venezuela, Mexico, Argentina, Denmark, Finland, Norway, and The Netherlands).

Messaging:

  • LATAM
    “We are aware that in certain markets ads appear to not be delivering on Yahoo properties. It appears Yahoo discontinued homepages of Columbia, Chile, Peru, Venezuela, Mexico, and Argentina earlier this year. Yahoo’s approach for portal design has been to build core portals that are representative of language and region. In Latin America, Spanish speaking countries homepages in Columbia, Chile, Peru, Venezuela, and Argentina have now been aggregated to espanol.yahoo.com. As a rule, from a marketplace point of view, we do not guarantee any ‘consistency’ or even distribution of delivery amongst our publishers, either owned and operated or syndication. In this case, the inconsistency is due to changes Yahoo has made, therefore we cannot set expectations or guarantee that ads will be delivered consistently. As we cannot guarantee consistency, we won’t be suggesting any workarounds at this time (that won’t work consistently).”
  • Nordics
    "We are aware that in certain markets ads appear to not be delivering on Yahoo properties. It appears Yahoo discontinued homepages of Denmark, Finland, Norway and The Netherlands earlier this year. Yahoo’s approach for portal design has been to build core portals that are representative of language and region. As a rule, from a marketplace point of view, we do not guarantee any ‘consistency’ or even distribution of delivery amongst our publishers, either owned & operated or syndication. In this case, the inconsistency is due to changes Yahoo has made, therefore we cannot set expectations or guarantee that ads will be delivered consistently. As we cannot guarantee consistency, we won’t be suggesting any workarounds at this time (that won’t work consistently).”
