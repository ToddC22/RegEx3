The customer wants to switch payment method for active subscription from PayPal to another payment instrument (PI).

Add new PI first, and then move the subscription to the newly added PI. You should be able to remove PayPal from the Microsoft account after the subscription is transferred.

In some cases, you may need to cancel the subscription if the first option doesn’t work, wait a month, and add the new PI.

Customer is unable to transfer outstanding balance between Business Profile and Personal Profile (see details in KB 3057034 Informational: Personal and Business Billing Profiles).

Note: Personal/Business profile differentiating is not visible to customers, but only in advocate tools.

The customer’s current subscription was on an older version of our billing platform. That is being retired, so we need to get them on the new billing platform, so their subscription renews properly.

Follow the process below to help customer to resolve this situation.

For monthly subscribers:

  1. Cancel the current subscription.
  2. Add the new payment instrument via MST.
  3. Provide a one-month token for the same SKU via ORST.
  4. After the customer redeems the token, turn on recurring billing so the customer keeps the subscription going with the new PI.

For annual subscribers:

  1. Cancel the current subscription.
  2. Add the new payment instrument via MST.
  3. Provide a one-month token for the same SKU via ORST.
  4. Customer redeems the token.
  5. POBO Microsoft 365 annual subscription.

Important: See KB 4578369 Procedure: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) Consent and KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) for information on POBO and mandatory consent actions.
