ASMS – Case Closure Handling (Unresponsive Customer, Confirmed Close)

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to close a case once a customer has either confirmed closure or has become unresponsive. 

Customers may become unresponsive in the normal workflow of a case. In these instances, you should follow the guidance below to ensure that the customer has a good experience and an adequate chance to respond before you close their case.

Policy

  • Drive the conversation with customers. If you’ve sent a solution or requested information and the customer goes unresponsive, follow up through other channels (such as email or phone) to complete the communication thread.

    • After sending a solution, check in with the customer to ensure your solution worked. Only close the case after the solution has been confirmed. If the customer is not responsive, follow the Strike process that best fits your case.

  • Do not suggest temporary case closure to the customer. You may receive cases where the request is not able to be fulfilled or resolved within a reasonable timeframe. The resolution may not be available until weeks or months later (ex: quota increase backlog, credit request). In these situations, you should keep the case open until the case is resolved.

    Note: Closing a case temporarily or prematurely can result in a survey being sent to the customer despite the issue being unresolved. It can also skew the data used to track case volume.

  • Do not send strike emails on weekends or holidays.

    • Emails that are sent on the eve of a holiday or a weekend will likely go unnoticed until the holiday or weekend is over. For example, emails that are sent Friday afternoon will likely only be responded to on Monday or Tuesday.
    • If you are aware of your customer’s location, it is a good idea to check a local holiday calendar before sending any strike emails. This is to ensure that you don’t accidentally send a strike email to them during a holiday.

    • See the Add Holidays to Your Calendar in Outlook for Windows article for directions on adding regional holidays to your Outlook calendar. 

  • If no solution has been delivered and the customer becomes unresponsive: Attempt to call your customer at least once using the number listed in the case, and document the result of the attempt in Service Desk – even if the customer’s preferred method of contact is email.

    • If you call your customer and reach a voicemail box, leave a message explaining the nature of your call.
    • Enunciate your message clearly.
    • Be sure to follow up any voicemail messages with an email to the customer.

Note: If a phone number is not given as part of the case, you should not call the number they have listed in CMAT.

  • When contacting a customer and the email bounces back as "undeliverable" you should follow the Three-Strike Process. This is to ensure that we have properly attempted to contact the customer, in the event there was an unintended system error on either our side or their side.
  • If you are striking a Premier customer, ensure the Enhanced Incident Manager (IM+) is included on all strike emails. 
    • If the IM+ is not listed on your case, include the Customer Success Account Manager (CSAM); formally the Technical Account Manager (TAM).
  • CSAMs or IM+ may request that support close a case due to contract expiry, even though the case is unresolved. Since ASMS support is free, we should continue to offer assistance to the Partner regardless of their contract status. If a CSAM or IM+ reaches out to you with such a request, engage your manager, who will work with them to decide on the best course of action. 
  • Be sure to follow the CSS Case Closure Templates outlined in the Last Quality Response (LQR) Procedure TSG. 
    • Last Quality Response (LQR) is a critical element of Case Closure that delivers a meaningful and consistent case closure experience with customers. It ensures all the necessary elements needed to conclude interaction with the customer is holistic and leaves the customer with a feeling of resolution. 

Process

Depending on the status of your case, use the following process:

  • Confirmed Closure Process
  • The One-Strike Process
    • Eligible One-Strike Scenarios
  • The Three-Strike Process
  • Customer Out of Office (OOF) Process


Confirmed Closure Process

If the customer responds with confirmation that the issue has been resolved or provides permission to close the case, follow the appropriate Standard Case Closure Template outlined in the LQR Procedure TSG. 

The One-Strike Process

Prerequisites
All three of the following conditions must be met before you can use the One-Strike Process.

The One-Strike process is an exception to the Confirmed Closure Process and can be followed provided all three of the following conditions are met.

  • You have delivered a solution and can validate that it has been delivered through our internal tools.
    Example: The customer asked for a refund, and you can verify in a tool that the refund has been applied.
  • One of the following scenarios applies:
    • Add/Change credit card
    • Port 25 enablement
    • Quota increase
    • Refund/credit
    • Security and compliance request
    • Terms of Use (TOU) violation (Example: free trial violation)
    • Unsupported Payment Instruments (for example: Pre-Paid credit card, PayPal)
  • It has been at least 24 hours since you provided a solution (in an email or as a call) to your customer.

One-Strike Email Checklist
If all three of the above conditions are true, communicate the case closure by following the Standard Case Closure Template outlined in the LQR Procedure TSG. 

Unresponsive Customer (Three-Strike or Customer OOF)

The Three-Strike Process

Prerequisites

  • If a customer has become unresponsive at any time during life of the case, apply the following process to give the customer a chance to respond, before you close their case due to inactivity.
  • Be sure to carefully review the Policy section, especially the part about weekends and holidays before you start applying strikes.
  • If you are striking a Premier customer, ensure the Enhanced Incident Manager (IM+) is included on all strike emails. You can find the IM+ details listed in the Contact section of the case or in Azure Support Center (ASC). Note: You will need to manually add this contact to your customer communications.
    • If the IM+ is not listed on your case, include the Customer Success Account Manager (CSAM); formally the TAM.
    • If the IM+ or CSAM are OOF, reach out to their respective back-ups.
  • If your customer responds during the three-strike process, discontinue the three-strike process and continue to work with the customer. If they become unresponsive again, re-start the three-strike process.
  • If the customer does not respond by Strike 3, the IM+ can also give approval to close the case.

Process

  1. First Strike: Send the first strike at least 24 hours after the customer has become unresponsive.
    • If you are striking a Premier customer, ensure the IM+ is on the To line.
    • Message the IM+ (via MS Teams) to notify them that the customer is not responding, and share the date for when you plan to send the 2nd strike.
  2. Second Strike: Send the second strike at least 24 hours after the first strike.
    • If you are striking a Premier customer, ensure the IM+ is on the To line, and your manager is on the Cc line.
    • Message the IM+ (via MS Teams) to notify them that the customer is not responding, and share the date for when you plan to send the 3rd strike.
      • Proceed with next steps or customer follow-ups based on the IM+’s feedback.
    • Message your Manager (via MS Teams) and notify them about the 2nd Strike.
      • Include the SR number and short summary of the case status.
  3. Call the customer after the second strike and leave a voicemail if possible. This call must be documented in the case logs.
    • For Premier customers, an ASMS Manager may contact the IM+ or the IM+'s Manager to request traction on the customer's case.
    • Note: If a phone number is not given as part of the case, you should not call the number they have listed in CMAT.
  4. If a solution hasn’t been delivered at this stage, set the SR Status to Waiting for Information.
  5. Third Strike: Send the third strike at least 24 hours after the second strike.
    • In your case notes, add a summary of the status of the case and any next actions in case the customer reopens the case.
    • If you are three striking a Premier customer, ensure the IM+ is on the To line.

Customer Out of Office (OOF) Process

There are two processes when a customer goes Out of Office. This process covers if the customer informs the SE they are going OOF or if the SE is not notified and receives an automatic OOF message.

Customer Informs Support of their OOF

  1. Request an alternate contact for customers going out of office (OOF).

  2. Verify with the customer whether or not there will be an alternate contact to continue working the case.

  3. If an alternate is provided and not already included under contacts, add the new name and continue working the case as appropriate.

  4. Follow the steps below if the customer does not have an alternate contact:

    1. Customer OOF / Restricted Change Access Log: Enter in Service Desk a Customer Contact Labor entry the following information:

    2. Customer<enter name> OOF

    3. Dates OOF / Change Access- mm/dd/yy through mm/dd/yy (date format optional as long as it includes month day and year)

    4. Adjust Severity: Request permission from the customer to lower Severity Level to C. If customer refuses, note in task and notify your Manager

    5. Schedule a Callback: Schedule an agreed upon callback time with the customer. If no confirmation of callback is made, schedule a callback within 48 business hours of the customer's return. Include the mm/dd/yy- time + time zone (date format optional as long as it includes month day and year)

OOF Auto-Response from Customer

In the event you receive an auto out-of-office response from the customer, follow the steps below:

  1. If an alternate contact is identified in the customer’s OOF message, attempt to engage that individual to continue working the case.

  2. If the alternate contact does not respond (or is not equipped to work on the case), follow the next best action below:

    1. If a return date is specified and no alternate contact is identified/available, leave the case open and use the Service Desk Follow up task to set a reminder to contact the customer on the return date specified in their OOF message.

    2. If no return date is cited in the customer’s OOF message, keep the case open and set the proper expectations with the customer. Using the guidance below, create the follow up task to set a reminder.

  • Inform the customer that you received their Out-of-Office message and will follow back up with them in one week.
  • If no return date is available, ask the customer to provide a date.

  • Suggest to the customer that if they plan to be back sooner, to provide us with the best time to contact them or if they need longer than a week before we follow up to let us know.

  • Ask the customer if there is an alternate contact we can work with in the meantime (if no alternate contact was previously provided.

  1. If you receive a response with either a return date or an alternate contact, proceed as described above. If not:

  • Follow up with the customer when prompted by your follow up reminder. If the customer does not respond to your follow-up, initiate standard three-strike process. The follow-up itself does not count as strike one.

  • Use your best judgment in customer-OOF scenarios. Leverage details from the customer’s OOF message to determine your approach.
