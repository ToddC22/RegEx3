1. Questions

1.1 Office redemption and Office activation

  • Office redemption is the process of associating a product key to the customer's Microsoft account before you install Office. This is done by going to office.com/setup, entering the product key (also known as a PIN) and associating it to a Microsoft account. Once the product key has been redeemed, the customer no longer needs it to reinstall Office.

Note: Customers may find the following video/article helpful: Redeem Your Product Key.

  • Office activation is the process of validating the product so that you can fully use it. This always occurs after installing the product but may be needed if the product needs to be validated again. For example, a PC with an Microsoft 365 install that hasn’t been connected to the internet for a while may require reactivation.

Activation for most newer versions of Office is done automatically but there may be times where a user will need to enter their product key or sign in with the Microsoft account they associated during redemption. Failure to activate a product will cause Office apps to go into reduced-functionality mode (RFM).

1.3 Differences in product keys

  • Product key used for redemption: Customers who buy Office full package product (FPP), point of sale activation (POSA) or product key card (PKC) through a retailer will be provided with a redemption key. Once they redeem Office, they won't need the redemption Product Key to reinstall Office. They will need to remember the Microsoft account associated with their copy of Office to go to their Overview page to reinstall Office. This only applies to consumer Microsoft 365 subscription plans, and Office 2019 perpetual consumer products.
  • Product key used for installation: Customers who buy in a market that has FPP with media will be provided with an install product key. This experience is the same as with previous versions of Office and they will need to retain the Product Key for future installs.

2. Experience

2.1 Redeeming Office product keys (2013 or later)

If the customer received a product key when they purchased Office 2013, Office 2016, Office 2019, or Microsoft 365, they will need to redeem their product key before they can install Office. They will go to https://office.com/setup, create and/or sign in with their Microsoft account and enter their product key to associate it with their purchase of Office.

For more information, go to Get help with office.com/setup.

Q: What is Family Sharing of your Microsoft 365 Home subscription?

A: If you have Microsoft family set up and you have an Microsoft 365 Family, then Office will be automatically shared with five members of your family.

Q: Does this count towards the six total users who can use my subscription?

A: Yes.

Q: How do I un-share my subscription with a family member?

A: Just like you manage anyone you share your subscription with, go to your Services & subscriptions page on account.microsoft.com and select the Sharing tab. From there, you can choose Stop sharing next to the individual.

Note: There is also a reminder email notification when Microsoft 365 has been shared with you, but you do not install and activate it. If you already have Office installed and activated, you will receive a tips email.

Q: What if I have more than five people in my family, who gets to share?

A: Family members are selected by tenure. Those who were added as a family member first are shared with first. However, you can always manage whom you share Office with by going to your Services & subscriptions page on account.microsoft.com and selecting the Sharing tab.

5. Install Office on behalf of the customer

Before you begin you need to determine what product the customer has and where they purchased it. Start with asking the customer questions:

What product has the customer purchased?

  • Perpetual - is one License installed on one computer (Office Home & Student, Home & Business, Pro Plus, Mac, or Office standalone applications).
  • Subscription - Monthly or yearly subscription. (Office 365 Home, Personal, or Office 365 University)

Where did the customer make the purchase?

  • From Microsoft - direct purchase (Microsoft Store or Office.com).
  • From online or Brick & Mortar retailer.
  • Third-party, like Best Buy or Staples (billing support provided by third party).

Has the customer redeemed the product?

Note: Redeeming a product means that the customer has either used their 5x5 key (PIN) or purchased Office online. Can the customer access their subscription page on account.microsoft.account and see the Office product they purchased?

  • Yes: Help the customer by going to the walkthrough for installing Office.
  • No: Who installed Office for the customer?

5.1 The customer installed it

Does the customer have multiple Microsoft accounts?

  • The customer has multiple Microsoft accounts: Ask the customer to log in to their other Microsoft accounts to see if the products are available for installation. If the product is found on the Office account page, educate the customer that they need to use this Microsoft account to manage their products, as there is no way to transfer the product to another Microsoft account.
  • The customer does not have multiple Microsoft accounts: Ask the customer if they have used a third-party email account when they redeemed their product. (For example, Gmail, Yahoo, or Comcast.) If so, they need to check that account for the verification email. If they can't find it in their inbox, ask them to check their junk mail folder.

If the customer claims they have not received the verification email or if the customer cannot locate their verification email in the junk or web email, look up the 5x5 key (PIN) in the ORST tool to verify associated Microsoft account. If there is no associated Microsoft account, restart the redemption process by going to office.com/setup. If you have no access to the ORST tool, transfer the customer to Up & Running.

6.2 Escalating an activation issue

If you need to escalate an activation issue, please gather the following information:

  • Note: For desktop client and activation-related issues provide the output from OLicenseDiag found here: \\offdepteam\tools\OLicenseDiag

  1. Copy the oLicenseDiag.zip file locally and unzip the file to a folder.
  2. Close all Office apps.
  3. Run licenseinfo.cmd from command prompt with Administrator Privileges.
  4. Follow the instructions on screen up to the point it requests you to press any key to continue.
  5. Launch an Office app to repro the issue.
  6. If prompted, sign into the dialog in the app.
  7. Close the app, go back to the command prompt and press any key to continue.
  8. Attach the resulting zip file located at: %localappdata%\Temp\licensingInfo_<machine_name>_<date>.zip

As a general practice for future troubleshooting, send the following steps to advocates to ensure all needed information is collected to diagnose and troubleshoot.

  1. Include the user’s email address is associated with Office. Usually this will be the email address used on their Microsoft account.
  2. Ask the customer to Send a Frown and include the screenshot and their email address.
    • This helps access the client sessions in our telemetry and get a screenshot of the error the user is seeing.
  3. If send a frown does not work, request a screenshot of the dialog they are seeing along with a screenshot of the About dialog.
    • The About dialog has the Office version and the session ID that helps find the user’s telemetry.
  4. We ask the user to run the Office activation troubleshooter. http://aka.ms/diag_officeact
    • If the troubleshooter is not able to resolve the issue, it will generate the logs in the folder: %localappdata%\ElevatedDiagnostics
    • Ask the user to zip the logs and send them in.
