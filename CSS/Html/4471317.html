1. Introduction

1.1 Parental consent and electronic signature

Depending on your region and the service the customer is trying to access, a parent or guardian’s permission may be required for a child to create a Microsoft account. Parents or guardians will use the electronic signature to provide their consent instead of providing a small charge against a payment instrument. See electronic signature sample below.

[Asset 4471335]

[Asset 4480012]

This is in accordance with certain regulations that promote child safety, along with greater visibility and management of data, that is stored about children online. For more information, see Parental consent and Microsoft child accounts.

Market impacted:

Region

Regulation Title

United States

Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

European Union

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

Note:

  • There may be similar regulations passed by individual governments. The list above only states the main known regulations.
  • All accounts where no date of birth exists on the account will be required to add a date of birth. If the date of birth shows the account owner to be under the statutory age, parental consent will be required.
  • New child accounts will have 14 days to gain parental consent before it is deleted.
  • Existing child accounts will have to gain parental consent or prove that they are an adult before the account is locked and inaccessible. Locked accounts will be deleted after five (5) years of inactivity.
  • Existing child accounts will automatically be unblocked if the account reaches the statutory age for their country.

1.3 Triggers for parental consent

The customer’s date of birth (DOB) is used to determine whether a customer will trigger the parental consent process.

  • IP detection will be used to determine the customer’s country to decide on whether parental consent needs to be triggered since not all countries require parental consent. The customer’s age as calculated from the date of birth will vary by country.
  • New Microsoft account customers will be required to enter their date of birth. Existing Microsoft account customers that did not have their date of birth stored in their Microsoft account will be prompted to enter their date of birth when they log into Microsoft services.
  • To prevent customers from accidentally entering a date of birth that would trigger the parental consent process, customers will be provided with a confirmation message to confirm their date of birth prior to proceeding.

2. Parental consent process

When signing into their Microsoft account, the child will be prompted to get parental consent. To provide parental consent, the parent/guardian must:

  • Be an adult (over the majority age).
  • Have a Microsoft account.

Note: If the parent does not have an account, they must first go through the account creation flow.

2.3 Providing consent

Note: Child privacy regulations prohibit recommending that a person under the statutory age create a new account with a date of birth that misrepresents themselves as an adult.

To provide consent, the parent/guardian must:

  1. Sign into their account using their account.
  2. Review the legal requirements by scrolling down the page and select Yes, I agree (web browser) or Yes, continue (Console).
    [Asset 4480124]
  3. Depending on the customer’s region, the check box My child can sign in to third-party apps may be either selected or unselected (see below).

European Union -
Third-party apps option not selected by default

United States -
Third-party apps option selected by default

[Asset 4480126]

In the European Union, the third-party apps box is not a default setting. As a result, parents/guardians will see a prompt “Are you sure?” after selecting the option “Yes, I agree” (on web browser) or “Yes, continue” (on Console).

The “Are you sure?” prompt clearly states that the child’s experience will be limited to Microsoft apps ONLY if the third-party apps box remains unselected.

[Asset 4480127]

  • By selecting Enable third-party apps, parents/guardians will give third-party apps access to the child.
  • If the parents/guardians select the option Yes, continue instead of enabling third-party apps, the child won’t have access to third-party apps.

Note: This setting can be changed at a later stage – see scenarios below.

 [Asset 4480128]

  1. Following the steps above, if the customer’s age is not yet verified, a prompt will appear requesting the customers to type in their name as shown in the signature box to validate they are adults. If the customer’s age has already been verified, the prompt below will not appear.
    [Asset 4480133]
  2. Once the parent ends the flow and agrees, the parental consent is provided, and the following is applied:
  • The child is added to the family
  • Age appropriate settings are applied
  • Ask a parent is turned on
  • Activity reporting is turned on

3. Customer scenarios

3.1 Account management: Child scenarios

Scenario

Advocate action

I have recently created a new account and my parent did not provide parental consent within the 14 days.

Can you please advise?

If the parents do not provide consent within the allocated time (14 days), the account will be deleted, and the username/alias will no longer be available for use.

  • Inform the child that the account will be deleted due to the lack of consent.
  • Inform the child that a new account will need to be created.
  • Educate the child on the parental consent timeframe when creating a new account (14 days).

The child created an account with a date of birth making them appear as an adult. Now, they want to transfer the content and/or subscriptions.

How can you help?
  • Recommend that the child update their date of birth to the correct date.
  • If that date requires parental consent, they would need to obtain that from their parents to be able to continue using the account.
  • Transferring content/subscriptions from one account to another is not a supported scenario. We are not able to assist with transferring content between two accounts.

I have an existing account, and I am prompted to get parental consent. It seems that my account is blocked. Why?
  • Educate the child on the requirement to request consent from their parent based on their account’s date of birth and the account’s country due to regulation such as GDPR (for EU) and COPPA (for US).
  • If they don’t get parental consent by the date shown on their screen, the account will remain blocked. (see sample below)

[Asset 4471352]

  • The account will be unblocked if one of the following occurs:
    • They receive parental consent.
    • The account reaches the statutory age for their country.

I have content and/or subscriptions on my existing account, but my parents do not want to provide consent. As a result, my account is blocked. Can I get a refund?

Digital content

  • If they are unable to obtain their parent’s consent, the digital content purchases on the account do not become eligible for refund.

Subscriptions

  • If they are unable to obtain their parent’s consent, they do not become eligible for a refund due to this situation.
  • If they anticipate that they will not be able to get a parent’s or guardian’s permission, you can assist them, in MST, by either turning off their auto-renewal or by removing their payment instrument.

Note: If the auto-renewal is not turned off, the subscription will continue to renew on locked accounts.

When requesting parental consent, I’ve made a mistake with my parent’s email. Can you please help?

or

My parent did not receive my parental consent request. Can you please help?

Recommend the child to sign in again into their account so they can enter a different email/phone number.

If the parent is available, recommend the parent follow the My parent can sign in now flow.
What happens when child keeps skipping the prompt?

There are different experiences for a new account versus an existing account when it comes to accounts that pre-existed before the age requirement was applied to customers affected by GDPR. In all instances though, the child is prompted to obtain parental consent from their parent once - every 24 hours. Also, if the parent wants to provide consent at a moment where the child had previously skipped the prompt, the parent can log into account.microsoft.com and start the process by adding the child’s account to a Family. The flow will recognize that it is an existing account that needs consent and will then put them right into that flow.

New child accounts: Have 14 days to obtain parental consent. If they don’t receive consent after the 14 days, the account is closed.

Existing accounts: Will see the prompt to obtain parental consent once a day, each time they log in. A date will be displayed for the customer in the message explaining when they need to obtain parental consent by before they will no longer be able to skip. As they have extended the allowed time for now, no customer has been locked out yet (We will update once new end date is set up).

3.2 Account management: Parent scenarios

Scenario

Advocate action

I already provided consent to my child and was charged a 0.50 dollar/euro fee. Can I get a refund?

As part of our previous process to comply with regulations, we charged a 0.50 dollar/euro fee to verify that an adult was giving a child permission.

Even with the new electronic signature flow in place, this charge remains non-refundable.

Can I remove consent for my child's Microsoft account?

Yes. If for any reason a parent no longer wants their child to have a Microsoft account, have them sign in to family.microsoft.com and follow the steps below:

  1. Once on the Family page, select Manage my child’s profile info to view the Manage permissions page.[Asset 4471353]
  2. Select Remove consent for this child’s account.[Asset 4471354]
  3. Select Remove consent.

My child wants to send me a parental consent request using a third-party email address. Will it work?

Work or school email addresses with private domains cannot be used. However, any other third-party personal email such as a Gmail or a Yahoo! email address can become a Microsoft account.

  • If the parent has already used the third-party email address with Microsoft, they can sign into it directly.
  • If the email has not been used with Microsoft before, they can create a new account using their email address on account.microsoft.com.

I'm an adult. Why am I asked to get parental consent?

Ask the customer to select Why am I seeing this? to:

  1. Update their date of birth accordingly.
  2. Enter their First name and Last name.
  3. Select Next to proceed to the change

[Asset 4471355]

When typing my electronic signature, I get an error message stating That doesn't match the name on your account. Try again or update it.

Can you please help?

It may be a typo issue.

Troubleshooting:

  • Ask the customer to type their signature again or to update their First name and Last name by selecting Update.

[Asset 4471357]

  • Ask the customer to type their signature again or to update their First name and Last name by selecting Update.

[Asset 4471359]

Console Troubleshooting:

[Asset 4480135]

  • Direct the customer to https://account.microsoft.com/profile/ so they can sign in to their account and correct their first and last name accordingly.

Why am I asked to fill in my First name and Last name when signing into my Microsoft account to provide consent?

If we detect that the account information is incomplete (possibly due to the customer using a lightweight sign up), a prompt will appear requesting the customer to update their name.

  • Explain to customers that these fields are mandatory to provide consent.

I get an error message stating "You need to be an adult to give consent." Why?

The date of birth on the account providing consent must indicate that the account owner is over the age of majority (18 in most countries) and not simply over the statutory age.

  • Recommend the customer update their date of birth or sign in with another account.

[Asset 4471360]

My child cannot use third-party apps. Can you please help?

During the provide consent flow, the parent did not select the box My child can sign into third-party apps.

[Asset 4471361]

As a result, the child’s experience is limited to Microsoft apps. For example, they won’t be able to sign into the mail app on their iOS or Android phone with their new Microsoft account.

Recommend that the customer modify the Family settings for the child by following the steps below:

  1. Sign in to family.microsoft.com.
  2. Once on the Family page, select Manage my child’s profile info to view the Manage permissions page.
  3. Select Manage this child’s access to third-party apps.

[Asset 4471364]

  1. Select Enable access so the child can access third-party apps.
