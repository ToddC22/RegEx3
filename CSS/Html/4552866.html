For Service partners

Engagement type and Audience

Partner Need

Program Requirements

What a Partner can expect

Tech Level

Starter  

Target audience: Service, ISV

Practice Initiation

MAPS/Silver/Gold

A Starter is a foundational service that provides introductory guidance to partners. This includes the fundamentals of starting a practice or expanding to a new workload. It is designed for partners who wish to attain an initial understanding of a Priority Scenario, to expand their capabilities, and to incorporate new Technical Scenarios. It is not associated with an active customer engagement, but rather, increase a partner’s ability to capture opportunities to sell and deploy new cloud solutions. A Starter is not product training or certification guidance in the technology.

Beginner – Intermediate

Presales

Target audience: Services partner

Sales Support

Silver/Gold

(Presales deal required)

Presales provide guidance for partners as they work to pitch and close a sales opportunity. This can include defining a value proposition, providing cost estimates, managing customer objections, responding to competitor messaging, demonstrating technical solutions, initiating a technical proof-of-concept in a lab, and architecture planning and design leading to a Statement of Work.

All

Deployment

Target audience: Services partner

Enhance Deployment Proficiency

MAPS/Silver/Gold

Deployment is designed to assist partners as they plan and implement solutions for their customers. This includes architecture planning and design, best practices and deployment guidance, product references and documentation. A Deployment provides deployment guidance to help partner deploy the solution and to shadow the deployment and provide best practices, helping remove deployment blockers through recommended architecture guidance. A Deployment is neither a step-by-step guide for deployment nor direct, hands-on deployment services or support.

Submitting a request for PTC engagement on behalf of a partner

For a video overview for how to do this process please see here

1. CED confirms that partner qualifies for PTC engagement.

2. Navigate to the PTC Field Request form to submit a ticket in OCPSR for the PTC.

[Asset 4566341]

3. Input the partner name. If it validates move to step 5, if not, continue to step 4. If the partner name doesn't populate an account automatically follow the below steps.

4. If the partner name does not validate, input the following information on the next screen.

[Asset 4566342]

5. Input the following information to create a new case and select Submit.

[Asset 4566343]

  • Partner contact: The primary partner POC name
  • Partner account: Partner company name
  • Case title: Partner name + 1 line description of the support needed
    • For example, Migrating on prem to Azure
    • For example, Teams deployment support
  • Description: Please provide a detailed breakdown of the issue description in the case title as well as the following information in the case description
    • Additional phone number (if applicable):
    • Additional Partner email (if applicable):
    • Partner availability: suggested date and time
  • Program: Either CED services or ISV depending on the partner
  • Service: Relevant service for the partner. See master taxonomy list above
  • Solution area: Relevant solution area for the partner. See master taxonomy list above
  • Key product/technology: Relevant key product/tech area for the partner. See master taxonomy list above
  • Priority scenario: Relevant partner priority scenario. See master taxonomy list above
  • Language: Partner language
  • Country: Partner country

6. Once the PTC engagement request has been submitted, the specialist should document the case number which will be used to look up the partner case when they need status updates.

7. CED will submit one field request per desired Solution Area.

a. If a Partner is interested in more than 1 Solution Area, they will need to be able to work with different PTCs.

8. CED creates the CED activity in ConnectUs as usual, noting that the partner has been submitted for technical support.

9. PTC duty manager accepts the request submitted by CED in OCPSR, confirms entitlement, and assigns a PTC to the case. PTC will contact the partner and work to build the customized service.

10. PTC SLA:

a. Accept case and route: four (4) hours

b. First Time Response two (2) business days per PTC SLA

11. PTC will include the CED specialist on the first contact attempt with the partner for visibility. It is at this point that the ConnectUs solution should be put in progress.

a. Starting June 1, 2020, CED specialists will have read access to the PTC instance of OCPSR. This will allow the specialists to get point in time status updates on the partners technical engagement.

12. At any point, the CED specialist can navigate to the PTC OCPSR to look up the status of their partners following the below steps.

14. CED follow-up activities during cadence calls:

a. The CED specialist will confirm with the partner the progress with the PTC services.

b. The CED specialist will remind the partner to go back with PTC team if they have questions and will facilitate this communication between the Partner and the PTC.

c. The CED specialist will confirm and monitor the business impact of the PTC engagement (Is the partner closing opportunities, accelerating, migrating apps, is ready to publish in the Storefronts, growing the practice?)

The CED specialist will confirm during the engagement with the partner if they have new opportunities or is interested in a new Solution Area. If any, CED will create a new engagement with PTC.
