2. Definitions

  • Landing page - It is the actual webpage people are taken to when they click your ad, which is different than the simplified URL (display URL) that is the webpage address that appears in your ad.
  • Navigation bar/Menu tab - The most common placement of a menu is in the site navigation area or navigation bar and is referred to as a navigation menu. Each link in a menu is called a menu item.
  • Hyperlink - A hyperlink is a word, phrase, or image that you can click on to jump to a new document or a new section within the current document. Hyperlinks are found in nearly all Web pages, allowing users to click their way from page to page.

3. Best practices and policies

3.1 Product details

Structured Snippet Extensions:

  • Appear only for high-quality ads with relevant snippets.
  • Can be associated at the account, campaign or ad group level. Each campaign or ad group can have 20 Structured Snippet Extensions associated with it.
  • Can appear with other ad extensions.
  • Can be imported from AdWords into Microsoft Advertising.
  • Can appear for mainline.
  • Are not allowed for adult advertising.
  • Show the number of snippet values displayed with the header, which is based on the number of characters in the values.
  • Are included in an ad only include once (one headline with 3 - 10 values) per impression. Keep in mind that the ads won't always show Structured Snippets and if they do show Structured Snippets, the format they appear may vary.
  • Are available in the following languages:
  • Danish
  • Dutch
  • English
  • Finnish
  • French
  • German
  • Italian
  • Norwegian
  • Portuguese (Brazil)
  • Spanish
  • Swedish
  • Traditional Chinese

Additionally, advertisers will need to choose between the following 16 Unique Headers before adding the snippet values that relate to the chosen header below:

  • Amenities: All values must list useful features or facilities of a building or place.
  • Allowed examples: Ski Storage, Swimming Pool, Restaurant, Wi-Fi, Gym.
  • Not allowed examples: Rated most comfortable, 100% satisfaction guarantee.
  • Brands: All values must be actual brands offered by the advertiser. Description of numbers of brands as well as promotional text is not allowed.
  • Allowed examples: Nest, Nexus, Chromebook.
  • Not allowed examples: Over 200 Brand names, Save on Brand names, and  Cheap Nexus phones.
  • Courses: All values must be specific classes’ educational provider offers and cannot be degree programs.
  • Allowed examples: Linear Algebra, Creative Writing, Data Structure.
  • Not allowed examples: MBA, M.Ed., and BS.
  • Degree Programs: All values must be courses of study at online or traditional educational institutions. Course duration is not considered as degree programs.
  • Allowed examples: Accounting, Biology, and Psychology.
  • Not allowed examples: 2 years, 4 years, Masters.
  • Destinations: All values must include city, state, or country names only. Promotional text is not allowed.
  • Allowed examples: Las Vegas, New York, Tokyo, Rome.
  • Not allowed examples: Hotels in 200 cities, Visit Paris.
  • Featured hotels: All values must include hotel names for which the advertiser is booking services. Promotional text is not allowed.
  • Allowed examples: Luxury Inn, Lakeside Hotel, Alpine Lodge.
  • Not allowed examples: 10% off Alpine Lodge, Lowest rate on Lakeside Hotel.
  • Goods: All values must include items held for sale in the regular course of business. For example, in a retail store. Promotional text is not allowed. Not to be confused with Services header and Items.
  • Allowed examples: Clothing, Books, Board games.
  • Insurance coverage: All values must be specific insurance coverage types.
  • Allowed examples: Liability, Collision, Comprehensive, Term life, whole life.
  • Not allowed examples: Quotes for all coverages, Save now.
  • Items: All values must be a part of a list, collection or set that is being offered by an advertiser.
  • Allowed examples: Stove, Blender, Dishwasher for Home Appliances Section.
  • Models: All values must list specific models or range of models offered by an automobile manufacturer or dealership only. This should not include names of automobile manufacturer or dealerships, promotional or descriptive text, year of model or any information such as price, and specs.
  • Allowed examples: Corolla, Camry, Prius.
  • Not allowed examples: Corolla L, Corolla LE, Corolla Premium.
  • Neighborhoods: All values must be sub-regions or districts within a city. Names of cities, countries or apartment buildings are not allowed.
  • Allowed examples: Downtown, Hayes Valley, Mission.
  • Not allowed examples: Westminster, Camden, King’s Cross.
  • Services: All values must be services provided by the advertiser. For example, accounting, banking, consulting, cleaning, landscaping, education, insurance, treatment, and transportation services. This header should not be confused with Service Catalog.
  • Allowed examples: Men’s haircut, Women’s haircut, Kid’s haircut.
  • Service catalog: All values must be specific offerings by a service provider (services that have a price attached to them).
  • Allowed examples: Oil change, Smog check, Packing, Auto transport.
  • Not allowed examples: Reviews, Quotes, Rankings, Compare five credit cards.
  • Shows: All values must list names pf performances or actual TV shows. Information about the show, time, or description is not allowed.
  • Allowed examples: Knights, American Dancer, The Voyage.
  • Not allowed examples: At 10am, 2 pm & 5 pm, Tickets required.
  • Styles: All values must be variations of a specific category (for example “cocktail” and “strapless” are types of dresses).
  • Allowed examples: Wingback, French Country, Scalloped.
  • Not allowed examples: Performance Apparel, Yoga Apparel, Athletic Jackets.
  • Types: All values must be variations of a product category (for example if the category is “lights”, the types provide variations of lights).
  • Allowed examples: Pendant lamps, Ceiling lamps, Wall lamps, LED.
  • Not allowed examples: Red, Blue, Green.

Note: You can find corresponding language per structured snipper per header in the Structured Snippet Headers file.

3.2 Policies

  • Structured Snippet Extensions must adhere to the Microsoft Advertising content and style policies.
  • Structured Snippet Extensions will be subject to all editorial policy checks in addition to the following requirements:
  • Snippet values must be relevant to the selected header.
  • Each value within a header must be unique.
  • A minimum of three, and a maximum of 10 snippet values are supported per header.
  • Each value should only include one relevant item.
  • Placeholder text is not supported.

Note: If Structured Snippet Extensions do not meet these guidelines, they will not be approved to show.

  • Products or services included in a Structured Snippet Extension must be offered by the advertiser.
  • Avoid infringing on the intellectual property rights of others. All Structured Snippet Extensions must comply with Microsoft Advertising intellectual property policies.
  • Snippet text must be directly related to the header. For example, a technical school advertising a Network Engineering Program may select the header “Courses”, snippet text may include “Basic Hardware”, “Server Essentials”, “Basics of Networking”, “Intro to the Cloud”, but may not include "Financial Aid".
  • Snippet text cannot be duplicated in the header. For example, "destinations" cannot be used as a snippet under the "Destination" header.
  • The values added under each header should be unique. For example, “Brand: Nike, Reebok, Puma, Adidas” is allowed; but “Brand: Nike, Reebok, Puma, Nike” would not be allowed.
  • Promotional text cannot be used in snippets. For example, "Travel Europe" cannot be used under the "Destination" header. Some examples of approved snippets under this header are, "New York", "Berlin", "Sydney", and "Moscow".
  • Structured Snippet text must not feature any disallowed content per our policies, for example hate speech or defamatory content.

4. ASD coding

ASD Coding for Structured Snippet Extension Creation (Proposal):

Ticket status (choose the one that best fits your case): Completed, pending, or cancelled 

Customer issue:  Optimization Tactic request

Topic: Extensions

Scenario: Structured Snippet Extensions Creation or Update

Problem: Structured Snippet Extensions Creation or Update

Solution delivered (choose the one that best fits your case): Structured Snippet Extensions Created or Updated, Structured Snippet Extensions Request Cancelled, Solution in Progress

Requirements: Structured Snippet Extension Creation (Proposal) requests are coded as Structured Snippet Extensions Creation or Update. These requests are for net-new Structured Snippet Extension.

The BOS advocate is responsible to create a new, separate ticket in the following scenarios:

  • Create then, upload Structured Snippet Extensions. The BOS advocate is responsible to create a new, separate ticket for Uploads service (total of two separate tickets for the request). The upload portion of the work follows the requirements in KB 4039244 AES: Process: Microsoft Advertising: Uploads (Bulk Campaign Management).
  • Implement, Edit or Delete Structured Snippet Extensions. This request is coded as Uploads and follows the requirements in KB 4039244 AES: Process: Microsoft Advertising: Uploads (Bulk Campaign Management).

The BOS advocate is responsible to provide the new case number for each portion of the work to the requestor in the original request to set proper expectation. Then, the newly created ticket/s must have a separate email communication and proper documentation in the ASD Case Notes must be completed.

5. Creating structured snippets

The following steps will need to be followed to successfully complete the Structured Snippet Extensions request:

5.1 Gathering requirements

The following section provides instructions on how you can gather all requirements necessary before proceeding in creating Structured Snippets.

Submission form review

Review the submission form. The requestor will specify which level they want the Structured Snippets to be created – Account, campaign or ad group.

The minimum account level suggestion is four Structured Snippet extensions, unless otherwise specified. In case the minimum suggestion cannot be met due to website limited content and/or due to Microsoft Advertising policy compliance, the BOS advocate should communicate the reason to the submitter to set expectations.

Also, the BOS advocate can suggest to lower the level of suggestion to campaign or ad group level if deemed applicable.

For example, the advertiser has a different website per campaign or ad group; the campaign or ad group has specific offerings.

If the requestor has not provided all the information needed in the submission form, you will need to send a follow-up email (asking for additional information) before you can proceed to the next step.

5.2  Creating Structured Snippet Extensions

The following section provides instructions on how you can create new Structured Snippets after gathering all requirements.

Website review

  1. Review the ad copies in the campaign/ad group. Look for key callouts in the advertising, based on the predefined headers.
  2. Go to the advertiser Destination URL or Final URL and review the landing page.

When creating Structured Snippet Extensions, you should review the customer’s website in detail to get insights into their product/service. This will help to ensure that the Structured Snippets you create are appropriate and highly relevant.

Get more ideas in the Additional Tips section of this article for how to come up with Structured Snippets suggestions.

Structured Snippet Header:

The category of products or services you would like to highlight in your ads. You can choose from 16 unique headers, which are listed under the Product details section of this article.

Structured Snippet Values:

Your specific products or services that relate to the chosen header.

Each Snippet value has a maximum character limit of 25. A minimum of three Snippet values must be entered, with a maximum of 10 snippets supported.

Create Structured Snippet Extensions

  1. Open the standardized Output File template and input the Output File Headers for creating new Structured Snippets. Refer to the Completing the Output File section of this article for the required headers.
 
  • Type: Indicates the level of Structured Snippet Extension.
  • Account Structured Snippet Ad Extension
  • Campaign Structured Snippet Ad Extension
  • Ad Group Structured Snippet Ad Extension
  • Account: Indicates the name of the Structured Snippet’s corresponding account.
  • Campaign: Indicates the name of the Structured Snippet’s corresponding campaign.
  • Ad Group: Indicates the name of the Structured Snippet’s corresponding ad group, if the Structured Snippets will be added on the ad group level. Remove this column if the structured snippet will be added on the campaign level.
  • Structured Snippet Header: Indicates the category of products or services that is seen in the ads for each Structured Snippet Extension.
  • Structured Snippet Values: Indicates the specific products or services that is seen in the ads for each Structured Snippet Extension.
  1. Create a minimum of two and a maximum of 20 Structured Snippets that reference the points you have identified based on landing page URL structure for campaign or ad group level.

In creating the Structured Snippet Values via Output File, each values should be separated by semicolon (;) in Structured Snippet Values column.

Note: The best practice is to use the LEN function for every Structured Snippet Values to ensure these do not exceed the character limit. Be sure to delete the column/s used for performing the LEN function after counting the characters.

8. Quality check

Prior to submission of the Output File, it is important that everything has been checked to ensure accuracy with the suggestions provided.

  1. Check the proposal whether it is Account, Campaign, or Ad group level Structured Snippet Extensions. Also check the headers used.
  2. Ensure that Structured Snippet Values met the character limits by using LEN formula in Microsoft Excel.
  3. Capitalization preference should be checked prior to submission. The two options that we have for the Capitalization Preference are Title Caps and All Word Caps and this is how they should look like:

Title Caps – Amazon.com musical instruments

All Word Caps – Amazon.com Musical Instruments

To avoid having errors when it comes to the Capitalization Preference, here are two (2) Excel formulas that you can use to execute the requestor’s preference:

Title Caps:

=CONCATENATE(UPPER(LEFT(cell,1)),LOWER(RIGHT(cell,LEN(cell)-1)))

[Asset 4502664]

All Word Caps:

=PROPER(cell)

[Asset 4502665]

Note: You still need to manually change the capitalization (if applicable) as you proofread your output file:

  • Acronyms will not be detected by the formula.
  • Terms that have special characters in between will be affected by the formula. (For example, We’Re, He’S).
  • If the Capitalization Preference is specified by the requestor, it should be followed to all applicable fields within the entity being proposed unless advised otherwise by the requestor.
  • Articles (such as the, a, an) and conjunctions (such as is, yet, but) should also be included when using the formula unless advised otherwise by the requestor.
  • Brand names with a unique capitalization format will not be detected by the formula. (For example, Mcdonald’s = McDonald’s).

a. It is a must to check the spelling before sending the proposal file to your requestor.

b. Highlight the column you need to check for misspellings.

c. Go to the Review ribbon then choose Spelling.

d. Select the most appropriate suggestion. Then, select change. Use Ignore Once if the suggestion is not applicable to your ad/ad extension.

e. Once done, close the toolbox. Proceed to the next column and follow the same steps.

  1. Structured Snippet Extension Header and Structured Snippet Extension Values should be highly relevant to the client’s website.
  2. Additional columns for formulas should be removed.
  3. Ensure that the minimum snippet values are met unless otherwise, preferred by the submitter.
  4. Output File should be formatted correctly.

9. FAQ

The following are some of the most commonly asked questions and their corresponding answers.

Q: If the website has only one Structured Snippet Header that can be used, can I still suggest the same Structured Snippet Header within the same Campaign/Ad group?

A: Yes. Structured Snippet Header can be the same but not for Structured Snippet Values. Structured Snippet Values should not be the same within the same Campaign/Ad Group.

Q: If the requestor did not provide the preferred Structured Snippet header on the request, can I proceed with the creation of Structured Snippet Extensions?

A: Yes. You can review the website and suggest structured snippet headers applicable or relevant to their line of business.
