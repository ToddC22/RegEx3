1. Identifying pen model/serial number, compatibility matrix

When attempting to receive support for a Surface pen issue, the customer needs to identify their pen model, serial number, and validate that their pen is compatible with the device and/or features that their pen offers on their platform.

Surface Pen models
Note: Follow How to use your Surface Pen to identify the different pen types.

These are the Surface Pen models currently available:

  • The newest Surface Pen with no clip
  • Surface Pen with single button on flat edge
  • Surface Pen with two side buttons
  • Microsoft Classroom Pen
  • Surface Slim Pen

Surface Pen serial number
The customer also needs to be able to identify the Surface Pen’s serial number (SN). There are a few different ways to locate the pen’s serial number. Use the below techniques that are laid out from easiest to hardest.

Note: The Surface Classroom Pen serial number will be a challenge for some customers to locate. Unless they have the shipping documentation, the customer will likely not have this information.

Important!

It is extremely important to record the pen serial number. You are expected to capture the serial number on every call.

2. Problems with writing (no ink)

Confirming the issue

When attempting to use their Surface Pen the pen does not write on the surface:

  • Surface Pen will not apply ink in applications such as OneNote or Fresh Paint.

  • The Pen can launch Cortana or OneNote or take a screenshot.

  • The pen does not need to be paired to ink.

Confirmation/paraphrase

“Just to make sure I understand your issue: When you attempt to write with your pen on your Surface device, even though it is confirmed paired to the Surface device and other features like app launching work, it will not draw?”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case:

  •   Accessories > Pen > Choose the Appropriate Pen > Problem with Writing (No Ink)

4. Problems pairing pen to Bluetooth

Note: Not applicable to Microsoft Classroom Pen

Confirming the issue

When attempting to use their Surface Pen the pen it does not function or appear to be Bluetooth paired:

  • The pen has LED activity but fails to pair in Settings > Devices > Bluetooth.

  • The pen can ink.

  • The customer has questions or needs information on how to pair the pen to the device.

  • This failed attempt at pairing may or may not yield an error message.

Confirmation/paraphrase

“Just to make sure I understand your issue: When you attempt to pair your pen on your Surface device, even though it can ink, there are issues pairing the device?”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  •   Accessories > Pen > Choose the appropriate pen > Problems Pairing to Bluetooth

Step

How

Why

Notes template:

  • Check known issues.
  • Run Windows Updates or SDT.
  • Press and hold the Pen cap for five seconds. (Document what the pen LED does)
  • Validate what device pen is current paired to.
  • Un-pair the pen from the other Surface or Bluetooth device.
  • Attempt to pair pen normally.
  • Restart Surface and attempt to pair pen.
  • Turn Bluetooth off and on, attempt to pair pen.

This ensures a consistent format for capturing and reviewing case information. Performing this standard procedure helps prevent instances where a step was neglected to be performed.

2. Check known issues.

From KB 4032394 Technical Guide: Surface: Surface Pen (All) known issues, select the customer’s Surface Pen model and check for the issue in the Surface pen (All) section.

There may be a current known issue that the customer is experiencing, with different troubleshooting steps.

Check known issues regularly.

3. Run SDT or Windows Updates.

For SDT: Have your customer go to http://aka.ms/surfacedt.

For WU: Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Updates > Check for updates

Note: Restart the Surface after installing updates.

Updates have fixes to common problems, or issues. Ensuring the customer has all the latest updates on their Surface will ensure they have the best possible experience.

4. Press and hold the Pen cap for five seconds. Document what the pen LED does

There are two issues listed based on outcome:

  • Blinks for two to three seconds (proceed to step 5)
  • Blinks continuously (proceed to step 7 – ready for normal pairing)

Validation step to confirm device functioning. This topic is if there is no LED activity.

5. Validate what device the pen is currently paired to.

Go to Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & Other Devices. Look for any Surface Pen devices.

When the LED blinks for two to three seconds, it means that the pen is paired to a host.

6. Unpair the pen from the other Surface or Bluetooth device.

Find other potential devices the pen could be paired to, and repeat step 5.

If the LED is blinking for two to three seconds, the pen is already paired to a device.

Locate the device and un-pair the Surface Pen.

7. Attempt to pair pen normally.

In Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & Other Devices window: add Bluetooth or other devices > under add a device select Bluetooth.

If the LED is blinking continuously, the pen is receiving power, and attempting to pair with a host device.

8. Restart Surface and attempt to pair pen.

Restart Surface and follow step 7 instructions.

 A device restart may restart the Bluetooth service enabling pen pairing.

9. Turn Bluetooth off and on, attempt to pair pen.

In Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & Other Devices window turn Bluetooth off, then on.

-OR-

Open Action Center and select the Bluetooth tile to toggle it off, then on again and follow step 7 instructions.

Restarting the Bluetooth service may enable pen pairing.

9. Pen Tilt compatibility requirements and verification

Confirming Tilt compatibility

When attempting to use Tilt with the Surface Pen, the following must be in place:

  • Tilt is only available with the V4 Surface Pen with no clip, no other pen utilizes this feature.
  • The following devices are compatible with the feature:
    • Surface Pro 5
    • Surface Pro 6
    • Surface Pro 7
    • Surface Pro X
    • Surface Laptop 3
    • Surface Book 2 (15”)
    • Surface Go
    • Surface Book 2 (13”) (when using updated firmware)
    • Surface Studio (when using updated firmware)

To validate feature is working:

Customer must be running Windows 10 Anniversary update for feature compatibility:

  1. Open Windows Ink Workspace:
  • Open by clicking pen cap on paired V4 pen (using default pen shortcuts)
  • Using taskbar docked icon 
    [Asset 4494562]
  1. Select and open either Sketchpad or Snip & Sketch (released in RS5).
  2. Once the app is open, select the pencil draw tool (will not work with pen or highlighter draw tool), and hold pen at 45-degree angle to screen.
  3. If the pen mark will be broadened (instead of the usual discreet tip) the feature is working as intended
