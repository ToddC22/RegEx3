Summary

Who should follow this procedure?

  • All Microsoft and vendor resources working in a Modern Support Pod for Broad Commercial customers.

What is this procedure about?

 

  • This procedure describes a Modern Support Pod case workflow end to end. There are two major groups of support engineers mentioned in this procedure:
    • Frontline support resources: Frontline Support Engineer (FLSE), in this article is called Ambassador, Vendor Tech Lead (VTL) or SME, Tier-2, Technical Advisor (TA), Team Manager (TM)
    • Backline escalation resources: Support Escalation Engineer (SEE), Escalation Engineer (EE), Embedded Escalation Engineer (EEE)

When should this procedure be followed?

  • When handling O365 Modern Support Pod cases for Broad Commercial customers.
back to top
Key Notes
  • A Modern Support Pod comprises of proficient technical skillsets on Exchange Online, Identity Online, MFP, Outlook Online, SharePoint Online, Skype for Business, OneDrive, OSUB, Yammer and Commerce, group of engineers that collectively own the support experience for customer’s end-to-end, with a mission to resolve 97% of the customer cases in 24 hours. The group of engineers may include but is not limited to: Ambassador, TAs, Team Lead/Team Manager and Escalation resources.
  • There may be regional variances in each Pod based on workload, this procedure is intended to cover an end to end standard workflow for Modern Support Pod cases.
  • Engineers know their Pod members and know how they can reach out to others in the Pod as needed.
  • EMEA ambassadors share the same support procedure for Modern support and Concierge support
  • Work Flow (Applicable to regions who still have T2/T3). For regions who do not have T2/T3, cases are worked between ambassador and SEE.

Procedure

The SE should follow the 4346835: Process: Commercial Case Management Standards procedure along with the following:
New Case
  1. Broad Commercial Office 365 customer calls Office 365 support to open a new case or creates a web case from the Office 365 Admin portal which is stamped for CSS Modern Support.
  2. Case is routed to CSS Modern Support support area. Standard case creation and entitlement procedures apply.  
  3. Ambassador will accept the case from incoming queue based on First in First Out (FIFO) order in the POD.
Taking over the case
  1. Ambassadors will mark themselves Available in Rave and accept case from the “Unassigned” queue with a mission to deliver solution within 24 hours.
    • If Direct Agent Offer (DAO) is turned on, a pop-up will offer a case with option for ambassadors to Accept or Reject in 30 seconds.
    • Rejecting the case or no-action on the pop-up will set ambassador status as Break.
  2. Takes immediate ownership of Case and complete the Acknowledgement procedure within 10 minutes for a call back; 30 minutes for email conversation and 60 minutes if there is no ambassador available:
    1. If the ambassador cannot take the case for any reason, they need to inform the VTL, Pod Manager or Technical Advisor to assign the case to an available engineer immediately.
    2. If customer prefers to work with an ambassador in a different language, use the Office 365 Modern Support Language Transfer in Rave table from the Office 365 redirect resource sheet
    3. Ambassadors are generalist. If there are technical specialties within the POD, work with your managers to reassign case accordingly within POD, this may vary depending on the regions.
    4. If a workload is not a part of Modern support (not yet moved to Rave, such as Azure), ambassadors will need to verify the transfer path in Rave itself, then decide if they need to work with a lead to create a new case in that product's ticketing system and transfer it accordingly following Office 365 redirect resource sheet or MSaaS Support Portal. See article 4488309 to understand product/ticketing system matrix. Close the RAVE case as a duplicate. Some workload may require approval before a case can be transferred.
  3. Customer Contact -
    1. Initial outreach contact must be ambassador initiated, internal note should be documented whether the contact was successful or not.
    2. Customer may provide more than one email addresses at case creation. When sending email to the customer in Rave, support engineers should pull the additional contacts from the case description and add them to the "Cc" line to ensure email communication is sent to the additional email addresses as well.
    3. During business hours, all customer FIRST contact should be by phone within 10 minutes of case creation and transfer in queue, if possible, regardless of the case severity. Unless the customer requested contact by email only.
    4. If initial phone contact does not reach customer, follow up within 30 minutes, if possible, setup an appropriate follow up meeting time. If this cannot be done, standard IR (IR template) needs to be met.
    5. More than one email message to setup a meeting can be sent during the same day if needed in order to reach the customer.
    6. All email correspondence with the customer must be done using the case ticketing system’s email feature.
    7. Initial Response is automatically determined by the Support Engineer’s first OneCloud call or by sending email to the customer within Rave. If Skype is used instead of OneCloud, the Support Engineer needs to email the customer in Rave immediately after the Skype call to ensure IR reporting is accurate.
    8. Follow KB 4457094-Procedure: Unresponsive Customer for unresponsive customers.
Case handling
  1. Scope the case following standard scoping guidelines, using Scope template (refer to the "template" section in 4339885: Office 365 Premier Support in Rave).
  2. If customer asks or indicates the case is a higher severity issue, update the case severity in the case Internal section in the My and Unassigned cases views.  [Asset 4541265]
  3. If the case comes from a Broad Commercial/Professional customer for an on-premise product, a new on-prem case needs to be created. Note that hybrid cases are considered as cloud cases, unless the troubleshooting needs to happen in the customer’s on-prem product, then a new on-prem case is needed. Support can advise customer to create a new case for the on-premise product through Microsoft Commercial online support or call Global Customer Service phone numbers to have a new on-prem cases created. 
  4. The ambassador follows 2524283: Office 365 Due Diligence and starts troubleshooting the case, all cases are managed with the same priority in Modern Support.
  5. VTL/Tech Lead, technical advisor (TA) or Manager review the case at different intervals to confirm the case is on track to meet the 24-hour resolution goal and provide the ambassador with guidance.
  6. The ambassador will inform VTL if the case is not progressing properly.
  7. Engineer/VTL/TL/TA/Manager - All issues, where there is procedure breakdowns or delays to deliver a solution have been identified, need to be documented in case along with the actions taken to bring the case back on track.
  8. Rave is partially integrated with DTM if attachment needs to be sent and received, see KB 2895143 for details.
  9. If a PII (case log) needs to be removed in a Rave case: After confirming with the SEM or TM, send the Rave case number and request details via http://aka.ms/o365SupportHelp as a non-critical issue.

Note: Engineers need to describe which part of the case should be removed in the request to the email alias. This is an example of a description: “Customer accidentally included a password in the case, please remove that password info from the ticket.”

  1. Consults:
    1. If assistance is needed from CSS Service Desk support team, contact TR (Global Customer Service phone numbers) to create a new free SD case asking for Collaboration. See article collaboration for additional details. 
    2. When inbound consult or case transfer needs to happen from CSS to Rave, refer to 4457521: Procedure: Transfer Ownership.
    3. For assistance in the same Pod, the ambassador may use the Pod collaboration procedure via Yammer, IM, phone, distribution list or Microsoft teams. If live collaboration may resolve case, escalation resources may join the call with customer and ambassador.
    4. The ambassador needs to ensure the collaborating engineer/TA/SEE has clarity on the expectations around the help required and the expected proper response time; document who and what time in “Post Internal Comment” section.
    5. If there are delays, the ambassador will contact the Tech Lead and manager of the Pod.
    6. If supplier Tech Lead finds the issue as Technical roadblock, then TL will consult with TA.
    7. If after consulting, engineer finds out the case should be transferred to a different engineer or team within Rave, obtain TA/TL/SEE’s approval in case log and re-assign the Rave case.
    8. Case reassignment (from one ambassador to another) can only be done by a TL/TM/TA who has admin role.

      Notes:
      • For EMEA/LATAM region, ambassador keeps the case until it's resolved, case will not be transferred or escalated to other teams.
      • If escalation resource is needed outside of the POD but within O365 for consult, ambassadors can submit an assistance request and wait for response and work with the responded SEEs from that point on.
  2. Ensure all case taxonomy is correct, paying attention to the following:
    • Document case notes to clearly show all the steps taken to resolve issue. Follow the 2524183: How to apply the Summary Template
  3. Follow the Clear desk/clear screen procedure at all times: 2622104: Clear Desk Clean Screen Security Policy.
  4. Determine if the Risk of data loss applies to the case: 2681495: Procedure: M365 TS: Customer Data Loss Prevention Guidance.
  5. If receiving a customer scheduled callback (CSC) case, follow article  4523301: Process: Using Customer Scheduled Callback (CSC) Feature in Rave to process.
  6. [Asset 4526904]
Escalating a case (This does not apply to EMEA/LATAM region, EMEA/LATAM ambassadors follow Consults procedure in Case handling section only) 
  1. During case consult/review by TL, if a higher technical competency is required then TA will be engaged for next input.
  2. However, If TA identifies that case as escalation candidate, political or Executive escalation, then TA to approval escalation for Tier 2 or Tier 3
  3. If Tier 2 receives the case then s/he to review the case and collect all the required details from ambassador and follow the action plan suggested by TA.
  4. If TA’s inputs do not resolve the issue then TA to engage Support Escalation (SEE), then re-assign the Rave case accordingly.
  5. For NOAM/APGC/JPN who don’t use assistance request to reach out to SEE or EE, regional email distribution list may be used to identify escalation resource if one cannot be identified through consult procedure.

  6. Once SEE picks the case then he/she needs to follow the standard escalation procedure and drive the case for resolution and closure.

  7. Above action would be applicable on high aged cases as well.

The Backline escalation resources of the Pod will take over the case.

  1. In some Pods, case ownership does not change hand. If the escalation resources are not in Rave and need a case to work on, a new case in that product's ticketing system can be created. The ambassador keeps a close relationship with the escalation resources to update customer. Once the issue is resolved, the ambassador can reference the new case number in Rave and close the Rave case as a duplicate.
  2. In NOAM Modern Pods, only TA can transfer Rave case ownership to SEE.
  3. If a customer is reporting an outage or service-related issue, follow the instructions at 2573241: Office 365 SI procedure.
  4. In the event of a CFL/Potential SI/Sev A case and your Pod escalation resources are unavailable, engage the larger group of the SEEs by using the established email address.
  5. Ambassador will inform the customer of the escalation.
  6. After taking ownership of the case, escalation resources will follow the standard procedure to work the case until close.

Ambassadors can re-open the case by performing below actions:

  1. In the Actions pane, Case action, chose Reopen.
  2. In the Reopen screen, chose the Reason, then click Reopen. Click OK on the confirmation screen.
  3. The case status becomes Active and the history shows it.

When a re-opened case is received, work the case based on the following guidelines:

  • If at any time the customer requests to work with the original ambassador or work with someone in their time zone, document the request and transfer the case. DO NOT attempt to influence the customer to transfer the case.
  • If unable to reach the customer due to time zone differences, document the attempt, do not strike, and transfer the case to the original case owner. This should also be done for those cases that are created based on the first guideline listed here.
  • If Issue mentioned during reopening the case is unclear, new ambassador to follow up with customer and drive to understand the issue further and work the case based on the above guidelines. Reach out to your TAs if there is any concerns.

Customer Feedback

  1. Ambassadors can select Get Feedback from the Case Actions dropdown.
  2. If the customer is in call, you can choose to get IVR and Web survey option. Else, you can opt for the Web survey.
  3. The customer will see and can fill the survey in the admin portal Need Help section.
  4. The Support Engineer will be able to see the survey results in Rave after it’s filled out by the customer.
Communication Guidelines

Follow the Communication procedure while handling the case.

Putting a Caller on Hold

During a support telephone call, you may have to put the customer on hold while the SE performs a task.

When keeping control of the call, use the following procedure:

As a courtesy to the customer, the duration of all holds should be as short as possible and the customer should receive frequent updates from the SE on the SE’s progress. These guidelines must be followed:

  • Ask the customer’s permission before putting the caller on hold. For example,

    “May I put you on hold for XX minutes?”

    “If it is agreeable with you, may I put your call on hold for XX minutes?”
  • Explain the reason a hold is necessary. For example,

    “I would like to do a quick search for the information that will help us continue with our troubleshooting.”

    “I would like to consult with another advocate in order to confirm the best course of action.”

    Note: In all cases, tell the customer how long the customer will be on hold.
  • Tell the customer your expected duration for the hold time.
  • Do not keep the customer holding for longer than 5 minutes without a status update. Although the hold time may pass quickly for the SE, for the customer the hold time is probably passing too slowly. For example,
    “I hope to take no more than 5 minutes, but I will definitely reconnect by then and let you know where we stand.”
  • If the customer does not approve the SE to put the call on hold, then the SE should politely explain to the customer how the hold time will let the SE further troubleshoot the customer’s issue. If the customer still does not approve a hold, then tell the customer that they will experience a period of silence while the call is muted to consult. Assure the customer that you are still there during this period of silence, and that you can still hear them.
  • If the customer does approve the SE to put the call on hold: Use the telephone’s Hold function and do not use Mute unless the customer expressly requests it or for conference call situations where the other participants want to continue their conversation during the hold. Use mute for situations where the advocate has to cover up a sneeze, cough, or other background noise, and not as an alternative form of Hold.
  • Reconnect with the customer in either of these ways:
  • If the hold duration is less than 5 minutes: Reconnect immediately but wait the agreed time for the customer to return to the call.
  • If the hold is more than 5 minutes: Check back with the customer at the 5-minute mark or sooner to provide a status update and request permission to extend the hold. Reconnect intervals should be no greater than 5 minutes apart.


Thank the customer for their patience. For example:

“Thank you for allowing me to put you on hold. This is what I was able to accomplish during your hold.”

Use of the term “bug”

Requests from Support to Product Engineering are often referred to as “opening a bug”. This is slang for communicating that a request was made of Product Engineering. It does not necessarily mean that an actual code bug has been found. A request to Engineering can consist of a DCR, RFC, CFL, PSI or an actual code bug fix request.

Support should be careful to not use the slang of the word “bug” when communicating with customers. Instead, support should be discussing open requests as a “request”. Otherwise, customers, who do not know the internal uses of the word “bug” may be misled to think that Microsoft is going to provide a code fix to a request when that is not the case. Yet request can also refer to additional information or a feature request.

Microsoft Delivery Partner Support Relationship Guideline
  • When answering a customer call, our supplier partners are allowed to say, “Hello, you have reached <supplier partner’s name> with Microsoft support” Or something similar.

  • If the customer asks about your direct affiliation or employment with Microsoft, never lie. If asked, Delivery Partners and their employees should identity themselves as distinct from Microsoft, and part of the Delivery Partner team delivering support for Microsoft products and services on behalf of Microsoft. Again, we are not asking you to proactively share this with customers, only share if specifically asked.

  • It is important to understand and represent to our customers that all Delivery Partners supporting Microsoft products, regardless of which company they work for, are part of the same overall team, represent Microsoft in the same manner and share the same key goals of providing world-class service to our customers.

  • If a customer is requesting traction on a case owned by another Delivery Partner, offer to support them by picking up where they left off. If the customer is insistent on working with or contacting the other Partner, engage your own management team to escalate the request internally. Do not try to find contact info of the other supplier on the Internet and give that to the customer. And do not discuss details of employment of other Delivery Partners with customers.
