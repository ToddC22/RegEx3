 1. Applies to

​​​​​​The following advocate roles: All roles
The following markets: All markets (For detailed information about product-specific availability, see 2748142 Informational: M365 Commerce: Markets where Microsoft Online Services are available.)
 

Scenario

A direct, prepaid or Volume Licensing (VL) customer contacts Support requesting a CPE credit for their online service. CPE (Customer and Partner Experience) relates to the goal of becoming more customer-focused and connecting with customers and partners. A CPE credit can be used to help retain a customer who is dissatisfied with a service or with their support experience.

2. Guidance

All online services:
  • CPE credits can be used to help retain a customer who is dissatisfied with the service or with their support experience. Commerce Support is authorized to approve CPE credits for customers according to the thresholds and approved scenarios listed in this document.
  • CPE credits are not to be used as an alternative for a denied Service Level Agreement (SLA) claim.
  • Do not share the credit amount with the customer until it has been applied in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT).
  • Use the pre-tax amount for credits applied in CMAT. The customer’s account will receive the approved credit amount plus any taxes paid.
  • We are unable to credit customers for more than what they have been billed.
  • If a credit request is more than a year and customers claim that they were not aware that the subscription was left active and it was continuously charging on their accounts, only 1 year can be approved.
Note: For the cases that need to escalate to your regional Tier 3 team, please follow the steps in article 2778091 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to escalate a case to Tier 3, Engineering.

3. Procedure

Skype for Business PSTN Calling and Conferencing plans:
Advocates are authorized to approve and apply a CPE credit up to $50 USD (or local currency equivalent) for poor voice quality and other issues. For conditions and steps, see “Credits” in 3164809 Informational: M365 Commerce: Supporting Skype for Business and Teams plans.
Office 365, Yammer, Microsoft Intune, Dynamics 365 and stand-alone plans:
Am I authorized to approve the credit?
Scenario Conditions Advocate boundaries
CPE credits
  • Customer requests financial compensation due to service issues outside of Service Level Agreement outage (known product bugs)
  • Poor customer experience
 Generally, CPE credits should be used as a last resort for:
  • Service Issues:
    • Identified bug (through a Message Center alert or the Service Delivery team)
    • Identified billing error
  • Poor customer experience
    • Incorrect or lack of documentation
    • Upgrade grievances
    • The customer is irate or threatening to leave the service
    • Delay in mitigation of a technical problem
 Direct customers:

Tier 1: Full month credit – maximum $650. 

Tier 3: Maximum of $2500. 

Prepaid customers:

Same thresholds for direct customers except that the end date is extended for the prepaid subscription according to the number of days in the

calculator

.

VL customers (includes Enterprise Agreement/EA and MPSA):
  • VL customers may be eligible for a CPE credit for customer satisfaction reasons as exceptional escalations only for the predefined scenarios noted below. Proof of Microsoft being the source of customer pain and resulting impact is required.
  • These credits are not guaranteed and should never be offered as an alternative to a denied SLA claim. These exceptional issues should follow the standard escalation process to Tier 3.
  • Scenarios potentially eligible for an exception (Exception requests must fall within the following scenarios and are considered on a case-by-case basis. They are not guaranteed.)
  • Feature deprecation forces a customer to accommodate in another way.
  • Microsoft-caused issue required a purchase of services or hardware.
  • Inadvertent service disablement caused by the Fraud detection team.
Direct subscriptions:
  • If ≤ $650 USD (or local currency equivalent), FL will apply the approved credit in CMAT following the procedure in 2996655 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT.
  • If > $650 but <2,500 USD (or local currency equivalent), FL submit credit via CMAT for Tier 3 review. Once approval is obtained, the credit will be automatically applied to the customer’s account.
  • If >2,500 USD (or local currency equivalent), submit a work item to Tier 3 for BG consideration. Upon approval, Tier 3 will update work item and FL will need to submit credit request via CMAT.

Note: If the total amount requested is above your threshold, do not split credit. Follow the procedure above for the amount requested.

For Prepaid subscriptions:
  1. Launch the credit calculator.
  2. Go to the Prepaid CPE tab.
  3. Choose the customer’s plan from the drop-down list in Column A.
  4. Enter the number of seats.
  5. Extend the subscription based on your Tier level in columns C, D, or E. If above Tier 1 authorization, create a work item for Tier 3.

Create a Call Logging Tool (CLT) Request for the Regional Operating Center (ROC) VL Team to request an approved CPE credit

For Enterprise Agreement VL customers:

i. Launch the Call Logging Tool. CLT can be accessed two (2) ways:
  • https://www.explore.ms Under the Tools section, click Call Logging Tool.
  • Direct URL – https://clt.partners.extranet.microsoft.com/CLT
ii. Click New Query to create a new case.

iii. Use the following taxonomy within the CLT case and click Next.

  • Business Stream: Commercial
  • Program Name: Enterprise
  • Type: Transaction
  • Reason: Credits/Adjustments
  • Sub Reason: SLA Credit (use this Sub Reason until choices include CPE credit)
  • Subject: CPE credit request for Volume Licensing customer
 



iv. Instructions: Paste the following completed template into the Comments section of the request.

Company name:

 

Tenant ID:

 

Email address (from CMAT):

 

BG Approver Name:

 

BG Approval (IcM Number/Task#):

 

VL Agreement#:

 

Reason for CPE credit:

 

Service(s) Impacted:

 

Amount of the credit to be applied:

 

Case ID #:

 

Month / Duration of the outage:

 

v. Add to the ticketing system notes both the CLT ticket number and the completed template.

vi. The ROC VL Team will apply the credit, update and close the CLT request once it is processed. Monitor the CLT request and update the ticketing system case once the request is complete.

vii. Notify the customer. The message to the customer is that approved credit memos will be issued within one to two billing cycles.

iii. Add to the ticketing system notes both the CLT ticket number and the completed template.

iv. The ROC VL Team will apply the credit, update and close the CLT request once it is processed. Monitor the CLT request and update the ticketing system case once the request is complete.

vii. Advocates have to differentiate between VL direct (Customer is paying the invoice to MS) and VL indirect (Partner is paying the invoices to MS).

  • For VL Direct, customers will receive a credit memo on their account.
  • For VL Indirect, partners will receive a credit memo on their account. The Partner is responsible for ensuring the credit is passed to the Customer.

viii. What can be shared with partner or customer depends on if they are VL direct or VL indirect, as well as who the submitter of the case is.

What can be shared with VL customers who get a CPE credit approved?
a. For VL direct:

Advocates can share the calculation with the customer, deliver credit note/memo and provide regional Credit Services team email so that team can provide guidelines on how the credit will be used if requested by the customer.

How to get the credit calculation (if the customer or partner is allowed to see it):

Open a new ticket from CLT, ask for the CPE credit calculation and a copy of credit note/memo.
If the customer does not know the partner or LSP, collaborate with your escalation team (Tier 3).

Question on how the credit will be used:

The World Wide Credit Services team will work with and guide the customers throughout the credit clearing procedure. The regional Credit Services team contact information is as follows:

  • Americas: Microsoft Credit Services (mscredit@microsoft.com)
  • EMEA : 
    • MEA Microsoft CS < meamcs@microsoft.com >; 
    • UK Microsoft CS < ukcs@microsoft.com >; 
    • France Microsoft CS < frmcs@microsoft.com >; 
    • Germany Microsoft CS < demcs@microsoft.com >; 
    • Western Europe North Microsoft CS < WENCS@microsoft.com >; 
    • Southern Europe & Benelux Microsoft CS < WESCS@microsoft.com >; 
    • Central & Eastern Europe Microsoft CS < ceecs@microsoft.com >
  • APAC (excludes Japan, India, and China): Asia Credit Services - APAC Collect Team acsapcol@microsoft.com) 
  • Japan: Japan AR Collectors (appprmj@microsoft.com) 
  • India: Asia Credit Services–India Collection Team (incol@microsoft.com) 

    • For all questions on this procedure, engage your escalation teams.

b. For VL indirect:

What can be shared with the customer?

Do not share calculation and credit note/memo with the customer. Advise the customer that by default, the credit will be used for open balance or for against future amounts if the customer wants to know about how the credit will be used. For further questions on calculation, credit note/memo or refund, advise the customer to connect with their Partner.

If the customer does not know the partner or LSP, please work with your escalation team.

What can be shared with the Partner?

The advocate can share the calculation with the partner, deliver credit note/memo and provide regional Credit Service team emails so that team can provide guidelines on how the credit will be used if requested by the partner as. Partner follows the same procedure as a VL direct.

4. Supporting documents
The following scenarios are prohibited for CPE credits. (Note: This is not a complete list; however, it represents many potential requests that are not authorized for a CPE credit.)
  • Customer seeks credit for the unused portion of the service.
  • A partner has many customers who have experienced an outage – bulk credit requests.
  • SLA claim is miscalculated or input incorrectly resulting in an incorrect credit to the customer.
  • The customer has received compensation from an SLA claim, but the amount received does not match the financial loss experienced by the customer.
  • A customer received an SLA credit but is seeking additional CPE credit funding to cover the financial loss of the outage.
  • Customer requests funds to implement disaster recovery architecture after a severe outage.
  • Customer requests credit for performance issues when not all technical issues have been resolved.
  • Tenant mapping issues cause customers to be unable to use the service, or they spent time and money to remediate the issue. The customer requests reimbursement.
  • Customer asks for credit or compensation due to deployment blockers or delays.
  • Auto-update or product release severely impacts the customer’s tenant or customer’s environment/business. Despite advanced notifications about the update/release, the customer would like a credit for the impact.
  • Microsoft makes a change in the service and later discovers it was not communicated and has impacted the customer’s business/environment/service.
  • The country law requires Microsoft to change product specification for which advanced warning was given; however, the changes were not made which resulted in the customer paying for services not rendered.
  • Issues with credit and rebill procedure/actions lead to license reduction.
  • A customer has purchased and activated the wrong plan.
  • A partner has purchased the wrong plan on behalf of their customer.
  • Service was mis-sold and additional/different licenses are needed for the service to function to customer expectations.
  • Field misrepresented an offer/plan.
  • Account Team misquotes customer on a sale, resulting in the customer being forced to pay above what was originally expected.
  • Miscommunication during a deal or deployment where most of the stakeholders are no longer around. The customer insists they were misled in some way, but has little or no evidence to show Microsoft fault as the original stakeholders have left the company.
  • Microsoft acquires a company which impacts the way a customer is billed for a service. No communication was sent out about the billing change. The customer only discovers they have been charged more than normal when they receive their invoice. The customer wants a credit.
  • Microsoft did not or could not provide adequate information, documentation, or remediation, but services cannot be used to remediate the situation.
  • Customer issue requires Microsoft Support to work with the 3rd-party support team. Due to communication or partnership breakdown, the customer suffers either with product performance, staffing, IT, etc. which causes additional costs to the customer. 
  • Compensation for after-the-fact 3rd party services required to remediate a Microsoft-fault issue.
