Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to support Office 365, Microsoft 365, Dynamics 365 (O365, M365, D365), and Volume Licensing (VL) scenarios that may come to ASMS.

  • Office 365 (O365) Microsoft Office 365 is a Software as a Service (SaaS) solution that includes Microsoft Office and other services, such as email and collaboration, from Microsoft’s cloud server.
  • Microsoft 365 (M365) is a subscription service aimed at providing improved integration and security between Microsoft products. M365 comes bundled as Office 365, Windows 10 Pro, and Enterprise Mobility + Security (EMS).
  • Microsoft Dynamics 365 (D365) is a cloud-based business applications platform that combines components of customer relationship management (CRM) and enterprise resource planning (ERP), along with productivity applications and artificial intelligence tools.
  • Volume Licensing is the practice of selling a license authorizing one computer program to be used on many computers or by many users.
     

Support Scenarios

Below are common misroute scenarios that ASMS receives regarding O365 and VL subscriptions. ASMS does not directly support these scenarios, but can assist in routing the customer to the proper support team.

  • Credit/refund for O365, M365, D365, and licenses
  • Cancellations for O365, M365, D365
  • Assistance with purchase
  • Extend AAD Premium licenses
  • License migration (O365 Subscription migration) from one tenant to another

Note: TAMs who reach out to ASMS requesting assistance with O365 related scenarios should be referred to TSG 2852289. 

O365/M365/D365

Based on the customer’s scenario, use the following guidance to assist the customer to the correct support channel.

Note: For the below scenarios that require TA/PTA engagement, you may consider educating the customer on how to directly contact O365 in the future while awaiting response from the TA/PTA.

  • Inform the customer we have assessed the scope of their request and it appears to be Office 365 related.
  • Let them know we are currently working to direct their request to the respective team which can provide better assistance.
  • Educate the customer that should they face a similar issue in the future, they can reach out to the Office 365 team directly by visiting: O365 direct support. If they are an admin: O365 Business Support Admin Help.

O365 Concierge
For customers you identify as having O365 Concierge support enabled in CMAT, send a task to your TA/PTA who will engage the aligned TAs. Those TAs will then reach out to the O365 Concierge team to determine next steps.

Note: Your task wil be updated accordingly, based on the response from the Concierge team. 

Data Protection
Cases where the Global Admin is no longer part of the company or is unable to login should be sent to the Data Protection Team.

  • Change the routing product on the service desk case to Office Products/Commerce O365/User Account Management to mirror a RAVE case.
  • If needed, additional engagement and troubleshooting guidance can be found in the Data Protection Wiki. 

Note: The O365 Security and Compliance team will handle security center related issues.

Volume Licensing (VL)

For scenarios where it is confirmed as VL scope (for a list of VL support boundaries see TSG 4524182) or it is unclear whether it is a VL issue or not, send a task to your TA/PTA (using the template in TSG 2929022).

In return, your TA/PTA will contact the aligned OneVL TAs who will determine next steps.

Note: While waiting on a response from the TAs, you may consider educating the customer on how to engage with VL directly in the future.

  • Inform the customer we have assessed the scope of their request and it appears to be Volume Licensing related.
  • Let them know we are currently working to get this request directed to the respective team which can provide better assistance.
  • Educate the customer that should they face a similar issue in the future, they can reach out to the Volume Licensing team directly by visiting: https://licensing.microsoft.com/Customer/ContactUs/.


Engagement and Escalation Path

For issues where it is unclear whether it is O365 or VL scope, send a task to your TA/PTA and they will work with the OneVL aligned TAs to determine next steps.

See TSG 2929022 for specific guidance on engaging your TA/PTA.

Note:

  • O365 Commerce aligned TAs can create an O365 case on behalf of the customer via CAP. If there is a CritSit case or Urgent escalation, engage your TA/PTA. All TAs should have access (read-only) to view and provide the status of RAVE cases.
     
  • O365 Commerce aligned TAs are also a part of CSS Global Enterprise Commerce team’s channel which we can use for urgent scenarios. Send a task to your TA/PTA to engage the aligned TAs.

Note: In addition to ASMS engaging O365, O365 may also need to contact ASMS. They may do so by email to WASMSEsc and providing the information detailed in TSG 4015188.
