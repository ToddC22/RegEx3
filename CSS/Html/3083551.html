1. Basics of purchase and return

1.1 Type of purchase

  • Physical goods purchase

A tangible physical product that is shipped to the customer.

Example: Surface, Xbox Console, Xbox controller, Mouse, or Keyboards.

  • Digital goods purchase

An intangible product that is distributed electronically.

Examples: Games, Apps, Game downloadable content, or consumable items.

  • Subscription (Recurring) purchase

Subscriptions such as monthly or annual.

Examples: Xbox Live Gold, Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, Microsoft 365, OneDrive, Assure, or Ad-Free Outlook.

Return to top

1.2 Definitions of refund, return, cancel

  • Return (Including Return Line Item, Return Order for cart purchase)

Return of a product for a refund. Return requires return of physical product or revocation of product license (for example, digital goods).

  • Refund

Credit received for a past purchase. Refund is used for a situation where the product/service is not returned/revoked, but providing just a refund – for example, service down, crediting the fee (shipping for wrong shipping).

  • Cancel (Subscription)

This is used for returning recurring purchased type which are active subscriptions. This will return the subscription and may provide a refund option if customer is eligible. There are multiple options to perform subscription cancellation.

See more details in KB 4053336 TUG: MST: Services.

  • For transactions where Microsoft did not handle the billing – refer the customer to the third-party that handled the billing.
  • Skype – see KB 2986071 Procedure: Skype: Refund explanations and exceptions.
  • Financed purchases – customers should be referred to the lending entity. 

For more about Surface or Xbox hardware repair refunds, see the articles below:

  • Surface: KB 2791320 Informational: Surface: Exchange and fulfillment landing page
  • Xbox: KB 2961825 Procedure: Xbox: Handling hardware refund requests

2.2 Out of scope fees

Bank fees (overdraft fees/international transaction fees)

Microsoft does not refund or reimburse for overdraft or international transaction fees. Overdraft fees are incurred from the financial institution, any fee disputes must be directed there.
If the customer experiences a chargeback reversal, you need to provide the proper entitlement for the disputed item.

3. Guideline overview

3.1 Scope

This Microsoft Store return and refund guideline applies to:

All consumer Microsoft storefronts:

  • On the web
  • On Xbox
  • On Windows

All product categories:

  • One-time digital purchases – for example, digital content, digital gift cards.
  • One-time physical purchases – for example, devices, accessories, and software.
  • Recurring subscriptions – for example, subscriptions and passes.

3.3 Standard return guideline: Microsoft Store terms of sale

Microsoft Store will accept returns and exchanges for eligible products up to 30 days (depending of regulation in country, some cases up to 14 days) of the date of purchase or download (or up to 30 days - depending of regulation in country - of release/launch date for pre-orders) for any reason including but not limited to, customer satisfaction, damaged product, defective product, or product dead on arrival. Note: There may be extended return timeframes for certain devices and times during the year.

Eligible products: Devices, accessories, and packaged software. This includes ESD software (where product key is provided digitally and is visible on the Microsoft Store order in MST).

Note: Packaged software and games must be returned with their seal intact and must include all media and product keys.

Physical Microsoft/Xbox gift card follows this guideline. Return/refund can be done for the full amount only. Once a gift card is used or redeemed, it is no longer returnable. For procedures and Make It Right, refer to the KBs below:

  • Microsoft Return/Refund procedure: KB 2988925 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Physical Products/Software Products: Returns & Exchange
  • Make It Right procedure: KB 4465058 Procedure: Microsoft Store Make It Right.

Some items are not eligible for return unless otherwise provided by law or by a specific product offer:

  • Digital apps, games, in-app content and subscriptions, music, movies, TV shows, and associated content
  • Gift cards and service/subscription cards (for example, Skype, Xbox Live)
  • Products that have been personalized or customized
  • Special order products, if not part of a Store promotional offering
  • Random access memory (RAM) products
  • Services that have been performed or consumed
  • Clearance items or those marked with a designation such as "Final Sale" or "Non-Returnable"

When you make a qualifying return, Microsoft will:

  • Credit the full amount, that doesn't include original shipping and handling charges (if there were any).
  • Not refund shipping/handling if customers are unhappy with the license agreement or warranty.
  • Typically process the refund in approximately three to five business days, any delays that may occur when receiving the refund should be taken to the financial institution first.

All refunds will be applied to the same account and using the same method of payment used to place the order (unless you choose a Store credit (CSV) in the amount of the refund).

Qualifying exceptions to the Microsoft Store Terms of Sale: Return Policy:

  • KB 4338530 Guideline: Return/refund for non-gaming related digital orders
  • KB 4338529 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Exception based cancel and return for recurring subscriptions

3.4 Regional exception 

This guideline does not apply to Australia. Consumers have the right to repair, replace, or refund in certain situations. Refer the customer to https://aka.ms/acl. Where a consumer has that right, you must follow the Australia Consumer Law guideline. For more information, see KB 4100081 Guideline: Microsoft and the Australian and New Zealand Consumer Law.

3.6 Refund issues

Currency conversions during purchase and refund

If you receive a question from a customer asking why a refund is a different amount than the original charge, check to ensure that the charge and refund amounts are identical in MST.

If the amounts are identical, provide the amount and currency of the charge and refund to the customer and explain that these are the amounts that were processed by Microsoft. For details regarding why the charge appears differently on their statement, the customer will need to reach out to their financial institution. It is possible that a currency conversion or other surcharge occurred while the transaction was being handled by their financial institution which could account for the difference.

If the amounts are not identical, attempt to verify if a partial refund was provided. If you are unable to determine why the refunded amount was different in MST, engage Empowered Resolution Advocates (ERA) team (for Japan/Korea/China, engage ACA) or Xbox Rangers for PayOps Team engagement.

PayPal refund not received

If it has been less than 30 days since the refund was issued or payment was made, advocates need to:

  • Request that the customer contacts PayPal support (who will ask them to verify their email address and bank account).
  • Have the customers confirm the email address and check the email account to claim the payment.
  • The customer may have received the refunds not on the original source and they need to check PayPal Balance.

Note: For instructions on how to confirm an email address, refer the customer to PayPal Customer Service: https://www.paypal.com/smarthelp/contact-us or +1 (888) 221-1161.

  • The customers won’t receive the refund until they confirm the email address.
  • The email address must be confirmed before you can make it the primary email address on the PayPal account.

Note: Ensure that the email they sent the refund to is added to their PayPal account and is confirmed.

If it has been over 30 days since the refund was issued or payment was made:

  • If payment is past 30 days, then the refund will be returned to Microsoft as unclaimed. You can perform the refund in MST against the charge incurred.

4. How to respond to a customer who is not eligible for a refund

Note: The following information applies regardless of whether or not the customer has used a subscribed service.

If a customer requests a refund for their subscription or purchase, but the account is not eligible, follow the guidance below:

  1. Acknowledge the customer’s request and inform our current procedure limitations.
    Messaging:

"We appreciate the business you are bringing to Microsoft and we deeply value you as a customer. Please understand that based on our procedure, this charge cannot be reimbursed."

  1. Tell the customer about the features of the product and encourage the customer to continue the service.

Customers may view the Microsoft Services Agreement at microsoft.com/servicesagreement/.

Direct the customer to paragraph nine, sections D and E, of the Microsoft Services Agreement, which indicates that Microsoft will automatically renew their subscription (and charge their account), unless the subscription is cancelled before the automatic renewal date.

Note: All customers receive recurring subscription renewal notices by email 30 days in advance of the subscription renewal, except for the Xbox Live month-to-month subscription.

The Microsoft Services Agreement may also be reviewed on an Xbox One console as follows:

  1. From the Dashboard, go to Settings.
  2. Go to the Sign-in, Security, & Passkey section and select Legal Documents.
  3. Select Microsoft Services Agreement.

Note: Section e. “If we make an error on your bill, you must tell us within 90 days after the error first appears on your bill.”, and section f. “If you believe that Microsoft has charged you in error, you must contact us within 90 days of such charge” - This 90 days applies unless the customer is within a region where consumer governing bodies require that we honor a refund for erroneous charges beyond 90 days. The customer needs to provide evidence (a website or official document) that we are required to refund them beyond 90 days due to regional agreements. These requests should be reviewed with ERA and/or Team Leads on a case by case basis prior to granting any exceptions.
