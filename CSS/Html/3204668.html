1. What is optimization consultation?

Optimization consultation is a complimentary service offered to existing customers in the scale segment who have the potential and opportunity to improve or increase their account performance. This service provides advertisers with customized optimization recommendations and assistance with implementing changes to improve their campaign health and performance. The service includes a one-session phone call with the customer, some potential tactical work conducted by you after the call, and an exit email with relevant resources to conclude the service engagement.

Return to top

1.1 Program description

The offer for optimization consultation is made via a marketing campaign with an invitation to contact our service team, referral from another team or through a personalized call from a service specialist.

When a targeted customer accepts a marketing campaign’s invitation to contact the services team, or when you proactively reach out to a pre-qualified customer who is interested in receiving the service, the person who engages with the customer delivers the service to that customer.

Service advocates are also empowered to invite customers they previously treated with another service (for example, onboarding consultation) to receive the optimization consultation. This service is conducted by service advocates and available only in the markets listed in section 1.2 below.

1.3 Eligibility 

Many active Microsoft Advertising customers are eligible to receive optimization consultation.

1.3.1 Eligibility requirements:

  1. Customer has a Microsoft Advertising account that is or was recently performing (performing means they have impressions or clicks in the last 30 days to ensure opportunities can be identified).
  2. Customer has a live business website compliant with Microsoft Advertising’s security and editorial requirements.
  3. Customer’s business model is compliant with Microsoft Advertising editorial guidelines.

1.3.2 A customer is NOT eligible for receiving service if:

  1. The account is flagged with a fraud status that will remain upheld.
  2. The account's QBR penalty or business model prohibits it from serving in the auction.
  3. Customer has an account manager or is currently engaged with another advocate (per UCM Advocate Dashboard).
  4. Customer’s language or time zone is not supported by Microsoft Advertising services.

Important notes:

  • When a customer contacts you on the services line and is not eligible for the service, treat it as a support case and code the ticket accordingly. Be sure to remind the customer of our support center contact info so that the next time they need help, they reach the right team. This is one circumstance where a service advocate will select Standard Support in the Program field of their ASD ticket.
  • In cases where a customer who was previously treated with optimization consultation, contacts you with a support issue, and it has been over 30 days since the treatment and the account has new optimization opportunities, you may deliver the optimization consultation service again to that customer. Be sure to create a new ticket for this treatment. If you already know the customer’s advertising goals, you may skip the interview portion of that workflow.
  • If a customer has been previously treated with onboarding consultation within the last 30 days and is now on an optimization outbound list or calls inbound for optimization help, you can proceed with treating them with optimization consultation.
  • In cases where a customer calls in and has been treated with optimization consultation in the last 30 days, you can still treat them and suggest opportunities. If you do not see any opportunities because the optimization has been too recent, tell the customer that you’d like to wait one to two weeks to see how the recent changes impact their campaign. You can schedule a call or an appointment for one to two weeks out to treat them at that time.
  • In cases where an outbound optimization lead is assigned to you in UCM that was on the UCM outbound lead list in the previous month, reject for duplicate lead.

2. Service advocate workflow

2.1 Customer engagement

2.1.1 Inbound calls

  • When a customer calls into the service team’s queue and requests to receive optimization service or you hear optimization consultation, prepare to deliver the optimization consultation service.

  • If the customer has an open SR, follow the standard procedure and either assist the customer or transfer to the advocate working the case.

  • If a new case, confirm customer is calling to get assistance getting started with Microsoft Advertising campaigns and verify the advertiser per standard procedures. Use the optimization consultation call guide (see section 7) to assist with the conversation.
  • Create an ASD ticket and accurately populate required fields including account number.
  • Assign yourself to the customer in UCM’s MyQueue (see instructions below). The customer will be added to an initiative called “Optimize Cons_GL_CSS_Ongoing”.
  • Move to conducting optimization consultation.

2.1.2 Outbound calls

  • See KB 3177673 Tool User Guide: Microsoft Advertising: UCM Agent Dashboard for your customer assignments aligned to the “Optimize Cons_GL_CSS_Ongoing” initiative.
  • Create an ASD ticket for all customers assigned to you and accurately populate required fields including account number.
  • Preview the customer’s account and website to determine their business model and confirm eligibility for the service.
    • If the customer is not eligible (see above), reject customer from UCM My Queue, correctly code ASD ticket with reason for ineligibility, and close SR.
  • Navigate to UCM and briefly review if there are any opportunities provided in the UCM Opportunities tab.
  • Call customer and pitch optimization service (using optimization consultation call guide, see section 7). If you are never able to connect with the customer prior to engaging them, reject the customer in UCM and select the max attempts reason from the drop down.
    • If the customer is not interested, release from UCM, code ASD ticket with reason and close SR.
    • If you don’t reach the customer, leave a message and send an email (using the optimization followup2voicemessage template, see section 7). Try one more time (phone with follow up email) to reach the customer before releasing from UCM and closing the SR.
    • If the customer schedules an appointment for a later date, keep the ticket open until you’ve completed the service.
    • If the customer is ready to receive service, move to conducting optimization consultation.

2.1.3 Personal invitation from advocate

  • If you recognize that a customer you treated in the past is a good candidate for optimization consultation, reach out and invite the customer to receive the service.

  • Assign yourself to the customer in UCM’s MyQueue (see instructions below). The customer will be added to an initiative called “Optimize Cons_GL_CSS_Ongoing”.

  • Create an ASD ticket for the customer. Accurately populate required fields including account number.

2.1.4 Appointment through scheduling tool

  1. A marketing campaign called “DASH” will be sent out to customers who meet the eligibility requirements that directs customers to schedule an appointment through the scheduling tool.
  2. You will be notified of any appointments made through the scheduling tool per a notification.
  3. An ASD ticket will be created automatically when the appointment is made. Your lead will ensure this is assigned to you. Accurately populate required fields including account number.
  4. Preview the customer’s account and website to determine their business model and confirm eligibility for the service.
  5. If the customer is not eligible (see above) correctly code ASD ticket with reason for ineligibility and close SR.
  6. Using the template in section 7, send the customer the appointment reminder email. Manage any changes to the appointment schedule as needed.
  7. At the time of appointment, when you can connect with the customer, self-assign to your My Queue (see instructions below) and select the Optimize Cons_GL_CSS_Appt Set Tool_Ongoing initiative.
  • If you don’t reach the customer, leave a message and send the missed appointment reminder email. Try one more time to reach the customer (using the missed appointment #2 email) before rejecting from UCM and closing the SR.
  1. If you are never able to connect with the customer because they cancel or no show to an appointment, reject the customer in UCM and select the max attempts reason from the drop down.
  2. During appointment, pitch optimization service (using the optimization consultation call guide, see section 7).
  • If the customer is not interested, reject from UCM as proposal not accepted, code ASD ticket with not interested reason and close the SR.
  • If the customer is interested in the service but prefers to have the consultation at a different time, schedule a call-back appointment (preferably within a week), and set the appointment or call back reminder on your Outlook calendar for one or two days before the appointment. Keep the ticket open until you’ve completed the service.
  • If the customer is ready to receive service, move to conducting optimization consultation.

2.1.6 My queue engagement

Steps to engage customers in My Queue:

  1. Ensure the customer has been assigned to your My Queue.
  2. Select the check box next to the customer name.
  3. Select the Engage button located directly above the Customer Name column.
  4. A message box will appear. Confirm you want to engage the customer by selecting Engage.
  5. The customer's status will now show as "engaged" in the customer list of My Queue.

Steps to self-assign a customer in My Queue:

To self-assign a customer to My Queue, complete the following steps:

  1. Locate the customer in UCM.
  2. Select the checkbox next to the customer name.
  3. Select the Engage button.
  4. Select the initiative Optimize Cons_GL_CSS_Ongoing.
  5. Confirm the selection and the customer will appear in My Queue.

Prioritizing customers

For optimization consultation, you should prioritize the list of customers by highest spend based on the last three months.

  • Set the time period to last three months
  • Ensure you have the column “Spend” in your My Queue as a selected attribute.
  • Sort by current spent, largest to smallest.

Important notes before engaging the customer in My Queue

Read the below notes on UCM My Queue engagement

  • Customers should only be engaged once you’ve established live contact with the customer – email doesn’t count until the customer replies!
  • Selecting Engage locks a customer in your My Queue until treatment is complete.
  • If self-assignment is not available, locate your manager to assign the account. When managers assign customers, customers will need to be manually engaged; see steps below to engage customer in My Queue.
  • If self-assignment is available and the customer is not already engaged with another advocate (as determined from UCM), you may take ownership of that engagement by self-assigning the customer to your My Queue. Self-assigned customers will be automatically engaged.

UCM engagement history & alerts

When looking at customers, it is important to understand their engagement history with Microsoft Advertising marketing and services and support. Understanding what tactics, emails and other engagements have happened can help you to formulate your pitch.

  • When you are in the customer view in UCM, start with viewing the engagement history of the customer. Engagement history can show you any previous engagement from marketing or other services or support advocates. This may help you see tactics the customer has previously implemented or marketing campaigns that were sent to them. Simply go into the customer view and select the Engagement History tab. You will be able to see the date of the engagement, type of engagement, account the engagement was applied to, the user, a brief description, details, and other fields you can select by adding columns.

Clearing alerts

Clearing alerts is a benefit to the customer to ensure that their account health is good. To see alerts, go into UCM and select the alerts tile or the alerts tab.

  • Selecting the link icon in the tab will take you to the Microsoft Advertising UI to see the alerts.
  • Going into the alerts tab will give you an overview of the alerts that includes the account number, alert date, alert type, severity (critical or standard), and description.
  • You can also add other attributes by going into Columns and selecting the desired indicators.

2.2 Conducting optimization consultation

  1. Tell the customer that we schedule 30-minute appointments for consultations, but if they need more time, you are happy to accommodate so they get what they need from the service.
  2. Set expectations that you will start by asking a few questions so you can learn about their business and advertising goals, and that there may be some follow-up activities addressed via email after you end the call.
  3. Ask the customer appropriate questions from the Get to Know You Interview Guide (see section 7) to help you understand the customer’s goals and prioritize the tasks you will implement during the service.
    Note: if you have treated this customer in the past and already know their advertising goals, skip this step.
  4. Check the UCM Advocate Dashboard for alerts and opportunities; determine your own ad hoc opportunities based on assessing the account and customer needs.
  5. Using the Service Tactic Checklist (see section 7), create a list of tactics to address during the service (you’ll want to keep this list for tracking purposes).
  6. Review all opportunities and recommendations with your customer; confirm which tactics the customer wants to implement or learn more about.
  7. Conduct any education tactics (for example, demo of campaign management tools). Use the Log Me In tool for a rich and interactive demonstration.
  8. Guide customer through implementing the opportunities that can be easily done in the UI (for example, tasks on the Opportunity Tab).
  9. Set expectations with your customer about activities you plan to do after the call and that you will let them know when the work is complete. Obtain customer’s verbal approval for you to make changes to the account.
  10. Inform the customer that you will email links to resources that will provide tips for successfully managing campaigns (you can pick and choose appropriate items from your Library of Links (see section 7) that are aligned to the customer’s interests, business model and goals).
  11. End the call and update your ASD ticket details. Be sure to select improved campaigns and whether a UCM opportunity was pitched or a non-UCM Custom recommendation was pitched.
  12. Track your opportunity pitches and accepts in the UCM dashboard per instructions outlined.
  13. [If applicable] Send requests to BOS and/or execute any offline tasks you committed to doing. Use child tickets and the BOS request process.
  14. Once all items on your approved tasks list have been completed, craft and send a customized exit email to the customer (use Consultation Service Exit email template, see section 7).
  15. Release customer in UCM, then update and close ASD ticket.

4. Definitions

Program terminology and definition guide:

  • BOS (Back Office Support): Team of non-customer-facing advocates who execute optimization tactics on behalf of AMs and Service Advocates working with customers.
  • Consultation (when appended to a program title): Service typically includes one consulting session plus off-line work by advocate and a customized email with a recap and links to resources to conclude the service.
  • Managed services: Program for premium Microsoft Advertising advertisers where they are awarded a dedicated AM or team of AMs to manage their Microsoft Advertising account.
  • Scale segment: Microsoft Advertising customers who do not have a dedicated Microsoft Account Manager (Also known as self-serve).
  • Service advocate: Onboarding and optimization specialist dedicated to conducting a variety of planned service programs in our Microsoft Advertising Offerings portfolio via inbound and outbound channels.
  • Support advocate: Customer support representative skilled in troubleshooting technical issues with Microsoft Advertising and assisting customers via inbound phone or chat.
  • UCM MyQueue (KB 3177673 UCM Advocate Dashboard): My Queue is the individual UCM user’s view of customers that are assigned to them and what treatment they should receive. My Queue is accessible through UCM AWS.

5. Value propositions

5.1 Pitching the optimization consultation service

When pitching this service, it is important that you prepare and personalize your pitch.

  • Identify at least one opportunity in the account that you can use to “hook” the customer.
  • Use your own personal style when pitching- show your personality.
  • Show your enthusiasm and confidence in your Microsoft Advertising knowledge.

Keep these things in mind when you are delivering your pitch:

  • “Because Microsoft values your business and we want to show our appreciation for being a loyal customer, I’d like to offer you a free campaign optimization that includes a review of your account, recommendations to improve ad performance and a personalized tutorial of features and tools that can help you maintain a productive and competitive ad campaign.”
  • “I can show you some exciting features and teach you how to use tools that will save you time with managing your campaigns and can help to boost your ad performance.”
  • “We’ll review your account to identify opportunities where you can boost your campaign performance with just a few changes.”
  • “I can help optimize your ad campaigns for better performance so that you’re getting the most from your investment with Microsoft Advertising.”
  • “Optimizing your campaigns specifically for Bing’s audience can increase your market share over your competitors.”

6. Handling objections

As you pitch the service offer to customers, you may hear objections. Effectively addressing objections may turn a disinterested prospect into an enthusiastic customer who is receptive to receiving your assistance improving their Microsoft Advertising campaign performance.

Here are some example objections you may hear with suggested responses to get customers more interested in your service offer:

Customer Objection: Google is better.

Messaging: “While Google’s search engine has been around longer, Microsoft is continually improving the Microsoft Advertising platform and tools to make things easier for our customers. With Bing, you’ll reach millions of unique searchers who are not on Google. And the best news is that our audience is also significant in online purchase activity! comScore confirms that searchers on Bing spend more than Google searchers.”

Customer Objection: I already know how to manage a Search campaign.

Messaging: “That’s great! I can also show you some advanced tools, useful reporting options and resources like web help, forums and webinars that will help you stay up to date on the latest industry insights and Microsoft Advertising news.”

Customer Objection: What if I don’t like the outcome of the changes you recommended?

Messaging: “Though this is rare, we want to know if you don’t like the outcome of the changes. Our goal is to help you improve your campaign, not hinder it. We use our search expertise to recommend options that we think will help optimize your campaign performance, though we can’t guarantee results. If, at any time, you want to go back to how your campaign was prior to optimization, just give us a call and we can help make those changes.”

Customer Objection: I get more traffic / impressions / share with Google

Messaging: “It’s true that the market share for Google is much larger. That is why Microsoft has been investing in expanding the Bing market share over the past few years. Through syndicated partnerships and our Search Alliance with Yahoo, we’ve increased market share significantly. In fact, comScore announced recently that Bing has reached 33% share in the US, or 1/3 of all search queries, and we expect that will continue to grow.”

Customer Objection: Click-Through Rate

Messaging: “Click-through rates can be affected by various factors, including ad position, targeting settings and how well your ad resonates with your target audience. To improve your CTR, we offer a variety of campaign management tools, seasonal and industry insights, tailored recommendations on the Opportunities Tab and personal assistance with Microsoft Advertising experts whenever you need help.”

Customer Objection: Results less than Google

Messaging: “The Bing audience is different from Google’s and in the US, Bing and Yahoo has 33% of the market share. We recommend you optimize your campaigns specifically for our audience of 581M unique searchers worldwide, especially because searchers on Bing and Yahoo spend more than Google searchers worldwide (The Yahoo Bing Network includes Microsoft and Yahoo Core Search sites in the US). It is definitely worth your time to maximize the opportunity with Bing’s audience to get better results.”

For detailed metrics about non-US markets, see the comScore kit in KB 3179894 Key Selling Points.

Customer Objection: Conversions

Messaging: “Several factors can impact conversion rates. Reducing irrelevant clicks, reaching your target audience, making it easy for customers to take action on your webpage and ensuring your ads clearly represent your business will help increase your conversions. To understand what’s limiting your conversion rate, we offer a variety of tools and personal assistance with Microsoft Advertising experts whenever you need help.”

Customer Objection: I don’t have time /It’s too much work

Messaging: “That is why we are here to help! We will work with you to optimize your campaigns, so you don’t have the workload that you do with some ad platforms. Although it takes time and energy to test online advertising strategies, it is worth the investment to determine what gives you the best ROI. For help maintaining a healthy and competitive ad campaign, you can receive customized tips on your Dash, import your Google campaigns directly into Microsoft Advertising, implement recommended changes on the Opportunities Tab or contact our support team for assistance whenever you need it.”

Customer Objection: You don’t know my business

Messaging: “You are the expert when it comes to your business, and I am an expert with the Microsoft Advertising platform. If you help me understand your goals for online advertising, I can help you customize your Microsoft Advertising campaigns to get the best results.”

Customer Objection: I will do this later

Messaging: “We know you are busy, and though this may seem time-consuming, we are here to help you. We don’t want you to miss out – comScore announced recently that Bing and Yahoo has reached 33% market share in the US, and we expect that will continue to grow. That’s exactly why you should consider optimizing your campaigns now– so that when you are ready, your advertising strategy is in place and waiting to successfully compete in the auction.”

For detailed metrics about non-US markets, see the comScore kit in KB 3179894 Key Selling Points.

Customer Objection: I don’t want to spend more money

Messaging: “Optimizing your campaign doesn’t mean spending more money, it means utilizing the tools you have through Microsoft Advertising to spend your advertising dollars more effectively. You only pay when your ad is clicked – and we want to help you make your ads the most effective they can be. When an advertising strategy is effective, you can invest more knowing you will also make more profit. That is exactly why you should optimize your campaigns to reach your target audience and invite them to click your ad and take your desired action. You can make several changes to your campaigns that will redistribute your budget and costs rather than increasing them.”

Customer Objection: Editorial issues

Messaging: “Microsoft is dedicated to providing the best possible experience for both our advertisers and our searchers, so we deliver the most relevant search results we can. At times, an ad or keyword that is rejected for violating our editorial policy merits an exception and can be appealed. For those rejections that can’t be appealed, we can find alternative options that align to our editorial policy.”

8. Additional tips and best practices

You may sometimes initially reach someone on the phone who is not the person that is managing or in charge of the Microsoft Advertising account. Below are some best practices and techniques for finding the right person, leaving great messages, and pitching the service effectively.

Activity

Best practices

When making outbound calls from Service lead lists and leaving a voicemail.

When you get the customer's voicemail, leave a message that mentions the email is coming in 5 min and to which email address it’s coming to. This lets the customer know you are legit.

Add the specific time of your next call attempt: "Sorry I missed you. I will try you again tomorrow at 10AM Pacific time."

Be casual and friendly in the voice message and use more professionalism in the follow-up email.

When trying to get past the gate keeper when making outbound calls for any service program.

When trying to get passed the gatekeeper, use the decision maker’s name: “Dan Brown requested a consultation to get set up on Microsoft Advertising.”

See "Best Practices: Getting Past the Gatekeeper when Making Outbound Calls" in section 7.2.

When talking with the decision maker and pitching Opti.

Don’t lead your pitch with a vague statement about the offer... (for example, “free services”) but rather be specific about issues and opportunities in the advertiser’ account to grab their attention.

State the problem and the solution in the first sentence..."Clicks are going to your competitor because your ads usually stop serving by 2PM. I can help you adjust your budget settings so that your ads remain competitive all day long."

Focus on the money aspect... “I can help you get the most out of your spend.”

If you spoke with the gatekeeper, mention that gatekeeper's name to the decision maker "Sally suggested I schedule an appointment with you..."

9. Talking points about specific tactics

  • “While reviewing your account I noticed some of your campaigns could benefit from using negative keywords. Negative keywords prevent your ads from showing up in search results that are unrelated to your business.”
  • “Improving your campaign structure can help you reach your optimal click-through rate.”
  • “It is recommended to have no more than three ads (including one dynamic) and no more than 100 keywords per ad group so you can better target those ads to only relevant searches.”
  • “You'll want to ensure your keywords are grouped by similarity; meaning that a specific ad copy will speak to all the keywords within that ad group.”
  • “You can decrease the budget in the <<campaign name>> campaign and use that money to increase the budget in the <<campaign name>> campaign to ensure you don't miss out on quality traffic from that campaign.”
  • “On average, your campaigns are being paused due to your budget around <<enter time>>, which means your competitors ads are showing while yours are unable to get impressions.”
  • “Last month, a portion of your keywords had zero impressions because of low bids. Boosting them will make your ads more competitive—and could even improve your ad position.”
  • “To help improve your click-through rate, I recommend you update your ad copy to ensure it reflects a clear call-to-action to make it more compelling for your potential customers.”
  • “You may be able to achieve a higher position on the Search results page by deleting ads with poor performance and introducing new, more relevant ads.”
  • “I recommend that you test different calls to action to find the most compelling ad copy for your target audience. This may improve your CTR and make you more competitive in the auction.”
  • “Improve your relevance by explicitly bidding on exact match terms. With exact match, you can more effectively target the consumers who search for your exact keywords.”
  • “Reach more of your target audience by adding <Phrase/Broad> match to your exact match strategy.”
  • [If customer sells products and has a secure checkout on their website] “I recommend setting up a Bing Shopping campaign to showcase your products in an engaging ad format by including images, promotional text, pricing and your company name. Serving Product Ads on the Yahoo Bing Network lets you reach 31 million retail searchers that don't use Google in the US*”

*comScore Search Categories Report (custom), US, June 2013.
