Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor
Not for Broad Commercial/ Professional Support

What is this article about?

This procedure describes Advisory Support for Microsoft Unified Support customers & Premier customers.

Advisory Services is an hourly fee-based or pre-packaged service, consultative support option that provides proactive support beyond the Customer's break-fix product maintenance needs. This support option includes working with a CSS Engineer for assistance on issues like product migration, code review, or new program development.

This service is typically used for shorter engagements and is designed for developers and IT professionals who do not require the traditional onsite consulting or sustained account management services that are available from other Microsoft support options.

Guidance:

  • If a case is created with the wrong type, inform the customer we can help but the case type must be changed. If they agree, document in the case and ask the Incident Manager (IM) for permission via email to change the case type as soon as possible. The support engineer is accountable for changing the case type from reactive to advisory in-Service Desk Case. If the customer does not agree or hesitates to involve the IM immediately.
  • For Unified support, if you want more information - please take a look at the 6 min training video. (RELINK)
  • For Premier Customers only, Advisory hours to be invested must be agreed with customer/IM before work is started. Engineer should not exceed the number of hours agreed before they get agreement (documented in the case) to extend the hours invested.
  • Premier Customers will not be charged with scope time if the customer declines scope.
    • Scoping should confirm if the issue is Advisory Services or break/fix
    • It is imperative that a time estimate be provided to the customer and the estimate agreed upon.

Note: More background information on Azure decrement can be found in the Azure Commercial Support Incident Decrement Policies document.


1. Steps

  • Engineer scopes issue with the customer and determines the incident type is Advisory based on the definitions for Advisory Services and Break/Fix issue.
  • Ensure case is correctly coded as Advisory.
    • For cases in Rave
      • For online cases no action is required as there is no way to differentiate Advisory or Reactive type.
      • For on-premise cases, Case Type should be set to Advisory.
      • Actions in Rave to correctly code the support case
        • Change to Summary tab along the top of the screen.
        • Set Case Type to Advisory.
        • Set Issue Type to Technical Issue.

      • In Rave there is no way to change the entitlement of a case. Even if you were able to change the eligibility from Reactive Incident to Advisory in the corresponding SD shadow case, it would not change the entitlement of the Rave case.

    • For cases in Service Desk
      • For Premier cases, CSS support engineers should confirm with IM before changing case eligibility. It is not necessary to get IM approval to change case type to Advisory for Unified Support cases.
      • In Service Desk, Case Types are directly related to the entitlement used on a case. Depending on the entitlement selected for the case, Service Desk will resolve the Case Type to Reactive Incident, Advisory, Support Consulting, or Developer Consulting. All Broad Commercial/ Professional cases are default to Reactive Incident and can't be associated to Advisory.
      • Actions in Service Desk to correctly code the support case
        • Scroll down to Entitlement section of the case and click Change Entitlement. 
          [Asset 4557506]
        • Enter the Access ID and click the popup of "Search by Access ID". 
          [Asset 4557507]
        • Select Advisory. 
          [Asset 4557508]
  • Obtain agreement from the customer on the estimated hours required to provide a solution.
    • For Unified Support, determine that the case is an advisory question that can be answered within 6 hours.
    • If you determine that a case would take longer than 6 hours after initial scoping and cannot be supported, then see Supporting Information below
  • Engineer delivers the solution to the customer.
    • When closing the Service Request, perform a final check to ensure the correct case type is noted.
    • For Unified Support Customers, if you have reached a total of 5 hours of labour on the case, evaluate if the case will be solved within the 6-hour agreement. If you determine that it will take longer than 6 hours, notify your Team Manager and the IM/Support Account Coordinator, and keep working on the case to complete scope.

2. Deviations or workaroundsu

APGC

Professional Level Advisory Services is not offered in APGC:

  • Customer contacts Microsoft CSR.
  • CSR will determine SR type based on the definitions for Advisory Services and Break/Fix issue.
  • If the issue is determined to be break/fix, CSR will ensure the Service Request has been entitled as such and route as break/fix.
  • Customer Premier customer:
    • If not Premier the case is closed as Professional Advisory is not offered.
    • If Premier route SR to engineer.
  • Engineer​ complete scope and estimate hours to complete the Advisory Service request:
    • Engineer determine SR type based on the definitions for Advisory Services and Break/Fix issue.
    • If the issue is determined to be break/fix, ensure the Service Request has been entitled as such and work as break/fix.
  • If Premier, contact the customer's IM for agreement before proceeding:
    • If customer does not agree IM will re-scope the SR or close the SR
  • Agreement is received from the customer:
    • Engineer proceeds with Advisory Service solution
India
  • Cases opened as Reactive, SE/TL will educate or advice IM/Customer and then change the case to Advisory or keep as Reactive.
  • If billable hours reach 40 hrs. IM needs to advise on next steps. Above 40 hrs. should be MCS cases.
MBS

MBS Support Engineers will follow the Global Process:

  • For VSA, it is the 'Call Reason' field that is set to 'Advisory Services'.
  • Customer or Partner contacts CSR or Engineer:
    • CSR creates service request.
  • Engineer begins scope with customer.
  • Determine SR type based on the definitions for Advisory Services and Break/Fix issue:
    • Assign to MBS Advanced Product Support Services.
  • APSS enter SR into SETA (APSS supports both Premier and Professional Customers).
  • Customer / Partner agrees:
    • Engineer will scope and estimate hours
  • APSS will send contract to customer:
    • Customer agrees.
  • APSS schedule work with Engineer.
  • Engineer delivers solution.
  • APSS reviews delivered solution for completeness and invoices customer or decrement hours.

3. Supporting information or articles

If you find that a Unified Support case would take longer than 6 hours after initial scoping, it is only valid to split the case into multiple cases if there are distinctly separate scopes. You cannot break up one single Advisory topic into multiple 6-hour cases. If the support case cannot be supported, then:

  • For Unified Core Support:
    • Engineer explains to the customer the Advisory Support scope and ask them if they want a Microsoft rep to contact them. If no, the Engineer closes the case.
    • If yes, the Engineer let the Support Account Coordinator team (sacteam@microsoft.com) know about the opportunity and closes the case.
    • SAC team will help identify the assigned seller who will follow up with the customer. Custom Proactive is not available to Core customers.
  • Advanced / Performance
    • Engineer explains to customer the Advisory Support scope and let them know that their IM will contact them to suggest a more suitable offer.
    • Engineer to contact the IM and close the case.
    • IM to follow up with the customer.
