  • Japan
  • Philippines

1. Basic process

When the customer contacts support for PC Accessories replacement/fix, follow the below steps.

1. Create a service request (SR) on ASD:

  • For more information see KB 2988026 Guideline: Service Request (SR) Creation and Case Ownership.
  • Note: For Xbox PC controller replacement requests, transfer the customer to Xbox support.

2. Basic troubleshooting:

  1. Ask the customer the about the issue with the product.
  • If they have already gone through troubleshooting with Windows support, proceed to the step 3 below. Also skip if the product is physically damaged. Basic troubleshooting can include trying the USB dongle on another port or device and changing the batteries. For other troubleshooting steps, refer to https://support.microsoft.com/hub/4338553/pc-accessories-help and check known issues in KB 4072657 Informational: Surface: Technical guides and known issues landing page.
  • If the issue remains, go to step 3.

3. Gather required information from the customer:

  • Photos of the Product – If you are unable to determine the actual product or cannot ascertain the defect based on the customer’s description, you must ask the customer to send photos including the Product ID.
  • Point of purchase.
  • Date of the purchase.
  • Product ID of the PC Accessory.
  • Has the customer’s PC Accessory been replaced for this issue previously?

4. Validate the warranty:

Based on the information from customer has provided, confirm the device was purchased within the warranty period, refer to PDF for country based warranty period. If yes, proceed to step 5. Note the difference in warranty periods for Surface-branded vs Microsoft branded accessories when advising customer of applicable warranty periods.

  • If the customer does not have a proof of purchase, see KB 4025417 Procedure: Devices: How to verify the warranty status of an accessory sold separately. Note that for refunds, a POP is necessary. If you encounter a customer with an accessory in warranty, for refund (no replacement available), but with no POP, agree on a solution for the customer with your TA.
  • If the device is out of warranty, explain to the customer that we can’t provide replacement.

5. Collect proof of purchase:

In ASD, send an email to the customer requesting the proof of purchase using the Data Transfer and Management Tool process. See KB 4135034 Tool User Guide: Data Transfer & Management (DTM).

Review the proof of purchase to confirm the device is in warranty.

Valid proof of purchase*

Purchased from Microsoft Store

Validate the purchase via MST.
Purchased third-party retailer

One of the following is required as proof of purchase:

  • Copy of invoice.
  • Electronic purchase confirmation (order confirmation email).
  • Copy of purchase receipt.

 

6. Determine the next step per country:

  • Philippines and Vietnam:
    • Proceed with the refund. See KB 4508757 Procedure: Supplemental Parts: PC hardware/accessories refund process.
  • Japan and Singapore:
    • Determine if the PC Accessory has a replacement using the matrix in section 3.
    • Refurbished replacements may be sent. Per the manufacturer's warranty, we either send reconditioned or new devices/covers for replacements. All devices/covers have been tested at the factory. All replacements (whether reconditioned or new) ship in plain boxes and not the retail packaging.
      • Warranty period for replacement product is based on customer’s proof of purchase of initial product.
      • If the replacement is an upgrade, you need to clearly explain to the customer that they will not be receiving the exact same product. Identify and describe the replacement to the customer and check if there will be any issues in using the said product (for example, it is not compatible with the current OS version).

2. Using the PC accessory replace/refund matrix for Japan/Singapore

Using the following matrix, define what options you can provide to customer based on their original PC HW/Accessories SKU.

The outcome will be one of the following:

  • Customer’s PC accessory has a replacement with same SKU, place replacement order.
  • Customer’s PC accessory has an upgrade replacement SKU, place replacement order with upgrade SKU. Explain to the customer that they will not receive the exact same product, but a similar product and it might be a different color.

To place order, see KB 4502445 Procedure: Supplemental Parts: Replacement/upgrade process in MST.

  • Customer’s PC Accessory has no replacement/upgrade, or product is not listed, proceed to refund. See KB 4508757 Procedure: Supplemental Parts: PC hardware/accessories refund process.

Proceed to next steps based on the section that product is listed in for each country.

    • Upgrade – Order replacement product via MST.
    • Upgrade or Refund – Offer the customer the replacement product, if they agree, order replacement product via MST. If the customer does not agree, proceed with the refund process.

3.2 Products without replacement or refund

For those products that do not have replacement products, proceed with refund.

If you are proceeding with the refund process due to no inventory for replacement order, cancel the order first (confirm the order status is cancelled) and provide a TFS WI that shows the order has been canceled on the refund request.

For manual refund process, see KB 4508757 Procedure: Supplemental Parts: PC hardware/accessories refund process.

4. Non-refund escalations

For all non-refund escalations to Tier 3 (for example, lost order), see KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios. For any other scenarios not covered in this KB or in KB 3072561, contact your TA.
