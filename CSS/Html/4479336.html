Two different license overage experiences are enabled for Volume Licensing that allow customers to assign OLS subscriptions in excess of the number of licenses (overages) so as to avoid unplanned loss of subscriptions following Step Up orders or Partial seat reductions.

  1. Allowed Overage (Double Provisioning) – Old Functionality

    Referred to publicly as Reserve Overage, allowed overage is an internal reference to a design feature that allows VL customers to assign up to twice as many seats as they are licensed for, using PowerShell scripts, or Group Based Licensing/GBL.
     

    IMPORTANT

    This overage functionality was applicable to EA Agreements (ONLY, for example, it did not apply to EDU or Sovereign Cloud) where the EA Agreement Created Date was prior to Oct 25, 2019, AND the Order placement date was after May 2018. As of August 9, 2020, all new orders placed will leverage Smart Provisioning, vs double provisioning previously provided.

     

    Internal Term Only

    "Smart Provisioning” is an internal only term and should not be used when communicating with customers. Customers are only aware than there was a change to our overallocation process.



    *Note that not all Offers have the allowed overage enabled, I.e. PowerBI Premium Offers. To verify if the allowed overage is enabled for the offer, you will need to look up the tenant in https://lynx.office.net/#/. Screenshots below, on how to validate.

    This provides the administrator a grace period in which to administer / reconcile subscriptions that are set to expire or be disabled following purchase count changes without unplanned data loss for subscribers. It does not change the customer’s eligible/purchased license count.

    Because the function is available in PowerShell and GBL, but not available in the Admin Center User Interface, it can lead to administrators being concerned that they are not compliant or that they will be invoiced for the overage amount.

    Microsoft does not issue communications or invoices relating to the assigned overage licenses, on the understanding that any use of overage quantities is temporary and is being used to avoid unplanned data loss for subscribers.


    Example of Allowed Overage (Double Provisioning)

    Customer with 100 seats E3 will see 100 seats available on the Admin Portal, but can assign up to 200 seats by running a PowerShell command, or GBL. 

    If they assign more than the 100 licensed quantity, they are no longer compliant (they have assigned more than they have purchased). The Admin Portal displays a message indicating there is a license conflict that must be reconciled.

    When the subscription goes into grace period, seats can still be assigned to users up to the original number of purchased licenses. If the customer places a new order with fewer seats than initially purchased, the Overage Amount available to use is the number of licenses when the subscription was in grace.
     

    Time Line of Allowed Overage Changes

    Allowed Overage was replaced in October 2019 with Smart Provisioning for new EA, EAS, SCE Enrollments.

    As of August 2020, Smart Provisioning has been applied to commercial EA, EAS, SCE Enrollments.

    Changes will be applied to Academic customers on October 23rd 2020.


  2. Smart Provisioning – New Functionality

    Internal Term Only

    "Smart Provisioning” is an internal only term and should not be used when communicating with customers. Customers are only aware than there was a change to our overallocation process.

     

    For Commercial and Government EA, EAS, SCE Agreements, overages are now calculated dynamically, based on Step Up or seat reductions. This is referred to as Smart Provisioning.

    Customer Communication on Overallocation on September 25, 2020

    MC222870: Update to license overallocation process for select subscriptions purchased through Microsoft Volume Licensing

     
    What is Smart Provisioning:

    Since October 25, 2019, new and renewing customers ordering OLS step-up SKUs or partially reducing seat counts have been able to temporarily use excess licenses to migrate to their new subscriptions with no risk of any loss of service. This automated process is called Smart Provisioning that makes excess licenses available to assign to Enterprise (EA, EAS, SCE) customers when they order step-up SKUs or action partial seat reductions with their Online Services (OLS) - commercial M365 unified offers. This enables customers to easily and smoothly transition their licenses, deciding which seats should undergo step-up or partial reduction.

    If a customer has enough unassigned licenses to carry out either operation safely, Smart Provisioning won’t intervene. If excess licenses are needed to ensure no loss of services occurs, Smart Provisioning will make them available. Smart Provisioning assesses the least number of excess licenses (overages) that the customer may provision to carry out the task without the risk of loss of service. NOTE : If a Customer is OPTED-IN for Smart Provisioning, they ONLY receive these Overage seats during Step Up’s and Partial seat reductions and quantities would vary as required.

    NOTE:

    Customers should unassign any licenses allocated in excess of the purchased count as soon as possible after the order is fulfilled.

     

    Default Smart Provisioning Choices –
    • Commercial Contracts are OPTED-IN. They can OPT-OUT as they wish if they are not currently using overage
    • Government Contracts are enabled with Smart Provisioning but OPTED-OUT. They can OPT-IN to Smart Provisioning at any time
    • EDU contracts signed/renewed effective Oct-23’2020 will be enabled with Smart Provisioning but OPTED-OUT. They can OPT-IN to Smart Provisioning at any time

     

     
    Key Points for awareness on Smart Provisioning
    • OPT-IN or OUT of Smart Provisioning
      • Partner commercial executive (PCE) can use Empowerment Guide – 2528 (Amendments - M644/M645) to help Customers to OPT-IN or OUT of Smart Provisioning.
      • Please check the empowerment guide to find out how to do this.
      • If no P-CE works in your organization, please contact your Commercial Executive in the local Microsoft subsidiary team to work on the necessary amendments.
    • Implications for Opting out of Smart Provisioning
      • Opting out might have implications on service continuity in the event of a step-up or a partial seat reduction associated with bringing the number of assigned licenses back to the purchased count.
      • Please refer to the examples in “Examples/Scenario’s” Section to understand in depth
    • Unassigning excess licenses to maintain compliance
      • It is critical that customers opted IN to Smart Provisioning unassign overage licenses ASAP – please provide them assistance to make sure the assigned licenses count matches the purchased count.
      • Customers continue to be responsible for their usage of overprovisioned licenses per the product terms.
    • On a periodic basis, overage allocation will be adjusted to reflect current need, and to more closely align number of available seats to the quantity purchased.
      • Customers previously relying on overage availability to manage daily fluctuations in business should purchase the necessary quantity needed to maintain availability.

     

     
    Smart Provisioning is being implemented in five phases.

     

    1. October 25, 2019
      • EA, EAS, SCE Commercial & Government Enrolments created from Oct 25, 2019, onwards have the Smart Provisioning feature.
    2. August 9, 2020
      • Smart Provisioning is replacing Allowed Overage (Double Provisioning) on new orders placed on agreements created prior to October 25, 2019.
    3. October 23, 2020
      • Smart provisioning will be extended to Academic customers (Campus & School programs).
      • Unused overage removal is planned to extend to existing EA/EAS/SCE Commercial customers November 2nd, 2020.
      • After this, customers that have relied on the overage ability to provision new users will not be able to do so.
      • Customers will need to make a reservation or place an order for when additional licenses are required.

Scenario 1 - The customer is using more seats than are being paid for

Example: customer purchases 1,000 seats with E3.

Note

This scenario is not applicable for Smart Provisioning, only Allowed Overage (Double Provisioning)

Seat Assignment

1,000 seats can be assigned via Admin Center UI.

2,000 seats can be assigned via PowerShell, or GBL but customer is not compliant when assigning more than 1000 licenses.

Customer experience

Admin Portal displays 1,000 seats available. If more than 1,000 seats are in use a conflict message is displayed in the the Status column on the Licenses page:  Admin Center > Billing > Licenses: “X users need valid licenses”.

Note: X represents the difference of active (assigned) users and available (licensed) seats.

[Asset 4479302]

The Admin Portal will also indicate when the customer needs to ‘buy more or unassign’ users as well as the assigned user count. Admin Center > Billing > Your Products

[Asset 4588623]

The administrator needs to reconcile this conflict as soon as possible, by either

  1. Freeing up existing licenses by manually unassigning them from users. See article Assign or unassign licenses for Office 365 for business for details.
  2. Purchase more licenses so that the total of seats matches the current active users.

During this license conflict period, individual subscribers / users are not impacted. Their subscription service continues as normal.


Suggested scripts

A license conflict is happening because there are more users currently assigned to this account than purchased.

Good news - Users who have been assigned licenses still have normal product experiences.

There are two ways to solve this.

  1. You can remove users who no longer need a subscription.
    Here’s how to  Assign or unassign licenses for Office 365 for business
  2. or you can purchase more licenses to match your current active users, either by submitting a reservation in VLSC, or work with your partner to place a manual order.

Meanwhile, licensing conflicts can arise when using Power Shell, or GBL, which allows excess licenses to be assigned before Administrators complete administrative actions for individual subscribers. This is to prevent unplanned data loss for individual subscribers, but you are required to reconcile this licensing conflict as soon as possible by ensuring your licensed quantity matches your active subscribers.

Scenario 2 - Customer purchased fewer number of seats for a subscription that was already in grace status

Example: After customer’s 1,000 seat E3 subscription goes into grace status; the customer purchased just 50 E3 seats.

Seat Assignment in Allowed Overage (Double Provisioning)

  • In total customer can assign 1050 seats.
  • 50 seats can be assigned via Admin Center UI;
  • 1,000 seats can be assigned via PowerShell.

NOTE

If they use more than 50 than they will see the conflict message on the Admin Center portal.

 

User experience

All 1,050 user can work as normal with no impact. If they are already assigned there are no impact on the users, if they want to assign the licenses during the original 90 days grace period, customer can do this via a PowerShell command. The 1,000-seat allowed overage will be available until the subscription goes into disabled status.

Policy

Customers is only allowed to use the overage licenses during the grace period of the original purchase. After 90 days they are not compliant.

  1. Removing users who no longer need a subscription.
    Here’s how to  Assign or unassign licenses for Office 365 for business.
  2. or purchasing more licenses to match your current active users , either by submitting a reservation in VLSC, or work with your partner to place a manual order.

You have 90 days from your prior subscription end date to reconcile the license conflict. Until then, individual users’ experiences continue as normal. After 90 days, there will be periodic adjustments made to the overage.

Where Smart Provisioning is applied for this scenario, the number of seats assigned will determine the overage.

If all 1,000 seats were previously assigned, an overage amount of 950 seat (1000 – 50 paid) is applied

If only 800 of the 1,000 seats were assigned, an overage amount applied is 750 (800 – 50 paid).

Scenario 3 - Customer does a Partial Step up

Example: Customer with 1,000 seats E3 steps up 400 seats from E3 to E5.

The base license* for E3 drops from 1,000 to 600 and does NOT go into grace status.

The base license for the E5 is 400.

*note exception scenario whereby EA Step base license count is doubled. See scenario 17 in 2820242 Informational: M365 Commerce: Supporting Volume Licensing customers.

Overage provisioning:

Allowed Overage Seat Assignment

1400 seats can be assigned

600 E3 seats can be assigned via Admin Center UI;

Overage Scenario: 1000 E3 can be assigned (600 though admin Center, 400 (overage) through PowerShell or GBL

400 E5 seats (purchased amount) can be assigned via the Admin Center 

Smart Provisioning Seat Assignment

Assuming all 1000 E3 seats were assigned at the time of step-up, the overage amount will be 400, to protect the 400 users who are migrating to E5 during license reallocation . It is not possible to assign additional users over the 400 amount.

400 E5 seats (purchased amount) can be assigned via the Admin Center

Customer experience

Admin Portal: displays 600 E3 and 400 seats E5 available,

If all the (original) 1,000 E3 seats were assigned, a license conflict message is displayed in the the Status column on the Licenses page:  Admin Center > Billing > Licenses: “400 users need valid licenses”.

The administrator needs to resolve this conflict by either:

  1. reassigning 400 E3 users the stepped up E5 licenses
  2. purchasing additional licenses to match the current active users count.

 

Suggested scripts for subscriptions where Allowed Overage is applied:

  1. Manually assigning existing user licenses to stepped up subscriptions. See article Assign or unassign licenses for Office 365 for business for details, or
  2. Purchasing more licenses to match your current active users.

For this scenario where Smart Provisioning is applied,

The amount of overage licenses depends on how many of the licenses which were assigned/used before the partial step up was made.

If all 1,000 seats of E3 were assigned – of which 400 are stepped up from E3 to E5, then

400 E3 will be applied as overage (to allow admin transition E3 subscribers to E5)

The base license for E3 = 600 E3 (1000 - 400 step up)

The license for E5 = 400

400 E3 will be applied as overage (to allow admin transition E3 subscribers to E5).

Scenario 5 - Allowed Overage Quantity cannot exceed Maximum Quantity

The allowed overage rule can cause a customer’s total seat count to exceed the allowed seat quantity for the plan they have purchased.

This will manifest itself as customer can see the licenses in VLSC but they will not provision in the Admin Center.

Example

Subscription Plan has an allowed limit of 80 seats.

Our system rules for Allowed Overage rule applies a 2*order quantity.

System interprets the total number of seats as the order quantity plus allowed overage quantity.

Scenario

Customer orders 20 seats, System applies an Allowed Overage of 20 seats. 

Therefore, order qty (20) and allowed overage qty (20) is within the allowed limit of 80 seats and customer faces no issue.

Customer orders 41 seats, System applies an Allowed Overage of 41. 

Therefore, order qty (41) and allowed overage qty (41) exceeds the allowed limit of 80 seats and seats will not provision.

Seat Assignment Rules

Some On line services have maximum quantity restrictions and are excluded from Allowed Overage Rules.

Smart Provisioning Exceptions

Customers should become compliant with their licensing. If additional seats are needed, they should place an order with their reseller or place an online reservation via Volume Licensing Service Center. Exceptions are to be granted in only certain scenarios. Guidelines are:

  • For approved exceptions, customers must meet one of the following:
    • Agreements created after October 25, 2019, are out of scope. The services from these agreements never had access to Allowed Overage. It should not be applied.
    • Do not offer an exception. If an exception is requested by the customer:
      • The customer must prove impact. Why is the reservation not possible? Impact could include, but not limited to:
        • Outage/user downtime
        • During deal negotiations
        • To allow time to revise assignment scripts & processes
      • The customer needs to understand that the exception is on at a subscription level and is only temporary. Once a licensing change (seat increase/decrease) occurs again, the subscription will revert back to Smart Provisioning.
  • If the customer meets the guidelines for an exception:
    • For VLSC: Work with T2
    • For Commerce: Escalate an AR clearly detailing the request and the amount of seats needed.
    • Follow KB below, scenario 20 for BET steps:  https://internal.support.services.microsoft.com/en-us/help/4101662
 
FAQ
  1. Is there a way for us to identify the number of licenses that can be over assigned for Smart Provisioning? The only details in SDK is overages are now calculated dynamically, based on Step Up or seat reductions. This is referred to as Smart Provisioning.
    1. There is not a way for customers to view the overage provided by Smart Provisioning. They could potentially determine this based off of the number of assigned users based on the time of the licensing change.
      1. Example: the customer had 100 users assigned before the licensing change. The seat quantity reduced to 25, the overage that Smart Provisioning provides is 75.
    2. Account teams can view the overage in Lynx under the ‘Allowed Overage’ section. Even though the column indicates Allowed Overage, the amount shown is the overage allowed by the subscription, which includes Smart Provisioning.
    3. In support, those that have access to Lynx or BET are able to view the overage.
      1. KB for BET scenarios:  https://internal.support.services.microsoft.com/en-us/help/4101662
  2. If customer would like to continue utilizing unused overages long term, what would be the process to follow?
    1. Encourage the customer to place an order to become compliant in their licensing by placing an online reservation or order with their reseller.
    2. Internal stakeholders only: periodically, Smart Provisioning logic will be reapplied. If the customer continues to have over assigned users, those users will not have their service disrupted.
      1. Example: If Smart Provisioning initially provided the customer with 75 seats of overage, during the 90 days, the customer reassigned 25 of those users, Smart Provisioning will reduce the overage to 50, to continue to allow the over assigned users service.
  3. Is the TAM community aware of Smart Provisioning? 
    1. Some communication has been sent to the field, but CSAMS may or may not have consumed this information. Additional field communication is being developed at this time.
  4. Is there a way to identify if customer is opt-out from Smart Provisioning?
    1. For commercial customers, agreements signed after Oct 2019, customers are automatically opted-in to Smart Provisioning. For commercial customers that have opted out, you could view this amendment in the 'Amendments' section when viewing the agreement in Explore.ms
  5. Who to contact if customer would like to opt-out? Is it the reseller or Microsoft Account Team?
    1. The customer should work with their Microsoft Account Team or Account Executive to execute the opt-out amendment.
  6.  If the customer opts out of Smart Provisioning, will the subscriptions revert back to Allowed Overate (Double Provisioning)?
    1. No. Opting out of Smart Provisioning removes overage altogether. This could result in loss of service, but gives customers who are concerned with compliance more control over their assigned seat count.
  7. Since customer has ability to opt-out, do they have ability to opt-in (after opting-out)?
    1. Yes, the customer can opt-in if they have already opted out. They should work with their Microsoft Account Team.
  8. Can customer assign licenses even if the subscription/EA is already expired (in-grace)?
    1. Not applicable for Smart Provisioning. Any overage provided by Smart Provisioning would be allowed for already assigned users. A customer could not assign users above the already over the overage provided by Smart Provisioning.
  9. Was there any customer communication / MC post regarding this change?
    1. No message center post was released to customers regarding this change. Due to this the customer will likely not be happy. Redirect them to their Microsoft Account Team regarding their concerns.
