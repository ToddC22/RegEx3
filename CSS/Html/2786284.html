ASMS – Support Entitlement Handling

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.  

 Overview  

Azure offers multiple support entitlement levels. Higher-level programs (e.g., Microsoft Unified Support, Pro-Direct, and Premier) have specific engagement paths and processes for handling support cases. 

  • For case routing information, consult the Policy section.  

  • For instructions on how to determine support entitlement for specific cases in MSSolve, see How to Identify Cases Entitled for Enhanced Support. 

  • For more detailed definitions and information on the different support plans, see the links in the Online References section.

Applies To:

This TSG outlines special handling for cases with enhanced support entitlements including:

  • Advanced Support for Partners (ASfP): Support program specifically for Partners.

  • Advanced Support for Startups (ASfS): Same program as ProDirect (below).

  • Cloud Solution Partner (CSP): Microsoft Partner program for Partners specializing in Cloud Solutions (including Azure).

  • Microsoft Unified Support: Microsoft’s newest commercial support offering, with more support levels and a menu of add-ons; currently expanding worldwide and with plans to convert existing Premier support customers over time.

  • Premier: Previous top-level “white glove” support service for Enterprise customers, developers, and Partners. New support contracts at similar levels to Premier will be transitioning to Microsoft Unified Support. These customers may also be a part of the Pulse Program, click here to verify. 

  • Pro Direct: Microsoft support solution for mid-size to large commercial customers that provides limited advisory and account management services, in addition to billing and technical support.

Cases with support entitlements that should be handled using standard ASMS case management process and FQR guidelines include:

  • Basic/Free: Default support levels offered with Azure.

  • Developer: Paid support plan for developers and other customers who require minimal support , primarily aimed at non-production environments.

  • Standard: Paid support plan that includes unlimited contacts/unlimited cases, 24x7 access to Support Engineers, and guidance & troubleshooting.

 

Policy

Routing

ASfP, ASfS, CSP, Microsoft Unified Support, Premier, and Pro Direct cases that come in with the correct entitlement should be worked by the Premier (3P) and US Government PODs . If you receive a case with such an entitlement, and it was incorrectly assigned to your POD (e.g. by the DM), do the following:

  1. Re-title the case by adding the entitlement (e.g., Microsoft Unified Support, ASfP, ASfS, CSP, PREM, ProDir) at the start of the title field.
  2. Transfer the case to the respective ASMS Premier queue (See TSG 3189895 for specific queue names).
    • If the case is out of scope for ASMS but still requires Premier support, work the DMs to find the correct queue.
  3. Email wadm@microsoft.com and let them know why the case was transferred and who it was transferred to. This will help ensure the Premier case is re-assigned and prioritized accordingly.

If a Premier customer incorrectly opens a Professional (e.g. Basic/Developer/Standard/ProDirect) case and they (or the TAM) later asks for the case to be transferred to/handled by a 3P engineer, do the following:  

  1. Ask the TAM and/or the customer to provide the Access ID so that it can be added to the case.
  2. Re-title the case by adding “Premier” at the start of the title field. This will ensure that the automatic prioritization rules in the queues are followed for any further re-routing to other Premier queues.
  3. Transfer the case to the respective ASMS Premier queue (See TSG 3189895 for specific queue names).
    • If the case is out of scope for ASMS, but still requires Premier support, work the DMs to find the correct queue.
  4. Email wadm@microsoft.com and let them know why the case was transferred and who it was transferred to. This will help ensure the Premier case is re-assigned and prioritized accordingly.
  5. Educate the TAM that in the future, the customer needs to open a Premier entitled case to ensure they receive the proper support.

Note: These processes are also to be followed when cases need to be transferred to teams outside of ASMS.

SLA

Support cases with entitlement for higher support tiers may have different ASMS SLAs and should be routed and handled quickly, in accordance with those SLAs. For current ASMS SLA information around response times, see the FQR TSG.  

Microsoft Unified Support/Premier

  • A customer’s Premier hours are not decremented for any Azure Billing/Subscription management case, or for Azure Technical cases. 

  • Click here to verify if the customer is part of the Pulse Program, to best determine how to handle the case. 

  • Be sure to read and follow anything noted in an MSSolve Flash if one pops up when you accept the case.

  • If both the customer and the TAM become unresponsive, follow the Three Strike process outlined in TSG 2589957.

  • Should you need to adjust the severity of the case, be sure to engage the TAM before doing so. If the TAM is not available, then a TA or Manager can approve the severity adjustment.

  • When adjusting the severity of an incident, be sure to include the following information in the email:

    • Previous severity and new severity

    • Reason for adjustment

    • Contact hours and frequency for new severity

    • How the customer can readjust the severity if required

  • If you receive a case from a customer regarding their upgrade from any paid support subscription to a Premier subscription, you are permitted to cancel the customer’s existing paid support subscription.

  • Microsoft Unified Support SRs that come through Tech Routers will have an SR Title containing “Microsoft Unified Support L#” where # = {1, 2, or 3}, corresponding with the Support Level. If the SR Title does not contain Microsoft Unified Support L# , then the SE should add it.

Issues Filing Premier Tickets in Ibiza

If a Premier customer is encountering difficulties when trying to file a Premier ticket in Ibiza, it may be due to a mapping issue.

There are three ways a Premier contract can be mapped in Ibiza:

  • Auto Match – If the customer logs in to the Premier Portal (MPO) with the same Microsoft Account (MSA) that they use to log in to Azure, we automatically add the Premier information into Ibiza for all of the customer’s subscriptions to which they have write access.
  • Cloud Knows Premier (CKP) – Based on TPID matching, if the same company pays for both Premier and Azure subscriptions, or the Technical Account Manager (TAM) performed a manual matching in Unicorn, Premier plans will also show up automatically in Ibiza. They will be linked to specific subscriptions as part of the matching. If the TAM needs assistance with TPID mapping, have them reach out finsup@microsoft.com.
  • Manual Linking – Using manual linking, the customer can add a Premier contract by using a Contract ID and Access ID, which will be linked permanently to their Azure account and available on all of the subscriptions to which they have write access.

Note: Premier customers are not decremented for any subscription, billing, or technical cases in Azure.

 Pro Direct/ASfS

  • If a case falls outside of ASMS/Technical Support scope, add pdazure@microsoft.com to the email informing the customer and transfer the case to the correct team using the MSaaS Support Portal. For more information on misroutes, consult TSG 3104062 .

  • When working with a Pro Direct customer you may have been engaged by a Pro Direct Manager (PDM), or you may need to engage a PDM. Be sure to include pdazure@microsoft.com on the cc line of all communications if a PDM has been involved in the case at any point.

  • If you are attempting to archive a case and there are contact tasks belonging to a PDM, send a request to that person and cc pdazure@microsoft.com asking them to close their open tasks. If it is during their off hours, you can close the tasks.

  • You should engage pdazure@microsoft.com if the customer becomes disengaged and/or the case severity needs to be adjusted. You are NOT allowed to adjust the severity of a Pro Direct support case without approval.  If the pdazure team isn’t available, then a TA or manager can approve a severity adjustment.

  • When adjusting the severity of an incident, be sure to include the following information in the email:

    • Previous severity and new severity

    • Reason for adjustment

    • Contact hours and frequency for new severity

    • How the customer can readjust the severity if required

How to Differentiate Legacy and Modern Subscriptions

The field titled Azure Channel Type can be found in the Details section of a Service Desk Case, to help differentiate between Legacy and Modern subscriptions. 

The following values are applicable to this field:

  • None = Legacy subscription
  • FieldLed = EA Modern subscription
  • CustomerLed = Web Direct Modern subscription
  • PartnerLed = CSP Modern subscription
  • Internal = Microsoft internal subscription

 

Support Scenarios

Cases are assigned to the respective queues, based on the support entitlement of the case. You should not push back on cases that have been assigned to you, as they have been assigned correctly. 

Note: ASMS CM is working to gather the correct nomenclature for entitlements and document​ guidance on how to determine the entitlement via a Service Desk Case.

Checking for an Active Technical Support Subscription

To see if a customer has an active Technical Support subscription, do the following:

  1. In CMAT, search for the customer.

  2. On the Subscriptions tab, review the purchase history to locate any Technical Support subscription payments.

Note: Tickets may come in from tech routers, so always check the Live ID of the subscription owner when verifying technical support subscriptions. These subscriptions can be re-enabled if they are not deleted completely in CMAT.
