1. Overview

When customers contact support for help creating or updating their business information on Bing, you should recommend them to use Bing Places to claim the listing. Once a listing is claimed and the user has been verified, you can then help the advertiser make changes or do this on their behalf.

Note: If the customer cannot or will not claim the listing, you will need to authenticate the customer as the business owner and escalate to Global Support.

Bulk management - Note that chain/agency customers will be able to make bulk edits to their listings much quicker than SMB users. If you need to claim or make bulk changes quickly to many listings on behalf of a user, (for instance, updating phone numbers, or opening or closing status) see KB 3178458 Process: Bing Places: Register chain businesses & upload multiple locations.

Out of scope

The following scenarios should not be solved by editing a listing, and will require a different support solution:

  • Former business' listings: Customers should not attempt to claim and update the listings for businesses that used to trade from their location. Instead, help the customer create a new business listing. Once verified, escalate the old listing to Global Support to mark this as closed.
  • Updating to a new address: Customers who have moved locations should not claim or edit their old location, attempting to change the address to reflect their new address. Instead, customers should use Location Closed when editing their old listing and add a new listing for their new business location. This avoids incorrect merging of third-party feeds with legacy YPID data.
  • Country information: Country information cannot be edited. If a listing has been created for the wrong search market, the customer will need to delete the listing and create a new one on the correct market. Even our escalation teams cannot overwrite the country information.

If a customer wants to update their listing information on Bing and the listing is not claimed or created in Bing Places, use the steps below to help the customer locate and claim their business.

2.1 Search for a business listing ﻿

Customers can search for a specific business by either logging into Bing Places or using the Get Started option on the homepage.

Ask customers to take the following steps:

1. Access Bing Places homepage and select Get Started option to search for a business first, or Sign in to log in with an existing account and then select Add New Business.

2. Customers will be prompted to choose their Business Type and Country/Region. SMB users should select Small or Medium business (1-10 locations).

  • This page also offers the option to import from Google My Business. See KB 4337642 Process: Bing Places: Account Imports for more information.

3. Advertisers then enter the following:

  • Business phone number (North America (NA) only)
  • Business/Professional name
  • Business location

4. Select Search to display all business listings that match the search criteria (if any).

5. Select My business is not listed in the search results to create a new listing.

Return to top

2.2 Claim an existing business listing ﻿

  1. Search and find the business listing using the process in section 2.1.
  2. Select Claim and edit details next to the listing.
  3. Sign into Microsoft account (if not already signed in).

Claiming an existing business listing from Search Engine Results Pages (SERP)

In addition to claiming directly within Bing Places, customers can also search for a YPID listing on Bing and claim a listing directly from SERP by selecting the Is This Your Business? or Your Business? Claim now hyperlinks, located directly beneath the YPID listing. The taxonomy may vary depending on the market.

Note:

  • Selecting the hyperlink will auto-load the customer’s business information, and they will be able to edit or check this information prior to selecting continue.
  • Customers will then be prompted to log in with their Provider ID to proceed with the usual claim and process as described below.
  • The option to claim a business directly from SERP will not be available if a YPID listing was updated from Bing Places within the last six months. In this scenario, any customers wanting to claim the business will need to do this from within the Bing Places UI.

2.3 Create and edit business information

After claiming or creating a business listing, the customer can now fill in the various information tabs relating to their business. A preview of their listing will also be visible on the right of the editing panels, which includes a map view of where they are located.

Note: Navigation between the tabs may be blocked if mandatory information is missing. Customers will need to complete all mandatory fields before they can move to the next tab.

These tabs are:

1. Basic info

  • Business name
  • Website
  • Address

2. Category information

  • Choose the business Segment.
  • For healthcare related segments, customers can then choose whether they are a medical professional or not, and the available categories will be filtered accordingly. See KB 3159579 Process: Bing Places: Professional Profiles Listings for more information.
  • Choose business Category (Minimum of one required – see KB 3064634 Process: Bing Places: Segment and Category for more information).
  • Apply Primary Category.
  • Describe Your Business (This is usually not public facing – see KB 2964808 Process: Bing Places: Ranking and Relevance for more information).

3. Additional business details

  • Service areas (UK/NA only) – Advertisers in selected categories (see FAQ for full list) can choose Yes or No to the question Do you deliver goods or services to your customers at their location? 
    • If they choose No, the Accessible Information question below will be hidden, as it is assumed they are offering in-call services. Full business name and address information will show in SERP, with a map pin located at the business’ location. Proceed to Store Code.
    • If they choose Yes, they can specify up to 20 service areas in which they operate (can’t be applied at country level). These service areas will be included in the SERP description (GB market only). On the Bing’s Maps page, service areas will show in the listing description.
  • Accessible address confirmation – Advertisers will be asked Are customers expected to visit your address?, and can answer either Yes or No. Selecting No will hide the street address in SERP and show only the city (and service areas, if applicable). The map preview will place the pin in the middle of the postal code location or show the service areas (if available). Selecting Yes will display the full address and map pin (and service areas, if applicable).
  • Store Code – (can also be accessed via the My Listing page in Bing Places).
    • Advertisers can enter a unique identifier code for each business location in their account. Can be made of any letter/number combination they wish.
    • Advertisers will be able to search on Store Code from within the listing overview dashboard, to quickly locate listings they want to view. 
    • This information is not published on the YPID. It is internal-only for advertisers to better manage their listings and easily search/discuss specific store codes with colleagues and support teams.
  • Is the customer a Business or Professional?
    • Customers will be prompted to select if they are a Professional or Business.
    • This information is not published on the YPID. It is used by the Bing Local Global Date Pipeline (GDP) to differentiate individuals (Professionals) from generic business locations (such as, firms/stores).
    • Will not be asked for healthcare listings due to the existing healthcare professionals workflow.
    • Customers should be careful they choose the correct answer here, to avoid multiple YPIDs being created (for example, if a user is claiming a Business location, but marks their BPID as a Professional, this would trigger a new YPID for the individual working for that business, instead of the business location itself).
    • Important: Professional profiles are only intended to represent independent service providers who do not operate from a fixed business location (such as, locksmiths or plumbers). Except for healthcare professionals, advertisers should not attempt to create separate BPID candidates for their business address and their professional address, if they operate from the same location. In this scenario, the professional and business listings will be merged together into a single YPID entity as a case of virtual containment (see KB 3175882 Process: Bing Places: Escalation handling for more information). This is by design, and we cannot support requests to unmerge.

4. Contact details

  • Phone (Note: Toll-free numbers will not be published, as these cannot be verified as local businesses. Advise customers not to enter a toll-free number.)
  • Email
  • Additional Contact Details (for example social links or third-party URLs).

Note: Additional contact details will not show on Bing Local Listings. These details will help associate the BPID and YPID successfully with third-party data feeds, to better improve the accuracy of Bing Local listings and avoid duplicate of listings.

Additionally, they do not guarantee reviews from those sources will be displayed on published YPIDs. We cannot prioritize or choose which sources provide our reviews. Do not escalate for missing reviews from Facebook or social sites.

5. Photos of your business

  • Add up to 10 images of your business. See KB 3195336 Process: Bing Places: Image Guidelines for more information.

6. Working hours

  • Default is Open 24 hours.
  • Up to two sets of hours can be included per day.
  • Some days can also be added for specific days off.
  • See KB 3064917 Process: Bing Places: Hours of operation for more information.

7. Price/menu (Restaurants only)

  • Menu URL (Published on YPID)
  • Use $ sign to indicate relative cost. For example, $$$ is moderately expensive.

8. Class (Hotels only)

  • Choose the Class of your restaurant (for example, five star or four star).
  • Use $ sign to indicate relative cost. For example, $$$ is moderately expensive.
  1. Choose which class of hotel applies to your business.
  2. Once the user has filled out the required information and reaches the last section, select Submit to save changes and proceed to the verification process.

2.4 SMILE data comparison

Upon selecting Submit for a newly claimed or edited listing, the Bing system will compare the data which the advertiser is about to submit with the SMILE data we have gathered on their business. SMILE data is compiled by crawlers, which review the business information on advertiser webpages.

If the SMILE data is different from the customer’s own information, an alert will be triggered prompting the customer to choose which data they would like to use.

For more information on SMILE data, see KB 4011536 Procedure: Bing Places: Data conflict alerts.

Return to top

2.5 Bing maps preview

Within the Bing Places dashboard, the Bing Maps preview functionality (available on the right-hand side of the UI whilst editing a listing) can be used to view where a business will be visibly displayed on Bing Maps, via a pin icon.

Select Move Map Pin and a pop-out page will load, allowing customers to move the pin to their desired or correct location.

The pin can be moved by selecting and dragging the icon, or by right-clicking on the map and choosing Set pushpin.

Advertisers can also select Reset pin to restore the default location.

Note: Even when a user selects Hide Address, the map pin will remain visible in the preview.

2.6 Order online and menu link

Restaurant (Food & Drink) category customers can enter direct web addresses for their menu and online ordering. These links will be displayed within their live listing once published and will link out to the external sites.

3. Updating listing content (claimed) ﻿

Once a business is claimed in Bing Places, the listing information can be updated at any time:

  1. Within the Home page find the business you want to edit.
  2. Select the box/arrow icon next to the business to narrow down further.
  3. Within My listing page, select Edit link next to the business name.
  4. Within the Edit business page, the user can go through each section and modify information as required. (See section 2.3 above for more guidance on this process.)

    Note: If the user is editing key information (for example, address or phone number) they will need to go through verification again for that business before the edited details will be published. Editing phone and address together is not possible without admin verify assistance.

3.2 Claiming a listing on behalf of a customer ﻿

Using the Sign In As feature, it is possible to claim listings on behalf of an advertiser/agency you are impersonating.

This can only be done if:

  • The customer has already claimed one listing successfully (thereby agreeing to the Bing Places Terms & Conditions).
  • The customer is seeking help claiming multiple unique business listings.

For each of the unique business locations, you will need to follow the usual verification process to determine that your customer is authorized to manage this listing.

Important: After claiming the listing, ask the customer to verify the accuracy of the listing information first, before using the Admin Verify option.

If a customer needs help claiming multiple locations for a chain business, they will need to complete the usual Chain Verification process.

4. Incorrect listing content scenarios ﻿

Scenario

Advocate action

Changes made to the name of the business are not reflected on the live listing. Could be an issue with the feed, the pipeline, or the user interface.
  1. Verify the information in the Bing Places user interface matches the changes the customer made.
  2. Verify the 7-10 business SLA has passed.
  3. Escalate to the appropriate engineering teams.

Changes made to the business address are not reflected on the live listing. Could be an issue with the feed, the pipeline or user interface.
  1. Verify the information in the Bing Places user interface matches the changes the customer made.
  2. Verify the 7-10 business SLA has passed.
  3. Escalate to the appropriate engineering teams.

Changes made to the business phone number are not reflected on the live listing. Could be an issue with the feed, the pipeline or user interface.
  1. Verify the information in the Bing Places user interface matches the changes the customer made.
  2. Verify the 7-10 business SLA has passed.
  3. Escalate to the appropriate engineering teams.

Changes made to other business details are not reflected on the live listing. Could be an issue with the feed, the pipeline or user interface.
  1. Verify the information in the Bing Places user interface matches the changes the customer made.
  2. Verify the 7-10 business SLA has passed.
  3. Escalate to the appropriate engineering teams.

Remove third-party reviews – The customer states that a review for the listing is still showing even though it has been removed from the original host site.
  1. Verify that the review has been removed from the site that hosted it.
  2. Advise the customer that their listing will be removed from Bing after a publish cycle has been completed, at which point the listing will automatically update to remove the unwanted review.
  3. If the listing has not been removed after a standard publish cycle, or if the customer is insistent that they cannot wait this long, escalate the ticket along with all details to Tier 3 as well as your Microsoft contact. Note: In your ticket, remember to confirm that the customer has removed the listing from the review site. Global Support will push back on tickets to confirm this, which will extend your resolution time!

Business status – Issues with changes made to the open/closed tag for the business listing.
  1. Verify the information in the Bing Places user interface matches the changes the customer made.
  2. Verify the 7-10 business SLA has passed.
  3. Escalate to the appropriate engineering teams.

Images – Issues with changes made and/or uploading images to the business listing, including missing images, blurry images and removing images.
  • Make sure max logo size of 480x360 is not exceeded.
  • Ensure format is jpg, bmp, gif or png.
  • To avoid blurry or truncated images, use a square dimension with a 1:1 ratio or 360x360.

6. FAQ

Q: Which Categories are able to show Service Areas?
A: All Categories listed here are eligible to display Service Areas. Only Categories within this list are eligible for escalations due to missing Service Areas.

Q: Why do I sometimes see a polygon on Bing, showing which service areas the business operates in?

A: We have some legacy data being shared with Bing from third-party sources which contains map data, but this is not the current by-design expected behavior. Do not escalate for missing map polygons.

Q: I’m still confused about where and when service areas will show.

A: This will depend on how you answer the Service Area and Accessible Information Questions:

   

Do you provide onsite service?
   

Yes

No

Are customers expected to visit your address?

Yes

Pin will only show on map. Service areas and address will show in description in MAP view.

Pin will show on map. Address will show in description.

No

Service areas will be shown as a list in description in the MAP view. Address/pin will be hidden.

N/A (Choosing no for service areas will bypass the accessible information question).
