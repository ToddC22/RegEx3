Procedure: Game Pass Ultimate downgrade, cancel, and refund requests

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Accounts & Billing

What is this article about?

This article provides guidelines and procedure details to follow when helping customers with downgrading, canceling, and refunding a Game Pass Ultimate subscription after upgrading from a constituent subscription (for example, Live Gold or Game Pass Console).

  1. Overview

  2. Support scenarios

1. Overview

Xbox Game Pass Ultimate is a subscription offering that combines the benefits of Xbox Live Gold and all Xbox Game Pass plans.

Customers who migrated from a constituent subscription can contact Xbox support for assistance with downgrading, canceling, or refunding their Ultimate subscription.

Public-facing position: Upgrades to Ultimate are not eligible for refund and cannot be reverted to prior subscriptions. This is described in this public FAQ:

Q: How do I manage or cancel the Xbox Game Pass Ultimate plan?
A: You can cancel your Ultimate membership at any time.

Visit account.microsoft.com/services and sign in to your account, select Payment & Billing (under Xbox Game Pass Ultimate), then select Cancel.

You (the customer) will have the option to end your subscription on its expiration date. Depending on your subscription status, you might also have an option to end it immediately with a refund of the price you paid for Ultimate only.

The upgrade of any prepaid time cannot be reverted to Xbox Live Gold or Xbox Game Pass and is not eligible for refund.

CSS exceptions: While remaining on Ultimate is likely a customer’s highest-value and best option, they are eligible for an exception to the public-facing position in some scenarios which are outlined below in section 2.

2. Support scenarios

Customers wanting to switch from Ultimate to individual Gold or Game Pass subscriptions
  1. Explain the value remaining on Ultimate:
  • Customers converted either Gold or Game Pass: The prior subscription time was upgraded to prepaid Ultimate time and includes more benefits.
  • Customers converted both Gold and Game Pass: The Ultimate subscription’s prepaid time includes benefits of both subscriptions and upgraded time added consecutively.
  1. If the customer still wants to return to individual Gold or Game Pass subscription:
    1. Turn off recurring billing on Ultimate.
    2. Advise the customer to re-subscribe to their desired subscriptions after Ultimate expires.

 
Customers requesting a refund within 30 days of Ultimate purchase or within exception guidelines.
  1. Explain the remaining value on Ultimate:
  • Customers converted either Gold or Game Pass: The prior subscription time was upgraded to prepaid Ultimate time and includes more benefits.
  • Customers converted both Gold and Game Pass: The Ultimate subscription’s prepaid time includes benefits of both subscriptions and upgraded time added consecutively.
  1. If the customer still wants a refund:
  • Cancel immediately with a refund for the Ultimate purchase.
  • Follow the steps in section 3 to calculate and issue Makegood tokens for unused Gold or Game Pass time that was upgraded to Ultimate.

References:

  • KB 4338529 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Exception based cancel and return for recurring subscriptions
  • KB 4485105 Procedure: Xbox: Refund Request Solution
Customers wanting to restore lost prepaid time after self-serve Ultimate was cancelled and refunded at account.microsoft.com.

Review their history and confirm that the Ultimate subscription was cancelled with a refund.

If confirmed, follow the steps in section 3 to calculate and issue Makegood tokens for unused Gold or Game Pass time that was upgraded to Ultimate.
The customer is unable to redeem a token due to 36-month limit and contacts us for help.

Customers may contact us for help redeeming a Live Gold or Game Pass token after receiving this message: “You've reached the limit of how far you can extend a subscription. Depending on the product it could be two to five years.” Often, they want to maximize time before upgrading to Ultimate.

You should inform these customers that they need to wait until the time added by the token, which will not exceed the 36-month limit. Customers looking to upgrade to Ultimate can:

  1. Upgrade to Ultimate, then wait if needed, and redeem at the converted Ultimate rate.
  2. Wait and redeem it while still active on their current subscription, then upgrade to Ultimate.
Advocates should not de-activate tokens and issue replacement time to reach the 36-month limit, even if customers are willing to lose time.
Customers whose non-Ultimate token is damaged, inactive, or active and in policy to replace but has an active Ultimate subscription.

If the customer has a token that they are unable to redeem and they meet the replacement requirements outlined in KB 818928 Procedure: Xbox: Code redemption issues (prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription) you should:

  1. Confirm the current conversion rate in this FAQ: https://www.xbox.com/xbox-game-pass.
    • See question, After I join Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, can I still redeem Xbox Live Gold and Xbox Game Pass codes?
  2. Then redeem on behalf of the Ultimate subscription to make them whole, rounding up to the nearest month.
Customers wanting to restore lost prepaid time after returning a reduced Xbox All Access and Game Pass Ultimate time.
 

If the customer converted prepaid Gold or other Game Pass time into Ultimate by redeeming an Xbox All Access Ultimate offer, they may perceive a loss of prepaid time after returning Xbox All Access.

  1. Review their subscription history to verify that the prepaid Game Pass Ultimate subscription time was reduced when the customer claims Xbox All Access was returned.
  2. Follow steps one to three in section 3 to calculate unused Gold or Game Pass time that was upgraded to Ultimate with Xbox All Access.
  3. Offer the customer one of the two options:
    • To remain on Ultimate as is.
  • To cancel Ultimate immediately, then redeem tokens on the customer’s behalf following steps in section 3.

Plan for unexpected issues not covered above.
  • You are empowered to use your best effort to make the customer whole.
  • It’s okay to error on the side of reasonable generosity to make things right (as good or better than before).
  • If you do not have visibility of an issue, it’s okay to give the benefit of the doubt to the customer.
  • You must document everything clearly. Report the new issue up the chain quickly via site Ranger so that it can be prioritized and codified appropriately.

3. Makegood procedure

Follow these steps to calculate make good time and issue tokens if directed in the steps above.

  1. Open the Makegood tool.

[Asset 4507425]

 

  1. Review the customer’s Xbox Services History, find the converted Gold and Game Pass subscriptions and enter the appropriate dates in the yellow cells:
    • The Ultimate start date is required.
    • Enter the Gold or Game Pass end dates at the time of conversion.

Example:

[Asset 4507426]

  1. Note the number of make good months displayed in green text.
  2. Redeem on behalf of the customer Gold or Game Pass tokens based on the number of months from three.
  3. Recommend enabling recurring billing (auto renew) on new subscriptions, the customer must complete this at https://account.microsoft.com/services/ and select Turn on recurring billing.

Note: See KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool sections that are specified:

  • For step 2 - See Services section > Xbox Live.
  • For step 4 - See Token Lookup Tool > Issue Token.
  • Token > Reason Code: Goodwill gesture
