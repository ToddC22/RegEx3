1. What is the difference between fortification and verification?

  • Fortification is when we confirm that the person we are talking to is the owner of the Bing Places account they wish to discuss with us. You do not need to fortify chat customers who are already logged into their Microsoft account because the four key customer details will automatically populate in the chat session: Username, login ID, user ID, and user type (such as SMB, chain, or agency).
  • Verification is when we confirm that the person we’re talking to is the registered/authorized owner of the business whose listing they wish to modify/claim. Note: Verification is also the term used for customers when they are verifying they are the owner of a business themselves, without support involvement. For more information on the verification options from the customer perspective, see KB 2964704 Process: Bing Places: Business listing verification.

2. Why do we do this?

  • We fortify customers to confirm that they are authorized to discuss information related to a Bing Places account. This protects Microsoft’s customer privacy and data integrity. 
  • We verify customers to confirm that they are the registered/authorized party for the business listing they wish to edit. We do this to protect the accuracy of Bing search information and protect businesses from malicious attempts to sabotage competitor data.

Note: Until a customer has been verified, we are unable to perform Admin Verification within the Bing Places UI.

3. When do we do this?

  • Fortification is required when:
    • A customer wishes to discuss specific details of a claimed listing within a Bing Places account.
    • A customer wants us to help modify a claimed listing within a Bing Places account (including performing admin-verify to speed up the re-verification process).
    • A customer wishes to use their Microsoft account (or business email) as part of the verification process.

Note: 

  • If you are not able to fortify a customer, you can still continue to provide how to guidance and support assistance; however, do not discuss account-specific details relating to the business or any claimed listings. 
  • Do not make changes to listings if a customer cannot also verify themselves. Instead, guide them through how to make changes in the UI.
  • If a customer is signed in to chat with their Microsoft account, you don’t need to go through the fortification process because the following customer details will automatically be provided during the start of the chat session: Username, login ID, user ID, and user type.
  • Verification is required when:   
    • We want to use admin-verify for an unverified listing a customer has claimed. 
    • A customer wants us to modify or escalate issues relating to their business listing, but they do not wish to claim the business themselves.

4. How do we do this?

4.1 Fortification

This is done either via ASD’s fortify option, or by asking the customer to send/receive an email from the email address associated with their Bing Places account.

ASD email note: Customers who contact support via email in ASD must complete a web-form, where they are asked to enter a Preferred Contact Email and their Bing Places Account Email.

Both are free-entry text boxes, meaning that the customer can enter anything they like. The ASD ticket is generated based on the preferred option, but we do not know for sure that the customer is the owner of these addresses.

Always fortify the email associated to the Bing Places account the customer wishes to discuss, no matter what email address the ticket is created with in ASD.

4.2 Verification

This can be done several ways, depending on whether the listing is being claimed or added. (You can tell if a listing is claimed, either by the Single-Claim status in the Admin dashboard, or simply by searching to see if the location name and address exists on Bing Local Listings already.)

Google Sync Callout: If an advertiser wants to create/verify listings which are already verified in Google My Business, they can use a Google My Business Account Sync to automatically sync and verify these listings in Bing Places. See KB 4515875 Process: Bing Places: Google My Business Account Sync for more information.

This is the recommended option to mirror GMB listings quickly and easily within Bing Places.  

For claimed listings:

  1. The advertiser has access to an email address which matches the company’s domain, and can complete the ASD fortification process. Be especially aware of any attempts to use similar domain names in a hostile attempt to access data. For example, using an email of "microsof.com” to attempt access to a “microsoft.com” website listing (known as masking).
  2. The advertiser’s website clearly calls out their email address as a recognised contact for the business, and you can successfully fortify this email address. (Can be accepted even if the email address is generic.)
  3. Call the business phone number displayed on the published YPID entity to confirm the business exists, and check they are aware of the attempt to claim their listing through Bing Places. Important: Do not share personal data relating to the claim.
  4. Call the number listed on the website, and confirm that the busines exists and that they are expecting someone to manage their listing on Bing Places.

For added listings:

The following options can only be used if the customer has a storefront that can be clearly seen via Google Streetview or Bing Streetside at the business location.

  1. The advertiser has access to an email address which matches the company’s domain, and can complete the ASD fortification process. Be especially aware of any attempts to use similar domain names in a hostile attempt to access data, for example, using an email of ”microsof.com” to attempt access to a ”microsoft.com” website listing (known as masking).
  2. The advertiser’s website clearly calls out their email address as a recognised contact for the business, and you can successfully fortify this email address. (Can be accepted even if the email address is generic.)
  3. Call the number listed on the website, and confirm that the busines exists and that they are expecting someone to manage their listing on Bing Places.

If the above options cannot be applied:

The customer will need to use postal PIN verification.

If the PIN has been received, but is not being recognised, you can ask the customer to provide a scanned copy of the PIN verification notice.

If Resend Postal PIN fails:

If a customer has attempted to use the Resend PIN feature, and this has failed, then in this scenario only you can attempt to locate a published listing for the customer on Google My Business. 

If you are successful, you can use the verification options listed above for Added listings.

If the customer is unable to pass using any of these methods, advise them that we will be unable to assist.

Ticket handling:

If a customer is reporting a missing PIN and this is within the usual twelve days maximum SLA for postal delivery (and the above admin verify steps are not applicable), you can set postal PIN delivery expectations and close the ticket.

If a customer is reporting a missing PIN and they are outside of SLA, you should use the Resend PIN function (if the above admin verify steps cannot be supplied) and keep your ticket open to confirm PIN was received.

Allow five business days for the Resend PIN delivery time, before taking further action. No need to wait the standard twelve business days in this scenario.

5. FAQ

Q: Why are you forcing people to prove their physical location/why have you changed your verification process? I rely on online/email verification to speed up the process! 

A: I understand you’d like to verify your business as quickly as possible to get your listings published on Bing. As part of our commitment to listing accuracy and integrity, we’re making sure that business locations are valid entities, and that the customers owning these businesses are expecting to be managed in our platform. We do this by verifying a location exists, and that the owner of that business location agrees to their listing being claimed and managed in our product.

Q: Can we mention that we are using Google Streetview to customers?

A: No – this is an internal process. If you are unable to locate the customer via Streetview or Bing Streetside, say that we have been attempting to confirm their physical location but we have been unable to do so.

Q: How will listings synced with GMB show up in the Bing Places UI?

A: If a listing is already verified in GMB, it will be given Verified status by default in Bing Places. If the listing is not verified, it will show as Single-Add. 

Q: If a customer has no website for their business and is having trouble receiving their postal PIN, how can we verify them as the business owner?

A: Here are a few things that can be done:

  • Ask the customer to trigger postal re-verification and wait five business days for delivery.
  • For claimed listings only: Check Google Streetview (or Bing Streetside) to verify that the business exists in the location claimed. If so, call the number of the published YPID to confirm the business exists and the user you’re talking to operates from this address.
  • If these options have been tried (or are not applicable) escalate to Global Support for missing postal PIN issues.

Q: How can I fortify the user if they are unable to access their Bing Places email?

A: If the user's Bing Places login is the same as their Microsoft account, direct the customer to the standard Microsoft account Help Pages online for help resetting their username/password.

If the user's Bing Places login is different from their Microsoft account, (for example, they use david@gmail.com to sign in, and cannot access their Gmail account) then we can do one of the following:

  • For Claimed or Verified listings only: Call the phone number on the customer's published YPID listing, and fortify via phone that we're speaking to the account user.

If the user is an agency/SEO, and they are unable to fortify due to differences between their Microsoft account and Bing Places login email, you can fortify using the following method:

  1. Ask the agency for the email they use to access Bing Places.
  2. Use the Sign-In As feature to log in to their account, and then under the Agency Information section select Edit to see the agency's email and phone number.
  3. Advise the customer that we can use this email to fortify instead, or we can offer to call on the number listed.

If agency/SEO is using a client's email, and does not have an agency account, we can ask them to sign into Bing Places and edit the information within the Preferences section of the account, so that either their preferred phone or email is displayed. We can then use this information to call/email and complete the fortification process, and change it back to the client's data after fortification has been completed.

Note: If the agency/SEO requires help with listings that haven't yet been verified by their client, we will still need to go through the usual verification process before we can help the agency edit or admin-verify listings on behalf of that client.
