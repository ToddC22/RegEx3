ASMS - Modern Commerce Overview

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides a high-level overview of the Modern Commerce Platform project and the support scenarios ASMS may encounter when assisting Modern customers.

The Modern Commerce platform includes a new rating system, a new catalog/offer system (BigCat), new agreements, new invoicing, and a new end-to-end purchase experience.

There are two Modern offers:

  1. 17G for standard subscriptions

  2. 148G for DevTest subscriptions.

Note: This offer covers all Modern offers. Each channel is reflected in documentation as Customer Led (Modern Web Direct), Partner Led (Modern CSP) and Field Led (Modern EA-like). All customer segments operate identically in the Modern Commerce Platform.

Policy

  • New Field Led customers are EA-like and some Field Led customers are renewing EA customers who are shepherded through the new system and Modernized.

  • Products available at GA will be Devices, Perpetual Software (Volume Licenses) and Azure.

  • Eventually all Commercial products will be available on Modern and will show up on one customer invoice (no ETA).

  • Additionally, the Azure Marketplace is fully Modern and all customer Marketplace purchases are on the Modern Platform.

  • Field Led Azure Modern (FLAM) customers are automatically entitled to an Azure Standard Support plan at no charge. They may choose to upgrade to a Premier Support plan which will show as an Asset in the customer’s CST profile. There are no plans at this time to charge FLAM customers for the Azure Standard Support plan.

  • Always engage ASE first when you are asked to engage ACE (unless there is an emergency).

  • Do not use the ASE template as a way to open IcM tickets to any other team. ASE is not the same as IcM.

  • Important: When assisting a customer, you may be required to provide them with a copy of details that contain Personal Identifiable Information (PII) (Ex: copy of invoice). In these scenarios, you must always be sure to delete these downloads from your computer’s hard drive as soon as you are done using them. This is to avoid accidently exposing the customer’s PII.

Customer Experience and Provisioning

The Modern Commerce Platform is organized around customer assets.

An asset could be (but is not limited to):

  • Usage Rating Asset, which is the container an Azure subscription uses to report usage to Commerce.

  • Invoice Sections, which are also known as Projects in CST. Invoice Sections provide customers with functionality to logically separate sets of Assets on a given Invoice..

  • Billing Profiles, known as Billing Groups in CST, which are used for:

    • Payment instrument management.

    • Pricing scope which defines the discounts and prices to which a customer is entitled.

    • Reservation products and marketplace SaaS products which were previously known as Marketplace Dev Services.

  • Billing Accounts, which are known as Organizations in CST. A Billing Account is the Legal Entity of a customer.

  • Commerce Root, which is an internal-only term and can have multiple Billing Accounts representing different organizations within a company (e.g. Contoso US and Contoso Asia).

  • Most assets will be transferrable both within a Commerce Root and also between Commerce Roots. ​

​For more information and an overview of Modern Assets, refer to TSG 4492506.

Platform Overview

Azure Modern Commerce is field led and includes net new, non-Azure customers, and some renewing Azure EA customers that meet a certain set of criteria.

  • FL = Field Led
  • CL= Customer Led
  • PL= Partner Led

The end-to-end flow at a high level is captured below:

​​[Asset 4491664]

End-to-End Flow

  1. The Customer receives communication from the Field through an Opportunity.

    • Field establishes base level of information (Sales Profile and Legal Entity).

  2. The Field builds a quote in Quote Center.

    • Quote can include (but not limited to):

      • Azure Pass (Commerce Rating Asset - 17G or 148G)

      • Devices (Hololens, Surface, etc.)

      • Volume Licenses (Perpetual Software)

      • Azure consumption discounts

        • Discounts can include (but not limited to) specific SKUs, resources, blanket Azure consumption discounts, etc.

      • Azure Pre-Payment, previously known as paid monetary commitment

      • Azure Promotional Credit, previously know as Monetary Credit

      • Monetary credit (sign up bonus from MS - applied to Commitment balance)

  3. The Customer receives an e-mail with a link to the Store for Business (SfB) or the Microsoft Admin Center (MAC) with a shopping cart experience with all the quoted material.

  4. Once the Customer has reviewed and approves the quote, they are sent through a purchase workflow in SfB/MAC to purchase all products in their cart.

    • During the purchase, the Customer will be prompted to fill in additional details about their company and provide a Payment Instrument (PI).

    • A Billing Account and Billing Profile (formally billing group) will be automatically created in the Modern Commerce Platform with Monetary Commitment (MC) as the primary/default payment method (if configured during quoting) and Check/Wire as the backup payment method.

      • Field Led customers (only) have the ability to create new Billing Profiles (Formerly Billing Group) via self-serve. More details on this ability can be found here: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/billing/billing-mca-section-invoice.
        Note: Self-serve transfers between billing groups is currently not supported. Support can assist customers with this action by using the Asset Transfer functionality in CST.

  5. After purchase of an Azure Pass is complete, an Azure subscription for each Azure Pass type (17G and 148G) will be automatically provisioned and attached to the Customer's Modern Commerce account.

  6. Upon consumption, Reservation purchases, and Marketplace purchases in Azure, each RP will send usage to Commerce.

    1. Commerce calculates price based on usage.

    2. Modifies based on the Azure Pass rating (Production or Dev/Test discount).

    3. Modifies based on Quoted discounts.

    4. Sends full cost to Modern Billing.

    5. Invoices on the 5th of every month for the previous calendar month.

  7. The Customer receives their invoice on the 5th of every month for consumption and purchases in the previous month.

    • This invoice includes all purchases through the above flow.

Customers can manage their Commerce assets through either Microsoft Admin Center or Ibiza.

For more detailed steps on possible customer experiences, review the Modern Commerce User Experience document.

Support Scenarios

Use the following support scenarios to best determine how to assist the customer. 

Offer Conversions 

  • Offer conversions supported (17G to 148G or 148G to 17G).

  • Use CST or CMAT to convert the offer as applicable. Reference the below table for details.

    • Note: If an attempt is made to convert a Legacy subscription on a MCA to another legacy offer via CST, it will fail with the following error message: "Get Product Subscripiton Eligibility Failed. LegacyToLegacyIsNotSupported - Conflict: account __ is linked to a modern account, but its type as the transfer destination is WD instead."

      This is by design, as this action is not allowed. Legacy offers under modern accounts must be converted to 17G only.

  • Note: ACE Tier 1 – Subscription Lifecycle should only be contacted through the ASE escalation path. ASE will engage ACE and get back to you with an appropriate response. For more information, see TSG 3093263.

Subscription Ownership Transfers (SOT) 

  • When assisting a customer with an SOT you should follow the normal SOT process outlined in TSG 3040383. However, you should note the following when assisting customers who have Marketplace purchases:

    • If the customer has a legacy account, you will need to look the customer up via CMAT.

    • If the customer is on modern, you will need to look the customer up via CST.

    • Self-serve asset transfer steps can be found here.

Note: If transfer eligibility details are not clear in CMAT or CST, escalate to PayOps for Partner Led and Customer Led customers. For Field Led MCA customers, escalate to Azure BizOps.

If a customer wants to migrate to a Modern Commerce Agreement (MCA), refer their information to Inside Sales using this CSS promotional form. Additional information about the CSS promotion can be found here.

Billing and Usage Validation 

  • Standard processes should be followed for Billing and Usage Validation.
  • To learn more about the billing permission levels see http://aka.ms/mcapiauthz. 

  • If a customer is unable to view the Pay Now option, provide the customer with the following link: https://portal.azure.com/#blade/Microsoft_Azure_Billing/PayNowBlade/invoiceId/<invoiceID>

    Note: <invoiceID> will be replaced with the invoice number the customer is attempting to pay.

  • Customer Support Tool (CST) specific actions can be found here or in the CST Overview training. 
  • When a customer signs an MCA and migrates their subscriptions, the charges against that subscription move with it to the new billing mechanism (MCA). Customers may inquire about overbilling or overlapping usage dates once they have received their first MCA invoice.

    In these instances, you should note that t    here is no double billing, as subscription charges are either rated under their legacy billing agreement or the MCA, not both. 
    • For example: 

      A PAYG customer moves to Modern on September 4th. Usage from that time forward will show up on the Oct 5th MCA invoice, generated for the September charges. However, the customer will also receive an invoice outlining the usage prior to the move, covering their July10th - Aug 9th billing cycle (as this is the way the Legacy billing cycles works).

      The customer should be able to download their usage files for each invoice and see when the charges stopped on the PAYG under the Legacy billing model, and charges picked back up on the MCA invoice post the migration.

Pricing 

  • Customers are limited to one of 17 currencies.

  • USD – US Dollar

  • AUD - Australian dollar
  • BRL - Brazilian real
  • CAD - Canadian dollar
  • CHF - Swiss franc
  • CNY - Chinese yuan
  • DKK - Danish krone
  • EUR – Euro
  • GBP - British pound
  • INR - Indian rupee
  • JPY - Japanese yen
  • KRW – Korean Won
  • NOK - Norwegian krone
  • NZD - New Zealand dollar
  • RUB - Russian ruble
  • SEK – Swedish Krona
  • TWD – Taiwan Dollar

  • All pricing is in USD and will have an exchange rate applied based on the customer's account address.

    • Exchange Rates (FXRates) are determined at the end of each month for the following month's rate and each invoice is calculated using this rate. 
    • Customer invoices will show the applied Exchange Rate each month.
    • Current and historical rates, as well as a rate calculator, can be found here: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/treasury/forex/Pages/default.aspx​​.
    • Currency mapping by location can be found in this spreadsheet: http://assetsprod.microsoft.com/modern-offers-country-currency-availability.xlsx​.
    • The pricing displayed to the customer in all local currencies supported in Modern is accurate at time of purchase. There is no additional FX rate applied.

  • All Azure consumption is priced in USD.

Accepting Microsoft Customer Agreements (MCA)

Note: MCAs are not eligible for the Public Sector.

When converting from an EA agreement to a MCA agreement, some customers are experiencing the following error when trying to accept their MCA Agreement. 

​"A Microsoft Customer Agreement is required to complete the setup of your billing account. We couldn't find an agreement associated with the Enterprise Agreement enrollment  ______. If you think this is an error, contact Azure Support."

In these instances, do the following to assist the customer: 

  1. Confirm that the email the customer is using to sign-in with, matches the authentication type in the EA portal.

    Note: ​If the email has a work or school account authentication in the EA portal, then they need to sign-in using that same work or school account to accept the agreement.
     
  2. If you have verified that the customer is using the correct email but still experiencing an error, engage the Azure Commercial Experience ​​Tier 1 team using this IcM.

Under MCA agreement, there should be 2 plans quoted:

  • Microsoft Azure
  • Microsoft Azure DevTest
If either of the plans are not available under assets, then EA to MCA transition will fail and we have to engage ACE team to contact the seller to quote the missing Azure Plan to the customer.
 

Note: ACE should only be contacted through the ASE escalation path. ASE will engage ACE and get back to you with an appropriate response. For more information, see TSG 3093263

Alternatively, if accepting MCA quote fails on Azure portal, this direct URL can be used by the person who received the email to accept the MCA quota: https://businessstore.microsoft.com/en-us/manage/organization/agreements. 
 
After this is accepted, when the same user goes to Azure Portal under Cost Management + Billing, they will see a new MCA Billing Account where their role will be Billing Profile owner.
 
Then they can start the transition from EA to MCA if they are the EA Admin or they can make an existing EA Admin Billing Profile Owner via Access Control IAM under Cost Management Billing Account blade.
 

Public facing article on how to setup billing in MCA: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/billing/billing-mca-setup-account

Public facing article on how to organize the MCA Account: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/cost-management-billing/manage/mca-section-invoice

Edit Company Information 

CST does not currently offer the option to edit customer name, address, and other company information.  Self-serve functionality is available in MAC for customers who do not have amendments to their MCA.

For customers who have amendments to their MCA, please follow the process outlined in TSG 4535714.  

If self-service fails via Microsoft Admin Center, please submit an IcM to update company information.

Glossary

Below is a list of common terms and acronyms for Modern Commerce, for the platform and FLAM.

Definition

Examples

Admin Center (SfB Admin Center)

Customer portal in SfB for managing invoices, payments, Commerce Assets, company profile, etc.

 Ibiza shows the same types of information as SfB/Admin Center.

Asset

 Management object in Modern Commerce which represents footprint of ownership and management usually acquired through a purchase. Azure Access Pass - Azure Standard, which can be multiple 17g subscriptions that roll up to the same Billing Group.

Azure Access Pass

Commerce Rating Asset that provides rating context and Commerce access to Azure subscriptions. Many subscriptions can be tied to one Azure Access Pass (Commerce Rating Asset).

An Azure Access Pass (Azure specific product) is purchasable throug Field Led quotes for free and is a pre-requisite for all Azure consumption and overage billing. It is used for rating and billing for consumption-based Azure (not RI).

The Access Pass is configured with a billing group to pay for the charges. Multiple Azure Subs can be billed against an Access Pass enabling collective graduated pricing. Customers can also move Azure Subs across Access Passes. Azure Subs are used as an Azure deployment and management container.

Customer can have 2 Production Azure subscriptions (0017G) tied to a 17G Commerce Rating Asset.
