1. Overview

Office 365 Government Community Cloud (GCC) for CSP is a set of offers made available for CSP partners. These offers have similar requirements to EDU in CSP and will be mutually inclusive to other GCC offers. The customers must have a tenant tag before the CSP partner can purchase the GCC offer. Office 365 Government GCC for CSP offers are available as detailed below, and the pricelist is available here.

Microsoft Office 365 Government GCC offers for CSP are available for the United States, effective August 2, 2018. Authorized US-based direct bill partners may begin transacting these offers through the Cloud Solution Provider program. Designed for the unique needs of US government organizations, these offers provide all the features and capabilities of Office 365 services in a segmented cloud environment that helps organizations meet US compliance and security standards. The Microsoft Cloud for US Government in the CSP program is available only to partners in United States that serve US Federal, state, and local government agencies in their governmental capacities. For sales, marketing, and enrollment details see the Related resources section below.

To get started selling these new offers, Indirect Resellers should contact their Indirect Provider. Direct bill partners will need to ensure they meet the eligibility criteria and complete the validation process to gain authorization to transact these GCC offers in Partner Center.

Notes:

-   Dynamics 365, EMS and Power BI will be available in September 2018. The eligibility criteria, validation process, and ordering steps for these offers will be the same as for Microsoft Office 365 Government GCC. Partners and their customers who are already validated for Microsoft Office 365 Government GCC will not be required to go through an additional validation for these offers.

-   Azure Government offers for the Microsoft Cloud for US Government were made available to CSP in June 2017. Direct bill partners can view onboarding guidance here. Interested US based Indirect Resellers should contact their Indirect Provider.

2. Validation process

If you would like to determine your customer's eligibility, complete the customer form here.

2.1 Partner validation process

IMPORTANT: Your organization must be enrolled in the Cloud Solution Provider program as it is a pre-requisite to being approved for Office 365 Government GCC for CSP.

Step 1: Navigate to the Office 365 Government GCC for CSP partner validation page. Complete the form and you will receive a response via email, within two business days outlining next steps. Ensure you have your Partner Tenant ID ready to complete this step.

The Partner Tenant ID can be found in the Partner’s Organization Profile in the Partner Center’s Partner Settings/Organizational profile labeled “Microsoft ID”.

[Asset 4519060]

Partner Validation Form:

[Asset 4519033]

Step 2: Once your organization is confirmed you will see “Government_community_cloud” appear as one of your organizations “Reseller Qualifications” in your Partner Center Partner Profile.

[Asset 4519034]

Your organization is now enabled for Government Community Cloud and can view customer GCC qualifications, transact, and create new GCC customers.

2.2 Customer validation process

2.2.1 New customer validation process

Once Microsoft Community Cloud has been added to your Reseller Qualifications in Partner Center Profile (see Partner validation process) you are ready to add new customers and submit them for validation for the Microsoft Office 365 Government for CSP which enables you to transact for such offers.

Step 1: In the Partner Center Dashboard à Customers page choose “Add customer”.

[Asset 4519035]

Step 2: Enter in the required customer information and choose “Government Community Cloud (GCC)” from the “Special Qualifications” drop down menu.

[Asset 4519036]

Step 3: A “Customer Validations” menu option will appear. Since this is a new customer, you will not see a Customer Validation option to choose yet, so click “What is this?” link above the drop-down menu.

[Asset 4519037]

Step 4: A help dialogue will appear. Click the “new request” link within the dialogue text.

[Asset 4519038]

Step 5: You will be taken to the Office 365 Government GCC for CSP customer validation page. Complete the form below on behalf of your customer (you will need your *Partner Tenant ID). It takes up to 2 business days for customer validation to be completed. During this time, Microsoft may contact the customer for additional information as part of the validation process. Once validation is complete, the customer’s status will be updated in their Partner Center account and you can begin placing orders for GCC offers.

*IMPORTANT: All partner types (direct reseller, indirect provider, indirect reseller) can submit validation requests on behalf of their customers. However, the partner tenant ID they use must be that of the transacting partner. For example, if you're an indirect reseller, you would use the partner tenant ID of the indirect provider.

[Asset 4519039]

Step 6: Once the customer has been validated you will see the customer’s status updated in their Partner Center account and you can place orders for GCC offers for this customer. See Check customer validation status section for details on how to do this.

2.2.2 Existing Microsoft Office 365 GCC Government customer your organization doesn’t manage

If the customer you wish to place an order for is not already in your Partner Center customer validation list you will need to request a reseller relationship and have the customer approve.

See also Partner Center Help article Connect with customers in Partner Center.

Step 1: To request a reseller relationship, select Customers from your Dashboard menu and then select “Request a reseller relationship”.

[Asset 4519040]

Step 2: On the next page, review the draft email message. You can open the draft message in your default email application or you can copy the message to your clipboard and paste it into an email. You can edit the text in the email but be sure to include the link as it is personalized to link the customer directly to your account. Select Done when you’ve completed this step.

Step 3: Send the email to your customer.

Step 4: After the customer accepts your invitation, they'll appear on your Customers page in Partner Center, and you'll be able to provision and manage the service for the customer from there.

Step 5: Check customer validation status to confirm the newly added customer is validated and ready for you to transact GCC offer orders.

5. FAQs           

Q: If a customer is GCC and the partner isn’t, should Request relationship just fail?
A: No, we will enable this scenario, the non GCC enabled partner just can’t sell products to the customer until they become GCC qualified.

Q: Can a Partner be both qualified to resell EDU and GCC?
A: Sure, why not? Some large resellers are vast across segments and will be qualified for both.

Q: Once a customer is flagged as GCC, can they purchase commercial?
A: No.

Q: Does the GCC qualification persist across partners?
A: Yes, it does. The customer qualification tag is a ‘tenant’ tag, so it should be enforced across all channels and all partners in the CSP channel.

Q: How will GCC orders be supported at Microsoft? How do we comply with government regulations of US Nationals / US soil?
A: Microsoft positions language wrt data centers hosting the government data as follows:  

  • Content from the government agency, and their end customers, is more secure because it is stored in a separate cloud, outside of Microsoft's commercial Office 365 services.
  • Government agency content is stored in cloud infrastructure within the United States.
  • Outside of the government agency, only screened Microsoft personnel can access the government agency’s customer content.
  • Office 365 Government GCC complies with certifications and accreditations required for US public sector customers, including CJIS, FedRAMP Moderate, IRS 1075, and DISA SRG L2.


Q: Does a partner need to create a new tenant for GCC or can they leverage an existing commercial tenant?
A: A partner should use their current CSP Partner tenant when applying to enable the transaction of GCC license based services (i.e. O365, EMS, Dynamics, etc).
There is not a need to create a new tenant. A separate tenant is only required if the Partner also applies for Azure Gov in CSP.
