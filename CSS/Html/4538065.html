1. Summary

Microsoft Partner Center is home to more than 300,000 partners. These include partners from small, medium and large companies. Many of these companies get acquired or merge into one another as part of the changing business landscapes. The legacy system (PMC) shows that every month 30-50 companies merge into one another. As we move into Partner Center as a one-stop place for Partners, it is imperative that Account Mergers as a feature is made available in Partner Center to support the changing business of our MPN partners. This feature is scheduled to be live in Q3 of FY20.

2. Glossary of terms

AAD - an acronym for Azure Active Directory

Tenant - represents the AAD tenant or identity partner wants to use

Account - represents what Partner Center creates as a partner footprint

CSP - Cloud Solution Provider

VAR - Value Added Reseller / Indirect reseller

PGA - an acronym for Partner Global Account (always identifiable by V-org ID or by direct mention in Prose of Account Type = PartnerGlobal)

PLA - an acronym for Partner Location Account (always identifiable by HQ ID/Location ID or by direct mention in Prose of Account Type = PartnerLocation)

Inviting Org - The account that sends the merge invite

Invited Org / Merging Org - The account that receives the merge invite and that will become PLA under the Inviting Org

3. Scope of feature

Scope:

  • Ability for a partner to initiate a request to merge another company MPN account into their existing company MPN account in Partner Center
  • Ability for a partner to accept or reject a request to merge their existing company into another company
  • All locations associated to the Invited Org will move into the Inviting Org MPN structure
  • All existing assets of the company being merged will move into the parent company – Membership Offers, Competencies, benefits included with competencies and the Action Pack, Referrals, Cloud Solution Provider (CSP), and Incentives
  • Ability for all involved partners to be notified about progress of the merge at significant milestones
  • Downstream systems should be able to get data about merge (new structure) by means of data feeds

Out of Scope:

  • Mergers and Acquisitions of CSP accounts (aka Tenant Accounts)
  • Merger of developer accounts
  • Merger of hardware accounts
  • Mergers of AAD tenants – only Partner Center accounts’ structure will be merged. AAD tenants and any assets within AAD tenants will not be merged.

4. Rules for Merge from an Accounts Perspective

  1. Only Account Admin roles have access to using this feature. Note that users with aliases #EXT# with Account Admin role (visible in Prose) will not be able to use this functionality.
  2. Accounts involved in mergers need to have completed vetting and have status Authorized.
  3. Only PGA MPN IDs can be used when requesting the merge.
  4. After merge is complete, user roles from the individuals of the merged Org should translate over into the new account.
  5. Merged Org’s MPN IDs become PLAs under the PGA of the Inviting Org.
  6. Respective tenants of merged accounts are kept independent, but get linked through the multi-tenancy feature.

5. Rules for Merge from a Competencies and Benefits Perspective

Important: Partner benefits will be merged together at renewal date, Competency attainment will continue to follow the same rules – only one MAPS, Silver/Gold Competency will be available for purchase per entire resulting account structure.

Same rules apply as in case of consolidation of accounts (find explained here and here).

  1. All the competencies of the two merging PGAs will be retained post consolidation.
  2. In case of same competency, competency with the highest proficiency level will be awarded.
  3. Highest AD of the highest membership level will be retained post merge for Silver / Gold memberships.
  4. Highest AD for the MAPS memberships will be retained post merge.
  5. Silver & Gold enrollment will run independently in PC post merge.
  6. All MAPS benefits will be merged. There will be one MAPS enrollment with the highest AD at the PGA.
  7. All ABTK benefits will be merged.
  8. Key’s state pre-merge will be maintained post consolidation.
  9. Users assignment state pre-merge will be maintained post consolidation.
  10. Offer purchase history will show up for both the Orgs together to download invoice capability.
  11. Merged Offer Purchase history will be seen in PC from merged accounts. Accounts admin, MPN admin and Global admin can get to see this page.

Also, any eligibility of the independent accounts before merge for the One-Year Benefit Extension Offer / Limited-time Offer / Others should be maintained even after the merge is completed.

6. Understand the impacts to programs and benefits when partners elect to merge accounts

  • All existing competencies (Gold/Silver), purchases (such as Microsoft Action Pack), and associated benefits are preserved during consolidation. If both companies have the same competency but one's is gold and the other silver, the competency with highest proficiency level will be awarded, and partners will have one set of silver benefits and one set of gold benefits for that competency until their next renewal.
  • Later anniversary date for Microsoft Action Pack will be retained after the merger. For example, if the company 1’s anniversary date for Action Pack renewal is June 2020, and company 2’s anniversary date for Action Pack renewal for is October 2020, Microsoft will use the October 2020 date as the new anniversary date for the merged company.
  • During the account merger and until your next renewal, each account will retain their Action Pack and/or competency benefits. At renewal, standard Action Pack and competency renewal rules apply.
  • Upon renewal, benefits that are included with competency attainment and Action Pack are implemented for the partner company’s partner global account:

• Microsoft Action Pack: The partner company will be able to purchase one Action Pack per partner global account.

• Competency: The partner company will receive one package of core benefits, associated to their highest attainment, plus competency-specific benefits the partner is eligible for per partner global account.

  • All benefits are subject to the Microsoft Partner Network benefits usage guide. For example: an activated O365 E3 token is functional for twelve (12) months after activation. Once a token has been activated for seats on a tenant, those seats may not be moved to another tenant.
  • The MCP ID associations for both companies will be retained and associated with the PGA MPN ID.
  • Go-to-market and technical benefits are offered as competency core benefit. Post-merge, it is recommended that you check your bank and tax information to ensure accuracy.
  • If your company is in the Azure Expert MSP program, benefits are retained until renewal.
  • If your company has earned advanced specializations, they are retained across both accounts.
  • Any software assurance vouchers are retained across both accounts.

7. User Experience in Partner Center

7.1 Inviting Org User Experience in Partner Center

Vetting must be authorized for the Inviting Org.

Partner with Account Admin user role goes under Settings wheel icon -> Account Settings.

[Asset 4578077]

Partner then selects Account Merge tab.

In the next window, Partner will be prompted to insert the PGA MPN ID of the account he is inviting to merge.

[Asset 4578293]

 

Note: If the provided MPN ID is not the PGA MPN ID or it is incorrect, Partner will be made aware of the following: “Account merge is currently not available for this MPN ID. Please try again later or use a different MPN ID.”

[Asset 4578078]

After providing the MPN ID, the Partner will be shown with the details of the invited account to be merged and can confirm sending the invitation to the acquired Org.

[Asset 4578080]

After the invitation has been sent, Partner will be prompted that the merge is in progress and pending the merging company acceptance to complete it. Also, the details of the invited Org to merge will be displayed.

In the next screen, Partner with Account Admin rights is advised of the fact that he has received an invitation to merge under a different account and he can review the details of the inviting org, as well as the result after the merge.

7.3 Finalizing the Merge process (by the Inviting Org)

The following email will be sent when a merge request is accepted:

Recipients: All Account Admins of Initiating Account

Subject: Partner Center Account Merge Request Accepted

[Asset 4538049]

After the merge invitation has been accepted by the invited org, the inviting org’s Account Admin will need to proceed with the last step of the merge confirmation by going to the Account merge tab in his Partner profile and clicking on the Initiate merge button, while being advised again of the fact that this action cannot be undone.

[Asset 4538050]

The status will then change to make the aware of the merging process taking around 30 minutes to complete and that he should afterwards sign in again to view the final details. Also, the details of the person who accepted the merge are displayed and merge history is updated to reflect the moment when the merge was accepted.

[Asset 4538052]

The following email will be sent when a merge completed successfully:

Recipients: All Account Admins of Initiating Account

Subject: Partner Center Account Merge Completed

[Asset 4538053]

Note: If the PC system detects a technical blockage that is preventing the merge from completing, the Account admins of the Inviting Org will receive an e-mail advising them of this.

The following email will be sent when a merge did not complete successfully:

Recipients: All Account Admins of Initiating Account

Subject: Partner Center Account Merge Failed

[Asset 4538054]

8. Q&A

Q: How will my partner learn about this capability?

A: Partners will see a notification in Partner Center about its availability. That notification will include a link to the online documentation page where they can learn more about how it works and decide whether it is right for their business.

Q: Are partners with multiple MPN accounts required to merge them?

A: No, it is not required. Partners should decide whether merging their MPN accounts meets their business goals and is right for them. Each partner organization’s situation is different.

Q: Why should a partner use this capability?

A: Partners managing multiple MPN membership accounts in Partner Center may wish to combine their accounts into a single MPN membership account for various reasons. This capability allows them to take those steps. Before beginning the account merge process, partners should review the online documentation at https://docs.microsoft.com/partner-center/account-merge to understand how it impacts their particular situation. Partners who combine their accounts are better positioned to realize:

  1. Improved revenue and performance attribution to help meet competency requirements and incentives criteria
  2. Reduced time needed to manage multiple MPN accounts
  3. Reduced enrollment fees for competencies and associated benefits
Q: Which accounts in Partner Center are included in the account merge capability?

A: With this account merge capability, partners can merge two Partner Center MPN accounts. It does not merge CSP accounts or tenants. Partners can have many relationships with Microsoft in Partner Center – developer, MPN, CSP, incentives, commercial marketplace, and Co-sell. This capability applies only to the Partner Center MPN account.

Q: Where does my partner get the details about account merge in Partner Center?

A: Your partner should review the online documentation at docs.microsoft.com/partner-center/account-merge. If they need assistance, they should open a support ticket in Partner Center. The partner support team has been trained on the capability and is best equipped to help partners understand and complete the process.

Q: When a partner initiates a merge or receives a merge invite, how long will it take for the partner to complete those steps?

A: The time needed to complete the process will vary by partner. Prior to performing the account merge, every partner should assess the impact to their competencies, Action Pack, and associated benefits that will take place at their next renewal after merging. If they need assistance, partners can open a support ticket in Partner Center.

Q: When a partner performs an account merge, are they permitted to transfer already activated product license benefits from one tenant to another?

A: No, this is not permitted. All activated product licenses are subject to the Microsoft Partner Network benefits usage guide.

Q: What will happen to partners who purchased Advanced Support for Partner (ASfP) or Premier Support for Partner (PSfP) if they elect to merge their MPN accounts?

A: Paid support plans remain in effect throughout the term of the agreement.

  • Partners with one or more CSP enrollments in the same region need one support contract.
  • Partners with one or more CSP enrollments in multiple regions need one support contract for each region where they have a CSP enrollment.
Q: Is there partner impact to ECIF if partners elect to merge their MPN accounts?

A: No. Continue to use the same process for ECIF as used today.

Q: Will my partner be prompted to check or update their bank and tax set-up for incentive pay-outs to ensure accuracy if they elect to merge their MPN accounts?

A: No. Partners must take proactive steps to validate this information once they complete the account merge.
