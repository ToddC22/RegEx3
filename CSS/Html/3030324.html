ASMS – Handling Disabled Subscriptions

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to handle disabled subscriptions.
Reasons an account may become disabled can include (but are not limited to):

  • The trial period ending on a Free Trial subscription.
  • The customer disabling or canceling their own account from the Azure portal.
  • The customer getting flagged and blocked by the Risk Management team.
    Note: Risk Management is an internal team name. Do not share this this name with customers.

Policy

Before reactivating a disabled subscription, obtain approval from the account administrator following the instructions in TSG 2646876.

Support Scenarios

Customer’s Subscription Shows as Disabled via the Azure Portal

Ask your customer to provide the following:

  1. Their subscription ID (If not already provided in the case details).
  2. A screenshot showing the disabled status in the portal.

Do the following:

  1. Check to see if the subscription is enabled on the Commerce Platform (CP) side by checking in CMAT.

    If Status, Entitlement Status and Fulfilment status (for OMS accounts), are not all Active, then the issue is with the CP. For more information about how to read the status see TSG 3052408.
  2. Check the subscription status from the RDFE side.
    1. In Jarvis, navigate to and run AzureRT/Subscription Management/Get Subscription with Details.
    2. Look for SubscriptionStatus in the output. (Will either be Active or Disabled.)
      • If it is Disabled, check for possible disablements from billing issues, such as charge declines or non-payment. If there are no obvious billing issues, and no issues visible from ASMS’s perspective, engage PayOps to further investigate the customer’s subscription.
  3. Check the subscription status from the Billing side.
    1. In Jarvis, navigate to and run Billing/General Management/From Azure Subscription ID Get Subscription Info.
    2. Look for the SubscriptionStatusInfo in the output. (Will either be Active, Disabled, or Expired.)
      • If SubscriptionStatusInfo is Expired, your customer is on a Free Trial account that has expired.
        • Have the customer convert their subscription to a Pay-As-You-Go using steps outlined here. 
      • If SubscriptionStatusInfo is Disabled, check the MonetaryCapStatus variable.
        • If MonetaryCapStatus:MonetaryCapHit, check with PayOps to confirm the status.
          • If your customer has reached their Monetary Cap, there is no way to increase it. The customer can wait for the billing cycle to reset which will reset the Monthly Spending limit.
          • Alternatively, the customer can remove the Monetary Cap by adding a credit card to the subscription, which will re-enable their account. Any usage beyond the Cap will be billed to their credit card.
      • If the SubscriptionStatusInfo is Enabled, check the MonetaryCapStatus variable.
        • If the MonetaryCapStatus variable has a condition that might disable the account, reach out to PayOps to enable the subscription.
        • If the status is Enabled but the customer is unable to access the subscription (especially if the previous PayOps step failed), engage the Windows Azure Operations Center (WASU) team to enable the subscription. See TSG 2630659 for more details.

Portal Shows Subscription as Disabled, but Jarvis and CMAT/Billing Shows Subscription as Active/Enabled

Ask your customer to provide the following:

  1. Their subscription ID (If not already provided in the case details).
  2. A screenshot showing the disabled status in the portal.

Do the following:

  1. Check to see if the Subscription is Active from the RDFE and Billing side.
    (Instructions provided in the previous scenario.)
  2. If it’s active and if it still shows as Disabled in the Azure portal, then it’s a portal Issue. You will need to engage the ASE team using TSG 3093263.

Subscription Is Disabled but Customer Is Still Getting Billed

Ask your customer for the following:

  1. Their subscription ID (If not already provided in the case details).
  2. Usage Summary and billing details.
  3. Times and dates of when your customer disabled their subscription.
    • Preferably in UTC.

Do the following:

  1. Check to see if the subscription is disabled at both ends [RDFE/Billing]. If the subscription is enabled, let your customer know that the subscription hasn’t been disabled.
    In Jarvis: RDFE/Subscription Management/List Operation History for Subscription

    Note:     Even if the Subscription is disabled mid-month, a bill will be generated for the entire month. Services for the subscription won’t be billed from the day the subscription got disabled. If there is billing occurring despite being canceled in this manner, gather a Usage Report from the customer.
  2. If the customer is still being billed for services even after the subscription got disabled at the RDFE/Billing ends (check from the Usage Report), then this will be a Billing Validation Issue and should follow as per billing validation issues.

    Note: When verifying whether a customer's subscription is fully activated or not, you must check in CMAT and Jarvis before asking the customer to check in their portal.
  3. In CMAT, check that Subscription Status, Entitlement Status, and Fulfillment Status are all set to Active.
  4. In Jarvis, run AzureRT/Subscription Management/Get Subscription With Details to check for SubscriptionStatus:Active.
  5. Unless all four statuses in CMAT and Jarvis show as Active, the subscription should not be considered fully active.

Subscription Disabled Due to 3rd Party VM on Benefit Subscription

Benefit subscriptions (MSDN, BizSpark, etc.) can become disabled due to the deployment of 3rd party VMs (despite still having credits available).

Ask your customer for the following:

  1. Their subscription ID (If not already provided in the case details).

Do the following:

Usually, you would suspect this scenario if you notice the customer has no billing issues and has credits remaining yet still has had their account disabled on a benefit subscription.

  1. Pull up the subscription in CMAT.
  2. Check if there are credits remaining and what the status of their subscription is. Normally, you will see that the spending limit has been hit despite having credits remaining.
  3. Check to see if a credit card is attached to the subscription.
    Note: Third party VMs require a credit card.
  4. Pull up the usage report and see if the following VMs are available: Red Hat Enterprise Linux or Oracle Software. Normally, you will see the customer has deployed this VM in the last couple days before disablement.
    • If any of these VMs are present, your customer will need to remove their spending limit and add a credit card in order to regain immediate access. This is not a great experience for customers, so use your customer support skills to soften the language. The FAQ section of the virtual machine pricing page also provides documentation of this behavior.
  5. If the customer is unable to remove the spending limit, or the services do not start (at least an hour after the spending limit has been removed), engage ASE (TSG 3093263) or WASU (TSG 2630659).

Helping Customers Recover from a Subscription Disablement

In situations where a customer's subscription has been re-enabled after having been disabled against the customers wishes, such as for non-payment, the customer may need technical assistance from Azure Support to get their services and data re-deployed. For example, the customer may need assistance re-deploying their Cloud Services (which get deleted when the subscription was disabled) or recovering their IP addresses (which get deleted unless the customer was using Reserved IPs).

Do the following:

  1. Gather your customer’s case information, and get the customer routed to the correct Azure Technical Support team that can help the customer.
    • Engage your Technical Advisor or Partner Technical Advisor (TA or PTA) if you aren’t sure which team your customer should be routed to.
    • Do not attempt to help the customer with their technical issues.

Engagement and Escalation Path

  • If you require Master Data Services (MDS) logs, request them by submitting a ticket to the Azure Service Engineering (ASE) team using this link: http://aka.ms/asecri.
