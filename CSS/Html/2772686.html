Scenarios
 

  • The customer has received a notification that his or her service has expired or has reduced functionality.
    • If the customer is located in Ukraine, special provisions exist for the Microsoft Online Subscription Agreement (MOSA). Please follow the steps in 2856716 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Support guidance about Microsoft Online Subscription Agreement (MOSA), Customer Portal Terms of Use, and Microsoft Online Services Agreement to help these customers. If customer’s account is disabled because of the scenario described in the linked article, do not re-enable the subscription.
    • If this is a direct customer, follow the procedure in this document.
    • If this is a Volume Licensing (VL) customer, see 2820242 Informational: M365 Commerce: Supporting Volume Licensing customers for the procedure to handle re-enable or end date extension requests.
    • If this is a Prepaid customer and the subscription status is In grace period, advocates can reactivate the subscription so the customer can add a new product key.
    • If this is a Prepaid customer and the subscription status is Disabled, advocates cannot reactivate the subscription. If the customer wants to continue using the service, they will have to create a new subscription.
    • If this is a subscription that has been transitioned to another using the transition wizard, do not re-enable the source subscription. See 2772811 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to use the switch plans feature in the Microsoft 365 admin center for more information on how to identify the source subscription.
    • If a Brasil customer subscription is disabled due to missing or invalid CNPJ ID, follow the procedure in this document.
  • Technical Support (TS) transfers a case to Commerce Support because a customer called them reporting their service has reduced functionality. TS identifies that it is not a technical support issue and that the account was disabled due to non-payment.
  • A customer calls and says their service is down or reports that they do not have access to their service.

2. Guidance

Re-enabling is by order.

Credit card customers whose subscriptions are in In grace period or Disabled status can, in many cases, re-enable (Reactivate) their subscription in the Microsoft 365 admin center. Choosing Reactivate will prompt the customer to update their existing credit card or add a new one. Once the credit card has been updated and the balance is cleared, the subscription will be re-enabled automatically. If the customer only wants to settle their balance and does not want to continue with their subscription, they should cancel their subscription after the balance is cleared (about 4 calendar days from the date the credit card was updated).

If the customer does not see the Reactivate option in the Microsoft 365 admin center, assist them using this document.

The procedure to enable an expired or disabled subscription is the same for trial and paid subscriptions. If the subscription is not disabled due to lifecycle and has been disabled due to fraud, see section Subscriptions (Credit Card/Invoice Paid and Trial, all purchasing channels: VL, MPSA, Prepaid) that are in Disabled: Fraud state.

Advocates are allowed to re-enable the disabled direct debt subscription for the purpose of updating the direct debit information or if the customer states they have already updated the direct debit information and would like their service restored.

3. Procedure

View the Subscription Status and Subscription Status Change History in the subscription details.
 
For Direct customers paying by credit card
Billing behavior for credit card customers whose subscription is in In grace period or Disabled status
When will the customer’s card be charged once the subscription is re-enabled?
  • If the customer re-enabled their subscription in the Microsoft 365 admin center, their card will be charged for the outstanding balance within 4 days of the credit card being updated.
  • If the advocate re-enabled the subscription in CMAT, the customer’s card will be charged at the next billing cycle.
Will the customer be billed for the time while in status In grace period or Disabled?
  • If the subscription was In grace period with Status Reason: Payment or Non-payment, they will be charged for the time they were in status In grace period.
  • If the subscription was In grace period or Disabled with any other Status Reason, they will not be charged for the time they were in status In grace period or Disabled. Do not share this information with the customer.

Credit card subscriptions that are in a Disabled state (not Fraud or LifeCycleLockout)
 
  • If the status is Disabled, check the Subscription Status Change History page. Does the subscription meet all of the following conditions?

    1. Did the subscription move directly from Active to Disabled (i.e. no Subscription State of In grace period)?

    2. Does the ReasonCode = “Payment”?

    3. Does the ChangedBy field = “MintEventNotification”?

    4. Is there a line item with Status equal to “Chargeback”?
     
    a. View the Charges for the Payment Instrument.
    b. Look through the Charges for a line item with Status equal to “Chargeback”.

    5. If the subscription meets all these conditions, it's possible the customer disputed a Microsoft charge with their credit card company resulting in a chargeback. (Don't use the term “chargeback” with the customer.) 
    a. Re-enable the subscription and ask the customer to login and add another card to the account. Use the following suggested script: "Did you or someone with your company dispute a charge with Microsoft? If so, please add another card or if another card is not available, ask your bank to issue a new card. In the meantime, we can re-enable your service while you work through the issue." Note: The charge that was disputed and recalled by credit card company/bank will not be re-charged.

    b. If the customer wants to continue to use their existing card, they must provide a statement from their bank that the bank will no longer block Microsoft charges. Tier 3 needs this information when requesting that the card be unblocked. Note: That previous credit will not be charged again even if the customer uses the same card which has been unblocked.
  • If the status is Disabled and does not meet the conditions stated above, re-enable the subscription.


Credit card subscriptions that are in a Deprovisioned state
If the status is Deprovisioned, it is impossible to re-enable this subscription. Please let the customer know that they will need to purchase a new subscription if they would like to continue using the service.

Invoice pay subscriptions that are in status In grace period
  • The invoice pay customer will not be charged once their subscription is put into In grace period.
  • They may receive a pro-rated credit for the balance of the period, similar to canceling the subscription with reason "User request".
  • Once the subscription is put into Active status, billing will begin again (with no charges for the time during In grace period). Do not share this information with the customer.
  • General QUESTIONS about why their subscription is in this state
    1. Inform the customer that there is an overdue balance on their subscription and that this balance must be settled before it can be re-activated.
    2. Check the subscription Current Status End Date in CMAT. Inform the customer that their subscription will be disabled if the balance is not settled before the Current Status End Date.
  • Customer requests to REACTIVATE their subscription
    For EMEA region only:
    i. View the CSS Refund checklist file. If the column "Total Overdue"= 0 or is a negative amount, the customer does not owe anything. Note in the case that the subscription was re-enabled based on the CSS Refund checklist file showing that the "Total Overdue" = 0 or is a negative amount. There is no need to create an assistance request for GFS unless the customer has stated that they have been contacted by a third-party collections agency.

    ii. If the CSS Refund checklist file shows that the customer has an overdue balance, but the customer can provide any of the following, re-enable the subscription:
    · Check number, or
    · Remittance advice detail, or
    · Promised date of payment (the date must be within 2 weeks of customer calling or submitting a case) 
    All other regions:
    1. DO NOT re-enable the subscription.
    2. Create an assistance request for GFS.
    3. Obtain and document in the case:
      1. Existing payment details, if they state payment was already made, or
      2. The date that the customer is committing to settle the balance.
    4. Assign the assistance request to GFS to track the payment.
    5. If the subscription’s End Date is less than 7 days from the current date, extend the subscription’s Current Status End Date by 7 days to allow more time for the payment to be received and applied to the account.

  1. If the status is Disabled and Status Reason is Payment, Non-Payment, User Request, Lifecycle or Unidentified, check to see if the subscription was disabled by GFS.

  2. If GFS did NOT disable the subscription, re-enable the subscription. Advocate Comments for GFS-disabled subscriptions will show either:
    • Disabled by GFS xxxxxx; or
    • Disabling tagged as test/fraud/never-paid/bad debt/delinquent.
    If the comments show that GFS disabled the subscription, there are 2 scenarios: 
    a. If the comments include: “No re-enablement without approval from GFS”, or “Disabling tagged as fraud”, DO NOT re-enable the subscription. Get the customer’s contact information and create an assistance request for GFS.

    b. If the comments include: “Disabled by GFS xxxxxx” or “Disabling tagged as test/never-paid/bad debt/delinquent”, re-enable the subscription ONLY if:
    EMEA ONLY:

    i. In the CSS Refund checklist file, the column "Total Overdue"= 0.00 or is a negative amount, i.e. the customer does not owe anything. Note in the case that the subscription was re-enabled based on the CSS Refund checklist file showing that the "Total Overdue" = 0.00 or is a negative amount. Note: The re-enablement is per order, not per tenant. 
    There is no need to create an assistance request for GFS unless the customer has stated that they have been contacted by a third-party collections agency.
    ii. If the CSS Refund checklist file shows that the customer has an overdue balance, but the customer can provide:
    · Check number, or
    · Remittance advice detail, or
    · Promised date of payment (the date must be within 2 weeks of customer calling or submitting a case)
    All other regions:

    If the comments include: “Disabled by GFS xxxxxx” or “Disabling tagged as test/never-paid/bad debt/delinquent”, re-enable the subscription ONLY if the customer can provide: 

    · Check number, or
    · Remittance advice detail, or
    · Promised date of payment (the date must be within 2 weeks of customer calling or submitting a case)
     
    c. If you re-enable the subscription based on either of the above conditions:
    • Advise the customer “Your service might be interrupted again if full payment of the overdue amount is not received within 2 weeks”, and
    • Go to the next step to create an assistance request for GFS.
  3. If you re-enable the subscription, create an assistance request for GFS using the template: GFS Re-enablement Template for the invoiced customer.
  4. GFS will investigate and determine in what state the customer's service should be within 2 business days.
    a. If NO payment details or promise of payment is provided in the assistance request, GFS will disable the subscription immediately and mark “NO re-enablement without GFS approval”.

    b. If payment details are provided in the assistance request, GFS will investigate the payment and close the assistance request with the comment “Payment will be checked.”
    i. If payment is received and posted correctly, no action will be taken by GFS.
    ii. If payment is received but not posted yet, GFS will post the payment.
    iii. If payment details are incorrect, GFS will follow up with the customer for the correct payment information.
    c. If a promise of payment is provided in the assistance request, GFS will close the assistance request with “Payment will be followed up by GFS”. GFS will check for payment in 14 days.
    i. If payment is received within 14 days, GFS will post the payment.
    ii. If no payment is received within 14 days, GFS will disable the account again.

Subscriptions (Credit Card, Invoice Pay, Trial, all purchasing channels: VL, MPSA Prepaid) that are in Disabled: Fraud state

Note: DO NOT use the word “fraud” when talking to the customer.
  • If a customer is repeatedly getting disabled by fraud, escalate to Tier 3 Engineering so they can follow up with the Fraud Ops team to launch an investigation for the root cause
  • If the status is Disabled: Fraud, follow article 3144221 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Risk/Fraud escalation to RiskOps team for details..  
  • If the Fraud Ops team re-enabled the subscription, use the following standard text:

    “As part of our strong commitment to protect our customers and our interest in preserving the quality and integrity of the Office 365 Marketplace, we perform reviews of accounts which may exhibit irregular activity. Unfortunately, we can't share specific details regarding why an account was selected for review, or how we reached our decision to close it. We’ll use this as an opportunity to improve our processes and prevent legitimate customers like you from an errant closure. We apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused and thanks for your patience.”
  • If the Fraud Ops team disabled the subscription due to fraud by mistake, offer the customer a credit for the period during which the subscription was disabled due to fraud:
    1. Offer a credit for the number of days the subscription was disabled due to fraud. (Monthly cost of the subscription\30 (days) x seats x days disabled due to fraud)
    2. If the credit amount is >$2500 (USD or local currency equivalent), escalate to Tier 3 to obtain approval. Use credit reason: Fraud/Unrecognized Charge.
    3. If the credit amount is <$2500 (USD of local currency equivalent), apply the credit in CMAT. 
      Use credit reason: Fraud/Unrecognized Charge. If the credit is greater than your approval authority, CMAT will prompt you to choose approvers for the credit.  See KB 2996655 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT.
  • If the subscription is already re-enabled after being disabled due to fraud by mistake, do not escalate to the Fraud Ops team.
    Provide the standard text above and close the case. If the customer asks for a credit, follow the steps in the previous section to process a credit.
 
Brasil customer’s subscription disabled due to invalid or missing CNPJ ID
  1. Is the customer's Preferred Currency "USD"?
    1. Yes – follow the appropriate section in this document for the payment type, subscription status and reason code.
    2. No - go to step 2.
  2. Customer's preferred currency is "BRL":
    1. Does the customer have a CNPJ ID in the Tax Information section of the Account page?
      • No
        i. Inform the customer that a CNPJ is required.
        ii. If the customer provides a CNPJ, enter it. If accepted by CMAT, re-enable the customer.
        iii. If the customer does not have a CNPJ or the CNPJ provided is not accepted by CMAT, do not re-enable the customer.
      • Yes
        i. If Tax id status = "Pending", re-enable the customer. 
        ii. If the Tax id status is equal to any other value:
        1. Navigate to the Subscriptions page.
        2. View the Subscription Status Change History.
        3. If the Reason Code for the status change is Lifecycle or UserRequest, re-enable the customer.
        4. If the Reason Code is Payment, follow the appropriate section in this document for the payment type, subscription status and reason code. 
        5. If the Reason Code is blank, do not re-enable.
        a. Inform the customer that their CNPJ is invalid and request they provide a new one.
        b. If CMAT accepts the new CNPJ, re-enable the customer.
