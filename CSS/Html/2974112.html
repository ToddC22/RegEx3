1. Microsoft account

1.1 Account closure scenarios

There can be a several reasons for customers to want to close accounts. In many cases, it may be due to an underlying problem that can be remedied without having to close the account, so advocates are encouraged to ask why to see if there’s a way to save the account first.

If the underlying problem prompting the account closure can’t be remedied, you can refer to How to close your Microsoft account for steps to close an account.

If the reason for closing an account is being made by the legal representative of a deceased customer, please refer the customer to Next-of-kin process for Outlook.com for more information. 

Return to top

1.2 Customer wants to restore a closed account

If 60 days or less since closure
  1. Advise customer to try and sign-in.
  2. If prompted, cancel Close account request.
  3. If not prompted, the account was closed over 60 days ago.
If over 60 days since closure
  1. Ask for alternate verification to confirm customer's identity.
  2. Advise that data from account cannot be restored after 60 days from the closure request.
  3. Share with customers that the best course of action is to create a new account to receive the services they want.

Note: Closing an account does not block Windows sign-in. However, any services connected to the account will no longer be accessible once the account is closed.

Return to top

1.3 Customer wants to install an app or service associated with a closed account

To keep personal information private, Microsoft permanently deletes all information associated with an account 60 days after a customer asks us to close it. Once the 60 days have past, we cannot restore most data or services.

Any exceptions to this policy are listed in the relevant sections below.

If the customer’s exception isn’t listed in the sections below, advise them that their best option is to create a new account and sign up for the app or service desired.

5. Skype FAQ

5.1 Customer is still being charged for Skype after closing their Microsoft account. This may occur when customers use their Microsoft account to sign in to Skype. Skype uses a billing platform that isn't connected to Microsoft accounts like others are.

Customers using a Microsoft account to sign in to Skype should contact Skype support to cancel their subscription after closing their Microsoft account.
5.2 Can customers keep using Skype after closing a Microsoft account? Yes, but only if the customer was using a Skype ID instead of a Microsoft account.
5.3 What if a Skype customer without a Skype ID closes their Microsoft account? Customers who don't have a Skype ID will not be able to sign in to Skype after their Microsoft account is closed. In this scenario, direct the customer to Skype assisted support:
  1. Go to http://www.skype.com/go/help.support.request
  2. Select the Account/Password option and any subtopic.
5.4 Customer wants to keep Skype after deleting their Microsoft account.
  1. Customer should sign in to Skype with their Skype name and password.
  2. In Account settings customers unlink their Microsoft account.
  3. Finally, they delete their Microsoft account.
Note: If they forgot their Skype name or password, they can recovery it at https://login.skype.com/recovery
5.5 The customer wants a refund before closing their account
  • Any refund request should be handled by Skype advocates according to the Skype Refund Policy.
    If the subscription is
    • unused it can be refunded within its validity
    • used, it cannot be refunded.
  • Skype credit can be refunded within terms of Skype Refund Policy
5.6 I've closed my account, but a friend has seen my details in the Skype directory.
  • Due to the 30-day waiting period, the customer’s details will be in the directory until this period has expired, when the account will be automatically deleted from the directory
5.7 I want to delete my conversations history.
  • Individual conversations or entire conversation history can be deleted. Once chat history is deleted, it cannot be recovered.
  • Skype for Windows: https://support.skype.com/en/faq/FA392/#8
  • Skype for Mac: https://support.skype.com/en/faq/FA10888/#Delete
5.8 I want to delete my account profile and / or be removed the Skype directory (search)
  • Refer the customer to https://support.skype.com/en/faq/FA390/ which explains who can see their profile information.
    • Note: By removing all personal information from their profile, their details will not be searchable using this profile information. However, their Skype Name will still be searchable and the only way to delete their Skype Name is to delete their account.

6. Windows Apps FAQ

6.1. Apps purchased from Microsoft Store now show an X or an error after a Microsoft account was closed The advocate will follow the White Glove Service (MIR) Policy to support the customer:
  • White Glove Service (MIR) Policy: Customer calls support within a set timeframe after the 60th day of their Microsoft account closure, we will assist the customer by providing promo CSV to the their new Microsoft account equal to the amount on the customers closed Microsoft account.
  • Set time frame: within 4 months past the 60th day, to give the customer time to contact us.
  1. Verify the customer's information.
  2. Since their Microsoft account is closed, the customer information will not appear.
  3. Engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA).
  4. ACA will search in MST using the customer name or alternate information to lookup customers previous purchase.
  5. The advocate will look up the amount of money the customer had spent using their Microsoft account in the Microsoft Store.
  6. Then, the advocate will issue a new promo CSV for that amount to new Microsoft account.
  7. The customer can then re-acquire apps from the Microsoft Store.
6.2 Windows customer can't find purchased apps after closing Microsoft account The advocate will follow the White Glove Service (MIR) Policy to support the customer:
  • White Glove Service (MIR) Policy: Customer calls support within a set timeframe after the 60th day of their Microsoft account closure, we will assist the customer by providing promo CSV to their new Microsoft account equal to the amount on the customers closed Microsoft account.
  • Set time frame: within 4 months past the 60th day, to give the customer time to contact us.
  1. Verify the customer's information.
  2. Since their Microsoft account is closed, the customer information will not appear.
  3. Engage the ACA.
  4. ACA will search in MST using the customer name or alternate information to lookup customer's previous purchase.
  5. The advocate will look up the amount of money the customer had spent using their Microsoft account in the Microsoft Store.
  6. Then, the advocate will issue a new promo CSV for that amount to new Microsoft account.
  7. The customer can then re-acquire apps from the Microsoft Store.
6.3 Customer closed their account with a positive CSV balance in their Microsoft account
  1. Verify the customer's information.
  2. Since their Microsoft account is closed, the customer information will not appear.
  3. Engage the ACA.
  4. The ACA will search in MST using the customer name or alternate information to lookup customers previous purchase.
The following scenarios can occur:

Scenario A

The customer wants a refund:

We can only initiate the refund when the account balance is at or below the refund threshold. Refunds provided for retail-purchased Gift Cards, and not for promotional Gift Cards/codes.
  1. In regions where not required by law to give refunds:

    The advocate will ask the customer to expend the remainder of the funds before closing out the Microsoft account.
  2. In regions where required by law to give refunds for retail purchased Gift Cards (Canada / Chile / US):

    Retail purchased currency (gift cards) may be refunded only for customers who live in the following US states or countries.
When there is no requirement to refund, we will not provide one. Below are the thresholds for State or Country (varies by State/Country):

California --> less than $10

Colorado --> $5 or less

Maine --> less than $5

Massachusetts --> $5 or less

Montana --> less than $5

Rhode Island --> less than $1

Vermont --> less than $1

Washington --> less than $5

Canada --> Consumers must be able to request and receive refunds for balances under $5

Chile --> Stored value must be refunded in cash or deposited in a local bank account. No threshold limit applies
  • Note: No mention of additional regions/countries outside this list because the list may change.
Scenario B

The customer wants to recover funds in their closed Microsoft account and move the funds to a new Microsoft account:
  1. The advocate will engage the ACA.
  2. ACA can look up in MST how much value was left and issue new promo CSV equal to the amount the customer had available.
  3. The customer should log into the Microsoft Store using a new Microsoft account and redeem that CSV.
  4. Follow the White Glove Service (MIR) Policy below.
Scenario C

The customer deletes their Microsoft account and contacts support to recover their CSV funds in their old Microsoft account to transfer over to a new Microsoft account.

The advocate engages the ACA.

The [Asset 4039877] team can look up in MST how much value was left and follow the White Glove Service (MIR) Policy below.
  • White Glove Service (MIR) Policy: Customer calls support within a set timeframe after the 60th day of their Microsoft account closure, we will assist the customer in providing promo CSV to the customers new Microsoft account equal to the amount on the customers closed Microsoft account.
  • Set timeframe: within 4 months past the 60th day, to give the customer time to contact us.
6.4 Microsoft Store Developer can't see apps after closing Microsoft account Neither CSS nor the customer can recover a closed Dev Center account.

Customers need to:
  1. Create a new Microsoft account
  2. Sign up/register with the Dev Center using the new Microsoft account
  3. Submit their apps to the Dev Center for certification and publishing.

7. Office FAQ


7.1 Office 2013 Perpetual

Customer closed their Microsoft account wants to get their product key.		 Set timeframe: within 5 years of the 60th day after account closure.

Note: CP data retention is 5 years. Customers may proactively delete their Microsoft account while an active Office perpetual license is associated to it, or if age out occurs. (Age out = 5 years or 1825 days of inactivity.)


7.2 Annual Microsoft 365 Subscription

Customer's Microsoft account was closed or aged-out		 Set timeframe: Within 4 months past the 60th day after closing an account, to give the customer time to contact us.

Note: CP data retention is 5 years. Customers may proactively delete their account while an active annual Microsoft 365 subscription is associated to it, or if age out occurs. (Age out = 5 years or 1825 days of inactivity.)


7.3 Monthly Microsoft 365 Subscription

Customer's Microsoft account was closed or aged-out		 Set timeframe: Within 1 month past the 60th day, to give the customer time to contact us.

Note: CP data retention is 5 years. Customers mayproactively delete a Microsoft account with an active monthly Microsoft 365 subscription, or if age out occurs. (Age out = 5 years or 1825 days of inactivity.)

7.4 Customer bought Office subscription from partner (Best Buy, Staples, others) and is still being charged
  1. The customer has closed their Microsoft account while an active Microsoft 365 subscription was associated to it, or it aged out.
  2. The advocate will verify customer information.
  3. Since their Microsoft account is closed, the customer information will not appear.
  4. The advocate will engage the ACA.
  5. The ACA will verify the proof of active Office subscription is retained in MST for 5 years
  6. The ACA team should work to identify if the root cause of the issue is related to Microsoft account being closed/aged out (5 years or 1825 days of inactivity) by asking relevant questions.
  7. If customer wants to keep the service, the Advanced Capability Advocates advocate will escalate to partner:
    • The advocate should ask the partner to cancel subscription by providing them the existing Partner Facing Subscription ID (PFSI) and ask them to provide replacement token to the customer.


7.5 Microsoft 365 customer wants to share Office subscription after closing their Microsoft account		 Set timeframe: for annual subscription: Within 4 months past the 60th day, to give the customer time to contact us.

Set timeframe: for monthly subscription: Within 1 month past the 60th day, to give the customer time to contact us.
  1. Microsoft cannot transfer subscriptions from one account to another.
  2. The advocate asks the customer for alternate verification to confirm the right customer identity, via billing address information.
Note: CP data retention is 5 years. Customers can proactively delete their Microsoft account while an active annual Microsoft 365 subscription is associated to it, or if age out occurs. (Age out = 5 years or 1825 days of inactivity.)

8. MSN FAQ

Can MSN customers get refunds after closing their Microsoft account?

Standard MSN refund policies apply:

If this is an MSN Dial-up or MSN IA customer, there is no refund available.

If this is an MSN Premium customer, a prorated refund will be made within 60 days of the original purchase.

If the customer decides to keep the service, and the account has been closed, the MSN customer will not be able to recreate the same Microsoft account.
