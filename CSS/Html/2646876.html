ASMS – Verify Customer Eligibility and Identity

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

Security is a top concern for organizations today and Microsoft takes data protection very seriously. Many security breaches take place by impersonating a legitimate user, so it is vital that ASMS verifies that every case submitter is eligible to access account information. Do not confirm or provide any account details before verification has been passed successfully.

For the protection of our customers and their data, the following verification steps are necessary before releasing any account specific information or processing any operations that fall within the responsibilities of the Account Owner.

Note: Most of these operations can be performed manually by the Account Owner at Azure Account Portal profile.

These operations include (but are not limited to):

  • Re-enabling or cancelling a subscription
  • Transfer of ownership or data migrations
  • Tax status
  • Profile information updates
  • Downloading invoices or usage reports
  • Updating credit cards

Verification is not required for presales information about Azure products and services, general questions which do not require access or details about a specific Azure account/subscription, or SMS sign-up issues.

Policy

  • When verifying the identity of a customer, first refer to CMAT, CST, or Jarvis.
    • More information about CST actions can be found in TSG 4487934.
    • More information about Jarvis actions can be found in TSG 2782736.
  • The account administrator, service administrator, or co-administrator on the subscription can submit a support request. Additionally any users with ‘write permissions’ (Owner/Contributor) to a subscription or with the built-in role "Support Request Contributor" can submit and manage a support request related to that given subscription.
  • In order to authorize another person to submit support requests on behalf of your Azure subscription, you have to be a subscription administrator or owner on the subscription. Assign the Support Request Contributor role, at the subscription scope, to all users who want to create and manage support requests for that given subscription. Learn more about role assignments in the portal on this public-facing page.
    • For more information about creating support tickets or authorizing others to do so, see the Azure Support FAQ.
  • When a Service Request (SR) arrives where a customer requests account information be changed or disclosed, the support engineer (SE) must validate the customer’s identity before the request can be honored.
    • Exceptions: Some accounts are associated with a company where the original Account Owner may no longer be with the company, and a colleague is now requesting access or to become the account administrator (AA). In these situations, follow the Forced Subscription Ownership Transfers guidelines in TSG 3040383 (How to Perform a Subscription Ownership Transfer).
  • If the person contacting you is a Microsoft representative calling on behalf of a customer, then ask the representative to verify eligibility on the customer’s behalf.
  • Once you have verified eligibility, add a note in Service Desk Case confirming their eligibility has been verified.

  • EA customers must have their eligibility checked each time they contact support. To do so, follow the steps below:

  1. Navigate to the EA Admin Portal.

  2. Under Manage Customer in the left navigation pane, click the List Enrollments link.

  3. In the List Enrollments tab, search for the customer by either Enrollment Number or Company Name.

Note: You can search the Subscription ID here to obtain the EA enrollment number. Standard EA Enrollment numbers are eight-digit numbers with no letters. China EA Azure enrollments always begin with the capital letter V, for example: V123456789012.

  1. Select the correct customer account, then assist them accordingly.

Note: Indirect customers will not be able to obtain pricing information for their enrollment from Support. They should contact their Partner for assistance with pricing information. The Partner name is displayed below the company name.

Support Scenarios

Use the scenarios below to determine the best process for validating a customer:

Case was submitted via Azure Portal, and submitter email or PUID matches the Account Owner

1. Verify that the case was submitted via the Azure Portal. Check the Customer Statement section of the case for the auto-populated information. If this information is not populated, the case did not come through the Azure Portal, and additional verification is needed (See scenarios below on how to verify).

[Asset 4522900]

2. Use the PUID in Service Desk to verify the Account Owner in CMAT/CST.

Note: The email ID in the Customer Profile/Details section of CMAT/CST should not be used as a means of account verification.

Account Owner approves request via email using MSA or ORG ID listed on account

Email the Account Owner to obtain approval in a separate thread or have the case submitter notify the Account Owner to email you.

  • Do not cc the Account Owner into the same thread with the case submitter or share the Accounts Owner’s information.

Use the email template below to obtain approval:

Hello,

My name is <engineer’s name> and I am an engineer from Azure Subscription Management Support. In SR # <inset SR#>, <name of case submitter> is requesting <define request>.

As you are the owner of the Azure Account, could you please respond to this email either approving or denying this request?

If you have any questions or concerns, please let me know.

Thank you,

<Insert Signature>

Customer who is both an Account Owner and a Billing Reader is unable to download an invoice after their subscription is disabled

In this case, SEs can share the invoice with the customer since the customer should have access to the invoice, and would if not for the aforementioned known issue. 

Customer confirms the following profile information (via phone or email)

View the customer's profile by navigating to the Account section in CMAT and validate the following information:

  • Account Owner, Microsoft Account, or Organizational Account
  • First and last name of the Business Contact
  • The telephone number on the account, including the area code
  • The Subscription ID(s) and products that are related to the request
  • Company name
  • Address ZIP Code or Postal Code

Note: If you are using CST, navigate to the Billing Account Details section to validate this information.

Note: To protect against social engineering, neither confirm nor deny which piece(s) of information the customer has gotten right or wrong. For example, if completing the verification over the phone, have the customer answer all six requirements before confirming or denying if verification is complete.

Case submitter is not Account Owner and is unable to provide verification using previous methods

  1. Collect as much detail about the account from the case submitter as possible, including but not limited to:
  • Subscription IDs
  • Credit card information
  • SR number
  • Details including their identity
  • Their position with the company
  • Their authority to make the change on the company's behalf
  • How to reach them with further questions
  • A business justification as to why the transfer is necessary
  1. Explain to the customer that Microsoft will verify the information that has been submitted.

Escalate to your Technical Advisor (TA), Partner Technical Advisor (PTA), or Team Lead (TL) for further review.

Customer requests a transfer of ownership or data migration without the permission of the Account Owner

See TSG 3040383 (How to Perform a Subscription Ownership Transfer) for more information on the Forced Transfer Process.

An internal Microsoft employee requests customer contact information

ASMS doesn't support these inquiries. There are multiple reasons this information cannot be shared, mainly due to customer privacy. Direct the requester to engage their financial management chains.

The customer requests to perform actions they do not have permissions to perform themselves
