Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges

[Asset 4049349]

Who should follow this article? 

  • Accounts & Billing 
  • Sales
  • Xbox

What is this article about?

  • This article provides information on how to determine and respond to customer claims of fraudulent or unauthorized charges.

When should this procedure be followed?

  • Whenever a customer says they have unauthorized charges, such as:
    • "I see some charges I don’t recognize."
    • "I am not a Microsoft Store (Xbox) customer, but I see several charges from Microsoft (Xbox Live) on my bank statement."
    • "I gave my child my credit card to sign up for a free trial subscription, and now I am seeing charges from Xbox Live."
    • "My child has an Xbox Live account, but I see some charges on the account that he says he did not make."

1. Overview

If a customer believes their Microsoft account has been compromised, resulting in fraudulent charges, follow the procedures in KB 930018 Policy: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account.

Otherwise, first verify the customer account. See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

Microsoft does not directly refund charges that occurred on a stolen or lost credit card or on Microsoft accounts not created by the customer.

If the customer requests assistance to prevent future unauthorized access or charges to the account or did not remove their Microsoft account from a sold device, assist the customer with changing their password to secure the account at https://account.microsoft.com/resetpassword.aspx.

If the customer has unauthorized charges because they claim their device was stolen, see KB 817558 Policy: Microsoft device theft and how to handle a stolen device claim.

Customers should dispute charges made from a sold/stolen device with their financial institution.

2. Procedure

2.1 Fraudulent accounts (unauthorized charges – not a Microsoft or Xbox Live customer)

For Mobile Operator Billing refer to KB 3100721 Informational: Mobile Operator Billing (Windows 10 and Xbox One).

For all the other unauthorized charges advise the customer to report the issue to their financial institution and, if the card is missing, to report the card as stolen. The unauthorized use might not be limited to charges on this account, and the customer must follow their credit card issuers’ dispute and chargeback procedure for fraudulent transactions.

Do not refund the customer for any charges on fraudulent accounts. The customer’s financial institution provider will handle any refunds through their dispute and chargeback procedures.

2.2 Unauthorized charges – account compromised

If the customer believes someone has accessed his or her Microsoft or Xbox Live account without authorization, follow the troubleshooting and refund guidance in KB 930018 Policy: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account.

Back to top 

2.3 Unauthorized charges – customer controlled

Parents who add their payment instrument to their child’s Xbox Live account may describe charges that they do not recognize as “unauthorized.” Charges are considered authorized when a customer has knowingly added their payment instrument to a Microsoft or Xbox Live account (whether their own account, a child’s account, friend’s account, etc.) that has not been compromised.

  • You may refund charges as allowed in KB 4338531 Policy: Return/Refund: Unauthorized purchase by a family member/known person
  • If you provide a refund, you must educate the customer on how to take steps to protect the account to prevent future unwanted charges:
    • Microsoft account: Your family
    • Xbox 360: Parental safeguards for Xbox Live and your Xbox 360 console
    • Xbox One: Prevent unauthorized purchases by children on Xbox One
  • Offer information on:
    • Updating billing contact information, so they will always receive notices about purchases
    • Managing services and subscriptions,
    • Checking recent transactions, and
    • Keeping billing information up to date.

Use KB 3083551 Policy: Microsoft Store: Return and refund policy, section 4, “How to respond to a customer who is not eligible for a refund”, for positioning with customers dissatisfied with the refund outcome.
