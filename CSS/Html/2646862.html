Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to verify the status and/or eligibility of tax-exempt or discounted tax customers. Tax exemptions are available to charitable and non-profit organizations worldwide who meet the tax exemption requirements. Discounted tax is available to customer related to Value Added Tax (VAT) in the European Union (EU).

Applies To: All Azure customers who meet the VAT, tax exempt or other tax requirements outlined in this article.

Policy

  • Customers worldwide are subject to tax laws and Microsoft must comply with these laws when customers make purchases.

  • Tax laws can differ by Country, State, County, Province, and Municipality. However, a majority of the tax issues are addressed automatically within the billing system.

  • The European Union(EU) uses VAT ID for taxation purposes. EU customers should enter their VAT ID when they purchase an Azure Subscription.

  • Customers who do not have their VAT ID available or who have questions about VAT IDs should engage their local tax authority or tax adviser. ASMS cannot provide VAT IDs or tax advice.

  • EU (except Ireland) customers who have eligible VAT IDs and other customers who are Tax Exempt will trigger a zero-tax status in the billing system and a corresponding “Reverse Charge” statement on their invoice.

  • Marketplace add-ons are not eligible for tax exemption and VAT IDs should not be added.

  • Chile now requires foreign service providers to charge a 19% value-added tax (VAT). This VAT applies to all digital sales from Microsoft to customers in Chile, including both business-to-business and business-to-consumer sales.

    • Additional information is available in the Chile VAT Launch FAQ.

    • See below for details on the Chile Tax Exemption Process.

Customer Requests a VAT ID Update or Receives VAT Error at Purchase

Customers should add a VAT ID to their account during the initial purchase and account setup. Should a customer later realize they need to add, update, or experienced an error with their VAT ID, they must contact support. In these instances follow the below steps to assist the customer.

  1. Validate the customer’s VAT ID using the VIES VAT number validation site.
    Note: This VAT ID should include both the country code and the number itself.

  2. Was the VAT ID valid?

    • No: Redirect the customer to their local tax agency or internal billing department for further assistance.

    • Yes: Update the VAT ID in CMAT for the customer.
      (Directions how to do so below.)

  3. In CMAT, navigate to the Tax Information section and click Add.

  2. Enter the new Vat ID.

  3. Add a comment describing the change you are making.

  4. Click the Save button.
    Note: If you receive an error in CMAT while or after adding a VAT ID, escalate the issue to PayOps using TSG 2651579.

Tax Exemption Status Requests

Customers who claim to be tax exempt but their VAT ID or tax-exempt status is not in CMAT, can submit their Tax Exemption Certificate to Microsoft and have their tax status updated. Typically, only charitable organizations are tax exempt, but some non-profit organizations may also be eligible.

Cloud Soulution Provider (CSP) Tax Exemption Process:
Partners wanting to add withholding tax certificates can download the form at https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/withholding-tax-credit-form and follow the noted process in the doc. 

Colombia Tax Exemption Process:

If a Colombian customer asks to have their Tax ID added to the profile, follow the steps below:

  1. Ask the customer to provide a Registro Único Tributario (RUT) certificate.
  2. Ensure that the customer's Tax Exemption form is attached to the case and that the customer filled it out completely.
  3. Locate the Número de Identificación Tributaria (NIT) number on the Tax Exemption form.
  4. Validate the NIT using the RUT Status Check page.
  5. In CMAT, click Add next to Tax Information.
  6. Under Type, select Vat ID.
  7. Enter the NIT number under ID.
  8. Add Comments (as needed), and then click Next.
  9. Add a case number and finish the process.

Chile Tax Exemption Process:

If a Chilean customer asks to have their Tax ID added to the profile, follow the steps below:

  1. Ask the customer to provide a Registro Único Tributario (RUT) Number.

  2. Navigate to https://palena.sii.cl/cvc/dte/ee_empresas_dte.html and enter the RUT and the Checker Digit.

  3. If the RUT is valid, proceed to CST and locate the customer's account.

  4. Navigate to Billing Accounts (LE).

  5. UnderTAX Details select Add Tax Details.

  6. Select INTLEXEMPT (it is the only available option).

  7. Add the RUT and the Checker Digit.

  8. Add a case number and comment.

  9. Click next to complete the process.

Updating GST ID for New Zealand Customers

New Zealand customers can update their GST ID numbers to avoid GST charges. When a New Zealand customer requests to update their GST ID number, first confirm the customer's current GST ID number. This GST ID number must be updated in the system to ensure that the customer is invoiced with NIL GST going forward, and also to enable credit and rebill if there has been an invoice issued with GST. The steps to update a New Zealand GST ID number are the same as for any other country.

Updating GSTIN ID for India Region Customers

Microsoft charges Goods and Services Tax (GST) on digital supplies to customers in India. To make sure that this GST is correctly charged, customers with an India account will need to add GSTIN information to their Azure account so that the correct TAX amount can be collected.

To update a GSTIN ID follow the steps below:

  1. In CMAT, navigate to the Tax Information section and click Add.

  2. In the Add Tax Information pop-up, select the correct country or region, and select GSTNID.

4034347

  1. Enter the new GSTN ID in the correct GSTIN format below (CMAT will not accept incorrectly formatted GSTIN IDs).

    • Microsoft does not validate the GSTIN against the specific customer or entity. Customers can add any valid GSTIN to their Azure account.

    • HSN Code (Harmonized Commodity Description coding) for GST is 9973.

  1. Add a comment, and then click the Save button.

  2. If you receive an error in CMAT, escalate the issue to PayOps using TSG 2651579.

India Region Customer Requests TDS Tax Credit

India customers are entitled to claim up to 10% Tax Deducted at Source (TDS). To do so, they must submit the following TDS documents to the APOC office:

  • Tax Deducted at Source Credit Form
  • Digitally signed TDS Certificate (provided by the customer).

SE's should help the customer submit their digitally signed TDS documents by engaging PayOps Execution following the guidelines in the link: Submitting a Service Request (select PayOps Execution option) or TSG 2647567 for further validation and application. This process is applicable for customers paying via Invoice pay or Credit card.

Note: There is no self-service option for the customer to submit TDS certificates directly to PayOps.

Processing TDS Tax Credits

Note: There is no self-service option for the customer to submit TDS certificates directly to PayOps.

Credit card customers:

  1. The customer’s card is charged 100% of the invoice amount automatically.
  2. The customer submits a completed Tax Deducted at Source Credit Form and digitally-signed TDS Certificate.
  3. ASMS executes PayOps and attaches the customer's completed form and digitally-signed TDS Certificate.
  4. PayOps validates and processes 10% of the TDS credit as a refund back to customer’s credit card.

Check/wire customers:

  1. The customer will pay 90% of their invoice amount.
  2. The customer submits a completed Tax Deducted at Source Credit Form and digitally-signed TDS Certificate.
  3. ASMS executes PayOps and attaches the customer's completed form and digitally-signed TDS Certificate.
  4. PayOps validates and create a VSO ticket for the GFS team.
  5. The GFS team clears 10% of the unpaid invoice amount in SAP as a payment from customer.

The customer will see this 10% amount included, as follows:

  • Legacy: In the Payment section of their next invoice.
  • Legacy and Modern: On the customer portal in the Amount Paid section.
  • SAP: If the customer has already paid 100% of their invoice amount, but they submit the TDS certificate later, GFS posts this 10% TDS as an over-payment in SAP. It then uses this amount to offset the customer’s future invoices, so they pay less for future invoices.

Note: Currently, over-payment amounts cannot currently be found on the Ibiza portal.

India Region Tax Reclamation: Miscellaneous Credits

Indian law prohibits tax credits to be returned to vendors (merchants) once the government has received them.

There is a Withholding Tax (WHT) process where customers pay part of their tax directly to the government and part of it to the vendor. This results in several important items:

  • The tax credits option has been removed in CMAT for Indian customers.

  • Indian customers are legally allowed to reclaim taxes they have paid.

    • PayOps issues these as Miscellaneous Credits at a loss since it has already paid the Indian government taxes and cannot legally reclaim them.

    • Use these templates when working with Payops:

      • ASMS WHT Request Template

      • WebDirect Credit Request

Brazilian Taxes

Customers in Brazil can pay their taxes directly to the Brazilian tax authority, and then short-pay their Microsoft invoices. The customer must provide a copy of their tax payment receipt to Microsoft to clear their balance.

Engage GFS, following the process outlined in TSG 2647567. Request a customer account adjustment and attach the customer’s receipts.

Validating Tax Exemption Forms

Only for USA Customers:

ASMS is empowered to review Tax Exemption forms from customers. If you receive Tax Exemption forms from a customer, follow the steps below before creating a technical advisor or partner technical advisor (TA or PTA) Task.

SE Actions:

  1. Ensure that the Tax Exemption form is attached to the
    Service Desk
    case and the customer filled it out completely.

  2. The following information must be included on the exemption forms:
    • Seller (MS) Name/Address
    • Customer (Tax Payer) Full legal Name
    • Signature and/or effective date of exemption
    • State(s) the certificate is applicable to
    • Customer (Tax payer) Number
    • Reason for exemption
    • Type of Property/Service being purchased
    • If certificate is applicable for all current and future transactions or just the current transaction
    • Signature
    • Title
    • Printed name of the signature
  3. Double-check the customer forms to ensure it has been properly filled out.
  4. Use the Internal ASMS Escalations template found in TSG 2929022 to engage your TA.

Note:

  • The customer’s address on the certificate does not need to match the address in our system. However, if the customer’s ship-to address does not match the State listed on the certificate or the ship-to address is later updated to a different state, we may need to request a new certificate as certificates are State specific.
  • Five US states don’t have a sales tax, so if you get customer requests where the ship-to address is in one of the states below, you don't need to ask for a Tax form or Tax ID:
    • Alaska (Note: some cities in AK have a local sales tax, so you may still need to obtain exemption certificates at the local level)
    • Delaware
    • Montana
    • New Hampshire
    • Oregon
  • Fields in the validation checklist are for reference purposes only. Since certificates vary by State and by type of exemption, they require different information. The general rule is to ensure that all fields on the certificates are filled out and the customer’s legal name matches the customer name in our system.
  • You may receive forms from USA customers with no place for a Tax ID number on the form, and when checking the customer informs you they do not have one in their state of residence. It is ok to pass these forms on for further validation. If the form has a place for a Tax ID but that field is not filled out with a Tax ID, then we should get the Tax ID from the customer before proceeding.
  • Different states can have different formats for Tax Exempt certificates issued by their tax authority. Here is list of exemption forms by State. Not all forms are listed, as some states (e.g. FL) only accept State-issued forms and the customer will need to get a form through official state channels.
  1. Engage via TA Task to have the TA review the Tax Exemption form.
  2. Once the TA has successfully updated the account, they will update the task, instructing you to proceed with refunding tax charges using the normal process.
  3. Follow the normal refund process outlined in TSG 2646823 and TSG 3084129.

Note: You can refund the customer as far back as they can provide documentation for exemption or up to one year prior, whichever comes first.

If a customer requests anything beyond one year prior, advise them to contact their local tax authorities, as we would have handed the tax over to them.

TA/PTA Actions

  • The TA or PTA will review the Tax Exemption form to confirm that the customer has filled it out completely and correctly using the guidelines in TSG 4012644.
    • For Legacy customers, they will then update the tax exemption status in CMAT.
    • For Modern customers, they will then update the tax exemption status in CST using TSG 4487926.
