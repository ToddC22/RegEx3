Procedure: Devices: How to verify the warranty status of an accessory sold separately

Who should use this article?

What is this article about?

This article explains how to verify the warranty status of the customer’s accessory sold separately. This article should be followed when troubleshooting does not resolve the issue and a replacement is necessary.

When troubleshooting determines an accessory needs to be replaced, you must verify the warranty status of the customer’s accessory. Accessories in scope are listed below. Keep in mind there are no changes to support boundaries.

  • Microsoft branded PC accessories such as keyboard/mouse
  • Surface branded accessories such as Surface Keyboard or Surface Pen purchased separately
  • Xbox branded accessories such as controller

Since you do not have a way to verify warranty status using advocate tools, you are expected to collect a receipt or other proof of purchase from the customer. If the customer cannot provide this, the article outlines how you can use the PID printed on the accessory to identify when the accessory was manufactured to determine the warranty status. The purchase date on the receipt or proof of purchase is more accurate. But in absence of this, validating the PID provides an alternative to just turning the customer away.

Surface advocates: This article mentions accessories you do not support. You will continue to only support accessories that are in scope of your support boundaries.

Follow the guidance below to verify the warranty status of the customer’s accessory.

2. Procedures

  1. Set expectations with the customer that you will now need to verify the warranty status of the customer’s accessory needing replacement. In some instances, replacement support options may not be available.
  2. Verify where the customer purchased the accessory and document this in the case notes.
  3. Ask the customer if the accessory was purchased online through Microsoft Store.
  • If the customer purchased from Microsoft Store Online, check MST for the transaction/purchase date. If the purchase date indicates the device is In Warranty (IW), follow the replacement procedures specific to the Line of Business (LOB) you support. If the purchase date indicates the device is Out of Warranty (OOW), handle as an OOW scenario.
  • If the customer purchased from Microsoft Store B&M or a third-party retailer such as Best Buy, go to step 4.
  • If the customer is a Commercial customer see KB 4514977 Procedure: Surface: Services Exchange and Repair Offers Overview section 1. Standard Exchange.
  1. Following the process specific to your LOB, request a receipt or proof of purchase from the customer. Keep in mind if the customer cannot provide the receipt while on the call, they may have to call back when the receipt is submitted.
    • If the customer has a receipt, and the purchase date indicates the device is IW, follow the replacement procedures specific to the Line of Business (LOB) you support.
    • If the customer has a receipt, and the purchase date indicates the device is OOW, handle as an OOW scenario.
    • If the customer does not have a receipt or proof of purchase, go to step 5.
  2. Decode the customer’s PID. PIDs follow different formats (12 digits, 13 digits, 13 alphanumeric, 20 digits) depending on accessory types. Use guidance in the “Additional information” section of this article for more information on how to decode the PID to determine warranty status.
    • If the customer’s PID is in warranty, assist the customer as necessary.
    • If the customer’s PID is OOW, handle as OOW.

3. Where to locate the PID on an accessory

PID location is not uniform. Most commonly the PID is in the battery compartment, or along the inside of the headband for headsets. For complete details on locating the PID, see KB 3196641 Procedure: Surface: Customers with Surface and some Microsoft-branded accessories but no registered Surface device.

4. How to decode the PID

4.1 20 digits:

Formatting (all numeric): xxxxx-xxx-xxxxxxx-xYYWW

The last four digits (BOLD) indicate the Year (YY) and Week (WW) manufactured.

  • The Year (YY) is always the last two digits of the year.

    For example:

    10 = 2010
    15 = 2015
    12 = 2012
  • The Week (WW) is the numbered week of the calendar year.

    For example:

    52 = the last week in December.
    01 = the first week in January.

Examples:

1634 = Year is 2016, the week is in August.

1549 = Year is 2015, the week is in early December

Resources You can use this link for help determining the month the week falls into:

https://www.timeanddate.com/date/weeknumber.html

4.2 12 digits / 13 digits:

Note: 12 digits used on Surface branded HDMI, VGA, and Ethernet adapters only.

Formatting (all numeric) – 12 digits: xxxxxxxYWWxx

Formatting (all numeric) – 13 digits: xxxxxxxYWWxxx

  • The 8th, 9th, and 10th digits (BOLD) indicate the Year (Y) and Week (WW) manufactured.
    • Digit 8 = Y
    • Digits 9 and 10 = WW
  • The Year (Y) digit ranges from 0 – 9 to represent a 10-year cycle, starting from 2010.

    For example:

    9 = 2019
    3 = 2013
    2 = 2012
    1 = 2011
    0 = 2010
  • The Week (W) is the numbered week of the calendar year.

    For example:

    52 = The last week in December
    01 = The first week of January

Examples:

634 = Year is 2016, the week is in August.

549 = Year is 2015, the week is in early December.

Resources: You can use this link for help determining the month the week falls into:

https://www.timeanddate.com/date/weeknumber.html

Return to top

4.3 13 digits alphanumeric:

Note: Used only on Surface power supply units (PSUs).

Formatting (alphanumeric): xxxxxxxxxxDYM

  • The 11th, 12th, and 13th, digits (highlighted) indicate the Day (D), Year (Y) and Month (M) manufactured.
    • Digit 11 = D
    • Digit 12 = Y
    • Digit 13 = M
  • The Day (D) digit ranges from 1 – 9 = 1st – 9th of the month, and A – Y is 10th – 31st.
  • The Year (Y) digit ranges from 0 – 9 where 9 is year 2009 and 0 is year 2010.

7. Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Scenario Action
What should I do if the customer cannot provide a receipt/proof of purchase AND PID decoding determines the customer is out of warranty BUT the customer disputes this?

While it is probable that the customer purchased the accessory six (6) months after it was actually manufactured, without a receipt/proof of purchase, the best we can do is determine state of warranty based on when the accessory was manufactured.

If the customer cannot provide a receipt/proof of purchase to indicate an alternative start date of the warranty, the start date of the warranty based on manufacturing date is final.
Where can I find warranty information on accessories that is customer facing?
  • Surface branded: https://www.microsoft.com/accessories/warranties
  • Microsoft branded: https://www.microsoft.com/accessories/warranties
  • Xbox branded: https://support.xbox.com/my-account/warranty-and-service/xbox-warranty-informatio
