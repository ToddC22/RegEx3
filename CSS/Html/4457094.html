Procedure: Unresponsive Customer

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

  • Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor | Team Manager

What is this article about?

While customers or partners may become unresponsive, support cases should not be left open indefinitely. To ensure a consistent experience of Microsoft Support, this procedure should be used to re-engage with a customer or drive the case to closure.

Note: there is no distinction between Cloud and On-premises Business in this procedure.

Steps

Please make yourself aware of the deviations which should be considered before applying this procedure.

Requirements

  • Every attempt to contact the customer must be documented in the case notes, along with the next intended contact date and time.
  • If a customer or partner becomes unresponsive for a Sev 1, Sev A or Unified Support Critical case, lower the severity by following 4456539 Procedure: Setting Severity before continuing with this procedure.

Definitions

  • Business days refers to the customer's or partner's working hours. Be aware of regional working days, hours, national holidays, and the time zone differences.
  • We define "Attempted contact" as:
    You tried to contact the customer or partner by using both email and telephone. The exception is when the customer or partner specifically asks to use email only or the service level or the case requires Web Response only.

Three-strike system

The three-strike system should commence when the customer has become unresponsive to contact attempts from CSS support engineers.

[Asset 4495381]

The strike system requires a minimum of 3 contact attempts before closing the case.

The customer must be permitted at least one business day to respond before initiating the first strike or progressing to the next strike.

Sample Striking flow:

  • Wednesday：Engineers take ownership of the case，first contact attempted - No success.
  • Friday: Contact attempted – No success – Send Strike One.
  • Monday: Contact attempted – No success – Send Strike 2 (include IM if Customer is Premier).
  • Tuesday: Contact attempted – No success – Send Strike 3 (proceed to close case at the end of the day).

Note: If customer responds at any instance, Strike process will be reverted.

Note: Each strike has different steps.

Strike 1

  1. Attempt to contact the customer by telephone.  If you’re unable to reach the customer leave a voicemail where possible.
  2. Send email message (using the General - Unresponsive Customer Strike template found on 4459286 Case Management Standard (CMS) Templates).
  3. Set the SR Case Status:
  • Rave: Waiting on Customer
  • Service Desk Case: Waiting for Information
  1. Document Strike Count as Strike 1

Strike 2

  1. Attempt to contact the customer by telephone.  If you’re unable to reach the customer leave a voicemail where possible.
  2. Send email message (using the General - Unresponsive Customer Strike template found on 4459286 Case Management Standard (CMS) Templates).
  3. For Premier customers, include Incident Manager on strike email to customer.
  4. Ensure the SR Case Status is set as below:
    • Rave: Waiting on Customer
    • Service Desk Case: Waiting for Information
  5. Document Strike Count as Strike 2.
  6. For Premier customers and partners, email the Incident Manager directly, explaining the current delays, any reason you're aware of for the delays, the timelines for us closing the case and clearly stating your request for the IM to engage with the customer to help drive case progression (using the General - Unresponsive Customer Involve IM (if known) template found on 4459286 Case Management Standard (CMS) Templates).

Strike 3

  1. Attempt to contact the customer by telephone.  If you’re unable to reach the customer, leave a voicemail where possible.
  2. Email message sent (using the Closure - Unresponsive Customer Strike template found on 4459286 Case Management Standard (CMS) Templates), closing the case.
  3. Set the SR Case Status:
    • Rave: Waiting on Customer
    • Service Desk Case: Waiting for Information
  4. Document Strike Count as Strike 3
  5. Close case using the following, even if a solution was offered:
    • Rave Resolution: Unresolved
    • Service Desk Case: Un-Resolved

If the customer or partner responds at any point in the workflow

  • Set a next contact date/time with them following the communication procedure in 4346059 Procedure: Communication.
  • Set the SR Case Status - Waiting on Customer (Rave), or Status (Service Desk Case) to reflect new status.
    • Important Exception: If the customer is unresponsive after a solution was offered, then comes back requesting more time, leave the wait state alone. It should stay at Pending Customer Verification (Rave), or Waiting for Confirmation (Service Desk Case) until the customer confirms the solution worked or didn't work.
  • Set the Strike Count to N/A. Although there is no strike count filed in Rave/Service Desk Case, the strike process should still be followed.

If they become unresponsive again in future the strike count will restart at 1.

Deviations or workarounds

  • If at any time you receive an Out of Office (OOF) or the customer advises they will be OOF follow the steps in 4507205 Procedure: Customer out of office (OOF).
  • Service Requests requiring recovery should not use this procedure without approval from a Manager, Partner Technical Lead (PTL), Technical Advisor (TA), Virtual Team Lead (VTL), Pod Lead as appropriate for your team.
