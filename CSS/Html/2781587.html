2. Procedure 

a. Escalations to PayOps

i. Creating the request for PayOps
Any process indicating escalation to PayOps team should be updated to T3, then T3 can open VSO request to PayOps, Tier 3 can follow steps from the article 3136172 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to Escalate to PayOps – Using VSO.
ii. Account reconciliation requests from credit card customers
Follow steps from article 4014723 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to provide statements of account to credit card customers in CMAT    
iii. Do Not Contact (DNC) requests for credit card customers
It is not possible to prevent credit card customers from being sent dunning emails. Do not send DNC requests for credit card customers to GFS or PayOps. Suggested script for credit card customers who want to know why we can’t prevent dunning emails being sent to them: "Our billing system is not able to block dunning emails from being sent to customers paying by credit card."
iv. Tax forms
  • Customers may need to mail physical copies of tax forms to PayOps. Should a customer require direct contact, the following addresses may be provided:

v. Delivery Acceptance Form (DAF)
If a customer contacts Support requesting a hardcopy DAF, ask the customer to provide the postal address to which they want the DAF delivered and a contact phone number. Include this information in the request for PayOps.

For information about countries that require DAFs, see 2871491 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Helping customers in countries that require a hardcopy invoice, DAF or MOSA.
vi. Discounts, special pricing and Microsoft Online Services Agreements (MOSAs)
For the Nonprofit, Government, Education customers, or the questions about discounted pricing, special pricing, and pricing adjustment, please see the article 401﻿0530 Procedure: M365 Commerce: How to support customer’s discount in CMAT.

Missing MOSA amendment:
  1. Ask the customer to send the signed and dated MOSA amendment directly to the MOSP Amendments Team at mospa@microsoft.com or fax it to 425-708-7047. If the customer has attached the MOSA amendment to a case, tell the customer, “Thank you for sending us your amendment. However, we cannot accept it in a Service Request. For the security of your document, please email the signed and dated MOSA to our MOSP Amendments Team at mospa@microsoft.com or fax it to 425-708-7047.”
    Note: The customer will not receive a response acknowledging receipt of the MOSA amendment from alias mospa@microsoft.com.
  2. When the amendment is received, the MOSP Amendments Team will send a case to Support to notify the customer of the discount status.

i. Collections and dunning for customers who pay by invoice
For invoice pay customers who are claiming bankruptcy
i. The advocate should obtain the contact information of the person who contacted us (name, phone number, email address) plus any statements that the customer has made (“I can't pay,” “I'm going into bankruptcy,” etc.) and create an Assistance Request for GFS (AR support area: CAICollections).
ii. Do not change the status of any subscriptions. GFS will evaluate, contact the customer, and determine whether the subscription status should be changed.
ii. Requests from Support to review a disabled invoice account that has been re-enabled by Support
Create an Assistance Request and assign to AR support area: CAICAT. See 2772686 Procedure: M365 Commerce: Enabling expired or disabled subscriptions for scenarios, process, support area and the template to use.
iii. Cases assigned to Support from GFS
Occasionally, when GFS contacts customers regarding past-due accounts, the customer will mention billing disputes, credit requests, or refunds. GFS has no way to support these issues. In these cases, the following process will be used:
  1. GFS creates an Assistance Request (maintaining ownership of the case) and submits it to the appropriate regional Tier 2 queue.
  2. Tier 2 takes ownership and contacts the customer using standard contact procedures, including 3-strikes (for EMEA, 2 strikes). See 2816223 Procedure: M365: How to initiate outbound contact with customers.
  3. Tier 2 documents their Assistance Request and closes it upon resolution or non-contact.
  4. GFS closes their case upon resolution.
iv. Customers who question the validity of the welcome email from bposcoll@microsoft.com asking for the contact information of their Accounts Payable contact
Invoice customers are sent a welcome email with their first invoice requesting the Accounts Payable contact name and contact information in case the accounts receivable team needs to contact the customer. Bposcoll@microsoft.com is the email address used for this welcome email. If the customer is concerned about this email being a legitimate request for information from Microsoft, please tell the customer that these are normal operational emails sent by the accounts receivable team to get accounts payable contact information in case questions about payments received arise (i.e. which invoices to apply the payment to, etc.). The following is a sample of the email sent to the customer:

Dear Valued Customer,

We would like to take this opportunity to welcome your company to Microsoft Online Services, and briefly review the invoice payment and dispute process. If you are not the Accounts Payable Contact, please provide us with your Accounts Payable Contact details so we can communicate with them directly about processing payments.

Please provide us with the following information:

Accounts Payable Contact Name

It also gives them permission to contact our customer support center directly with any inquiries.

As a courtesy, we have attached your first invoice. Please review it carefully to ensure there are no billing discrepancies. If the invoice is discrepant, please log a service request into Office 365 Customer Portal or Azure Customer Portal and a customer support advocate will contact you shortly. To review or download future invoices, please visit the online customer portal.

You are kindly reminded that payment is due within 30 days from the invoice date. Please reference your invoice number on the payment remittance so that we can post the payment to your account promptly.
