Intune: Questions for initial contact with customer to clearly define the issue and goal (scope) for customer before troubleshooting

[Asset 4476087]
  • Content Idea (CI): 101035

Support Topic:

  • Azure\Microsoft Intune\General Guidance or Advisory

This KB provides you a codex of general questions that follow the mindset for open and closed ended questions, to help you clearly identify the customers issue and have a clean starting point for investigation\progression of the case. This is called setting the scope, once performed you can then move into utilizing support articles, knowledge base articles, teams SME channels and workflows to troubleshoot the customers issue.  Always take a moment to review the case, the data already in the case to assure you do not ask redundant questions, as that could make the customer feel their time and efforts are not respected.


Examples of initial questions you should ask during all first contact with a customer, to start defining a clean and clear undertanding of the customers issue:

1) Can you describe the issue you are having?
2) When did this issue that you are having start?
3) Where there any changes made prior to the issue occurring?
4) What platform iOS\Mac\Android\Windows\Blackberry is the issue affecting?
5) How many Users \ Devices (percentage) are affected by the issue?
6) What is the impact to your business as a result of the issue?

Examples of feature questions, to fully define a clean and clear understanding of the customers issue:

[App deployments]
1) Is this a Store application or Line of Business application?
2) Is this being deployed to devices that are enrolled\not-enrolled\both?
3) Does this application install correctly outside of Intune aka manually from Store or Side-load?
4) Is the assignment for the Application Required or Available?
5) Are there errors being presented?

[Device Actions]
1) Are you attempting to perform an device action?
2) What kind of device action are you attempting to perform?
3) Is the device action occurring on the target devices at all?

[Enrollment]
1) Are you using any enrollment restrictions?
2) Do you have MFA enabled?

[Intune SDK]
1) Was the Line of Business application wrapped with the latest Intune SDK?

[Mifo]
1) Have they attempted to set or configure the MDM Authority?
2) Are they using an account that is an Administrator?
3) Are they using an account that has a license assigned for Office MDM or Intune?
4) What browser were they using for the process when the issue occurred?

[Policies]
1) What kind of Intune policy is this Application Protection\Compliance\Custom Configuration\Device Configuration\Device Restriction\Software Updates?
2) Are you assigning the policy to a static or dynamic security group?
3) Are you targeting the policy to a UPN or Device?
4) Are the devices targeted by the policy enrolled\not-enrolled?
5) What is the name of the policy in question related to the issue?

[Android]
1) Is the device Android or Android for Work enrolled?
2) Is the device configured as Dedicated device or Full managed?
3) Is the device being encrypted?

[Android - Managed Google Play]
1) Are you getting any sync errors?
2) Are you using a custom Intune Admin Role?

[Android - Work Profile]
1) What is the name of the Android Work Profile? 

[Apple]
1) Is the device DEP\Apple Configurator\JAMF enrolled?
2)  Is the device being encrypted?

[Apple - DEP\Apple Configurator]
1) Is the device with\without user affinity?
2) If user affinity, is authentication being done during enrollment via Setup Assistant or Company Portal?
3) What is the name of the enrollment profile related to the issue?

[Apple - Jamf]
1) Are you using the latest Company Portal App?
2) Was the Application key recently expired or updated?

[Apple - VPP]
1) Is the VPP token new\existing\recently renewed?
2) Is this licensed per UPN or Device?
3) Do you have more than one VPP Token?
4) If yes, which VPP token is the app related to the issue associated with?
5) Are you getting any sync errors?

[Apple - Provisioning Profiles]
1) Is this provisioning profile  new\existing\recently renewed?
2) Has the existing provisioning profile expired or is about to expire?
3) Are there any integrity errors presented?
4) Are there any capability enforcement errors presented?

[Windows]
1) Was the device enrolled via Access work or school, Autopilot, Company Portal, GPO or ConfigMgr enablement?
2) Is the device Education, Enterprise or Professional?
3) What is the OS version on the device showing the issue?
4) Is the device being encrypted?

[Windows - Autopilot]
1) Is this for existing or newly imported devices?
2) Did they experience any errors when importing the CSV into Intune?
3) Did they experience any errors when assigning the Autopilot Deployment Profile to device group?
4) Are they assigning a user to a specific device?
5) Is this Hybrid Azure AD with Autopilot?
6) Are they using a proxy for Intune AD connector?

[Windows - Co-Management]
1) Is the device first enrolled into ConfigMgr (Path 1) and then Intune or s the device first enrolled to Intune and then ConfigMgr (Path 2)?
2) Is the ConfigMgr environment a CAS with Primaries or a Stand Alone Primary Site?
3) What is the version of the ConfigMgr environment?

Note:
If you find there are questions that you feel should be added to this list, please contact me with those questions. Please keep in mind this is not a troubleshooting workflow but a codex for general open and closed ended questions to help truly clarify the customers issue and be a jump point (scope) for the investigation.
