General information

Premier and Unified Support

Premier and Unified Support provide a complete managed support offering for enterprise customers, including on-premises support. Technical experts with deep knowledge are available to deliver the highest level of Premier support services, 24x7 via phone and email. Premier customers are also assigned a IM to manage this support offering to the customer or an ADM who is development focused.

Volume License Services Support

 

VL Support is non-technical, non-critical, non-paid support and works on FIFO model during business hours. They do not have the concept of severity and VLSC does not fall under Premier support. These teams are using Service Desk.

VL Support Boundaries including escalation aliases – 4524182: Procedure: Redirects to Volume Licensing

Transfer process to VL – 4488085: Transfer to VLS/DEV/LEX

VL Tier 2 Escalation process to Enterprise Commerce (EC) previous name ECIT – 4536367: Procedure: VLS: Tier 2 Escalations to Enterprise Commerce (EC)

Note the customer also can open cases via VL support to address OLS provisioning issues. In most cases both Office 365 and VL Support teams can handle the issue – but they have different SLAs, support tools and follow different escalation processes.

Out of business hours for VL OLS Provisioning issues that cannot wait for standard support times please leverage Office 365 support see below

Procedure

Billing related questions for Office 365, Dynamics, Intune and Azure AD subscription

For Office 365, Dynamics, Intune and Azure AD subscription Billing issues, please advise the customer to open a support case by clicking “Need Help” from the Office 365 Portal, properly describe “Billing” issue then following the same procedure that they do for technical cases: https://aka.ms/admincenter.

Please note that creating a support case on behalf of the customer is not recommended, as it results in a longer resolution times and a worse customer experience overall. Cloud Service Engineering teams are investing continuous additions for self-help, automation and diagnostics. When we move the customer away from the cloud portals, they cannot benefit from this investment and they get a worse customer experience.

Escalation for Billing issues should be handled in the same path as other case escalations. See http://aka.ms/ReactiveEscalation.

 

Office 365 Assistance afterhours CritSit Support

For O365 Assistance outside of regional business hours, follow the instruction from Commerce After Hours Weekend Support section in article 2500396:

Relates to

This section should be used for handling the following scenario only (refer to the guidelines in the Commerce Support (Mon-Fri) section for all other Commerce issues).

After hours subscription enable

  • Paid customer is disabled due to non-payment AND Commerce is currently closed. Fraud team clearance is required if Fraud check is in place.
  • Note:
    • This procedure does not apply to subscriptions that are “In Grace.”
    • This procedure does not apply to Trial Subscriptions.
    • This procedure does not apply to Prepaid Subscriptions.
    • Customers calling in with a disabled trial subscription can either contact Commerce during Standard Hours of Operation for their region or purchase paid licenses using the Office 365 Portal.
    • Customers calling in with a Prepaid subscription can either replace their prepaid product key through their original purchasing channel or purchase a new compatible offer through the portal.
  • For regional Commerce contact information, see section Commerce Support (Mon-Fri) in this article.

HOOPS

Instructions

Procedure:

Note: The team that handles these requests will not speak directly with the customer. The original support engineer will continue to own customer interaction.

Confirming subscription

  1. Confirm the subscription is PAID.
  2. Confirm the subscription is NOT PREPAID.
  3. Confirm the subscription is in a Disabled state. Note: If the subscription is “In-Grace”, reassure the customer that services remain functional during the grace period. Tell the customer they can contact Commerce during normal business hours.

If you do not receive an answer within an appropriate amount of time, run the following steps:

Steps:

  1. Send an email to CommAH@microsoft.com using the following format and info:
    • Subject: Commerce After Hours Paid Subscription Enable Request
    • case Number:
    • Tenant Domain:
    • Subscription Type: (Office 365, CRM Online, and so on.)
    • Seat Count:
    • Subscription Status: (Disabled, In-Grace, and so on.

      Note: Any requests without the above being sent before calling will be rejected
  2. After the email has been sent, an ACK will be sent within 10 minutes from an engineer to ACK the email.
  3. If no ACK is received, call the Commerce on-call EE to enable the customer’s subscription: +1 (425) 421-2126

    **Do not transfer the customer to this number.
  4. If you receive the on-call EE’s Voicemail, please leave a message and try the number again within 15 minutes if you do not receive a reply to your email message.
  5. Provide the resolution to the customer, advise that in order to avoid further interruption the customer should contact Commerce during normal business hours and close the case.

Billing related questions for Azure subscription

For Azure Billing issues, please advise the customer to open a support case from the Azure Portal with Issue Type “Billing”, following the same procedure that they do for technical cases: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/azure-supportability/how-to-create-azure-support-request. 

Please note that creating an Azure support case on behalf of the customer is not recommended, as it results in a longer resolution times and a worse customer experience overall. Azure Engineering is making a big investment in the Azure Portal support blade, with continuous additions for self-help, automation and diagnostics. When we move the customer away from the Azure Portal, they cannot benefit from this investment and they get a worse customer experience.

Escalation for Billing issues should be handled in the same path as other case escalations. See http://aka.ms/ReactiveEscalation (2nd page).
