Sponsorship Renewals 

The type of Sponsorship a customer has will determine the renewal process. ASMS CM is working to define the processes for each Sponsorship type (see Message Center Post here for more details).

All customers requesting a renewal should be guided to reach out to the Microsoft sponsor/contact that granted them the Sponsorship. That sponsor/contact should provide them further guidance on renewing.

In this scenario, you should keep the case open.

  • Continue to follow up with the customer until they have confirmed that they have contacted their sponsor.
  • Do not try to persuade or suggest temporary case closure to the customer.

Note: This is a change from previous guidance which required Sponsorship cases to be transferred to the Sponsorship escalation team. We have since learned that that team is not authorized or empowered to perform sponsorship renewal or extensions.

Monthly Statements

Azure Business Operations have decided not to send statements for Sponsorship customers after May 2018, as the sponsorship portal provides usage data that’s close to real time (refreshes every 15 minutes) and most of the customers prefer just the usage data. ASMS still may receive SRs from customers requesting for the statements, if you do, please communicate this to the customers:

As you know, during your free Microsoft Azure sponsorship we provide continually-updated usage and charges on the Microsoft Azure Sponsorship site. This makes it very convenient to check your usage at any time. Since the site was introduced, we have found that most sponsorship participants prefer using the site to receiving a monthly informational statement. Because of this, we have discontinued sending the monthly statement and we encourage you to refer to the sponsorship site at any time for fresh information.

Customers may request to transfer their entitlement from one account to another. In these instances, do the following before engaging your TA/PTA for assistance:

  2. Determine if adding an Owner RBAC role to the Destination subscription will resolve the customers’ ask.
    • In most cases, customers ask for a transfer the subscription in an effort to grant the Destination account access to the sponsored sub. However, adding an Owner RBAC role to the Destination may resolve the access need.
    • If this is not an option for the customer proceed to the next step.
  3. Verify there is an AIRS Registration under the Source Account.

Azure CSP Sponsorship

Azure CSP Sponsorship, also known as Azure Access CSP Proof of Concept (PoC), provides Azure credit to end customers’ subscriptions created in CSP.

Microsoft pays for the subscription consumption instead of the partner, during the PoC period. The upcoming charges are then automatically reverted back to the CSP partner to continue the process of executing the sales cycle, by moving from PoC to production environment.

Azure Access CSP PoC is ideal for opportunities where a customer needs a partner to provide managed service for their Azure solution. The partner provisions an Azure CSP subscription to run the PoC; the same subscription can be used as the production environment once the PoC ends.

The Azure CSP Sponsorship offer is similar to a standard -36P offer, however only the CSP partner can inform end customers of details such as their remaining credit.

What is a Sponsored Account?
Microsoft can 'sponsor' an account so that an outside user / company can use Azure services with the billing being charged back to Microsoft.  This allows Microsoft to set the duration of the offer, and the dollar amount of credit the user has to spend. All usage will come out of the AIRS system, they will not get a standard PDF like standard MOSP users. When the account is set up for Sponsorship, the Azure BG inputs the following properties for the Sponsorship:

  • Start Date of the sponsorship
  • End Date of the sponsorship
  • Total monetary credit of the Sponsorship amount

Once the sponsorship is activated, the customer will be able to run one or more -0036P subscriptions inside of the account.  All 0036P subscriptions will pull from the same monetary credit amount and will all have the same start and end dates. For example of the sponsorship is for a total of $10,000.00 USD, all of the 0036P subscriptions in the account will draw from the same $10,000.00 credit. Each subscriptions will not have its own $10,000.00 monetary credit. 

Sponsorship subscriptions will end in one of three ways:

1) The end date of the sponsorship is reached.  The subscriptions will automatically be converted into a PayG subscription at that time. Any remaining monetary credit from the sponsorship expires and does not carry forward and is not available for a refund.

2) The total monetary credit of the sponsorship is exhausted. The subscriptions will automatically be converted into a PayG subscription at that time.

3) The Azure account is converted to EA. The subscriptions will automatically be converted to EA subscriptions at that time. Any remaining monetary credit from the sponsorship expires and does not carry forward and is not available to be added into the customer EA agreement.

Pay Instruments
All sponsored accounts need a PI.  It is OK for users on this offer to be approved for the invoice option (if they request it), you must still follow standard process and get Global Financial Services (GFS) approval first.  That won't get billed on the invoice until the duration is up or the credit is used up, whichever comes first. Invoice whitelisting DOES NOT need approval from the BG, just follow standard Invoice whitelisting procedures. For urgent Invoice PI approvals for 0036P customers, engage your TA.

Initiation of a Sponsored Account
If we get a request to set up a user with a sponsored account ( 0036P ), the process is as follows:

  • ASMS sends the BG the following info:
    • WLID/ORGID of Azure account to be sponsored
    • PC code of the internal MSFT group who is ‘paying’ for the sponsorship (groups cross charge info)
    • Start and end dates of the sponsorship (may need to discuss this with both customer and BG to get approval/on same page)
    • Total Credit amount available via the Sponsorship
    • Budget approver alias (e.g. KimL)
    • Any additional customer emails that the customer wants to receive the monthly Sponsorship usage emails (in addition to the account WLID, these will be generated out of the AIRS system)
  • The BG inputs the info into AIRS to finalize the sponsorship
  • The BG confirms with ASMS that sponsorship is setup
  • ASMS converts the customer’s existing sub to -0036P or instructs customer to sign up for a new -0036P in their portal

NOTE: This sponsorship is ACCOUNT based, so if there are more than one 0036P subs in a Sponsored account they will all be tied into the same sponsorship start and end dates and will all ‘pull’ from the same credit pool.

They must log in with the other account type. For example, if the customer logs in with their Microsoft ID, and receives the below message, then they need to log in with their Org ID in order to provision the Sponsorship subscription.
  3. If the customer still has issues in provisioining their Sponsorship subscription, please send a problem with the Account ID to the Azure Sponsorship Offer queue to verify if the account ID is entitled to the Sponsorship offer. If not, then ask to help to find out which one it is.

 

Conversions

  1. Please ask the customer for their approval letter to the program so that you can verify that they are on the sponsorship program.
  2. Create a problem to the Azure Sponsorship Offer  team to verify if the account the customer provided is on the Sponsorship program and have them specify which account type as well (Work/School or Microsoft Account).
  3. Once it is verified that the account type (Work/School or Microsoft Account) and account ID matches in AIRS, then proceed with the conversion. If the sub is not on the same account type as in AIRS (i.e. AIRS entitled their Microsoft Account and customer is logged in with their Work account), then ask the customer if you can move the sub to their other account (i.e. Microsoft Account). If the customer does not want to move the sub to their other account type, then create a problem with Azure Sponsorship Offer  to change the entitlement to their other account type (i.e. Work Account).
    1. Example:
  1. michael@contoso.com is a Work account
  2. michael@contoso.com is entitled in AIRS as a Microsoft Account
  3. Customer wants to convert their PAYG sub on their Work Account to the sponsorship offer
  4. Create problem to Azure Sponsorship Offer  to verify what account type the customer’s sponsorship is on (Work or Microsoft)
  5. Azure Sponsorship Offer  states that the customer is entitled to their Microsoft Account
  6. Ask customer if you can move their PAYG to their Microsoft Account
    1. If the customer says yes, transfer and convert to 36P
    2. If the customer says no, create a problem to Azure Sponsorship Offer  to move entitlement to their Work account
    3. Once Azure Sponsorship Offer entitles the Work account, then convert the sub to 36P

Note: Before converting the customer’s subscription to the Sponsorship offer, go to https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/regions/offers/ and verify the customer' existing subscription does not have regions that are unavailable under the Sponsorship offer and inform the customer.

Example: Customer has a PAYG with VM's deployed under West US.  West US is not one of the regions available under the Sponsorship offer and so the customer will not be able to continue deploying new VMs under the West US Region.

1. I am on a sponsorship, why does the Azure Accounts portal show $0 balance?

a. The Azure Accounts portal will not show your sponsorship balance, as this is a $0 offer.  Basically you are offered a 100% discount to all services on your account for the duration of your sponsorship, therefore the balance will show $0.  To find the balance on your account, please sign in with your account details here: https://www.microsoftazuresponsorships.com/balance

a.     If the destination account is registered as a sponsored account, then yes à please confirm with azdesk to ensure that the destination account is in AIRS

b.     If the destination account is not in AIRS, then contact azdesk to get this created.

  Can I purchase third party products (VSO, Marketplace, etc..) with my sponsorship subscription?

a.     Yes, but they will be billed to the credit card on file separately, you cannot use your sponsorship credits on third party purchases.

Cloud Platform competency refresh (36p Offer)

(Retirement of 27p & 28p  offers)

 

Historically, Microsoft offered a $250 (silver) and $500 (gold) monthly credit to partners enrolled in the Cloud Platform competency.  After the September 26, 2015 PVP update, partners will see an annual credit (also known as a bulk credit) of $6,000 USD for silver partners and $12,000 USD for gold partners.   Partners can consume the annual credit by accessing the link for the credit in the PDD, just as they consumed the monthly credit.  Although the credit is available as an annual credit, partners may consume the credit as they like, for instance all in one day or in smaller amounts spread out over their competency enrollment period.

 

You can find more information about 27p & 28p offers here: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/ASMS/ASMS%20Community%20Documents/VKBDocs/Cloud%20Platform%20competency%20refresh%20(36p%20Offer).docx
