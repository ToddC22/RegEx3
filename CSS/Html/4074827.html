Procedure: Surface: How to assist customers with a cracked screen

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Surface

What is this article about?

This article provides specific instructions on how to assist customers who report a cracked screen on their Surface device.

This article should be followed when the customer reports a cracked screen on their Surface device.

1. Overview

Some customers may report that their screen cracked. Follow the guidance below within this article for handling.

Cracked screen scenarios indicate damage that is customer induced, and this is not covered under the terms of the warranty and requires an out of warranty payment or an accidental claim against the Microsoft Complete plan (if the customer purchased Microsoft Complete).

  • GLOBAL ENGLISH ONLY: You are expected to proactively refer customers that cracked their screens online to create their own order at https://support.microsoft.com/devices.
  • NON-ENGLISH ONLY: Continue to write the order on behalf of the customer without having to proactively refer the customer online.

2. Procedure

  1. Confirm with the customer if there is a screen protector on the device.
    • The screen protector could be glass and the customer may think the screen is cracked when it’s only the screen protector. This may be more common for commercial devices.
  2. Refer the customer to the online resolution, using the following messaging:

“We are sorry to hear that your screen is cracked. Please visit https://support.microsoft.com/help/4023535 and select Option 1 to get your order created online and the process for repair started.”

Exception:

If the customer does not have access to the internet or does not have a functioning device to create an online repair order, write the order for the customer using MST. Document the exception reason in the case notes.

Note: When processing an order for China or India customers, they must pay for out of warranty servicing at a Walk-in Center (WIC). See KB 3128488 Procedure: Surface: Creating a Walk-in Center exchange order for customers in China/India.

  1. Offer to send the customer an email of the template provided here with additional instructions.
  2. Once the customer confirms they understand
    • Document the case following section 3.
    • Code the case following section 4.
    • Close the case.

3. Information to gather in your notes

  • Capture information about the damage and how the damage occurred.
  • If you created the order in MST on behalf of the customer, include the reason for exception.

Return to top

4. How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case based on how the customer said the damage occurred:

  • Surface Device > Physical Damage > Cracked Screen > Cracked Screen – Customer claims no damage
  • Surface Device > Physical Damage > Cracked Screen > Cracked Screen – Customer caused damage

5. FAQ

Scenario

Advocate action

Customer insists they did not crack their screen.

Customer claims a device defect caused the screen to crack under normal use.

Messaging:

“We are sorry to hear that your screen is cracked. Cracked screens are not covered under the warranty terms. You can create an order online at https://support.microsoft.com/devices. Would you like me to send you additional details in email right now?”

If the customer insists or pushes the issue (reactive):

“There are costs associated with repairing your device. The warranty terms are available online at https://support.microsoft.com/help/4040687. I can send you additional details in email so when you choose to repair your device, you have everything you need to get the process started.”

Customer asks to speak with a supervisor (because of disagreement).

Messaging:

“I am doing my best to provide you the available options. What I can do for you is send you additional details in email, so when you choose to repair your device, you have everything you need to get the process started.”

If the customer insists or pushes the issue (reactive):

“Regardless of who you speak with, there are costs associated with repairing your device. The warranty terms are available online at https://support.microsoft.com/help/4040687. I can send you additional details in email so when you choose to repair your device, you have everything you need to get the process started.”
