What is this article about?

This article covers known issues for Surface Pro 4. Use this article to determine whether a customer’s symptom is associated with a known issue.

Before you begin

Check for updates or run Surface Diagnostic Toolkit (SDT).

  • For information on Surface Diagnostic Tool, see KB 4014334 Procedure: Surface: Using the Microsoft Surface Diagnostic Toolkit.
  • To check for updates, go to Settings > Update & security > Windows Update and select Check for updates. Install any necessary update and restart the Surface.

1. Accounts, security, sign-in and Windows Hello

1.1 Windows Hello stops working after 10/11/17 Update

Symptom:
After taking the October 11th, 2017 Windows Update, some customers may experience an issue with Windows Hello. The customer may receive the error Windows Hello failed to Start or something similar.

Resolution:
A fix has been shipped via Windows Update. Have the customer run SDT, or Windows Update to resolve. Customers should be on the latest version of Windows available when possible.

2. Battery, charging and power supply

2.1 Surface Pro 4 high battery drain when off

Symptom:
Users may notice that when they turn off their Surface Pro 4, the battery charge lasts for only a week or so, whereas previously it would last weeks when the device was turned off.

Resolution:
This is a known issue that will be addressed in a future update.

Messaging:
“Thank you for your feedback and the great information. We understand the concern and are currently investigating the issue. Please continue to check for Surface and Windows Updates to make sure that your device has the most current firmware and software available.”

4. Network connections (Wi-Fi, Ethernet, LTE) and Bluetooth

4.1 Unable to connect to Wi-Fi after upgrade to Marvell 15.68.17013.110

Symptom:
The customer is unable to connect to Wi-Fi after upgrading to the latest Marvell Wi-Fi driver version 15.68.17013.110.

Resolution:
Roll back to the previous version of the Marvell driver. A service order for a replacement should not be setup for this issue.

Messaging:
Note: This messaging should only be shared with customer contacting us via phone or chat and should not be posted as a response on community or social sites.

“Thank you for reporting the issue. We are testing a fix that will release as soon as it passes our quality testing. When the fix is released it will be made available through Windows Update.”

6. Pen

6.1 Fixed – 12/7/2018: Pen no longer inks, but single touch works after device wakes from sleep

Symptom:
Some customers may experience an issue where their Surface Pen no longer inks, but single touch functionality still works after their device wakes from sleep. It is likely also that multi-touch (such as pinch to zoom) also does not work.

Resolution:

  1. Restart Surface to recover touch functionality.
  2. Run SDT and Windows Update.

In December of 2017, a new Intel Precise Touch driver was shipped that addresses this issue.

10. Touchscreen (touch, screen, and display)

10.1 Surface Pro 4 only - Touch (full screen) and pen input fails after July 25 update

Symptom:
Users who have installed the July 25, 2018, Surface Pro 4 update (or the applicable MSI package) may experience device touch (full screen) and pen functionality failure. This behavior is related to an installation error for their Surface ME drive (Code 10 – failure to start).

To confirm the issue:

  1. Select the Start button, and then select Settings > Update & security > Windows Update > View update history. Check to see if the July 25 Surface Pro 4 update was installed.
  2. Go to Device Manager. Select Firmware and look for Surface ME. Check to see if it is marked with an exclamation mark (!). If it is, double-click Surface ME. A Code 10 error should be noted.

Resolution: Only for customers who meet the condition noted above, replace the device. If the customer is out of warranty (OOW), consult your Tech Lead.

Tech Lead: Work with a Super User to temporarily extend the warranty for a customer who is OOW. This will allow you to write an in warranty (IW) order. Use SDT bypass. Consult Devices ACA as needed.

Note: Customers who receive the replacement and install the update could again experience the issue. If you have a customer in this “repeat” scenario, alert your Tech Lead immediately to notify Microsoft.

Coding:
Code the case as follows:

  • Feature = Surface Device
  • Topic = Touchscreen
  • Scenario = Touch
  • Issue = Touch does not respond (Entire Screen)

Update the Subject line of the service request to include SP4 touch stops responding, ME driver error.

10.2 Screen Scramble and Flicker issues on Surface Pro 4

Symptom:
Note: Screen Scramble and Screen Flicker are not the same, be sure to use the correct terminology when talking to customers.

Some users may contact support describing issues commonly referred to as flashing, flickering, shaking, jittering and scrambled.

Ask probing questions to understand the customer’s symptom. Use this document as a guide to symptom recognition and how to support these customers. Once you have determined the customer’s symptom, proceed to the appropriate resolution (flicker symptom guidance below.)

Scramble symptom:

  • The Surface display image on screen appears to shift either horizontally or vertically very quickly. Customers may identify it as a “fuzzy” or overlapping duplication image.
  • The duplicate images can also appear static as a combined/interlaced image.
  • Finally, touching the screen may temporarily mitigate these issues described above.

Note: For customers with a scramble specific issue, we will replace eligible Surface Pro 4 devices for up to three years from the date of purchase, free of charge, for more information see public-facing article, Surface Pro 4 screen flickers or is scrambled.

Note: If a customer describes black horizontal lines on the screen (edge to edge) this is not a scramble issue, and the case should be coded/remediated as the following existing issue (not as scramble). See KB 3215844 Technical guide: Surface screen issues.

  • Surface Device/Touchscreen/Screen/Line(s) through screen
 

Scramble example screen:

[Asset 4294432]

Scramble resolution:

Attempt to follow all the resolution steps in KB 3215844 Technical Guide: Surface: Surface screen issues before continuing.

Commercial: See KB 4294168 Procedure: Surface Commercial: Scrambled Display Call Handling for Surface Pro 4 Devices for resolution steps.

Consumer: Use the following guidance in Service Order Management (SOM) tool to process a replacement.

Note: When you process a scramble-related replacement, record in the case notes whether the customer was using a cover or case. This does not affect replacement of the device. The information is just needed to help investigate causes.

  1. Verify the status of the customer’s warranty – see KB 4053533 TUG: MST: Devices section.
    Note: For Screen Scramble issues we will replace eligible Surface Pro 4 devices for up to three years from the date of purchase, free of charge. Many SP4 devices will exceed this three-year total now, if the SOM tool does not present the following offer, the device has exceeded this special offer timeframe, and the customer should be advised on paid warranty device options.
  2. When creating the repair order in SOM tool, select Display Scramble from the Problem Type pull down.
  3. Select the appropriate repair offer.
    • If the device is in warranty, select the best and most appropriate offer.
    • If the device is out of OEM warranty, use the below offers based on your location:
      • China only: Surface Pro 4 – Screen Scramble Warranty Bypass Same Unit Repair (offer 100118)
        Description: This offer allows you to write a “no charge” order even when the device is considered OOW. Only use this offer when the customer’s issue matches the symptom description for Scramble, above.  Important! While the warranty status is bypassed, the service center may reject the device if any physical or water damage is found on receipt of customer device
      • Rest of world: Surface Pro 4 – Screen Scramble Warranty Bypass Standard Exchange (offer 100117)
        Description: This offer allows you to write a “no charge” order even when the device is considered out of OEM warranty (but within the three year scramble policy). Only use this offer when the customer’s issue matches the symptom description for Scramble, above.
        Important! While the warranty status is bypassed, the service center may reject the device if any physical or water damage is found on receipt of customer device.

Note: If the SOM tool does not present the Surface Pro 4 – Screen Scramble Warranty Bypass Standard Exchange offer, the device is no longer eligible for the three year no cost warranty program as it has exceeded that three year total, and the customer should be advised of paid warranty options (for example, paid OOW) or the new CSV option. See KB 4504389 Procedure: Surface: CSV offer for Surface Pro 4 (SP4) scramble issues.

4. Code your case as below:

Surface Pro 4 > Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Scramble
Messaging:

For messaging, use the below link for guidance. This link can also be sent to the customer.

Surface Pro 4 screen flickers or is scrambled

Flicker symptom:

  • The entire Surface Screen or sections of the screen will NOT shift position quickly but will “flash” to a black screen or black section, where the image on the screen goes away for a very brief point in time.
  • Another significant characteristic is the impression of screen tearing.

Note: Screen scramble and screen flicker are not the same, devices experiencing flicker are NOT eligible for scramble options, and standard in warranty and OOW options should be provided.

  2. Code your case as below:

There is no prescribed messaging for this issue.

Customers seeking a refund for previously PAID repair

Commercial: See KB 4294168 Procedure: Surface Commercial: Scrambled Display Call Handling for Surface Pro 4 Devices for resolution steps.

Consumer: Follow the guidance below and issue a refund if appropriate:

Some customers may have already paid for an out of warranty replacement because of this issue. Follow the below guidance if a customer makes this claim:

  1. Obtain the customer’s service request (SR) order number and review the customer’s case notes. The issue should be for “Flickering and Flashing”, or “Shaking, or Jittering” only, no other screen scenarios are applicable.
  2. Check the customer’s order history and validate if a paid order exists for this issue.
  3. Follow section 3 of KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) to issue a refund for the replacement.
  • China: Follow section 3.1 of KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios to consult with an Advanced Capability Advocate (ACA). Since we cannot refund transactions using the SOM tool, a manual check will need to be cut and sent to the customer.
  • Rest of world: Follow section 3.1 of KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios to consult with ACA if a refund in SOM tool cannot take place. For example, the customer no longer has the credit card used to charge the repair fee.

Scramble symptom FAQ
Use the below as guidance for answering customer questions.

Question

Answer

What devices are covered in this program?

Only Surface Pro 4 (SP4) devices of any configuration and currently not covered by any warranty, are covered.

Why are you extending free replacement?

We are aware that a very small percentage of SP4 customers have experienced screen scramble symptoms. We are extending free replacement for SP4 devices affected by screen scramble for up to three years from date of purchase. If you believe your device is experiencing a screen scramble symptom, I can help diagnose this now to determine next steps.

What is a very small percentage?

The call center does not have this information. But I can help diagnose your device now if you are experiencing scramble on your screen.

Why did Microsoft take so long to address this problem?

We monitored and investigated the situation closely for a very small percentage of customers affected to arrive at this program.

I’ve seen a flicker on my screen, should I replace my device?

I can help diagnose this now to determine if your device needs to be replaced.

Can customers repair other issues as part of this offer?

The extension of free replacement is applicable only for the SP4 devices which are experiencing this screen scrambling. You are entitled to the existing Limited Warranty for other support or repair needs.

Are you repairing the device or sending customers refurbished devices?

Microsoft exchanges with devices that undergo a rigorous refurbishing process that includes hardware and cosmetic quality inspections, software updating, and diagnostic testing.

If a replacement is needed when SP4 is no longer available, what will the customer receive?

If Microsoft runs out of SP4 exchange inventory to support this free replacement, a suitable upgrade to another Surface Pro device will be offered.

Will both consumer and commercial customers be able to get a replacement device?

Yes, this offer is extended to both consumers and commercial customers experiencing screen scrambling.

Will customers who have already paid the out-of-warranty cost for a SP4 with screen flicker be reimbursed?

If you paid an out of warranty fee for repairing this particular screen flickering issue, I can issue you a refund. Let me verify previous records (or ask clarifying questions) to determine if you may be eligible for a refund.

Advocate determines customer "not eligible" for free repair: What do you mean I am not eligible for the free replacement?

Based on the diagnoses of your symptoms it does not appear that your device is experiencing the "screen scramble" symptom and are not eligible for this free replacement program. However, I can help you further diagnose the device to determine next steps.

Advocate determines customer "not eligible" for refund of previously paid repair: What do you mean I am not eligible for a refund?

Based on the case notes (if any exist) OR how you described the symptoms: it is not in the scope of this program. If you are experiencing any issues with your device, I can help diagnose that now.

14. Surface recovery and BMR known issues

14.1 Reset takes approximately two hours to complete

Symptom:
If a customer is running a reset from either a USB or from the hard drive partition, the reset might appear to get stuck at 7 percent or at 99 percent for an extended period of time. This makes it appear as though the reset is frozen or not functioning correctly.

Resolution:
This is expected behavior, the device is not frozen. The reset is progressing normally. We need to revise customer expectations and let them know that resets on their device may take approximately two hours to complete. The reset is processing and is not frozen. If the device fails to reset after approximately two hours, notify a tech lead right away.
