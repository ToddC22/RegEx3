Procedure: How to handle billing account violations (formerly account locks)

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Accounts and Billing

What is this article about?

There are several types of billing account violations (formerly known as billing account locks) that can prevent customers from accessing Xbox Live or making purchases through the Microsoft Store or Marketplace. For instance, when a customer states they are unable to access their Xbox billing account, make purchases, or redeem PPC/token codes, and a billing account violation is found on the account, or when a customer receives this message from the console: Can't retrieve information from Xbox LIVE. Status code 80169d94. This policy describes how to determine whether a customer's Xbox Live billing account is locked and how to resolve the issue for them.

1. Guidelines

  • Do not apply a billing account violation to a customer's account for any reason.
  • Do not remove a billing account violation unless it is in one of the allowed scenarios (see below).
  • Do help a customer manage their account and payment instruments through account.microsoft.com.

Return to top

1.1 When can I remove a billing account violation?

  • If the violation comment indicates it was locked by customer request.
  • Removing the violation may be permissible during certain other scenarios; see the scenario table below.

Return to top

1.2 When will an account be locked?

Billing account violations may be placed on an Xbox billing account for the following reasons:

  • By Account Takeover investigations support: As part of an Account Takeover investigation, the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates will lock the customer’s billing account to prevent further purchases. For information about how to resolve this issue, see the corresponding topic in section 2.
  • For potential fraud or suspicious behavior: Billing accounts are rarely locked for fraud because a risk block is usually placed against the questionable transaction.
  • After the customer proceeds with closing their Microsoft account: Occasionally instead of a closed status, an account will display a locked status after a customer has closed their account.
  • By a Microsoft product group for various reasons: Product groups within Microsoft may have a reason or need for a billing account violation to be placed on an account. Normally these reasons are unknown to CSS and require investigation.

1.3 Similar symptoms

Although exhibiting similar symptoms, the following issues should be addressed using the included references:

  • Microsoft account is marked as compromised: If the customers Microsoft account is marked as compromised, the billing account is not locked; however, the customer will not be able to sign in until they can recover the account. If the customer signs in on the console, they will get an error message indicating that the account has been compromised. If the customer signs in online, they will get a message prompting them to change their password and update their security information (proofs). Follow the process in KB 930018 Procedure: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account and use the Microsoft account status feature in the Alerts section of MST to determine whether the customers Microsoft account is marked as compromised.
  • Subscription in decline: The billing account is not locked; however, because the subscription is in a suspended state, the customer will not be able to access online gameplay. See KB 2852430 Process: Subscription payment is declined and KB 818928 Troubleshooting: Prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription redemption issues.
  • Transaction is flagged as a potential risk (risk rejection): Questionable account activity may result in individual transactions being declined during the purchase process. The billing account is not locked, but transactions will be declined. To troubleshoot, follow the process in KB 2896711 Process: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops.

2. Identifying the violation

When a customer's billing account is locked, follow the steps below to verify the type of account lock.

1. Check the Alerts section in MST to verify that the customer's account has an alert for a billing account violation. If no alert exists, review section 1.3.
[Asset 4466394]
2. Once the lock is confirmed, check the System Activities section in MST Comments to locate the most recent AddViolationAccount comment that explains the reason the violation was added.

  • Remember that some accounts have multiple billing account profiles for different regions.
  • If you have difficulty finding the most recent Account Violation addition, keep in mind the date range and review the entire history of the account.
  • If the comment says “AddViolationSubscription”, this process does not apply. See KB 2852430 Process: Subscription payment is declined for guidance.

[Asset 4466396]

3. Read the comment and determine the type of billing account violation that has occurred and follow the specific troubleshooting for that type in the table below.

3.1 Billing account is locked for potential fraud or suspicious behavior.

If the account was locked because of suspected fraud, you will see the following comment:

"Suspicious or fraudulent sign up and or account activity. Account has been locked. Refer to KB article 931798 (Xbox) or 941243 (Zune) for proper handling."


If the above comment is the most recent add violation comment; review the date the violation was placed.

  • If the violation was placed more than 365 days ago, remove the violation through the alert in MST.
  • If the violation was placed within the last 365 days, engage using the taxonomy in section 4.1. Make sure to save the service request in an open state.

3.2 Billing account was locked by Account Takeover investigations support.

If the account was locked by an Account Takeover investigations support agent, the billing activity comments should include the following agent’s comments about an Account takeover investigation.

“Locking account due to fraud claim. SR: XXXXXXXXXXX”

If the account was locked as part of an open Account Takeover investigation, do not unlock the billing account because the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates is still investigating the account. Use the following steps to determine what action to take:

  1. Review the Incidents section in MST and locate the Account Takeover service request.
    • If the lock was placed over 6 months ago, it will be likely that no Account Takeover service request can be found.
    • If no Account Takeover service request is found, engage with an Advanced Capability Advocate, see section 4.2 for more details.
  2. Review the service request status and communications sent to the customer.
    • If the case is open and no communications have been sent: Inform the customer that they will be contacted as needed in the course of the investigation.
    • If the case is open and the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates has sent communications: Inform the customer to reply to the email sent to them by the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates.
    • If the case is closed: Engage with an Advanced Capability Advocate using the taxonomy provided in section 4.2.

Do not unlock the billing account violation for this type.

Engage with an Advanced Capability Advocate if no Account Takeover service request is found or if the Account Takeover service request was closed.

Engage using section 4.2.

3.3 Billing account violation is placed by non- Account Takeover Advanced Capability Advocate or Tier 1 support.

If the account was locked by a customer support agent, the billing activity comment will reflect the advocate's reason for the violation being placed, such as a request from a parent to prevent purchasing by a minor.

Review the comment for the Billing Account Violation to ensure this comment does not refer to the above sections 3.1 or 3.2 for an unauthorized access claim or “Suspicious or fraudulent sign-up.”

You may remove billing account violations specifically added by a non-Account Takeover customer support agent after investigating the account to ensure no current service requests are open that would pertain to the billing account violation.

Check the surrounding comment details on the day the violation was added to verify who viewed the account to place the violation. If viewed and added by a Tier 1 or Advanced Capability Advocate CSS agent, it falls under this section. However, if you are unable to decipher the reason or the group that added the billing account violation due to an emerging or unknown billing account violation being placed, refer to section 3.5 for further instructions.

Return to top

Do not engage with Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates.

3.4 Billing account violation is placed after customer proceeds with closing their Microsoft account.

Historically, Microsoft account closures once resulted in an account lock rather than a closed status on the Microsoft account.

These lock types are identified by the following comment:

“This lock means the Microsoft account has been closed.

Follow KB: 2955912 - How to respond to a customer who wants to re-open their Microsoft account."

Return to top

Do not engage with Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates.

3.5 Emerging/unknown billing account violation.

A product group may find an emerging issue or unknown problem that requires placing a billing account violation on an account.

If the comment found is not addressed in the above sections, engage with an Advanced Capability Advocate for review as an "Emerging/unknown billing account violation" engage using the taxonomy in section 4.2.
