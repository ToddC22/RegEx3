1.2 Services & subscriptions

This section lists all the paid, free, and expired subscriptions and services associated with the customers Microsoft account. The customer can manage each service or subscription from this page.

Option

Definition

Show More

When reviewing a service or subscription, customers can select Show More for that subscription. This will give more details and options specific to that subscription or service. These options can include:

  • Manage devices - Allows customers to manage the devices that can use that service.
  • Install Some Subscriptions - Allows for installation on multiple devices and the customer can launch that install from here.
  • Share Some Subscriptions - Allows customers to add accounts to share their subscriptions and services.

  • Review Settings - Some settings, such as space available on OneDrive or Bing Rewards points, are available for review after selecting Show More.

Renew

Customers can renew monthly or expired subscriptions by selecting Renew if that option is available for that subscription.

Cancel

Customers can cancel recurring billing subscriptions.

1.5 Family

This section allows customers to review and manage family safety settings.

[Asset 4345583]

Option

Definition

Add a family member

Add a child or parent account.

Remove from family

Remove a child or parent account.

Activity

This option allows customers to manage the activity of a child account:

  • Manage: Turn on the activity reporting and set weekly email reports to stay up to date on the activity on the child account.
  • Apps and games: Set an age limit to block inappropriate content. Anything that exceeds the content ratings customers have decided are appropriate for their child will need parent approval. This setting applies to Windows 10 and Xbox One devices.
  • Web browsing: Turn this on to block inappropriate websites and help protect children from websites and search results that are too mature. This setting only works with Microsoft Edge and Internet Explorer on Windows 10 and Xbox One devices and blocks commonly used browsers to help keep a child safer.
  • Searches: When a child searches the web, customers will see what they searched.

Screen time

Turn this option on to create a screen time schedule for a child. The screen time applies to Windows 10 and Xbox One devices. It controls how much screen time customers allow the child account each day and when a child can use the devices.

Selecting this option will allow customers to manage and review the child account activity, including all the options listed above in the Activity section.

Content restrictions

Selecting this option will allow customers to set restrictions for purchases and to block inappropriate content. This can also be done using the Activity section.

  • Ask a parent: Customers can turn this on to require adult approval for a child account. This does not apply to what they get with gift cards or money in their Microsoft account.
  • Apps, games and media: Customers can set an age limit to block inappropriate content. Anything that exceeds the selected content ratings will need adult approval. This setting applies to Windows 10 and Xbox One devices.

Spending

Customers can add money to child Microsoft accounts so they can buy what they want without spending too much.

Customers can also set restrictions on inappropriate content. This can also be done using the Content restrictions section.

Find your child

Customers can stay up to date on their child's location to make sure their kids are okay. They’ll find them on a map when they have a Windows 10 phone or an Android device running Microsoft Launcher.

Note: This feature requires Microsoft Launcher version 4.10 and above.

1.7 Privacy

The privacy dashboard located at account.microsoft.com/privacy is where customers can manage their privacy settings for the Microsoft products and services they use, and where they can view and clear the data that Microsoft saves to the cloud. There are several options under the Privacy section.

[Asset 4466525]

  1. Overview section.

Note: All the options listed below are directly linked to the Activity History dashboard to manage settings.

Option

Definition

Browsing history

Applies to Microsoft Edge only:

If the browsing history in Cortana is turned on, the customer’s Microsoft Edge browsing history is sent to Microsoft so Microsoft features and services may use this data to provide customers with timely and intelligent answers, proactive personalized suggestions, or to complete tasks for them.

Search history

Bing uses customer’s search history to give them better results, including personalization and autosuggest. Cortana also uses that data to give customers timely, intelligent answers, personalized suggestions, and complete other tasks for them.

Location activity

To give customers directions and show them data relevant to where they are, we use locations that they provide or that we've detected using technologies like GPS.

Voice activity

When customers use voice commands with Windows, Cortana, and other cloud-based, voice-enabled content from Microsoft, we will collect and store their audio recordings so that we can enhance your experience with better speech recognition and other personalized speech experiences. Microsoft uses their voice command data to improve the ability of its products to correctly recognize their pronunciation and speech patterns.

Media activity

When customers watch movies or TV on a Microsoft app or service, we collect data about your media activity so that we can provide them with more relevant recommendations for entertainment and content.

Product and service activity

When customers use a Microsoft product or service, we may save data about their activity to their Microsoft account, such as information about which apps or services they use and how they use them. This information helps improve our content.

Product and service performance

When customers use a Microsoft product or service, we collect reliability and performance data to measure the quality of their experience. This data tells us about the health of the products and helps us fix and improve them.

Cortana’s Notebook

To help customers avoid traffic, remember anniversaries, text the right “Jennifer” in their contact list, and in general do more, Cortana needs to know what customers are interested in. The Notebook is where Cortana keeps track of their interests to give them a personalized experience.

LinkedIn

When customers connect their Microsoft and LinkedIn accounts, Microsoft shares some of their account data with LinkedIn, such as calendar and contact data. Likewise, LinkedIn shares some of your account data with Microsoft, such as profile and connections data.

Health activity

Microsoft Health, HealthVault, and devices can help customers collect, understand, and manage their health data. Their data can include activity and fitness data like heart rate and daily steps taken. It can also include any health records stored in HealthVault. And HealthVault gives them the ability to share health records with caregivers.

 

  1. Activity history.

The information listed in the Activity history dashboard represents the most relevant customer’s activity linked to their Microsoft account. From here, the activity history can be reviewed and cleared. Once the customer clears their data, Microsoft will delete their information from the dashboard and start the process of removing other copies from our systems.

Options Description

Apps and services

This section provides a link to the Manage permissions page that allows customers to control what apps they purchased in the Microsoft Store to access on their device. For example, they can manage:

  • Video

  • Search

  • Browse

  • Media

  • Location

Search

List all the search activities if customers:

  • Perform searches from Bing.

  • Perform searches from Cortana that land on Bing pages.

  • Sign into Bing with your Microsoft account.

Note: Activities won’t appear if customers:

  • Perform searches from Cortana that don't land on Bing pages.

  • Perform searches from the Cortana app on a phone that uses an iOS or Android operating system.

Perform searches using Bing, but haven’t signed in to Bing with their Microsoft account, though you can still view your search activity on the Bing.com Search History page.

Browse

Browse activity only appears if customers:

  • Enable Cortana in Windows.

  • Turn on browsing history in Cortana.

  • Enable Cortana In Microsoft Edge.

Locations

Microsoft uses the locations customers provide or that we've detected using technologies like device GPS to give customers directions, display more relevant search results, help them find their device if it’s missing, and provide other location-aware services. Clearing this data means that these services might not work as well for them.

Download your data

The information listed in the Download your data dashboard is where customers can download a copy of the data that appears on the Activity history page.

How is general data collected?
  • Device location

It is collected by a Microsoft device when Find My Device feature is on.

If you've lost your device, go to Find My Device.

  • Windows

Microsoft collects this data when you turn on the location service in Windows. Having the location service on helps other apps and services in Windows provide you with locally relevant information and experiences. To change this setting, on your Windows device, go to Start > Settings > Privacy > Location.

  • Bing

If you gave your browser or the Bing app permission to use your location, or if you use Cortana for searches, Bing uses your location to give you better search results.

  • Cortana 

Microsoft collects data when you turn on visit history in Cortana to get route and traffic suggestions. To change this setting on your device, go to Cortana > Notebook > Getting around and turn on or off Keep a record of my visits.
Account Takeover

If the customer receives the error messages "Help us secure your account" at sign in, “Help us protect your account. We've detected something unusual about this sign-in...”, or if the customer does not recognize the verification methods while trying to rest password, transfer the customer to Accounts & Billing (if product is Xbox transfer to Xbox Accounts and Billing) for possible further engagement.
