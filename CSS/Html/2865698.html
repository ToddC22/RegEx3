1. Summary

  • The appropriate escalation team has been contacted but that team is not meeting its published Service Level Agreement (SLA) for follow-ups or a resolution.
  • There is a question about a policy that has already been defined but is perceived as incorrect or ineffective.
  • Clarification or additional information is needed about a policy. 

1.1 Purpose/Description 

The purpose of this article is to explain the MPN Global Support Escalation process.

Back to top

2. Process 

This section will explain how to escalate, what is needed to escalate most issues, and what type of issues we escalate. 

Back to top

2.1 What type of issues do we escalate? 

  • Policy exceptions, override requests, partner positioning and program issues related to Partner Center Dashboard – Accounts, Onboarding & Access, Co-sell, MPN and CSP.

  • Technical issues (for example Partner Center error messages, discrepancies, or slow performance).

  • Additionally, technical issues not specifically related to the above example may be sent to Global Support if the correct team that can handle the scenario is unknown or is not responsive.

  • Partner issues that are related to one of the following scenarios:

    • The appropriate escalation team has been contacted but that team is not meeting its published Service Level Agreement (SLA) for follow-ups or a resolution (for example Frontline advocates raised PayOps SRs but are not getting traction)

    • There is a question about a policy/offer that has already been defined but is perceived as incorrect or ineffective (for example COVID19:  Competency Requirements Waiver)

    • Clarification or additional information is needed about a policy (for example Benefits exception policy)

2.2 What is needed to escalate? 

1. Prior to escalating an issue to MPN Global Support the Frontline team will be required to gather all necessary information from the partner. Please add screenshots (recording steps are needed) to help the escalation team understand technical issues more precisely and to avoid misunderstandings.

2.3 How do I escalate? 

1. A Frontline advocate receives a contact regarding policy exceptions, override requests and\or program issues. 

2. Complete basic trouble-shooting steps and any known workarounds.  

3. Request the required information from the template in-order to escalate the issue to MPN Global Support.

4. After acquiring all of the information the Frontline advocate will follow their current escalation process for their region in Service Desk tool. Please see below:
