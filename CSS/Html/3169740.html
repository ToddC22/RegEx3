Process: Microsoft Advertising: Compromised Account/Unauthorized Access

[Asset 4509089]

Who should use this article?

  • Microsoft Advertising

What is this article about?

This article discusses what advocates should do on the rare occasion the owner of a Microsoft Advertising account has a compromised account. Compromised accounts are those that have been hacked or hijacked by an unauthorized third-party. This often leads to excess charges on the advertiser’s account.

1. ﻿Guidelines for discussing suspicious activity or account compromises

This issue is usually brought up in one of two ways:

  1. The account holder contacts us inquiring about unknown charges or other suspicious account activity.
  2. The internal fraud team identifies the compromise and follows the steps in KB 4032951 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Requests to T1 Support from Internal Partners to have support contact the advertiser and rehabilitate the account.

If an advertiser claims an unknown party has made changes to their account, do not use the words fraud or hijack when communicating with the advertiser. Until the account is escalated and confirmed as compromised, indicate that the issue requires further investigation.

2. ﻿﻿Investigating customer claims of unauthorized account access

If an advertiser contacts us inquiring about suspicious activity on their Microsoft Advertising account but all users and campaigns are active, follow the steps below to investigate and resolve the issue:

  1. Validate whether the user in question is active or suspended and if there is a QBR compromise penalty on the account. If suspended and there is a QBR penalty for compromise, proceed with to section 4. Otherwise, treat the interaction as a standard billing dispute and proceed to step 2.
  2. Pull a change history report and review the Changed by column with the advertiser to determine which changes were completed by a legitimate user. Highlight any disputed changes in the report and attach it to the ticket. This report is required for the fraud team to review the dispute and confirm validity.
  3. Let the advertiser know that we will need to do a deeper investigation into their account, which will take up to five business days. Advise them to pause their campaigns during this time if they do not wish to accrue further charges.
  4. L2s/SMEs: Escalate the ticket to the fraud team, see KB 3170756 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Escalating to Partners using UCMT, COSMIC, and CAP for further details. The fraud team will do a deeper investigation to determine if the account has been compromised and will get back to you either way. Be sure to specify the customer's callback number in the escalation.

Once you have confirmation of a compromise, it will take about 24 hours for the outreach details to appear in UCMT. In the meantime, you can use these talking points:

“Our investigation has confirmed unexpected activity on this account. Rest assured the account has been paused until rehabilitation is complete.”

Once UCMT outreach details are available, confirm if it’s the same person who escalated to CSS. If it is, you can move to section 4. If the user is NOT the same as outreach contact details, provide the talking point below:

“We are taking steps to rehabilitate the account and for security reasons, there is no additional information we can provide at this time. We will be working with the account administrator and assuming rehabilitation are completed, accounts may resume regular activity. Please work with the account administrator if you have further questions or concerns about future access to this account.”

If the user is not in the UCMT outreach details but claims they are the right contact, move to section 4.1.

  1. If the fraud team confirms there was no fraudulent activity, see section 3 for messaging. If the fraud team confirms the account was compromised, use the information provided by them in the resolution (such as, compromise date range and suspended users) and proceed to section 4, Rehabilitate a compromised account.

4. Rehabilitate a Compromised Account

Once FraudAds investigation confirms a compromise, they will relay this to CSS. Following initial confirmation (typically within 24 hours) Fraud will provide rehabilitation details in UCMT. If not speaking with the eligible user, then advise them to contact an administrator for their advertising account. Otherwise, create a new Microsoft account. It could be that the account was proactively identified as compromised, but we were unable to establish contact at the time it occurred. If a UCMT ticket exists, you do not need to escalate again to revalidate but you do need to ensure the UCMT ID is properly documented in your ASD ticket.

4.1 Inbound compromised user authentication

Regular inbound authentication is insufficient to rehabilitate a compromised account from an inbound contact. To be eligible for reinstatement, the user in question MUST be listed in the UCMT ticket for the proactive outreach as being eligible. Do not escalate the user to the fraud team for reinstatement until the following actions are complete:

Note: If the customer references the SRX from a proactive rehabilitation, the caller can be considered automatically authenticated and you can skip to User Rehabilitation.

  1. Microsoft account password is reset.
  2. Two-factor authentication (TFA) is enabled.
  3. Once the password is reset and TFA enabled, the Microsoft account is fortified via phone/text. Note: If the customer references the SRX from a proactive rehabilitation outreach, the caller can be considered automatically authenticated and you can skip to user rehabilitation.
    • Fortify the inbound caller using Phone/SMS-avoid email fortification whenever possible. Until the user is secured, do not send any other emails to the customer.
      • If the OneCloud caller ID or customer's preferred callback number matches the UCMT reliable contact phone number, proceed with user rehabilitation.
      • If the caller does not provide a phone number that matches the reliable phone number provided in the UCMT ticket, do the following in order:
        1. Ask for an alternative phone number to match to the UCMT ticket.
        2. Refer them to another Super Admin to assist them with regaining access to the account (see talking point for: If the user is NOT the same as outreach contact details in section 2, step 4).
        3. End the call and attempt to reach them using the reliable contact info provided in the UCMT ticket.
        4. Ask the customer to create a new advertising account and offer to assist with campaign migration (legitimate campaigns only—be sure to remove fraudulent content).
  4. Proceed with the user rehabilitation steps in section 4.3.

4.2 Outbound compromised user authentication

  • When a request for proactive rehabilitation is received from FraudAds, per KB 4032951 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Requests to T1 Support from Internal Partners , attempt to establish phone contact (do not use email) using the reliable contact information for user recovery provided by FraudAds. The fraud team will provide contact information for non-compromised users first, or they may provide most reliable contact information sourced for the compromised user. Reach out to FraudAds if you have any questions regarding the contact information. If phone contact is not established:
    1. Leave a voicemail for the contact and provide the SRX ID and inbound phone support number. By having this SRX ID, the customer can be considered authenticated if they contact inbound support.
    2. Do not update the customer email - Leave the ticket associated to the requestor alias (FraudAds msbabos). Do not send any email communications to the compromised user email.
    3. If no contact is established after standard outreach attempts (two times by phone + voicemail (no email) over two business days), proceed with billing rehabilitation only, respond to the FraudAds requestor to confirm that contact was not established, and close the ticket. FraudAds will suspend all users and Traffic Quality will QBR the affected account(s).

4.3 User rehabilitation

  1. Assist the advertiser with securing their existing Microsoft Account. Important: These steps must be followed for the user to be eligible for reactivation.
    1. Direct the advertiser to login.live.com and have them log into their Microsoft account.
    2. Ensure the advertiser reviews and updates their contact information and security settings:
      • Reset their password.
      • Add a phone number to their Microsoft account- they should have at least two (three preferred) alternative contact methods.
      • Turn on two-step verification (also known as TFA or two factor authentication).
    3. Download the Microsoft Authenticator app.
  2. Once the Microsoft account is secured with the steps above, submit an escalation to FraudAds using the user reinstatement escalation template and request user reactivation. 
  3. Once confirmed as active, ensure the newly secured/created user is associated to the ASD ticket for future reference. Email communications going forward are acceptable after the user is secured.
  4. Work with the Super Admin to rehabilitate any other compromised users on the account:
    • Advise the newly secured user to review other users.
    • Ensure the Super Admin understands and requires all other Microsoft account users to review the User Safety Tips: Microsoft Advertising: Steps to safer online experience, enable two-step verification, and use Microsoft Authenticator app.
    •  Educate the Super Admin on how to send new user invites to re-add the Microsoft accounts after they are secured.
  5. Content will not serve until the campaigns are rehabilitated and the QBR compromise flag is lifted.
  6. Once the Super Admin is secure, proceed with billing and campaign rehabilitation steps in section 4.4 and section 4.5.

4.4 Billing rehabilitation​​​​​

  1. Advise the newly secured user to review and delete any payment methods specified in the UCMT proactive account rehabilitation outreach template (find it in UCMT).
  2. FraudAds will provide the total refund value eligible for reversal. If over $10K, this may be provided in an ART form designed specifically for FraudAds. There is no need to pull additional reporting to validate the refunds that Fraud recommends. However, customer approval on the amount of the reversal should always be obtained prior to any adjustments. If you disagree with the value proposed and ART form details, work with the Fraud team to ensure all parties are in agreement of how to proceed.
  3. Once customer approved, reverse all charges directly resulting from the fraudulent activity but DO NOT exceed the total refund amount stated by FraudAds per payment instrument. Offline refunds for declined online or offline refunds will not be considered unless the user is fully secured (otherwise, the funds will go to unclaimed property). For prepay accounts, be sure to reverse only fraudulent activity incurred during the compromise and separately reverse any payments made from stolen payment instruments (PIs), as confirmed from the UCMT proactive account rehabilitation outreach template.
    • Adjust activity up to the amount specified in the UCMT proactive outreach rehabilitation template (found in UCMT) to ensure the full total refund amount is processed. In threshold accounts, this will reverse all charges to the correct payment methods. In prepay accounts, you may also need to process payment reversals incurred because of the compromise.
      1. Start with the most recent invoices and work your way back to the oldest invoice containing fraudulent activity.
      2. Use Adjustment Reason: Fraud - Other and specify in the comments "[SRX ID], Hijack/Compromise, [UCMT ticket ID], approver initials]."
      3. Add the credit memos as you go to ensure you do not exceed the total refund amount.
    • Reverse any prepay payments incurred during the time of the compromise.
    • Reverse any prepay payments made by cards designated as ‘eligible for full refund’ (stolen PIs) in the UCMT proactive outreach rehabilitation template (found in UCMT).

Note: Billing rehabilitation questions may be directed to FraudAds from a new child ticket.

Return to top

4.5 Campaign Rehabilitation

  1. Campaigns may be rehabilitated. Use the “undo” feature in the Change History tab for all changes during the compromise time period.
  2. Manually undo any remaining changes highlighted in the change history report provided by the fraud team.
  3. Replay to the fraud team (in original compromise outreach request) to confirm campaign and billing rehabilitation is complete and request the QBR flag/account level suspensions be lifted (do not create a secondary escalation to the QBR/editorial team, or SLA will be longer).
  4. Confirm all necessary campaigns have resumed delivering.
  5. Provide the user safety tips to the customer, Microsoft Advertising: Steps to safer online experience, before closing the ticket.
