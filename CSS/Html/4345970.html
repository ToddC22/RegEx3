Procedure: Callback Commitments

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor

What is this article about?

This procedure details the setting and documentation of next contact commitments to help drive case progression.

1. Steps

  • Drive case progression by setting and documenting a next contact commitment with the Customer or Partner after every communication. If they have the next action suggest when you will contact them back if you have not heard from them before:
    • Suggest scheduling a callback commitment in Rave at a date and time that you and the Customer would be available. That may ensure a fixed timeframe, and a rapid progression on the pending actions. (see Deviations for Rave engineers)
    • If customer is not willing to schedule any specific callback contact, or if this is not the best option for the engineer or case, try to agree a call-back window (for example, Thursday or Thursday 9AM-1PM). This gives freedom to balance your workload and avoids Customer or Partner disappointment if you cannot make the agreed time.
    • Confirm the next contact date in your follow up email detailing next actions.
    • Even if there is no update on current actions, Customer or Partner contact should be re-established as soon as possible to set expectations of next contact commitment. A case without a future commitment may be considered idle.
  • Meet all commitments.
    • Plan ahead and if you recognize that you are likely to miss a commitment then reach out to the Customer or Partner to set expectations early.
    • For urgent cases, work within your team to share workload and ensure all commitments are met.
  • Work to the Customer's or Partner's expectations of ongoing response times and not standard idle triggers. Where a case is identified as idle:
    • Contact the Customer or Partner as soon as possible even if there is no update.
    • Consider engaging the 4457522 | Procedure: Recovery.

Unresponsive Customers/Partners

  • If a customer does not respond to any methods of contact on the next contact date agreed by them:
    • Continue to maintain communication.
    • Set contact dates for when you will next try contact.
    • Follow the 4457094 | Procedure: Unresponsive Customer, ensuring you follow Incident Manager (IM) engagement actions for Premier cases.

End of shift, 24x7 handovers and transfers

  • At end of your shift a case may have to be worked by a different region. Where you have been unable to discuss the case with an engineer in the receiving region:
    • Continue to set a call-back commitment. However, be cautious of setting expectations where you cannot know for sure which engineer will be engaging.
    • Do not set commitments at the very beginning of shift for the next region.
    • Try to set a call-back window to permit prioritization in assignment, for example:
      • Sev A - 1-hour call-back window
      • Sev B - 2-hour call-back window
    • Ensure that the commitment time is documented:
      • Rave: In Internal Notes, set Call back schedule in Case Status.
      • Service Desk Case: In a Follow-up task.

2. Deviations or workarounds

  • Rave engineers should follow the callback commitment guidance provided in this article if they proactively engage this activity. Both Rave engineers and M365 customers are able to select very specific hours to work together using Schedule Callback or Customer Scheduled Callback (CSC) features, some of the guidance in this article will not apply (such as the large callback windows...). For Rave specific instructions, see article 4523301: Process: Using Customer Scheduled Callback (CSC) Feature in Rave for details.
