ASMS – Azure US Government

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

Azure Government offers U.S. Public sector customers physical and network-isolated instances of Microsoft Azure. Only Fairfax (FF) Support Engineers (SEs) can provide support to FF customers.

If you are a Non-Fairfax SE, see the For Non-Fairfax Support Engineers section of this TSG.

Fairfax to Arlington 

Arlington is a backend change where Fairfax customers will have all authentication and endpoints in US Government Cloud only to comply with International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) compliance requirements. More information about Arlington can be found here.

The following offers are available to Fairfax customers:

  1. MS-AZR-USGov-0003P (Government Pay-As-You-Go)
  2. MS-AZR-USGov-0015P (Internal Government Offer for internal customers who want to deploy to US Gov. Datacenters)
  3. MS-AZR-USGov-0017P (External Government Enterprise Agreement (EA) offer)
  4. MS-AZR-USGov-0036P (Sponsored offer with a payment instrument (PI))
  5. MS-AZR-USGov-0044P (Free Trial offer)
  6. MS-AZR-USGov-0143P (Sponsored offer without a PI)

Differences Between Fairfax and Commercial Azure Offers

  • Fairfax has a separate Management Portal (https://portal.azure.us).
  • There are a limited number of services offered (although the Engineering team continues to release services to reach parity with Commercial Azure.)
  • All credentials are manually provisioned. Office 365 Directory Integration with Azure is not supported.

Policy

General

  • Confirm that your customer has a Fairfax subscription. (See the list of available subscriptions in the Overview section.)
    • A government email address does not automatically mean that your customer has a Fairfax account.

Non-Fairfax Support Engineers

Non-Fairfax SEs are not allowed to work Fairfax cases.
If you think you’ve received a Fairfax case, check your customer’s subscriptions against the list of possible subscriptions in the Overview. For guidance on how to identify an ASMS Azure Government Case in Service Desk Case, click here. 

If confirmed that you have received a Fairfax case incorrectly, reach out to the asmsffxmt DL for assistance and next steps.

Customers That Opened a Fairfax Ticket from Their Commercial Account

If there is a need to open a US Gov. case, walk customer through opening one using https://portal.azure.us/.

    • Fairfax Management portal (https://portal.azure.us)
    • Fairfax Account portal (https://account.windowsazure.us/Subscriptions)
  • Services (by datacenter) are available on the Azure regions page.

  • Steps to add a Partner via MPN ID in the Azure Government portal can be found in TSG 2836909.

How to Convert an Offer to a Government EA Account

You can convert an offer using the following process:

  1. Check that the account has been added to an Enterprise Agreement, and that it has been activated.
    Note: Azure Government Free Trials and Sponsored Accounts require that your customer has already purchased an EA and has added it to their account.
  2. Notify the customer that any remaining entitlement or benefits will be lost once the conversion occurs.
    (For example, if the customer still has $200 USD left on their Trial, it will be lost once the account is converted to an Azure Government EA subscription.)
  3. After the account has been successfully added to an EA Government enrollment and activated, the customer’s existing Azure Government subscription will be converted to an Azure Government EA subscription.

How to Perform a Subscription Ownership Transfer (SOT)

Subscription Ownership Transfers (SOTs) in the EA portal are self-serve to customers. The Help section of the EA portal should have documentation available to your customer on how to complete the process.

If your customer needs help with a Subscription transfer, refer to TSG 3040383 for information on how to handle a SE-assisted transfer.

Note the following:

  • Both sides of the transfer need to be within the Azure Fairfax Tenant.
    • SOTs from Azure Commercial to Azure Government (Fairfax) and vice versa are not supported.
  • For EA Government Subscriptions, both the source and destination account must also be added as an Account within the EA Government enrollment.
  • Warning: Transferring to a different Azure Government domain/tenant will result in your customer losing all Service Administrators (SAs), Co-Administrators (CAs) and anyone configured in roles-based access control (RBAC). Be sure that your customer is aware of this and that the SA, CA, and RABAC personnel need to be re-added.

How to Request a Quota Increase

Use the Capacity Management template below (Fairfax Quota Template) to submit a request for a quota increase. Quota Increase requests for multiple regions and/or VM Types must be split into multiple RDQuota tickets per Region and per SKU basis.
Exception: More than 10 regions/VM types can be created as a single RDQuota ticket.
Note: If you need access to the Capacity Management DevOps (TFS) instance, see the Engagement and Escalation Path section.

How to Perform a Data Migration

For data migration, like commercial subscriptions, we do not support selective service transfer. (Transfers of only a selected hosted service or storage service from one subscription to another).
If a data migration occurs, everything must migrate.
Warning: Data Migrations from Azure Commercial to Azure Government (Fairfax) and vice versa are not supported.
Note: Data migrations are not supported for Azure Fairfax subscriptions if the tenant ID is different, as we are unable to add a Live or Microsoft ID to an Azure Fairfax subscription.

Fairfax Engineers
Work with your customer on confirming that the prerequisites are met for a migration.

How to Generate a Usage Report

  • Free Trial – Customers can download their report by going to the Fairfax Account portal.
  • Enterprise Agreement – Customers can download their report by going to the EA portal.
  • Sponsorship – See the following steps:
    1. Open a private browser window.
    2. Navigate to www.microsoftazuresponsorships.com.
    3. Click Check Your Balance.
    4. Sign in with your Azure Government Sponsored account email.
    5. You will then be directed to a page where you can see the following information:
      • Remaining Balance
      • Used
      • Sponsorship Amount
      • End Date
      • Start Date

How to Update the Service Administrator

Use the following process to change or update the Service Administrator:

  1. Log into the Fairfax Account Portal using the Account Owner’s ID.
  2. Click on the Subscriptions tab.
  3. Select the subscription that you wish to change the Service Administrator on.
  4. Click on Edit Subscription Details.

How to Troubleshoot a Premier Support Notification

We occasionally have cases in which a Fairfax customer sees that they have Premier Support in the Management portal but has never purchased a Premier Support plan.
Use the following steps to remove the notification:

  1. Use the Cloud Knows Premier (CKP) tool to search by the subscription ID and look up who the Technical Account Manager (TAM) is.
  2. Reach out to the TAM and let them know about the customer’s issue.
  3. Ask the TAM to decline the match in CKP.
  4. Add your case notes.
  5. Transfer the case to the appropriate technical team and request assistance for engaging the Platform team. 　

Note: You can read more about support contract mapping in TSG 3216942.

How to Request a Password Reset

If you receive a Fairfax Password Reset case from a customer or a Duty Manager, take the following steps to route it over to the Identity team:

  1. Update the internal case title to include: MISROUTE TO ASMS.
  2. Enter a Pre-Scope task (or equivalent) with a short explanation of why you are routing the case to the Identity team.
    • Example: Support of Azure Gov password resets is owned by the Identity team.
  3. Update the Routing Product of the case to Azure AD Sign-In and MFA and the Support Topic to Routing Azure Active Directory\Passwords\Problem with a user resetting or changing their password, in which the case will then be routed to the correct queue based on those settings.

Note: If your customer is a MSCInternal Trial customer, you can have them email the Fairfax Internal Subscription Admins for a password reset.

How to Handle USNAT Cases

Cases entitled for USNAT require the owning support engineer to have USNAT permissions enabled in Service Desk. Also, the USNAT program is only available for Premier customers. To be approved for USNAT permissions you must be a U.S. citizen.

If you are assigned a case locked down with an USNAT entitlement and you do not have USNAT permissions in Service Desk, use the following directions:

  • If you are a US citizen, follow the instructions on the Microsoft US Government Support site to get temporary access for the specific case.
    • You should also begin the process of attaining permanent (“standing”) USNAT access by using the same link.
  • If you are not a US citizen, let the duty managers (WADM DL) know that you do not have USNAT access and that they will need to assign the case to an engineer that does.

Note: USNAT is a different program than US Gov. However, most ASMS US Gov engineers also have USNAT permissions enabled in Service Desk.
