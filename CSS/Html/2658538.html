ASMS – HIT Team Engagement (Formerly ART)

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This article provides guidance on how and when support engineers (SEs) should engage the Human Intelligence Team (HIT).

Note: This team was formerly known as the Account Research Team (ART).

HIT may reach out to ASMS via task to accept a case that was misrouted to them, please yank the case if a task requests you to. 

Policy

Overall Policy

  • The HIT team is available 24/7 and resolves most HIT escalations within two hours. Occasionally, additional information may be required to complete the investigation, in which it may take longer than two hours. In those instances, do not submit requests for status updates if it has been less than two hours. This will only prolong the overall review.
  • We offer immediate support for Enterprise Agreement (EA) customers, Partners, and Executive Escalations only.
    See the EA, Partner, and Executive Escalations Only section for specific instructions.

Support Scenarios

Scenarios Requiring Task Escalation to HIT

The following scenarios require a task to be sent to the HIT team. Follow the instructions that best align to your customer’s situation.

Scenario

Directions

Customer Received an Error Message While Attempting to Purchase a Visual Studio (VS) Marketplace Subscription

Some customers who are attempting to purchase a Visual Studio Professional or Visual Studio Enterprise subscription from the Visual Studio Marketplace may receive the following error message, after clicking “Confirm” on the last step of the purchase experience:

“Your purchase could not be completed at this time. Please contact support for assistance.”

In these scenarios, you will not see a message from HIT in CMAT letting you know why the customer’s purchase failed.
You will need to engage HIT by doing the following:

  1. Ask the customer to provide a screenshot of the page with the error message.
  2. Engage HIT via a task, using the Engagement and Escalation Path section below.
    • Be sure to include the screenshot or error message in the HIT ticket.
  3. HIT will review the request and provide next steps.

Customer Reports an Unauthorized Charge and States It Is Not Their Account

    Note: Do not provide the account’s email address to the customer if the customer is not an account administrator (AA), service administrator (SA), or co-administrator (CA) of the subscription.
    • If you can locate the account and the customer confirms it is not theirs, proceed to the next step.
    • If you are unable to locate the account, inform the customer you were unable to locate the account and do not escalate to HIT.
    • Work with your technical advisor (TA) or partner technical advisor (PTA) if you are unsure of next steps.
  1. Request the following information from the customer:
    Note: Include this information in the task that you send to HIT.

    • Billing Address:

    • Phone Number:
    • Credit Card Last 4 Digits:
    • Bank Name:
    • Bank Phone Number:
    • Reference number provided on credit card statement:
    • Date of Charge:
    • Amount Charged:
  2. Escalate to HIT via a task, following the Engagement and Escalation Path section below.

Customer Wants a Quota Increase on a Fraud-Disabled Subscription

To act on quota increases for reactivated subscriptions, do the following:

  1. ASMS owns the case and opens a collaboration task for HIT following the Engagement and Escalation Path section below.
  2. HIT reviews the case.
    • If upheld, HIT takes ownership and closes the case.
    • If reactivated, HIT resolves the task and provides notes for ASMS.

Customer Account or Subscription Suspended by HIT Due to Non-Payment

If HIT has suspended an account or subscription due to non-payment, one of these indicators will appear in CMAT:

  • The Billing Account Status is Suspended. (See Account Information.)
  • The Subscription Entitlement Status is Suspended (Financial Fraud) (See Lifecycle and Status.)
  • The Activity Log shows Operation Name as Cancel Subscription, and the Agent Comment is:
    “Customer has been suspended for non-payment. Please do not reactivate unless customer agrees to settle outstanding amount. If customer does agree to this, please escalate to HIT so account can be re-activated.”

Settling Outstanding Charges

If the customer agrees to settle their outstanding charges, submit a collaboration task and any proof of payment to HIT.

  • Once HIT re-activates the account status, ensure the customer’s payment has been applied before enabling their subscriptions.
  • Work with existing processes to settle the payments via credit card, check, or wire-transfer.

For invoice customers, enable the subscription once proof of payment is received.

  • Work with Global Financial Services (GFS) (formerly known as Global Credit Services (GCS) or Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS)) to settle the outstanding charges. For more information on GFS engagement, see TSG 2647567. 
  • If GFS cannot track the payment, or it has not been received, engage HIT to suspend the subscriptions.
Customer Subscription suspended due to “serviceAbuse” status
If a Subscription is listed as suspended in CMAT with the “serviceAbuse” status, this is due to a violation of the terms of service. In this situation, the HIT team must be engaged for further review. HIT will initiate a communication with CDOC and will work with the customer to resolve the issue.
 
If possible, HIT will reinstate the subscription and update the customer.

Scenarios Requiring Case Transfer to HIT

The following scenarios require the entire case to be transferred to the HIT team,  following the directions in the Transferring Cases to HIT section of this TSG.

 

Customer’s Account or Subscription Is Suspended, or the Customer Has Received an Error Message
  1. Check CMAT to see if HIT has suspended the account.
    Note: If it has not been suspended by HIT (as detailed below), continue to work the case as normal and assist the customer with their issue.
    • If HIT has suspended it, you should see one of the following indicators present in CMAT:
      • The Billing Account Status is Suspended. (See Account Information.)
      • The Subscription Entitlement Status is Suspended (Financial Fraud) (See Lifecycle and Status.)
      • The Activity Log shows Operation Name as Cancel Subscription, and the Agent Comment is Fraud.
  2. After confirming that one of the above indicators is present, transfer the entire case to HIT following the guidance in the Transferring Cases to HIT section of this document.

Customer Unable to Complete SMS Whitelisting

Customers may experience difficulty with the SMS validation process during sign-up, causing them to become blocked. This may happen for a number of reasons:

  • They do not have a phone capable of receiving SMS messages.
  • They are in a location where they are unable to receive an SMS message.
  • They receive a message stating the verification code they entered was incorrect.
  • They do not receive the verification SMS.
  • They receive a message stating Azure was unable to verify their account.
  • FTE's wanting to activate their MSDN subscription, but they are in China.

In these instances transfer the entire case to HIT following the guidance in the Transferring Cases to HIT section of this document.

Customer Has a Free Trial Policy Rejection

Customers are only allowed one Free Trial, IT Pro, or Dev Essentials subscription. If a customer has already signed up for any one of these in the past, they will be rejected.

  • To confirm that the customer has been rejected due to the Free Trial Policy, check the “Declines” section in CMAT for multiple “Free Trials.”
    • You may send these cases directly to HIT, following the Transferring Cases to HIT section below, without communicating with the customer first. HIT will review the case and allow an exception or uphold the policy. HIT will then communicate the outcome with the customer.

Customer Requests Access to a Closed Account to Retrieve Data

If a customer requests access to retrieve data from an account closed for fraud or Terms of Use (TOU) violations, and HIT has previously confirmed the account will remain closed, then do the following:

  1. Ask the customer to provide a detailed list of each of the services and deployments they need access to.
  2. Transfer the entire case to HIT following the guidance in the Transferring Cases to HIT section of this document.

Customer Is Unable to Do One of the Following: Sign up, Add a Payment Instrument, or Complete a Purchase

Check CMAT to see if the issue is because of a risk rejection.
Note: If the issue is not a risk rejection (as detailed below), continue to work the case as you normal and assist the customer with their issue.

  1. In the Declines section of CMAT, select All Transaction Types.
  2. Look for any lines with a Risk Decision value of Rejected.
  3. Expand the line item to see the rejected Reason Code. Check for the following:
    • If the Risk Decision is Rejected and the Reason Code indicates to Follow normal procedures for review, then you can transfer the entire case to HIT following the guidance in the Transferring Cases to HIT section of this document.

If the Risk Decision is Rejected and the Reason Code indicates “DO NOT ESCALATE” then do not escalate. Instead, advise the customer according to the message presented.
Example: If you see “DO NOT ESCALATE: VOIP numbers are not permitted for SMS verification. Customers should use a non-VOIP number.” Then do not escalate to HIT. Advise the customer to use a different (non-VOIP) phone number.

Engagement and Escalation Path

Reminder: Cases you assess as blocked from purchasing or the subscription is disabled (including TOU violations) and you see in CMAT that this is because of Fraud/Risk, you should Transfer the entire case to HIT by allowing Service Desk to choose the correct queue based on that SAP. These details are also outlined above in the Support Engineer Policy section. 

For all other instances follow the guidance below.

Creating Tasks

To engage the HIT team via a Collaboration task, follow these instructions.

  1. Gather the information needed to engage HIT.
    • HIT will not handle communications when you send tasks to them.
    • HIT only handles all communications if you have sent the entire case or SR (see Transferring Cases to HIT below).
  2. Create an escalation task in Service Desk and manually override the routing to send the task to the RiskOps queue.
    1. On the Tasks tile, click the +Escalation link.
    2. Select the Route by Area Path option button.
      Note: You can leave the other values as their defaults.
    3. Include the administrator’s email and the subscription ID in the Description field.
    4. Click Next.
    5. Click the Manual Override link.
    6. Search for (and select) the RiskOps Service Team queue.
    7. Click Next.
    8. Confirm that the queue name is the RiskOps Service Team queue.
    9. Click Submit.
    10. Click OK.
  3. No further action is needed on your part. The HIT team will own the interaction after this point. Note the following about the interaction after this point:
    • HIT will own all customer communications.
    • If HIT decides to re-enable the subscription, they will do the following:
      1. They will communicate to the customer that their subscription will be re-enabled.
      2. They will re-enable the subscription.
      3. They will close the whole case.
    • If HIT decides to not re-enable the subscription, they will do the following:
      1. They will communicate to the customer that their subscription will remain disabled.
      2. They will close the whole case.

Transferring Cases to HIT

  • If you receive a case where the customer has been blocked from purchasing or the subscription is disabled (including TOU violations) and you see in CMAT that this is because of Fraud/Risk, do the following :
    • Transfer the entire case to HIT by allowing Service Desk to choose the correct queue based on that Support Area Path (SAP). HIT will own and close the case following their team process.
    • In Service Desk, update the SAP to reflect the following: //Azure/Subscription Management/Account Review.
  • If you receive a case where there is no evidence of Fraud/Risk actions in CMAT, but you still need HIT’s consultation (such as fraudulent cc charges, account is compromised, or error while purchasing Visual Studio), do the following:
    • Send a Collaboration Task to the HIT team.
    • In Service Desk, update the SAP to reflect the following: //Azure/Subscription Management/Account Review

Note: If you determine that you will need to engage HIT in any of the following scenarios, do not contact the customer after a task has been sent to HIT.
HIT will handle all interactions after that point.

  • When communicating with the customer, it is okay to inform them that you are working with the Human Intelligence Team on their issue.
    However, under no circumstances should you do the following:
    • Tell the customer they were disabled for fraud.
    • Add another individual to an email thread with a customer.
      Example: During an investigation of unauthorized charges, the credit card owner may want to know the information of the person who used their credit card or may ask to have them added to the thread. Such a request is a violation of our privacy policy and might expose personally identifiable information (PII). In this scenario, the customer would be responsible for adding any additional people to the thread.
    • Include HIT directly in any communications with the customer.
    • Share any names, email addresses or phone numbers for HIT with the customer.
    • Redirect the customer to HIT.

EA, Partner, and Executive Escalations Only

After a task has been submitted to HIT, escalate to your TA for awareness and send a task to them with the summary of the issue.

Additionally:

  • For EA: Send a task to the Enterprise Agreement Team (EA queue MSaas Azure EA Portal Support) for awareness.
  • For Executives: Send an email to kimlftedr@microsoft.com and let them know that an executive escalation has occurred (along with the relevant case details.)

Coding and Closing Procedures

  • After customer confirmation, code and close the case using the guidance provided in TSG 2658158.
  • If HIT instructed you to end all further communication with the customer, code and close the case as Unresolved - Not Supported.
    This coding should be used in the following scenarios:
    • When HIT has confirmed that a customer’s account will remain closed.
    • When the customer has been flagged for violating TOU, such as attempting to sign up for multiple trials.
  • For cases related to Visual Studio Marketplace error messages, close the case using one of the following causes:
    • Root Cause - Azure Sub Mgmt V1\Bugs and Issues\Purchase Issues and bugs\Unable to Sign up/Purchase\Risk Issue – Rejected
    • Root Cause - Azure Sub Mgmt V1\Bugs and Issues\Purchase Issues and bugs\Unable to Sign up/Purchase\Risk Issue – Approved

  • The RISK section of the root cause classification tree is to be used only by the HIT team. Do not code and close your cases using the Root Cause- Azure Sub Mgmt V1\Risk\...
