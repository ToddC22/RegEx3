Summary

On April 2nd 2020, Gavriella Schuster has made a blog post, responding to COVID-19: Microsoft Partner Network update. Blog was updated on August 4th 2020, informing that Partners with AD between July 1 and December 31, 2020 are now eligible for the COVID-19 extension plan. The Global Admin or MPN partner admin will see information specific to their company in Partner Center by the end of August 2020.

In the blogpost the approach that Microsoft will be taking to offer Partners the support they need in the weeks and months to come was outlined. 

In alignment to what was shared in the aforementioned blogpost, please find bellow the comprehensive guide that enables you to understand who’s eligible for the adjustments made to programs, additional Partner facing resources and further Partner actions.  

Please note that for adjustments to Competencies and Azure Expert MSP programs more information will be offered during this month to our Partners so we highly encourage you to state the same to any Partners contacting us with questions related to this blog post or topics included in itself.

FAQ

FAQ specific to COVID-19 extension

Q. My competency anniversary date was in the January – June 2020 timeframe. Can we still renew our competency through this offer, without needing to meet the exam and performance requirements?

A. The offer is no longer available to partners with anniversary dates from January 1 – June 30, 2020.

Q. My competency anniversary date is between now and December 2020, and we are unable to meet competency requirements due to impacts related to COVID-19. What should we do?

A. If your competency anniversary date is between July 1 and December 31, 2020, we are extending your existing competency or competencies through your next anniversary date in 2021. You will not need to meet exam or performance requirements, but you will still pay the competency fee. You’ll see information about this specific to your organization in Partner Center.

Q. My competency is expiring in the next several weeks, and we are unable to meet exam and performance requirements due to impacts related to COVID-19. What should we do?

A. If your competency anniversary date is between July 1 and December 31, 2020, we are extending your existing competency or competencies through your next anniversary date in 2021. You will not need to meet exam or performance requirements, but you will still pay the competency fee. You’ll see information about this specific to your organization in Partner Center.

Q. My 30-day competency renewal timeframe is ending, and the offer isn’t available yet in Partner Center. What should we do?

A. If you are at the end of your 30-day renewal timeframe and not able to complete your renewal in Partner Center, we are further escalating your scenario internally for further considerations and will comeback with an analysis of your options once completed.
Call to action – Frontline team should escalate to MPN Global Support

Q. I have already renewed my competency in PC for the following enrollment year at my anniversary date - Am I still going to receive the extension mentioned in the blogpost?

A. If you already renewed your competency then there are no further actions available for you to perform in your account. We are further escalating your scenario internally for additional considerations and will comeback with an analysis of your options once completed.
Call to action – Frontline team should escalate to MPN Global Support

Q. We had a gold competency but renewed at silver because we were able to meet those requirements. Can we get our gold competency back?

A. If you already renewed your competency then there are no further actions available for you to perform in your account. We are further escalating your scenario internally for additional considerations and will comeback with an analysis of your options once completed.
Call to action – Frontline team should escalate to MPN Global Support

Q. How do I know whether my organization is eligible for the competency extension?

A. Your Global Admin or MPN partner admin will see information specific to your company in Partner Center by the end of August 2020.

Q. Do we have to pay the competency fee if we’re eligible for the competency extension?

A. Yes, you will still need to pay the competency fee.

Q. What do I have to do to get the competency extension?

A. If your organization is eligible, you’ll see information specific to your company in Partner Center on the Membership offers page by the end of August about how to take advantage of the extension. If you are at the end of your 30-day renewal timeframe and not able to complete your renewal in Partner Center, we are further escalating your scenario internally for additional considerations and will comeback with an analysis of your options once completed.
Call to action – Frontline team should escalate to MPN Global Support

Q. Our competency renewal timeframe has passed. What should we do?

A. If your organization is eligible, you’ll see information specific to your company in Partner Center about how to take advantage of the extension. If your renewal window has ended, you can reactivate your competency and regain access to resources and benefits by paying your fee. This extension is available to partners with anniversary dates between July 1 through December 31, 2020 If you take advantage of this offer after your renewal window has passed, you will have a new competency anniversary date.

Q. When will the Partner Contributions Indicator metrics (PCI) take effect for attaining the Cloud Business Applications competency?

A.  We have set a new date of April 1, 2021 for the new PCI-based requirements for the Cloud Business Applications competency to take effect. Along with that, we have postponed the Functional Consultant Growth metric baseline snapshot from March 31 to October 1, 2020. Learn more on the Microsoft partner website at https://partner.microsoft.com/membership/competencies#tab-content-2

Q. My organization has the Cloud CRM competency. Will that be included in this offer?

A. No, the Cloud CRM competency will retire as planned in October 2020. We recommend working toward attaining the Cloud Business Applications competency: https://partner.microsoft.com/membership/cloud-business-applications-competency

Q. Will I still get product licenses/IUR benefits with the competency extension?

A. Yes, you will pay the competency fee and activate your benefits. Please note that your tokens will not be extended. You will receive a new set of benefits. Information specific to your organization will be available in Partner Center by the end of August 2020. Review the partner programs guide for more information about what’s included with competencies.

Q. What happens with my IUR/product licenses when I renew or reactivate my competency?

A. Once you renew or reactivate your competency, you will have access to the resources and benefits associated with your competency, including product licenses. Note: Licenses and subscriptions will not automatically renew once you pay the competency fee, please go to the membership benefits tab to manage your benefits. Review the partner programs guide for more information about what’s included with competencies.

Q. Do the product licenses (“IUR”) that are included with a competency come with Microsoft Teams?

A. Yes, the product licenses included with your competency include Office 365 E3. Once your Office 365 E3 subscription has been activated, the subscription admin will be able to deploy Teams through the Teams admin center.

Q. What happens with my incentives when I renew or reactivate my competency?

A. Once you renew or reactivate your competency, you will have access to the resources and benefits associated with your competency, including incentives.

Q. I’m a partner with an ASfP agreement and I have been severely impacted by COVID-19. What should we do?

A. Please reach out to your Services Account Manager (SAM) to discuss.

Q. We can’t take the required exams to attain a new competency. What is Microsoft doing to address this?

A. The Worldwide Learning team recently published a blog post that explains the steps we’re taking to make it possible for partners to get the training and certifications they need while working remotely or from home. Read the blog post here.

Q. Some of the exams we have been studying for to meet competency requirements were set to retire on June 30, 2020. We weren’t able to take the exams by then. What should we do?

A. We have updated the retirement dates for these exams to January 31, 2021 to give you more time to take them. You’ll see the latest information about competency requirements in Partner Center and on the Microsoft partner website, and can find the latest information about certifications and exams on Microsoft Learn.

Q. We have applied for the Azure Expert MSP program, and I may not be able to complete the Spring 2020 Audit Checklist by June 30, 2020.

A. We are giving you more time, and the Spring 2020 Progress Audit Checklist will be due by September 30, 2020. The Fall Audit Checklist will take effect on October 1, 2020.

Q. We are a renewing Azure Expert MSP partner and need more time to complete the Spring 2020 Progress Audit Checklist.

A. If you are a renewing Azure Expert MSP partner with an audit date before June 30, your program anniversary date will be extended and you will be audited against the Spring 2020 Progress Audit Checklist and due on or before December 31, 2020.

· If you are an Azure Expert MSP partner due to renew between July 1 and December 31, 2020 against the Fall 2020 Progress Audit checklist, your program anniversary date will be extended to allow you to audit on or before December 31, 2020 and you will be audited against the Spring 2020 Progress Audit Checklist, not the Fall Progress Audit checklist.

· For both scenarios, the information about this specific to your organization, and the action you will need to take, will be available on Partner Center.

Q. What is Microsoft doing for partners participating in advanced specializations / the Azure Expert MSP program?

A. Please refer to our 2 April 2020 blog post for details about what we have implemented for partners participating in these programs.

Q. Are ISVs included in this competency extension?

A. All partners with competency anniversary dates from July 1 through December 31, 2020 are eligible for this offer.

Q. Do I need to do anything to get an extension on existing Deployment Planning Services and Software Assurance Training Vouchers?

A. These vouchers are typically valid for 180 days after date of creation. We’re extending active vouchers created prior to April 15, 2020 for 90 days. No action is required from partners or customers. Microsoft will adjust its records and systems to reflect this remote engagement allowance as well as the active voucher 90-day extension.

Q. What about Software Assurance Premium Support offers? Are they being extended?

A. There are no changes to the dates for support offers.

Q. We heard that Microsoft would begin enforcing the Microsoft Partner Agreement in April 2020. Is that still happening?

A. To help prevent disruption to partner and customer businesses, we are postponing the enforcement date until later in 2020 and will update you when we have set a new date. If you have not accepted the agreement, please do so as soon as possible.

Q. Are we extending payment terms for customers and partners for Cloud Solution Provider invoices?

A. We are listening to feedback from you, our partners, and considering what we can do across the board to help. We have no updates on payment terms and will continue to monitor your feedback.

Q. My Action Pack subscription is expiring, can I extend it?

A. No. If you have the CC option as method of payment, you must pay for the renewal membership.

FAQ for wrongly provisioned Incremental Benefits

Q. We recently assigned a Visual Studio subscription to a person in our organization, and now those subscriptions no longer show up in Partner Center. What should we do?

A. We recently uncovered an error in our data that resulted in some partners seeing competencies they did not attain, and the associated incremental benefits that they were not eligible for, in Partner Center. We’ve corrected the issue and the information you now see in the Benefits section of Partner Center accurately reflects your organization’s competencies and benefits. If you assigned an incremental subscription and it’s no longer available, please check your current Benefits to see if you have another Visual Studio subscription you can assign.

Q. Why were these Visual Studio subscriptions (or other licenses) available to me in Partner Center?

A. An error in our system resulted in some partners showing that they had completed competencies in Partner Center that they were not eligible for. The incremental benefits of those competencies – Visual Studio subscriptions and Azure credits in particular – were visible in Partner Center. We have corrected the error in our system.

Q. Why can’t we keep these extra benefits, if it was your error?

A. After reviewing the details of this situation, we made the decision to remove the incremental benefits if partners were not eligible to receive them. Rewarding extra benefits based on a Microsoft error is inconsistent with our program requirements and inequitable to partners who have otherwise attained these benefits.

Q. My organization needs Visual Studio licenses. How can we purchase more to add to our benefits?

A. The Visual Studio subscription that is included with competency attainment is roughly equivalent to the Visual Studio Enterprise Standard subscription. Learn more at https://docs.microsoft.com/visualstudio/subscriptions/program-mpn.
