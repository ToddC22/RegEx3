1. Overview

Microsoft competencies are certifications designed to help organizations demonstrate their Microsoft expertise to their current customers and provide credibility when prospecting new customers. Competencies provide a variety of benefits including Internal Use Software licenses (IURs), technical support, and sales and marketing resources. Partners must be enrolled in the MPN program prior to obtaining competencies.

2. Competencies View

Under the Competency tab, the Frontline advocates can access a broad view of the Partner’s competencies and the partner’s anniversary date (date when the competency will need to be renewed). This view is exactly what the partner sees in the Partner Center dashboard. The advocates can see the competencies attained by the partners and a brief description of those. The advocates should click the competency name to see detailed information about the partner’s competencies and statuses. 

Using this tab, the partner can check and verify competency levels are correctly reflected after migration. If partner does not remember what competencies they had before the migration to PC, this can be checked in PMC (which is now read-only but still available at https://partners.microsoft.com).

Migration of competencies can take up to 48 hours to fully reflect over to Partner Center.

3. Membership Renewal “At Risk” warning and reasons why it's triggered

If competencies are at risk, the partner will notice a yellow banner at the top, a triangle under the risk section and a message in the notification icon.

[Asset 4504939]

[Asset 4504940]

[Asset 4504941]

Reason why the “At Risk” warning is triggered

  • The competency has a requirement update which the partner does not meet
  • Microsoft Certified Professionals left the organization and their certifications no longer count for the organization’s competency achievements.
  • There are users that missed associating their Microsoft Account to Partner University and/or the Microsoft Certifications site, hence their exams/certifications/assessments are not counted. After transition to Partner Center, MCPs need to perform Microsoft Learning profile mapping. If anniversary date is close, it might be received the above email notification email until the respective competency criteria for targeted level is met.
  • Systemic issues: performance suddenly drops to 0, which automatically triggers the At Risk notification (Please check article 4505016 for proofs to escalate performance issues)

4. Skills Report

During the preparation for attaining a competency, Global Admin or MPN Partner Admin have the possibility to analyze who has completed an exam / certification / assessment and check the progress against the requirements of the competencies by accessing ‘Competencies’ Tab and then ‘Download skills report’.

How to download the skills report

Partners can download skills report from the Competencies page within their PC Dashboard, in the right upper corner.

Frontline advocates can download the skills report from Prose.

When the file is downloaded and opened, partner will see a table containing the details below.
Note: When a user associates their “Microsoft Exams and Certifications” and / or “Microsoft training & assessments” for the first time or completes new skills- it will take up to 72hours for the skills to be displayed in the Skills Report.

[Asset 4555298]

How to read the skills report

1. If your users are currently only associated to your PMC account, make sure that they complete their association in Partner Center.

  • If the same user completed both exams and assessments valid for a specific competency, but only associated either the Learning account or the Partner University account in PC, the system will not recognize the user as meeting the competency requirements unless he associates the remaining Learning or Partner University account in Partner Center.

2. Only skills relevant to the competencies and programs of Partner Center are shown in this report (some retired skills may be shown for 12 months or more after retirement). Make sure you check the requirements under competencies in Partner Center or select the competency on the Microsoft Partner Network portal. Unless an exam / assessment / certification is specifically mentioned as part of the requirements listed under the competency, it will not be considered towards meeting the competency.

3. If there are no skills shown for a user then the user may be associated in Partner Center but he has not gained the relevant skills or he has merged their skills to a MCP ID that isn’t associated in Partner Center. If user has duplicate accounts, required skills may split into two or more accounts (applicable for Microsoft Learning) For merging MCP accounts to resolve the issue, Partner himself needs to contact the designated team here.

If partner has the MSA tied to an MCID in the skills report and on the certification of transcript the same MSA has mentioned another MCID, the advice is to request merging the MCID from the transcript into the MCID already displayed in the skills report. Once the MCP merge confirmed, partner will not have to re-associate his MSA, as is already associated in PC. Once MCP id merge is confirmed, allow 24 hours to be reflected in Partner Center.

Possible escalated scenarios for MCPs and skills report

1. Names associated to an MCP are missing from skills report

Currently, in case an admin user is managing multiple tenants of the same company (see article 4495700 for more details), in the skills report they will only see the names of the MCPs associated to the selected tenant, hence the column "Name" will be blank for MCPs belonging to another tenant of the same company. For seeing the names of MCPs associated to another tenant of the same company, the admin must select that different tenant and download again the Skills report. There is no negative impact upon competency evaluation.

2. Error when downloading skills report

If from partner’s end, when trying to download the skill report, he sees the message “No users found with skills data. Check to see whether users have associated their Learning or Partner University accounts with their profiles”, the partner should check if the MCPs associated their Learning or Partner University accounts within their profiles in PC as described on the article 22966380 Link or unlink an MCP ID to or from a partner organization.

[Asset 4504945]

3. Empty skills report downloaded from Prose In this scenario, users didn’t associate their Learning or Partner University accounts with their profiles. If the exams/assessments completed by the users are not relevant for any of the competencies requirements within Partner Center, the message “NotPassedAnyRelevantSkills”will appear in the skills report.

[Asset 4555117]

[Asset 4555299]

4. MCPs who left organization but are still displayed in the skills report

If partner claims that on the skills report are displayed users who used to be part of the organization but they left and the Global Admin does not want to have them reflected in the skills report due to internal reason (example: audit), although they appear only as associated in PMC and not in PC, Frontline advocate must  escalate the case to MPN Global Support for removing the user from the skills report.

6. Scenarios from partners claiming “missing” exams/assessments from the competency requirements

Frontline advocate should encourage partners to check the MS partner portal to see if any of his completed skills has been retired. Explore Retired certifications and Retired exams. The retired skills will be considered only for 12 more months after the retirement date.

Frontline advocate should encourage partners to check the Training gallery page to compare if the skills completed are also mentioned as requirements relevant for the competencies in Partner Center. Currently, in Partner Center we only recognize certification from two sources, i.e. Microsoft Learning and Partner University. Any other certificate taken from any other sources (Example: OpenedX) is not considered.

Example 1:  Partner claims he should be shown as qualified for Cloud Platform competency in the skill report and presents the below assessment.

The presented assessment is not from the accepted two sources, so it’s not counting for attaining the competency requirements, as it is taken from OpenedX- which can be noticed also at the very bottom of the assessment. The assessment provided MS azure241x Azure Fundamentals is not included in the list of required assessments to attain the competency. Following the Training gallery page can be seen that the description mentions being an Assessment Preparation which provides fundamental level knowledge about Microsoft Azure. This is optional, not mandatory. The mandatory one is the Technical Assessment for Cloud Platform (for example).

Frontline advocate should inform partner about the necessity of completing one of the assessments/exams specified on both MS partner portal and his own PC Dashboard.

7. Proofs to include in the escalations in case of missing exams/assessments from the competency requirements

For competency requirements related to exams/certifications/assessments not recognized by Partner Center, Frontline advocate should provide:

  • Partner Center User report (which can be exported by the AccountAdmin or MpnPartnerAdmin from User Management) or screenshot provided by user from “My profile” showing the completed association.

8. Escalation process

Frontline advocates must refer to article 4033695 PMC to Partner Center Migration: How to escalate cases. Cases dispatched without respecting the escalation template will be dispatched back by MPN Global Support team to the owning team.

Scenario Frontline call to action
For errors while downloading skills report/user report Frontline advocate must escalate the case to MPN Global Support team, including relevant proofs (PSR)
For assets relevant for the competencies that are not displayed in the skills report although MCP associated or are displayed in the skills report but the status is not reflected as “qualified”
 
 Frontline advocate must escalate the case to MPN Global Support team with relevant details described in section 7. Proofs to include in the escalations in case of missing exams/assessments requirements
Global Admin requires for a user who left organization to be removed from the skills report, as it still shows that user as "Associated in PMC "
 
 Frontline advocate must escalate the case to MPN Global Support for removing the user from the skills report
MCP claims he passed the relevant skills, but the MCID from the transcript is different than the one from the skills report, although the MSA is the same

Frontline advocate must advise Partner to contact himself the designated team here to request a MCP id merge.
