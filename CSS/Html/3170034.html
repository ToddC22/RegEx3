What is this article about?

This article describes the requirements and steps for setting up monthly invoices. Monthly invoice billing is a post-pay payment method available only to qualified advertisers. Qualified advertisers are issued an SAP ID to be used as the payment method in the account and will need to complete insertion orders to enable ad delivery (see KB 3175371 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Insertion Orders for more information).

1. Data security requirements

Data Transfer Management must be used to collect/send sensitive documentation to/from customers (for more information, see KB 4056786 Microsoft Advertising: Data Transfer Management). Avoid gathering and relaying any personal data via email (even via capattach@microsoft.com) and use a Data Transfer Management secure workspace whenever possible. If email must be used to relay sensitive customer information, be sure to lock all files with the XID as the password.

2. SAP/credit line setup process

This process should be used for new SAP setup requests and changes to existing credit lines that require a secondary assessment of risk (for example, credit limit increase or sold-to conversion to bill-to). Other minor administrative changes like address or accounts payable contacts may go directly to Global Financial Services (GFS), also known as Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS), and do not require a Customer Setup Form.

Before getting started, ensure the customer is properly fortified (or manually authenticated by establishing contact with the requestor via the email domain or phone number published on the advertiser’s website). This will help to ensure the requestor is not using legitimate business information to establish a fraudulent credit line.

Important: If additional documentation is required for the customer to proceed with credit approval, use KB 4056786 Microsoft Advertising: Data Transfer Management to securely acquire sensitive customer information, such as financial statements.

  1. A self-serve feature in UI for Monthly Invoice application will be available for customers beginning February 2020. If the customer does not see this option, CSS must first confirm eligibility and requirements for SAP/New Credit Line Setup and either enable into pilot OR proceed with completing CSF on behalf of customer. See section 2.1 for more details on eligibility requirements before proceeding with manual steps below.
  2. Complete the CSF with the customer. See section 2.2 for more details.
  3. Ensure the CSF passes validation. See section 2.3 for more details.
  4. Submit the CSF form to GFS (also known as WOCS). See section 2.4 for more details.
  5. Once the credit line is approved, Upgrade the CID to premium shell type and create eIOs to prevent disruption to delivery. See section 2.5 for more details.
  6. Set the SAP as the payment method. See section 2.6 for more details.

2.1​​​​ Eligibility and requirements for​ SAP/New Credit Line Setup, Sold-To to Bill-To Conversion, and Add SAP to new CID (CSF submission is required but customer will not be put through credit checks again)

A credit check must be passed before the account can be enabled for monthly invoice billing.

Ensure the advertiser meets eligibility requirements (spend and registration requirements effective February 20th, 2018):

General requirements

Notes

Advertiser meets minimum Microsoft Advertising age and spend history requirements.

The company that requests invoice setup must have advertised as the Bill-to party on Microsoft Advertising for at least 12 months and must meet the minimum spend requirement in at least three of the 12 months.

For agencies, aggregate client spend can be used to determine minimum spend eligibility.

Exceptions to the 12 month spend history may be made if the customer meets the three months spend criteria and is willing to provide financial statements or a security deposit.

Spend Minimums:

US and Canada: $5000 USD/CAD per month.

Other Mature Markets: $3000 USD Equivalent (Includes Australia, Western Europe: Belgium, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, Luxembourg, Portugal, Spain, and the United Kingdom).

Emerging Markets: $2000 USD Equivalent (Includes BR, NL, the APAC Region, the LATAM region, the CEE region - Central and Eastern Europe, and the MEA region - Middle East and Africa).

The company is registered on the regional site for their place of business.

Conduct a general search on regional validation sites to verify registration in the company’s place of business. The company must be registered to proceed.

 

The advertiser’s URL is functional, informative, and adheres to editorial policies.

The website meets the following requirements before requesting monthly invoice billing:

  • Contains information about the business. For example:
    • Nature of business
    • Name/Address of business
    • Available products/services
    • Registered Company information (Including reference to the legal trading name/address)
  • All links must be functional and direct users to the correct and expected page. For example, not redirecting them to a different site or back to the homepage.
  • Includes a Privacy Policy
  • Includes Terms and conditions

Advertiser can be billed and pay in a supported currency.

See Choosing a payment method for Microsoft Advertising and What you need to know about monthly invoice billing for more information on how to pay for Microsoft Advertising.

 

Advertiser can be billed on standard net terms.

See section 2.1.1 for more on net terms.

Advertiser agrees to standard credit limit and/or understands they will need to provide a deposit.

Minimum $1K USD or market-equivalent (value required will vary based on customers assessed risk level and expected spend).

GFS (also known as WOCS) may provide tentative approval of the credit line pending receipt of a security deposit.

See additional requirements under Credit line in section 2.2, and set expectations with the advertiser, if they request a non-standard credit limit, financial statements (see section 5) or a security deposit may be required (see section 6).

The company’s website URL must match the legal company name.

Business justification must be provided if different.

Microsoft Advertising requirements

Notes

The account is not already set up for monthly invoicing.

Check whether there is an SAP payment method available in the customer shell. Confirm with the advertiser whether they have any other logins that could already have invoicing established. Work with them to consolidate logins appropriately.

If the customer already has an SAP ID, it can be entered in more than one customer shell. Complete the setup process to request the SAP be added to a new CID (credit check not required).

Requestor can demonstrate association to the business.

The requestor must be a fortified Super Admin on the account or otherwise accessible through contact information provided on the website (email domain or phone). Note: If the customer was fortified or manually authenticated in the ticket.

The advertiser has an existing CID/XID that can be upgraded.

At least one threshold account must be present in the account for GFS (also known as WOCS) to add SAP as a post-pay payment method in the shell. Create one without a payment method, if needed.

There are no egregious issues on the Microsoft Advertising account.

Advertiser is not on credit hold, flagged with fraud, and has no QBR penalties within the past year.

Declined threshold payments due to transaction limits imposed by financial institutions or credits/rebill will be considered by SMEs on a case by case basis.

The company's address in Microsoft Advertising matches the legal company address on the CSF and all official company documents (for example, financial statements).

The legal address must be the same in the Microsoft Advertising shell as is on the CSF (if adding the SAP to another customer shell, the legal address must also be consistent with the existing SAP record).

The company's name in Microsoft Advertising is consistent with their legal company name.

The customer's name in Microsoft Advertising must be consistent but does not have to match exactly since businesses may have more than one customer ID.

As a best practice, Accounts Payable contacts should be a valid user on the account.

At least one threshold account must be present in the account for GFS (also known as WOCS) to add SAP as a post-pay payment method in the shell. Create one without a payment method, if needed.

An AP user in the account ensures their contact information is valid and can be authenticated in any future interactions.

If a user for an AP contact cannot be established on the account, confirm the AP contact's association to the business with the requestor and ensure the requestor is aware that email authentication is required within the next five days.

If no AP contact is designated for the client accounts, the primary contact must be the super admin of the agency whose email matches the agency/bill-to company domain.

For Client/Sold-To setup, it is ideal to establish AP contacts as users on the client accounts for authentication purposes.

2.2 Complete the internal Customer Setup Form (CSF)

Note: As of February, eligible customers may be opted in to UI invoice onboarding to self-serve this application process directly with MSNCRED in the UI. Directions below remain only if there is a desire to manually submit a request.

If possible, complete the internal Customer Setup Form (CSF) v. 2.16 while speaking with the advertiser.

If necessary, pre-populate the customer-facing Invoice Billing Setup document and request the customer complete the form and host it in the DTM link for the support ticket.

After you receive the completed Customer Facing CSF from the customer, use the information to fill out the internal CSF. Instructions for how to fill out the CSF are below.

The CSF is divided into five parts. Guidance for filling out each part is below:
  1. Customer set-up
    • Date Requested - Enter today's date
    • Request Type - Specify if this is a new credit line request or existing credit line increase
    • Microsoft Advertising Customer ID - Enter the CID in which the SAP must be entered
    • Business Segment - Enter the customer’s segment (“Scale”)
  2. Legal identity
    • Region - Enter the region of the legal address (APAC/EMEA/LATAM/NA)
    • Legal Structure - (Partnership, Corporation, Limited Liability, or Sole Proprietor. See descriptions below)
      • Partnership - Typically found in professional services such as accountants, lawyers, doctors, or dentists where the partners can share expertise and skills. Partnerships compromise two or more people and any profits, debts, and decisions related to the business are shared.
      • Corporation - Owned by shareholders who each put an amount of money into a central pool. This pool of capital is then added to by borrowing from other forms of finance. Directors run the business on behalf of shareholders, and they all receive a portion/share of the profit.
      • Limited Liability - Intended to benefit professional partnerships such as lawyers or doctors. Offer the business a form of protection for company shareholders and limited partnerships. For these individuals, the maximum sum they can lose from a business is the sum of money they invested in the company.
      • Sole Proprietor - Simplest and most common type of small business. The sole proprietor is responsible for everything the business does. The owner has no separation of his/her own assets and liabilities which means he/she is personally responsible for any debts that the business has.
    • SAP type – Most requests will be for Bill-To SAP types. Historically, both a Bill-To and Sold-To SAP were required for Agencies (used to establish an SAP ID for an agency client when the agency is the Bill-To), but the notion of a Sold-To is being deprecated (March 2020 timeline) in favor of using ABL for Sold-To details. Until that time, Advertiser/Clients can upgrade Sold-To SAPs to Bill-To at any time (CSF submission to GFS (also known as WOCS) is required).
    • Company Full Legal Name
    • Microsoft Advertising Customer Name
    • Company URL
    • Legal Address
    • Mailing Address
    • Accounts Payable Contacts (1 or 2) - Accounts payable contacts are responsible for authorizing payment to Microsoft. Specify the Accounts Payable (AP) contact(s). The AP email address(es) or domain should match with the customer’s company name/website. If it does not, please ask the customer to explain the reason for this difference in domain name and provide a justification for the difference within the notes of the CSF/submission.

If the AP contact domain does not match the company name, the AP contact must be manually authenticated either by:

  1. Add the AP user as a contact on the account (preferred for long-term authentication),
  2. Being looped into the email conversation with the fully authenticated requestor and Microsoft Advertising Support specialist, or,
  3. The Support Specialist must be able to call the Contact Us phone number on the website and successfully be connected to the designated AP contact.
    Specify in the notes of the CSF what method was used to authenticate the AP contact. Note: Only valid Microsoft Advertising users are eligible for OneVet bypass.

Tax information

  • VAT (Value-Added Tax) Registration Status - There are certain EMEA countries (listed in Why we need your business location (and maybe VAT information) ) that are subject to VAT. If the customer is not located in one of these countries, select ‘Not VAT Registered’.
  • VAT registration number - If the customer resides in a VAT country, a valid VAT registration number is required OR they can provide a certification of incorporation as a non-profit or financial institution.
  • VAT exempt - Specify whether the customer is exempt from VAT tax. If the customer is VAT exempt, a VAT Exception Certificate must be provided.

    Note: Failure to provide a valid VAT ID or Exception Certificate for customers located in a VAT market will result in the customer being charged value added tax (VAT).

If they insist on invoice billing, they will be required to pay a deposit matching the amount that they are requesting for a credit line. The minimum is $1K USD. For the entire time they are advertising with Microsoft Advertising, Microsoft will retain this deposit.

2.4 Submit the CSF form to GFS (also known as WOCS)

You may choose to leverage BOS/AES to complete the fraud review and submission to GFS (also known as WOCS).

2.4.1 For new credit line requests, submit the completed CSF to Fraud for review (CSF MUST NOT be emailed)

For new credit line requests, the completed CSF must first be submitted to Fraud for risk review.

  1. The Subject of the ticket sent to FraudAds must specify new invoice setup risk review. Complete issue-specific details for Issue Category: Fraud Escalations; Issue Type: Risk Review for Monthly Invoice Setup.
  2. Relay the CSF to the fraud team in one of the following ways:
    • Password protect the CSF and attach it to the child ticket (either via CAP or capattach email).
    • Create a DTM workspace for the child ticket and load the CSF. Ensure the DTM workspace link is in the ticket and refer the Fraud team to access the SFC in the workspace.
  3. Only when the request is cleared by Fraud should the CSF be submitted to GFS (also known as WOCS) for a credit check. If the customer fails the Fraud risk review, leverage fraud status closure messaging to communicate the outcome to the customer as needed, see KB 3169386 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Fraud Status.

2.4.2 Submit the Completed CSF to GFS (also known as WOCS)

  1. Submit a ticket to GFS (also known as WOCS) via the UCMT escalation process. Issue Category: Billing & Payments Escalations; Issue Type: SAP/Credit Line Request.
  2. Access the UCMT ticket and attach the following, as appropriate:
    • Completed CSF.
    • VAT certificate.
    • Financial documents for customers in APAC.
    • Any other documentation required by GFS (also known as WOCS) to approve the credit line.
  3. GFS (also known as WOCS) will provide confirmation that the request was received and will proceed with Accounts Payable contact verification through the OneVet tool.

    OneVet Authentication: AP contact should expect to receive the verification email within 24 hours of escalating to     GFS (also known as WOCS), who will confirm when the email is sent. The verification email will arrive from the MSNCred@microsoft.com alias and will trigger in the language selected for the invoice setup in the CSF. The verification link in the email is valid for five days. GFS (also known as WOCS) may close their ticket if the AP contact does not authenticate within 24 hours of receiving the email. Reopen the UCMT ticket with GFS as necessary. Any concerns regarding the delivery of the OneVet authentication email or completing the authentication steps should be directed to GFS (also known as WOCS) in the existing UCMT ticket. OneVet is used globally for all unmanaged advertisers.
  4. Once the AP contact is verified, the SLA for GFS (also known as WOCS) to complete the review and establish the SAP with credit line is six business days.
  5. GFS (also known as WOCS) may request additional information or a deposit from the customer.
    1. If financial statements are requested, use Data Transfer Management to acquire the documents from the customer (see KB 4056786 Microsoft Advertising: Data Transfer Management). Once received, attach them directly to the GFS (also known as WOCS) ticket and send an email update to the ticket to inform the owner the documents were added.
    2. If a deposit is required, send the customer the Security Deposit Agreement (updated with the correct Microsoft bank information). See section 6, Security Deposit, below for templates and,
      • Refer the customer to Choosing a payment method for Microsoft Advertising for additional guidance on their deposit.
      • Request the customer confirm when the deposit was sent.
    3. Notify GFS (also known as WOCS) when the customer confirms that the deposit was submitted and GFS will confirm when the credit line is approved, and the SAP is propagating. It takes 13 hours for the new SAP to become available for addition to the Microsoft Advertising wallet.
    4. If the customer does not pass the credit check, communicate the reasons or criteria that disqualified the advertiser and move forward to offer setup of credit card billing as needed.

2.6 Set the SAP payment method

Once GFS (also known as WOCS) confirms the SAP is added to the account, set the new SAP as the payment method on the appropriate accounts and ensure all accounts have eIOs are submitted to prevent disruption to delivery.

To switch a prepay account to monthly invoice, you must first switch the prepay account to threshold.

2.6.1 Switch the Payment Option from prepay to postpay
Once the SAP ID is available for selection as the payment method, you can proceed with changing the payment option from prepay to postpay.

  • Advise the customer that a credit card will be required to temporarily switch the account to threshold billing to upgrade to monthly invoice billing. Work with the customer to add a credit card to their payment methods tab.

    Messaging:

“At this time, it is not technically possible to upgrade directly from prepay to monthly invoice billing. While this functionality is being developed, I have a workaround that can be used to retain your performance history, but it does require you to temporarily add a credit card as the payment method on the account until I can finish the upgrade. We can temporarily pause your campaigns and immediately remove the card and complete your transition to monthly invoicing, so you won’t accrue any charges.”

  • The prepay balance will be automatically refunded if the advertiser paid with a credit card or PayPal and switched from prepay to postpay threshold.

    Note: If the payment method is wire, check the Boleto or SEPA. The prepay balance will need to be refunded offline. SEPA refunds should not be processed unless they are eight weeks old. Otherwise, the customer will need to increase the negative balance with a new payment to continue using the account.
  • Switch the payment option from prepay to postpay threshold:
  1. Access accounts and billing – Account details and edit the payment settings.
  2. Change the payment option dropdown to switch to postpay.
  3. Select the payment method (debit or credit) that will be temporarily associated with the account and select switch.

Return to top

2.6.2 Switch the payment option from Threshold to monthly invoice
If a postpay advertiser is switching to invoice billing the system will attempt a swipe for the amount owed before switching the account to invoice billing. If the swipe is unsuccessful, the system will not allow the switch. The error message will let the user know the payment failed.

  • Access accounts and billing – Account details page and edit the payment settings.
  • Change the payment option dropdown to monthly invoice.
  • Change the payment method dropdown to the correct SAP and save.

2.6.3 Help the advertiser set up an eIO/Manual IO

An insertion order is a signed document or electronic setting submitted on behalf of or by the advertiser that specifies how much the account can spend over a designated time frame. Microsoft Advertising will not allow spend beyond the IO budget or end date.　

Electronic IO (eIO)
Electronic IOs are the fastest method for creating an insertion order. Only Super Admin and API User roles can perform this action. The customer must be listed as the Bill-to party on the account. See How do I create and edit an insertion order? for customer-facing steps to create and manage insertion orders.
Customer eIOs, in general, will be auto-approved. A small subset of IOs may still require manual approval from an internal user and will continue to be subject to manual approval SLAs.

Manual IO
If the advertiser is unable or unwilling to submit an eIO, frontline advocates may request BOS generate the manual Insertion Orders: submit the request through a child ticket to BOS if needed.

BOS should draft and attach the manual IOs that can be delivered to the customer.

Non-standard terms/IOs are not supported for unmanaged customers. If the customer sends you a modified version of the IO where any language has been changed, let the customer know that we only accept standard IOs.

2.6.4 Manual IO quality checklist

Confirm that:

Standard security deposits are calculated as three times the total requested credit limit and are held for up to six months, to be returned upon confirmation of a good payment history. Good payment history is defined as having all non-disputed invoices paid on/before the due date for a minimum of six months.

7. SAP IDs

SAP is a billing application used to keep track of amounts owed and paid by monthly invoice advertisers. SAP IDs are created for unique Company Name + Address combinations. All address updates must be conveyed to GFS (also known as WOCS) so that they can make the proper changes in SAP.

  • SAP IDs will not be issued without being qualified and providing customer information.
    • Customers can self-serve in UI, if the option is available to them OR
    • CSS can qualify customer and submit a CSF to MSNCRED (on behalf of the customer)
  • An SAP ID may be classified as either Bill-To (with an associated credit line) or Sold-To (no credit line).

Note: With the planned deprecation of Sold-To customer ID’s by March 2020, only a Bill-To will be required. All Sold-To information will be pulled from ABL details in the near future).

  • It is possible that the same SAP ID is used for search and some display advertisers, and therefore may already exist for customers that are new to search.
  • SAP IDs are created and may be entered an existing customer shell.
  • One Bill-To SAP ID may be used in more than one CID, and
  • One CID may contain multiple Bill-To SAP IDs if there is any agency association.

Bill-To: Specifies who pays the bill. An advertiser who has a Bill-To (no hold) SAP has been approved for a credit line with Microsoft Advertising.

ABL (formerly Sold-To): Specifies who services are provided for. An advertiser with a Sold-To SAP cannot be the Bill-To on the invoice because he/she does not have credit approval. Sold-to setup is typically used for clients of agencies where the agency is the Bill-To.

Beginning February 2020, the requirement for SAP Sold-To will refer to ABL details.  

Application for an SAP ID’s going forward are based on Bill-To information only.

Subsidiary SAPs: SAP IDs are created for unique Company Name + Address combinations; therefore, subsidiaries may have their own SAP ID even if the Bill-To address is the same as the parent. Subsidiary SAP IDs can be established if there is some public identifier that the subsidiary legally exists.

Agency SAPs: If an agency would like to create an additional account under their shell for themselves and already has an existing No Hold SAP ID, the agency's existing No Hold SAP ID may be used as the Sold-To if the SAP address matches the account.

8. Billing cycle and invoices

8.1 Invoice/Document rendering (including credit memos)

Monthly invoices are generated and available as PDFs in the UI by the second of each month and are sent via postal mail afterward (for those accounts not enrolled in paperless invoicing). The billing and due dates (as specified by term agreement) are specified on the invoice.

Invoices detail charges, tax, and payment remittance instructions for the bill-to advertiser. Invoices are calculated by adding up the billing activity for all ad groups during the invoice period for Microsoft Advertising. We do not have flexibility for off-cycle invoice and credit-note printing.

If you or advertisers have questions about invoices or payments that have been made, refer the customer to the regional customer service alias on their invoices or escalate the ticket via their regional GFS (also known as WOCS) Collections team in UCMT.

Credit memos: A credit memo is a document generated at the end of a billing cycle because of an activity adjustment on a monthly invoice account. The Monthly invoice for the period will still reflect the full amount of spend generated and the advertiser will need to specify in their payment remittance, how they would like the credit memo (with document ID) to be used against unpaid invoices.

9.1 Credit hold

As a customer's account(s) risks approaching their applied credit limit (limit is only visible to internal users) users will be notified via email and UI alerts that payment must be made immediately to avoid going on credit hold. The current unpaid balance and historic balance outstanding (as derived from SAP) will be totalled and compared against the customer's credit limit to determine whether the customer should be placed on credit hold.

Once the credit limit is fully utilized, the customer will either need to pay down their outstanding balance or request an increase in their credit limit.

If the advertiser isn't sure how much is due or requires a payment plan, direct the advertiser to contact the alias on their invoice for further assistance. Depending on the length and severity of the delinquency, a payment plan may not be possible and/or the credit hold may be permanent.

Invoice advertisers will receive a notification that they’re nearing their credit limit at approximately 14 days based on their current run rate. These email and UI notifications will continue as the limit is approached.

Return to top

9.2 Notifications to advertisers and UCM alerts

 

All credit lines will be reviewed at the time of customer notification to determine if a customer is eligible for a credit limit increase. Customers with consecutive months of successful payments made in full and on time are more likely to be eligible for these courtesy increases.

If the customer continues to receive notification that they're nearing their credit limit, it means that it's unlikely they are eligible for an increase and must pay to avoid going dark.

An email will be generated to the customer and there will also be a critical alert surfaced in UCM when:

  • Customer is going to hit credit limit in (approximately) 14 days or less.
    • The 14-day estimate is based on the advertiser’s current run rate. A run rate is calculated by the average daily spend over the past seven days.
    • Account Run Rate = Sum (total spend of the last seven days) divided by seven.
    • Advertisers may go dark sooner.
  • Customer has hit the credit limit.
  • Customer is put on hold due to reaching the credit limit.

9.3 Monthly invoice payment applied, account still on hold

If payment has been collected and the account is current, the credit hold should typically be lifted within two business days of receipt of payment. However, GFS (also known as WOCS) regularly assesses customers risk level and reserves the right to review and refine SAP invoicing eligibility at any time.

If the credit hold has not been lifted after payment is received, gather and submit payment details (for example, payment amount, when and how it was sent, or how they want it applied) to the regional Collections team in UCMT to confirm that the payment has been received and if the hold has been lifted. If the hold has been lifted by the accounts receivable team but persists in the Microsoft Advertising UI, escalate to Global Support for further assistance.

Messaging:

“Can you send me a copy of the check you sent or the transaction number from your online statement? I will check with our billing department to ensure the payment was received and that your account hold will be lifted.”

When a customer does not pay, it's possible their debt will be written off after a certain amount of time. These debts may show as 'paid' in the UI, even though they were not paid by the advertiser, thus the credit hold will persist. If a credit hold persists unexpectedly and the customer desires to use the same (invoice) or new payment method, contact the appropriate GFS (also known as WOCS) Risk team to request the following:

  • Define the customers intentions (to pay an old/outstanding debt, to lift SAP hold, to lift CID hold to allow use of another payment option).
  • Request itemized details of outstanding debt.

GFS (also known as WOCS) Risk teams should facilitate internal transfer of tickets to their appropriate teams (for example, Risk or Credit) when necessary. However, if you find there is not adequate traction around a hold being lifted, you can add the escalation alias: MSAHOLD@microsoft.com.
