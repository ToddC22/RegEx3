Procedure: Microsoft account: Customer cannot regain access to account

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer

What is this article about?

If a customer is unable to regain access to an account after you have walked the customer through the recovery process, the only option left is to create a new account. This article will help you inform a customer that they should create a new account and what you can do to help.

This article is only for use when a customer is in one of the following scenarios and you are not able to resolve their problem after following the steps in the linked articles corresponding to that scenario:

Account has been deleted due to inactivity

KB 2979990 Procedure: Microsoft account: Handling "That Microsoft account doesn't exist" error

Customer has forgotten their account name

KB 2979990 Procedure: Microsoft account: Handling "That Microsoft account doesn't exist" error

Account was compromised

KB 2980022 Procedure: Microsoft account: Help us secure your account

KB 3123259 Procedure: Microsoft account: The "Recover your Microsoft account" form

KB 930018 Procedure: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account

Customer forgot their password

KB 2984712 Procedure: Microsoft account: Password reset

KB 2984706 Procedure: Microsoft account: Handling "Sign-in is blocked" contacts

KB 3123259 Procedure: Microsoft account: The "Recover your Microsoft account" form

*Two-Step Verification account has lost access to all security info

KB 2984708 Procedure: Microsoft account: Two-step verification (TSV) contacts

*Note: When two-step verification was set for the accounts, you cannot use "Recover your Microsoft account" form.

1. What to say

1.1 Keep empathy in mind

Be empathetic!

When the customer has tried everything to get back into an account, suggesting it’s time to give up and create a new account can add to the customer’s frustration. Keep in mind that the customer may not just lose a favorite account name, but is also losing access to important email, photos, files, apps, games, subscription services, or even music and videos. So, above all else, remain empathetic.

1.2 What you can say

Talking points:

  • Microsoft takes account security very seriously.
  • We are always watching for suspicious activity.
  • If we see any, we validate it's really you.

We do this by:

  • Using a phone or email you entered previously.
  • Sending a code and asking you to enter it

Once we know it's you, we have you change your password for additional safety.

You should review what steps have been taken to try and regain access to an account. After doing so, the following is a suggested script that you can choose to follow or use as a basis for this difficult discussion:

"I understand your frustration, but we’ve covered everything you can do to recover your account. Please understand that Microsoft takes your security very seriously, but to protect customer privacy we can’t do anything to an account beyond helping customers verify their ownership through the steps we’ve already tried.

I encourage you to keep trying to recover your account using the methods we’ve tried if you remember any new details that might help, but it may be time to consider creating a new account instead. If you would like, I would be happy to help you create a new account and give you some tips that will help prevent this from happening again.

Would you like to do that now?"
  • If the customer asks for help creating a new account, skip to section 2.
  • If the customer asks you to keep trying, or demands an escalation:

"I understand your frustration but there is nothing else we can do until you are able to verify that you are the account owner through the methods we covered."
  • If the customer asks if there is some way to access their old data, subscriptions, or services with a new account:

"If you ever shared your OneDrive files or folders with anyone else, you might be able to access those through that person. Otherwise, we cannot access or grant access to any email or data you have stored in our services and we cannot transfer ownership of any subscriptions or services."
  • If the customer asks about a subscription they are still being charged for on their old account:

"We cannot cancel subscriptions nor remove the payment instrument when we cannot verify the account owner. Instead, please report unauthorized use of a payment instrument to your financial institution and work with them to put the account on a hold and potentially have a new card issued if it has been compromised.”

See KB 2885545 Procedure: Handling a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner for more information.

2. Help the customer set up a new and more resilient account

If the customer agreed to set up a new account and would like your help, follow the steps and associated tips below, to help them set up an account that is in good standing and will help prevent future support contacts and issues.

  1. Have the customer go to account.microsoft.com and begin filling out the form using some of the attached notes in the image below to guide the customer.[Asset 4529955]
    • Skip this step if the customer used an existing email address as their account name or already did this step.
    • If the customer didn't use an existing email account name or just wanted to create a new email account, follow these steps to create one and then return to the previous step for help with the rest of the form.

      Create a new account using a new email address:
      [Asset 4074831]

      [Asset 4074832]

      [Asset 4074833]

      Create a new account using a phone number:
      [Asset 4074838]


      Notes      :

    • If the phone number the customer wants to use has already been used to create a Microsoft account, they will need to use an email account.

    • If the customer needs help creating a strong password, see How to create a strong password for your Microsoft account.

      [Asset 4074839]
  2. Advise the customer, "Now that you have a new account, let’s make it more secure so you won’t have to do this again." Then they should select Security on the top menu.

    [Asset 4074842]
  3. Customer should select Update Info.
    [Asset 4074843]
  4. They will choose which alternate phone number or email address they would like to use to receive a security code, enter the details to verify that contact point, then select Send code.
    [Asset 4529956]
  5. The customer should keep this page open while they copy the code from the email or text message, then enter that code here. But, before they continue, if they are using a device that they own and don’t share, have them make this what we call “a trusted device” by selecting the checkbox that says I sign in frequently on this device. Not only does this reduce the number of times they need to do this, but it also helps if they ever need to recover their account. Then they select Submit after they’re done.
    [Asset 4074845]
  6. If they see a page that asks if you’re tired of receiving security codes, they should skip it for now by selecting No thanks. The customer will be able to set up the “Authenticator app” later. The Authenticator app will generate security codes on their smart phone, so they don’t need to have them sent to them.
    [Asset 4074846]
  7. The customer should be automatically directed to the Security settings page. This is where they manage those alternate contacts to receive security codes and it’s important that they keep these up-to-date and keep as many of those alternate email addresses and phone numbers as they can.

    If the customer keeps at least two or three of those up-to-date, they shouldn’t have problems getting back into their account if anything should ever happen again. If they want to add another contact, just select Add security info.

[Asset 4074847]

  1. Messaging: "Now that I’ve helped you set up a new account, remember to keep your security info up to date and to change your password regularly. Also, try not to use the same passwords across different accounts or sites because your account will be at risk if one of those other sites gets hacked. Do you have any subscriptions on your old account that you would like cancelled?"

If the customer does have recurring billing subscriptions and has the payment instrument used to pay for those subscriptions, see KB 2885545 Procedure: Handling a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner.
