What is this article about?

This article covers the escalation best practices for Bing Places, and covers in detail some of the more common escalation requests you will need to create on behalf of customers.

We have implemented a temporary workaround process for handling requests to change business hours (or mark locations as closed) for selected business types. See section 5.2.2 for more information.

COVID-19: Temporary process for Note to Customers information in SERP
For a limited number of chain users, the YPID entity may include Note to Customers information, which is being pulled directly from the chain’s website. (This is an automated feature and cannot be recommended to customers.  They will have this added automatically, if eligible based on impressions.)

If a customer contacts you to ask that this information be changed, note their BPID information and the desired changes, and escalate to Global Support for assistance. For non-BPID customers, direct them to Suggest an Edit as normal to report issues with YPID listings.

1. Escalation process

Before escalating Bing Places issues to Global Support, ensure the following steps have all been taken:

  1. Help the customer claim the listing, make the changes they want, and allow one standard publish cycle to complete before escalating to Global Support.
  • Note: Although some markets benefit from super-fast publish (SFP) for certain attributes, you should still allow changes to take a full standard cycle publish to complete, as it is known that SFP will not always be successful.
  1. Confirm the BPID is verified (see KB 2964704 Process: Bing Places: Business listing verification). If not, help the customer verify and allow the usual time for publishing to resolve the issue.
  2. Confirm the BPID is published (see KB 3138298 Process: Bing Places: ​Publishing Cycle). If not, advise the customer to allow the correct publishing cycle to complete. Unless they quality for an FTW correction, see KB 3186053 Bing Places: When to Use FTW Correction Instead of Force Matching.
  3. If possible, check the Published/Pipeline Views (see KB 3138577) to confirm:
  • Is the BPID information being normalized? (see KB 3204962 Procedure: Bing Places: Normalization)? If so, we are unable to update this on Bing.
  • Is the BPID information being overwritten by other third-party sources? If so, advise them to update these third-party sources.
  • Is the customer trying to update the Bing Places ID (BPID) to a new location? If so, they should mark the old location as closed (if applicable) and create a new BPID for their new location. See section 5 for more information.
  1. Check to see if the customer has a ‘Suspended’ status for their listings. If listings are suspended, check that the customer has a local business location or offers local services. Do not escalate for help if the customer is only offering online/phone support services.

Note: If you suspect that this business is involved in illicit business activity (for example, online scam), check if the user account is blacklisted as in the screenshot below. If this is the case, see the guidance for Blacklisted Customers in the When Not to Escalate section further down this article.

If the user is not blacklisted, but you still have reason to believe they are a scammer, escalate to Global Support and ask them to conduct an investigation into whether the customer should be blacklisted.

[Asset 4555464]

  1. Make sure the customer is not asking you to break by-design Entity logic. (See section 4 for more information.)
  2. Check that you are not escalating to have a Temporarily Closed entity marked as Open/Closed. The Temporarily Open/Closed field is sent to the YPID via a different feed and cannot be corrected by Global Support. Customers should claim, update, and allow the standard publish cycle to run. If the correction hasn’t taken effect after this time, you can raise to Global Support as a bug request.
  3. If the issue cannot be resolved using these steps, escalate to Global Support for assistance.

Important: Do not request Global Support take specific actions on a listing. For example, unmerge or apply Fix the World (FTW) correction. Global Support will determine the correct solution to fix the problem, using the dedicated tools and processes given to them by engineering.

In your escalation notes state:

  1. The symptoms of the problem. For example, “Yellow Pages ID (YPID) is not showing the same information as BPID.”
  2. The steps you have attempted to reproduce the issue (repro).
  3. The desired outcome. For example, “YPID should show the same phone information as BPID.”
  4. GMB Sync Only:
    1. GMB account name only (explicitly tell the customer not to include password information).
    2. Relevant screenshots.
    3. Repro steps captured via Step Recorder.

Global Support reviews the cause of the problem and determines the most effective solution.

When correcting data discrepancies, as a best practice we should always advise customers to update their website and third-party feeds to support the changes in Bing Places (especially Yelp, which is one of our major listing providers).

Screen recording

After escalating, you may find that the Tier 2 owner will require a screen recording of the customer’s repro-steps and the error they’re encountering, in order to provide further assistance. 

If an escalation owner asks for a screen recording, ask the customer to use the Record steps to reproduce a problem help guidance to capture a recording of the error they are facing, and share this with you. 

You can then forward this to the Tier 2 owner and await their further guidance.

2. Downloading listings with Get User Businesses and guidance on raising bulk escalations

Downloading the Get User Business file

To make it easier to download and escalate customer listings in bulk, the Bing Places Admin UI has the option to Get User Businesses. You can download this using the following process:

  1. Log in to the Admin UI and search for a customer account using the Sign In As feature, which is located at the bottom of the webpage, in line with the hyperlinks for Legal, Help, Support.
  2. Select Get User Businesses.
  3. This initiates the Excel file download, containing all business listings, including YPID information and LocalProbe links.
  • Note: As this file contains LocalProbe links, it is considered internal only and should not be shared with customers. Remove the LocalProbe column before sharing with customers.
  • Note: Shared listings will not download into the Excel file when you use this feature. If you need to bulk-escalate for shared listings, note these manually by calling out the BPIDs, the incorrect attributes, and the correct attribute information.

Escalating bulk listings

You can use the Get User Business file to provide Global Support with listing information relevant to your customer’s issue. Before submitting this file:

  1. Make sure that the BPID for each listing was downloaded.
  2. Make sure that the BPID attribute information is what the customer wishes to see on the Search Engine Results Page (SERP).
  3. Add a new column including the last modified date. Note: If the last modified date is still within usual publishing times for that market, do not escalate the listing. Educate the customer on the publishing cycle instead. See KB 3138298 Process: Bing Places: Publishing Cycle for more information.
  4. Add a new column, including incorrect attribute information, and updates as follows:
  • Single Attribute Correction: Copy and paste the incorrect YPID information in this column.
  • Multiple Attribute Corrections: List which attributes are wrong (for example, phone and HOOP information).
  1. Validate that the changes you are requesting are valid by comparing the published YPID information with the BPID information. If an advertiser has helped you compose the file, double-check their requests are valid before you submit the file to Global Support.
  • Note: Files with invalid changes will be passed back to CSS to validate.
  1. Non-English markets: Update the column headers to English language to avoid pushbacks or confusion with the escalation team, who only speak English.

Bulk template file

Global Support have also prepared a Bulk Escalation File which contains several tabs for common escalation scenarios, along with examples of how the form should be completed. Delete examples before submitting.

These columns are the minimum required to process bulk requests, but feel free to add additional columns/callouts that you may deem relevant to the escalation.

3. When not to escalate

Do not escalate cases to Global Support when any of the following applies:

  • Duplicate listings are not eligible for merge or unmerge requests, as outlined in section 4.
  • No BPID: Customers who are unable (or refuse) to claim a listing in Bing Places cannot be escalated to Global Support for assistance in correcting changes. Instead, advise the customer to use Suggest an Edit to report changes to the publishing team. The publishing team will reach out to the customer to confirm once changes have been made.
  • Cosmetic change requests: If customers ask for cosmetic changes (for instance, normalization of business details or URL changes that do not impact the landing page or web-tracking capabilities), educate the customer that these changes are by design and are used by all local listings, to provide a consistent and high-quality end-user experience.
  • Website tracking in URL: Bing Places/Local Listings do not currently support tracking parameters in their URLs. Do not escalate for advertisers requesting help adding tracking parameters to their listings. Instead, educate them and add the details of their case to the open UserVoice entry.
  • ‘Spam’ in the business name: The following are all examples of attempts to spam a listing, and should not be escalated for correction:
    • Adding Location to the Business Name – for example, Miami Pizza London Bridge.
    • Adding former Business Names – for example, Miami Pizza, formerly Pizza Mario.
    • Keyword Stuffing in Business Name – for example, Miami Pizza – pasta, calzone, burgers, chips.
    • HOOP addition to Business Name – for example, Miami Pizza – open 4 PM to late.
    • Descriptive Text added to Business Name – for example, Miami Pizza – Great quality food by Chef Luigi.
  • Blacklisted customers: If engineering has identified a customer as malicious or fraudulent, a Blacklisted status is applied in their Bing Places account. If you see this status, do not attempt to help the customer claim or modify listings. Instead, use the following messaging:

"<Customer Name>,

Thank you for your interest in promoting your local business with Bing Places.

Based on our review, it seems that listings within the Bing Places account associated with [insert Microsoft account] have been permanently suspended. For privacy and security reasons, we cannot provide details around your specific account suspension, but the most common reasons that require us to suspend Bing Places accounts include:

  • The account was accessed without authorization.
  • The account is related to an account that was previously closed.
  • The account is associated with listings operating outside of our Microsoft Services Agreement.

Please know that we have taken this matter seriously and have thoroughly investigated the issue. This decision is final, and we consider the issue closed.

Sincerely,

Bing Places Support Team”

Once this message is delivered, discontinue your chat, and close the ticket. 

There is no need to engage in follow-up discussions to explain why a listing was blacklisted, or to try and appeal such decisions.

Use the blacklisted coding in ASD so that these will be tracked in reporting.

4. Matching/Distinguishing entities

4.1 Overmatching/Undermatching

Customers may complain about duplicate listings on Bing. These are usually the result of overmatching or undermatching occurring on Bing:

  • Overmatching: When listings which should be discrete and separate are incorrectly combined into a single listing.
  • Undermatching: When listings that should be combined are instead listed as separate entities or listings.

CSS will usually escalate these cases to Global Support, who may choose either to merge, unmerge, apply a correction, or leave as-is, depending on whether the duplication is by-design or not.

CSS will need to convey the outcome of these escalations to the customer. (If there is uncertainty about a proposed solution, raise this with your SME/TA for review.)

4.2 Distinguishing entities

The following fundamental principles govern all Bing Places conflation decisions when matching candidates and determining whether an escalation is required:

  1. An Entity is identified by its Name and Address. Candidates match if the name and address refer to the same entity as the target.

Although there may be slight differences in candidates due to incompleteness, additional noise words, and formatting, if the name and address refer to the same business and location, then they are the same entity.

For example, the following are separate listings with small variations in names that refer to the same entity:

Listing#1:
Name “Summit Memorial Funeral”
Address: 7 Summit Ave, Summit, NJ 07901

Listing#2:
Name “Daniel Winther, Summit Memorial”
Address: 7 Summit Ave, Summit, NJ 07901

Note: This does not apply to Professional Profiles – if one of the YPID entities is for a Service Professional’s profile, then this is considered a separate entity from a standard local listing, and should not be considered a duplicate.

  1. If the address, phone, and website information relate to one entity, but the name indicates another, then the name overrules the other attributes. Name is the most important attribute when determining whether candidates match.   

  1. Ghost or junk listings – When the name is the same, but the address is different. These can occur when:
  • One or more of the duplicates refers to an old location. In this case, the old listing entity should be tagged as closed.
  • The name and address have been incorrectly combined to form an entity that never existed at a location at least 100m/330ft away from a true location. (These are considered ghost or junk entities.)
  • An entity does not exist currently and there is no proof that the entity ever existed, even if there is a possibility that it did exist. (These are considered ghost entities.)
  • An entity exists at a different location, but never existed at the location of the listing that is being evaluated as a duplicate. (These are considered junk entities.)
  1. Duplicate listings – These occur when the name is different, but the address is the same. These can occur when:
  • A business is renamed or taken over. For example, When Jurys Inns Heathrow Airport was renamed to Hilton Garden Inn Heathrow Airport.
  • A Professional Profile exists alongside a Clinic/Hospital listing (for example, Dr Leonard McCoy, 210 Enterprise Rd, San Francisco and Federation Hospital, 210 Enterprise Rd, San Francisco).

In this scenario, the candidates are considered separate entities and cannot be merged (the name overrides all other attributes, creating a separate entity.) Advise the customer to mark the old entity as closed.  

  1. Entities that are contained within a larger entity, whether physically or virtually, are not automatically considered a match:

Physical containment is defined as occupying a subset of space within a larger entity, such as a single building or set of nearby buildings that form part of a larger campus/park.

Examples of physically contained candidates that we would consider as separate entities would be:

  • Separate stores in a shopping mall.
  • Separate restaurants inside a Store.
  • Specialised facilities (for example, emergency rooms) or physicians inside a hospital.
  • A public ATM outside a bank (or other location).

General rule – if you can find the entity on Google Streetview, and it has a distinct access point and signage, we can consider this a valid stand-alone entity.

Virtual containment is harder to define, as there are various types of virtual containment. Examples would be:

  • A parent company that has a subsidiary.
  • A chain that has a sub-chain.
  • The headquarters of a chain and its branches.
  • An entity that has a department (for example, Costco is the entity, but within this there are gas, tire centre, food court, and pharmacy). 

For department scenarios only, you can escalate to Global Support to request a merge/unmerge of candidate listings.

For all other virtual containment issues, we do not currently support linkage between parent and child candidate listings. Do not escalate for merge/unmerge requests, as these cannot be processed.

A common example of virtual containment issues that we do not support would be car dealerships who operate from the same location, but attempt to promote themselves under their affiliated car-manufacturer names. For example, ‘Motorline Renault Ashford (Aftersales)’ and ‘Motorline Toyota Ashford’ both operating at the same retail park location.

4.3 Pre-escalation entity checking

Before escalations can be raised to resolve duplicate issues, we should attempt to confirm whether a customer is asking us to make corrections to a valid entity, using the following steps:

  • Check the websites for each entity (if possible) to find out the company address. You can use this information to determine whether the addresses are the same (duplicate entity) or different (valid entity). Use the guidance above to determine if a listing should be merged or not.
  • Check Street View on Maps (if available) to confirm location information is accurate for the listings.
  • Check the customer is not trying to create multiple listings for virtual containment scenarios. For example, van rental listings or car dealers trying to create differing listings per make/model of car they support.
  • Check the customer is not asking you to unmerge by-design entity matches.

5. Escalation types

Although all Bing Places escalations should follow the steps outlined above, there are several specific requests which may require additional information or a slight change in basic handling.

These are outlined below:

5.1 Location Open escalations

If a customer’s BPID listing is showing as Location Closed but they are actually open, we can escalate these cases to Global Support immediately for their assistance. We do not need to wait for a publish cycle to complete after marking it as Open in the UI.

Global Support investigates why the Location Closed flag has been applied to the YPID and help in updating this to open status again as quickly as possible. Avoid setting expectations with the customer in case an FTW solution cannot be applied.

  • Customers may also request the removal of third-party listings due to them being inappropriate, irrelevant, or inaccurate.

Given the high volume of queries, we must understandably prioritise key services and industries whose operation directly impacts the health and wellbeing of our search users. We appreciate your patience and understanding and advise you to use our new ‘Temporary Closed’ feature or modify your operating hours using our existing tool functionality.”

Standard process:

This option can be implemented by both customers and internal L1 Specialists without the need for an escalation. By marking a listing as closed, it will be updated on Bing Local to display the Location Closed status.

Listings marked as closed will continue to serve in SERP for a period of six months, after the six months they are removed from the results pages. This timeframe is for the benefit of search users who may have recently interacted with, or visited, the business listing/location. We allow them time to see that the business is no longer trading at a location before we remove this from Bing SERP.

5.2.3 Applying an FTW correction ﻿

Although it will not remove data from Bing, FTW corrections can be used to overwrite YPID attribute data received from data feeds. Once applied, an FTW change bypasses the normal publishing process and applies the change straight to the published YPID. However, in many cases this will prevent future changes to the attributes being made.

For this reason, this scenario should only be applied to a listing when:

  • Changes have been submitted through the normal publishing process but have failed to take effect after a complete publish cycle.
  • The customer has a verified BPID and reports Personal Data or competitor information in their YPID listing which requires urgent correction. For example, we have verified them as the owner of the account, checked that the BPID is verified, but the customer notes that the published YPID for their listing is showing either a personal phone or competitor’s website URL.
    • Note: FTW may not always be the appropriate solution to this problem. When escalating for Personal Data/competitor data reasons, Global Support checks to see where the incorrect attribute data originates and may decide to suppress this data source rather than use FTW to modify the YPID itself. This would achieve the same results but would require a standard publish cycle to take effect. If this is the result, explain to the customer and only push back on Tier 2 if the customer threatens legal action to resolve their issue.
    • Customers who want their data to publish quickly, but who do not fall into the above scenarios, need to wait one standard publish cycle before we can escalate to request an FTW correction.

5.2.4 Merging YPID data ﻿

This option can only be implemented by Global Support. By merging the YPID of two separate listings, we connect their data feeds and create a single YPID, which is fed and updated by all these data feeds together.

This scenario should be applied to a listing when:

  • Identical duplicate listings for the same business name/location appear on Bing Local listings.
  • “Similar listings”, which fall into these scenarios:
    • Business name is the same, address is different but within 100m/330ft of the true location.
    • Business names are different variants, but address is the same.

When requesting a YPID merge, remember to include the BPID for the accurate listing as well.

Note: The true location should be claimed in Bing Places.

Restrictions:

  • If the BPID is showing any status other than ‘Published’ do not request a merge. The pending pipeline data will overwrite the merged YPID information once published.
  • Once a merge request is created, advise the customer not to make any further edits, or to resubmit their listing. This creates a new pending feed, which will overwrite the merged YPIDs when it is published.
  • If the customer does not have at least one BPID claimed, they need to use Suggest an Edit.

For any other merge request (for example, the customer reports similar listings that they believe to be theirs, but which do not fall into the criteria listed above) advertisers need to use Suggest an Edit to report their concerns, as we are unable to confirm they are the correct owner of the ‘duplicate’ listing. For example, the business name/address may be valid for someone else’s business, however the URL has been accidentally associated and simply needs to be updated.)

5.2.5 Suppressing a listing ﻿

This solution completely suppresses all data relating to this business on Bing. If we use this to remove valid data, then we damage the accuracy and reliability of Bing search functionality, which is why this option is only used in Location Closed scenarios:

  • Business has been claimed, marked as Location Closed, and the customer complains about one of the following:
    • New business location is not showing, but closed YPID is showing.
    • New business is showing, but closed business is ranking higher in SERP.
    • Business has ceased trading and they are receiving calls/visits from people still interested in their business.

For the above scenarios only, we should first attempt to educate the customer on why we keep closed listings visible for six months, see section 5.2.2 for more information. If the customer continues to insist on suppressing a listing after you explain this, for these scenarios only, we can escalate for suppression.

Because of the impact suppressing a listing has on Bing search integrity, it is essential that we verify the customer as a valid business owner/representative before we submit a suppression request on their behalf.

  • Note: If possible, check the YPID in LocalProbe to confirm that this is not a Protected Entity because they cannot be suppressed normally, as this causes a block in the publishing pipeline. See KB 3154540 Bing Places: Local Probe, BPID, YPID, & FTW Corrections for more information on how to look up this status in LocalProbe.

If you observe a Protected Entity shield, include this callout in your escalation notes and explain that we have verified a customer who claims this information is incorrect. The escalation team reviews this information and will share the next steps with you.

For customers who do not fall into the above scenarios, we should educate them on how Bing Local Listings work and reassure them that closed locations will be removed after six months.

Unable or unwilling to verify

If a customer wants to suppress a listing on Bing and they are unable/unwilling to claim the listing first, then we are unable to assist them with their request.

They will need to report a privacy/legal concern, via the Feedback option on Bing to request this.

5.2.6 Reporting concerns via UI Suggest an Edit or Feedback links

The YPID results on Bing contain the option to Suggest an Edit (in markets supported by Local Listings) or share Feedback on the Bing experience (markets unsupported by Local Listings).

Advertisers wanting to correct data inaccuracies on Bing should use these options to share their concerns directly with the Bing team in the following scenarios:

  • The customer is unable or unwilling to claim the YPID in Bing Places.
  • The customer wants to correct inaccurate data about the business.
  • The customer or user claims a business never existed at the YPID address.
    • Note: For Personal Data/suppression concerns, you should always use the Feedback option, and select Report a Legal or Privacy Concern regardless of whether the advertiser is in a supported market.

Local Listing markets only: 

Customers should log into Bing with their Microsoft account before submitting their request, so they can select the option to receive an email from the team. The email arrives within a few hours, and they will be able to reply to the email and correspond with the team for updates.

For all online submissions, the SLA for issue resolution is usually one to two weeks, so CSS advocates should set the expectation with customers and close their tickets, rather than keep open for resolution.

Lack of update – Local Listing markets only

If the customer complains that they have reported a concern and no action has been taken after 14 days, use the following process:

  1. Confirm the customer signed in with a Microsoft account and received their confirmation email. If this did not occur, ask the customer to resubmit after signing in, and wait 14 days.
  2. Ask the customer to provide you with the email they received (this also allows us to confirm the data they claim to have contacted support).
  3. Share with your SME, who will reach out to the TA. Your TA will reach out to the Bing Publishing team, to share this concern and understand if/how we can resolve the problem. 
  4. The TA will pass the results back to you via the SME. 

Privacy/legal concerns

Legal/privacy concerns (for listings that are unclaimed) can be reported via the Feedback option on Bing.

This should be used by any customers who want to request the complete suppression of a listing. For example:

  • A business listing is reported as fake/spam.
  • An address is Personal Data and should not be shown.

5.2.7 Third-party review removal

Customers sometimes contact CSS to request that reviews are removed from their published YPID; usually due to inaccurate or inappropriate feedback or sometimes due to the listing being irrelevant.

This is not normally required, however; reviews are normally updated daily, with the most recent reviews shown first. 

The number of reviews, and review sources, vary depending on the market and segment of the YPID. The sources are prioritised based on the category/type of the YPID. We cannot choose which source shows, or request suppression of valid sources even if the advertiser wants to use a different review site.

Specific reviews can only be considered for manual refresh in specific scenarios:

  • The reviews are abusive/unrelated to the advertiser’s business, and they have already been removed at the source.
  • Reviews that display on SERP do not accurately reflect the sentiment towards the company – for example, if we only show bad reviews in SERP, but the review site has primarily positive reviews.

In the above scenarios, follow this process:

  1. Confirm with the customer that the abusive/unrelated reviews have been removed by the third-party source (if this scenario applies).
  2. Escalate to Global Support to explain the issue and ask if we can refresh the review feed we have on this business.
  3. Global Support works with engineering to see if/when we can refresh the data, and they share this timeframe with CSS.
  4. Pass this information to the customer and set their expectations. Most reviews are updated daily, but in some rare cases of crowd-sourced reviews this may take longer.

5.3 Image related escalations

The following customer issues may be escalated to Global Support in relation to images:

  • You have used the Image Suppression Override to push through a high quality image, but this is still not showing after the usual publishing timeframe, even though fewer than twenty images are showing on the YPID (meaning that better-quality images from other feed sources are probably not preventing the BP image being shown).
  • The customer wants more information about the images chosen from the alternative feeds.
  • The customer wants to remove an image for one of the following reasons:
  • Images display people’s faces or bodies.
  • Images are sensitive – for example, they contain graphic content, profanity, or hate speech.
  • Images are irrelevant/unrelated to the business.
  • Images are water marked.
  • Images display too much text.
  • Images are blurry or low quality.

The customer wants their own Bing Places images prioritised to show over third-party images (can only be done if Tier 2 find the images to be of higher quality and better relevance).

Follow established processes and escalate to T2 for review. Global Support (T2) will follow standard SLA for initial reply and issue resolution.

Image escalation notes:

  • Include screenshots of the images you need published.
  • For image removal requests, clearly explain why the image is being escalated for removal, using the scenarios outlined above.

Non-urgent image removal requests (for example, not offensive/sensitive)

Use the following process before escalating image-removal requests:

  1. Locate the SERP image the customer wishes to remove and identify the feed source. (You can usually see this by either hovering over an image with your cursor or clicking the image to see this called out.)
  2. Ask the customer to confirm whether they own a business listing on the third-party site. If so, obtain the URL for their business and ask them to remove the image. 
  3. Manually confirm the image was removed on the third-party site, then allow 15 calendar days for our system to update.  If the image continues to display after this date, escalate to Global Support for help refreshing the data.
  4. If the customer does not own a business listing on the third-party site, or insists that their image has been removed longer than 15 calendar days and you are unable to access the third-party page to manually confirm this, you can also escalate to Global Support for help refreshing the data or suppressing the image.

Non-escalation scenarios

  • Customers complaining about specific BP images not being shown in their YPID, when there are already twenty high-quality images being shown. In this case, educate them that images can be chosen from multiple sources, as mentioned in section 2.
  • Customer wants to have valid and relevant images suppressed from third-party feed sources.
  • Customers asking to have images removed because they are old or out of date (for example, hotels have been renovated, storefronts have been updated).

In this scenario, we can request to have Bing Places images prioritised, but we would not accept requests to remove/suppress old images. This is to protect the user experience for search users interested in seeing how the business may previously have operated/appeared, and ensures we accurately portray the business entity both past and present.

6. FAQ

Q: I already have business listings with you! Why am I being sent to the online ‘Suggest an Edit/Feedback’ link? Why won’t you help me?

A: The Bing Places for Business platform (and Bing Places Support teams) can help in claiming/editing Local Listings which you’re able to successfully verify, but for listings with address information that cannot be verified we partner with the Bing Publishing Team via the Suggest an Edit process to report concerns with local listing accuracy.

Before we update a local listing that isn’t linked to a claimed Bing Places listing, our Publishing team needs to first investigate and make sure that this isn’t a valid business whose attributes have been merged with your business details on the back-end. For example, ‘Contoso Print Team’ and ‘Contoso Printing Team’ may be two valid and separate entities, who have accidentally been merged due to the similarity in name.)

To ask the team to review a local listing, you can use the Suggest an Edit link beneath the local listing on Bing, to share your concerns with them directly. These requests are processed within one to two weeks.

In supported markets, you can also sign into Bing first using your Microsoft account; this way, once you submit feedback via Suggest an Edit, you can choose to receive a confirmation email. This lets you communicate directly with the publishing team to ask for updates or clarify any confusion they might have.

Q: Why won’t you remove my old business listing from Bing? This location is closed, and I don’t want to see it anymore. This is confusing to my customers!

A: We take the accuracy and integrity of Bing’s search results very seriously. We understand that when a business ceases to trade at a location, the business owners will update their marketing to avoid confusing customers, who may continue to look for services at a previous location.

To support customers who may have recently interacted with your business listing, or visited your store, we show listings as Location Closed on Bing for a period of six months. This gives our users time to see that businesses they are interested in have moved or ceased trading and avoids confusion with searchers who may be looking for a business they previously knew to operate in a location.

Q: Can I claim a listing and then use the ‘delete' function in the UI to delete the listing from Bing?

A: This is not recommended. The delete function in the Bing Places Dashboard only removes the Bing Places feed source from your published listing. If your published listing is being supported from other sources, (for example: Yelp or TripAdvisor) it will continue to show, and may also become inaccurate if the third-party data is out of date.

Q: I have observed duplicate displaying on Bing. I have claimed the correct listing in Bing Places. Will this remove the duplicate listing?

A: Yes, this will go through our publishing pipeline and merge with the duplicate listing. This will remove the duplicate from Bing after our next publish cycle completes.

Q: My competitor has three listings in Bing with exactly the same information. This is unfair and ruins my chances in getting ranked in Bing.

A: You can report this incident to our engineering team via the Suggest an Edit or Feedback link, displayed against the listings on Bing. This feedback is reviewed by our engineers, usually within one to two weeks.

Q: I’ve claimed my old listing and updated it – but now you’re telling me this isn’t the right process? Why can’t I just update my old listing?

A: When a listing appears on Bing, it is a combination of data feeds from both Bing Places and other third-party sources. Each listing is assigned a ‘YPID’ number, which you can find in the URL of the listing itself. This YPID is unique to the listing.
When you update an old listing in Bing Places to a new address, the data feed for Bing Places sends this new information to the YPID listing, however because it’s already associated to other feeds related to your previous address, this causes data conflicts and your updated information may not show as a result.
To avoid this happening, the best practice is always to create a new listing in Bing Places for any new address your business moves to – this will trigger a new YPID to be generated exclusive to your new address, and any third-party data feeds relating to this new location will be merged over time through our publishing process.
You can use the Location Closed feature in Bing Places to make sure old addresses are clearly marked as closed. After 6 months, closed locations will be removed from our search results.

Q: Why can’t I choose which review source I want to use?

A: Bing automatically displays the review sources which best align to your business segment/category. We understand that some advertisers may prefer specific review sources; however, to ensure fair representation and equal competition for all advertisers, Bing will only display trusted review partners which closely align to your business area.

Q: Why can’t you remove reviews I am unhappy with?

A: We understand that advertisers want to attract customers to their business, and we are happy to investigate any cases where the reviews displayed on Bing do not match with the majority of reviews/ratings being shown on a review provider’s website. To provide Bing’s search users with the most reliable, trustworthy experience, and to provide our advertisers with a fair competitive landscape, we are unable to remove negative reviews for our advertisers unless they have been deemed invalid and removed by the review provider first. In this scenario, work with the provider, and contact Bing once the review has been successfully removed, and we are happy to assist.

Q: I want to manage the images which show for my listing in Bing Places, but you’re telling me I’m not allowed to block third-party images. Why?

A: The Bing Places data feed is one of many sources we use to populate the information for local listings on Bing. While we strive to provide our business users with the best platform for keeping their information accurate, we also focus on providing Bing’s end-users with relevant and informative information relating to their search query.

By including relevant, quality images from our trusted partners (for example, TripAdvisor or Yelp) we can harness the power of crowd-sourced images in order to provide our users with great quality images that truly reflect our customer’s businesses.

Assure the customer that if they see images which are not relevant to their business, or they believe to be poor quality, we can ask our publishing team to remove these for them.

Q:  Why won’t you let me use my preferred Business Name? It’s my business and I’ve claimed it (for example, why can’t they say ‘Miamia Pizza – Pizzas, Burgers and More’ instead of just ‘Miami Pizza?’)? 
A:  Bing Local Listings are intended to be an accurate reflection of the business name/address found at your physical location, or on your website. 

If you want to provide customers with more information regarding your business’ services/offerings, we recommend using Microsoft Advertising to create richer, more informative ad copy to target your customers. 

Q: Which markets are supported by ‘Suggest an Edit’?

A: The following markets can use Suggest an Edit:

  • de-DE - Germany
  • en-AU - Australia
  • en-CA – Canada - English
  • en-GB – United Kingdom
  • en-IN - India
  • en-US – United States - English
  • es-ES - Spain
  • fr-FR - French
  • it-IT - Italy
  • ja-JP - Japan
  • zh-CN – China
