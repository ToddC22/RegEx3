ASMS – PPP Case Handoff Process

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This article provides guidance on how and when to handoff a Premier, Pro-Direct, Partner (PPP) case or task. This includes local language cases that require 24/7 support.

Policy

  • When can a whole case be handed off to another engineer?

    • The case requires 24x7 ownership (i.e. Prem Sev A) or the customer requests it to have 24x7 ownership.

    • The customer formally requests to have the case owned by an engineer in a different timezone.

    • The initial case owner is going on vacation and has been unsuccessful in finding a new owner themselves.

  • When can a Task be handed off to another engineer?

    • The case owner is in a different timezone than the customer, and the customer requires a callback during their daytime (i.e. 3-strike callback; screenshare for troubleshooting, etc).

  • When can the receiving engineer request a case credit from the Duty Managers for taking on a handoff case/task?

    • If the receiving engineer will need to invest as much or more time into that handoff case as they would a new incoming case, and that time will be invested that day, they can request case credit from the Duty Managers (DM).

  • When is a warm handoff between the current and new engineer required?
    Note: A warm handoff is when an engineer hands their case off to a new engineer, ensuring that the new engineer is both introduced to the customer and appraised of the current situation with the case (preferably by phone).  

    • For planned OOF periods of >3 days (as per TSG 2793303).

    • For Premier Sev A cases, 24x7 Sev B and other similar scenarios where the customer requests to continue working immediately with the next engineer. 

    • For any case where you think it is in the best interest of the customer.

Handoff Process

As the case owner, follow the process below to properly handoff your case/task.

  1. Verify and confirm that the case/task meets the requirements for a handoff as outlined in the Policy section above.
  2. If you are not the original owner and the previous owner is on shift, let the previous owner know that you are assigning them a case/task for handoff that they owned previously. After receiving confirmation from the previous owner that they are available to accept the case, assign the case to them and notify them via email, with their manager cc’d. An exception to this process is if the previous owner transferred the case in order to align to the customer's time zone.
    • If the premier case is owned by PPP Mindtree (PPP MT) email ASMS PPP MT DL to make them aware of the case. Operation hours for this team is Monday - Friday 12:00AM to 8:00AM PST (7:00AM to 03:00PM UTC) only. 

      Note: The PPP MT team will not be monitoring the Premier POD alias. If a MT case request comes in outside Mindtree’s staffing intervals, please handle the case internally.
  3. Create a Note in the case that includes the following details, or, if handing off a task, add these details to the body:
  • Reason for hand off (i.e. customer requested, vacation OOF, timezone callback, critsit):
    • Was an attempt made to reduce the severity (if case is a sev A or sev B 24x7)?:
    • Business justification provided by the customer (if not applicable just add N/A):
  •   Case Details:
    • Detailed issue description:
    • Customer’s role (Owner, AA, BA):
    • Subscription GUID (If not available or applicable just add N/A):
    • Subscription Type (EA, Pay-As-You-Go, etc.):
    • Troubleshooting done so far:
    • Customer’s mood/Severity:
    • All Partner team(s) tickets (RDQuota, ICM, VSO...) associated with this case:
    • TA Advisory:
    • POD queue the hand-off is within:
    • Specific actions for hand-off recipient:
    • Customer’s expectations for being contacted by hand-off recipient:
  1. Update the Task/Case Internal Title: CASE HANDOFF | Region to handoff to | Date to be assigned | Previous owner alias (as appropriate)
    • Example: CASE HANDOFF | NA | Dec. 1 | bobsmith is the previous owner
    • Note: Engineers will need to use Case Buddy to update the internal title of a task, as it cannot be changed in Service Desk.
    • Note: For cases/tasks where the customer’s preferred contact method is email, the case/task does not need to be handed off to the same time zone. SEs from any time zone can take up cases where email is the contact method. Therefore, for the Internal Title of your handoffs that have the email contact method, put "ANY" as the region.
      • Example: CASE HANDOFF | ANY | Dec. 1 | bobsmith is the previous owner.
    • Note: If an Azure government handoff task needs attention outside of NA business hours, send it to the ASMS US Gov MT queue.
  2. Determine if a Warm Handoff is needed.

    • If YES, follow the "PPP Sev A and 24x7 Sev B Handoff" process mentioned in the next section

    • If NO, transfer the case/problem/task to the respective internal Premier/USG queue:

      • MSaaS ASMS POD Premier AP (for Accounts & Portal cases)

      • MSaaS ASMS POD Premier BPI (for Billing cases)

      • MSaaS ASMS POD USG (for all USG cases)

PPP Sev A and 24x7 Sev B Handoff

  1. No less than 60 minutes before the end of their shift, the existing case owner updates the case notes with all relevant information (as already documented in the Handoff TSG).
    1. Case owner also updates the internal case title to be PPP-AP/BP Sev A HANDOFF to timezone – Next contact @ date/time.
    2. Case owner dispatches the case to the MSaaS ASMS Premier Billing and PI or MSaaS ASMS Premier Accounts and Portal queue in Service Desk depending on the topic of the case.
      Note: If you have previously received the case as a handoff from another engineer and they are available now to take the case back as a handoff, please do so.
    3. Case owner emails notification of the handoff to WADM:
      • including the case number 
      • the queue it was dispatched to
      • the expectations for time of next contact
  2. Duty manager sees the Handoff case in the queue.
  3. Duty manager locates the next available engineer based on the existing RAT/rotation model we use today.
  4. Duty manager assigns the handoff to that new engineer.
  5. Duty manager sends the case assignment email to the new engineer and cc’s the previous case owner who handed it off.
  6. New case owner and old case owner convene on Teams call for a warm handoff.
Note: The existing case owner must not leave for the day without having a warm-transfer discussion with the new owner.
 
If there is an emergency and the source case owner must leave, they need to alert a manager before they leave so the manager can take over the re-assignment duties.
 

Case Credit Request Process

If you have been assigned a handoff case/problem/task, follow the steps below to receive a case credit. 

  1. Verify and confirm that the case meets the requirements for a case credit, as outlined in the Policy section above.

  2. If the handoff is eligible for a credit, the recipient of the handoff will need to email the DMs  using the following email:

    To: Azure Subcr & Billing Duty Manager (wadm@microsoft.com)
    Cc: your manager
    Subject: Case credit for Case #case_number
    Body:

Hello DMs,

Please grant me Case Credit for handoff #case_number/task_number/problem_number. This is a handoff case due to <reason_for_handoff>.

Thanks,
<SE Signature>

Engagement and Escalation Path

If you are unclear about the processes listed above, engage your TA for guidance.
