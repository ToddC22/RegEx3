Procedure: Partner Center: 715-123220 message

[Asset 4090831]

Who should use this article?

All CSS Frontline advocates globally supporting Partner Center CSP.

What is this article about?

The article provides an overview to the Frontline support teams related to an error message that the partners might receive in Partner Center. The 715-error message is not limited to Partner Center portal and it might be received in any of the Microsoft portals such as Azure, VSS, etc.

However, this is the article of reference that should be used for Partner Center CSP. Only CSP escalations will have the 715-123220 code. Please do not use any guidance from other articles related to 715 error messages.

1. Overview

The Microsoft Partner Network program suffered some changes that may result in a number of system-generated messages in Partner CSP.

This message can be received due to any number of different actions, but most popular are related but not limited to:

  • Partner is trying to add a customer.
  • Partner is trying to add/edit/remove users individually or via bulk upload.
  • Partner is trying to update customer bill-to address

2. Process and Impact

If any of the above scenarios, or new ones, occur, escalate to the OneVet DF Escalations team by e-mail, sentesc@microsoft.com, CC'ing the MPN Global Support alias for visibility.

Also inform partners that our system is detecting a problem, and we need to escalate the case to a higher level of support. The partner should never be included on any communications with the OneVet DF team.

3. Escalation Template

Title / email subject: Sev 1 or Sev 4 - SRXXXXXXXXXXX – PC CSP - Message code 715-123220

Description:

  1. User’s business email address
  2. User’s Microsoft account ID/email
  3. The MPN ID
  4. Transaction ID from error message screen
  5. Date and approximate time the attempted transaction produced the error
  6. Business/organization name
  7. User’s first/last name
  8. User’s country

The priority is established based on these criteria:

  • Sev 1-Extreme High Priority (GM level or higher visibility)
  • Sev 4-Standard escalation

Type of Work

SLA-Response

SLA-Resolution

Escalation - Sev 1

1/2 hour

4 hours (24x7, 365 days/year)

Escalation - Sev 4

1/2 hour

72 hours (US mainland business hours on business days only – no US holiday support)


After the OneVet DF Escalations team concludes their investigation of the issue, they will reply using one of the following scenarios:

Scenario 1:

“The issue that prevented you from completing your registration/transaction has been resolved. Please try again.”

Call to action: Do not close the case in the Signposting queue. Use the queue where the case was managed and make sure the CRC is accurate.

Scenario 2:

“Your account does not satisfy Microsoft criteria.”

Call to action: Close the case in the Signposting queue.
