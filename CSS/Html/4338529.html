2. Subscription specific cancel and refund guidelines overview

Note:

Regional exceptions may apply. Review section 3.1 for regional exceptions.

Customers acknowledge that all sales are final when a subscription is purchased. All customers can cancel their subscriptions or turn off recurring billing at any time. Advocates can cancel subscriptions with past due balances without requiring the customer to pay first. Any actions beyond this are exceptions, as outlined in this article.

A refund upon cancelation may be granted as an exception in two scenarios:

  1. If the customer cancels within 30 days of the first charge. For subscriptions that start as trials, look at Order history to determine the actual date of renewal charge – there may be a delayed charge because of dunning or bank delays. Use the most recent charge to determine the 30-day eligibility window.
  2. Once per the lifetime of a Microsoft account, at the customer's request, within 30 days of a renewal charge for the subscription.
    • Note: This applies once per subscription type (for example, Live Gold, Game Pass Ultimate, or Game Pass for PC), including monthly and annual subscriptions. You should check the customer’s refund history for that specific subscription to confirm eligibility before processing this exception.

If customers contact us after 30 days from their first charge because they don’t want the recurring purchases, guide them to Turn off recurring billing through account.microsoft.com/services. If there are issues, you can perform it on behalf of customers using MST. Service will remain available until the end of the subscription period, and there will not be another charge for the subscription.

There are two types of cancelation available using MST:

  1. Cancel subscription: Immediately cancels subscription and revokes access. 
  2. Turn off auto renew (recurring billing): Keeps the current subscription active but disables future recurring purchases. Note: No refund takes place.

3. Exceptions

Note: These exceptions apply in addition to common cancellation and refund guidelines in section 2.

3.1 Regional exceptions

Region Exception

Australia

Consumers have the right to repair, replace, or refund in certain situations. Refer to aka.ms/acl. When the consumer has that right, you must follow the ACL guideline.

Brazil

Renewal: Residents of Brazil are eligible for a full refund if the subscription is cancelled within seven days of renewal.

Israel

If they cancel their subscription outside of 30 days of an initial purchase or recurring billing, under the Israeli consumer protection statute, customers are also eligible to receive a pro-rated refund.

Customers that cancel online do not receive their pro-rated refund automatically. For those that reach customer support, calculate the pro-rated refund amount and issue the refund.

To calculate the refund amount, see KB 4489972 Procedure: A&B: Calculating the pro-rated refund amount on regional exception scenarios.

Korea

Renewal: Eligible for a full refund if the subscription is cancelled within 30 days of renewal. Only allowed once per Microsoft account (lifetime).

If they cancel their subscription outside of 30 days of an initial purchase or recurring billing, customers are also eligible to receive a pro-rated refund.

Customers that cancel online do not receive their pro-rated refund automatically. For those that reach customer support, calculate the pro-rated refund amount and issue the refund.

To calculate the refund amount, see KB 4489972 Procedure: A&B: Calculating the pro-rated refund amount on regional exception scenarios.

Residents of the province of Quebec, Canada

Renewal: Eligible for a pro-rated refund when they cancel their subscription after it recurs.

3.2 MSN - long distance charges

Scenario

A phone number must be used to establish a dial-up connection. Customers may enter the number themselves or be provided one by our software or website.

Refund criteria

Long distance charges are caused by the wrong dialup connection number being given to the customer by Connection Center software (CC) or support.msn.com.

Additional note

Lifetime value of the subscription can be granted up to a $20 (or local currency equivalent) refund.

Advocate procedures 
  1. Process the charges using the Refund Line Item option against the subscription costs.
  2. If the refund amount is over US $20, engage A&B ERA team (for Japan/Korea/China, engage ACA). 

Frequency

As occurs.

4. Procedures

Follow the procedures listed here, unless stated otherwise in section 3.

  1. Confirm they are eligible to cancel the paid subscription.
  2. In the Manage Subscription section under Services in MST, locate the subscription to cancel.
  3. Inspect the History section and look for OrderCompletedSuccess in the Event Description column.
    • If not present, advise the customer you will process the cancel/refund after our billing system fully processes the charge. Give SLA of 24 hours.*
    • If present, then proceed to step 4.
  4. Select Cancel Subscription and Cancel immediately to start the refund. You will see the actual amount captured in that option. If you only see zero amount, proceed to step 5 for additional refund procedures.
  5. Go to the Order section and locate the latest charge of the subscription. Process refund of the latest charge using Return Line Item.

For additional information see KB 4053336 TUG: MST Services.

*Note: If “OrderCompletedSuccess” isn’t present, then that means the order hasn’t yet fully completed, and advocates should try again later. Set customer expectations as needed, wait until the end of their shift and check again before processing the cancellation + refund.

5. More information

5.1 Xbox scenarios

Example for 3-month Xbox Live renewals:

[Asset 4459226]

Eligible

  • The customer is eligible for a refund if they contacted us by June 1st and June 30th (30-days from initial purchase).
  • After renewal, the customer is eligible for a one-time refund exception as described in section 2 if they contacted us between September 1st and September 30th, or December 1st to December 30th (30 days from the renewal date).
  • The service will be deactivated for the remaining period of the subscription.

Note: The customer had recurring billing turned off, they cancelled the subscription and after three months they come back onto a new three-month subscription, the customer will be eligible for a refund if they contacted us within the first 30 days of being billed.

Not eligible: Regional exceptions may apply, see section 3.1.

  • If the customer cancels after June 30th or September 30th or December 30th, they are not eligible for a refund. Customers can turn off recurring billing option on any subscription.
  • Service remains active until the end of the subscription.

5.2 Other scenarios

Scenarios

Advocate procedure

The customer contacts support because the previous refund request has not been processed.
  1. Validate previous case notes.
  2. If the refund was not processed, issue a refund by using Refund Line Item under Order section.
  3. Cancel the subscription under Subscription.

The customer states the billing and renewal date do not match.

Educate the customer on the difference between the two dates and how it will impact them. See KB 840535 Informational: Subscription and billing anniversary dates and pending charges.

The customer wants to modify or update an invoice to include a VAT ID for consumer M365 subscription purchase.
  1. Advise the customer that we cannot modify an invoice to include VAT. If the customer wants a VAT ID to be part of the order, a business subscription (like M365 Business Basic) can be purchased.
  2. Offer to cancel the existing subscription they have purchased and provide a partial refund based on the remaining months left.
    • If a subscription does not self-cancel, issue a cancellation as well.
  3. Share this link to the M365 Business product info page
    • https://www.microsoft.com/microsoft-365/business/compare-all-microsoft-365-business-products?&activetab=tab:primaryr2
