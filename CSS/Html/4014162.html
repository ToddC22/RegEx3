Procedure: Microsoft account: Rename alias

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer

What is this article about?

This article describes the procedure to follow when customers contact wanting to change their alias on their Microsoft account, and are facing an error or unable to self-serve.

  • The customer should already have followed the steps outlined at How to change the email address associated with your Microsoft account. If the customer has not followed these steps, guide them through this procedure.

This procedure must be used as a solution in the following scenarios:

A customer wants to change the primary alias on their Microsoft account but is unable to. This can happen due to one of the following reasons:

  • They are unable to verify their third-party primary alias (no longer has access to receive verification code).
  • They receive an error message when trying to rename an account with a Microsoft domain (such as live.com, outlook.com, Hotmail.com, and xboxlivefamily.com).

This procedure is NOT to be used as a solution in the following scenarios:

  • The customer has deleted the data associated to their Microsoft or Xbox Live account. Data deletions are permanent.
  • They deleted a @hotmail, @live, or @msn alias. Deletion of these aliases is permanent and should be not be escalated to Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA), as there is no further support available.
  • They can’t sign into their account. Follow KB 2984712 Procedure: Microsoft account: Password reset.
  • They can’t access their security proofs. Follow KB 2984702 Procedure: Microsoft account: Handling "Security Info Replacement" contacts.
  • They are currently in a Microsoft account 30-day security update waiting period (they should receive this message before being able to access or change security info).

1. Procedure to rename an alias

1.1 Customers will add a new email address

  1. The customer must sign in to their Microsoft account.
    • If the customer is unable to sign in, follow KB 2984712 to assist them.
  2. Select Your info at the top of the page.
  3. Select Manage how you sign into Microsoft.
  4. The customer may be prompted to re-enter their password or enter a code we send to the email address or phone number listed on their account as security information.
    • If they are unable to verify their security information, follow KB 2984702.
  5. Select Add email.
    • If the customer sees the message “For security reasons, you need to verify your primary alias before you can add another alias” at the bottom of the page, they will need to either verify their current primary alias or select Rename if they are unable to verify it (this is common if the email address was not entered correctly or if access to that email is no longer available). If they select Rename, see section 1.3.
    • If the customer wants to create a new email address to use, they must select Create a new email address and add it as an alias, and then follow the instructions.
    • If they have a non-Microsoft email address (such as an @gmail.com or @yahoo.com email address) that they want to use, they must select Add an existing email address as a Microsoft account alias, and then Add alias.
      • To add an existing email address, they must verify that they own that account. To the right of the alias they added, select Verify, and we'll send a message to that email address to get confirmation.
      • When they receive the verification email, they will need to select the link provided to verify that they own the account.
      • Note: The email address that they enter can't already be in use as a Microsoft account alias.

1.2 Setting the new email address as the primary alias

After the customer has added the new email address, they must set it as their primary alias to have it displayed when they sign in to all devices and services by following the below steps:

  1. On the Manage your account aliases page, the customer will select Make primary under the email address they want as primary under Account alias.

    Note: Only email addresses can be used as primary aliases.
  2. Generally, it is recommended that the customer remove any email address they no longer want to use to sign in with. To remove an email address, select Remove next to that alias on the Manage your account aliases page, and then follow the instructions provided.

Note: If the customer chooses to remove an alias that uses a Microsoft domain such as @hotmail.com, @outlook.com, or @live.com, that alias will be completely deleted and will not be available to sign in to any Microsoft services.

Important: Do not remove this email address if they still want to use it to send and receive email messages.

1.3 Renaming an unverified primary alias

If the customer was unable to verify their primary alias, navigate them to the Manage how you sign in to Microsoft page, select Rename next to the primary alias and then follow the below steps:

  1. Select either Use an existing alias or Use a different alias.
    • If they have a verified alias you would like to make primary, they will select Use an existing alias, and choose the desired email address from the menu. Otherwise, go to step 3 to add or create a new one.
  2. If they select Use a different alias, follow these steps:
    • If they want to create a new email address to use, they will select Create a new email address as your primary alias, and then Rename primary alias.
    • If they have a non-Microsoft email address (such as an @gmail.com or @yahoo.com email address) that they want to use, they will select Use an existing email address as your primary alias, and then Rename primary alias.
      • When they add an existing email address, they'll be taken to the Verify your email page to verify that they own that account. We'll send a message with a security code to that email address to get confirmation.
      • When they receive the verification email, enter the provided security code in the field and select Verify to confirm that they own the account.

        Note: The email address that they enter can't already be in use as a Microsoft account alias.
  3. If successful, they’ll be shown the message “You’ve changed the name of your primary alias.” On the Manage how you sign into Microsoft page, their previous unverified primary alias will be removed and replaced with the new address they have chosen.

1.4 Important note for removing an alias

You must only provide steps for a customer to remove an alias from an account if they are asking how a username or email can be completely disconnected from their account.

Removing the alias will have the following impacts:

  • The customer can’t sign in with that username
  • They can’t access emails to that address if it was a Microsoft provided email address. If they remove a non-primary Microsoft provided email alias, they won't be able to use it to send or receive email through Outlook.com, but existing mail will remain in their inbox.
  • They will not be able to re-register that email address later.

Alias management will only change the username associated with their account. Removing an address will not change any behavior on the account except for the associated username and potentially the email that is registered within the account if it’s a Microsoft provided email.

Never recommend a customer change or remove an alias to try to resolve an issue the customer is encountering on their account or give them the impression that they can use change alias to move account assets between accounts (for example, gamertags).

1.5 Engagement: Alias rename exception

In certain cases, the customer may not be able to change their alias even if they can sign-into their account and can access their security proofs.

  • If all the above steps in sections 1.1 - 1.3 have been completed, and the customer still cannot rename their alias, follow the steps below.
  • If the customer receives the error message: "You already renamed this account, please try again later.", they may have hit their rename limits. The ACA team can override the rename limit once per year, so check to see if the customer has been previously escalated to them for this same issue within the last year.
    • If they have not, escalate to ACA using the procedure below.
    • If they have been previously escalated to ACA for this same issue, explain to the customer they will have to wait a period of time and try to rename again later. Do not reveal to the customer that there are rename limits as that may expose security risks.

To verify a customer’s account and match their provided Unique ID:

  1. Search in MST for the customer by the gamertag or Microsoft account that they provide.
  2. Verify ownership of the account (see KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification).
  3. Confirm the customer’s Microsoft account using MST.

[Asset 4463158]

  1. Direct the customer to http://account.microsoft.com and tell them to try to sign-in with the exact Microsoft account provided in MST. Once there, have the customer follow the steps below. If they can’t sign-in, refer to KB 298471 to assist with password reset.
  2. Once there, have the customer follow the steps below.

Note: If the customer is asked to verify their security proofs, have them verify. If the customer does not have access to their security proofs, see KB 2984702.

  1. Select Security and more security option.
  2. At the bottom of the page, review the Unique ID, which is 16-characters long that's mix of numbers and letters.
  3. Make sure the customer’s Unique ID matches the PUID in MST. You will see this below the gamertag. If the ID matches, engage with ACA. Be sure to notate that you checked for this in the notes; however, DO NOT include the customers provided PUID anywhere in the notes, as that is PII and compromises customer security.

  • The primary alias and the new alias need to be verified before the primary alias can be changed/updated.
    • Primary aliases with Microsoft domains are verified upon creation. If the customer has a Microsoft domain primary alias, and is being asked to verify and is unable to, take note of the error message and engage with ACA.
  • If the customer has a Microsoft domain primary email and they don’t have to verify it, you should be able to resolve this. Assist the customer with updating their primary alias through the procedure in KB 2984702 Procedure: Microsoft account: Handling "Security Info Replacement" contacts and public content, How to change the email address associated with your Microsoft account.
