3. Procedure


Customer asks what purchase amount must be met to qualify for Invoice as a payment method.
 
Customers whose purchase meets or exceeds a pre-determined threshold may choose Invoice as a payment method. The purchase thresholds that qualify for Invoice as a payment method differ depending on the customer’s country. Invoice thresholds, accepted credit cards, currencies, local currency settlement, and pricing currencies by country can be found here.

Note: The Total of the purchase in the shopping Cart (including tax) is the amount used to determine if invoice pay will be offered as a payment method. The monthly or annual cost of other active orders on the tenant are not used to calculate the invoice threshold for the current purchase.
Back to the top
EMEA/APGC/NOAM customer wants to pay by invoice but does not meet the newest invoice pay threshold.
Tier 1 Instructions for EMEA/APGC/NOAM advocates:
If a customer would like to pay by invoice, and they meet the "old" invoice pay threshold, add the customer to the invoice pre-approval list in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT). Once the customer has been placed on the pre-approval list, ask them to retry the purchase. They will now have the option to select invoice pay as a payment method.

Customer asks what methods of payment are available to Invoice Pay customers.

How soon are payments applied to an Invoice Pay customer’s account?
The payment is applied within 2 business days of clearing the customer’s bank account; however, the payment will not appear on the customer’s invoice until the next billing cycle.

 Customer is trying to increase the number of seats for an already existing subscription, but he receives the error message "There is a problem. Try again later."​


You can guide the customer to check the “Ship To Address” for that specific Order ID to see whether the two letters listed there, representing customer’s country are written with capital letters. If they are not, help the customer update those to be written with capital letters, like for example “RU” for Russia, not “ru”.  This is a potential trouble shooting step to resolve the error.  If the error message is not fixed, continue with standard escalation procedure.

Adding a customer to the invoice pre-approval list - Commerce T1 advocate steps:
 
i. In CMAT, click the Quick Access menu in the top right and choose Manage Invoice Pre-approval list.
ii. The option Add customer into invoice pre-approval list is selected by default.
iii. Set Identity type to Tenant Guide.
iv. Enter the customer’s Tenant ID in the Input the ID field. 
v. In the Comments section add a note indicating the approximate value of the customer’s purchase.
vi. On the confirmation screen enter the case number and confirm the change.
vii. Use the following issue coding for the request:
Code Level 1: Commerce
Code Level 2: Order-Purchase
Code Level 3: Help making a purchase
Code Level 4: Add customer to invoice preapproval list

Customer would like to know where they could send their payment (For customers outside of NOAM and Canada).

For business customers who are asking for the payment instruction for their specific country and the copy of the invoice is not available (newly purchased plan, change in the lifecycle of the subscription will cause the invoice to generate on the next billing cycle), we can provide this support link:

https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/office365/admin/subscriptions-and-billing/pay-for-your-subscription?view=o365-worldwide

In searching for payment instructions for specific countries found listed on the drop down, if the country or location of the customer is not listed, frontline should reach out to their TLs/SMEs.

TLs/SMEs may reach out to GFS (Global Financial Services)Conference Bridge for further assistance so they can check with their Lead, or submit an Assistance Request to CAICashApps based on regional delivery structure.
