Procedure: Xbox: Code redemption issues (prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription)

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Xbox

What is this article about?

This article describes how to use token tools to troubleshoot code statuses and describes the procedure to follow when a customer has an issue redeeming a code. There are three important customer scenarios to verify before moving forward. First ensure that this isn’t an account issue. If the customer is in one of the following account states, follow appropriate content for instructions. Be sure the customer understands the redemption issues are tied to their account, not the code.

1. Guidelines

Is the customer banned?

If the customer’s account is banned, codes cannot be redeemed on the account. Do not compensate or provide any refunds for a customer banned for violating the Xbox Live Terms of Use. See KB 818917 Procedure: Xbox: How to respond to a customer who wants to cancel an Xbox Live Gold membership for additional details.

Are they in grace or locked for being in dunning?

If the customer is in dunning (has a past due balance), see KB 4547191 Procedure: Xbox: Dunning support for Xbox subscriptions for guidance on how to handle these cases.

Does the customer have an issue or questions about Xbox Game Pass Ultimate?

If the customer is having issues or questions about Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, see KB 4504690 Procedure: Game Pass Ultimate downgrade, cancel, and refund requests.

1.1 General procedure

Follow the troubleshooting process below for code redemption issues:

  • Provide customers with the self-help information provided on Troubleshoot Xbox Live code redemption issues for any future issues.
  • If the customer’s account is banned, codes cannot be redeemed on the account. Do not compensate or provide any refunds for a customer banned for violating the Xbox Live Terms of Use. See KB 818917 Procedure: Xbox: How to respond to a customer who wants to cancel an Xbox Live Gold membership for additional details.
  • If the customer is having any issues retrieving the game code for the “Name Your Game” Xbox One bundle, follow KB 3151186 Procedure: Xbox: Xbox One bundle content issues. For troubleshooting the game codes that customers have received for this offer, follow standard troubleshooting below.
  • If the customer is receiving a risk reject alert indicating the code needs to be redeemed from Brazil or Mexico, direct the customer back to the retailer. Do not deactivate and replace the code. See KB 4013188 Informational: Xbox: Xbox Live Geo Fencing FAQ.
    • Xbox Live codes originating from Brazil can only be redeemed within Brazil.
    • Xbox Live codes originating from Mexico can only be redeemed within Mexico.
  • Do not provide more than the listed amount owed to the customer for replacement in the scenarios of this article. In any instance that you have sent codes that are beyond the amount due:
    • Deactivate the appropriate excess codes.
    • Advise the customer of the error and explain that the excess codes have been deactivated and cannot be used.
    • Note in the service request (SR) that these actions have been taken.
    • Customers must have a billing profile for codes to be issued. (The customer has set up a payment instrument or has a positive Microsoft account balance.) If no billing profile exists, the customer must add a credit card to trigger the system to create a billing profile. 
  • When issuing a token, provide a brief description detailing the reason for the token and the SR’s in the Comments field for subscription transfers.
  • If the subscription is not in dunning and has an issue where it must be canceled and reapplied to the same account for troubleshooting, use reason code: Entitlement issue.
  • If the subscription was cancelled in error by an Xbox support advocate. See KB 2487647 Procedure: Xbox: Subscription cancelled in error by an Xbox support agent. If the customer is owed time after reviewing the article above, use reason code: Entitlement issue.

1.2 Proof of purchase (PoP) and token (PPC) image guidance

When a Proof of purchase and/or a token (PPC) image is required for any of the below scenarios, follow this guidance:

  • Review the customer’s SR history to determine if they have contacted support about a similar issue. If possible, check for similar submittals of PoP where customers may attempt to use the same images for different 5x5 codes. Also, if physical images had a previous SR written on it, make sure to check it. If it was scratched out by the customer, ask the customer what the SR was.
  • If the customer has already received Xbox Tokens and continues to contact support using the same PoP, mention the previous SR where they received the replacement time and decline further assistance. Report the customer through the MST Fraud & Phishing button and advise that their account may be escalated for further investigation.
  • Sensitive/highly confidential and confidential personal data must be handled through the Data Transfer and Management (DTM) tool. Note that images provided by the customer should not be deleted by advocates unless requested by customers. If requested, note within the SR why images were deleted.
  • A valid proof of purchase could be a screenshot of the receipt or an email attachment of the receipt from the retailer.
    • If the PoP is a physical receipt and PPC, request the customer write down their SR number on both the receipt and the back of the 5x5 card without using image editor software or apps and obtain images that are not blurry. Also, the back of the PPC images must show the whole PPC with nothing covered or obscured.
    • If PoP is an e-receipt or PDF sent by the retailer, the customer must provide a copy of the original email order summary sent by the retailer as an additional attachment (to ensure it is not coming from a random seller or individual).
      • Do not inform the customer of the required criteria in this article. This is internal information to help confirm if the receipt images are valid. If the below criteria are not met on any image, seek guidance from a Ranger to advise the customer we may not be able to assist further.
      • If an e-receipt or PDF is provided, the included copy of the order confirmation email sent by the retailer must have the following information:
        • Retailer name
        • Retailer email address
        • The date & time the email was received (usually in the top right or left corner of the email). 
        • Look for words like digital or shipped in the retailer email to ensure the customer’s story matches the retailer email or receipt.
    • PoP receipt images must show the following information:
      • Name of the retailer
      • Invoice/bill number
      • Purchase amount
      • Date of sale
      • Product description

1.3 Handling customer inquiries on codes to confirm the state, redemption date, expiration date, or description

Customers may contact support asking to determine the current state, expiration date, redeemed date or type of code, so they know what they can resell. Direct customers to https://redeem.microsoft.com/ so they can self-serve to obtain metadata on the codes. Do not provide any details such as redemption date, description of the 5x5, or expiration date. The online flow provides information on whether the code is invalid, inactive, has been redeemed already, or active and will show an image of the content before it can be redeemed.

3. Code status troubleshooting 

3.1 Active

Status

Reason Code

Active

Replacing subscription time – Active

 

1. Ask the customer to re-enter the code online and on the console to make sure they are entering the code correctly. See below for valid characters used in codes.

2. Verify the account is not in dunning or risk rejection.

An account can be suspended for the following reasons:

  • Risk rejection/account violation
  • If there is a billing account violation on the account, see KB 931798 Procedure: How to handle billing account violations (formerly account locks).

3. Verify the customer is in dunning by checking for a past due balance in the Manage subscriptions tab. If the customer has a modern account, you may not see a balance in the main MST tab so Purchase History/Subscriptions to verify. KB 2852430 Procedure: Subscription payment is declined.

[Asset 4517062]

4. Verify the customer has a billing profile attached to the account.

  • Check the Payment instrument section in MST. If they have any information listed, they do have a billing profile.
  • If they do not, select Add PI, which will give you a message stating that they do or do not have a billing account.
  • If Add PI revealed the customer has no billing profiles, ask the customer to sign in to account.microsoft.com and update their billing information, because a code cannot be applied without it.

5. Verify the content of the code.

  • If the code is for content that has not been released yet, such as a game or DLC, the code will not be redeemable until the release date.

6. Check the subscription status:

  • If the subscription is in dunning, the customer may have trouble redeeming codes until the balance has been paid. KB 2852430 Procedure: Subscription payment is declined.

In these cases, you may suggest canceling a subscription in dunning for an Xbox customer to redeem a token. Do not suggest a refund.

  • Trial subscription codes can't be redeemed on active Gold subscriptions, nor can they be redeemed on accounts that have already had a trial of that subscription type. The customer must wait until active subscriptions have ended before redeeming a trial code.
  • Check to see how many subscription codes are already stacked on the account. Customers can only stack a maximum of 36 months per account.

7. Check the region:

  • Confirm what region the account is set to from the Xbox Live Service Status section in MST.
  • Confirm the country the system believes the customer is connecting from by checking the sign-in history of the Xbox Live Service section in MST.
  • If the customer is redeeming an Xbox Live subscription code, the customer’s region must be on the Xbox Live Countries and Regions list, or they will not be able to redeem an Xbox Live subscription code.
  • Ask the customer what region or country the code was purchased in.
  • All three regions checked above must match, the region of the code must match the region the account is set to and the region the customer is connecting from.
    • If the code does not match the other region settings, follow the code replacement procedure if the customer has not received a similar code for out of region in the past year.
    • If the region the account is set to doesn’t match the connecting region, advise the customer to change their account region via Xbox.com region migration. For any issues with the region migration, see KB 3038830 Procedure: Helping customers with account migration.

8. Ask the customer for any error message they receive when attempting to enter the code. Using the Error Code Lookup tool in the solutions and tools sidebar of ASD, look up the error code and follow any troubleshooting it provides.

9. If the customer can't redeem the code after exploring all previous troubleshooting, obtain and review an image of the proof of purchase and images of the front and back of the customer’s code to ensure they are valid.

  • Once PoP and code images are verified, deactivate the original code, and provide new a code via the Add promotional CSV function of MST.
    • Review the customer’s support contact history for previous similar code redemption issues.
  • If the customer has had previous code redemption issues, include the previous service request number in your notes so the current redemption issue can be addressed.
  • If this is the first contact for a redemption issue on this account, credit the customer’s account with the amount of CSV needed to process a purchase on behalf of (POBO) for the replacement of the code content and then process the POBO. See KB 4578369 Procedure: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) Consent for guidance, and see KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) for information on POBO and mandatory consent actions.
  • Report the missing content of the Issue Token tool to a site Ranger.
  • If the option to deactivate is not shown in the token lookup tool, engage your Ranger for deactivation.
  • For CSV eGiftcards/codes, Tier 1 advocates may replace the value of the CSV eGiftcard/code up to $100 USD (or market equivalent) in CSV via MST. For replacement requests that total over $100 USD for CSV eGiftcards/codes, engage the Ranger/Supervisor for specific approval.

10. If all the above troubleshooting has been completed and the customer is still unable to redeem the code, engage a Ranger or Supervisor.

3.3 Damaged

Status

Reason Code

Damaged

Damaged token

Note:

Engagement with the Ranger/Supervisor required for specific approval only for replacement scenarios when the code is for CSV over $100 USD in value.

Codes are considered damaged when the code is illegible due to physical damage.

The customer may have scratched too hard or used a sharp object to remove the silver, or the code may have been damaged in transit. All reasons are considered in scope under this procedure.

For all damaged code issues, obtain digital pictures of the physical card (front side and backside) and the customer’s proof of purchase. You may be able to obtain the code info if the back of the card has a serial number in the bottom left corner.

  1. Use the MST Token Lookup tool to reverse look up the serial number of the damaged 5x5 (The 5x5 code or serial number may not be shown for all code types, in which case move on to the replacement criteria section).
  2. If the Token Lookup tool returns the 5x5 and shows it as active, compare the code information to the proof of purchase and images of the card the customer provided.
  3. If the information matches, you can provide the active 5x5 to the customer for redemption with no need to generate a replacement.

[Asset 4490624]

Replacement criteria:

  • We can replace any cards for CSV or Xbox Live subscriptions as well as any card that is included in a Microsoft-boxed product.
  • We do not replace codes that are printed on receipts. Customers must return to the retailer for replacement.
  • Codes that do not meet the criteria cannot be replaced, and the customer should be directed to the publisher or retailer for assistance.
  • Here is a contact list for the top third-party publishers:

http://support.xbox.com/browse/xbox-one/games/Third-party%20games

Replacement procedure:

  1. Review and validate the proof of purchase and pictures of the card that were obtained during the start of the call.
  2. Confirm there's a replacement code in the token tool.
  3. After verifying the customer’s proof of purchase and 5X5, provide a replacement code via the Issue Token function of MST.

Note: For CSV eGiftcards/codes, Tier 1 advocates may replace the value of the CSV eGiftcard/code up to $100 USD (or market equivalent) in CSV via MST. For replacement requests that total over $100 USD for CSV eGiftcards/codes, engage Ranger/Supervisor for specific approval.

  1. If the code content is not listed in the Issue Token tool, credit the customer’s account with the amount of CSV needed to process a POBO for the replacement of the code content and then process the POBO. Then, report the missing content of the Issue Token tool to a site Ranger.
    • For details on how to purchase on behalf of a customer follow guidance in KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) and for information on POBO and mandatory consent actions see KB 4578369 Procedure: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) Consent.

Note: If a replacement code is issued, the old code must be deactivated.

3.5 Redeemed/Consumed

This status will also appear as claimed in MST

Status

Reason Code

Redeemed/consumed

For redeemed before the date of purchase – Reason Code: Replacing subscription time – Redeemed/Consumed.

For redeemed subscription to incorrect Microsoft account – Reason Code: Replacing subscription time - Subscription Transfer.

For redeemed but not applied to the account – Reason Code: Entitlement issue.

Note:

Engagement with a Ranger or Supervisor is required for specific approval only for replacement scenarios when the code is for CSV over $100 USD in value.

2-day, 14-day, and 1-month Gold trials redeemed on an incorrect Gamertag or Microsoft account cannot be transferred and shouldn’t be replaced.

Check the Customer Redeemed field in the Token Lookup tool to see whether the code was redeemed to the current profile.

  • If the code was redeemed on the customer’s account, confirm the code has been applied to the account.
    • After looking up the 5x5 to validate it was redeemed on the account check the Entitlements section in MST via Windows Store and Xbox One Entitlements to see if there is an active entitlement that matches the redeem date.
      • Redeemed but not applied: If there is no active entitlement, proceed to the replacement procedure outlined below and use the reason code: Entitlement issue
      • If there is an active entitlement, proceed with additional troubleshooting steps see KB 921272 Procedure: Xbox downloadable content (DLC) license/entitlement issues.
  • If the code was redeemed on another account, attempt to verify the customer on the account the code was redeemed to.
    • CSV, Game or DLC codes: If the item was redeemed on the incorrect Microsoft account (the account redeemed to can be verified), inform the customer we are unable to transfer it to a different account. Inform the customer that gifting may be an option to consume the CSV to provide content to the desired Microsoft account. Do not issue replacement codes or CSV for redemption on the desired account.
    • Subscription codes: Check the Services section in MST to locate the subscription that aligns with the code. Check the History tab of the subscriptions for insight, as the subscription may have been canceled after the code was redeemed. Do not replace the remaining subscription period.
    • If a customer redeemed a three-month or longer Xbox subscription on the incorrect Microsoft account and contacts us within 30 days of redemption Advocates must cancel the subscription and redeem a replacement token on the customer’s desired gamertag or Microsoft account using the reason code: Replacing subscription time - Subscription Transfer. If the customer redeemed a one-month code, they must contact us within five days of the redemption to qualify for redeeming a replacement token on the desired gamertag or Microsoft account.
      • Note: Do not use individual one-month codes to replace longer subscriptions; use the closest duration codes available.
      • Follow guidance in section 4 of KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool to redeem on behalf of (ROBO) customers.

        For example, to replace a 24-month Xbox All Access Game Pass Ultimate subscription, ROBO two 12-month Ultimate tokens.

      • After confirming the subscription is active on the correct account, cancel the subscription the customer redeemed on the wrong Microsoft account.

  • If the code was redeemed on another account that the customer cannot verify, refer the customer to the retailer from which they purchased the code.
    • Additional guidance if the customer has contacted the retailer and they are unable to replace the code when the proof of purchase shows that the product was acquired after the consumption (Redeemed before date of Purchase): You can obtain the proof of purchase and card to validate the date it was purchased. If the 5x5 was consumed before the purchase date shown on the POP and the customer has not received a replacement you can use token tool to replace the code using reason code: Replacing subscription time – Redeemed/Consumed

Replacement procedure:

  • Confirm the customer's content is available in the token tool. If the code content is not listed in the Issue Token tool, credit the customer’s account with the amount of CSV needed to process a POBO for the replacement of the code content, then process the POBO.
  • CSV SKU – CSS Xbox Store Make it right
  • Redeemed but not applied – Reason: Goodwill Make It Right gesture
  • Redeemed before the date of purchase – Reason: Replace value of content for prepaid code/token that cannot be redeemed
  • Report the missing content in the Issue Token tool to a site Ranger. For details on how to POBO a customer follow the guidance in KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on behalf of (POBO), and for information on POBO and mandatory consent actions see KB 4578369 Procedure: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) Consent.
  • For CSV eGiftcards/codes, you may replace the value of the CSV eGiftcard/code up to $100 USD (or market equivalent) in CSV via MST. For replacement requests that total over $100 USD for CSV eGiftcards/codes, engage Ranger/Supervisor for specific approval.

If a customer is reporting they no longer have access to the content (Entitlement shows as Revoked), but the order status is listed as Canceled Reason: Fraud, direct them to the retailer where the code was purchased. Do not replace the code, even if the customer can provide a proof of purchase.

5. Redeeming a subscription code when a legacy membership is in decline or suspended

If the customer does not have access to a console or cannot sign in online, have them call back later. You cannot proceed with any form of assistance until you can confirm the customer will be able to redeem the code on the console or online.

  1. Validate the code is active.
  2. Use MST to search the code provided by the customer. See section 15.2 Using the Lookup Tool in KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST).
    • An Active status means the code is valid and it has not been used by someone else.
    • If the status returned is anything other than Active, you cannot use this procedure. See KB 2896711 Procedure: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops to resolve the code issue.
  3. Update the payment instrument if needed.
  4. If a subscription is in Dunning, see KB 4547191 Procedure: Xbox: Dunning support for Xbox subscriptions for steps to correct the balance.
  • If the subscription was in decline, check the status of the payment instrument and take the necessary steps to update or remove it.
  1. Once the customer has paid the balance and MST is showing no balance. help the customer redeem the code (if it hasn’t already been redeemed) by using the following link: How to redeem gift cards and codes.
  • If a current subscription is a legacy subscription, you may need to cancel the subscription and add the missing time back to the account using the token tool. When applying the token reason code: Entitlement issue and help the customer redeem the code again.
  1. If the customer is still unable to redeem their token, follow the replacement steps listed in section 3.1 step 9.
  2. When the customer has successfully redeemed the code, confirm that the customer can access Xbox Live Gold features.
