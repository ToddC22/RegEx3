What is this article about?

This collection of articles detail the steps that Tech Router will take to assist customers with new or continuing technical support cases in the Dynamics for Microsoft (DfM) tool.

Dynamics for Microsoft vs OneSupport

The "official" name of this tool is Dynamics for Microsoft or DfM; however, the development team referred to the tool during creation as "OneSupport" as that is the URL used to access it.

You may notice videos or KB content mentioning OneSupport, this is simply the pre-production name of DfM.

 

Dynamics for Microsoft is accessed at:  https://aka.ms/onesupport

Because DfM doesn't use your standard Microsoft credentials, if you are logged in with our MS credentials and attempt to open DfM you will receive an error that "You are not a member of this organization"

TIP

The DfM login will be in the format of:

  • alias@microsoftsupport.com

While you can log out in your browser (or open an InPrivate window), then access the site, and then log in with your alternate credentials this:

  • Conflicts with other resources you may be accessing using your Microsoft credentials
  • Is time consuming and requires that you repeat the same steps every time you access DfM

The recommended method is to create a 2nd browser profile with your DfM credentials, which greatly expedites accessing it each day.

  1. Within Edge in the Top/Right corner of the browser click on your account avatar (next to the ellipses)
  2. Click on Add profile
    [Asset 4569272]
  3. A new window appears, click on Confirm
  4. The browser refreshes, click again on the avatar and you will see a new Profile with the option to Sign in, click on Sign in
  5. Choose Work or School Account, and then Continue 
  6. Enter your credentials 

The profile will be saved and you can select it to use DfM.  Clicking on the new profile opens a new browser window logged in with that new account.  This account can have its own separate favorites (e.g. independent of your primary MS login info's profile).
