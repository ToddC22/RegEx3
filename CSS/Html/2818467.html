2. Guidance

Academic specific offers are available only to customers that are verified as supported academic institutions.
The first verification is conducted when the customer adds their vanity domain to their account during the sign-up flow for activating Office 365 Education A3 for Faculty Trial or Office 365 Education A3 for Students Trial.
If the vanity domain is not approved, the customer will require manual verification and tagging.
The EDU tag allows the customer to see and purchase the Academic offers in their portal. 

NOTE: Once a customer has been tagged as EDU, they will be able to see and provision both EDU and Commercial offers for their tenant in the Microsoft 365 admin center. If a customer tagged as EDU wants to have their tag removed in order to convert their tenant to a non-EDU tenant, refer to Scenario 5. Customer who is tagged as EDU wants to buy non-EDU offers.

Institutions eligible for Academic offers:

Requirements to qualify for Office 365 for education plans depends upon the region. Generally, the following types of institutions will qualify:
  • Education institutions - Accredited institutions organized and operated exclusively for the purpose of teaching its enrolled students.
  • Administrative offices and Boards of Education - Organized and operated for the administration of educational institutions.
  • Public institutions:
    • Public libraries - Primarily provide general library services without charge to all residents of a given community, district or region.
    • Public museums - Public or private agency or institution organized on a permanent basis for essential education or aesthetic purposes.
  • Religious institutions - Must provide educational services to the public at large on a regular basis, with or without charges.
  • Hospitals, Healthcare Systems, and Research Laboratories - If they are wholly owned and operated by a qualified Educational Institution as defined above. “Wholly owned and operated” means that the Educational Institution is the sole owner of the said hospital, healthcare system or research laboratory and the only entity exercising control over the said institution’s day-to-day operations.
You can refer to this site for regional differences for Educational Institutions: http://www.microsoftvolumelicensing.com/DocumentSearch.aspx?Mode=3&DocumentTypeId=7.

Note: These institutions don’t guarantee that the customer’s account will be approved for EDU offers and they still need to be validated.

3. Procedure

Customers that have NOT activated an Edu trial on their tenant need to be redirected to the online process. Ask the customer to sign up for an Edu trial at this link and most importantly complete the domain verification process (self-help available at this link). This will kick off the Edu validation process in the background. Even if the customer is not auto-qualified from the domain add, the domain being verified on the tenant is required before the EDU tag is added in CMAT.

Note: If the customer has been using a Commercial tenant which has stored data, please proceed to manual validation instead of asking the customer to follow the online process.

Requests might take up to 2 weeks. Ask the customer to contact support again on the 15th day, in the unlikely event that no reply is received to the validation request. Close the case.

NOTE: A verified vanity domain (** not onmicrosoft.com domain) on the customer’s account is required even for manual validation request. This is to ensure the requester owns the domain being approved for edu qualification.

Validation & Tagging steps


This section describes the validation steps completed by advocates empowered for EDU validations.

Step 1: Validation:
  1. Review URL:

    Agents are able to validate EDU customer right away through the phone during the support by checking the customer’s website to see if the customer is a qualified Academic institution as per
    http://www.microsoftvolumelicensing.com/DocumentSearch.aspx?Mode=3&DocumentTypeId=7*
    • If yes, proceed to Step 2.
    • If no, communicate to the agent (case owner) that we are unable to verify eligibility at this stage and the questionnaire needs to be sent to the customer. (step 1.b.)
    Please consider below best practices for website checking:
    • Does the website URL correspond to the name of the institution?
      • If not, search (using Google/Bing/etc) for the school name found on the website being reviewed. If the results match the website being reviewed, move ahead.
        • If the search comes back with a different URL for the school, fill out the questionnaire in step 1.b. and escalate to the regional EDU POC for further investigation.
    • Religious institutions – if they have a mainstream education (science, math, language, etc.) we can approve them.
    • An organization such as a church or temple where the only education is a “Sunday School” or something equivalent to exclusive religious material does not qualify.

      NOTE: If upon validation and the organization has been denied because it falls under this category, the agent should set the tagging to decline in CMAT after informing the customer.
    • Best things to look for:
      • Staff Listing – Teachers, Faculty, etc.
      • Classes – Schedules, Grades, Groups, etc.
      • Certifications – Ofsted Report (UK), other government recognized awards/labels/organization membership.
      • Subjects – types of subjects (science, math, language, reading, writing, etc.).
      • Diplomas or Degrees Offered – k-12 (or equivalent for that country), Bachelor’s, Master’s, other advanced degrees or certifications.
  2. Review questionnaire:
    If the agent is unsure the eligibility of the customer at this stage, ask the customer questionnaire (embedded above) over the phone and document answers in case notes. Use questionnaire answers to determine if the customer qualifies. Obtain answers to the questionnaire filled out by the customer from the frontline and then complete the review to define if the customer is a qualified Academic institution as per http://www.microsoftvolumelicensing.com/DocumentSearch.aspx?Mode=3&DocumentTypeId=7*
    • If validated successfully, proceed to Step 2.
    • If no, escalate to regional EDU POC.
  3. Escalate to regional EDU POC:

    This process should be used as the exception if the request cannot be validated successfully due to lack of information or language barrier.
    • Select the appropriate regional EDU lead based on the country in Regional POC lead list.
    • Email regional EDU POC if you cannot validate the request due to the language barrier and/or insufficient information provided by the customer.
      1. Regional POC responds with approval or denial.
      2. No Response, Response Not Received: Agent sends 1 reminder per day after the first request.
        If no response within 3 business, escalate by adding EDU Lead to the CC line of the request until resolved.
        EDU Lead will take over emails to Regional POC and advise agent what they will need to do and when/if the request is approved/denied.

      NOTE: Italian customers determine their own EDU eligibility. If Italian customers reply to their questionnaire claiming eligibility for EDU, we are to approve in CMAT. There is no need to send to Italian Regional POC for additional approval.

No. Please engage Tier 3 through the POD, indicating that the customer has been tagged as EDU but continues to be unable to purchase academic offers. Please be explicit about the offer the customer is unable to see in the portal. Refer to 2778091: How to escalate a Case to Tier 3, Engineering.

Scenario 2. The customer has received an email informing them that they have been denied the EDU qualification, and they do not agree with this decision.
a. Verify the customer Tags in CMAT.
Suggested script “I apologize for the inconvenience, let me confirm the status in our system.”
Steps View the TAGS (QUALIFICATIONS) field on the ACCOUNT page of the tenant in CMAT.
[Asset 4551794]
Validation Is the EDU tag present and is set to “approved”?

Yes. Verify with the customer on the phone that they are now able to see the EDU offers available for purchase in the Microsoft 365 admin center. If not, proceed to step d in scenario 1: Customer has the EDU tag in CMAT but does not have the option to purchase academic offers in their portal.

No. Proceed to step b.
 
b. The customer does not have EDU in the Tags field or EDU tag is set to “declined”. Look for an existing case with reasons on why EDU tag was “declined” and communicate the reasons to the customer. If “declined” is due to “customer unresponsiveness”, resubmit the request with customer info to regional POC for validation again. 

NOTE: If an existing case states that a customer has been declined EDU eligibility as not being a valid academic institution, the tenant isn’t eligible. There is no further escalation path. If the customer has changed status and is now a valid institution, the agent can re-validate without escalation.

Scenario 3.﻿ Customers with an existing tenant who want to be validated and tagged as EDU, use the following guidelines:﻿
  1. The customer has not signed up for a new EDU trial – follow the same steps above to validate and tag the existing tenant.
  2. The customer signed up for a new EDU trial less than 30 days ago (is still in the trial period) – follow the same steps above to validate and tag the tenant. 
  3. The customer signed up for a trial more than 30 days ago, so is already paying full price – follow the same process above to validate and tag the tenant. Once the tenant has been tagged, follow KB 2996655 Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT to credit the amount the customer should not have been paying.﻿

Scenario 4. Bulk validation requests. Allow customers with multiple domains to raise a single request:


This process allows customers to raise one request for bulk validations, instead of having to contact Commercial Accounts & Billing support for each domain.

  • If the Frontline team receives a bulk validation request from the customer, the agent must create a new case for each tenant that needs to be validated.

Note: The cases should be logged under the appropriate tenant in the ticketing system and all new cases must be cross-referenced with the original master case.

Scenario 5. A customer tagged as EDU wants to buy non-EDU offers.

Note: All EDU subscriptions must be in a non-Active state (In grace period, disabled, retired) prior to removing the EDU tag. 

a. Check CMAT to see if the tenant is tagged as EDU and the tag is approved. 

Suggested script

Not Applicable

Steps

i.  Verify the customer has an EDU tag on their tenant that shows approved.

[Asset 4014500]

Validation

Yes. Proceed to step b.
No. Advise the customer on how to purchase using 2793018: Helping a customer buy subscriptions and add-ons.

b. Transfer the case to the Tier 3 team.

Suggested script

"I will need to transfer your request for processing. I will follow up with you when complete."

Steps

i. Transfer the case to the Tier 3 team using article 2778091: How to escalate a Case to Tier 3, Engineering, including the following information in the template.

  • Please decline the <edu> tag on this customer’s tenant.
  • Reason: Customer no longer would like to use this tenant for <Academic> only offers.
  • Organization Name:
  • Tenant Domain (*.onmicrosoft.com )

ii. The Tier 3 team will perform the requested action within 1 business day and send the ticket back to Support for customer follow-up.

c. When the case is returned to Support from the Tier 3 team:

Steps

i. Check the EDU tag. The tag must be declined to allow the customer to purchase the standard enterprise subscriptions.

Validation

i. Is the tag correct?

Yes. Follow up with the customer.
No. Transfer ticket back to the Tier 3 team restating the original request.

d. Questions:

  • What happens to the old EDU subscription?
    • The old EDU subscription will go through the normal subscription lifecycle. The EDU subscription and its lifecycle state will continue to be visible as a line item on the Subscriptions page in the Microsoft 365 admin center, even after the customer has purchased a non-EDU subscription.
  • Can users from the EDU subscription be assigned to the non-EDU subscription?
    • Yes.
  • Can an EDU trial be converted to a non-EDU paid subscription?
    • No.
