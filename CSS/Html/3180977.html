Technical Guide: Surface: Surface battery and charging

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Surface

What is this article about?

This article will help you in addressing common Surface battery issues that your customer may encounter. This article should be used any time you work with a customer to resolve their Surface battery issue. If there is a physical problem with the customer's PSU, use KB 3158368 Technical Guide: Surface: Power supply unit issues instead.

Before troubleshooting this issue, confirm with the customer whether the PSU tip is on, off, or flashing. If the PSU tip is on, then also confirm what the battery charge status is in Windows by hovering over the battery icon in the taskbar.

Table of contents

  1. PSU tip doesn’t light

  2. PSU tip is blinking while connected to Surface

  3. Requires PSU: Battery not detected (red X) or Plugged in - not charging or Plugged in – charging

  4. Runs on battery: Won't charge to 100% - plugged in - not charging or Won't charge to 100% - plugged in - charging

  5. Tip lit: No charging status displayed in Windows

  6. Short battery life - lasts hours

  7. Short battery life - lasts minutes

  8. Sound/Noise from Surface while charging 

1. PSU tip doesn’t light

Confirming the issue

When the customer connects the PSU to wall outlet and to the Surface device the PSU tip (White) doesn’t light up.
[Asset 4495639]

Note: White light is not on.

Confirmation/Paraphrase

“Just to make sure I understand your issue: When you plug in your PSU the PSU tip (white) light on the connector doesn’t come on?”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Surface Device > Battery and charging > Power Supply-Surface > Tip doesn’t light

Resolution steps

Follow the steps in the order below until the issue is resolved or until all the steps have been completed. While following the steps, use the "Why" column to inform the customer why you are performing the step and, if successful, to better understand what the cause may have been. As you follow the steps, document the result of each one in your notes. Then proceed to the "Is the issue resolved?" section at the end of the table for next steps.

There may be a current known issue that the customer is experiencing, with different troubleshooting steps.

Check known issues regularly.

3. Disconnect the PSU and check connections.

[Asset 4495572]

  • A: Unplug the power connector from side of device.
  • B: Unplug the PSU from the wall outlet.
  • C: Check that the power cord is securely attached to the PSU.
  • D: Unplug any USB devices from the charge port on the PSU (Not available on all PSUs).
  • A: Wipe the pins on the power connector with a soft, dry, lint-free cloth (microfiber cloths work great).
  • B: Plug the PSU back into the wall outlet. If possible, try a different wall outlet.
  • A: Plug the power connector back into the side of the device. Press the side of the connector to ensure it's fully inserted.

Important: Be sure to wait 10 seconds after unplugging the PSU from the wall before reconnecting it. This step lets the PSU reset.

Unplugging from the wall outlet for at least 10 seconds resets the PSU.

 

Verifies all connections are secure.

Dirt or debris on the connector’s pins could be preventing a power connection.

4. Check the PSU and cables for any physical damage.

Ask the customer to check PSU and cables for any frayed or damage. See KB 2791320 Informational: Surface: Exchange and fulfillment landing page for information on customer induced damage and warranty.

Damaged PSU or cables may cause the PSU to not function as intended.

5. Determine whether the charger is the in-box charger, a different Surface charger, or a third-party charger.

Ask if the customer is using the charger/power supply unit (PSU) that came with their Surface, a different Surface PSU, or a third-party PSU. If they are not sure, direct the customer to take a photo of the back of the PSU block and send it to you. There are several methods to identify the type of PSU, including:

  • The model number is listed – see #1 in the image below.
  • Depending on the PSU, the wattage of the PSU may be listed the bottom also - #2 below.

A Microsoft PSU should contain icons/certifications similar to the following image:

[Asset 4493886]

 

It’s important to determine if the PSU was supplied by Microsoft or a third party.

Note: If it’s a third-party PSU, advise the customer to contact that manufacturer.

Note: If the customer is not using the Surface PSU that came with their device, they may need to switch to the correct PSU. For more information, see How to charge Surface (see Surface power supplies and charging requirements section).

6. Surface 3 only: Attempt to use different a USB cable than what came in-box with the Surface.

Ask the customer if they have a Micro to USB cable they can try instead of the one that came with Surface.

 

This will help isolate if the Micro to USB cable is the issue.

7. Change the orientation of the power connector

Try turning the power connector over and plugging into the device from a different orientation.

Surface devices are designed to be charged with the power connector orientated in either direction.

If the power connector only works in one orientation, there is likely a problem with the Surface PSU.

8. Try another Surface branded PSU.

Ask the customer if they have another Surface charger they can try.

This will help isolate if this issue is with charger or the device.
9. Test the customer's charger on another Surface. Ask the customer if they have another Surface device that the charger could be tried on. This will help isolate if this issue is with charger or the device.

Is the issue resolved?

Yes, close the case: Once resolved, have the customer check for updates.

For SDT enabled sites, let the customer know about the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit.

For all other sites, check Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update for updates.

KB 4014334 Procedure: Surface: Using the Microsoft Surface Diagnostic Toolkit

Making sure Surface and Windows are up to date may help avoid this problem in the future.

No, replace the PSU: Set up an exchange for the customer based on the status of their warranty.

Use KB 2791320 Informational: Surface: Exchange and fulfillment landing page landing page to create a service order.

There is likely a hardware problem with the Surface PSU.

3. Requires PSU: Battery not detected (red X) or plugged in - not charging or plugged in – charging

Confirming the issue

The power supply is connected, the power connector light is on, and when attempting to charge the Surface, the customer observes one of the following, and the Surface turns off when the customer unplugs it.

The battery icon will display a power plug and a red X.

[Asset 4456513]

The battery icon has a power plug, but when you hover on the battery icon in the desktop notification area, it shows plugged in, not charging.

[Asset 4456514]

The battery icon has a power plug, and the battery shows a charge.

[Asset 4456515]

 

Confirmation/Paraphrase

“Just to make sure I understand your issue: Your device will only work when the power supply is connected. When you unplug it, it immediately turns off.”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Surface Device > Battery and charging > Battery > Requires PSU – Battery Not Detected (Red X)
  • Surface Device > Battery and charging > Battery > Requires PSU – Plugged in – Not Charging

5. Remove and reinstall battery driver.

1. From Device Manager, expand the Batteries category.

2. Double-tap or double-click Microsoft Surface ACPI-Compliant Control Method Battery, select the Driver tab, and select Uninstall > OK.

Note: For Surface Book devices there will be two battery drivers to uninstall.

3. From the Action menu, select Scan for hardware changes.

More info at Surface battery won’t charge.

An incorrect battery driver could be installed on the Surface.

6. Run SDT or Windows Updates (WU).

For SDT: Have your customer go to http://aka.ms/surfacedt.

For WU: Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Updates > Check for updates.

Note: Restart the Surface after installing updates.

Updates have fixes to common problems, or issues. Ensuring the customer has all the latest updates on their Surface will ensure they have the best possible experience.

7. Force the device to shut down and restart (varies by new vs old generation).

Hold the power button for 10 seconds to force Windows to quit. Press once again start Windows. If that does not work, follow these steps by model:

Studio (all), Laptop (all), Pro 1796 (5th Gen), Pro 6, Book 2, and Go: Press and hold the power button for 20 seconds.

Other devices: Press and hold the volume-up button and the power button at the same time for at least 15 seconds and then release both.

The Surface may boot to UEFI. This is normal. Have the customer exit UEFI.

Video available at Force a shut down and restart your Surface.

The device may be in a “hung” state. Force the Surface to completely turn off all power and restart.

Performing a forced shutdown is like removing the battery from a laptop or unplugging a desktop PC. It resets the hardware and clears all the cache and memory for your hardware.

8. Reset using the most appropriate choice after explaining the options to the customer.

Good: Fresh Start

Go to Start > Settings > Update & security > Recovery.

Under “More recovery options,” select Learn how to start fresh with a clean installation of Windows. Follow the on-screen instructions.

Better: Reset from within Windows

Go to Start > Settings > Update & security > Recovery.

Under “Reset this PC,” select Get Started. Follow the on-screen instructions.

[Asset 4464331]

Fresh Start (good):

  • Fresh Start is the least intrusive form of reset.

  • Customers will not lose any files or personal information.

  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop programs.

  • Fresh Start also ensures the customer has all available updates once complete.

  • If there is an issue with Windows itself, the issue may still occur after a reset.

Reset from within Windows (better):

  • This option uses the devices Hard Drive Partition to reimage the device.

  • This option can delete personal data (customer can choose Keep my files or Remove everything).

  • The customer will need to reinstall desktop apps.

  • The customer will need to run updates upon completion.

  • If there is an issue with the partition, the issue may still occur after the reset.

USB recovery (best):

  • This option reimages the entire device, including the partition on the hard drive.

  • It is the most complete form of reset.

  • Customers will need to back up their personal data.

  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop apps. Customers will need to run updates after completion.

If the issue occurs after a reset, then the root cause is very likely hardware related.

4. Runs on battery: Won't charge to 100% - plugged in - not charging or won't charge to 100% - plugged in - charging

Confirming the issue

The power supply is connected, the power connector light is on, and when attempting to charge the Surface, the customer observes one of the following, and the Surface remains on when the customer unplugs it.

The battery icon has a power plug, but when you hover on the battery icon in the desktop notification area, it shows plugged in, not charging.

[Asset 4456518]

The battery icon has a power plug, and the battery shows a charge.

[Asset 4456519]

The battery does not fully charge to 100%.

 

 

Confirmation/Paraphrase

“Just to make sure I understand your issue: Your device appears to not be charging but will remain on when you unplug it.”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Surface Device > Battery and charging > Battery > Battery > Runs on Battery – Won’t Charge to 100% - Plugged in – not charging
  • Surface Device > Battery and charging > Battery > Battery > Runs on Battery - Won't Charge to 100% - Plugged in - charging

3. If the battery never charges above 50%, check for Battery Limit settings.

If charging is above 50%, skip to the next step.

1. Boot to UEFI and select Boot configuration.

2. Check if Battery Limit is enabled.

3. If the customer has this unintentionally enabled, disable it.

More info at Battery Limit setting.

The Battery Limit option is a UEFI setting that changes how the Surface device battery is charged and may prolong its longevity. This setting is recommended in cases in which the device is continuously connected to power, for example when devices are integrated into kiosk solutions.

Setting the device on Battery Limit changes the protocol for charging the device battery. When Battery Limit is enabled, the battery charge will be limited to 50% of its maximum capacity.

4. Is the device a Surface Book?

If the customer has a question about one of the batteries in the Surface Book not charging, this may be expected.

The batteries in Surface Book may charge at different rates, depending on the power state of each battery. Whichever battery has less than 20% charge will be charged first. When both batteries are at 20%, the clipboard will charge to 85%, and then both batteries will charge to 100%.

This may be expected behavior based on how the Surface Book charges one battery and then the other.

4. Check the Power mode slider.

On applicable models, the Power mode slider is found by selecting the battery icon on the Windows taskbar.

[Asset 4456521]

For best battery life, the slider should be set to Recommended.

If the customer needs to use the device for an extended period of time while on battery, the slider should be set to Battery Saver.

Surface devices have a configurable Power mode that lets the customer define their preference between Best battery life and Best performance.

The setting is configured separately for when the device is plugged and when it is on battery. This allows a customer to have this set to Best performance while on PSU and for it to automatically change to a different setting, like Recommended, while on battery.

5. Run Power troubleshooter.

From the Search box, type troubleshoot power and then open Find and fix problems with your computer’s power setting.

-OR-

This troubleshooter will automatically detect and fix these potential issues:

  • Time before display dims is too long.

  • Time before hard disk goes to sleep is too long.

  • Time before display goes to sleep is too long.

  • Power plan set to High Performance.

  • Time before computer goes to sleep is too long.

  • Minimum processor state is set too high.

  • Display brightness setting is too high.

  • Screen saver is enabled.

  • USB Selective Suspend is disabled.

  • Wireless adapter is not optimized for power saving.

6. Check Battery saver settings.

Open Settings > System > Battery

Check that Turn battery saver on automatically is enabled and that it is set to at least 20%.

Check that Lower screen brightness while in battery saver is also enabled.

Battery saver extends battery life by limiting background activity and push notifications.

7. Check app usage.

Open Settings > System > Battery and then select See which apps are affecting your battery life.

From Time period, select 6 hours, 24 hours, or 1 week depending on which period is closest to the customer’s reported problem.

From Show, select Apps with usage.

Looks for apps that have high battery consumption. If there are apps with high usage that the customer is not using, the customer should close them.

If the apps start automatically, open Task Manager and then select the Startup tab. The customer can disable any apps that they don’t need to start automatically.

Unnecessarily running programs can cause slow system behavior and affect overall stability of the PC.

Good: Fresh Start

Better: Reset from within Windows

Go to Start > Settings > Update & security > Recovery.

Under Reset this PC, select Get Started. Follow the on-screen instructions.

[Asset 4464331]

Fresh Start (good):

  • Fresh Start is the least intrusive form of reset.

  • Customers will not lose any files or personal information.

  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop programs.

  • Fresh Start also ensures the customer has all available updates once complete.

  • If there is an issue with Windows itself, the issue may still occur after a reset.

Reset from within Windows (better):

  • This option uses the device’s hard drive partition to reimage the device.

  • This option can delete personal data (customers can choose Keep my files or Remove everything).

  • The customer will need to reinstall desktop apps.

  • The customer will need to run updates upon completion.

  • If there is an issue with the partition, the issue may still occur after the reset.

USB recovery (best):

  • This option reimages the entire device, including the partition on the hard drive.

  • It is the most complete form of reset.

  • Customers will need to back up their personal data.

  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop apps. Customers will need to run updates after completion.

If the issue occurs after a reset, then the root cause is very likely hardware related.
