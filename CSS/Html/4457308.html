What's the article about?

This article describes how to engage the interpretation services CSS Engineers may utilize to provide support in a language their customers understand or speak.

CSS serves the World and our customers speak many languages. In servicing our customers, CSS Engineers may require assistance with spoken language interpretation or written language translation.

In many support teams it is commonplace for peers who are native speakers to provide ad-hoc assistance with live interpretation, or for a native speaker of the customer’s language to take ownership of a support case.

Where assistance cannot be provided by peers and there is a need for spoken language interpretation, CSS Engineers may utilize interpretation services provided by Language Line Solutions.

Return to top

Typically, a language interpreter is utilized to:

  • Join an ongoing call to provide language interpretation.
  • Assist in placing a call to a customer and providing language interpretation during the call.

When you engage an interpreter, be certain to describe your situation and explain what assistance you seek (additional tips can be found below). When interpretation is no longer needed:

  • Continue with troubleshooting activities and move into solution delivered, as necessary.
  • Re-engage interpretation as needed.

To engage the interpretation service, follow these steps:

  1. Dial the U.S. toll free number (877) 261-6608 or the international direct dial number at +1 224-875-6404
  2. When you are prompted for a Client ID, enter 774033
  3. Select one of the following options:
    • Press 1 for Spanish
    • Press 2 for All Other Languages
    • Press 0 or hold for assistance
  4. Provide the representative with the following information
    • Personal Code 1: <<Your customer’s case id>>
    • Personal Code 2: <<Service level>> (Premier or Software Assurance)
  5. You will be connected to an interpreter who will provide their ID number; set their expectations
    • Identify yourself and explain the situation.
    • Ask the interpreter to be prepared to introduce themselves and their role to the customer.
    • Inform the interpreter whether you will conference in the customer or you need their assistance in placing a call to the customer.

  • Speak to the customer as if they were an English-speaking customer. Give the interpreter time to translate each sentence.
  • Speak clearly and slowly so that the translator can understand the words correctly.
  • Speak to the customer as if only the two of you are on the telephone call. Avoid beginning a sentence with "Tell the customer"
  • Avoid technical jargon. Those words do not translate effectively.
  • Assume the translator knows nothing about the technology or troubleshooting Microsoft products.
  • If there are any concerns that the translator may have misunderstood what is needed from the customer, have the translator repeat the requested instruction.
  • Repeat instructions if the results from the customer are not what is needed. Be aware that, even though you are using an interpreter, the customer may be able to understand some of your English. Always remain courteous and professional.

Tools such as Microsoft Word and Bing Translator are effective at translating written text. When using text translation always remove PII information from text you wish to paste into a translation tool.

For the best customer experience, engage a native speaker to validate any email you wish to send to a customer that is written in a language you do not speak.
