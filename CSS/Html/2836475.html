Scenario

  • If the customer is listed as a Global/Company, Tenant, or Billing Admin on the account, AND they are wanting to remove a subscription or delete their tenant.
  • If the customer has an available billing account ID (trail or paid) shown on the account page in CMAT, AND is asking for a billing country change.

Note: If customer has a CSP relationship, they will not be able to follow the self-serve tenant delete process.

Note: If customer has Windows Store for Business subscription, look for Windows Store for Business trial and cancel the subscription. Subscription will be deprovisioned in 10 calendar days.

Guidance

Company/Tenant admins now have the ability to self-remove (aka lockout/de-provision) a subscription within MAC “Preview Mode” portal and self-delete their tenant from the Azure Active Directory within AADAC portal.

These capabilities are restricted to Managed Tenants ONLY, and the ability to self-remove and/or self-delete can only be performed by the Company/Tenant Administrator role.

  • Only ‘Managed Tenants’ can be self-deleted. Unmanaged Tenants (aka Viral tenant) cannot be self-deleted.


Support should always recommend and assist eligible customers through the customer led self-remove/self-delete processes.

NOTE:

  • Tenant deletion is an irreversible process and will result in permanent data loss.
  • Tenant deletion is the only way to change the billing country of a tenant who has an available Billing Account ID (trail or paid) shown on the account page in CMAT.

Procedure 

1. How to delete a subscription
 
Customer/Tenant now have the ability to self-remove all subscriptions including ‘Company Owned’ and ‘Individually Owned/ IW or self-service sign-up’ subscriptions.

  • Using the M365 Admin Center Portal
  • Using the Azure Active Directory Admin Center portal
  • Delete a subscription

For NOAM

If customer is unable to delete the subscription using the steps provided above, Tier 1 ambassadors can request an exception for Manual subscription deletion by submitting Rave AR request to Tier 3.

If the option to delete is missing or the customer is getting an error removing following standard process and needs the subscription removed without waiting for lifecycle deletion (Currently Volume license and GCC subscriptions cannot be self-deleted):

Please email the customer the following template to authorization the deletion of the subscription(s). Verify the details in the authorization to ensure that is fully completed and the correct subscription are listed for deletion. Then attach the form to the AR request for review and processing.

Exceptions

Explain the process to the customer and obtain their approval to proceed the tenant deletion process.

Please clarify that this process is irreversible and will result in permanent loss of data despite any other agreement that the customer may have with Microsoft.

It may take up to 15 business days to process the request. Use the following script to set up a call with the customer “May I schedule a follow-up call to give you an update on the progress of your request? What day and time would work best for you?”
