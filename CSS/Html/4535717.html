Scope

  • The scope of the Modern Workplace Global After-Hours team is to provide seamless after-hours support for all regions including ASIA, EMEA, Americas & LAPRE.
  • The support will be in English language only and primarily covering Critical (severity A) incidents. Critical cases which get lowered to Non-Critical severity but with 24X7 flag & requiring active troubleshooting will continue to leverage this process.

Guiding Principles

  • Support should be available for our customers, as per their availability, regardless of their time zone.
  • Engineer should not be asked to extend their shift beyond their working hours.
  • Minimize case transfers – Multiple case owners are known to lead to poor customer experience.
  • Once the first engineer took the case, there is always a person accountable for the case.

General Case Transfer Guidelines

  • Always assess if the customer is willing to continue working on the case if your shift is going to end. Please do not assume the customer is willing to work on it 24x7, and do not offer a case transfer solely due to a time zone or language difference.
  • Discuss with the customer your availability to re-take the troubleshooting as needed without the need to transfer the case to someone else.
  • Assess if the situation is critical (or political) and as such, requires a warm hand-over.
  • Warm handover as needed from above questions/decisions. If no warm handover is required, transfer the case as per the scenario (See FAQs for specific scenarios). Use Follow-up Tasks, validate if 24*7 needs to be checked based on the next contact agreement.
  • Always Document with notes to ensure the next engineer does not have to repeat any question or troubleshooting:
    • Reason for hand-over
    • Troubleshooting done
    • Next steps
    • Any further important details (optional)
    • Document which work items you want the receiving Engineer to take ownership of, whether just a task or the whole case as well.

Do not move cases back to the queue:

  • Because the customer is unresponsive.
  • Because the customer is in a different time zone than you. See FAQs below for situation you should transfer.
  • Because the customer does not speak your language. Refer to the Local Language POD process.
  • Because you will be out on vacation or training – re-distribute your cases amongst your team members prior to your planned OOF. Refer to the OOF management POD process.

Generic Case Transition Procedures

SCOPE: Critical & Non-Critical (24X7), ALL Types (Active | POC| Callback| Unassigned)

REGIONS: APAC, EMEA, AMERICAS & After-Hours POD

Note: When a particular region transitions to their After-hours, CRITS are picked by SPO AH POD team, while other severity cases will be handed as per usual process with rest of the team.  Automated emails are sent with a list of case transitions at various handoff times & they will have recipients from region & AHPOD.

    Transition Cutoff time: Defined as time when the destination region or AHPOD will assign owners for cases in the Email & finish transitions.

No further email is sent after the email cutoff time is passed.

    [Asset 4553085]

  6. Once you select a button for particular region transfer, you need to submit the required fields to be able to submit the form successfully.
    1. Case Number:
    2. Company:
    3. Severity: Critical | Non-Critical
    4. Transition Type: Indicating the type of transition
      1. Active (Warm)
      2. Callback (Cold)
      3. Point of Contact (Cold)
      4. Unassigned

      Note: Unassigned <choose this if there is a an unassigned /unhandled case which needs to be handled by next Region / AH POD>. 

      • Choosing Transition type = Callback will bring in a Time Dropdown, where time needs to be entered in UTC time only, so please convert the time and add accordingly. It is important to add the correct time in UTC to avoid confusion for the new engineer receiving the case.
      • Choosing Transition type = Unassigned will additionally bring up the missed SLA checkbox, which can be used to indicate if SLA is already missed on that case.
    5. Business Impact Check Box: Use these tags to mark business impact of cases.
      1. Missed SLA
      2. Politically hot
      3. Outage
      4. Planned Maintenance
      5. Exec Escalation
    6. Special Instructions: additional details around the case which you may need to pass onto the next resource. ICM link, bridge details etc. Note: All engineers are also encouraged to add the Customer local time callback information in special Instructions field. 
  7. Once you have fill in all the required fields & submit, you should see an acknowledgement of the submission. 
    [Asset 4553088]

    Note: There is time check on the submit button, so in case you filled the form and did not submit before cutoff time, the submission will be blocked. This is to ensure that we don't miss any cases due to late submission, hence they were not part of automated email. Again, it will be responsibility of the engineer who needs handoff to update the transition email manually.

  8. All submitted cases which have not been processed by the automated Email can be reviewed from the listed viewer webpart on the AKA.MS link for the technology provided at pt.1 for this section.

  9. Some technologies may have an additional Field on the form for capturing additional metadata for the cases which aids in assignment, for e.g.  the SME Area, Contract Offering being used in Exchange on-prem technology.

  10. Once the Email is out after the Cutoff Time for any of the Handoffs, Engineers, Technical Advisors or Queue Trackers of team receiving Handoffs will align resources to the cases. Once all cases are handled, transitions will be marked as complete.
