Procedure: Xbox: Global Xbox repair and fulfillment information and options

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Xbox phone and chat advocates who deal with hardware repairs and accessory fulfillment

What is this article about?

This article describes global repair options, shipping options, turnaround times (TAT), and warranty coverage periods for Xbox products. Use this article when the customer’s console requires a repair.

This procedure contains information about Xbox 360, Xbox One, and accessories for the following:

  • Repair and replacement options for serialized devices and accessories
  • Advance exchange hold amounts
  • Out of warranty repair pricing
  • Shipping options
  • Turnaround times (TAT)
  • Original and extended warranty periods

For Australia: Consumers have the right to repair, replace or refund in certain situations, see KB 4100081 Guideline: Microsoft and the Australian and New Zealand Consumer Law. Where a consumer has that right, you must follow the ACL policy.

1. Types of service offerings 

Serialized devices

  1. Same Unit Repair (SUR) or Exchange (EXC): Microsoft will provide a shipping option for the customer to send the product to the service center. Once the product is serviced, Microsoft will ship it back to the customer.

  1. Advance Exchange (AE): Microsoft will ship a replacement product to the customer first, and then the customer will send back their non-working item.

    • Not available in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, France, Netherlands, Switzerland, Sweden, Austria, and Belgium. Customers should use Expedited Shipping for the quickest shipping option.

    • Only available through an advocate; not available through devicesupport.microsoft.com.

    • A premium service fee, varying by country, is charged immediately to the payment instrument except for devices covered by Microsoft Complete. 

    • To initiate an AE order, the customer must agree to a temporary hold on the payment instrument (PI), which is released upon return of the ticketed device. If the PI is a debit card, those funds will not be available for use until the order is complete.

    • Once placed, an AE order is not cancellable. It will remain open until the ticketed device is received or the PI is charged for a modified or non-returned device. See KB 2931657 Procedure: Xbox: Failed delivery by carrier for Repair Order/Fulfillment Order.

    • The return shipping label enclosed with the advanced device should be used with the same packaging to return the ticketed console. If no shipping label is found in the box, see KB 2229086 Procedure: Xbox: Problems with a delivered product.

    • Any device returned other than the ticketed one will result in the PI being charged for the hold amount. See KB 2961825 Procedure: Xbox: Handling hardware refund requests.

    • For registration issues involving ticketed or advanced devices, address them as warranty disputes, request proof of purchase and photo of the console serial number as available.

  2. Out of Region Repair (OOR): This applies to products that a customer purchased in a region other than where they reside. The warranty will not apply, and the customer will receive a localized console if they place a repair order.

  3. Rapid Response Team (RRT) Repair: This repair type applies to products where there is a concern related to safety or property damage. For further details see KB 2764515 Procedure: Surface & Xbox: Handling Product Safety (RRT) Contacts.

  4. 30-day grace period: We offer a 30-day courtesy extension of warranty service offer for consoles. If the console’s original manufacturer warranty expired in the past 30 days, check that the console region is the same as the customer’s country of residence. If they are the same, capture screenshots of the Device Warranty Details and Repair/Exchange Offers and then escalate to the Offline team for further investigation.

  5. Post-service warranty: If the Xbox console or Kinect sensor was serviced, the device will receive either the remainder of the original warranty or 95 days from our shipment, whichever is longer.

    This service warranty only applies to serialized devices, not accessories, in regions that offer out of warranty service, and does not apply to extended service plans, such as Microsoft Complete.

Accessories

You may provide fulfillment of accessories to customers by using the registered device in MST. You are not able to write a fulfillment order in MST without a device registered to the customer’s Microsoft account. For console accessories fulfillment when the customer does not have a registered console, see the section Instructions for customers without a console below.

  • Exchange Fulfillment (EXC FO) – a paid or free-of-charge order for accessories requiring the customer to send the item to the service center before the replacement is returned. The inbound shipping method depends on the country. However, if the customer is required to pay for shipping, we ask that they use a traceable means, such as FedEx or UPS, so that we can ensure that the item is received by the service center. Items sent without tracking cannot be confirmed received by the service center.
  • Send Immediately Fulfillment (SEN FO) – The service center sends the accessory directly to the customer. This may be a paid or free-of-charge order, usually dependent on the console’s warranty status.
  • Game disc – For issues with first party and bundled game discs, see KB 2522626 Procedure: Handling game disc replacement requests and scratched-game disc claims.
  • Xbox Design Lab custom controller – For support boundaries or return and repair scenarios, see KB 3055957 Procedure: Original Xbox Support Boundaries.

     

  • Limited Edition items – Limited edition accessories such as controllers, headsets, and consoles are limited production items that may not be available in stock for replacement in an acceptable time or permanently. Customers should be made aware that in cases where the same model is not available, they may receive a standard item instead. As this is considered an alternative item replacement, it does not qualify as an incorrect item for escalation under KB 2229086 Procedure: Xbox: Problems with a delivered product.
  • Xbox One S Kinect Adapter – The offer of a free Kinect adapter for original Xbox One customers ended on June 30th, 2017. Direct customers to paid offers.
  • Wireless adapter for Windows, Xbox One Chatpad, and Xbox Elite Controller– We can replace these accessories.
  • Kinect for Windows – Through troubleshooting, find out if the issue is with the Kinect or with Windows.
  • If the issue is with Windows, transfer to Windows technical support.
  • If the issue is with the Kinect, follow your usual hardware troubleshooting steps.

Instructions for customers without a console

Follow this process for customers who do not have a console and need a fulfillment order. You will obtain an available serial number (CSN) that will be assigned to the customer for the duration of the fulfillment.

  1. Troubleshoot the customer accessory and determine if replacement is necessary.
  2. Verify the warranty status of the accessory. Refer to the Warranty section in this article.
  3. Consult with the Offline Team/Team Manager (TM)/Subject Matter Expert (SME), who will provide you an available serial number that can be used to register against the customer’s Microsoft account to write an order in MST.
  4. If the Offline team member is not available, contact your SME/TM.
  5. The customer hardware profile cannot be updated with address details without a device registered in MST. You must authenticate the customer’s Microsoft account before proceeding, or, on callback, proceed with authentication.
  6. Using the serial number provided by the Offline team, register the device to the customer's Microsoft account.
  7. Once the order has been completed, remove the CSN from the customer’s profile in order to make it available again to be used in other SRs.

Note: You are only authorized to write orders for accessories listed in scope of this article. Proper usage is monitored and will be enforced.

2. Shipping options

  1. Print a label (Online label, OSI): This applies to when a customer self-creates a repair order online and they are able to print a label through devicesupport.microsoft.com.
  2. Email label (E-label, ESI): This applies for an advocate-created repair order where the carrier will email the label to customer on Microsoft’s behalf.
  3. Convenience store drop-off (XYD): This applies when customer opts to take the console to convenience store for shipping.
  4. Express (OSX, ESX): This applies when the customer opts for expedited shipping with OSI or ESI.
  5. Courier pick-up (CSC or COU): This applies to situations where Microsoft has arranged for the courier to pick-up the ticketed product.
  6. Walk-In Center Drop-Off (DOS or VMF): The customer takes their product to the walk-in center to process the order.
  7. Self-ship (PIC): This applies when customer is fully responsible for shipping the product to Microsoft including any packing materials or services. Microsoft recommends customers use services that provide tracking numbers when sending items.
  8. Deliver Label (LSC): This applies to situations where a shipping label is delivered to customer, and the customer then ships the product to service center.
  9. Standard (STD): This applies to situations where a shipping box with a return shipping label is delivered to customer. The customer then ships the product to service center.

Device Repair Alerts via SMS Text Messaging

Customers will receive a confirmation email that offers them the opportunity to OPT-IN to receive SMS text messages on the status of their repair orders. Once the customer has opted-in to receiving Device Repair Alerts, they will begin to receive SMS text messages to their mobile device. These SMS messages will inform the customer of the status of their repair or return order. Customers may contact Support with the following questions about this service:

Q: How do I opt into SMS text updates?

A: Once you receive your confirmation email, simply follow the link to the SMS opt-in page and input your phone number. You will receive a confirmation message asking you to subscribe to Device Repair Alerts. Just reply with YES or Y to subscribe.

Q: How frequently will I get SMS text updates?

A: You will receive an SMS alert when there is an update to your order status. Normally, only three text messages are sent for each order.

Q: What if I have multiple service requests?

A: You will need to follow the unique link to opt in for each service request in the confirmation email.

Q: How do I opt out of SMS text updates?

A: Text STOP to 68892 to stop all SMS alerts.

Q: Why do you need my phone number?

A: Your phone number is required to subscribe to Microsoft Device Repair Alerts.

Q: What is Microsoft doing to protect my data?

A: You can read Microsoft’s privacy policy at https://privacy.microsoft.com/privacy.

Q: Is there a charge to receive SMS text updates from Microsoft?

A: This is a free service from Microsoft. However, message and date rates may apply. Consult your carrier before subscribing to Microsoft Device Repair Alerts.

Q: Can I receive text message updates in Canada, EOC, APOC, etc.?

A: Microsoft Device Repair Alerts are only available in the United States.

Q: Can I change my phone number once I have opted in?

A: Your phone number cannot be changed once you have subscribed to Microsoft Device Repair Alerts. However, you can text STOP to opt-out with the first number, and then start the process over by opening the original email and opting in with the new phone number.

4. Warranties for Xbox 360 S & E consoles, Kinect sensors, and accessories 

  • In warranty (IW) and out of warranty (OOW) offers for Xbox 360 S and Xbox 360 E consoles, Kinect sensors, and accessories have been turned off in MST. Customers who are OOW will need to purchase replacement products via retail or online channels. Customers who are IW will either be provided a replacement or a refund for their product depending on regional availability. After obtaining the customer’s proof of purchase (PoP) to validate the customer’s warranty status as in warranty (IW), the customer may be offered CSV or a paper check as appropriate refund method per below:

    • CSV refund: CSV in the amount of the refund may be applied to the account using the following instructions
      • Comments: Xbox 360 EOL Accessories
      • Reason: Goodwill Make it right gesture
      • CSV SKU: CSS Xbox Store make-it-right
    • Paper Check refund: Advocates must escalate a child SR to ACA with a request to process the check.

  • All other accessories and items that are included in the console bundle receive the standard accessory warranty.
  • Warranties vary by country. See the table below for more information.

Note: Power supplies and power cords purchased separately receive a 90-day warranty.

Updated procedure on in warranty Xbox One and Series X/S accessories service orders

You will have send-immediately SKUs enabled for Xbox One accessories for customers in Canada and the United States until further notice. You will still have standard exchange SKUs enabled and should be using exchange SKU once a customer has already received ONE SEN per product type (Controller, headset, PSU, and others), not by product model or serial number for previous year (365 days). Any requests for exceptions to this should be raised by the advocate, case by case for evaluation..

Example customer scenarios for advocate guidance:

  • A customer contacts CSS and has a broken controller that needs to be replaced. Advocate confirms, no prior SEN has been created for this customer, and then creates a SEN for a standard controller.
  • Same customer contacts CSS an hour/day/week/month later complaining about a broken Elite controller. Troubleshoot and determine it needs to be replaced. Identify that the customer had a SEN previously for a controller and create a standard exchange order for an elite controller.
  • Same customer contacts CSS an hour/day/week/month and complains about a broken chat headset. Identify that customer has not had a SEN for a headset and create a SEN for a headset.
    • Process:
      • Do not follow normal processes to determine if a device needs to be replaced.
      • Do not use existing taxonomy.
      • If the device needs replacement, you must follow existing processes to validate that the device is in warranty.
      • Check the customer's service order history to ensure that no send-immediately orders were processed during this time frame for the same device/product.
