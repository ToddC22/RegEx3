ASMS – Handling Orphaned Accounts

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG explains how to address a variety of unexpected behaviors which indicate that a customer's Azure account is in an “orphaned” state.

When an Azure account is orphaned, the MINT account associated with the Operation Management Suite (OMS) Commerce Account ID (CAID) is exposed in CMAT. For guidance on identifying orphaned accounts, see the Support Scenarios section below.

Customer Experience and Provisioning

A customer may experience a variety of unexpected behaviors in their portal which indicate that their Azure account is in an orphaned state, such as:

  • When the customer logs in to the Azure portal, a message may appear stating something like “You do not have any subscriptions.”

    • In this case, the customer is not able to view or manage their existing subscriptions.

  • The customer may not be able to do the following:

    • Log in to their account in the Azure portal.

    • Change their billing information or payment instrument (PI).

    • Download invoices or usage reports.

    • Remove the spending limit of a subscription that has hit its monetary capacity.

    • Redeem tokens or Online Services Activation (OSA) keys.

    • Add credits via credit card.

    • Perform certain actions on their subscriptions, such as clicking the Add Digital Partner of Record button.

Guidance on addressing these issues can be found in the Support Scenarios section below.

Support Scenarios

Identify an Orphaned Account

The following actions by a customer may result in the orphaning of their account:

  • Changes the Microsoft Account (MSA) or Organizational Account (Org ID) email address of the Account Administrator (AA).

  • Deletes the AA’s Org ID account in the Azure Active Directory (AAD).

  • Deletes and then recreates the AA’s Org ID account in the AAD.

  • Removes the AA’s email address from the associated directory.

  • Adds an alias to the MSA or Org ID of the AA’s email ID.

Orphaned accounts may also result from systemic issues in the Azure platform, such as an error during the backfilling of an account from MINT to OMS.

Verify that an Account is Orphaned

When a customer’s account is orphaned, their existing subscription will not appear when they log in to the Account Center. Instead, they will see an option to create a new subscription.

Searching for an orphaned account in CMAT using the customer’s email address will display the associated MINT account. However, searching for the account using the customer’s subscription GUID will show that the account is already on OMS and is thus considered orphaned.

For example, a search in CMAT using the subscription GUID 7a0392cf-0f7a-4eda-8953-e63a9780**** yields an email address field of N/A. However, a search using the email address associated with this Azure account will show the MINT account bmVpLgAAAAAAA*** .

  • This means that the email address associated with the Azure account is deleted from the AAD. As a result, the associated Azure account is considered orphaned.

Address an Orphaned Account

Leverage the Scenarios and Solutions table below to determine the steps needed to address the customer’s specific issue.

Scenarios Involving SOTs

In most cases, orphaned account issues can be resolved using a Subscription Ownership Transfer (SOT) by following the guidelines in TSG 3040383.

If the solution to the customer’s issue involves a SOT:

  • Ensure that the customer’s PI is valid so that their balance can be processed before a SOT is performed.

  • If the customer has an outstanding account balance that is greater than the empowerment of the Service Engineer (SE) or Technical Advisor/Partner Technical Advisor or Manager (TA/PTA/M1) , ask the customer to make a one-time wire transfer to clear the outstanding balance before proceeding with the SOT .

    • For guidance on performing wire transfers, see TSG 2658076.

Add AIO Credits to an AIO Subscription on an Orphaned Account

The customer wants to perform a data migration to another subscription, but they are out of AIO credits. They cannot add more AIO credits to their existing AIO subscription because it is suspended and is under an orphaned account.

  1. If the amount is outside the SE’s empowerment, engage a TA/PTA/M1 for approval.

    Note: TAs/PTA/M1s cannot add AIO credits to orphaned accounts.

  2. Engage PayOps by following the guidance in TSG 2651579 to add the AIO credits.

    • If TA/PTA/M1 approval was required, include a screenshot of the task.

Orphaned Azure In Open (AIO) Member Offer

The orphaned Azure account contains an AIO member offer.

Note: In this case, a SOT cannot be performed.

  1. To obtain a temporary token, do one of the following:

    1. Engage a TA/PTA/M1. They can follow the guidelines in TSG 3188143.

    2. Ask the customer to work with the reseller to get a temporary token.

  2. Direct the customer to do the following:

    1. Activate the new AIO subscription on the destination account.

    2. Perform a data migration from the existing AIO subscription to the new subscription by following the guidance in TSG 2856526 .

  3. Engage a TA/PTA/M1 to credit the new destination subscription the same amount that was remaining on the source AIO subscription.

  4. After the customer verifies the migration has been completed, cancel the source AIO subscription by following the guidance in TSG 2646764. This will ensure that any remaining credits cannot be used by the customer in the future.

Orphaned Member Offers

The orphaned Azure account contains a member offer (such as MSDN or BizSpark).

Direct the customer to link a new Microsoft account by doing the following:

  1. If the customer does not have a new Microsoft account, direct them to sign up for one at  https://signup.live.com.

    • Advise them to either add a Pay-As-You-Go Azure subscription or perform the sign-up process at least through the step to add a PI.

  2. Log in to the MSDN portal.

  3. Navigate to ACCOUNT > My Subscriptions, then click Change Microsoft Account for this subscription.

Orphaned MSA

The orphaned Azure account is an MSA.

Perform a SOT by following the steps in TSG 3040383.

User Deleted

The user was deleted from the On-Prem/Azure directory/tenant.

Note: If the orphaned Azure account is an Org ID, an additional action is required (see Solution step 1a).

Retrieve the customer’s previously deleted profile from the recycle bin by doing the following:

  1. In the Azure Support Center (ASC) Tenant Explorer , check if the deleted profile is in the recycle bin for 30 days from the day of deletion. This is also known as a “soft-deleted” state.

    1. If the deleted user is tied to a subscription that has benefits (for example, an MSDN subscription) and is associated with an Org ID, engage the Windows Cloud Identity T2 team via a collaboration task to restore the deleted user profile from the recycle bin.

    2. Notify the AAD team using the Support Area Path (SAP) Azure\Azure Active Directory Directories, Domains, and Objects\Users.

    3. If the user was deleted from the directory more than 30 days prior, the profile will have been automatically deleted from the recycle bin. There is no recovery option other than setting up a new Azure account and performing a SOT by following the steps in TSG 3040383.

  2. If the desired profile is found in the recycle bin, in the AAD’s Deleted User blade, move the it from the recycle bin to the active user list in the directory/tenant.

    Note: ASMS cannot retrieve a profile with the same user’s principle name (UPN) if one already exists under the directory. If the UPN is the same, the only option is to set up a new Azure account and perform a SOT by following the steps in TSG 3040383.

Subscription Missing in CMAT

The subscription is not in CMAT.

Note: A subscription that is missing in CMAT but appearing in MINT, also known as a “MINT leakage,” occurs when the subscription was left out during the last attempt to backfill the account.

File a backfill request with PayOps by following the guidance in TSG 3040383. In the request, be sure to explicitly call out that the subscription is missing in CMAT.
