1. Overview

For scenarios utilizing CSV in the Make It Right (MIR) scope, see KB 4465058 Procedure: Microsoft Store Make It Right.

1.1 Microsoft accounts and currency stored value

Currency stored value (CSV) is a digital form of payment associated to a unique billing account. Multiple billing accounts may be associated to a single Microsoft account based on region and type (consumer or commercial). Customers may refer to CSV as “gift card”.

Customers can review and manage the billing profiles associated with their Microsoft account at https://account.microsoft.com/profile under Payment options, in the Payment & billing menu.

Billing accounts cannot be merged, migrated to other regions, or change type, and once applied, CSV balances cannot be transferred to another region.

Customers can use CSV to purchase any content on Microsoft Store, including Xbox subscriptions, the only exceptions are Microsoft 365 subscriptions and backward compatible Xbox 360 titles.

See section 4 for a list of countries that can't use CSV.

Promotional CSV credited to the customer’s account may have an expiration date. In this case, Microsoft sends an email notification to the customer before the CSV expires. Limited time promotional CSV must be used before standard CSV. If a customer’s CSV has expired from their account funds, you may provide a CSV credit for replacement of the exact same amount as the expired funds. See KB 4465058 Procedure: Microsoft Store Make It Right for the correct CSV SKU and reason to be used in these situations.

Note: This is a one-time exception per Microsoft account, once issued, the customer needs to spend the provided funds within the next year.

2.2 Region migration and CSV

CSV is non-transferable between regions on an account, all CSV currency remains in the region it was first redeemed. After a region migration, the customer may not be able to see their previous CSV balance. All customers are advised of this limitation in How to update your Microsoft account if you're moving to a new country or region.

Xbox One customers can change the region as needed on the console as a work around but may still be subject to geo fencing.

If the customer wants to use their CSV currency, they need to change their region back to the region where the CSV was redeemed.

4. Troubleshooting CSV issues

4.1 The customer cannot make a purchase using CSV

  1. Check the Alerts or Orders sections of MST for declined purchases on the account. If a purchase is declined due to risk, follow normal risk rejection process in KB 2896711 Procedure: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops.
  2. If a purchase is declined for other reasons, review the error message for mitigation and check to see if the customer has enough CSV to complete the purchase, including any tax that may be applied to the final purchase price.
  3. The customer may be able to complete the purchase with a split CSV payment using a secondary payment instrument for the remaining balance.
  4. If the customer has a CSV balance on one of their billing profiles and cannot see the balance on their device, ask the customer to verify the device the region is set to is the same region that shows in MST.
  5. If the customer is still unable to see the balance on the device, engage A&B ERA team (for Japan, Korea, and China, engage ACA) or Xbox Ranger to engage PayOps.

Return to top

4.2 Split profiles

  1. A customer may claim state cannot see their full CSV balance, usually after redeeming a CSV prepaid code (PPC).
  2. Assist the customer in reviewing their recent account history, which should include the PPC in question.
  3. Using the token tool, confirm the PPC was redeemed to the profile the customer verified and the region matched that of the customer’s billing profile, as some PPC’s are geo fenced (only redeemable in certain countries).
  4. Review the customer’s billing accounts to rule out a split profile, which is defined as:
    • Multiple billing accounts of the same in the same country
    • CSV balance on more than one account of the same type in the same country.

If you find that the customer has CSV on a split profile, engage A&B ERA team (for Japan, Korea, and China, engage ACA) or Xbox ranger for PayOps engagement.

4.5 The customer is unable to redeem CSV due to error “CsvDepositLimitExceed”

Customers in France can only redeem up to €250 per month or use CSV for purchases up to €250 to comply with recent changes in the Payment Services Directive PSD2 laws in France. The customer needs to wait until the next month to continue redeeming tokens when encountering this error.

5. CSV limits

There are two CSV limits.

Both limits are customer impacting; one limits customer redemptions and the other limits the spend of the Microsoft account balance.

Return to top

5.1 CSV daily limit

The CSV daily limit restricts the amount of CSV a customer can redeem into their Microsoft account. This limit is set by CELA to align with policies and regulations. Daily limit calculations are daily (except for France which is monthly) and are calculated by adding the beginning balance (BB) of the day and the redemptions made that same day.

The total of the beginning balance and redemptions are not permitted to exceed the predetermined daily limit (by country). Once the limit is reached, the customer will no longer be able to redeem CSV that day into their Microsoft account. Spending CSV does not impact the limit. The limit is only impacted by the beginning balance and redemptions occurring that day.

5.2 CSV lot limitation

What is a lot?

A lot is created when a customer redeems a CSV token; each redemption creates one new lot. The currency denomination and brand of the CSV are irrelevant. It does not matter how much the token was worth, any redemption of any amount creates one lot.

What is this limitation?

This limitation impacts customers trying to complete an order that requires 18 lots or more:

  • If an order amount requires 18 lots or more (in total), the possibility of the transaction timing out increases as the lot count increases.
  • If an order amount requires 85 lots or more (in total), the transaction will not process.

Why is there a limitation?

The limitation of 85 lots or more is by design due to the risk of financial misstatement. The limitation of 18 lots or more is because each lot is treated as an individual transaction which requires more time and may result in the process timing out and the redemption failing.

What is the customer impact?

If the customer is trying to complete a purchase which requires an aggregate of over 18 lots (and times out) or has over 85 Lots (and the transaction is not processed), the impact to the customer is the same, the customer cannot spend the CSV balance in their Microsoft account.

How can Microsoft support help?

You can help troubleshoot with the customer by breaking down the order into multiple orders and/or purchasing a lower priced item to decrease the lot count needed in order to complete the order. If the order cannot be broken apart (since the customer is only trying to purchase a high value item like a Surface), there is no automated solution at this time and the short term manual solution is not scalable so each individual case must be reviewed.

Keep in mind, in addition to the lots limitation, there is also a CSV daily limit. The CSV daily Limit (Microsoft account balance) for most countries is around 1K USD/EUR equivalent.

How the daily limit impacts the lot limitation issue: A customer has $900 in their Microsoft account from 18 individual $50 CSVs. The customer tries to buy a Surface for $900 and the transaction times out. The customer calls Microsoft support. We cannot break down the order or purchase a lower priced item. We cannot provide additional funds since the Microsoft account balance is already $900, $100 under the daily limit.

Note:

Information regarding the lot limitation is not to be provided to the customer at any point for any reason. CSS is to let the customer know that they will be creating a ticket to the appropriate teams for additional research and will get back to them shortly.

 

  1. Verify the customer and review their account and order(s) as applicable.
  2. Troubleshoot with the customer. If the order has multiple items, try breaking it into multiple orders, or purchasing a lower priced item to decrease the lot count.
  3. If the issue remains, create a PayOps service request containing:
    • The customer's information (Name, PUID, and Account ID).
    • The customer'a issue explained in detail.
    • Purchase information (What are they trying to buy, the price of the item, what store are they trying to purchase from, country, currency).
    • The customer's Microsoft account balance (CSV balances broken out by Paid and Promotional).
    • Where did the customer purchase the CSV tokens.
    • Screen shots:
      • The error the customer is seeing.
      • Actions CSS took to confirm this was not fraudulent.
      • Actions CSS took to troubleshoot with the customer (if applicable).
      • Customer’s balance in MST.
  • An estimate of how many lots the customers balance consists of (remember each redemption = one lot).
  1. Email AskCSV@ with the subject CSS - lot limitation impacting customer with the SR number.
  2. The CSV team engages with Fraud, Risk, CELA, Payments Engineering and PayOps to perform the necessary checks and initiate the manual solution if it is determined we should do so.
