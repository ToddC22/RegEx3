ASMS – Service Desk Case Workflow

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to use Service Desk Case (SDC) and features you might encounter when working on support requests (SRs).

Note: You should also read TSG 2636922 (Case Management and Wellness) for best practices on case handling.

Workflows

The workflows within Service Desk should be used throughout the life cycle of a case. Review the following features to determine how and when they should be used.

Case Creation

Some circumstances may require you to create a case. If you need to create a new case, take a look at TSG 2895582 (Creating a Grace Case) to help create a new case for your customer. If you are unsure, the following examples may provide help.

Examples

  • Your customer is unable to create a case themselves.

  • You have finished helping your customer and they ask about a new, separate issue.

    • If the new issue has (or may have) a separate coding and closing reason, create a new case for your customer.

    • If you are unsure if their question qualifies for a new case, or should be worked using your current case, use your best judgement.

    • Do not refuse customers who need help.

Case Assignment

Cases are assigned to support engineers (SEs) by duty managers (DMs). The DM also sends an email notification when they assign a case to you.

Note: No DMs are assigned to a select number of queues. Instead, these queues operate on a pull model, in that SEs can assign cases from the queues to themselves. If you are unsure which model your queues operate under, engage your technical advisor (TA) for guidance.

Follow these steps when you receive a case:

  1. Review the customer’s verbatim and case details to determine if the case falls within Azure Subscription Management Support’s (ASMS’s) scope.

    • If it does not fall within ASMS’s scope, follow TSG 3104062 (Misroutes) or engage your TA for guidance on who should own the case.

  2. Verify that the Product Family is set to Azure.

  3. Verify that the Product is set to the appropriate value, depending on the customer’s issue:

    • Billing

    • Subscription Management

    • Azure Portal sign-in or subscription

    • Service and subscription limits (quotas)

Note: Some cases may come to ASMS via a non-standard path which have a non-ASMS routing product. Update the Product and Product Family to align to the customer’s issue in these scenarios. See TSG 2658158 (Product and Root Cause Coding Guidelines) for more details.

  1. Check to ensure your customer’s operating hours (in their local time zone) align with your working shift hours.
    • Set expectations about your working hours in your first quality response (FQR) to the customer if your shift is not aligned with their time zone.
  2. Check the case severity for the appropriate service level agreement (SLA).
    • Some customer's get a faster SLA based on their entitlement, such as Premier, ProDirect, and Partners.
    • See TSG 2786284 (Support Entitlement Handling) for more details.
  3. Review the customer’s previous cases to check for duplicates and similar prior requests:
    1. In Service Desk, navigate to the customer name tab.
    2. Under the Support History pane, review the open cases.
  4. Use your customer's preferred contact method (email or phone) for your FQR.

Email Case Communications

Email communications can be handled through Service Desk, or through the Outlook email client.

The following sections address the processes related to email communications.

Review these TSGs before communicating with your customer:

  • TSG 2777192 (First Quality Response)
  • TSG 3051819 (Email Signature Block)

Follow these steps to access your case communications in Service Desk:

  1. Open a case that you wish to see the email communications for in Service Desk.
  2. Scroll down to the Communications tile.
  3. Click View All to see the full list of messages associated with the case.

If desired, you can make all your email case communications from this tile.

Follow this process to continue your case communications in Outlook:
Only use the following process for customers you haven’t interacted with. If you are already communicating with a customer using the Service Desk email client, continue to do so until the case is resolved.

  1. Send an initial email to your customer from the Service Desk Case mail client.

    • Include support@mail.support.microsoft.com and yourself on CC.

  2. Leave support@mail.support.microsoft.com on CC and the subject line unaltered in subsequent Outlook emails.

    • This is how Service Desk attributes emails to your case.

Create Outlook rules to ensure that customer communications reach your inbox (or folder of your choice):

  1. In Outlook, select any customer email that matches the following description:

  • From: Microsoft Support Notifications (unmonitored) (ngimmail@microsoft.com)

  • Subject: Online update from customer for the case <Case Number>

  1. In the Home tab, click the Rules button, then click Create Rule from the drop-down menu.

  2. Check the boxes From and Subject contains.

    • Ensure they match the descriptions in Step 1.

  3. Check the box titled Move the item to folder, then click Select Folder.

    • Select your preferred folder.

  4. Click OK to apply the rule.

Customer Requests to Avoid Sharing Files Over Email

If a customer opposes sharing screenshots, documents or other files via email, provide them with the below steps to upload the files:

  1. Navigate to Manage support ticket.
  2. Select the support ticket for which related files must be uploaded.
  3. Select the File Upload option.
     

Case History and Case Notes

Case History

A case history is generated as you work with your customer. The following actions are automatically added to your case history:

  • Opening a case for editing

  • Changing the Status or State of a case

  • Editing the details of a case

  • Adding, editing, or deleting a contact

  • Creating, editing, or closing a task

  • Sending an email

  • Logging a phone call

  • Adding notes to your case

Note: Manually entered Case notes should be used for any actions taken outside of these items.

Case Notes

Case notes provide a complete understanding of the case in your notes. These notes might be used by other support engineers to work your case. They can also serve as a reminder so that you can reacquaint yourself with a case if you work on other cases and come back to the customer’s issue.

Note: Case labor is not automatically logged and will need to be manually added. See the Labor Logging section for more details.

Creating a Task

Creating a task can allow you to track your own work, engage other teams for assistance, or allow you to escalate your case.

To create a task:

  1. Open the case that you wish to create a task in.

  2. Scroll to the Tasks tile.

  3. Select which type of task that you wish to create:

    • +Follow-up – Use for self-assigned tasks to help you track your own work and next steps.

    • +Collaboration – Use when you need to engage other SEs or teams.

    • +Escalation – Use to drive escalations of your case.

  4. Fill out the information to route your task as needed.
    Note: Tasks do not have subject lines. Specify who needs to look at the task and what actions are needed in the first line of the task.

For more information on Tasks and their specifications, see the Service Desk Case User Guide.

Transferring Cases or Tasks

You may be required to transfer a case or a task to a TA, another support engineer, internal partner team, or manager. In those instances, follow the guidelines for the most applicable scenario:

  • TSG 2793303 (Out of Office (OOF) Handoff)

  • TSG 2927087 (Internal Teams)

  • TSG 2929022 (ASMS Internal Engagements)

  • TSG 3137642 (TA Only – Manager Recovery)

Note: If you are unsure which queue to use, you can use Service Desk’s built-in tool to determine where your case needs to go.

Labor Logging

You should log your labor in the Labor tile of your case.

Timers are not included in SDC, but you can use the Stopwatch function of Alarms & Clock (Windows 10 app) to help track your time spent.

Track time using the following labor classifications in your cases:

  • Communications – Making phone calls and writing emails about your case.

  • Research – Researching the customer’s issue and possible solutions.

  • Tech Review – Reviewing the status of a case that has been escalated to you.

    • This is primarily used by TAs and partner technical advisors (PTAs).

  • Troubleshooting – Reproducing issues and helping the customer with possible solutions.

Add a log labor entry in your case with these steps:

  1. Navigate to the Active case tab of your case.

  2. Click the Edit button in the upper right corner.

  3. Click the +Labor button on the Labor tile.

  4. Select the appropriate Labor Classification from the drop-down menu.

  5. Select an appropriate date in the Labor Date field.

  • If you enter a prior date, you will be asked to enter a Late Labor Reason.

  1. Enter the time you worked in the Labor Duration box.

  2. Add a description of your labor in the Description box.

  3. Click Next.

  4. Review your submission for accuracy, then click Submit.

  5. Click OK. Your labor should appear in the Labor tile.

Edit a log labor entry in your case with these steps:

  1. Navigate to the Active case tab of your case.

  2. Click the Edit button in the upper right corner.

  3. Click the ellipses (…) to the right of your chosen labor entry and click Edit.

  4. Update the boxes of your original entry.

  5. Select a reason from the Update Labor Reason drop-down menu.

  6. Click Next.

  7. Review your submission for accuracy, then click Submit.

  8. Click OK. Your labor should appear in the Labor tile.

Setting the State and Status

When your case is in Edit mode, you can set the State and Status of your case to track its progress.

Use the following statuses and states as appropriate for your case.

State – Open

[Asset 4514956]

  • Identifying the Issue

    • You are gathering information about the customer’s issue and filling out data in their case.

  • Initial Contact Pending

    • You have not yet contacted your customer.

  • Troubleshooting

    • You are working with the partner teams and your customer to resolve their issue.

  • Waiting for Confirmation

    • You have provided a solution, but the customer has not yet confirmed the solution resolved their issue.

  • Waiting for information

    • You have requested information from your customer, but they have not yet provided it.

State – Closed

Note: Read and follow the Coding and Closing Procedures section before closing your case.

[Asset 4514957]

  • Disconnect/Hang-up

    • Your customer abruptly hangs up or intentionally discontinues the troubleshooting process.

    • Verify that your customer intended to end the case and the disconnect was not an accident.

  • Duplicate

    • The case is a duplicate of another case that is already in progress.

  • Resolved

    • The case has been resolved.

    • Your customer confirms that the solution was successful.

  • Resolved External

    • Your customer confirms that the case has been resolved by a third party (external to Microsoft).

  • Resolved by Customer

    • Your customer has resolved the issue on their own.

  • Un-Resolved

    • The issue is unresolved, but your customer requests or approves a case closure.

Test Cases

As test cases are created manually using the +CASE  button in Service Desk or from the portal, these cases must be attributed appropriately in order to be excluded from reporting. Should you need to create a test case, ensure your case contains the following:  

  1. A Support Area Path (SAP) of Windows / PSS Other.
  2. The words "Test Case" documented in the following fields:
    • Customer Statement
    • Internal Title
    • Case Title
    • Notes (as needed)
  3. A Root Cause or Symptom if required.  Some taxonomies have Other/Test/etc. as options.  Otherwise, select a value that is most appropriate.
  4. Close the case with Status of Duplicate.
     

Coding and Closing Procedures

Before closing a case, check the following:

  1. Is the Product Family set to Azure?

  2. Is the Product set to one of the following values (depending on the customer's issue)?

    • Billing

    • Subscription Management

    • Azure Portal sign-in or subscription

    • Service and subscription limits (quotas)

  3. Is the Support Topic accurate?

  4. Are all case notes and task logs complete and accurate?

  5. Are all partner tickets (such as CRI, TFS, IcM, etc.) closed or mitigated?

  6. Is there a Root Cause Classification present and is it set to the lowest possible level?

    • See TSG 2658158 (Product and Root Cause Coding Guidelines) for detailed steps on how to properly code a case before closing.

  7. Has the customer agreed to close the case?

    • If not, and they are not responding to emails, have you attempted to call the customer during their business hours?

    • If the customer does not respond to calls or emails, have all three strikes been sent?

      • See TSG 2589957 (Case Closure Handling (Unresponsive Customer, Confirmed Close)) for more information on how to handle strikes.
