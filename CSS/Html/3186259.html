3. Procedure

 
Refunds for credit card customers



If the customer contacts Support asking for a refund:

Note: Advocates can escalate to Tier 3 to check the customer’s refund status in Payment Central.

  1. Confirm there is a credit balance on the order in CMAT.
  2. Does the customer have at least one active subscription on the same order ID?
    • If yes, the balance will be refunded automatically to the customer’s credit card within the billing cycle.
    • If no active subscriptions exist on the same order ID or if the customer pays annually, go to the next step.
    Note:

    The credit on a credit card subscription cannot be used to clear a balance on the invoice order or any other credit card. The balance must go to the order or back to the customer’s card.

    The credit only applies to the order from which it came. If the customer does not have another active subscription on their order for the credit to apply to, the balance goes back to the card.
  3. Determine if a refund to the credit card has been attempted.
    i. Have 14 calendar days passed since the statement with the credit balance was issued?
    1. Yes, proceed to the next step.
    2. No, advise the customer that the refund to the credit card won’t be issued until 14 calendar days from the date of the invoice showing the credit balance.
    ii. If CMAT STATUS field shows the refund Succeeded, this indicates that the refund was successfully pushed to the customer’s card. There may be a slight delay before the financial institution posts the credit to the card. Ask the customer if they have checked their credit card account today for the refund.

    iii. If CMAT STATUS field of the refund is blank AND 14 calendar days have passed since the invoice showing the credit balance, click Process Balance on the offset transaction to push the refund to the customer’s card. The refund will be pushed to the customer’s card the next day. (Note: This may vary depending on the financial institution). For multiple sitting credits, click Process Balance on the payment instruments tab to get the total amount of sitting credit. For more detail, refer to 2996655: Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT.

    iv. If CMAT STATUS field shows the refund Failed, advise the customer of the failed refund attempt and ask if they want to update the credit card on their account.
    a) If the customer wants to update their credit card, they can re-enable the account in the Microsoft 365 admin center to perform the update or the advocate can do this for the customer. Once the customer updates the card, disable the account.

    Note: The credit balance will be issued to the updated credit card that the customer added once the advocate clicks on Process Balance.

    b) If the customer does not have a valid credit card for the refund, capture a screenshot of the failed refund attempt and proceed to Manual Refund Request.
    c) If the refund has failed and the card on file is up-to-date and active, escalate to Tier 3. See 2778091: How to escalate a Service Request to Tier 3 Engineering.
    Failed refund attempts to a credit card can be found by viewing the Charge Attempts in CMAT. Look for TYPE: Refund and STATUS: Failed.
    Refunds which have not yet been attempted will show TYPE: Refund and the STATUS field will be blank.
    Successful refunds will show TYPE: Refund and STATUS: Succeeded.

Note: Microsoft will attempt to refund the credit amount back to the same credit card used to make the purchase. After 3 failed attempts, the charge status will be set to ManualRefund in CMAT.

  1. Verify the charge status for the credit transaction in CMAT.
  2. If the charge status for the credit transaction is set to ManualRefund,
  3. Create an assistance request for CAICollections, complete and attach the GFS Refund Template.

Note:

  • Microsoft cannot send a check refund to a post office box (P.O. Box) address. If the customer provides a P.O. Box address for their refund, ask for an alternate postal address.
  • If CMAT shows that the refund is “Succeeded” on the customer’s old/invalid card, you may reach out to PayOps by submitting AR to T3. This is for PayOps to provide proof to their financial institution that the refund was successfully processed on the customer’s old card so they can get the refund.

Refunds for invoice pay customers

Important note: The GFS Case template for check refund request must have the exact customer contact/company name and email that will be used throughout the process. Advise the customer that this should be the same contact until the check is received.

Once the GFS Refund Template has been completed and is correct, GFS will validate the request to be submitted for approval or rejected within 2 business days. 10 working days from validation of refund request with correct template and approval or rejection from CAT team lead/ACM/ GM will be needed. (Payment central included).

Please refer to below table for the timeline of the overall refund process (including AP Team’s).

Owner Description of Step SLA
CAT Analyst Validation of refund request 2 business days
CAT Team Lead Approval from CAT Team Lead 2 business days
GFS Assistant Credit Manager Approval from ACM 2 business days
GFS Credit Manager Approval from CM 2 business days
Customer Completion of Banking Details or Physical Address 30 days
AP Team Create MS Invoice and queue to approver once completed by customer 2 business days
GFS Credit Manager Provides approval in MS Invoice 2 business days
AP Team Release payment via bank wire payment or check payment 2 business days


Please also take note of the timeframe on when the payment will be received by customer once AP Team processed their refund.

  • For Wire Refund: It will reach customers bank within 4-15 working days. 
  • For Check Refund: It may take 7-14 working days before check arrive to customer.

Refunds to EMEA customers are in the form of wire transfer only. No check refunds for EMEA customers.

Note: A credit balance on an invoice pay customer’s account will not trigger an automatic refund to the customer.

Note: Russian customers who want a refund to be wire transferred into an account labeled in RUB (Russian currency) need to provide a VO code. It is not required for USD accounts located in Russia. (Providing a VO code is a requirement of the Russian authorities and NOT Microsoft.)

Local banks should provide customers with VO codes which are sourced from the Russian authorities.

Customers should provide the codes before an escalation is made as it is a requirement for the Accounts Payable (AP) team to issue the payment. Note: When AP team assists the customer, the initial support language is English and then potentially change to the customer’s native language.
  1. Open a case.
  2. Check the following conditions:
    1. If all orders on the same tenant are in DISABLED/DEPROVISIONED status, proceed to the next step.
    2. If the customer has an active Order ID set up via invoice, the refund request will not be processed and instead, the credit balance will be used to pay part or full of the outstanding balance.
    3. If the order is ACTIVE and MONTHLY billed, no refund is provided. The credit will be applied the next invoice.
    4. If the order is ACTIVE and ANNUALLY billed, as long as an invoice will not be generated within 60 days, the credit amount will be refunded. Proceed to the next step. More information about the billing cycle for online services can be found in 2821120: Information about Microsoft Online Services billing cycles.
  3. Create an assistance request for AR support area CAICAT. 
    1. In CMAT, check the UNPAID AMOUNT field of the Order to verify a credit balance is showing. Do not submit a refund request to GFS until a credit balance is reflected on the order in CMAT.
    2. Fill out the GFS Refund Template with information provided by the customer.
    3. Use the following subject line in the assistance request for GFS:
      • For EMEA: [POD EMEA], [AR], {the 2 digit escalation code:} 4C, the Case number, Refund Customer
      • All other regions: Subject – [GFS to Review] Refund of Customer [Namexxxxx]/Order[IDxxxxxx]/Country
    4. Attach the completed GFS Refund Template to the assistance request.
  4. Once GFS closes their assistance request with the approval or denial, notify the customer.

Advocates can provide the two PDFs (Corporate registration and Registration of individuals) explaining the requirements of IFCS for the customer if he/she enquires.

    In which in some cases advocates provide to AP team directly if the information is available in CMAT. If not, advocates need to ask for assistance to CSS Team / Collections team to ask the customer to provide.

    • Microsoft Payment Central: The customer will receive an e-mail from Microsoft@e-mail.microsoft.com asking them to log in to the Microsoft Payment Central Payee Portal and fill out required details for the refund. The Payment Central profile must be completed within two weeks of the customer receiving the information, or the link will expire, and the request will need to start from the beginning with GFS.

    See Step by Step details on how Payment Central works for the customer in Microsoft Payment Central (MPC) ‘How To’ Guide.

    Note: Payment central is available to be used for all regions and countries.

    Note: After payment request is submitted in MPR by GFS and approved, the invitation email will be sent to the customer and it will be expired within two weeks. In case that the invitation email is expired, “Final reminder” email copying MPR program owners will be sent out to the customer, and the entire process needs to be started over.

    Note: 

    • If the customer does not receive the invitation and the payee status is “Pending Payee Action”, they can navigate to https://paymentcentral.microsoft.com and log in by using the email address that was sent to GFS in the original request. This will only work if the invitation was actually sent. 

    • If the status in Payment Central is still “Pending Payee Action”, and customer contact who got the email is telling he/she is not the right person to enter Payee details, then GFS team can share steps to add the right person via Payment Central as steps mentioned below. 

    i. Customer who got the email logs into Payment Central tool, click on Manage Users tab, then hit Add New User.

    ii. Fill in the details needed in the box that will pop-out.

    iii. In the drop-down menu, click Administrator or Contributor, add the correct user details then SAVE. (Ask the new customer contact to log in to Payment Central and complete the onboarding process)

If the customer contacts Support again about their refund, ask them if they have contacted the Account Payable alias to resolve the issue. This information was provided to the customer when the case was closed.

  • If they did not contact the Accounts Payable alias, suggest they do so because this is the team who can directly resolve their issue.
  • If they did but still have an unresolved issue, create a new case and email the concerned branch Accounts Payable team from CAP to resolve the customer’s issue.

The Accounts Payable (AP) team alias:

AP team alias is by country/company code. You are able to find the AP alias on ms.com.

  • For EMEA: apie@microsoft.com

  • For Japan: apjp@microsoft.com

  • For Korea: apkr@microsoft.com

  • For APGC: Reaching out to AP team by emailing at MS Singapore AP <apsg@microsoft.com> if the company code is 1291; emailing at MS India AP SMSG <apinsms@microsoft.com> if the company code is 1098. The email code is determined by the customer’s country and date of the order being placed:

    • For any Indian order placed on or after Oct 6th, 2016, it’s billed under Microsoft Corporation India Private Limited (MCIPL), company code 1098.
    • For NOAM: Reaching out to AP team by emailing at MS US AP <prohelp@microsoft.com> if the company code is 1010,1727,1776,1988,1899,1227,1929; emailing at MS US AP <aocap@microsoft.com> if the company code is 1944,1888,1654; emailing at MS US AP <CHIPFIN@microsoft.com> if the company code is 1429; emailing at MS US AP <moiap@microsoft.com> if the company code is 1548.

EMEA legacy refund procedure for Invoice Pay customers who want support in their language
  1. Create an assistance request for GFS.
  2. Check that an invoice showing the credit balance has been generated. Do not submit a refund request to GFS until the credit balance is reflected on an invoice.
  3. Fill out the GFS Refund Template with information provided by the customer.
  4. Use the following subject line in the assistance request for GFS: Subject – [GFS to Review] Refund of Customer [Namexxxxx]/Order[IDxxxxxx]/Country
  5. Attach the completed GFS Refund Template to the assistance request.

    i. GFS will submit a refund request through their internal tool. The customer will receive an e-mail from Microsoft@e-mail.microsoft.com asking them to log in to the Microsoft Payment Central Payee Portal and fill out required details for the refund.

    ii. Once the customer submits the form, their refund will be processed.
  6. If the customer requests language-specific support, advocates are allowed to keep the case open instead of closing it after GFS confirmed the refund is in progress.
