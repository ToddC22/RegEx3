QBR classifications and talking points


Quality Based Ranking, or QBR, is a score that can be attributed to an account (XID level) to impact the delivery of all ads and keywords within that account. When it is applied, it completely suppresses delivery for that account.

QBR penalties and reasons can be seen in UCM and AdInquiry.

UCM Instructions

Look up the customer, and then check the Alerts tab. The QBR information will show as a line item if the advertiser is impacted by QBR.

[Asset 4032494]


Ad Inquiry Instructions

Just query an account under the Campaign Items tab.

[Asset 4089243]

Then look under the Account column for UPM score: High (500000).

[Asset 4089244]

If you see a 500000 score in AdInquiry, you may be able to find the QBR classification in UCM. Query the account in the search box on the left, and then click the matching account name:

[Asset 4089245]

From the account screen, select the Alerts tab, and look for QBR High.

[Asset 4021922]

If you do not see a classification in UCM, and your review of the account does not identify a clear reason for the 500000, you may escalate for further review.
 


When discussing the delivery issues possibly caused by the QBR score, discuss relevancy and landing page/site quality with advertisers and how improving both would improve the delivery. Be sure to refer the advertiser to our Relevance and Quality Guidelines whenever discussing any information related to quality and/or relevance. One of the key components to provide an effective experience for advertisers and a relevant experience for consumers is to help ensure our team reviews the quality of keywords, ads and landing pages.

Never ever use the terms QBR Penalty or Policy when speaking with an advertiser! These are internal terms only. Instead, reference the acceptable external terms below:

Most actively enforced QBRs are not appealable, due to the user risk associated with the violation. This is the case even if the advertiser has changed or removed the non-compliant content or behavior. In rare cases where it is evident the account was never in violation, and the QBR was applied in error, an appeal may be appropriate.

Even when a QBR classification is appealable, an agent should confirm three things before escalating:

  1. Has the violating content or behavior been entirely addressed throughout the account?
  2. Is the account clear of other violations? If an advertiser has addressed longstanding pop-up violations, but their content adds steps to monetize the user, the account is not appropriate for an appeal.
  3. Is there a pattern of QBR→Appeal→Overturn→QBR? Advertisers who abuse the appeals process will no longer have penalties removed.

Risk-focused classifications currently applied by Ad Quality

3PTS-Manual/3PTS-Auto Impact

NOT APPEALABLE

Indicators: Online third-party tech support - site promotes immediate solutions to tech issues via phone number or online chat.

Policy page: Misleading content policies

Talking points: Your sites or landing pages promote online technical support to consumers for products or services that you do not directly own.

 

Additional steps to monetize the user

NOT APPEALABLE

Indicators: Site promises chance to win gift card/iPad, etc., but the user must enter excessive PII for a "chance."

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies

Talking points: Your sites or landing pages within the account delay or obstruct the user’s access to requested content, products, or services by adding steps solely designed to monetize the user.

 

Browser interference

NOT APPEALABLE

Indicators: Changes browser preferences or resets default homepage. Resizes browser window or disables browser buttons. Landing page content differs between standard browser experience and experience on a proxy server.

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies

Talking points: Behavior of your sites or landing pages does not comply with our policies. Please refer to the Microsoft Advertising Relevance and Quality policies.

Doorway pages or cloaking

NOT APPEALABLE

Indicators: Landing page content differs between standard browser experience and experience on a proxy server.

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies
 

Talking points: Your account currently employs behaviors that impede the site user experience using doorway pages and/or cloaking. Please refer to the Relevance and Quality policies for additional information.

 

Excessive bundling

APPEALABLE

Indicators: Software does not provide disclosures of all secondary download offers and ability to easily decline.

Policy page: Software and downloads policies

Talking points: All software that is included in the download bundle must be clearly disclosed to the user, and legitimate software cannot be bundled with other software that is not allowed. Any software download must include an uninstall function, and the uninstall process must not be difficult. Please refer to Microsoft Advertising Software and Downloads policies for more information.


Malicious PII collection (Phishing)

MAY BE APPEALABLE - Appeals can be escalated if the fraud team has confirmed the account was compromised.

Indicators: The site's main purpose is to collect user information in a deceptive manner.

Policy page: Privacy and data protection policies

Talking points: Site(s) within your account are requesting personal information from the user that should not be disclosed. Please review our User Safety and Privacy policies for additional information.
 


Malware

MAY BE APPEALABLE - Appeals can be escalated if the fraud team has confirmed the account was compromised.

Indicators: A site within the account has been confirmed to exhibit security issues to users.

Policy page: Software and downloads policies

Talking points: **Note: Never use the term “malware” when talking to an advertiser. **
It appears there may be an issue with your account. I will need to engage a specialist for further investigation.

 

Built for search engines

MAY BE APPEALABLE - Appeals should be denied since enforcement is "not in our control"; if advertiser doesn't accept explanation, ticket can be escalated, calling out Filter 106 issue to ensure proper triage.

Indicators: An account with significant KW spam volume, typically low-quality content, impacted by engineering via "Filter 106."

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies

Filter 106 guidance:
When advertisers consistently violate our policies by submitting large volumes of irrelevant keywords, Engineering may apply filtration on the backend known as “Filter 106” (internal only term) which will suppress delivery and have broad account impact. Customers will notice a steep drop in traffic when the filtration is applied. There is currently no UI signal to indicate when an account is impacted by Filter 106, but there are discussions with Engineering on a long-term in-tool indicator.

Some Filter 106 accounts have been labeled with a “Built for Search Engines” 500k penalty. If you see this QBR reason, treat it as an enforcement issue that is not in our control, with talking points below. If the advertiser persists, you may check the UI and see if there is indication of KW spamming history. If not, you may then escalate to Ad Platform & Tools to request removal from the filter. Refer to the Escalation Guide for Sub-Team details.

If there is no “Built for Search Engines” QBR, follow the ad delivery troubleshooting steps as you normally would, then escalate to Global Support for assistance. If they say the impact is due to large numbers of irrelevant keywords, then it is a Filter 106 issue. Refer to the talking points below, and code the ticket as Editorial > Performance Restricted > Delivery Suppression/TM.dat > Restriction Upheld upon closure.

Talking points:
Thank you for your patience. For this customer shell, our system is either currently detecting or has previously detected advertising or business practices that were potentially harmful or deceptive, or in violation of Microsoft’s policies. As a result, ads in this customer shell’s accounts are being impacted. Please note, sites operated by advertisers who consistently violate our Microsoft Advertising policies by submitting large numbers of irrelevant keywords are disallowed. For more information, please see the User safety and privacy policies.

If you would like to continue advertising with Bing, you will need to remove all non-compliant, irrelevant keyword bidding. Please ensure that all keywords are relevant to your ads and landing page to improve the likelihood your ads will be able to serve.

 

Misleading, deceptive or fraudulent (copy, content and behavior)

MAY BE APPEALABLE - If account has business model associated with KW spammers, they may request a review if this is the first appeal and they have fully removed irrelevant KWs.

Indicators: Business models that attempt to mislead site visitors, particularly with unsubstantiated claims of endorsement/affiliation with Dr. Oz or government agencies.
May also be used for Display URL misuse of top-brands like Facebook.com or Amazon.com. 
Also applies to KW spamming for accounts with erectile dysfunction, anti-aging, hair growth, weight loss, diabetes, or sex dating content.

Policy page: Misleading content policies
 

Talking points: Brands and logos may not be used in a way that suggests that the site, product, or service is owned, managed, promoted, or endorsed by the owner, unless a qualified relationship exists. Advertisers must not claim to provide a service that can only be provided by the actual owner of the product or service advertised. For example, a third party may not claim to be able to reset email passwords.

 

490k  

APPEALABLE - This QBR is a temporary penalty, so if the status is not updated within 2 days, the issue may be escalated.

Indicators: New accounts, or existing accounts with new inventory (campaigns, ad groups or ads), may experience a temporary pause while Editorial team reviews content to declare sites clear of violations.

Talking points: Your account is currently under review. The status will be updated within 24 to 48 hours. If your ads are still not serving after 48 hours, please contact us again for additional investigation.
 


Quality-focused classifications no longer applied by Ad Quality, but still may be impacting accounts.

Content supporting ad monetization

APPEALABLE - This QBR is an old penalty and can be appealed if the issue has been fully addressed and the account is otherwise compliant.

Indicators: Over 50% of the "above the fold" content is ads. Site must offer a value to user. Some ads are acceptable, but there must be greater amount of substantial content than ads.

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies
 

Talking points: Sites or landing pages within this account feature content that functions primarily to support ad monetization. Relevant and useful content must be added to the landing page to be compliant. Please refer to the Microsoft Advertising Relevance and Quality policies for additional information.

Displaying consistent error messages

APPEALABLE - This QBR is an old penalty and can be appealed if the issue has been fully addressed and the account is otherwise compliant.

Indicators: Landing page inaccessible or links within site inaccessible.

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies

Talking points: Destination URL(s) are broken and the landing page(s) does not load. Please update the links or address the technical issue.

 

Excessive or disruptive popups

APPEALABLE - This QBR is an old penalty and can be appealed if the issue has been fully addressed and the account is otherwise compliant.

Indicators: Site spawns popups from third-party advertisers or popups that prevent users from leaving the page.

Policy page: URLs and landing page policies
 

Talking points: Sites and/or pages within your account contain disallowed popups or dialog boxes. Please review your landing pages and remove all popups. Please refer to Microsoft Advertising URLs and Landing Pages policies for additional information.

High proportion of advertising

APPEALABLE - This QBR is an old penalty and can be appealed if the issue has been fully addressed and the account is otherwise compliant.

Indicators: Main purpose of site is to re-direct users to advertising. Almost all sponsored links (both side bar and main line). If content exists, information is very basic/obvious.

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies

Talking points: The landing pages in your account provide primarily ads and sponsored links. The sponsored ads should be secondary to relevant and useful content. Please refer to Microsoft Advertising Relevance and Quality policies for additional information.

Site/landing page does not load

APPEALABLE - This QBR is an old penalty and can be appealed if the issue has been fully addressed and the account is otherwise compliant.

Indicators: Site and or URLs do not load properly for a majority of their ads reviewed.

Policy page: Relevance and quality policies
 

Talking points: Destination URL(s) are broken and the landing page(s) does not load. Please update the links or address the technical issue.

 

480k

APPEALABLE - This QBR is an old penalty, and can be appealed, as long as the account appears to be compliant with all Microsoft Advertising policies.

Indicators: n/a

Policy page: n/a

Talking points: We need to escalate this to our specialists for review. This could take up to 48 hours.
 


1300 and 2000 (low and medium scores) no longer impact accounts and do not require an appeal. Other legacy scores include 1000 and 1001, and these also do not suppress delivery.

The only scores that will impact the Advertiser's account are 480k, 490k, and 500k.

If the advertiser has related editorial rejections, refer to the Relevance and Quality policies.
