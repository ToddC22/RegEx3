Procedure: Helping customers with account migration

[Asset 4049349]

Who should follow this article?

  • All Xbox agents who support account management and apps, games, and social scenarios


What is this article about?

The account migration procedure for all accounts with an Xbox gamertag. Use this article anytime you need to troubleshoot region migration issues and determine if an [Asset 4039877] ([Asset 4053626])engagement is required. 

Contents

  • 1. Overview

1. Overview

Xbox customers who moved from one country to another may want to change the region in their Xbox Live account. The name for this is account migration. Customers can migrate their account to a new region through live.xbox.com/AccountMigration

Agents are to help customers resolve account migration issues. Note that when a customer changes the region of their account and their account does not have a billing profile for the new region, the creation of a new billing profile occurs. As an effect, the billing profile for the old region will become inactive, and the billing profile for the new region will become the primary profile. Support will still be able to view the old billing profile after performing a region migration. Do not remove these old billing profiles.

2. Common issues associated with account migration

These are the common concerns some customers encounter after region migration. Customers can review them at: change the region of your Xbox Live account
 

Use the same link to help customers troubleshoot their related concerns. Do not engage [Asset 4053626] for these issues:

  • Unable to perform account migration – See Conditions Preventing Account Migration, below.
  • Customer accidentally migrated account to an incorrect region – The customer must wait 90 days before changing the region again.
  • Lost/inaccessible content – Content from one region may not be available in another region. Content may not function when an account is migrated to a different region. We do not allow refunds for these cases.
  • Lost/missing funds in the Microsoft account – Local currency does not transfer from one region to another after region migration. The currency is still in the account. However, it is only viewable and accessible if the account is reverted to the original region.
  • Downgraded subscription/membership to Xbox Live - Gold Membership can be lost if the account is migrated to a region where Xbox Live is not supported. See Xbox Live countries and regions.
  • Lost Groove Music Pass – Groove Music Pass is canceled after migration.
Note: Xbox 360 Customers can change regions again after 90 days from the first region migration for example, if they are unhappy with the loss of content, a subscription, or Microsoft Account funds.

Xbox One customers do not need to revert the Xbox Live account to the old region/country to access lost content. Customers using Xbox One can change the language and location on the Xbox One console to access their purchased content in other regions.

3. Conditions preventing account migration

If a customer calls in and reports that they are unable to migrate their account, determine the conditions that are preventing migrating the account and provide or perform the appropriate advice or action. You can find these conditions at Change the region of your Xbox Live account.

Do not engage [Asset 4053626] for these scenarios:

  • The account has changed regions within the last 90 days.

    Advise that the customer must wait 90 days from the last account migration before making another region change.
     
  • A subscription on the account is in a suspended or a violation state.

    Advise that the customer must bring the subscription current migrating the account.
     
  • The account has an Entertainment for All contract.

    Advise that the customer cannot migrate regions unless they cancel the EFA subscription and pay the early termination fee.
     
  • The date of birth on the account shows the customer is a minor (as defined by applicable law) in their current region but will not be a minor in the region that they want to change to.

    Advise the customer that region migration is not possible in this situation. Use MST to determine if the account is a child account. See Definition of a Microsoft child account to determine country specific ages.
  • The date of birth on the account shows the customer is not a minor (as defined by applicable law) in their current region but will become a minor in the region that they want to change to.

    Advise the customer that region migration is not possible in this situation. Use MST to determine if the account is a child account. See Definition of a Microsoft child account to determine country specific ages.
  • The account has a subscription that will not change regions (examples: OneDrive, EA Play).

    Advise the customer that the subscription must be canceled first, or they must wait till the subscription ends to proceed with the migration.
     
  • The customer has not verified their security proofs and are not able to pass validation to proceed with a region change.

    Refer to Verify the security information for your Microsoft account. Have the customer verify the proofs before trying to migrate the account.
  • The account has an outstanding balance or declining charges that have not settled.

    Use MST to determine if the customer needs to settle any outstanding balances. Advise the customer that they will need to settle the outstanding balance in order to migrate the account.
     
  • The phone number in the Billing Information does not have 3 digits in the area code.

    This applies to all regions. The area code must have 3 digits even if you must move one of the digits in the next set of numbers.

    For example: (44) 4555-6666 is incorrect; it would need to be (444) 555-6666 for the phone number. Use MST to update the phone number.
     
  • The account has a billing account lock on it.

    Use MST to verify if there is an account lock. Follow the process in KB 931798 Policy: How to handle billing account violations (formerly account locks) to determine if the account can be unlocked.
  • The customer is trying to migrate to Korea. Due to the iPin requirement for all accounts in Korea, accounts cannot be migrated to Korea, The customer must create a new account with their region set to Korea and go through the age verification process required by the Korean government.

4. When to engage Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates

You may engage [Asset 4053626] for the following scenarios. Please review carefully before engaging.

Important: Never engage [Asset 4053626] for a child account requesting a region change.

Account takeover with region migration

If a customer's account has been taken over, it is possible that an attacker modified the account information, and this may have included migrating the account to another region. Engage [Asset 4053626] for this scenario as an account takeover case. Follow the processes in KB 930018 Policy: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account.

  • Xbox.com change region tool failure

    The customer used the Change Region feature on Xbox.com at     live.xbox.com/AccountMigration but the account does not reflect the region change on the console, or the account was migrated to the incorrect region. Follow these steps to determine if you need to engage [Asset 4053626]:
    1. Ask the customer if they have already successfully changed regions in the last 90 days. If so, explain the 90-day policy. Do not engage [Asset 4053626].
    2. If the customer has not had a successful region change in the last 90 days, verify the customer’s region setting in MST. Determine which region the customer was attempting to change to.
      • If MST and the attempted region do not match, ask the customer to attempt to migrate once more at live.xbox.com/AccountMigration. If the customer is able to accept the new region's terms of use after changing region, verify after refreshing MST that the region has successfully changed. Do not engage [Asset 4053626] if region changed to desired region.
    3. If the customer receives a message stating that they could not do a migration and the account is an adult account, engage [Asset 4053626].
    4. If the account is a child account, do not engage [Asset 4053626]. Advise that the customer must wait 90 days from the last account migration before making another region change or wait until they can promote the child account to an adult.
  • Billable delegate admin on billing account preventing region migration

    If a customer has a business billing account administered by a delegate admin, they will not be able to migrate the account to another region. Engage [Asset 4053626]for this scenario to remove the delegate admin and migrate the region.
