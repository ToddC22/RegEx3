What is this article about?

This article describes how to retain customers who want to cancel their monthly or yearly subscription of Microsoft 365 Family, and Microsoft 365 Personal.

  • India: Advocates are unable to cancel Office subscriptions due to Microsoft legal. You need to warm transfer these calls to A&B.
  • Japan: The save process includes the O365 Solo product only.

Note: If the customer wants to switch Office products or services to something different, see KB 3076290 Procedure: Office: Office Solve scenarios.

1. Information and process

1.1 What is save and retention?

Save and Retention helps you walk a customer through understanding their current service’s value and, if appropriate, helps find a better suited offering to improve customer retention and satisfaction.

Save is taking a customer who says, “I want to cancel” and then, through education, remains on their current offer or selects a better fitting Office offer (perpetual included).

Retention is a customer who continues to use their Microsoft 365 subscription long term (90 days plus).

1.2 Save strategy

  • Save on value of the existing Microsoft 365. If this is not successful then:
  • Save subscription by offering alternative SKUs. If this is not successful then:
    • Note: Alternative SKUs should lead with subscription and then follow with perpetual unless the customer has clearly stated a desired payment style preference.
  • Save the subscription by offering discounts, as available and applicable, as a last resort.

1.3 When to have a retention conversation

If a customer wants to cancel their O365 subscription and stop service, it’s important to have a retention conversation, however it may not always be appropriate to save the customer (see section 1.5, When not to retain customers section further on in this article). Microsoft would like to keep their customers, so assuming the customer is open to a conversation, your role is to acknowledge and understand your customer’s needs and provide information to them to try and save the customer.

Return to top

1.4 When to downgrade or upgrade an O365 subscription

Instead of cancelling, a customer may want to switch from a subscription to a perpetual licensed product or to a small business edition of O365. Educate the customer on the difference between the products, answer any questions, and then take the necessary steps.

Summary of the downgrade and upgrade process:

  • Switching from perpetual to subscription: If the customer purchased a fully packed product or token from MS Store and is within the return period, see KB 2844641 Procedure: Office: Product exchanges to assist them with returning the product for a refund. The customer can then purchase the subscription. If the customer wants to switch after the relevant return period, let the customer know that they can switch products, but will not get a refund.

    Note: If the customer purchased the fully packaged product from a third-party retailer, direct them to the retailer for a refund.
  • Switching to Small Business: If the customer wishes to switch to Microsoft 365 Business Premium, you must cancel the customer’s existing consumer subscription before transferring the customer to Commercial support to purchase the Business Premium subscription.
  • Switching from subscription to perpetual: Cancel the customer’s subscription and help the customer purchase the perpetual product. If the customer is within the refund/return period, process the refund. See KB 2844641.
  • Switching subscription products within refund/return period: Cancel the customer’s original subscription and refund as needed using MST. Then, process the purchase of a new subscription product, using MST.

1.5 When not to retain customers

In some scenarios, it is not appropriate to attempt to retain the customer. If the customer is not receptive to discussing reasons for cancellation or their product needs, do not try to retain them. The goal is to ensure a pleasant support experience with Microsoft and in these cases, if you continue to try to save the customer, they may become frustrated.

To find out if a customer should be retained:

  1. Check the customer’s case history to determine if they previously called to discuss cancelling their subscription/disabling recurring billing and retention was already attempted. See KB 2988026 Guideline: Service Request (SR) Creation and Case Ownership.
  2. If the customer mentions any of the following reasons for cancellation, do not attempt to retain the customer, just cancel the subscription, following the appropriate process:
    • Death in the family
    • Duplicate account due to purchasing two subscriptions
    • Fraud or billing issue
    • Incompatible operating system (OS)
    • Military deployment
  3. If the customer wants to cancel a subscription for a product which is out of scope (see below), this article does not apply. Follow the appropriate handling process:
    • O365 Business (Commercial): KB 2912243 Procedure: All-up landing page for Support Boundaries
    • Xbox (Xbox Live, Xbox Game Pass and EA Play): KB 2912243 Procedure: All-up landing page for Support Boundaries
    • MSN: KB 4338529 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Exception based cancel and return for recurring subscriptions
    • Assure: KB 3039170 Procedure: Windows: Microsoft software support services refunds
    • OneDrive: KB 2913995 Informational: Consumer Online: OneDrive Storage
    • Skype: KB 2912243 Procedure: All-up landing page for Support Boundaries
      • Premium Outlook.com see KB 4021072 Informational: Outlook.com Premium
        • KB 2913989 Procedure: Returns and refunds support boundaries
        • KB 3163896 Procedure: Phone and Chat Transfer
        • KB 2769823 Procedure: Transfer codes for other lines of business
      • The following KB articles provide guidance on handling calls for products that are out of scope:
        • KB 2769823 Procedure: Transfer codes for other lines of business
        • KB 3163896 Procedure: Phone and Chat Transfer
        • KB 2913989 Procedure: Returns and refunds support boundaries
  4. Lost or dropped call.
  5. Unable to locate customer.
  6. Unable to verify the customer: See KB 2885545 Procedure: Handling a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner.
  7. Customer has free subscription via academic institution (College, High School).
  8. Partner Billing – For any subscription not billed through Microsoft, refer customer to original retailer.

2. How to have a save conversation

If a customer wants to cancel their subscription and stop service, this is considered a subscription cancellation. Consult with your customer to answer their subscription questions and find the best resolution.

The save conversation is a specific type of sales call with a customer who wants to cancel their subscription and stop service. The purpose of a save conversation is for you to consult with the customer, answer their subscription/product questions and find the best resolution for both parties. The intent is for the customer to choose to keep their Microsoft 365 subscription or switch to a perpetual Office product.

Important: Do not mention the words Save or Retention in the conversation. These are internal terms and should not be used with a customer.

3. Save and retention FAQ 

I want to cancel my Microsoft 365 subscription. Will I receive a refund?   Customers who purchase a yearly or monthly subscription from Microsoft may be eligible for a refund if within the refund guidelines. See KB 4338529 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Exception based cancel and return for recurring subscriptions.

I want to cancel my Office 365 University pre-paid subscription purchased through the Microsoft Store. Will I receive a refund?

Note: Office 365 University decommissioned in 2019. 		The answer depends on whether the cancellation request is within the Microsoft Store refund guidelines. See KB 4338529.

I purchased my Microsoft 365 subscription via a retailer/partner. Can I cancel via Microsoft?

If the customer purchased a pre-paid subscription from a Microsoft partner, Microsoft is not able to cancel the pre-paid subscription.

Refer the customer back to the original retailer.

I want to cancel my Microsoft 365 subscription. Will I lose access immediately?

The Microsoft 365 subscription will continue to work for the duration of the subscription. Remind the customer that once the subscription ends, they will lose benefits, like the 1TB OneDrive and premium Outlook.com features, and free Windows 10 support. 

 
 

I want to cancel my subscription. What happens to my documents?

Customers will be able to keep their Office documents and will be able to view and print them. If they want to edit them, they can use the Office Webs Apps or renew their subscription for full functionality. If they no longer want a subscription, they can also purchase a perpetual version of Office.

My subscription has changed, and I cannot use Office in the same way as I did yesterday. What happened?

When an Office subscription has expired, it goes into reduced functionality mode. Customers can restore full functionality by purchasing another subscription or renewing a subscription. Either way, they won’t lose their documents.

I have received a Microsoft email offer to enable recurring billing my subscription.

Typically, this offer is complimentary time on the customer’s subscription if they enable recurring billing. Ask the customer to select the unique URL in the email. This will take them to Office.com where they can redeem the offer.

Email offers are limited to the USA and UK.

I have charges on my payment instrument (PI) after cancelling my Microsoft 365. Is my subscription still active?

 

It's important to explain to the customer why they are receiving this charge. Do not transfer the call to another advocate:

  1. Assure the customer you can figure out why this has happened and that there is probably a very valid reason.
  2. Ensure their subscription was in fact cancelled.
  3. Check the date the subscription was cancelled in MST.
  4. If it has been cancelled, check the customer’s billing date in MST:
    • Due to customer’s billing cycle, they may not have been billed for the time used after their last payment, for example, if the subscription was cancelled prior to the billing date. The charge they received will be for the previous month.

      For example: Jack is a previous Microsoft customer who purchased a new O365 monthly subscription on MicrosoftStore.com on January 1st. He is a pre-existing customer with a billing anniversary date of January 20th. He uses the subscription for a month and a half, and then calls to cancel on February 15. In this case, Jack’s payment instrument would be charged on February 20th for use of the subscription from January 21st – February 20th, even though its five days after cancelling the subscription.
  5. Review the customer’s subscription page in MST. If there is a Violation on the subscription account, this could be due to a missed or overdraft payment which may have occurred at any time during the subscription:
  • Explain the charge was because of a past due balance. If the new charge on their payment instrument has brought their account balance to zero, let them know they should see no additional charges.
  • If, however, their account was in overdraft or their credit card was no longer valid when Microsoft attempted to collect, explain that we will continue to attempt to collect until the account balance is paid.
  • If this is the case, assist the customer by taking a payment and bringing their account balance to zero so they are not charged again, and advise the customer they will receive a confirmation email about their account status.

Workflow steps

Important: Always perform issue coding in ASD (Save via perpetual customers) or MST (“Save on” SKU customers) regardless of the tool they are using.

The workflow steps below provide guidance on the tools process for supporting a Save customer:

  1. Search and verify the customer account. See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.
  2. Launch Save & Retention Tool in ASD through the Customer Tab drop down and review the customer information for opportunities.
  3. Select Manage Subscriptions for details on the customer’s subscriptions.
  4. Select Action from drop down list:
  • Add Payment Instrument
  • Cancel Subscription
  • Change Payment Instrument
  • Convert Subscription
  • Turn off recurring billing
  1. Select the Cause from the list of options based on the reason the customer wants to make the change (see examples below) and select Next:
    • No changes to current subscription
    • Switch to Monthly
    • Switch to Yearly
    • Cancel at the end of cycle and do not renew [No refund]
    • Cancel immediately [Estimated refund including tax ______]

Note: Messaging, specific to the Cause, will appear under the Reason & Rebuttal information to guide your conversation.

Depending on the selection, MST will either record the save, or proceed with the flow to cancel/turn off recurring billing/remove payment instrument.
