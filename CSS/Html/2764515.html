Procedure: Surface & Xbox: Handling Product Safety (RRT) Contacts

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Band

  • Xbox

What is this article about?

This article describes how to identify and handle RRT cases. This article should be used when the customer mentions that there is a possible safety concern with their Microsoft device (Band, Surface, Surface accessories, Xbox, Kinect, or Xbox accessories). This should be followed immediately when the customer mentions an RRT Keyword.

The safety of the users of our products is our top priority. Any situation brought to our attention by a customer regarding a safety concern, possible injury, or property damage while using our products should be taken seriously.

Deviation from these steps could cause the case to be incorrectly handled. Advocates should follow these steps exactly and should not offer opinions or other solutions to resolve a product safety issue, such as recommending medical treatment, technical opinions, and so on.

1. Identifying RRT (Product Safety) issues

If an emergency is happening at the time of the call, refer the customer to their local emergency contact.

If the customer uses one of the RRT keywords listed below, handle the case as an RRT issue and escalate as appropriate. If the customer's issue is not on the following list, do not handle as RRT. Continue with the appropriate standard troubleshooting.

Property damage

Caused to something other than the first-party Microsoft device.

Fire and flames seen

Fire and flames seen emitting from the device.

Melting

Device is seen to be melting.

Leaking alkaline battery in an IW first-party accessory

This scenario only applies to alkaline batteries in an in-warranty Microsoft accessory. Leaking from anything else is not RRT.

Expanded, swollen, exploded battery*

For Surface tablet devices: Also described as raised, lifted, separated, or bulging screen/keyboard.

If the customer reports an expanded/swollen battery only in a Surface device and there are no other RRT issues reported with the Surface, do not handle as RRT and follow KB 4508455 Procedure: Surface: Expanded/Swollen battery or bulging screen: Overview

Note: If the customer reports the battery has physically exploded in a Surface device, then follow the RRT process below.

If the customer reports an expanded battery AND reports another RRT issue with the Surface continue with the RRT process below.

For all Surface accessories and all other Microsoft products (Xbox): Expanded battery is a valid RRT issue. Continue with the RRT process below.

Burn, burnt, burned

Injury or property damage.

Personal injury

Includes: rash, blisters, skin irritation, allergic reactions, bleeding, bruises, nausea, headache, eyestrain, or seizure (caused by the first-party Microsoft device or Microsoft accessory).

Hearing Issues (Injury)

Hearing issues or injuries caused while using a first-party Microsoft headset/headphones.

Applies to Microsoft first-party headsets/headphones only (issues/injuries caused by third-party headsets are not RRT, customer needs to be referred to the manufacturer instead).

Shock

Caused only from first-party Microsoft device or accessory while connected to AC power, or if customer states medical attention was received.

Smoke seen

Smelling smoke only is not RRT.

Sparks seen

  

Loose object

Any first-party device that plugs directly into an AC wall outlet, like a power supply.

Seizure or seizure-like symptoms

For Xbox: Third-party games not published by Microsoft, continue with the RRT process but refer the customer to the publisher of the game for full resolution.

Ingestion, swallowing, or choking on a piece or part of a MS product

Including batteries, figurines or toys.

2. Exceptions before handling as RRT

2.1. Exceptions for ALL RRT REPORTS

If the customer rescinds (takes back) their product safety (RRT) concern:

Clearly document in the notes that the customer rescinded their concern, and handle as a normal case. You do NOT need to escalate the form to Tier 3.

The customer refuses the interview process in whole or part (and they have NOT rescinded their product safety concern):

Fill out as much of the form as possible and escalate to Tier 3 RRT.

Bare minimum information required are listed below. These are most often required for the customer to receive normal support. Inform the customer that you will have a specialist review the case.

  • Customer full name
  • Shipping address
  • Phone number
  • Device serial number

Do NOT create a normal repair order.

The customer is in a non-launch/unsupported country:

Have the customer answer the interview questions.

Escalate the form to Tier 3 RRT.

Set expectations with the customer that since they are in an unsupported country or region, there may not be any resolution available, but their concerns will be reported.

Externally caused event:

If the RRT issue is caused by an external event (for example, power issues or surges, lightning storms, using the wrong power supply in their country, power converters, or liquid damage) the issue is NOT RRT and should be handled normally.

Do not select the RRT options in ASD. Follow normal troubleshooting for the issue.

2.2 Exceptions for Surface and Band advocates ONLY (the following does NOT apply to Xbox RRT scenarios).

If one of the keywords is mentioned, determine if any of these exceptions apply before continuing with RRT handling.

Pinching on Surface Book hinge

Pinching caused by the Surface Book hinge is an RRT if the customer reports an injury such as bleeding, blisters, or bruises in connection with the “pinch”. Move forward with the RRT process.

If a customer says that the hinge pinched them but there was no injury reported, you do not need to go through the RRT process.

Use the following reactive scripting guidance to help the customer avoid future pinching:
"Microsoft regrets any discomfort or inconvenience you experienced. To avoid this in the future, we suggest being aware of the hinge location and keeping it away from your skin or fabric while opening the unit."

Excessive Heat

If the customer is reporting that their Microsoft device or accessory is too hot to touch when charging or during heavy processor use and no RRT Keywords are used (for example, no visible smoke, flames, melting, injury, or similar is reported) this is not considered an RRT scenario.

Advise the customer that the device or accessory is designed to shut down automatically if it reaches pre-determined temperatures that are well below international regulatory safety thresholds. If the customer still perceives that the device is too hot, they are welcome to request an exchange.

Shock (Touch Current)

While handling the Surface device itself (not the charger) while connected to AC power, some customers may describe the following:

  • I can feel the current or a “tingling sensation.”
  • I can feel the electricity.
  • It is uncomfortable to touch or hold.
  • I can feel a static charge.

For this scenario, provide the following messaging:
"Microsoft is aware of reports of sensations similar to pinpricks when touching Surface while its power supply is plugged into AC wall outlets. The current involved is far less than the amount allowed by international safety standards and does not pose any risk of harm. This is not an indication of malfunction with your Surface product.”

If the customer owns a Surface Pro 3, or newer Surface model with an external power supply, there is a method to reduce or remove the touch current sensation received from the AC power adapter.

Walk the customer through the following steps while the system is powered down.

  1. Direct the customer to keep the AC cord plugged into the wall outlet. It is important that the orientation of the AC plug does not change for this to work.
  2. Disconnect the figure-eight style connector end of the AC cord from the PSU brick, and rotate just the figure-eight style connector end 180 degrees.
  3. Then, reconnect the rotated figure-eight style connector end back to the PSU brick.
  4. Check with the customer if symptoms have improved.
  5. If the customer experiences this same type of discomfort in the future after connecting the AC cord to a different wall outlet, recommend that they repeat the instruction in steps 1-3 to again remedy the issue.
  6. If the customer insists they have been “shocked” after following the steps above and/or they use other RRT Keywords, fill out the RRT escalation form and escalate to Tier 3.

Color transfer from a Touch or Type Cover

If the customer is reporting that color from their Touch or Type Cover transferred onto another object (such as, sofa, coffee table, and others).

Follow the procedure in KB 2991451 Procedure: Surface: A Touch or Type Cover has transferred color onto an object.

C-SKU Devices:

A C-SKU is a working device sold without warranty.

Microsoft is not obligated to provide ANY support, including Software Tech Support unless the customer is reporting an RRT situation with their C-SKU device.

In that case, fill out the RRT escalation form and escalate to Tier 3.

3. Creating or escalating an RRT case (Universal for Xbox, Surface, and Band RRT scenarios)

Once you have verified that the issue the customer has is RRT, (if the issue has not been verified as RRT, return to the “Identifying RRT (Product Safety) issues” section above), the following steps MUST be followed.

  • Note: Any deviation from these steps will cause the case to be mishandled and may prevent timely resolution for the customer.
  • Make sure to follow these steps in this order and do not skip around. Doing things out of order may cause the issue to be mishandled and cause delays for the customer.
  • If the customer declines to answer or go through the RRT process at any time, move forward with completing the case with as much information as possible, and escalate to Tier 3 RRT and clearly note the reason for any missing/incomplete information on the SR.

Process steps:

Advocate actions

Notes
  1. Confirm with the customer that they are at least 13 years of age.

If under 13 years of age, ask to speak with someone in the household that is at least 13 years of age. If no one of age is available, offer to call the customer back.
  1. In ASD, select New Case, then search for the customer's account.
  
  1. Is there an existing Microsoft account for the customer?

Yes? – Continue to Step 4.

No? – then:

  • Choose Other Options and select Create Hardware Customer.
  • Fill in ALL customer information and then continue to step 5.
  1. Account is located and has been verified.

Confirm that the customer's Microsoft device (Xbox, Surface, or Band) has been registered.

  • Is the customer’s Microsoft device registered to their hardware account?
      • Yes? – Continue to step 5.
      • No? – then:
    • Register the Microsoft device to the customer’s account by launching MST.

Note: If for any reason the Microsoft device cannot be registered, note this on the SR and move to step 5.
  1. Verify that the address, phone number, and email on file is correct.

If the customer’s information is incomplete or inaccurate, update the customer information in MST or instruct the customer how to update this themselves.

Note: It is important that the customer’s address, phone number and email is correct. If this information is not correct or missing, this will cause significant delays in any solution for the customer.
  1. On the ASD form, set the below field to "Consult with Advanced Capability Advocate":

For Xbox/Accounts & Billing, the field is: Solution Delivered.

For Surface, the field is: Additional Actions.
  1. Is this the Child SR?

The "Is this the Child SR?" field will appear, based on the customer's preferred language, set this field as per the below:

English: Set field to Yes; continue to step 8.

All other languages: Set field to No; continue to step 9.
  1. English Cases

For English cases, complete the Advanced Capability Engagement Form on the Parent SR; continue to step 10
  1. All Other Languages

For all languages other than English, select New Child SR.

  • Select Edit to copy information from the Parent SR.
  • Continue to step 10.
  • Change the Is this the Child SR? field to Yes.
  1. Fill out the entire form

For the Lifecycle field, select Rapid Response Team (RRT), and continue to the RRT form.

  • Fill out the RRT questions in ASD and all the required fields with detailed notes.
  • Consider, "Would I understand these case notes if they were sent to me?"
  1. For Microsoft Band only (Accounts and Billing):

Populate the RRT form by setting the following fields in the reason for contact – accounts and billing section:

  • Customer Need: Returns & Refunds
  • Customer Question: I need to exchange/replace a product
  1. RRT form will populate

Completely fill out all fields and select Save.
  1. Continue to section 3.1

3.1 Completing the RRT case for escalation

Advocate actions

Notes
  1. Complete the RRT form.

Let the customer know that we will need pictures of the damaged items involved.
  1. Send the customer an email from ASD that they can use to send pictures to.
  
  1. Let the customer know we will need CLEAR pictures of the following:
  • Any visible damage on the Microsoft item(s) that was involved in the RRT.
  • The full power supply and AC Cord that is used with the Microsoft device.
    • For Xbox One S and Xbox One X customers, there is no power supply only an AC Cord.
  • The label side on the power supply brick (this is the side with the writing).
    • For Surface customers, also ask for a clear picture of the PID on the power supply. See KB 3196641 Procedure: Surface: Customers with Surface and some Microsoft-branded accessories but no registered Surface device, section “Locating the Product ID (PID)”.
  • Any damage that the customer reports to other items.
  • If an injury was reported, pictures of the injury if the customer is willing to provide those.
  1. DO NOT wait for the customer to reply with pictures. Encourage the customer to send them as soon as possible to avoid delays in resolving the issue.

After the email with the picture request has been sent to the customer, continue with next steps.

If you are unable to request pictures from the customer, clearly note this on the SR.
  1. Let the customer know that the case will be reviewed by a specialist and an update should be provided within 48 business hours.
  
  1. Instruct the customer to hold onto all items that were involved, and not to dispose of anything.

Disposing of any items may result in no resolution for the customer.
  1. Make sure that the customer’s listed address is updated and complete before completing the case and escalating.

DO NOT create any replacement orders for the customer, this will be done by Tier 3 RRT once the case is reviewed.

If the customer refused to provide this or you were unable to collect this information, clearly note on the case why.
  1. DO NOT promise any specific resolution for the RRT issue. Let the customer know that a specialist will review and provide the appropriate next steps to resolve the issue.
  
  1. Select Save and Complete.

Xbox/Surface: Once complete, select Save and Complete. The SR will auto-transfer to the correct RRT queue.

Microsoft Band: Select Transfer, and set the following fields accordingly:

  • Transfer Type: all sites to t3 msft: ab
  • Transfer Destination: Advanced Capability Advocates – S&S Accounts
  • Select Apply Transfer, then select Complete

If you run into any issues during the RRT process, reach out to your on-site leadership first for assistance.

If further assistance is needed, contact askRRT@microsoft.com.

  • Only use this alias as a last resort, this is not monitored 24/7 so replies will not be immediate.
  • Do not use this alias for live, real time help with a customer.
  • Use of this alias should be limited to: Questions about the RRT process (in general), RRT issues that may not be mentioned in this article, issues with an SR escalating, or questions about a case AFTER it has been escalated to T3 RRT.

3.2 RRT reminders

  • If the customer declines the RRT process or refuses to answer some of the RRT questions, continue the RRT process as best as possible.
  • DO NOT create replacement orders (exchange orders or advance exchange orders) for RRT items (unless otherwise noted).
  • Make sure the customer is fully aware of the next steps, and only let the customer know that they will be contacted back if the article states this will happen. Customers should be provided with the appropriate next steps prior to ending the call (see above sections for appropriate expectations).
