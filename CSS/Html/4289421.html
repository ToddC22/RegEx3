What is this article about?

It can be difficult for customers to understand a charge on their statement, especially if they share devices or payment options with a family member. This article provides steps to help explain charges customers may see on their account.

1. Customer’s process

To see why a charge from Microsoft is appearing on their statement, the first step a customer should take is to:

  1. Sign in to the Microsoft account.
  2. Review Order history
  3. Compare their order history to their statement to find any purchases.

2. Support process

  1. Verify the customer’s Microsoft account and load the customer's profile in MST.
  1. Ask for the customer's account and proceed with account verification. If you can verify, go to step 4. If you can't verify the customer’s account, go to step 3. For account verification process see KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.
  2. Ask the customer for the details on the payment instrument that was charged. Search with the payment instrument (PI) and locate the Microsoft account associated with the PI: 

a. Credit Card

  • Name on card, Last 4 digits of CC, Expiration (or)
  • Credit Card Type and full 16 digits of CC

b. PayPal

  • PayPal email address

Warning

Do not perform this step via chat, you need to switch to phone or use DTM tool to collect data (see KB 4135034 Tool User Guide: Data Transfer & Management).

 

Proceed with the account verification process. If you could not locate or verify the account, go to scenario 5.

  1. Review the Order section with the customer, and ask the customer to look up the following information about the charge:
  • The payment instrument that was charged (credit card/PayPal/other payment instrument).
  • The amount of the charge.
  • Date of the charged.
  • If the customer remembers purchasing anything with Microsoft (timeframe and purchased items).
  • If the customer ever shared the payment instruments with any family members or friends.
  • If this is the first-time they have received a charge from Microsoft that the customer wasn’t aware of.
  • If this is the first-time contacting Microsoft about this issue.

3. Scenarios

See the scenarios below to asisst customers:

Scenario 1: Customer calls about the charges they see on their bill.

  1. Review the order with the customer.

[Asset 4293709]

  1. If the customer still doesn’t understand the charge, review if any of the scenarios below could account for the charge:
Charge Scenario Resources
  • A subscription such as Xbox Live, Microsoft 365, OneDrive, or Skype was automatically renewed.
  • Multiple purchases were grouped into a single charge.
  • Order was split into multiple billings.
  • Authorization hold.
  • A previously declined charge was charged again.
 What to do about unexpected charges from Microsoft
  • Un-authorized purchase.
  • Accidental purchase.
 KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund- section 3.2
  • Pre-order
 KB 2889231 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Pre-Order scenarios
  • Pre-authorization charge
 In some markets, a charge in the local currency equivalent to $1 USD may be applied for credit card authorization. These charges are issued by a financial institution for authorization only.
Explain to the customer that the charge may take three to five business days to drop from their statement.
If the customer has any further questions or concerns regarding an authorization charge, direct the customer to contact the financial institution.
