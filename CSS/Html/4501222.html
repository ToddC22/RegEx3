2. Scope of service and Service Level Agreeemnt (SLA)

2.1 Scope of services (support boundaries)

In scope:

  • Using Intelligence to expand keyword lists based on top 100 performing keywords in active and budget-paused campaigns/ad groups.
  • Scrubbing list of recommended keywords for relevancy and editorial policies.
  • Recommending keyword categorization into existing campaigns and ad groups using "seed" keywords.
  • Bid recommendations for keywords using Intelligence monetization.
  • Creating new broad match modifier (BMM) recommendations based on existing BMM terms.
  • Keyword expansion for net-new, no performance and paused accounts.
  • Keyword expansion using keywords from SQR report.
  • Keyword expansion by Match Type.
  • Providing URLs based on the seed keyword final URL (formerly destination URL).
  • Manual categorization of keywords into campaigns and/or ad groups.
  • Scrubbing list of recommended keywords based on bid amount. You will suggest all generated bid values, unless otherwise specified.

Note: Seed keywords are base keywords you can input in Intelligence to search for new keywords using the More Research option.

Out of scope

  • Providing {Param} values
  • Recommending final URLs for new or existing keywords.
  • Keyword expansion for inactive and deleted accounts.
  • Content or audience (native) ads keyword expansions.
  • Keyword expansion for Bing Shopping and DSA campaigns.

Out of scope for all of Advertiser Enablement Services (AES)

  • Adult advertisers.
  • Language translation and localization.
  • Work requiring advocates to log into non-Microsoft products and services (for example, Google Ads) due to privacy concerns.
  • System/tool limitations that may exist for a particular service.
  • Making any modifications directly to advertiser accounts without explicit direction or approval from requestor.
  • Providing service for accounts who are currently in bad standing with Microsoft Advertising (for example, credit hold or quality based ranking (QBR) penalties).
  • Any services that are not listed on the AES Menu of Services; however, out-of-scope requests are considered on a case-by-case basis and may be handled with a stated turnaround time that is outside the norm.
  • Any customers in non-supported AES markets.

2.2 SLA

The standard SLA for the following keyword expansion is two business days.

  • Standard keyword expansion using Intelligence and SQR
  • Match type expansion
  • Keyword expansion for net-new accounts

The keyword expansion for accounts without performance has a standard SLA of two business days for the first 2000 seed keywords. For the following 2000 seed keywords, BOS advocates are to seek confirmation from the requestor to agree with the amount of time required to complete the work as this requires expansion to all existing keywords.
