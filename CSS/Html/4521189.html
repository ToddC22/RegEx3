1. Motherboard replacement (OEM)

[Asset 4527361]

Alternative option to validate if the customer redeemed the digital offer:

  • Check under Xbox Live and then Windows Store and Xbox One Entitlements to review entitlements and see if the customer redeemed the digital offer after the purchase date.

Scenario

 Customer handling

I had my motherboard and hard drive replaced, but since I never redeemed the complimentary gift, where do I get this back?

Help the customer in a MIR, providing them with an exact replacement per offer type once we have verified that they are eligible.

Refer to the Make It Right procedure (see section 6).

I replaced my hardware and HDD, which I thought would give me another redemption offer. Why doesn't the new motherboard come with a new offer?

Explain to the customer that once redeemed, the gift/offer is viewable from the customer's Microsoft account and this is applicable only for DSB built OEMs.

Ask the customer to go to account.microsoft.com to check their redemption history.

For OEMs built for the new Digital Attach experience, customers will see the redemption history within the store client app.

Note: Console returns for Digital Attach are no different from standard console returns.

2. OS change (OEM only)

Scenario

Customer handling

I bought a device with Windows 10 on it and was told that there is a complimentary offer in Microsoft Store that comes with it? I reinstalled Windows 7, but now I don't know where to retrieve the offer. Can you help?

Explain to the customer that this offer is only available at Microsoft Store on Windows for Windows 10 RS4 and above.

Lower versions of Windows 10 will not have the redemption capability.

I brought my PC home from my workplace and I thought I could redeem a complimentary offer for this laptop. Why doesn't Microsoft Store prompt to redeem an offer?

Explain to the customer that this offer is only available in Microsoft Store on Windows for Windows 10 RS4 and above, excluding volume licenses (VL).

I just reinstalled my Windows 10 RS4 OS, can I still redeem the free offer?

BIOS and the redemption flow is from Microsoft Store on Windows. The customer is still eligible for the offer, and will be prompted to accept the promotional offer in Microsoft Store on Windows.

Guide the customer on how to redeem the gift/offer. (See section 1)

I just reinstalled my Windows 10 RS4 OS. Can I still redeem the free offer if I've already redeemed it before?

Explain to the customer that once redeemed, the gift/offer is viewable from the customer's Microsoft account and this is applicable only for DSB built OEMs.

For OEMs built for the new Digital Attach experience, customers will see the redemption history within the store client app.

Ask the customer to go to account.microsoft.com to check his entitlement.

3. Redemption scenarios (OEM and Xbox)

Scenario

Customer handling

I'm getting an error during redemption in Microsoft Store on Windows. It is asking me to retry, and that something went wrong. Can you help?

Capture the error and the error code look up, to see if there is content to be troubleshooted. If there is no results, escalate to Rangers, SMEs, and TMs.

I bought my device in Canada but have returned home to another country. How come I can't redeem the complimentary offer that is included with this device?

If customer bought device in country A, and returns home to country B, and tries to redeem:

Explain to the customer that cross-border purchases and redemptions are not supported. If the customer resides in China, an embargoed country, and is included on denied parties list, they will be unable to redeem the offers. There is no MIR scenario for this contact type.

I made a mistake and redeemed the offer on the wrong Microsoft account. How can I transfer over to a new Microsoft account?

Explain to the customer that since the entitlement is associated with a specific account, they can install that app or game on the device and all users can have access.

Note: We do not have the ability to transfer apps or games from one Microsoft account to another, and customers will not be able to redeem the offer again.

If the customer redeemed a subscription to an incorrect account, follow KB 818928 Procedure: Xbox: Code redemption issues (prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription).

When I redeem my complimentary game in the Microsoft Store on Windows, I am granted Forza Horizon 3, instead of Gears of War 4. Can you help me?

Use MST to validate what was supposed to be included and obtain proof from the customer regarding their offer. Obtain images from OEMs web site to help confirm if what the customer is reporting is accurate. If after validating the customer’s claim, and you confirmed that is valid to assist in a MIR, provide the customer with an exact replacement for the missing content.

Refer to the MIR procedure in section 6.

I am supposed to get a free game with this device. I've contacted the device manufacturer, and they cannot help. It doesn't look like anything was attached to the device for me to redeem. Can you help?

I didn't see that the game offer was part of the complimentary promotional offer, so I went ahead and purchased the game recently. Can I cancel my purchase, so I can redeem the complimentary offer?

 

The customer mistakenly purchased the game before seeing the complimentary offer for redemption.

Verify that the customer is eligible for a refund and follow our existing common refund guidelines to refund them. See KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund.

Inform the customer that they may be able to redeem the complimentary offer through the Microsoft Store.

I already own this game. Looks like I cannot redeem the same game to my Microsoft account. Can I get another game in its place?

Explain to the customer that each content type license is tied to one Microsoft account.

Suggest to the customer, that they can redeem it to another Microsoft account if they want to use the complimentary offer.

I just went through the initial Xbox/PC setup. I am supposed to get a free copy of a game or subscription from Microsoft Store with this device, but I don't see anywhere to download it for free.

For PC users

Out of box experience (OOBE): If the customer disables the Windows updates in the first 5 to 15 minutes after OOBE, the Microsoft Store experience will not update enabling the Microsoft Store product redemption.

Confirm that the customer has enabled Windows update by asking the customer to review their System settings options and has the most current version of the Store app.

  • Launch store. Select Downloads and updates and begin the Store update.
  • Or get the latest version of the Store app at: https://www.microsoft.com/store/apps/9WZDNCRFJBMP.

Ask the customer to log into Microsoft Store with their Microsoft account, then select My Library to view complimentary offers to redeem.

Note: OOBE experience is the experience a customer has when preparing to first use a new product. It is the process that runs and configures everything when you first turn on a fresh install of windows 10.

For Xbox users

They will need to make sure their console was connected to the internet during OOBE and if not, then they’ll need to connect to the internet. Once connected to the internet, they will need to reset Xbox but keep games and apps so they can go through the OOBE set up once more to claim digital offers.

See KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool for validating Digital offers via MST.
I already own this game or subscription, but I want to give it do a friend or family member?

The ability to gift digital content that was included in your bundle to a friend or family member is not supported right now, but we are looking at the feature for the future.

How do I give the content to a friend?

Customers will need to have their friend log into the customer’s console on their account and redeem the content so they can receive it. It is against the Microsoft Terms and conditions to share account details, so make sure you are not advising customers to share their account details.

4. Resale Scenarios (OEM only and not for Xbox)

If you are unable to validate via MST, you are required to confirm the proof of purchase and follow steps below (see steps in section 3 of KB 4521187 Procedure: Dynamic Store Bundling (DSB)/Digital Attach redemption issues with validation of offers for validation using MST).

Note: Any MIR for digital attach or Roma must be coded to “Used fulfillment service to issue replacement missing content and reported as an issue”, and the issue must be reported by the advocate.

  • Request a digital picture of the customer's purchase receipt to validate the date of purchase and for any indication that digital content was supposed to be included with their purchase. If you are unable to determine via the proof of purchase that the machine should have included digital offers, you can review their Microsoft account to make sure they haven’t already contacted support to receive a replacement. If there are no concerns, use the MIR scenarios.

6. Make it Right (MIR)

In a situation where the customer will not be able to redeem the gift/offer for various reasons, DO NOT always refer them to OEM. As Microsoft support advocates, you will be using this Make it right (MIR) procedure to ensure customers are well supported. MIR allows you to apply an exact replacement per offer type:

Scenario

Make It Right

Motherboard replaced, HDD replaced, offer never redeemed.

Give customers the exact replacement per offer type:

  • Gift card for Gift card
  • App for app
  • Game for game
  • Subscription

Motherboard replaced, HDD not replaced/reimaged, never redeemed.

Wrong product configuration in BIOS (OEM error).

No product was configured in BIOS.

Product configured in BIOS, but Microsoft Store on Windows not seeing the redemption flow.

Important:

To provide customers with a gift card replacement, use the Promo CSV tool. (See Add promotional customer stored value (CSV) section 7 of KB 4053337 TUG: MST: Payment instrument section).

For games offerings use the Token Tool. (Refer section 4. Issue token within KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool).
