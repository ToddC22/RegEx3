Scenario

A customer claims that he has recently made a payment and is looking for confirmation that the payment has been successfully received and applied to his account.

2. Guidance

GFS (Global Financial Services) posts all payments within two business days of receipt of the payment.

3. Procedure

For regions: APGC, Japan, LATAM and NOAM:

A. Has the payment cleared the customer’s bank account (i.e. The customer claims to have made a payment and can prove it cleared the bank)?

  • Yes (Note:All the following required information is non-confidential information.)

    i. Paid by check (For U.S. and India):
    1. Ask for proof of payment. Information needed:
      • The post office box (sometimes referred to as the Lockbox number) to which the check was sent (from the front of the check).



         
      • The Microsoft bank account where the check was deposited (found on the back of the check).



         
    2. Create an assistance request to CAICashApps to investigate the payment and a request to Tier 3 to add the customer to the Proof of Payment (POP) Do Not Contact (DNC) list.
    3. Inform the customer that the payment must be investigated and that we will contact them with an update within 2 business days from receipt of the cancelled check information.

    ii. All other payment methods (wire transfer/EFT/ACH):
    1. Ask for proof of payment. Information needed:
      • Microsoft account number the payment was sent to
      • Amount and currency of payment
      • Date payment was sent
    2. Create an assistance request to CAICashApps to investigate the payment and a request to Tier 3 to add the customer to the POP DNC list.
    3. Inform the customer that the payment must be investigated and that we will contact them with an update within 2 business days from receipt of the canceled check information.
  • No (payment has not cleared the customer’s bank account):

    i. Paid by check (U.S. only):
    • If the payment was sent > 30 days ago:
      1. Suggest that the customer cancel the payment and reissue a new one to the address listed at the bottom of their invoice.
      2. Escalate to Tier 3 to request they flag the invoice(s) as PTP.
    • If the customer mailed a check to the One Microsoft Way address found in the heading of the invoice:
      1. Tell the customer than can either cancel their check and reissue another, or wait for the payment to be routed to the correct address and posted.
      2. Escalate to Tier 3 to request they flag the invoice(s) as PTP.
    • If the payment was sent ≤ 30 days ago:
      1. Confirm the address to which the check was sent, the check date and number, and the invoice number(s) paid.
      2. Inform the customer it can take up to 30 days from the date they mailed the check to be received and applied to their account.
    ii. All other payment methods (wire transfer/EFT/ACH):

    Funds for wire transfers/EFT/ACH are deducted immediately from the customer’s bank account.  If the customer has issued a payment using one of these payment types and the funds have not been deducted from their bank account, they must work with their bank to determine what happened to the payment.
How to escalate for missing, misapplied or misdirected payment:
  1. Create a request and obtain the proof of payment (POP) information from the customer.
  2. Fill out the Payment Inquiry Template.
  3. Contact Tier 3 (Tier 2 for Japan) via the POD to request a Promise to Pay (PTP) flag on the customer’s account. Provide the invoice numbers that were paid.
  4. While Tier 3 evaluates the request and information provided, open a new request.
    i. Including below details when file the request (apply this to the new request for Tier 3 only – do not apply this template to the parent case).
     
    Subject Invoice Payment Proof of Payment T1 Request
    Comment Request to set Promise to Pay flag on customer's Past Due InvPay account, Proof of Payment

    Company Name
    Order Number(s)
    Invoice Number(s) (Payment amount for each invoice if one payment covers multiple invoices)
    Amount paid by the customer
    Currency
    Payment Date
    Reason for inquiry (Please provide details to help fast resolution)
    Payment Details
    1. If paid by check,
      • Check No.
      • Address check was sent
      • Last 4 digits of Microsoft Bank account number
    2. If paid by wire/ACH, Remittance details (Attach bank remittance advice)
      • Last 4 digits of Microsoft Bank account number
    3. If credit offset
      • Credit transfer out order ID (the order number the credit is being transferred from)
      • Credit transfer in order ID (the order number the credit is being transferred to)
      • Credit transfer amount

    ii. Set the customer’s country, as appropriate, and save the request.
    iii. Provide Tier 3 with the request number and transfer the request to the appropriate regional Tier 3 queue.
  5. Proceed as follows based on Tier 3’s decision:
    • The request is complete and valid:
      i. Tier 3 will add the customer to the POP DNC list and update the request.
    • The request has incomplete information:
      i. Tier 3 will reject the request as incomplete.
      ii. Obtain the missing information and contact Tier 3 via the POD again.
      iii. Tier 3 will evaluate the request and information provided. Follow the procedure based on Tier 3’s decision.
    • The request is invalid (payment has been received and applied to the correct invoice):
      i. Tier 3 will reject the request as invalid.
      ii. Inform the customer their payment has been received and applied to the correct invoice.

For EMEA region only:

If payment was made by wire transfer

  • Phone Call

     

    1. Ask the customer for the date the payment was made.
      • Payment made less than 5 business days ago:
        1. Check CMAT to see if the payment has been received and to make sure the subscription is not disabled or will be disabled within the next 5 days. If the customer is requesting to have the subscription re-enabled, please follow the procedure for invoice customers in 2772686: Enabling expired or disabled subscriptions.
        2. Confirm with the customer he transferred money to the correct Microsoft account.
        3. If CMAT does not show the amount yet, tell the customer it can take up to 5 business days for the amount to be received and that he should be able to see the payment in online portal after 5 business days.
        4. Thank the customer for making the payment. Inform the customer that he will continue to get payment reminder email notifications until the payment is applied to their account.
        5. In the unlikely case the money does not appear in the online portal after 5 business days have passed, tell the customer they can contact us again.
        6. Close the case.
      • Payment made more than 5 business days ago:

        Check CMAT to see if the payment has been received.
        • If the payment has been received, confirm this to the customer and close the case.
        • If the payment has not been received:

          i. Confirm with the customer he transferred money to the correct Microsoft account.

          ii. Make sure that the subscription is not disabled or will not be disabled within the next 5 days.

          iii. Get the payment details from the customer and add them to the Payment Inquiry Template.
          • Microsoft bank account details (i.e. where the payment was sent)
          • payment amount and currency
          • transaction/reference number
          • invoice number
          • date payment was sent


          iv. Open an assistance request and transfer to CAICashApps who will check SAP.

          v. Inform the customer that the payment will be investigated and that we will contact them with an update within 2 business days.

          vi. Inform the customer that they will continue to get payment reminder email notifications until the payment is applied to their account.

          vii. Inform the customer that if the payment cannot be located in our payment system, they will then be asked to provide Proof of Payment to investigate further. Proof of payment for wire transfers is:
          • Microsoft account number, the payment was sent to
          • Amount and currency of payment
          • Date payment was sent


          viii. Consider sending a request for DNC to GFS. 

          ix. Add GFS to the beginning of the Parent case subject line

          x. Once GFS has confirmed the payment has been received, send a confirmation email and close the case.

        Note: For security protection reasons, never capture any customer bank account details.

        Important: Note that there is no need to ask the customer to submit a proof of payment. It is only if the payment cannot be located in SAP, that Proof of Payment is required.
  • Email Submission

     

    1. Try to reach the customer by phone. If you can reach him, follow the procedure for phone calls above.
    2. If you cannot reach the customer, check CMAT to find out if the subscription is going to be disabled soon. If the customer is requesting to have the subscription re-enabled, follow the procedure for invoice customers in 2772686: Enabling expired or disabled subscriptions.
    3. If the subscription will be disabled within the next 5 days, follow the general strike process and try to get the following information from the customer and add to the Payment Inquiry Template:
      • Microsoft bank account details (i.e. where the payment was sent)
      • payment amount
      • transaction/reference number
      • invoice number
    4. Open an assistance request and transfer to CAICashApps.
    5. If you cannot reach the customer, but after checking CMAT you can see the subscription is not going to be disabled within the next 5 days, send the customer the following email and close the case:
    NOTE: For security protection reasons, never capture any customer bank account details.

    Important: Note that there is no need to ask the customer to submit a proof of payment. It is only if the payment cannot be located in SAP, that Proof of Payment would be required.
     
If payment was made by ACH, the customer must contact their financial institution if Microsoft did not receive the ACH payment. Microsoft cannot investigate an ACH payment failure with the customer’s financial institution. We do not have access to customer banking information.
