1. Overview

1.1 Assure refund policy overview

General

Note: Regional exceptions may apply which supersede the general policy. Review Regional exceptions below.

Microsoft software support services has a standard 30-days money back guarantee policy with an additional 5-day (unpublished) grace period, allowing a full refund up to 35 days after purchase.

A full refund may also be issued up to 90 days following a recurring billing of Assure subscriptions, if the customer verbally confirms they have not successfully used the service since renewal.

See the Microsoft Support Service Agreement for more details.

Microsoft software support services purchased from a Brick and Mortar (B&M) Microsoft Store are out of scope for this policy. Direct customers to contact the Microsoft Physical Store where they purchased the services for refund request.

Advocates in India will not be able to process a refund and should transfer the customer to another region for assistance.

Important! Make sure you have offered additional troubleshooting to resolve the root cause (a Redo), if the customer states their issue was not resolved, or they want a refund because they are not satisfied with the resolution.

Regional exceptions

Israel

Under the Israeli consumer protection statute, customers are also eligible to receive a pro-rated refund if they cancel their subscription outside of 35 days of initial purchase or 90 days following a recurring billing.

Customers that cancel online do not receive their pro-rated refund automatically. For those that reach customer support, calculate the pro-rated refund amount and issue the refund.

To calculate the refund amount, follow KB 4489972 Procedure: A&B: Calculating the pro-rated refund amount on regional exception scenarios

1.2 PSS refunds

  • Service was purchased within 35 days, including a 5-day grace period beyond the published policy
  • Customer states issue was not resolved, or they are not satisfied with the resolution
  • Advocate has offered additional troubleshooting to resolve the root cause (a Redo)

-OR-

  • Advocate identifies the customer’s issue is unsolvable or hardware-related during troubleshooting (proactively refund)

2. Refund steps ﻿

2.1 Step 1: Verify the customer profile

Attempt to verify the customer profile following the process in KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.                                                                                     

If you can successfully verify the customer, please proceed to investigate the refund request.

Notes:

  • If you cannot verify the customer due to special circumstances (such as, customer forgot the account used to purchased, or just created a Microsoft account for this purchase, or is unable to receive code, or deleted account, and other things):
    • First, assist the customer to find the Microsoft Account (KB 4479728 Procedure: Microsoft account: Forgot my username).
    • Once customer has access to the account:
      • Educate customer and turn off recurring billing
      • Verify account and process the refund
  • If any of these steps fail, warm transfer to A&B.
  • A&B advocates should escalate the case to Tier 3 (A&B) for refund and manual verification, following the process in KB 4506610.
  • If the customer can’t provide Order Number or Email address, following the process in KB 4506610.

2.2 Step 2: Investigate refund request (before you decide to issue a refund)﻿

  1. Determine what type of refund the customer is requesting:

a. PSS Windows

b. PSS Office

c. Microsoft Assure Support Plan

  1. Determine the reason the customer is requesting a refund:
    • Determine if there is a larger issue at hand to avoid a poor customer experience. For example:
      • Is the customer still experiencing technical issues?
      • Is the customer dissatisfied with their support experience?
      • Did the customer purchase the correct service?
    • If a customer is requesting a refund due to a poor customer experience or is still experiencing technical issues, please proceed with the following customer recovery process.
    • If the customer declines further assisted support and simply would like a refund, proceed to Step 3.
  2. Customer recovery process:

    1. Apologize to the customer for any inconvenience experienced during their support experience

    2. Reassure the customer that you are here to assist with the technical issue they are experiencing.

    3. Ask the customer if they would allow us to assist with identifying a solution for their issue.

    4. If the customer agrees to additional troubleshooting that is outside of your scope, let them know you are transferring them to another advocate for further assistance.
  • Warm transfer the customer to the appropriate technical support team. See KB 2769823 Procedure: Transfer codes for other lines of business for more information.

Suggested messaging:

“I see that the last Microsoft Software Support service did not resolve your issue completely. May I ask if I could assist you once again to resolve the issue free of charge before we move on to refund?“

“I’ll be happy to assist you with a refund today. However, I would like to get to the bottom of this issue. Would it be ok if I take a last look to see if I can resolve the issue before we proceed with the refund? ”

2.3 Step 3: Determine if the customer’s order was placed within the last 35 days OR fits a regional exception from section 1.1

Has the customer’s order been placed within the past 35 days OR fit a regional exception from section 1.1?

  • If YES: Proceed with the Processing refund requests section of this article.
  • If NO: Explain the Refund policy to the customer, advising that we cannot process the refund request.

3. Processing refund requests (Assure subscription and PSS)

For more information on processing an offset or partial refund using MST, see KB 4053523 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST) – Orders section.

  1. Find the order in Orders section of MST. Select icon and proceed with Refund line item.

  2. In the following screen, enter
  • Total refund amount (pre-filled) OR partial refund amount
  • Reason code - select Technical Service charge and
  • Refund option - select Refund to payment instrument immediately
  • Comments field – enter the service request number the refund is processed on and select Next.

  1. If everything is indicated correctly select Submit. The refund is now completed.
  2. If the purchase was for an Assure subscription, follow the process in section 4, Use MST to cancel the Microsoft Assure subscriptions.

4. Use MST to cancel the Microsoft Assure subscription

  1. Once an active Assure subscription is refunded, you will need to also cancel Assure subscription. Go to Home > Other Services.


  2. Find Assure subscription and see if status still shows enabled.

  3. Select icon (see image above) and select cancel subscription.
  4. Select Next. It will proceed to the subscription cancellation screen.
  5. The Assure subscription cancellation process includes identifying the reason for the customer’s request and provides talking point to reinforce the benefits of the service, summarized in the table below for reference.

Cancellation reason

Talking points

I prefer a different service or offer

Empathize with the customer. Reinforce the benefits of why the customer should keep their product based on their technical support needs.

I have experienced fraud or theft

For example, Assure provides broader coverage than other services such as Microsoft 365, Live or other; covers all devices under owner's Microsoft account.

I don't value the service: I can do this on my own

Assure is essentially an insurance plan or extended support contract for your software support needs - including virus removal and PC tune-up.

The price is too high

Price compare: Advise the customer that they are getting unlimited support for a one-year subscription vs. pay per incident (do a cost comparison).

The customer bought a new device with Complete and no longer wants Assure

If the customer still wishes to cancel, submit the cancellation. If they request a refund, provide the refund according to policy (see How to Process a Refund in this article).

Other

Let the customer know they can come back at any time to re-subscribe if needed and provide them Microsoft’s contact information.

I have a billing issue

Let the customer know you understand they are concerned about charges on their credit card.

In the case of an issue relating to Microsoft’s current billing systems and policies, explain the policy and why they may be seeing their billing charges listed.

If the customer still wishes to cancel, submit the cancellation. If they request a refund, provide the refund according to policy (see How to Process a Refund in this article).

Let the customer know they can come back at any time to re-subscribe if needed and provide them Microsoft’s contact information.

My original issue was never resolved

Empathize with the customer. Ask for details on the error message or issue and offer assistance to resolve.

Offer to resolve the issue and if you cannot, let them know that you can transfer them to a technical specialist to help resolve their issue.

If they don't want to be transferred or cannot solve the issue over phone, tech support should suggest they try a local Microsoft Store located near them (provide the nearest location) and note that it is part of their Assure service plan value.

If the customer still wishes to cancel, submit the cancellation. If they request a refund, provide the refund according to policy (see How to Process a Refund in this article).

Let the customer know they can come back at any time to re-subscribe if needed and provide them Microsoft’s contact information.
