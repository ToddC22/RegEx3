1. Display issues

Confirming the issue

When using the Surface device, the customer sees the following:

  • Garbled picture/distorted picture
    • The image on the display does not appear correctly or as expected.
    • This can be in the form of waves, pixilation, or other distortions on the image.

[Asset 4294948]

  • Lines through screen
    • There are easily visible lines on the screen that go from edge to edge.
    • These lines can be vertical or horizontal.
    • These lines can be black, white, or multi-colored.

[Asset 4294949]

  • Screen flicker
    • Screen flicker is not the same as screen scramble. When talking to customers be sure to use the correct terminology.
    • The entire Surface screen or sections of the screen will NOT shift position quickly but will “flash” to a black screen or black section, where the image on the screen goes away for a very brief point in time.
    • Another significant characteristic is the impression of screen tearing.
    • The images cut in and out; going back and forth between a black screen and an image.
    • This can occur on the entirety of the screen, or in portions of the screen.
    • The image can sometimes appear to be stretched or pulled while flickering.

[Asset 4294950]

  • Screen scramble
    • Screen scramble is not the same as screen flicker. When talking to customers be sure to use the correct terminology.
    • The Surface display image on screen appears to shift either horizontally or vertically VERY quickly. Customers may identify it as a “fuzzy” or overlapping duplication image.
    • The duplicate images can also appear static as a combined/interlaced image.
    • The customer describes lines of the screen missing (black single pixel lines from edge to edge)
    • Finally, touching the screen may temporarily mitigate these issues described above.
    • The image appears fuzzy or as if it’s “shaking.”
    • It looks as if multiple images are on top of one another.

[Asset 4294951]

 

Confirmation/paraphrase

Messaging: "Just to make sure I understand your issue: When your Surface is on…”

  • Garbled picture/distorted picture: “… the image on the screen is distorted or unclear and does not appear as expected.”
  • Lines through screen: “… there are visible lines on the screen that go from edge to edge.”
  • Screen flicker: “… the image on the screen cuts in and out. As if the screen loses back light and goes to a black screen.”
  • Screen scramble: “… the image on the screen looks fuzzy or as if multiple images are on top of each other.”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Garbled picture/distorted picture
    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Garbled Picture/Distorted Picture
    • MST Repair Code: Display Issue (if device is replaced)
  • Lines through screen
    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Lines Through Screen
    • MST Repair Code: Display Issue (if device is replaced)
  • Screen flicker
    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Flicker
    • MST Repair Code: Display Issue (if device is replaced)
  • Screen scramble
    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Scramble
    • MST Repair Code: Display Scramble (if device is replaced)

2. Check known issues

From KB 4072657 Informational: Surface: Technical guides and known issues landing page, select the customer’s Surface model and check for the issue.

Note: For customers with a Surface Pro 4, see KB 3149169 Technical Guide: Surface Pro 4 known issues the Screen Flicker/Scramble on Surface Pro 4 section after completing the troubleshooting steps below.

3. Restart

Go to Windows > Power > Restart

Restarting the device puts the device, drivers, and operating system in a known good state.

If the issue is resolved after a restart, we know it’s a driver or software issue. Ensure the device is up to date

4. Force the device to shut down and restart.

(varies by new vs. old generation)

Studio (all), Laptop (all), Pro (5th Gen), Pro 6, Book 2, and Go: Press and hold the power button for 20 seconds.

Other devices: Press and hold the volume-up button and the power button at the same time for at least 15 seconds and then release both.

The Surface may boot to Unified Extensible Firmware Interface (UEFI). This is normal. Have the customer exit UEFI.

Video available at Force a shut down and restart your Surface.

The device may be in a “hung” state. Force Surface to completely turn off all power and restart.

Performing a Forced Shutdown is like removing the battery from a laptop or unplugging a desktop PC. It resets the hardware and clears all the cache and memory for your hardware.

5. Boot to UEFI and see if the issue is present.

If the issue occurs in UEFI, replace the device.

If the issue does not occur in UEFI, continue troubleshooting.

Go to Windows > Power > Shutdown

Once the device is shutdown, press and hold the volume up button, and single press the power button.

The device will boot to the Surface logo, and you should see spinning dots. After the Surface logo, the device should boot to UEFI.

Note: Surface Pro 4, Book, and newer models ship with Surface UEFI which is a white screen with several options on the left-hand side. Older devices (Surface Pro 3, Surface 3, and older) ship with an American Megatrends UEFI that has a black screen and only a few options.

UEFI is an environment that exists completely outside of Windows and other installed applications.

If the issue exists in UEFI, then we know it’s a hardware failure.

If the issue does not exist in UEFI, then we know the symptom is being influenced by software such as Windows or other applications.

6. Reset

Good: Fresh Start

Go to Start > Settings > Update & security > Recovery.

Under Fresh Start, select Additional info > Get Started.

Better: Reset from within Windows

Go to Start > Settings > Update & security > Recovery.

Under Reset this PC, select Get Started. Follow on screen instructions.

Best: USB recovery

The customer will need to download and set up a recovery image on a USB drive. Send the USB recovery link to the customer and help them get started. Information on what size and type of drive is provided at the destination page.

-OR-

Set up an order to have a USB recovery drive sent to the customer.

 

Fresh Start (good):

  • Fresh Start is the least intrusive form of reset.
  • Customers will not lose any files or personal information.
  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop programs.
  • Fresh Start also ensures the customer has all available updates once complete.
  • If there is an issue with Windows itself, the issue may still occur after a reset.

Reset from within Windows (better):

  • This option uses the devices Hard Drive Partition to reimage the device.
  • This option will delete personal data.
  • The customer will need to reinstall desktop apps.
  • The customer will need to run updates upon completion.
  • If there is an issue with the partition, the issue may still occur after the reset.

USB Recovery (best):

  • This option reimages the entire device, including the partition on the hard drive.
  • It is the most complete form of reset.
  • Customers will need to back up their personal data.
  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop apps. Customers will need to run updates after completion.
  • If the issue occurs after a reset, then the root cause is very likely hardware related.

Is the issue resolved?

Yes, close the case: Once resolved, have the customer check for updates.

 

For SDT enabled sites, let the customer know about the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit.

For all other sites, check Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update for updates.

KB 4014334 Procedure: Surface: Using the Microsoft Surface Diagnostic Toolkit

Making sure Surface and Windows are up to date may help avoid this problem in the future.

No, replace the device: Set up an exchange for the customer based on the status of their warranty.

Use KB 2791320 Informational: Surface: Exchange and fulfillment landing page to create a service order

Note: For customers with a Surface Pro 4 exhibiting “screen scramble” from KB 3149169 Technical Guide: Surface Pro 4 known issues the “Screen Flicker/Scramble on Surface Pro 4” – validate appropriate OOW replacement options, and provide applicable solution.

There is likely a hardware problem with the Surface device.

2. Screen quality issues

Confirming the issue

When using the Surface device, the customer sees the following:

  • Dead pixels
    • One or more dark or bright spots that are noticeable to the customer.
    • These spots can be clustered together or spread out across the screen.
    • They are likely very small and may be hard to see.

[Asset 4294952]

  • Discoloration (yellowing/browning)
    • A yellow or brown line that goes from edge to edge of the screen.
    • Most commonly seen on the left side or right side of the screen but can be other locations too.
    • Is easiest to see against white backgrounds.
       

[Asset 4294953]

  • Backlight bleed/light leakage
    • Light appears to “leak” or “bleed” from the edge of the screen.
    • There may be one or multiple visible bright spot or can run across the entirety of the edge.

[Asset 4294954]

Confirmation/paraphrase

Messaging: "Just to make sure I understand your issue: When your Surface is on, the screen appears to have: ..."

  • Dead Pixels: “… white or discolored dots on the screen.”
  • Discoloration (yellowing/browning): “… a yellow or dark line that is visible on the screen.”
  • Backlight bleed/light leakage: “… light form the outside edge of the screen that is visible.”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Dead pixels
    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Dead Pixel
  • Discoloration (yellowing/browning)
    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Discoloration (Yellowing/Browning)
  • Backlight bleed/Light leakage
    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Light Leakage (Backlight Bleed)

3. Brightness/dimming issues

Confirming the issue

When using the Surface device, the customer sees the following:

  • The screen’s brightness cannot be changed in Windows, by using the keyboard, or both.
  • The screen dims randomly, or unexpectedly.
  • The screen’s brightness is not as bright as expected (too dim).
  • Surface’s auto-dimming feature is not automatically adjusting brightness based on the customer’s environment.
     

[Asset 4294955]

Confirmation/paraphrase

Messaging: "Just to make sure I understand your issue: When your Surface is on, the Surface screen brightness or dimming does not appear to be working.”

How to code

    • Surface Device > Touchscreen > Screen > Brightness Too Dim

3. Turn automatic screen brightness on/off

Change screen brightness

Go to Settings > System > Display

Under "Adjust my screen brightness automatically" select On or Off.

Try to reproduce the issue in both settings.

Note: This will not be available on Surface Studio as Surface Studio does not have an ambient light sensor. Ambient light sensors are used to help save battery life by automatically dimming the screen based on your environment.

This setting may not be preferred by the customer.

If the customer prefers auto brightness off, advise the customer that this may impact battery performance, and make sure they are comfortable with turning the feature back on.

If the customer wants to keep auto-brightness setting enabled, and the issue is not resolved, continue to the next step.
4. Adjust adaptive brightness and contrast settings

Adaptive brightness and contrast on Surface devices

Install and then open the Intel Graphics Command Center app and adjust the power settings.
 

Surface devices also adjust the screen brightness and contrast based on the content displayed. For example, if you open something that's mostly black, then you switch to something that's mostly white, the brightness and contrast may adapt. This is normal.

The content adaptive brightness and contrast feature provides optimal brightness and contrast levels, while saving power and providing longer battery life. You can adjust this option if you want it to behave differently—but if you do, your device won't run as long on battery power.

To temporarily disable this feature, just connect your Surface to the power supply.

5. Run the Screen Brightness Troubleshooter

Send this link to the customer via email.

In the File Download Dialogue box select Open and follow the onscreen instructions.

The troubleshooter will run a series of automated scripts that will attempt to detect problems with the drivers and Windows settings that enable screen brightness.

Once completed, the troubleshooter may or may not find an issue with brightness. Follow recommended solutions provided by the troubleshooter.
