Technical Guide: Surface: BitLocker

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Surface

What is this article about?

  • This article will help you address common BitLocker issues that your customer may encounter. This Technical Guideline should be used any time you work with a customer to resolve their BitLocker issue.

Table of contents

Issues covered by this document include:

  • Prompted for BitLocker recovery key
  • Recovery key (where to find, how to use)
  • How to manage BitLocker
  • Problems using BitLocker 

Confirming the issue

When the customer turns on the device, it continuously prompts for a recovery key. After entering the key, the device may not boot.

[Asset 4345725]

Confirmation/paraphrase

 “Just to make sure I understand your issue, when you turn on your Surface you are always asked to enter a BitLocker Recovery Key.”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Windows OS > Security / Privacy / Accounts > BitLocker > Prompted for BitLocker Recovery Key

4. Is the device being prompted for a BitLocker recovery key after installing updates to Surface UEFI or TPM firmware?

If yes, follow the steps in KB 4057282 Prompted for BitLocker recovery key after installing updates to Surface UEFI or TPM firmware on Surface device.

If the device recently took a UEFI or TPM update, there is a small chance that a customer could see the following symptoms under the right conditions:

  • At startup, you are prompted for your BitLocker recovery key, and you enter the correct recovery key, but Windows doesn’t start up.

  • You boot directly into the Surface Unified Extensible Firmware Interface (UEFI) settings.

  • Your Surface device appears to be in an infinite reboot loop.

Confirming the issue

When the customer turns on the device, it is locked out and prompts for a recovery key. There are several reasons that might make a PC go into recovery mode. For example, your organization might have a password security policy that locks you out after a certain number of failed attempts to sign in. Or, perhaps your PC encountered a hardware malfunction, an unexpected configuration change, or another security event. Requiring a recovery key helps ensure that only an authorized person can unlock your PC and restore access to your encrypted data.

[Asset 4345725]

Confirmation/paraphrase

 “Just to make sure I understand your issue, you are prompted to enter the BitLocker recovery key, but you don’t know where to find it?”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Windows OS > Security / Privacy / Accounts > BitLocker > Recovery Key (where to find, how to use)

3. Guide the customer to the BitLocker recovery key site.

On another device, have the customer sign in to the following site, https://account.microsoft.com/devices/recoverykey to locate their recovery key.

Try searching the older site if the recovery key is not visible: https://onedrive.live.com/RecoveryKey

 

 

If the customer completed OOBE by using a Microsoft account, the recovery key is automatically saved to that Microsoft account as part of the automatic enablement of BitLocker.

If the recovery key is not found on the site, they may not be using the Microsoft account that first completed OOBE on the device.

If there are multiple Microsoft accounts being used on the device, the customer will need to have the owner of the other accounts sign in to the site to see if the recovery key was saved to their account instead.

4. Is the customer locked out of their MSA?

Have the customer go to the Reset your password page to resolve problems with their Microsoft account.

The customer will need to unlock their MSA.

5. Did the customer complete OOBE using an offline account?

If the customer used an offline account and enabled BitLocker, they would have three options to save their recovery key:

  • Save to your Microsoft account.

    The customer needs to Sign in with a Microsoft account. If they did this, by changing their account settings on the device, the key will be saved under the Microsoft account they used.

  • Save to a file. 

    The customer can save the recovery key to file that is not on the drive being encrypted. E.g. a USB flash drive. If they did this, they need to locate where they saved the file.

  • Print the recovery key. The customer can print the key to sheet of paper. If they did this, they need to locate that printout.

If the customer elected to use an offline account using OOBE, BitLocker is not automatically enabled. When the customer goes to enable it, they will be asked to save the recovery key. There are three options to select from, that will determine where the recovery key is stored.

6. Did the customer complete OOBE using an organizational account?

Redirect the customer to an IT administrator for the customer's organization.

If the customer selected Set up for an organization or the device was already connected to their company’s domain, their recovery key may not be listed under their Microsoft account.

7. Recover from a drive.

The customer will need to download a recovery image for their Surface and boot from it by using a USB drive. When prompted for the recovery key, they must select Skip this drive.

All information on the drive will be erased.

If the customer is unable to locate the BitLocker recovery key, there is no other mechanism to unlock the drive.

Confirming the issue

The customer wants to suspend protection, back up their recovery key, turn off or on BitLocker.

Confirmation/paraphrase

 “Just to make sure I understand your issue, you would like to…”

  • “...suspend BitLocker protection.”

  •  “...backup the recovery key.”

  • “...turn off/on BitLocker protection."

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case.

  • Windows OS > Security / Privacy / Accounts > BitLocker > How to manage BitLocker

Resolution steps

2. Check known issues.

From KB 4072657 Surface known issues landing page, select the customer’s Surface model and check for the issue.

There may be a current known issue that the customer is experiencing, with different troubleshooting steps.

Check known issues regularly.

3. Open the BitLocker Drive Encryption Control Panel.

Search for “Manage BitLocker” in Cortana or Windows Settings.

From this app, you can:

  • Suspend protection. This will temporarily turn off BitLocker protection. If the customer does not manually enable it again, it will resume automatically the next time they restart the PC.

  • Back up your recovery key. The customer can save the recovery key to their online account, save to a file, or print the recovery key. file that is not on the drive being encrypted. For example, a USB flash drive. If they did this, they need to locate where they saved the file.

  • Turn off BitLocker. This will permanently disable BitLocker and decrypt the drive.

  • Turn on BitLocker. This will enable BitLocker and encrypt the drive.

Customers don’t commonly need to modify any of these settings for Surface devices, but depending on how they went through OOBE, they may need to manually enable BitLocker.

You can run the admin PowerShell command manage-bde c: -protectors -get to view the recovery key for advanced troubleshooting purposes. For back up purposes, we recommend the customer use the available options in the BitLocker control panel

Yes, close the case: Once resolved, have the customer check for updates.
