Guidance   

Certain scenarios require the viewing/changing of VL agreement information. In those cases, additional personally identifiable information (PII) authorization is required. Please refer to 2801563: How to verify and entitle Volume Licensing customer(s). This is called out on a scenario-by-scenario basis in this document.

Support boundaries

If the customer comes in through either VL or Commerce, advocates will not bounce the customer back but will continue to support them following their respective procedure.

Note: If the scenario the customer is describing does not fit with any of the scenarios below described in this article, or if advocates are uncertain if a case should be transferred to the Volume Licensing team or kept under Commerce ownership, please also review the article 4539127: Procedure: M365 Commerce: How can Support Transfer a Rave Case to Volume Licensing Team.

Which admin roles from each tool are entitled to support?

  • CMAT - The person either shown in the Tenant Discovery Emails or the VL payload.
  • VLSC - VL admin (Blue man icon), OSM Admin (yellow triangle) and VL Contact in VLSC Impersonation tool.
  • Explore.ms - Notices Contact, Primary Contact, Online Admin and OSM on the VL agreement.
When does a VL subscription move from Active to InGracePeriod?
If a VL subscription has moved past its end date, the service will change to HAR or END state immediately. The status on the VL side can be viewed in the Customer Management Agent Tool (CMAT) by searching VL Information > Agreement Number.

VL Status in CMAT:
ACT = Active
HAR (Held Awaiting Reactivation) = InGracePeriod or Disabled or De-provisioned
END = InGracePeriod or Disabled or De-provisioned
 

How long does a VL subscription stay in the InGracePeriod state?

InGracePeriod update notes

Starting with September 2017, for all O365 subscriptions, the grace period should be 90 days.
NOTES:

  • Prior to September 2017, the length of InGracePeriod status could vary, based on the subscription offer.  

  • Other cloud subscriptions have different lifecycles and will be aligned in the future.

For more information check KB 2812947 - Information about the subscription lifecycle


The length of InGracePeriod status also varies, depending on the subscription.

  • If the subscription is already in InGracePeriod, advocates can check the Current Status End Date in CMAT to determine when the subscription will move into Disabled status.
  • If the subscription is still Active and the customer wants to know how long the grace period is, Commerce advocates can collaborate with Commerce T-3 or VL advocates to identify the specific grace period.

Scenario 2. Customer renewed their agreement and now has duplicate subscriptions on their tenant.
a. Suggested script

Instruction: Advocate should request agreement number.

"What is your VL agreement number?"
  Steps 1. Entitle the customer on the agreement, following 2801563: How to verify and entitle Volume Licensing customer(s).
2. Search by VL Information/Agreement Number.
[Asset 4528185]
3. Go to the Subscriptions page.
  Validation Are there duplicate subscriptions?
  • Yes. Proceed to step b.
  • No. Please ask the customer to confirm the VL agreement number, and return to the beginning of step a with the new VL agreement number.
b. Suggested script

Instruction: Advocate asks if a new or renewed agreement exists.

“Did you recently renew your service and receive a new agreement number?”
  Validation
  • Yes. This is by design. Confirm that the customer sees their licenses pooling (customer sees all licenses of a service type in one group). Please inform the customer that because the new subscription is the same license type, they will not need to move users. The users will be associated with the active licenses automatically.

    Note: If a new agreement was signed and resulted in two duplicate line items, disable the subscriptions associated with the old or expired EA.
  • No/Don’t Know. Contact your Team Lead, and escalate the case to OVLS POD for collaboration.

Scenario 11. Customer requests an extension on their Volume Licensing subscription.


Guidance to request VL subscription extension.
This guidance applies to VL account teams requesting an extension to an existing VL customer’s subscription.

Extension guidelines:
  • For non-government subscriptions, the account team can request a 1-time up-to-90 day extension to the subscription’s grace period. No Business group (BG) approval required.
  • For Government contracts, BG must approve the extension.
  • The account team may request a second extension or a longer extension if the customer has subscriptions totaling > 10K seats. In this case, the BG must approve the extension submitted by T3 through M365 Deal Desk dashboard.
Note:
  • In all cases, the account team must have written evidence from the customer (in email form) that they want/approve this extension (this is required for data deletion compliance).
  • This procedure only applies to scenarios where the account team is contacting support, not when the customer contacts support. All requests must have involvement from the Microsoft Account Team.
  • If BG Approval is needed, then the Customer’s Account Team can raise a concession request here: https://empguide-v4.trafficmanager.net/concession/1990. In the meantime, please extend the customers Grace period on the subscriptions for 7 days until the decision from BG is decided.


To allow for the best experience for customers, the time a Volume Licensing subscription is in InGracePeriod can be extended. This workaround is to be used to provide service to the customer while their Volume Licensing subscription renewal or extension is being processed. Users will continue to have access to their service when the subscription is in InGracePeriod status.

Tier 1:
  • After proper entitlement:
  • If the customer’s subscription is in Active status, do not extend the end date. Users will have access to their service even when the subscription moves into InGracePeriod.
  • If the customer’s subscription is in Ingraceperiod status:
    1. Confirm with the customer that their Volume Licensing renewal or extension is in progress or they are working towards renewing or extending their existing service.
    2. Ask the customer how long they believe they will need to complete the renewal or extension. Note this time.
    3. Extend the current state end date to the desired date as determined in step 2. The maximum extension allowed is 90 days from the current date, if requested.
  • If the customer’s subscription is in Disabled status:
    1. Confirm with the customer that their Volume Licensing renewal or extension is in progress or they are working towards renewing or extending their existing service.
    2. Ask the customer how long they believe they will need to complete the renewal or extension. Note this time.
    3. Escalate to Tier 3 Engineering per 2778091: How to escalate a Service Request to Tier 3, Engineering.
Tier 3 Engineering steps to extend the end date of a disabled VL subscription:
  1. Re-enable the subscription.

    Note: A VL subscription in HAR or END should never be left in the Active state.
    • If the re-enable option is not available, update the end date to one day beyond the current status end date. This will allow the subscription to be re-enabled.
  2. When the subscription is Active, immediately cancel the subscription to put it in “InGracePeriod” status, then select Extend current state end date, edit the Current Status End Date field to the desired date as determined in Step 2. The maximum extension allowed is 90 days from the current date, if requested.
  3. If more than 180 days grace period is needed, submit request through Dashboard here and email the Microsoft 365 Deal Desk microsoft365dealdesk@service.microsoft.com to inform them that a request is pending for approval. (ETA SLA for Deal Desk to respond is 3 business days.)

For Bulk extensions see new article: 4574687: Procedure: VLS: Office VL Subscription Bulk Extension

Scenario 13. Tenant remap instructions for Enterprise Mobility Suite (EMS) customers

Note: Enterprise Mobility Suite (EMS) is a suite that includes Microsoft Azure Active Directory Premium, Microsoft Azure Rights Management, and Microsoft Intune. It is sold through Volume Licensing, MPSA, Cloud Service Provider and prepaid. This subscription will be supported the same as all other Volume Licensing and prepaid subscriptions we already have in place. See articles 2821797: Information about Office 365 Prepaid Plans and2821257: Helping customers with prepaid plan product key issues.

Tenant remap instructions:
  1. Perform a tenant remap for the EMS subscription as you would with any Volume Licensing service. The customer will have an "Access to Azure Active Directory" subscription under their Azure account. Ask the customer for the Microsoft account this Azure subscription is under.
  2. After the remap has been completed, transfer the case to Azure support (choose “Azure Active Directory” with the following request: "This customer's EMS subscription has been moved from tenant ID to tenant ID. Please link the "Access to Azure Active Directory" subscription to the new directory (tenant), using customer’s Microsoft Account."
Scenario 14. The customer wants to expedite the lifecycle process of their VL subscription from InGracePeriod to Disabled or Deprovisioned.


Tier 1 advocate should escalate to Tier 2 or Tier 3 and they will check the EA situation (for example end date) and then approve the request as valid. As long as the EA subscription is in HAR or END status, the advocate can move it to Disabled.

[Asset 4528197]
[Asset 4528199]


If the customer provides a good reason for the subscription being disabled (e.g. the customer doesn’t want to see the licenses or the customer wants to ensure the licenses cannot be used on the portal when their subscription is in the status of InGracePeriod), or they don’t accept the explanation about the subscription’s normal life cycle, set the subscription status from InGracePeriod to Disabled.

If customer request to de-provision, please assure the VL subscription status should be Disabled or InGracePeriod. Please do not de-provision active VL subscription. If de-provisioned by the wrong action, please try to contact VL hub. For details, see article 2836475: Self-serve subscription removal and tenant deletion request process.
 

Note:

If the subscriptions are with Active status and the customer still wants us to disable them on our side, please refer the customer to the Partner/Account team to have the licenses cancelled.

Tier2/Tier3 cannot disable them until the subscriptions are with InGracePeriod status. In order to disable the customer's subscriptions, we need a written approval (email) from an entitled Admin.

Scenario 16: EA Step-up licenses – Base subscription seats count doubled after step-up.

A systems limitation corrected on Nov 22th 2018, can result in customers who completed an EA Step-Up having twice the count of base subscription seats.

Prior to Nov 2018, EA Step-up licenses processed manually / or electronically (excluding orders placed through POET /RO – which the system decremented correctly) resulted in the base licenses count being doubled, as the base licenses were not decremented and the stepped -up licenses were added. This is visible in the Admin Portal and the VLSC, as well as in internal tools such as CMAT.

There is no solution to decrement the base licenses for such orders at this time and the issue will remain until the Po ends (e.g. anniversary, end for agreement). Notice there is no impact on billing.

From Nov 2018: Step-Up orders processed manually or electronically after Nov 22th2018 will correctly decrement base offerings. Customers will see the correct number of seats as the base license count is immediately decremented and the higher level step-up offering is provisioned, ensuring that the customer is not over-licensed for either remaining base license offering or step-up offering.

This will not correct license count for step up orders processed prior to Nov 2018

Customer ready message:

“Your step-up order resulted in a double-provisioning of services on the Admin Portal and double license count for the base subscription on VLSC because of a known system limitation. This double license count will remain for the duration of your subscription /order term. From a billing perspective, you won't be charged for these additional licenses.

This duplication won't occur on any new orders you place, but it's not possible for Microsoft, or our Partners, to correct the existing duplicated base counts. We apologize for this inconvenience. “

Scenario 17:  Allow overage / Smart Provisioning
  • Allow Overage is an internal reference to a design feature that allows VL customers to assign up to twice as many seats as they are licensed for, using PowerShell scripts in order to avoid loss of service. Allow Overage applies to Agreements created up until October 25, 2019, with an Order Placement date that is after May 2018.
  • Smart Provisioning is the capability for commercial EA / EAS / SCE Agreements which were created after October 25, 2019, to have overages created dynamically, based upon Step Up or seat reductions.​​

Guidance - DO NOT share any information about this feature with Customers or Partners. Scripting is provided for use in reactive support cases.

When the customer encounters any provisioning issues, advocates are allowed to inform the customer that they can use the overage licenses until the provisioning issue is solved.

Provisioning issue is caused by Microsoft:

“We apologize that the Subscription provisioning in Volume Licensing is not working properly.

Our Engineering team is aware of the issue and is working on a resolution which they will deploy as soon as possible.

For a limited period, it is still possible to assign seats via Power Shell Scripts or using Group Based Licensing up to the previously licensed quantity. This is done to avoid unplanned data loss for previously licensed subscribers. Just keep in mind, it will generate a license conflict in the Admin Portal which will be disappear once the provisioning issue is resolved.

We apologize for the inconvenience this issue may have caused.”

Provisioning issue is caused by Customer /Partner:

“We see there was a mistake (provide the reason, for example base and add on are not on the same agreement and therefore the subscription are not provisioning) .

For a limited period, it is still possible to assign seats via Power Shell Scripts or using Group Based Licensing up to the previously licensed quantity. This is done to avoid unplanned data loss for previously licensed subscribers. Just keep in mind, it will generate a license conflict in the Admin Portal which will be disappear once the provisioning issue is resolved.”

  • Advocates can see the overage licenses in BET: https://internal.support.services.microsoft.com/en-US/help/4101662
  • For more info go to https://internal.support.services.microsoft.com/en-us/help/4479336
