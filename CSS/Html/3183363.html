1. System requirements

Microsoft Advertising is a web-based program that can be used on either Mac or PCs that meet the following requirements:

  • They have an internet connection.
  • A supported operating system:
    • Windows 7 or newer.
    • Mac OS X 10.9 or newer.
  • A supported internet browser:
    • Microsoft Edge
    • Internet Explorer (IE11 or newer)
    • Google Chrome
    • Apple Safari 9.0 or newer
    • Mozilla Firefox (Version 38 and newer)
  • ActiveX – When first-time users sign into Microsoft Advertising, they may be prompted to download and install ActiveX control.

2. Account structure limitations

Advertising accounts are divided into a hierarchy, as follows:

  • Manager Account Number (10-digit alpha-numeric number that maps 1:1 to Manager Account ID (CID))
  • Account Level (Account Number or XID)
  • Campaign Level
  • Ad Group Level

Linking of hierarchies is now possible at the Manager Account level, allowing larger advertisers and agencies to structure their Microsoft Advertising set up exactly how their business is structured. The resources below provide additional information on this option:

  • Help content for hierarchies: https://help.ads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/60035/-1
  • Choosing between hierarchy and multi-account management: https://help.ads.microsoft.com/#apex/3/en/60036/-1
  • Hierarchies & shared libraries internal user guide: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/StandardSupport/l1resources/Agent%20Resources/Hierarchies%20%26%20Shared%20Library/Hierarchy-and-Shared-Library-user-guide-EN.pptx?d=w7a99cc7b1a8f4c2f9ba6328941d7b970
  • Hierarchies & shared libraries internal FAQ: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/:w:/t/StandardSupport/l1resources/EREJBUPdYkhIoGLIg8oj-7UBF1V_YQhF5QK31UcIqq7e_w
  • Hierarchies & shared libraries Feature Guide: https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/StandardSupport/l1resources/Agent%20Resources/Hierarchies%20%26%20Shared%20Library/Hierarchy-and-Shared-Library-user-guide-EN.pptx?d=w7a99cc7b1a8f4c2f9ba6328941d7b970

Individual ads and keywords are created within ad groups, and links to managing these can be found in section 6.

To prevent system abuse, the Microsoft Advertising UI has a limit on the number of attributes that can be added within each hierarchy level.

3.  Alerts and notifications

Banner alerts along the top of the UI will flag any critical issues or important updates that advertisers should be aware of.

Additionally, floating action-triggered alerts will show in the bottom-center of the page depending on user-driven activity. These alerts can be dismissed manually or allowed to automatically dismiss after 10 seconds. There are two types of action-alert: Success messages (green, with a green tick), and warning messages (amber, with an ‘!’ icon).

Selecting the advertiser’s sign-in name (top right of the UI) will allow them to edit their settings/preferences and access the Accounts & Billing pages.

Unified notifications

At the top-right of the screen advertisers will see a bell icon. When one or more alerts require attention, this bell will have a red dot, with a preview to show the most high-priority alert in the queue.

[Asset 4500483]

Selecting the bell icon/preview bar will load up all notifications across all accounts the user has access to, ordered by severity, in descending order form high to low severity. Note: When a user is within a specific account/campaign in the UI, the alerts listed in the Unified Notifications section will be filtered to only that account/campaign.

[Asset 4500484]

Alerts which apply to multiple accounts will be consolidated, as shown in the screenshot. Selecting View will open the Notifications grid on the Account Summary page. From there, users can act on these alerts, or apply/remove filters to view other alert types. 

Users can dismiss alerts from within the Unified Notifications section; however, if there are other users in the customer shell who also have access to those accounts/campaigns, they will continue to have visibility on the notifications in their queue. Note: Sales/support teams will not have visibility on all user alerts. Use UCM to see a complete list of alerts/notifications impacting the account.

To keep the alerts fresh, notifications will auto-dismiss once they have been resolved, or if they no longer apply.

4. Accounts Summary homepage dashboard

When customers sign into Microsoft Advertising, they will be taken to the Accounts Summary homepage dashboard.

The homepage dashboard provides:

  • An Accounts Summary dashboard for an at-a-glance view of advertising performance in each account and Key performance indicators (KPIs) summaries.
  • An Overview tab providing several visual representations of account performance via cards (for example, Accounts, Top Changes, and Devices).
  • Performance trend graph (See section 7.1 for more insight).
  • Modules for quick access to information tailored to advertiser needs:
    • Add/Move Columns
    • View Segments
    • Apply Filters
    • Management of any saved Performance Reports
  • Account-level Recommendations (sometimes referred to as MCC Recommendations), located on the accounts Overview page, allows advertisers to view, edit, and bulk-apply automated optimization recommendations to multiple managed accounts. Advertisers can also see an applied-recommendations history page as well. See the Recommendations portion of section 6.1, for more information on how these Automated Insights function.
  • A Notifications tab where advertisers can view, action, and dismiss many kinds of alerts impacting their campaigns. Note: Currently in pilot as part of the Unified Notification Queue and may not be available to all markets.
  • An Import tab where advertisers can see a summary of imports run across all accounts they have access to. Summary includes account names, import details, and errors, and a link to View Schedule.

With the Microsoft Advertising Accounts Summary homepage, you can:

  • Quickly assess account performance.
  • Easily identify issues to resolve.
  • Easily find opportunities to optimize accounts.
  • View, action, and dismiss account alerts.

For more information, see Get the big picture from the Accounts Summary page.

Performance Insights Pilot note: 

A small number of customers are included in the Performance Insights Pilot. This uses machine-learning to call out fluctuations in clicks, impressions, or spend performance, and offers root cause analysis and suggested actions to resolve the fluctuation.

Performance Insights can be surfaced in the Top Changes card (on the Overview tab) or via the Performance option, available in the drop-down menu on the Accounts Summary page.

Provides advertisers with overview tools and insights to monitor the success of their overall account performance. See section 4 for more information.

This tab contains all the campaign management tools an advertiser would need to set up, edit, and manage their online advertising accounts on Bing. See section 6 for more information.

From here, advertisers can schedule or run reports based on a wide variety of KPI reporting options to see the success of their advertising, look for possible optimization areas, and identify potential issues with their setup or performance. 6. Campaign management

Campaigns

Selecting the Campaigns tab will take the advertiser to the All Campaigns sub-tab page (unless they have chosen to make the Overview page their default).

Like the Accounts Overview page, the Campaigns page provides advertisers with a campaign summary for all campaigns within their chosen account. This includes:

  • A campaigns summary dashboard for an at-a-glance view of advertising performance.
  • Key performance indicator (KPI) summaries.
  • Performance trend graph (See section 7.1 for more insight).

Advertisers can create new campaigns, make in-line edits to their campaign name, budget, and status, and see alert icons if their campaigns are not eligible to deliver for any reason.

See How to get your campaigns up and running in Microsoft Advertising for more information on basic setup and structure.

Copy/paste campaigns/ad groups

Advertisers can easily copy/paste entire campaigns and ad groups from the Campaigns tab, allowing them to keep the same structure and settings as the original. Performance history/statistics will not be copied.

Simply highlight the campaigns/ad groups that should be copied and select Edit > Copy in the UI to copy and Edit >Paste to apply these to the account. Note: You can also use the keyboard hotkeys Ctrl +C to copy and Ctrl + V to paste.

You can share the Copy/Paste One Sheet with customers for their reference.

Overview

The Microsoft Advertising Overview page can be customized to view the information that matters most to an advertiser. Note: This tab only shows information for a single account. Users will need to choose which account to view using the drop-down menu at the top left of the page.

Users can:

  • Select desired date ranges to view performance.
  • Apply custom filters.
  • Filter by label.
  • Add visualization tiles.
  • Move/resize tiles to create a preferred look.

The Overview page currently offers the following tiles:

  • Tips – See Tool Tips and Best Practice suggestions (tips can be dismissed if unwanted).
  • Performance – See an all-up graph view of spend, conversions, clicks, impressions, and CTR.
  • Campaigns – See individual campaign overviews for conversions, clicks, and impressions.
  • Devices – See a graph breakdown of performance by device, showing clicks, impressions, and spend.
  • Opportunities – The Opportunities tile proposes relevant and actionable suggestions to improve the performance of a campaign. They are based on a variety of factors, including campaign settings and performance, and Microsoft Advertising marketplace dynamics.
  • Top Changes – The Top Changes tile identifies the campaigns and ad groups responsible for the biggest increases and decreases to performance metrics during a specified time period.
  • Keywords – See performance for the top six keywords for a given metric.
  • Searches – See performance data for search queries used by users on SERP when triggering an advertiser’s ads. With the option to add these terms as keywords/negative keywords.
  • Competition – See comparison KPIs with competitors, and areas of the campaign where performance is behind the competition.
  • Top Locations – See a visual representation of the top 10 locations an advertiser is performing in, within their targets. (Campaign level only.)

If customers would prefer to skip the Overview tab and go directly into their Campaigns tab, they can select the hyperlink option to Make the Campaigns tab my default landing view. Once this is done, the Campaigns tab will display a new message Make the Overview my default landing view so that customers can switch back to the new experience.

Mixed Mode Ad Groups (Pilot)

Mixed Mode is currently in pilot to the following markets:

  • AT, AU, BE, CA, CH, DE, ES, FR, IE, IT, NL, NZ, SE, UK, and US.

With Mixed Mode, advertisers can create either Standard or Dynamic Ad Groups:

  • Standard Ad Groups contain EXTAs, RSAs and Keywords.
  • Dynamic Ad Groups contain DSA and Auto Targets.

In Mixed Mode enabled accounts, we will remove DSA Campaign Type and DSA will be incorporated into specific Goals during the new campaign creation workflow.

When selecting a new campaign goal, customers can select Enable Dynamic Search Ads. When enabled, new fields for Website, Website Language, and Targeting Source will become available.

At the Ad Group creation stage of the workflow, the user can choose either Dynamic Ad Groups or Standard Ad Groups. (Only one Ad Group of either type is needed to continue, but the user can create multiple of each.)

Users can also import mixed campaigns from Google Ads using the following logic:

  • Net-New Mixed Campaigns: Will be fully imported as new Mixed Mode campaigns containing both Dynamic and Standard ad groups.
  • Mixed Campaigns, previously imported as Search-Only Campaigns: DSA ads will be imported into new Dynamic ad group within the existing campaign.
  • Mixed Campaigns, previously imported as DSA-Only Campaigns: Only DSA ads will be imported, as DSA-Only campaigns are unable to support EXTAs. (Customers will need to remove the existing DSA campaigns from their account to import Mixed campaigns from Google Ads.)

Note: There is no requirement that customers must migrate their DSA into mixed-mode campaigns. Later in 2021 we plan to migrate all existing DSA campaigns.

Change history:

This page will help advertisers see what changes have been made, and by whom, within a given time period.

The Dimensions tab provides customized, automated reporting based on certain aspects (dimensions) within an account. Advertisers can navigate here to see at-a-glance reporting insights. See Review your campaign data by a specific pivot or dimension for more information.

Competitions:

The Competitions sub-tab has two sections:

  • Auction Insights: Provides visuals and metrics to show competitive KPIs compared with other Microsoft Advertising advertisers. You can choose to view data over a period of time or in summary and apply segments/filters.
  • Recommendations: Provides insights into areas of an advertiser’s campaigns where they are not performing as well as the competition and gives actionable recommendations to improve performance and stay ahead of the competition.

For more information on viewing/applying recommendations, see About the Competition tab.

Experiments

From this tab, advertisers can run controlled experiments to test changes they want to make in campaigns (such as bid, budget, and targeting) and study the performance impact, prior to applying these settings across their live campaigns.

Advertisers can assign a percentage of their volume to the experiments, whilst continuing to run their primary campaign as well, to avoid any major loss of performance whilst running the experiment.

They can choose to split their experiments traffic either by search-based or cookie-based targeting.

  • Search-based = The same cookies (users) may see ad impressions from both the control and experiment versions of the customer’s campaign.
  • Cookie-based = The same cookies (users) will only see one type of the advertiser’s campaign: Either control or experiment, but not both.

Once they’re satisfied the experiment is complete, they can choose to apply the new settings to their campaign or end the experiment.

For more information, see Discover the possibilities with experiments.

Best practices:

  • Check Experiment Status to ensure there are no errors after creating an experiment.
  • To get volume quickly and test effectively, run a 50:50 split of volume.
  • Run experiments in A/A mode for the first two weeks (allow the experiment to perform using the same settings as the original campaigns). Then apply the desired change (A/B mode) after that stage. For the A/B portion of the experiment, we recommend running it for at least two weeks (so at least four weeks in total).
  • We recommend testing end-user facing features such ad copy, landing page, or ad extensions using cookie-based split. This ensures that users (cookies) are exposed to only the original campaign or the experiment campaign (not both), thus allowing a more accurate comparison between the two. Audience related features do not require cookie-based traffic split. ​

7.1 Performance trend graphs

Performance trend graphs appear on the Accounts Summary, Campaigns, Ad Groups, and Keywords tabs.
On any performance trend graph, you can choose to view multiple KPI metrics such as clicks, impressions, spend, or average CPC. These can be broken down by time to see how performance is trending.

Peaks and valleys in the graph show where you have had good performance and where you haven’t. Using the graph, advertisers can compare recent performance:

  • To your performance in a previous period.
  • To a moving average, a performance baseline for measuring your campaign's success.
  • Between weekdays and weekends.
  • In the context of changes that were made to your campaign, using the change history graph.

In addition, any filters applied to the grid will also be applied to the graph, so advertisers can view their filtered data visually as well.

See Use graphs to see data for every level of your campaign for more information.

7.3 Audience segmentation

Audience segmentation has been launched to allow our advertisers to view comparative data, for each audience as well as non-audience, to help support features like In-Market Audience and Similar Audience (features coming soon).

Audience segmentation allows advertisers to slice their data in two ways:

  • Audience Name (each audience has a row visible in the UI).
  • Audience Category (remarketing, in-market and custom).

When this segmentation is applied, the UI will also create a new row labelled Users not in audience segment to show performance for customers who don’t fall into an audience segment. Its system generated and only shown if the ad group has audience association.

Advertisers can download audience segmentation reports in the usual way.

For more information on audience targeting, see KB 3184201 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Remarketing & Audience Targeting.

7.4 Custom Columns (accounts summary only during pilot)

In addition to standard metric columns, advertisers can use Custom Columns to monitor custom metrics that they wish to focus on. These columns are based on equations and segmentation of existing metrics in our system.

Notes:

  • Custom Columns are stored on the user level. Users will not be able to see one another’s Custom Columns.
  • They can be used in conjunction with existing reporting features like segmentation, period comparison, filtration, sort, and download.
  • Available on Account Summary, Campaigns, Ad Groups, Ads, Ad Extensions, Automated Extensions, and Keywords pages.

To create/edit Custom Columns:

  1. In either the Accounts Summary or Campaigns grid, select Modify Columns.
  2. Select Custom Columns.
  3. Select +Column (or select the Edit icon).
  4. Enter a Column Name, Description, Formula, and Column Format (user creates the formula by selecting (or typing) existing metrics and applying the relevant mathematical functions to calculate their desired custom metric).
  5. Select Save.

The new column will now be visible in the KPI grid.

7.5 Optimization score (closed pilot)

Optimization score is an estimate of how well an account is set to perform and runs from 0% to 100% (with 100% meaning that an account is performing at its full potential).

Recommendations and their associated impact represent the headroom to achieve the 100% optimization score target. Each Recommendation suggestion indicates the percentage increase it can offer to the optimization score.

[Asset 4591127]

The optimization score will update as/when Recommendations are actioned/dismissed and will be surfaced in multiple reporting channels within the UI.

Optimization score is regularly updated based on continual campaign performance analysis.

8. Guided Tours

The Guided Tours feature, available in all markets, provides in-tool self-help tours for advertisers with step-by-step instructions on how to manage their account, complete key tasks, and improve campaign performance.

The tours are built into all tabs within the Campaigns page currently. New tours may be added to all pages of the Microsoft Advertising UI in the future.

How it works:

  1. After selecting the appropriate guided tour link, a welcome message with a brief overview of the tour displays.
  2. Customers progress through the tour using the Next button. The tour feature may progress customers to the next step if it detects that they have completed the prescribed action.
    • Should a prompt suggest the customer change something within their campaign that they would rather not update, the customer can move forward by selecting the Next button even if they haven’t acted.
  3. Customers must save when prompted to ensure that the changes they made are applied.
  4. As customers progress through the guided tours, the feature will point out if they miss a crucial field, or if the information they entered will cause an error.
  5. Once completed, customers are shown a “Congrats” or “Nice Job” message noting the end of the tour. If appropriate, this message will display links to additional help information. The tour may also suggest they continue with the guided tour of a related or advanced feature.

9. Troubleshooting

User cannot access the UI:

Many connection issues can be easily solved by clearing the browser cache and cookies. When the advertiser is finished, they should close all browser windows and restart the browser before trying to access the Microsoft Advertising site again.

To clear the browser cache and delete cookies in Windows Internet Explorer, follow these steps:

  1. To clear the browser cache:
    1. Start Internet Explorer.
    2. Select Tools menu, and then select Internet Options.
    3. On the General tab under Browsing history, select Delete.
    4. In the Delete Browsing History window, select Delete Files.
    5. Select Close, and then OK.
  2. Restart Internet Explorer. To restart Internet Explorer, close all Internet Explorer windows and then open a new Internet Explorer window.
  3. Try to access Microsoft Advertising again.

Note: Try adding the URL to your list of trusted sites.

System incompatibility issue:

When assisting the customer, verify that they meet the system requirements listed in Microsoft Advertising system requirements.

If the issue is not resolved using the troubleshooting steps covered in this article, document the following information, and escalate the issue.

  • Browser used and the version.
  • Customer operating system details.
  • A description of the error or unexpected behavior and the repro steps along with screenshots.

Third-party software error:

Some advertisers are reporting the following error:

"Please enable your Internet browser to accept cookies and make sure that JavaScript is enabled. If you aren’t sure how to do this, refer to your browser’s Help."

This error is caused by third-party software and not due to the browser itself being unsupported. The password management tool, https://agilebits.com/onepassword, is a common cause. It is available for Windows, MAC, and mobile OS.

Check with the advertiser to see if they have any third-party software installed with the browser. Removing this software may resolve the issue.

Firefox compatibility:

10. Experiments FAQ

Q: How many experiments can I have running at one time?

A: Ten experiments per campaign, with one experiment running per campaign at a time. We recommend scheduling experiments so that you stagger them appropriately.

Q: How long do experiments take to be active?

A: It takes about 10 seconds for creation, during which you’ll see the status as “Creating experiment…”  When adopting experiments, allow about 15 minutes for your recorded changes to go into effect.

Q: Can I use an experiment with a campaign that has a shared budget?

A: No.

Q: Can I change the name, status, or budget of my experiment?

A: No.

Q: What is the difference between a search-based traffic and cookie (user-based) traffic split?

A: Under a search-based traffic split, the same users (cookies) may see ad impressions from both the control and treatment campaigns, but they can’t under a user-based split. Search-based splits are recommended for most types of changes other than ad copy/landing page testing.

User-based traffic split will be available at the time of the Campaign Experiments general availability. Once user-based split is available, you’ll be able to better leverage Campaign Experiments for testing audiences.

Q: How does Bing randomize and split the traffic? When does the split happen?

A: Bing splits traffic based on Search (auction-based) and is pre-auction. Cookie (actual user) will be available at the time of GA. Once user-based split is available, you’ll be better able to leverage Campaign Experiments for testing audiences.

Q: Do responsive search ads (RSA) work with Campaign Experiments?

A: Not yet. We don’t recommend running any experiments with RSA since it would cause data to be skewed.

Experimenting with Automated Bidding:

Q: What are the requirements for testing Target CPA or Maximize Conversions?

A: First, your campaign should have conversion tracking in place. Second, Target CPA and Maximize Conversions both currently have a minimum conversion requirement of 15 conversions in the last 30 days. These agents cannot be enabled on the campaign until the requirement is met.

Therefore, to test these bidding strategies, we recommend starting your experiment campaign without any changes (for example, an A/A mode) and allow your conversion history to build for the treatment campaign (campaign B). Run the A/B test for four weeks instead of the usual two.

Q: Can I test Enhanced CPC without conversion data?

A: Yes. While the results are better with conversion tracking enabled, it’s not required.

Q: What bid strategies can I test when the advertising is using a bid management tool?

A: Layering Enhanced CPC will not break third-party bid management tools, as it always uses the bids set by the bid management tool before applying any adjustment. Bing’s automated bidding strategies such as Maximize Clicks, Target CPA, and Maximize Conversions can be tested with Campaign Experiments; however, you’ll have to opt out of your automated tool’s strategy and opt into Microsoft Advertising’s automated bidding strategy (excluding eCPC).

Q: Can I run experiments on top of the Target CPA or Maximize Conversion campaigns?

A: Since campaigns have an eligibility requirement of 15 conversions in the last 30 days, a treatment campaign with Target CPA or Maximize Conversion settings can’t be created due to a lack of conversion data.
