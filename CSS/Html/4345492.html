1. Overview

Achieve More is a collection of activities intended to drive customer product engagement and account performance.

To achieve more means the customer was deliberately informed of resources or opportunities that are relevant to the customer’s goals and anticipate the customer’s needs.

Achieve more resources should be custom, derived from, or otherwise be relevant to the context of the conversation. To achieve more means to exceed the customer's expectations.

You can deliberately inform customers of resources, alerts, performance opportunities, or Services consultations at any time during the interaction.

1.1 How to achieve more

There are five primary ways to achieve more on any eligible interaction, some of which can be easily derived from UCM-A Alerts and Health Check tabs. At least one of the following must be done to achieve more:

  1. Provide resources that might help the advertiser in the next phase of their advertising journey.
  2. Facilitate product feedback via User Voice.
  3. Raise awareness of alerts that might affect ad delivery or account performance in the Health Check tab. In this tab you have an overview of the account issues, and can run a report on the issues to share with the customer.
  4. Refer to Services for a consultation if a customer is new or just needs help improving account performance. When in doubt, send the customer to Optimization Consultation. Most advertisers are eligible for a consultation unless they are managed, on credit hold, inactive account/website, flagged for Quality Based Ranking (QBR), or fraud.
  5. Recommend campaign improvements. Familiarize yourself with any opportunities and be ready to mention them.

Note: Onboarding and campaign management issues are not disqualifiers for services referrals and recommending campaign improvements (for example: budget pause, paused campaigns, or no PI). Resolve the blocker before attempting to refer to services or improve campaigns.

1.2 Best practices and messaging

As a "By the way..." or "Did you know..." conversation. These types of pitches are useful during non-talk times (for example, while you're waiting for a report to run or for some other action to occur before you can progress with issue diagnosis/resolution). "By the way" conversations are great for addressing value-add opportunities that may not be directly required to resolve the issue.

Example: “You mentioned you had trouble using/doing <feature>. Can you tell me about that experience while we wait? I’d like to get feedback to our engineering teams if you're willing to help me better understand what didn't work for you.”

As a "Would you like me to..." conversation. These types of pitches are useful when the customer is willing and able to spend a bit more time working with you.

Example: “Sounds like you might benefit from some customized account recommendations. I can either take a quick look and see where I might be able to help, or we have a team specialized in helping with <account setup/performance>. I can absolutely help you resolve the issue with <insert break/fix issue> and (help you with that/can get you over to that team) as soon as we're done here.”

2. Eligibility requirements

Check the Opportunities tab. If the opportunity Refer to Optimization Consultation is available and has a status of Open, the customer should only be referred to the Optimization Consultation service. If they decline the referral, then proceed with other achieve more options.

Achieve more activity

Eligibility criteria

Product engagement resources and alerts

All customers are eligible

Refer to services or improve campaigns

Support issue is/will be resolved, will not conflict with optimization, and basic delivery blockers are removed (for example, budget pause, paused campaigns, or no PI).

Customer’s issue is not a misroute, unrecoverable cancellation request, permanent closure, dropped contact, insufficient information, duplicate, unresolved, Presales inquiry, QBR upheld.

Microsoft Advertising account is active/pending, fortified, unmanaged, not on credit hold, non-adult, using a live website and compliant business model per Microsoft Advertising editorial guidelines.

Refer advertisers to Onboarding or Optimization consultation if…

Customer can be supported within services hours and languages and: 1) might benefit from onboarding/optimization assistance, or 2) has a Refer to Optimization Consultation opportunity.

When in doubt, send the customer to Optimization Consultation.

5. UCM alerts

Alerts can be viewed from UCM and Microsoft Advertising.

The Total Alerts tile provides a quick overview of the number of open alerts grouped by severity.

  • Severe: Accounts have gone dark.
  • Warning: Action required.
  • Attention: Review needed.

If you select the tile, the Alerts tab opens and filters the grid.

You can open the Alerts tab directly to see all the alerts. The Description and Next Steps will provide guidance on what must be done to resolve the alert.

Alerts come from a variety of resources so the refresh rate for each is different. Because of this, you might see an alert on the Alerts tab that has already been resolved.

Alerts are grouped by categories.

  • IO alerts - Most IO alerts automatically dismiss when the issue is resolved.
  • Payment alerts
  • Fraud alerts
  • Editorial alerts

Access UCM Help (next to the gear icon in UCM-A) and search for alerts for detailed descriptions of each alert type.

Other alerts may be visible directly in Microsoft Advertising – be sure to check campaigns and billing tabs since alerts may not persist across all tabs.

6.2 Warm transfer

Using your OneCloud favourites establish a consult call with Services using your favourites list to contact the correct queue, inform the Services advocate of what the customer needs and if they’ve been fortified, then conference the customer in, make introductions, and close your call with the customer. When in doubt, send the advertiser to Optimization Consultation.

6.3 Schedule an appointment

  1. Open the Appointment Scheduling Tool for the customer location, then go to the Find free coaching in your region page.
  2. Add the customer’s preferred email address and select Book New Appointment.
  3. Fill in the required fields with the customer’s details.
  4. Select the topic that the customer is most interested in addressing.
  5. Select Referred from Microsoft Advertising Support in drop down about the service invitation.
  6. Confirm and select the appropriate time zone for the customer.
  7. Select View Available Appointments.
  8. View the calendar for appointments (highlighted in green).
  9. Discuss with customer which option is available and best for them.
  10. Click to Book. A screen confirming the appointment will resolve and the customer will receive an email confirming the appointment, including theservice request (SR) number. The confirmation email will include a link to modify or cancel the appointment.
  11. Confirm the date and time with the customer and remind them to be available at the number they provided.

7. How to improve campaigns

First, check the Recommendations tab (see KB 3183363 Tool User Guide: Microsoft Advertising UI for more information on this feature) and help the advertiser implement some/all of the suggestions here during your interaction. 

If there are no automated suggestions, try quick wins based on their reason for calling support. For example:

If you…

The advertiser may benefit from…

Assist the customer with adding new keywords and identified the customer is on budget pause (or has a history of going on budget pause)

Increase or reallocate budget.

Provide education on how to add funds

Increase bids so ads appear in the mainline or coach them through Site Links implementation.

Provide education on how to apply broad match modifier

Update the ad copy, update the destination web address, add negative keywords.

Coach customer through the account set up process

Import google campaigns, improve their targeting, additional keywords.

Provide a recommendation to increase impressions

Improved ad copy, add more keywords and refine their targeting.

Note: To achieve more for campaign performance inquiries, you must suggest more than one improvement recommendation to achieve more.

It is helpful to understand the customer’s goals. Once you understand their goals and challenges, there are two primary methods of using UCM to identify and raise awareness of performance opportunities:

  1. Build a UCM Proposal to create an optimization plan based on the customers goals.
  2. Pitch custom recommendations based on your own understanding of the advertiser needs and goals.
    1. Review the UCM Opportunity Tactic Library for more ideas on how to help the customer meet their advertising goals.
    2. Try setting the UCM timeframe to 14 days and check performance squares from UCM Opportunities tab to review spend, impressions, clicks and share of voice.
    3. Learn more about opportunities.

7.1 Understanding goals and challenges

The customer should feel the recommendations are personalized and can meet their needs. Here are four example questions to help you understand the customer’s needs:

  1. How do you make money using Microsoft Advertising? Refine account settings.
  2. How do you set yourself apart from the competition? Refine ad copy.
  3. Who are your customers and where are they located? Refine targeting.
  4. What has been your biggest challenge advertising on Microsoft Advertising? Identify other improvement categories & tactics to improve their Microsoft Advertising experience.

7.2 Recommend opportunities

For optimizations presented under the UCM Opportunities tab, use the messaging by selecting on the '...' to the right of the opportunity name. Also look for optimization opportunities outside of UCM such as location targeting and ad copy improvements.

When making your recommendation:

  • Confirm your understanding of their goals and challenges
  • State the benefit of the recommendation as it relates to the goals and challenges you heard from the customer.
  • Tailor your recommendations to the customers goals and challenges.
  • Attempt to use the same language the customer uses. For example, if you hear the customer say 'invest', use that word in your recommendation. 'Investing in higher bids should help increase clicks."

Campaign improvement categories & tactics

Volume: More impressions, traffic, or reach

Ad distribution

Used to expand distribution of ads. This includes opting into search syndication (note that some markets are automatically opted in without the ability to opt out), native ads (US only) content ads (US only).

Bid recommendations

Tactic used to help advertisers fine tune their bid strategy to increase the likelihood their ads will appear on the first page or mainline. UCM opportunities present personalized bid recommendations to help the advertiser achieve first page or mainline positioning.

Bing Places and cross-sell

Free Bing service that enables local and small businesses to show their business listing in local search results. The listing will show the business name, site URL, address and phone number.

Budget recommendations

Used to help advertisers control how their advertising dollars are spent, to ensure there are enough funds to run ads throughout the day or month.

Editorial assistance

Service provided to the advertisers to ensure their ad copy, keywords and landing page aligns with editorial guidelines to keep search results accurate, clear and relevant. UCM alerts will present any disapprovals, pending manual review (PMA) or QBR penalties that can be reviewed, corrected, or improved.

Keyword recommendations

Recommend additional or different words or phrases for bidding with the goal to match these words to search queries. UCM opportunities presents keyword suggestions that will help the advertiser.

Payment method applied to pending account

Used when helping an advertiser apply a payment method to an account to remove pending status.

 

Response: More clicks, higher CTR

Ad Copy

Coach advertiser through changes to their ad copy such as: ad descriptions, describe offers or promotions, add call to action, differentiate from competition. This also may include updating destination URLs or implementing dynamic text/keyword insertion.

Ad Extensions

Feature used to enhance ad copy and direct searchers to specific information with the intent to attract relevant customers. This includes Site links, Image Extensions, Location Extensions, App Extensions, and more.

Bing Shopping campaign

Feature allowing advertisers to showcase their latest products in an engaging and impactful format via image, price, and store name.

Location and Advanced Targeting

Technique allowing advertisers to choose which audience to target ads based off the location they are performing their search. Let it be by country, state, region, city, or radius to a location.

Match Type

Technique used to help Microsoft Advertising determine how closely a search query must match an advertiser’s keyword. This includes Broad, Phrase, Exact, and Broad Match Modifier.

Negative Keywords

Technique used to define specific words or phrases that help prevent ads from showing to searchers who are unlikely to select the ad or, in some cases, the word or phrase is not relevant to the advertiser's product.

 

ROI: Return on investment, value

Universal Event Tracking

UET helps advertisers track their searchers’ actions post-click. Enabling this feature will allow the customer to track conversions, informing which keywords and ads are leading to products sold, forms completed, downloads, etc.

 

Account Health: Ad management

Account Shell promotion

Tactic used to provide greater flexibility to advertisers and agencies with larger accounts.

Agency linking request

Coach agencies through features in Microsoft Advertising that allow them to them to effectively manage their customers’ accounts.

Microsoft Advertising insights

Industry insights, marketing trends and customer stories available on advertise.bingads.microsoft.com providing tips for advertisers using Microsoft Advertising.

Campaign tools education

Recommend supplementary tools to help advertisers effectively manage their campaigns, such as Editor, Keyword Planner, and Intelligence.

Create New Microsoft Advertising account

Used when a customer contacting support does not have a Microsoft Advertising account and has questions about advertising with Microsoft Advertising, resulting in you successfully coaching new advertiser through the account creation process using the sign-up wizard.

Import campaigns

Functionality of copying existing campaigns from another advertising platform (most commonly importing Google campaigns) or other third-party platforms.

Monthly invoice setup

Offered reactively (by request of advertiser) from Services teams only. Not an optimization add-on tactic for support.

Service program upsell

Free consultative services such as onboarding consultations or Optimization Consultations available for eligible advertisers.

UCM alert notification

Service provided to resolve potential issues that may lead to preventing ad delivery. These are all visible under UCM alerts.

 

Handling objections

Customer objection

Advocate response

I get more impressions and clicks with Google

While Google’s search engine has been around longer, Microsoft is continually improving the Microsoft Advertising platform and tools to make things easier for our customers. With Microsoft Advertising, you’ll reach millions of unique searchers who are not on Google. And the best news is that our audience is also significant in online purchase activity! ComScore confirms that searchers on the Microsoft Advertising Network spend more than Google searchers.

Click-through rate

Click-through rates (CTR) can be affected by various factors, including ad position, targeting settings and how well your ad resonates with your target audience. To improve your CTR, we offer a variety of campaign management tools, seasonal and industry insights, tailored recommendations on the Opportunities tab and personal assistance with Microsoft Advertising experts whenever you need help.

Conversions

Several factors can impact conversion rates. Reducing irrelevant clicks, reaching your target audience, making it easy for customers to act on your webpage and ensuring your ads clearly represent your business will help increase your conversions. To understand what’s limiting your conversion rate, we offer a variety of tools and personal assistance with Microsoft Advertising experts whenever you need help.

I don’t have time. It’s too much work.

Although it takes time and energy to test online advertising strategies, it is worth the investment to determine what gives you the best ROI. This should take no more than 10-15 minutes, and I'm happy to schedule time with you if there is a better time. For help maintaining a healthy and competitive ad campaign, I can provide you recommendations that can help you achieve your advertising goals.

You don’t know my business.

You are the expert when it comes to your business, and I know a lot about Microsoft Advertising. If you help me understand your goals for online advertising, I can help you customize your Microsoft Advertising campaigns to get the best results.

I am in a hurry, and I don't have time right now.

I completely understand. I would be happy to schedule sometime with you when you are available, as I mentioned it would only take 10-15 minutes. Is there a better time?

I don’t want to spend more money.

When an advertising strategy is effective, you can invest more knowing you will also make more profit. That is exactly why you should optimize your campaigns to reach your target audience and invite them to click your ad and take your desired call to action. You can make several changes to your campaigns that will redistribute your budget and costs rather than increasing them.

I just wanted the free trial.

That’s why we offer promotions like this, so you can try it out with no risk. How did it go? What were some of the positive results shown from your free trial? What were the challenges you experienced?

If Microsoft Advertising did not meet your expectations during the free trial, I can help you tweak your campaigns so that they better align to your objectives. What are your advertising goals?

My performance is already good.

Yes, I can see you have achieved great performance with your campaigns. There might be some Microsoft Advertising features you are not taking advantage of, or some ways we can save you time in managing your advertising campaigns. I would love this opportunity to make sure you are not missing out on anything if you have 5-10 minutes.

8. ASD coding guidance

Unless there is a blocker/dependency (for example, QBR review in progress), every customer contact is an opportunity to achieve more.

Note: If you create two or more tickets for the same customer interaction, Achieve More should be coded the same in all tickets.

For ASD field…

Select Yes when…

Further product engagement resources provided?

You provided contextually relevant resources, materials, and links in addition to those that were necessary to resolve their inquiry that will help the advertiser increase Microsoft Advertising usage, gain trust in the product.

User Voice recommended?

You located an existing External User Voice suggestion that is relevant to their experience/issue, which they can vote on.

Or

You submitted/voted on an Internal User Voice feature suggestion on behalf of the customer and informed them you did so.

Note: If a feature suggestion exists in the external forum but not in the internal forum, it is valuable to also submit it on the internal forum with supporting ASD/UCMT tickets and a cross-link to the external record. Engineering uses both internal and external forums to prioritize feature requests and establish their roadmap. 

UCM alerts addressed?

You informed the customer of possible blockers that might affect ad delivery.

Referred to services?

You informed the customer of what the Services/Platinum team is/does and offered to put them in touch.

Referral type

Select the action you took to refer the customer to services: warm transfer (preferred), scheduled an appointment (get the commitment while you can), or sent them the appt scheduler link.

Note: Even if the customer declines real-time, you can still provide the link via email in case they change their mind.

Improvements recommended?

You informed the customer of ad delivery opportunities that might improve their account performance (minimum of two if the initial inquiry is performance related). Select Yes if you recommended options even if the customer is not interested.

The field Why were you unable to achieve more will automatically appear so long as all Achieve More fields contains No.

10. FAQ

Q: I tried to recommend campaign improvements, but the customer was not interested. How do I code that in ASD?

A: Achieve More is about proactively informing customers on how to more effectively use Microsoft Advertising, remove blockers, and improve performance. As such, you achieved more even if the customer declines and you should select Yes in Improvement Recommended.

Q: What if I create multiple parent tickets for a single call?

A: Code all tickets originating from a single interaction the same.

Q: Why do I still have to select a value for “did you achieve more beyond issue resolution”?

A. You won’t have to for much longer. That field will be removed in mid-February.

Q: How can I Achieve More if a customer is not authenticated?

A: Only two of the four primary tactics (resources, alerts, refer, improve) are account specific. You can still provide resources or inform a customer of what Services can offer, even if they don’t have an account or weren’t authenticated.

Q: Why are proactive tickets considered eligible for achieve more?

A: When you conduct a proactive outreach, you’re deliberately informing the advertiser of something that is/will affect their account that they probably weren’t aware of before contact, therefore, you have achieved more by default.

Q: Do we still have qualifications for achieving more or is the expectation that we achieve more on everything?

A: The same qualifications hold true – see Microsoft Advertising Achieve More, Eligibility requirements to see which issue types are considered ‘exempt’ from Achieve More KPI calculations. You can still achieve more with these issue types, but it is not expected.

Q: Isn’t it that Achieve More tactic should be different from the resolution/concern raised by the advertiser or we can provide related articles about the concern/resolution then we tag the ticket as achieve more?

A: Related. Absolutely related/relevant/otherwise useful and not directly part of or required for their issue resolution. It is very subjective and can sometimes be a grey line between resolution and adding value. Adding value means you shared something that instilled trust in our Support and deeper commitment to the product. Consider whether the customer perceives our assistance to have done more than what they asked for?

Q: How should we code the ticket if the account is managed?

A: Even if you are deferring support for a red scenario, you can still raise alerts to the customer’s attention but there’s generally no expectation you achieve more on an interaction you’re deferring to an account manager (AM) as these are considered misroutes. The Microsoft Advertising Achieve More article has also been updated with the Appointment Scheduler link for SMB Platinum customers so you can still achieve more via Referral to Services (just send them to their Platinum Optimization team instead).

Q: Why aren’t adult advertisers considered eligible for improvement recommendations?

A: Adult advertisers aren’t eligible for optimization/improvement recommendations due to the nature of their campaigns, but they may still have alerts, have feedback on the product, or could find value in additional customer-facing content; therefore, they are eligible for Achieve More.

Q: What about Platinum advertisers?

A: Platinum advertisers are still eligible for Achieve More. Per Managed Advertisers, Platinum advertisers are eligible for full support. This includes all Achieve More value adds. However, it may be more practical to leverage the Platinum AM for optimization assistance, especially if the customer is specifically requesting optimization or has a complex ask beyond what Support is able to provide. If the customer ask will fall dramatically outside Support optimization capabilities, review Customer segmentation for details on how to leverage Platinum Appointment Scheduler or Help Needed requests to global XR-AMs in order to Achieve More.
