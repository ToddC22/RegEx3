1. VAT overview

A VAT ID is a registration number, which businesses and citizens obtain from their local tax authority in EMEA.

VAT IDs are not the same as National Identification numbers, Social Security numbers, or Government issued IDs, and thus are not considered highly confidential.

A VAT number of a limited company identifies a legal entity, separate from an individual. Personal data is considered private if it is associated with an individual. A person’s SSN or TIN may be considered personal data. In case a document will need to be created to reflect the customer Tax Identification Number (TIN), that document does not need to be shared with the customer via the DTM tool.

VAT ID can be referred to in various ways in different languages: TVA (France), IVA (Spain and Italy), and BTW (Netherlands).

Questions related to VAT invoicing that EMEA customers may contact Microsoft with:

  • Customer needs to find an existing VAT invoice.
  • Customer needs VAT refund or updated invoice.

Note: Microsoft CSS Store support/CSS Accounts and Billing does not offer VAT refund, or a credit note and rebill. Customers can claim their VAT exemptions by going to their local tax authority and presenting their invoice showing their VAT charge. This can be obtained from Order History on AMC.

A simplified invoice is sufficient if we are invoicing a B2C and that invoice must include:

  • Date of issue
  • Sellers (Microsoft) VAT identification number
  • Type of goods or services supplied
  • VAT amount payable

Note: Inform a business customer prior to purchase that they will receive a simplified invoice.

2. VAT rate and registration numbers by country

The applicable VAT rate for paid business-to-consumer (B2C) online services (such as Xbox live subscriptions, Microsoft 365 Family Edition, and downloadable Apps) and FPP products is calculated by the prevailing rate applied in the purchaser’s country of residence.

Example: For a Digital product sale to a consumer living in the UK, the UK VAT rate will apply. The applicable Belgian VAT rate will apply if the consumer lives in Belgium.

  • MIOL Ireland VAT number is used for sales to Irish and non-EU customers (apart from Norway, Switzerland, and South Africa). This number is VAT ID#: IE8256796U.
  • MIOL local EU VAT number is used for the EU, Norway, Switzerland, and South Africa.

The following countries listed in section 2.1 and 2.2 will have auto generated VAT invoices via account.microsoft.com.

It may take up to 48 hours for the VAT invoice to be available on account.microsoft.com. If the customer doesn’t see it after five days, engage the Accounts & Billing Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (A&B ACA). See KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing Scenarios.

4. Modifying VAT invoices

4.1 What can be modified in a VAT invoice

No manual modifications or updates are allowed on automatically generated VAT invoices.

  • There is no obligation to provide the customer with a signed or stamped hard copy tax invoice (HCTI) of the VAT invoice.
  • When a customer asks for a physical copy through regular mail, inform the customer that they can print the invoice PDF received via e-mail. We are unable to mail physical invoices.
  • The VAT refund for any diplomat should not be refunded by MIOL. Individuals claiming this should be directed to file for a refund from their own tax authority by completing the appropriate documents available on their local country tax office website.

Return to top

4.2 Exception for consumer customer in Portugal

We can modify a VAT invoice, if a consumer customer in Portugal requests to add their National Identification number to the automatically generated invoice.

The Numero de Indentificacao Fiscal (NIF) is a unique number, formed from nine digits and assigned to individuals for tax purposes. Consumers need this number on the invoice for their tax declaration.

You can update the automatically generated invoice, as long as only the NIF number is added to the invoice and issued automatically to the Portuguese consumer customer. This is for consumer only and will not impact tax purposes.

Process:

  1. The customer sends CSS an automated invoice from a purchase under account.microsoft.com.
  2. Tier 1 should escalate the case to Tier 2 for NIF to be added to the invoice.
  3. When Tier 1 gets the updated invoice, send it to the customer.

Note: Do not upload updated invoice or save it to any location outside of ASD/MST.

5.1 Skype credit purchase and auto-recharge

See VAT changes for Skype customers in EU.

  • VAT is not charged when customers buy Skype Credit or when Skype Credit is automatically topped up.
  • Customers are charged exactly the amount of credit they buy, for example, if a customer buys €10 worth of Skype Credit they are charged exactly €10.

Pay As You Go, Skype To Go, and SMS:

  • All rates are VAT-inclusive for EU customers.
  • VAT is charged at the time customers make PAYG or Skype To Go calls or send SMS at the prevalent rate of the customer's country of billing address.

Subscriptions and Skype Numbers:

  • VAT is charged when customers buy or renew these products and at the time a recurring payment is taken.
  • The prevalent VAT rate of the customer’s country of billing address is applied at payment.

Return to top

5.2 Skype Manager

See VAT changes for Skype Manager customers in EU.

Purchase or allocation of Skype Credit and auto-recharge:

  • VAT is not charged when administrators buy Skype Credit or when Skype Credit is allocated to one of their members. The same applies where auto-recharge has been enabled for a member.
  • Skype Manager Admins are charged exactly the amount of credit they buy, for example, Admin buys €200 worth of Skype Credit and is charged exactly €200.

Pay As You Go, Skype To Go, and SMS:

  • All rates are VAT-inclusive for EU members of Skype Manager.
  • VAT is charged at the time members make PAYG or SkypeToGo calls or send SMS at the prevalent rate of the member’s country of billing address.

Allocation and renewal of subscriptions and Skype numbers:

  • VAT is charged when Admins allocate these products and at the time of renewal as well as when a recurring payment is taken.
  • The prevalent VAT rate of the Skype Manager Admin's country of billing address is applied at payment.

Processing VAT registration numbers:

  • Skype does not accept or process VAT registration numbers.
  • Skype Manager customers who previously provided us with a valid VAT registration number now pay the applicable VAT rate in their EU member country.

5.4 Monthly statements

See What are monthly statements and how do I get them?.

  • The monthly statement contains an itemized summary of Skype Credit and subscriptions purchased, details of how customers have used their Skype Credit (for example, to make calls or send), together with any other adjustments including refunds to the account over a calendar month.
  • Key features
    • Available to all customers.
    • Covers a full month of paid activity.
    • Contains no tax or VAT related information.
    • Downloadable in PDF format; Download button is available in MyAccount.
    • Issued on the last day of each month and available to download from My Account from the fifth day of the following month.
    • Available for six months from the date of issue.

Example: The monthly statement for October 2019 is available on November 5th, 2019 and will stay available until April 2020.

5.8 Skype VAT scenario FAQ

EU VAT rules

Q: I need more information about the new EU VAT changes?

A: See VAT changes for Skype customers in EU.

Q: How does this affect Skype Manager?

A: See VAT changes for Skype Manager customers in EU.

Q: I am a VAT-registered business in the EU. Is VAT exempted on my Skype transactions?

A: Skype is provided for consumer use only. We do not accept or process VAT registration numbers. Skype Manager Customers who previously provided us with a valid VAT registration number now pay the applicable VAT rate in their country.

Monthly Statements

Q: I need more information about monthly statements?

A: See What are monthly statements and how do I get them?.

Q: Where can I get these monthly statements?

A: The monthly statements will be available for download in MyAccount, simply scroll down and select Purchase history.

Q: Will I be able to download monthly statements for past months?

A: We’ve had to rebuild our systems from the ground up to make all these changes possible. As a result, the new monthly statements won’t be able to provide past data. Monthly statements are available from October 2014 onwards and accessible for six months after date of issue.

Q: What does all the information on my monthly statements mean?

A: Skype Support has a guide to the parts of your monthly statement. See What are monthly statements and how do I get them?.

Q: What if I'm a Skype Manager user?

A: Monthly statements are available to your members. No changes to Skype Manager admins.

VAT invoices

Q: How do I get VAT invoices?

A: Monthly VAT invoices are available to customers in some EU member states and will contain only transactions which have an effect for VAT purposes. VAT invoices for January 2015 are available from February 5th, 2015, and on a monthly basis after that.

See How can I download a VAT invoice?.

Q: Why can't I get a VAT invoice in my EU member state and others can?

A: We are only providing VAT invoices in EU member states where it is a mandatory requirement for invoices to be issued to consumer customers. EU customers who do not have the ability to download VAT invoices are to be advised to consult their monthly statements.

Q: Why can't I see Skype Credit purchases on my invoice?

A: Skype Credit on monthly VAT invoices will reflect actual Credit used, not the amount purchased as VAT is charged when credit is used. Customers should consult their monthly statements for purchase records.

Q: Will I be able to download VAT invoices for past months?

A: We have had to rebuild our systems from the ground up to make all these changes possible. As a result, the new VAT invoices cannot be run for periods prior to January 2015.

Q: Do I still get VAT invoices in Skype Manager?

A: VAT invoices can be downloaded via the Skype Manager Admin dashboard.

See How can I download a VAT invoice in Skype Manager?.

These cater only for allocations of subscriptions and Skype numbers. If VAT is incurred on any other activities, they will be contained in the member's VAT invoice.

As VAT is not charged when you buy or allocate Skype credit, these transactions will not be reflected on VAT invoices.

Q: Why did the previous invoice system change?

A: We recently started charging VAT when you use your Skype credit, instead of when you purchase it. This made a lot of things simpler, like seeing the total cost of each of your calls at a glance. But it made one thing more complicated and that was showing on your VAT invoice when you are charged tax.

If you purchased €10 of Skype credit before the change, we could create one VAT invoice for that purchase showing that you paid €1.50 in tax. But now the VAT might be split over several months as you use up your Skype credit. We needed to make changes to our systems to show that.

6. FAQ

How long does it take for the VAT invoice email notification or VAT invoice icon to appear in my purchase history?

The VAT invoice email and icon usually appears within 48 hours after your purchase, but sometimes can take up to five days to appear. If it has been over five days since the purchase and the invoice icon is not available in the order details on account.microsoft.com, consult with Tier 2 for A&B ERA.

How does a customer claim their VAT exemption?

Customers can claim their VAT exemptions by going to their local tax authority and presenting their invoice showing their VAT charge. This can be obtained from Order History on AMC.

Why can we not offer a refund in amount of VAT and new invoice to reflect tax exemption?

Due to system limitations, at this moment we are not able to manually update invoices or issue VAT refund.

Can I offer a Make it Right to customer who is complaining that we are not able to offer VAT exemption, Credit Note and Rebill?

New Make it Right category will be created to reflect the option to provide make good.

How do I explain a (business) customer that he will receive a "simplified invoice"?

The Microsoft Online Store is designed for consumer purchases only and if you use your Microsoft account with the credit card or any other associated payment instrument to purchase products you purchase as individual and consumer. This applies to consumer products as well as to commercial standalone products and subscriptions sold at the Microsoft Online Store. As a result, you will only receive a "simplified invoice"1 linked to your Microsoft account address. Such “simplified invoice” cannot be edited, will not contain business relevant data (such as VAT ID), and may not qualify as valid VAT invoice in your country. If you agree on being processed as a consumer with your private Microsoft account, I am happy to help you. If you would be in favor of placing a Purchase Order, I am happy to transfer you to our SMB support line.

Important:

  • Only transfer business customers what are intending to buy.
  • Items of a value of more than 3K and willing to be processed via Purchase Order process.

If a customer needs a full VAT invoice, see KB 2912243 Procedure: Consumer landing page for Support Boundaries. For example, do not transfer requests for Visio Project to the SMB Team.

Should I inform a business customer prior to purchase that they would receive a simplified invoice?

Yes. Explain according to the script.

What is the difference of a "simplified invoice" versus a full Vat invoice?

A simplified invoice is sufficient if we are invoicing a B2C and that invoice must include:

  • Date of issue.
  • Sellers (Microsoft) VAT identification number.
  • Type of goods or services supplied.
  • VAT amount payable.

View an example of a simplified invoice in section 3. VAT invoices available on account.microsoft.com.

A full VAT invoice is required for B2B transaction. A full VAT invoice is only available on a purchase via the SMB team thru the Purchase Order (PO) process (deal size equals or above 3.000 EUR/GBP):

  • Date of issue.
  • Unique invoice number.
  • Customer’s VAT identification number (if the customer is liable for the tax on the transaction).
  • Sellers full name & address.
  • Customer’s full name & address.
  • Description of quantity and type of goods supplied, or type and extent of services rendered.
  • Date of transaction or payment (if different from invoice date).
  • VAT rate applied.
  • VAT amount payable.
  • Breakdown of VAT amount payable by VAT rate or exemption.
  • Unit price of goods or services – exclusive of tax, discounts, or rebates (unless included in the unit price).
  • Customer liable for the tax (for example, under the reverse-charge procedure) – the words ‘Reverse charge’.

What about products that are for commercial use, for example, Windows 10 Pro, Visual Studio? Customers are not eligible for VAT invoice with company ID? Should customers be redirected to Microsoft partners?

The Microsoft Online Store is designed for consumer purchases only and if you use your Microsoft account with the credit card or any other associated payment instrument to purchase products you purchase as individual and consumer. This applies to consumer products as well as to commercial standalone products and subscriptions sold at the Microsoft Online Store. As a result, you will only receive a "simplified invoice"1 linked to your Microsoft account address.

If a customer needs a full VAT invoice, see KB 2912243 Procedure: Consumer landing page for Support Boundaries. For example, do not transfer requests for Visio Project to the SMB Team.

Products with 0% Tax are not reflected in the e-invoice (such as Complete or free Surface Pen) what will happen with these kinds of invoices since they will appear incomplete?

Guide customers to their Microsoft account history.
