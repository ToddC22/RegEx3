2. Process

Both PayOps Execution and PayOps teams use VSO as their ticketing system, therefore all escalations to these teams should be done via this tool.

Step by step instructions and a recorded demo on how to submit a Service Request in VSO can be found here. 

When filling out the Title field of your VSO SR, please refrain from using tags and just write plainly in natural language what the request is about.

Example:

  • Bad Title: POD / APGC / TDS credit / Sample Partner
  • Good title: Credit processing needed for Sample Partner
  • The Operations team can be contacted via CSPPLM@Microsoft.com for:
    • CSP Enrolment /Onboarding process queries
    • Any contracts that do not fit the ‘click-to-accept’ MCRA/MPA scenario
    • Vetting Issues related to CSP enrollments

Important:

If the cases raised to PayOps CBO Execution, PayOps Redmond and Operations (BizOps) get escalated through GetHelp also, please make sure to add at the beginning of the title of the Payops SR the following mention: GetHelp || GetHelp case number.

PayOps SLA

The following are the prescribed SLA for PayOps Execution and PayOps BizOps cases. 
P1 - 2 days
P2 - 7 days
P3 - 14 days

If cases are not resolved within SLA, please escalate to MPN Global Support to get traction.

Discounts / Special pricing

PayOps Execution are engaged by the local MSFT subsidiary contact once the discount has been approved by Partner Business Desk (PBD). PayOps Execution specialists validate the discount via MOPET and apply it in CMAT. They are not involved in the business discussion, which only happens between the local partner’s contact and PBD. For an overview of the process, please check this deck.

How to handle typical scenarios:
  1. Partner claims they have a special pricing agreed but they are charged at full price

RSC agent should check relevant subscriptions in CMAT if a discount has been applied. If yes, follow the steps described in scenario n. 2. If not, ask the partner to provide the Discount Authorization Form (DAF). Note: DAF is the only valid document for special pricing – no other document type (ie: addendum or amendment to MCRA) is used for discounts.

If a DAF can provided, escalate to PayOps Execution (The authorisation form(s) needs to be added as an attachment to the VSO SR). If a DAF cannot be provided, redirect partner to contact the local MSFT contact who worked with Partner Business Desk (PBD) on special pricing for them.

Typically, the partner thinks they have a discount but the correct process has not been followed by the local subsidiary, so discount has been implemented in the system. There is no need to keep the SR open as – once the DAF is approved, PayOps Execution will be engaged by the local subsidiary contact to apply the discount in CMAT.

  1. Partner has questions related to a discounted subscription

RSC agent should check relevant subscriptions in CMAT and find out what discount type has been applied.

If discount reason = DAF, follow these steps:

Check in CMAT if the discount has been applied properly by PayOps Execution (comments and the relevant VSO SR# are available in CMAT). Agents can look up this information by following the instructions below.

If the discount has been wrongly applied, escalate to PayOps Execution.

If the discount has been correctly applied but the partner is incorrectly charged, escalate to PayOps Redmond for investigation and technical fix in the billing system.

If the discount has been correctly applied and partner is charged accordingly, but partner disputes that the discount amount is wrong, inform the partner that the discount has been applied correctly. Redirect partner to the local MSFT contact who worked on the special pricing for them to seek clarification and action. If a change to the DAF is required, the local MSFT contact should follow the CSP discount process with PBD and PayOps Execution will get engaged at the end to apply the newly agreed discount. There is no need to keep the SR open as – once the correction to the DAF is made, PayOps Execution will apply the discount in CMAT.

 

If discount reason = Promotion, escalate to PayOps Redmond -> as this is a system-generated discount type.

    3. If discount reason = anything different from DAF/Promotion, escalate to PayOps Execution to start the investigation. Partner would like to transfer the discount to other subscriptions ID’s (maybe because they are going to use a different tenant).

Inform the partner that discounts applied to CSP subscriptions are approved on a per-subscription basis tied specifically to the subscription ID's listed in the approved DAF for those discounts. The discount details as listed under a DAF only apply to those tenant's subscriptions and cannot be used for the new subscriptions. To proceed with adding discounts to other subscription ID’s, a new DAF for those subscriptions is required.

  • KB 4535497 - Procedure: Partner: Self-service capability for CSP partners to upload WHT certificates

DAF (Delivery Acceptance Form)

Requests for DAF or any other hardcopy tax document must be escalated to PayOps Execution as described in this article:

  • KB 2986051 - Cloud CSP & Syndication: HCTI, DAF, and other hardcopy tax documents

Status confirmation letter for CSP Partners

The CSP Status confirmation letter can be requested from PayOps Execution by raising a VSO ticket.

Processing of CSP credits and discounts above $2,500 (or local currency equivalent)

Partner frontline support advocates are responsible for seeking approval for credits above $2,500 as per procedure explained in article Procedure: Partner: Cloud: Applying a credit/discount using CMAT but their application in the tool cannot be done by support advocates. Credit application in CMAT/CST needs to be done by the Payops Execution team.

The same applies to discount application where the estimate discount value is above $2,500 for scenarios 4. Special/Exceptional circumstances and 5. System error documented in article id 4517737. Partner frontline support advocates are responsible for seeking approval for these discounts but discount application in CMAT/CST needs to be done by the PayOps Execution team. In the VSO SR please include evidence (email or ADO Task id#) that approval was received for the credit request.

Manually generated revised invoices and credit memos:

This specifically refers to the VAT return system in place in the EU and other EMEA countries. For partners who do not accept that the VAT credit is only visible on next month’s invoice and are blocking payment, support advocates can request manually revised documents aligned to the partner needs. See specific procedure called out in paragraph 4. How to request old invoices to be re-generated and credit memos, article id 4037109. These requests must be escalated to PayOps Execution.

For traction or to discuss any disputes with tickets raised to the PayOps Execution team, reach out to payopsexecution@microsoft.com and make sure to include the VSO SR# in the subject line.

2.3. Escalations to Operations (BizOps)

2.3.1 Enrolment Process inquiries

These issues are handled by the Operations Team, therefore the escalation is via CSPPLM@Microsoft.com for:

  • CSP Enrolment /Onboarding process queries
  • Any contracts that do not fit the ‘click-to-accept’ MCRA/MPA scenario
  • Vetting Issues related to CSP enrollments

Ex.: Partner was onboarded as T1-Direct but they applied for T2-Indirect Provider.

Note that the Operations team can only help with status requests or correcting mistakes related to how the partner was onboarded. They cannot fix Partner Center errors that might occur during the enrolment process. For Partner Center errors we need to engage PayOps Redmond.

2.3.2 Any contracts that do not fit the ‘click-to-accept’ MCRA/MPA scenario

Examples include:

  • MCRA/MPA amendments
  • Negotiated MCRAs/MPA
  • Multi-Tier contracts (Partner authorized to transact as both CSP Direct and CSP Indirect Provider)
  • Riders to the Microsoft Cloud Reseller Agreement/MPA

These types of contracts require an “off-portal” (i.e. outside of Partner Center) process of electronic signature activities.

To get traction on cases sent to Operations,  reach out to MPN Global support.

2.3.3. Vetting Issues related to CSP enrollments

In Partner Center, all partner types are going through a vetting process. The vetting status, process, and troubleshooting steps are described in article 4054653 - Vetting Status and Process in PC. For vetting issues related to CSP enrollments, agents should use the troubleshooting and resolution steps described in that article. If the suggested resolution steps do not work, agents should escalate to the Operations team via CSPPLM@Microsoft.com for:

  • CSP Enrolment /Onboarding process queries
  • Any contracts that do not fit the ‘click-to-accept’ MCRA/MPA scenario
  • Vetting Issues related to CSP enrollments
