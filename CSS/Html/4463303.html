What is this article about?

This article is part of the MST TUG and provides information on how to report fraud and phishing incidents in MST.

For more information about potential suspicious activity, follow KB 2991467 Process: Handling social engineering, fraud, and phishing incidents.

1. Steps to take prior to reporting an incident

Review account activity to determine if the customer is possibly engaging in fraud.

To improve Microsoft’s ability to identify and hold fraudulent parties accountable:

  • Clearly communicate when a customer’s request is denied.
  • Document within the service request when a customer' request is denied and summarize the reasons why it was denied.
  • Determine if there is a history of policy abuse by reviewing previous service requests and escalate if the customer has already received a denial message for the same or similar requests.
    • If there are no previous service requests on the account, search for the customer by using their phone number to see if there are related accounts. Report if you find multiple accounts associated with the same phone number showing similar behavior.


Do not report if:

The account does not show a history of refund/credit, promotion, subscription, or hardware replacement abuse:

  • There are no previous SRs on the account in question and no secondary accounts are located when searching with the customer’s phone number (for example, no history of possible fraud or abuse linked with the account).
  • A denial message has not been previously delivered and documented by another support advocate.

2. Reporting social engineering, fraud, or phishing incidents in MST

To report phishing or fraud activities, select the Report Fraud or Phishing from the action menu in the main customer information screen.

[Asset 4463323]

Complete the entire Fraud Reporting form. See details below on how to complete the form.

[Asset 4463324]

Details on the type of information that should be entered in the Fraud Reporting form:

Input field

Description

Service Request

Service request number associated with the incident.

Supplier Name

Name of your employer.

Supplier Site

Location where you work.

Contact Method

How the customer contacted you.

Category

Type of phishing or fraud you are reporting for investigation.

Product/Service

Product or service associated with customer’s reason for calling.

Incident summary

Summary of the incident you are reporting. Include information to explain why you feel the interaction or customer is suspicious and include details to aid with the investigation.


In the Category section, select the type of phishing or fraud you are reporting for investigation from the drop-down menu:

[Asset 4463325]

Descriptions of each Category option:

Category option

Description/When to use

Hardware Abuse

Customers showing history of multiple device or accessory exchanges, repairs, or replacements.

Product Key/PPC Abuse

Customers associated with multiple product key replacements, status checks, or prepaid code.

Repeated Refund Abuse*

Repeated contacts by customer seeking refunds.

Repeated Support Harassment*

Repeated contacts by customer who did not have valid reasons for contacting support, while also using offensive language or engaging in abusive behavior.

For guidance related to threats or seeking manager assistance to de-escalate upset customers who are contacting us with a valid concern, see KB 2626175 Process: Main Consumer processes to handle abusive customers or threats.

Suspicious Payment Instruments

Multiple credit cards attached to one account (with different names, addresses, and others).

Tampered Proof of Purchase

Documents submitted for validation appear to be modified or fake.

*Example for completing the Incident Summary section:

Category option

Incident summary example

Repeated Refund Request

Customer requested a refund again for the same content that was previously denied (did not qualify per policy). Additionally, the customer has received multiple refunds in the past for the same situation (even after being told ‘no’ but Microsoft Support). See SR123123123 for when a refund denial response was delivered to the customer denying the previous refund request.

Repeated Support Harassment

Customer has contacted support numerous times recently but does not have an issue. Based on previous Notes and my most recent interaction, the customer appears to contact us only to harass us (see SR history from May through August). The customer has been warned in the past (see SR 123123123) that he will be reported for harassment if he keeps this up, which could results in a Terms of Use violation.

4. Reporting inappropriate Gamertag/Comments (Xbox only)

1. Go to Xbox Live. Select Report inappropriate Gamertag. [Asset 4582560]
2. Complete comment section, if reporting customer name on the account this needs to be stated in the comments. [Asset 4582561]
3. Review and select Submit. [Asset 4582562]
4. Confirm the report and select Ok. [Asset 4582563]
5. Complete. [Asset 4582564]

For inappropriate comments in an email or webform comment 

  1. Report on Xbox Safety Escalation Form.
  2. A Ranger will email details to Xbox Safety.
