1. Ticket creation

1.1 Confirm preferred email and contact information

If the customer has a preferred email that is different than the primary email associated with the account, enter this in ASD under the ‘preferred email’ field.

This will be the email where the customer receives communications associated with the ticket, and the email that the customer satisfaction survey will be sent to.

Never divulge customer information until a user is fortified. After fortification, be sure to mask/redact personal data appropriately when confirming customer contact information or discussing other users. For example:

  • “If we get disconnected, do you prefer follow up with you at the phone ending 1234” and
  • “Is it ok if I follow up with you at ****herc@contoso.com?”

1.3 Coding for insufficient information vs dropped contact

Insufficient information and dropped contact are both used when you have customer contact information to create a ticket, but the customer becomes non-responsive.

Dropped contact should only be used when the customer becomes non-responsive before you can identify the L1 Customer Issue.

Insufficient information should be used when you have a general problem statement from the customer (you can code for L1 Customer Issue) but you do not have enough information to code beyond L2 topic.

Code for the most appropriate solution delivered if you can code through Issue Code 4 (Problem) before the customer becomes non-responsive, do not code the ticket for insufficient information if you at least have a root cause or problem. Instead code for the most likely Solution Delivered and set the ticket to Closed-Unresolved. For example: If a client is unable to add a Credit Card and you provide all the troubleshooting but before escalating customer becomes unresponsive, you should not close the ticket as insufficient because you can code through L4. Code for the closest solution to ‘educated’.

1.4 Ticket creation best practices

Do create a new ticket

Do not create a new ticket

For all new inbound support issues that have a different level 1 (customer issue) in ASD.

Example: The advertiser wants to add a payment method which is coded under Billing and Payments. At the same time, the advertiser asks for information on reports, which would be coded under Reports and Data.

Always check the customer history in ASD to ensure that there isn’t a ticket already open for the same issue for the customer.

If there is an existing ticket, follow guidance in section 6. If there is a closed ticket for the same query, make sure the reopen policy is followed.

Do not create a new parent ticket if the advertiser has multiple, brief questions related to the same issue or root cause.

Example: An advertiser wants to add a payment method which is coded under Billing and Payments. At the same time, the advertiser requests information about the difference between prepay and postpay. In this scenario, since all recommendations are covered by Billing and Payments, it is not required to create separate parent tickets.

Example: An advertiser has general questions about auto-tagging, then the conversation evolves to discuss Editor in detail. A single ticket for Editor is sufficient unless an escalation or follow up is required for the auto-tagging query.  

Example: An advertiser has a set of editorial appeals, which require multiple communications with NQS to fully resolve.

If a follow-up or escalation is required for a secondary question or concern (even for the same L1 customer issue).

For example:

  • If the advertiser indicates they didn’t receive an expected coupon code in addition to their payment method inquiry, both issues would be coded under Billing and Payments, but a follow-up is required to deliver the code.
  • For bad match investigations, if additional terms are provided that require a new investigation (the scope of the investigation is different from the original request).

Do not create a new ticket if there is an existing SR and the advertiser wants to wait for the current ticket owner to resolve the issue.

Simply make a note in the existing ticket outlining the call and any expectations that were set.

Example: Issue has been escalated to L2 or customer called in just asking for an update and wishes to continue communicating with the previous advocate.

Select Edit Comments in the ticket to notify the ticket owner that the customer contacted us again for an update.

Foreign language support

If language is a barrier to support, follow the steps below to redirect the ticket or end the chat:

  1. Use https://www.bing.com/translator to understand the customer’s problem statement and identify the language used.
  2. Use the Get local support page to determine if the language is supported. Note: Traditional Chinese is not supported by Microsoft, and customers must be redirected to the Get local support page for assistance.
  3. For supported languages, use https://www.bing.com/translator to deliver the following messaging: “You’ve reached an <English> Support team. Can we talk in <English>?”
    1. If the customer can converse in the advocate language, proceed with support as usual.
    2. If the customer cannot converse in advocate language:
      • Translate and deliver the following to request contact/account info: "I am unable to assist you in <customer language> but will redirect your ticket for follow-up from the <customer language> team. Can you please provide your account number, email, and phone so they can follow-up with you in <customer language>."
      • If the account/contact info was acquired:
        • Translate and deliver the following messaging: “I am only able to offer Support in <advocate language>. However, another team can provide Support in <customer language>. I will transfer your ticket to our <customer language> team to get in touch with you. You may also Get local support and reference <SR>. Thank you for contacting Microsoft Advertising.”
        • Clearly document in the ticket that you were unable to assist the customer due to language and request outreach in the advertiser’s preferred language and transfer the ticket to the appropriate team. See section 5 for more information on how to relay chat inquiries to a different advertising support team.
      • If account/contact info is not acquired, deliver the following message: “I am only able to offer Support in <advocate language> but you can Get local support. Thank you for contacting Microsoft Advertising."
  4. For unsupported languages, deliver the following message in the supported language (do not translate this message into the customer’s language to avoid setting false expectations that we’ll continue to support them using translator):
    “I’m sorry, Microsoft does not offer support in <customer language>. Please     Get local support by selecting the most appropriate language for your needs. Thank you for contacting Microsoft Advertising."
 

Unsupported issue types

If the customer's email address is available (either provided by the customer or derived from an account number) then the email address should be associated to the ticket. Do not ask for an email if the customer does not proactively provide one. ﻿"noemail@microsoft.com" ﻿may be used in lieu of a customer email address. The ticket should be coded appropriately and closed with no additional follow-ups.

If a ticket is created in an email queue for an unsupported issue, send a modified closing email to direct the advertiser to the correct location, if known, and set expectations that the ticket will be closed. Close the ticket immediately.

Unsupported issue interactions should not exceed five minutes. See KB 2020667 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Transfer Guidance for Out of Scope/Misrouted Issues for details on handling misroutes/unsupported issue types.

1.7 ASD incident form properties

Subject: Specify the account number, Microsoft Advertising account name and issue type in the subject of the ticket. The subject should be customer-facing, so be sure to avoid using terms that are disallowed in conversation with advertisers. Always double-check the subject of outgoing mails. The ticket subject will prepopulate into the subject of any outbound mail and must not contain any personal data or sensitive information (including the username).

Queue: When a new ticket is created, the ticket will be generated in the default queue assigned to the ASD user. Always ensure the ticket is created in the queue that best aligns with the intake language and region. Note: If the user's default queue needs to be updated, a manager will need to be notified to update the user template. The queue may be changed at any time before the SR ID is generated. Once the SR ID is generated, the ticket will need to be transferred between queues, as necessary.

SR email templates: Follow-up, Lost Contact Closure, and Closure templates are available to populate outbound emails. These should be modified accordingly. Issue-specific templates are also available but the content in the body should not be edited unless directed to do so in the corresponding SIR (for example, fraud response templates should not be modified).

Save and Complete buttons: Use Save to save any changes while retaining access to view the ticket. Use Complete to save and exit viewing the ticket.

Issue Status: Issue Status determines the state of the ticket and should be selected as Open, Closed – Resolved, or Closed – Unresolved as appropriate. This field determines whether the ticket is recognized in the system as opened or closed. Once moved to a closed status, additional comments can still be left on the ticket.

2. Authenticating a customer: Fortify or manual verification

Authentication is the process by which you validate you are working with an authorized user on the account. Authentication of a user on the account must occur before any account details may be discussed and before any action is taken on behalf of the advertiser. This process is established to protect customers' Personal Data and to help maintain account security.

Authentication can occur in one of three ways:

  1. Chat and phone “Request a contact” auto-authentication (users are automatically authenticated if they are logged in and request a support contact from within their account).
  2. ASD Fortification – send the one-time use access code to the Microsoft account on file for the account.
  3. Manual verification – specific data points from within the account can be used to validate legitimacy of ownership claims.

Return to top

2.1 When to authenticate 

Authentication is required when:

Authentication is not required when:
  • You need to access the customer's account and the contact is not automatically authenticated (as it is for click-to-contact).
  • You need to access a different account that is outside the fortified user's scope (such as agency linking or campaign migrations).
  • A call or chat is transferred in and you don’t know whether the contact was fortified.

 

  • You can answer general questions without accessing the account.
  • When a call or chat is transferred to you and the original advocate specifies the contact was fortified.
  • Making an outbound follow-up call to a pre-determined call-back number specified in the initial interaction in which the customer was authenticated.
  • Conducting a proactive outreach to contact information derived from the account.

To help protect your customer accounts from fraud, follow these best practices:

  • Be on the alert for phishing and social engineering. Phishing for account information leads to fraud and compromise of customer information.
  • Always validate you are talking to the actual account owner and authenticate or verify the user.

Don’ts:

  • Don't proactively provide information that is considered personal data to a customer (for example, first name, last name, address, telephone number, or last four digits of a credit card).
  • Don't proactively provide Microsoft Advertising account, billing, or campaign information that could be used as proofs, even after the caller is verified. Account or campaign information include all personal data as well as any account-specific information (for example, customer/account name, campaign names, settings, and web addresses).
  • Don’t give hints or any verbal cues.
  • Don’t tell them if their guesses are close.
  • Don’t give initial letters to words they are asking for.
  • Don’t tell them the type of credit card they have on file.
  • Don’t tell them if an account is active.
  • Don’t request personal data via email. Instead use DTM.
  • Don’t divulge information about the support tools you are using. For example, names of tools, what you can use for search criteria, what you can/can’t do or can/can’t see on the tool screens.
  • Don’t fortify or manually verify users that have been suspended. Users are only suspended when there is confirmed fraudulent activity on the account.
  • Don't lead a customer or provide hints that enable the customer to pass fortification or manual verification.
  • Don't divulge user information or manual authentication proofs (for example, their username, card type, or last four digits of their card) even if the caller completes strong (manual) authentication. This information is considered high business impact (HBI) because it is used for authentication purposes.

    Once the customer is fortified, you may ask the customer if pieces of the information in their account are correct. Do not disclose the entire HBI information. For example, you may say, “In case follow-up is needed, is it okay if we reach out to the Outlook address you have on file, or the phone number ending in ####?”

Messaging: Why do we need to authenticate?

  • “In order to keep our customers’ account information safe, we need to authenticate users who contact us to ensure they have permission to access and act on behalf of the account.”
  • “We take our customers security very seriously, so to ensure the protection of customer information, we need to authenticate users to ensure they have permission to access and make changes to the account.”

2.2 Communications console and request a callback

When a customer is logged into Microsoft Advertising, they can chat or request a callback. In both cases, the customer is considered automatically authenticated because we know definitively that they have access to the account. While in request a callback, connecting to the user’s phone number is sufficient proof of access to the account.

For chat, you can see if the customer is logged in, you will see their advertising account number, user email, username and more.

  1. If you see their account details, they are logged into Microsoft Advertising and are automatically authenticated.
  2. If a customer is not logged in or requests a chat from the Contact Us webpage, the account details will not be available, and you will need to verify the customer.

Messaging:

Auto-authenticated
“I can see you are already logged into your Microsoft Advertising account. Just to confirm, you’re talking about account ID starting X123.”

Verification is required
“To help with your request, I need to send you a code to validate your access to the account. May I first have an email address associated to your Microsoft Advertising account?”

Note: Since it is the Microsoft account user that is authenticated, agency emails used to access multiple accounts need only be authenticated once. However, not all users have access to all accounts, and you should always check whether the user that was authenticated actually has access to all accounts in question.

2.3 ASD fortify

If the customer provides their Microsoft account email associated to their account, set expectations on what you are about to do. The customer can choose to receive the validation code via email, phone, or web. They have five minutes to retrieve the code before it expires. If one method fails or is not available, try another.

  1. Search for the customer email in the Membership Support Tool (customer search) within ASD.
  2. Select Microsoft account.
  3. Select the arrow to the left of the Customer Name.
  4. If the email is a Microsoft account, the account information will load automatically to enable fortification.
  5. Under the Fortification section, select the hyperlink for the option the customer prefers.
    1. Call or text fortification option is only available if the email is a Microsoft account in which the customer has entered their phone details in their Microsoft account profile page. If the customer prefers call/text verification, they should access their account through login.live.com and update their user profile and recovery methods.
    2. Email/Web fortification options are always available. For web, guide the customer to visit: https://verifyme.microsoft.com, sign in with their Microsoft account, generate the code, and read it back.
  6. The customer must read the code back to you.
  7. Input code and select validate to authenticate the customer.
  8. After authentication, you will be able to see customer details.

Messaging:

  • “I need to send you a six-digit code to validate your ownership and access to the account. Would you like to receive that code by Email, Call, or Text?”
  • “The code is on the way you should get that very quickly. Can you read it back to me please?”
  • Unable to fortify and there is another Super Admin on the account: “Since you don’t have access to the Microsoft account email on file, you will need to work with the Super Admin on the account to regain access before I can assist.”
  • Objection Handling: “I understand. These precautions are in place to protect accounts from unauthorized access. I am happy to provide assistance once you or the Super Admin can verify access to the account.”

2.4 Manual verification

Note:

Manual verification is a social engineering risk. Use this method only if fortification cannot be performed and it is not possible to redirect the requestor to another Super Admin or Standard User.

Manual verification requires the customer to provide specific information regarding their account that is unique to the account and cannot be easily spoofed.

Scenario Advocate actions

To help with general support for tasks within the User’s Role.

If you are unsure whether you can assist with a specific action based on the user’s role, reach out to your TA.

Two strong or one strong + two weak proofs (four weak proofs are acceptable if there is no billing or payment information in the account at risk of exposure or unexpected spend).

 

Important: You are responsible for changes you make to customer accounts – Exercise caution when asked to make changes that increase spend—while it is generally ok to set changes live, you can reach out to your TA or pause modified ads/ad groups/campaigns and inform the requestor they must log in to activate the changes. As a best practice, always pause large-scale changes such as bulk uploads and campaign migrations across accounts that are guaranteed to have a high impact on spend.

2.5 Unable to authenticate

If the customer is unable to authenticate, set expectations that account support is limited to general questions only.

“I can answer general questions about Microsoft Advertising but until you’re authenticated, I’m unable to assist with account-specific issues.”

Objection handling: “I understand. These precautions are in place to protect accounts from unauthorized access. I am happy to provide assistance once you or the Super Admin can verify access to the account.”

4. First contact policy

Note for chat advocates: Whenever sending links to advertisers, always advise the customer to right-click the link and open in a new tab or window. Otherwise, the chat may be disconnected if their current page is redirected to a page that does not contain LiveEngage code.

Initial Response Time (IRT) SLAs

  • Inbound phone contacts should be greeted within 60 seconds.
  • All chats should land with an advocate within 60 seconds of requesting the chat. Inbound chats should be greeted within 20 seconds of connecting to an advocate.
  • Inbound email should be answered within one business day.
  • Transferred or un-owned tickets should be answered or owned within one business day.

4.1 Dropped contacts

Sometimes a call or chat is dropped before the issue is known and before contact information is collected.

Phone
Before disconnecting a phone conversation, use the standard opening 3 times at 10 second intervals. On the final attempt, append the opening with the following messaging before disconnecting the call.

"I'm sorry, I'm unable to hear you. If you can hear me, please call us back at [insert support number]. Thank you for calling Microsoft Advertising. Have a good day."

Chat
Before disconnecting an ongoing chat conversation (where the issue has been specified), advertisers should be given three opportunities within a six-minute window to respond.

Return to top

4.2 Phone hold and chat idle policy

Callers should not be placed on hold for more than three minutes without an update or unless expectations are otherwise set with the advertiser.

Chat conversations should not idle for more than two minutes without an update unless expectations were otherwise set with the advertiser.

Return to top

4.3 Handling requests for call recording or chat transcript

Phone recording:
A call recording over phone is not customer-facing and should never be shared with customers.

US customers are recorded by default, but advocates may suspend recording if requested by the caller. In rest of world, recording is off by default and advocates may turn on recording if advertisers don’t object. Advertisers should be informed the call may be recorded for quality and training.

Chat transcript:
A customer may directly copy chat transcripts from the chat window before ending the chat session with the advocate.

For all Microsoft lines of business, when a customer requests a chat transcript, advocates must first authenticate the customer before providing the transcript via email.

5. Transferring contacts and tickets

Transfer a call

Always offer to assist the advertiser and take ownership of the incident as outlined in section 6. If the advertiser insists on being assisted by the original ticket owner, inform the advertiser that you can either help with the issue now or you can leave a note for the current ticket owner that immediate assistance is required. If the advertiser is willing to wait for the original ticket owner to get back to them, set expectations with the advertiser that you've left a note in the existing ticket and the ticket owner will get back to them as soon as possible.

In some cases, a warm transfer to a different program or team is necessary because the issue is out of scope for inbound support. Adult and Services are the only programs that currently accept warm phone transfers.

Refer to KB 2020667 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Transfer Guidance for Out of Scope/Misrouted Issues as needed.

Requirements:

  • Set expectations with the customer that you will attempt transfer before placing the customer on hold.
  • Initiate a Consult.
    • Select the Favorites (star) icon.
    • Select the most appropriate receiving team line.

Provide the receiving advocate’s customer name, SR number, confirm authentication, and provide a brief issue summary to the receiving advocate. Wait until the receiving advocate has had an opportunity to pull up the ticket.

  • Patch the customer into the consult.
    • Select the Instant Call Conference icon.
    • Select active consultation.

Ensure that the customer has successfully joined the conference and inform that you have the requested team on the line. Ensure that the connection is successful between the receiving advocate and the customer before disconnecting.

  • If the receiving advocate is unavailable, leave a message if possible.
    • If a message is left, be sure to specify the ticket number and include a detailed summary of the customer interaction in the ticket, including contact information and next steps expected by the advertiser. All relevant notes should be captured in the comments of the existing ticket. There is no need to open a duplicate SR.

Return to the advertiser and provide them with the correct phone number in case they would like to call themselves. Inform the advertiser that you've left a message (if applicable) for the correct team and that you have notated the ticket to request outreach as soon as possible.

Transfer a ticket

ASD push transfers can be used when the ticket must be manually relocated from one queue to another for the purpose of overcoming language barriers and time zone restrictions.

All L2 queues are enabled for global push transfers. If you must move an L1 ticket to a different site’s queues, you may need to coordinate with an L2/SME to first put the ticket into an L2 queue.

Transferring the ticket may cause ticket ownership to be removed. Document the SR number before transferring the ticket to a different queue to easily re-assume ownership of the ticket.

6. Taking ownership of existing tickets

If there is an existing ticket for the advertiser’s current issue and none of the exception scenarios listed below apply, you should take ownership of the ticket immediately.

Some advocates may not have the permissions necessary to pull parent and child tickets. If you are encountering this issue, contact your local senior advocate (for example, L2/SME/Escalation POC) to pull the respective tickets.

The goal of this process is to ensure that customer issues are resolved as quickly and efficiently as possible by the advocate who is in the best position to move the investigation forward, regardless of site.

Exceptions -- Do not take ownership in the following scenarios:

  • Tickets that have Escalations - Do not take ownership This applies to open escalations via email or UCMT (the intent is to not interfere with existing escalation conversations though you can provide coaching/clarification to the ticket owner, if needed). If a child ticket is not created per process (for example, Help Needed requests or refund approval requests), the escalation details must be clearly documented in the ticket notes.
  • Supervisor tickets - Do not take ownership if supervisors/managers at the original ticket owner's location have ownership of the ticket and are intervening in the customer engagement for recovery. Original ticket owners must be sure to clearly document the supervisor engagement in the ticket notes.

Note: For the first two scenarios listed above, it may still be appropriate to take ownership of existing ticket if the advertiser is adamant on being helped immediately by a new advocate.

  • Advertiser’s request - Do not take ownership if the advertiser has explicitly requested to continue to work with existing ticket owner and the previous ticket is still open or has been closed for less than seven days.
  • DSAT recovery - Do not take ownership if the same issue is already being handled by another advocate via an open DSAT recovery ticket for the customer. ​​

If you do not take ownership, updates and documentation requirements:

If any of the above scenarios are met and you determine that you should not take ownership of the ticket, it is critical that the following steps be taken immediately to ensure that the existing ticket owner is successful in helping the advertiser:

Update advertiser - Inform the advertiser about the status of the ticket (for example, SLAs for Traffic Quality investigations).

  • Set expectations with advertiser - When informing the advertiser about the next steps that will be taken by the existing ticket owner, do not make commitments that they might not be able to fulfil.
    • Do not promise that certain actions will be taken (for example, a refund will be issued) when you don't know the outcome of the investigation.
    • Do not tell the customer that they'll receive a response within a specific timeframe (for example, within the same day) as the existing ticket owner might be off work.
    • Example messaging: 

"I'll document our conversation and (Insert Existing Ticket Owner Name) will reach out as soon as they have an update."

  • Document interaction - Document within the ticket notes:
    • Update provided - The update you provided to the advertiser.
    • Expectations - The expectations you set with the advertiser.
    • Miscellaneous - Any miscellaneous information that may be of benefit to the advocate who owns the ticket. If the customer provides new information which was not originally shared with the existing ticket owner, make sure to document it in the ticket notes so the existing ticket owner is aware.

Cross-site communication for ticket ownership requests

When an advocate has identified a ticket that requires reassignment based on the ticket ownership process (defined above), inform your team lead/manager/SME who is responsible for sending a communication to the respective site which should take ticket ownership. To ensure accountability, each site should designate one primary point of contact and a backup per shift who are responsible for submitting and managing ticket ownership requests.

The Ticket Ownership SharePoint located here should be used to communicate and document all ticket ownership requests. To maintain quality and avoid confusion, make sure to follow the usage guidelines:

Check the SP regularly to ensure all requests are handled within SLA.

  • Do not create multiple copies of the SP doc. We should only have one original file to avoid confusion. Make sure to save your changes to the original file.
  • After saving your changes, exit the SP file right away. Do not keep the file open when not in use to ensure other users can access it and make changes.
  • Indicate your site name before each new update to a case so we can easily tell which comments belong to which site (for example: "Alorica: We have taken ownership of this ticket and will work with the customer.").
  • Do not delete any previous requests, updates, or information. The SP should serve as a central location for historical ticket ownership requests so all content should be maintained. The status of resolved ticket ownership requests should be changed to Closed by the requester once ticket ownership is taken correctly and no further actions are needed.

The ticket ownership request must be submitted to the other site within 24 hours (excluding weekends) of interacting with the customer to ensure the ticket does not sit idle and there are no delays in handling the customer's issue.

For urgent communications or emergency situations, the following distribution lists can be used to communicate with each respective site. However, they should only be used as a last resort as the SharePoint is the primary submission method for ticket ownership requests.

  • Alorica: b2bAloricaMStrainingandComm@alorica.com
  • Arvato Manila: ASI-PH_SMBNAEscalationsTeam.arvatousa@arvato.com
  • Arvato Muenster : qualityteammuenster@majorel.biz and adCenter_Leads@majorel.biz

7. First call resolution

First call resolution (FCR) means that the advertiser's inquiry was fully addressed and resolved during the first interaction. It is preferred to extend the duration of the first call as necessary to resolve the issue without additional follow-ups.

If you can obtain confirmation of issue resolution during the first contact, there is no need for additional follow-ups.

If first call resolution is achieved, before ending the call, offer to provide the advertiser the SR ID for this issue in case the advertiser determines the concern was not actually resolved and needs to reopen the ticket.

Give the advertiser the option of a final email summarizing resolution. A follow-up email is not always required.

8. Customer communications

8.1 Writing style

Follow the guidelines in the Microsoft Writing Style Training when drafting emails to customers.

How we say things is just as important as what we say.

  • Use a warm, relaxed tone.
  • Keep communications crisp and clear.
  • Avoid overusing the customer's name.
  • Review Microsoft Advertising Support – Sample Language.

Protect customer information

  • If it is ever necessary to proactively reference contact information in the account (either for confirming contact preferences or referring a customer to another user on the account), always mask/redact the information and provide just enough to enable the customer. See section 1.1 for examples.

Font size and color

  • Use Segoe UI font as a first choice. If it is unavailable, use Tahoma or Calibri.
  • Use the same font style and size throughout the entire email.
  • Do not type communications in font lower than size 10 or above font size 14.
  • Do not use any font colors.
  • Do not use bold, italics all CAPS or underline text when communicating to customers except to highlight important information such as XID, SR number.

Templates

There are customizable templates available in ASD that you can use to help craft your communications. These templates offer a good framework for Microsoft Writing Style communication but are optional.

When using templates:

  • Remember to change any information highlighted in blue, and then change the font color to all black before sending the message to the customer.
  • Customize the template to the individual customer interaction to avoid sounding robotic.

10. Follow-up procedure

A follow-up with an advertiser is only necessary if the issue requires further action that cannot be completed during the initial interaction or if additional information is required from the customer before the investigation can proceed (for example, there is no need to schedule a follow-up with the advertiser if the issue was resolved or a solution was provided on the first contact).

Follow-ups should be scheduled to occur within 48 hours unless otherwise requested by the advertiser. Any exceptions must be documented in the ticket as being requested by the advertiser.

If an advertiser requests a follow up more than seven days in the future, advise the advertiser to call back when time permits to address the issue and a new ticket will be created at that time.

Call-backs should always be logged in the SR for any scheduled follow-ups.

Performance tickets may be left open as needed for up to three days to monitor performance improvement as the result of changes.

Be deliberate about scheduling follow-ups at a specific time rather than leaving it open (for example say "Is there a particular date and time I can call you back to discuss the progress of your case" rather than "I'll follow-up with you in a couple days to let you know how things are going").

Return to top

10.1 Follow-up/Closing procedure summary 

When conducting a call-back, advocates must first identify themselves and where they're calling from before requesting to speak with the advertiser. Once the advertiser is on the phone, the advertiser must be informed that the call may be monitored or recorded for training purposes. 

First phone follow-up failed:

  1. Leave a voicemail using the suggested script as necessary.
  2. Log the failed call-back in the incident form.
  3. Immediately send a follow-up email modified appropriately for the situation and including resolution if available.

Second phone follow-up failed. Should occur 24 hours after first follow-up if there is no response from the advertiser:

  1. Leave a voicemail using the suggested script as necessary.
  2. Log the failed call-back in ASD.
  3. Immediately send the Closing or Lost Contact email modified appropriately for the situation and including resolution if available.
  4. Close the ticket.

Second phone follow-up suggested voicemail script:

Should you have any other questions or concerns regarding your Microsoft Advertising account, please feel free to visit our online support center at support.microsoft.com or contact us at [Insert support #]. Thank you for choosing Microsoft Advertising.”

10.3 Chat follow-ups

For new chat contacts that are dropped but contact information is known, a single email follow-up should be conducted before the ticket is closed.

For ongoing chat contacts (for which the issue has already been communicated by the advertiser), advertisers should be given three opportunities to respond being two minutes each. (Total time given to respond being six minutes.) After this the advocate should provide the re-join link in Comms Console and advise the customer to come back to us if needed.

10.4 Follow-up email

The follow-up email should be tailored for each interaction. Use the follow-up email template and insert additional guidance and content as necessary to resolve the issue or acquire additional details to proceed with the investigation. The follow-up email template is accessible within ASD and may be inserted directly into an outbound email.

Always check the recipient and subject and proofread and spell check any outbound emails before sending.

10.5 Sending emails from ASD

Whenever an email is sent from ASD, the customer is the default recipient in the To line and the default subject of the ticket will be the SRX ID appended by the current subject line of the ticket. It is not possible to remove the SRX from the subject of outbound emails from ASD. Because the ASD ticketing system will automatically route all inbound emails to the appropriate ticket based on the SRX ID within the subject regardless of the destination queue email, emails cannot be sent from one ASD ticket to another.

Email templates exist for follow-up, closure, and issue-specific notifications (for example, fraud permanent closure). Insert and modify templates as needed to optimize your time spent writing emails. Note: Fraud-specific templates have been approved by legal and the body of the message should not be altered.

11. Non-responsive advertisers

An advertiser should not be considered non-responsive until 24 hours after a scheduled follow-up passes without response from the advertiser (either by phone or email) and there is no documentation within the ticket indicating the advertiser prefers a different follow-up cadence.

Advertisers should be given 24 hours to respond to a missed contact attempt before they are considered non-responsive.

  • If an advertiser missed a scheduled phone follow-up and does not respond to the follow-up email within 24 hours, a second follow-up by phone should be attempted prior to closure.
  • If an advertiser does not respond to the follow-up email within 24 hours and is not available for the second phone attempt, the closing email should be sent, and the ticket closed.
  • A first chat contact may be considered non-responsive once the advertiser fails to respond to three follow-up attempts in a six minute period. The chat window may be closed after the final follow-up attempt.

13. Ticket closure

To close a ticket change, the Issue Status from Open to either Closed – Resolved or Closed – Unresolved as appropriate. Whoever delivers resolution to the advertiser should own the closure of the ticket.

Tickets should be closed if an advertiser is non-responsive, regardless of issue resolution state. An issue may be considered resolved if there is no further action that can be taken to alter the outcome, even if the outcome is perceived as unacceptable by the advertiser.

All call-backs must be cleared and child tickets closed before a parent ticket can be closed.

Child tickets should be closed in the queue most appropriate for the language, channel, and ticket owner (for example close it in an L2 queue if the L2 provided resolution for a child).

Close the ticket immediately after the closing email is sent (IF a closing email is sent). Note: Closure mails are not required for tickets but are helpful if additional value can be added to improve the campaigns or the customer's experience with Microsoft Advertising. Advertisers may refuse a closure/summary email, but you should specify in the ticket why a closure/summary mail was not sent.

Escalated tickets: Tier 2 Escalation teams may have customer satisfaction surveys where Tier 1 advocates are the ‘customer’ and can provide feedback on how well your escalation was handled. These survey links are presented in UCMT closing mails that may reopen the Tier 1 ticket unless Tier 2 is properly informed of ASD closure.

If you want to close an ASD ticket with an unresolved/active Tier 2 escalation, you must inform the escalation partner of your ticket closure with the messaging “The Tier 1 ticket is ready for closure due to <reason>. Unless you require further action from the customer, please don’t reply as it will reopen our ticket and will impact the customer experience.” See Customer satisfaction (CSat) survey below for details on how to provide feedback to Tier 2 UCMT ticket owners in this scenario.

14. Making account changes (change protocol)

Do not make any changes to an account unless you have fortified the customer.

Support teams may make changes to the account at the customer’s request (or to implement performance improvements) but must thoroughly set expectations and acquire permission to set the modified campaigns live. Alternatively (for example for email), written approval to set the campaigns live after making changes is sufficient, otherwise, pause the modified ad groups/campaigns and advise the advertiser to un-pause and log their approval of the changes. Document the scope of changes and customer permission in the ticket.

16. Customer satisfaction (CSat) survey

16.1 Customer satisfaction 

Surveys consist of the following questions:

  1. Did we resolve your issue? (yes, in one contact; yes, in multiple contacts; no)
  2. How satisfied were you with this MS Support experience? (1-5 stars)
  3. Tell us more about your experience (free text).

Email surveys are delivered every time a phone or email ticket is closed. Customers will be presented with an in-line option to select an answer for the first question, which will pop the customer out to a web-based experience to complete the last two questions.

Chat Surveys are presented at the end of every chat.

17. Ticket management expectations

Idle policy

ASD SRs should not idle for more than two workdays. Therefore, updates should be provided to the advertiser consistently. Any exception to this cadence should be documented within the ASD ticket.

Aging case policy

  • Tickets over 20 days are considered aging. Tickets should not age beyond 20 days unless there is valid cause (issue is under higher-level review within Microsoft for bug resolution or customer is engaged but communications are ill-timed for completion).
  • On average, tickets should be closed in four days or less.
  • Cases should not age due to non-responsive advertisers.

Reopen policy

You can re-open an existing ASD SR if a customer is calling about the exact same issue within seven days of the ticket closure. Otherwise, a new parent SR should be created.

Roles and accountabilities

The following metrics are goals and should be actively managed by all ticket owners and managers.

  • Idle: Maximum two days
  • Aging: <20 days
  • MTTC: four days is the goal; under six days is considered healthy.

All advocates (typically L2s/SMEs) are accountable for engaging Microsoft escalation points as needed and managing communications to ensure thorough and accurate resolution for Microsoft Advertising customers. This includes:

  • Ensuring partners are properly informed of relevant case information to appropriately resolve the issue in a timely manner.
  • Ensuring partners are informed if the ASD Support ticket is resolved/closed prior to partner ticket resolution/closure.
  • Ensuring escalations to partners are progressing appropriately and that any stalled cases are raised in the Tier 1/CSS Sync call.
  • Ensuring process, content and large billing dispute concerns are appropriately raised to the TAs for issues related to specific tickets or to Speak Up! for any issues process concerns/issue not related to specific tickets.
  • Any responses from Microsoft escalation points are scrubbed for confidential/internal information before providing responses back to customers.
  • Any trending process/knowledge issues and gaps are raised to Speak Up! to be addressed by Microsoft.
  • Coaching and feedback on L1 performance is provided to managers for delivery.
