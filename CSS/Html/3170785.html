1. Acquisition vs. retention codes

There are only two types of promotion codes: Acquisition and retention.

Acquisition codes

Retention codes
  • There are two types of acquisition codes:
    • Standard Acquisition codes.
    • Performance Based Acquisition codes.
  • Only one acquisition code is allowed per customer for the lifetime of the customer. If the customer attempts to enter a second acquisition code, an error will show on the customer’s account.
  • Acquisition codes must be redeemed within 30 days from the day the customer created their Microsoft Advertising customer shell.
  • Performance based acquisition codes require the advertiser to spend a specified amount in order to activate the coupon value (spend amount must occur before the coupon can be used and the spend amount required does not include tax).
  • Check code type by looking in the billing tab at the coupon section, these promotional code names begin with an A.
  • Note: Performance-Based Coupons are Acquisition Coupons.
  • Not limited to new advertisers and do not have a spend requirement to activate the offer.
  • Can be applied to new or existing accounts and can be distributed by support to mitigate a poor customer experience.
  • Retention codes are not limited to one per account.
  • They can be redeemed by advertisers older than 30 days but do have an expiry date and must be used by that date, or the value will be lost.
  • Check the code type by looking in the billing tab at the coupons section and tally codes starting with R.

Promotional codes are minted or created for redemption in a specific account country/currency combination. However, promotional code are considered global, therefore, if an advertiser redeems a code in a country/currency combination that is different from their account, Microsoft Advertising will convert the code value to the currency of the account.

Coupon codes:

  • Redemption date: The last day on which the advertiser may add the coupon to their account.
  • Expiration date: The date by which the coupon value must be redeemed, or the value will be forfeited.

Note: For all promotional offers, the customer must have a valid payment method set up in their account.

2. Redemption requirements ﻿

The account must meet all the following conditions to qualify:

  • For retention codes, the account must have primary payment method that meets payment method requirements.
  • For performance-based coupons (PBC), all payment method types are eligible.
  • The customer shell must not be marked as fraud, quality based ranking (QBR), deleted, or disabled.

Advertisers may not be able to redeem a promotional code if their account does not align with the promotional code type or offer validity period. There is error messaging that will let both the customer and advocate know if the promotional code is expired, not a Microsoft Advertising promotional code, has already been redeemed on that customer’s account, or if it has been redeemed on another customer’s account.

Promotion codes in prepay accounts

Promotion codes in postpay accounts
  • A primary payment method must be added and associated to the account.
  • Cannot be redeemed on prepay accounts using an offline payment method. Users must add a credit card to redeem a coupon on a prepay account.
  • If the account is eligible for delivery, coupons may (depending on the type) create a positive balance and the account will deliver until the coupon is used or expires.
  • If funds are available and ads are serving in the account at the time the code is added, those funds may deplete until the code is active (this may take up to five minutes after redemption).
  • Once the account balance is $0 the account returns to pause status.
  • If the account has a positive balance after the coupon is consumed, the account will continue to deliver unless the advertiser manually pauses their campaigns.
  • A primary payment method must be added and associated to the account. If the account is eligible for delivery, the coupon will be consumed first.
  • The primary payment method will be charged if ads are serving in the account at the time the coupon code is added until the code is active (this may take up to five minutes after redemption).
  • Once the coupon is fully consumed, the account will continue to deliver, and the advertiser will be billed on their primary payment method per normal threshold billing unless the advertiser manually pauses their campaigns.

Note for services: We can apply a coupon to an account after a consultation, even if the account is over 90 days old. This is true for scenarios where the customer appears to be committed to working with us and using our platform moving forward. For this scenario, TA approval is not needed.

3. ﻿How to apply a code

Assist the advertiser in applying the code using the steps outlined in Microsoft Advertising promotional offers (coupons).

See Reactive Support Ticket handling > Customer Communications > Sharing Help content for localization guidelines.

After a code is added, the user will receive popup confirmation that the code was successfully applied. The coupon balance will appear on the details and billing page as ‘coupon balance’ within five to ten minutes and the customer (super admin) will receive an email confirmation. For the promotion code to be visible in the coupon balance, the user may need to refresh the page.

Starting September 2018, marketing and engineering teams will begin testing auto-PBC codes that will prepopulate into the account during the sign-up process if the customer clicks the sign-up link from enabled marketing offer pages. Coupon abuse will be monitored throughout the pilot and its possible repeat customer sign-ups will have the coupons removed retroactively from their account. Escalate to Global Support to confirm if a customer was affected by the pilot and automated abuse/coupon removal policies.

Important: Promotional codes CANNOT be applied through API.

How to tell if a promotion code has been applied to the account:

  1. Navigate to the Billing tab.
  2. Select the appropriate account from the Selected account dropdown (note that no information will show if All Accounts is selected).
  3. You will see the coupon balance on the How you pay Billing Tile if a coupon has been redeemed. Select the view details link next to the balance to view redemption details about the coupon such as date redeemed, class, code, expiration date, balance, and spend.
  4. After you add a coupon code, it can take up to ten minutes before the coupon balance is added to the account and appears on the Billing tab.

4. Code expiration

  • Offer expiration: Available in the coupon Terms & Conditions (T&Cs). This is the date by which the coupon must be redeemed.
  • Activation date: The date by which the spend requirement must be met to activate PBC or spend to get coupons. If expiration occurs after activation timeline, coupon is not eligible for a replacement coupon.
  • Value use-by date: Always 90 days after redemption, at which time, any remaining unspent coupon value will be forfeit.

5. Error when redeeming code

If the advertiser has added the promotion code in the UI and does not see the code but has not received an error, advise the advertiser to refresh the page before proceeding to troubleshoot. Promotion codes can take up to ten minutes to show in the account, but the page must be refreshed for them to be visible.

Note: When troubleshooting errors where the code or offer has already been redeemed, Authorized Users may use the coupon lookup tool as needed to identify which accounts may have redeemed a code, no information about any other accounts can be divulged to any callers unless both accounts are properly authenticated.

See Troubleshooting promotional offers (coupons) for more information.

See Reactive Support Ticket handling > Customer Communications > Sharing Help content for localization guidelines.

5.1 Offer is expired

If the advertiser just received the coupon before the expiry, or the coupon had a very short expiry (less than a month as shown in coupon lookup tool), you can issue a replacement. For cases where the advertiser had the coupon for a long time and didn’t apply it to their account, or with traditional expiration dates (three to six months), use the messaging below.

Messaging:

“This offer is no longer valid. I can show you how to opt-in to marketing communications if you’d like to be considered for future offers.”

5.2 Offer is only available for new advertisers

Acquisition codes cannot be redeemed more than 30 days after the customer shell was created. If an advertiser sees an error like:

“We're sorry but you cannot redeem this new customer promotion because you started advertising with us more than 30 days ago, the system will not allow them to redeem the offer.”

  1. Check to see if the advertiser is a new advertiser using performance reports by looking at the customer create date in Ad Inquiry. If the customer is new, attempt to apply the code on the their behalf. If this doesn’t work, escalate to Global Support.
  2. If the customer is not a new customer, use the guidance below for next steps:

If the customer is just outside the 30-day window by a few days

Issue a replacement code from the retention bank.

If the customer is way outside the 30-day window
  1. Check UCM to see the marketing email that the customer received. If the code was received within the last month, issue a replacement from the retention bank.
  2. If you don’t find an email from marketing in UCM, ask the customer to provide the email they received. If they can provide it (even a screenshot) and the code was received within the last month, issue a replacement from the retention bank.
  3. If the advertiser is not able to provide a marketing email, but the account is less than 90 days old and the customer only created campaigns and has performance in the last 30 days, issue a replacement from the retention bank.
  4. Provide the customer with a link to the PBC Promotion T&C's: https://about.ads.microsoft.com/h/a/PBC-offer-details

5.4 Offer has already been redeemed/used

The advertiser may receive this error if the code has already been redeemed by the customer on one of their accounts. To verify if a coupon code has already been redeemed:

  1. Access the coupon lookup tool (Only L2s/SMEs have permissions).
  2. Select Lookup at the top left.
  3. Paste the coupon code into the search box and select search.
  4. The tool will show you what account the code was redeemed on and when.

Messaging:

“It seems that this offer has already been redeemed. Each offer can only be redeemed once, but I can show you how to opt into marketing communications if you’d like to be considered for future offers.”

  1. If the coupon lookup tool does not show that the coupon has been redeemed (for example, the Customer ID and Account Number sections show 0 or are blank), try to apply the code to the advertiser’s account yourself. If this does not work, issue a replacement from the retention bank.

5.5 Code is not usable with offline payment method

This error code should not surface in markets with Performance Based Coupons because all payment types are acceptable for PBCs.

  1. Check the advertiser’s payment method. If they are using a valid offline payment method like Single European Payments Area (SEPA) , Visual Basic for Applications (VBA) /Boleto, proceed with issuing a replacement from the retention bank and apply it directly to the advertiser’s account.
  2. If the advertiser is not using SEPA, VBA/Boleto, inform them that an online payment method is required to apply a coupon code to their account and proceed with helping them set one up if interested.

    Note: It is unacceptable to instruct an advertiser to add a valid payment, then revert back to an unaccepted payment after the coupon is applied.

An open child ticket for coupon application indicates the ticket should not change ownership for the purpose of TCO management.

7. Performance based coupons

Performance based coupons are enabled for all Microsoft Advertising markets, but not all markets have current promotions in PBC form. For an up-to-date list of PBC promotions, see KB 2643959 Informational: Microsoft Advertising: Current Promotions in Market. Advertisers must spend a designated amount within a specified time limit to receive their promotion. In other words, PBC requires an upfront investment from the customer to receive their free ad spend.

Requirements:

  • Customer’s CID must be no older than 30 days old.
  • The customer must not be on Fraud or QBR.
  • The customer must spend designated amount within specified time frame (for example, spend $25 within 30 days to get a $100 PBC). Note: This is not a requirement we must check to give a code, this is a system requirement.
  • The spend value does not include tax. Advise advertisers to submit offline payments large enough to accommodate tax deductions for prepay accounts in taxable markets.
  • Note: There is no requirement on PI type. All payment types are eligible for PBCs.

Important dates:

  • CID age – The customer shell must be under 30 days to receive and apply a PBC.
  • Add-by date – The PBC must be added before the advertiser’s CID is 30 days old.
  • Spend-by date (expiration date) – The advertiser must spend the coupon by the coupon expiration date. This means that they must spend the designated amount on their own PI first before the coupon starts spending and must spend the coupon balance by the coupon’s expiration date.

8. Automated coupon redemption experience

When the customer receives a coupon offer from Microsoft, they may be provided a link to sign in and redeem their coupon. This process is known as automated coupons or auto-PBC (though this process may also be used for retention code redemption as well).

Automated coupon redemption links are single use. The automated coupon redemption link is specific to the customer, account, and coupon code. The coupon code (cco) may also be redeemed manually, if necessary.

Example link: ads.microsoft.com/cc/paymentmethods

Once the coupon is applied, either manually or through the automated redemption steps below, the code cannot be used again.

9. ﻿Performance based coupons FAQ

Q: What are performance based coupons (PBC)?

A: Performance based coupons (PBC) require customers to add a valid payment instrument and spend ($X) before receiving ($Y) in Microsoft Advertising credit. The goal of PBC is to reduce fraud and to increase the % of valuable acquisitions that bring money to Microsoft Advertising. Advertisers who do not perform in 30 days tend to churn out completely, resulting in a steep survival curve. PBC enables new advertisers to perform faster because it requires customers to start spending sooner if they want to unlock the free credit. The sooner advertisers start to see performance, the sooner they become more invested with the product and thus become less likely to churn out.

Q: What happens if we give an advertiser who already has a PBC, a retention coupon?

A: Retention codes override PBCs and pause their offer, which could have an impact on whether the advertiser is able to redeem the offer. For example, if an advertiser with an active PBC also receives a retention code from support they will immediately start spending on the retention code with the expiration clock still ticking on their PBC. Once they spend the retention code, they will resume spending on the PBC but will lose any amount they are unable to spend on the PBC when it expires (usually 90 days after being issued).

Q: Can advocates add the PBC to the advertiser’s account?

A: Yes, if a customer is eligible for PBC, you can add the code on behalf of the customer, as desired. It used to be that the user who adds the coupon receives the emails about the promotion, but this is no longer accurate. No matter who applies the coupon, the promotional emails will be sent to the advertiser.

Q: Are the PBC emails available to view in UCM?

A: Not currently, but it is an open feature request.

Q: If the advertiser has started spending, but hasn’t added the PBC, will the previous spend apply to the amount they need to reach for the coupon to start?

A: No. The spend required to apply the PBC is calculated from the time the PBC is added to the account.

Q: How many PBCs can be given to one customer?

A: The policy for replacements is one per customer but redemption is managed at the account level. If you are aware (through previous ASD tickets) that the customer may already have been given a PBC, do not issue another one. Check the XIDs for the prior tickets to confirm whether they’ve already received a code before issuing a replacement.

Q: The policy for replacements is one per customer, but what about agencies that have their customers set up with accounts under their customer shell?

A: For agencies who have customers set up as accounts under their shell, we can give coupons for each account that is under 30 days old. There will be system monitoring to prevent abuse.

Q: Does adding a prepaid balance of $25 count towards the spend to activate a code?

A: No. Spend is counted as money spent on ad delivery, not added to a prepay balance.

Note: The below internal FAQ is related to Auto PBC – live as of 2018.

Q: What is expected with the Auto PBC feature?

A: This feature streamlines the process to redeem a performance-based coupon by automatically applying a coupon code to eligible customer accounts during the sign-up process.

Q: Who is this feature targeted at?

A: New SMB customers just signing-up for Microsoft Advertising coming from specific Marketing campaigns that offer a performance-based coupon upon sign-up.

Q: How will this feature help internal partners?

A: Auto PBC is expected to decrease inbound coupon inquiries and allow customers to go live with coupon offers much faster.

Q: Will all coupons be automatically applied once this feature is released?

A: No. Only performance-based coupons used for new customer acquisitions will be automatically applied if the customer is signing up from a special Marketing offer. Other coupons, such as those used to incentivize feature adoption, will be tackled in a later release.

Q: How do we decide if a customer qualifies for the new customer promotional offer?

A: We will be asking for the customer’s website in sign-up and we will use this to check if the customer has ever advertised with Microsoft Advertising before. If we find that the website has advertised on Microsoft Advertising before, the customer will be ineligible for the offer (according to the coupon offer details, the promotional credit is only valid for customers that have never advertised on Microsoft Advertising before).

Q: How long does the customer have to use the promotional credit once it is automatically applied?

A: The customer needs to add their payment method within 30 days to successfully redeem the coupon. Once the coupon is redeemed, the customer has 90 days to unlock spend and spend the free credit.
