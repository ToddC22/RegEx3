Process: Microsoft Advertising: Stolen credit card and unknown charges

[Asset 4509089]

Who should use this article?

  • Microsoft Advertising

What is this article about?

Use the following troubleshooting guide for when a person contacts us about unknown charges on their credit card. These inquiries can come from the cardholder (either they are an existing Microsoft Advertising customer or have never heard of Microsoft Advertising) or law enforcement.

Table of contents

  1. Inquiries from cardholders

  2. Credit security tips

  3. Inquiries from a bank, or law enforcement representing the cardholder

1. Inquiries from cardholders

  1. Determine whether the cardholder is suggesting their card was stolen, or if the caller is raising an account billing dispute. If the cardholder is the account owner, refer to KB 3173012 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Credits, Refunds, & Rebills.
    • Is the caller the account holder?
    • Did an authorized user of the card create the account on the cardholder’s behalf?
    • Does the cardholder own a business that advertises with Microsoft Advertising?
  2. Attempt to locate any accounts that may be using the card using the following methods:
    • Cardholder name as username (use partial search for better results).
    • Business name as either account or customer name.
    • Business URL reverse lookup in AdInquiry.
  3. For any identified accounts using the card, attempt to authenticate and reconfirm that the card was stolen or if this is an issue of a billing dispute because of unexpected charges.
    • Note: Do not divulge any account-specific information to the cardholder without strong authentication using the authentication/fortification process. Specify in the ticket notes whether you were able to authenticate the caller or not.
  4. If you can determine that the cardholder does not have a Microsoft Advertising account, escalate to the fraud team using the unknown charges template. Include the following information:
    • If the caller is willing to provide a copy of their credit card statement, any personally identifiable information should be removed by the caller before submitting. The copy/screenshot should include:
      • Transaction reference numbers for the charges in question.
      • Values of the suspicious charges.
      • Dates of the suspicious charges.
    • Note: Information from the account should not be used to complete the issue-specific details in this scenario outside of sharing the XID (if known). If you know the account number associated to the stolen charges, pause all campaigns. Do not delete them, as this may hinder a fraud investigation.
    • Set expectations with the customer that this investigation could take up to two business days.
  5. Use the instructions below to determine your next step based on the outcome of the investigation:
    • If the fraud team determines charges are legitimate:
  • Modify and send the advertiser the following message:

“Thank you for your patience as we investigated further your request to have charges removed. At this point, we believe the charges are valid. If you were not aware of this account activity, you may wish to check with anyone else who may have access to this credit card. In the meantime, we have paused your campaign(s) to avoid accruing additional charges. You may log in to your Microsoft Advertising account and resume your campaign(s) at any time once you feel your account information is set up properly.
Please be advised that because we have determined that the charges are valid, you will still be billed for charges that have been accrued up to this point.

If you feel that we have reached this conclusion in error, please respond to this email with additional information you feel is relevant for our reconsideration.

Once we receive this information, we will conduct an additional review and get back to you within 2-3 business days. Please note that the decision we make after a second review is final and cannot be appealed further.

Sincerely,


[Insert Agent Name]
Microsoft Advertising Accredited Professional
(800) 518-5689 | Microsoft Advertising Support”

  • If the fraud team determines that the card was stolen, and the caller is eligible for a refund, the fraud team must also confirm the XID in which the charges to the card in question should be reversed.
  1. Access the account in question and process adjustments for any activity paid by the card in question. As a best practice, check the activity column of the billing history to locate the document ID on which the charges paid by the card were accrued to make reversal within the statements tab easier.
  2. Inform the caller of the outcome using the Unknown charges/Stolen Card - Refund Messaging email template (modified appropriately for any known values that have been reversed). Refer the caller to Credit Card Security Tips.
  3. Only reverse charges paid by the card in question. Charges to other cards in the same account may have already been reported and reversed through the other cardholders' banks as chargebacks. If credit memos for charges on a stolen card are declined, advise the caller to dispute the charges through their bank.
  4. Use Adjustment Reason Fraud - Other and specify in the comments of the adjustment [SRX ID], Stolen Card, [Fraud ticket ID].

“Dear [Contact Name],

We apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused. We will refund the charges within 7 to 14 days. No further action is needed on your part. However, we strongly advise you to notify your lending institution that your credit card has been compromised and to monitor your credit report for the next 90 days to identify any unusual activity.

We appreciate your patience and thank you for your business.

Sincerely,

[Insert Agent Name]
Microsoft Advertising Accredited Professional
(800) 518-5689 | Microsoft Advertising Support”

Note: For cases where someone set up fraudulent invoice billing, do not provide a refund and instead reach out to the cashapp team (cashapp@microsoft.com) to write the money off of the account via a credit memo.

For cases where the fraudster set up an invoice account under the stolen credit card, escalate to the ROC team to get a credit applied in the UI. Include a completed ART form in your escalation.

  • eocadv@microsoft.com (EMEA customers)
  • aocadv@microsoft.com (Americas, AU, NZ, and APAC customers)

2. Credit security tips

If the advertiser asks about card security features, you can share some of the following tips from the Federal Trade Commission to help avoid suspicious activity on credit and charge accounts, at http://www.ftc.gov/bcp/edu/pubs/consumer/credit/cre07.shtm.

You can also share standard dos and don’ts when it comes to credit and charge accounts.

Do

Don’t
  • Sign your cards as soon as they arrive.
  • Carry your cards separately from your wallet, in a zippered compartment, a business card holder, or another small pouch.
  • Keep a record of your account numbers, their expiration dates, and the telephone number and address of each company in a secure place.
  • Monitor your card during the transaction and get the card back as quickly as possible.
  • Void incorrect receipts.
  • Destroy receipt copies.
  • Save receipts to compare with billing statements.
  • Open bills promptly and reconcile accounts monthly, just as you do with your checking account.
  • Report any questionable charges promptly and in writing to the card issuer.
  • Notify card companies in advance of a change in address.
  • Lend your cards to anyone.
  • Leave cards or receipts lying around.
  • Sign a blank receipt. When you sign a receipt, draw a line through any blank spaces above the total.
  • Write your account number on a postcard or on the outside of an envelope.
  • Give out your account number over the telephone unless you are making the call to a company that you know is reputable. If you have questions about a company, check it out with your local consumer protection office or with the Better Business Bureau.

3. Inquiries from a bank or law enforcement representing the cardholder

In cases where a credit card was stolen and used fraudulently within Microsoft Advertising, we may receive an inquiry from a bank or law enforcement agent representing the cardholder.

Provide the requestor with access to our internal CELA LENS Team, allowing them direct contact with CELA specialists to appropriately direct their inquiry.

United States

For any non-emergency inquiries from US, foreign government, or law enforcement agencies regarding customer data and service legal process, direct callers to the CELA LENS Team: subpesc@microsoft.com.

US Law Enforcement Hotline: 425.722.1299

These tickets should be coded as Fraud Upheld. No further action is necessary.
