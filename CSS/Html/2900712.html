2. Procedure

Billing period: The billing period is based on the Service Dates shown on page 2 of the invoice. If the beginning month of the Service Dates is a month which has 30 days, the billing period is for 30 days. If the beginning month of the Service Dates is a month which has 31 days, the billing period is for 31 days. For instance: an invoice with service dates 6/20/14 - 7/19/14 has 30 days in the billing period because June has 30 days. The billing period for annually-billed customers is 365 days.

a. Understanding an invoice

Which invoice format is displayed to customers depends on their region:
  • Old invoice format: Europe non-EU, Middle East, Africa, APGC, and Japan
  • New invoice format: North America (NOAM), European Union (EU) and LATAM

Service Recipient The service recipient address is also known as the “Ship-to” address. It can be updated by the customer on the Subscription Details page of the Microsoft 365 admin center.

Example 2 – Page 2 of a license number change in previously billed month


For license number change, we can refer to Standard credit calculator here when the advocate need to explain the calculations to the customer. 

When a customer changes their license count, the billing adjustment will appear on the next invoice following the subscription anniversary. In the case of a license increase, there are three line items on the invoice to reflect the license count change. The activity will display in the following order:
  1. Credit to the customer for the amount billed on the original license count.
  2. Prorated charge for the original license count for the previously billed period.
  3. Prorated charge for the new license count for the previously billed period.
  4. Current charges
  • In this example, the customer had 11 licenses in April, increased to 18 on May 1, and reduced to 11 licenses on May 10. The customer was previously billed for the service period of May 1 – May 31 for 11 licenses at the unit price of $4.00.
Field Name Description Example
  1. Credit the customer for the amount billed on the original license count.

Full credit for the previously billed service dates: May 1, 2014 – May 31, 2014. Please note that the day, month, and year order will vary by country.

  2. Prorated charge for the increased license count in the previously billed period:

Formula: Charge per license/Days in billing period *number of days at higher license count* # of licenses


The number of days at the increased license count: 9


Calculation:
  • Monthly Charge for the increased license count, divided by the number of days in the billing period ($4.00/31 days = $0.12903).
  • That amount is multiplied by the number of days at the increased license count ($0.12903 * 9 days = $1.16129 per license).

Total Charged for 9 days: $20.90: $1.16129 per license * 18 licenses		  

[Asset 4090848]
  3. Prorated charge for the reduced license count in the previously billed period:

Formula: Charge per license/Days in billing period *number of days at higher license count* # of licenses

Number of days at the reduced license count: 22
  • Monthly charge per license for the reduced license count, divided by the number of days in the billing period ($4.00/31 days = $0.12903225806).
  • That amount is multiplied by the number of days at the reduced license count ($0.12903225806*22 days = $2.8387096 per license).
Total Charged for 22 days: $31.23 ($2.8387096 per license*11 licenses rounded to the nearest penny)

Detailed description for new invoice


Microsoft has introduced a new invoice design for Office 365 customers for all NOAM and EU customers. The new invoice has been updated with improvements to help customers better understand their current charges, how charges and credits are calculated, and how to pay their bill.

New invoice format for NOAM customers


i. Page 1

The first page has been simplified to show only the information the customer needs.

[Asset 4090861]

Here are the term definitions:
  1. Month created: Month and year invoice is created.
  2. Invoice date: The date the invoice was created.
  3. Invoice number is unique to the invoice.
  4. Due date: The date payment is due.
  5. Total amount due: For this invoice only. Does not include any prior unpaid balance on the order.
  6. Bill To: Who is responsible for payment.
  7. Sold To: Company address.
  8. Service Usage Address: Where the service is being used, also used to calculate the tax rate.
  9. Order Details
    • Product: For our business this will be "Online Services" and differentiates from other groups such as XBOX & MSStore.
    • Order number: Can be used to look up the customer and order in CMAT.
    • Billing period: The billing period spans the time since the last invoice was created.
    • Payment terms: Indicates if the customer pays monthly (30) or annually (365).
    • Due date: Date the customer’s payment must be received to avoid impact to their service.
  10. Billing Summary
    • Charges: Cost of all services on the order.
    • Discounts: Total of all discounts for this billing period.
    • Credits/Adjustments: Total of the credits/adjustments for this billing period.
    • Tax: Taxes by region will display here.
      CAN will display GST/PST.
    • Total: Total of all above.
  11. Payment instructions: Clear instructions of what the customer must do to pay their invoice and by when.

  12. Contact Support options: How to contact support with the phone number and a link to self-help documentation.
ii. Page 2

The second page has been reformatted to improve readability. Each subscription will have its own section or block.

[Asset 4090868]

[Asset 4090870]

Here are the term definitions:
  1. Service Period: This tells the customer what dates they are paying for.
  2. Days: This refers to how many days the customer is being charged (can also be calculated by subtracting the dates in the service period).
  3. Qty (Quantity): The number of licenses the customer has purchased and is being billed for.
  4. Monthly price: The cost of the subscription. If annual, this will be updated accordingly.
  5. Charges: The total you get when calculating the service period, quantity and monthly price.
  6. Discounts: If the customer has any discounts, they will be listed here.
  7. Credits: If the customer has any credits, they will be listed here.
  8. Sub-total: This line provides the total due before tax.
  9. Tax %: Provides the tax rate the customer is being charged. This is calculated from the service usage address.
  10. Tax: The tax the customer is charged (tax % multiplied by sub-total).
  11. Total: Tax plus sub-total.
  12. Grand Total: Total due for all subscriptions.

b. Advocate steps to retrieve an invoice from CMAT

Customers can download copies of their invoices in Adobe ® Acrobat format in the Microsoft 365 admin center. If the customer requests a bill be sent to them through email, direct the customer to download the invoice themselves.

100% of customers worldwide have the option to have their billing statement sent in an email. This will be a separate email from the current "statement ready" mail sent to customers.

In order to receive their billing statement as an attachment, the customer will need to do the following:

  1. Navigate to Billing > Billing Notifications
  2. At the top of the page, toggle "Receive billing statement as attachment" to On.


Advocates should only send an invoice to the customer if the invoice is unavailable in the portal, but is available in CMAT. Advocates can download invoices from CMAT and retrieve a copy, follow these steps:

Commerce Support Workflow – send a copy of the bill to the customer  
Scenario Customer requests a copy of their bill be sent to them and the invoice is unavailable in the portal, but is available in CMAT.
1. Go to the Orders tab.  

[Asset 4090875]
2. Change the Date Range to include the billing period you want to view and click OK.

[Asset 4090876]

3. Click the down arrow next to the Order ID.

[Asset 4090877]

4. On the order details page, click the Related Information icon.

[Asset 4090880]

5. A list of invoices will be displayed. Click the invoice icon in the Invoice URL column to open the invoice in question. Save the file as directed by your supervisor.

[Asset 4090882]

6. Send the Invoice.pdf file to the customer.
