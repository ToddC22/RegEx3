Policy summary 

Rarely, a situation arises during a support or service contact that is unusual and may not involve a Microsoft Advertising product. These may include various threats, and irate or abusive behavior.

The policies and procedures for handling such situations are outlined in this article. Note that these procedures have been verified by Microsoft and should be followed carefully and exactly for legal reasons.

Note: When instructed to record a phone conversation below, you may be unable to do so based on your call center setup. In such cases, take detailed notes describing the conversation. Remember to mark the time of the call.

Threats of suicide

You must inform the designated internal Microsoft team of the suicide threat. Do not provide advice to the person who is making or reporting the suicide threat.

Someone reports a suicide threat 

  1. Record the call or preserve the chat session and/or e-mail message(s).  
  2. Involve your team supervisor or lead. Try to keep the person who is reporting the threat on the telephone until your supervisor or lead takes the call or gives you more instructions. 
  3. Collect and record as many details as possible. This includes the answers to the following questions and any other details that could possibly verify the threat: 
    • Who made the threat?
    • When did the threat occur?
    • What is the location that the threat is coming from? 
  4. After you document the information, escalate the issue by sending e-mail messages to BOTH cdoc@microsoft.com and subpesc@microsoft.com at the same time with the following subject and body:  
    • Subject: SUICIDE THREAT
    • Body: ATTENTION MSNALERT: The following is a threat of suicide by an MSN user. Escalate to the Windows Live Compliance team on-call member by telephone
    • Include any personal identification information for the contact, such as e-mail address, telephone number and street address.
    • Note: The cdoc@microsoft.com and subpesc@microsoft.com alias' are Microsoft internal alias' only. Do not give these addresses to external contacts. 
  5. Keep all records of the threat and the actions taken in response.

Someone MAKES a suicide threat 

  1. Record the call or preserve the chat session and/or e-mail message(s). If a threat is not being made through the Internet, try to learn the exact location of the threat. Then, record any information that could possibly verify the threat.
  2. Involve your team supervisor or lead. Try to keep the person who is making the threat on the telephone until your supervisor or lead takes the call or gives you more instructions.
  3. Gather the following information: 
    • The name or the e-mail address of the person who is threatening to commit suicide.
    • The telephone number of the person who is threatening to commit suicide.
    • Where the person who is making the threat is located.
  4. After you document the information, escalate the issue by sending e-mail messages to BOTH cdoc@microsoft.com and subpesc@microsoft.com at the same time with the following subject and body:  
    • Subject: SUICIDE THREAT
    • Body: ATTENTION MSNALERT: The following is a threat of suicide by an MSN user. Escalate to the Windows Live Compliance team on-call member by telephone.
    • Include any personal identification information for the contact, such as e-mail address, telephone number and street address.
    • Note: The cdoc@microsoft.com and subpesc@microsoft.com alias' are Microsoft internal alias' only. Do not give these addresses to external contacts.
  5. Keep all records of the threat and the actions taken in response.

Threats of violence, terrorism or cyber terrorism (hacking) 

Any threat or report of a threat that Microsoft receives directly or through a customer support center and that includes a threat of a bomb, a threat to hack any Microsoft-owned sites or products, or a threat of violence against a person or persons must be reported to local law enforcement. Microsoft Corporate Security must also be contacted with all the details of the threat.

Real-time bomb and violence threats 

Real-time threats include both in-person and telephone/chat threats

  1. Contact Microsoft Corporate Security immediately at (001) (425) 706-0000.
  2. Record the call or chat session.
  3. Collect and document as much information as you can by asking questions. Keep the caller on the telephone or keep the person in the chat session for as long as you can, and document as much information as possible. 
  4. Pay extra attention to background noises, such as a motor running or music playing. These sounds may provide clues about the location of the caller.
  5. Listen closely to the caller's voice (male, female) and voice quality (calm, excited), and note any accent or speech impediment.
  6. Ask the following questions 
    • Can you please repeat what you said?
    • What type of bomb is it?
    • Where is the bomb located?
    • What time will the bomb go off?
    • What is your name?
  7. Contact local law enforcement and follow these steps 
    • inform them of the threat
    • share the information you collected above
    • inform them that the issue will be escalated to Microsoft Security
  8. After the call ends, service or support personnel involved in the call must each write a declaration to explain what occurred, and they must sign the declaration with the date and time. The declaration must include the following: 
    • statements about the threat that were heard directly from the person making the threat
    • any specific names the caller mentioned
    • any actions that were taken

      Forward copies of the declarations to       Microsoft Corporate Security. Be sure to retain all original records of the threat and records of actions taken in response.
  9. Remain available in case law enforcement personnel need to interview you.

Real-time cyber-terrorism or hacking threats 

Real-time threats include both in-person and telephone/chat threats

  1. Contact Microsoft Corporate Security immediately at (001) (425) 706-0000
  2. Record the call or chat session.
  3. Collect and document as much information as you can by asking questions. Document as much information as possible. Ask the following questions: 
    • Can you tell me more about the situation?
    • Can you give me the names of people who might be affected by this situation?
    • Can you please repeat what you said? Where are you located?
    • What site(s)/products did you say you will target?
    • What is your name? When will this occur?
    • Why are you threatening to hack this site/product?
  4. When the call or chat session has ended escalate to a lead immediately alerting them of the situation.
  5. Leads should email InfoSec Ops CSIRP csirp@microsoft.com immediately. CC the site TA and include the following information: 
    • User alias
    • Date reported
    • Event date (When hacker threatened to attack the site(s)/products)
    • What site(s)/products the hacker threatened to attack
    • Details of the call/chat. Include as much information as you can.

E-mail threats 

  1. Save a copy of the email message. Do not respond to the email. 
  2. Escalate the issue to Microsoft Corporate Security
    • email: security@microsoft.com
    • telephone: (001) (425) 706-0000

Legal threats 

Legal threats are defined as cases where the customer is threatening to sue Microsoft/Microsoft Advertising. When you respond to contacts who threaten legal action, follow these guidelines:

  • Focus on helping the contact resolve any service or tool issues
  • Be understanding and patient.
  • Do not offer any contact information for Microsoft Legal Department unless the contact requests it.
  • Do not discuss legal liability or wrong doing by Microsoft.

Real-time legal threats 

Follow these steps to respond to a legal inquiry:

  1. Ask the contact to describe the issue. Focus on resolving any problems that concern Microsoft Advertising tools and features.
  2. Work with the contact to resolve the issue without referring to legal action.
  3. If you cannot resolve the issue and the contact insists that you provide contact information for the Microsoft Legal Department, give the contact the following address (Note: do not provide any other telephone numbers, names or email addresses to the contact. Do not promise any kind of follow up):

    Microsoft Legal Department
    One Microsoft Way
    Redmond, WA 98052
    U.S.A.
  4. Escalate the ticket to a senior or SME for review before closing to inform them of the potential threat.

    Any legal threat may be raised to your TA if there may be a chance to resolve the customer issue without legal action. TAs will partner closely with Global Support to assess best course of action.

Abusive or Irate Contacts 

Abusive language and irate tone include the following types of speech: ethnic/racial/religious slurs, personal insults, profanity, unwelcome sexual advances, and yelling.

To control the call/chat and help a contact who is using abusive language, an irate tone, or both, use the following scripting:

  • "I am going to do everything that I can to help you."
  • "I understand that you are frustrated, and I am going to do everything that I can do to help you."
  • "To resolve this problem, we must keep this call professional."
  • "Microsoft is a company that values diversity."
  • "I find your comments offensive. So that I can help you, we must keep this call professional."
  • "I know that you have had a bad experience to this point. However, I want to change that."
  • "Please do not swear at me. I understand that you are frustrated, but we must keep this call professional to resolve the problem."
     
    DO: DO NOT:
    • Take control of the call
    • Try to help the caller
    • Try to calm the caller
    • Maintain professionalism
    • Be patient
    • Listen to the caller
    • Be mindful of your tone
    • Be sincere
    • Try to resolve the problem
    • Set the caller's expectations
    • Take it personally
    • Match the caller's tone
    • Yell
    • Stop listening
    • Let the caller intimidate you

Escalating the call 

 If the caller asks to speak with a manager at the start of the call/chat, use the following scripting:

  •  "I can contact my manager for you. However, if you are willing, I might be able to help you."

If the caller asks for the manager again, escalate the call. Never refuse to escalate a caller to a manager.

Ending the call/chat

End the call if the caller displays the following behavior:

  • The caller continues to be unprofessional after three warnings that the call will be discontinued if unprofessional behavior persists. To warn the caller, use the following scripting: "This call must remain professional. If you cannot keep this on a professional level, I will have to end the call.”
  • The caller continues to be abusive.
  • The caller is upset to the point of being irrational.

Before you disconnect a call, use the following scripting: “I am going to end this call now. I would very much like to help you. But you insist on being unprofessional. Please call back when you are willing to work with us. Thank you for calling Bing Support."

Blocking abusive customers over chat

If the customer continues the abusive behavior after three warnings and the chat is ended as a result, the agent can escalate to a SME or L2 to block the customer from future chats.

Blocking can be done in LiveEngage by clicking on the 'survey' tab, and selecting 'yes' to 'block future chat sessions'. L1 agents should always escalate to a SME or L2 before implementing the block.

Unresponsive or disconnected contacts 

Disconnected call 

If either you or the contact is unintentionally disconnected during a phone call, follow these steps:

  1. Wait 3-5 seconds for a response.
  2. Ask whether there is anyone on the line.
  3. Wait 3-5 seconds for a response.
  4. Again ask whether there is anyone on the line.
  5. Wait 3-5 seconds for a response. If there is still no answer or if you hear a busy signal, end the call.

Support team members should log (in the service request) that the call was unintentionally disconnected. Also, describe the troubleshooting stage reached before the call ended.

Law enforcement requests 

Law enforcement requests are defined as inquiries from local or federal law enforcement about policies or activity of Microsoft Advertising customers. These can also include inquiries from attorneys/lawyers, but not the press.

US

These channels should be used for any inquiries from US government agencies around customer data and service of legal process.

Inquiries:
Law Enforcement Hotline: 425.722.1299
Email inquiry channel: MSNDCC@Microsoft.com

Service of Legal Process:
Fax: 425.708.0096
Email: USLEREQ@Microsoft.com

International 

This channel should be used for any foreign government inquiries seeking customer data.

Inquiries:
GlobalCC@microsoft.com
[Service channels vary widely according to country of origin, so it will be best to redirect any inquiries to the GlobalCC alias; my team can then instruct LE on the appropriate in-country contact or service channel.]

Emergency (life-or-death) escalations 

If law enforcement indicates a life-or-death emergency scenario, please direct them to the below channels.  Both contact channels initiate 2-way escalation processes with MOC to engage the LENS Global Fulfillment team on a 24x7 basis. 

Phone: English-Language Hotline: 425.722.1299 x1

Email: Service of emergency requests: LEALERT@Microsoft.com
