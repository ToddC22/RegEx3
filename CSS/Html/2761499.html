Scenario

A customer or Microsoft employee, vendor or contractor contacts Commerce support to extend the expiration date or increase the seats for their Microsoft Online trial subscription.

Please note that CSP trial subscriptions cannot be extended and the below process does not apply for CSP purchases.

2. Procedure

i. Self-service trial extension

Self-service trial extension is available (for subscriptions in Active status only). Customers with an active Office 365 or Dynamics 365 trial can extend their trial for another 30 days without contacting Support. They can perform the following steps, and their trial will be extended for another 30 days.

1. Login to the Microsoft 365 admin center, expand BILLING and click Subscriptions.

[Asset 4537164]

2. Click the Extend trial link on the right.

[Asset 4537165]

3. Read the information and enter your credit card information. Then click Submit.

[Asset 4537166]

Note:

  1. The credit card is for verification purposes only. There is NO pre-authorization charge. Customers need to pass the credit card verification step in order to extend their trial using this feature.
  2. At the end of customers’ free trial period, their trial subscription will automatically convert to a paid subscription. The paid subscription will default to the plan they are currently signed up for.
  3. If the customer decide to cancel their trial before the free trial period ends, go to the Microsoft 365 admin center and turn Recurring billing off. The trial will automatically expire when their month is finished, and their credit card won't be charged
  4. Customers can only extend once using the Extend trial feature. If a customer tries to extend again, a message will appear advising that it's not allowed.
  5. If customers call support to extend the trial, the advocate should advise customers they can extend the trial themselves in the Microsoft 365 admin center. If a customer insists that the advocate extends the trial on their behalf or if the customer doesn’t have a valid credit card, advocates will extend the trial using the rules in this document.
  6. If customers call support stating they cannot pass the credit card verification procedure and claiming they are using a valid credit card, do not troubleshoot the error. Extend their trial using the rules in this document and code the ticket subject line as O365 E3/Trial extension credit card verification fail/DI/3/Country.

ii. Rules for trial extensions and seat (license) increase

For any online service product provisioned within M365 and visible in CMAT, support will provide 90 days for trial extensions and up to 250 seats for seat increases.

Before applying seat increase in CMAT, check commoffertool to confirm there is no seat limit restrictions.

Before proceeding with Trail extension, please check the subscription status in CMAT as follows:

  • If the subscription is with Active status, please directly extend the subscription and DO NOT calculate the In Grace Period as part of the extension time.

    Example:     If the Subscription End Date says 1/30/2020, then 30 days extension would mean to extend the subscription until 2/29/2020. The Change Subscription End Date option can be accessed from the bottom left side of the window, once the Offer Name line is extended in CMAT:
    [Asset 4537167]

  • If the subscription is with In Grace Period status, change the status from In Grace Period to Active, then extend the subscription with the agreed number of days, from the Change Subscription End Date option.

Only customers with an account team can request more than that and the account team can make a request through the Customer Trials Sales Team Support. (https://microsoft.sharepoint.com/teams/Office365trials/SitePages/Home.aspx)

Note: Commerce does not support trial extensions for Global Service Monitor (GSM) trials. Customers who need GSM support, including a trial extension, will need to be redirected to 1-800-Microsoft (642-7676).

iii. Rules for Microsoft full-time employee, vendor (v-), and contractor (a-) trial extensions

Beginning January 1, 2016, direct the employee, vendor or contractor to the Office 365 Demo site where they can create a 90-day demo account. The Office 365 Demo site is here: https://demos.microsoft.com/login#/.

Note: If the caller is an Office 365 support engineer (CSS ONLY), redirect them to 2524669 Procedure: M365 TS: How Office 365 Support Engineers (CSS Only) request trial extensions for test and demo accounts. If they are unable to access the links, they may contact their manager or vendor for help.

iv. Dynamics 365-related subscription trial extensions

Suggested script

How long do you want to extend your trial subscription?

Steps
  1. Determine the length of the current trial period.
    • Subscription start date
    • Subscription end date
  2. Calculate the number of months for the current trial period, and document the results in the case private notes:

    Subscription End Date − Subscription Start Date = number of approved months for the current subscription period

    Example: February 15, 2013 − January 15, 2013 = 1 month (30 days)
  3. Use this number to determine the extension period in the next steps.

Validation

Is the current subscription period less than 4 months (120 days) from the start date?

 

Yes:

  1. Extend the trial subscription up to 120 days from the subscription start date.
  2. Notify the customer.
  3. Follow normal procedures to close the case.


No:

Escalate to the Business Desk for approval. Use template and send an email to the below alias:

Zack Sauvageau: v-zasau@microsoft.com

v. Dynamics 365-related trial subscription seat increases

1. Dynamics 365 related subscription seat increase requests for customers


Tier 1 steps

Suggested script

How many seats would you like to add to your trial subscription?

Steps

1. Is the current subscription seat count plus the requested seat count increase total less than 25 seats?

Yes:

  1. Increase the seat count up to a maximum of 25 seats per subscription.
  2. Notify the customer.
  3. Follow normal procedures to close the case.


Seat increase calculation examples:

Current customer seat count + additional seats requested = Total seat count for subscription

15 + 10 = 25

For this example, the customer is requesting that their total seat count is increased to 25. The seat count request does not exceed the 25-seat limit for Tier 1 approval. The customer’s request is approved, and you can increase the seat count.

15 + 25 = 40

For this example, the customer is requesting that their total seat count is increased to 40, which exceeds the 25-seat count limit for Tier 1 approval. Tier 1 would continue to the next question for this example.

Note to Tier 1: Do not exceed 25 seats per Dynamics 365 trial subscription. If the customer request exceeds this limit, continue to the next question.

No: Continue below.

Suggested script

For business justification, can you please tell me the reason(s)?

Steps
  1. Collect and document the customer's justification for the seat increase in the case.
  2. Send an email to business desk for approval through belwo alias:
    Zack Sauvageau: v-zasau@microsoft.com
