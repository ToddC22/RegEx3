Discount Rates

Many Visual Studio and BizSpark customers don't understand the discount they receive, and request a price sheet. However, it is not that simple, as the discounts are dependent on what type of VM they choose to spin up. Here is an example of how discounts work:

Example: The customer is running a SQL server VM. Instead of paying for a Windows VM and the SQL server license costs (these two charges are broken into two lines on the CSV file and shown as two separate charges on the website as well), the customer would only pay for a Linux VM. For guidance, reference the Linux Virtual Machines Pricing site.

PAYG rate:

1 A4 Standard Windows VM = $536 a month
A4 Standard SQL license = $2,232 a month
Total cost = $2,768

BizSpark /Visual Studio rate:
1 A4 Standard Linux VM = $357 a month
Total cost = $357 a month

Note: In this instance, the BizSpark/Visual Studio user would see an 87% discount.

Customer Experience and Provisioning

Visual Studio Cloud subscriptions provides developers with an easy way to pay for monthly access to Visual Studio Integrated Development Environments (IDE).

  • Annual subscriptions: Until December 2018, cloud subscriptions came with standard VS subscriber benefits and were offered on an annual basis, billed for a full year. This program is still available to customers who purchased it before December 2018. 
  • Customers can initiate the purchase via Visual Studio Marketplace and pay using any Azure subscription where they are an owner, contributor, admin, or co-admin.
  • Customers can access their purchased subscriptions via the VS Subscription Management portal. Their initial bill is prorated based upon a calendar month (i.e., June 15 - July 1) and then on the 1st of each month until they cancel the renewal.

Note: Customers may experience issues canceling or changing the quantity of purchased VS subscriptions. In these instances, the customer can follow the self-serve steps outlined at How to purchase Visual Studio cloud subscriptions. 

If the customer cannot self-serve to cancel or change the number of subscriptions they can do the following to obtain assistance:

  1. Open a support ticket at VS Subscription Support. 
  2. Click the Contact Us button. 
  3. Select the Account, Subscription and Billing Support option. 
  4. Follow the prompts.

Customers can also transfer Visual Studio Benefits from Visual Studio Portal to different Microsoft Accounts (personal accounts) by following the below steps:

Credit Card for Initial Purchase

Customers are not required to add a credit card during the initial purchase process. However, some customers will still be prompted to enter a credit card when purchasing; this is due to a fraud check set by country and IP.

If this is the case, inform the customer that a credit card is required in their location at this time. Do not tell the customer about the fraud check.

Adding or Changing Credit Card to Convert the Subscription

If the user adds a company credit card to the VS subscription and removes the spending limit, our automation will just convert the subscription to PAYG and whatever services left running will get billed.

Customers should monitor their own accounts and subscriptions when employees leave. However, if they are granting the VS benefit to accounts OUTSIDE of their domain and tenant (like to a personal gmail account), our automation will clean those up just by removing the VS entitlement. Also, if they are absolutely sure that the only subscription under a former-employee’s account is on the VS offer, then the automation will clean it up. However, customers should be ensuring that their former-employee’s company-owned resources are rescinded.

Marketplace Purchase

If a Visual Studio customer wants to make an Azure Store or Marketplace purchase, they will need to add a credit card to do so.

You can send the following steps to the customer:

Please follow these steps to enter credit card information for your Visual Studio Subscription:

  1. Navigate to the  Azure Account Subscriptions page
  2. Click the message “This subscription has a spending limit on it” for the subscription you want to enter credit card information to.
  3. Select “Yes, remove the spending limit”.
  4. Click on add payment method.
  5. You will then have the option to add credit card information. Click the confirmation button when finished.
  6. Select “No, I do not want to remove the spending limit”.
  7. Click the confirmation check at the bottom.

Once this process is complete, a credit card will be associated with your subscription and you will be able to purchase from the Azure Store/Marketplace.

Technical Support for Microsoft Partners
Termination

There is automation in place to detect when an Azure subscription no longer has a valid entitlement. When this happens, several emails are sent to the subscription owner advising them to either get a new VS entitlement, or remove the spending limit (which compels them to add a payment instrument) so that we can convert the sub to Pay-As-You-Go. If the sub has a spending limit set, we cancel it. If the spending limit is removed, we’ll convert it to PAYG.

Note: Initial detection to conversion/cancellation usually takes just over a month. We average about 1,000 subs per day, and due to constraints set by ACE engineering, we can only process about 1,900 per day as our upper limit. Excess subs are backlogged, and added to the process as capacity allows.

Customers can have their VS administrator remove the VS entitlement and shut down the Azure VS benefit subscription. However, as a best practice, they should be shutting down their own Azure subs and accounts (if those accounts are in their domain and/or tenant).

Termination Scenario

If a user no longer with the company has spun up subscriptions on other offers like a Free Trial or a PAYG subscription, the automation won’t recognize or clean up Azure subscriptions that are not Visual Studio offers. Many Azure customers have multiple subscriptions under their accounts with different Azure offers.
Xamarin University support scenarios

ASMS manages Xamarin University issues related to billing, transacting, payments, and purchasing inside the Visual Studio Marketplace or Azure Billing Portal. Issues include:

  • Top of Form
  • Errors with Billing
  • Difficulty with Purchasing Process (Xamarin University support also tries to resolve these, though we don't have insight on Azure subscriptions)
  • Removing Credit Card Spending Limits
  • Trouble with Azure Billing Account or Portal
  • Refunds
  • Account admin is no longer with organization 

Always verify the scenario before transferring a case as detailed in the Xamarin University Support Cases guide.

If you engage Xamarin University support using the dedicated email for Xamarin support scenarios, follow these guidelines:

  1. Include the customer's email address and contact information in the body of the message as well as an overview of the issue (in English).

  2. Wait for Xamarin support to reply confirming they will continue troubleshooting with the customer.

  3. Inform the customer that the Xamarin University support team will contact them to continue troubleshooting.

  4. Once Xamarin support has contacted the customer you may close the ASMS ticket.

If you receive a case outside the scope of ASMS and is not a dedicated Xamarin University support scenario, please use the MSaaS Support Portal to find the appropriate team.

Misroutes

Below are scenarios handled by the VS team: 

  • Assigning VS subscriptions (bulk and single assignments)
  • Reassigning VS subscriptions (bulk and single)
  • Removing VS subscriptions (bulk and single)
  • Manual transfers of VS subscribers from one agreement to another (i.e., expired agreement to new agreement)
  • Editing VS subscriptions
  • VS Technical issues and inquiries

Customers may contact ASMS when experiencing issues with VS subscription purchase or assignments as well as other issues unrelated to billing (i.e., accessing benefits or activation). However, ASMS is unable to support these requests. In these instances the customer will need to do the following: 

  1. Open a support ticket at VS Subscription Support. 
  2. Click the Contact Us button. 
  3. Select the Account, Subscription and Billing Support option. 
  4. Follow the prompts.

Note: If the customer does not agree to file a new ticket, engage your TA/PTA, who will reach out to the OneVL aligned TAs.
