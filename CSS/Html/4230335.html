1. What's new

Azure reservations monthly payment options now available at no extra cost

Impacted Audience

  • All CSP Partners transacting Azure reservations
  • Reconciliation file changes – please see details below

Summary

Partners can purchase RI billing plan under both Legacy Azure (145P offer) and Modern Azure (Azure plan – 17G offer). The purchase can be made from both Partner Center and Azure portal. Partners get added flexibility of paying either upfront at the time of purchase, or each month for the length of the term. This will help partners to scale efficiently and reduce cash flow pressure on their journey with Azure.

Starting September 4, 2019* Microsoft began rolling out a new monthly billing option for customers and partners to pay for Azure reservations with more flexibility at no extra cost. Today, customers and partners enjoy significant cost savings from reserving their Azure needs in advance, but it only allows upfront payment at the time of purchase. (*) Note: it will be available in all regions where Azure reservations are supported in by mid-September.

Going forward, all customers and partners can enjoy the same cost benefits from making their 1- and 3-year commitments as they have before, with the added flexibility of paying either upfront at the time of purchase, or each month for the length of the term at no additional cost. With these two payment options, customers and partners can purchase Azure reservations easily and can continue to scale efficiently on their journey with Azure.

With the addition of new monthly billing option, Azure reservations will be available in the following combinations:

Current:

  • 1-year commitment – Paid upfront
  • 3-year commitment – Paid upfront

Starting September 4, 2019:

  • 1-year commitment – Paid upfront or monthly
  • 3-year commitment – Paid upfront or monthly

2. Azure Reservations Overview

Azure resellers currently can purchase usage-based offers for their customers. The prepaid and yearly commitment will give resellers more flexibility and purchase options for configurations they know about ahead of time. Azure Reservations enables Partners to reserve Virtual Machines on a one or three-year term and provide up to 72% cost savings versus pay-as-you-go prices.

In addition, Azure Reservations gives Partners price predictability and helps them improve budgeting and forecasting. Azure Reservations also provide unprecedented flexibility. Partners will have the option to exchange their reservations or cancel them and get a refund.

3. Azure Reservations Purchase and Management

3.1. Pricing

With Azure Reserved VM Instances for example, commercial customers can now save up to 72% versus pay-as-you-go Azure VM pricing simply by purchasing - or “reserving" - the virtual machine for a 1- or 3-year period. Windows Server customers with Azure Hybrid Benefit, included with Software Assurance, will be able to save up to 80% versus pay-as-you-go pricing.

The discounts applied depend on the VM size, region, and quantity. For more details about pricing, customers can access https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/pricing. Customers can use Azure Pricing Calculator and TCO Calculator to estimate the pricing.

3.4. Refund/Return Process in Partner Center

Note: This should only be followed if the Partner calls us and has NOT submitted a web submission.

Azure Reservations VM Instances may be returned after 5 calendar days after order date for a 100% credit.

Azure Reserved VM Instance returns greater than 5 calendar days results in a pro-rated credit and a 12% early terminate fee of the pro-rated credit.

  • Example:
    • If you buy a 1-year Azure Reserved VM Instance for $100 the daily proration is 0.274 (the result of $100/365).
    • You purchase Azure on January 1 and return it on June 1, meaning there are 214 days remaining in the term when you return the order. You will receive a credit for $58.636 (result of 214 * 0.274) and you will be charged an early termination fee of $7.036 (result of 12% * 58.636). Net credits you will receive on your next billing is $51.60.

1. Log in to Partner Center and access the partner account.

2. From the Reservations page, click on Refund. The partner will be redirected to the Azure portal (Ibiza).

3.5. Exchange Process in Partner Center

The Azure Reservations exchange option allows the partners to change their reservation if their business requirements change. There is no Early Termination Fee applied because the partner is rebuying the reservation. The partner will receive a pro-rated refund which will be applied as soon as possible to their original purchase instrument, in the month when the exchange was requested. Partners can use Exchange as often as needed, as long as it is within their reservation period and the new purchase has a higher value than the cancelled one.

If the partner calls and requests information on how to exchange Azure RI, walk them through the below exchange process from Ibiza. If they send an email requesting the same information, escalate the issue to the ASMS team, as described in section 2.4.

1. Log in to Partner Center and access the partner account.

2. From the Reservations page, click on Manage. The partner will be redirected to the Azure portal (Ibiza).

[Asset 4230360]

3.6. Billing Process in Partner Center

Any RI purchase will be first adjusted against the available Monetary Commitment (MC). If the partner’s account has enough MC balance to cover the purchase, the purchase amount will be deducted from that. In case the account does not have enough balance to cover the RI purchase, the MC balance will be the first adjusted with RI purchase and the remaining cost will be billed as overage with the regular billing cycle.

In the Dashboard Overview, the partner can find general billing information, but more detailed billing information is available in the Billing tab.

[Asset 4230363]

The new experience for RI purchases means that detailed billing information will be available in the ‘One-time’ tabs of the billing page. The billing information is split by currency, payments are registered much faster than the old system, and the partners will receive separate RI invoice and reconciliation file for each currency. Also, for refunds, in case the partner request new invoice, a new invoice will be generated, and it will also include a credit note.

The partners can view the Invoices and Recon files directly from the Partner Center Billing page. For one-time purchased subscriptions in different currencies, they will see a tab for each currency.

3.7. CSP Self-serve Customer Purchase

Microsoft's CSP Partner team has introduced a new functionality in Partner Center for partners to allow customers to self-serve purchase Azure RI.

With this new capability, partners have the choice to give customers permission to buy their own resources with agility within the existing Azure subscriptions that was previously set up by the partner. Partners also have the choice to forego enabling this option and continue to buy Azure reservations on behalf of their customers as they did previously.

6. FAQ

Q1: When the CSP partner is trying to purchase a VM instance for a client, before adding the purchase to the cart, they receive error "Impossible to handle this line" underneath a specific line item, and error "An error occurred" under the cart line item.

[Asset 4338254]

A1: Download the SPOCK file and escalate the case to PCTS.

Q2: When will the charge begin for Azure Reservations purchases made by the CSP Provider in Partner Center?
A2: The purchase will be charged when the CSP Provider is adding the Azure Reservations to the customer subscription, or when the Reseller is adding the Azure Reservations via the providers own portal which uses the APIs. For more details, see also https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/azure-reservations.

Q3: Are the prices for the Azure Reservations purchased by the customer / indirect reseller through the Azure portal calculated as pay-as-you-go or is CSP discount included?
A3: Use the Azure Pricing Calculator and TCO Calculator to estimate the pricing and compare it with the web direct prices.

Q4: Can the existing VMs in a subscription change from Pay-as-you-Go to Reserved VM instances purchased in CSP without any migration work?
A4: Yes, because Reserved Instances are applied at subscription level and not a VM level, so they will be applied to any VMs using that Azure Reserved Instance family and that are running in the subscription.

https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/virtual-machines/windows/reserved-vm-instance-size-flexibility

Q5: What are the countries where Azure Reservations will not be available on May 10th, 2018?
A5: The countries where Azure Reservations are not available in US Gov Arizona, Iowa, Texas, Virginia and in Germany Central, North, Northeast and West Central

  • Blog post: Better together: Azure Reserved VM Instances and server subscriptions - https://blogs.partner.microsoft.com/mpn/better-together-azure-reserved-instances-server-subscriptions/
  • Docs: Azure reserved VM instances (RI) + server subscriptions for Azure - https://review.docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/azure-ri-server-subscriptions?branch=pete-azure-ri-server-subscriptions

Q6: While trying to provision Azure Reservation in CSP, the partner received the below error. What is the cause of this error and how can the Partner proceed?

[Asset 4338255]

A6: To purchase Azure RI the Azure Reservation must be registered first. Follow the information available here to complete this process.

Q7: Will ARI auto-renew upon anniversary date of the Reservation?
A7: No, it will not. 
 
Q8: Will there be a prompting (email reminder) to announce me my Reservation will expire upon my one-time purchase end date?
A8: You will receive email notifications 30 days prior to expiration, and again on the expiration date. Once the reserved instance expires, deployed VMs will continue to run and will be billed at the then current pay-as-you-go rate

Q9: Is there any difference in exchange transactions between upgrading to a more expensive Azure Reservation and downgrading to a less expensive one? Will both be handled as a refund without early termination penalties?
A9: The Exchange transaction is an option that allows you to apply the monetary value of a remaining Azure Reservation term to the purchase of one or more new Azure Reservations, of equal or greater monetary value. The exchanges are currently handled as "Cancel and Rebuy". The policy is that exchanging the transaction should generate neutral or positive cash flow. On this condition, Microsoft will not apply the cancellation fee. If not, Microsoft can charge the fee for accounts subject to the cancellation limit set by Microsoft (currently $50,000 per year).
Both Exchange and Cancellation processes are currently manual, but the engineering team is working to automate these processes as soon as possible.
 
Q10: Is the cancellation limit (currently $50,000 per year) calculated per customer account for all his subscriptions?
A10: The cancellation limit is calculated per customer (in CSP per customer tenant across all CSP subs) and for a calendar year.
