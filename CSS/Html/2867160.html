What is this article about?

This article details scenarios where the service center may send back the customer's device return unrepaired as the result of customer induced damaged and what to do for each of those situations.

1. Overview

There are scenarios where the service center returns the customer's original device unrepaired. On receipt of the customer's device, the service center closely examines the condition of what is received. If the customer’s device was received by the service center in warranty (IW) and is found to have physical damage, it will be sent back unrepaired. The customer will have options for out of warranty (OOW) or Accidental Damage Plan (ADP) exchange or repair options. In the case of tamper/damage disputes, in warranty flag bypass SKUs are available as an exception. See KB 40413646 Procedure: Surface: Customer disputes a tamper or damage claim for guidance.

See the grid below for expected outcomes based on how the original order was written. There are times when testing of the device received by the service center finds no hardware faults, and the customer’s device is sent back unrepaired.

2. Procedures

  1. If a customer claims they received back their device unrepaired (they will have received a letter with the returned device), you can check in the SOM tool for the reason the product was returned back to the customer unrepaired.
  2. In the MST Devices section, devices with active repair orders in progress will display Repair Order in Process. Select the device to display a Repair Orders for customer view. You can expand this view to display further details – see below.
Details

[Asset 4034068]

  1. The expanded view will display repair order progress steps. Some progress milestones are displayed for a device being returned to a customer unrepaired due to severe physical damage being detected on the device.
  2. Note the Final order Result code – RTN and visual inspection code – SDF are displayed under the milestone header “Service Center Processing Complete” view.
  3. See below table for complete list of disposition and visual inspection codes that may be displayed under a progress milestone view.
  4. See KB 4053533 TUG: MST: Devices section for more details on repair order status and history.
  5. If a customer disputes a damage claim see KB 2916575 Procedure: Surface: Handling a damage dispute for guidance.
