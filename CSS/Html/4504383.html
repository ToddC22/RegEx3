Use this process to transfer to other teams within in RAVE for the Fairfax Support Area. This applies to any team that works in Fairfax in RAVE. For a list of teams look in Fairfax Collaboration & Transfer Wiki.

This can be used to get back to Exchange Online, which is supported by the original Gov MOSSUP frontline team.

 If the team listed works out of Service Desk, use this process instead: Restricted Access Service Desk Fairfax transfer process

Before you initiate a transfer, ensure you have the most up to date information on the case ready to document in the transfer template in step number 1 below.

  1. Send a mail from the RAVE case the customer that you are engaging additional support and from what team. Avoid the word "transfer".

  2. Prepare a transfer template in Notepad.

    Transfer template
    ====================================
  3. Feature area requested:
    Customer’s email:
    Customer’s phone:
    Customers working hours:
    DTM link:
    <Summary of the issue and why it is being transferred. Location of any relevant screenshots and logs.>

    Note: Sometimes this is a copy/paste of the problem statement if it is 100% clear. Or it could be a cleaned-up summary of the issue that more accurately represents the current state of the problem. Include or call out any troubleshooting, workarounds, attachments provided or other helpful information that has been discussed with customer or in the TEAMS channel that will bring the next engineer up to speed as quickly as possible. 

  4. Select Transfer and select Fairfax. In the Description / Justification field type the following:

    Feature area requested: <input the Office 365 Services name from the Fairfax Collaboration & Transfer Teams Wiki>

    For example:

    [Asset 4524597]

    [Asset 4524598]

    The Justification turns into red text that is now visible in the Unassigned queue.

    [Asset 4515105]

  5. Copy the ticket number and go to the Search RAVE bar. When a ticket is transferred it closes the initial ticket and opens a new instance. It does not have any of the detail from the previous instance. 

  6. Click on the latest Active instance and copy/paste in the transfer template you prepared in step 1. This will allow the new engineer to get to understand the latest status of the case.

    [Asset 4524599]
     
  7. Prepare the following template to alert folks in the Fairfax Collaboration & Transfer Team so that it can be assigned out to a Fairfax person in that specialty.

    Feature area requested: 
    Customer name: 
    RAVE #: 
    DTM link:

  8. Then go to the Fairfax Collaboration & Transfer Team. If a specific channel exists for the feature you need input your template there. If not, then use the General queue to alert folks.

    Start typing @fairfax until you see this pop-up (no space after @).

    [Asset 4515107]

    9. Select it and then paste in your template and hit enter. If SevA, include that near the top.

    Ensure that the team name is highlighted. If that doesn’t work, try again with just the name in the same conversation. Any replies you make are to the same thread, not a new conversation.

    If you make a mistake, copy your text to the correct conversation and delete the new thread you created in error.

    [Asset 4515108]

  9. Wait for a response and answer any questions they might have.
    Note: You are still responsible for case activity even though the current case will show 'closed' once you do the transfer.  Please continue to monitor the status of the transfer via the respective Teams channel you posted the transfer in. If there has been no response or acknowledgement of the case transfer within a reasonable amount of time (3 days max), bump the thread again and also mention 'GCCHLeads' so we can be alerted of the status.

    • If the case is not been picked up after within an hour for a SevA, email the Duty Manager Contact listed in the  Contacts table. 
    • These are distribution groups for the Duty Managers covering this service area.  If they are not able to find a restricted support engineer, they will contact you to start the customer consent process as documented in https://internal.support.services.microsoft.com/en-us/help/4487456.
