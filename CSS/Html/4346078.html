Guidance on completing the template

Use:

  • This is an internal facing template.
  • Whenever a case is transferred or escalated to a queue after scoping is already completed.
  • Complete the template in English (except Japan and Korea).

Case Originated from:

  • Region and / or queue.

Transfer reason:

  • For example End of Shift, Lack of Expertise, No Resource Available, Escalation.

Transferred by Tier Level:

  • Tier 1, Tier 2 or Tier 3.

Source Engineer Availability:

  • For example 08:00 GMT to 16:30 GMT Monday to Friday.

Best Contact Method:

  • By Phone, Email or Skype?

Customer Language Constraints:

  • Only if relevant.
  • For example; French or English, English with Difficulty, German Only.

Impact:

When creating your impact statement consider the following:

  • How many users or computers are affected?
  • How many dollars are being lost?

  • What quantifiable impact is there to mission critical business processes?

You do not need to provide answers to all of the questions, but must document at least one.

Scope Agreement:

  • Specific closure conditions.
  • Describe an end state where customer will be happy.

Engineers Assessment:

  • Your opinion or possible diagnosis.
  • Key Error Messages or information.

Environment:

  • What Product / Version is involved?
  • Is it a Cloud, On-Prem, Hybrid or other implementation?

Logs:

  • Name and Location of any specific logs to call out.

Action Plan:

  • When completing an action plan details in this section should be concise, but not contain vague references:
    • Action on Microsoft and Action on Customer/Partner should contain enough details for any reader to follow-up on them and progress the case.
    • Next Contact should contain date and time of expected next contact. If the customer said in a couple of days, then set a time for your follow-up. It is recommended you also create a commitment task if your incident management tool allows or an Outlook reminder.
