1. Overview

1.1 What is transfer of case ownership?

It is the procedure to be followed when a case you currently own needs to be reassigned to someone else. This means that the next person who takes case ownership is responsible for progressing the case to resolution and ensuring all customer expectations are met.

The transfer of case ownership may be to someone within your team within your time zone, or outside your time zone. It can also be to a different team that supports a different product category that you do not support. Majority (~75%) of case transfers stay within the same Support Queue Name.

  • If you are Swarming/Collaborating on a case, follow 4464163 | Procedure: Swarming or 4346610 | Procedure: Collaboration.
  • For a case that needs routing to a team supporting a different product category, follow 4456157 | Procedure: Misroute.
  • For a case in consideration of an escalation, follow 4346629 | Procedure: Escalation.
1.2 Why is it important to get transfer of case ownership right?

It is important because customer satisfaction (CSAT) for cases that have more transfers drops significantly. For example, having more than three (3) transfers impact CSAT negatively by ~10% or ~0.4 points. It also results in increased negative sentiment. Customer experience degrades as case ownership changes increase.

We need to ensure transfer of case ownership only occur on cases that require it. This decision is based on balancing what may be in the best interest of the customer given the status of their case, entitlement, and eligibility.

1.3 Overall Guiding principles
  • Minimize case churn by not initiating unnecessary case ownership transfers. This ensures a better customer experience overall.
  • Always validate customer need and their understanding around what it means to continue working on the case in a different time zone. If you feel it may be in the best interest of the customer that you continue working on their case, you should recommend this.
  • If case ownership must be transferred, ensure the next owner can hit the ground running without the customer having to repeat themselves. This can be achieved through quality case notes.
    • For case notes guidance, always follow 4548162 | Procedure: Case Documentation (CXD Capability).

2.4  Prepare for transfer

Now that you determined Warm or Cold transfer (if not, return to Section 2.3 Determine method of transfer), you now need to prepare the case.

  • Rename the title of the case to include Warm or Cold.
  • Ensure quality case notes.
    • Follow 4548162 | Procedure: Case Documentation (CXD Capability).
    • Make sure it is written in a good format, so it is easy to understand case facts.
    • Make sure case documentation is sufficient that someone can fully understand case progression without having to read through email threads.
    • Make sure the last contact with the customer is clear.
    • Make sure next contact expectations were properly set as a time range and not a specific time. While there is no guarantee that this can be met, the receiving team will do their best to meet this.

NOTE: If the time cannot be missed due to the circumstances of the case, ensure you coordinate with your Manager/TA as necessary.

  • Ensure the transfer template is complete.
    • Make sure details such as all contacts involved in the discussion of the case are included so the receiving engineer does not need to reconstruct or reestablish.
    • Check Section 5 Templates.
2.5  Initiate transfer method

Now that the case is ready for transfer (if not, return to Section 2.4 Prepare for transfer), you can initiate the transfer.

For reference, methods used for Warm or Cold transfer may vary by team. Follow your team’s approved methods. Some examples are listed below.

  • Use of Virtual Duty Manager (VDM) with or without Dynamics CRM-FTS.
  • Chain of Custody (COC) with or without “handover” conference calls.
  • Manual tracking and assignment.
  • Teams chat.
  • Afterhours Transition Tool.

NOTE:

  • Do NOT move cases back into the queue unless your team’s published “handover” process instructs you to do this.
  • If you are new on the team and need help, consult your Technical Advisor (TA) or Manager.

4.2 Glossary
Terminology Definition
Transfer of case ownership

The current owner of the case needs to be transitioned to another engineer. This may be due to the current owner is at end of shift and the customer is eligible and prefers to continue working on the case outside of the case owner’s working hours OR the customer wishes to work in a different time zone even if the engineer is not at the end of their shift.

Service Desk calls this “handover”.
Warm transfer It is a type of transfer where the current owner of the case discusses the case with the receiver before the ownership is transferred.
Cold transfer It is a type of transfer where the current owner of the case does not discuss the case with the receiver before the ownership is transferred.
Misroute The case is routed wrong and the issue type may be outside of your typical support boundaries. Misrouted cases may need to be transferred to a team supporting a different product category.
