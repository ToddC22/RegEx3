1. UCMT destinations: Global Support, Editorial, GFS, Fraud

Note: Global Financial Services (GFS), also known as Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS).

UCMT is the ticketing tool used by most of escalation teams. The ASD-UCMT integration allows ASD to generate Tier 2 tickets in UCMT. Issue information is automatically sent to the UCMT ticket and the ASD ticket is automatically updated with UCMT ticket details.

ASD and UCMT are connected by an API that links one ASD ticket to one UCMT ticket. Child tickets should be used to escalate from ASD to UCMT.

The UCMT ticket owner should be notified if the investigation is cancelled prior to resolution (for example, the customer has abandoned the interaction and a Tier 2 investigation is no longer required). UCMT can be accessed at https://ucm.bingads.microsoft.com/Ticketing/#/BookOfTickets.

1.1 Submit a customer escalation to Global Support from ASD

To escalate to UCMT from ASD, authorized agents must complete each of the following steps. Consult with your SME/TL if you are uncertain whether you should be submitting escalations to a UCMT partner team.

Frontline L1 Note: Child tickets should be used to generate UCMT tickets. This keeps customer communications separate from Tier 2 communications and the child subject line can be updated to include the UCMT ticket ID to minimize errors. The “To:” line of all outbound emails will still need to be overridden with the Tier 2 destination email alias.

BOS Agent Note: Escalations should be raised from the ticket-level you are already working from, depending on the requestor (for example, An AM request would be escalated from within the Parent SR, whilst a CSS L1 request would be escalated from within the Child SR). Do not create additional child tickets.

  1. Complete all required incident form details. Note: When choosing ASD Issue Coding, use the appropriate Technical Issue coding, depending on the nature of your issue. Once an investigation is complete, you should re-code your ticket to reflect what the problem was, unless a product Bug or Known Functionality issue was identified. Remember never to close a ticket under ‘Solution in Progress’.
  2. Create a child ticket to submit the escalation
  3. Select issue-specific details required = YES and complete all required fields in the section. (Required fields are indicated by the exclamation mark.) The fields will vary depending on the nature of your escalation, and the screenshots below are for demonstration purposes only.
    [Asset 4013192]
  4. Select Tier 2 escalation required =Yes and complete all required fields in the section. The details entered in this section will determine the UCMT queue in which the ticket is handled. Be sure to select the Tier 2 Receiving Team and Tier 2 Sub-Team according to the destination listed in the Escalation Guide or process-specific article.
    [Asset 4013225]
  5. Apply the SMB Escalations to Tier 2 UCMT > UCMT Escalations transfer (available from global L2, BP, and BOS queues).
    [Asset 4013223]
  6. Complete the ticket. The ticket ownership and origination queue will be retained in ASD.

  7. The UCMT ticket details section of the incident form will be auto-populated and updated by UCMT within minutes of the initial escalation and whenever the UCMT ticket is saved.
    [Asset 4013226]

  8. Update the subject line of the ticket to include the UCMT Ticket ID (this ensures the UCM ID is included in all replies to UCMT, even though you will still need to update the To: line with each outbound email.)
    [Asset 4010979]

1.4 Send an update to UCMT

As a best practice, UCMT tickets should be created from an ASD child ticket to separate Tier 2 communications from customer communications. This will also allow you to update the child ticket subject line with the UCMT ID to prevent duplicate UCMT tickets being created from follow up emails.

  1. Ensure the child ticket subject is updated to include the UCMT ticket ID.
  2. Use ASD Email follow up on UCMT tickets.
  3. Override the customer email with the appropriate destination alias, which can be found in the ucmt ticket details > UCMT Team field.
  4. Include appropriate update info in the body of the email.
  5. Reply to the email from UCMT to follow up and/or provide necessary information. You will need to override the email destination with each response.

1.6 Resolving and escalating integration issues

Integration issues should be reported directly to the most appropriate support team.

Symptom

Issue

Resolution

Escalation guidance

ASD tickets are 'stuck' in the UCMT Escalations queue.

After a downtime, the UCMT does not pick up any backlogged tickets when the service resumes.

OR

There is no originating queue to return the ASD ticket to because the ticket was somehow created within the UCMT Escalations queue. The integration now has logic to return a ticket to the originating queue without creating escalation if Tier 2 UCMT Escalation Required? = No

If newer tickets are escalating successfully but older tickets appear to be stuck in the escalations queue, the UCMT service may have temporarily gone down. Use the pull transfer to reclaim ownership of the ticket in your default queue and attempt to re-transfer. No further action is necessary if the ticket escalates successfully.

If the UCMT ticket doesn't generate because there is no originating queue on the ASD ticket, recreate the ticket in an appropriate working queue and reach out to your TA for assistance with closing the ticket.

If the UCMT ticket doesn't generate because Tier 2 UCMT Escalation Required? = No, update this field to YES, ensure the issue-specific details are complete, and re-escalate.

If none of the above resolve the issue, escalate the concern.

Use your v- to email ucmsupport@microsoft.com and CC your TA.

ASD Fields are not relayed to UCMT as expected.

The UCMT dev team may have updated their logic for which information they want to pull from ASD/CAP.

Tier 2 dictates the information they wish to pull from ASD/CAP. They will need to submit a change request/bug to investigate the current state and request changes.

TSEs should be directed to submit change requests to ucmsupport@microsoft.com, if they believe they have encountered a bug.

UCMT ticket details are not showing ASD as expected.

The UCMT dev team may have updated something that had an unexpected impact on the code used to push information from UCMT back to CAP.

OR

UCMT ticket details or Tier 2 responses are populating into CAP but not ASD.

Access CAP to confirm whether the information made it to CAP and if the issue is between UCMT & CAP or between CAP & ASD.

Verify with your TA/MAST tools team that the information should be relayed from UCMT to CAP.

Issues with data population between UCMT and CAP should be directed to ucmsupport@microsoft.com and cc your TA.

For issues with data population between CAP and ASD, submit a new ticket for ASD mission support.

The escalation did not route to the correct team.

Some UCMT teams have auto-routing logic implemented. Other teams have manual triagers. The issue could be a manual process or the auto-routing logic is not functioning as expected.

Review the UCMT Destinations above to confirm which team the ticket should have been directed to.

Change requests from Tier 1 should be directed to the continuous improvement forum.

1.7 Escalating to Global Support for non-customer issues

If there is a need to escalate to Global Support for an issue that is not a blocker to ticket resolution (for example, request for assistance with an internal issue such as UCM-A or UI page navigation concerns), an escalation may be submitted directly to Global Support via email to adsupport@microsoft.com. Be sure to include the Global Support General Escalation template.

Note: When responding to UCMT Tickets via email, you must ensure the UCMT ID is in the subject line within brackets (for example, "[UCM1234567890123]").

2. Escalating to CAP: BOS

To engage BOS for assistance, create a child ticket in ASD, complete the necessary issue specific details, and transfer the ticket to the appropriate BOS queue.

Sending attachments to BOS

You may simply create a child ticket and corresponding DTM workspace to host documents pertinent to the work request. Once the child ticket is transferred to BOS, the ticket owner will become owner of the DTM workspace as well. Just be sure to note in your ticket notes that the documents are in the DTM workspace.

What to expect

A manual triage process is required before CSS will receive the initial response from SANOps. This means initial response time can vary with each submission. Typical responses are within the hour during the working hours (9AM-6PM) for the region the request is submitted. If you are sending a request outside of these regional hours, it's unlikely you will see a response until the next business day begins.

SLA's
There are two primary request types, each with its own SLA. Keep in mind that SLA's do NOT include the total time required to complete a request. They should be used as general guidance for when to anticipate a follow up from SANOps. The exception to this is the offline refund process, which has varied SLA's depending on the stage of the request.

Transactions: 48-hour SLA

Inquiries: 24-hour SLA
  • Adjustments (includes C&R)
  • Agreement review & signature
  • Proforma/Invoice revisions (splits/consolidations)
  • Offline Refunds& Payouts
  • MIO signature & order entry

  • What is needed...?

Note: Once you get the autoresponder, always add the Cosmic ID to the subject line. This will ensure each response gets attached to your case.

4. How to send emails to ASD tickets

To properly attach an email communication to a specific ASD ticket, two things must be true:

  1. The email recipient must be an ASD/CAP queue (<queue>@css.one.microsoft.com).
  2. The full ticket number, including prefix and suffix, must be in the subject line ("SRX1234565789ID").

Most CSS and internal processes ensure these 2 points are true for ongoing conversations related to escalations. However, in rare cases, the CSS queue email may be looped off an internal email conversation. Internal teams who must respond to an open CSS ticket, but only have the ticket ID, may use ASDupdate@microsoft.com to send or request updates to a specific CSS ticket. If the ticket status is unknown, reach out to themets@microsoft.com to request assistance with updating the team or ticket.
