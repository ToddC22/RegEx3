This article includes step-by-step instructions of the processes required to execute Invoice Billing Setup service for our BOS advocates.

2. Definitions

Customer Setup Form (CSF) – A submission form used for advertisers who wish to be setup for invoice billing.

Invoiced Accounts – Advertisers can be setup for invoiced billing which requires a Billing ID, also known as an SAP ID, to be associated to their Microsoft Advertising accounts. This allows the customer to make payments against their SAP ID upon receipt of their monthly invoice.

Microsoft Risk Assessment – The Microsoft Credit Team are required to conduct certain risk assessments on Microsoft advertisers who wish to be billed by invoice. This helps to minimize the financial loss to the business.

Flip Requests – A common phrase used to refer to Advertisers who would like to change their existing prepay/post-pay Microsoft Advertising account to invoiced billing in order to keep their historical performance.

SAP –  it is a billing application used to keep track of amounts owed and paid by monthly invoice advertisers. SAP IDs are created for unique Company Name+Address combinations. All address updates must be conveyed to Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS) so that they can make the proper changes in SAP.

  • SAP IDs will not be issued without a completed Customer Setup Form.
  • An SAP ID may be classified as either Bill-To (with an associated credit line) or Sold-To (no credit line).
  • The same SAP ID is used for search and display advertising, and therefore may already exist for new search advertisers.
  • SAP IDs are created and may be entered into an existing customer shell.
  • One Bill-To SAP ID may be used in more than one CID, and
  • One CID may contain multiple Bill-To SAP IDs if there is any agency association.

Bill-To – Specifies who pays the bill. An advertiser who has a Bill-To (no hold) SAP has been approved for a credit line with Microsoft Advertising.

Sold-To –  Specifies who services are provided for. An advertiser with a Sold-To SAP cannot be the Bill-To on the invoice because he/she does not have credit approval. Sold-to setup is typically used for clients of agencies where the agency is the Bill-To. Sold-To SAPs may be converted to Bill-To with the submission of another CSF form to WOCS.

Bill to Agency – company service business dedicated to creating, planning and managing advertising (and sometimes other forms of promotion) for its customers. Sample services: Branding strategies, Overall marketing, Sales promotions, SEO, and SEM.

Bill to Advertiser – advertiser is the Web site owner or merchant who pays affiliates for sending traffic to their site to purchase or generate leads. Organization or company that places advertisements in order to target customers.

Subsidiary SAPs –  SAP IDs are created for unique Company Name+Address combinations, therefore, subsidiaries may have their own SAP ID even if the Bill-To address is the same as the parent. Subsidiary SAP IDs can be established if there is some public identifier that the subsidiary legally exists.

Agency SAPs –  If an agency would like to create an additional account under their shell for themselves and already has an existing No Hold SAP ID, the agency's existing No Hold SAP ID may be used as the Sold-To if the SAP address matches the account.

3. Best practices and policies

  1. Ensure that all documents with customer PII, MBI and HBI are shared using the DTM tool. Use the shared credential or yours @msncs.com to access the link.

Shared Credential Username: cssba_msft@msncs.com.

See KB 4056786 Data Transfer Management  for the password.

Note: Do not send any unencrypted files to Tier 1 support. All files should be encrypted using CSSMicrosoft Advertising password.

  1. For New SAP ID creation, ensure that the advertiser meets the minimum Microsoft Advertising age and spend history requirements. The company that requests invoice setup must have advertised as the Bill-to party on Microsoft Advertising for at least 12 months and must meet the minimum spend requirement in at least three of the 12 months.

For agencies, aggregate client spend can be used to determine minimum spend eligibility.

As of December 2019, there is a new minimum spend threshold for unmanaged advertisers. Advertisers must meet the following spend requirements in at least three out of the prior 12 months:

  • US and Canada: $5000 USD/CAD per month.
  • Other Mature Markets (Includes AU, FR, DE, JP, KP, KR, NZ, NL, SG, UK, WE Region): $3000 USD equivalent per month.
  • Emerging Markets (Includes BR, NL, and APAC/CEE/LATAM/MEA Regions): $2000 USD equivalent per month.
  • Exceptions to the 12-month spend history may be made if the customer meets the three months spend criteria and is willing to provide Financial Statements or the required Security Deposit.
  1. Ensure that the account currency is accepted for Monthly Invoice Billing. Use this link to validate the currency-market partnership permitted by the system.

Monthly invoicing is not supported for all currencies, and the currencies that are accepted differ depending on the billing center the advertiser is aligned to.

  1. Determine from the CSF preliminary setup form, whether the customer exists and must be upgraded or must be created. New customer shells will need to be created by the customer first, before an SAP ID can be applied; please advise your requestor to ensure the customer has a valid Microsoft Advertising account before submitting to WOCS.
  2. Check that there are no duplicate SAP IDs already existing by searching on the Business Name and/or VAT number in the SAP ID Report.
  1. Ensure that requestor has provided a fully completed the CSF Template and has requested invoice billing in a supported currency. If the advertiser's business location is in an APAC country, ensure that the requestor submitted two years of financial statements with their request as these documents are required. Do not proceed if the financial statements are not included if the advertiser is in an APAC country.
  2. Check to see if this is a Sole Proprietor setup request. Sole Proprietors are defined as being a business that is owned and run by one natural person, in which there is no legal distinction between the person and the business. You can check to see if a customer is a Sole Proprietor by looking up their business in the market-specific government site listed in the Online Validation Links file.

If the customer is a Sole Proprietor, their business will not show up in these sites or will not have a tax ID associated with hit. If this is the case, first suggest that they use a credit card instead of invoice billing. Let them know that there are additional requirements for setting up invoice billing for Sole Proprietors, and they will be required to:

  • Pay a deposit matching the amount that they are requesting for a credit line. The minimum is $1,000 USD. Microsoft will retain the deposit for the entire time they are advertising with Microsoft Advertising.
  1. Deposit Form (to be sent to customer).
  2. MSFT Banking Information and Remittance Instructions can be found on KB 3175877 Process: Microsoft Advertising: How to Pay.
  3. The customer's credit limit will not increase over time. It will be fixed based on their deposit amount. If they want to increase it, they will need to add more to their deposit. Microsoft will retain the deposit for the entire time they are advertising with Microsoft Advertising.

Note: If the customer becomes a registered business at some point, they will be able to apply for a standard credit line without a deposit, that will increase over time. This will be subject to a separate risk evaluation from WOC.

  1. Confirm the advertiser is not currently on Credit Hold (should be clarified to the requestor for resolution prior to continuing setup process and ticket closed as cancelled) and also ensure that the advertiser's 'Customer' information within Microsoft Advertising UI matches the information being submitted via CSF.
  1. Check that the information provided by the advertiser matches their legal registration and/or VAT information. You can do this by accessing the relevant link from the Online Validation Links file, and following the guidance contained in this Excel.
  1. Check that this information also matches the registered information displayed on the company's URL.
  1. Check if the Accounts Payable details are valid.
  2. Do not submit a setup request to WOCS until your requestor has been able to provide a customer's business justification/explanation for any discrepancies you have discovered.
  1. Static Website Requirements: Websites without any clickable URL pages (known as 'static' sites) will always require Credit Manager Approval from WOCS. To improve the chances of being approved, ensure they contain the following:
    • Nature of business
    • Name/address of business
    • Available products/services
    • Registered Company information (Including reference to legal trading name/address.)
    • Functional links that direct to the expected landing page
    • Privacy policy
    • Terms and conditions
    • Social media links (If relevant)
  2. If you are also being asked to create XID accounts, in addition to creating the SAP ID, check that the Requestor has given you the following information:
    • IO start date
    • IO end date
    • IO budget
    • Super admin name
    • Super admin email

You will need this information to continue with account creation, after the SAP has been provided by WOCS. If the requestor has not provided you with these details, proceed with SAP setup (if possible) and email the requestor back, to ask for this information. This will save any unnecessary delays later in the process.

Requested credit limit

Instructions:

  • Check if the monthly credit limit specified in the CSF is within the market default. If not, the customer will need to submit necessary requirements depending on the requested amount.
  • Mature markets default:
    • Standard Business Credit Limit- $60,000 (Includes AU, FR, DE, JP, KP, KR, NZ, NL, SG, UK, WE Region).
  • Emerging markets:
    • Standard business credit limit- $30,000 (Includes BR, NL, APAC Region, CEE Region, LATAM Region, MEA Region).
    • APAC advertisers are required to submit 2-year audited Financial Statements or Security Deposit.

Note: You may need to convert the credit limit amount to USD if the currency specified in the CSF is not in USD. No need to update/change the credit limit in the CSF once the amount has been converted. This is only to check if the requested credit limit is within the market default.

Any requested amount over the limit, but under $100,000 must provide the following in order to be considered:

  • The balance sheet, which summarizes what a firm owns and owes at a point in time.
  • The income statement, which reports on how much a firm earned during the period of analysis.
  • The statement of cash flows, which reports on the cash inflows and the outflows during the period of analysis.

If the advertiser is unable to provide financials for two complete fiscal years, a security deposit is required. Security deposits are calculated as three times the total requested credit limit and are held for up to six months, to be returned upon confirmation of a good payment history. Good Payment History is defined as having all non-disputed invoices paid on/before the due date for a minimum of six months. Payment can be submitted through check or wire transfer.

Any requested amount over the limit and over $100,000 must provide the following in order to be considered:

  • Standby letter of credit from their bank or parental guarantee (for subsidiaries). Note that for parental guarantees, WOCS will need to confirm that the parental organization is credit-worthy via internal risk checks before the invoice setup can be complete.

A standby letter or credit is defined as an "irrevocable letter of credit with their bank in Microsoft Corporation's name". This must be three times more than the requested credit limit.

5.1 Fraud risk review

New CSF credit line requests must now be submitted to Fraud for risk review. Only when the request is cleared by fraud should the CSF be submitted to WOCS for a credit check.

Steps to escalate to the fraud team:

1. For CSS/L1 request, create a new parent ticket that is queued under BOS AES queue. Note that the child ticket from L1/CSS is reserved for MSN Credit Escalation. Replicate the ASD Code from the original child ticket request from L1/ CSS advocate and the subject line should contain "Monthly Invoice" or “Monthly Invoice Application".

When creating a parent ticket for Fraud review purposes, please use "noemail@microsoft.com" as the customer email address.

Note: For AM request, there is no need to go through Fraud escalation as the account is already managed. Proceed to No. 8.

2. Next, go to Issue-Specific Details Required? section and select Yes. Fill out the issue-specific details section:

  • Issue Category: select ‘Fraud Escalation’
  • Issue Type: select ‘Risk Review for Monthly Invoice Setup’
  • Customer Identification Number (CID): input CID
  • CSF Attached: refer to DTM link
  • Specific what help is needed with this issue (if applicable): input

1. Risk review for monthly invoice setup.

2. DTM link wherein the CSF and additional customer documents can be found.

  • Minimum spend criteria met: Select the appropriate option.
  • Minimum age requirement met: Select the appropriate option.
  • Specific why this customer should be considered for an exception, if applicable: fill out if applicable.

Note: Some customers have exceptions, for example, not meeting the eligibility but is willing to provide a security deposit.

3. Then, fill out ucmt escalation section.

  • Tier 2 Escalation Required?: Select Yes
  • Tier 2 Receiving Team: Select FraudAds
  • Tier 2 Sub-Team: Select FraudAds
  • Customer Support Region: Select the appropriate option. This is the region where the business is located.
  • Delivery Market/Language: Select the appropriate option. Delivery Market/Language should match the language used on the ad copies and location being targeted by the customer. In case the customer is targeting multiple market/language, kindly add this to your documentation and select the most applicable.

4. Select Save and transfer the ticket to:

  • Transfer Type: SMB Escalations to T2 UCMT
  • Transfer Destination: UCMT Escalations

Note: Make sure to apply the transfer to the correct path.

5. Once transferred, refresh the queue and wait for resolution from Fraud Team. The resolution can be either:

  • Fraudulent/suspicious activity: Hello – Our team has researched this account and found suspicious activity. We have closed this account for fraud concerns or,
  • No fraud concerns: Hello – Our team has researched this account and found no suspicious activity. Process the following application via normal channels.

6. Only when the request is cleared by fraud should the CSF be submitted to WOCS for credit check. If the customer fails the Fraud risk review, inform your submitter by relaying the resolution and cancel the request.

The estimated SLA for Fraud reviews is two hours.

Frontline escalated tickets: If BOS advocates receive any notifications from the Fraud team with specific actions the customer must take in order to continue with the setup process, the advocate must pass on the notification details on to the Frontline advocate and then close the ticket.

7. Once the Fraud Team approved the request, BOS advocates will then continue to escalate to WOCS/MSN Credit team. See the WOCS UCMT Escalation Process section of this article. Note: Make sure to close the created Fraud ticket.

8. WOCS will provide confirmation that the request has been received and will proceed with the Accounts Payable contact verification through OneVet tool once the customer passed the credit checks. Inform your submitter once you received WOCS instruction that the AP contact verification was sent.

OneVet Authentication: As part of the Fraud review, the WOCS team use the OneVet tool. This tool sends out an email notification to the customer to verify who they are. The AP contact should expect to receive the verification email within 24 hours of escalating to WOCS. WOCS will confirm when the email is sent. OneVet is used globally for all unmanaged advertisers.

The verification email will arrive from the MSNCred@microsoft.com alias and will trigger in the language selected for the invoice setup in the CSF.

The verification link in the email is valid for five days. WOCS may close their ticket (UCMT) if the AP contact does not authenticate within 24 hours of receiving the email. However, our ticket should remain open for five business days as a leeway for the submitter to provide the AP contact confirmation from the customer.

Note: We are permitted to reopen the UCMT ticket with WOCS within five business day SLA by replying to WOCS last email communication.

In case the submitter cannot provide an update after five business days, BOS advocate can now close the ticket as cancelled and advise the submitter to redirect to WOCS once they received the customer’s confirmation. Provide the UCMT no. to the submitter for reference.

Any concerns regarding the delivery of the OneVet authentication email or completing the authentication steps should be directed to WOCS in the UCMT ticket.

Note: WOCS SLA is two business days. In case you have not received any response, email them for an update.

9. Once MSNCred has approved an SAP ID and entered it into the system, they will notify you by email and you will be able to proceed with account creation. Be aware that SAP IDs may take up to 24 hours to filter into the UI before you can proceed.

10. Once available, go to Accounts tab, edit the Customer details section. Next, change the Service Level to Premium. Then select Save. This will enable the Monthly Invoice option as one of the Payment setting. Note: Do not change the Service Level without WOCS confirmation.

11. On the Accounts tab, edit the Account details section. Next, edit the Primary contact based on the submitter’s preference. Then, on the Payment section, change the Payment setting to Monthly Invoice and select the preferred SAP ID.

Note: You cannot select a Super Admin with a pending invite status. Make sure that the user already accepted the invite unless the submitter to still use the old Super Admin/Primary Contact.

If a post-pay advertiser wants to switch to invoice billing the system will attempt a swipe for the amount owed before switching the account to invoice billing. If the swipe is unsuccessful, the system will not allow the switch. The error message will let the user know the payment failed.

Note: The customer should be aware that any pending balance will be refunded/charged once the account has been flipped. The BOS advocate should confirm this before flipping.

12. Once the SAP ID Payment Method is already setup, create the Insertion Order. This will prevent the account to turn gray.

If you encounter errors when trying to flip an account, ask WOCS for resolution. In case they cannot resolve this, escalate to AdSupport.

13. The setup is complete. Send your completion email containing the SAP ID and Insertion Order details.

Scenario 1: Agency is linked to a customer and will be paying the customer’s bills.

Setup:

The bill-to must be whoever is paying the bills to Microsoft.

9. Duplication or transfer of SAP ID requests

Sometimes customers wish to transfer or duplicate their SAP ID to a new customer shell. This is allowable with business justification. There is no standard business justification. These requests will be reviewed on a case by case basis.

Sample content of customer email confirmation:

  • Ownership of the customer shell (where the existing SAP ID is currently living).
  • Business justification on why they would like to transfer or share the SAP ID.
  • The customer agrees to share or transfer the existing SAP.

Send requests to MSNCred@microsoft.com including the business justification/customer reasoning.

Note: You should pay special attention to whether the SAP ID is Sold-To or Bill-To.

Account shells using a transferred/duplicated Sold-To SAP will not be able to make this the primary payment method for their accounts, however they will not automatically be associated to the same agency that was managing the Sold-To SAP in the original UI Shell either.

Therefore, you will need to link the new CID to the correct Bill-To Agency once the SAP ID has been transferred/duplicated.

In case the submitter requested to share/transfer the existing SAP ID to the new CID shell and update the AP contact as well, the BOS advocate should follow the steps in sending the transfer/share request to WOCS through UCMT escalation. Then, send a separate email to appropriate Collections Team (without any UCMT ticket number in the email subject line). See KB 3188687 AES: Process: Microsoft Advertising: Billing Information Update for details.

Note: In case the customer has access to the new customer shell, for example Super Admin of both CIDs, they can use the Link to an account option by taking over the billing. See Linking to an existing account help page for guidance.

13. Additional tips

Scenario 1: The advertiser has less than 6 months spend history with Microsoft Advertising.

Requirements:

In order to be set up on invoice billing the advertiser must submit a security deposit equivalent of three times the requested credit limit. This deposit may be refunded or applied towards any outstanding balance once good payment history is established. Good payment is history is defined as having:

  • No past due invoices within six months and
  • Monthly spent should not be lower than 30% of approved credit line for three consecutive months.

Scenario 2: The requested credit limit is above the market threshold (as outlined in the CSF).

Requirements:

The required documents vary by the amount requested (see specifics above in CSF chart).

Scenario 3: There is no credit bureau report (as confirmed by WOCS).

Requirements:

  1. The balance sheet, which summarizes what a firm owns and owes at a point in time.
  2. The income statement, which reports on how much a firm earned during the period of analysis
  3. The statement of cash flows, which reports on the cash inflows and the outflows during the period of analysis.

If the advertiser is unable to provide financials for two complete fiscal years, a security deposit is required. Security deposits are calculated as three times the total requested credit limit and are held for up to six months, to be returned upon confirmation of a good payment history. Good Payment History is defined as having all non-disputed invoices paid on/before the due date for a minimum of six months. Payment can be submitted through check or wire transfer.

Scenario 4: Required Accounts Payable contacts for US and FR customers - Sold To setup.

Accounts Payable contacts for Sold-To setup are required and should be valid users on the account. Otherwise, the AP contact must also be able to complete email authentication triggered by WOCS through a tool called OneVet.

AP contacts for Sold-To setup are only required for customers located in the US or FR for compliance with sequential liability regulations.

Scenario 5: Advertiser needs to confirm/justify their requested SAP ID type to match with their business offering.

The advertiser’s SAP ID type needs to match their business type in order to be set up for monthly invoice billing. The WOCS team uses the following definitions to determine SAP ID type:

  • Bill to Agency – company service business dedicated to creating, planning and managing advertising (and sometimes other forms of promotion) for its customers.

 Sample services: Branding strategies, Overall marketing, Sales promotions, SEO, SEM.

  • Bill to Advertiser – advertiser is the Web site owner or merchant who pays affiliates for sending traffic to their site to purchase or generate leads. Organization or company that places advertisements in order to target customers.

14. FAQ

Q: An FR Sold-To Customer wishes to receive a copy of their XID Invoice, under the Loi Sapin law.

A: BOS can provide a copy of the PDF invoice for the XID/s in question. Please obtain this from the UI on behalf of the customer. You (or the requestor) will need to ensure that the customer is verified before you can provide them with this information, using the normal Microsoft Advertising customer verification process.

Q: I need to request an agency SAP; however, the customer type is ‘Advertiser’. What should I do?

A: See scenario 5 table in this article for guidance. Also, we should no longer check for the customer type in Microsoft Advertising UI due to the multi-linking improvements. One of the upgrades included in these improvements is that there is no need to “become an agency” in order to manage accounts. This setting is removed, and any account can be linked to any other account, including accounts created by an agency or vice versa.

Q: What should I do if an agency/client is requesting a parallel copy of the invoice received by their agency/client? (For example, a client contacts you to ask for the most recent invoice their agency received on their behalf.)

A: The way this should be handled will depend on who the Bill To and Sold To parties are in the agency/client relationship:

  • A non-Bill To Agency asks for a copy of the invoice for their Advertiser. The Advertiser is listed as Bill To.
    • In this scenario, the Advertiser is the ‘Microsoft Client’ and not the Agency.
    • The Agency must ask the Advertiser to provide them with a copy of the invoice.
  • A Sold To Advertiser asks for a copy of the invoice, and their agency is listed as the Bill To.
    • In this scenario, the Advertiser is still entitled to ask for a copy of the invoice as a Microsoft Client and we can provide this to them.
  • A Bill To Agency/Advertiser asks for a copy of the invoice
    • In this scenario they are already the Bill To and can retrieve this themselves as a PDF via the Billing tab.

Q: Who may share the SAP?

A: There are two scenarios where an SAP ID can be added under a different CID (customer shell):

  • Agency set up – where Sold To SAP ID will be flipped under Bill To (Agency) customer shell (CID)
  • Advertiser set up – where Bill To SAP ID is flipped to different CIDs. The customer shell (CID) must be owned/related to Bill To SAP ID.

Q: Does the customer type of the new account need to be similar with the account where the SAP ID is currently living? (For example, direct Advertiser to Direct Advertiser or Agency to Agency).

A: Historically, the requirement was that the SAP ID should be added to the CID with the same customer type, for example, Advertiser SAP ID to Advertiser CID. There is now a new feature in Microsoft Advertising where any Bill-to can do multi-linking, so MSNCred have amended their process to allow for the addition of SAP IDs to CID of different customer types.

Q: Does the customer information (legal company name and address) of the new account need to be the same with the existing SAP ID?
A: Yes.

Q: Do we follow a strict rule that there should be one SAP per company or are there any exemptions?

A: As per process there should be only one SAP ID per customer however there are exemptions, for example, for resellers. All exemptions should be approved by an MSNCred Credit Manager.

Q: Can we assist a request to create additional Monthly Invoice Account?

A: Yes. BOS should check the following required fields to continue with the request.

Required Fields for Monthly Invoice Account creation. 

Account settings:

  • Account name
  • Primary contact
  • Billing language
  • Account time zone
  • Auto-tagging

Payment:

  • Currency
  • SAP ID
  • Bill To
  • Sold to

Insertion order details:

  • Start date
  • End date
  • Budget

Note: Request to create a customer shell is now out of scope.
