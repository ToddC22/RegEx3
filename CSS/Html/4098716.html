Nonprofit Offers in CSP-

As technology advancements accelerate, we want to ensure that nonprofits, and those they serve, are not left behind. In support of this objective, we are launching NEW nonprofit offers in CSP which can be sold from early April. Cloud Solution Providers do not need to qualify to sell to charity organizations, however the organization needs to qualify for nonprofit pricing. Nonprofit qualification criteria can be found here.

Nonprofit offers include: Office 365, Power BI, EMS, Dynamics 365, M365, and Windows.

Additional information around Solutions, Products, Eligibility are located here.

Partner center | Offer Management | Offers & Programs | Charity qualification for customer tenant

Customer has an existing tenant that they want to apply non-profit qualification to

Customers with existing tenants that are actively being used will need to complete the following steps:

  1. Complete Nonprofit sign up here: https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/#/register.
  • The customer will create a new Microsoft account as part of sign up.
  1. Wait for eligibility validation process to complete (process can take up to 20 business days)
  1. Receive email notification with eligibility validation result.
  1. If eligible, go to Contact Us and submit request for updating existing tenant for nonprofit.
  • Customer will need to provide the new tenant created during nonprofit sign up and the existing commercial tenant to update to nonprofit.
  1. Microsoft will complete the update on the backend and Contact Us support team will inform customer that process is complete.
  1. Customer will need to inform the partner that the process is complete.
  1. Customer can then work with their partner for help migrating commercial subscriptions (aka commercial SKUs) to nonprofit subscriptions (aka nonprofit SKUs).

Partner center | Offer Management | Offers & Programs | Applying charity qualification to existing customer tenant
Customer tenant does not have Non-profit qualification, but they have previously purchased Education or Govt offers. Customer tenants cannot co-host Non-profit and EDU or GOV SKU's. This is consistent with webdirect. If they do not have active Education/Govt subscriptions, the partner can remove the Education qualification from the customer account (through partner center) and then contact tsipsupp@microsoft.com for assistance in getting the Non-profit qualification applied. Q: A Partner wants to provision over 2,000 Office 365 E1 for Charity via CSP but cannot due to a seat count limit. Partner mentions that there isn’t such a limit for the same offer via web-direct and wants to know why this difference.

A: In November Microsoft implemented a 2000 seat cap for our donation E1 offer across all channels. Customers who acquired the old unlimited E1 offer prior to November are still using this offer, but no new customers can access it via web-direct anymore. Over time we plan on moving these customers over to the capped offer. The new web direct offer is listed here: https://commoffertool.catalog.cp.microsoft.com/Prod/Office365/offer/details/83dca3b9-3d6c-4547-8c5f-ba1f69b130f8.

Q: Can you please clarify what does "Volunteer" exactly means?

A: In the context of nonprofit offers, “Volunteer” refers to volunteers of eligible nonprofit organizations (as opposed to FTEs who are entitled to Nonprofit Staff Pricing).

Q: Why there are "Volunteer (Nonprofit Staff Pricing)" and "(Nonprofit Staff Pricing)" in "Office 365 Enterprise E1" while in F1 there is only "Office 365 F1 - Volunteer (Nonprofit Staff Pricing)?

A: For eligible nonprofit organizations we provide 2000 free E1 seats for eligible staff. We also make E1 available at a 75% discount to nonprofit volunteers. For the F1 SKU we have decided not to make it available for free – instead offering it at a 75% discount.

Q: Why the "Office 365 Enterprise E1 (Nonprofit Staff Pricing) : 83dca3b9-3d6c-4547-8c5f-ba1f69b130f8" prices shows as 0 while the ones with "Volunteer" has their own prices even though their "License Agreement Type" are all charity?

A: See above.
