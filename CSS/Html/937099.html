Procedure: TR: Public Sector Support for Premier customers

[Asset 4489629]

Who should use this article

  • Commercial CS Post-Sales Advocates (aka Tech Routers or TRs)
  • PubSec TR Advocates

What is this article about

This process helps route Public Sector (PubSec) Premier Customers who are part of either

  1. U.S. National Support Program (USNAT)
  2. Microsoft Azure Government Support Program (Azure Govt)
  3. Microsoft Dynamics Cloud Government Support Program (Dynamics Govt)
  4. O365 Government Cloud (O365 GCC High and DoD)
  5. Premier Support


PubSec customers should dial a Designated Support Number (DSN) routing directly to PubSec CS advocates who handle these issues. If a PubSec customer accidentally dials one of the standard support lines, TR advocates identify them as PubSec customers and route them to the dedicated PubSec CS advocate for further assistance.
 

Customers falling under this process should always be supported in Service Desk.  Never create incidents in Rave for these customers.

Technical Routers (TRs)

PubSec customers should dial in on a dedicated DSN that routes directly to PubSec CS advocates. However, if a PubSec customer dials in on a normal support line, you can identify them by the support account line item description related to a PubSec offering or PubSec related FLASH.

Once you have identified a PubSec customer, discontinue normal call handling and follow the steps below instead:

1. Do not ask the customer for the technical details of their issue.

2. Use the scripting below to set the customer's expectations about how their PubSec support works and the steps you will be taking.

"Your support account provides assistance in certain scenarios from a dedicated customer service team for your support account who can determine your support options and route your case to the appropriate group.

I will transfer you to that customer service team, but if you'd like, I can also provide their direct phone number for future reference."

Tip: The phone number is 800-936-3200.

3. Warm transfer the customer to a PubSec CS advocate.

Microsoft offers the Public Sector (PubSec) offering to give Premier customers the option of having their first technical contact be a U.S. citizen. This offering allows for companies with sensitive operations to feel secure in their dealings with Microsoft. It is essential that all Premier customers with the PubSec offering are routed to a national citizen for all qualifying Service Requests, as Microsoft is legally required to meet this contractual obligation.

Microsoft Government Cloud (Azure, Dynamics and Office) support offers the option of having the first point of technical contact be a US Person (US Citizen or Green Card)

Our contract does not specify that Customer Service advocates who assist PubSec customers must be national citizens.

PubSec Support Hours & Callback Expectations

  • PubSec support follows the normal commercial business hours of 6 a.m. – 6 p.m. Monday – Friday PST.
  • Sev A issues are supported via a 1-hour callback 24x7
  • Sev B issues are supported via a 2-hour callback, with the customer’s option of support 24x7 or during business hours only.
    NOTE:  The default option for newly created cases in North America is that support for SEV B issues will be 24x7. If the customer opts to receive support during business hours, you must open the case and uncheck the 24x7 support box.
  • Sev C issues are supported via 4-hour callback during business hours only.

Note

The support hours and callback expectations apply to Premier customers with PubSec support.  While the O365 Government Cloud offering includes a Professional level support option, customers utilizing it will not have an Access ID for PubSec support and must submit all issues via the information in their Admin Portal.  Therefore, advocates will never create support cases for these customers nor set expectations for callback.

Continuing SR Access Restrictions

Several PubSec Offerings use Access Restrictions, which prevents widespread visibility to potentially sensitive case information:
  • USNAT Service Requests created using an “Included” support line item (but not an “Excluded” line item)
  • Azure Government
  • Dynamics Cloud Government
  • O365 Government Cloud

New technical request call handling is not impacted, but for continuing cases:
  1. If the customer’s case IS restricted, some of the information you typically have access to will be unavailable in Service Desk. Use the steps below to assist customers:
    1. Leverage the information that is available.
    2. If necessary to accurately determine severity and correct routing, ask probing questions about the customer’s issue. If the customer is curious why you are asking these questions, use the following suggested scripting:

      “The security on your Premier support account restricts access of your issue’s technical details to your Technical Account Manager and assigned Support Professional. I apologize for the inconvenience, but because I don’t have visibility to the potentially sensitive details of your case, I need to gather just a bit of additional information to ensure I accurately process your request.”
    3. For additional information on continuing Restricted Access cases, see:
      4518866 Procedure: TR: Restricted Access Case Call Handling in Service Desk


Setting Customer Expectations Regarding PubSec Support Offerings

Because of the sensitive nature of all PubSec issues, advocates must be completely clear about what type of support they should expect; verified US citizen, Microsoft screened, etc.

Customers may not be guaranteed a Citizen or screened SR owner for several reasons, including:
  • The customer’s product/issue do not qualify for a PubSec Support Offering, either because they are calling about an excluded product or because you are unable to entitle the case with a PubSec line item.
  • The customer is using a premier account or “Excluded” line item.

Always set PubSec customers’ expectations correctly, based on the line selected during the case creation process. If the customer requests an alternate type of support entitlement and routing, explicitly document this in the SR notes.
