2. Definitions

  • Negative Keywords - are specific words or phrases that help prevent the ad from showing to customers who are unlikely to click the ad.
  • Microsoft Advertising Intelligence - is a powerful keyword planning tool that allows you to build and expand on your keyword lists using the familiar Microsoft Office Excel interface. It enables you to easily research keywords and gauge their performance on the Microsoft Search Network, and then apply those insights to improve your keyword selection and campaign performance.
  • Negative Expansion using Microsoft Advertising Intelligence - A process of finding irrelevant keywords using Microsoft Advertising Intelligence. These negatives are derived from the advertiser’s keywords using reports such as Keyword Expansions, Searches With Your Keywords, Associated Keywords and Related Searches that may trigger the advertiser’s ads to show. The data is based on historical searches from Microsoft Advertising.
  • Negative expansion using Search Query Report (SQR) - A process of finding irrelevant keywords from SQR that converted unwanted clicks. The negatives are then converted to Exact match type as this will prevent the ads to show again once added.
  • Negative keyword conflicts - Negative keywords can cause conflicts when advertisers bid on a keyword, but that phrase (or a word in it) is also in the negative keyword list. This conflict will prevent the display of the ad.

Example:

Keyword: cheap car insurance

Negative keyword: cheap

Result: ad doesn’t display

  • Negative keyword unionization - Previously, if a user established negative keywords at both the ad group and campaign levels, Microsoft Advertising would use only the ad group level negative keywords to filter traffic. Now, it applies negative keywords from all levels throughout the account hierarchy to filter traffic. If the user established the keyword black socks at the ad group level and gym socks at the campaign level, Microsoft Advertising would use both terms to filter traffic. This change helps ensure Microsoft Advertising delivers the ad in response to only the most relevant queries.
  • Negative keyword Lists and shared libraries - This functionality let advertisers create lists of negative keywords and apply them simultaneously to as many campaigns as they want. These lists are in one place in Microsoft Advertising online, where advertisers can easily add or remove keywords in bulk, and link or unlink them from campaigns.

Advertisers can have up to 20 lists per account, with a max of 5,000 negative keywords per list. All lists are created at the account level, and can only be applied to campaigns, not ad groups. See Entity Hierarchy and Limits for details.

3. Best practices and policies

3.1 Product details

  • You can add negative keywords to:
  • Campaigns: Campaign-level keywords apply to all keywords in a campaign. Be aware that negative keywords assigned at the campaign level will also be applied at the ad group level (in addition to any ad group-specific negative keywords).
  • Ad groups: These negative keywords will be applied to the ad group in addition to any negative keywords set up at the campaign level.
  • A negative keyword list: With a shared negative keyword list, you can apply entire lists of negative keywords to multiple campaigns and make changes across campaigns by editing a single list.
  • You can specify a maximum of 20,000 negative keywords at both the campaign and ad group level.
  • Negative keywords are applied as phrase or exact matches.
  • Phrase match: For a negative to block an ad from serving on a query, the phrase negative keyword is found in the search query in the same order, without any words in between. Additional words can be added to the beginning or the end of the search query.
  • Exact match: For a negative to block an ad from serving on a query, the exact negative keyword must match the search query. No additional words can be added.
  • The following characters cannot be added to negative keywords by design: ! , @ < > | and *
  • An advertiser can assign negative keywords to an individual campaign or ad group. If the specify negative keywords are added at both campaign and ad group levels, both sets of negative keywords will be in effect for the corresponding ad group.
  • Negative keywords can also be added and deleted from a shared negative keyword list. The negative keyword list can be shared or associated with multiple campaigns. The negative keyword lists associated with a campaign are also effectively applied to all ad groups in the campaign.
  • For example, all negative keywords shown in the diagram below are applied to the Fall Sale ad group either directly or through inheritance from the campaign level associations.
  • For other ad groups (not pictured) within the Shoe Sales campaign, the negative keywords sandals, thongs, flip flops, slides would not be in effect, unless those ad group also have the same negative keywords at ad group level.

3.2 Policies

When creating negative keywords, keep the following rules in mind:

  • Negative keyword lists, including commas, are limited to 1,024 characters at the keyword level.
  • Users can add thousands more negative keywords at the ad group level and campaign level.
  • Each negative keyword can contain a maximum of 100 characters.
  • Commas must separate negative keywords or negative keyword phrases.
  • In Microsoft Advertising, do not place a minus sign (-) before a negative keyword.

The following special characters are ignored in negative keywords:

  • commas
  • newline (\n) in the middle of text
  • tab (\t) in the middle
  • carriage return (\r) in the middle

The following section provides instructions on how to generate keywords using Microsoft Advertising Intelligence and choose negative keywords after gathering all requirements. 

Generating keyword suggestions

  1. Load the account in Microsoft Advertising Intelligence. Select the correct Country/Region and Language.

  3. Run the following More Research functions:
  • Keyword Expansions
  • Searches With Your keyword
  • Associated Keywords
  • Related Searches

Use the settings below for each of the functionality:

  • Maximum number of keywords: 100
  • New Worksheet

[Asset 4505184]

These will be generated by default on separate Excel tabs.

  1. Create a new Excel tab, and name it New Keywords.
  • Name Column A ‘Seed Keyword’.
  • Name Column B ‘New Keyword’.
  • Copy and paste the output (both Columns A and B) of each suggestion tab into the New Keywords tab. Do not copy the headers, just the keyword data itself.

[Asset 4505185]

  1. Trim the keywords in the New Keyword column to remove extra spaces from the newly generated keywords (if there are any) before de-duping and removing conflicts. A sample formula that you may type in cell C2 for the example above would be:

=trim(B2)

Note: There are many ways of using formulas. This step suggests one way of trimming the keywords. You may also opt to incorporate the Trim function into the ‘Vlookup’ formula when de-duping. Depending on your objective, you may find other ways of accomplishing the exact same thing.

  1. Copy and paste special values back into the New Keyword column.

Removing conflicts

1. Before removing conflicts, de-dupe the set of new keywords against itself.

  • Highlight both columns in the New Keywords tab.
  • Go to Data > Remove Duplicates.
  • Uncheck Seed Keyword in the pop-up box.
  • Select OK.

[Asset 4505187]

2. De-dupe against the existing keyword list to avoid negative conflicts.

  • On the third column of the New Keywords tab, perform a VLOOKUP comparing the New Keyword column values against the Trimmed column values in the Original keywords tab. The formula that you would type in cell C2 of the example above would be:

=vlookup(B2,’Original Keywords’!A:A,1,0)

  • lookup_value: New Keyword column value in New Keywords tab
  • table_array: Trimmed column in Original Keywords tab
  • col_index_num: column number of Trimmed columns in Original Keywords tab
  • range_lookup: 0

Notes:

  • Aside from typing numbers or texts in a formula, we can also use cell references by directly selecting the cells of the values we want to input in the formula.
  • How the arguments are written in a formula vary depending on the sequence of your columns and how you label your reference sheets. Make sure to familiarize yourself with the function’s syntax and the arguments you need to input.

  • Keyword - Indicates the negative keywords
  • Match type - Indicates the corresponding match type of the negative keyword
    • Exact
    • Phrase

9. Additional tips

Choosing negative keywords play a vital role in helping advertisers prevent their ads from being displayed when a search query or other input contains their keywords but is irrelevant to their landing page content. Below are some further reminders to help you choose quality negatives.

  • Avoid duplicate keywords – Like in keyword expansion, variations you should not include are those negative keywords that only differ in capitalization (for example, Bikes and bikes), and negative keywords that differ by simply adding punctuations (for example, bike-repair and bike repair). These count as duplicates and Microsoft Advertising will automatically mark them upon upload.

[Asset 4505384]​​​​​​​

Note: Negative keywords are normalized before comparing them to the raw query. That is, any symbols are generally removed from both keywords and queries before determining whether a keyword is a match to a query and letter case is ignored. Accented characters are preserved. So, a negative keyword such as Bing@ would become Bing and block the query Bing.​

Outside of normalization, only the precise negative keyword will be filtered out. Variants (such as plurals), synonyms and common misspellings of the negative keyword are not filtered out. To have variants, synonyms, and common misspellings filtered out as well, you will need to enter each one as a separate negative keyword.

  • Avoid invalid keywords – Keywords you should not suggest as negatives include keywords in foreign languages that are outside your scope of support as we are not expected to decipher these keywords using translators which may not always provide accurate results. Another keyword that is considered invalid and you should avoid suggesting is the keyword *email address removed* as this only means that email information is being automatically concealed by the system and is not advisable to suggest as a negative.

10. FAQ

Q: If the request is to suggest Ad Group-level negative keywords, can I suggest negative keywords that are irrelevant to the specified Ad group but relevant to the other Ad groups?
A: Yes, so that the Ads from specified Ad group will not be displayed wherein the search query is not relevant. However, Ads for another Ad group will display since the search query is more relevant.

Example:
Negative Keyword: Dancing Shoes

Ad group 1: Shoes

Ad group 2: Sports Shoes

In this example, the Negative Keyword should be applied in Ad group 2 since “Dancing Shoes” is not relevant to Sports Shoes. We can’t add Dancing Shoes to Ad group 1 negative keyword since it is relevant to Shoes.

Q: In relation to the question above. What if the advertiser’s website is not offering Dancing Shoes?
A: We can suggest Dancing Shoes negative keyword to both Ad group 1 and Ad group 2.
