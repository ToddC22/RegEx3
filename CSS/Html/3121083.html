What is this article about? 

This article provides Team Managers, Team Leads, Subject Matter Experts procedures to facilitate the transfer of case ownership (TOCO) between sites, advocates, and suppliers in event of a case being identified as one that needs to be transferred to another advocate/site per KB 2988026 Guideline: Service Request (SR) Creation and Case Ownership.

Note: Due to local laws in India, advocates located in India cannot perform any monetary transaction with the customer, including charging the customer, refunds, turning on/off Recurring Billing. Do not deny these transfer requests from advocates located in India.

1. Who should own the case?

The last person who speaks to the customer on a particular issue should own the case with the single exception:

  • When a customer is contacting to confirm their callback appointment, do not take ownership of the case. See KB 3099069 Procedure: Call Handling: Holds, disconnects, callbacks and ghost calls.

2.2 Submitting a TOCO request 

As a Team Manager, Team Lead, Subject Matter Expert, if you have identified a case which should have been transferred to another advocate/site per KB 2988026 Guideline: Service Request (SR) Creation and Case Ownership, TMs/SMEs should request TOCO within one business day of the case creation/modification to notify the correct site to take ownership of the case.

  • If the request is towards different supplier, submit a ticket via TFS.
  • If the request is towards same supplier, use internal email or submit a ticket via TFS.

When submitting a TOCO request, combine all cases related to same issue for the customer. For example:

  • Advocate A creates case number 1 for an issue for a customer
  • Advocate B creates case number 2 for the same issue for that same customer and performs a transfer
  • Advocate C is the last to talk to the customer, and creates case number 3 for the same issue
The last advocate who interacted customer needs to be the owner of all three of these cases, which in the above scenario would be Advocate C.

 2.3 Responding to a TOCO request

When you receive a TOCO request, you must respond within one business day. You can find these requests within TFS shared queries, under TOCO, and look for your supplier’s query.

To respond to a TOCO request, you need to ensure an advocate takes ownership or documents why a transfer of ownership to that supplier is inappropriate (see section 2.4.2 Disputing a TOCO request).

To accept a TOCO request:

  1. Change the State field to Resolved
  2. Change the Resolved Reason to Yanked
Any changes or discussion regarding the TOCO should be entered into the History field. Refresh TOCO queries every four hours to ensure timely resolution for these cases.

 ﻿2.4 Disputing a TOCO request

To respond, you should either accept or dispute. Any changes or discussion regarding the TOCO should be entered into the “History” field.

2.4.1 To accept a TOCO request via TFS

  1. Change the State field to Resolved
  2. Change the Resolved Reason to Yanked

2.4.2 Disputing a TOCO request

If you receive a TOCO request that you feel is in error, you may dispute it. Disputes must be submitted within one business day.

Valid reasons for dispute:

  • An advocate at a call center site outside of yours interacted with the customer last, but your call center site currently has the case.

Case reassignment DOES NOT involve groups listed under Exceptions of this article. The following are not acceptable reasons for dispute:

  • Any reason OTHER THAN correcting assignment of the case to the advocate that last interacted with the customer.
  • Case reassignment involves groups listed under Exceptions of this article.

To dispute a TOCO request via TFS:

  1. Change the State field to Resolved
  2. Change the Resolved Reason to Disputed
  3. Email your Microsoft SDM (or Technical Advisor, if this is the procedure for your line of business (LOB) and region) and include:
    • Original TOCO Justification provided in TFS TOCO item
    • Valid reason and explanation for dispute
    • Link to the TOCO TFS
SDM (or Technical Advisor, if this is the procedure for your LOB and region) will decide if the TOCO is valid and ask the correct site to take ownership and yank/resolve the case.

2.5 Resolving a disputed TOCO request

The site who needs to take or keep ownership of the case as decided by Microsoft SDM (or Technical Advisor, if this is the procedure for your LOB and region) will resolve the case and update the TFS item:

To resolve a TOCO request that was rejected via TFS:

  1. Change the State field to Resolved
  2. Change the Resolved Reason to Rejected

To resolve a TOCO request that was approved via TFS:

  1. Change the State field to Resolved
  2. Change the Resolved Reason to Yanked
