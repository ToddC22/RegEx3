ASMS – Local Language Cases in Delivery Partner Queues

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This TSG provides guidance for Delivery Partners when they receive local language cases from the following queues:

  • MSaaS ASMS Billing and PI TKE

  • MSaaS ASMS GQA TKE

  • MSaaS ASMS Billing and PI MT

  • MSaaS ASMS GQA MT

  • MSaaS ASMS Accounts and Portal MT

ASMS Local Language (LL) teams send cases to the above queues to assist with surge volume. Local language cases recieved in this manner should be supported by Delivery Partners through the various translation options available in this TSG.

Policy

  • The LL teams will only route cases to the Delivery Partner queues if timely initial responses and/or service-level agreement (SLA) fulfillments are at risk .

  • All case types are eligible for transfer to the Delivery Partner queues except for the following:

    • Premier cases

    • Pro-Direct cases

    • Partner cases

    • Severity A (Sev A) cases

    • Cases where the initial response (IR) has been met

    • Sovereign Cloud cases (for example, Black Forest)

      Note: If a case of one of these types is mistakenly transferred to a Delivery Partner queue, send it back to the appropriate LL queue.

  • All local language case verbatims must be translated using the Microsoft Translator app .

  • If you are supporting the customer over the phone and need interpretation services, see TSG 2658542.

    • Note: The exception to this is if you are delivering strikes for unresponsive customers. See the Supporting LL Customers section for more details.

  • The LL team should ensure that there is more than 1 hour available on the IR SLA clock for transferred cases.

    • This ensures that the Delivery Partners have sufficient time available to achieve an initial response in the SLA time frame.

  • The LL teams must always prequalify a case before routing it to a Delivery Partner queue.

    • This means that the actual case content needs to be verified (by opening the case and going through its description) by the LL engineer.

    • Some LL customers might request local language only support for their case during case creation. On such occasions, the case should not be routed to a Delivery Partner queue.

Support Scenarios

Supporting LL Customers

  1. Contact the customer using their preferred communication method and help them with their issue.

    • If the customer requests local language support, or is completely unable to understand English, skip to step 6b.

  2. If the customer is unreachable via phone, achieve IR by using the appropriate local language email template found in TSG 2976198.

    • If the resolution to the issue is immediately apparent, perform a First Quality Response (FQR) .

  3. If the case is Sev A and your shift is ending, hand over the case to the next shift.

  4. If the case is Sev A and the customer does not respond within one hour from the first point of contact by phone, adjust the case’s severity to Severity B and continue to work the case.

  5. Await a response from the customer.

    • If the customer is unresponsive, follow the normal 1-Strike or 3-Strike process as per TSG 2589957 and eventually close the case.

    • Note that for the phone callbacks for strikes, you should send a collaboration task to the relevant LL queue to perform the callback.

      • After the task is completed, and after the LL team has confirmed that the customer did not reply to their call, you can continue the strike process.

    • Route the case back to the relevant local language queue if the customer explicitly requests support in their native language.

      • Do not volunteer this as an option.

  6. If the customer responds, run their response through the translator if necessary. There are two possible paths:

    1. The customer is content to work in English or with a translation service.
      In this scenario, continue working the support request as normal.

      • Leverage the Translator app and interpreter services called out in the Policy section to assist in supporting the customer.

    2. The customer explicitly requests local language support.
      In this scenario, do the following:

      1. Inform the customer via email that you will transfer their case to a local language team.

      2. Also inform the customer that the LL team might not be immediately available. Volunteer that translation services are available if the customer needs immediate support.

      3. Document all communications with the customer and the reason for the transfer in the case notes.

      4. Transfer the case to the appropriate LL queue.
