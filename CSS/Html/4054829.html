1. Required information for lost in transit investigations

The following information is required for any escalation related to lost in transit scenarios.

  • Inbound to service center:
    • The customer’s name as it appears on the order.
    • The customer’s physical mailing address (no PO Boxes).
    • The customer’s email address.
      • For Surface consumer or Xbox, the customer's Microsoft account email address.
      • For Surface commercial, the customer's contact email address.
    • The customer’s contact phone number.
    • Service Order service request number.
    • The ticketed device serial number (for device or Type cover orders), the type of accessory (for accessory fulfillment orders).
    • The courier used.
    • The inbound tracking number.
  • Outbound to the customer:
    • The customer’s name as it appears on the order.
    • The customer’s physical mailing address (no PO Boxes).
    • The customer’s email address.
      • For Surface consumer or Xbox, the customer's Microsoft account email address.
      • For Surface commercial, the customer's contact email address.
    • The customer’s contact phone number.
    • Service Order request number.
    • The replacement device serial number (for device or Type cover orders), type of accessory (for accessory fulfillment orders).
    • The courier used.
    • The outbound tracking number.

2. Lost in transit – Inbound to service center

  1. Confirm the status of the package using the courier's tracking website.
    • If the tracking website states the package is still in transit and the order is still within estimated turnaround time, advise the customer of the estimated arrival date and to keep an eye out for an automated email indicating the service center has received the package.
    • If the tracking website shows the package is still in transit and the order is outside the estimated turnaround time.
  2. Gather all the required information outlined in section 1 and record this in the case notes.
  3. Follow the steps outlined below based on the specific region the customer resides in and the courier used to ship the package to the service center.

2.1 AOC

Fed Ex

Follow the steps outlined in KB 4054957 Procedure: FedEx OneCall to file a package trace on the inbound tracking number.

  • If FedEx indicates the package is lost and provides a claim number, permission to create an LSKU (Device or Type Cover) is granted without the need to consult Tier 3 or GRAT.
  • If FedEx states they are unable to determine what happened with the package or the Package Trace has been open for longer than 10 business days with no update or resolution from the courier, see section 5, Escalations for guidance.
    • Prior to escalation, collect proof of purchase for the device/accessory from the customer. Validate the proof of purchase (PoP) provided by the customer using the directions found in section 3 of KB 2821662 Procedure: Handling requests for warranty adjustments.

UPS (non-self-ship scenarios)

Follow the steps outlined in KB 4054958 Procedure: Devices: UPS trace and intercept handling to file a package trace on the inbound tracking number.

  • If UPS indicates the package is lost and provides a claim number, permission to create an LSKU (Device or Type Cover) is granted without the need to consult Tier 3 or GRAT.
  • If UPS state they are unable to determine what happened with the package or the Trace/Intercept request has been open for longer than 10 business days with no update or resolution from the courier, refer to section 5, Escalations for guidance.
    • Prior to escalation, collect proof of purchase for the device/accessory from the customer. Validate the proof of purchase (PoP) provided by the customer using the directions found in section 3 of KB 2821662.

USPS (Self-Ship scenarios only)

Advise the customer that Microsoft cannot contact USPS on their behalf, they must work with the courier service to locate the package.

Canada Post (Self-Ship scenarios only)

Advise the customer that Microsoft cannot contact CP on their behalf, they must work with the courier service to locate the package.

3. Lost in transit – Outbound from service center

  1. Confirm the status of the package using the courier's tracking website.
  • If the tracking website states the package is still in transit and the order is still within estimated turnaround time, advise the customer of the estimated delivery date.
    • Look up the turnaround time in the appropriate country article under KB 4514977 Procedure: Surface: Services Exchange and Repair Offers Overview, section 2.
    • If the tracking website states that the package is in transit to the service or fulfillment center it was originally shipped from.
      1. Advise the customer we will create a new order once tracking confirms the package was returned.
      2. After confirmation is received, create the order following the steps in the Return to sender scenarios section.
  • If the tracking website shows the package is still in transit and any of the following are true continue to follow the next steps.
    • The order is outside the estimated turnaround time.
    • The package was delivered, but the address on the order was incorrect.
    • The package shows delivered, the address on the order is correct, and the customer claims the package wasn’t received.
  1. Gather all the required information outlined in section 1 and record in the case notes.
  2. Follow the steps outlined below based on the specific region the customer resides in and the courier used to ship the package to the service center.

USPS (Accessory fulfillment orders)

Advise the customer Microsoft cannot contact USPS on their behalf, they must work with the courier service to locate the package.

  • If the customer has reached out to USPS and USPS has advised them the package is lost, ensure the case notes indicate USPS advised the customer the package was lost and create a Send Immediately Order (SEN FO) to send the customer a replacement accessory.

Note: All accessory fulfillment orders (FOs) in the United States are shipped from the regional fulfillment center via FedEx SmartPost. Due to federal privacy laws only a customer (recipient of the package) may contact their local USPS office to request additional information or action. Representatives from Microsoft or FedEx OneCall cannot assist with packages sent using SmartPost. Additionally, USPS is not able to redirect or trace these packages. They are considered delivered once they reach the local post office (not the customer).

4. Lost in transit - Returned to service center/fulfillment center

4.1 Standard exchange orders, accessory exchange orders, accessory send immediately orders

  1. Ensure the previous order is closed.
  2. Confirm the delivery address with the customer.
  3. Create an LSKU order to send the customer a replacement device. Ensure the case notes indicate that the original replacement device was returned to the service center and an LSKU order was needed to send the customer a replacement.

Return to top

4.2 Advanced exchange orders

  1. Follow the procedure outlined in KB 4040839 Procedure: Manually cancelling open service orders to cancel the open advanced exchange.
  2. Once the order is closed and the hold removed from the customer’s payment method, work with the customer to create another exchange order (standard exchange or advanced exchange).

Return to top

4.3 All accessory orders

  1. Ensure the previous order is closed.
  2. Confirm the delivery address with the customer.
  3. Create a SEN FO order to send the customer a replacement accessory. Ensure that the case notes indicate that the original replacement accessory was returned to the service center and a SEN FO was needed to send the customer a replacement.

5.1 Surface Repair Specialists

Follow the escalation guidelines outlined by KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios for guidance and resolution.

  • Lost in Transit, Inbound to the Service Center: Courier Issues > Lost in Transit, Inbound to Service Center.
  • Lost in Transit, Outbound to the Customer: Failed Delivery > Lost in Transit - Outbound to Customer or Lost in Transit - Returned to Service/Fulfillment Center.

5.2 Xbox Repair Specialists

Follow the escalation guidelines outlined by KB 3072567 Procedure: Xbox: Engagement Scenarios for guidance and resolution.

  • Lost in Transit, Inbound to the Service Center: Failed delivery - repair or fulfillment order > From customer to SCV - lost shipment with tracking number or Microsoft-initiated shipping.
  • Lost in Transit, Outbound to the customer: Failed delivery - repair or fulfillment order > From SC to Customer or Shipper confirmation of lost package.

6. FAQ

Q: Can I use the LSKU for any other reason?

A: No, LSKU orders should only be used when a device is lost in transit. The following Special SKUs must be used instead of an LSKU to fulfill an order for the customer where Microsoft doesn’t expect to receive a device and there is no lost device. These can only be used with GRAT approval via a TFS incident:

  • In Warranty-Flag Bypass-Advanced Exchange
  • CARE Executive Escalation Advanced Exchange

Note: The CARE executive Escalation Advanced Exchange SKU is exclusive to the Customer & Field Engagement Team, formerly known as the Executive Escalation Team (EET). Tier 3 advocates outside of this team who wish to use this SKU must receive GRAT approval via a TFS incident and document that in the notes of the ticketing tool.
