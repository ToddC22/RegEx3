The purpose of this article is to provide proper explanations for frontline Partner Advocates regarding the account clean-up process, what it is and how such cases should be treated for a proper resolution. This article applies to all frontline advocate teams supporting the PMC to PC migration and Partner (Center) Dashboard Partners.

AAD - an acronym for Azure Active Directory 
Tenant - represents the AAD tenant or identity partner wants to use 
Account - represents what Partner Center creates as a partner footprint
CSP - Cloud Solution Provider
VAR - Value Added Reseller / Indirect reseller
Active Enrollment – an active MPN offer subscription on the account (MAPS Active / Silver Active/ Competency Active)
Account Clean UP - the process, done in one or multiple phases, that erases the connection made upon migrating from Partner Membership Center (PMC) to Partner (Center) Dashboard, between the MPN Membership of a Partner with an AAD tenant that the Partner considers as being incorrect, and that enables the Partner to re-migrate and re-associate his MPN membership with the tenant the Partner considers as being correct. The process also applies to new MPN accounts created in Partner Center or to CSP accounts.

3.1 Scenarios for cleanup

Both frontline support and Partners can notice that the account/tenant used for migration from Partner Membership Center (PMC) to Partner Center is incorrect. 

1) Partner used a wrong tenant to setup Partner Center Account. Here are reasons for that:

a. They ended up creating a new tenant and later realized they had an AAD identity in an existing tenant.

b. They were logged in with some other AAD identity and ended up using that. Later realized that it was not their corporate identity they want to setup PC account with. 

2) Partner was managing multiple V-orgs and ended up migrating a V-org from PMC using AAD tenant of an organization which was not owner of V-org. In this case, user is working with multiple companies. 

3) Partner ended up enrolling as a net new partner and now wants to migrate their existing organization from PMC using same tenant.

3.2 Frontline support call to action

Frontline support should first check that the cleanup request comes from the Global Admin on the tenant. In order to check this, search in CMAT with the tenant and see if the user has Company Administrator role. Note there may be multiple admins; any one of them can request the cleanup.

Then check if there is an active enrollment (subscription) on the account or not in Prose:

a) If there is an existing active enrollment (subscription) on the account (visible in Prose), frontline support should check to see if Anniversary Date is less than 3 months in the future - they should advise Partner to wait until their anniversary date and afterwards for the cleanup to be performed. In this way, Partner will experience uninterrupted access to his benefits management and account. If the cleanup would be performed now, Partner would lose temporarily the access to the management of his account.

b) If there is an existing active enrollment (subscription) on the account (visible in Prose), frontline support should check to see if Anniversary Date is more than 3 months in the future - Partner should be set with the correct expectations that the cleanup procedure can take around 1.5 weeks average, depending on the complexity of his account.

Note:

If purchase is visible in the Offline PI tool, then the request for benefit and enrollment cancellation should be escalated as per escalation path in VKB article 4134503 (if payment was made by Partner via Wire Transfer) or as per escalation path in VKB article 4340330 (if payment was made by Partner via Credit Card) prior to escalating to MPN Global Support for the cleanup itself.

Any account cleanup request with an active enrollment on the account and with an order that is not visible in Offline PI tool should be escalated to MPN Global Support, using the recommended template from VKB article 2865698

While the account cleanup is being done, Partner will, in phases:

- lose access to benefit management from Partner Center;

- have his MPN enrollment (subscription) de-activated from Partner Center;

- not be able to access the account altogether until the re-migration occurs.

After the account cleanup procedure is completed, Partner will receive a new link that he should use to migrate to his tenant/account of choice in Partner Center. Partner should perform the re-migration in an InPrivate browser window, making sure that his user has Global Admin credentials.

IMPORTANT NOTES:

  • If Partner has used any of the license benefits, those will not be affected during the cleanup process.
  • If Partner has used any of the user-assigned benefits, those will be interrupted during the cleanup process.
  • If Partner has purchased in PMC (he migrated to the incorrect tenant an active enrollment), then he will not receive a refund and once he performs the re-migration, he will have his active enrollment migrated back to correct PC tenant.
  • All other refunds in account cleanup scenarios are subject to the refund policy in place.
  • If there is a CSP/ VAR enrollment on the same tenant and with the same Account ID to be cleaned up, we need to inform the partner that cleanup would lead to cleanup of CSP and VAR enrollments as well. They would need to re-enroll as CSP / VARs after cleanup is done to gain access to the program again.

Regarding Partner’s benefits, after an account cleanup the following applies:

  • If purchase was made on the Partner Center platform and an account cleanup is performed, regardless of Partner being eligible or not for a refund, when he performs the re-migration, Partner will have to re-purchase the desired offer/s.
  • If purchase was made on the PMC platform (active enrollment was migrated to PC) and an account cleanup is performed, their benefits will be re-migrated together with the re-migration of the MPN Id to the new tenant after both account cleanup and re-migration are completed.

c) If there is NO active enrollment on the tenant to be cleaned up - If the MPN account is net new (created in Partner Center, not migrated from PMC), check if the PGA or any locations are connected to a CSP account. If yes, cleaning the account may create issues for the partner’s CSP account in the future, therefore explain to the partner and request written confirmation to proceed with the cleanup. Partner should be set with the correct expectations that the cleanup procedure can take around 3 business days average, and partner advocates should escalate to MPN Global Support, using the recommended template in article 2865698.

For frontline support awareness only (no action to be taken):

When there is an existing active enrollment (subscription) on the account, the account cleanup procedure is split between three engineering teams that perform the following phases of the account cleanup:

1) Deactivation of enrollment (active enrollment /subscription status is set to status DENIED/CLOSED in the system and will reflect as such in PrOSE).

2) Decommissioning of benefits (all benefits from the account are disassociated from the tenant that is being cleaned up).

3) PMC dates are being reset (the new anniversary date obtained in the moment of enrollment purchase, will be changed in the backend to reflect the previous Anniversary date that the Partner had) – applicable only for purchases made in PC.

4) Account cleanup (in this last phase, the last disassociations between the MPN account and the tenant are being performed. In this very last phase, MPN Global Support will perform the account cleanup.

If there is NO existing active enrollment (subscription) on the account, frontline support should set correct expectations with Partner that account cleanup procedure can take around 3 business days to be completed and that at some point in this timeframe, he will not be able to access the account in Partner Center until the re-migration occurs. After the account cleanup procedure is completed, Partner will receive a new link that he should use to migrate to his tenant/account of choice in Partner Center. Partner should perform the re-migration in an InPrivate browser window, making sure that his user has Global Admin credentials. In this case, MPN Global Support will perform the account cleanup.

[Asset 4488277]

[Asset 4488281]

To clean up the tenant, only after performing the mandatory checks/actions explained in table above (benefit decommissioning, enrollment cancellation and, if necessary the PMC data reset) and the steps 1.) to 5.) above, click on Clean Up Account button.

Partners can also request to clean up their CSP accounts for various reasons, see below common scenarios:

  1. They enrolled on the wrong tenant and they want to enroll on a different one.
  2. They want to use that tenant for multi tenancy on an MPN account and this is not possible if the tenant is already in use.
  3. They already have a CSP account on a different tenant and have applied again by mistake.
  4. The CSP account is no longer needed and they want to clean it up so that the tenant is free of any enrollments.

If you receive CSP clean up requests, please escalate all cases following escalation template in article 2865698, to MPN Global Support.

Necessary checks before escalating:

Check if the user requesting the cleanup is a Global Admin on the tenant. In order to verify this, search in CMAT with the tenant and see if the user has Company Administrator role. Note there may be multiple admins; any one of them can request the cleanup.

  1. If the CSP account is in “Active” state, inform the partner that they will lose anything related to the CSP and they will need to start over if they still require a CSP account.
  2. If the CSP account has a “Suspended” status, this means they were offboarded and we need to check the reason with the Offboarding and Remediation Intake team. Escalate the clean-up cases to MPNGS team who will reach out to them. If the Offboarding team approve the clean-up they will re-instate the account and change the status to “Active”. Only then the clean-up can be done.

Note: Through the Offboarding process the CSP account remains in Partner Center/Prose under the "Suspended" status and the partner will not be able to use the tenant for anything. Request the offboarding only if the partner does not wish to use the tenant for anything in the future.

Account Clean-up does not prevent re-enrollment. Clean-up is basically removing the AAD tenant association with the Partner Center account. It was intended for scenarios where the partner migrated with the wrong identity – the cleanup removes that association and they can use the tenant to migrate/enroll again.

You can see more details on the Offboarding process in the Partner Center Offboarding article.

  1. If the CSP account has a “Declined” status, in most cases they have failed the vetting process. This means that they have not yet signed a contract, however we need to check with OneVet team the reason the partner failed the vetting. The MPNGS team will reach out to them. Depending on the reason and if they approve, we can then proceed with the clean-up.

REQUIREMENT: Please ask the partner to provide a written confirmation for the CSP clean-up, regardless of the status on the account (active, suspended, declined etc.).

  • You may encounter less common scenarios and you can escalate those for cleanup as well. The MPN Global Support team will review each case and provide additional guidance if needed.

6.2. Benefits of V-org consolidation

Firstly we need to set up the expectations with the partners and let them know that this feature was implemented  for them to manage multiple V-orgs for their company and to consolidate them into their single Partner Center account.

Based on our experiences, some partners chose to consolidate all their accounts for ease of access as well as attainment of competencies, partner management understanding the fact that they might see a decrease in IURs. Hence for these partners, they chose to go through the route of  purchasing their other licenses through Commercial Licensing as they will have better control of what specific licenses they utilize within their organization as well as control over the numbers of licenses they need for each user within their organization.

Why consolidate?

  1. You will find it easier to manage MPN, Cosell, solution publishing under one account structure.
  2. You will need to pay for competencies and MAPS one time.
  3. You will have consolidated reporting for earnings and payments. No need to log on to separate v-org accounts for full picture.
  4. You may be eligible for more competencies after consolidating other accounts.
  5. If you become eligible for more competencies you can gain eligibility to incentive programs you were not previously eligible for.
  6. If you consolidate with an existing active enrollment (Active SILVER, GOLD), One year Extension offers are unlocked post Consolidation which makes up for the Benefits you enjoyed in PMC as individual organizations, for an additional year to help you plan moving forward.   

Important Note: please inform the partner that this process is a very complex one and it may take up to several weeks to be completed. They might not be able to use their benefits while we are doing the deconsolidation.

6.3. Frontline support call to action:

  • Present the partner the benefits of the V-org consolidation feature and all the helpful public articles.
  • Ask the partner to provide the reason and the Business Justification Letter.
  • Check the purchases of the consolidated accounts and present and set the expectations to the partner according to the refund policy.
  • Additionally, as the V-org de-consolidation process involves an account clean up the impact on the partner side will also be the one from general account clean up:

1.) Any cloud products and on-prem (software) activated tokens will continue to run until their expiry date.

2.) Any user assignment subscriptions for the account will be disabled (Azure Bulk Credit / Azure monthly credit / Visual Studio Subscription / Technical Benefits – Advisory hours / Products support -, Go-to-market).

3.) Any benefit not consumed will be deprovisioned.

4.) Gold enrollment will be disabled.

5.) Any MCP associated in the old PMC platform, will still show up after the re-migration is completed.

6.) Any MCP associated only on Partner Center will need to re-associate themselves after the re-migration is completed under

  • Make the partner aware of the fact that this is a complex process and they may not be able to access their inactivated benefits in this time as per rules outlined in above bullet point.
  • After above process has been performed and all the necessary documents have been gathered, please escalated accordingly to MPN Global Support queue by following the steps outlined in article 2865698.

How to guide the Partner through the re-migration process after cleanup is complete?

When accessing the link for re-migration they should firstly use MSA account login (associated to a PMC Admin) and afterwards the company work e-mail (e.g..:
tom@tomscompany.com). Please make sure that the partner is accessing those accounts with the users that have GLOBAL ADMIN rights.

Please ask the Partner to do the following: ​

1.) login to PMC from an InPrivate browser window​

2.) copy paste in the InPrivate window following link (unique to each Partner and provided by MPN Global Support after the cleanup process has been completed):​

https://partnercenter.microsoft.com/pcmsa/accountsettings/setmsampncontext?mpnId=XXXXX&programName=partnernetwork

3.) ask the Partner to read notice on screen and then to click next​

4.) when prompted to login, ask Partner to use their AAD credentials​

5.) after signing in give the portal a few moments to work through the association​

6.) when page loading is successful, click on Accept and Continue button to finalize migration

Refund eligibility

Regarding Partner’s benefits, after an account cleanup the following applies:

  •  If purchase was made on the Partner Center platform and an account cleanup is performed, regardless of Partner being eligible or not for a refund, when he performs the re-migration, Partner will have to re-purchase the desired offer/s.

  •  If purchase was made on the PMC platform (active enrollment was migrated to PC) and an account cleanup is performed, their benefits will be re-migrated together with the re-migration of the MPN Id to the new tenant after both account cleanup and re-migration are completed.

Are all account clean-up scenario for AD more than 3 months in the future subject to refund request? If so, we’re to follow the refund policy?

NOTE: If we follow the normal refund policy, no partner “with AD more than 3 months in the future” would ever receive a refund. Considering the complexity and particularities of the account cleanups, it was agreed that some special way of actions would apply.

As said before, if Partners inquire about the refund aspect, they should be advised that their eligibility for a refund will be checked together with the engineering team, during the account cleanup process.  Partner must also be informed that there is a possibility that he might not be eligible for a refund

  • Scenario 1: Partner’s purchase is visible in CMAT and more than 30 days have passed / benefits used – partner is usually not eligible for a refund but confirmation of this aspect will come on an one by one case basis, after an analysis according to a multitude of factors and cleanup complexity.
  • Scenario 2: Partner’s purchase is visible in Offline PI and it was made via CC – partner might be eligible for a refund but confirmation of this aspect will come on an one by one case basis, after an analysis according to a multitude of factors and cleanup complexity.
  • Scenario 3: Partner’s purchase is visible in Offline PI and it was made through Wire Transfer – partner MIGHT be eligible for a refund and each Frontline team should work first with the Global Finance team to obtain refund for Partner and only after escalate to MPN Global Support.
  • Scenario 4: Partner’s purchase is not visible in CMAT or Offline PI tool, but it is visible in PMC – partner is not eligible for a refund since their benefits will be remigrated together with the re-migration of the MPN ID to the new tenant.

 

Will Partners receive their benefits back once the cleanup process is completed?

There are two scenarios to answer this question:

1.) If there is an active enrollment (MAPS/Silver/Gold) migrated from PMC in the account to be cleaned up, then after the cleanup is performed and the Partner re-migrates to desired tenant, previous active enrollments (MAPS/Silver/Gold) will also be re-migrated over to PC.

2.) If there is an active enrollment (MAPS/Silver/Gold) purchased from PC in the account to be cleaned up, then after the cleanup is performed and the Partner re-migrates to desired tenant, Partner will have to re-purchase to have an active enrollment.
