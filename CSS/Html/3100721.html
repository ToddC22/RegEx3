1. Mobile Operator Billing overview

The Microsoft Store offers Mobile Operator Billing (Phone Billing) as a payment method for devices running Windows 10, phones running Windows 10 Mobile, and Xbox One consoles. If a customer’s mobile operator supports this capability, the customer can add Mobile Operator Billing as a payment method and use it to make Store purchases using their mobile account.

  • If a customer’s mobile operator offers Mobile Operator Billing, but the customer can't find this option on their device, see Buy stuff from Microsoft Store with mobile billing.
  • Mobile Operator Billing should be handled as any other payment instruments where Microsoft is fully accountable for handling Windows and Microsoft Store purchases issues, refunds, etc. Customers should not be transferred to their Mobile Operator (except for cellphone hardware or cell network issues).
  • A list of Mobile Operator Billing operators currently supported is available at Mobile operator billing.

There are two billing options:

  • One-time purchase
  • Recurring purchases: subscriptions

  • The payment instrument is no longer attached to the account.
  • The operator billing purchase are blocked by the users' mobile account.

2.1 Familiar and unknown fraud

Scenario

Customer handling

I see a charge on my mobile bill from Microsoft that a minor child made without parental/guardian permission. Can you help?

Educate customers on how to review their order history or their family purchase history on account.microsoft.com.

Help customers to understand what the charge is for and to take any additional secondary actions as needed (cancel subscription, print receipt, refund) by asking them to review their profile.

  • Manage your Microsoft account

Guide the customer to the following resources:

  • Unknown charges from Microsoft
  • Set the right limits for your child

Important note: In cases of “family” fraud, customers may not know what account was used for the purchase, and advocates may not be able to locate the account in MST. In such case scenario, please engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates with the guidance in KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing scenarios Engagement Scenarios - Accounts and Billing.

I see a charge on my mobile bill from Microsoft and I don’t know what it is for or who did it. Can you help?
  1. Locate the account using the mobile number, Microsoft account, or Gamertag in MST.
  • Use the + symbol and country code followed by the customers number and then select Search By Phone Number as Payment Instrument to locate the account in question.
  1. Verify the account belongs to the customer or someone familiar to the customer such as a family member. If so and the customer still requests a refund, follow KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund to determine if the account is eligible.
  2. If the customer does not own the account or recognize it engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates following KB 817557 Guidelines: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement as an Account Takeover (unauthorized access) engagement type and provide the Mobile Number.

2.2 Usage and billing

Scenario

Customer handling

How can I increase the mobile billing threshold amount?

Explain to the customer the purchase limits are set by the carrier, based on customer creditworthiness and any relevant regulations (for example, a 50 EUR single-purchase limit due to PSD2 regulations in Europe), and is not something Microsoft can control. If the customer is requesting a threshold increase inform them there is nothing we can do for them.

How come my mobile provider is not on the list of supported Telecom Partner?
  • Explain to the customer that this program is based on Mobile Operator participation. A list of live operators is available in Mobile operator billing and is always expanding.
  • Customers may contact their mobile operator for more information on the operator’s future participation.

I used to pay my purchases using my Mobile operator billing. How come I cannot use that feature any longer?
  • Search for the account in MST.
  • If you’re unable to locate the account, explain to the customer that the payment instrument may have been blocked by the mobile operator. The customer will need to contact the mobile operator to understand why the mobile number cannot be used as a payment option.
  • If the customer is requesting a refund, engage the Advanced Capability Team. Please include the mobile number when filling in the Advanced Capability Team engagement form (see KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing Scenarios).

I changed my telephone number. How do I reconfigure my ability to pay using my operator bill?

Educate the customer on how to update their Microsoft account by following the steps located in Change the payment method or credit card linked to your Microsoft account.

I changed my cell phone provider. How do I reconfigure my ability to pay using my operator bill?
  • Explain to the customer that if their new carrier is part of the participating list of mobile carriers, then the customer will be able to add a new payment for this new carrier.
  • To reconfigure a payment method, advocate will educate the customer on how to update his Microsoft account, following the steps detailed in the previous scenario.

I did not receive an SMS text message with the confirmation codes to complete my new payment method setup. Can you help?
  • When adding Pay by Phone as a payment method, an SMS text message is sent to the configured phone to complete the process.
  • Verify with the customer that he has entered the correct telephone number with which they want to enable Pay by Phone.
  • To reconfigure or verify a payment method, advocate will educate the customer on how to update his Microsoft account, following the steps detailed in the previous scenario.
  • Ensure the customer has selected the correct mobile operator from the drop-down.

I have a question about my cell phone bill. Can you help?
  • Direct the customer to their mobile operator to look up their telephone bill.
  • If the customer has a problem with a purchase from the Microsoft Store app, our advocates will assist the customer by looking at the purchases the customer has made in Store, using MST.

I have a dual SIM device. Can I enable Operator Billing on both SIMs and choose which Mobile payment when I make a purchase?
  • Explain to the customer that with dual SIM cards, if both mobile operators are supported, they will act as two separate payment methods in our system.
  • The customer can select either one of them to make a purchase. If only one mobile operator is supported, then only one SIM card will be turned on for Pay by Phone.

I get an error when I try to add my phone as a payment option. Can you help?

Check the following:

  • Make sure the customer’s Mobile Operator is supporting billing as a payment method (see an updated list in Mobile operator billing).
  • Ask the customer if they have received the SMS text confirmation.
  • Ask the customer to re-enter the SMS text code.
  • Ask the customer what error message they are receiving.
    • Note: In some cases, operator would not allow some type of customers to use operator billing therefore they can’t use their payment method (for example, pre-paid, corporate users). In such case scenario, customers need to contact their operator.
  • If the issue persists and the customer cannot add their Mobile Telephone as a payment method, engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates. Please include the mobile number when filling in the Advanced Capability Engagement Form (see KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing Scenarios).
    • Note: Only engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates if the error is technical on our side and nothing to do with the operator decision not to allow operator billing for that customer.

2.3 Refunds

Important note: Refund is subject to strict policy (See KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund for more details on refund policy):

  • Unauthorized purchases by a minor.
  • Purchased wrong content.
  • Content does not work.

For refunds, bank/mobile statements are no longer required at Frontline, but at the escalation level for specific scenarios one may be required.

Example: A pdf statement might be needed at ACA's discrepancy for MOBI ATO requests.

Scenario

Customer handling

I’d like a refund, but I’ve removed my mobile operator billing as a payment method from my Microsoft account. Can you help?

Explain to the customers that we cannot provide a refund if the payment instrument is no longer listed in their Microsoft account. Even by adding it again, it will still be impossible for advocate to refund.

Engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates. Please include the mobile number when filling in the Advanced Capability Engagement Form (see KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing Scenarios).

I bought a game using my mobile number, and I need a refund. Can you help?
  1. Locate the account using the Microsoft account, Gamertag, or mobile number in MST and look for the charges.
  2. Follow KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund if a refund can be processed.
  3. Attempt to process the refund, If the refund request succeeds with no errors, set expectations with the customer around when they should expect to see the fund returned to their account.
  4. If the refund fails with the error below, reattempt the refund two more times and check for errors.

[Asset 4465785]

  1. If the refund continues to fail, offer the customer CSV for the failed refunds.
  2. If the customer declines CSV, escalate to ACA, who will provide a check to the customer.

Note: If the account cannot be located, or the charge does not appear on the account, engage the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates. Include the mobile number when filling in the Advanced Capability Engagement Form (see KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing Scenarios).
