2. Guidance

All online services:
  • Refer complete Guidance section in 3078460: Processing a credit request due to a service outage for Direct and Prepaid customer.
  • For VL customers we differentiate between VL direct vs. VL indirect:
    • VL direct (Customer is paying the invoice to Microsoft). The credit memo from Microsoft goes to the customer for the SLA outage.
    • VL indirect (Microsoft invoices the Channel Partner; the Partner invoices the Customer). The credit memo from Microsoft goes to the Channel Partner (Licensing Solution Provider/LSP) or Distributor (depending on the VL Program) for an SLA outage. As the LSP charges the customer, the LSP must also reimburse their customers for the outage.

MPSA program is supported by the Volume License support team supporting VLSC (In some regions only a subset of advocates are dedicated to MPSA / NGVL).  While CMAT does not display MPSA Agreement or Order numbers, it is still possible to identify a VL-subscription as MPSA. See 3174133: Supporting Microsoft Product and Services (MPSA) Customers for details. Contact your regional VL HUB/Tier 3/POD lead for Agreement and Purchase Account Number. 

How MSFT Determines Service Credit:

Service Credits are calculated by Engineering based on the best data available to determine actual customer impact.
Service Credits are calculated on a Per Tenant basis.
Service Credit is based on each tenant’s Monthly Uptime Percentage as determined by the formula:

(User Minutes -Downtime )/User Minutes x 100

Where

  • User Minutes represents the number of minutes in the month multiplied by the theoretical maximum number of users who could potentially use the service during the month. This maximum number of users is represented by the total number of licenses a tenant has for the given service.
  • The Downtime period is calculated by multiplying the Incident Duration by the number of Impacted Users)
  • Incident duration is the length of time between the start of the service incident to the point in time service has been restored.
    Note: Incident duration is not necessarily equal to the incident start / end times posted on the SHD. In some cases, an Incident may be left open for days or weeks even though impact has been remediated and service is restored.
  • Impacted Users is the actual or estimated number of users impacted by the incident as determined by one or more of the following methods:
    -- M365 Service Telemetry / log data (e.g. number of unique user sessions connected or attempted to connect to the service during the Incident Duration)
    -- Physical infrastructure (e.g. number of mailboxes contained in an impacted Exchange Database Access Group)
    -- Historic / trend information (e.g. Peak number of users observed for like time periods as the incident time period).
    -- Historic information is primarily utilized when service telemetry is unavailable or incomplete.
    -- Historic information can be based on periods prior to the incident as well as after the incident

     

Credit and Proration and Service percentages (Customer ready language)

Service Credits

Service Credits are your sole and exclusive remedy for any performance or availability issues for any Service under the Agreement and this SLA. You may not unilaterally offset your Applicable Monthly Service Fees for any performance or availability issues.

Service Credits apply only to fees paid for the particular Service, Service Resource, or Service tier for which a Service Level has not been met. In cases where Service Levels apply to individual Service Resources or to separate Service tiers, Service Credits apply only to fees paid for the affected Service Resource or Service tier, as applicable. The Service Credits awarded in any billing month for a particular Service or Service Resource will not, under any circumstance, exceed your monthly service fees for that Service or Service Resource, as applicable, in the billing month.

If you purchased Services as part of a suite or other single offer, the Applicable Monthly Service Fees and Service Credit for each Service will be pro-rated.

If you own a bundled sku (e.g. E1, E3, E5, F1, G3, G5, etc.) and have downtime impact which qualifies for an SLA credit – the credit will be applied against the entire sku only when the entire O365 service was impacted.

When an individual service is impacted, only that services pro-rated percentage of the cost of the bundled sku is credited. If you own multiple bundled sku’s (e.g. both E3 and E5), credit will be applied against each applicable bundle that contains the impacted service/feature, however the amount of credit will vary by bundle as:

-- There are different sets of services in each bundle, therefore the associated percentage a given service represents will vary across bundles.
-- Different SKU bundles may have different features / capabilities of the services. Depending on the incident, not all bundled sku’s may have been impacted.
-- Microsoft frequently adds new services to these SKUs which may have their own SLAs, therefore over time, the pro-ration numbers change
-- If multiple services were impacted, each impacted service will determine its service specific impact (downtime) and if applicable, it is resulting credit %. Each services credit % is applied against the services pro-rated amount.

Note: It is conceivable that Service 1 and Service 2 could result in different SLA credit percentages (e.g. Service 1’s SLA impact resulted in a 25% SLA credit, while Service 2’s impact resulted in a 50% SLA credit).

Only Impacted users are included in the calculations (Customer ready language)

While all services use the general SLA impact formula of: (𝑈𝑠𝑒𝑟 𝑀𝑖𝑛𝑢𝑡𝑒𝑠 −𝐷𝑜𝑤𝑛𝑡𝑖𝑚e)/ (𝑈𝑠𝑒𝑟 𝑀𝑖𝑛𝑢𝑡𝑒𝑠) 𝑥 100, how “Downtime” is determined may vary by service.

In the majority of impact scenarios, only ACTIVE users of the service at the time of the Service Incident are included into the Downtime calculation. Clarifications for “Downtime” calculations are contained in the “Service Specific Terms” for each service. Per the SLA Guide, most specify “Downtime is measured in user-minutes; that is, for each month, Downtime is the sum of the length (in minutes) of each Incident that occurs during that month multiplied by the number of users impacted by that Incident.” For some Service Incident scenarios, all licensed users are considered as impacted.

Here is an example calculation to help explain how SLA calculations are processed:

Example Calculation for an Office 365 Service Impacting Event:
Office 365 Users = 1000
Impacted users = 100
January has 44640 Minutes
Single incident in the Month with 180 minutes of downtime.
User Minutes (1000 * 44640) – Downtime (180 Min * 100 Users) / User Minutes (1000 * 44640) which yields an SLA attainment for the service of 99.960%

Due to the variations in downtime calculations and complexities of customer specific contracts, we do not provide the impact or $ amount calculations utilized in determining final credit amounts granted.

3. Procedure

Read and understand 3078460: Processing a credit request due to a service outage for Direct and Prepaid customer. Same procedure steps are not repeated in this document. 

Workflow from advocate handling perspective:﻿

Step 1 (Within 24 hours):

Frontline advocate validates the request and rejects if the request falls under the rejection criteria, refer complete Step 1 Process in 3078460: Processing a credit request due to a service outage for Direct and Prepaid customer.

During the VL subscription lifecycle, customers are able to reserve licenses for immediate use, however, these licenses are not paid for until the annual renewal date. If an SLA credit request is validated but the subscription has not been invoiced, advise the customer that they need to resubmit the request after the anniversary date. If support receives a case where the customer said that they were told to call back after their subscription was invoiced, look for the previous case if possible and escalate the case to T3 for processing.

﻿Step 2 (Within 24 hours):

Calculate the SLA credit amount and determine if Tier1/Tier 3 are authorized to approve the credit amount or if Tier 3 needs to obtain the service outage verification and uptime % from the appropriate team.

  1. Determine % of the Service credit using Duration time in Lynx article 4339246: How to search incident in Lynx.

    4013318 
  2. Calculate the credit amount in the SLA credit calculator.

    Note    : Finance and Operations (F&O) is 90.7% of the component cost for Dynamics 365 (D365) plan.

Calculate the SLA credit and determine if you are authorized to approve the credit amount for VL customers.

 
Calculate the SLA credit for VL:

Note: Follow this process to estimate the credit amount to determine if you are authorized to approve the SLA credit (< $2500 USD or local currency equivalent). For standalone services which are not part of a suite (ie Dynamics 365, Skype for Business), calculate the credit from the full price of the subscription.

i. Launch the SLA credit calculator.
ii. Go to the Direct SLA tab.
iii. Choose the customer’s plan from the drop-down list in Column A.
iv. Choose the impacted service from the drop-down list in Column B. This will populate the Component % of suite value.
v. Enter the number of seats and the monthly price per seat (pre-tax price).
vi. Find the credit amount based on the Service Credit Percentage from Columns F, G or H. This is the SLA credit amount. 

a). If the SLA credit amount is < $2500 USD (or local currency equivalent), Support Advocate are authorized to approve the SLA credit. Go to Submit the approved VL credit request in the Call Logging Tool.

b). If the SLA credit amount is ≥ $2500 USD (or local currency equivalent), you are not authorized to approve the credit. Create a work item for Tier 3 asking for validation of the SLA violation and the approved credit percentage to be applied to the customer’s account. If validated and approved, go to Submit the approved VL credit request in the Call Logging Tool.

c. If the request is outside of advocate empowerment, Tier 3 (depending on regions) will obtain the service outage verification and uptime % from the appropriate team as needed.
 
Note: It is important that correct customer expectations are set from the beginning. 
 
SLA outage verifications are validated one time per month.
 
Once a request is raised to Business Group (BG), it can take up to 60 days to receive a response.

For MO type incidents the response time can take up to 80 days or more, depending on how the MO affected the M365 workloads and the customer subscription environment.
 

Once confirmation is received that a request has been raised with BG, use the following suggested customer facing script:

 

"We are currently in the process of validating the outage you have experienced and determining whether credit is due. Please note that this process can take up to 60 days to complete. For MO type incidents the response time can take longer. (only add this info if the case is actually MO related response)

 

I will continue to monitor the validation process and will let you know as soon as I receive more information. Please do let me know if you have preferences regarding the frequency with which you would like to receive updates."

i. Escalate to Commerce T3 Engineering Escalations according to 2778091: How to escalate a Service Request to T3, Engineering.

For VL indirect:

What can be shared with the customer?

Do not share calculation, credit memo, invoice number and Total SLA credit amount with the customer. Inform the customer that their credit has been approved and applied.

-        If the customer wants to know about how the credit will be used, advise the customer that by default, the credit will be used for open balance or for against future amounts.

-        If they have questions on calculation, credit memo or refund, advise the customer to connect with their Partner/reseller whom they purchased through.

-       If the reseller is not willing to assist, support should engage with the reseller/partner on behalf of the customer to let them know that they need to provide the credit and details as Microsoft support can’t share the details since the billing is done with that partner/reseller.

-       If the customer does not know the partner/LSP, check Look up Partner or Licensing Solution Provider(LSP) from Explore.MS tool (2768896) or engage VL Hub to get the information.

What can be shared with the Partner?

The advocate cannot share the calculation with the partner. Partner contacts ROC team via CLT (if they have not received the credit memo) to request the credit memo.
