2. Guidance 

Cancellation terms

  • Cancellation. The process to end the subscription so that no functionality is available.
  • Deprovision. The deletion of all data, folders, and mailboxes associated with the subscription. Deprovisioning occurs within 90 days of the cancellation request.

Customers must backup their data before the subscription is deprovisioned (See 4. Exceptions.)

Customers are responsible for removing their data before the subscription is deprovisioned. It is important that the customer understands the difference between cancellation and deprovisioning. Set customer expectations appropriately so that no loss of data results from a cancellation. If a customer needs assistance with backing up their data, provide the following links. If they have any issues, they can contact Technical Support or their Partner for additional help.
  • Download a copy of your data
  • Use Outlook to move information between email accounts
  • Information about manual migration of SharePoint Online content

Cancellation guidance

Most subscriptions are set to auto-renew by default at the time of purchase. When the subscription nears its end, customers are notified electronically that their subscription is coming to an end. If no action is taken, the subscription will automatically be renewed for another 12-month term. Microsoft 365 Apps for business, Microsoft 365 Business Standard, and Microsoft 365 Business Basic plans have both annual commitments and month-to-month subscriptions. Annual subscriptions are set to auto-renew by default at the time of purchase. “Month to month” subscriptions renew monthly and can be cancelled at any time.” (by the customer or advocate). Customers can cancel a subscription at any time, and the following guidances apply: 
  • Cancellations within the first 30 days are allowed at no fee. However, customers are responsible for paying for the first 30 days. If monthly commitment, customer is credited automatically for the balance of the first 30 days. If annual commitment/annual pay customer is credited for 100% of the first 30 days. If annual commitment / monthly pay, no automatic credit is issued for the balance of the first month. 
  • If the customer cancels within 30 days after renewal, the auto-credit functionality is the same as the first 30 days after purchase.
  • If you have subscribed to Office 365 for a yearly period, but want to cancel early, you will be charged an early termination fee (ETF) of one month's cost for the service you are canceling. This applies to either your entire subscription, or to any number of licenses that you remove from an annual subscription. 

    NOTE: This fee is currently waived but may be charged in the future. Customers can see an ETF warning when they self-cancel their subscriptions in the Microsoft 365 admin center.
  • A cancellation fee may be applied by product (i.e., SharePoint, Exchange Server, Skype for Business Online (formally Lync Online)).
  • When the primary subscription is cancelled, such as SharePoint, any add-ons such as extra storage that are dependent subscriptions to the primary subscription must also be cancelled. If a customer requests that a subscription be canceled that has a dependent subscription, the customer must be informed prior to cancellation that the dependent services will be cancelled, as well. Note: Cancellation of dependent subscriptions is not automatic and must be performed by the advocate. If there is another qualifying primary subscription on the tenant, advocate do not need to cancel the dependent subscriptions.
  • Microsoft 365 Apps for business, Microsoft 365 Business Standard, and Microsoft 365 Business Basic customers can opt out of the auto-renew feature through the Microsoft 365 admin center. If the customer decides to retain the service after opting out of auto-renew and the subscription has gone into InGracePeriod (expired) or Disabled status, they can reactivate the subscription in the Microsoft 365 admin center. 
    [Asset 4533552]
  • If Reactivate is not an available action for the subscription, the customer must contact Support to have the subscription reactivated.
  • Retention of data varies for each service suite. The Exchange mailbox is available for 30 days, but the customer has 14 days to recover mail items. Note that if a license is removed for a mailbox any emails coming to that mailbox will also not be available, and only the items that were in the mailbox until the license was taken away will be in available. SharePoint is available for 14 days after the cancelation is executed.
  • Customers should update their contact preferences and privacy options before the subscription is cancelled. Please refer to article 2917623: How to help customers manage their contact preferences for more information.
  • All data associated with a trial subscription is permanently deleted at the end of the trial period. Please note that the customer will receive notifications when the trial is set to expire. This may cause confusion for the customer if they also have a Paid subscription. Please set customer expectations appropriately. For more information, see 2812947: Information about the subscription life cycle .

Additional cancellation guidances for Microsoft Intune 

  • If a customer contacts Support to cancel their Intune subscription, the advocate must ask if the customer also has the Microsoft Desktop Optimization Pack (MDOP). If the customer has both, then cancel both subscriptions. MDOP is a dependent offer for Intune. See article 2940765: Product information about Intune for more information.
  • Intune customers are entitled to upgrade rights to Windows 8 or Windows 7 Enterprise for all PCs that are covered by Intune as long as they meet the minimum system requirements for Windows 8 or Windows 7. If the customer wants to continue with the Intune benefits after canceling their subscription, they can purchase a service agreement or a buyout option. See article 2762780: How to handle Intune customers who cancel their subscription for more information.

3. Procedure

A customer calls to cancel their subscription.
 
All regions - Transfer the customer or transfer the case to the Retention team.
How customers can cancel their subscriptions in the Microsoft 365 admin center
You can cancel your paid or trial subscription in the Microsoft 365 admin center before the end of your term if you have fewer than 25 licenses assigned to users. You can also cancel any subscription, no matter how many licenses are assigned, within the first 30 days after purchase.

For any other type of cancellation that is not covered by the above-mentioned scenarios, the customer will be required to call Support to cancel their subscription.
 
  1. On the Microsoft 365 admin center page, click Subscriptions under BILLING. On the SUBSCRIPTIONS page, click the active subscription you want to cancel. Click Cancel subscription.
  2. On the Cancel subscription page, you will see a warning that if you cancel your subscription today you will be billed an early termination fee equal to your one month subscription fee. Click on Learn more, you can know more about the early termination fee. Currently this fee is waived but may be charged in the future.
     [Asset 4533553]
    [Asset 4533554]
  3. Read the message about important dates regarding the subscription, provide feedback to indicate your reason for cancellation and click Cancel subscription.[Asset 4533555]
     
Note: If you purchased your subscription with an Office 365 product key, you can cancel through Microsoft by calling Support. However, you can’t get a refund from Microsoft for subscriptions purchased with a product key.
How to cancel a customer’s subscription in CMAT
  1. In CMAT, click the Subscriptions tab and expand the subscription to show the subscription details.
  2. Click the Status link (either Active, Inactive, InGracePeriod) to open the Change Subscription Status window.
  3. In the Change Subscription Status window, choose Disable from the Action drop-down list and choose User request from the Reason drop-down list.

1. Cancellation

Direct, not VL, customer cancels within the first 30 days

NOTE: No credit will be issued automatically to subscriptions with an annual commitment which are billed monthly.		 Customer must contact Support within 30 days of purchase.
  1. Check CMAT for any credit automatically generated after the subscription was cancelled.
  2. Calculate the credit for the unused part of the first 30/31 days (from the cancellation date to the end of the first month of service):
    1. Lookup the invoice to determine the pre-tax amount charged, unit price, seat count, and service dates from page 2 of the invoice.
    2. Use Standard credit calculator here.
    3. The formula in the worksheet calculates the pro-rated credit as follows:

      Amount charged for first 30/31 days – (Unit price / No. of days in billing period * No. of days used) * Quantity) = Manual credit needed
  3. If the customer is unhappy about not receiving a credit for the entire 30 days, the advocate can submit a credit request in CMAT for the full 30 days (minus any auto-credit generated after cancellation). 
 No limit within stated conditions.
2. Cancellation

Direct, not VL, customer cancels within 90 days due to:
  • Customer never used the service
  • Customer thought it was a trial
  • Partner order on behalf
 Customer must contact Support within 90 days of purchase.
  1. Check CMAT for any credit automatically generated after the subscription was cancelled.
  2. Calculate the credit for the elapsed time on the subscription (start date to date cancelled).
    1. Lookup invoices for prior months to determine the pre-tax amounts billed previously.
    2. Lookup the current invoice to determine the pre-tax amount charged, unit price, seat count, and service dates from page 2 of the invoice.
    3. Use Standard credit calculator here.
    4. The formula in the worksheet calculates the credit due as follows:

      Amounts previously billed minus the automatic credit (found in CMAT) resulting from the cancellation = Manual credit needed
No limit within stated conditions.
3. Cancellation


(Direct, not VL customer) Billed after cancellation 		Customer must contact Support within 90 days of attempted cancellation.

No proof of attempted cancellation is required.
  1. Check CMAT for any credit automatically generated after the subscription was cancelled.
  2. Calculate the credit from the date they intended to cancel to the day it was actually cancelled.
    1. Lookup the invoice for the billing period that includes the initial cancellation date.
    2. Lookup invoices created after the initial cancellation date.
    3. Lookup the current invoice to determine the pre-tax amount charged, unit price, seat count, and service dates from page 2 of the invoice.
    4. Use Standard credit calculator here. Use a separate row for each month.
    5. The formulas in the worksheet calculate the credit due as follows:

      Calculate the pro-rated credit for the month that includes initial cancellation request + pro-rated credit for the month that includes actual cancellation + any full months billed in between
 No limit within stated conditions.
4. Cancellation

(Direct, not VL customer) Purchased incorrect subscription		 Customer must contact Support within 90 days of purchasing the new subscription.
  1. Check CMAT for any credit automatically generated after the subscription was cancelled.
  2. Calculate the credit for the price difference between the intended subscription and the incorrect subscription up to the time of cancellation. (A credit will be automatically generated for the remainder of the current billing period: from the cancellation date to the end of the current billing period.)
    1. Lookup invoices for billing periods that include the start date of the incorrect subscription and the cancellation date and any invoices in between.
    2. Use Standard credit calculator here.
    3. The formula in the worksheet calculates the credit due as follows: Price difference between incorrect plan and intended plan/No. of days in billing cycle * No. of days to credit for incorrect subscription) * Quantity = Credit needed
 No limit within stated conditions.

ii. Determine your approval authority and apply the approved credit
 
  • If the credit amount is ≤ $650 USD (or local currency equivalent), apply the credit in CMAT following the steps in 2996655: Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT. 
  • If the credit amount is between $650 and $2500 USD, FL submit credit via CMAT for Tier 3 review. Once approval is obtained, the credit will be automatically applied to the customer’s account.
  • If the credit amount is > $2500 USD, create a work item for Tier 3 for BG consideration. Upon approval, Tier 3 will update work item and FL will need to submit credit request via CMAT.
    Once the request is approved in CMAT, the credit is automatically applied to the customer’s account.
For EMEA: For the subscription canceled on the same day, if the refundable transactions are visible in CMAT, advocates can apply the credit in CMAT following the process described in article 2996655: Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT without waiting for the invoice to be generated.
Note: The case owner always maintains communication with the customer. The case owner will monitor work items for updates or closure and update the case accordingly and will communicate the resolution to the customer. The message to the customer is that, if approved, it can take up to two billing cycles for the credit to appear on the customer’s invoice once approved and applied.
Billing credits after cancellation
Cancellation within the initial 30/31 days:
Annual commitment / annual pay - If the customer cancels within the first 30/31 days, they will receive a 100%credit
Annual commitment / monthly pay – No automated credits will occur for customers who canceled their subscription within 30 days from when the subscription was purchased.

Cancellation after the initial 30/31 days:
Billing is stopped on the cancellation date, and a credit is automatically generated for the remainder of the subscription term if annually paid or the remainder of the current billing period if monthly paid.

If the cancellation results in a credit balance:
  • Cancellation of all active paid subscriptions

    A refund may be automatically processed depending on the customer’s payment method.

    Credit card customers will see the refund 10 days after the generation date of their next invoice.
    Invoice pay customers must request a refund. The request must be from a global or billing administrator. See 3186259: Helping customers who request a refund.
  • Cancellation with at least one subscription left active

    Check the CMAT order ID of both the active and disabled subscription:
    If the order ID of the subscriptions are the same, the credit will be applied to the next bill and will be used to settle future charges.
    If the order ID of the subscriptions are different, a refund may be automatically processed depending on the customer’s payment method. Credit card customers will see the refund 10 days after the generation date of their next invoice. A manual refund needs to be requested for invoice pay customers. See 3186259: Helping customers who request a refund.

4. Exceptions

There's no data loss if the customer wants to manually expedite the de-provision of the trial while there are active paid licenses on the same tenant. For example:
  • If the customer already has One Drive for Business Plan 2 subscription purchased and activated and would like to expedite the One Drive for Business Plan 2 Trial de-provision, there will be no data loss in the customer's One Drive for Business service;
  • If the customer used E5 trial and then purchased & activated SharePoint Standalone offer only later, and if advocates de-provisioned E5 trial, SPO service data should reserve while other data will be removed.
This has no difference from the scenario where trial subscription got de-provisioned automatically based on lifecycle after the customer purchased paid version of the same subscription/service. Another example is SMB transition, after the customer transitioned from SMB to new Business subscriptions, SMB will eventually de-provision based on lifecycle but no data loss will happen.
