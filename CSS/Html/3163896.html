1. Transfer guideline

1.1 When to transfer

Scenario: You can transfer phone or chat sessions if your customer’s support scenarios are not in your support boundary. See KB 2912243 Procedure: All-up landing page for Support Boundaries for more information.

India advocates: Due to local laws in India, advocates located in India can’t perform any monetary transaction with the customer, including charges, refunds, and turning on/off Recurring Billing. Do not deny these transfer requests from advocates located in India.

Language support: If a customer requests support in another language, transfer them to the team who can assist. If you are comfortable and fluent in the customer’s preferred language, you can provide support in their language. If you are not fluent in their preferred language, go to step 3.

Note: Let the customer know that although you can help them in their preferred language, if they need any type of call back, the advocate who contacts them will be speaking in the language of the original queue. Tell the customer how they can get to their preferred language.

  • For information regarding transferring to other language support lines, see KB 2769823 Procedure: Transfer codes for other lines of business.

Time: You can transfer when the “transfer to” support team is in business hours.

For specific transfer case scenarios see the below KB articles:

  • KB 4457150 Procedure: A&B: Transferring Call/Chat to Commercial Support Teams
  • KB 4516280 Business Rule: Surface: Customer Transfer.

  • Give the customer the virtual agent link and steps to contact the right support team.

1.3 Transfer destinations

Primary teams: You will be transferring the case as well as call/chat:

  • Accounts & Billing
  • Office
  • Sales (Phone only)
  • Surface
  • Windows
  • Xbox

Other Teams: You will just be transferring the call/chat not the case.

Return to top

1.4 When transfer destination is not available

Outside of business hours - When the team who needs to accept the transfer is not currently open:

  • Tell the customer that the transfer destination support team is currently out of business hours.
  • Provide the customer with:
  • Steps for how to contact the right support team (See note in section 1.2 for more info)
  • The business hours of the support team.

No advocate available – Transfer destination support teams are all busy and not available to receive the transfer:

  • Tell the customer that transfer destination support teams are all busy and currently not available.
  • You can switch to cold transfer if the customer is willing to wait.
  • If not, provide the customer with:
    • Steps for how to contact the right support team (See note in section 1.2 for more info)
    • The business hours of the support team.

2. Transfer type

  1. Warm Transfer - A warm transfer is when you need to stay on the phone/chat with the customer during the transfer to another Microsoft support team.

When to use “Warm Transfer”:

  • Always start with this when transferring customers to another support advocate unless it is stated otherwise.

Pros:

  • Less effort for the customer - Provide all the information you received from the customer, so they do not need to repeat themselves
  • The ability to talk to the “transfer to” advocate before transferring
  • The “transfer to” advocate can pick up where you left off
  • Being responsible for your customer: Make sure the customer lands with the correct advocate to resolve their issue.

Notes:

  • Make the conversation be about getting the customers to the right advocate, rather than talking about what you can’t do.
  • Tell them that you are going to include their info so that the next advocate will know what the issue is, and what you have already discussed.
  1. Cold Transfer (Currently this option is not available for chats): A cold transfer is when you do not need to stay with the customer on the phone before you reach another Microsoft support team.

When to use “Cold Transfer”:

  • Scenarios stated in this article
  • When your SME/team manager approves
  • When there is specific guidance

Cons:

  • If you are transferring to the primary team: The customer must provide the case number to new advocates, and the “transfer to” advocate must read the case notes before they can start supporting the customer.
  • If you are transferring to another team: The customer will have to repeat what they have shared with you to the new advocate.

Note:

  • Focus the conversation on getting the customers to the right advocate, rather than talking about what you can't do.
  • When you are transferring to a primary team, tell the customer that you are going to include their info in the case notes so that the next advocate will know what the issue is, and what you have already discussed.

3. Transfer process

3.1 Warm transfer process

  1. Tell the customer that you are transferring them to the right support team for their issue, and they will briefly be on hold.
  2. Create the case during the conversation with the customer, capture the customer’s info and issue in the case. Complete all the information, and save the case before calling the right support team.
  • KB 2988026 Guideline: SR Creation and Case Ownership
  1. If this is a phone transfer, add the service request (SR) number to the SR Number field within the OneCloud case Information.
  2. When you are transferring to a primary team, after you have greeted the customer, give them the case number for their reference.
  • ​​​​​​​​​​​​​​Note: When warm transferring to another advocate, keep it professional and simple. Do not use phrases such as “I’m handing you over to our best technician who will resolve your issue for you” Keep it factual, and don’t make promises on behalf of the next advocate as they may not be able to deliver on them. Here are some sample messages:

I will be transferring you to a team member who will further assist you…

Allow me to transfer you to one of our customer support advocates / specialist who will work with you to resolve your issue….

I am going to transfer you to one of our customer support advocates who will help you ….

I am going to transfer you to another team member who will help get the information you need.

  1. Warm transfer the customer to the “transfer to” advocate.
  2. You should not spend more than three minutes to get to “transfer to” advocates. If it takes more than three minutes, follow the steps below:
  • ​​​​​​​Phone: Switch to cold transfer, continue from step 3 in section 3.2.
  • ​​​​​​​Chat: Inform the customer that the transfer destination support team are all busy and currently not available. Give them information on:
  1. At the beginning of the transfer, you should both exchange your aliases and make a note of these in the case notes.
    Example:
    Advocate 1: Hello, my name is Advocate1 and my alias is adv1, I am calling in from the Office consumer support team.
    Advocate 2: Hi, my name is Advocate2 and my alias is adv2, and you have reached the Windows consumer support team.
  2. Provide the following information to the “transfer to” advocates:
  • The case number (only if you are transferring to the primary team)
  • Summary of the customer's issue and the troubleshooting steps you have done so far
  • Explanation of why you are transferring to them.
  1. Use Qview to review the transfer case in two to four hours, and if this is not taken by a transferred advocate, transfer the case to the Tier 2 queue so a manager can take a look at it.

3.2 Cold transfer process (phone only)

  1. Let the customer know that you are transferring them to the right support team for their issue, and that they will be on hold and then connected to next available advocate.
  2. Create the case during the conversation with the customer, collect the customer’s info and issue in your notes, and make sure you save the case.
  • ​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​KB 2988026 Guideline: SR Creation and Case Ownership
  1. If this is a phone transfer, add the SR number to the SR Number field within OneCloud Case Information.
  2. When you are transferring to a primary team, give the case number to them, and ask the customer to share it with the next advocate.
  3. Cold transfer the customer to the “transfer to” queue.
  4. Disconnect from the call.
  5. Use Qview to review the transfer case in two to four hours, and if this is not taken by a transferred advocate, transfer the case to the Tier 2 queue so a manager can take a look at it.

Manager oversight process:

The Tier 2 (manager) will monitor it for 48 hours. If the case ownership has not been transferred to the intended team, the manager should:

  • Check the case to see if the receiving advocate updated it, but did not take ownership
  • Search ASD to see if a new case has been created for the customer’s issue.

If neither of these happened, follow up with the customer to make sure their issue has been resolved, and if neccessary, transfer the call to the correct team to resolve.

If there are additional case notes or other cases have been created for the customer with the same issue, the manager should proceed with the TOCO process.

  • KB 3121083 Procedure: Transfer of Case Ownership (TOCO Request)

5. Refusals or invalid transfers

An invalid transfer is a transfer you receive that is outside of your support boundaries.

If the “transfer to” advocate won't accept the transfer during the warm transfer discussion, follow the below steps.

  1. See section 1, and double check that the transfer destination is correct. If not, transfer to the right one. If it is the correct destination, continue to step 2.
  2. Attempt the warm transfer at least two times. On your third attempt:
  • Phone: You may use cold transfer.
  • Chat: Guide the customer to the support.microsoft.com site, and assist them on how to reach the right team via the chat.
  1. If you receive an invalid transfer request as a "transfer to" advocate, accept the transfer and explain the proper process to them for the future.

Note: The transfer process is not intended to allow advocates to push a difficult call to another advocate. Follow the transfer guidelines in this article.
