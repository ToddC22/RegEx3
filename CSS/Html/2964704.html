1. Verification process

To create a business listing in Bing, users must verify that they either own or are a legitimate representative of the business. After adding business details in the Bing Places dashboard and selecting submit, users will be taken through the verification process.

If the verification process is successful, the listing is marked as verified and is submitted for publishing. See KB 3138298 Process: Bing Places: Publishing Cycle for further details.

Return to top

1.1 Verification options

Bing Places provides users a list of verification options. The option provided is based on whether the business is new or if it already exists in Bing. 

  • Postal mail
  • Email
  • Phone
  • Text (SMS)
  • Online (only available in EN-US)
  • App (AU, CA, IN, UK, US)

Regardless of the verification method chosen, customer listings will initially be placed in Review status – this allows time for the listing to be automatically or manually reviewed.

This process can take up to four business days. Do not escalate for listings stuck in review status, unless you have seen this still in effect after five or more business days from the point of listing claim/creation.

1.2 Verification for claimed listings 

  • If you change the phone number, this will trigger a postal mail verification request.
  • If you change the business address, this will trigger a phone verification request.

1.3 Verification for a newly created listing 

When a Bing Places user creates a new business listing, they will be presented with verification options: email and postal mail. Email verification is recommended as PIN delivery is faster than postal mail.

  • Recommended verification is by email.
  • Default verification is for postal mail.

2.1 Postal mail verification

The customer will be presented with recommended verification options depending on whether the business already exists in Bing or needs to be created. If other verification options are provided (for example, email or phone), but users cannot verify using the recommended methods, the user can select Post Mail under Alternative methods. The customer can receive the verification PIN at the business address by postal mail.

To use post verification:

  1. If the email verification option is offered, select Alternative methods tab.
  2. Select the option By Post on the verification screen.
  3. Check the displayed address to confirm it is correct.
  4. Use the Attn: field to specify a designated recipient for the PIN postcard if needed.
  5. Select Verify Now to start the process.
  6. “Verification by Post” pop-up window confirms that the user will receive the PIN within five-six business days. Select OK. Note: It can take up to 12 business days for these to be delivered.
  7. PIN received: Once the email PIN is received, the user can log into Bing Places and locate the business within the Home page. The user can select the Enter PIN link next to the business to enter the verification PIN they have received.
  8. The user selects Submit to complete verification. If the PIN is valid, Bing Places provides a successful verification confirmation pop up.
  9. Within the Home page, the “Enter PIN” and “Resend PIN” links are no longer shown as the listing has been verified (not yet published).

Note: If users need more help, select Help, located in the upper right corner, will display popular help content related to the page in addition to a link to complete help content.

2.2 Email verification

You can verify your business listing with Bing Places for Business quickly and easily through your business email. 

  • The email verification PIN is sent to the business email account. 
  • Email verification is not available for public domain email accounts such as Outlook, Yahoo and Gmail accounts. This is to protect your listing on Bing and prevent unauthorized claims.
  • Only business domain email accounts must be used.
  • Email verification is not available for bulk uploads. 

To use email verification:

  1. Within the Verify your business window, select the Receive your verification PIN via email option.
  2. Complete the business email domain address and select Check email. If the email domain is valid and supported by Bing Places, confirmation message will be displayed as shown below.

    [Asset 4488027]

    Note: If an email domain is not available or supported for this business, user must select another verification option.
  3. Confirm that the business email address is correct and then select Verify now.

    Note: A confirmation pop up window informs the user that an email will be sent to the business email account provided within one hour.
  4. Email received: User will receive an email with the PIN, and instructions on what this refers to and how to apply it to verify their listing.
  5. Home: Once the email PIN is received, the user can log into Bing Places and locate the business within the Home page. The user can select Enter PIN next to the business to enter the verification PIN they have received.
    Note: If user needs a new PIN, user will need to go through verification again by selecting Resend PIN.
  6. PIN received: If PIN has been received, the user selects Verify now, enters the PIN, and selects Submit to complete verification. If the user is starting at the Home page, they will select Enter PIN next to the business listing first. If PIN is valid, Bing Places provides a successful verification confirmation pop up.
  7. Within the Home page, the “Enter PIN” and “Resend PIN” links are no longer shown as the listing has been verified (Note that it may not yet be published).

2.3 Phone verification

Verifying via Phone is an option offered only if the existing business listing already contains the business phone number that has not been edited.

To use phone verification:

  1. Under the Recommended methods tab, select the phone option. Note: If the Phone number is incorrect, user should use the other recommended verification options (For example, email, Postal Mail).
  2. Add any extension as required in the slot provided.
  3.  Phone PIN received: Within the Verify by Phone window, enter the PIN and select Submit. If the PIN is not received, user can select.
  4. Call me Again to receive the phone call again.
  5. Home page: If verification was not completed, the user will see Resend PIN and Enter PIN links next to that listing until the PIN verification is complete. The user can choose to use a different verification method by selecting Resend PIN.

    [Asset 4488028]

    Note: If user needs a new PIN, user will need to go through verification again by selecting Resend PIN.
  6. PIN received: If the PIN has been received, the user selects Verify now, enters the PIN, and selects Submit to complete verification. If the user is starting at the Home page, they will select Enter PIN next to the business listing first. If PIN is valid, Bing Places provides a successful verification confirmation pop up.
  7.  Within the Home page, the “Enter PIN” and “Resend PIN” links are no longer shown as the listing has been verified.

2.4 Text (SMS) verification

The user can verify their business listing with Bing Places for Business quickly and easily through text message. They can complete the claim and verification process in one session. The primary phone number must already be known by Bing Places and must be able to receive text messages. No charges are incurred from the SMS option. Additional phone numbers entered in the Other phone section of a listing's contact details will not be considered for SMS verification.

Note: To avoid failed SMS attempts, Bing Places will only offer SMS verification to mobile and non-fixed VOIP numbers. (VOIP numbers will see a warning, asking the customer to confirm their number can receive SMS messaging, before they can select Verify Now.) Fixed line, toll-free, and unrecognized numbers will only see an alert, informing them that SMS/Test verification is not available for the phone number they have provided.

To use text (SMS) verification:

  1. Within the Verify your business window, select Send SMS/text to option.
  2. Confirm that the phone number displayed is correct and then select Verify now.
    Note: If the phone number is not correct, select another verification option.
  3. SMS PIN received: Enter PIN and select Submit.
  4. Home: If verification was not completed, the user can return to the Home page at a later time and select the Enter PIN link next to the business.

    Note: To receive another verification PIN, the user will need to go through verification again by selecting Resend PIN.
  5. PIN received: If the PIN has been received, the user selects Verify now, enters the PIN, and selects Submit to complete verification. If the user is starting at the Home page, they will select Enter PIN next to the business listing first. If the PIN is valid, Bing Places provides a successful verification confirmation pop up.

    Note: Within the Home page, the Enter PIN and Resend PIN links will no longer be shown if the listing has been verified just not yet published.

2.5 App verification

When postal verification is the only option available to a user and they select Verify now to request the PIN, a pop-up will prompt the advertiser to either Install our mobile app and receive your verification PIN instantly or Continue with verification by post:

  1. The user can choose to either Continue with verification by post to resume the standard postal PIN process, or they can select the app download button for their operating system.
  2. Once a user has installed the app, their listing will be visible in the app as verification not started, with an option to select Verify your business.
  3. Selecting Verify your business (in the mobile app only) will then prompt the user to choose a verification method:
    [Asset 4491459]

  4. The phone/SMS is sent to the number in the business listing. Once received, the user can then enter their PIN in the app. 
    Note: If phone/SMS verification options are missing, ask the advertiser to install the latest version of the app and try again. This should resolve their issue.
  5. A confirmation pop-up will let the customer know their listing is under review and will be verified in one to two business days. The listing will show in the UI as In Review. Note: Listings can stay in Review status for up to four business days.
  6. A member of engineering will then review the business manually, and either approve or reject.

See section 9.1 for guidance on how to support customers with rejected listings.

3. Instant verification (within Bing Places UI) 

In addition to the standard verification workflows, the Bing Places UI offers eligible listings the chance to use instant verification methods to quickly confirm listing accuracy.

Instant verification methods will be available within the My Listings page of the UI and highlighted directly to customers from the listing page. Options include:

  • Phone
  • SMS
  • Email

After the customer selects an option, a pop-up may appear asking them to confirm additional aspects of their listing such as the address or phone. The pop-up will indicate the current confidence level for each attribute and explain that attributes with lower confidence may not show on SERP.

Once a customer confirms or edits their information, they can select Proceed with verification and complete instant the process using their preferred verification method.

3.1 Sign-in as (Admin) 

The “Sign in as” functionality allows you to log into Bing Places as the customer. With this functionality, you can:

  • View information available to the customer.
  • View listings that have been claimed or verified.
  • Verify a listing on behalf of a customer. 
  1. Locate the Sign In As link in the dropdown under the account name in the upper right.
  2. Select the Sign In As link, a pop-up will appear prompting you to provide the customer's email address. Enter the customer’s email address and select Sign In to log in to Bing Places as that customer.
  3. After signing in as a customer, you can view listings that have been claimed or created by customers as well as see the Admin Verify link and the Bing Places ID (BPID):

    [Asset 4488035]

    Note: See the Admin verify function section below before verifying on behalf of a user.

    Identify the Bing Places ID (BPID) for a given listing, for example, 562949954259549. (ID required for certain ticket escalations sent to Engineering teams and troubleshooting using Local Probe). This can be found by selecting the business name on the Home page. The following page will show business details along with the Bing Places Id.

3.2 Admin verify function

This feature is only available to advocates. You can only use this feature when you can verify that the customer is either the owner or representative of the business.

Note: If you are unable to verify a customer as the owner/representative of the business they will need to use the Resend PIN for the business listing within the Home page.

In either case, educate the customer on how to self-manage their verification via the 'Resend PIN' option, going forward, and teach them how to use the Home page to view the list of actions for business listings or view the verification and publication status.

3.5 In-product guidance for verification and publishing issues

Use the information in this section to assist customers in resolving their Bing Places verification issues. 

  • Postal mail not received: 
    • Customers should receive the PIN in five to six business days. The business listing will continue to show the Enter PIN and Resend PIN options until the PIN has been entered by the customer. Note: It can take up to 12 business days before the PIN is delivered.
    • If, after 12 days, the customer still doesn’t have their postal mail, use the Resend PIN functionality to reissue the PIN. 
    • If Resend PIN fails, see KB 3189002 Process: Bing Places: Fortification versus Verification for more information on how to assist the customer. 

New email/phone/text PIN required: The PIN is not working, or the customer never received the PIN for phone, text, or email verification. User can use the Resend PIN link located under the business listing to start verification again. When selecting Resend PIN, the user can choose any available verification method. They are not required to use their previous choice.

  • Incorrect pin: If the PIN is correct, users will receive a notification popup which contains Enter PIN link to try again or OK button to dismiss and return to the Home page.
  • Publishing status: From the Home page, users can select a business listing name to drill into more information. Users will find the Publish status window if business listing completed the verification flow and have progressed into the publishing flow.
  • If the customer asks when their business listing will be published: If published, Users will find options to view the published listing on Bing and share their business listings via Twitter or Facebook. If it is not published yet, they can go within the My Listing page, and users will find a Publish status (see screenshot below) window that shows the expected publish date, or it will show if there is a problem with publishing and tell them to contact support.


[Asset 4488035]

  • I need help:
    • The customer doesn’t understand what to do next: Regardless of what page the user is in within Bing Places, the user can select Help at the top right corner and it will display the popular and relevant articles based on the page that user is currently on.
    • The customer thinks they need support: When users select the Contact Support link, they are redirected to the support page. Within the Support page, users will be provided with the popular articles within the FAQ list.

3.6 Webmaster tools integration

Bing Places now integrates with Webmaster Tools. Note: To use this feature, a listing must be claimed, not added. The URL information in the Listing must also match the URL info on Bing Local...if these do not match, the customer will need to complete PIN verification instead.

From the Home > My listing drilldown page, you can see a widget promoting “Bing Webmaster.”

Customers who have already registered and set up their business's website with Bing Webmaster will see some tables of information automatically pulled from Bing Webmaster into their dashboard. To drill further into the Bing Webmaster data or edit, the customer can select the links below the tables which take them to the Webmaster UI.

Benefits of Bing places and Bing Webmaster Tools integration:

  • Single sign-on: Customers who uses Microsoft account to sign into Bing Places account can access Bing Webmaster Tools without creating a new user for Bing Webmaster. Likewise, Bing Webmaster Tools customers can access Bing Places without creating a new Bing Places user.
  • Faster verification: When a customer claims a business listing in Bing Places, it will be verified immediately if the customer has already completed verification for that business within Bing Webmaster.
    [Asset 4023568]
  • Additional verification options: Using the Webmaster verification, customers can choose between three methods in the Bing Places UI:

    When the Bing Webmaster verification option is selected, the scrollable box will appear just below and contains instructions for each option.

    If the customer's site has not been set up correctly for one of the verification options, they will receive an error message explaining possible issues.

5.1 Search for a business listing 

Users can search for a specific business by either logging into Bing Places or using the Get Started option from the Bing Places homepage. Ask users to take the following steps to search for a business:

  1. Log into Bing Places with a valid Microsoft account or select Get Started from the Bing Places homepage.
  2. Search for a business listing by entering one of the following:
    • Business phone number
    • Business name and location
    • Specify country from drop-down list
  3. Select Search to display all business listings that match the search criteria.

9.1 Rejected listing

Listings that have been rejected in the Bing Places dashboard are unable to use the Resend PIN option.

If you are contacted by a customer with rejected listings, we can help in one of two ways:

  1. Advise the customer to create a new listing and go through the creation/verification process again.
  2. We can collect proof of business ownership from the customer, and escalate to Global Support to help appeal the rejection. Accepted proofs are:
    • Storefront photograph.
    • Utility bill in the name/address of the business.
    • Government-approved document (of any kind) that contains the business name/address, as proof of business operation.

If a customer can provide at least one of the items listed in option two, you can escalate the case to Global Support for assistance.

9.2 Postal PIN verification scenarios

Customer Issue: Location does not accept mail

The customer is unable to receive mail at the address listed for the business listing.

  • Ask customer to work with the local post office to forward mail to the appropriate address. Offer the admin verification method as an alternative, provided the customer can prove ownership of the business.

Customer Issue: PIN not received

The customer does not receive PIN via post within the five to six-day SLA.

  • Advise the customer it can take up to 12 business days for postal PINs to be delivered. If the customer contacts after 12 days to report the PIN missing, use Resend PIN.

Customer Issue: PIN sent to wrong address

The customer reports that the pin received is for a business different than the one he or she is trying to verify.

  • As it can take up to 12 business days to send another postcard to the customer, offer the admin verify option as a courtesy. Follow the workflow described previously.

Customer Issue: Error on verification page

The customer attempts to enter the PIN on Bing Places page but receives an error which prevents completion of the verification process.

  • Offer the admin verify option, provided the customer can prove ownership of the business or business listing.

Customer Issue: How to verify

The customer’s questions are not fully answered by the Bing Places FAQ.

  • Fully explain the verification process.

Customer Issue: No option to enter PIN

The customer finds that there is no option to enter a PIN on the Bing Places page.

  • Offer the admin verify option, provided the customer can prove ownership of the business or business listing.

Customer Issue: PIN incorrect

The customer enters the PIN in the Bing Places user interface but receives an error stating that the PIN is incorrect.

  • As it can take up to 12 business days to send another postcard to the customer, offer the admin verify option as a courtesy, provided the customer can prove ownership of the business or business listing.

9.3 Phone PIN verification scenarios

Customer Issue: No call or PIN sent to wrong phone

The customer did not receive a phone call after phone verification was selected.

  • Offer the admin verify option, provided the customer can prove ownership of the business or business listing.

Customer Issue: Automated phone system

Some businesses have an automated answering service tied to the business phone, so phone verification will not work.

  • Offer the admin verify option, provided the customer can prove ownership of the business or business listing.

10. FAQ

Q: What if the customer has a company website and public email address, but the domain registrant does not verify ownership?

A: Let the customer know that we can send the PIN to a company email account and request a company email address. Also state that the email must match the company domain. For example, if the company site is Starbucks.com, then the email must be something like name@starbucks.com.

Q: What if the listing does not have a website or company email?

A: If this is the case, the customer will need to use the postal mail verification since we are unable to verify ownership using other methods.

Q: What if the customer wants to retrieve a PIN for a listing that is no longer in that location?

A: We cannot give the PIN if the customer is no longer at that location. Escalate to GDP/BP and the team will remove the listing after validating with other source providers.

Q: What if the customer states that we emailed the incorrect PIN?

A: The customer may have submitted the listing multiple times. Provide the PIN code for each submission and include the submission date with the PIN. Let the customer know that it was submitted multiple times and one of these PINs should work.

Q: How long does it take to publish a listing?

A: Publishing a new listing or a modified listing can take up to two weeks (10 business days).

Q: I didn't receive my PIN, so how do I verify my listing?

A: Customers do have the option to request a new PIN in the user interface by simply selecting the verify link next to their listing again. However, our support advocates can verify a listing on behalf of a customer by going through the admin verification process.
