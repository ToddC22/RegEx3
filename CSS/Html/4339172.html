Summary


Who should follow this procedure?
  • Premier General Technical Support Engineers using Rave to support Office 365 cloud customers.

What is this procedure about?

  • This procedure describes the process to be used when a Premier General Technical Support Engineer needs to transfer a critical case in Rave to Premier Escalation Engineer.
When should this procedure be followed?
  • When a Premier customer requests a Non-Critical case to be elevated to Critical severity and the case needs to be transferred to a Technical Premier Escalation Engineer.
back to top

Assumptions

  • When cases arrive in Rave as Critical severity, they are handled by in-house Premier engineers/Premier Escalation team first.
  • Incident Manager (IM) information is available by navigating to the company's page in the Support information section on the Profile tab.

 

Commerce and Data Protection case handling procedure

  • When a technical support engineer sees a Commerce or Data Protection related critical case in a technical support area, transfer the case to EnterpriseCommerce or Data Protection support area accordingly.
  • When a Commerce/Data Protection advocate sees a critical case, engage customer and reset expectation, downgrade the case severity to non-critical, and work the case per regular procedure.
  • Commerce offers 24x5 (English) support with Regional assistance and limited support during weekends. For subscription enablement during the weekends, see Commerce after hours support in KB 2500396. Weekend Commerce support will only be provided in English including Critical Severities.
  • For Data Protection Support topics and hours, see
    Office 365 Data Protection for Global English section in 2500396.

Procedure

There are three ways for a Premier customer to raise a CritSit case in O365:

  • Premier customer can check the "critical issue" box in Microsoft 365 Admin Center to raise a critical case online in Rave. 
  • Premier customer can also call in to open a critical case over the phone.
  • From an existing case, support engineers can go to "Case action" and choose "Update severity" to change the ticket severity in Rave to "Critical" based on a request from a Premier customer.

[Asset 4339185]

Support engineers will be able to identify Critical cases in Rave:

  • Rave ticket will show "CRITSIT" tag next to the case number.
  • On the Case Summary panel on the right side of the Rave case you will see Severity: Critical.
  • The customer’s IM will be notified by the standard CritSit procedure.

Critical case handling procedure

When customer requests a case to be elevated to the Critical Severity during APGC, EMEA or LATAM business hours, customer language specific resources would be eligible to take the case:

  1. The expected initial response for these critical situations remains 1 hour (the current defined in Classic support). However, these cases are likely to be the priority when looking into unassigned cases (at least to contact the customer and scope the case to understand the real business impact).
  2. If the case is scoped as a CritSit, it should meet one or more of the requirements below:
    • The problem has been determined to be unrelated to an active Service Incident.
    • The IM or customer requests to increase to CritSit. (If IM or customer requests a regular case escalation or ask for a senior engineer, work with your TA or manager to contact the escalation team).
    • A high business impacting issue where a technical roadblock has been reached, meeting the CritSit definition described in article 4343326.
  3. If customer requests to elevate a case to critical severity and support verifies that it’s a known SI, FL support can keep the case and follow KB 2573241 for Office 365 SI procedure.
    Note: Support Engineer should set proper customer expectation.
  4. Before the Premier General Support Engineer escalates the case, the engineer needs to prepare below template first, then work with your TA/TM/QM (who is a Rave admin).

 

COMPLETED ACTIONS TAKEN AND RESULTS: (required)​

(Repro steps and Test/Results) ​

==================== ​

PLAN (required)​

======================​

  1. Rave admin will increase the case severity to critical, refresh the page to ensure that “CritSit” badge is displayed, then transfer the case to the proper support area:

    [Asset 4466454]

    • Select the support area of the case and add a justification. Transfer will only allow simplified case transfer back to the same support area.
      [Asset 4466455]
    • Justification will show on the case description on the unassigned view:
      [Asset 4466456]
    • Then search for the case again without taking over the case ownership, and place above template in the internal case notes section of the case.
  2. Premier EEs should work the escalated CritSit case until it’s closed, do not transfer the case back to general support area even if the case severity was lowered during trouble shooting stage.
    (EXCEPTION) For Japanese language cases, Premier EEs can transfer the case back to general support area after severity was lowered if the case doesn’t meet the CritSit criteria.
  3. If the case is scoped to be an on-prem issue, engage the IM immediately to start a new Rave case for the on-prem issue. The Support Engineer will document the details in Rave and close the existing Rave case as a duplicate.
