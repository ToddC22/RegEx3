Procedure: Xbox: Problems with a delivered product

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Xbox

What is this article about?

This article describes how to assist customers who received a product through repair or exchange, including Advance Exchange, that is damaged, defective, incomplete/missing, or incorrect and that is still within the warranty period. This includes all Xbox branded products (consoles, games, and accessories) including PC accessories without a related console.

2. Process

2.1 Damaged in transit/Damaged products

There are occasions when a customer will receive a package that has sustained damages that may not have been reported or a product that was in less-than-perfect condition when it left the service center. To help these customers, it is important to document the occurrence for the quickest resolution and best customer experience.

As all repaired or exchanged serialized devices receive the longer of the remainder of the original warranty or a 90-day repair warranty and accessories receive the unbundled accessory warranty for their country (see KB 967172 Procedure: Xbox: Global Xbox repair and fulfillment information and options), this section only applies within the first 30 days after receipt of an item from the service center. Beyond that period, the item should be considered defective; see defective products section for more information.

1. Verify the customer Microsoft account information. See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

2. Request the service request number (SR) for the order in question.

3. If the packaging is not damaged, ask the customer to inspect the contents for cosmetic, structural, or water damage.

  • If there is no cosmetic, structural, or water damage to the product, follow the troubleshooting in defective products section.
  • If there is only cosmetic damage (scratches or marks in the plastic, uneven color on the outer plastic shell, or so dirty or dusty that it cannot easily be cleaned) and no structural or water damage to the product, email the customer requesting that they reply to the email and attach photos of the product that include the damaged areas to document the issue.

Important: Photos must be attached to the reply, not pasted into the body of the reply. Recommend that the customer reply from a laptop or desktop, as most cell phones paste photos into the email instead of attaching them. Pasted photos will be stripped from the reply and not usable for this purpose.

Have the customer test the device for functionality. If the product is functional ask the customer if they will accept the product.

  • If they agree, note in the SR accordingly, and then save and close the SR.
  • If the customer insists on a replacement accessory, offer to place another accessory exchange order. As the warranty does not cover cosmetic damage, standard shipping options apply. If no free of charge SKUs are available, create a new SR and skip to step 5 to engage the Offline Team.
  • If the customer insists on a replacement console and is in an exchange only country (see KB 967172 Procedure: Xbox: Global Xbox repair and fulfillment information and options), create a new SR and process a new exchange (repair) order for the device.
  • Include the original repair SR number and a brief description of the issue in the order comments field.

Note:

All Kinect sensors are exchanged globally, not repaired.

  • If the customer insists on a replacement console and is in a same unit repair country, create a new SR and process a corrective repair order for the device. Include the original repair SR number and brief description of the issue in the order comments field. Skip to step 5 after creating the corrective repair order to have the Offline Team request a console exchange.
  • If there is structural or water damage to the product, do not troubleshoot further. Email the customer, requesting that they reply to the email and attach photos of the product that include the damaged areas.
  • Offer to place another accessory exchange order. Create a new SR and corrective order, including a reference to the original SR in the order details. If the customer contests paying for shipping their item to the SCV, where applicable, skip to step 5 to engage the Offline Team.
  • If the customer insists on a replacement console and is in an exchange only country, open a new SR and process a corrective exchange (repair) order for the device.
  • If the customer insists on a replacement console and is in a same unit repair country, create a new SR and corrective repair order for the device. Include the original repair SR number and brief description of the issue in the order comments field. Skip to step 5 after creating the repair order to request a console exchange.

4. If the packaging is damaged or water damaged, email the customer on the original order SR, requesting that they reply to the email and attach photos of the package exterior, including the tracking number and contents.

Important: Photos must be attached to the reply, not pasted into the body of the reply. Recommend that the customer reply from a laptop or desktop, as most cell phones paste photos into the email instead of attaching them. Pasted photos will be stripped from the reply and not usable for this purpose.

  • If there is no visible water or structural damage to the product, perform troubleshooting. If the product is functional, ask if the customer will accept the product.
  • If they agree, note the SR accordingly, and then save and close the SR.
  • If the customer insists on a replacement accessory, offer to place another accessory exchange order. Create a new SR and corrective order, including a reference to the original SR in the order details. Standard shipping requirements apply. As the product is not damaged, the courier will not reimburse against a claim.
  • If the customer insists on a replacement console and is in an exchange only country, create a new SR and corrective (repair) exchange order.

Note:

All Kinect sensors are exchanged globally, not repaired.

  • If the customer insists on a replacement console and is in a same unit repair country, creative another SR and process a corrective repair order for the device.
  • Include the original repair SR number and brief description of the issue in the order comments field.
  • Proceed to step 5 after creating the repair order to have the Offline Team request a console exchange.
  • If the product is visibly or water damaged or non-functional, have the customer replace all the materials in the package, leaving it unsealed.
  • Proceed to step 5 to have the package retrieved by the shipper and a claim filed (for serialized devices) or replace the accessory.

5. For package retrieval requests, console exchanges, or missing free of charge exchange SKU’s for accessories, utilize P-Chat, suppliers IM tool or your sites specific method to contact your Offline Team for assistance.

If directed by your Offline Team, transfer the customer’s SR to your specific Variance Queue. For transferring, refer to KB 817557 Guideline: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement.

For Offline Teams, after receiving instruction to escalate from the GSCES team, include the following information:

  • Why was the issue unable to be resolved on site?
  • Have you received approval from the GSCES team to escalate? Use the following taxonomy:
  • Reason for Call – I need help with a repair or exchange order
  • Root Cause – Customer received damaged or defective or missing parts
  • Solution Delivered – Engage to

Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA)

  • Engagement Type – Problem with Product Delivered

6. The following information is required before transfer:

  • Repair/fulfilment order SR number in question.
  • Customer address.
  • Carrier.
  • Tracking number or enter "EMEA FO" for EMEA fulfillments.
  • Description of the issue, noting “Damaged package retrieval,” “Console exchange request,” or “Accessory exchange” as the first line. Include as much detail as possible regarding package and product damage, troubleshooting, if performed, and the SR for which pictures were received.

7. Advise the customer to expect a response according to the SLA information that is provided.

2.2 Defective products

1. Verify the customer Microsoft account information. See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

2. Request the SR number for the order in question.

3. Ask the customer for details about the defective product.

4. Perform full troubleshooting on the replacement product.

If the product in question is a console or Kinect and the received item is failing in a similar fashion, also consider environmental factors and alternative causes, such as a bad power supply, faulty power strip/surge protector/power adapter, incompatible cables, or damaged HDMI ports.

After confirming the product is defective:

  • Create a new SR and corrective repair order for console or Kinect sensor.
  • Include the original repair SR number and brief description of the issue in the order comments field.

Notes:

  • If the customer is requesting a console exchange in a country that only offers same-unit repair, proceed to step 5 to have the Offline Team request a console exchange.
  • For Advance Exchange (AE) orders, the customer will need to return the ticketed console to complete the open order before a corrective repair order, including AE, can be created. Returning the advanced console is strongly discouraged, as it may result in the customer being charged the hold fee.
  • For a defective accessory, offer to place another accessory exchange order. If the customer contests paying for shipping their item to the SCV, where applicable, create a new SR and go to step 5.

5. For console exchanges and review requests for multiple replacement items, utilize P-Chat, suppliers IM tool or your sites specific method to contact your Offline Team for assistance. If requested by your Offline Team then transfer the customer’s SR to your specific Variance Queue. See KB 817557 Guideline: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement for more information.

For Offline Teams, after receiving instruction to escalate from the GSCES team, provide below information:

  • Why was the issue unable to be resolved on site?
  • Have you received approval from the GSCES team to escalate? Use the following taxonomy:
  • Reason for Call - I need help with a repair or exchange order
  • Root Cause – Customer received damaged or defective or missing parts
  • Solution Delivered – Engage to Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA)
  • Engagement Type – Problem with Product Delivered

6. The following information is required before transfer:

  • Repair/fulfilment order SR number in question.
  • Customer address.
  • Carrier.
  • Tracking number or enter "EMEA FO" for EMEA fulfillments.
  • Description of issue, noting “Console exchange request” or “Defective product exchange request” as the first line.

7. Advise the customer to expect a response according to the SLA information that is provided.

2.3 Incomplete/missing products

This scenario includes:

  • One or more parts of an ordered product were not received in the delivered shipment.
  • Missing return shipping labels for Advanced Exchange orders.
  • Additional items sent with an order, but not returned from the Service Center (SCV).

1. Verify the customer Microsoft account information. See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

2. Request the SR number for the order in question.

3. Ask the customer for details about the incomplete/missing products.

  • Determine what the customer sent in and was expecting in return.
  • Determine what the customer actually received.
  • If the product that the customer sent in was end of life/end of support, refer to KB 967172Procedure: Xbox: Global Xbox repair and fulfillment information and options, regarding end-of-life products.
  • If the expectation was for the complete replacement of an item with multiple parts, such as the stereo headset adapter and USB charging cable but only one item was received, send a replacement item (SEN FO) if available. If not available, skip to step 5.

Note:

A courtesy replacement (SEN FO) should be limited to one incident per year and should be considered a concession.

  • If the customer included products that were not part of the repair (for example, power supply, controller, game and media discs) explain to the customer that Microsoft does not guarantee that they will receive such products back, as Microsoft is not responsible for returning anything in addition to the ticket of the repair product.
  • For AOC, the service center will only return retail game discs. Video and other non-retail discs will not be returned and are not eligible for good will gestures.
  • For LATAM, not including Brazil, no discs are returned.
  • For Brazil, all included items are returned separately from the replacement console.
  • For EMEA, all included items are returned with the repaired or replacement console.
  • For rest of world (RoW), if the unreturned item was manufactured or published by Microsoft or all attempts to remove disks from the console were documented as being unsuccessful, proceed to step 4.

Any other unreturned third-party items are not eligible for replacement or compensation.

4. Check the order if there are any additional comments regarding the products received.

For first-party published (1PP) games (see KB 969167 Informational: Xbox: Game Support) provide a token for the customer using the Token Tool. See KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST). 1-time exception allowed per account.

For third-party published (3PP) games: Continue to step 5 to escalate for a one-time exception.

5. To check the status or request the return of additional items included with an order, utilize P-Chat, suppliers IM tool or your sites specific method to contact your Offline Team for assistance, if requested by your Offline Team then transfer the customer’s SR to your specific variance Queue. For transferring, refer to KB 817557 Guideline: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement Tier 1 Variance Queues.

For Offline Teams, after receiving instruction to escalate from the GSCES team, provide below information and the following taxonomy:

  • Why was the issue unable to be resolved on site?
  • Have you received approval from the GSCES team to escalate? Use the following taxonomy:
  • Reason for Call - I need help with a repair or exchange order.
  • Root Cause – Customer received damaged or defective or missing parts.
  • Solution Delivered – Engage to.

Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA).

  • Engagement Type – Problem with Product Delivered.

6. If no replacement disc has been provided and the customer passes steps 1-3, follow the next steps based on game type below:

  • 1PP - Use the Token Tool (15.4 Issue Token for steps) to provide a token for the customer. 1-time exception allowed per account.
  • 3PP - Reach to the Offline Team to handle a CSV. 1-time exception allowed per account.
  1. The Offline advocate will need to look up the PDP for customer region to get the current MSRP for the game.
  2. Use the customer shipping email address in MST to see if there is any sales tax to include in the total amount. (Sales tax rates can also be found on bing.com, or you can ask the customer if they know the local sales tax rate.)
  3. Confirm the amount of CSV which will be provided to customer (MSRP + sales tax), so they can purchase the game from the Xbox Online store.
  • Use reason code: Goodwill_SKU
  • Walk the customer through purchasing the game to replace the disc.

7. The following information is required before transfer:

  • Repair/Fulfilment order SR number.
  • Customer address.
  • Carrier tracking number or enter "EMEA FO" for EMEA fulfillments.
  • Description of issue, including the title/description of unreturned or missing items on the first line.

8. Advise the customer to expect a response according to the SLA information that is provided.

2.4 Incorrect products

The following process applies for incorrectly ordered items; for example, the customer needed a controller but received a controller battery door. Or the customer sent in an in-region console and received an out-of-region console from the service center and requires a repair.

  1. Verify the customer Microsoft account. See KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.
  2. Request the SR number for the order in question.
  3. Ask the customer for details about the incorrect product.
    • Determine what the customer sent in and was expecting in return.
    • Determine what the customer actually received.
      • If the product that the customer sent in was end of life/end of support, refer to KB 967172 Procedure: Xbox: Global Xbox repair and fulfillment information and options regarding end-of-life products.
  4. For an incorrectly ordered accessory, skip to step 6.
  5. For customers who are only provided out-of-region exchange options on a second or subsequent repair attempt, review the previous repair order. If the customer relocated after the prior order or retained an out-of-region console, explain that their product’s warranty is limited to the region in which it was purchased or previously exchanged, as appropriate.
    • If the previous order was placed in the same country and was not a same unit repair to maintain an out of region console, proceed to step 6.
    • If the customer relocated after the prior order or retained an out-of-region console, explain that warranty coverage is only applicable in the country of origin. Offer paid options as available.

Note:

Hong Kong, Singapore, and Taiwan consoles are interchangeable, as are consoles for many EMEA countries.

  1. For customers unexpectedly receiving out-of-region offers for a previously repaired or exchanged console, where an in-region offer should be available, utilize P-Chat, suppliers IM tool or your sites specific method to contact your Offline Team for assistance, if requested by your Offline Team then transfer the customer’s SR to your specific variance Queue. For transferring, refer to KB 817557 817557 Guideline: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement Tier 1 Variance Queues.

    For Offline Teams, after receiving instruction to escalate from the GSCES team, provide below information and the following taxonomy:
    • Why was the issue unable to be resolved on site?
    • Have you received approval from the GSCES team to escalate? Use the following taxonomy:
      • Reason for Call - I need help with a repair or exchange order.
      • Root Cause – Customer received wrong replacement unit.
      • Solution Delivered – Engage to Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA).
      • Engagement Type – Problem with Product Delivered.
  2. Make sure that the following information is captured before transfer:
    • Repair order SR number.
    • Customer address.
    • Carrier.
    • Tracking number.
    • Description of issue, noting “Out of region issue” or “Incorrectly ordered accessory” as the first line, as appropriate.
  3. Advise the customer to expect a response according to the SLA information that is provided.

2.5 Customer lost time on subscription due to service order

When a customer has had a service order on an Xbox One console and contacts Support to request compensation (1-month max per console service order) for the lost time on an Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, Xbox Live Gold, and/or Game Pass subscription advocates may provide compensation if the customer meets the following requirements:

  1. Customer has had a service order on their Xbox One console within the last 30 days.
  2. Customer has/had an Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, Xbox Live Gold and/or Game Pass subscription during the service order time frame.
  3. Customer has not been given a 1-month sub for this service order already.

If the customer meets all three requirements, advocates may provide the customer one 1-month Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, Xbox Live Gold, and/or Game Pass token per console service order via token tool. Utilize the “Goodwill gesture” reason in MST when issuing the token.
