What is this article about?

This article gives instructions for the cancellation or refunds of non-gaming related digital content purchases made by customers.

  • For all gaming related digital content refunds and returns, see KB 4505053 Guideline: Xbox: Refund eligibility for Digital Game products.
  • For Xbox Refund Request Solution available countries and procedures, see KB 4485105 Procedure: Xbox: Refund Webform Process.

1. Microsoft Store refund and return guideline

For Microsoft Store refund guidelines, see KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund. All purchases are final and non-refundable, unless the customer’s scenario falls within an exception listed in this article.

Determine if the customer’s refund request for their digital content is gaming related. If so, see KB 4505053 Guideline: Xbox: Refund eligibility for Digital Game products.
 

Unless otherwise stated in section 2.2, purchases may be refunded within 14 days of purchase. For billing errors that were made by Microsoft, purchases may be refunded within 90 days of purchase. See section 9, subsection g. of the Microsoft Services Agreement under Payment Terms.

For all exceptions listed, if a refund is issued for a purchase made with Virtual Currency (v-bucks), you must use the Return Line Item selection and not the Refund Line Item.

2. Exceptions

2.1 Regional exceptions

Australia: Consumers have the right to repair, replace, or refund in certain situations. Refer to aka.ms/acl. Where a consumer has that right, follow the guidelines provided by the ACL (Australian Consumer Law).

See KB 4100081 Guideline: Microsoft and the Australian and New Zealand Consumer Law.

Notes:

  • Brazil consumer law, under which customers are eligible to cancellation or refund for seven calendar days after the purchase, does not apply to online purchases.
  • Portugal consumer law, under which customers are eligible to cancellation or refund for 14 calendar days after the purchase, does not apply to online purchases.

2.2 Exceptions by scenario

Accidental purchase scenario

Scenario

When a customer purchases an item by accident. This includes the following examples:

  • Incorrect item purchased.
  • Incorrect version purchased.
  • Used wrong account for purchase.
  • Already owns all or part of the content, such as:
  • Individual downloadable content that is part of a season pass.
  • Or an individual TV episode that is part of a season.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • The purchase is a single transaction.
  • The purchase price is 150 USD or less, or the local currency equivalent.
  • The customer contacted support within five days of the original purchase.
  • The customer has not received another refund for an accidental purchase within 365 days.

Procedure

Use Return Line Item to process the return/refund.

Frequency

Limited. Customers are only eligible for this exception if it’s been at least 365 days since the last exception or no refund exceptions have ever been made.

Content does not match description in Microsoft Store scenario

Scenario

Facts within the product description are incorrect. This exception does not include opinion-based elements in the description or pricing errors.

Fact examples:

  • The rental period says it’s 48 hours but expired in 24 hours.
  • The game is listed as having 10 free levels, but it only includes one or only allows the customer to play one without requiring DLC or additional in-game purchases. Requiring people to beat a level to get to the next level does not qualify.
  • When the customer purchases an episode of a game, but when they try to play it, a different episode or different game plays instead.

Refund criteria
  • Customer describes this scenario, and you can confirm the discrepancy.

Additional notes
  • Issues with content on non-Microsoft published apps or games need to be referred to the publisher if there is a technical issue.
  • Issues caused by to the user’s environment are not eligible for refunds, such as network issues.

Procedure

Use Return Line Item to process the return/refund.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Customer can’t use the purchased product scenario

Scenario

Issues where the customer successfully completes a purchase, but has been unable to download, install, launch, play, or use the content due to a distribution, fulfilment, or licensing failure. This includes in-app purchases where the customer cannot access the add-on content.

Note: This does not include streaming quality issues.
 

Refund criteria
  • A Microsoft service outage is causing the problem.
  • The problem is due to a failure associated with Microsoft Store, Xbox services, or a technical defect in the content.

Additional notes
  • Technical issues associated with specific third-party apps, games, or add-ons may need to be referred to the publisher.
  • Issues caused by to the user’s environment are not eligible for refunds, such as network issues.

Procedure

Use Return Line Item to process the return/refund.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Customer can’t use the purchased product due to a restriction scenario

Scenario

Issues where the customer’s content has some restriction (such as age or market), and they are unable to use their content.

Refund criteria
  • The content was given to the customer.
  • The purchased content has limitation.
  • The purchaser was not aware of the restriction on the user’s account.

Procedure

Use Return Line Item to process the return/refund.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Microsoft Store Content Policies: https://msdn.microsoft.com/library/windows/apps/dn764944.aspx

Refund criteria
  • Customer is seeing a clear violation of the content policy.

Use Return Line Item to process the return/refund.

Content/app/game no longer available in the Microsoft Store scenario

Scenario

The customer is trying to download a purchased item and cannot find it within the Microsoft Store/marketplace it was purchased. This includes in-app/in-game purchases but does not include video or audio rentals or streaming.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • Content was removed from the marketplace within seven days of purchase.
  • Customer has not already installed/used the content.

Procedure
  1. Validate installation/usage through recent activity in MST.
  2. Use Return Line Item to proceed the return/refund.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Return to top

Pre-ordered digital content cancellation scenario

Scenario

The customer is requesting to cancel pre-ordered content that they have paid for, but it isn’t available yet.

Refund criteria
  • Item has not yet been released/is not available for use.

Additional notes
  • Content may be preinstalled in certain cases.
  • Cancelling the pre-order does not remove the content from the customer’s computer or device.
  • They will be prompted to purchase the item if they try to access it after a refund is processed.

Procedure
  • Use Return Line Item to process the return/refund.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Duplicate charges scenario

Scenario

The customer was charged multiple times at the same time (within 15 minutes) for the same purchase. Under this scenario, consumable items are not covered. See KB 2969415 Informational: Understanding Game Transaction Types.

Refund criteria
  • You identify duplicate charges occurring within a 15-minute time span for the same item.

Additional note
  • If the customer is reporting issues with consumable content in Xbox games, see KB 969167 Informational: Xbox: Game Support.
     

Procedure
  • Use Return Line Item to process the return/refund.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Return to top

Incorrect price was charged scenario

Scenario

The customer was not charged the price listed in the marketplace or a promotional price was not applied successfully.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • The customer purchased content at regular price during the promotional period listed in the promotions calendar for the region in which they reside.
  • The transaction listing in MST confirms the customer was charged a higher price.

Procedure

For price discrepancies, including active or expired promotions:

  1. Process the refund charges using the Refund Line Item option.
  2. Contact your supervisor and report the issue to Microsoft via CSS Command Center (CSS CC).

Frequency

As it occurs.

Streaming quality issue scenario

Scenario

Lag, excessive buffering, or poor audio or video quality on streaming content

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • A Microsoft service outage is causing the problem.
  • The issue can be attributed to something on Microsoft’s end.

Additional notes
  • Technical issues with content on non-Microsoft published apps or games should be referred to the publisher.
  • Issues caused by to the user’s environment are not eligible for refunds, such as network issues.

Procedure
  • Process the refund using the Return Line Item option.

Frequency

As it occurs.

China Only: Third-party disc replacement scenario

Scenario

All games are considered first-party published titles in this country. If your Technical Advisor or Service Delivery Manager confirms a replacement token is not available, they will authorize a refund on the purchase of the digital title.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • Customer has provided information required in KB 2522626 Procedure: Handling game disc replacement requests and scratched-game disc claims.
  • Technical Advisor (TA) or Service Delivery Manager (SDM) has confirmed replacement token is not available, approving refund for this customer.

Additional notes
  • Technical issues with content on non-Microsoft published apps or games should be referred to the publisher.
  • Issues caused by to the user’s environment are not eligible for refunds, such as network issues.

Procedure
  • Process the refund using the Refund Line Item option.

Frequency

Once per rolling 12-month period.

3. Refunds for gifted digital content

3.1 Guidelines

For Xbox token redeeming issue, see KB 818928 Procedure: Xbox: Code redemption issues (prepaid, trial, in-game, and subscription).

  • Returnable 14 days from send date or until token is redeemed, whichever comes first.
  • You can view the token status via Token Lookup tool on MST. See KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool.
  • Gift cards can only be refunded via the purchasers’ account. If the gift receiver contacted support, ask the purchaser to contact support.
  • Gift cards purchased through a third-party cannot be handled by Microsoft, redirect customers to the third-party.
  • For Australia, see section 2.1 for details  

Note: For physical gift card return/refund guidelines, see KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund.  

Return to top

3.2 Procedure

  • Refund a digital gift in MST by using the offset transaction function, the same way refunds are performed for other MST transactions.
  • The offset transaction function will only be revealed for gift cards if the gift card has not been redeemed.
  • Once the gift card has been refunded, it will automatically be banned, so the gift card becomes void.

3.3 Digital gift card scenarios by status

Created/Inactive scenario

Scenario

Some cards are created but are not activated until the retailer has processed the card at checkout.

Refund criteria
  • Received proof of purchase for the pre-paid card.

Additional note
  • Pre-paid cards may take some time to activate. If they just purchased the card, recommend they try it again after 24 hours.

Procedure
  • If refund criteria are met, replace with promo CSV. See KB 3044887 Procedure: Currency Stored Value (CSV) for instructions and use reason code “Replace value of content for prepaid code/token that cannot be redeemed”.
  • If refund criteria are not met, refer to point of sale.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Return to top

Active scenario

Scenario

Active cards should be usable but there are several reasons why a customer may have a hard time redeeming.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • Have the customer re-enter the code to validate it’s been entered correctly.
  • Confirm they are not trying to redeem a subscription token on an account with a subscription that is in decline.
  • Confirm that the card is for their region.
  • Requires proof of purchase.

Additional notes
  • Digital e-gifting of CSV: Customers can correct this issue by canceling their order from https://www.microsoft.com/store/b/gift-cards.
  • Customer who cannot provide proof of purchase should be referred to the point of sale.
  • Out of region customers can only be helped if they fall in the Office Geo-blocking Make it Right exception, see KB 2967407 Procedure: Office: Geo-blocking, otherwise they need to return to point of sale.

Procedure

See section 3.2.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Return to top

Invalid scenario

Scenario

Invalid tokens may be entered incorrectly or misprinted.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • Have the customer re-enter their code to validate it’s being entered correctly.
  • Requires a picture of the front and back of the card.
  • Requires proof of purchase.

Procedure

If refund criteria are met, contact the Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) for review.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Return to top

Incomplete/Damaged scenario

Scenario

These tokens cannot be looked up because the card itself is damaged or the number is incomplete.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • Confirm that the code is not complete.
  • The code is not missing due to physical damage to the card.
  • Requires a picture of the front and back of the card.
  • Requires the proof of purchase.

Additional note

See section 3.2

Procedure

If refund criteria are met, contact the Microsoft-based ACA for review.

Frequency

As it occurs.

Return to top

Redeemed/Consumed scenario

Scenario

These tokens have already been redeemed. The customer may have redeemed the code to the wrong account.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • The customer has redeemed the code to the wrong account.
  • The token is for an Xbox Live subscription of three months or longer or an Office subscription of any length.
  • Customer contacted within 14 days of token redemption.
  • The redeemed service has been cancelled on the incorrectly redeemed account.

Procedure

see section 3.2.

Frequency

As it occurs.

4. Other scenarios

Scenarios

Procedure

Customer contacting because the previous refund request has not been processed.
  1. Look at the case notes left by the other advocate that handled the case.
  2. If the refund was not processed, issue a refund by using Refund Line Item under Order section.
  3. Cancel the subscription under Subscription.

Customer complaining that the billing and renewal date are not matching.

Educate the customer about the differences between these two dates and how it will impact them. See KB 840535 Informational: Subscription and billing anniversary dates and pending charges.
