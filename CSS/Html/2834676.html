Overview

This TSG provides details on how Support Engineers (SEs) can engage the Capacity Management (CM24x7) team for quota increases.

Common Scenario Available (Wiki)

You can also leverage the wiki based workflow for this support topic on the ASMS CSS Support Wiki > Select Quota.

Note: If the service you are looking at is not handled by Capacity Management, refer to TSG 4086154 for all other quota service offerings.

Policy

Keep the following guidelines in mind when providing support to your customers:

  • Cases come from both the quota blade and from the technical blade that is administering the service.
  • For all subscription types and compute requests – When your customer requests that you increase their compute quota, send a DevOps ticket to the Quota Automation Service (QMS).
    All tickets must be created separately, you can't use the Multiple option any longer.
    • Note: If you need to create more than 1 ticket that will get assigned to QMS, make sure the first ticket you create is not assigned to QMS until you are done with all other tickets, otherwise QMS will see only one ticket, fulfil the request and close your support request (SR). If you have other actions in the case that would be affected by QMS closing your case when all quota is completed, create a placeholder ticket and add the term placeholder to the title. Then, assign the placeholder ticket to yourself. That will block QMS from closing the case. When you have completed all actions on the case, you can go close your placeholder ticket and close your case.
    • Exceptions:
      • If the ticket has already been through QMS, assign it to CM24x7.
      • If the ticket requires a credit check (as seen in step 3), assign it to CM24x7 
    • Note: ​​When ASMS receives a collaboration task from another team (for example, the Technical team) that requires us to engage CM 24x7 via an RDQuota request, remember to always add a suffix next to the case number in the RDQuota request field MSSolve ID to mark the case as a placeholder. This will prevent QMS from automatically closing the case.
      • ​Example: A placeholder suffix can simply be a number, such as "120040321001431-1."
  • Communications with the customer:
    • The communications around how each request is handled lies solely with the Capacity Management team. If CM 24*7 does not provide you with customer messaging, ask them to provide it.
    • For all quota cases, look at the verbatim, communications, and task logs to see if the quota request details have already been provided by the customer or from the automated system. Be sensitive to your customer’s efforts in providing you with information and double check that the information that you are asking for hasn’t already been provided.
    • When responding to your customer, use Capacity Management’s message in its entirety. ASMS only helps with providing Capacity Management’s predefined messaging (included in the ticket when they respond to you) to the customer.
    • ASMS SEs should never interpret or create messaging, but only copy and paste what is being provided by the Capacity Management team within the ticket. Only approved messaging may be communicated to the customer.
    • All messaging that Capacity Management provides to ASMS has been approved by Engineering and Marketing.
    • If you feel the need to paraphrase the message (Example: You have not recieved a update from CM 24*7 but the customer is engaging you for an update), you can leverage this paraphrasing guide.Refrain from providing sensitive information such as Azure max quota limits or specific criteria for credit checks. Providing such information increases vulnerability.

    • After the quota has been increased by the Capacity team, ask the customer to click the Refresh button on the Usage+quota page in the Ibiza Portal, to see the updated quota. Note, this page must be manually refreshed, in order to see the updated quota. 

  • Internal Customers – If an internal customer is already in direct contact with Capacity Management for a quota increase, check with the internal customer if they need your help. If not, they can work with Capacity Management directly, and you can close your case as resolved.
  • Free Accounts – Free Accounts cannot have their quotas increased. (Once your customer has removed their monetary cap, it is no longer a Free Account, and their quotas can be increased through the normal process).
  • Customer Feedback – If your customer wants to submit feedback about capacity related issues, direct them to the Azure Feedback site.
  • Additional Resources (for Internal Microsoft (MSFT) Representatives)
    • GetHelp – Customers who experience difficulty getting the proper attention on their case, may escalate to GetHelp instead of coming directly to ASMS. In some instances, MSFT Representatives (i.e. TAMs) may also leverage this support path on behalf of their customer.
      Note: ASMS should not re-direct a customer to open a GetHelp ticket, as the ticket will only be re-directed back to ASMS.
    • Azure Reliability Portal – Internal MSFT Representatives can leverage this link if they want to check on the capacity status of Azure regions.
    • MCIO Capacity Update Meetings – If an Internal MSFT Representative would like to attend the weekly call with MCIO team about current constraints, they can email ASMS Change Management to have the meeting invite forwarded.
  • Keep cases with backlogged quota tickets open. Whenever a quota request ticket is in a backlogged state, the case needs to remain open.

    Tickets in a backlogged state feed into Microsoft's capacity planning efforts and are needed to understand the actual demand. Closing a backlogged ticket leads to a low forecast.

    Please always leave the case open when the associated quota ticket is backlogged and refrain from suggesting the customer to close the case before resolution. From the customers' perspective a backlogged quota ticket means the case is not resolved – and that is why the case needs to stay open until the quota request is completed.

Support Scenarios

Capacity Management handles the following scenarios:

Allocation Errors for VMs

In this circumstance, check to see if your customer has sufficient quota to allocate the VM for the specific VM SKU in ASC or Jarvis.

  • If the customer has insufficient quota, they will have error messages that specifically call out quota, in addition to mentioning how many cores are available and how many are needed for the customer’s desired allocation.
    • Guide these customers through expanding their quota limits.
  • If the customer has sufficient quota, they may get errors due to the cluster they are on being full, despite the customer themselves having sufficient quota. Error messages calling out this scenario will specifically call out allocation failure errors.
    • These customers will need to be helped by the technical team (IAAS). Transfer the case over to them as a misroute by doing the following:
      1. Update the case’s Support Topic of the case to Received an Allocation Failure.
      2. Update the Support Subtopic to Unable to deploy or start my VM.

Cannot Resize Pre-Existing VMs After Quota Increase

There are multiple reasons that may prevent customers from resizing pre-existing VMs after their quota has been increased, though they can deploy new VMs without trouble.

For these cases, create a collaboration task for the VM team.

Note: Do not advise customers to directly contact technical teams if you encounter technical issues after the main issue (Capacity issue) has been resolved.

Classic VMs

Only customers presently using classic VMs will be allowed to continue using them or requesting more classic VMs.  Other users will be blocked. 

If you get a request from a user that wants access to classic VMs, you can send an IcM to https://portal.microsofticm.com/imp/v3/incidents/create?tmpl=I112k1 and that team can whitelist the user to use classic VMs.

Specialty SKU

  • Every batch account will be allowed to use either Standard SKU’s or Specialty SKU but not both. Customer requests to increase Batch quota for Specialty SKU's (NCSV2, NCSv3, NC, NV) will have to create a new batch account just for the specialty SKU's.
  • Once the Batch Quota is stamped on the new account, the new batch account will be limited only to Specialty SKU's (NCSV2, NCSv3, NC, NV) and will not be available for other generally available SKU's (Av2, Dv2, Dv3, Ev3, DSv2, DSv3, ESv3, F, FS, BS, Eiv3, ESiv3). Single new batch accounts can be used for multiple Specialty SKU’s (NCSV2, NCSv3, NC, NV).

If the customer has Premium Storage: Do not create an IcM for Premium Storage to increase storage scalability.
Respond to the customer with the following, and close the request:

  • Premium Storage has a hard limit of 35 TB provisioned capacity per account, and it cannot be increased.
  • Our recommendation is to use Managed Disks which eliminates this constraint per account.
  • If Unmanaged disks are needed, create multiple Premium storage accounts and spread the capacity among them.

If the customer has Standard Storage: Use the IcM: https://portal.microsofticm.com/imp/v3/incidents/create?tmpl=SE382u

Specific customer information must be gathered for an IcM, and all (Mandatory) fields must be filled.

  • If the account is in use, ask them to provide their current storage account usage today (i.e. how much TB, Gbps, IOPS they consume) – nice to have
  • For existing accounts, discuss with customer where they are facing limit issues currently.
    • Are they approaching 500TB limit or are they getting throttled on Gbps or IOPS? Or something else? (Mandatory)
    • If customer is experiencing errors due to throttling, ask for and error messages, logs if possible, and date/time when throttle errors surfaced.
  • Discuss the scenario they need higher limits for.
    • Is the scenario in production or for some imminent PoC? (Mandatory)
    • What kind of workload characteristics (i.e. Machine learning workload pushing 100 Gbps from 100 nodes; Transactional data collected into batch and run into a big data pipeline). We need to know the data volume, data velocity and data variety that drive the PB, Gbps, and IOPS for the account (Mandatory).
    • How much do they need today and what are their growth projections over the following time scales – 30 days; 90 days; 6 months; 1 year (Mandatory).

Workflow Process

Before the Case Comes to You

All compute cores requests go through QMS. If the quota increase is within the current thresholds, QMS approves that request. If the request is outside of the threshold for QMS, the case is moved to the ASMS queue. An ASMS SE then owns the communication with the customer (only use the verbatims from the tickets when answering about quota) and collaboration with CM 24x7 team and/or core approvers.

Note: QMS will automatically close a case 48 hours after it was opened if both of the following conditions are true:

  • All tickets on the Support Request are closed.
  • There have been no communications from the customer.
  • This potentially means that a case can be closed by QMS prior to a customer response to your FQR.
  • All other types of cases come directly to ASMS.

Step 1: Review the Customer’s Request and Account Information

  • Carefully look through your customer’s case, verbatim, and communications in Service Desk.
  • Check if the customer requires special case handling, as outlined in the Specific Support Guidance TSG (4039985). 
  • Suspected Fraud – For cases that are flagged with “Suspected Fraud”, check the account’s status in CMAT and the communication section in Service Desk.
    • If the account status is Disabled or Warned, engage HIT.
    • If the account status is Active, proceed as normal.
  • Check the case for any CM24x7 tickets.
    • Check to see if the Status is set to Incomplete, and the ticket is assigned to CM24x7. If both of these are true, send an email to CM24x7 with a request to check the ticket. ​​​
      (Typically we would expect a ticket in an Incomplete state to be assigned to ASMS​​.)
    • If the DevOps ticket is not assigned to you, add your alias to the title of the ticket. (It should resemble the example below.)
      That way, once the ticket has been assigned to an ASMS SE, CM24x7 can assign it to the respective case owner.

      Example:
      Update the title to read "SQL DB | Region Enablement <US East 2> | <SE Alias>"

A new credit check is required if a customer exceeds the current credit limit noted in CMAT, even if they have had a previous credit check completed.

Do not process a quota increase for a customer who has an outstanding or overdue payment. Account Payments must be up-to-date before their quota is increased.

These types of offers do not get quota increases until they are converted to Pay-As-You-Go accounts.

Step 5: GFS Approval

Did the GFS team approve your customers credit increase request?

  • Yes – Update the customer’s credit limit in CMAT.

    2. Click on the Payment Instruments Tab.
    3. Click on the Change Invoice Credit Check Threshold menu button.
    4. Enter the New Invoice Credit Check Threshold amount provided by GFS.

    8. Click the check mark button to save your changes.

Step 6: Engage Capacity Management to Request a Quota Increase
Note: When an ASMS SE assigns a quota request to the CM24*7 team after fulfilling the quota request, the CM approver may assign the TFS ticket back to the QMS, triggering an automated closure of the SR.

Create RDQuota Tickets

In most cases QMS/Automation will have created all the tickets for the case already. Make sure that the tickets have been correctly created. If one or all of the tickets are missing, follow the below steps to create them.

  1. Navigate to the Ticket Templates Ticket Templates(OneNote) page.
  2. Select the appropriate template from theTicket Templates page.
  3. Read the RGuidelines for your template type.
  4. Fill out your ticket using both your customer’s information and the guidelines mentioned in the previous step. Keep the following in mind as you fill out your TFS tickets:
    • If the ticket is for a Premier customer, add "Premier" to the title of your ticket.
      • This allows Capacity Management to prioritize the ticket appropriately.
    • Only one region can be selected per ticket.
    • Only one Azure Service (Compute Cores/SQL/Storage) can be selected per ticket.
    • Compute Cores – Only one VM Type can be selected per ticket.
    • Region/Service/Service Type selection can be made through drop-downs.
    • If the ticket is a new compute request (that has not been through QMS and didn’t require a credit check) you should assign the ticket to the Quota Automation Service (QMS).
      • If it does require a credit check or has already been through QMS, assign it to CM24x7.
      • Important: If the case requires multiple tickets, ensure all ticket numbers are listed as part of the case.
  5. Click the Save button.
  6. Once you click the Save button, a ticket number should appear in the Ticket Details section of the DevOps ticket
    Important: Be sure to document the Ticket Number in your case.
  7. Check back in on your ticket on a regular basis to determine if resolution has been provided, or if next steps are needed.
    Protip: To ensure that customers get the information of their completed quota increase request as soon as possible, every SE should enable and leverage the notifications feature. You can follow this link showing how to Enable Quota Increase Notifications.
  8. If the customer asks any questions, be sure those questions get added to the capacity management ticket so that capacity management can review and answer them.
  9. Once your ticket has been updated by Capacity Management, communicate the solution or next steps with your customer and update the SR Wait State and Solution tasks accordingly.

Step 7: Following up on Your Ticket

Capacity Management tracks tickets through the State and Status fields (hereafter referred to as the State/Status of your DevOps ticket.) There are several circumstances provided below in which you will need to follow up on tickets. (In all cases where you need to act, the ticket will be assigned to you with messaging from Capacity Management regarding what they expect of you.)

Note: The Ticket State and Ticket Status tables provided at the end of this section are informational and are not intended to be comprehensive. Capacity Management will assign the ticket to you when you need to act on it. Do not change the status of a backlogged ticket just to get an update, if CM noted a timeframe and that timeframe hasn't elapsed yet, you can add a note in the ticket, but don't change status.

  1. When a quota ticket is assigned to you in Incomplete state, provide the requested clarification to Capacity Management in theticket. Change the state filed to In Triage and update the field Assigned to as CM 24*7.
  2. Make sure to answer all questions raised by capacity management to avoid delays.

Note: If the quota is no longer required and you are closing the SR, find all the tickets associated to your SR, no matter who its assigned to and no matter what state it is in (unless fulfilled), change the state to Cancelled, add proper notes to explain capacity management why you are canceling it and Save. Do not leave the SR in Incomplete status.

Circumstance 1: How to find Quota tickets assigned in your name in Incomplete state

You can run a simple query to identify quota tickets assigned in Incomplete state. Follow the steps below to run the query:

  1. Navigate to the following URL: https://dev.azure.com/CapacityRequest/Quota/_queries/query-edit/89fe4b15-70ce-49ab-aade-ddc8ad28daa9/
  2. Select Assigned to: <your name> and select State: Incomplete
  3. This query will list all the tickets assigned in your name in Incomplete state.

    The customer can see the updated quota by clicking the Refresh button on the Usage+quota page in the Ibiza Portal. Please note that this page must be manually refreshed, in order to see the updated quota.

Ticket State

Tracking the progress of a ticket is conveyed by the following ticket States:

State

Meaning

Submitted

Your request has successfully been created and is awaiting acknowledgement.

In Triage

Your request is under review by the Capacity Management Triage team.

In Review

Your request has been escalated to the Capacity Management Review team.

Incomplete

Your request needs further details or clarifications before a decision can be made.

In Fulfillment

Your request has been approved and is awaiting a quota update.

Completed

The requested quota change has been updated and is pending customer verification.

Closed

The customer has verified the quota change.

Canceled

The customer has cancelled their quota request.

Special Handling

This case is going through an exception process where the Capacity Management team would directly reach out to the customer. If this is assigned to you, Capacity Management will provide next steps.

Ticket Status

Capacity Management’s decision for a change request is communicated by the following Status messages:

Status

Meaning

Approved

Your request was reviewed and approved.

Backlogged

Your request cannot be approved immediately and will be regularly reconsidered until fulfilled.

Forelogged

Your request does not require immediate fulfillment and will be scheduled to be reviewed at a future date.

SQL Escalation

Your SQL Quota requests are being escalated to the SQL Team.

 

Engagement and Escalation Path

Handling Escalations from Internal teams

​If an account team or a field representative escalates a quota or capacity-related scenario to you, refer them to Ge​tHelp. These escalations will then be addressed by the Capacity Management team within a business day.

Escalating to CM 24*7 for Quota issues

For quota increase cases where you have not received a response by CM 24*7 within a 1 to 4 hour timeframe, you can contact them directly at CM24x7@microsoft.com  and Cc ASMSCMPOC@microsoft.com. 

If you still don't receive a response after emailing the above aliases, escalate to your TA or PTA following the normal process. In return, they will escalate to the ASMS internal POD queue. 

Reminder 

  • Do not email or ping CM 24*7 engineers directly.
  • Do not loop in customers, TAM's or any other internal or external parties to that email exchange. 
  • If a ticket is backlogged and the customer is asking for an ETA, address the customers ask with CM 24*7.  
  • Do not email SCM or Azure global.
  • Do not email American, EMEA, or APAC customer operations.
