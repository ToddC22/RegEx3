1.1 Dependency on operating system (OS)

Because Office is an app (or suite of apps) that runs on an operating system (OS) (for example, Windows 10), the product does have some dependencies that can impact our ability to resolve issues.

Guideline: Make sure the product the customer is calling about is covered by reviewing sections 1.1 and 1.2 of KB 2912243 Procedure: Consumer landing page for Support Boundaries.

Once you have verified the Office product is within our scope of support, you can refer to the scenarios outlined in this article.

Return to top

1.2 Dependency on Windows

We support these Office versions as long as they are within the Microsoft Support Policy. The exception to this is if the customer is running Office on Windows 7 and we determine their issue is caused or is related to Windows 7 and is not an issue with Office.  New features of Microsoft 365 need Windows 10. Microsoft won’t deliberately prevent a customer from connecting to the service, but the quality of their Microsoft 365 service may diminish over time, and new features won’t be available.

1.3 Dependency on Mac or Mobile OS

Your troubleshooting should be comprehensive for the Office product, and any limitations you run into related to these operating systems should be referred to the OS (Apple or Android) company for further resolution (for example, updating to a new Mac OS to run the latest version of Office). You can check the versions of Mac OS, iOS and Android that Office is compatible with by going to System requirements for Office.

2.3 In-app support

In-app support is the primary support available for customers with:

  • Outlook.com (computer or mobile OS)
  • OneDrive (computer or mobile OS)

In-app support is available for some, but not all, customers using Microsoft 365 and the following applications. Remember, you must still support all scenarios for these, do not refer customers to in-app for the issue they contacted support about.

  • Outlook client for Mac on Microsoft 365
  • Outlook client for Windows on Microsoft 365
  • Word, Excel, or PowerPoint on Microsoft 365

Return to top

2.4 Office on OEM

Customers can get Office when they purchase a new device. We support all scenarios, including DIA and AFO, where Office came with a new computer or tablet. We should never send customers back to the OEM.

For more information on OEM/AFO, see KB 4012605 Procedure: Office: Activation for Office.

Return to top

2.5 Exceptions

Community supported scenarios

Refer consumer customers with these specific scenarios to Communities for support:

  • Macros, programming, VBA, OLE automation (all Office Apps).
  • Add-ins
  • Excel: pivot tables, pivot charts, solver.
  • PowerPivot or Power Query.
  • Getting data from other sources, databases.
  • Accessing files on network, working with files on a network.
  • Outlook: Exchange account issues, BCM custom forms.
  • IRM, digital signature, custom forms, and templates.

2.6 Home Use Program (HUP)

The Home Use Program (HUP) is an employee purchase program offered exclusively through a customer’s company, enabling their staff employees to purchase Microsoft products and services. The current offer includes our premium, up-to-date software with Microsoft 365 Family or Personal, and other Office products at a substantially discounted rate for use on their personal devices. Employees can find more information at https://www.microsoft.com/homeuseprogram.

The various consumer supported products available through HUP depend on the employer’s program.

2.7 Add-ins

We define add-ins as: A device or piece of software added to an application to provide extra features or functions. Add-ins are available for Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, Project, and Word. Customers can find add-ins in the Office Store. We do not handle technical support for third party add-ins.

Add-ins we do not support

  • Add-ins for business: Be sure the customer has a consumer product.
  • Customers with a consumer product, but the add-in is for a non-consumer type functionality.

For more info on how to find, use, and troubleshoot add-ins see the below links:

  • Windows: View, manage, and install add-ins in Office programs
  • Mac: Get Add-Ins for Office for Mac
