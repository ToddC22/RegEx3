1. Overview of education products

1.1 What is Office 365 Education?

Office 365 Education is a collection of services that allows students and teachers to collaborate and share schoolwork. It provides self-service sign up for students, faculty, and staff using their school email addresses. It’s available for free to teachers who are currently working at an academic institution and to students who are currently attending an academic institution. The service includes Office for the web (Word, PowerPoint, Excel, and OneNote), unlimited personal cloud storage, Yammer, and SharePoint sites.

Some schools allow teachers and students to install the full Office applications on up to 5 PCs or Macs for free. If the customers school provides this additional benefit, they’ll see the Install Office button on the Office 365 home page after they’ve complete sign-up.

2. Office 365 University

Office 365 University is a four-year subscription to Office 365 Personal for full- and part-time enrolled university and college students, faculty, and staff in accredited institutions. Alumni of these institutions are ineligible.

Note: As of July 2019, the Office 365 University SKU is no longer available for sale or renewal. This means that students will no longer be able to purchase this four-year SKU or renew it for another four years.

Students should see if their university, school, or college offers free Office and can check eligibility at www.office.com/getoffice365.

If their school isn’t eligible, they should be encouraged to purchase Office 365 Personal or Home. If they want to try it first, they can try Office 365 Home https://products.office.com/en/try. Note: Only Home is available for trial.

Or they can use Office on the web.

We will support Office 365 University through the remainder of the customer’s subscription, as we do now.

3. FAQ for Office 365 A

Q: What are the eligibility requirements?
A: You must be a full-time or part-time faculty or staff member or student at an academic institution and:

  • Have a school-specific email address provided by school (for example, contosos.edu) that can receive external email.
  • Be of legal age to sign up for an online offer individually.
  • Have internet access.

Q: How long can I use this plan?
A: You can use the plan as long as you are working at a qualified school. Your eligibility may be re-verified at any time. When your Office 365 Education plan expires:

  • Online services associated with the school email address—for example, Office on the web and OneDrive—will no longer work.
  • The Office application enter a reduced-functionality mode which mean that you can view documents, but you cannot edit or create new documents.

Q: How long does it take to be approved as a qualified academic institution?
A: Verification of a domains academic eligibility can take up to 30 days.

Q: What can the customer do if they are not eligible?
A: Office provides a range of options for students. Go to Learn about ways you can get Office for more information.

Q: Who manages the OneDrive account provided with this offer?
A: Prior to the school taking over this account, the individual will be responsible for managing it. Once the account is taken over by the school, management will be shifted to the school’s IT department. Permissions and access can be changed by the school’s IT administrator at any time.

Q: Can this plan be shared with others?
A: Office 365 Education is licensed for use only by the eligible teachers and students.

Q: How can customers migrate their files and settings to Office 365 Personal?
A: View the migration guide for:

  • Windows 10
  • Windows 8.1
  • Windows 7
  • Mac

Q: Students, faculty and staff in my institution are reporting difficulties downloading Office through this program. What resources are available to help them with this?
A: If students, faculty, and staff are having issues installing Office, installation instructions are available in Install Office on your PC or Mac with Office 365 for business.

4. Office 365 A1 Plus

4.1 What is Office 365 A1 Plus?

Eligible students and teachers can sign up for Office 365 A1 Plus, which includes everything From Office 365 A1 as well as Office 365 ProPlus. Office 365 ProPlus is productivity software, including Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Outlook, OneNote, Publisher, Access, and Skype for Business, that is installed on your desktop or laptop. 

Return to top

4.2 What is Pro Plus benefit?

Pro Plus benefit is a benefit available to students at educational institutions that have enrolled in volume licensing programs (Enrollment Education Solutions (EES) and Open Value Subscription – Education Solutions (OVS-ES)) and have purchased either Office Professional Plus or Office 365 ProPlus for all Faculty/Staff. These students who have email addresses from eligible schools will be able to sign up and use Office 365 services, including Office 365 Pro Plus and OneDrive for Business. Microsoft will enable the capability for students to sign up for Office 365 by simply using their school email address (ex: Mary@student.edu). Upon sign-up, students will be able to get started with the full functionality of Office right away.

4.3 Referrals and support boundaries

Office technical support advocates

  • For in-scope issues as outlined in this KB, provide assisted support.
  • For out-of-scope issues not supported (OrgID password resets, Activation issues/errors related to OrgID sign-in), redirect the student to their school IT Admin or to the forums at Office 365 Community.
  • The Pro Plus benefit is a benefit of the eligible universities and is therefore not considered a ‘subscription’ to receive assisted support. Consult with the student and if customer doesn’t wish to purchase Assure/PPI, provide link to Office 365 Community.

Case creation

  • Create a new case for all in-scope U&R issues.
  • If no customer record exists, create a Desktop customer.

4.4 FAQ

Q: What link can I send to students, faculty, and staff for the Office 365 Pro Plus benefit?
A: Send them here for direct sign up: http://office.com/getoffice365

Q: Where can students go to manage their installs?
A: Students who have already successfully signed up and need to manage their installs:
https://portal.office.com/OLS/MySoftware.aspx

Q: How do you know if a customer is a Pro Plus benefit customer?
A: You will need to ask the customer a few questions to determine this.

  • Ask what the Microsoft account or email is that they used to sign up with.
  • Ask if they purchased Office.
  • Direct them to ﻿﻿office.com/getoffice365﻿﻿ or office.com/verify.

Q: What does the Office 365 ProPlus benefit mean exactly?
A: There are now two options to provide Office 365 ProPlus benefit in education:  

1) Institution-led Office 365 ProPlus benefit: Your organization’s IT staff creates an Office 365 Education tenant (domain, such as @SCHOOL.edu, anyone from the University taking part in the ProPlus benefit program), obtains Office 365 ProPlus licenses from a reseller, assigns these licenses to your students, faculty, and staff in the tenant (populated via Dirsync, etc) via Powershell or GRAPH API (see here for sample scripts), and then individuals download Office 365 ProPlus here and log into Office for valid usage. 

Or  

2) Sign up yourself Office 365 ProPlus benefit: Customers without Office 365 Education tenants can provide students an option to obtain Office 365 ProPlus without having IT setup a tenant, without having to obtain licenses from a reseller, and without IT having to assign Office 365 ProPlus licenses to users.  Existing Office 365 Education tenants can also use sign up yourself for auto-account creation and/or auto-assignment of Office 365 ProPlus licenses to users for easier enablement.

Q: What are the requirements for the Sign up yourself Office 365 ProPlus benefit to work with auto-account creation and auto-license assignment?
A: There are four requirements:  

  1.  Student must have an academic email to validate they are a student. Student will sign up using their academic email.
  2.  Institution must be eligible for Office 365 ProPlus
  3.  Student must be age 13 or over.
  4.  No ADFS for federated login with their Office 365 Education tenant and the need for auto-account creation. When using the ‘Sign up yourself’ option, the auto-account create portion does not work with federated logins to Office 365 Education; however, the sign up yourself auto-license assignment portion does work with federated logins.

Q: What are the requirements for the Sign up yourself Office 365 ProPlus benefit to work with just auto-license assignment with existing Office 365 accounts?
A: There are two requirements:  

  1.  Institution must be eligible for the Office 365 ProPlus benefit.
  2.  User has a valid login for Office 365 Education to use with the Sign Up Yourself link.  

-  Administrators can also obtain a pool of Office 365 ProPlus benefit licenses using Sign Up Yourself feature.  This account would be used to obtain the Office 365 ProPlus pool of licenses for auto-assignment to the users inside of an existing Office 365 Education tenant for scenarios when no academic email exists or under-13 scenarios.

Q: How long can a student leverage Office 365 ProPlus?
A: They can leverage this until they graduate or are no longer attending your school. At that point, you must disable their Office 365 ProPlus license.

Q: How many machines/devices can I run this subscription on?
A: Each subscription license allows you to run Office on up to five machines, Mac or PC. You can also run Office for iPad on up to 5 tablets.

Q: What if an Admin contacts me?
A: If Admins contact us, they should be directed to log in to the admin portal (portal.office.com) where they can submit a support ticket via the support section of the portal. Read the official blog post at http://blogs.office.com/2013/10/15/announcing-student-advantage/

Q: What does Office A1 Plus for Students include?
A:

  • Office 365 ProPlus allows students to install the latest version of Office on up to 5 PCs or Macs* and on other mobile devices, including Windows tablets and iPads.
  • Office for the web: Word, Excel, and PowerPoint
  • 1 TB of OneDrive for Business storage, managed by the school, for schoolwork and homework.
  • SharePoint Online

    *Outlook, Access, and Publisher are available on PC's only.

The Office 365 Education for Students subscription is valid for one year from the sign-up date. If the tenant (domain, such as @school.edu and anyone from the University taking part in the ProPlus benefit program) is managed by an IT administrator, they can choose to extend that license period.

Q: Who can enroll?
A: Schools qualify for this benefit when they license Office institution-wide for faculty and staff through Enrollment for Education Solutions (EES), Open Value Subscription Education Solutions (OVS-ES), or a school contract, and they are enrolled to allow students to sign up directly with Microsoft. After the school qualifies, its active full-time or part-time students are eligible and they can get the plan directly from Microsoft at Office.com/GetOffice365 if they meet all three of the following requirements:

  • They have a school-specific email address provided by the school (for example, contoso.edu) that can receive external email.
  • They are of legal age to sign up for an online offer individually (13 years old).
  • They have Internet access.

Students will not be able to sign up if:

  • Their educational institution does not currently participate in the ProPlus benefit program.
  • The Student does not have an email address with the education institute.
  • Students have been blocked from joining; either by default or by choice of the school.
  • Students are under the age of 13.

Q: What’s new since ProPlus benefit launched in December 2013?
A: It is important to understand that Office 365 Education for Students has been available to eligible students for some time, but has previously required the IT administrator of the school to setup and assign the appropriate license to their students. Beginning 17 September 2014, the student sign-up capability removes the need for the IT administrator to be involved in the setup and provisioning of eligible students.

Q: Are there any regional restrictions?
A: Asia: In China, Office ProPlus benefit is not yet available; therefore students in China will not be able to take advantage of the student sign-up capability at launch.

EMEA: In the European Union (EU), some additional restrictions are applied to the student sign-up experience:

  • Students who already have an Office 365 account will be able to sign-up for Office 365 Education for Students by default.
  • Students who do not already have an Office 365 account and where there is no tenant already associated to the school domain will be able to sign-up for Office 365 Education for Students by default.
  • Students who do not already have an Office 365 account and where an existing tenant is already associated to the school domain, the student will not be able to sign-up for Office 365 Education for Students by default.
    • This feature can be manually enabled by their school IT administrator. This can be done via a PowerShell script. See the Enabling and Disabling Student Sign-up section for details. This limitation is required for Microsoft to remain compliant with regional and local regulatory requirements.

Q: Isn't this the same SKU as Office 365 University?
A: Office 365 University is a paid 4-year subscription that includes fully installed Office applications – Word, Excel, PowerPoint, OneNote, Outlook, Publisher, and Access (app availability and features vary by Platform and device). Link to details

The full, installed Office experience can be installed on up to 2 PCs, Macs, or tablets (including iPad and Android tablet) and 2 phones. It also includes 1 TB of online storage, plus 60 Skype minutes of calling per month to mobile phones in 8 countries and landlines in 60+ countries

Under the ProPlus benefit, students who attend academic institutions that have purchased Office for all of their faculty and staff (available at www.Office.com/GetOffice365) can receive Office 365 Education for Students for free. This plan allows students to install Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, OneNote, Publisher, and Access on up to 5 PCs or Macs and Office apps on other mobile devices including Android, iPad®, and Windows tablets. The plan also includes 1TB of OneDrive storage, managed by the school, and students can edit and collaborate using Office for the web, Yammer, and SharePoint sites.

Download issues
 

Is Internet access required for Office?

Internet access is required to install and activate all the latest releases of Office suites and all Office 365 plans.

Internet connectivity is also required:

• To access OneDrive

• To access Skype minutes

• To manage the subscription account (for installation on other PCs or Macs)

• To manage billing options

See Is Internet access required for Office? in external FAQ.

How long does it take to download and install Office?

Download and installation is dependent on the customer's internet download speed. Typically, a high download speed of 4 Mbps or more should result in a complete install of 30 minutes or less. Lower speeds will take more time.

Note: If Office detects a slow connection when it starts the installation, it will switch from a streaming experience to a download experience. This means the download will need to be fully complete before any Office app can be used.

How do I download the 64-bit version of Office?

The 32-bit version of Office is installed by default. If the customer wants to download and install the 64-bit version, they will need to choose to switch it with the advanced link. Have customer visit the articles in the provided link.
