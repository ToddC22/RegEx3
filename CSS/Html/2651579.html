ASMS – PayOps Engagement

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This article explains how and when to engage with PayOps, as well as a list of scenarios that they support.

Note: If you are supporting an EA customer, this TSG does not apply. Instead, refer to TSG 2872061 for guidance.

Support Scenarios

In the following scenarios where your customer cannot resolve the issue themselves, or the Support Engineer (SE) cannot resolve it with the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT) or Jarvis, engage PayOps following the steps outlined in the Engagement and Escalation Path section of this TSG.

If the case is related to a Modern Commerce Account (MCA) Field Led (FL) customer, do not engage PayOps using any of the paths below. Instead, see the Engagement and Escalation paths outlined in TSG 4490627.

The following scenarios are supported by ASMS:

  • Azure Marketplace issues.
     
  • Changing your customer’s payment instrument (PI).
     
  • Re-enabling subscriptions.
     
  • Provisioning failures or issues.
     
  • PI issues while purchasing Azure subscriptions.
     
  • Unable to remove spending limit
     
  • Regenerate an invoice (extenuating circumstances only). Regeneration of invoices should be considered only in the following circumstances: 
    • The customer has updated their name in the portal to align with their payment instrument, but they have an open balance/unpaid invoice that has already been generated.
    • There is a tax impact, where the customer's name on the invoice is not aligning with their name for tax filing, and they need alignment to claim a tax benefit.
    • The customer has attempted to make changes to their account details (and shows proof of doing so) but a system issue prevented it from being processed.
    • The customer request to update past invoices. This will be considered on a case by case scenario, in situations where the timespan is 12 months or less. 

You should also note the following about regeneration of invoices:

  • For all regeneration request, a business justification and proof to support the request is required.
  • If the invoice has already been paid and the customer is unable to provide business justification, we will not support a regeneration.
     
  • Declined Transaction – Banned PI (as noted in CMAT)
    • Declined by Provider
      • Tell your customer that their bank declined the transaction and they will need to unblock the PI.
      • If your customer says that they have unblocked their card, unban their PI using CMAT. See TSG 4010492 for more information about this process.
    • Declined by Risk
      • Try to unban your customer's PI in CMAT. See TSG 4010492 for more information about this process. 
      • If the above fails, escalate your case to the Human Intelligence Team (HIT).
  • Any customer concerns with invoices or usage detail reports.
  • Manually processing your customer’s credit card payment.
    • PayOps can manually process a credit card charge for a card that was previously declined. This only works if the customer has first resolved the issue with the credit card.
    • PayOps can also postpone charges.
       
  • Remove your customer’s PI.
    • Only engage PayOps if your customer is unable to remove their PI, and you are unable to do so in CMAT.
       
  • Your customer has an inquiry about a charge from Microsoft.
    • You can confirm if the charge is for the customers Azure account by collecting relevant information such as the Invoice ID and Azure account details.
    • Try to locate the charges using CMAT. If you are unable to locate the charges, you can ask the customer to provide their transaction details, such as the last four digits of the credit card charged, the date when charge occurred, and the Transaction ID.  PayOps can use this information to locate the charge.
    • PayOps will restore the Azure account details from the time when the charge was triggered or any other Microsoft online service.
       
  • If your customer is unable to locate the account for the charges on their credit card statement, follow the steps below before escalating the case to PayOps:
    1. Verify you are viewing the Charges screen in CMAT and not the Transactions screen. The Charges page shows the aggregated amounts that should align with what customers see on their credit card statements.
    2. Navigate to the Office 365 Partner Template to search for any possible accounts there as well.
    3. If you are unable to locate any associated accounts, obtain the following information from your customer and engage PayOps:
  • Full customer name:
  • Company name (must be exactly as spelled on the Invoice):
  • Date and amount of each relevant charge/payment (from the customer’s credit card statement):
  • Last 4 digits of the credit card billed, type of card and card expiration date:
  • Zip or Postal Code associated with the credit card:
  • Phone number:
  • Customer country and currency:
  • Invoice ID or Order ID:
     
  • If you get a case related to PII issues like credit card decline, switch CC failing, getting an MRN number, etc.:
    1. Check CMAT and see if the required information is available (e.g., reason code for credit card decline, ART blocker, etc.,).
    2. If not, engage your TA/PTA by sending a Task to your POD queue.
    3. The TA/PTA will use the tool Timeline to investigate the issue.
      1. If they are unable to determine the cause or if further investigation is needed, engage PayOps by providing the analysis done by TA/PTA.
         
  • Your customer is requesting a Delivery Acceptance Form (DAF).
    • Ukrainian law requires a signed hard copy DAF  to be printed and mailed by the customer to the Dublin office address in Section 9 of the Microsoft Online Subscription Agreement. This allows PayOps Commerce Business Operations (CBO) to send a hard copy of Invoices and DAF’s to Ukraine customers.
      Note: Free accounts are exempt from this requirement. A DAF will be required once your Ukrainian customer converts to a paid account.
       
  • Active Directory deletion is blocked because the Azure TAG is active even though the Azure subscriptions are in a deleted state in CMAT, AZURE RT and ARM.
    • Please consult The Azure Active Directory (AAD) TSG (TSG 2832928) for the process to fix this, and instructions for PayOps engagement.

Note: Verify the area path is set to OSGS\BizOps\PayOps\Execution or PayOps will not be able to access your request.

  • Customer is in dunning and their subscription has been disabled for nonpayment AND they don’t have a cc that can clear the charges (i.e. the credit limit is too low, etc.) AND wants to make a one-time payment via invoice or wire transfer, or a split payment between 2 credit cards.
    • Follow the guidance outlined in TSG 2651536. 
       
  • For Indian customers, if the currency in the account is set to USD, PAYOPS can convert the default currency to INR.
     
  • Requests for a Statement of Account (SOA) for Credit Card Customers.
    • If you have a case where you need to request an SOA for a credit card customer, send an email to CP Finance Operations (wocscp@microsoft.com) with the request details and the Order ID.

Common PayOps Misroutes

  • Unable to login to the management or account portal.
    • Engage the ASE team. See (TSG 3093263).
  • Unexpected UI behavior or error after logging into the portal.
    • Engage the ASE team. See (TSG 3093263).
  • Cancelled/Deprovisioned subscription still appearing in customer's portal.
    • Engage your TA.
  • Delete shadow account – SDE Bug creation to Org ID.
    • Follow the Authentication TSG (TSG 2820621).
    • Remove the shadow account
      • If the URL or Network trace shows an MSPP error with error code 2147217320, this is usually a shadow account issue.
    • Your customer may see this error message on the portal: "An error occurred when we tried to process a WS-Federation message. The message was invalid".

Recon File Issues
  1. Use these guidelines for escalating to PayOps, including steps to fill out your TFS ticket:
    • Submitting a Core Commerce Service Request
    • Input the SR open date in the Tier 1 Case Created Date field.
    • Any Priority 1 requests raised with PayOps needs to have a valid business justification along with a TA approval log on the ticket.
  2. To help prioritize your request, you can use the following fields (These fields are not mandatory): 
    • Customer Urgency
      • Examples of when to use this field: Subscription is disabled, Customer is unable to perform a SOT, ASMS unable to re-activate subscription due to a tool error, etc.
    • Customer Blocked
      • Binary (True/false) toggle 
  3. What If my Service Request has not been triaged? 
    • Triage of new Service Requests is a daily activity, so if your Service Request does not appear to have been worked on after 6 hours first check for a few common submission mistakes:
  • Is the Path: field set to OSGS\BizOps\PayOps or OSGS\BizOps\PayOps\CBO so it's created for the proper team? If not, triage queries will not capture these misrouted requests.  Select the proper area path and re-save the SR.
  • If the Assigned to: field contains your own name or was not set to Active when the Service Request was initially created, it will be misrouted.
  • New Service Requests must specify the proper Area Path and be assigned to Active in order to be triaged on time.

Note: If the Assignee field displays as Active beyond 2 business days from your submission, you can contact your TA to get this expediated. For more information, see the following details at Managing a PayOps Service Request to Closure.

  1. How do I reactivate a Service Request if the issue isn't actually resolved?
    • If you need to reactivate a Service Request because the issue remains unresolved, refer to the following guidance outlined at Managing a PayOps Service Request to Closure.

Escalation Paths

Please use the following escalation path when you need to expedite a PayOps ticket:

Expediting PayOps tickets during weekdays:

  • Engage your TL/TA/PTA and provide the following information:
    • PayOps SR number:
    • Summary of the issue:
    • SLA missed? (Yes/No):
    • Business or Revenue Impact:
  • The TA/PTA will review the information and check if the escalation is valid based on the missed SLA and the business impact.
    • If yes: The TA/PTA will send a task to the ASMS Internal Engagement queue and update the title to 'PayOps | Expedite <<PayOps Ticket Number>> | Issue'.
    • If no: The TA/PTA will transfer the task back to the SE and educate them on when to escalate these types of cases.
  • Available ASMS PayOps POCs will then review the Task and post the details in Teams to get the ticket expedited.

Note: Do not email the PayOps DL (payops@microsoft.com) during weekdays.

​Expediting PayOps tickets during weekends:

  • Email the PayOps DL (payops@microsoft.com) with the above template to get traction on the ticket provided there is real-time business impact for the customer which cannot wait till Monday.
