1. Purchases

Transactions in the Modern Commerce Platform are broken down into purchases. For Azure and Marketplace consumption, each resource generates a purchase line and the lines are grouped into a Purchase Order (PO).

Each PO has a Purchase ID that shows on the Invoice in the Purchase Order tab. Reservations and Marketplace Subscription based purchases produce individual purchase records that are not grouped with other purchases. All purchases roll up to the Asset responsible for purchase as shown below. For Azure consumption, the purchasing Asset is the Azure Access Pass (rating asset), not the Usage Asset, so all Azure consumption roll up to the Azure Access Pass.  This roll up is especially useful in month to month re-occurring billing (such as Reservation Purchase Plans and Marketplace subscription-based purchases).

  1.  Log into CST, scroll down to find the purchases section and select View All

    [Asset 4526041]
  1. Search for the purchase by:
    • Date Range
    • Project: Project ID tied to the purchase 
    • Product Type: Type of product the customer purchased (for example, Marketplace Products, SaaS, or Free Trial)

      [Asset 4526043]
    • Asset ID: Dropdown of all the Asset IDs that are tied to the products purchased
    • Custom Search: This is a free form field used to search for product names in this example. It can be used to search by other options like Subscription GUID.

      [Asset 4526044]
  1. Expand the product information by selecting the Product Name (for example, SendGrid-Free)

    [Asset 4526045]

The Details Tab contains:

  • ​Unit Price: Pre-tax billed amount
  • Tax: Local tax on billed amount
  • Total: Total of Unit Price and Tax after a refund has been applied.
  • Refund Amount: Amount refunded at some point in the past
  • Tax Refund: Amount of tax refunded during previous refunds
  • Purchase ID: ID of the purchase block that refers to Purchase Order ID on the purchase's invoice
  • Product ID: ID of product that can be matched on the recon file and Azure Usage Detail file
  • SKU ID: N/A, where not applicable
  • Line Item ID: N/A, where not applicable
  • Quote ID: Pre-purchase quote generated by the Commerce system. Deep links to Quote blade with ID details
  • Asset ID: ID of the Azure Access Pass to which the Usage Asset reported consumption
  • Product Title: Name of Product in the Azure catalog
  • Publisher: Name of Publisher
  • ISV: If 3rd Party, will show the creator of the Marketplace offer
  • ISV Contact: Support contact as published on the Marketplace
  • SKU: Tier of product purchased
  • Product Type: Azure First Party, Marketplace Products (3PP), SaaS Entitlement, etc.

[Asset 4526046]

The Events Tab Captures the Event Details

  • Event ID: Unique Identifier tied to the Event
  • Event Type: Type of Event Captured (Dunning Created, Captured, Billing, Tax, Revenue)
  • Amount: Amount where applicable
  • Status: Status of the Event (Successful, Unsuccessful)

[Asset 4526047]

The Customer Tab captures detailed customer information (Customer refers to the legal entity):

  • Name: Full name of the customer
  • Address: Address tied to the customer
  • Contact: Contact summary (name, email and phone number)
  • Audience: Segment (Commercial, Enterprise, ISV)
  • Customer ID: Unique identifier tied to the customer
  • Description: Any further description of the customer (such as a secondary email)
  • Last Modified: The date the last modification occurred on the customer’s account

[Asset 4526049]

The Recipient Tab refers to the purchaser:

  • Name: Full name of the recipient
  • Address: Primary address tied to the recipient
  • Contact: Contact summary (name, email and phone number)
  • Recipient ID: Unique identifier tied to the customer
  • Description: Any further description of the customer (such as a secondary email)
  • Last Modified: The date the last modification occurred on the recipient’s account

2. Transfer Asset or Subscription

This is one of the most common actions that is performed by a Support Agent. This allows the Agent to transfer an Asset to another source based on Offer and User.  The following step walks you through performing an Asset/Subscription Transfer:

“Transfer Asset” is now known as “Transfer Subscription” in CST.  The process to perform this action remains the same and is outlined below.

  1. Log into CST; Select the Billing Account
  2. Navigate to the “Assets + Subscriptions Blade”; Select the Asset (Hover over Asset ID) you are wanting to transfer

    [Asset 4576577]
  3. Select “Transfer Subscription” from the Action Bar at the top of the Overview page.

4. Issuing Refunds

Refunds are provided for each Azure usage purchase record and can be done through single or multiple resources. Each resource will have a corresponding line (represented by that will show in the Purchase tile and the View All for Purchases. Line items (think resources in Azure) will then be grouped into a purchase record. This data all intersects with PurchaseOrder (Invoice tile, Purchase Order tab) = OrderID (recon file) = PurchaseID (Purchase tile, line detail).​

The following walks you through how to perform a Refund or Credit at the line item level.  If you wish to perform a Bulk Refund, Navigate to the Batch Function Section from the CST Blades

  1. Log into CST; Select the Billing Account
  2. Navigate to the “Purchases” Blade; Select the Product you would like to issue the Refund or Credit (hover to validate full product name).

    [Asset 4576590]
  3. Select “Refund/Cancel” from the Action Bar

    [Asset 4576591]
  4. Enter the following required information:
    1. Case ID – Free form field to track the request
    2. Refund Amount – Enter partial or full amount
    3. Reason Code – Select the reason code that corresponds with the Refund/Credit
    4. Comments – Free form field to track the request
  5. Select Next
  6. Confirm information is request; Submit

Note: Processing a credit for the customer in CST. If the customer has no negative balance (balance owed to Microsoft) in the next billing cycle, or Microsoft does not amend (change) the invoice, the customer can expect to receive the appropriate credits applied back to them as a refund to their card.

6. Usage

Customers and support are enabled to download various usage-based files through the Azure portal and CST. "Azure Usage Details" provides the Azure side of usage before it has been rated by Commerce. This file can be downloaded against an invoice or current, unbilled usage. An invoice "recon" file provides the rated, billed usage for a given month. These files can be used to troubleshoot and resolve customer billing and invoicing related issue.

6.1 Download Usage Detail

Support engineers can download usage on a given invoice in .CSV format from a customer’s profile. This usage file is identical to the usage file the customer downloads via Ibiza and can be filtered by a number of columns, including Subscription. Select the blue options button with ellipses (…) and select Download Invoice or Azure Usage Details.

[Asset 4526074]

6.2 Download Unbilled Usage Details

Support engineers can download unbilled (not yet invoiced) usage through the Billing Group tile by selecting the blue options button with ellipses (…) and selecting Unbilled Azure Usage Details. This is a net new feature​​ that downloads a .CSV file of usage up to the current time with some delay (~-1 hour).

  1. Go to Billing Group Section
  2. Select the blue options button with ellipses (…) then select Download Unbilled Azure Usage  [Asset 4526075]
  3. Select the current month to download                                                                                 [Asset 4526076]

7. Cancel/re-enable asset (subscription)

Support Agents can cancel an active subscription (Including Marketplace) or re-enable a disabled subscription on behalf of a customer. Cancelling can be done immediately (delete subscription) or disabled. 

Note:

Cancel Immediate is available only to TAs.

To Cancel or Enable:

  1. ​Select the options menu and select "Manage".
  2. Input the case number.
  3. Select the type of cancellation.
    • CustomerImmediateCancellation - Will disable and immediately deprovision/delete the subscription.
    • CustomerCancellation - Will disable the subscription and maintain data for 90 days.

8. Recalculate Tax OR Regenerate invoice

 Recalculating Invoice and Regenerating Invoice options depend on the customer scenario. 

  • Recalculate invoice: When a customer wants to recalculate the tax on the Invoice or have credit/refunds applied.  This is an immediate action. (for example, the customer calls in on the 15th of the month after getting their invoice and wants to recalculate and flush a new one for that same month).
  • Reasons for recalculating an invoice
  • Tax ID/tax type change: The customer has new tax information and needs a new invoice generated with the updated tax shown
  • Refund: The customer has a refund from last month and wants it to immediately reflect on that invoice
    • Tax refund: After tax is refunded the customer wants an invoice recalculated or regenerated
  • Regenerate invoice: When a customer wants a new invoice generated due to cosmetic changes (Address, phone, etc.) This is an immediate action and will generate a new Invoice that day.
  • Reasons for regenerating an invoice:
  • Change contact details: The customer wants to update the contact details on the invoice (Address)
    • Updating the PO is done through its own action and called out in the documentation (select Invoice, Expand,  select the blue options button with ellipses (…), select Update PO)
  • Invoice cycles:
    • Field Led: 5th of every month
    • Partner Led: 8th of every month
    • Customer Led:  9th of every month

8.6 Recalculate tax

This feature allows you to recalculate the tax on a product(s). If the customer just made a major purchased and notices the tax rate changed prior to receiving the invoice, they may request to recalculate the tax on that single purchase.  If they have already received the invoice recalculate a new invoice.
