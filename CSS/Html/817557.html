1. Overview

1.1 When to engage

Only engage an Advanced Capability Advocate (ACA) for the scenarios in the appropriate Engagement Scenario KBs linked above.

  • The listed scenarios may require ACA assistance when available troubleshooting is completed, and the customer’s issue remains unresolved.
  • All engagement requests require supervisor approval. To learn how to engage, follow KB 2981105 Procedure: Advanced Capability Advocate engagement.
  • When there is highly confidential personal data transmitted to the ACA, always use the Data Transfer & Management (DTM) tool. Follow KB 4135034 Tool User Guide: Data Transfer & Management (DTM) on how to use the DTM tool.
  • For information on personal data, see KB 3064766 Procedure: Privacy: Handling Microsoft customer personal data.

1.2 Customer follow-up

Tier 1 will be the main point of contact for the customer through the engagement procedures, and it is important to provide awareness and updates to the customer as needed.

  • The Advanced Capability Advocate (ACA) will provide the guidance needed to resolve the customer’s issue via child Service Request (SR) engagement, and it is important to set expectations with the customer around when the issue will be resolved.
    • See section 1.3 for ACA response time and set follow-ups for 24 hours to either provide the resolution to the customer or provide information requested by ACA to resolve the case.
  • The ACA will provide updates in the parent SR once a resolution has been determined, or additional details are needed.
  • The child SR will always be closed by ACA when they are done working on the request, but ACA may leave the SR open for up to 3 days before closing the child SR for any questions about the resolution.
  • Follow procedure for customer callback management in KB 3099069 Procedure: Call Handling: Holds, disconnects, callbacks and ghost calls.
  • If additional details are requested, please add them to the child SR once they are obtained.

1.3 Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocate response time

Once a child SR is sent to ACA for engagement, the parent SR will be updated with guidance within 24 hours.

  • ACA will close the child SR once resolution of the customer’s issue has been provided.

1.4 Invalid engagement

For a full list of valid engagement scenarios, see the KBs linked in the What is this article about? section at the top of this article. An engagement can be considered invalid for the following reasons:

  • Parent SR sent for engagement rather than a child SR.
  • A previously closed child SR is re-opened for engagement.
  • Required information for the Engagement Type is missing.
  • A documented solution exists that does not require ACA engagement.
  • Failed follow-up with customer or ACA.

Any parent SR sent for engagement is invalid, only child SRs may be sent for ACA engagement. Additionally, a closed child SR should never be re-opened. See KB 2988026 Guideline: Service Request (SR) Creation and Case Ownership.

  • In an event that required information for the Engagement Type is missing on the child SR, ACA will update the parent SR advising the specific information that is required for the engagement.
  • The child SR will remain open with ACA until a resolution to the customer’s issue has been provided.
  • If a documented solution exists, ACA will update the parent SR with guidance pointing to the documented solution and close out the child SR.

3. Additional support

3.1 Suggested messaging

A suggested message means educating the customer and setting the appropriate expectations in a positive manner. If the issue does not fit any of the valid escalation scenarios, you may have to deliver a suggested message to the customer. Suggested messages should not be escalated if the message can be communicated at Tier 1, as this delays the resolution to the customer. When delivering a suggested message, always keep in mind:

  • Discuss messaging with your team leads when you have questions on positioning.
  • Explain what can be done, instead of what can’t be done.
  • Give the customer other options when they are available.

Note: Look at the applicable documentation to support your messaging (KB, Xbox Communications Center, solution assets, and support.xbox.com).

3.2 Emerging issues  

There are issues, events, and scenarios that may not be captured in the list above but do require an engagement to resolve or investigate. These are the true unknown exceptions to process. Ensure that you have checked all available solution assets to assist the customer, including the ASD News portal, KB, and support.microsoft.com and other solution assets.

If the issue is emerging, follow the procedure outlined in KB 3038858 Procedure: How and when to engage CSS Command Center (CSS CC) all regions.

3.3 Supporting documentation

For engagement types such as “Warranty or service contract dispute” or “Prepaid Card/Token Issues”, supporting documentation may be a requirement for engagement. Ensure that valid documentation is attached to the service request prior to completing the engagement request.

Examples:

  • Exact product purchased and console serial number if on receipt.
  • Pre-paid card information (such as game or content and subscription type).
  • Purchase information (such as date and price).
  • Retailer information (name, physical address, phone number).

3.4 FAQs

Q: Should I engage an Advanced Capability Advocate if the customer asks to speak to someone else?
A: No. Use your immediate resources, such as supervisors, chat support, and red flag.

Q: Should I engage an Advanced Capability Advocate if I cannot find the appropriate solution to the customer’s issue?
A: No. Inform the customer that you are seeking assistance from your supervisor. Follow standard process to ask a supervisor to help. The supervisor should review the issue and the steps you've taken and provide you and the customer with the resolution.

Q: I have exhausted all troubleshooting steps available, my supervisor has reviewed the service request, and the service request matches one of the engagement scenarios listed below. Should I engage an Advanced Capability Advocate?
A: Yes. Set proper turnaround time expectations with the customer and ensure that all the data in the incident form is accurate and complete.

Q: The call was disconnected. Should I engage an Advanced Capability Advocate?
A: No. For guidance on how to handle, see KB 2720271 Procedure: Xbox: Handling a Call that Disconnects Unexpectedly.

Q: Should I engage an Advanced Capability Advocate if the customer has multiple billing accounts?
A: No. Troubleshoot and identify the customer’s root cause/issue. Only engage if the issue cannot be resolved through troubleshooting.

Note: Multiple billing accounts attached to a single Microsoft account is not a valid engagement scenario.

Q: Should I engage an Advanced Capability Advocate if the customer has an account/console/feature ban?
A: No. These customers should be referred to enforcement.xbox.com according to KB 981729 Procedure: Xbox Live: Enforcement activity (bans).

Q: Should I engage an Advanced Capability Advocate if the customer has an original Xbox account?
A: No. See KB 3055957 Procedure: Original Xbox Support Boundaries.
