What is this article about?

This article outlines the process of collecting customer data, protecting sensitive information, and general information on the European GDPR requirement . At Microsoft, building consumer trust and protecting the Microsoft brand through proper privacy practices is everyone’s responsibility. It’s important that you understand Microsoft’s privacy policies and incorporate strong privacy practices into your day to day work.

Note: If an advertiser would like to report a personal data violation, direct them to report the violation at Microsoft.com/support > Contact Us > More Options > Report a violation.

﻿1. Overview

Every reasonable effort should be made to ensure that all customer information is properly collected and protected throughout the Microsoft Advertising ecosystem. Proper data protection ensures the safety and security of advertisers and searchers and reduces the risk of social engineering and fraudulent activity.

Depending on the data classification and categorization, varying levels of protection are required. For simplified and consistent data handling, all Microsoft Advertising teams must implement the customer data protection guidelines outlined in this article.

﻿﻿2. Requirements for handling customer data

2.1 Collecting, using, and sharing customer data

Microsoft is committed to protecting the privacy of its customers. Data points within each data category may have different classifications which can change encryption requirements. Always follow the encryption requirements for the most sensitive data point in a data set. See section 2.2, for Microsoft Advertising encryption requirements.

The following practices should be used when handling a customer’s personal information:

Do’s:

  • Only collect the data you need from a customer to provide the support they need – do not collect any additional information that you do not need.
  • Only use approved tools to provide customer support, and only store customer data in approved systems.
  • Only make updates to a customer’s personal data through a service request (SR) or an approved process.
  • When you suspect or are involved in a data protection incident, report it to your manager (for example, sending an update email to the wrong customer).

Don’ts:

  • Never expose more information to a customer than necessary. For example, never proactively provide the customer’s personal data/HBI to the customer, even if authenticated. You should only receive personal data/HBI from the customer.
  • Never ask a customer for their credentials or password or use their credentials or password to troubleshoot an issue, even if the customer initiates the request. If the customer proactively emails their username/password combination, they must confirm their password changed or it must be reported and removed from the ticket.
  • Do not document customer personal data/HBI such as the phone number or email in the case title/subject or unencrypted files on your computer. This is a Microsoft legal requirement and protects our customers’ personal information.
  • Don’t use tools inappropriately, such as using them to look up celebrities or old friends.
  • Don’t send/accept a request to connect on social media with customers, even if the customer initiated the request.
  • Never access or transfer any diagnostic data from a customer's machine via LogMeIn. Exception: If the customer has been notified that the data will be transferred and agrees to the data transfer.

2.2 Document encryption requirements

Virtually all Microsoft Advertising customer interactions are classified as confidential under these data classifications and categories. To ensure consistent handling of potentially sensitive customer information, Microsoft Advertising quality standards require encryption of all documents at rest and in transit. Files stored in DTM do not require encryption; however, locally downloaded copies of files from DTM do require encryption. As a general rule, if you can encrypt, then you should encrypt, unless you’re using DTM. If neither DTM nor file encryption are viable methods of securely relaying files, see Escalating to partners...>Attaching Files to UCMT

For details on how to escalate to partners, see KB 3170756 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Escalating to Partners using UCMT, COSMIC, and CAP.

MAST escalation partners, see section 3, DTM guidance for MAST escalation partners.

2.3 Escalations

Escalations to UCMT

The ASD-UCMT integration is enough to securely relay any data required to investigate without further action to encrypt. This applies only to the initial escalation, since all subsequent communications with UCMT occur via email.

When relaying documents to UCMT, all attachments should be encrypted and emailed directly to the ticket. If the file type is unencryptable, use DTM and share the DTM workspace with the receiving ticket owner. If DTM is down for any reason, engage your TA for help. 

Escalations to COSMIC (email)

Some processes and data points required for SanOps assistance are granted unique exceptions to relay information via email (such as, offline refunds), which we hope to resolve with additional DTM & ticketing system functionality in the future.

Combinations of the data points below are approved to send in the body of an email escalation to SANOPS (aka AdOps).

  • SRX
  • Issue description
  • XID
  • CID
  • Currency
  • Amount
  • Billing Document IDs
  • Legal Customer/Payee name
  • Customer/Payee Email Address

3.3 Send files to T1

Do not send any unencrypted files to T1 Support. ASD ticketing system must not receive unencrypted files via email since this would violate our data protection policies and may require additional escalation to remove the attachment from the ASD ticket.

  1. If you already have access to a DTM workspace for the ticket, simply load the files under the Internal tab of the workspace for the T1 case owner to retrieve.
    [Asset 4464450]

  2. Load customer-facing files to the DTM workspace using the ticket-specific, customer-facing link. Request the customer-access link from the ASD case owner, as necessary. The URL begins with https://support.microsoft.com/files/workspace=.

  3. Otherwise, load the file directly to UCMT, and inform the ASD case owner they can retrieve the attachment from the UCMT Attachments tab.

4. Removing sensitive customer data from tickets

ASD will automatically redact personal data; however, some data points must be manually removed, regardless of redaction. These include but are not limited to:

  • Customer's password – If the customer sends their password to the ticket, make two attempts to advise them to change it. If they don’t, it will need to be reported for removal from the ticket per the guidelines in KB 4094950 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data protection incidents. Be sure to note in the ticket if the customer confirms they changed their password.
  • Full credit card numbers – Full credit card numbers should always be reported for removal from the ticket. Last four digits alone are not considered sensitive unless paired with another personal data point like full name, date of birth, or address.
  • Government issued information (such as, Tax IDs or SSN) – Tax IDs are critical to conduct business with Microsoft Advertising and are not required to be removed, but other IDs, such as social security number, are not required to do business with Microsoft advertising and should be removed from the ticket per the guidelines in KB 4094950 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data protection incidents.
  • Financial statements – Must be collected via DTM if required for monthly invoice setup (see KB 3170034 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Monthly Invoice Setup for more information). If the customer inadvertently sends the documents directly to the ticket via email, move the documents into the DTM workspace and submit a request to remove sensitive data.

This is a non-exhaustive list, so it’s ok to report other data points for removal and err on the side of caution – just consider what data points you, as a consumer, would be ok with a business transmitting and storing.

Refer to KB 4094950 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data protection incidents for further guidance on requesting data point removal from tickets. You will need to specify the timestamp and location of the communication containing the data.

To remove sensitive data erroneously added to a UCMT ticket, as the UCMT ticket owner to assist with the removal.

5. Definitions

5.1 Personal data

Personal data is any information that can be used to identify an individual. Not all personal data points must be removed from tickets. Review personal data/HBI below for details on handling/scrubbing data points from tickets. As a best practice, ensure email body does not include too much personal data and that all files are password protected in transit or at rest.

5.2 Data classifications

Data may be classified as highly confidential, confidential, general, public, or non-business. For data not listed in the examples below, v- users may refer to the Data Classification Wizard to determine appropriate data protection measures.

Highly confidential data

If highly confidential data were to be inappropriately disclosed, modified, or destroyed, it would result in regulatory or legal impact, revenue loss, loss of competitive advantage, permanent loss of stakeholder trust, and have direct impact on Microsoft, Microsoft's shareholders, partners, or customers, the Data Steward’s organization, or dependent or reliant organizations.

Examples:

  • Authentication Data
  • Full credit card number
  • Receipts and payment data
  • Future or active sales and marketing plans
  • Sensitive personal employment data (such as, religion, political affiliation, lifestyle, medical, SSN, mother’s maiden name, or cc info)
  • Hardware/software tokens
  • Material/unannounced financial data - must be relayed via DTM if required for monthly invoice setup (see KB’s 4056786 and 3170034).
  • Geo-location tracking data
  • Credit card and transaction information
  • Government issued information
  • Personal identification number
  • Social security number
  • Username and password pairs

Highly confidential data, if required, should always be encrypted at rest and in transit (collect by phone or use DTM).

Some (not all) highly confidential data must be removed from tickets. See section 4, for details.

Confidential data

If confidential data were to be inappropriately disclosed, modified, or destroyed, it would result in regulatory impact or fines, result in permanent loss of stakeholder trust or failure to meet defined SLAs, therefore impacting Microsoft, Microsoft's shareholders, partners, or customers, the Data Steward’s organization, or dependent or reliant organizations.

Examples:

  • IBAN/Bank Account Numbers - should always be submitted by customers directly to Microsoft Payment Central for refunds (see KB 4033775 Tool User Guide: Microsoft Advertising: Microsoft Payment Central). Microsoft account numbers are available in external help: Choosing a payment method for Microsoft Advertising.
  • Current systems configuration data
  • Data or software file shares
  • Future or active processes or procedures
  • Operating procedures/manuals
  • Personal Employment Data (such as, first/last name, personnel number, email, location, or standard title)
  • Media product features and release schedules
  • Product keys
  • Confidential source code
  • Microsoft trade secrets
  • Address
  • Age
  • Email
  • Fax number
  • Gender
  • IP address
  • Microsoft account ID
  • Name (first & last)
  • Phone number
  • Executed sales contracts
  • Receipts
  • Sales account data
  • System diagnostic data
  • Support tickets or notes

Confidential data should always be encrypted at rest (automatic in ASD) and in transit (collect by phone, use password encryption on files, or DTM).

General data

If general data were to be inappropriately disclosed, modified, or destroyed, it would result in limited loss impacting Microsoft, Microsoft shareholders, partners, or customers, the Data Steward’s organization, or dependent or reliant organizations.

Public data

Data which is intended for public disclosure, such as published financial information, public web page content, and freely available software.

Non-business data

Non-business is data which does not belong to Microsoft or our customers.

5.3 Data categories

For full descriptions and examples of the following, v- users may visit Data Classification: customer data categories in the Services division.

Content data

  • Content data is any information generated directly by the customer, generated by the customer's representatives, or co-created by the customer in conjunction with CSS and support consultants, which contains their intellectual property or the customer's own content of documents, emails, spreadsheets, websites, or other confidential data.
  • May include personal data or confidential information, and if so, that information must be handled as content data.
  • Encryption required at rest and in transit.

Support and consulting interaction data

  • Support and consulting interaction data is any information the customer provides to CSS and the support division when requesting assistance, such as via a support ticket or by requesting help from a support engineer or consultant. Interaction data includes data required to provide the service to the customer, troubleshooting information, consulting engagement artifacts, or information such as text, sound, software or image files intended to better enable responding to their request for assistance.
  • If interaction data from a customer contains personal data or confidential business information, it should be handled as content data.
  • Encryption required at rest, recommended in transit.

Contact data

  • Contact information about customers or employees, or customer contacts that is needed to maintain the business relationship such as email addresses and names.
  • If contact data from a customer contains interaction data or content data, it should be handled as the respective data class.
  • Payment card information must be encrypted at rest and in transit; otherwise, not required

Account data

  • Account data is information that is needed to support the business relationship with the customer, such as account information, sales history, and contract documents.
  • Account data also includes business account information related to opportunities and engagement planning.
  • If account data contains contact data, it should be classified as contact data.
  • Payment card information must be encrypted at rest and in transit; otherwise, not required

Microsoft business data

  • Data generated during support operations. Does not contain content or contact data, and is not linkable or associated to a user or customer.
  • Encryption not required.

6.2 California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) is a comprehensive privacy statute that imposes requirements on certain qualifying businesses and gives California residents a number of rights related to their personal information, including the right to opt out of the "sale" of their personal information to third parties.

To learn more about the impact of CCPA on Microsoft Advertising, review the CCPA FAQ and sample emails to client inquiries. This document includes information for internal teams about the Microsoft Advertising CCPA plan and sample email responses to address questions received from advertisers and partners. Any media inquiries should be directed to the Microsoft Advertising PR team. This document shouldn't be posted to any external sites or sent via email externally.

To learn about what information you can share directly with clients, review the Microsoft Advertising CCPA FAQ. Use this content to respond to clients' specific questions. Only send the entire FAQ document to clients if it's necessary.

External messaging:

For customers requesting access or deletion: “Thank you for your inquiry. You can exercise your rights by visiting the Privacy Dashboard or using the Privacy Response Center. Please see our Privacy Statement and Microsoft Trust Center - CCPA for more information about your rights.”

Response to Customer CCPA inquiries: “Thank you for your inquiry about Microsoft’s CCPA position. Microsoft prioritizes the privacy and security of our users’ personal information. Microsoft is leveraging its extensive expertise in protecting data, championing privacy, and complying with complex regulations to prepare for the CCPA. We believe that the CCPA, as well as other applicable state, federal, and international data privacy laws, is an important step forward for giving individuals greater rights in their data. We are committed to CCPA compliance across our business, including Microsoft Advertising, as of January 1, 2020, when the law takes effect.

As a business subject to the CCPA, Microsoft will provide certain notifications to consumers regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of their personal information and honor certain consumer requests in accordance with our privacy policies, including providing descriptions of applicable consumer rights and how to exercise them.

Under the CCPA, Microsoft Advertising is a business when it collects personal information directly from consumers or a third party when it receives personal information from its clients. It is not a service provider. Being a business or third party gives us the flexibility necessary to process personal information in order to provide you with the full range of Microsoft Advertising services similar to what we provide today. For these reasons, we are not executing service provider addendums with our customers. For general information about Microsoft’s privacy practices, please see our Privacy Statement.”

6.3 Data Subject Rights Requests: Individual requests to access, edit, and delete customer data

Assure the requestor that Microsoft has always been focused on the responsible collection and transfer of data associated with its products and services – and Microsoft Advertising is no exception.

Refer the customer to the Privacy Response Center (PRC) per the guidelines in KB 4094951 Procedure: Privacy: Handling data subject rights requests.

If the requestor is a legal professional asking on behalf of their customer, you may direct them to the Privacy response Center in addition to informing them that formal complaints may also be submitted through the Microsoft Legal Department. See KB 3174388 Guidelines for Unexpected Events During Client Contacts for details.

Data Subject Rights Request/Privacy Response Center ticket status inquiries

You may tell customers inquiring about the status of their DSR inquiry to the Privacy Response Center whether the PRC ticket is open or closed. An issue code to capture these inquiries will be added soon.

Code them under: Privacy Inquiry> Data Protection Inquiry> Data Subject Rights Requests> Customer has general...> Redirected customer until a 'status inquiry' option is created and deployed in ASD (in progress).

See the Microsoft Advertising GDPR FAQ for a scripted response to customers requesting Data Processor Agreements.

General Data Protection Regulation (EU) - GDPR

Microsoft Advertising GDPR FAQ - 5.23.18 (External-facing, may be shared with customers)

General Data Protection Regulation is designed to harmonize data privacy laws across Europe and give greater protection and rights to individuals. It specifies how consumer data should be used and protected in the context of selling goods and services to the citizens of the EU, regardless of whether the organization is in the EU or not. Organizations can be fined up to 20 million Euros or 4% of global turnover for non-compliance. GDPR comes into force on May 25, 2018.
