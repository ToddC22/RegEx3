1. Overview

This process that allows for exceptions to current policies as defined by Microsoft, within either SDK content or XCC posts. In-policy resolutions do not require exception approval as Microsoft has already established these as acceptable solutions.

Important:

Exception approvals are specifically designed for advocates to engage site rangers and gain approval when providing resolutions that do not fall within policy guidelines. The steps in this article must be followed by advocates and rangers with every policy exception unless a specified exception process is already called out in a separate SDK article. CSS Fraud Prevention Ops proactively reviews casework and any exceptions that are made outside of this process may result in a corrective action.

2. CSS exception request

2.1 Procedures

Note: The following approval SR notation procedure must be followed with every exception approval request case.

  1. Advocates use all available resources to review the scenario and associated policies, if it is determined that an exception to the established policy should be made, request approval for exceptions from the site ranger. Indicate the type of exception and the reasoning for the proposed exception. Advocates include appropriate notation and refund coding while citing KB 4576497 Procedure: Xbox: CSS Exception process as the associated KB.
  2. The site Ranger will review the case to confirm whether the issue and potential resolution are already within established policy:
  • In policy: Rangers provide guidance to the advocate regarding where to find the information and how to assist the customer.
  • Not in policy:
  1. Rangers ensure this is a valued customer by reviewing the account to check for tenure and a healthy purchase history as well as any potential history of excessive SR creation, phishing, fraud, or abuse of policy. See KB 2991467 Procedure: Privacy: Handling social engineering, fraud, and phishing incidents for more info. Note: Be cautious of newly created accounts without a purchase history.
  2. Rangers make a decision: Should I provide this exception or not?
  • Notes:
  • Be conservative in the amount of exceptions you are making. Ensure that you are thoroughly reviewing the account and situation as every scenario should not be automatically approved as an exception.
  • An exception must be appropriate to the case and the customer’s scenario. Be conservative in your exceptions. Don't give 12 months of Xbox Live when one month will do.
  1. Using their MSNCS credentials, the approving supervisor, SME, ERA or Ranger leaves a comment regarding the outcome of their review and their approval on the account. It is not necessary for Rangers to take ownership of the SR.

To leave a comment:

  1. Secure access to the account.
  2. Scroll down to the Comments section.
  3. Select Add Comment.
  • Note: Reviewed and approved per KB 4338531 Procedure: Return/Refund: Unauthorized purchase by a family member/known person, under section 5 If the refund request is over $1000 USD in value.
  1. Enter the comment select Next, then Submit.
  1. If a Ranger has given valid approval and an exception is decided on, advocates ensure the customer is provided appropriate context and the proper expectations are set. Ensure the customer understands that this is a one-time exception.

Important note:

If you find that there is a common exception scenario that is consistently approved, then please create a Speak Up! item to request that the exception be worked into policy. (for example, an issue Microsoft is responsible for that commonly arises, yet does not have an established official policy for resolutions.)

Reminder!

The exception approval comments/templates should only be utilized by Xbox Rangers. Only exception comments left by Ranger MSNCS credentials will be considered valid. Please be certain to add the Ranger exception approval notation within 24 hours of the exception being provided to the customer. Approvals are only considered valid if noted by the Ranger within this timeframe. Don't forget to notate!
