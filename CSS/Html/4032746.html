1. Overview

We have introduced new user roles to help employees in your organization easily manage your Microsoft partnership on Partner Center. Each role carries specific permissions and responsibilities. It’s important to understand the new roles and the business opportunity associated with each one.

The following table shows each role permission, and the function(s) that each role is allowed:

Role

Why is this role important?

 

Who should be assigned this role?

What are the benefits to this role?

MPN Partner Admin

We created this role to easily manage your organization’s partnership and benefits with Microsoft.

 

The MPN Partner Admin should be the same person or team who manages the MPN relationship in your organization today.

Learn more about the MPN benefits here.

Business Profile Admin

The new Partner Center referral capability provides one place to manage consolidated lead sources, making it easier for your organization to find the right customers and for the right customers to find you. 

 

​In order to acquire and capitalize on new leads, create a business profile and assign a Business Profile Admin to help you highlight your areas of expertise. Successful profiles have a narrative that is designed to connect with the right audiences.

 

The Business Profile Admin should be a marketing person or team within your organization who works on your company brand and value prop, as well, can help maintain and update the profile as your organization grows and develops new offerings.

Learn more about the MPN benefits here.

Referrals Admin       

The Partner Center Referrals has been updated with various stages—New, Evaluate, Negotiate, and Archive—that partners can see when they log into the dashboard. In order to prevent the outcome of previous deals impacting which referrals are surfaced, the status of each stage must be updated by the partner in Partner Center.

 

While each stage has a time window when partners must respond, we recommend that partners don’t wait until that time expires. Customers are reaching out to multiple partners and the faster you respond, the higher the chances of closing a deal. Assigning a Referrals Admin to manage each stage’s status can help prevent lost business and missed referrals.

The Referrals Admin should be the sales or marketing person within your organization who is responsible for turning referrals into relationships. This person determines which referrals to actively engage, when to update their status, and how quickly to respond to them.  

 

This person should also regularly evaluate your profile and work with the Business Profile Admin to make necessary updates. 

Learn more about the MPN benefits here.

Account Admin role 
 

 The Account Admin role is a business role that can manage relationships with Microsoft, without being a technical role, unlike the Global Admin of the tenant role. This role does not replace any existing role – instead, augments as an additional role, being also an alternative for partners that used to rely on Global Admin for everything. However, this role can do non CSP related functional delegations and work.

Once rolled out, the person onboarding to the MPN program (basic) or migrating to PC from PMC will be made an Account admin and MPN partner admin by default. There can be more than one Account admin for an organization, the scope of Account admins being the entire organization – all locations listed in the organization.

The account admin can assign themselves business roles: Business profile admin, referral admin, incentive admin, incentive user roles.

 Learn more about the MPN benefits here.

2. Frontline Partner Advocates Call to Action

Help partners understand how to:

1. Navigate through the new Partner Center Roles and Permissions.

2. Identify which roles map to specific partner types, and specific function capabilities.

Partner Center access is role-based, so you can assign permissions to customize the user's view to show only the features the user needs to complete their specific tasks.

For each user, you must select two permission levels though one of these levels can be the default level which is Manages your organization's account as. If you leave role selection blank, they will not have those permissions:

Agent permissions control what kind of customer data and account information the user can see and change.

Admin permissions control the level of access the user has to Partner Center features. This setting has impact outside of Partner Center - a billing admin can access bills for all Microsoft services (even those unrelated to CSP), and a Global Admin can access user accounts and customer accounts beyond CSP as well.

This also means that a user can be assigned admin permissions but not agent permissions in which case you would leave the agent permissions set to the default.

Important: The default setting should always be Manages your organization's account as, unless the user's role requires additional access to complete tasks and support customers. If you don't select at least one role for a user, they will not be able to work in Partner Center.

The following table explains what each role can do in Partner Center. When thinking about how you want to assign roles, consider the work a user does in your organization but also consider that some roles are mutually exclusive meaning you can only have that role. For example, the admin agent, sales agent, and helpdesk agent roles are mutually exclusive. The Business Admin and the Referral Admin can have more than one role, for example, you might assign one person to handle both of the roles.

3. Roles and Permissions

Role

What the role allows

Global admin (both CSP and MPN)
  • Access all Microsoft account/services with full privileges
  • Create support tickets for Partner Center
  • View agreements, price lists, and offers
  • Billing
  • View, create, and manage partner users
  • Assign roles within organization
  • Activate support contracts and view Access ID + Contract ID*

Billing admin (CSP only)
  • Access all bills from Microsoft with full privileges
  • View agreements, price lists, and offers
  • Billing

Admin agent (CSP only): has access to all Partner Center features

  • Customer management
  • Add device list to the Partner Center
  • Create and apply profiles to devices
  • Subscription management
  • Service health and service requests for customers
  • Request delegated administrator privileges
  • View pricing and offers
  • Billing
  • Administer on behalf of a customer
  • Register a value-added reseller
Sales agent (CSP only): adds customers and manages their subscriptions
  • Customer management
  • Add device list to the Partner Center
  • Subscription management
  • View support tickets
  • Request a relationship with a customer
  • Manage customer leads
  • View the customer agreement
  • Register a value-added reseller
Helpdesk agent (CSP only): helps resolve customer issues with their services
  • Search for and view a customer
  • Edit customer details
  • Help resolve customer issues with billing or subscription management
  • Request support on behalf of customers (Note: You must be an admin agent to complete this task for Office 365 subscriptions)
  • Manage subscriptions and billing issues on behalf of customers (Note: You must be an admin agent to complete this task for Office 365 subscriptions)

User management admin (CSP only)

 

 
  • View, create, and manage users
  • View all partner profiles
  • View, create, and manage partner users

Business profile admin (both CSP and MPN)

 

 
  • View, create, and manage business profile
  • View, create, and manage partner service requests

Referral admin (both CSP and MPN)

 

 
  • View, create, and manage business profiles
  • Receive and manage referrals
  • View, create, and manage partner service requests

MPN partner admin (MPN only)
  • View, create, and manage partner service requests
  • View legal, organization, business, and MPN profiles
  • View user details and their skills data
  • View competencies
  • View and manage benefits
  • View and purchase MPN offers
  • View MPN offers order history and invoices
  • Can see users and roles but cannot assign roles
  • Can add non-tenant users
  • View partner contribution data
  • Can work in the Voucher Validation tool
  • View customer data analytics
  • Activate support contracts and view Access ID + Contract ID*

Account Admin (MPN only)
  • Can add users with non-tenant roles
  • Add locations
  • Enroll locations into programs (exceptions are CSP and indirect reseller programs)
  • Manage profiles related to the location accounts they administer
  • Assign MPN partner admin, Business profile admin, referral admin, incentive admin, incentive user roles to users
  • Activate support contracts and view Access ID + Contract ID*

* MPN Users can view Access ID + Contract ID in Partner Center MPN \ Benefits \ Technical benefits (they cannot activate the support contract). See article 2904733.

5. FAQ

Partner highlights the issues below related to their MPN account. What is the root cause and guidance to be provided?

I can’t see any benefits.
I can only see Benefits menu item under MPN but no other menu items are visible.
I can only see Technical Support Benefits but all other benefits are gone.
I can’t see where I can purchase/renew MAPS/Silver/Gold.
Where can I see my invoices.
I can’t see Competencies page.
I was able to access benefits last time I logged into PC (a couple of months back) but I can’t see benefits/offers/competencies today.

The root cause might be that the user does not have the right permission to view Offers, Competencies and Benefits. If the user does not own a MPN Partner Admin role, recommend user to contact an Account Admin or Global Admin within their organization which can assign him/her the right permission.

If user sees only Benefits in left Nav and not Offers & Competencies, user clearly doesn’t have MPN partner admin role. Guide user on who can give him MPN partner admin role. Cogs icon on top right corner has “User management” menu. Go to the page and filter users who have Global Admin or Account admin roles. (Refer the image below). Any one of these users can assign MPN partner admin role.

[Asset 4494851]

Partner claims that some of the users have lost their permissions they had in PMC.

During the migration process, the user who does the migration to PC is automatically granted MPN partner admin role apart from keeping his Global admin role, but all other users within the organization are “users” only and no longer admins they were back in PMC.

How do we check if a user has MPN partner admin roles assigned?

Best recommendation is to ask user himself to check in his User Management the roles he has assigned. Make sure the MPN partner admin checkbox is enabled. You may check as well in CMAT the corresponding roles: Global admin = Company Administrator; MPN Partner admin = BSP Purchaser. However, double checking with the user is mandatory.

How can I assign myself roles and permissions in PC account? 

To assign roles and permissions to yourself or to others, you need to have the following roles: Account Admin role or Global Admin role.

Example:

  • If the Partner wants have the Business Profile admin and Referrals admin roles assigned, but he does have the Account Admin role or Global Admin role, ask the Partner to tick the Business Profile admin and Referrals admin roles for himself and save the changes. Afterwards he needs to logout and login again to PC for changes to take effect.
  • If the Partner wants to have the Business Profile admin and Referrals admin roles assigned, and he also does not have the Account Admin or Global Admin roles, guide the Partner to click on the User management tab and filter the results by either Global Admins or Account Admins in his tenant and reach out to those individuals in his organization to assign the necessary user roles to their credentials.
