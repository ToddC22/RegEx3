1. Overview

Surface Support will provide troubleshooting support for customers with Surface and some Microsoft-branded accessories (see section 2) regardless of whether the customer owns a Surface device. For countries in scope of the Surface Out of Warranty (OOW) Diagnostic fee, see KB 3164620 Procedure: Surface: Out of Warranty (OOW) paid incident support for any exemptions.

You are expected to troubleshoot to determine if replacement of the accessory is necessary. If a replacement is necessary, create a fulfillment order in your service order tool (MST/ROBO/BSFS) if the customer’s accessory is in scope (see below section). If the accessory type is not in scope, warm-transfer or redirect the customer appropriately, so the customer can get their accessory replaced.

Note: If the customer owns a Surface device, write a fulfillment order against the customer’s device if replacement is necessary. Additional instructions are provided within this article on how to create a fulfillment order if the customer does not own a Surface device.

3. Case handling

If a customer owns an accessory considered in-scope of support, provide troubleshooting support. Depending on the scenario, you will need to warm transfer/redirect the customer for replacement support.

Consumer: Code the Product field on the ASD Incident Form accordingly.

If the customer does not own a Surface device and is calling about an accessory not in-scope, refer them to Supplemental Parts for a replacement (there may be a replacement fee). Alternately, the customer can contact the point of purchase to determine if a replacement is available.

5. Replacing in scope accessories for customers without a Surface device

5.1 Frontline steps

You will be unable to create a fulfillment order in your fulfillment tool (MST/ROBO/BSFS) without an appropriate registered serial number. Follow this procedure to have an unused serial number assigned to the customer for the duration of the fulfillment.

Support actions

Notes
  1. Troubleshoot the customer’s accessory to determine if a replacement is necessary.
  
  1. Verify the warranty status of the customer’s accessory with one of the options on the right.
  • Obtain a copy of the customer’s Proof of Purchase via email.
  • Decode the customer’s PID to determine the manufacturing date.
    • See section 6.
    • For additional information see KB 4025417 Procedure: Devices: How to verify the warranty status of an accessory sold separately.
  1. Document the details of the customer's accessory warranty in the case notes.
  
  1. Consult with a Tech Lead or Team Manager to request a serial number.
  • The request will be rejected if a serial number can be located for the device according to KB 4514977 Procedure: Surface: Services Exchange and Repair Offers Overview section 9, Serialized vs Non-Serialized.
  • They will obtain a serial number that will be registered to the customer.
    • If necessary, arrange a callback for the customer while the process is completed.
  1. Ensure the customer’s Microsoft account is authenticated before proceeding.

The customer’s hardware profile cannot be updated with address details without a device registered.
  1. Register the device to the customer’s Microsoft account.
  
  1. Update the customer’s profile.
  
  1. Proceed with writing a fulfillment order.

Note: You are only authorized to write orders for accessories listed in scope of this article. Proper usage is monitored and will be enforced.
  1. Follow standard processes to track and close the customer's replacement and SR.

5.2 Tech Lead/Team Manager steps to obtain serial number

Actions

Notes
  1. Review the Tier 1 serial number (SN) request.
  
  1. Verify that troubleshooting was performed.
  
  1. Verify the appropriate guidance to validate the accessory warranty followed.
  • No serial number should be issued for devices that are considered as a serialized device according to KB 4514977 Procedure: Surface: Services Exchange and Repair Offers Overview section 9. Serialized vs Non-Serialized.
    • Reject the request if necessary.
  1. Go to the Tech Lead document on the Surface Advocate Blog site.
  • DO NOT use another sites spreadsheet and/or attempt to borrow codes.
    • Notify your site management.
    • We will be monitoring spent codes along with the designated SR, and replenishing volume, as necessary.
  • Tech Leads that need access to the site will contact their Site Lead or representative and they will get you added to the SG.
  • Site Leads or representative will keep the SG updated when Tech Leads are no longer employed.
  1. Open the correct serial number spreadsheet.

Consumer:

The spreadsheet will correspond to the region you are in, NOT the region you support. See the following guide.

  • North America = Concentrix Nicaragua
  • Europe = TP Rabat, TP Athens, Arvato Brasov, Arvato Lisbon
  • Asia 1 = CVG Cebu, CVG Kuala Lumpur, Arvato Clark, Relia, TCK
  • Asia 2 = CVG Suzhou

Commercial:

  • Commercial - TL spreadsheet of SNs
  1. Find the first available serial number and fill in the spreadsheet.

Find a serial number that is not currently in use and fill in the following fields:

  • Date Checked out: Date the serial number was checked out
  • SR number: The associated case #
  • TL alias: Your email address / alias
  • Accessory to fulfill: The accessory that the serial number is for
  • Site name and location: Your site name and location
  • Notes: additional notes as necessary. For example, it can be the device configuration the accessory is used with.
  1. Provide the serial number to the requestor.

 
  1. Consumer Only: Once the order is complete follow the steps on the right.
  • Remove the serial number from the customer profile.
  • Update the Unregistered date field with the date you unregistered it from the customer.

6. Locating the Product ID (PID)

Accessory

Instructions

Wedge Touch Mouse Surface Edition

Turn the mouse upside down. There is a battery release button. Pressing this button will release the cover. With the cover extended, look for the PID as shown below.

Arc Touch Mouse Surface Edition

Turn the mouse upside down. Remove the battery door and remove the batteries. Locate the PID.

Surface Dial

Turn the Dial upside down. Remove the magnetic base from the bottom of the dial to open the battery compartment, then remove the batteries. Locate the PID in the battery well.

Surface or Microsoft Modern Keyboards with fingerprint ID

Turn the Keyboard upside down. Slide the battery casing horizontally away from the top edge of the keyboard to open the battery compartment, then remove the batteries. Locate the PID in the battery well.

Surface Mouse/Microsoft Modern Mouse

Turn the Mouse upside down. Remove the battery cover, then remove the batteries. Locate the PID in the battery well.

Surface Ergonomic Keyboard

Turn the Keyboard upside down. Remove the battery cover, then remove the batteries. Locate the PID in the battery well.

Microsoft Classic or Pro Intellimouse

Turn the mouse upside down. It will be located below accessory information.

Surface USB-C to HDMI adapter

Check the backside of the adapter.

Surface USB-C to VGA adapter

Check the backside of the adapter.

Surface/Microsoft Precision mouse

Check inside the battery compartment.

Wireless mouse

Wireless mouse will not have a serial number.

All replacement orders will need to be written against an unused Surface serial number. See Replacing in scope accessories for customers without a Surface device section above.

If no Surface SN is available (third-party OEM), orders will need to be written against a "dummy SN".

7. FAQ

Scenario

Solution

Customer owns Surface, owns non-Surface branded Microsoft Hardware (mouse or keyboard): Why can’t Surface Support replace the accessory?

While the customer owns a Surface device and you can have it registered in MST, the Service Center that serves Surface does not carry inventory of non-Surface branded Microsoft Hardware. As a result, you cannot write an order.

How can I provide the status of an order?

For orders created in MST, see KB 4053533 TUG: MST: Devices section, section 4 Order status and history.

For previous contacts that were warm transferred OR redirected: Warm transfer OR redirect to the same entity/channel.
