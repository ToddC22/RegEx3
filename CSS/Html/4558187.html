What’s the article about

This article describes Co-sell, a collaborative selling initiative that enables partners and Microsoft to drive joint revenue and customer success.

1. Co-sell program overview

Partner Center is becoming the one stop place for modern co-selling which is a modern way that allows partners to share and receive deals from Microsoft sellers. This new connector will allow partners to share and manage deals through Partner Center via their own CRMs. Co-sell is a collaborative selling initiative that enables partners and Microsoft to drive joint revenue and customer success. Co-sell is centered on collaborating to land relevant partner solutions with Microsoft managed customers to meet their business needs.

Co-sell Ready solution requirements include:

Co-sell requirements Being discovered for co-sell across all three channels begins with an active MPN membership and by publishing your solution in the commercial marketplace. Co-sell solution requirements depend on the channels you want to pursue.

Commercial Marketplace requirements link: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/marketplace/

2. Co-sell Ready description

Co-sell Ready refers to solutions that have met the Co-sell Ready requirements and are eligible to participate in the Co-sell program. Co-sell Ready solutions are discoverable by Microsoft Sellers in the OCP Catalog, are enabled to share and receive leads with Microsoft sellers, and have access to Sell with and GTM benefits.

IMPORTANT:

To co-sell with Microsoft, partners need a commercial-ready solution, an engaged sales team, and a strong go-to-market strategy. They must also meet specific additional criteria. For more info, see Become co-sell ready -link: https://partner.microsoft.com/en-US/membership/co-sell-with-microsoft#become-ready

3. Types of Co-sell opportunities
  • Co-sell - This is where one or more Microsoft sales representatives are actively engaged in the deal to help solve a customer problem. The opportunities can originate from a customer account where a Microsoft sales representative invites the partner to participate in a co-selling activity or from a partner where they need additional help from Microsoft sales to close the deal.
  • Partner to Partner (P2P) – This is where one partner can invite other partners in the Microsoft Co-sell ecosystem to collaborate. Partners can optionally invite a Microsoft sales representative to the deal to help close the deal. Both partners and Microsoft can be invited even after creating a deal, as long as it is prior to complete. (Note that complete includes won, lost, declined, and expired)
  • Private pipeline deal - These are opportunities that a partner decides to work on independently while creating them in partner center. Partners have the capability to invite Microsoft sellers or other partners after creating the deal to make it a collaborative selling activity as an optional step. The details of the deal are not shared with Microsoft sellers till the time Microsoft sellers help is explicitly sought by the partner.

5. Creating a Co-sell opportunity

Select the customer

After clicking on “+ New deal” partners will need to identify the customer for this specific deal. They should enter their location and start typing the name. The auto suggest will start showing recommendations based on the search criteria. Since many companies have more than one business entity in the same location, partners may see multiple results. If they're not sure which to choose, you can confirm which D-U-N-S Number belongs to their customer. They should be sure to select the exact match for the customer they're working with.

Once they Select the customer, they’ll be prompted to enter the name, phone number, and email address for the person who's the main point of contact for this deal. These details are optional and are required only if they are planning to invite Microsoft seller to help them with the engagement. After they have decided to give or ignore this info, they should click Next.

Add their employees (Add team members)

After adding the deal details, partners should add the employees that will be working on this specific deal. They will need to enter the name, phone number and email address of the employee. These details are mandatory, and they need to have at least one contact with all the details entered for them to create a deal. These details can be changed even after creating a deal. Recent contacts from their previous deals are shown on the right side for them to quickly to add them to the deal.

Add solution(s)

In this section, partners need to provide the information related to the solutions that will be part of this deal. This is a mandatory section where they need to add at least one solution for them to create a deal. The solution details can be changed even after creating a deal. There are multiple types of solutions that can be added to a deal which are described below

  • My company’s solutions: These are Co-sell ready solutions that are published by partner’s company
  • Microsoft: These are solutions owned by Microsoft
  • Other third-party solutions: These are Co-sell ready solutions that are published by other partners in the Microsoft Co-sell ecosystem

Important

Although partners can add multiple solutions to a deal, they can add only one incentive eligible solution. This can be either IP incentive eligible solution or an ISV connect offer.

Once they have provided all the necessary information, they should select Next to move to the section where they can decide the collaborative selling type. They have three options here as described below.

  1. Private pipeline deal: If partners don’t invite Microsoft or any other partner and create an engagement at this step, it will be of the type private pipeline. Microsoft sellers will have no visibility in to the details of this deal.
  2. Important: Deal registration is not applicable for Private deals. Exercise caution while creating a private deal with incentive eligible solutions as they will not be eligible for deal registration in Partner Center

Co-sell deal: If partners switch the toggle to yes for the question “Would you like Microsoft's help?”, the deal turns in to a Co-sell deal where a Microsoft seller can potentially help them with closing the deal. A request to help from Microsoft is no guarantee that a Microsoft seller will participate in the deal and they are given 14 days to decide if they want to help partners with the specific request made while inviting Microsoft. Do mention in detail the kind of help required from Microsoft in the notes section when they are inviting Microsoft.

Important: To co-sell with Microsoft, partners need a commercial-ready solution, an engaged sales team, and a strong go-to-market strategy. They must also meet specific additional criteria. For more info, see Become co-sell ready.

Responding to a Co-sell opportunity

Each opportunity moves through a life cycle of its own and this section will help understand the different stages and the actions that partners can take in each stage.

Received stage: In this stage they have received a new Co-sell opportunity either from a Microsoft seller or from other partners in the Microsoft Co-sell ecosystem. Partners will need to review the details, and feel free to contact the customer if they want to learn more about their business needs. They can take two action in this stage. Either accept or decline the referral:

  • Accept: Partners should enter a name for the deal, edit the estimated deal value, and the estimated purchase timeframe based on their review. They should also provide info in the Notes field to explain more about what the customer is looking for once they have established the contact with the customer either directly or with the help of the employees from the company which invited the specific company to participate in the deal. They can optionally enter their CRM ID (for the reference only), the marketing campaign ID which resulted in the respective opportunity and add contacts from their company who will be working on this deal. When they’ve finished, they should select Next. We'll move the referral to the next stage, which means they plan to actively engage with the customer to address their need. We'll also use this information to help partners find similar deals in the future.

[Asset 4569627]

  • Decline: Partners should select the reason they are declining the deal and add any notes they would like to include, then select Close deal. We'll archive it as Declined and notify either Microsoft or the partner who sent them this opportunity.

If they don't respond within the allotted time (currently 14 days), we'll archive it as Expired and notify either Microsoft or the partner who sent them this opportunity.

[Asset 4569643]

Accepted stage

Partners will need to work to close the deal with the customer. If they want to change any of the information they’ve provided for an accepted referral, they should select Edit. They can then update the deal name, estimated purchase date, estimated value, notes, CRM ID and/or the marketing campaign id. They can also select Add your team to provide the name, phone number, and email addresses of any additional people who are working on the deal. Solutions can also be edited based on the customer need.

All the deals that they have created will be in Accepted stage by default.

IMPORTANT: Once you started working on the deal, you can provide the details of the progress that you are making by marking the sales stages in the deal lifecycle. 7. Partner to Partner (P2P) deal

You can invite other partners to the deal by clicking on the Invite partner link. Below is the process for creating a P2P deal.

  • Select a partner: After clicking on Invite partner, you will be able to start typing the partner name to get suggested list of partners matching the name that you are entering. Select the partner you are interested in to fill additional details for that partner. You can only search for partners who are in the Microsoft Co-sell ecosystem and are transacting in Partner Center.
  • Estimated close date: This is the date by which you expect the invited partner to complete their part of the deal. The date is pre-filled so that you can choose to modify the date only if necessary. It is a mandatory field and can be edited by the partner you are inviting after creating the deal. You can’t modify this field after creating the deal.
  • Estimated value and currency: This is the value of the deal that the invited partner will have in the overall deal. Make sure that you enter correct value here so that the Important Deal registration is not applicable for Private deals. Exercise caution while creating a private deal with incentive eligible solutions as they will not be eligible for deal registration in Partner Center. The invited partner can decide if they want to be a part of the deal or not. The invited partner can change this value after creating the deal. You cannot modify this field after creating the deal.
  • Notes: Add the details for why you are inviting the partner to be a part of this deal. Detailed information will help the invited partner to decide if they want to participate.
  • Add your team: Add the employees from your company who will be working with the invited partner. If the invited partner accepts the deal, they can add their own employees so that both companies have a view of the entire team collaborating on the deal. You can only modify these details before creating the deal. Employee details entered in your deal data are pre-filled to make it easier for you to choose the employees who be working with this specific partner.
  • Add solutions: Add the solutions that you want the invited partner to bring to the table. At least one solution is mandatory. The invited partner can modify the solutions once they accept the invitation.
  • Identify the type of help: Finally, identify the specific help you need from the invited partner. Repeat this for all the partners you want to invite to be a part of this deal. A partner to partner deal can also have Microsoft seller involved where you are inviting both Microsoft and the partners to the deal. You can also invite both Microsoft and the partners later, after creating the deal.
