Procedure: How to handle a CritSit

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor | Duty Manager | Team Manager | Incident Manager

What is this article about?

Critical Situation (CritSit) is a Global process with tools designed to allow Microsoft to effectively manage critical (Severity 1/A) Premier Support Customer situations and provide a consistent, repeatable support experience around the world.  This document outlines the procedure for effectively working a CritSit case. This includes taking ownership, transferring,  or handing over to another engineer to continue working the case.

1. Steps

  • Take ownership of the case even if the Customer is not currently on the phone. This assists the Incident Manager and CritSit Owner with escalation management. For every case created in Rave, a Service Desk shadow case is automatically created. A "Critical " Rave case will trigger the CritSit process. See 4339172 | Office 365 CritSit process for Premier customer in Rave for details. 
    • There is a deep link into the Rave case in Service Desk where CMET team can click and view Rave case details. In Service Desk, a link to the Rave case is added in Resource url. Click View additional case details link.
  • Follow the 4457092 | Procedure: Documenting Case Notes for any information about special handling of the Customer.
  • Continue to update case notes every 60-90 minutes.  This helps the IM and CritSit Owner measure the progress being made on an issue and properly set the Customer's expectations.
  • Be available on internal Microsoft communications. There are times when the Customer's management has communicated additional concerns to the CritSit Owner or IM that needs to be shared with the engineer.
  • Know the CritSit Owner: Access the CMET Bot and input the case number to find the current CritSit Owner information. 
  • If you are discussing PFE onsite with the Customer, immediately engage the CritSit Owner who should be the main POC for the Customer onsite requests. Support Engineers should not make any decisions regarding onsite requests without the CritSit Owner.
  • For any questions, contact the CritSit Owner using methods below.
    [Asset 4563594]

 Note: The resonse target does not reset when the severity or case ownership changes (or the CritSit reopens)

  • The case owner must keep the incident severity current and should lower the incident severity to Non-critical in Rave or B/C in Service Desk Case before closing the case. Follow 4456539 | Procedure: Setting Severity.
  • Continue to own and monitor supportive cases until closing.

5. Frequently asked questions

Q: Can the CritSit team team take my case when it is still business hours in my local region?

​A: No - The CritSit team can only take cases that are out of hours in your local region.

Q: Does the CritSit team have access to queues or engineers?

​A: No - The CritSit team does not have direct access to engineers or queues. We use the same duty manager teams and manager resources that are available to all within Microsoft.

Q: How do I contact the Duty Manager team?

A: Click on this link for DM contact info - https://portal.sposites.com/sites/CTSDM/Lists/Contacting%20DM/AllItems.aspx

Q: How do I explain Severity setting to customer / CSAM / engineer

​A: See 4343326 | Informational: CritSit Severity Definition for information on severity settings.

Q: Who is CritSit Team?

​A: The CritSit Management & Escalation Team (CMET) is a global team delivering on the Critical Situation Management (CritSit) process after hours to Microsoft’s Premier customers and also act as an escalation point during business hours. The CMET team delivers world class management of critical situations with swift relief for Premier customer's most severe business impacting issues through process adherence, customer communication and resource allocation.
