Summary


Who should follow this procedure?
  • Office 365 (O365) Frontline support engineers (SEs) who handle mail flow issues or issues specific to Exchange Online (EXO) or Exchange Online Protection (EOP).
What is this procedure about?
  • This procedure is about the blocks imposed by Office 365 on outbound mail senders or IP addresses and Support's response to each delisting scenario.
  • The Anti-Spam Team at Microsoft proactively monitors all outbound traffic from all Office 365 tenants. If the outbound traffic from these IP addresses or senders shows a marked percentage or volume as spam, then automatic protections will take immediate action to mitigate the effect on our network and reputation. Customers, as an administrator, can unblock user by themselves after the customer has addressed the user with the issue. Customers can also contact Support to request a sender be unblocked if they have exceeded the maximum number of unblocks.

    If the customer continues to hit the thresholds, or the customer uses their Microsoft Online Distinguished Routing Domain (MODRD, also known as the onmicrosoft.com email address) to send spam, the Anti-Spam Team will do any of the following:
    • Redirect the outbound mail services for that source through the High Risk Pool (HRP)
    • Disable the IP or outbound services by enacting one of several different IP guidance blocks
    • Set up a guidance rule to block all outgoing email messages from an offending source
    • Add the offending sender to a banned sender list
When should this procedure be followed?
  • This procedure should always be followed when customers receive any of this article’s described issues. Note: Users who are blocked from signing in are excluded from the scope of 2836052 Procedure: M365 TS: How to handle high priority cases for Office 365.

Assumptions

  • Frontline training. The SEs handling the cases in this procedure:
    • Understand the Anti-Spam Team’s role in monitoring and acting on outgoing spam activity.
    • Understand the different types of blocks and different IP lists and recognize how to distinguish between them. This is important because the procedures differ based on the cause.
    • Distinguish between customer issues and non-customer issues:
      • Office 365 customers have many escalation paths and should use the correct one for their issue; for example, they should not use delist email address for opening issues with anti-spam features or mail flow issues which are not specific to these specific blocks.
      • Non-customer issues can be tricky; malicious senders and bulk mail senders may try to use social engineering techniques to convince you that their IP or domain should be unblocked. Non-customers should be directed to the public delisting portal (https://sender.office.com) unless they provide evidence of a different type of block – which will need to be escalated to anti-spam analysts for review. The public delisting portal has methods in place to mitigate against abuse. Support should no longer be providing significant IP delist actions on behalf of anyone.
      • Note that for now, Hotmail & Outlook.com (consumer) issues cannot be handled through this process. Office 365 (commercial) is not an escalation path, for non-customers. Office 365 customers can open regular tickets which can be escalated to address issues with Hotmail/Outlook.com (consumer).
    • Understand that for Office 365 customers the focus is not on simply unblocking the IP or sender, but also help the customer to understand:
      • Different ways this could have happened (account or device compromise through weak policies, phishing attacks, malware, etc.) and suggested actions that must be followed before unblocking (reset password, scan for malware, etc.); understand that deep investigations of a critical nature should be redirected to the appropriate teams per their processes and with their limitations and exclusions.
      • Be prepared to address the fact that no security feature or product alone can prevent all breaches. Microsoft recommends defense-in-depth strategies and encourages customers to be prepared to deal with incidents when they occur.
      • Help customers to learn techniques and product features that can help them to break the cycle. Office 365 is a large product with many add-ons and offerings. Our job is not to sell or push a particular solution, but to educate and improve awareness of different features available to them and how they can work together to help the customer meet their particular requirements and address risk at a level which meets their needs.
    • Are fully knowledgeable about and trained on this article's procedure, including how to identify admin information in Rave.
  • Deferral criteria. Cases for the issues discussed in this article cannot be deferred unless all of the following conditions apply:
    • The SE has first made contact with the EOP or O365 customer’s admin as listed in Rave.
    • The issue could not be resolved during the contact.
    • The customer’s unavailable status corresponds with an acceptable deferral scenario based on 2729443 Procedure: M365: How and when to defer a case.
  • About the EOP Delisting Portal tool:
    • Discontinued. To look up an IP address or blocked sender, use Rave. In Rave, use the Antispam IP diagnostic, or Antispam Domain & Antispam Sender diagnostics, respectively, to check the status and history.

Procedure


Determine whether the issue is with an outbound IP address, an outbound sender, or the customer’s Office 365 or EOP account and then use the following table of scenarios for next steps.

Important: When speaking with customers about any of these issues, please explain the value of their organization using certificate-based inbound connectors from on-premises, and/or registering all of their domains in the EAC. As of February 2017, only certificate-based connectors will be allowed to send on behalf of unregistered domains. See https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/3169958.
 
All Frontlines: Issue raised by customer Frontline response
EOP or O365 user account is blocked from sending (NDR 5.1.8 when sending emails) Go to the EOP or O365 or user account is blocked section if the customer experiences any of the following symptoms:
  • Receives an NDR (non-delivery report) when sending from a particular account. Typically/currently the NDR code is 5.1.8.
  • Cannot send email from an MODRD (onmicrosoft.com) UPN.
Noncustomer outbound IP issues Refer to 2694746 Procedure: M365 TS: Noncustomer IP delist requests.
Support experiences a delay of greater than 2 minutes in EOP Delisting Portal IP or sender queries If the issue is for a banned sender or IP on the TBL, always ask the customer to try performing the action themselves using the self-help options.

For issues which require support action, follow standard consult procedure to engage your TA. Do not escalate the case or transfer it to a TA or another SE unless you are given explicit approval to do so.

For more information on bounce messages and what to do, you can check this public article. If self-help delisting tools are not working or functioning as expected, please get an error or attempt to reproduce the problem, and then escalate by engaging your TA as appropriate.

This section discusses the response to any of the following symptoms:

  • Cannot send email from an MODRD (onmicrosoft.com) UPN.
For incoming calls or Web cases (MFP, EOP and EXO SEs)

The Anti-Spam Team has blocked an EOP or O365 account. Customers can set up notifications in the Microsoft 365 admin center that alert them any time the Anti-Spam Team bans or blocks a sender (refer to Configure the outbound spam policy). SEs might receive a call or Web case from admins who have resolved the issue and want Support to remove the block because they are unable to do so themselves using the Compromise Recovery tool.

More information

Administrator alerts for outbound spam activity

The Anti-Spam team has implemented features which notify customers of outbound suspected spam. These features allow administrators to monitor suspicious mail being sent by their users so they can proactively address problems before an account is blocked. These features should be promoted to any customer opening a blocked sender case. For more information for EOP and EXO customers, see article Configure the outbound spam policy.

Troubleshoot the remainder of the case as per the document’s earlier steps.
