What is this article about?

This article provides the process how to assist customers who lost their Product Key and need a replacement.

​​​1. Determine the product and distribution channel

Customers may contact support after getting into situations where they lost their product key while trying to reinstall or reactivate a product. In that situation, work with the customer to determine the product and distribution channel and make every effort to help the customer locate the product key or determine whether the key is valid.

  1. Ask the customer if they have a disc (CD or DVD) for the software, if they do, ask what the disc for the product says. Look for these specific phrases:
    • "Not for OEM or retail distribution" – Volume License.
    • "For Distribution with a new PC only" – OEM.
    • "Do not make illegal copies" or "Not for resale" – Retail.
  2. Ask the customer if the software was included with their computer (indicates OEM), or if they purchased the software online (indicates digital distribution).
    • If the customer purchased Windows from Windows.com ask the customer to check their confirmation email for Product Key. When you purchase from Windows.com, it sends you an order summary email which includes Product Keys.
    • If the customer purchased the product from the Microsoft Store ask the customer to check for their confirmation email or visit Microsoft Store and sign in with their Microsoft account. On the account page, select digital content link to view product keys.
      • Customers who purchased Office 2013 or Microsoft 365 can reinstall the product by logging into http://www.office.com/myaccount using their Microsoft account.

Use the following table which shows where the product key is located for the distribution channel to see if the customer can find the product key.

The product key is on an orange sticker affixed to the installation CD/DVD cover or case.

Desktop computers

The Certificate of Authenticity (COA) sticker is attached to the chassis or the tower (the computer box).

The COA is attached to the bottom of the computer.

On small computers, the COA may be inside the battery compartment (you may have to remove the battery).

Open, Select, Software Assurance

Home Use Program (HUP)

Customer who obtained their software through the Home Use Program (HUP) for Software Assurance can retrieve their Product Key by returning to the HUP Website and accessing their Order History.

 Note: Customers can only retrieve their Product Key if their employer’s Software Assurance agreement is still valid and they are an active employee of the company.

Volume License

Microsoft Action Pack Subscription (MAPS)

MAPS products are provided to Microsoft Partners who participate in the MAPS program. For Product Key or media fulfillment issues, direct the customer to the MAPS Regional Service Center at (1) (877) 283-1925 FREE or maps-na@microsoft.com.

For more information, see KB 331221 Microsoft Action Pack Subscription - Policy and process.

Volume License

Volume Licensing Academic Program (VLAP) (formerly eAcademy program)

Schools and universities have the opportunity to sell various Microsoft products to students a greatly reduced price through the eAcademy program. The software is volume license and available by download.

Customers who purchased eAcademy software may mention that they purchased a downloaded version of the software directly from their school or university.

Direct customers who need Product Key assistance to their school/university. Microsoft does not provide replacement Product Keys for eAcademy software.

MSDN

 

Product keys for MSDN subscribers can be obtained by logging into the MSDN subscriber download site:
https://msdn.microsoft.com/subscriptions/securedownloads/cc137106.aspx

If the customer's MSDN Subscription has expired and they cannot log on to the site, they can call MSDN at (800) 759-5474 FREE for a product key.

3. Invalid product key errors/product key troubleshooting

1. If the customer's key is not accepted by the software, refer to the KB article for the product to determine whether there are known issues with the product keys.

2. Verify that the customer has disabled antivirus software during the installation and checked the CD/DVD media for scratches or dirt. Have the customer make sure that Caps Lock is off.

3. Determine the distribution channel of the software – see step 2 above.

4. Use KIT to determine whether the key is appropriate for the product being installed. See KB 3150228 Tool User Guide: Key Information Tool (KIT).

Check if:

  • You have captured the key correctly by using the phonetic alphabet.
  • The key is valid and being entered correctly.
  • The customer is not misreading any characters in the product key. The following characters may appear similar and are frequently misread:
  • B or 8
  • G or 6
  • O and zero are not included – these are never used in product keys.
  • The key matches the description on the disc. Ask the customer to read the information from the CD/DVD, including the part number. Use PKValidator on the key and compare the results to the information on the disk.

5.  If the customer is eligible for support or wants to use paid support options, create a case and provide the support to customer.

6. If the customer's product key is valid, and matches the product and distribution channel but is still not working:

  • If the customer is eligible for support or wants to use paid support options, create a case and provide the support to customer. 

7.  If the customer's product key does not register as valid, go to section 4 to purchase replacement product.

4. Purchase replacement product key

Note: In some case, agents can provide a Windows Product Key to a customer at no charge. See the Product key privacy section of KB 3045181 Procedure: Windows: Product Key Replacement for additional information.

The customer need to purchase replacement product key. Follow below for placing an order for replacement product key via MST.

  • KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST)

After selecting New Order, select Country, select Product ID and use DG7GMGF0FL7X to search for replacement product key SKU.
