1. Customer segmentation

All customers are either managed or unmanaged. During times of re-segmentation (the beginning and end of each fiscal half or typically June/July and December/January) it is common for the Sales teams to shift books of business. This can cause some confusion in ownership as customers are being moved from old to new segments or may be transitioning account teams. Exercise patience and diligence during these times and escalate any help needed to your TA.

If a customer is listed as unsegmented with no account management team, this can be considered unmanaged/SMB and is eligible for full support. If the customer is listed as unsegmented with a designated account team assigned, always reach out to the assigned contact in UCM before proceeding with support on red scenarios.

Managed

  • High-spending advertisers who have a dedicated Account Team to support their account.
  • They have an Account Team identified in UCM.

Unmanaged

  • Medium or modest-spending advertisers who do not have a dedicated account team.
  • These customers self-manage their accounts and contact our support teams if they run into any issues or need any help.

Internal Sales Support (such as, AM’s or AE’s)

  • Account Manager inquiries are generally considered out of scope and inquiries coded as Misroute.
  • As of October 2020, chat support for India Sales House employees (InMobi) are in scope with limited capacity.
  • Chat support for internal employees in India should follow special verification, only be supported for select scenarios, and have their own TCO process. See section 5 for details.

Nothing is changing with regard to our support of customers in India when those customers contact us directly. Continue to follow segmentation protocol for customer support scenarios.

 
 

Segment

Description & support model

Strategic, Enterprise, and Corporate

These are top-spending customers, and often large corporations or recognizable brands with large account teams. They will have a dedicated account team in UCM.

CSS support model:

Customers in this segment are considered managed and will have a dedicated account team with titles such as Account Manager (AM), Partner Account Manager (PAM), and Account Executive (AE). They are eligible for CSS support, except for red scenarios (see red scenarios matrix in section 2), whereby account team assistance should be requested. Regardless of assistance provided, account teams should always be notified when managed advertisers have been supported by CSS (see section 2.1).

Services model: These customers do not receive assistance from services.

 

Channel Partner (a.k.a. resellers)

Channel partners are a type of advertiser model that often mimics an agency or reseller set up. Typically, the primary partner has direct contact with the account team, while the reseller accounts (aka long tail advertisers) will rely on either the primary partner or CSS for support.

CSS support model: Only the “Partners” within in this segment will have a dedicated account team listed in UCM. The type of support CSS provides can only be identified at the account level by checking for the red CP CSS label in UCM (see directions below). Due to constant change, partners under the “Wordstream” customer shell should also be considered fully supported (whether or not they have the CP-CSS label). Accounts with this label in UCM indicate that the account is eligible for full support (all scenarios, no need to contact or inform AM). Customers identified as channel partners who do not have the label, follow typical managed account scenarios (with only red scenarios being out of scope), in which case the AM would be contacted for assistance and UCM updates provided following CSS assistance.

Services model: Currently no channel partners are eligible for an outbound services consultation. Channel partners are eligible to receive assistance from the services team if the client calls inbound or we receive a lead via the Scheduling Tool.

Here is how to see if an XID is fully CSS support eligible:

  1. Enter the advertiser’s XID in the search box and confirm if that XID falls under the Channel Partner segment.
  2. If the XID does fall under Channel Partner, then select the Search Result link in the breadcrumb.
  3. The search result will reveal whether that XID has the red CSS Support label, CP CSS.

Note: You may need to expand (+) the account view to see the label.

SMB- Platinum

Platinum customers usually have a proactive only account team. They are considered unmanaged with some special exceptions, listed below.

CSS support model: These accounts are eligible for full support for any scenario (see below exceptions for refunds and optimizations). Even if they have an account team member showing in UCM, they are technically considered unmanaged and should be fully supported. There is no need to inform account team members of CSS support or redirect requests to them for assistance. However, refund requests over $5K require additional review by platinum segment owners, which will be facilitated by the TA.

Services model: These customers are not services eligible. Campaign performance/optimization inquiries should be referred to:

  • North America: The Platinum Appointment Scheduler whenever possible. XR name may be shared for the purpose of scheduling an appointment.
  • Rest of World: Appointment Scheduler Links TBD. Assist the customer with optimization requests to the best of your ability. Help needed request may be sent to the platinum account team as necessary.

SMB– Silver, Bronze, and Gold

SMB Silver, Bronze, and Gold customers will occasionally have a temporary C&R advocate aligned in UCM or complementary onboarding/optimization consultation services. They may also have a vendor Account Manager, as designated by XR – Account Manager.

CSS support model: These accounts are eligible for full support for any scenario. Even if they have an account team member showing in UCM, they are technically considered unmanaged and should be fully supported for all issue types and should never be redirected. There is no need to inform account team members of CSS support or redirect requests to them for assistance.

 

SMB– Copper

SMB Copper customers will occasionally have a temporary C&R advocate aligned in UCM for complementary onboarding consultation service (Copper segmentation is exclusive to new customers).

CSS support model: These accounts are eligible for full support for any scenario. Even if they have an account team member showing in UCM, they are technically considered unmanaged and should be fully supported for all issue types and should never be redirected. There is no need to inform account team members of CSS support or redirect requests to them for assistance.

SMB-China

Customers in China often have a dedicated account team in UCM and they are strictly managed by Westwin Agency and are not supported by CSS. Westwin customers should not be directed to the AM in UCM, but rather directed to the appropriate contact at Westwin. For the company contact information see KB 2743346 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Support Global Contact Information.

Customers in China without an account team listed and are not part of the Westwin CID’s below, are likely self-serve and are full support eligible. If the customer is set up in EN, is not part of the Westwin Agency shells listed above, and does not have an account team in UCM, then CSS can continue to support the customer per standard unmanaged process.

Unsegmented

This is typically a temporary assignment in UCM. Unsegmented customers are eligible for full support (including red scenarios in section 2) if there is no account team listed. If they do have an account team listed in UCM, always defer to the AM for red scenarios. Note: There is no need to defer to XR - Account Manager, follow standard full E2E SMB support model.

2. Checking support eligibility

First check in UCM to determine the customer’s segment and whether they have an account team. Note that XR – Account Managers are vendor Account Managers and associated accounts are full E2E support eligible (no red scenarios).

  • Customers with business address = CN, TW, and HK with account teams showing in UCM are managed and not eligible for support. Managed customers in the TW and HK markets are only supported through the Yahoo relationships, and China customers who are managed are supported by account managers under Westwin. See KB 2743346 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Support Global Contact Information for detailed support and redirect guidance. If customers are considered unmanaged by parameters set in these markets, they are eligible for full support.

In general, if the customer is considered managed, support should be provided for all requests except the red scenarios below. Red scenarios should be redirected to the Account Manager (see segmentation grid for exceptions: for example, designated channel partners).

If the advertiser wishes to contact their AM, never share AM details and instead collect the customers preferred email address and contact number.

Inform the customer that you will reach out to their AM on their behalf and that follow up may delay their request.

Red scenarios types – Defer to (P)AM (unless otherwise noted as full support eligible in segmentation grid).

Note: Be sure to check UCM for open escalations for managed advertisers. Do not duplicate escalations that have already been kicked off by AMs and follow the red scenario process if an escalation exists.

RED SCENARIOS

Accounts and Billing

Campaigns, Delivery, and Tools
  • Account compromised
  • Billing adjustments (payment inquiries and pre-paid balance refunds are supported)
  • Linking/unlinking
  • Pilot enablement and support
  • Optimization and performance improvement inquiries
  • UET setup (ongoing support is green)
  • MSAN and Audience features

4. Segmentation program

There are ongoing efforts to ensure all advertisers are in the correct segment based on their current spend, and engagement.

Return to top

4.1 New advertisers

New advertisers will spend their first 90 days in the acquisition and onboarding phase. During this time, they are segmented as Scale-Copper. Once the advertiser has finished their 90-day acquisition and onboarding phase, they will be assessed for an account team and will be re-segmented based on their spend and engagement.

  • Unmanaged or Scale customers will be internally segmented as platinum, gold, silver, or bronze. Platinum and gold advertisers will be treated by Scale Top Advertiser (STA) after acquisition and onboarding. Silver and bronze will be treated by the MAST/CSS Optimization Consultation team.

Return to top

4.2 Existing advertisers

As a result of this work, some advertisers may move from managed to unmanaged, or from unmanaged to managed outside of the managed services nominations process. Twice annual segmentations will also occur. The business will re-evaluate the customer’s segment based on the customer’s spend and stability at that spend level. The segmentations are tentatively slated for January 31st and August 15th.

  • Advertisers moving from unmanaged to managed will be contacted directly by the new Account Manager.
  • AMs receiving requests for assistance from unmanaged advertisers can either redirect the customer to support channels or submit the customer information to MASTSearchTA@microsoft.com for proactive outreach.
  • Advertisers moving from managed to unmanaged will receive the “Transition to Scale Email” which provides reactive support pathways for advertisers. If one of these advertisers contacts us and has questions about their re-segmentation, the following messaging can be provided:

Messaging:

“The Microsoft Advertising Team is constantly checking to ensure we provide the best service and support to help our customers achieve their search advertising goals. After this recent review, your account has been selected for support and service from our team of search specialists. As a result of this change, you will no longer have a dedicated contact aligned to your account; however, I'm happy to assist you.

Our team is available over the phone during standard business hours, or over chat 24x7 (if you prefer English language), and through email (European markets only) to help you get the most out of Microsoft Advertising. How can we help you today?”

5. India Sales House Support (InMobi)

Beginning October 12th, 2020, we have agreed to support Sales House POC’s in India if they reach out to us via chat only, for questions that would help resolve their customer inquiries. We will not be customer facing or make changes to customer accounts, and we will only assist the India Sale House partners by providing quick answers and the correct internal POCs or information to resolve their customer inquiries.

Note: Nothing is changing regarding our support of customers in India when those customers contact us directly, continue to follow segmentation protocol for customer support scenarios.

5.1 Support process for internal chat

Process details: Chat only support had unique fortification, green scenarios, ticket coding, and TCO. Refer to the processes below to ensure accuracy.

Modality used: Chat only

InMobi/AM requestors are expected to:

  • Use their b@microsoft.com email only (it is also possible they will use their @inMobi alias, but this should be on rare occasions).
  • Must complete the prechat survey.
  • Selfidentify in chat that they are “inMobi India Sales House AM”.
  • Not complete survey after chat has ended/closed (no postchat survey completion).
  • Contact us for green scenarios only (note that these only include general product, process, and troubleshooting).
  • Kindly be redirected to the correct team for all other scenarios (for example, provide pushback using canned response and share the appropriate escalation POC).

Ticket coding:

  • Always use ‘Amber’ Color Emerging Issue Code.
  • Code remainder of ticket appropriately.
  • No need to escalate on behalf of requestor.

TCO process:

  • Expect outreach to conclude with answered question (i.e. first contact resolution, with minimal follow up or open tickets expected).
  • If additional customer/account information or details are needed from the AM, CSS does not wait for the AM before closing the ticket as resolved
  • CSS will send one followup email, then close our ticket.
  • Standard seven days reopen policy applies (AM’s can come back within this timeframe if follow up is needed on their same query).
  • Like our advertisers, there may be multiple questions per chat contact; however, we follow standard protocol of one ticket per issue, when possible (or per account).

Escalation protocol:

  • Feedback or assistance from InMobi should come via: themets@microsoft.com alias.
  • Feedback or assistance from CSS to InMobi should come to: themets@microsoft.com alias.

5.2 FAQ for India (InMobi) support

Q: What scenarios will we support? Is it the same red/green scenarios as we do for ‘managed advertisers’?

A: We have created new red/green scenarios for this support model (as outlined above). We will only support Green Scenarios or questions that come from internal stakeholders that do not require changes in the account. Most of our assistance will be answering general questions or recommending the Sales House POC reaches out to the appropriate team. The red/green scenarios anything in the account.

Q: It was mentioned that the request will be primarily informational only. Since the scenarios will include general product, process, and troubleshooting, does this also mean that any escalation will be handled by the ‘AM’ and we just need to give them the right direction on where to escalate and whatnot?

A: Yes, exactly. We will only be providing advice and guidance, without doing the work on behalf of the AM or advertiser. We cannot make changes in the UI, even if the AM requests, we do so. We will only be offering our recommendations (such as, investigative, suggesting opportunities, performing troubleshooting, or recommending another escalation point).

Q: Do we still need to perform ASD fortification/manual verification or do we just check for the AM's name/email in the account team in UCM? In case the alignment in UCM is not updated, what should we do to authenticate the AM?

A: The person contacting you is unlikely to be in UCM (only the Sales House leads are in UCM, not the AM/vendor team supporting the customers). For that reason, the Sales House desiring our support is required to:

  • Use their b-@microsoft.com alias when chatting in.
  • Declare that they are with InMobi, supporting customers via the India Sales House.

Once they identify as an InMobi rep that is supporting our customers, you can proceed with their general customer questions. If they do not provide this information proactively, we should assume it is not a supportable scenario.

Q: Is there any scenario where CSS would be required to contact the advertiser directly? If not, do we have any talking points we can use in case an AM does make this request?

A: At this time, we should not be contacting the advertiser directly or taking requests from the AM to contact them. If this occurs, I urge you to escalate via themets.

Messaging for this scenario:

“Our team is not scoped to provide proactive outreach to advertisers that have been identified as being supported by the InMobi Sales House. We can offer our assistance to your team with general guidance on delivery/performance issues and product or process questions.”

Q: Do we have any talking points in case an AM requests for CSS to make changes to the account?

A: Messaging for this scenario:

“Our team cannot make changes to an advertisers account at the request of an Account Representative. We can offer our assistance to your team with general guidance on delivery/performance issues and product or process questions, allowing you to support your customer or provide recommendations back to your customer to improve the performance of their account.”

Q: Do the AMs have access to the internal SDK knowledge base? Can share or refer them to SDK articles when answering their queries?

A: AM’s do not have access to the CSS KB. The Sales House model will have their own training repository and use of standard external resources (such as, help content). If questions cannot be easily answered, use our red/green scenarios matrix to redirect them to the appropriate team to assist appropriately. Known process gaps can be shared via themets and we will coach accordingly.

Q: Is Achieve more still required for these AM contacts?

A: Yes. We expect that the bulk of this work will be advising and providing resources or recommendations. It is assumed that doing this work will provide ample ability to achieve more.

Q: As stated below, they shouldn’t be completing post survey, would they be blocked in doing so and if not, would surveys coming from them be scrubbed (in case we receive)?

A: No, they will not be blocked from completing the post survey. However, this is something we will be watching and coaching to. It is also okay for you to remind them there is no need to do so.

“Thank you for contacting our Support Team today. We appreciate the opportunity to help with your customer scenario. The survey you will receive after our chat is not valid for internal users and we ask that you please do not complete it. If you do have feedback to share, please consider sending an email to our leads via themets@microsoft.com. Thank you”

Q: TCO process provided below is different from our current TCO which might confuse our advocates. Is there a reason behind it? Or can we just stick with our current TCO process?

A: TCO has been updated so that we close the ticket immediately after the interaction. For instances where the AM needs to obtain more information from the advertiser, it means we can reopen the ticket for up to seven business days. We are not expected to conduct follow-up to the AM when more information is needed to answer their question, that responsibility is with them. In rare instances where the CSS advocate needs more information to accurately answer the question, we may consider follow up, but this should not be a common scenario. We will not limit the number of questions the AM can ask, but we should limit by account (for example, questions pertaining to a new account should be coded as a new ticket).

6. Managed services nominations

As of January 2017, the managed services nominations process is on hold for all markets. We are working on creating an exception process and set of criteria, but for now the sales team isn’t accepting any nominations.

Messaging:

“The Microsoft Advertising Team is constantly checking accounts to ensure we provide the best service and support to help our customers achieve their search advertising goals. Your account has been selected for support and service from our team of search specialists and is not eligible for a dedicated Account Manager. Our team is available over the phone during standard business hours, or over chat 24x7 (if you prefer English language), and through email (European markets only) to help you get the most out of Microsoft Advertising. How can we help you today?”

Options for specialty services if not qualified for managed services:

  • For advertisers interested in optimizing their campaigns, follow the improved campaigns workflow.
  • Advertisers who do not qualify for managed services but would like an Account Manager for their Bing search advertising campaigns can be referred to Optimization Consultation for temporary assistance.

Messaging:

If the advertiser is persistent about having a dedicated AM but does not pre-qualify for managed services, tell the advertiser:

“Thank you for your interest in Managed Services with Bing search advertising. A review of your account shows that you are best aligned to our self-serve management structure where you have free support available to you 6 days a week by phone, email or live chat. I want to assure you that you are in good hands with Bing Support and encourage you to reach out to them next time you need assistance with your account, so that you can see firsthand the great level of service that you’ll be provided. In addition to a team of specialists we have in place to serve you, be sure to visit Microsoft Advertising Help for robust resources available to you to get the most from your Microsoft Advertising campaigns. We value you as an advertiser and look forward to continuing to serve your advertising needs.”
