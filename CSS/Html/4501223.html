2. Definitions

  • Broad Match Modifiers (BMM) – Broad match modifiers are used to fine-tune or restrict, how the broad match is being applied. Adding the plus (+) sign broad match modifier to a keyword means that the word with the BMM must be in the search query for the ads to be eligible to serve.
  • Keyword Expansion for no performance – This process provides a list of new suggested keywords for Campaigns or Ad Groups with no performance at all, from clicks and impressions.
  • Keyword Expansion Net New – Intelligence research based on category and vertical, provided by the requestor for Net New accounts.
    • Category – Specifying if it is for brand, non-brand, or Match Type specific.
    • Vertical – A description of the product and services being offered on the website provided by the requestor such as items and offerings.
  • Match Type expansion – This process provides a list of all keywords that are missing in other Match Types.
  • Search query Keyword Expansion by clicks or impressions – Keyword opportunities based on terms that have led to successful clicks. SQR can be pulled from the Reports tab in the Microsoft Advertising user interface (UI).
  • Search query Keyword Expansion by conversions – Keyword opportunities based on the terms that led to successful conversions. SQR can be pulled from the Reports tab in the Microsoft Advertising UI.
  • Seed Keywords – Seed keywords are base keywords you can input in Intelligence, to search for new keywords.
  • Standard Keyword Expansion – Intelligence research is based on the top 100 historically performing keyword or Net New keywords.

3. Best practices and policies

Keywords must be relevant. This means that the advertiser’s website must be a satisfactory destination for users, based on a primary user intent of the keyword(s) the advocate selected.

The keywords should relate directly to either:

  • The specific range of products, services, or content on the landing page.
  • The general content, purpose, and theme of the website.

Note: Extremely popular or trending queries may be subject to a higher standard of relevancy and ads must align with the primary user intent of the keywords and queries.

The following section provides best practices and reminders for BOS advocates when processing a Keyword Expansion request.

3.1 Tactic guidelines

  • Keyword expansions are done at the Ad Group level.
  • There is no minimum number of keywords to return a keyword expansion. For lower numbers (less than 30) it’s required to provide context as to why, because it’s helpful for the account team.
  • If there are a limited number of keywords produced by the requested keyword expansion type, then we can also offer another keyword expansion proactively. First, check for the account structure and advertiser’s line of business, then select which is the most appropriate keyword expansion for the account. Make sure to communicate this with the requestor.
  • For accounts with performance, default timeframe pull is 90 days for Keyword Expansion using Intelligence and SQR.
  • For Keyword Expansion SQR, avoid suggesting keywords under deleted campaigns or Ad Groups.
    • SQR by clicks and impressions – Clicks column must have a value of at least one, since these terms led to successful clicks.
    • SQR by conversion – Conversion column must have a value of at least one, since these terms led to successful conversions.
  • For Keyword Expansion Net New - Since the Microsoft Advertising account has no Campaigns or Ad Groups yet, the BOS advocate must ensure that the requestor provides the following:
    • Category
    • Working URL of the landing page or website.
    • Key sales drivers or terms, to be suggested such as services, and items.
    • Specifics about the preferred campaign structure. For example, brand or non-brand, and keyword Match Type specific. 
  • All keyword suggestions must be Net New.

3.2 Applicable policies for Keyword Expansion

  • All keywords must adhere to Microsoft Advertising policies.
  • Each keyword can contain up to 100 characters, including spaces.
  • Some common variations of keywords, such as adding punctuation ("," "!") to a keyword, count as duplicates in your list. Microsoft Advertising automatically removes all duplicates from the list, but these variations due to punctuation, are still considered valid or distinct keywords for your ad.
  • If using the Broad Match Modifier, ensure that the plus (+) sign immediately precedes the keyword it modifies, with no spaces.

For example:

  • Correct: Hawaii +Hotels
  • Incorrect: Hawaii + Hotels

Note: When reviewing the website, familiarize yourself with:

  • The products or services the advertiser offer.
  • How the site is structured by going over the homepage, About Us, FAQ, resources pages (if available) and the navigation links on the site.
  • This is very important! Relevancy is the biggest criteria we look for at the end of the day.

11. Additional tips

Choosing keywords play an important role in optimizing a search marketing campaign. Below are some reminders to help you choose quality keywords.

  • Identify the line of business and the products or services offered by the advertiser.
  • Choose words and phrases that describe the advertiser’s business – Ensure that the keywords you choose are directly related and highly relevant to the advertiser’s business or services they offer.
  • Use common variations – If the seed keywords have common variations (for example, travel advocate, travel agency) or spelling (for example, web site, website), you may include those variations to the keyword list.
  • Suggest words and phrases suitable to the Campaign or Ad Group structure – We can suggest keywords that best describes the campaign and/or Ad Group name.

  • Match Type Modifiers – Ad Group overrides Campaign settings. Generally, the more precise the Campaign or Ad Group Match Type, the higher the conversion rates and the lower the volume tends to be. Finding the right balance between conversions and impressions can help maximize the advertiser's ROI.
  • Brand versus Non- Brand – A branded keyword search query includes your brand name or some variation of it. For example, branded keywords for NIKE include search queries like:
  • NIKE rubber shoes
  • NIKE rubber shoes for men
  • rubber shoes for women, NIKE

Other terms (keywords) that don’t reference the brand name are considered non-brand keywords.

  • Rubber shoes SALE
  • Discounted rubber shoes
  • Basketball rubber shoes

    • Word Specific Ad Group Name – There are accounts that were specifically structured based on the Campaign or Ad Group name. With this, we need to be very careful in giving our proposal. Most likely, the hint here is to follow the Ad Group structure name.  [Asset 4501777]

12. FAQ

Q: If all campaigns in the account do not have any performance, how should we proceed with the request?

A: If no performance data was pulled up via Editor as a workaround, pull up a Keyword Performance report on Microsoft Advertising UI. For detailed instructions see Keyword Expansion – Account Has No Clicks and Impressions .​​​​​​​

Note: If the keywords in the Keyword Performance report do not have any performance, proceed on Keyword Expansion for Net New or No Performance Account process. Ask your support to agree with the best approach for this scenario.

Q: Why is it important to expand keywords?

A: Expanding keywords opens new opportunities and new potential customers. Identifying unique and well performing keywords assists all areas of your business, including product development and client communication. New keywords can improve the site’s traffic and find new leads and customers.

Q: Can I proactively offer another keyword expansion to the requestor?

A: Yes. First, check for the account structure and advertiser’s line of business, then select which is the most appropriate keyword expansion for the account. Make sure to communicate this with the requestor.
