Procedure: Return/Refund: Unauthorized purchase by a family member/known person

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

What is this article about?

This article describes the Microsoft Store return/refund guidelines for when a family member (that is identified as a minor), has gained access to a Microsoft account and used the payment instrument of the owner for purchases without explicit permission from the adult (or owner). These purchases may occur on the payment instrument owner’s account or another account using the customer’s payment instrument.

For gaming content refer to KB 4505053 Guideline: Refund eligibility for Digital Game Products first as it has specific information regarding this content.

1. Guideline scope

This article covers the return guidelines for Microsoft Store purchases made by a family member, or a known person identified as a minor.

Refer to the following articles for possible scenarios not covered in this article:

  • KB 928204 Procedure: Fraudulent or unauthorized Microsoft account charges
  • KB 930018 Procedure: How to handle account takeover on a Microsoft account
  • KB 2885545 Procedure: Handling a customer who is the payment instrument owner but not the account owner

2. Handling guidance

  • Validating if it was truly a family member or a minor who made the purchase is extremely difficult, so do not suggest this scenario to customers. Only use this scenario if the customer’s description of the issue matches.
  • This situation may occur in more than one transaction, so check with the customer and via MST that there are no additional existing transactions. If there are multiple items, use Bulk refund on MST. Refunds can’t be processed and split across multiple separate contacts (for example, a customer can’t call in one day for $125 USD of purchases and then call in the next day for an additional $125 USD).
  • Note: Check KB 4485105 Procedure: Xbox: Refund request solution if the customer is requesting a refund for Xbox content and speaks a language supported by the webform refund process.

3. Refund criteria

For this scenario to apply, all of the following must be true:

  • The Microsoft account has not received another refund for this same exception within the last 365 days.
  • Purchases must have been made by a minor.
  • Purchases were made within last the 45-days.

Note: If a purchase was made by a family member or a known person that isn’t a minor, refund exceptions (outside the refund guidance) will not be issued.

Follow guidance in KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund if the scenario in this article does not apply.

4. Refund procedure

  • For Xbox content: Check if the customer’s language is supported (See KB 4485105):
    • If the customer’s language is supported in the webform, direct them to complete the Refund Webform at http://xbox.com/refunds as indicated in section 2.

1. Determine if the customer has received a refund within 365 days. If not, proceed to step 2.

2. Identify the refund amount:

  • Refund amount ≦ $500 USD (less than or equal to $500 USD or local currency equivalent) per account, proceed to step 3.
  • Refund amount > $500 USD (more than $500 USD or local currency equivalent) per account,
    • For non-Xbox content: Engage Accounts and Billing (A&B) Empowered Resolution Advocates (ERA) team. If the customer is in Japan/Korea/China, engage Advanced Capability Advocate (ACA).
    • For Xbox content: If the customer's language is not supported in the webform, refer the case to site Offline Team (ERA) or Ranger, for review.

3. In MST, select Return Line Item to proceed.

4. Take notes in the refund comment if the purchaser is a minor or an adult and their relationship to the customer.

5. Add the keyword Refund (without quotes) to the beginning of the Subject line.

6. If the refund currency is not in USD, use Bing search (bing.com/) to validate the local currency equivalent value (for example, “500 USD to euro”).

7. Help the customer understand how to limit or control purchases in their scenario. This may require moving the child’s account into a proper family structure (Microsoft Family), using payment controls, or removing a credit card from an account.

5. Engagement guidance

Make sure the following criteria is met before engaging ERA team or Xbox Rangers for PayOps engagement (see KB 3072553 Procedure: A&B: Tier 1 Billing Scenarios).

If a refund request requires engagement, a partial refund should not be processed up to the limit allowed for the remainder to be refunded by the ERA team or Xbox Rangers. ​​​​​​

Note for high-value refunds:

  • If the refund request is over $500 USD in value: Engage ERA team or Xbox Rangers to determine eligibility for refund.
  • If the refund request is over $1000 USD in value: Validate approval with Supervisor, SME, or ERA team/Xbox Ranger, who should verify that the High Value Refund Matrix was used correctly. Notate approval in the case notes.
  • If a customer is requesting a refund exceeding $10,000 USD: You must receive explicit approval from an FTE member of A&B or Xbox CSS Team.
    • Engage the ERA team or site Ranger.
    • ERA team or Ranger will contact the A&B or Xbox CSS Team FTE and discuss the case.
  • When the refund amount is in another currency, first verify that the amount noted is equal or over $1000 USD before escalating to ACA.

When engaging ACA, advise customers on the following:

  1. Do not remove the payment instruments prior to engagement, as this may prevent any refunded amount from being applied once processed.
  2. We will review associated accounts (such as, Microsoft Family) in determining refund eligibility of requests under this exception.
  3. For the result of the request, refer the customer to account.microsoft.com to review the items and amounts refunded. Do not promise a customer that they will receive a refund or that the expected amount will be refunded. The customer’s request will be reviewed by a Microsoft internal team to determine eligibility.

6. Educate customer to prevent future issues

The customer can prevent the future unauthorized purchase by a family member by using the Microsoft account family feature. They can add the family member’s Microsoft account as child account to their own Microsoft account, and set the Ask a parent settings under Content restrictions in Family. Share the resources below with the customer for further details.

  • Require kids to ask a parent before buying stuff in Microsoft Store 
  • Add members to your family group
  • Create an Xbox One passkey
  • Change purchase sign-in settings for Microsoft Store on Windows 10 or Xbox One
