2. Cross-Border Shipping

2.1 Overview

In EMEA, Microsoft store online supports shipments to 30 countries. We ship to the EU, EEA, UK and Switzerland except Malta and Iceland.

  • Full list (30): FR, DE, IT, NL, BE, LU, IE, DK, EL, ES, PT, AT, SE, FI, EE, LV, LT, PL, CZ, SK, HU, SI, CY, RO, BG, HR, NO, LI, UK, CH.

Customers from any of the countries listed above have the option to purchase physical products through any of these 30 countries’ Microsoft Store portals and have the product shipped to their location.

Cross border purchase is regulated under Single Market Strategy. ​The directive requires online merchants doing business in the EU to allow customers to transact across borders without discriminating based on nationality, place of residence, or place of establishment (for businesses) within the EU. ​

  • The regulation applies to all traders offering their goods or services to consumers in the EU, regardless of whether they are established in the EU or in a non-EU country​.
  • Countries in scope = EEA (European Economic Area): 32 in total.
    • 28 EU countries (including United Kingdom).
    • 4 Non-EU countries (Switzerland, Iceland, Liechtenstein, and Norway).
    • The Single Market Directive also applies to the outermost territories including Guadeloupe, French Guiana, Saint Martin and the Canary Islands, and Channel Islands.

See KB 4563743 Informational: SMB: EU Single Market Directive4 for more information regarding Single Market Directive.

Actively promote available cross border purchase options to customers from ship to countries where any given physical product is yet not available.

Note: Cross border purchase is applicable to physical and digital products. Warranties (like Microsoft Complete) can never be sold via cross-border.

2.2 How do I do this in MST?

Under Shipping address field, MST will only display the list of countries where cross-border shipping/billing is supported. If your customer is in one of these locations, you will see the other available countries listed during the checkout process.

Countries where cross-border shipping/billing are not supported are filtered out and will not show up in the MST list.

2.3 When do customers need this?

Cross-border purchase options should be offered to customers from ship-to countries where any given physical product is yet not available.

Example 1: A customer in Belgium wants to purchase a physical product that is only available for sale in Microsoft.com/store for France. In this instance, you should promote the cross-border purchase by offering to place an order via the French Microsoft Store portal, with shipping to Belgium.

Example 2: A customer in Romania wants to purchase a Surface product. Romanian Microsoft Online Store is currently offering only digital products for sale, meaning Surface can’t be purchased through the Romanian Store site. In this case, actively promote the cross-border purchase option to the customer from physical storefronts in EU/EFTA zone that carry the product. Inform the customer that they can purchase the product on their own, or you can assist with the cross-border purchase.

Example 3: A Finnish customer residing in Sweden wants to purchase an M365 subscription using their Finnish credit card. Advise the customer that they can choose Finland as the bill to country in the checkout page and use their payment instrument from Finland to complete the purchase on the Swedish webfront.

2.4 Do customers need a billing address in the country from which the purchase is made?

No, customers will not need to have billing address in the country where they are looking to purchase product from (only applicable to countries listed in this article). The customer can use a shipping address and billing address from a different country than the one they are purchasing from.

2.5 How to purchase from another country Microsoft Store - customer experience

Experience in the checkout page:

Bill-to country dropdown is enabled on the checkout page and a customer can use a credit or debit card from any in-scope market on any in-scope site.

  • In scope: EU, EEA, Switzerland, and the UK.
  • Full list of countries (32): FR, DE, IT, NL, BE, LU, IE, DK, EL, ES, PT, AT, SE, FI, EE, LV, LT, PL, CZ, SK, HU, SI, MT, CY, RO, BG, HR, NO, LI, IS, UK, CH.

2.6 Technical support and product warranty for cross-border purchases

  • Confirm that the EU customer you are assisting is a genuine Microsoft Online Store customer and that the device was purchased within the two year time frame.
  • Once confirmed, offer warranty services.
  • Customers can register their device online and contact a product support specialist by describing their issue there, going through self-help options and finally landing on support team contact details (Chat/phone).

You can assist the customer by offering to provide warm transfer to Technical support teams.

Return to top

2.7 Cross-border shipping and tricks

  • Set customer expectations regarding delivery timeframe and inform them that an extra one to two business days should be added to standard delivery timeframe provided on the website.
  • In case of different currencies, check the price and mention possible price differences to the customer. This could happen if a product is out of stock on the customer’s local Microsoft Store, but is available on a different region.
  • Be sure to educate customers regarding different keyboard layouts when purchasing from a different country’s Microsoft Store.
    • Customers that are looking for the US keyboard layout in Europe should order from the Dutch Microsoft Store, as that’s the only country that has the US layout available.
