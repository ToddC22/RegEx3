Technical Guide: Surface: Start-up failures

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Surface

What is this article about? 

This article helps you handle issues where the Surface does not boot into Windows. For example, the device may not turn on at all, it may turn on but hang at the logo, or it may turn on and display an error or warning or simply turn back off.

Since there are many specific types of startup failures, identify the most specific scenario based on the customers description of what is happening. Follow the steps and case coding for that specific scenario.

If an exchange is needed, use the Problem Type coding recommendations listed for the scenario.

 

Table of contents

  1. No power, black screen, won’t turn on or wake from sleep

  2. Boots to the Surface or Windows logo screen then hangs or turns off

  3. Stuck or looped at the automatic repair screen

  4. Stuck at the choose an option screen

  5. Boots to UEFI - Couldn't find a bootable operating system

  6. Stuck at the UEFI/bios screen

  7. Stopped at the thermal warning

  8. Stopped at low battery warning

  9. Secure boot disabled (red screen)

  10. Customer forgot UEFI password

  11. Stuck at Recovery Screen - Boot Configuration Data error

  12. Boots to Microsoft logo screen with the SSD progress icon

 

1. No power, black screen, won’t turn on or wake from sleep

Confirming the issue

When pressing the power button the customer sees:

  • The Surface screen is blank or black, and the backlight may or may not be on.
  • There is no Microsoft logo, Surface logo, icons, or cursor displayed on the screen.

[Asset 4288202]

Confirmation

“Just to make sure I understand your issue: When you press the power button, the Surface screen remains black and it does not turn on. Is that correct?”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case:

  • Surface Device > Startup - Pre-Windows > Start-up Failure > Won't Power On (No Logo)

Resolution steps

Follow the steps below until the issue is resolved or until all the steps have been completed. While following the steps, use the Why column to inform the customer why you are performing the step and, if successful, to better understand what the cause may have been. As you follow the steps, document the result of each one in your notes. Then proceed to the Is the issue resolved? section at the end of the table for next steps.

2. Stuck at the Surface or Windows logo screen then hangs or turns off

Confirming the issue

When pressing the power button, the customer sees the following:

  • The Surface turns on, and never goes past the Microsoft logo screen.

  • There is no progress bar or message beneath the Microsoft or Surface logo.

  • There may or may not be dots spinning in a circle below the Microsoft logo.

  • After the Microsoft logo appears the device turns off.

    [Asset 4290737][Asset 4290744]

Confirmation

“Just to make sure I understand your issue: When you press the power button, the Surface turns on but it never moves past the Microsoft or Surface logo. Is that correct?”

How to code

After confirming the issue, code the case, based on the customers issue.

Spinning dots

  • Surface Device > Startup - Pre-Windows > Start-up Failure > Stuck at Surface Logo Screen - With Spinning Dots

No spinning dots

  • Surface Device > Startup - Pre-Windows > Start-up Failure > Stuck at Surface Logo Screen - No Spinning Dots

Boots to logo and turns off

  • Surface Device > Startup – Pre-Windows > Start-up Failure > Boots to Microsoft logo screen – Then turns off 

Resolution steps

[Asset 4486344]

Step

How

Why

1. Copy and paste the template to the right to begin your note taking for this scenario. Document your findings for every step performed.

Notes template:

  • Check known issues >>
  • Verify whether or not a System Update is installing >>
  • Remove all external devices >>
  • Force the device to shut down and restart >>
  • Startup Repair >>
  • Reset the PC >>
  • Recover from a drive >>

This ensures a consistent format for capturing and reviewing case information. This helps prevent skipping steps.

2. Check known issues

See KB 4072657 Informational: Surface: Technical guides and known issues landing page, select the customer’s Surface model and check for the issue in the Startup Failures section.

There may be a current known issue that the customer is experiencing, with different troubleshooting steps.

Check known issues regularly.

3. Verify whether or not a System Update is installing.

If an update is installing, let it complete.

A progress bar for system firmware updates may appear below the logo.

[Asset 4286962]

 

System firmware updates are installed when the system boots. There will be a colored progress bar to indicate if updates are being installed.

If an update is being installed, we don’t want to interrupt it. This may take up to 10 minutes. During this time, do not attempt to power off or restart the device.

4. Remove all external devices.

Leave the Surface plugged into the power supply included with the device and remove all external devices including USB drives, SD cards, or any keyboard or mouse dongles.

External devices can sometimes cause the device to boot incorrectly.

 

5. Force the device to shut down and restart.

(varies by new vs old generation)

All current devices: Press and hold the power button for 20 seconds.

Pro 1, Pro 2, Pro 3, Pro 4, Book 1, RT, Surface 2, and Surface 3: Press and hold the volume-up button and the power button at the same time for at least 15 seconds and then release both. The Surface may boot to UEFI. This is normal. Have the customer exit UEFI.

 

Video available at Force a shut down and restart your Surface.

The device may be in a hung state. Force he Surface to completely turn off all power and restart.

Performing a Forced Shutdown is like removing the battery from a laptop or unplugging a desktop PC. It resets the hardware and clears all the cache and memory for your hardware.

6. If the customer sees no spinning dots, proceed to the USB Recovery step.

If the customer sees spinning dots. Force the device to open advanced repair options.

Force the device to shut down again and restart, after two consecutive failed attempts to start Windows, the Recovery menu will appear. Select See advanced repair options to open a Choose an option menu.

Advanced repair options may be available to perform, including Go back to the previous version, Startup Repair, System Restore, and Reset this PC.

7. Repair and Reset.

Good: Startup Repair

From the Recovery menu, select See advanced repair options.

From the Choose an option menu, select Troubleshoot > Advanced options > Startup Repair.

If prompted, enter the BitLocker recovery key.

Better: Reset the PC

From the Recovery menu, select See advanced repair options.

From the Choose an option menu, select Troubleshoot > Reset the PC > Keep my files.

Best: USB recovery

The customer will need to download and set up a recovery image on a USB drive. Send the USB recovery link to the customer and help them get started. Information on what size and type of drive is provided at the destination page.

-OR-

Set up an order to have a USB recovery drive sent to the customer.

When using devices with only Type C ports, confirm with the customer that they have a USB C male to USB A female adapter, or a Surface Dock to use the USB recovery drive with.

Recommended: Recovery options in Windows 10.

For devices running Windows 20H1 (Version 2004) or newer.

In settings, advance to Update & Security > Recovery from the Recovery menu. Under Reset this PC, select Get Started in the pop-up window. Confirm with the customer and then proceed to Remove Everything or Keep my Files, then select Cloud Download, and continue with the recovery steps. Make sure the device is plugged into power and connected to a stable network.

Reset the PC (better):

  • This will remove apps and settings and reinstall Windows.
  • This won’t affect personal files.

USB recovery (best):

  • This option reimages the entire device, including the partition on the hard drive.
  • It is the most complete form of reset.
  • Customers will need to back up their personal data.
  • Customers will need to reinstall desktop apps. They will also need to run updates after completion.

If the issue occurs after a reset, the root cause is very likely hardware related.

Is the issue resolved?

Yes, close the case: Once resolved, have the customer check for updates.

 

For SDT enabled sites, let the customer know about the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit.

For all other sites, check Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update for updates.

KB 4014334 Procedure: Surface: Using the Microsoft Surface Diagnostic Toolkit

Making sure Surface and Windows are up to date may help avoid this problem in the future.

No, replace the device: Set up an exchange for the customer based on the status of their warranty.

Use KB 2791320 Informational: Surface: Exchange and fulfillment landing page

There is likely a hardware problem with the Surface device.

“Couldn’t find a bootable operating system. Check the Boot configuration to try to fix this.”

[Asset 4588171]
