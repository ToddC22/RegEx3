Procedure: Recovery

[Asset 4462485]

Description

Recovery is an activity that can be initiated at any stage of the case by either the customer/partner or anyone in Microsoft. Ask questions throughout the life of the case to verify the customer’s/partner's level of satisfaction. Recovery calls after the solution is delivered are less valuable than identifying CSat risk early in the case.

Applies to

Region:       All (Global)
Role:           Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor | Team Manager
Service:       Premier | Partner | Broad Commercial

1. Steps

** Please also see the deviation section**

  1. Ask questions throughout the life of the case to verify the customer's/partner's level of satisfaction.
    • Recovery calls after the solution is delivered are less valuable than identifying CSat risk early in the case.
  2. Initiate recovery according to the triggers listed in the related documents or information section at the bottom of this article.
  3. Empathize and listen to the customer/partner to hear their concern:
    • If at any time, the customer/partner states that they have retained a lawyer, cease negotiation and refer them to MS Legal through the corporate operators (425-882-8080).
  4. Take responsibility and apologize to the them for their experience and inconvenience as appropriate:
    • Avoid setting expectations during meetings or written communication.
    • Do not:
      • Adopt actions that may be considered by the customer as a validation of the claim.
      • Make commitments or promises (time, money, and the like) to the customer (or the partner, if applicable) until you have consulted with the Team Manager and Incident Manager (IM) (for Premier or Unified Support), SAM (for managed Dynamics partners) and RM (CRT and/or CELA team, Dynamics critical escalations cases)
  5. Types of recovery:
    • Technical Recovery
      • Engage a Technical Advisor or senior engineer to assist with discussing technical case blockers with the customer/partner and reassure them that the correct technical skills are in position to progress their case.
      • Keep the Team Manager and IM (for Premier or Unified Support), SAM (for managed Dynamics partners) and RM (CRT and/or CELA team, Dynamics critical escalations cases) informed
    • Political, Support Experience or Product limitation Recovery
      • Engage Team Manager and IM (for Premier or Unified Support), SAM (for managed Dynamics partners) and RM (CRT and/Or CELA team, Dynamics critical escalations cases) as appropriate to assist discussing any concerns the customer/partner has.
    • Competitive
      • For Premier engage the IM.
      • For Broad Commercial use CompHot.

2. Deviations or workarounds

Microsoft Dynamics
  • If at any time, the customer/partner states that they have retained a lawyer, cease negotiation and contact the Dynamics CRT team.
  • As Dynamics solutions are business critical recovery may often face legal/compensation request threats. See the information at "What you say matters" and specifically the Dos and Don'ts pages to undersand what statements to avoid as they may be used in a legal action.

3. Supporting information or articles

Initiate recovery if the customer/partner:
  • Makes a verbal / written complaint to Microsoft or expresses dissatisfaction through a feedback mechanism (On telephone, follow-up email, direct email, web).
  • Wants another Support Engineer to work on their issue or their issue escalated.
  • Asks to speak to a Manager.
  • High level management CEO(CTO/CIO) contacts support directly and requests clarification about the troubleshooting process, expresses dissatisfaction, requests financial compensation or threatens, etc.
  • The call screener has recorded, in the case notes, that recovery needs to be initiated.
Consider initiating recovery in these situations:
  • Issue not resolved.
  • High Age/TMPI.
  • Customer/partner resolved the issue on their own.
  • Customer/partner requested escalation.
  • Complex issues requiring multiple technologies or case owners.
  • Internal Process Breakdowns.
  • Missed call-back or action commitments.
  • Customer/partner expresses disappointment of any form.
  • Service outage.
For Premier customers engage a IM and for Professional Support - Managed Partners engage a SAM to assist with recovery in these situations:
  • Any political reason.    
  • 'Abusive' customer/partner.
  • Customer/partner not happy with the time taken to resolve.
  • Customer/partner threatening compensation/legal action/trading standards.
  • Customer/partner requesting a refund/exchange of product/telephone bill refund.
  • Replacement CD Keys or Bypass Keys.
  • Situation requires the customer/partner receive a gesture of goodwill (for example goodies).
  • Complaint about queue times or time it took to reach technical support.
  • Case chargeability dispute.
  • Complaint about level of services received at the Contact Center.
  • Issue with Microsoft services or programs which may not be directly linked to technical support such as TechNet, MSDN, Events etc.
  • Customer/partner unhappy because of product limitation / by design.
  • Customer/partner has complained about an unrelated non Microsoft issue.
  • Issue not resolved.
