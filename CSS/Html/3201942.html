1. Applies to 

The following services: All services
The following advocate roles: Tier 1
The following markets: All markets (For detailed information about product-specific availability, see 2748142: Markets where Microsoft Online Services are available .)
 

Scenario
 

Customers contact support because they ended their relationship with their CSP (Cloud Solution Provider). They need to have that CSP relationship removed. This will require all CSP subscriptions under customer's tenant to be in "Deleted/Suspended" status. Removal of CSP relationship on the tenant can be done even if there’s no subscription on that tenant.

2. Procedure

To remove the CSP relationship, complete the verifications below and gather customers’ approval prior to escalating to Tier 3.

a. Identify the CSP relationship in CMAT

Look up the customer’s tenant in CMAT. The relationship is located in the Account Tab under CSP RELATIONSHIPS.



 

b. Verify that the CSP Subscription(s) is in "Deleted/Suspended" Status (Only CSP can cancel the CSP subscription). See steps below:

  • From the Subscriptions Tab, Click on the Drop Down Option to select Reseller. 

  • Select the appropriate CSP from the drop down menu. 

  • Confirm that the Subscription is showing in "Deleted/Suspended" Status. 

If NO:
Inform the customer that he/she needs to contact their CSP to have them delete the subscription.

Note: If the customer can’t contact his or her CSP, ask him/her for the Partner’s contact information and contact them on behalf of the customer. If they are still unresponsive, then escalate to Tier 3 to get in contact with that Microsoft representative Partners. Tier 3 will engage the local Partner team for further support and guidance. The Partner team, as a best effort, can contact the partner and try to solve this. However, they will still need the customer’s partner to delete the subscriptions and remove the relationship. The Partner team cannot force the partner in any means.
Once that has been completed, T1 advocate will be able to assist with the removal. 
If YES:
Process DAP (Delegated Administration Partner) removal on Microsoft 365 admin center
  • Log in to Admin Center, go to Settings and choose Partner Relationships page, select the partner of interest.
    [Asset 4580954]
  • In the details pane, select Remove delegated admin. In the confirmation pane, select Remove.

    Note: If the customer does not have an option to remove the delegated admin, either the icon is not showing or grayed out, follow the troubleshooting steps in article 2778091, then submit an Assistance Request for Portal Error.

Send the following authorization to the customer for them to complete and then please follow the process described in article 2778091 (How to escalate a case to Tier 3, Engineering) to escalate to T3 and remove the CSP relationship.

Customers Approval Template:

Tenant name:__________
(For example: {your domain}.onmicrosoft.com)

CSP relationship to be removed:

 

CSP Subscription(s) Type/Name:__________
(For example Office 365/Plan E3)

Seat count:__________
(Number of users within the tenant)

Do you authorize Microsoft to manually delete the CSP relation, and permanently remove the CSP indicated earlier?

Please indicate your authorization to continue by typing your name and your OrgID in the space here.

Name:_______________________________
OrgID:_______________________________
(For example: {user}@{domain}.onmicrosoft.com)
