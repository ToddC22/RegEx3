ASMS – Process for Quota Increases (Non-Capacity Management)

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.  

Overview  

This TSG provides guidance on how to increase quotas that are not supported by the Capacity Management Team (CM 24x7).

Common Scenario Available (Wiki)

You can also leverage the wiki based workflow for this support topic on the ASMS CSS Support Wiki > Select Quota. 

Note: If determined the quota you are looking for is supported by the CM 24/7 team, refer to TSG 2834676 for specific details. 

Policy  

 Keep the following guidelines in mind when providing support to your customers: 

  • For all quota cases, review the verbatim, communications, and task logs to see if the quota request details have already been provided by the customer.   

  • Refrain from providing sensitive information such as Azure max quota limits or specific criteria for credit checks. Providing such information increases vulnerability. 

  • Free Accounts – Free Accounts cannot have their quotas increased. (Once a user has removed their monetary cap, it is no longer a Free Account, and their quotas can be increased through the normal process.) 

  • Suspected Fraud – For cases that are flagged with “Suspected Fraud”, check the account’s status in CMAT. 

    • If the account status is Disabled or Warned, engage the Account Research Team (ART) using the ART TSG. Follow ART’s directions as to how to continue (or discontinue) communications with your customer 

    • If the account status is Active, proceed as normal.  

  • If a region needs to be enabled for one of the below quota types, follow the region enablement guidance in TSG 2834676.

 Media Services

Media Services Team

To increase quota for Media Services, submit a IcM.

Media Reserved Units (MRUs) are used for processing files.

  • Ask the customer to set their account to the desired MRU type (S1, S2 or S3), and have at least 1 MRU provisioned.
    • Otherwise the request cannot be honored.
    • Also, inform the customer that they must not change the MRU type after the quota ask has been honored, as it will reset the quota defaults.
  • What is the new desired limit for MRUs is (ex: default limit for S3 units is 10 in the Portal, and the customer may need to go to 100)?
  • What is the expected duration of the increase? (Do you need additional RUs for a few days/weeks/months)

Streaming endpoints are used to stream videos from the service.

  • What is the duration of the increase (Do you need additional endpoints temporarily for X weeks or permanently)?

Channels (or Live Events), are used to stream live events from the service.

  • Are you using a “pass through” Channel/LiveEvent, or are they using live encoding in Azure Media Services?
  • What is the duration of the increase (Do you need additional Channels/Live Events temporarily for X weeks or permanently)?
  • Do you plan to run multiple Channels/Live Events concurrently – if yes, provide the expected max. number of concurrent Channels/Live Events?

 Other entities related to their Media Service account (such as Assets).

  • Which entity needs to be increased?

Leverage the public facing pages for the most up-to-date information on this service.   

  • Media Services Overview
  • Media Service Limits

Storage Accounts (ARM)

 Azure Storage Team

To increase quota for Storage Accounts submit a IcM. Details required for this increase can be found in the IcM template.

  • Default limit of accounts per region in a subscription: 250
  • Max limit of accounts per region in a subscription: 350*
  • The system maximum account limit is 500 but we will initially raise the limit to 350.

To increase storage accounts beyond the max limit, submit a IcM.

  • If the customer is reaching the current published limits (500TB, 30Gbps, 20k IOPS) then the account limits can (if feasible) be increased to up to (5 peta bytes, 50 Gbps, 50K IOPS).

Leverage the public facing pages for the most up-to-date information about this service:

  • Storage Accounts Overview
  • Storage Account Limits

    Note: Storage account limits are driven by an engineering need in the Azure platform to limit the total number of storage accounts that exist globally. We encourage customers to be judicious in the number of storage accounts they create, and to consolidate usage to the greatest extent possible. In certain circumstances, exceptions can be granted to this quota limit. These exceptions are typically small and intended to mitigate any short-term pain related to unexpectedly running into these limits.

If customers are expecting better performance with these increased limits, then please educate them that this is not a performance feature. They should work with technical support to identify a root cause of slowness and a fix.

ERROR: "Exceeded quota of 800".

The default and maximum number of Subscription-level deployments per location is 800. If a customer reachs the limit of 800 deployments, they will see the following error when they try to add more: 

{'code':'DeploymentFailed','message':'At least one resource deployment operation failed. Please list deployment operations for details. Please see https://aka.ms/arm-debug for usage details.','details':[{'code':'Conflict','message':'{\\r\\n \\'error\\':; {\\r\\n \\'code\\':; \\'DeploymentQuotaExceeded\\',\\r\\n \\'message\\':; \\'Creating the deployment 'applicationInsightselect' would exceed the quota of '800'}

The customer will need to delete deployments from the history that are no longer needed before adding any new deployments. If the customer is unwilling to delete deployments, engage the ARM team via IcM to see if the limit can be removed. 

Note: Deleting an entry from the deployment history doesn't affect the deployed resources. From more information, see or direct the customer to the following links:

  • Quota Exceeded 
  • Resource Group Limit
