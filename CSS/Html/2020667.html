Thank you.”

1. Bing Places for business ﻿

Advertisers asking about Local Listing results on Bing Search should be transferred to the Bing Places for Business team, who support advertisers looking to claim/edit local listings. (Note that requests about Bing Maps and other Bing search functionality outside of Local Listing results would not be considered in scope for Bing Places transfer.)

Use the following messaging and ensure all three attributes are obtained:

“Our Microsoft Advertising team focuses on helping sponsored listing advertisers, but if I can collect a few quick pieces of information I’d be happy to arrange for a member of the Bing Places support team to reach out to you within 24 hours to follow up. I’d just need to confirm:

  • Your preferred contact phone/email for the follow up (please let us know which you’d prefer us to use).
  • Your Bing Places account email. (If you’ve already registered)
  • The Bing URL of the listing you’re enquiring about. (Please click the ‘Is This Your Business’ link located underneath the Local Listing on Bing, and copy/paste the URL from the page that loads.)”

Obtaining the URL:

"I’ll share this with one of my colleagues along with a summary of the issue you’re facing, and they’ll be in touch as soon as possible to follow up.

If you need more urgent assistance, feel free to reach out to the Bing Places support team via https://www.bingplaces.com/Dashboard/Support, quoting the ticket number I’m about to provide, and this will bring up all the details of our interaction.”

Once the customer has shared this information, search for the provided email address in ASD, include their details in your Case Notes and follow the Cross Site Communications process, outlined in KB 3171493 Process: Microsoft Advertising & Bing Places: Reactive Support Ticket Handling Policy, to transfer this ticket to the appropriate delivery partner using this logic:

  • EU-EN and all non-EN requests: Majorel Muenster
  • ROW EN: Majorel Manila

Note: The team can only support queries relating to Bing Places business listings. Make sure the customer is reporting an issue with their own business listing before transferring to Bing Places. Queries related to Bing Maps functionality, or other local listing providers, are not supported and should not be transferred. See section 2 for more guidance on Maps inquiries. If someone is calling about a Bing Places business listing that is not their own business, they can simply click the Suggest an edit link of the listing.

3. Bing Webmaster

Refer the customer to the Bing Webmaster Help Center. Selecting the Support icon will connect them with the team who provides Webmaster support.

The Bing Webmaster support team assists customers who own or manage websites (for example, webmasters) that appear on Bing organic search results. A variety of Webmaster Help content can be found on the Bing Webmaster Tools Help Center to assist webmasters in improving performance, traffic, and reaching more users on Bing (the industry term is SEO – Search Engine Optimization which pertains to organic search results).

Their average SLA is seven days.

3.1 Bing Webmaster support team scope

The following issues are in scope for the Webmaster support team:

  • Website configuration - submit and manage sitemaps from Webmaster tools.
  • Website indexing - submit URLs to Bing.
  • Geo-targeting websites for specific audiences.
  • Crawler or Bingbot inquiries.
  • Bing Webmaster reports, data, diagnostics and tools.
  • Bing Webmaster content guidelines.
  • Removal of organic search listings, but only for two specific scenarios: broken links or outdated cache pages via the Content Removal Tool (see the Bing Organic Search Results section below for all other types of content removal complaints).

If the customer already submitted a broken link or outdated cache page request via the Content Removal Tool, ask how long ago the submission occurred. If it’s been over a week, gather the customer’s contact info and send an email on the customer’s behalf to bwtsupport@microsoft.com (do not share this alias with the customer) via a child ticket in ASD. Inform the customer that someone from the Webmaster Tools team will reach out.

4. Bing Organic search results

Organic search results are the non-sponsored listings that appear on the Bing search engine results page (SERP) when a user performs a search (all results except the ads).

If a customer complains about the search results on Bing, first collect as much information as possible to determine whether they’re talking about an ad, a business listing or an organic listing. Ask them what keyword they were using for their search and for a screenshot. Try to locate the listing yourself by performing the same search and looking through the results.

4.2 Organic search website complaints

If a customer has a complaint about the content of a website which appears in Bing search results, use the following messaging:

“Please understand that Bing does not control the content that websites publish, or which appear in search results. Removing a website from search results does not remove the content from the web; it can still be discovered by going to the site. To remove content from a website, your best option is to contact the site's webmaster and request that the content be deleted or removed.”

4.3 Organic search FAQ

Q: Why are unrelated images/results showing on my Organic Search listings?

A: The Bing search algorithm uses several methods in order to determine the relation between organic search results and the words or phrases entered by Bing users during a search. The intent is to provide the best possible results to a customer to answer their question and help them make an informed decision. Although these results may not be owned by you, the nature of our Organic Search logic ensures that customers are presented with relevant options based on the nature of their search query.

Q: How do I remove unwanted images/results showing on my Organic Search listings?

A: Organic Search results change and evolve over time, based on the relevancy of the terms being used and the behavior of Bing's users. We are unable to influence Organic Search results/images as these operate on the logic algorithms of Bing itself. If you believe a set of images/results is not relevant to the search query, you can submit feedback to our Bing Search Engineering team for review. (Follow the feedback submission process outlined above.)

6. Internal Microsoft employees or vendors

Inquiries from internal Microsoft employees or vendors (email addresses @microsoft.com) are out of scope for our team because internal support channels are available for them to use.

To validate a customer is an internal Microsoft employee, ask them for their email address. If it ends with @microsoft.com, this is sufficient authentication to share the below messaging. Raise these cases to your TA for internal follow-up to prevent recurrence.

Messaging:

"Unfortunately, we don’t provide support for Microsoft employees or vendors; however, the good news is you have direct access to the internal teams who can resolve your request more efficiently. For technical or billing issues, please contact adsupport@microsoft.com. For editorial issues, please contact (provide the market-appropriate Editorial team alias). For billing questions, please reach out to your regional MSAdOps team alias.”

Close your ticket using the following issue coding: Misroute > Advertising >Internal user > Internal user > Redirected to internal support team.

8. Publisher Advertising (PubCenter)

Microsoft Advertising PubCenter is a powerful tool to empower developers to place sponsored ads within their Windows applications. When users click on these ads, the publisher earns money from the advertiser via Microsoft.

PubCenter is the tool publishers should use when they want to enable other people's sponsored advertising to show in their applications. This tool does not require publishers to create advertising themselves to use the resources.

If an existing PubCenter customer needs support, direct them to the Microsoft PubCenter homepage or pubhelp@microsoft.com.

If a customer wants to become a new publisher, reach out to your TA for guidance.
