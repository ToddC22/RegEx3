2. Guidance

  • Specific offers for nonprofit organizations are available only to customers that are verified as eligible for Microsoft nonprofit offers, based on the eligibility guidance defined on the Eligibility page.
  • Billing and technical support issues are the same as for any standard Office 365 and Dynamics 365 subscription. Advocates should follow our normal content workflow and standard operating procedures.
  • Dynamics 365 nonprofit customers are not required to meet a minimum threshold to select invoice when purchasing. For customers using other nonprofit subscriptions, refer to article 3091140: Helping Invoice Pay customers with issues and questions for details on invoice threshold.
  • If the customer does not have an existing paid subscription or a nonprofit trial, direct the customer to Get Started (https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/#/register) to register for Microsoft nonprofit offers. Customers must be registered with Microsoft and validated as an eligible organization in order to qualify for nonprofit offers. Registration includes the creation of a Microsoft account with Office 365, which is utilized on the backend to show the nonprofit catalog in Microsoft Admin Center. Once eligibility validation is complete, the customer will receive an email notification from Microsoft with the validation result. While waiting for validation to complete, the customer can track his/her status in the Nonprofit Portal.
  • If the customer already has a nonprofit trial or has a paid subscription and now wants to move to a nonprofit plan, follow the steps in this article under the escalations section.

3. Procedure

Overview of Nonprofit registration:

  1. Customer will register with Microsoft and provide proof of their organization's eligibility (tax identification number or registered documentation from government entities, like a charity commission) as described on the Eligibility page.
  2. During registration customers create a Microsoft account. This account username and password is used to log into the Microsoft Nonprofit Portal. (Note: The person completing registration will be the Global Administrator of the account by default.)
  3. Microsoft’s eligibility validation partner will verify the organization’s eligibility.  The eligibility validation partner, TechSoup or their local partner, may contact the customer for additional information. (Note: We strive to make eligibility determinations as quickly as possible, but this timeframe can vary depending on non-profit organizations’ responsiveness to requests for additional documentation and country requirements. Therefore, the process might take up to 20 business days.)
  4. While waiting for eligibility validation, customers can view their application status in the Microsoft Nonprofit Portal. No credit card information is required for the eligibility validation process.
  5. Once validation is complete, the customer will receive an email with their eligibility results for our nonprofit offers. If eligible, the email will include a link to the Microsoft Nonprofit Portal to access our nonprofit offers.

Note: Being registered with TechSoup and/or having a TechSoup token does not equate to eligibility for Microsoft nonprofit offers.  Customers must be registered with Microsoft directly.  Advocates should never direct customers to go register with TechSoup.

Nonprofit Trial

When customers sign up to get validated for nonprofit offers, they will get access to the nonprofit trials: Office 365 Nonprofit E3 trial or Office 365 Nonprofit Business Premium trial.  Nonprofit trials last 45 days to allow time for validation.  Advocates should never direct customers to sign up for a trial on their own outside of the nonprofit registration process.  Customers should always be directed to Get Started first to register, which starts the validation process and includes gaining access to the nonprofit trials.

Note: After completing sign up the customer will receive an email from Office stating a trial for “Nonprofit Portal (Nonprofits Pricing) Trial and the trial period is 10 years. The trial referenced in this auto-notification is for access to the Nonprofit Portal.  It is to enable a link to the Nonprofit Portal so the customer can easily navigate between the Microsoft Admin Center and the Nonprofit Portal.  This is why it has a long trial period.  It is NOT a product usage trial.  To actually begin a product usage trial, the customer must go to Microsoft Admin Center and add the trial subscription.

Tenant Tagging

To access nonprofit discounts and offers, customers with existing Office 365 non-nonprofit tenant need to go to Nonprofit Portal and Register. Once the validation process is complete, customers can request for Microsoft/Philanthropies to apply a charity tag to their existing tenant. The charity tag can be found in CMAT by clicking on the Tags (Qualification) section on the tenant’s main account page after entering the domain name or the tenant ID. The tag will show as charity “Approved” for eligible organizations or “Denied” for ineligible organizations.

Note: Tagging is done on the backend through Microsoft automated systems. In some cases, the system may fail to tag customers once they have been approved. If there is confirmation from Microsoft stating that the organization has been approved but the tag is not present in CMAT, T2/T3 should verify nonprofit's eligibility status in Nonprofit Support Lookup tool. All charity tag update requests need to be submitted through an email to Philanthropies Business Operations team alias (NPOperations@microsoft.com) as customer requested tag changes can potentially trigger other tag/discount removal. Request response SLA is within 2 business days. Make sure that customer is aware of the change impact.

Eligibility Validation

To properly set customers’ expectations, once the nonprofit is validated, the customer will receive an automated email of approval from Microsoft (sent from ngooffer@microsoft.com) with a link to the Nonprofit Portal to see available offers. Nonprofit offers will also be available in the Microsoft 365 admin center. In many cases, the response time is instantaneous once a nonprofit submits the registration form. In cases where a nonprofit organization’s status needs to be verified, eligibility validation can take up to 20 business days. That said, typical turnaround times in countries with established charity registrations are 2 to 5 business days.  The validation process can be delayed if the eligibility validation partner needs more information to complete validation, reaches out to the customer, and the customer is not timely in responding. 

Customers receive two automated email communications from Microsoft (sent from ngooffer@microsoft.com) during the validation process:

  1. Submission received
  2. Validation complete:
    • Denied if deemed ineligible
    • Approved if deemed eligible

The customer has reached the cap of Office 365 Nonprofit E1 seats

There is currently a soft cap of 2000 seats on O365 E1 nonprofit for new customers. This cap will be forced to all users on July 1, 2018. If customers want more than 2000 seats, this is the new process:

  1. First, the customer should delete inactive users and reassign the available E1 licenses to meet their license needs, within the 2,000 seat cap.
  2. If the customer has more than 2000 employees and staff members that they want to assign to the Office 365 Nonprofit E1 offer, send an escalation to Nonprofit Engagement  tsisales@microsoft.com to initiate an Enterprise Agreement licensing discussion. The following information must be included in the escalation email:
    1. Nonprofit Information – Organization location and Admin contact
    2. Contact preference (phone or email)
    3. Number of Employees
    4. Number of Volunteers
    5. Annual Operating Budget and IT Budget
    6. Current technology stack information (optional)
    7. A brief statement about the problem they’re looking to solve with E1 licenses and the solution requirements (optional)
Extensions for Nonprofit trials

If customers call requesting an end date extension for their Office 365 Nonprofit E3 trial or Office 365 Nonprofit Business Premium trial, trials can only be extended when the validation tag is showing anything but "denied". (Charity status can be seen in the Tags (Qualification) section near the bottom of the ACCOUNT page in CMAT.)
If customers do not have a validation tag of denied, they are eligible for a trial extension - however, the guidelines for trial extension described in article 2761499: Rules for trial extensions and seat increases still need to be followed.

NOTE: Do not escalate nonprofit trial extension requests to TechSoup.

Customers that have previously received other donations from Microsoft may still need to register

All customers wanting access to nonprofit offers need to be registered with Microsoft, including customers that have:

  • Never received an offer (aka brand new customers)
  • Only received on-premises software donations via TechSoup or a Microsoft Philanthropies’ strategic grant
  • Received the Azure and/or Open Charity Sales offers BUT did NOT create an O365 Nonprofit account
  • Registration will not affect their current Azure subscription or charity sales engagement
    To summarize, if the customer does not have an O365 tenant ID tagged as charity, the customer will need to register with Microsoft.

Note:

  • Customers that have an existing O365 Nonprofit account do not need to register again; these customers can simply log into the Nonprofit Portal and access offers
  • Having a TechSoup token does not equate to eligibility; customers that have a TechSoup token, but have not registered with Microsoft directly will need to register with Microsoft

If customers state they have received software donations or other nonprofit offers in the past using a TechSoup token and /or through TechSoup, you will need to inform the customer that Microsoft now requires that all customers be registered with Microsoft. 

 

Instruction for the customer:

Microsoft now requires all organizations to be registered with Microsoft directly in order to access our nonprofit donation and discount offers. You will need to register with Microsoft to gain access to the offers. As part of that process, you will create a Microsoft account, which you will then use to access the Nonprofit Portal. We apologize for any inconvenience. You can register by clicking Get Started (full URL: https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/#/register).

Escalation processes to support Office 365 and Dynamics 365 nonprofit customers

If the customer calls support or raises an online submission for any of the following scenarios, follow the designated escalation process:

  1. Request for an update on their nonprofit verification status
  2. Request to appeal a denied request for nonprofit status
  3. An existing Office 365 or Dynamics 365 commercial customer wants nonprofit pricing
  4. Nonprofit Dynamics 365 customer is unable to sign up for the commercial Dynamic 365 Trial
  5. Dynamics 365 nonprofit customer whose promo code has expired
  6. Customer has validated for nonprofit but does not have the option to buy nonprofit offers in the Microsoft 365 admin center

 

Scenario 1: Request for an update on their nonprofit verification status

If a customer contacts Support to check on the verification status, check the customer’s tags in CMAT to see if the customer has been tagged as charity. If not tagged, and the customer has already submitted a validation request through the Microsoft nonprofit registration, advocates can check the customer’s status through the Check Nonprofit Status tool located at https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/#/support.  Note that only users with an @microsoft.com account can access this tool.

  • The tenant admin is an actual user’s name and not a generic “admin”.
  • Verify in CMAT that the charity tag is pending approval.

Scenario 3: An existing Office 365 or Dynamics 365 commercial customer wants nonprofit pricing


Purpose: We have a temporary solution* for handling customers with existing tenant account seeking nonprofit and do not want to create or migrate to a new tenant. 

Process overview:

Note: This is a highly manual process that should only be used for customers actively using the tenant.  Support should check CMAT to determine if tenant contains subscriptions.

  • If no subscriptions exist, the customer should follow the regular sign up path (https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/#/register) which includes tenant account creation. 
    Use template #1 for reply to customer.
  • If subscriptions exist, the customer will need to follow the workaround steps described below. 
    Use template #2 for reply to customer.

This workaround should not be used as an alternative to regular nonprofit sign up.  This process takes longer than regular nonprofit sign up and requires more manual steps by the customer. 

*We are actively pursuing an automated solution that would enable any customer with an existing tenant regardless of whether it is being actively used or not to get access to our nonprofit offers.  This solution is expected in calendar year 2018. 

Edge cases: This process should also be used for the following edge cases.

  • Customer has an existing tenant pending nonprofit and an active nonprofit trial, but the organization did not complete sign up. 
    • Old processes had a loop hole that created this scenario.

Workaround steps:

Customers with existing tenants that are actively being used will need to complete the following steps:

  1. Complete Nonprofit sign up here: https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/#/register.
    • The customer will create a new Microsoft account as part of sign up.
  2. Wait for eligibility validation process to complete (process can take up to 20 business days)
  3. Receive email notification with eligibility validation result.
  4. If eligible, go to Contact Us and submit request for updating existing tenant for nonprofit.
    • Customer will need to provide the new tenant created during nonprofit sign up and the existing commercial tenant to update to nonprofit.
  5. Microsoft will complete the update on the backend and Contact Us support team will inform customer that process is complete.
  6. Customer can sign into the Nonprofit Portal to access offers. 

Customer should work with Office Support or Partner for help migrating commercial subscriptions (aka commercial SKUs) to nonprofit subscriptions (aka nonprofit SKUs).

Email templates:

Email templates #1 and #2 are intended for support team that received the initial inquiry from customer about using an existing commercial tenant for nonprofit.

Please use your standard opening (e.g. Hi Laura,) and closing (e.g. Thank you, Microsoft support team) for customer communications.

Template #1 – No workaround; need to create a new tenant

When to use: customer has existing tenant created, but tenant is not actively being used.  There are no subscriptions associated with the tenant.

Email template:

Thank you for your interest in our nonprofit offers.  It doesn’t look like the account you provided is actively being used.  As a result, you will need to complete the Microsoft nonprofit registration process to get validated as an eligible organization.  Please go to Get Started to register.  Registration does include creation of a new Microsoft account for nonprofit.  You will not be able to use the same account name as your existing tenant.  Please enter an alternate name for this new account. 

We realize this means that you will not be able to use the account name that you originally created.  We apologize for the inconvenience, but we can address this in a different way.  Please note that once your new Microsoft account for nonprofit is created, you can add a different domain to your nonprofit tenant account.  This action will enable you to use a custom domain, like nonprofit.org, rather than having to use an onmicrosoft.com domain.  You can learn how to add a domain here: Add a domain to Office 365.  If you need help adding a new domain to your Microsoft Account for nonprofit, please contact Office Support or your Microsoft Partner for assistance.

Template #2 – Workaround instructions

When to use: customer has existing tenant created, and tenant is actively being used.  There are subscriptions associated with the tenant.

Email template:

Thank you for your interest in our nonprofit offers.  We can provide a workaround that will enable you to use your existing commercial tenant for our nonprofit offers.  Below I have outlined the steps you will need to take.  Please note that you will need to complete the Microsoft nonprofit registration process to get validated as an eligible organization.  Registration does include creation of a new account.  You will see below that you will need to provide us with that new account information once you receive notification of eligibility in addition to the existing tenant you are wanting to use for nonprofit.

  1. Go to Get Started
  2. Follow prompts and complete nonprofit registration
    • Registration includes creating a Microsoft account. You will not be able to use the same account name as your existing tenant.  Please enter an alternate name for this new account.  You will need to complete this step.
    • You will receive an email notification from Microsoft confirming receipt of our submission.
  3. After you submit your registration, we will begin work to validate the eligibility of your organization.  This process may take up to twenty business days. 
    • You can log into the Nonprofit Portal to track your status during the validation process.
  4. Once the validation process is complete, you will receive an email from Microsoft with the validation result.
  5. If your validation result is approved (eligible), follow the steps below to enable your existing commercial tenant for nonprofit offers:
    • Go to Contact Us
    • Complete and submit form
      • For issue category, select “Eligibility”
      • For issue subcategory, select “Eligibility validation process or status”
      • In the issue description field, state that you need to enable an existing tenant for nonprofit.  Please include both your existing tenant information (e.g. nonprofit.onmicrosoft.com or nonprofit.org) and your new nonprofit tenant information (e.g. nonprofit2.onmicrosoft.com) in the description field. 
    • Microsoft will update your existing tenant
    • The Contact Us support team will let you know once your tenant has been updated for nonprofit

Scenario 4: Nonprofit Dynamics 365 customer is unable to sign up for the commercial Dynamic 365 Trial

A customer who is tagged as charity and receiving the nonprofit pricing for Dynamics 365 will not be able to sign up for a commercial Dynamics 365 trial due to mutual exclusivity of the two offers. A nonprofit paid customer will also no longer have access to a nonprofit trial.

If a customer wants to sign up for a trial for testing purposes, a Dynamics 365 nonprofit trial is available using a promotion code. Create a work item for Tier 3 using standard escalation processes to request a promotion code.


An existing nonprofit customer who wants commercial Office 365 or Dynamics 365 Online pricing must contact support, so the Charity tag can be removed from the tenant. This action will remove the nonprofit offers from the Microsoft 365 admin center and expose the Commercial offers.

Note: Skype for Business Add-on is currently not available for Nonprofit. So the process here is only applicable to other commercial plans.

Use the following process steps to assist the customer:

  1. Create a work item for Tier 3 using standard escalation processes to have the Charity tag removed from the tenant.
  2. Once the tag has been removed, assist the customer with purchasing the commercial offers.
  3. The customer must then move their users over to the new commercial subscription.
  4. Finally, assist the customer in canceling the nonprofit subscription.

Scenario 5: Dynamics 365 nonprofit customer whose promo code has expired

If a customer was set up with nonprofit pricing through a promo code that has expired, they may not be tagged as a charity in CMAT. Follow the steps in Scenario 3 above.

Scenario 6: Customer has validated for nonprofit but does not have the option to buy nonprofit offers in the Microsoft 365 admin center

  1. Confirm the customer’s nonprofit trial subscription.
  2. Verify the customer has a charity tag in CMAT.

    Customers who have active nonprofit subscriptions will show up as approved in CMAT. When the customer is qualified as a charity in CMAT, the charity tag will appear as approved in the Tags (Qualifications) section of the Admin page.


Customers who cannot be validated as nonprofit will be tagged as declined. Customers receive automated emails when their status is updated.

  1. Does the customer have a charity approved tag?
    • Yes. Ask the customer to log out of the Microsoft 365 admin center, close their web browser instance, re-open the browser, and log in again. If needed, follow basic portal troubleshooting steps: 2892161: How to troubleshoot browser error messages and compatibility issues. If the customer still cannot see the nonprofit offers, follow the escalation process in 2778091: How to escalate a Service Request to Tier 3, Engineering.
    • No. Follow the process in section An existing Office 365 or Dynamics 365 commercial customer wants nonprofit pricing in this article.
Mixed EDU/Church customers asking about eligibility for Nonprofit

The organization is a nonprofit church and also an educational facility and currently uses EDU for their school side of the business.
What happens if the organization wants to also get Nonprofit for their nonprofit church side of the business?

The organization is unable to combine EDU and Nonprofit subscriptions on a single tenant, so they would need to set up a second tenant if they want Nonprofit licenses for the church.
The organization should create the second tenant by completing the Nonprofit sign up here: https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/#/register.
For more information about user eligibility license restrictions please check the following page: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/nonprofits/eligibility
