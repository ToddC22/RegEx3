Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Surface Repair Specialists

What is this article about?

This article outlines the valid escalation scenarios for Service Order Management (SOM) issues to Surface Repair Escalation Advocates (REA) or the Global Repair Advisor Team (GRAT). This article should be followed when you are unable to resolve a customer’s repair or exchange issue.

This procedure does not apply to product safety issues. For those use one of the following procedures:

  • Consumer: KB 2764515 Procedure: Surface & Xbox: Handling Product Safety (RRT) Contacts
  • Commercial: KB 2971041 Procedure: Surface: Product Safety (RRT) Issues (Surface, Hub, HoloLens)

This procedure does not apply to technical issues. For those use the following procedure:

  • KB 4523231 Procedure: Surface: Technical Collaboration and Escalation

This document outlines how to get help when you cannot resolve the issue yourself. The scenarios in this article are the most common scenarios where escalations are needed. All other scenarios should be brought to the attention of your Tech Lead or manager for further guidance.

3. Warranty leniency request

A warranty leniency request refers to exceptions to Microsoft policy (up to and including the Terms and Conditions of the OEM Warranty). You cannot grant exceptions.

You may at times feel there is a business case to support an exception, or a customer may have been through a negative support experience. In these scenarios you may escalate to ask for warranty leniency exception.

Note:

When submitting a warranty leniency request, do not make any promises to the customer or set incorrect expectations. Advise the customer you have submitted a request for an account review, and you will provide them with the appropriate resolution once the review is completed.

The following information is required to be documented in case notes for all warranty leniency requests:

  1. Does the customer live in a country or region where consumer laws provide specific protections for the customer and current frontline documentation does not provide resolution? See the appropriate regional article under KB 4514977 Procedure: Surface: Services Exchange and Repair Offers Overview for any specific regional laws or guidance.
  • If the customer resides in Australia or New Zealand and has completed two or more device exchanges, refer to the guidance provided by KB 4502115 Procedure: Assisting customers from Australia or New Zealand who have had two or more device exchange orders.

Note: At any time if a customer sites a local consumer law that isn’t currently covered by a regional KB, document the information, and continue with these escalation requirements.

  1. Customer name.
  2. Customer Microsoft account or contact email address.
  3. Customer shipping address.
  4. Serial number of the customer’s device or type cover.
  5. History of device exchange or repair orders, collect the following information for each exchange order starting with the most recent:
    • Service Request number
    • Order creation date
    • Order type (Standard exchange or Advanced exchange)
    • Problem Code/Problem with Device Code
    • Ticketed serial number
    • Replacement serial number
  6. Collect the original or a copy of the proof of purchase for the product from the customer and attach to child SR DTM or TFS Incident.
    • The purpose of this is to confirm the customer’s original warranty entitlement (warranty start and end dates based on the date of original device purchase).
  7. Confirm if the customer has encountered the exact same issue with each replacement device or a different issue with each replacement device.
  8. Is the issue with the device a known issue as defined by the known issues article for the customer’s specific device?
    • Refer to KB 4072657 Informational: Surface: Technical guides and known issues landing page then locate the article specific to the customer’s device.
  9. Is the customer requesting a specific remedy or resolution?
    • If yes, what is the customer requesting?

Once the information has been gathered, follow the appropriate path to escalate the exception request. When escalating, use the below case notes template in the child SR or TFS Incident respectively.

Repair Specialist Ask: Please check if customer’s request for < refund, brand new or free replacement depending on request > can be granted.
