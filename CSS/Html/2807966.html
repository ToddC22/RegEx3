1. Applies to

The following services: All services
The following advocate roles: All roles
The following markets: United States, Colombia, Puerto Rico and Canada
 

Scenario
 

  • A customer has purchased Minecraft Education Edition and is requesting to be tax exempt and/or requires credit for sales tax to be billed. Follow article 3160067: Supporting Microsoft Store for Business customers.
  • A customer would like to be confirmed for non-profit.

The advocate should ask the customer if they are also a tax-exempt so we can also process the tax exemption for them.

NOTE: Not all customers that are nonprofit are tax exempt, make sure to follow above process only IF the customer is tax exempt.

  • A customer has a State Sales and Use Tax Exemption and should not be taxed on their subscription. This article describes the steps to process the certificate and request or approve a credit for sales tax billed.
  • A customer who has the Sold to or Service Recipient city address listed as Chicago asks about the tax on their invoice.
  • For customers not in the US, Canada, Colombia or Puerto Rico, see 2849736: Supporting Value-Added Tax and EOC Tax Exemption.

2. Guidance

Each customer's State Sales Tax Exemption has legal guidelines that are specific to that State Department of Tax Revenue (or similar department) and has to be managed accordingly.

This means that while most states have blanket certificates that are renewed annually, there are states that may renew at two or more years. The customer can verify this information by either checking their Certificate or by checking with their State for the appropriate renewal dates.

Notes:
  • The tax exemption is per tenant, request process is the same for all markets in this article including Chicago.
  • Customers eligible for tax exemption in CMAT will still see tax charge on the checkout page when making new purchases. The tax charge will appear only during the purchase process, but not on the final bill of the customer.

3. Procedure

A customer would like to be confirmed for non-profit.
  1. Direct customer to https://nonprofit.microsoft.com/register for the non-profit validation.
  2. Create a case for the tax exemption and set customer expectation in the process of applying the tax exemption on their account. In this case include the following:
    • Get validated for nonprofit
    • Have purchased a paid nonprofit subscription
    • Have requested a copy of the tax certificate
    • Started the process of validating tax exemption using the steps described below.

For state sales and use tax exemption requests


A. Request a copy of the Sales and Use Tax Certificate and the Tax ID or Federal Employer Identification Number (FEIN) from the customer. Validate the certificate is complete.

1. (Tier 1) Request a copy of the Sales and Use Tax Certificate and the TAX ID or Federal Employer Identification Number (FEIN) from the customer and send it to Tier 2/SME/TL for validation. Advise the customer to also check on below items:

  • Company name and address listed on the Tax Certificate should match the tenant’s company name and address.
  • Make sure that the customer’s tax ID or FEIN is on the certificate.
  • If the certificate has space for the signature, check for the date and signature.
  •  If there is a line for seller/vendor, make sure that the form has “Microsoft”,  "Microsoft Corporation" or “Microsoft Canada Inc” listed in the seller/vendor field. “Microsoft”, “Microsoft Corporation” and “Microsoft Canda Inc” are acceptable entries. Any other value is not acceptable and the customer should change this value.

Note: Since February 18, 2019, most Canadian sales currently under Microsoft Corp (1010) are now sold by Microsoft Canada Inc (1075). As part of this change, exemption certificates related to these sales will need to be issued to and saved in TEAMS under Microsoft Canada Inc. rather than Microsoft Corp.

  •  If there is a field for the description of the property, make sure that the form has “Office 365” or the type of purchased service.

Note: Documents from the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) are not acceptable for sales tax exemption. The IRS determines exemption from federal income tax, not from state sales tax.

  1. (Tier 2/SME/TL) Validate the certificate is complete:

    i. Make sure that the customer name and address are on the certificate and that the customer name matches the name on the tenant.
    ii. Make sure that the customer’s tax ID or FEIN is on the certificate. 

    Note: If there is no field on the certificate, the advocates request the customer to write down on the form themselves. If the Tier 2 advocates want to add the tax ID or FEIN to the ticketing system Notes to Tier 1 for tax exemption reference, it MUST be on the form, not just in the ticketing system.

    iii. If there is a line for a signature, it must be signed and dated. Not all state certificates require a signature, but most do.
    iv. If there is a line for seller or vendor, make sure that the form has "Microsoft”, "Microsoft Corporation" or "Microsoft Canada Inc" listed in the seller/vendor field. "Microsoft”, “Microsoft Corporation” and “Microsoft Canada Inc" are acceptable entries. Any other value is not acceptable and should be changed by the customer.
    v. SME/TL review the State/Province specific requirements here: https://teams7.teamscert.com/microsoft/forms.asp?Client=microsoft. 

    • Look for Exemption Process tab, read Exemption Doc Examples for the US, CAN, Colombia, Puerto Rico as appropriately, follow the instructions in Exemption US/Canada/Colombia/Puerto Rico.
    4012627 
     • If the document is missing any information or does not meet the specific requirements of the State, please reject the customers’ information and inform them why you are not able to process the request.
    • SME/TL convert the document to a PDF via Fox Reader. https://www.foxitsoftware.com/products/pdf-reader/
    • Send the Frontline Advocate the date that the document was signed asking to have the document reviewed. They will need this to calculate the total refund to the customer.
    • As with any scenario, if you have additional questions, reach out to Pod Lead.
    • Examples of unacceptable values are Microsoft Online, MSFT, MS Corp, and MS.

B. (Tier 1) Locate the customer’s invoices in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT) and tally the sales taxes billed. Issue the appropriate refund.
  1. Find the invoices generated since the effective/issue date of the certificate or the date the certificate was signed by the customer.
  2. Save each invoice to the location specified by your supervisor.
  3. Attach the invoices to the Service Request.
  4. Tally the sales taxes billed on the invoices since the effective/issue date of the tax exemption certificate or the date the certificate was signed by the customer.
  5. Issue refund on sales tax, follow the guidance and procedure in article 2996655: Applying a credit to a customer’s account using CMAT.

    NOTE: Additional approval is not needed from the BG regardless of the amount as this has already gone through vetting with the tax team. You can process the request in CMAT and send it for approval to Tier 3 if it exceeds the empowerment limit. 

  6. Update the tax exemption status in CMAT.
    1. On the Account page, click Add in front of Tax Information section.

      4012630 
    2. For US customers, select US Exempt, enter the customer’s tax identification number in the ID field, Comments that the tax-exempt status was validated and approved and then click on the next button.

      4012631 
    3. Enter the ticketing system case number and then click on the submit button.

      4012634 
    4. Once the tax information has been added, the Tax information section will display below information. On successful validation, the status should change from Pending to Valid. If the Tax ID Status shows as Invalid, escalate to Tier 3 so it can be further escalated to PayOps.

      4012640 

      Here is a list of different Tax ID Status that you can expect in CMAT.
    • Pending: A temporary status that appears until a VAT ID is validated by an external service (VIES). Once VIES validates the VAT ID, the Tax ID status changes to either Valid or Invalid. If the VAT ID isn’t validated, the status changes to Pastdue.
    • Deleted: The Tax ID was deleted.
    • Note: If the status is Deleted, you can immediately add the customer’s exemption information again and it should push the status to valid.
    • Expired: The Tax ID has expired and won’t be applied to transactions.
    • Note: If the status is Expired, the customer will need to submit a new tax-exempt request with the latest information.
    • Past due: The VAT ID wasn’t’ validated by external validation service (VIES) with the set amount of time.
    • Note: If the status is Pastdue, you can immediately add the customer’s exemption information again and it should push the status to valid.
    • Invalid: The Tax ID is not valid and won’t be applied to transactions.
    • Note: If the status is Invalid, this will require an escalation to PayOps. Follow your current escalation process to engage with Tier 3 so they can further escalate it to PayOps.
    • Valid: The Tax ID is valid and will be applied to transactions.
    • Note: The change from Pending to Valid is usually instant. However, at times it requires that we apply the exemption status a second time to push CMAT to accept the exemption status. As the change is immediate, you can continue to process the credit straight away.
C. (Tier 2/SME/TL) Submit tax exemption form to Tax team in the ECM Portal.
  1. Go to the ECM Portal and enter the appropriate information: https://enterprisevertex.microsoft.com/ECM. Follow article 4012644: Work instructions: send tax exemption certificates.
    Note: There is now a Canadian ‘Lines of Business’ as a drop down on the ECM Webform. This same Line of Business should be included on the top of the exemption certificate when submitting to Sovos.
    4012643 
  2. Upload exemption certificates directly to the ECM Webform instead of email separately. This will help reduce inconsistencies between customer information and exemption certificates on file. A customer number and line of business will still need to be written on the top of the exemption certificate.
    Please note that users need to scroll to the far right to upload the certificate.

For Chicago lease tax exemption requests
The City of Chicago began imposing a 5.25% Lease Tax on Chicago customers who purchase certain types of software as of January 1, 2016. The Lease Tax is similar to sales tax but is levied on software that is accessed via cloud services. Microsoft did not charge customers for January and February Lease Taxes that were due. Microsoft paid the sales tax on behalf of those customers for those two months. In March 2016, Microsoft began charging the lease tax to customers with impacted subscriptions. The change has been communicated to customers and partners.
  • Impacted Products: certain non-downloadable Office 365, Dynamics 365 and Intune subscriptions (See spreadsheet showing subscriptions and how they are impacted by the tax. It is easier to search by the last 4 digits of the SKU rather than service/plan name).
  • Impacted Customers: Customers and Partners with Chicago as the city listed in the Sold to or Service Recipient address field.
  • Here is the legal ruling citing the specific code sections.
  • Here is the information bulletin from the city of Chicago.
There is an application for business exemptions here. The customer can complete this form and submit it to the Chicago Department of Finance address listed on the form to obtain a lease tax exemption certificate. The customer can then submit the exemption certificate to Commerce Support who will calculate the lease taxes billed since March 1st, 2016, and process a credit request to accordingly.

For New York governmental entities tax exemption requests

Per the New York Taxation and Finance Department, New York governmental entities do not require a tax ID or tax exemption certificate to be tax exempt on purchases. The Department provides a letter. Here is an example letter for tax exemption.

For CMAT code, the entity’s EIN (Employer Identification Number) is used as the “Tax ID” or all zeros.

Note: We can prevent fraud by validating the information on the letter such as name, address, etc., and ensure that the ship-to address matches with the address in the letter.

For Colombia tax exemption requests

Microsoft is expecting to collect tax for sales of digital products/services into Colombia beginning 8/1/2019 for B2C sales and 11/1/2019 for B2B sales (unless the customer claims a business exemption).

In order to be eligible for a tax exemption, business will need to provide:
(1) their Tax ID
(2) a completed copy of the RUT form (here is a sample RUT) with Section 53 showing “11” as one of the values. This form will need to be validated and then uploaded to TEAMS via the ECM WebForm following the same process as US and Canada exemptions. As November approaches, the ECM WebForm will have ‘Colombia’ as a value in the drop-down in the Customer Bill-to and Ship-To State fields and this value should be selected.
