ASMS – TA Only – Deprovision/Delete Subscription

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.  

Overview  

This TSG provides guidance to TA's on how to handle subscription deletion (immediate cancel) request.  

Deprovisioning a subscription is a process by which an Azure subscription is removed from the Azure system in such a way that: 

  1. It takes effect both in CP and RDFE.  

  1. It cannot be re-enabled.

  1. All data in the subscription is permanently deleted with no provision for recovering it again. 

Note: Deprovision requests usually take around 7 days to take effect. Make sure customers are aware of this.

Policy  

General

  • Access to Active Directory (AAAD) Subscriptions: Deletion requests for these subscription types require no approvals. Proceed with the deletion as requested.
  • Support Subscriptions: If the cancel/delete request for a support subscription is coming from the owner of the subscription, additional approvals are not required. Proceed with the deletion as requested.

Prerequisites

Before a subscription can be deprovisioned the following must happen: 

  1.  A written (email) approval from the Account Administrator (AA) must be obtained. 

  1. The AA must acknowledge that they understand all data will be permanently deleted: 

    • With SDC, customers can log into the portal with the AA ID and add a log to approve the deprovision and acknowledge disclaimer: "Deprovisioning is an irreversible action. Once the process has been executed all data will be lost".

    • You can find the approval under communication and the email they used to log in. You can consider this AA approval/acknowledgment to deprovision Azure subscriptions      

    • If the customer is unable to do the above, follow the forced SOT process outlined in TSG 3040383.  

  1. Justification for deprovisioning.  

  1. The subscription should already be SUSPENDED by the customer. The only exception is if the account is inaccessible by the customer, preventing them from suspending the subscription. Cancellation at this stage will be subjected to PII validation and can only be done by TA/PTA's. Please do not cancel subscriptions on Customer's behalf.

Note: Deprovisioning is an irreversible action. Once the process has been executed all data will be lost. 

Customer Experience and Provisioning  

Most customers request deprovisioning of their subscriptions for the following reasons:   

  • To delete all their services permanently. 

  • To remove the credit card from their account.  

  • To activate a new subscription under a different member offer (of which only one could be active per subscription) of which he/she already has one.  

 Once this action is completed, it’s irreversible, all customer data for that subscription will be deleted.  

Support Scenarios  

Deprovisioning a Subscription

Follow the steps below to deprovision a subscription: 

1. Open the account in CMAT and search for the subscription.  

 [Asset 4095300]

2.  Make sure you have the approval email from the Account Admin.  

3. Click on the link for Subscription Status -> Set the Action field as Delete-> Select a Reason from the dropdown -> Enter your comments in the Comments field.   

[Asset 4095302]

 4. Review the values entered and add the [Asset 4497459]SR#. Once reviewed, complete the action by clicking the check mark button.  

 

 

Deleting Subscriptions Under Orphan Accounts

TA/PTA's cannot delete subscriptions which are under orphan accounts so to delete orphan account subs, do the following:

  • Engage PayOps to cancel the subscription informing PayOps to Immediate Cancel the subscription.
  • After PayOps moves the subscription to the status of Immediate Cancel, it will follow the lifecycle and be deleted by the system. Complete the deprovisioning process following the guidelines in TSG 2646764.
    • If deleting the subscription fails, consult your TA/PTA and engage ASE to delete the subscription.
  • For Support plans work with PayOps and ensure the support plan is Immediate Cancelled and then PayOps needs to create a bug in the OSGS\Store Core\Catalog and Fulfillment\FnL\Fulfillment path.

 

Engagement and Escalation Path  

If issues arise during the cancelation procedure, escalate to PayOps (TSG 2651579).
