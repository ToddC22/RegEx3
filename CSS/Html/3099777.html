Procedure: Windows: Windows 10 upgrade exception

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • Surface

What is this article about?

This article provides the steps for the Windows 10 upgrade exception policy and steps for customers who missed the Windows 10 free upgrade offer, which ended December 2016.

Overview

As of December 2016, Microsoft has made it easier for IT pros and commercial customers to use the Media Creation Tool to deploy licensed copies of Windows 10. When upgrading from Windows 7 or Windows 8/8.1 to Windows 10, the Product Key entry screen has been removed.

Important: Global PA advocates should refer to KB 3100924 Procedure: MPA: Windows 10 upgrade exceptions.

Surface advocates ONLY: For scenarios 2 and 3 outlined below, transfer the customer to a Windows Technical Support advocate or provide them with the Global Customer Service phone number for assistance.

1. Customer is using assistive technology and wants to upgrade

*Assistive technology refers to customers that can benefit from or use MS accessibility features. See https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/accessibility/

Recommend that the customer make sure their PC meets the system requirements for Windows 10 and visit the PC manufacturer’s website for any additional information about driver and hardware compatibility.

The link below will launch the upgrade immediately. For more information about this upgrade experience, see KB 3184381 Informational: Windows 10 Upgrade Exception – Customer Experience . The entire process can take over an hour and require a download of around 3.5 Gb of data. The upgrade process will verify they are compatible with Windows 10 and then proceed to download and install Windows 10. The link will provide an upgrade to Windows 10 with the latest update.

  • TS & UR support - http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkID=822784
  • Surface support - http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkID=822785
  • Sales support - http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkID=822786

Advocate Only: For piracy reasons, the above links may be refreshed at the end of each month. Make sure to always refer to this KB before providing the link the customer.

2. Customer has already upgraded to Windows 10 but is not activated

2.1 Motherboard was changed on a previously activated Windows 10 PC

Devices that upgrade to Windows 10 are activated using digital licenses, hence the customer will not have a 5x5 key for Windows 10 to reuse for reactivation. For activation after motherboard replacement and out of OEM warranty, see KB 3082672 Procedure: MPA: Windows 10 Motherboard Replacement (MBR) and Device Exchange

Customers should contact their OEM if the device is within OEM Warranty. If OEM refuses to help the customer make note of the OEM name and the reason for refusal in case notes. Follow KB 3082672 Procedure: MPA: Windows 10 Motherboard Replacement (MBR) and Device Exchange to help make it right.

2.2 Customer has done a clean install instead of upgrade and is calling to obtain a Windows 10 license

(DO NOT USE THE BELOW STEPS FOR MOTHERBOARD REPLACEMENT)

While the Promotional Offer has ended, we can provide an exception to obtain Windows 10 if we can verify they have a valid Windows 7, Windows 8 or Windows 8.1 Product Key.

  1. Find the product key:
    1. Did the device come preinstalled with Windows 8 or 8.1?

      If yes, have the customer open a Command Prompt, running it as administrator, and run the following command:

      wmic path SoftwareLicensingService get OA3xOriginalProductKey

      The customer should see a product key returned in the format XXXXX-XXXXX-XXXXX-XXXXX-XXXXX
    2. If the device was upgraded from Windows 7, or the product key is not returned by the command above, ask the customer to check for the following:
      • Product key on a sticker on the back or bottom of the PC, or for a mobile PC, possibly under the battery.
      • Product key provided on a card when the device or software was purchased.
      • Product key received in an email
  2. If the product key is not available, check for Proof of Purchase instead.


If customer is NOT able to provide Proof of Purchase or the Product Key from their previous Windows installation:

If the customer has not yet installed Windows 10 on the device, we can guide them to the Microsoft online Store. This will enable them to purchase a product key for Windows 10.

Or

If the customer has already installed Windows 10 on the device, we can guide them through the Get Genuine experience through Microsoft Store. This will enable them to purchase a digital license for Windows 10 at standard cost.

Stop here if the customer can’t provide Proof of Purchase or Product Key. The remainder of this article only applies to handling customers who have a key or a proof of purchase.

If customer is able to provide a Product Key from their previous Windows installation:

  1. Use KIT to verify that the product key is valid and not blocked. If the key is invalid or blocked for activation we cannot proceed.
    For the below countries, check to make sure ROT is less than 5.

  2. Verify that the key also matches the edition of Windows 10 that is currently installed.

Note: If the customer provided a Windows 7 Home or Windows 8/8.1 Standard key but their current installation is Windows 10 Pro, they will need to reinstall Windows 10 Home in order to activate with the key that will be provided.

  1. Document the following in the SR:
    • The method of validation that was done (Key validation in KIT or write the receipt/invoice number in SR)
    • Full name and edition of the Windows operating system the customer upgraded from
    • Edition of Windows 10 the customer installed
    • DO NOT include the customer’s old 5x5 product key in the SR notes or in PID Plus.
  2. See Next steps, below.


If customer is able to provide a Proof of Purchase:

Note: If the customer provides details for Windows 7 Home or Windows 8/8.1 Standard but their current installation is Windows 10 Pro, they will need to reinstall Windows 10 Home in order to activate with the key that will be provided. Guide the customer to the software download website to obtain Windows 10 media.

  1. Document the following in the SR:
    • The method of validation that was done (receipt/invoice number)
    • Full name and edition of the Windows operating system the customer upgraded from
    • Edition of Windows 10 the customer installed
  2. See Next steps, below.
Next steps:
 
  1. Using PID Plus, select the Edition appropriate PKPN and generate a Windows 10 Product Key for the customer at no additional cost to the customer.

    X19-98877 ProdKey6 WIN HOME N 10 03270 DLA Retail Select Retail for “Product Type”
    X19-98841 ProdKey6 Win Pro 10 03308 DLA Retail Select Retail for “Product Type”

    X19-99460 ProdKey6 WIN HOME SL 10 03275 DLA OEM:NONSLP Rylty PKEA Select OEM for “Product Type”
  2. In PID Plus select the Delivery Mode as Phone.
  3. Provide customer a Windows 10 5x5 key.

Note: If you have reached the key limit in PID Plus, engage a Lead/Supervisor/SME to provide customer the replacement product key.

3. Customer purchased a Windows 10 license from a retail store, found out about the exception policy, and wants to return the product or wants a refund

3.1 Customer purchased Windows 10 from retail (other than Microsoft Store) - Box is unopened

Ask the customer to return the product to the retail establishment to get a refund.

Note: If the customer claims that the retailer did not accept a return/refund, escalate to [Asset 4039877] team.

  1. Provide the customer a free upgrade option.
    • If the customer has not yet upgraded to Windows 10, provide the customer with the link to download Windows 10 for free, found in section 1.
    • If the customer has already upgraded to Windows 10, follow the process in section 2.2.

Return to top

3.2 Customer purchased Windows 10 from retail store (other than Microsoft Store) – Box is opened or no returns allowed

Not all regions allow return/refund and retailers may not accept opened boxes. Engage the customer issue to Microsoft-based Advanced Capability Advocates (ACA) team.

Return to top

3.3 Customer purchased Windows 10 from the Microsoft Store

For Microsoft Store purchase, transfer the customer to the Sales team.

  1. Sales will provide a refund without returning the product itself. (Please DO NOT make them sign an ELOD agreement)
    • Please ask Tier 2/Supervisor to perform the actual refund without return.
