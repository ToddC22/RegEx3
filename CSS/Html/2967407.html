1. Activation and redemption

Geo-blocking happens either during online activation, or during redemption. This article focuses on redemption geo-block issues.

  • Activation: Customer has a product key (not a redemption PIN) and has previously installed the product.
    • Activation happens by using product activation IVRs and support advocate activation tools.
    • If the customer is using a product key, then follow the procedure in KB 4054504 Procedure: MPA: Geo-Block Overview & Call Handling.
  • Redemption: The customer has a redemption PIN/Token and is trying to install for the first time.

It’s important to note that product keys and redemption PINs/Token will appear the same to the customer as they are both 5x5 alphanumeric codes.

2. How to determine if the 5x5 key is a redemption PIN or product key?

You will need to use KIT (Key Information Tool) to check if it’s a PIN or product key. KIT has a field called IS PIN. If display shows Yes, then it’s a redemption PIN.

Note: You can use the geo-block tool only for redemption PINs (not product keys!)

3. Handling geo-block redemption key

When a customer attempts to redeem Office outside of the country where it was intended, they will receive the following error message if their product is geo-blocked: This product is not intended for your identified region. Please contact support for help.

Return to top

3.1 Eligibility overview

All advocates: Inform the customer that we will be checking for eligibility requirements such as Proof of Purchase (PoP). If eligible, a cost of $40 will apply to replace the Office product (make sure the customer agrees with the payment and replacement before proceeding).

Advocates in China: Once the customer passes eligibility and agrees to the $40 charge, direct them to https://www.microsoftstore.com.cn/msscncategory/premiumsoftwaresupport/p/micjs0796 to make the purchase.

India advocates: Once the customer passes eligibility and agrees to the $40 charge, transfer this type of request to a site that performs the financial steps.

When it is OK to provide a replacement Office product:

  • The customer is traveling and purchased Office in a different country and is not able to redeem Office in their destination country.
  • The customer purchased Office in their region but is trying to redeem it in a different country while traveling or has recently moved.

When we should not provide a replacement Office product:

  • Products purchased from different regions to take advantage of cost.
  • Products purchased in bulk that are geo-blocked.
    • Purchase in bulk means more than one.
    • If the customer purchases more than one copy of Office and is geo-blocked, the unblock process doesn’t apply.
  • Products sold by individuals on third party websites that are geo-blocked.

3.2 Customer eligibility requirements

To help identify if an exception, the following conditions must be met:

  1. Check if the customer is a repeat caller (check in ASD by name/phone/Microsoft account) and have a history of contacting us repetitively for unblocking geo-blocked keys.
    • If yes, escalate the case to Tier 2 immediately. The Tier 2 team should collect all relevant information from the customer and contact the TA team.
  2. The customer needs to provide information on where and when they purchased the product and why they are redeeming it in a different country (document this in the case notes).
  3. The customer needs to provide valid proof of purchase (PoP), captured in the Data Transfer Management (DTM) tool . Office advocates, see section 1.5 from Solve KB 3203023 Procedure: Office: Office Solve scenarios (Goodwill version). All other support advocates see section 1.3 from Solve KB 3076290 Procedure: Office: Office Solve scenarios.

Valid PoP could be a screenshot of the receipt or email receipt attachment from the retailer.

  • If the PoP is a physical receipt and PPC, request the customer to write down their service request (SR) number on both the receipt and back of the 5x5 card.
  • If the PoP is an e-receipt or PDF sent by the retailer, then the customer must provide a copy of the original email order summary sent by the retailer as an additional attachment in DTM (to ensure it is not coming from random seller or individual).
  • Do not inform the customer of the required criteria below. This is internal information to help confirm if the receipt images are valid. Seek guidance from Tier 2/Supervisor to advise the customer we may not be able to assist further.
  • PoP receipt image must have the following information: 
    • Name of the retailer
    • Invoice/bill number
    • Purchase amount
    • Date of sale
    • Product description
  • If e-receipt or PDF, the included copy of the order confirmation email sent by the retailer must have the following information: Retailer name, retailer email address, and the date & time the email was received (usually in the top right or left corner of the email).
    • Within the email sent by the retailer, look for words like "digital" or "shipped" to ensure the customer’s story matches the retailer email or receipt.

3.3 If customer is eligible – online Microsoft Store purchase

If all the conditions outlined in section 3.2 are met and the customer purchased directly from the online Microsoft store, let them know that you will refund their purchase, and they will be able to re-purchase the correct product from the store for their region. Refunds should be issued within refund guidelines. We can’t refund any purchases made from a Brick & Mortar (B&M) Microsoft Store.

India advocates send the case to a non-India Office troubleshooting (TS) team to perform the next steps due to monetary transaction being involved.
  1. Verify the customer if you have not already.
  2. Locate the product in MST.
  3. Issue refund according to refund guidelines. See KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund or KB 4338529 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Exception based cancel and return for recurring subscriptions.
  4. Help the customer find the product for their region in the Microsoft Store or help then make the purchase using POBO. Important: See KB 4578369 Procedure: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) Consent and KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) for information on POBO and mandatory consent actions.
  5. Immediately deactivate the geo-blocked 5x5 pin using Pin Lookup Tool in MST. See KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool. If you are unable to block the pin, send the information to Tier 2. Tier 2 will follow the below step:
    • Tier 2, see KB: 3147392 Procedure: MPA: Handling Escalations for Blocked, or to Block, Product Keys.
  6. After all steps above are complete, close the case as there is no need to escalate the case.

US military personnel:

For US military personnel, do not ask them to provide a photo of their military ID card. You will need to send an email to their military ID and have them reply back to confirm that they are an active duty military personnel.

There will be no $40 charge and you can send the token to the customer.

3.4 If customer is eligible – Third-party purchase

If the conditions in section 3.2 are met, identify if the customer purchased from a third-party. If so, follow these steps:

  1. Inform the customer that there will be a $40 charge if they want to replace the key. We also will deactivate the original key. If the customer agrees to both conditions, proceed to the next step.
    • Note: If the customer disagrees to either condition, seek guidance from Tier 2/supervisor to advise the customer we may not be able to assist further.
    • India advocates should advise the customer of the $40 charge and receive agreement from the customer. After, send the case to a non-India Office TS team to perform the next steps due to monetary transaction being involved.
  2. Charge the customer $40:
    • Log in to MST.
    • Select New Order, the country, and then search for Product ID: DG7GMGF0FL7X and select the $40 SKU.
  3. Use the Geo-Block tab in ORST to send a new token.
  4. Immediately deactivate the geo-blocked 5x5 pin using Pin Lookup Tool in MST. See KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool. If you are unable to block the pin, send the information to Tier 2. Tier 2 to follow the below steps:
    • Tier 2 should send the 5x5 sent to DSCSUP@microsoft.com. Follow instructions in KB: 3147392.
  5. Close the case after sending the 5x5 information to Tier 2 as there is no need to escalate the case.

US military personnel:

Active duty military personnel, serving outside the United States, provide a copy of their military ID as proof. Capture it in DTM. There will be no $40 charge. Send the token to the customer.

Office purchased from Microsoft Employee Store:

Microsoft employees can purchase Office from the corporate Microsoft employee store. This is not to be confused with the Microsoft online store or Microsoft brick and mortar store. In cases where a Microsoft employee purchase outside their region and try to redeem, or in cases where they gift the product to someone outside their region, we will support these customers and provide them with a token free of charge.

You must collect the following and upload to DTM:

  • Customer’s Microsoft email address
  • PoP
  • Token or POSA screenshot

Tier 2 will reach out to their region TA to verify the employee and will send the token via ORST.

4. Tips for identifying suspicious customers

  • The customer refuses to provide data: Case history shows refusal to provide PoP and/or shows continuous disconnects when it’s requested.
  • Information mismatch: The customer claims they purchased the product from a Microsoft Store, but the PoP displays a third-party name as the retailer.
  • If PoP does not align with the customer’s story, then decline the Office replacement.
  • Customer uses the same PoP: The customer’s previous PoP already submitted are the same.
  • The key shows up as VLK (volume licensing) or Bypass key. Escalate the customer to Tier 2, who would need to work with the TA team for next steps.

Return to top

5. ORST geo-block tool error

If you get an error while processing in the Geo Block tab in ORST, immediately report it to your Team Manager. Team Manager/Tier 2 will work with TA.

Do not use the Make it Right tab in ORST to process a new token for a geo-block scenario.
