Overview

This TSG provides information about our various Microsoft Partner Network (MPN) offerings.
This information includes support boundries, escalation paths, and the benefits of the various offers available. 

Introduction

The Microsoft Partner Network (MPN) is designed to make Azure resources available to a technology companies so they can build a business around Microsoft technologies. These partners help upsell and resell Microsoft products to variety of industries customized to their preference and work environment. Based on the success of this partnership with Microsoft, Microsoft partners receive perks such as Azure, O365, and other product licenses.

The MPN program consists of three levels of Azure benefits:

  • Action Pack – Also known as Azure Benefit for MPN, this offers partners $100 of credit per month to use for Azure Services.
  • Silver – Also known as Azure Benefit for MPN Silver Cloud Platform Competency (Azure Sponsorship 6k), this offers partners $6,000 USD per Enrollment year to use for Azure Services.
  • Gold – Also known as Azure Benefit for MPN Gold Cloud Platform Competency (Azure Sponsorship 12k), this offers partners $12,000 USD per Enrollment year to use for Azure Services.

MPN Benefit: Action Pack (25P)

  • This offer is restricted to one per organization and one activation per Azure account.

  • The MPN Action Pack entitled Azure subscription (25P) is eligible to receive $100 monthly Azure Credit (Offers - Azure Benefit for MPN).

Manage Cloud Services Benefits

  1. Select Cloud to see your list of subscriptions to cloud-based services.
  2. Find the subscription or product you want to start using, and then select the down arrow at the far right of the row to expand the subscription's or product's details.
  3. To activate Microsoft Azure usage-based subscriptions, assign yourself as a user. Go the Microsoft Azure portal to manage the subscription.
  4. To activate license-based subscriptions, select Get keys to copy the product license key(s), and then follow any special instructions to activate the subscription.

 

MPN Benefits: Visual Studio

Visual Studio Enterprise (MPN) subscribers receive $150 per month of Azure credit.

Manage Visual Studio Subscriptions

  1. Select Visual Studio subscriptions to see your list of Visual Studio subscriptions.
  2. Find the subscription you want to start using, and then select the down arrow at the far right of the row to expand the subscription's details.
  3. To activate a Visual Studio subscription, select a user from the list and then select Assign user.
  4. Important: If the user you want to assign is not in the list, you can add new users in Account settings.
  5. Repeat this process for each subscription you want to assign. Users can manage their subscriptions in the Visual Studio portal.

You can reassign subscriptions any time by removing an assigned user and then assigning a different user.

Digital Partner of Record

Digital Partner of Record (DPOR, or POR) is an authorized Microsoft Partner, with a Silver or Gold Cloud Competency rating, that establishes a business relationship to help customers plan or implement Azure solutions on a specific subscription. Customers may request to have a DPOR added at a business relationship level to their subscription.

Note: A DPOR is not a Service Administrator or Co-Administrator and does not have any admin rights to the subscription. Also, DPOR is only available to OMS accounts, not MINT accounts. More information about Azure DPORs can be found on the Microsoft Azure Partner Incentives page or in the DPOR FAQ.

Customers can add a DPOR in the Account Portal by following these steps:

  1. Select an active subscription.
  2. Select Partner Information.
  3. Enter the Microsoft Partner ID.
    • The user will need the MPN Partner ID from their Partner or from the MPN admin in their company.

Note: Once the DPOR has been added it may take up to one billing cycle (30 days) for the update to show on the account.

Note: Azure Government customers can perform the above operation in the Fairfax Management Portal.
 

SkyKick Cloud Backup Offer

Microsoft has included SkyKick as part of their IUR programs, which offers partners free SkyKick benefits. While all partners get a 30-day free trial for their customers, this IUR offer allows customers to back up their company’s Office 365 Exchange, SharePoint, and OneDrive for Business data on Azure. Customers also receive unlimited retention, automated backup snapshots (up to 6 per day), and lightning fast search and restorations.

The Azure SkyKick Cloud Backup IUR offer is free for partners forever, but partners have to setup a backup subscription. More details can be found on the Azure IUR SkyKick Cloud Backup Offer web page.

Note: See the Benefit Activation Workflow in the Support Scenarios section below.
 

About the MPN Portal

There are two MPN portals:

  1. Classic / Partner Membership Center / PMC
  2. New / Partner Center / PC

Starting in 2019, all new sign-ups happen in the Partner Center portal. Existing customers are migrated in phases from the Classic portal to the new portal. This is an automated activity and the order in which customers are transitioned cannot be changed.
 

Differences Between the Old and the New Portal

It is important to understand which portal the customer is using since the benefit activation workflow, benefit assignment, and troubleshooting steps are portal-specific. Only TAs can identify the customer’s MPN portal version (Partner Membership Portal or Partner Center Portal) with the VMT/Prose tool. Ask your TA to validate which version of the portal applies to your scenario. The main difference between the two portals is that the Classic portal allows only MSA accounts to perform any activities, whereas the New portal allows only ORG accounts.

Note: Customers cannot remove or re-assign Silver or Gold Azure Benefits in the Partner Portal. SEs should take the following steps to assist customers who want to remove or re-assign Silver or Gold Azure Benefits:

  1. Check if the user has an active Azure Internal Registration System (AIRS) entitlement and/or has an Azure Subscription.

  3. If the user does not meet the requirements in step 1, engage the OneVL Team to work with MPN Global Support to force the transfer of the benefit or re-trigger AIRS so that step 2 can be followed.

Offer Conversions

Follow the directions in TSG 2647585.

If you do, you will receive the following error message in CMAT: "User limit is exceeded for Offer id 'MS-AZR-0025P".

Subscription Ownership Transfer (SOT) of MPN (-25P only)

Leverage the steps outlined in TSG 3040383 on how to perform a transfer. 

However, before transferring the MPN benefit-entitled Azure subscription to another account, ensure that the destination account is entitled to receive the benefit. The destination subscription may already be entitled, or the benefits may need to be assigned. 

If the destination already contains an MPN, and the customer wishes to proceed with the SOT, the existing MPN subscription in the destination account must be converted to a different offer.

Note: By design, an account is only allowed to have one MPN subscription (even if suspended). The existing MPN subscription must be deprovisioned (deleted) for the account to be eligible for a new MPN subscription. 

Allowed SOT Paths

Old/PMC portal:

  • MSA to MSA – Allowed after entitlement transfer.
  • MSA to ORG – Allowed without entitlement transfer. Domain name on source and destination should match - if not engage your TA.
  • ORG to ORG – Allowed without entitlement transfer. Domain name on source and destination should match – if not engage your TA.

New/Partner Center Portal:

  • MSA to MSA – Not allowed, new portal only supports ORG ID.
  • MSA to ORG – Allowed after entitlement transfer.
  • ORG to ORG – Allowed after entitlement transfer.

DPOR Errors

The following instances may give your customer errors when they try to add a Partner ID into the DPOR field. Review each instance to find the best solution for your customer’s issue.

The Partner ID is invalid

Sometimes you can access the partner info directly by typing the MPN ID to the end of the URL, like this (in bold): https://ocpinsights.azurewebsites.net/Partner-ID-here

If the customer is not sure if their ID is valid, you can check the Partner ID at OCP Insights.

Note: To have a valid Partner ID, the Partner must also have Gold or Silver Cloud Competency.

No Record of PartnerID
Customers may receive the following error message when trying to associate a partnerID to a subscription: "We don't have a record of this PartnerID. Validate and try again.". 

In these instances, you should do the following: 

  1. Validate if the Partner is using their Location ID, and not their Virtual Partner ID.
    1. You can validate this under https://ocpinsights.microsoft.com/ by Searching with the Partner ID and navigating to MPN Org Hierarchy section.
    2. Look for Partner ID under the Org Type set to “Location” and ensure customer is using the right one based on their desired location (as there may be multiple locations)
  2. If the error still persist with a valid LocationID, do the following:
    1. Engage PayOps to check if they can bulk update the Partner ID.
    2. In an event PayOps is unable to do so, then capture a screenshot, network trace, and timestamp from customer. Then engage ASE to analyze the issue.
    3. Once you have the analysis from ASE, share the details with the MPN Global Support (MGS) team via a task and request for them to engage PACEOps/PX Engineering to investigate and fix the issue. Ensure to provide them complete details.
      • MGS Queue Name: CSS PE Global MSFT Bucharest MPN T3 
    4. MGS will share a PACEOps SR details for your review and follow-up.

MPNID is missing "Code 99"

There is a known issue where the MPNID is valid, has Gold or Silver Cloud Competency, but is missing a property called "Program Code 99" in the MPNID records database. Program Code 99 is required to add the ID as the DPOR in the account portal.

Do the following in CMAT to identify if Code 99 is missing:

  1. Switch the CMAT template to Office 365.
  2. Click the MPN Partner ID tab from the search query.
  3. Enter the MPN ID.
  4. Look for Program Codes under the Partner Information section.
  5. If Code 99 is missing from the list, send an email to partsup@microsoft.com to have them add Code 99 to the MPN ID.

Issues in the Account Portal

The customer tried to add a valid Partner ID and the error they receive is due to a problem in the Account portal. Before determining the error is due the portal, check for the following using the steps above:

  • The MPN ID is valid
  • The MPN ID has the right Competency
  • Program Code 99 has been added

Once these have been verified, collect the following data from the customer and open an IcM to the ASE team (see TSG 3093263):

  • A screenshot of the error: Include the whole screen showing the subscription details, login name, and the error.
  • A screenshot of the MPN portal showing the Partner ID is valid and has Silver or Gold Competency.
  • A network trace that captures the actions of your customer logging in to the Account portal. End the trace when the error message is received.

Note: For general MPN questions about the MPN ID, engage your TA. They will address your questions or direct you to the proper team for help.

Customer is unable to make the updates on their own

If the customer cannot make updates after troubleshooting with ASMS and needs ten or more subscriptions updated with a new DPOR, engage PayOps with the following information so they can make the bulk updates:

  • Subscription IDs
  • Corresponding tenant GUID
  • Current POR ID (if any)
  • New POR ID

Assist your customer if they need help with less than 10 subscriptions.

FAQs for Action Pack issues

  1. To successfully activate MAPS subscription, what are the conditions that must be met?
    • The account that is used for sign-up should be entitled for MPN benefits.
    • The subscription should not already be activated.
       
  2. To transfer the MPN Azure subscription from one account to another, what are the eligibility criteria?
    • The destination account must be entitled.
    • The corresponding SOT path should be allowed (See section Subscription Ownership Transfer (SOT) of MPN 25P only)
       
  3. You are working with a customer who is certain that they are entitled for MPN Action Pack Azure benefits. However, when trying to activate it, they get an error that “This offer is only available to Microsoft Partner Network members.” All we have is the partner ID and the customer is using an MSA for activation. What details do you need to proceed and what are the troubleshooting steps that you will perform?
    • Work with a TA for advice on how to proceed. Only a TA can check the entitlement status and the email address or PUID that is entitled for Azure benefits.
       
  4. The customer wants to activate the MPN under the ORG account and they have been migrated to the new Partner Center portal. What do you suggest?
    • Inform the customer to use their ORG ID to sign in to the Partner Center portal and use the ORG ID to complete the activation.
       
  5. The customer could not activate MPN Action Pack and the email the customer used was not entitled for MPN benefits. Further investigation shows the company does not have MPN benefits. What would be the next course of action?
    • Work with a TA, as they can engage the RSC team. They will get the required details for the customer and a contact from the MPN team if required.
       
  6. While transferring the MPN subscription from one account to another, does the destination account need to have benefits entitled?
    • Yes, so that the subscription does not get suspended when the BG runs an entitlement check.
    • In certain cases, we can perform the SOT if the destination does not have the entitlement. Work with your TA to assess the situation before performing the SOT.
       
  7. The customer is in the old MPN portal and has not been migrated to the new Partner Center portal. The MPN Action Pack Azure subscription is already active and they want to have it moved to another account. In this case it is an ORG account. What must be done to accomplish the transfer?
    • Work with a TA and they will advise accordingly.
    • For more information, see the Subscription Ownership Transfer (SOT) of MPN 25P section above.
       
  8. The customer has been migrated to the new portal and wants to transfer the existing Azure MPN subscription to a work account. How do we do this?
    • The new portal supports the addition of work accounts.
    • Once the customer adds the desired work account or if the account has already been added, the subscription can be transferred from CMAT.
       
  9. A customer who had an Azure MPN subscription under a work account has been migrated to the new Partner Center portal and wants to have it transferred to another work account from the same tenant. Can this be done and what is the process?
    • Yes. The benefits from the current work account can be reassigned so that the Global Admin can assign the benefits to the desired account
    • The subscription can then be transferred from CMAT.
