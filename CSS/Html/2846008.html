Who should use this article?

What is this article about?

This article covers several common billing errors and the contact handling process.

Notes:

  • The table shows two types of handling: One for when the customer is requesting assistance, and the other when the advocate is in purchase on behalf of (POBO).
  • Please use Ctrl+F keys and search for specific error code that customer is seeing.

Important: See KB 4578369 Procedure: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) Consent and KB 4053527 TUG: MST: Purchase on behalf of (POBO) for information on POBO and mandatory consent actions.

Error: PUR-CatalogProductDataNotFound

Description: The availability on the order has changed between the time the product was selected to the time that the user checks out.  Most frequently, this is because the content owner has changed the price.  There are other circumstances that may result in an availability ID change. The user probably has the store app opened for a while with stale information on the PDP and then decides to hit buy.

1. Check the local Microsoft.com/store page to verify if product is still showing in/out of stock (OOS).

2. If in stock, offer to POBO or have the customer try again.

3. If not in stock, inform customer that they received the error because the product they were attempting to buy went OOS just as they were trying to checkout.

Suggested messaging: “This rarely occurs, and we are sorry. Please keep checking back regularly and the website will indicate when the product is back in stock.”

1. Check the local Microsoft.com/store page to verify if product is still showing in/out of stock.

2. If in stock, retry.

3. If not in stock, inform customer that they received the error because the product they were attempting to buy went OOS just as they were trying to checkout.

4. Advise the customer to try later.
