1. Ad Extension overview

Ad extensions are additional pieces of information which advertisers can use to enhance their ads, improve customer engagement, and drive clicks. Ad Extensions can be associated at the account, campaign, or ad group level. For more information, see What are Ad Extensions.

In addition to manually created Ad Extensions, Microsoft Advertising can also provide Automated Extensions, (formerly called Ad Annotations) which are dynamically generated and automatically included with the ad when Bing predicts that it will improve the ad’s performance. These can only be opted-out at account level. To learn more, see Automated Ad Extensions.

Ads Lab Extensions note: Some of the extensions outlined below will be labelled as ‘Ads Lab’. Ads Lab extensions are only available to managed customers and should not be proactively discussed or promoted to SMB advertisers. We include these here for your awareness in case you are asked questions about the functionality by managed customers or search users who may have seen these extensions delivering.

1.4 Ads Lab FAQ

Q: How many extensions/associations can be shown in the Microsoft Advertising Web UI?

A: 1,000. Users with more than this limit will have to use bulk import/export.

Q: Will an Ads Lab Extension deliver 100% of the time when the ads deliver and have the extension set up?

A: No. All Ads Lab extensions serve on Mainline currently. We may also trim the coverage using certain thresholds to improve the user experience. Like with other ad extensions we have launched before, advertisers shouldn’t expect they will serve all the time.

Q: What position will ads with Ads Lab Extensions serve?

A: Only Mainline ads. We will revisit if we should serve on the bottom of page or sidebar (in markets still supporting this feature) when demand ramps up.

Q: Will an Ads Lab Extension serve with other extensions?

A: Yes, Ads Lab Extensions can serve in combination with any other extensions, regardless of whether they are from Ads Lab or not. There are some restrictions due to conflict of space or space limitations though.

Q: Are there restrictions on the number of campaigns that can have Ads Lab Extensions?

A: We support the association of multiple Callout Extensions per campaign/ad group/account. Other extensions are a 1:1 association.

Q: What’s the character limit?

A:

  • Callout Extension Text: 25 characters including spaces.
  • Review Extension: Text + Source is 67 characters including spaces.
  • Action Extension: Action Type is 16 characters including spaces. URL is 1024 characters.

Q: What markets are Ads Lab Extensions available in?

A: US with international release following.

Q: How long will editorial review take?

A: There is no editorial review now. We will take down bad extensions manually if needed. We will have editorial support before GA for all Ads Lab pilots.

Q: What action types are available for Action Extensions?

A: Advertisers are encouraged to use one from the recommended list.

Q: How do I sign into Ads Lab?

A: Using a connected Microsoft account.

Q: How can agencies work on connected accounts?

A: The agency needs to know the login of each account to log in. Select the Microsoft account login (usually primary user of the account which is a mail ID). Enter the Customer ID. Select the account.

Q: How can advertisers delete via bulk import/export?

A: For an extension, remove the entire row in the extension tab. For an association, remove the extension ID (last column) in the entity (for example, campaign) that you want to remove the association from. No change in other columns for that row is needed.

Q: Can I modify multiple Ads Lab extension types in one bulk import/export operation?

A: No, you cannot modify multiple Ads Lab extension types (for example, Review or Action) in one bulk import/export operation.

Q: Is reporting available and will clicks be tracked?

A: Yes, reporting will be just like other extensions including impressions, clicks, CTR, spend an average CPC. Initially, they will be available only in ad extension by ad and ad extension by keyword reports.

Q: If we opt into multiple ad extensions across all campaigns, would both extensions show? (I'm assuming that Image Extensions only show if you are in the top position, but Action Extensions might be able to show every time?)

A: Not necessarily. It depends on the quality of the ad and extension as well. The operating point will change as we optimize. General guidance to advertisers is that the extension is likely to serve on a high-quality ad with a high-quality extension at the top position.

Q: Would we have access to data that shows us what % of time those extensions show up, or would they appear every time our ad is served?

A: The Ad Extension by Keyword or Ad Extension by Ad reports have data on impressions and clicks of each extension type by keyword or ad. Advertisers can use that information to determine the serving %.

Q: Is there a maximum amount of campaigns you can opt into the pilot or can we test extensions across all campaigns?

A: They can test extensions across all their accounts/campaigns, in fact that is encouraged at this point. For Ads Lab extensions we have account-level association, which means advertisers associate an extension at the account level and then all campaigns under that account will be eligible to serve with the extension.

3. Affiliate Location Extensions (Ads Lab – EN-US only)

Affiliate location extensions will show a search user the locations of nearby store locations for the businesses they are viewing. They can also get directions to this location directly from within the ad. This makes it easier for customers to make an in-person purchase, but also helps drive CTR.

FAQ

Q: What if the product is available at multiple local retail locations?

A: Only the closest location will be shown.

Q: How do we ensure the product is in stock at the location?

A: We do not ensure products are in stock.

Q: What if other retail locations see this extension and wonder why they aren’t shown?

A: All retailers are not guaranteed to show. Could be lack of data in Bing Places (or other data sources) or distance.

Q: Which advertisers are eligible? Can others be added if they see the extensions and want to participate?

A: Any interested advertisers/manufacturer who sells products through retail chains. Currently reaching out to CPG advertisers (Consumer Product Segment). We may expand after holidays.

Q: What reporting is available?

A: Like other Ad Extensions in Ads lab, two reports will be available under Reports tab: Ad extension by keyword, Ad extension by Ad. They have all the metrics like clicks, impressions, CPC, and CTR.

4. Customer experience

4.1. Get A Ride Extension

Get A Ride is a feature of Location Extensions (LEX) that includes a clickable Get A Ride (car) icon in ads, giving search users a clear call to action and simple one-click way to initiate a call for a ride (via Uber) to an advertiser’s business location.

Advertiser eligibility

US advertisers with LEX enabled are eligible. If an advertiser does not want this feature to serve as a part of Location Extensions, they may contact support or their AM and request to opt out. Advocates/AMs may use the annotations opt out process. This user experience (UX) will serve for users with iPhone in the US. Future Android availability is TBA. Windows Phone availability is not currently on the roadmap.

Example: When the user selects the Get A Ride car icon, the Uber UX is triggered.

UX scenarios:

If the user is logged into an Uber mobile account:

Selecting Get A Ride initiates the Uber UX with locations pre-populated.

If the user has, but is NOT logged into an Uber mobile account:

Selecting Get A Ride takes the user to the uber sign in/register page. Upon signing in, they will need to enter the destination location.

If the user does not have Uber installed:

Selecting Get A Ride directs the user to the Uber website, m.Uber.com, for the next steps. Once they have installed Uber and registered, they will enter the destination location.

To learn more about adding a Get A Ride icon to an ad, see Add a Get a Ride icon to your ad.

5. Location Extensions

The Location Extensions feature in Microsoft Advertising allows advertisers to add their business addresses and phone numbers to their paid search ads. This feature makes ads more relevant by providing the address and phone number for searchers within a specified range of the business location.

Things to know about Location Extensions:

  • Markets: All, except China. (Note: Although Belgian advertisers can create these extensions, due to in-market restrictions location extensions cannot be targeted to Belgium and will not be displayed on Bing for this market.)
  • Business addresses can be displayed without a phone number, but phone numbers cannot be displayed on their own.
  • They will be displayed on Bing and Yahoo but not on the syndication network.
  • Advertisers can import Location Extensions from Google AdWords.
  • Advertisers can update all business locations with the same phone number to show the same number when the business address is displayed in the text ad.
  • Currently, bulk support is available only through the Campaign user interface and API.
  • Phone numbers should be entered in the format typical for each country in which the ad will be delivered.
  • Advertisers can add Location Extensions at the account level and then select different extensions for different campaigns. Premium rate and charge-free phone number (0800) will not be accepted as phone extension.
  • Google Location Extensions offer the ability to opt in individual ads for Location Extensions. Microsoft Advertising does not support this.
  • The directions link takes searchers to a map of the business address.
  • Advertisers can now track performance at the extension level within the Ad Extensions tab and ad extension reports.

Return to top

5.1 Promoting and enabling Location Extensions

Location Extensions should be used by advertisers with Brick & Mortar (physical store) locations. Relevant verticals include retail, beauty and fitness, and home improvement. To promote location extensions to advertisers, use a one sheet.

Benefits of Location Extensions

Displaying your business address and phone number helps the searcher to learn more about your business with a single search. It also shows that the business is local, which encourages the user to click through to your business’ website, driving traffic and leads to your website or store.

  • Enabling the Location Extensions feature for search ads may help increase traffic and leads, both in-store and online, by making ads more actionable.
  • A higher click-through rate can drive more visitors to a website and business locations.
  • There is no cost to enable this feature; advertisers will only pay for the clicks their ads receive.

Known issue with the business image: The functionality is currently available to upload the image, but the image will not currently display. There is no ETA or workaround available for the moment.

Enabling advertisers with multiple business locations

  • If there are multiple locations, Location Extensions will show the nearest business location within 50 miles (80 kilometers) or the value selected for Radius Targeting.
  • Location Extensions account limits.

Radius for location display

The radius for the location display is 50 miles (or 80 kilometers). If the advertiser does not have a business location entered that is within 50 miles (80 kilometers) of the customer, no address will be shown in the ad.

If there are multiple locations, Location Extensions will show the nearest business location within 50 miles (80 kilometers) or the value selected for Radius Targeting. Note: You do not need to use Radius Targeting to use the Location Extensions feature.

Bing uses multiple signals to determine location—for example, latitude/longitude from mobile, information provided by users or reverse IP. Location is 50 miles (80 kilometers).

Read more in the Microsoft Advertising blog.

API information

With the release of Location Extensions Version 2, Location Extensions Version 1 will no longer work. API users need to update their applications for Location Extensions Version 2.

5.4 GMB/Bing Places Import FAQ

Q: Why aren’t my locations showing? I imported from GMB/Bing Places successfully!

A: Make sure you’ve manually associated your locations after importing, as a one-time setup. The steps to do that can be found at Import Business Location Data.

Q: What happens if I delete a location in GMB/Bing Places?

A: Once deleted, that location will be deleted in Microsoft Advertising as well. If the location is associated at the account, campaign, or ad group level, it will no longer serve with the ads.

Q: How do I use the filter options?

A: The filters can be used to specify which locations you want to import from GMB or Bing Places using the business name, label, and category fields that exist in GMB/Bing Places.   

Q: What happens to manual Location Extensions already in my account?

A: These are kept in your account; however, for ease of management we recommend deleting all manual location extensions when using GMB/Bing Places importing, to avoid duplication and confusion.

6. Call Extensions

6.1 Overview

With Call Extensions, advertisers can display a phone number directly in their search ad on PC, tablet, or smartphone, and get calls from customers who either select or dial the number in the ad.

With Call Extensions, advertisers have two options: They can use their own phone number or use a Microsoft Advertising forwarding phone number.

When using a forwarding number, Microsoft Advertising assigns a unique phone number that displays in the ad. When a customer calls this number, the call is routed to the advertiser’s business phone number. The Microsoft Advertising forwarding number allows them to track all calls from the ad, no matter where the call was made from, so that they can analyze the ad performance. All advertisers will default to using a local area-code number when they opt into using Microsoft Advertising forwarding number.

Ability to set up in: All Bing markets.

Serves in: Available in all Bing markets on smartphones only, except for Belgium and China. Available on PC devices for Bing, AOL, and Yahoo syndicated search partner sites only.

6.2 Call Extensions FAQ

Q: What is happening with Call Extensions on PC traffic? Why are Call Extensions not serving on PC traffic?

A: Call Extensions are serving on smartphones only, except for Belgium and China. Available on PC devices for Bing, AOL, and Yahoo syndicated search partner sites only.

Q: What calls can be tracked through reporting?

A: When you choose Call Extensions on all devices you use a Microsoft Advertising forwarding number for your ad and have access to reports that provide insights, such as call duration and missed calls, on all calls that your ads have generated.

  • With the Call Details report, you can track details such as call start/end time, missed/completed calls, call duration and call type.
  • With Account, Campaign and Ad Group performance reports, you can track metrics such as phone impression count, call count, phone-through rate and phone spend.
  • With the Ad Extension by Ad and Ad Extension by Keyword reports, you can track details such as click type, impressions and clicks.

When you choose Call Extensions on all devices using your own phone number, you display your own phone number in the ad. These numbers on a smartphone are clickable, and a click on the phone number results in a call and is tracked as a click in performance reports. Performance reporting is available at the account, campaign, ad group, and extension level. We also provide click type information (phone call vs. ad title) at the extension level. The numbers on a PC/tablet ad are not clickable.

Q: Does bidding higher increase the chances that the Call Extension will serve more (as with other ad extensions)?

A: Higher bids do improve the chances of ads getting impressions.

Q: If an advertiser has Call Extensions enabled, will it serve with every impression of that ad?

A: It is possible that publishers may have their own user experience treatment flights, in which case (minority scenarios) ads may show up without a call extension. This can happen only for a fraction of all query volume. Note that the phone extension will be displayed more frequently on first and second position. Attempt to get the average position in this range to ensure the best delivery.

Q: Does this feature only work when device targeting is set to smartphone?

A: Call Extensions and Location Extensions can be shown with ads for all campaigns. However, if you are creating a Call Extension intended for smartphones only, make sure that the device targeting settings for your campaign includes smartphone devices.

Q: With Skype forwarding numbers, what's the difference between toll-free and local phone numbers?

A: There is no difference in terms of functionality. These two options are provided to give advertisers flexibility in choosing number that is most suitable for their campaign scenarios.

Q: If you are advertising for numerous different locations and can only set up one Call Extension per campaign, what are some best practices?

A: It is worth noting that it is not guaranteed that the same area code local number will be assigned to phone numbers operating across multiple locations.

Q: I'm running a forwarding number campaign and the budget ran out. Will it redirect to the actual phone number?

A: When the campaign is paused, we also temporarily stop routing calls for those numbers. This will block most calls you would normally receive from the forwarding number. However, the number will remain available in the campaign for another month, so if a campaign gets re-enabled, ads will start serving with the same forwarding number and calls will be routed to your business.

Q: Why can't I get Call Extensions to show the Microsoft Advertising forwarding number? (See Add a phone number to your ad with Call Extensions for more information.)

A: To get a Microsoft Advertising forwarding number, an ad group within a campaign using Call Extensions must have at least five clicks and $5.00 in spend for the last month's billing cycle. Until this criteria is met (including for new campaigns and ad groups), the advertiser will still be able to select the forwarding number options, but the ad groups will deliver with the advertiser's phone number only on smartphone devices and without any phone number on desktop and tablet devices. As soon as an ad group becomes eligible, it automatically gets assigned the forwarding number for that campaign and ads will start serving with a forwarding number.

Q: What happens if an advertiser has a local valid number and requests a local number?

A: The system tries to assign a number from the same area code as the advertiser’s phone number, but the number may not be available. In that case, another area code may be assigned.

Q: Why don't I see my Call Extension calls in the Call Forwarding report?

A: We can only track calls made through a forwarding number.

Q: So why did my Call Extension deliver on PC?

A: We are flighting Call Extensions on PC, so some delivery is possible.

Q: Why doesn't my Call Extension look like the help page screenshots?

A: We are constantly flighting different UX and cannot guarantee appearance at this time.

Q: Why do I see calls without impressions?

A: If someone saves the forwarding number and calls back later, there will be a call in the Call Forwarding report without an impression.

Q: Can I get conversion tracking for calls?

A: We don't currently support that directly. Advertisers can use offline conversions and third-party solutions as a workaround:

  • Tracking offline conversions
  • Infinity and Bing Ads uncover the true impact of search campaigns
  • Call Tracking Metrics
  • Access online and offline conversion data in Microsoft Ads

Q: Can I set up a call-only ad?

A: Not fully - Targeting only mobile and setting the Call Extension to "Show just my phone number" will result in a call-only ad most of the time but it will deliver as a regular ad if the extension doesn't deliver.

7. Sitelink Extensions

Sitelink Extensions are set at the campaign or ad group level. The ad group extensions override the campaign extensions. Sitelink Extensions are only displayed for ads in the mainline position.

Things to know about Sitelink Extensions

  • Markets: All
  • Sitelink extensions only show in the mainline.
  • Google has a similar feature called Sitelinks.
  • Sitelink extensions are available on Bing, Yahoo, and partner sites.
  • Sitelink extensions are more likely to display if the campaign has performance history.
  • Sitelink extensions should be added in order of importance. For example, Sitelink extensions entered first will be more likely to show first on Bing. If the advertiser wants to change the order on Bing, they should rearrange the list in order of importance within the UI.
    • Note: Although this is a recommended best practice, Bing may still arrange Sitelinks in a different order based on factors such as query, ad, or user context. There is no guarantee what order the Sitelinks will display, and we should not escalate because of Sitelinks that don’t display in the customer’s intended order.
  • Advertisers can associate up to 20 sitelink extensions. The amount displayed on Bing will vary based on device type.
  • Microsoft Advertising can serve Location Extensions and Sitelink Extensions together so there will be instances when either or both are displayed for any given ad.
  • To use Sitelink Extensions, the feature must be enabled with at least one deep link.
  • Sitelink extensions can be set at the campaign or ad group level.
  • Unique URLs are not required to use Sitelink Extensions.
  • Sitelink Extensions landing pages have the same requirements and are monitored for quality like any other landing page. Therefore, ‘Approved Limited or Disapproved’ statuses must be handled according to existing editorial appeals processes, see KB 3169212 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Editorial Process for more information.
  • Only the first click on an ad with Sitelink Extensions will be billed. Subsequent clicks are recorded but not billed.
  • There are no additional fees or charges for Sitelink Extensions.
  • Microsoft Advertising provides direct import of Sitelink Extensions from AdWords to Microsoft Advertising via the Ad Extensions tab and via Microsoft Advertising Editor.
  • Available in all markets.

7.3 Enhanced sitelinks

  • Bing: Live in all markets
  • Yahoo: Live in all markets

Enhanced Sitelinks allow advertisers to add two lines (70 characters total) of text below each sitelink; plus, when the sitelinks serve, the font size of the links automatically increase. Enhanced Sitelinks allow advertisers to create richer, more relevant ads, that take up even more real estate on the search results page.

Things to know about Enhanced Sitelinks:

  • The two lines of text are limited to 70 characters total.
  • Both lines of text must be used for each sitelink for Enhanced Sitelinks to serve. An error message will be shown in Microsoft Advertising when you enter the descriptive text if both lines are not used.
  • There is a 25-character limit for the sitelink itself (the headline text) when using Enhanced Sitelinks. This is because of the larger font size. If the sitelink exceeds 25 characters, an error message will appear in Microsoft Advertising and the sitelink will be rejected.
  • Enhanced Sitelinks are shown in the mainline one ad position when the bid and ad quality are very high, which is reflected by performance measures such as a high click-through rate.
  • Enhanced Sitelinks are enabled at the campaign level.
  • Up to 10 Enhanced Sitelinks can be created per ad. However, a minimum of two and a maximum of four will display.
  • Descriptive text must adhere to our Microsoft Advertising policies.

Messaging:

  • "By adding additional links to your website, you can give your potential customers a broader glimpse of what your business offers. For example, you can display a link to a menu, an order page or photo gallery right in your text ad. This gives visitors to your website more direct access to your site content."
  • "To take advantage of this feature, you should optimize your ads for the mainline, which is the only ad position in which Sitelink Extensions display."

7.4 Sitelinks with {PARAM1} as a sitelink URL

Dynamic URL parameters may be used for more precise tracking of clicks, which adds more granularity to your Sitelink Extensions performance monitoring. Sitelink Extensions can have {param1} as a Sitelink URL. {param1} applies to the advertiser-defined click-through URL of an ad extension. Within the current Microsoft Advertising offering, Sitelink Extensions are the only product that offers such URLs. The copy parameter ({param1}) can be used against the following three fields in order of logic:

  1. Keyword Destination URL (this will be the first place Microsoft Advertising checks for a URL).
  2. Param1 (if a URL does not exist in the Keyword Destination URL, Microsoft Advertising will check for a URL here).
  3. Ad Destination URL (if URLs don’t exist in Keyword Destination URL or Param 1, Microsoft Advertising will check for a URL here).

Copy param cannot be used with param2 or param3. If a copy parameter refers to a URL parameter that is not present in the keyword destination URL, Param 1 or Ad Destination URL, the copy parameter should result in specifying an empty parameter.

9.3 App Extensions FAQ

Q: What devices and app stores are supported?

A:

  • Devices include smartphones and tablets. PCs are TBA.
  • Operating systems include iOS, Android, Windows 8, Windows 8.1, and Windows Phone.
  • App stores include iTunes, Google Play, Windows Phone stores, and Windows.

Q: Are these ads different from app install ads?

A: Yes, these ads include a link to the advertiser’s website in addition to an app store where users can install the app onto their device.

Q: What position will ads with App Extensions serve?

A: Mainline, bottom of page and sidebar (in markets still supporting this feature).

Q: Are these ads for mobile only by default? Will there be a way to target just mobile?

A:

  • Targeting is based on the app provided. If a customer is searching on their iPhone, only apps that fit that requirement, meaning only mobile and Apple apps, will serve.
  • It won’t be mobile only. We support tablet apps on iOS.
  • No additional settings are required to target.

Q: Is reporting available and will clicks be tracked?

A: Yes, reporting will be just like other extensions including impressions, clicks, CTR, spend, and average CPC. Third-party tracking is also available for install tracking.

Q: Can App Extensions run with other extensions?

A: Yes.

Q: Are there restrictions on the number of campaigns that can have App Extensions?

A: No, but only one App Extension will show at a time.

Q: How can I promote my app to users on mobile devices after the Unified Device Targeting migration is complete?

A:

  • Advertisers can use App Extensions to promote their apps to users on mobile devices. App Extensions will automatically detect the searcher’s device and OS and serve the extension that will take searchers directly to the advertiser’s page within the relevant app store.
  • Advertisers who are currently using Sitelinks to promote their apps are encouraged to switch to App Extensions moving forward. Because Sitelink Extensions are designed to point searchers to web locations, not app stores, Sitelinks may direct searchers to the wrong app store.

Q: What information does the advertiser need to get started with App Extensions?

A: OS, App ID/Package Name, Link text, and Link URL.

Q: Where can advertisers find their App ID?

A: Advertisers can go to their app store, select their app, and copy the URL.
http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/example-app-name/id#########
http://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.example.appname
http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/store/app/example-app-name/id#############
http://apps.microsoft.com/windows/en-us/app/example-app-name/id#############

Q: What types of URLs may be used with App Extensions?

A: Advertisers may use the app store URL (for example, http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/example-app-name/id#########) or a redirect URL to bring the user to the store. A redirect URL is used for third-party tracking purposes to track app installs.

Q: Are multiple App Extensions supported?

A: Only one App Extension can be displayed per text ad (unlike Sitelink Extensions, with which you can add multiple links to different pages of your site). You can still create multiple App Extensions, one for each app or each app store.

Q: What vendors can be used for third-party tracking?

A: To find a list of partners, see the Create measurement/tracking URL for app install conversion section of How to track mobile app installs as conversions.

Q: Will deep links be supported?

A: Not currently.

Q: What’s the character limit?

A: 35 characters.

Q: How long will editorial review take?

A: Typically review will be within 24 hours, but it can take up to 48 hours.

Q: What markets is this available in?

A: US, followed by an international release.

Q: What are the specs for the icon image?

A: Icons are automatically included from the app store.

Q: Is it possible for my App Extensions to serve on the wrong device? For example, could an iPhone be served an extension that drives customers to an Android download page?

A: No. App Extensions will not serve on the wrong device because there is automatic targeting by device type.

Q: How are App URLs validated?

A: There are three different checks implemented:

  1. Upfront block: The App ID and ID in the URL are different, or the user is entering a random string of characters.
  2. Fast check: These are checks that usually take up to an hour. It happens when the App ID is valid, but the user is using a redirect or link. The delivery status will be pending editorial review during the check or URL invalid if the validation fails.
  3. Offline periodic check: These jobs run on the back end to check the URL to see if it is still valid because it can be invalid over time for various reasons (for example, application deleted from app store or redirect URL changed destination).

Q: When should I use App Extensions?

A: Advertisers should use App Extensions to promote their apps available in iTunes, Google Play, and Windows Store. App Extensions is designed to detect the searcher’s device and OS and to take searchers to the correct app store. With App Extensions, iOS users are taken to iTunes, Android users to Google Play, and Windows Phone users to the Windows Phone Store.

Q: What’s the difference between using App Extensions and including a Sitelink Extension to an app store?

A:

  • App Extensions are designed to automatically detect the searcher’s device and OS and serve the extension that will take searchers directly to the advertiser’s page within the right app store.
  • Unlike App Extensions, Sitelink Extensions do not automatically target by device. Sitelinks (and main destination links) work best when they point to web locations, not app stores. If Sitelinks point to app stores, they may send users to the wrong app store.
  • Advertisers are encouraged to promote their apps using App Extensions.

Q: What happens if a user searches from a computer, can we set a preferred app store to display?

A: This is not currently available as multiple App Extensions will show up. For example, if an advertiser has apps across all three stores, all three apps will be shown in their ad. On mobile, the ad will only show the App Extension for the given store that matches the user's device platform.

Q: What are the stipulations for showing App Extensions?

A: For mobile, we do imbedded device targeting for App Extensions (App Extensions will match platform of the user to show up). For example, iOS App Extensions will show up on iOS devices and Google Play on Android devices.

Q: Will clicks and conversions be logged at the keyword and search-term level or just ad group level?

A: This will behave the same as all other extensions.

Q: My client is asking if we can add a new third-party app tracking partner. Who can I contact to learn more about the accreditation process?

A: Jason Schauer jaschaue@microsoft.com.

Q: Will star ratings/metadata show in the App Extensions?

A: This is not a feature currently offered.

Q: Are we able to associate at the account level?

A: Currently we only support at the campaign/ad group level. Account level support is in our backlog and will be targeted in future releases.

12.1 Requirements for Review Extensions

Review text will be subject to all editorial policy checks in addition to the additional requirements:

  • Review source must be from credible, third-party sources. The advertiser is responsible for ensuring that they have permissions to use the reviews in their ads (if permissions are necessary).

We prohibit the following review sources:

  • Second-hand reporting (rather than directly linking to source).
  • Individual customer reviews and testimonials.
  • Reviews from aggregate reviews and rankings from user review websites (however, you can show user review ratings with your ads by using merchant ratings).
  • Press releases created by the advertiser, no matter who distributes/republishes it.
  • Paid endorsements.
  • Websites not accessible to the public (like sites that require signing up or signing in to see the review content).
  • Websites that are not appropriate.
  • Reviews requiring extra clicks to view (such as in videos, audio, and PDFs).

Review text is accurate and current:

  • The review's content should be visible in the text on the source page.
  • The tone and content of the review must match the original source.
  • Reviews that mention periodic awards should also include the time period of the award.
  • Ellipses (...) should be used to show missing words or phrases in the middle of the review.
  • Reviews must reflect the overall content of the source page.
  • Reviews should not be more than 12 months old.

Things to know about review extensions

  • Reviews must come from a reputable third-party source (advertiser provides the URL for the source).
  • Review Extensions appear only for high-quality ads with a relevant review.
  • Reviews can be either paraphrased or an exact quote from the source.
  • Review Extensions can only be associated to the campaign or ad group level. Each campaign or ad group can have one Review Extension associated with it.
  • Review Extensions should be distinct from the ad description.
  • The review text should not include the name of the advertiser's business.
  • Advertisers must have permission to use the third-party review in their ad.
  • Advertisers should not use personal reviews from customers. Such reviews will be rejected.
  • Review Extensions can appear with other ad extensions.
  • Review Extensions can be imported from AdWords into Microsoft Advertising.
  • Review Extensions can appear for mainline, bottom of page and sidebar ads (in markets still supporting this feature).
  • Review Extensions have character limits:
    • Review Text + Review Source limit: 67 characters
    • Review URL limit: 255 characters (includes http:// or https://)

13. Price Extensions

Price Extensions display an advertiser's products or services, along with their corresponding prices, to search customers.

They are available to all markets except China, Taiwan, and Hong Kong.

Price Extensions allow searchers to see exactly what they’re looking for when they’re looking for it. This helps to increase clicks, drive potential customers straight to converting experiences, and increase advertiser’s visual share in mobile results to improve their chances of raising their click-through rate.

Notes:

  • Price Extensions can be created or edited at the account, campaign, or ad group level.
  • All Price Extensions must adhere to the Price extension policies.
  • Each Price Extension must have a minimum of three items and a maximum of eight items (header, description, price qualifier, price, unit, final URL, or mobile final URL).
  • Up to 20 price extensions can be associated to a single campaign/ad group.
  • All price extensions are manually reviewed and will be disapproved if found to exceed 20% of the price found on the landing page, to prevent misleading ads.
  • Google Import is supported.

For setup/management guidance, see Add price extensions to your ad for more information.

14. Automated Extensions (formerly known as Ad Annotations)

Automated extension data is taken from a variety of sources, including: Ad copy, copy on the advertiser’s landing page, domains that ad copy points to, or various internal or external data sources. Certain data for reviews and ratings or social annotations may come from third-party providers/sites.

All advertisers are eligible for the full range of Automated Extensions available in their markets, unless they opt out.

An Automated Extension, or combination of Automated Extensions, may show with an ad if Bing determines that the extension can improve ad performance. Extensions are not guaranteed to serve as there are dynamic marketplace factors that determine when, and which, extensions may be served.

All clickable Automated Extensions will be charged, using standard bidding logic.

Advertisers will be able to view the success of Automated Extensions via a reporting grid in the Ad Extensions sub-tab, on the Campaigns tab.

Note: Microsoft Advertising continually tests new extensions, and variations of existing ones, with the goal to provide a better experience for both search users and advertisers. From time to time, advertisers may notice design or placement variations for existing extensions, or new extensions that Bing is testing (for example, flighting).

To learn more about specific Automated Extensions, check out Enhance ads with Automated Extensions.

Incorrect data updates

There are some Automated Extensions where Bing may auto-populate information found on the advertiser's landing page or other data sources. If an advertiser notices that the information isn't accurate, they can escalate the issue to their Account Manager or a CSS advocate. Advocates/AMs: Fill out the request template based on the advertiser's information.

Opting out

Advertisers can opt out of any of the Automated Extension groups from within the Microsoft Advertising UI.

Opt-out is done at the account level and opting out of a particular group will opt the customer out of all automated extensions included within that group.

Bulk opt-outs

Currently, only Microsoft Advertising Engineering can implement bulk opt-outs across multiple accounts.

If you are contacted by large advertisers who wish to opt out of Automated Extensions in bulk across many accounts, escalate to Global Support and ask them to contact Engineering to help implement the customer’s request. If possible, obtain the customer’s business justification so that we can share this with the engineering team for insight.

Global support may need to work with you to collect the account ID list required for opt-out. They will provide any guidance within the escalation.

 16. Video Extensions (Managed Pilot – DE, UK, and US only)

Video Extensions allow advertisers to feature videos directly in their search ads. These videos can provide detailed information about their business, including the products and services they offer. Some examples of videos include video commercials, brand imaging, and product and service demonstrations.

Featuring videos in search ads can lead to increased engagement, click-through rates, and conversions. You can also use video extensions to promote your services, demonstrate your products, highlight reviews, or build your brand image.

For PC only, advertisers can also add Action Text (such as, ‘Learn More’ as a clickable button) to act as a call to action and encourage clicks.

Things to know:

  • Mainline ads only.
  • Bing PC/Mobile traffic.
  • Can serve in addition to other Ad Extensions.
  • Videos must be uploaded – cannot use links such as, YouTube or Facebook.
  • Cost per click on a video is the same as cost per click on the ad.
  • Clicks on Action Text will not be charged as a separate click.
  • Videos must comply with our Image, audio, and video policies.
  • Videos must be 15-60 seconds. Videos outside this range will not be accepted.
  • wmv, avi, mp4 and mpeg formats are allowed.
  • Resolution: 16:9, with 1080p (1920x1080) as maximum resolution.
  • 200MB limit.

Creating Video Extensions

Refer the customer to their Account Manager for help and support with Video Extensions.

For more information on video extensions go to Microsoft Advertising pilot program policies.
