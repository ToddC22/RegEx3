What is this article about?

This article provides guidelines for determining if the customer is eligible to receive a refund for gaming related digital product purchases on their account. The listed rules are specific to gaming products only, so they may differ from the rules applied to non-gaming related products.

Non-gaming related products also includes:

  • Movie
  • TV show
  • TV season

1. Overview and additional information

All sales are considered final, but Microsoft considers extenuating circumstances for same cases.

The customer’s purchase is eligible for a refund if it meets the criteria for that specified product type, based on factors like time since purchase or release and use of product, or if it meets one of the special scenarios detailed in this article.

  • Once eligibility is determined, further checks are not required.

1.2 Definitions of refund criteria

  • Time (Non-preorder): The elapsed time between date/time of purchase and date/time of refund request. Make sure that times are in the same unit (time zone).
  • Time (Preorder, post launch): The elapsed time between date/time of launch and date/time of refund request.
  • Usage: Usage of product type, either implied from time of ownership, launch status, or actual game play metrics from purchase to case review date.
  • Frequency: Limit is determined by product type and may reduce if Final Criteria is used. Time period is a one-year period by date from case review date.
  • Example: 1/31/2019-1/31/2020

1.3 Determining if an account is within limits

To calculate if the customer can receive a refund, determine how many products and the total dollar amount that has been refunded on an account in a one-year period by date from case review date (for example, 1/31/2019-1/31/2020) and the current number of products and total dollar amount being requested.

  1. Go to Order history and select view all orders.
  2. Change the start date to one year ago from refund review date.
  3. Change Status to all refunded.
  4. Determine the refunds received by counting any products (order line items) refunded for the following product types:
  • AddOn
  • Application
  • Bundles
  • Consumable
  • CSV
  • DigitalDownload
  • Durable
  • FeeServiceCharge
  • Game
  • GiftCard
  • InAppSubscription (shows as Durable in entitlements)
  • Movie
  • TvEpisode
  • TvSeason
  • TvSeries
  • Unknown (Avatar, gamertag in entitlements)
  • UnmanagedConsumable

Exclude the following:

  • Subscriptions (PASS)
  • Xbox Live Rewards Credits
  • Miscellaneous Credits (CSV)
  • Physical Product (PhysicalGoods)
  • Pre-orders cancelled and refunded prior to the general availability date (section 3.3) for any of the included product types.

Note: To see if the pre-order was cancelled before the release, open PDP and look at the release date for the version that was refunded.

[Asset 4521838]

  1. Add up the currency amounts for the orders. If the purchase is in non-USD currency, convert the currency to USD before determining eligibility. The currency value is based on the total purchase price including tax.

[Asset 4521839]

  1. Based on the refund eligibility criteria used, decide if refund limits have not been exceeded.

Example:

  • The customer has a refund for a $60 game and a $40 game in their history ($100 and two).
  • The customer is requesting a $55 game today.
  • For check 1- 3 eligibility - limit is $250 and six ($150 and four remains).
    • The customer receives refund of $55.
  • For check 4 eligibility - limit is $125 and three ($25 and one remains).
    • The customer not eligible for a refund of $55.

2.1 Games/Games with add-on

Scenario

When the customer requests a refund for a game or a game with an add-on (for example, game plus durable, or game plus consumable) purchased on the customer’s account or for a child in the family.

Not included: Games purchased as a gift. See section 2.11 Digital Game Gift. A gifted game will not show in entitlements and order details will show as Giftee Email/Gamer Tag.

[Asset 4550039]

 

Note

Product is usually able to be revoked:

360 and 360 back compatible games are not.

Note: For Ask a Parent purchases, look up the purchased for account via Family Services to determine eligibility for frequency and criteria.

[Asset 4550158]

 

Frequency

Limited. Customers are only eligible for this exception if refunds are within a one-year period by date from the case first review date, and the current requested product on the account meets both criteria:

  • Total refund amount is less than or equal to USD $250 or local currency equivalent.
  • Total refunded orders are less than or equal to six orders.

Check 1 Criteria

Time: Less than or equal to two hours from request date.

Check 2 Criteria
  • Time: Less than or equal to 14 days from request date.
  • Usage, meets one of the following:
    • Game is not launched, does not show in-game activity date.
    • Game last played is before the purchase date.
    • Game being refunded is a lower version of an item the customer has since purchased (for example, refund for Standard, has purchased Ultimate).

Check 3 Criteria
  • If game play and achievements both exist for an item, achievements take priority.
  • Use Order date/time under events tab where it shows in UTC to match achievement times in UTC.
  • If game play and achievements are not available or only game play is available and exceeds the limit, go to final check.
  • Time: Less than or equal to 14 days from request date.
  • Usage since game purchase to case review:
    • Game play is less than or equal to two hours.
    • Gamer Score awarded for achievements is less than or equal to 30 - Process refund.
    • Gamer Score awarded for achievements is more than 30 - Deny refund.

FINAL Criteria
  • Time: Less than or equal to five days from request date.
  • Usage: No usage data available or exceeds two hours game play with no achievement data.
  • Reduce limits above:
    • Total refund amount is less than or equal to USD $125 or local currency equivalent.
    • Total refunded orders are less than or equal to three orders.

Process

In ASD, use Return Line Item to complete the return/refund.

In MARS, select Approve.

2.3 Clawback consumables

Scenario

When the customer requests a refund for consumables that can be clawed back.

Note

Clawback consumables = Content can be revoked by the first or third-party product.

Current clawback consumables.

  • Fortnite

Frequency

Limited. Customers are only eligible for this exception if refunds are within a one-year period by date from the case first review date, and the current requested product on the account meets both criteria:

  • Total refund amount is less than or equal to USD $250 or local currency equivalent.
  • Total amount of the orders is less than or equal to six orders.

Check 1 Criteria

Time: Less than or equal to two hours from request date.

Check 2 Criteria
  •  If game play and achievements both exist for an item, achievements take priority.
  • Use Order date/time under events tab where it shows in UTC to match achievement times in UTC.
  • If game play and achievements are not available or only game play is available and exceeds the limit, go to final check.
  • Time: Less than or equal to 14 days from the request date.
  • Usage since consumable purchase to case review:
    • Customers reporting they did not get the consumable content which they had paid for, follow the steps outlined in KB 2969415 Informational: Understanding Game Transaction Types to confirm that the last modified date matches the purchase date, which would mean they have received the in game currency. If the last modified date matches the purchase date, the customer is not eligible for a refund. But if the modified date does not match the purchase date, then you can refund the purchase and should escalate to your lead so it can be investigated.

Note: If it has not been 24 hours since purchase, advise the customer to wait.

  • Game play is less than or equal to two hours, usually only applicable if game and consumable was bought at the same time.
  • Gamer score awarded for achievements is less than or equal to 30 - Process refund.
  • Gamer score awarded for achievements is more than 30 - Deny refund. 

FINAL Criteria
  • Time: Less than or equal to five days since purchase from request date.
  • Usage: No usage data available or exceeds two hours game play with no achievement data.

Reduce limits above:

  • Total refund amount is less than or equal to USD $125 or local currency equivalent.
  • Total refunded orders are less than or equal to three orders.

Process

In ASD, use Return Line Item to complete the return/refund.

Note: Return line item must be used to start the clawback process.

In MARS, select Approve.

If the last modified date matches the purchase date, the customer is not eligible for a refund. If the modified date does not match the purchase date, then you can refund the purchase and should escalate to your lead so it can be investigated.

Note: If it has not been 24 hours since purchase, advise the customer to wait.

  • Gamer score awarded for achievements is less than or equal to 30 - Process refund.

3.1 Regional exceptions

Australia and New Zealand

Consumers have the right to repair, replace, or refund in certain situations. Refer to https://www.microsoft.com/en-au/legalau/australian-consumer-law/. Where a consumer has that right, you must follow the ACL policy.

Note: This policy applies to both physical and digital goods. It allows refunds for quality issues and does not cover “change of mind” or “accidental purchase” scenarios.

3.2 Content does not match description in Microsoft Store

Scenario

Fact-based elements of the description are incorrect; opinion-based elements are not in scope for this exception. This exception does not include pricing errors.

Examples of facts:

  • Rental period is stated as 48 hours but expired in 24 hours.
  • Game is listed as having ten levels for free; it only includes one or only allows the customer to play one without requiring DLC or additional in-game purchase (requiring people to beat a level to get to the next does not qualify).
  • Episode three of <title> was purchased, but when they try to play it, episode two of <different title> plays.

Refund criteria

The customer describes this scenario, and you can confirm the discrepancy.

Additional note
  • Issues with content on non-Microsoft published apps or games may need to be referred to the publisher if there is a technical issue.
  • Issues caused by to the user’s environment (such as network issues) are not eligible for refunds, but we should provide best efforts to resolve technical issues.

Process

In ASD, use Return Line Item to complete the return/refund.

In MARS, select Approve.

Frequency

As it occurs. Once per title.

Refund Requested within 14 days.

3.3 Digital pre-order cancellation

Scenario

Important: If the customer is requesting a refund after the pre-order launches, follow guidelines for the content type in section 2, this includes early access granted as part of the pre-order.

3.4 Incorrect price was charged

Scenario

The customer was not charged the price listed in the marketplace or a promotional price was not applied successfully. This includes Games with Gold or Game PASS charge during the applicable time frame.

Refund criteria (all must apply)
  • The customer purchased content at regular price or within the promotional period listed in the promotions calendar for the region in which they reside.
  • Corresponding transaction listing in MST confirms the customer was charged a higher price.

Advocate Process

For price discrepancies, including active or expired promotions:

  1. Process the charges using the Refund Line Item option in ASD or select Approve in MARS.
  2. Contact your supervisor to report the issue to Microsoft via CSS Command Center (CSS CC).

Frequency

As it occurs. Refund Request within 90 days.

5. Refund review

5.1 Checking SR history for fraud/phishing

  • If phishing or fraud is suspected, report it via the MST Report Fraud or Phishing button (See KB 4053535 TUG: MST: Token Lookup tool, section 6) and provide relevant details.
    • Report phishing if there are four or more SRs for the same order item with different purchase dates and different refund reasons.
    • Report fraud on any attempts with suspicious verbiage or usage.
    • Report if the account is newly created, six months or less, with no play history on the account.
    • Report if the customer says that they gave their password to a friend (breaking Microsoft Terms of Use) and the friend purchased some games.
