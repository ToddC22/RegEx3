Scenario
 

  • A customer needs assistance entering, editing or viewing a VAT number on their account during or after sign-up.
  • A customer may request a credit for VAT taxes billed if they have a valid VAT number.
  • A customer in a province or city that should not be charged VAT wants to be excluded from the VAT and wants to receive a credit for VAT charged in error.
back to top
 

Guidance

  • Commerce Support agents are authorized to apply a credit for VAT billed on the invoices for the last 6 months if the credit amount is ≤$650 USD (or local currency equivalent) if the customer has been billed in error. For VAT credit requests >$650 USD (or local currency equivalent), follow the current credit/refund policy found here. The scenarios where an agent can provide a VAT credit are:
    • The customer experienced a problem entering their VAT ID at purchase.
    • The customer skipped entering their VAT ID during the purchase process.
    • A valid VAT ID needs to have been entered before a credit can be provided.
  • Customers worldwide are subject to tax laws, and Microsoft must comply with these laws when customers purchase Microsoft Online Services.
  • Customers who do not know their VAT number or who have questions about VAT numbers should contact their local tax authority or tax adviser.
  • After the customer’s VAT number has been entered, no VAT will be charged on future invoices unless the VAT ID is removed from the account by the customer.
back to top
 

Procedure

Customers should enter their VAT number when purchasing Microsoft Online Services. The customer can add, change, or remove their VAT number in the Microsoft 365 admin center. Agents can also view, add and edit the customer’s VAT number in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT). See the section, "Update a VAT number."

If the customer wants a credit for the VAT charged before the VAT number was entered, they must request a credit. See the section, "VAT: Determine whether a credit is due (Tier 1 and 2 steps)."

Changes to VAT - rates and implementations

In an effort to consolidate and quickly share the information, the Tax and Trade team is keeping track of VAT change plans in a SharePoint. That is where advocates can find the most current status about VAT rate changes, affected countries and regions and the contacts managing those changes from Operations and Tax teams.

Please visit the Tax and Trade Sharepoint - Change page for more information. You can use the filters in each column to browse through the list.

Completed changes

VAT Implementation in Chile on October 1, 2020

Microsoft will begin charging Value Added Tax (VAT) on digital content and services purchased by our consumers who didn’t provide valid VAT ID in Chile from October 1, 2020.

VAT Implementation in Bahrain on January 1, 2019

On January 1, 2019, VAT will be implemented in Bahrain. More details will be shared as the Government publishes further regulations.

VAT Law Update in South Africa on April 2019

Effective April 1, 2019, South Africa will impose a requirement on non-resident enterprises supplying electronic services to South African B2B customers to register for, pay and report 15% South African VAT. Customers will be able to update their South African business VAT Identification Number through the online Portal. When this announcement was created, there were about 2,000 customers affected. There is no VAT exemption in South Africa regime at the moment.

Questions about the VAT rate
The country, province, state and/or city of the tenant determines the VAT rate rather than the physical location of the customer. If customers have VAT rate questions, refer them to their local taxing authority.

Customer is unable to add a VAT number at purchase
Customers may receive one of the following errors when entering their VAT number while purchasing services:
Error 6031 (Sorry! We can’t process your request right now.)
  • This error indicates that the VAT number entered is invalid.
    i. Verify that the customer is not entering any spaces when typing the VAT number and that they are entering the VAT number prefaced with their two-digit country code.
    ii. Validate the VAT number according to the “Validating a VAT” Section of this article.
    iii. Enter or edit the VAT in CMAT on the customer’s behalf.
    iv. If these steps do not resolve the issue, create a work item for Tier 3 Engineering per 2778091: How to escalate a Service Request to Tier 3, Engineering.
Error 6098 (The service is temporarily offline.)
  • This error indicates that the VAT verification service is down and the customer should try again later.
    i. Try validating the VAT for the customer. If your attempt fails, create a work item for Tier 3 Engineering per 2778091: How to escalate a Service Request to Tier 3, Engineering.
Note The VAT number must be entered with the ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 country code (except for Greece, which uses the nonstandard country code EL) and no spaces (for example, GB123456789).

Validating a VAT


European Union Countries

Validate the VAT in VIES
Validate the VAT against the VIES for EU member countries only.

  1. Navigate to http://ec.europa.eu/taxation_customs/vies/vieshome.do?selectedLanguage=EN.
  2. Select the member state based on the ship-to country.
  3. Paste the VAT number into VAT Number field.
  4. Click Verify.
  5. Review the results.
Is the VAT valid?
  • Yes: Go to "Update a VAT number."
  • No: Ask the customer to verify the VAT number, as it appears to be incorrect.

Decipher VIES VAT Results

VAT results table  
Yes, valid VAT number Valid VAT
No, invalid VAT number Number entered is not valid, at least on the date specified
Service unavailable. Please resubmit your request later. Some problem with either the network or the web application

Note See "Request time-out" for more information.
Member state service unavailable. Please resubmit your request later. Member state whose VAT number you are trying to validate is not available

Note See "Request time-out" for more information.
Error: Incomplete (VAT + Member State) or corrupted data input
Request time-out. Please resubmit your request later. System unavailable

See http://ec.europa.eu/taxation_customs/vies/viesspec.do for a complete list of scheduled downtimes for the site.
