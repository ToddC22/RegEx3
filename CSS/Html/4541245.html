Microsoft promotes our marketplaces, Microsoft AppSource and Azure Marketplace, as the launchpad for partners to market and sell their cloud solutions and services. It's how partners "Go-To-Market with Microsoft".

Microsoft has made digital marketplaces such as “AppSource” and “Azure Marketplace” available to its community of customers and partners. These digital “storefronts” allow customers to find, try, and buy applications and services that accelerate their Digital Transformation, and help publishers grow businesses, thanks to an increased access to Microsoft’s customers and partner ecosystem.

Partners can publish their solutions on one of two storefronts, based on their target audience.

  1. Azure Marketplace for IT professionals and developers, or technical users.

  1. Microsoft AppSource for line-of-business decision-makers and business owners.

Publishers get free access to Microsoft's Go-to-Market resources, robust technical support, global partner ecosystem, and sales team, who through the Co-Sell program, get incentivized to sell their solutions. They can also make their offers available to Cloud Solution Provider in Partner Center.

As customer adoption for marketplaces continues to grow around the world, we expanded our global marketplace reach. Azure Marketplace and AppSource offerings can be purchased in 141 geographies as defined by the customer's billing address, and transactions can be completed in 17 currencies.

Azure Marketplace is the preferred online marketplace for IT professionals and cloud developers to find, try, buy, and deploy leading IT management, support, and development solutions. It offers a catalog of 8,000+ listings including Azure building blocks like Virtual Machines, APIs, SaaS applications, and consulting service offers. These technical solutions and services from Microsoft and partners are built to extend Azure products and services. Examples include databases, security, blockchain and developer tools.

AppSource is the preferred online marketplace for business customers to find, try, buy, and deploy leading cloud applications that support business workflows and drive productivity. It helps them get solutions tailored to their industry that work with products they already use.

Azure Marketplace is the starting point for all joint Microsoft Go-To-Market (GTM) activities. We focus on helping partners to reach more customers. You can publish new listings, and also use Azure Marketplace to conduct promotional and demand generation campaigns, perform joint sales/marketing activities with Microsoft.

Azure Marketplace provides a market for Microsoft Partners to promote and sell products and services to Azure customers. Publishers instantly gain access to 140 global markets, our 300,000+ partners, and Azure's network of enterprise customers. The marketplace includes more than 90% of Fortune 500 companies and many of the world's leading developers.

Marketplace Rewards

New partners in Azure Marketplace are automatically offered a set of no-cost Go-To-Market benefits (GTM) to help boost awareness of their offers in Azure's marketplace. See also Commercial Marketplace Rewards SDK.

Cloud Solution Providers

Publishers can also take advantage of Microsoft’s Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) partner program, the largest channel program in the industry with more than 60,000 channel partners serving millions of Microsoft customers worldwide. Publishers can make their transactable Azure Marketplace offers available for distribution through the CSP channel. Partners in the CSP program will be able to sell, deploy, and bundle Azure services with Azure-optimized ISV software from the marketplace to better serve customers and grow their managed services business.

There are different types of offers that can be published to Marketplace. The following do not have any publishing fees:

  • Listing – a basic listing with 'contact me' for lead generation
  • Trial – offers a free trial to demonstrate solution’s capabilities and help convert customers faster
  • Bring your own licence (BYOL) – for customers to quickly deploy an offer

If a solution is purchased via Commercial Marketplace (Transact), then revenues for software licence are split between the publisher and Microsoft. This is done in accordance with the terms and conditions in the Marketplace Publisher Agreement.

Publishing options

Publisher pricing options and fees for transact offers

Microsoft enables the sale of third-party software, and deployment of some offer types to the customer's Azure subscription. The publisher must consider the billing of infrastructure fees, and the publisher's own software licensing fees, when selecting a billing model and offer type.

The transact publishing option is currently supported for the following offer types: Virtual Machines, Azure Applications, and SaaS Apps.

[Asset 4541243]

Publisher payout

The standard rate for publishing is a 20% fee for transactions conducted via Azure Marketplace or AppSource, Microsoft field-assisted (Eg Co-sell), and Cloud Solution Provider (CSP). As an example: If a product was listed at $100.00 (USD), when sold Microsoft would pay $80.00 to the publisher and retain $20.00. IP Co-Sell eligible SaaS applications qualify for 10% off the standard publishing fee; the effective fee for transactions is then 10%. Using the same example above, Microsoft would pay $90.00 to the publisher and retain $10.00 for the SaaS application.

There is a 'Commercial Marketplace portal' within Partner Center, where partners who go to market with Microsoft and take advantage of the marketplace can create, publish and manage offers, provide test drive demonstrations, and view analytics on offer performance.

Currently, Partner Center only supports the creation and management of Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) offers, and as Partner Center evolves to better integrate all aspects of Microsoft's relationship with software publishers, all other offers and functions currently in the Cloud Partner Portal will also be migrated to Partner Center.
