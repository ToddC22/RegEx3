2. Guidance

  • The monetary thresholds listed in this article are per request.
  • For a subscription cancelled on the same day as purchased, if the refundable transaction is visible in CMAT, advocates can apply the credit in CMAT without waiting for the invoice to be generated.
  • Commerce advocates are authorized to process approved credits in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT) for Credit Card, Direct Debit, and Invoice Pay customers without creating a request for PayOps, except for withholding tax and sales and use tax exemptions.
  • Advocates in EMEA are authorized to apply VAT credits for customers per conditions explained in 2849736: Supporting Value-Added Tax and Tax Exemption .
  • If the approved credit is ≤$650 USD, CMAT will apply the credit to the customer’s account without triggering the approval workflow. IMPORTANT: If Tier 1 or 2’s approval limit is lower than the CMAT threshold of $650, the advocate must obtain approval for the credit before submitting the credit in CMAT.
  • If the credit is > $650 USD, CMAT will prompt the advocate to select approvers in the Refund Transaction workflow. The approvers will be notified of the pending credit needing their approval. Once the transaction is approved, the credit is applied to the customer’s account.
  • This procedure is for direct subscriptions only.

3. Procedure

Please ensure that you have validated and approved or have received validation and approval before applying a credit.

Note: credit threshold varies on what the type of credit request. Credit scenarios can be found in the following KBs:
  • Cancellation credits, please see article 2812902: Procedure: M365 Commerce: MOS Cancellation Guidance .
  • Credit for direct customer moving to Volume Licensing agreement, see article 2820242: Supporting Volume Licensing customers.
  • Value Added Tax credits, see article 2849736: Supporting Value-Added Tax and EOC Tax Exemption.
  • Withholding tax credits, see article 2809401: Supporting withholding tax credit requests from customers.
  • Sales and Use Tax credits, see article 2807966: Processing a Sales and Use Tax Exemption Certificate for customers in the United States, Canada and Puerto Rico.
  • CPE credits, see article 3081494: Processing a CPE Credit.
  • SLA credits, see article 3078460: Processing a credit request due to a service outage for Direct and Prepaid customer.

a. What to do if an incorrect credit is applied to an account:

1. If you make a mistake applying a credit to an account, create a credit reversal, navigate to CMAT and select Transaction: verify that the line item to be reversed displays as a negative amount. If the amount to be reversed is positive, process as a standard offset.

Note: Reversals can only be completed one Line item at a time and must be 100% reversed. Ex. A credit was submitted for $50.00, so the reversal must be entered for $50.00.

b. Steps for applying a credit: 
1. Is this an SLA credit? If this is an SLA credit, first check the Message Center Alerts to see if there is an alert about the outage. If the alert states that automatic or proactive credits will be issued, do not request or apply a credit.
2. Is this a cancellation credit? If this is a cancellation credit, first check the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT) to see if an automatic credit has been applied to the account since the cancellation. An automatic credit may affect the credit amount you plan to apply.

3. In CMAT, search for the customer.

4. Once the customer is selected, go to the Transactions page. Determine the line item to which you will apply the credit. 

5. Expand the line item. Canrefund must be “true” in order to use this line item. Click Refund Transaction.
[Asset 4016726]

6. Change the Refund Amount, if needed. The amount entered should be Pretax.

7. Select the credit reason from the Reason drop-down list.[Asset 4533818]

 

8. Select the Refund Option based on the payment method:
[Asset 4533819]


8.a. For Invoice Pay customers: 
Select Refund to billing account. This will apply the credit to the customer's next Microsoft invoice. Refund to billing account is the only Refund Option available for Invoice Pay customers.

8.b. For Credit Card customers (use one of the following options):

  • To apply the credit to the customer's next Microsoft invoice, select Refund to billing account (use this option if the customer still has an active order on the tenant).
  • To refund to the credit card on the next calendar day:
    1. Select Refund to billing account. This creates an Offset transaction with the credit amount.
    2. After you finish this refund transaction, you must then click Process Balance on the offset transaction to push the refund to the card the following day. Note: Before pressing the button to process the balance to the customer’s credit card, advocates should ask for confirmation that the credit card is still valid.
  • To refund to the credit card in 10 calendar days:

    1. Check the status of the charge on the Charges page:

    Succeeded means the charge to the credit card was successful. You can proceed with the refund.

    Open or Failed means the card has not yet been charged. Do not proceed with the refund.

    2. If charge status is Succeeded, select Refund to Payment Instrument immediately.

    If an offset transaction already exists for the credit, and the customer is asking for a refund to their credit card, advocates are authorized to use Process Balance to refund to the credit card.

8.c. For Direct Debit customers:

  • To apply the credit to the customer's next Microsoft invoice, select Refund to billing account (use this option if the customer still has an active order on the tenant).
  • If the customer requests a refund:
    1. You must wait 10 calendar days from the date on the Charges page before proceeding with this step.
    2. What is the status of the Charge Attempt?
      • Failed: Do not proceed. The bank declined the charge.
      • Complete: Go to the next step.
    3. Select Refund to Payment Instrument immediately. The refund to the customer’s bank account will be applied in approximately 5 business days.

c. How to cancel a pending Refund Transaction request (if you choose the wrong approver)
  1. Go to My Requests on the Requests page.
  2. Expand the Pending Request to see the Details page. Scroll to the bottom and click Cancel Request.[Asset 4533825]
  3. Enter relevant information in the Comments field and click the checkmark to submit the cancellation. [Asset 4487408]
