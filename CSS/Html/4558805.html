1. Process

When a customer contacts support for PC Accessories replacement/fix, follow the below steps.

1. Create a service request (SR) in ASD.

For more information see KB 2988026 Guideline: Service Request (SR) Creation and Case Ownership.

Note: For Xbox PC controller replacement requests, transfer the customer to Xbox support.

2. Basic troubleshooting:

  1. Ask the customer about the issue with the product.
  2. If they have already gone through troubleshooting with Windows support, proceed to step 3 below. Also skip if the product is physically damaged.
  3. Basic troubleshooting can include trying the USB dongle on another port or device and changing the batteries. For other troubleshooting steps, see Microsoft accessories help and check known issues on KB 4072657 Informational: Surface: Technical guides and known issues landing page. For connectivity issues and where a customer needs support with Windows updates, you can refer the customer to Windows support for advanced troubleshooting. If the issue remains, go to step 3 below.

3. Gather required information from the customer.

  • Photos of the product – If you are unable to determine the actual product or cannot ascertain the defect based on the customer’s description, you must ask the customer to send photos including the Product ID.
  • Point of purchase.
  • Date of the purchase.
  • Product ID of the PC accessory.
  • Has the customer’s PC accessory been replaced for this issue previously?

4. Validate the warranty.

Based on the information customer has provided, confirm the device was purchased within the warranty period, refer to PDF for country based warranty period. If yes, go to step 5. Note the difference in warranty periods for Surface-branded vs Microsoft branded accessories when advising customer of applicable warranty periods.

  • Ask for a proof of purchase (PoP). If the customer needs time to look for the proof of purchase, proceed to step 5. Provide instructions how customers can send us the proof of purchase once found. If the customer comes back and does not have a proof of purchase, see KB 4025417 Procedure: Devices: How to verify the warranty status of an accessory sold separately.

Note: PoP is necessary for refunds. If you encounter a customer with an accessory in warranty, for refund (no replacement available), but with no PoP, agree on a solution for the customer with your TA. 

  • If the device is out of warranty, explain to the customer that we can’t provide a replacement.

5. Collect proof of purchase.

In ASD, send an email to the customer requesting the proof of purchase using the Data Transfer and Management tool process. See KB 4135034 Tool User Guide: Data Transfer & Management (DTM).

Review the proof of purchase to confirm the device is in warranty.

Valid proof of purchase:

Purchased from Microsoft Store

Validate the purchase via MST

Purchased third-party retailer

One of the following is required as proof of purchase:

  • Copy of the invoice.
  • Electronic purchase confirmation (order confirmation email).
  • Copy of the purchase receipt.

 

6. Determine the next step per country

  • Determine if the PC accessory has a replacement using the matrix in section 2. Refurbished replacements may be sent. Per the manufacturer's warranty, we either send reconditioned or new devices/covers for replacements. All devices/covers have been tested at the factory. All replacements (whether reconditioned or new) ship in plain boxes and not the retail packaging.
    • Warranty period for replacement product is based on customer’s proof of purchase of initial product.
    • If the replacement is an upgrade, you need to clearly explain to the customer that they will not be receiving the exact same product. Identify and describe the replacement to the customer and check if there will be any issues in using the said product (for example, not compatible with current OS version).

2. PC accessory replace/refund matrix for Korea

2.1 Using the matrix

Using the following matrix, define what options you can provide to customer based on their original PC HW/Accessories SKU.

The outcome will be one of the following:

  • Customer’s PC accessory has a replacement with same SKU, place replacement order.
  • Customer’s PC accessory has an upgrade replacement SKU, place replacement order with upgrade SKU. Explain to the customer that they will not receive the exact same product, but a similar product and it might be a different color.

To place order, see KB 4502445 Procedure: Supplemental Parts: Replacement/upgrade process in MST.

  • The customer’s PC Accessory has no replacement/upgrade, or product is not listed, proceed to refund. See KB 4508757 Procedure: Supplemental Parts: PC hardware/accessories refund process.

Proceed to next steps based on the section that product is listed in for each country.

Return to top

2.2 Matrix for Korea

  • Procedure based on Kind of replacement:
    • Identical – Order same product via MST.
    • Upgrade – Order replacement product via MST.
    • Upgrade or Refund – Offer customer the replacement product, if customer agrees, order replacement product via MST. If the customer does not agree, proceed with the refund process.

2.3 Refund submission

For those products that do not have replacement products, proceed with refund. Make sure that your child SR have proof or purchase, names, and addresses.

If you are proceeding with the refund process due to no inventory for replacement order, cancel the order first (confirm the order status is cancelled) and provide a TFS WI that shows the order has been canceled on the refund request.

Explain to the customer the detailed refund process after this submission. See section 3, Refund process (Korea), on KB 4508757 Procedure: Supplemental Parts: PC hardware/accessories refund process.
