1. Introduction

Microsoft family is just one of the many benefits of having a Microsoft account. It’s a free service that helps families stay connected and keep kids safer on Windows 10 and Xbox One devices with features like activity reporting, screen time limits, and content restrictions. You’ll find these settings on family.microsoft.com, where you can also track kids’ spending and add money to their Microsoft accounts.

Read more at https://support.microsoft.com/help/12413/microsoft-account-what-is-family.

Note for child local account on Windows 7 and Windows 8:

Starting January 29th, 2020, we will no longer support showing activity or managing settings for child local accounts using Windows 7 and Windows 8 computers on account.microsoft.com. For both operating systems there will still be a path for parents to use parental controls or update, setup as below.

Windows 7:

Current setting will stay in place but no changes can be made. In order to user family safety settings on Windows 7 devices, uninstall Windows Live Family Safety, and use the Parental Controls control panel to set the limits and see activity reports.

Windows 8.1:

Current setting will stay in place but no changes can be made. In order to use family settings on Windows 8 devices, follow below steps.

  1. Open Settings.
  2. On each child account, change them from Child to Standard Users. This will disconnect their device from our online features.
  3. Once you’ve changed all accounts to Standard Users, you’re able to move them back to Child to keep using family safety settings on the device.
  4. Go to Control Panel > User Accounts and Family Safety > Parental Controls to make any changes.

2. Family feature

2.1 Activity

Parents can view their child’s usage for apps and games, web browsing, searches, and screen time by week.   

Return to top

2.2 Screen time

Parents can set up screen time limits for your child by devices such as Xbox One and Windows 10. See the Set up screen time limits for your child article for more information.

Return to top

2.3 App and game limits

Parents can set a one app and game limit that spans multiple platforms on this page. This is an easier way for adults in a family to manage how much time children are spending in specific apps and games, across their devices such as Windows, Xbox, and Android (excluding iOS). See KB 4519323 Informational: Microsoft account: Cross platform app and game limits for more information.

Return to top

2.4 Content restrictions

Parents can set an age limit to block inappropriate apps, games, and media on this page. Anything that exceeds the content ratings that you specified appropriate for your child, will require your approval.

  • This setting applies to Windows 10 and Xbox One devices.

Return to top

2.5 Spending

  • Ask a parent: Parents can turn this feature on to require adult approval for the things their child wants to buy in the Microsoft Store.
    • Note: This excludes requiring approval to use the gift cards or money in their Microsoft account.

See the Require kids to ask a parent before buying stuff in Microsoft Store article for more information.

  • Microsoft account balance/Add money: Parents can add money to the child’s Microsoft account so they can buy what they want, without spending too much.
  • Purchase history: Parents can view their child’s purchase history.

Return to top

2.6 Find your child

Stay up to date on your child's location when they're with friends or when you're stuck at work. You'll find them on a map when they have a Windows 10 phone or an Android device running Microsoft Launcher.

In scope

All CSS advocates are required to support family for Windows 10 and Xbox One platforms.

  • Primary support: Accounts & Billing, Xbox, Windows
  • Secondary support: Remaining LOBs

Out of scope

Xbox 360 will remain on legacy platform. No new scenarios. Family settings support will remain as is.

4. Customer support scenarios

4.1 User management

Scenario

Customer handling

I am missing a family member. What should I do?

Educate the customer on how to add a family member by going to family.microsoft.com and following the steps located in the Add members to your family group article.

Note: Up to 20 members can be added to a Microsoft family.
I’m trying to add a family member but I’m getting the following error message: “Your family group is full”. Can you please help?

Explain to the customer that a Microsoft family is limited to 20 family members. The error message means that their Microsoft family has reached the maximum number of members.  

To work around this limitation, customers may either remove someone from their family or create another Microsoft family.

Refer to the following to help customers:

  • Remove a family member
  • Set up a new a Microsoft family:
  1. Sign into family.microsoft.com.
  2. Select Create a family group and invite family members to join.

I tried to add an account to my family, but it still says it is pending. Why?

Explain to the customer that, when joining a family, the parent must accept the invitation to join the family from the email they’ve received. If the invitation is not accepted, it will show as a pending state.

More information can be found at Add members to your family group.

I am prompted to get parental consent to sign into my account. Why?

Educate the customer on the parental consent flow.

More information can be found at Parental consent and Microsoft child accounts.

More detailed information can be found in KB 4471317 Informational: Microsoft account: Parental consent.

I see a family member I don't recognize. What should I do?

Educate the customer on how to remove a family member by following the steps detailed at Remove members from your family group.

Note:

  • The customer must have an adult account and be the family administrator/owner.
  • If the customer fails in removing the family member using family.microsoft.com, advocates can assist through MST (Removing can be done through our tool. However, note that adding is not supported).

I don't want a parent to manage my account anymore. What can I do?

Educate the customer on the following options:

  • If the child is over the age of majority, they can remove themselves from the family.
  • If the child is under the age of majority (as per their country regulatory age), the parent is able to remove them for the family; however, they may not be able to use certain services or have settings changed until they are managed by an adult.
  • Prior to removing, recommend the customer turn off the account settings/limitations like web browsing limitations, purchase limitations.

When setting up my family I realized I have accidentally created two separate families. What should I do?

Educate the customer on how to add and remove family member as needed to only keep one single family. Furthermore, inform the customer that a Microsoft account can only be part of one single family.

  • Add a member: Add members to your family group.
  • Remove a family member: Remove members from your family group.

Important: Do not have the customer use Microsoft account SWAP.

I accidentally created an account for my child, but he already has an Xbox account. What should I do?

Educate the customer on how to remove the duplicate or unneeded account and how to add the correct account.

  • Add a member: Add members to your family group.
  • Remove a family member: Remove members from your family group.

Important: Do not have the customer use Microsoft account SWAP.

I upgraded to Windows 10, but now, my family features settings do not work. Why?

Inform customers that if they had previously set up family features for a child account and then upgraded to Windows 10, there are a few steps they need to take to turn on family settings again.

The child will need to sign in to Windows 10 with a Microsoft account, and then the customer will need to add that account to the family at family.microsoft.com. Follow the detailed steps located at Fix family features after Windows 10 upgrade.

4.3 Account restrictions (activity, screen time, content restrictions, spending)

Scenario

Customer handling

I'm a child account in a family. My parent has screen time limit block set for my account. If I ignore the screen time messages and my account gets logged off in the middle of my game, will the system save my game before kicking me out of the system?

Inform the customer that the game save progress will depend on the game. Normally, games will reload from the last saved progress point.

If I set a screen time limit to a specific child account, how are they displayed?

Explain to the customer that a popup will occur during their active session. Parents can add more time over the shoulder login with their parent Microsoft account credentials.

How come I am receiving a message on my device that I have screen time limit set? How can I turn this off?

Explain to the customer that an adult account in the family has set a screen time limit on the account.

Recommend that the customer contact the family administrator to change/turn off the screen time limit.

I am getting a screen time limit notification on my console when playing a game, but when I check online, I still have unlimited time that day on account.microsoft.com. Can you please help?

Educate the customer on the difference between daily allowance and the set a time limit allowance options on account.microsoft.com.

  • Daily allowance controls how many hours per day a child can play a game or use content.
  • Set a time limit allowance controls the time range per day a child can be active on Windows 10 or Xbox One Console.

Example: If the current time is 7 PM and if a child is allowed one hour of playtime per day, but the time limit allowance is set between 10 AM and 5 PM, in this case, the child will not be able to play their game even though they did not use any of their one hour playtime allowance.

I've set a screen time limit for my child's devices, but they are not working. Can you help?

Review the customer's Family settings to ensure they meet the following:

  • Screen limits are not turned off
  • Child is not using local account
  • Customer is not using a legacy/unsupported device

Note:

  • The default screen time is set from 7 AM to 10 PM.
  • If the child needs more time, recommend they contact the family owner to get more time.
  • Screen time is reported on a weekly basis to the parent.

I am an adult account. I’m still getting notifications for screen time limit. Why?

If the Microsoft account is an adult account, customer can navigate to their own account to update content restrictions.

Recommend the customer remove the affected Microsoft account from the family account and re-invite it as an adult OR update all the content restrictions then remove the account from the family to remove the restrictions.

How come my website filtering feature does not work for Chrome?

Explain the customer that web site filtering is currently only available for Microsoft Edge and Internet Explorer. We do not have details if this feature will be available for other web browsers.

As a parent, I've blocked my child from accessing a certain website. Why is it is not working?

Review the customer's Family settings with the customer to check that:

  • The Block Inappropriate websites feature is not turned off under content restrictions websites, it is only blocked on Edge/Internet Explorer.

Note:

  • If the customer is using a third-party browser, the blocking won’t work.
  • On Windows 10: Ensure that the child is not using a local account. Family features are only supported on a Microsoft account and not a local account.
  • On Xbox One: Ensure the console is online
  • The option only allow these websites is not enabled: Ensure the option is turned on.
  • The customer is not using a legacy/unsupported device: inform customers that Windows 10 or Xbox One is required to support this family feature.

As a parent, I've set age appropriate ratings to Teen and up for my child to play certain games, but they can still play M rated games. Why?

Guide the customer to review the child account settings:

  • Navigate to family.microsoft.com
  • Select the child’s account
  • Ensure the account has content restrictions ENABLED and that the maturity level age is set to the appropriate level.
  • Ensure the games have not been granted an exception.

Note: They can confirm this lower on the page where they see games that are allowed.

I’m requested to get an adult approval for buying things in the Microsoft Store on Windows 10 and Xbox One devices. Why?

Explain to the customer that an adult account in the family has set a purchase content constraint limit on the account. The Needs adult approval to buy things is turned to On.

[Asset 4340667]

Recommend the customer to contact the family administrator to change/turn off the content restriction.

Note:

  • If the child has enough CSV to buy and if the child age is appropriate, they can complete purchases without parent involvement. The parent will get an email if they enabled purchase notifications.
  • The parent approval is needed when either the child does not have enough funds or if another payment instrument than CSV is involved.

4.4 Sharing Office

Scenario

Customer handling

I just bought Office and I just received an email stating that I can share Office with family members. Is that true?

 

I already own an Office subscription, and I just received an email stating that I can share Office with my family members. Is that true?

Explain to customers that if they have Microsoft family set up, and already own an Office 365 Home, then Office will automatically be shared with a maximum of six users including the purchaser of Office.

Customers can install Office on an unlimited number of devices—and be signed into their accounts on up to five of those devices at the same time.

However, if customers do not have family members, they can share with friends using the guest access.

What if I have more than five people in my family, who gets Office?

Explain that family members are selected by tenure. Those who were added as a family member first are shared with first.

However, you can always manage whom you share Office with by going to your Services & subscriptions page on account.microsoft.com and selecting the Sharing tab.

I see a prompt to share my Office subscription. What does this mean?

Explain to the customer that this feature enables them to access Office.com so they can easily share their Office subscriptions with their family member.

Note: This notification will only appear to the customer if they are the subscription owner.

To share a subscription, select Share with your family to be able to select a family member, or to send an email to share the subscription.

Customer handling
