1. Account Recovery

Note: This template should be used for substantiated ATO cases where the Microsoft account was not changed. Cut out any information that does not pertain to your case (gamertag change, console specific instructions, etc.).

Email Subject: Regarding Your Report of Account Takeover
Service Request [XXXXXXXXX]
Gamertag: [INSERT]​
Microsoft Account ID: [INSERT]

​Hi,

My name is [Advocate’s First Name] with Microsoft Customer Support. I understand that a situation like this one can be difficult, and I appreciate your patience while I performed an investigation of your account. I am contacting you today to provide the results of my investigation.

Account security is a top priority at Microsoft and our Advanced Capability Advocates is dedicated to investigating and validating fraudulent activity. The account and billing activity associated with your Microsoft Account was thoroughly reviewed by our fraud team and I can confirm your account was compromised. I’ve taken the appropriate measures to secure your account. Please see the information below for steps to regain control of your Microsoft account.

(OPTION 1)
The investigation revealed that purchases were made while your account was out of your control. I’ve refunded [INSERT REFUND AMOUNT] to your payment method(s) and added [INSERT CSV AMOUNT] to your local currency balance. Generally, refunds are received within 7 days but may take up to 30 days depending on your financial institution’s individual policies. Please note that if there are any outstanding balances on your account, the amount of the balance will be deducted from the refund. If you’d like to review the transaction activity on your account, please visit https://account.microsoft.com and sign into your Xbox Live account for a detailed view of your charges and refunds.
OR

(OPTION 2)
The investigation revealed that no purchases were made while your account was out of your control.
(Delete this item if No gamertag change occurred.)
Per my investigation, your gamertag was changed while the account was out of your control. As a result, your account has been granted a one-time free gamertag change. You can change your gamertag back to what it was or something else of your choosing. Please be aware that once you change your gamertag you will have to pay if you want to change it again. Choose wisely.

Please take the following steps to regain control of your Microsoft account:
Step 1: Reset your Microsoft account password​

  1. Check your email in the next 24 hours for a password recovery email from the Microsoft Account Team.
    • ​Note: If the reset link expires or you can’t find the password recovery email, go to https://account.microsoft.com, type in your Microsoft account, and then click Can't access your account? Or, respond to this email, and we’ll send the password recovery email again.
  2. Use the link in the email to reset your Microsoft account password. The stronger you make your password, the better. For tips on creating strong passwords, see Create strong passwords.
  3. Update the security information for your Microsoft account. We strongly suggest that you not skip this step because it will help you protect your account from possible future attacks. Your security information may include alternative email address, secret question, cell phone number, and trusted PC.

Step 2: Delete your Xbox Live Profile
Now that you've reset your password, you will need to delete your profile from the console and redownload it with your new credentials. To do this, please perform the following steps:
Xbox One Console:

  1. Press the Menu button.
  2. Select Settings.
  3. Select All Settings.
  4. Navigate to the Other People heading and select Remove accounts.
  5. Navigate to the profile that you want to remove and select Choose this person.
  6. Select Remove from this Xbox.
  7. Once the account has been removed, select Close.

Xbox 360 Console:

  1. Go to Settings and select System.
  2. Select Storage.
  3. Navigate to Storage and select Storage Device. Note if you don't connect any external storage devices to your console, select Hard Drive.
  4. Navigate to Profiles then select the profile that you want to remove.
  5. Select Delete to remove and Close.

Step 3: Download your Xbox Live profile on your console
For your security I’ve removed access to your profile from all consoles it was associated to. You must now re-download your profile. Complete the following steps on your console:
Xbox One Console:

  1. Press the Xbox button to open the guide.
  2. On the Sign in tab, move all the way down and select Add & manage.
  3. Select Add new.
  4. Enter the email address for the Microsoft account, and then select Enter. Important Make sure that you do not select Get a new account unless that's what you want to do. If you select this, you will create a brand-new Microsoft account.
  5. Enter the password for your Microsoft account, and then select Enter. If you've forgotten your password, select I forgot my password.
  6. Read the Microsoft Service Agreement and Privacy Statement, and then select I Accept.
  7. Follow the steps to configure your Sign-In & Security preferences to add your Microsoft account to your Xbox One console.

Xbox 360 Console:

  1. Press the Guide button on your controller.
  2. Select Download Profile. Note: Not seeing Download Profile? This means that you're signed in under another profile. Press the X button on your controller to sign out, and then select Download Profile.
  3. At the bottom of the Download Profile screen, select Download Profile. Enter your Microsoft account and password when prompted.
  4. If you use external storage on your console, select the storage device on which you want to save your Xbox profile. If you don't use external storage, your Xbox profile will begin downloading.

Step 4: Set your Xbox One home console
Complete the following steps on your Xbox One console:

  1. Go to the Settings hub and choose My Xbox.
  2. Select My Home Xbox.
  3. Select Make This My Home Xbox.

If you would like to understand more about account security, please visit the following pages:

  • Account Security: https://support.xbox.com/my-account/security/add-security-contact-information
  • Additional Microsoft Account Security Steps: https://support.xbox.com/my-account/security/update-security-information
  • How to Protect your Microsoft Account: https://support.microsoft.com/help/12410/microsoft-account-help-protect-account

I apologize for any inconvenience this issue may have caused you. To make up for lost gaming time, I'd like to extend the time on your Xbox Live membership by giving you the following prepaid code:
[INSERT CODE]

Redeem this prepaid code on your Xbox Live account. (Learn how to redeem a prepaid code.)
Thank you for your understanding and patience during the investigation of your account. We value you as a customer and look forward to seeing you back on Xbox Live!

Happy gaming,

4.1 No Evidence - Refund

Note: This template should be used for ATO cases where ATO is not substantiated but a one-time refund has been granted.

Email Subject: Regarding Your Report of Account Takeover
Service Request [XXXXXXXXX]

Hi,

My name is [Advocate’s First Name] with Microsoft Customer Support. I want to thank you for taking the time to contact us regarding your account takeover claim. I understand that a situation like this can be difficult and I appreciate your patience during this investigation.

I’m happy to report that your account is secure and has not been compromised. During my investigation, I was able to confirm that all activity for the purchases in question originated from the Xbox and/or location primarily associated with your gamertag and Microsoft account. Please understand that account security is a top priority at Microsoft and a thorough investigation has been performed.

Although my investigation yielded no evidence of an account takeover, I understand that these charges were unexpected, and I do have the ability to provide a refund for you. I’ve processed a refund in the amount of <Amount> for all unauthorized purchases made between <Date> and <Date> to the payment method that was used to make the purchases. Generally, refunds are received within 7 days but may take up to 30 days depending on your financial institution’s individual policies. Please note that if there are any outstanding balances on your account, the amount of the balance will be deducted from the refund. If you’d like to review the transaction activity on your account, please visit https://account.microsoft.com and sign into your Xbox Live account for a detailed view of your charges and refunds.

As my investigation shows that your account was not compromised and the purchases in dispute originated from your primary device, this is a one-time exception. To ensure that you don’t incur any additional charges, I’ve removed your payment method(s) from the account. I understand the frustration that can be involved with this specific issue and would like to provide you with resources that have been built into the Xbox Live service that can help further prevent unnecessary purchases by family members or friends in the future:

  • Xbox 360: Parental Controls/Child Accounts
  • Xbox One: Parental Controls/Child Accounts
  • Prepaid Cards in Lieu of Adding Credit or Debit Cards
  • Passcode Required on Login
  • Payment Instrument Removal on Xbox.com, Xbox 360 and Xbox One Dashboard

If you have any further questions or need assistance in setting up parental controls, please contact Xbox Support.

We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience that you may have experienced and I appreciate your understanding and patience while I investigated your account.

Thanks,
