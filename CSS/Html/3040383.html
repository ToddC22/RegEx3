Overview

This TSG provides guidance on how to execute a subscription ownership transfer (SOT). 

Note: When a SOT is not applicable, a simple or forced migration may be required. Refer to the following TSGs for guidance: Simple Migration or Forced Migration.

Applies To: All customers.

Policy

Customers may contact support for assistance with transferring either the services or the billing of their subscription from one account to another. In these instances, inform the customer that these actions are self-serve and can be performed by following the steps outlined in the public facing document: Transfer billing ownership of an Azure subscription to another account. 

Note: Billing-only SOTs performed by SEs for customers must be done in CST.

If the customer has already attempted the self-serve option but experienced an issue, you can assist the customer by performing the SOT on their behalf. Before doing so, you should note the following: 

  1. Ensure the customer's subscription is listed as one of the subscription types supported for SOTs.
  2. The following legacy subscriptions and scenarios are not eligible for transfer of ownership:
    • Free Trial subscriptions.
    • SOTs from Azure Commercial to Azure Government (Fairfax) and vice versa.
    • Cloud Solution Provider Partner (CSP) administrators are not allowed to transfer Reserved Instances (RIs) between partners. See the Offer Specific Restrictions section of TSG 4054281 for more details.
    • Azure Pass subscriptions.
      • A migration may be considered as a workaround. Refer to TSG 2658611 for more details.
    • Azure in Open (AIO) subscriptions.
      • It is not possible to transfer AIO subscriptions between accounts. However, if a customer wants to transfer an AIO subscription to an OrgID, you can refer them to the instructions on how to link their Office 365 tenant to their Azure subscription.
      • If a customer requires further assistance with this process, engage the Cloud Identity Team.
      • Refer to TSG 2989663 for more details.
  3. The following modern subscriptions and scenarios are not eligible for transfer of ownership: 
    • Destination Account’s Azure Access Pass is a Free Trial and the Cap State is Active.
      • Attempting to perform an SOT on such a subscription will yield the following message: Error: Code StatusInvalid, Message: Cannot transfer subscription to rating asset with cap state Active.
      • Convert the Free Trial in the destination subscription to a paid offer. Then proceed with the SOT.
    • Destination Account’s Azure Access Pass is a Free Trial and is in an Inactive state due to the Cap State being expired.
    • Destination Account’s Azure Access Pass is Paid but the Azure Access Pass is in an Inactive state due to Dunning/Overdue Invoices.
  4. Orphaned legacy accounts, cannot be transferred to Modern.
    • To resolve this issue, transfer the legacy subscriptions to a non-orphaned legacy account and transfer that account into Modern.
  5.  Some marketplace subscriptions can be transferred during a SOT. See TSG 2778151. 
  6. Microsoft Partner Network (MPN) entitlements cannot be moved once used. However, customers who have -25p (only) can transfer via the self-serve option in the portal.
    • MPN subscriptions (-25P) can be transferred from MSA accounts to OrgID accounts with the same email address.
  7. Customers must pay all Dunning, Delay Write-off, or Write-off charges before an SOT can be performed.
    • An SOT should not be performed if the subscription has any outstanding charges. Escalate to your TA for approved exceptions.
  8. Enterprise Agreement (EA) subscriptions are exempt from the Payment Instrument (PI) policy.
    • EA customers can use the EA portal or contact support to have an SOT performed. 
    • An Enrollment may be billed across multiple countries.
  9. Subscriptions can be transferred to a different account in a different region (or country.)
    • In order to change regions or countries, your customer must create a new account in the region they wish to move their data and services to.
    • After the new account is created, your customer can perform an SOT via self-serve, to move their data and services to the desired region (new account).
      • If your customer is not able to perform a self-serve SOT, perform the SOT on their behalf.
    • Note: Azure Internal Registration Systems (AIRS) subscriptions are not limited and can be transferred between regions.
  10. Paid Technical Support Subscriptions are eligible to be moved via an SOT.
  11. CSP (-145P) can only be moved to another account containing -145P subscriptions.
    • Refer to TSG 3078989 for guidance on how to perform an SOT for CSP partners.
  12. Approval is required for customers who want to transfer a subscription to an account with sponsored subscriptions (or to a sponsored account).
    • Refer to TSG 3014508 for additional guidance.
  13. After an SOT, Visual Studio Team Services (VSTS) accounts connected to Azure subscriptions may temporarily lose access when the connected Azure subscription is cancelled.
    • If it has been over an hour since the SOT process was completed and the customer still does not have access to their VSTS account, refer to TSG 2909152 for guidance.
  14. Customers will lose access to RI benefits unless the RI purchases are also moved to the same account. In these instances, assist the customer in moving the RI purchases between accounts by following the guidance outlined in TSG 4054281.
  15. If a Support Plan or RI service needs to be cancelled and re-purchased post SOT, only the source account payment instrument will be eligable for the refund.  
  16. SOT from a Microsoft Online Subscription Program (MOSP) account to an EA account.

    2. Make a note of the subscription tenant ID from Jarvis.

      1. Select Transfer Subscription.

      2. Set Select Destination Transfer Type to "EA".

      3. Transfer the non-EA Azure subscription to the account in EA as an Enterprise Agreement/Enterprise DevTest offer.

      • Confirm in the source account if the subscription Status, Entitlement Status, and Fulfillment Status are Suspended.
      • Confirm via CMAT if the subscription has been transferred to the destination account and is active.
      • Once the subscription is transferred successfully, update the tenant ID of the destination account to the subscription on Jarvis. Refer to TSG 2820621 for guidance on updating tenant IDs.
      • Ask the customer to change the service admin of the subscription, then try to access it on the management portal.
      • Note: Co-admins do not get moved if there was a change of tenant. However, RBAC permissions and Managed Identities are lost when transferring to a different tenant. For more details see the public facing page here. 
      • Should you receive an error when performing this action engage the CEE - Enterprise (Azure Ops) team. 
  17. SOT from an EA account to MOSP account.

  18. When both subscriptions are on MINT and AIRS is not involved, an SOT can be performed.
     
  19. SOTs when the source and destination accounts are on different platforms (OMS vs MINT) are not supported. MINT Accounts must be backfilled before an SOT can be executed. Follow the guidance in the Backfilling MINT Accoounts section below.
    • The "Backfill" process must be used to align both accounts on the OMS platform.

Within Tenant Transfer

If the source and destination accounts are both OrgIDs and are part of the same domain (e.g. joe@contoso.com and bob@contoso.com) then the SOT is considered to be within the same tenant.

SOT within Tenant (Within Company Transfer) – If the checkbox is checked for “Is Service Ownership Transfer”. 

  • RBAC Roles – Maintained, no destruction or change
  • Destination billing owner is added as service administrator
  • Subscription Directory – No change
  • Account Administrator – Becomes destination e-mail
  • Billing responsibility – Becomes destination e-mail

SOT within Tenant (Within Company Transfer) – If the checkbox is not checked for “Is Service Ownership Transfer”.

  • RBAC Roles – Maintained, no destruction or change
  • Subscription Directory – No change
  • Account Administrator – Becomes destination e-mail
  • Billing responsibility – Becomes destination e-mail
     

Cross-Tenant Transfers

  • If the source and destination accounts are both OrgID but the AAs have different email domains (e.g. joe@contoso.com and bob@fabrikam.com) then the SOT is considered to be cross-tenant.
  • If either the source account or destination accounts are MSA, or if both are MSA, the SOT will be considered a cross-tenant.
  • Any services or deployments that are dependent upon the specific AAD tenant will be interrupted during a cross-tenant SOT. In this case the customer will need to manually re-configure the AAD tenant. You must inform the customer of this and obtain customer approval before performing the SOT.

Cross-Tenant SOT – If the checkbox is checked for “Is Service Ownership Transfer.”

  • RBAC Roles and Managed Identities – Removed from subscription (source no longer has any access to resources or subscription); destination e-mail becomes Subscription Owner
  • Subscription Directory – Changes to destination directory
  • Account Administrator – Becomes destination e-mail
  • Billing responsibility – Becomes destination e-mail

Cross-Tenant SOT – If the checkbox is not checked for “Is Service Ownership Transfer.”

  • RBAC Roles – RBAC will remain in the source account
  • Subscription Directory – Remains in the source directory
  • Account Administrator – Becomes destination e-mail
  • Billing responsibility – Becomes destination e-mail

IMPORTANT: Transferring services and billing from one account to another when the accounts are on different tenants has serious effects that the customer must be aware of prior to the SOT. If your customer’s accounts are on different tenants, note the following:

  • If you transfer only the billing of a subscription to a recipient in another Azure Active Directory (AAD) tenant, you can also choose to move the subscription to the recipient’s tenant. In return, this will: 
    • Remove all existing role-based access control (RBAC) permissions and Managed Identities previously configured to manage Azure resources in the subscription.
    • Only allow the recipient to have permissions to manage the Azure resources.
  • If you uncheck the box (indicating that you do not wish to transfer billing ownership) and do not move the subscription to the recipient’s tenant,  you should expect the following outcome: 
    • The existing RBAC permissions to manage Azure resources will be maintained.

Billing Only SOT and Legacy Background

In Azure, there have always been separate ‘containers’ for Resources (Service Administration) and for Billing (Billing Administration). Until July 29, 2019, these containers have been linked in all operations. Support engineers operate on the Billing container in CMAT, which affects the Resource container.

This new “Service Administration Transfer” feature checkbox, in both CST and in the Self-Serve experience, for SOT unlinks the Resource and Billing containers from each other.

That means an SOT without the Resource container transfers only the Billing container to the destination tenant. The Resource container remains in the original tenant. The RBAC controls in the destination tenant now apply to the Billing container, while the RBAC controls in the original tenant now only apply to the Resource container.

Special Support Scenarios

Forced Subscription Ownership Transfers (Forced SOT)

There are certain scenarios where a customer may contact support asking for a SOT, data transfer, or cancellation without permission from the current account owner. A few examples include (but are not limited to):

  • Company A has taken over Company B. 
  • The current account owner’s employment was terminated, or they have left the company.
  • The current account owner is not cooperative, or the requestor does not want the account owner to use the Azure account for malicious intent.
  • The account owner is deceased.

All Forced SOTs must go through a two-step approval process before the SOT can be performed. Before proceeding with a Forced SOT, note the following: 

  1. All SEs (FTEs and Delivery Partners) must obtain pre-approval from their TA/PTA before a Forced SOT can be performed.  
    • Before approaching a TA/PTA for a pre-approval decision, confirm that the requestor has been properly validated and all the below Forced SOT Requirements have been met.
    • Only after a TA/PTA has reviewed the request, provided pre-approval, and engaged a M1 for final approval, should the SOT be performed.
  2. The TA/PTA will review the request and work with the SE to obtain any additional details needed to make a decision. Then, the TA will contact a manager (M1) for final approval.
    • The TA/PTA must confirm that the requestor has been properly validated and all Forced SOT Requirements have been met before making a decision to pre-approve the forced SOT.
    • Once the TA/PTA has made a pre-approval decision, the TA/PTA must engage a M1 to authorize the decision as the final approver.
  3. The M1 will review the TA/PTA’s decision and determine if the Forced SOT should be executed.
    • If the M1 requires further input, they will use the M2 Review Template to engage the M2.
  4. The M2 will review the M1 request and determine if CELA needs to be consulted before the Forced SOT can take place.  
    • Only the M2 should engage CELA.

Forced SOT Requirements

Verify the following requirements are checked before approaching a TA/PTA for a forced SOT approval.

  1. The email address for the current account owner is verified as invalid.
  • Email the current account owner’s email address to confirm that it is invalid. After you send the email, you should receive a bounce back notification.
  1. The domain on the current account (source) matches the domain of the new account (destination).
    • If the current account owner’s email is a personal email address or MSA (hotmail.com, Live.com, google.com, etc.), then a SOT should not be performed under any circumstance without the confirmation of the current owner.
      • Even if someone claims they are an employee of the account owner, a SOT should not be performed without the confirmation of the current owner.
    • If the account owner’s company domain does not match the requestor's (or new account owner's), then the requestor must provide a valid reason for not being able to contact the account owner to initiate a SOT. Valid reasons include:
      • The account owner’s employment was terminated.
      • The account owner is not cooperative or the requestor does not want the account owner to use the Azure account for malicious intent.
      • The account owner is deceased.
    • If the account is an AIRS (@microsoft.com, @gbl, @ame) subscription, approval from the former employee’s manager or from the next person in the management chain is required.   
  2. The email of the current account owner is provided by the person/company requesting the transfer/cancellation.
    • Some companies use Privileged Identity Management, in which a Global Admin (GA) will only be in that role for a short period of time. In this case, you should obtain a screenshot from the GA to confirm that they were a GA and use our tools to review any past GAs.
    • Ideally, the GA should remove the existing user from their tenant and provide us with details of who the new user should be.
  3. The Company Name on the account is same as that of the requestor.
  • If the account is associated with an EA enrollment, legal ownership of that account is held by the company enrolled as an EA. In that case, ASMS may perform the ownership transfer if the person that signed the EA contacts us and can be verified.
  • If the account is associated with a BizSpark or MS4SU subscription, the Startup Company associated with the BizSpark subscription owns the account. If the account owner leaves the company for any reason, their name should be removed from the BizSpark entitlement and therefore the account ownership may be transferred. See TSG 3070105 for guidance on how to associate the benefit to a new account post SOT.  
  1. The requestor can provide the details mentioned in section D of the M2 Review Template.
    • Once provided, confirm that these details match what is provided in our tooling.  
    • If the credit card associated with the account is not the company’s corporate credit card and is the personal credit card of the current account owner, ASMS should not transfer ownership without approval from the current account owner.

Note: You can also leverage TSG 2646876 to help validate the requestor’s eligibility.

Backfilling MINT Accounts to OMS

MINT accounts are not supported as part of the SOT process. To perform an SOT in these scenarios, the account must be converted to an OMS account.

The following scenarios may occur that require an account to be backfilled: 

  • For external customers:
    • A subscription belongs to an existing legacy MINT account and an OMS account has been created that prevents the user from seeing their old subscription.
    • A subscription belongs to an existing legacy MINT account, but the customer wants the subscription moved to a different account which can only be created in OMS.
  • Internal customers
    • AIRS subscription needs to be moved to a different account for security or other reasons, and a data migration is not a feasible option.

When a backfill is required you should note the following:

  • Non-Azure subscriptions on the MINT account (e.g. OMEX, XBOX, Windows Phone, Azure Store subscription) must be cancelled and deprovisioned before a backfill can be completed. Backfill will not be done for accounts that have active non-Azure subscriptions.
  • Subscriptions must be active to be backfilled. Disabled subscriptions must be reactivated or cancelled and deprovisioned.
  • The Azure profile must be complete.
  • You must obtain an email from the customer which confirms that the subscription can be reactivated or deprovisioned as well as confirmation they understand the implications of deprovision (i.e. customer must backup data as it is not recoverable).
  • If the MINT account has two or more subscriptions, the TenantID of all subscriptions must be the same. If they are not the same, you must first update them so they are the same before the backfill will be possible. For information on updating Tenants refer to TSG 2820621.
  • The backfill will change the account anniversary date to the date in which the conversion took place. Be sure to inform the customer of the billing date change, as the account anniversary date is based on when the MINT account was created even after it’s backfilled.

Engagement and Escalation Path

  • If you have questions about the SOT process engage your TA or PTA.
  • You can engage the ACE Subscription Lifecycle Tier 1 team to assist in scenarios where the customer is stuck or received and error while completing the SOT via self-serve.

    Note: ACE should only be contacted through the ASE escalation path. ASE will engage ACE and get back to you with an appropriate response. For more information, see TSG 3093263.
  • Service Administrator Not Updated After OMS Transfer to Modern. 

    When an OMS subscription is transferred to Modern, the Service Administrator (SA) remains on the subscription and continues to have access to the subscription. Customers do not have the option to change the SA (the option is greyed out), nor can support perform the operation for external subscriptions.

    In these cases, engage ASE by following the guidance in TSG 3093263. ASE will not remove the SA, but will instead update the SA to the Account Owner's email ID.
    • When engaging ASE, mention the reason for the SA update (for example: SA left the company, security/compliance concern).
