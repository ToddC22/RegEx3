Procedure: Premier Non-Decrement

[Asset 4462485]

Who should use this article?

Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Technical Advisor | Team Manager

What is this article about?

Customers and Partners are eligible for a refund on incident charges when the incident is within policy or for CPE (Customer Partner Experience) reasons. This procedure details how to handle incidents where a request is made for the whole or partial refund or non-decrement of a case.

1. Steps

General

  • Engineers are not to close out any Service Requests as non-decrement by using the Manager Override function:
    • All non-decrement decisions or refund decisions for Premier customers are to be handled by the Incident Manager (IM).
  • It is the responsibility of the engineer to set the correct Issue Type, SR Type and Product before case closure. For Non-Chargeable Issue types and Products providing unlimited support these values then automatically determine whether a case is customer billable.

Details

Note: Please also see the Deviations or workarounds section for Windows 10 upgrade and installation.

  1. When closing a Service Request always set the Issue Type and the Product and SR Type accurately:
    • For more information on Malware or Security Bulletins or Service Request type usage see 4456926 | Procedure: Malware and Security Bulletin Support.
  2. If a Customer or Partner closes their Service Request prior to troubleshooting:
    • Notify their Incident Manager (IM) and provide the reason why, they may decide to give a whole or partial refund, or non-decrement.
  3. If the Customer or Partner requests a full or partial refund or non-decrement of chargeable SR and Issue Types:
    • Set expectations that refund or non-decrement requests on chargeable Issue Types and SR Types are handled by their Incident Manager (IM).
  4. If a Service Request is closed with a non-chargeable Issue Type listed in the table below no further action is required, they are automatically handled by the system and do not require further approval.
  5. For chargeable Issue Types send an email to the Customer or Partner and their Incident Manager (IM) including the reason for refund or non-decrement, ensuring the email is tracked in the support case:
     
    • Notify the Customer or Partner that their Incident Manager (IM) will be in contact with them regarding their refund or non-decrement request.
    • If the Incident Manager (IM) is not available escalate to their designated alternative contact or manager.
    • Redirect any further questions on charging to their Incident Manager (IM).
  6. If the SR is ready to be closed, proceed with closure and leave the Decrement field to the default setting (Decrement Type field set to "Hourly" or "Incident").
  7. When CSAT is at risk, use 4457522 | Procedure: Recovery.

5. Frequently asked questions

Q: Does a Premier customer have a way to ask for a refund on Azure monthly payments due to service outage (just like Office 365 customers can)?

A: For Service down, Yes. All customers have the ability to ask for a refund on the Azure Service itself, not on the monthly support subscription or a non-Azure offering contract. This would be the same for Premier.

Q: What is the Azure offering decrement situation since the offerings launch back in November 2012?

A: Our customers pay for their incidents as part of the Developer, Standard, Professional Direct, & Premier Support for Azure (PSfA) offerings:

  • These offerings have a monthly fee with unlimited support
  • A PSfA should be used if they plan to use it primarily when working with Azure
  • Premier customers who do not use their PSfA agreement will have their incident(s) decremented by default
  • Customers are not required to submit a case during an outage since they should be received updates via the Service Health Dashboard and via their IM/ADM
