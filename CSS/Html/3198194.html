1. Overview

1.1 What is the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program?

The Microsoft Advertising Partner Program is a loyalty program for agencies, channel, and technology partners that invest in the Microsoft Advertising platform. At its core, it is a relationship between Microsoft and the most competent and qualified partners in the advertising industry to ensure our current and future clients exceed their goals:

  • Distinguishes partners through public recognition.
  • Enables partner growth through exclusive access to training, technical, marketing and sales resources.
  • Advertisers admitted to the program will have exclusive access to a partner portal.
  • The objective is to surface all partner related information in a single dashboard.

Program structure:

  • The Microsoft Advertising Partner Program consists of three tiers: Elite, select, and partner and are available for agency, channel, and technology partners.
  • These tiers are based on criteria such as partner size, engagement levels and annual spend.
  • Partners can move up or down tier levels depending on the results of an annual performance review that takes place in December timeframe.
  • Program benefits increase as a company upgrades through the tiers.

For more information about the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program, visit Microsoft Advertising Partner Program.

Note: This program is separate from the Microsoft Partner Network and the Microsoft Advertising Accredited Professional Member Directory. Some customers calling in with questions about being a partner might be confused about the difference between being in the Partner Directory and being in the Membership Directory. Read this article, including the clarifying questions in the FAQ to determine the root cause of their question.

2. Eligibility criteria

2.1 Overview

Existing agency customers, resellers (aka channel partners), and tool providers (aka technology partners) are eligible for the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program.

  • The Microsoft Advertising Partner Program is not available to direct advertisers.
  • Eligibility and participation are based on meeting a set of criteria, including being an existing Microsoft Advertising customer for at least six months and maintaining a minimum combined total Microsoft Advertising Network spend for the geographies that you serve.
  • Partners must have at least six months of revenue in Microsoft Advertising (except for channel partners) to qualify.
  • A new agency not advertising in Microsoft Advertising will have to go through the new customer set up process first and demonstrate spend for six months before they can be evaluated for the program.  

If an agency is incorrectly set up with a direct advertiser shell in Microsoft Advertising, they will have to adjust their set up to use an agency shell or with the use of their account to manage on behalf of others, to be qualified.

3. New partner nominations – program enrollment

Any existing Microsoft Advertising agency, channel, or technical partner is eligible to join the program at the partner tier year-round if they meet the entry level requirements specified above. The enrollment period for select and elite tiers occurs from January to March each calendar year.

If a prequalified partner (that meets the requirements listed above) wants to become an enrolled partner, provide this page to them so they can apply directly:
https://about.ads.microsoft.com/en-us/partners/request-access-information

Note: There is no need to keep the ticket open after providing this link. The partner program will be in contact with the customer regarding their eligibility to enroll within 72 hours if they meet the qualifications.

4. Agency set-up requirements

To enroll in the Partner Program, an agency must correctly be setup as an agency in Microsoft Advertising. Prior to multi-account-linking, an agency was identified as having an agency shell CID type by selecting the become an agency option on the accounts and billing page. This feature no longer exists.

Primary use as agency:
Now, the Primary use setting is used to identify an agency. The primary use is selected during the sign-up process and external users cannot change this setting after sign-up. Only internal users can change the primary use after signup, which means an agency will likely not be aware of this setting or how to change it if incorrectly setup.

If an agency was denied entry into the Partner Program because their account type is not an agency but you are able to verify the business model is an agency, check the primary purpose and change it on behalf of the agency.

Suggested messaging:

  • “Thanks for letting me know you’re an agency. I’ll update our internal system accordingly. Within a couple of days, our system will be updated, and you can apply to join the Partner Program once again. Since your account setting is now an agency, you shouldn’t run into any issues with incorrect account type.”

How to change Primary use to Agency:

  1. Navigate to https://internal.ads.microsoft.com.
  2. Search for the advertiser name (CID search does not work for this support dashboard).
  3. Once the advertiser name populates, select it.

    • To provide services to other businesses as an ad agency (agency setting).
    • To promote this business (direct advertiser setting).

5. Microsoft Advertising partner directory

  • Partners are not automatically listed in the directory. They need to log into the portal and create a profile using the edit profile link on the top left of the page.
  • For a partner to be listed in the directory, they must fill out their profile page completely.
    • Incomplete profiles are not submitted to the portal team.
  • Once the profile is complete, it will be added to the portal within three business days.

6. Support for partners

Occasionally, an agency may have questions related to the Partner Program. If we are unable to address login, eligibility, and status questions through the troubleshooting guidance here, an additional escalation to the Partner Program team may be necessary.  

If you are unable to support an inquiry and are confident it is related to the Partner Program, follow the process below to escalate (using the appropriate subject line and escalation template per below):

Escalating to Partner Program:

Alias

Microsoft Advertising Partner Program <msftadspartner@microsoft.com>

SLA

Five business days
  1. Be sure you have coded the original ASD ticket Issue Code 1: Customer Issue: Microsoft Advertising Partner Program.
  1. Send escalation via email to Microsoft Advertising Partner Program alias: msftadspartner@microsoft.com.
  • form:Subject LineUse

"Microsoft Advertising Partner Program Escalation: CID ##### Customer Name”

  1. Always include the following template details in your escalation (and every sub-sequent escalation, unless there is an initial response):
    1. CID     
    2. Customer name        
    3. Company/Agency name      
    4. Company/Agency location  
    5. Nature of request    
  2. Inform the advertiser of the escalation and tell them to expect a response within five business days.
  • The Partner Program will provide a resolution or follow-up questions within five business days.
  • Communicate the final resolution or follow-up questions to the agency.

Appropriate scenarios to escalate:

  • Agency doesn’t know what tier of the Partner Program they are in and this is not able to be determined using UCM.
  • Agency contests rejection reason for Partner Program enrollment and wants an appeal based on additional information.
  • Agency never heard back from the Partner Program after asking for more information or to enroll.
  • Agency wants to know where they currently stand in the enrollment process.

7. Login support

Our partner team has limited resources, yet they are seeing several inquiries that CSS is empowered to resolve. Follow the guidelines below before escalating to their team:

  1. The Partner Program website FAQ here details the troubleshooting process to help resolve issues for Microsoft Advertising partners encounter difficulty signing in to the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program website.
  2. If an escalation to the partner team is required, follow this guidance:
    1. Always include the recommended template details.
    2. If a follow up is required (due to non-response), repost the full template details or prior communication so that the new POC has the full details. Our ticketing email system limits the historic information their team can view which means a new POC may not know what you are following up on.

Requirements:

  • A Microsoft account tied to Microsoft Advertising account.
  • If the caller doesn’t have a Microsoft Advertising login, they may need to consult their company to learn their Microsoft Advertising credentials, to get into the Partner Portal.

Once a partner is sent an invitation to the program, their Super Admin or Standard User can log in and sign the terms and conditions agreement.

First time login:
The first time the advertiser logs into the portal, they will need to sign the terms and conditions to access the dashboard. The Super Admin or Standard User must sign the terms and conditions. Once the terms and conditions are signed, the account can access the portal.

9. FAQ

Q: I just got my link, but I cannot access the portal.

A: Super admins and standard users should have access rights to sign-in and access their partner portal. Once the customer is identified, look up their CID in the Partner Data Warehouse:

  1. Do they appear in the report?
  2. In the approved column, is there a “1”? This means they are approved to access the portal.
  3. In the T&Cs column, is there a “1”? This mean they have accepted/signed the terms and conditions.
  • If the answer is yes to all three questions - The partner should have full access to the portal.
  • If one of the answers is a no - escalate the issue to the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program support team for additional troubleshooting.

To raise an escalation to the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program support team, email the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program at msftadspartner@microsoft.com.

Note: Be sure to use the subject line and template details (CID, Customer name, Location, Nature of request), see section 6 for more information.

Q: I have taken “the test”, but do not see my name in the Partner Directory.

A: In this case, it sounds like the user has taken the Microsoft Advertising Accreditation test, which is not the same as being in the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program. Refer them to the Microsoft Advertising Accredited Professional Directory page here.

If the partner still is interested in joining the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program, have them follow the enrollment steps. See section 3 for more information.

Q: Does the login page require a Microsoft account?

A: Yes. If the user hasn’t set up a Microsoft account yet, they will be prompted to do so before they can log in.

Q: There are several programs and associated resources related to what people may refer to as ‘Partner’, can you help me make sense of the differences?

A: Here are definitions to some common terms:

  • Microsoft Advertising Accredited Professional (AP):  The Microsoft Advertising Accredited Professional is a Microsoft certified professional program which requires testing certification to become an accredited member.
  • Member Directory: A global list of certified Microsoft Advertising AP members hosted in one location: Membership Directory. This forum allows others to view details about location, language, verticals, and membership status of individual members and the companies they represent.
  • Partner Data Warehouse: Requests for information on this should be sent to the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program Support team, email the Microsoft Advertising Partner Program at msftadspartner@microsoft.com.
  • Channel Partners: These are managed customers that tend to manage accounts for businesses that don’t have the time or resources to maintain their own online advertising. For details on how to support each channel partner business model, see KB 3168877 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Managed Advertisers.
  • Search Syndicated Partner: This is a large-scale content site like Facebook or AOL that hosts ads for Microsoft Advertising customers. Currently Microsoft Advertising does not have a program like Google AdSense that allows smaller sites to host ads for profit.
