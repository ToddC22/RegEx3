Procedure: Escalation

[Asset 4462485]

Description

This procedure details what to do in the event a case needs to be escalated ​for technical or non-technical reasons.

Applies to

Region:       All (Global)
Role:           Support Engineer | Escalation Engineer | Team Manager | Business Process Manager | Technical Account Manager | 
Service:       Premier | Partner | Broad Commercial
Systems:     MSaaS Support Portal, Customer Assistance Portal, Service Desk Case

1. Steps

General

  • Escalation may occur for technical or non-technical reasons.
  • Escalation requests may not be accepted by escalation teams if procedures are not followed, collaboration has not been completed or if a resolution is identified during escalation:
    • Unnecessary escalations create backlogs in the escalation teams, slowing down case progression.
  • For the steps below the following definitions apply:
    • Source Engineer is the current Service Request, Problem, or Task owner initiating escalation.
    • Receiving Engineer is the engineer who will be taking ownership of the escalation.

Source Engineer steps:

  • Inform the customer or partner that you are collaborating with senior resources, however do not discuss escalation of the case until the escalation has been accepted. While waiting for escalation to be accepted the current case owner retains responsibility for communicating with the customer and attempting to progress the case as best they can.
    • Exception: If you need to escalate to an engineer outside of the customers spoken language (e.g. Greek speaking customer to an English only resource):
      • Advise the customer the escalation resource will be English Language.
      • If the customer raises a concern over their ability to communicate in English, ensure the receiving engineer is made aware by documenting the concern in the Transfer or Summary template below.
  • Review the reason for escalation to ensure it is in the Customer's or Partner's best interest:
    • If the Customer, Partner, or Incident Manager (IM) requested escalation:
      • First understand why they are requesting escalation and consider if implementing the 4457522 | Procedure: Recovery would be more appropriate.
    • If you are technically stuck, or a product issue has been found:
      • Ensure collaboration or 4464163 | Procedure: Swarming with peers, SMEs, Technical Advisors, and Escalation teams has been attempted before requesting escalation.
    • If a Technical Advisor or Team Manager has requested escalation:
      • Ensure the request is documented in the case before requesting escalation.
  • Ensure all case information and documentation (per the 4457092: Procedure: Documenting Case Notes) is current and accurate including:
    • Customer contact details
    • Product and Support Topic
    • Scope
    • Action plan (Ensure all details on actions taken already are included to prevent the customer from having to redo previous troubleshooting steps)
    • Attached files
    • Commitments
  • If swarming is available for your technology area, then use the 4464163 | Procedure: Swarming to collaborate in real time with escalation resources. Work with the escalation level resources to assist case progression while retaining ownership and managing the customer experience end-to-end. Only if the Source Engineer’s skillset is no longer contributing to case progression should case ownership change to the Receiving engineer, ensuring there is customer consent and a warm handoff.
    • If swarming does not enable real time collaboration with escalation level resource then continue with escalation procedure.
  • If an escalation resource is required immediately (e.g. Sev 1 / A CritSit) work to identify an individual to escalate the case to directly and not through a queue:
    • You can use MSaaS Support Portal to identify a contact as necessary.
  • Create a new Escalation task:
    • If escalating to a known person (direct escalation) paste in and complete a Summary template.
    • If escalating to a queue paste in and complete a Transfer template.
    • Complete the templates in English (except Japan and Korea).
  • Transfer the entity to the known person or queue:
    • Follow routing rules for selecting queue. If you notice an incorrect queue was selected, then check that Product and Support Topic are correct.
      • You can refer to MSaaS Support Portal to identity the correct queue or contact if necessary.
    • Expected Response time for an escalation request matches the initial response goal by service level and severity.
  • Follow up on all Customer or Partner commitments while you wait for a response to the escalation request:
    • If your SR or Task is not picked up by an engineer within the expected response time send an email to request an update, or engage your TA or Manager.
      • You can use MSaaS Support Portal to identify the correct queue contact to email.

Receiving Engineer steps

  • If Swarming is available for your technology area, then work with your team leads to ensure there is adequate availability within the team to respond to swarming requests in real time. When swarming as an escalation resource you should consider taking ownership of the support case, and customer experience end-to-end, only if Source Engineer’s skillset is no longer contributing to case progression and with the customer’s consent.
  • If accepting ownership of an escalation request from a queue:
    • Review case and entitlement for any special handling requirements (USNAT, EEA, or Azure Government) to ensure your skills or qualifications are appropriate for accepting the case.
    • Review the Transfer or Summary template added in the Escalation task.
    • Contact the previous owner to complete the handover, bringing the Customer or Partner into the conversation as necessary.
      • For CritSits, there must be a warm handover of the customer with the escalating engineer.
    • Meet any outstanding commitments.
    • Contact the Customer or Partner to confirm any change of ownership of their case, next steps and contact information.
  • If you do not speak the customer's primary language and you experience difficulties with communication:
    • Engage the source engineer for assistance according to the contact details and their working hours provided in the escalation handover.
    • Use translation services should the source engineer be unavailable.
