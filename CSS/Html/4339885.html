Summary


Who should follow this procedure?
  • All Microsoft and vendor resources supporting Office 365 Premier cases in Rave, such as Cloud Knows Premier (CKP POD) engineers, Project Beacon engineers, in-house Premier Support Engineers and Premier Escalation Engineers supporting Office 365 cloud and hybrid product.
What is this procedure about?
  • This procedure provides a general end-to-end case workflow to support Premier customer in Rave. It focuses on the new support processes or procedures when using Rave as the ticketing system.
When should this procedure be followed?
  • When handling O365 Premier Support cases in Rave.

Key Notes

  • There may be regional variances in each CKP Pod or Premier delivery team based on workload.
  • Engineers will know their Pod members and know how they can reach out to others in the Pod as needed.
  • Engineers are properly configured in Rave based on their skills, support areas and roles. See KB 4340207-Rave for Admin for details.
  • Rave does not support labor tracking for Cloud support.
  • O365 cases in Rave do not differentiate Advisory or Reactive case type. 
  • Incident manager (IM) information is available by navigating to the company info page in the Support information section on the Profile tab. If the IM information is missing, follow workaround described in article 4501876. 
  • Do NOT create test cases if avoidable. Rave does not have a test environment, every test case created will be routed to Production and may incur cost.
Expectation on Service Desk Shadow cases
  • With every Premier Rave case creation, a shadow case is also created in Service Desk. However, if a Rave case is missing a Shadow case.
    • It could mean that the customer is not CKP matched and the Tech Router created the case as Premier.
    • It is also possible that there are issues with the entitlement API during the Rave case creation and a shadow case is not created.
  • On the Case Summary panel on the right side of the Rave case, you will be able to see the SD Case in the Secondary ticket field.
  • A link to the Rave case is added in Resource uri. Click View additional case details link, Resource uri Is added with https://rave.office.net/cases/a04cb6f4-3e0f-4115-958f-49b906047a9a (example).

  • Shadow cases are designed to trigger the Premier CritSit process when a Premier customer opens a critical or Sev A Rave case.

Procedure

Support engineers should follow the Case Management Standards procedure along with the following:

New Case

The following steps provide important details about what happens when a Premier customer calls the Office 365 Support phone number to open a new case, or create a web case from the Office 365 Admin portal.

  1. The case will be routed to CSS Premier Support.
  2. All web cases created from the Office 365 admin portal are automatically entitled as a Premier O365 case.
  3. Only in-house Premier Support Engineers will be able to see and work on the web cases with PTSolve or Premier Escalation tag (Yellow diamond indication). 
  4. Only O365 Admins can create support cases. If a person who is a contact on a premier Contract but is not an O365 Admin attempts to create a case, they will be asked to have the Admin open a case for them.  
  5. Engineers will accept cases from the incoming queues based on First in First Out (FIFO). Note: Critical or Sev A cases should always be worked first.
Taking ownership of a new case

 

  1. The following steps describe the necessary tasks and requirements when taking ownership of a Rave case, when the intention is to troubleshoot and resolve the issue.
  2. The Engineer must mark themselves as Available in Rave before they can Accept a case from the “Unassigned” queue.
  3. After taking ownership of the case, the Support Engineer is required to do the Initial Response by contacting the customer inside the SLA times:

Premier Customer response time SLAs for Rave are:

Sev A – 1 hour, Sev B - 2 hours, Sev C - 4 hours

Unified Support Customer response time SLAs for Rave are:

Critical – 30 minutes, Non-critical - 2 hours

  1. SLA Customer Wait Time (CWT)/Initial Response (IR) is met by the Support Engineer’s first OneCloud call or by sending email to the customer from Rave. If Skype is used instead of OneCloud, the Support Engineer needs to email the customer from Rave immediately after the Skype call to ensure SLA reporting is accurate.

For guidance on how to configure the Rave/OneCloud integration, refer to 4462817.

  1. If the Support Engineer cannot take the case for any reason, they must inform the Duty manager, Vendor Tech Lead, Pod Manager or Technical Advisor to assign the case to an available engineer immediately.

Managing Customer Contact

  • Initial contact must be established by the case owner and an internal note should be made indicating whether the contact was successful or not.
  • Customer may provide more than one email addresses at case creation. When sending email to the customer in Rave, support engineers should pull the additional contacts from the case description and add them to the "Cc" line to ensure email communication is sent to the additional email addresses as well.
  • During business hours, the FIRST contact should always be by phone unless the customer requested contact by email only. For in-house Premier support, if phone call is not possible as First contact, set up an appropriate meeting time via email with customer.
  • If the customer is not contacted with the first phone call the case owner should send out the Initial Response (IR) template via email. They should then try to follow up with an additional phone call within 30 minutes or set up an appropriate follow-up meeting time via email.
  • More than one email can be sent during the same day to set up a meeting if required to reach the customer.
  • All email correspondence with the customer must be done in Rave by selecting “Email customer” tab. If you are an FTE skilled for PremierEscalation, you will see the option to “Compose in Outlook” and have the ability to send emails from your mailbox using this option.
    [Asset 4560546]
    [Asset 4560548]
  • Email communications with customer address on To or Cc line are visible to the customers, and are included as part of the previous conversations (history) in the email threads (up to 10 email threads).  The only way for internal emails to be excluded from customer views is to make sure that every sender/recipient on that email ends in @microsoft.com. Then this particular email will be excluded from the email history between support and customer. 
  • For premier Escalation support engineers working in below support scenarios, email communication can be sent using the option of “Compose in Outlook” in the “Email customer” tab in Rave to prevent customer PII/past email history to be exposed to a 3rd party or partner.
    • If communication with a 3rd party (non-Microsoft email address) is needed and that communication should not be visible to the customer.
    • If a 3rd party (non-MS email address) is added by the customer to further discuss support request with Microsoft.
  • If the customer directly emails the engineer and explicitly removes the support mailbox from to/cc line or does not ‘reply all’, then the engineer can:
    • Inform the customer to not remove Microsoft support mailbox from the email header.
    • Add the previously CC’d email address back to the thread when responding back to the customer (should be something like o365pmr@microsoft.com).
  • Follow KB 4457094-Procedure: Unresponsive Customer for unresponsive customers.

Transitioning a Case to Accommodate Language

If a customer prefers to work with an engineer in a different language, use the transfer steps below: (You must be the case owner or an admin to execute transfers)

  1. Select Transfer from the Case action drop-down menu in the Actions pane.
  2. The transfer panel will ask for the Transfer reason. Select Customer speaks another language.
  3. From the Language drop-down menu, select the customers desired language.
  4. The transfer panel will refresh to show the estimated waiting time next to Information.
  5. Click Transfer to proceed with transferring the case.

Case handling
  1. Scope the case following standard scoping guidelines, and using the Scope template.
  2. If the service request comes from a Premier or Unified customer for an on-premise product, the current case owner needs to advise customer that: “This issue is related to On Premise technology <name, if appropriate>. I will route this case to the correct OnPrem support team. However, I would like to make you aware that this could lead to a charge against your support contract, if applicable.”

    Email the customer from Rave, CC the IM and then transfer the case to the right OnPrem support area.

    For Premier support, we are permitted to work multiple issues in a single case.
    • If customer has an additional request that needs extensive troubleshooting and it is within the case owner’s technical capability in the same workload, they should continue to work with the customer in the existing case.
    • You may create a new Rave case on behalf of the customer if needed. This is usually done to facilitate clear discussions during IM and Customer case reviews.
    • If the additional request is regarding a different technology, workload, or can be supported better by a different SME, then you should create a new Rave case on behalf of the customer and transfer it accordingly.
  3. Case severities:
    • If a customer has Unified contract, case severity will be Critical and Non-Critical.
    • If a customer has legacy Premier contract, case severity will be Severity A, B or C. By default, a case will be created as Severity B.
      • To change severity, go to Case action, select Update severity, click OK. Choose the appropriate severity from the Severity dropdown list and click Update Severity.
        [Asset 4469395]
    • If the case is Sev A or Critical, follow the process described in KB 4339172-Office 365 CritSit process for Premier customer in Rave.
  1. VTLs/Tech Leads, Technical Advisor (TA) or a Manager should review cases at regular intervals to confirm the case is on track to meet the resolution goal and to provide the case owner with guidance, if needed.
  2. The case owner must inform their VTL/Tech Lead/TA or a Manager if the case is not progressing properly.
  3. If there is a process breakdown or delay in obtaining a solution, this must be documented in the case along with the actions taken to bring the case back on track.
  4. Rave is partially integrated with the Data Transfer and Management (DTM) platform and if an attachment needs to be sent or received, see KB 2895143 for instruction on its proper usage.
  5. If a case log needs to be purged from a Rave case due to PII leakage concerns you should confirm this action with the SEM or TM and then submit the Rave case number and request details via http://aka.ms/o365SupportHelp as a non-critical issue.
    Note: Engineers need to describe which part of the case should be removed in the request to the email alias. This is an example of a description: “Customer accidentally included a password in the service request, please remove that password info from the ticket.”
  6. Consult with your VTL/Tech Leads, TA or a Manager if any assistance is needed due to the technical complexity of the case or when dealing with technology that is outside of our support boundaries. SEEs may be involved as needed.
    • This includes using Request Assistance feature when receiving a Root Cause Analysis request (in addition to the standard PIR - can be found in LYNX by typing the incident ID, e.g. EX123456.) from a premier customer.
  7. For assistance within the same Pod, an engineer may use the Pod collaboration process via a face-to-face discussion, Skype, Yammer, IM, phone, distribution list, or Microsoft teams. If live collaboration is required to resolve a case, an Escalation Engineer may join the call with the customer and the Support Engineer.
  8. For “Call me now” case, please refer to Call me now process under the section of “Case handling” in article 4024872: Office 365 Modern Support case workflow process for Pod.

Setting Expectations

  • Whenever we need to collaborate, transfer, or escalate a case, the case owner needs to ensure that the collaboration partner has a firm understanding of the help required, the expected response time and they must ensure that they document "who and what" in the Internal note section.
  • Always set proper expectations with the customer before transferring a case or introducing a collaboration partner. You should warm transfer the case if possible when re-assigning the case.

Note: Please document customer contact attempts in the internal case notes section.

If a Premier Rave case needs to be transferred to Service Desk for case ownership change, and there is no direct transfer path in Rave transfer list, follow below procedures:

  1. If support is having a phone conversation with the customer, advise the customer that you will conference the customer with Tech Router to create a new case for the product (non-O365 product/support) that needs to be supported. Based on the country which the customer is located, select the phone number in that region from the contact list to engage TR accordingly. Close the existing Rave case as a duplicate.
  2. If support is responding to the customer in email, advise the customer that the current issue needs to be supported by a different team, you can open a case on their behalf, however, they need to provide their Access ID, Customer Contact First and Last Name, Phone number, Email address, Product and Problem Description.
    • If support is able to collect above required info in email:
      • Call the phone number based on the country which the customer is located from the TR contact list to open a new non-Rave case.
      • TR will ask your existing Rave case number and your name (owner of the Rave case) to verify that you are calling on behalf of the Rave customer. 
      • Add the newly created case number in Rave case notes and case closure email to the customer (see case closure template).
      • Close Rave case as a duplicate.
    • If support is not able to collect required info or TR is not able to create a new case based on the info you provided, reply back to the customer and ask them to contact their company CSM or IM to open a new premier case. Close the existing Rave case as a duplicate.
  3. In the regions where support engineers have Service Desk access and are trained on how to properly create new SD cases, they can create cases by themselves instead of calling TR after receiving required information from customer.

Closing a Pod Case

When closing POD cases you should follow the documented Case Closure procedure. If the customer or partner would like you to work on additional issues:

  • If you are comfortable that you understand the problem and feel that you can offer sound advice with significant effort, you should answer the question.
  • If the problem is in the same workload, you believe it will require extensive troubleshooting and it is within your technical capability, you should continue to work with the customer.
  • You may create a new case on behalf of the customer if needed. This is usually done to facilitate clear discussions during IM and Customer case reviews
  • If the issue is regarding a different technology/workload or can be supported better by a different SME, create a new case on behalf of the customer and transfer accordingly.

Engineers can re-open a Rave case by performing below actions:

  1. Select Reopen from the Case action drop-down list in the Actions pane.
  2. In the Reopen screen, chose the Reason, then click Reopen. Click OK on the confirmation screen.
  3. The case status becomes Active and this will be reflected in the case history.

When a re-opened case is received, you should work the case based on the following guidelines:

  • If at any time, the customer requests to work with the original engineer or work with someone in their time zone, document the request and transfer the case. DO NOT attempt to influence the customer to transfer the case.
  • If unable to reach the customer due to time zone differences, document the attempt, do not strike, and transfer the case to the original case owner. This should also be done for those cases that are created based on the first guideline listed here.
  • If the problem mentioned during reopening of the case is unclear, the case owner should follow up with the customer to better understand the problem and then work the case based on the guidelines above. Please contact your TA if there is any concern.

All closed cases can be found in Rave via Search. However, case PII will be scrubbed 30 days after the case close date.

Customer Feedback
  1. Engineers can select Get Feedback from the Case Actions dropdown. A survey is being triggered after confirming “OK”. If Get Feedback action is not manually triggered from Case actions, upon case closure, a survey will be sent to the customer in the widget by default.

    Notes:

    • This action should be used only when solution was confirmed by customer and case is ready to be closed.
    • Using this option will set the Case status on “Pending” in Rave, but will show as “closed” for the customer in the widget after survey is completed.
      • If you are on a call with the customer (using OneCloud) and the customer wants phone IVR, you can then enable phone survey. That will disconnect your call with the customer’s and LMI session, then transfer them to IVR.
        • IVR flow is:

          PleaseTakeTheSurvey

          Don’t hang-up just yet, please take a short survey about the support you just received.

          SurveyQuestion_Cu

          Use the numbers on your phone to rate your support experience on a scale of one through five. One means poor, and five means excellent.

          SurveyResolved

          Did we resolve this issue to your satisfaction? Press 1 for yes and 2 for no.

          SurveyMoreFeedback

          Thanks for your feedback. If there is anything else you want to tell us, press one to leave a message. Otherwise, press two or just hang up.

          SurveyVoicemailFeedback

          Please leave your message after the beep.
      • If the customer does not want phone IVR, you can either:
        1. Enable widget feedback which will set the case as pending and allow the customer to provide feedback via the widget.
        2. Do nothing and when you close the case, widget survey will be enabled.
      • Customer has 24 hours to complete the widget survey, if not completed, a feedback email will be sent to the admin associated to the case the next day. The email survey is available to the customer indefinitely. (For questions on any KPI calculation, reach out to your regional/local Lead.) 
  2. The customer can access the widget survey from the admin portal Need Help section.
