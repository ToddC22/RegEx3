What is this article about?

This article provides information and guidelines about the types of Surface paid incident support, options, and pricing. Use when a customer contacts support with a Surface device that is currently out of warranty (OOW) and is seeking support and assistance.

1. Overview

Customers with out of warranty (OOW) Surface devices needing technical support of any kind is subject to an OOW paid incident fee called Premium Support Surface. The fee must be collected before troubleshooting the customer’s issue.

If device servicing/exchange is determined necessary through troubleshooting, you are to refund the customer the OOW paid incident fee and charge the OOW repair/exchange fee should the customer choose to proceed.

  • Customers with an active Assure Software Support plan are entitled to receive free troubleshooting support.
  • Customers with Assure are not exempt from paying the standard device servicing/exchange OOW fee in cases where a device exchange is necessary.

Note: For Australia warranty support, consumers have the right to repair, replace, or refund in certain situations. See KB 4100081 Guideline: Microsoft and the Australian and New Zealand Consumer Law for more information. Where a consumer has that right, you must follow the ACL policy and you must not charge or collect the incident fee.

2. General call flow

As the contact begins, you must quickly verify the customer’s issue and verify the state of the customer’s entitlement to receive support.

  • When you determine a device is OOW, the customer must pay the Surface OOW Diagnostic fee before troubleshooting can begin.
  • If the customer device needs to be serviced/exchanged to resolve the issue, you will refund the OOW incident fee in lieu of the device servicing fee being charged. If the issue can be resolved through troubleshooting and does not require device servicing, the customer will be charged the OOW incident fee for the services rendered.

You are expected to build rapport with the customer to diagnose the customer’s needs and determine the best option for the customer (either the OOW Paid Incident or resources for self-help if the issue would not require servicing/exchange).

  • Note: If OOW servicing options are not available/offered for the customer’s device type, the Surface OOW Diagnostic fee should be refunded post troubleshooting If it is determined that their device requires servicing to resolve their issue. See KB 3164686 Procedure: Surface: Refunding Out Of Warranty (OOW) paid support incident and check the refund status for complete details.

    Messaging: “Your Surface device is currently outside the warranty period. I can help you troubleshoot your issue for <insert price>. If troubleshooting determines your device needs servicing, I will refund this fee in lieu of the repair/exchange fee, so you will not be charged twice. Would you like to proceed?”

Remember, before subjecting the customer to these fees identify what the issue falls under. If the scenario is an OOW exempt scenario, the customer would not have to pay for OOW support. Alternatively, do not allude the customer to expect that they will receive free troubleshooting if their device does not fall into the OOW exempt coverage. Check section 4.2 OOW Exempt Scenarios for more details.

Not all countries have Surface paid support options available. Check section 3.1 for more details about global pricing and countries in scope.

For guidance around requirements on authenticating the customer’s account, see KB 4506610 Procedure: Microsoft account verification.

3. Entitlement and offers available

When the customer is OOW, what you can offer the customer is the Surface OOW Diagnostic offer. The customer needs to purchase this service before you start OOW troubleshooting. While the customer having Assure on their account provides entitlement to free support, Assure cannot be sold by a Surface advocate as an offer. To avail of Assure, the customer must have Assure on their account prior to reaching Surface support for assistance.

Note: Some customer situations are OOW exempt, meaning they will not have to pay for troubleshooting support even while their device is OOW. However, this does not exempt the customer from paying the device servicing/exchange fee to have their device exchanged. See section 4.2 for complete details.

3.2 Determine if the customer has Assure, Microsoft 365, or previously paid for Surface OOW diagnostic

Verify subscription

  1. Verify the customer account.
  2. In the Services section of MST, select the Services in topic (refer to KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST)).
  3. Select Manage Subscriptions.
  4. Look for the Service in topic from the below list and check if the status is Active.
    • Assure
    • Microsoft 365
  5. After confirming the subscription in topic is active, proceed to provide technical support to the customer.

Note: As a reminder, do not update/change or refund any subscription services that display. Use this view to confirm the customer’s subscription.

4. Validating warranty status

Validating warranty status - device registered

With the customer’s Microsoft account verified or authenticated, check the Devices section. See KB 4053533 TUG: MST: Devices section.

Validating warranty status - device not registered

If the customer does not have their device registered, registering the device in MST requires account authentication. However, you can follow steps below to quickly verify the state of the customer’s warranty without having to register the device.

  1. In ASD, select New Case.
  2. Go to Advanced Search.
  3. Select Check Warranty.
  4. Enter the serial number into Search by Serial Number field. Select Go. [Asset 4094100]
  5. Results are returned.

[Asset 4094104]

Once the customer warranty status is identified provide options accordingly.

  • If the customer device is OOW, and the customer accepts the OOW paid troubleshooting fee, proceed with charging the customer.
  • If the customer device is OOW, but the customer declines the OOW paid troubleshooting fee, document the outcome of the interaction. 
  • If the customer device is IW, proceed with troubleshooting.

4.1 Chat advocate – customer is OOW

Do not provide any part of a customer’s credit card information via chat. Only the customer can provide credit card information to help identify the right payment instrument.

Suggested response to confirm payment instrument: “Which credit card would you like to use for the purchase? Please provide the last four digits of the card. (Please note, do not enter your credit card number in the chat window.)”

If a customer would like to pay with a payment method that is not on their Microsoft account, they need to add the payment instrument to their Microsoft account themselves.

Suggested response for adding a payment instrument to their Microsoft account: “<Customer name>, you can add another payment instrument by going to account.microsoft.com. Select Your info in the blue banner. Then select Edit your billing info.”

4.2 OOW exempt scenarios

Refer to the following for scenarios exempt from Premium Support Surface.

  • All issues in ASD coding to Feature = Windows OS -> Topic = Install / Upgrade / Activate
  • Windows Update related issues
  • All issues listed on KB 4072657 Informational: Surface: Technical guides and known issues landing page
  • Rapid Response Team (RRT)
  • Troubleshooting Surface accessories
  • Checking on repair/servicing status
  • Processing OOW repair orders for devices with physical damage such as ‘cracked screen’ where troubleshooting is not necessary
  • Sending an e-mail with self-help resources when offer declined
  • Non-troubleshooting related pre-sales feature/accessory questions
  • Account questions and assistance
  • Customers with an active Assure Software Support plan or Microsoft 365 subscription

Follow the appropriate guidance per scenario as outlined in the following articles. Not all options are available through Surface support. We do not have a support option dedicated to Microsoft accounts at this time. Some examples of areas we can provide guidance/assist with:

Thank you for contacting Surface Support. In resolving your issue, you purchased Premium Support Surface. Here is the Service Agreement related to your support session.

If you require assistance in the future, the following options are always available:

  • Get help from other Surface owners at the Surface forum.
  • Purchase Premium Support Surface for [insert price] and receive assistance from Surface Support.

[Remove if SDT not available in customer language] Additionally, if your device powers on and Windows boots properly, running the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit (SDT) may resolve most issues.

Thank you for choosing Surface and have a great day!

Regards,

7. Choosing the correct Entitlement Outcome value on the incident form

The Entitlement Outcome must be selected on the Incident form before you are ready to close the customer record. Choose the option based on the table below.

Entitlement Outcome values in ASD

Definition on when to choose

IW: Device/Assure/Within 30 days of Paid

The customer is considered IW due to one of the following conditions being true:

  • Device is IW (excludes Microsoft Complete).
  • Customer has an active Assure subscription.
  • Customer is following up within 30 days of a previously paid support incident of the same issue.

IW: Microsoft Complete

The customer is considered IW because the device has an active Microsoft Complete plan.

OOW Support Provided: Paid Diagnostics Fee - No Exchange / Upgrade

The customer paid for Premium Support Surface, and their issue was determined to require a device exchange/repair to resolve. Customer chose not to setup a device exchange/repair or upgrade.

OOW Support Provided: Paid Diagnostics Fee – Charge Refunded

You issued a refund due to one of the conditions below being true:

  • The customer’s issue was not resolved through troubleshooting and a device exchange/repair is not an appropriate resolution (For example, issue is not hardware-related).
  • The customer goes through an OOW device exchange/repair.

OOW Support Provided: Exempt Scenario

The customer issue falls into one of the OOW Exempt categories and was not charged the paid incident fee. See the FAQ scenarios to understand what scenarios are exempt from the fee.

OOW Support Declined: Diagnostic Fee Too High

The customer is OOW and specifically stated that the fee for Premium Support Surface is too expensive and chose to decline support.

OOW Support Declined: Exchange Fee Too High

The customer is OOW and declined device exchange/repair because the fee is too expensive. One or more of the following conditions are also true:

  • The customer’s issue was not resolved through troubleshooting, was not software related and a device exchange/repair was necessary.
  • The damage on the device is customer induced (For example, cracked screen).

OOW Support Declined: Other

The customer declined to proceed with OOW support for a reason that does not fit in the options displayed above. Provide sufficient case notes to elaborate the reasons why the customer refused support.

Transfer/Redirect

The customer issue requires another LOB/support area to assist. This can also result if the customer issue has complexity requirements above that of Surface boundaries. For example, the customer issue is Office configuration related that would necessitate the need for an Office support advocate to assist further.

Be sure to refer the customer to contact those channels regarding assistance. Currently, there is no method to transfer the case.

9. FAQ

Scenario

Solution

My device is IW but the accessory I am having issue with is OOW. Do I need to pay the OOW fee?

No, this procedure applies to devices and covers.

My device is OOW, but I am having an issue with an accessory still considered IW?

(Example: Video adapter was purchased 2 months before device went OOW. Adapter still has 10 months of warranty left, but device is OOW.)

Confirm that the accessory is IW. If the issue is specific to using the device with the IW status accessory (and there are no other outstanding issues with the device), provide free support, even if the device is OOW.

If the customer can confirm the accessory works on another device, process the OOW paid incident specific to the OOW device. The functional state of the IW accessory would not provide eligibility for the OOW device if it has no issue.

Be sure to check that the customer is using a first-party accessory and not a third party product.

I paid $XX for paid support, and the issue requires device servicing to resolve. I want to upgrade the device instead of exchange/repair. Do I get my $XX back?

Yes. If the customer expresses intent to upgrade (whether through Surface Support or otherwise), refund the customer.

I have an active Microsoft 365 subscription. Does that entitle me to free support?

Yes. Consumer customers with an active Microsoft 365 subscription entitles the customer to unlimited Office and Windows 10 technical related support. Refer the customer to Office support if the customer has an Office-related issue.

I paid for the device servicing fee (OOW paid incident fee refunded) and received my exchange device, but the issue is still occurring, do I have to pay again?

Troubleshoot the customer’s issue for free. If troubleshooting does not resolve the issue, another exchange order needs to be raised. Consult a Tech Lead.

Tech Lead/Super User: Write a “Charged Ticket Recreation” order in MST.

How long does the customer have since the last paid contact to get help again for free? What if the issue is different?

The customer has 35 days from the time of support. This includes requesting re-dos or calling back for the same issue.

Only the issues declared during the initial contact is in scope of free support.

Note: The listed practice is 30 days; however there is a grace extension to 35 days that is NOT listed externally facing.

What if the customer refuses to pay the OOW Paid Incident on the grounds they previously paid for Surface OOW Diagnostic already, or claims to have Assure but your tools don’t reflect this?
  1. Re-verify with the customer where it was purchased. If on another account, pull up the correct account and re-verify.
  2. If on the right account but not reflected, have the customer provide a proof of purchase.
  3. If the customer continues to be insistent, provide a one-time grace and provide the support for free. (This does not exempt the customer from any OOW device servicing fees.)

Note: Be sure to check the customer account for any signs of abuse or flags for attempts to bypass paid systems. If the customer displays signs of abuse, consult with a Tech Lead before proceeding with a grace scenario.

What if the customer refuses paying for Surface OOW Diagnostic and is demanding free support?

Have the conversation with the customer focus around what you can do, while remaining firm around the payment requirements. What you can do is provide assistance for a fee. You can also point the customer to an abundance of self-help topics online at www.surface.com/support or answers.microsoft.com.

Is there a limit to the number of issues the Surface OOW Diagnostic fee covers?

What if a call back or escalation is needed? Does the Surface OOW Diagnostic fee cover the entirety of it, or does it stop at a certain point?

What if the contact needs to be redirected to another support group?

What if the issue remains unresolved? What if the issue is targeted to be resolved in a later update?

No, there is no limit if the issue was declared on the initial contact. The customer can declare as many issues on the device the specific customer is contacting us about.

Some cases may need to be escalated or the customer redirected. The customer is entitled to support for the duration of the escalation.

The customer is to be refunded for an issue not resolved through escalation.

The customer is entitled to a refund if redirected to another support group such as TS or UR.

If an issue is to be resolved in a future update, unless the case is escalated, refund the customer and close the case.

If a perceived issue is determined to be “by design”, the Surface OOW Diagnostic fee stands.

What if the customer is unsatisfied by the resolution/answer provided? Example: Resolution is a no-fix.

If the issue as reported by the customer is determined to be “by design”, the customer will not be eligible to receive a refund. The customer may seek a re-do if they are unsatisfied with the support received.

Shouldn’t complexity of the issue dictate the price for support?

Issue complexity does not change the charge amount. The amount charged is set the same for all OOW issues.

What if the customer has multiple devices exhibiting the same issue, and it is not related to a single point of failure?

The Surface OOW Diagnostics fee entitles the customer to troubleshooting support for the specific device they paid to have supported. How the customer applies the fix to other devices they may own is up to the customer.

If there are separate issues unrelated to each other on both a device and a cover, does it require a separate $XX fee for both?

If the customer calls in and mentions both issues at the same time, both are covered. Cover issues are entitled the same as device issues. If the cover has a different issue than the device, it will still be covered under the same OOW paid incident fee at the time of contact.

If the customer contacts on another later date for a differing issue, the cover issue will need a separate OOW paid incident fee.

Does the customer need to pay the Surface OOW Diagnostic fee first on a device that has customer induced damage such as a cracked screen?

What if the customer demands to just exchange the OOW device without troubleshooting? Can the customer just pay for the exchange/repair fee?

No. Write an OOW exchange/repair order if the customer declares physical damage such as a cracked screen.

If there is no declared physical damage, you must troubleshoot the customer’s issue to determine is a device exchange/repair is appropriate resolution.

What if the customer declares severe physical damage or liquid damage?

If the customer has paid the OOW device servicing fee, their device will be exchanged by the service center.

How are RRT scenarios handled for an OOW device?

Start the RRT interview procedure and do not charge for the OOW paid incident fee. See KB 2764515 Procedure: Surface & Xbox: Handling Product Safety (RRT) Contacts.

Can customers self-troubleshoot to go straight to device exchange/repair?

No. You are expected to troubleshoot the customer’s issue. If the customer wants to setup their own order, they can do so on www.surface.com/support.

What if the customer called in for an issue when the device was still IW, the issue was not completely resolved because of it being intermittent or otherwise, and called back when the device was OOW? Should the customer receive free support being the issue was reported while still IW?

If the customer warranty period has ended recently and/or is within 35 days of the IW contact for the initial issue, continue to support the issue without an OOW paid incident fee. Outside of this, the customer will need to pay the fee.

How are OOR devices handled for this OOW paid incident? What if the customer has an OOR device from a country that has differing warranty period?

Use what is reflected in MST for warranty state. This will provide the IW status if still IW or if OOW.

Is having an ESP or Microsoft Complete exempt from the OOW incident fee until the period ends?

Yes. Having these plans extend the device warranty period appropriately and MST will reflect this status.

I called in before and paid a higher price for the OOW diagnostics fee, and now it is lower. Can I get a refund for the difference in price?

Review the customer’s terms outlined in the article KB 3164686 Procedure: Surface: Refunding Out of Warranty (OOW) paid support incident. If they qualify within the accepted terms (for example, within 30 days of initial contact) they may seek to receive a refund for the amount they paid for OOW diagnostics fee during that initial contact.

I called in before and was quoted a lower price for the OOW diagnostics fee, now it is higher. Can’t you honor that old price?

No, the price quoted was valid for when you contacted us.

The customer has a C-SKU device and is seeking OOW support and/or OOW device servicing.

Customers with C-SKU devices will not be entitled to receive OOW paid or unpaid diagnostic troubleshooting, or device servicing. Be sure to verify the device state in MST, and if the series under Product Information shows up as Non-Supported the customer will not be able to receive support. See KB 2821662 Procedure: Handling requests for warranty adjustments section Customer has a C-SKU Device for complete details on identifying this device type.

I have Windows insider build on my device, is this considered OOW exempt?

Windows insider build is not considered as OOW exempt.

If on a build other than what came on the device, we recommend resetting the device to reproduce the issue. If the issue exists with production operating system, follow the appropriate replacement process.
