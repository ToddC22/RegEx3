1. Overview

1.1 Definition

Determining support boundaries consists in identifying whether the product is actively supported by Microsoft or not.

Return to top

2. Procedure

  1. Identify whether the product is actively supported by Microsoft. Use the Microsoft Lifecycle Policy page to make sure that the product is still supported.

[Asset 4555493]

  1. Identify if the request/issue falls into your area of expertise:
    • If it is within your support boundary, help the customer and proceed to solution discovery.
    • If it is not within your scope, follow the guidance in the appropriate line of business support boundary information.

  1. Confirm if there are any exceptions to the product. For example, although Windows XP/Vista and Office 2003/2007 are no longer supported, we still support Product Activation.
  2. Refer to the table below to determine support boundaries based on products, services, and scenarios.

3.2 Commercial Support boundaries out-of-scope

Out of scope

Products

Customer handling

Commercial Pos-Sales support
  • Windows Server
  • SQL Server
  • Visual Studio
  • Office products set up with an Org ID:
    • Redirect users to M365 Admins, if an Admin, have them submit a ticket through the M365 Admin portal.
    • Office products supported with a Microsoft account through a https://account.microsoft.com/services are consumer products and supported.
  • Access, Visio, Project stand-alone technical support issues:
    • Consumer will support download, installation, and activation for these products, but not technical support beyond DIA. These DIA scenarios are set up through a Microsoft account, not an Org ID.
  • HoloLens
    • Customers experiencing issues with devices can be referred to the Mixed Reality Team. Documentation for self-help resources: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/hololens/hololens2-display
    • Commercial customers can also open a support case using the following link: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/supportforbusiness/productselection?sapid=3ec35c62-022f-466b-3a1e-dbbb7b9a55fb

Expectation is that Consumer advocates can assist Commercial customers with high level product information.

Commercial Pre-Sales support

Commercial customers with pre-sales questions can be supported by different teams within Microsoft support.

The commercial customer transfer destination is determined by product type and/or size of order. There are two different teams that can support commercial pre-sales questions:

  • Business Sales (SMB)
  • Commercial Pre-Sales

Important note: Expectation is that consumer advocates can assist commercial customers with high level product information and transfer them to the Business Sales team that supports SMB customers as needed. (Refer to KB 4457150 Procedure: A&B: Transferring Call/Chat to Commercial Support Teams)

Advocates should inquire with customers on their needs and requirements to ensure the transfer designation can be confirmed.

Guideline to transfer to the Business Sales (SMB) team:

  • The items they are purchasing are to be used for their business.
  • In many cases, customers via purchase order process (PO).
  • The commercial product is hardware.
    • Surface Hub
    • HoloLens
    • Surface Commercial configurations (see Microsoft Store Online – Surface for Small Business for more information).
    • Azure Kinect DK
  • The customer asks specifically about commercial version of:
    • Microsoft 365 or Microsoft 365 Business Premium.
  • Even though customers may not identify their needs as commercial, the size of their large order may determine the customer should be transferred to Business Sales (SMB).
  • If the customer order exceeds the maximum cart limit for a product SKU in online store and MST, refer the customer to Business Sales (SMB).

Guideline to transfer to the Commercial Pre-Sales team:

  • If the customer is asking about commercial products other than those on the list above, they should be transferred to Commercial Pre-Sales. Aside from Microsoft 365, Commercial Pre-Sales will support commercial software products.

3.3 Identify the user environment

A consumer product such as Windows can be used in a commercial environment, and we do not support these devices. A commercial environment for PCs can be identified with the network and deployment configuration. A simple way to identify these environments is to check if the PC is attached to a domain.

If you identify that a machine is attached to a domain, you should advise the following to the customer:

  • There may be company policies that prevent our ability to resolve the issue.
  • Best bet: They should contact their company's IT department for the best support.
  • Microsoft does provide professional support, however there may be a cost involved.
