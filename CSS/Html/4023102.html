2. Policy

2.1. Cancellation policy terms

Cancellation

The process to end the subscription so that no functionality is available. Terminology used:

Partner Center Suspended
Microsoft Cloud Reseller Agreement Disablement and Cancellation
CSP Program Guide Cancellation
CMAT Suspended

Deprovision

The deletion of all data, folders, and mailboxes associated with the subscription. Deprovisioning occurs within 90 days of the cancellation request. Terminology used:

Partner Center Deleted (How-to)
Microsoft Cloud Reseller Agreement (not mentioned)
CSP Program Guide (not mentioned)
CMAT Deleted

See https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/create-a-new-subscription#suspend-or-cancel-a-subscription
For comparison with non-CSP subscription lifecycle, see VKB 2812947: Information about the subscription life cycle in the CPC VKB database.

2.2. Partners must backup their customer’s data before the subscription is deprovisioned

Partners/customers are responsible for removing data before the subscription is deprovisioned. It's important that the partner understands the difference between cancellation and deprovisioning. 2.3. Cancellation policy

All Office 365, Dynamics CRM Online, and Intune subscriptions are set to auto-renew by default at the time of purchase. When the subscription nears its end, customers are notified electronically that their subscription is coming to an end. If no action is taken, the subscription will automatically be renewed for another 12-month term. Partners can cancel a subscription at any time, and the following policies apply:

  • Cancellations of new subscriptions within 30 days of purchase are refunded at 100%.
  • If a subscription is cancelled after 30 days of purchase 1) Microsoft may provide a pro-rated credit for the un-used pre-paid portion of the cancelled subscription in the partner’s subsequent invoice and 2) Microsoft will not invoice the partner for any future scheduled billings for that subscription.
  • When the primary subscription is cancelled, such as SharePoint, any add-ons such as extra storage that are dependent subscriptions to the primary subscription must also be cancelled. If a customer requests that a subscription be canceled that has a dependent subscription, the partner must inform the customer prior to cancellation that the dependent services will be cancelled, as well.
  • If partners want to opt out of the auto-renew feature, then they should set the subscription to Suspended in Partner Center before their next billing date after the 12-month term is completed. If the partner decides to retain the service within 90 days after opting out of auto-renew and the subscription is not yet deprovisioned (deleted), they can re-enable the subscription in Partner Center.
  • More in-depth info about cancellation scenarios and their impact on CSP billing can be found here: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/common-billing-scenarios and https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/faq-about-new-billing-features.

2.4. Cancellation scenarios

This policy and process are for CSP subscriptions sold to Direct CSP partners or Indirect Provider CSP partners only. It is therefore very important that the credit request is submitted by the CSP partner (not by the end customer or the Indirect Reseller), along with all the required information, such as the CSP credit refund form downloadable from Partner Center.

If the Cancellation credit is approved, Microsoft will process the credit onto the CSP Partner’s account (the credit is normally visible on next month’s invoice). It is the partner’s responsibility to provide credit to their customer(s).

3. Billed after cancellation

A partner is entitled to a full refund for any billing that occurs after they have cancelled a subscription in Partner Center

  • If the partner cancels within the first 30 days, they will receive a 100% credit
  • If the customer cancels after the initial 30 days, billing is stopped on the cancellation date, and a credit is automatically generated for the remainder of the subscription term if annually paid or the remainder of the current billing period if monthly paid.
  • A refund should be automatically processed by the system.  If not, partner is entitled to prorated credit for the remainder of subscription term

3. Process

3.1. How partners can cancel their subscriptions in Partner Center

Cancel whole subscription: Refer partner to https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/create-a-new-subscription#suspend-or-cancel-a-subscription.

Remove seats:

  1. From the Dashboard menu, select Customers, then choose a customer from the list.
  2. Choose the subscription you want to manage.
  3. In the Quantity section, reduce the license count. Then Submit your changes.

3.3. How to process a cancellation credit request from the customer after cancellation

CSP Partner should submit a credit request via Partner Center following these steps:

  1. From your Partner Center dashboard, select Support from the left nav.
  2. Select Partner support requests and then select CSP - billing and payments/ Credit requests and refunds
  3. Download the Credit request and refund form and complete the information
  4. Ensure to attach the form when creating your service request

IMPORTANT: If the partner omits to attach the form, advocates should request the partner to send a fully filled out form. This is important to ensure consistency of the information requested from partners and to back up all processed credit requests with appropriate documentation.

Remember:

  • Do not share the credit amount with the partner until it has been applied in the Commerce Management Agent Tool (CMAT).
  • Use the pre-tax amount for credits applied in CMAT. The partner’s account will receive the approved credit amount plus any taxes paid.
  • Note: The case owner always maintains communication with the partner. The case owner will monitor work items for updates or closure and update the SR accordingly and will communicate the resolution to the partner. The message to the partner is that, if approved, it can take up to two billing cycles for the credit to appear on the partner’s invoice once approved and applied.
