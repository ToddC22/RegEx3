2.1. General ERP competency information

Partner inquiry

Frontline Support team response or action

“Where can I find information about the ERP competency?”

Provide positioning to the partner:

“Please refer to the Enterprise Resource Planning competency details page for details on the benefits and requirements of this competency.”

“What type of organizations should consider enrolling in the ERP competency?”

Provide positioning to the partner:

“The Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) competency is intended for organizations that offer their customers deployment, customization, maintenance or software applications related to of one or more of the below Microsoft Dynamics enterprise resource planning (ERP) products. If your Dynamics 365 business practice does not include any of these products, consider attaining the Cloud Business Applications Competency to differentiate your organization within the growing Dynamics 365 partner ecosystem.”

Microsoft Dynamics 365 NAV

Microsoft Dynamics GP

Microsoft Dynamics SL

Dynamics 365 Business Central.

 Many of the benefits and requirements that are specific to this competency are related to these products.”

As of October 2019, AX on-premises will no longer count toward any competency. For partners driving Finance and Operations in the cloud, consider the Cloud Business Applications competency.”

2.2. General requirements

Partner inquiry

Frontline Support team response or action

“What are the requirements to attain the Enterprise Resource Planning competency?”

 

 

Provide positioning to the partner:

“The requirements vary by the enrollment option (silver or gold) and by your business type (VAR or ISV). Please refer to the Enterprise Resource Planning competency details page to view the current requirements.”

Please note that most of these requirements are validated using the information in your PartnerSource Business Center account. Therefore, in addition to the requirements listed on the competency detail page, it will be necessary for your organization to enroll in the SPA.

Ensure that your ORG ID is entered in your PartnerSource Business Center account and that your PSBC account number/ID is inserted in the MBS ID field of your Organization Profile in Partner Membership Center.”

Notes:

Although it is not an ERP competency requirement, a SPA is needed for Microsoft to be able to track revenue in PartnerSource Business Center. (It also grants the partner the right to place orders with the Microsoft Regional Operations Centers.)

The requirements to enroll in the SPA are similar to the requirements of the competency.

For more information on the SPA, refer to section 2.4.

“What are ISV and VAR revenue referring to?”

The requirements of the Silver and Gold ERP competency are based of the partner’s business type. These are referred to as ISV/VAR license revenue in requirements on the PC Dashboard and MS partner portal page.

The partner will need to meet requirements within one of the following options:

The ISV license revenue applies to partners that develop software applications related to Dynamics solutions.

The VAR license revenue applies to value-added resellers (partners that provide sales or service related to Dynamics products and solutions.)

The partner must meet the performance thresholds  within either the ISV or the VAR license revenue in order to attain ERP competency.

2.4. PartnerSource Business Center, PartnerSource and SPA 

Partner inquiry

Frontline Support team response or action

 “What is PartnerSource?

 

“What is PartnerSource Business Center?”

 

 

PartnerSource is a resource for partners that sell and support Microsoft Dynamics solutions. These resources include training, sales tools, demos, case studies, marketing materials, pricing, knowledge base articles, communities, promotions and events. Additionally, users can submit and review support requests.

PartnerSource Business Center is a resource within PartnerSource that allows partners to manage several key aspects of their Microsoft Dynamics relationship including their profile, sales, orders and Dynamic service plan support incidents.

“How can I access PartnerSource and PartnerSource Business Center”

The organization must be enrolled in MPN, additionally they must sign the SPA or the Software Advisor fee addendum or have the ERP competency.  After the partner meets any of these requirements, they will be contacted with instructions to access.

The individual that completes the initial access process will be an administrator. This administrator will be able grant access to others in their organization.

For any additional access issues the Frontline Support team will direct to the Dynamics IT Support team: itmbssup@microsoft.com

Note: The partner will access PartnerSource/PartnerSource Business Center using an authorized Microsoft account (formerly “Windows Live ID”).

During the PartnerSource association process, it is possible for the individual to use any Microsoft account (“Windows Live ID”).

“I have access to PartnerSource/PartnerSource Business Center but need assistance in using it.”

 

Direct the partner to the How to use PartnerSource Business Center page in PartnerSource.

The partner will submit these questions using the Contact Dynamics Operations form. (Access requires PartnerSource login)

“I have question regarding product Dynamics orders, ordering policies, licensing, registration keys, etc.” 

 

The partner will submit these questions using the Contact Dynamics Operations form. (Access requires PartnerSource login)

“I have technical questions on Dynamics products, including installation, configuration, advisory, and break/fix incidents.”

Direct the partner to the Dynamics Technical Support request form.

2.7. Dynamics Service Account Manager (SAM)

Partner inquiry

Frontline Support team response or action

“What is a Dynamics SAM?”

 

“What services does a Dynamics SAM provide?”

 

A Dynamics SAM is an account manager for partners that have purchased a Dynamics Partner Service Plan. The Dynamics SAM can assist with the following:

  • Dynamics Support and Services best practices

  • Service Delivery Plan

  • MPN and Partner Advantage/Advantage Plus service plan benefits

  • Support & Services escalation Point of Contact for all Microsoft Products (Dynamics included)

  • Customized learning plans for partner Support, Consulting, and Sales organizations

  • Partner Help Desk best practices

  • Maximize Service Plan value (when and how to best utilize benefits)

  • Improved collaboration across Microsoft Break-fix and Advisory Services resulting in more efficient service for Microsoft & Partner

“How do I get a Dynamics SAM?”

A Dynamics SAM is a benefit of enrolling in a Dynamics Partner Service Plan.

For details, direct the partner to the Partner Service Plan page and the Partner Service Plan Flyer. (Requires a PartnerSource login to access.)

After the partner enrolls in a Dynamics Partner Service Plan, they will be contacted by a SAM.

“What is the name and contact information of our Dynamics SAM?”

Log into PartnerSource then select PartnerSource Business Center. The Dynamics SAM is listed in PartnerSource Business Center on the top-right of the page.

Go to PSBC - > https://businesscenter.mbs.microsoft.com. Under Partner Focus tab -> click on the Organization Mappings and Details link. In case after 72 hours the issue persists, please escalate to MPN Global support including the screenshots showing the mapping is in place and screenshots with the VAR ERP and ISV ERP values from PSBC and Partner Dashboard.

4. FAQS:

Q: What are the current qualifying products for ERP competency?
A: As of October 2019, performance requirements only recognize Dynamics 365 Business Central, NAV, GP and SL for ERP competency. AX on-premises will no longer count toward any competency. For partners driving Finance and Operations in the cloud, partners should consider the Cloud Business Applications competency. Partners have been informed about these updates since March 2019 and the information is partner facing available on MS partner portal page, by clicking on “Learn more” button within the banner announcing that we are updating the Business Applications competencies to help you better serve your customers’ needs.

[Asset 4540838]

Q: Why is Dynamics 365 for Finance and Operations revenue no longer counted in the ERP Competency?

A: The Cloud Business Applications Competency now recognizes Finance and Operations, and we encourage partners with the focus to meet the requirements of the Cloud Business Applications Competency.

Q: Why are there no exam requirements for the ERP Competency?

A: There are no new certifications made available for NAV, GP, or SL to include in the competency. Dynamics 365 for Business Central certification is planned for release in calendar year 2019. It will be included as part of the competency attainment in the future.
