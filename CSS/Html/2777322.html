Azure Support - Paid Technical Support

Important note: Please remember that we should not try to support customer issues that fall outside our support boundaries.  Instead, we should help the customer get in contact with the technical team experts who can give the customer the right answer.

Definition & Overview: 

Technical support requires a support subscription.  Subscription management support does not. 

Some users may need to upgrade or add a support subscription, those details are here as well.

Customer facing content -   How do I purchase Azure Support?

http://www.windowsazure.com/en-us/support/plans/ for plan details.

Paid Support conversions can be processed through CMAT after the support languages are verified as available (only Pro-direct is limited).

EA customers plan length can either be 12 months, or connected with their EA agreement.  EA customers purchase their support subscriptions from their LAR.

Publisher Support is not covered by paid support and only works on Sev C cases.

For more details, refer to TSG 4344998 (Windows Azure Paid Support).

 

Standard support eligibility/promo is upgraded for EA customer

If a customer with Enterprise Agreement (EA) is unable to submit technical support tickets, and their verified Support Level is listed as N/A in the EA portal, engage the EA team with a task to validate and enable the Support Level to Standard. Customers should be entitled to Standard by default.

If a Premier customer is having issues filing a Premier ticket in Ibiza, it may be due to mapping.

There are three ways a Premier contract can be mapped in Ibiza:

  1. Auto Match – if the customer logs in to MPO (Premier Portal) with an MSA, and they use the same MSA to login to Azure, we automatically add their Premier information in Ibiza (for all customer subs the have write access to)

  2. Cloud Knows Premier (CKP) – based on TPID matching, if the same company pays for both Premier and Azure, or the TAM performed a manual matching in Unicorn, Premier plans will also show up automatically in Ibiza, linked to specific subs part of the matching.  If the TAM needs assistance with TPID mapping, have them reach out to the finsup alias.

  3. Manual Linking – using manual linking, the customer can add a Premier contract by using a Contract ID and Access ID, which will be linked permanently to their Azure account and available on all subs they have write access to

If a customer contacts ASMS regarding their upgrade from any paid support subscription to Premier, you are permitted to cancel the existing support subscription. 

If a user requests a refund for a support contract: 

If a user requests a refund for their technical support subscription: 

  • If they are within the first 30 days AND have not used the subscription to access technical support, then a refund can granted without further approval. 

  • If a user is outside of the 30 day window or has used the support subscription, then normal policies for credit/refund requests apply. 

 

 

EA customers may need to be entitled in the EA portal

If an EA customer comes to ASMS and notes they have made a Support Subscription purchase and need immediate assistance you will need to temporarily enable their support subscription in the Admin EA portal.  If they contact us and haven't made the purchase with their LAR yet, but are going to, please enable the paid support now so they can get the technical support they need and they can work with their LAR later.

 

  1. Search for the EA partners enrollment using the EA Admin page.
  2. Once the EA has been found Do not click on it. Instead scroll to the right and click on the little icon.
  3. Click on the action drop down and then the Add / Upgrade Temporary Paid Support. Next you will have the option on which support plan to add and for how long.
  4. When you select Save, it updates the Support Level and Support Coverage period to what was just added.  The start date should be today date.  The end date as noted would be the first day of the following month but can be edited.

MSDN customers

You can give the customer this public facing content - https://www.visualstudio.com/my/technical-support-vse 

MSDN subscriptions come with a specific number of incidents depending on subscription type. Customers will need to contact MSDN Support first to have them enable Technical Support:

  • Contact the Professional Support team at 1 (800) 936-5800, Press option 2 for "Business" and select option 1 for "Technical Support". You will need to select the software you are encountering difficulties with, or you can also press 0 at this time to be connected to an agent directly.

  • Provide the information of the subscription, listed on the My Account tab of the MSDN portal, and request for the Contract ID and the Access ID to be set up. (This information will be sent via email or on a different call)

  • Once the information is received, customers would now have the Technical option available when creating a support case via their Azure Portal or set up the technical support incident over the phone, for any difficulties on the site.

Note: Microsoft internal employees that have the free MSDN subscription do not have technical support with their subscription.

You can review the webpage showing the number of available incidents at - http://msdn.microsoft.com/en-US/subscriptions/buy.aspx

 

 Under addition benefits you will see how many technical support incidents each type of MSDN subscription gets.

  •  -0010P, -0045P, -0053P – 2 incidents

  • -0011P, -0012P, -0047P, -0049P, -0057P – 4 incidents

 MPN has advisory hours that can be used to get technical support.  Some MPN subscriptions contain MSDN subscriptions that do have access to Azure technical support incidents.
