1. Introduction

Microsoft Advertising customers are increasingly turning to social media platforms for support and solutions. Currently, CSS provides support through the following social handles on Facebook and Twitter:

  • @MSFTAdsSupport Twitter Handle (owned by you)
  • @MSFTAdvertising Twitter Handle (owned by Microsoft Advertising Marketing, with CSS handling reactive support questions)
  • @MicrosoftAdvertising Facebook page (owned by Microsoft Advertising Marketing, with CSS handling reactive support questions)

Our goal with Social support:

  • Deliver high quality service (using our Social Service Standards) shown through strong survey results.
  • Create more options and give the advertisers more choices in how to engage with support.
  • Respond to all customers Tweets/Direct Messages/comments within two hours (during business hours).

In addition to supporting our advertisers directly, CSS advocates will be responsible for engaging with the @MSFTAdvertising handle (and potentially others) as part of our ‘Business Avatar’ process.

This provides an informal, friendly persona and personality to our social posts, allowing us to engage with other support handles and providing our followers with a reason to become fans and share their Microsoft Advertising experience.

2. Support scenario types

Depending on the complexity of the inquiry made by the customer, you will need to reply with either a ‘simple’ or ‘complex’ response. Regardless, continue to follow the logic of Total Customer Ownership (TCO), one agent owning all follow-up interactions, wherever possible.

For Business Avatar engagements, you will not be engaging with customers but with other Microsoft social handles. The aim of these interactions will be to foster a fun, friendly, and team-focused atmosphere to delight our social followers, drive positive sentiment, and grow fans.

Note: Throughout this article we will refer to numerous ‘macros’ in Sprinklr that can be used to claim/edit posts. For more information on macro definitions, see KB 2792466 Procedure: Social Media: Sprinklr: Case management - Macros, team-specific tagging and survey detail, but be aware this article applies across CSS, and is not a replacement for processes outlined here.

2.1 Simple inquiry

This process is designed for scenarios when you can address the customer’s query with a simple reply, or by providing a link to help content or videos. Simple inquiries do not require you to have the customer's Microsoft Advertising account to answer the question.

Note (Specific to Twitter): With some simple inquiries, a solution may run longer than 280 characters. If this happens and you are sure that your answer is accurate, a multiple message response can be an alternative. It is important to include (1/3), (2/3), and/or (3/3) at either the beginning or the end of your Tweet. Each Tweet should be a complete sentence, rather than a run-on sentence. If you cannot give a concise sentence in each Tweet, or if it requires more than three separate messages, switch to the “Complex Inquiry” process, as outlined below.

Simple inquiry process:

  1. Once logged into Sprinklr, navigate to the Microsoft Advertising Support | Agent Dashboard. Here, you will find the My Inbox column; this is where you will find posts that have been assigned to you by the Social Support Lead, ranked from oldest to newest. Note: You will need to claim each message assigned to you by applying the Macro: Claim Message before replying. Doing this also ensures a case has been created (if it is a new case).
  2. Once claimed, you will work the case from the Assigned to Me column. Review the post and determine if you can answer the question with a simple reply, or link to existing help or video content. If you need the customers’ Microsoft Advertising account number, follow the complex inquiry process.
  3. Using the Social Service Standards, reply to the message.
  4. If applicable, copy and paste the help URL into the reply, using the URL shortener labelled msft.it (Microsoft) to shorten your URL.
  5. If space allows, ask if this helps answer the question. For example, “LMK if this helps”. (Always ask on Facebook as there is a large character limit).
  6. Post your reply and apply Macro: Responded – Survey Pending. Wait 24 hours for a response.
  7. If customer confirms resolution, thank them for letting you know and Achieve More, if possible. If the customer doesn’t respond, move to the next step.
  8. After your public signoff, or if the customer does not respond and you were able to answer their query, send the customer a link to a survey using Direct Message and apply Macro: Resolved – Case Closed.  There is no need to send a follow-up post for simple inquiries.

    Note (Specific to Twitter): If the customer is not following @MSFTAdsSupport or does not have their Twitter privacy settings enabled to allow anyone to direct message them, you will receive an error when attempting to send a survey.

Code your ticket in ASD following the ASD ticket guidelines for Sprinklr and attach the Sprinklr transcript.

2.2 Complex inquiry

This process is designed for scenarios where you cannot resolve or explain the issue within your first response, or if you need to view the customers’ Microsoft Advertising account and verify the customer's identity. With complex inquiries, these will need to move to Direct Message.

Use this process when:

  • You determine the answer to the customer's message does not live in Microsoft Advertising help content.
  • You cannot answer the question in your initial response.
  • You need to access the customer's Microsoft Advertising account number and verify their identity.
  • The customer’s question is complex or sensitive.

Complex inquiry process

1. Once logged into Sprinklr, navigate to the Microsoft Advertising Support | Agent Dashboard. Find the My Inbox column; this is where you will find the posts assigned to you by the Social Support Lead, ranked from oldest to newest.

Note: You will need to claim each message assigned to you by applying Macro: Claim Message before sending a DM. Doing this ensures a case has been created.

2. Once claimed, you will work the case from the Assigned to Me column.

3. As this is a complex inquiry, you will need to move the conversation to Direct Message.

  • Twitter: Using the Social Service Standards, reply to the Tweet asking the customer to accept your Direct Message invite and, from within the reply text box, select enable private messaging. In your reply, ask the customer for their Microsoft Advertising account number and any additional information that will help you troubleshoot. Apply Macro: Awaiting Reply – Msg Closed.
  • Facebook: Using the Social Service Standards, reply to the comment using Message (this will send the customer a Private Message). In your reply, ask the customer for their Microsoft Advertising account number or any additional information that will help you troubleshoot. Once you have sent the customer a direct message, you will need to alert the customer. To do this:
  1. Return to the most recent customer's message.
  2. Select (…).
  3. Select Reply to reply inline to the comment.
  4. Within this message, let the customer know that you have sent them a private message and to check their inbox. If they cannot see the message, ask them to check their junk messages. Return to then Apply Macro: Awaiting Reply – Msg Closed.
  5. Suggested verbiage: “Hi NAME. I’ve sent you a PM to ask for some additional info to troubleshoot this for you. Please check your inbox. (This may go into your filtered inbox.)”

4. Once the customer replies you will need to remember the following:

  • Twitter: Only respond to the customer using Direct Message (not Reply, as this will make the response public).
  • Facebook: Only respond to the customer using Message.

5. If you need to contact the customer, request that the customer share their phone number and account number. Never ask for a customer’s email via DM as this is considered sensitive personal data and cannot be collected via a third-party platform such as Twitter/Facebook.

6. Once you feel you have resolved the customer’s inquiry, send a Direct Message/Private Message confirming their issue is resolved. Apply Macro Responded – Survey Pending:

  • If the customer does not respond within 24 hours, send a second reply and continue to apply Macro: Responded – Survey Pending.
  • If the customer does not respond to the second reply and you are sure that the answer provided will resolve their issue, or if the customer responds yes (that the information was helpful), send the customer a link to a survey using Direct Message/Private Message and apply Macro: Resolved – Case Closed.

7. If the customer ceases to be responsive before you can provide a solution, the final status of the case would be considered Unresolved. Apply the macro: Unresolved – Case Closed.

8. Code your ticket in ASD following the ASD ticket guidelines for Sprinklr support and attach the Sprinklr transcript.

2.3 Business avatar process

In addition to reactive support assistance, you may be assigned posts created by @MSFTAdvertising or other handles which do not require break/fix help, but where there is an opportunity to interact with them informally as an avatar for the @MSFTAdsSupport handle.

As an avatar, the aim is to give a human persona and character to our support handle, we achieve by retweeting or making informal, funny, or positive comments on posts made by our partner handles. For example:
[Asset 4518495]

The intent of these interactions is to give our followers a source of amusement and engagement, which helps promote fans and can lead to our material being re-posted and shared to a larger audience.

You are free to create your own comments; however, you should follow these general guidelines:

  • We are fun and friendly – posts should be positive and supportive.
  • We can use either one emoji or one gif per message.
  • Attach emojis and gifs at the end of the message, after final punctuation.
  • Copy and paste gif links directly from https://tenor.com/ into Sprinklr. Do not use URL shortener, and do not use other sources for Gifs.
  • Consider hashtags to reinforce your message (though these are not required).
  • We are One Microsoft – never be critical or dismissive of any Microsoft products/services/teams.
  • We are neutral – avoid any references to politics, religion, other brands or companies, or sensitive topics.
  • If in doubt, think…what would the comments be like if your post went viral?
  • Avoid using trademarked content or catchphrases.

Note: There is no need to add signatures to Business Avatar posts – we are acting as the persona of the Support handle, rather than providing individual advocate commentary.

Good examples:

  • “We’re so excited by this feature! Can’t wait to see it being used to enhance our adverts on Bing! #BingShopping #Shopify”
  • “We’re here to help, any time! Our team is always ready to answer questions and help you optimize your campaigns!  #MSFTAds”

Bad examples:

  • “Need help with your advertising?  Who ya’ gonna’ call?” (Trademarked catchphrase.)
  • “We’re always ready to help! Our support teams are always there, unlike Google’s! #googlesucks #MSAds” (Inflammatory comments targeting an individual/business, and using the wrong hashtag for our handle).

Remember, if you’re not sure about a reply, you can ask your SME or TA to review before you post!

The process for making a business avatar post is:

  1. Navigate to the Microsoft Advertising Support | Agent Dashboard. Here, you will find the My Inbox column; this is where you will find posts that have been assigned to you by the Social Support Lead, ranked from oldest to newest. Note: You will need to claim each message assigned to you by applying the Macro: Claim Message before replying. Doing this also ensures a case has been created (if it is a new case).
  2. Once claimed, you will work the case from the Assigned to Me column. Review the post content, any associated links, and create a reply using the Business Advocate guidance described earlier, and using the Social Service Standards. As a best practice, include your reply as part of a retweet of the original post if you feel it should be shared with our own followers (for example, for product launches or significant industry announcements).
  3. Add an emoji or gif to your post, if applicable.
  4. Before posting, select the MSFTAdsSupport_BAP tag, in the Team-Specific Tags section of the reply attributes.
    [Asset 4531502]
  5. Post your reply and apply macro: Resolved – Case Closed. (There is no need to keep these interactions open for 24 hours, send follow-ups, or send surveys.)
  6. Code your ticket in ASD following the ASD ticket guidelines for business advocate posts and attach the Sprinklr transcript.

Support Lead processes:

3.2 Assigning posts from Microsoft Advertising inbound | self-triage to earned inbound | self-triage

This process is to be performed by Social Support Leads when they see Microsoft Advertising Support inquiries addressed under the Microsoft Advertising Inbound | Self-Triage column.

  1. Access Microsoft Advertising Support | Manager Dashboard.
  2. From the Microsoft Advertising Inbound | Self-Triage column, select the post you wish to assign. All Tweets and Facebook posts are visible from Microsoft Advertising Inbound | Self-Triage.
    • Once selected, the box background color will change, showing it has been selected.
    • Default status will show Not Set -Not Assigned.
  3. Select the Macros icon (wand).
  4. Select the Macro: Self Triage - Pull Msg to Inbox.
    Result: The status of the post is updated to Transfer – Not Assigned status and is no longer visible under Microsoft Advertising Inbound | Self-Triage and moved to the All Microsoft Advertising Support Inbound column.

3.3 Picking up posts detected by Sprinklr’s ‘Earned Inbound | Self-Triage’ Column

This process is to be performed by Social Support Leads when they see Support related posts in the Earned Inbound | Self-Triage column. These are posts that were not directly targeted to @MSFTAdsSupport or our Microsoft Advertising Facebook page. They were posted elsewhere containing support related keywords, which indicate that a support query is likely being made.

  1. Access Microsoft Advertising Support | Manager Dashboard.
  2. From the Earned Inbound | Self-Triage column, select the box that is hosting the customer question that is Microsoft Advertising Support related.
    • Once selected, the box background color will change, showing it has been selected.
    • Default status will show Not Set -Not Assigned.
  3. Select the Macros wand icon.
  4. Select the Macro: Self Triage – Pull Msg to Inbox.

3.4 Referring posts detected by Sprinklr’s ‘earned inbound | positive keywords’ Listening Module to Microsoft Advertising Fans

These are Tweets or Facebook posts that contain positive sentiment which we believe would make the owner eligible for referral to the Microsoft Advertising Fans program.

This process is to be performed by Social Support Leads when they see Tweets and Facebook posts in the ‘Earned Inbound | Positive Keywords' column:

  1. Access Microsoft Advertising Support | Manager Dashboard.
  2. From the Earned Inbound | Positive Keywords column, select the box that is hosting the customer’s post.
    • Once selected, the box background color will change, to show it has been selected.
    • Default status will show Not Set -Not Assigned.
  3. Select the ‘wand’ Macro icon.
  4. Select the Macro: Self Triage - Pull.
  5. Have a CSS advocate create a ticket and invite the customer to Microsoft Advertising Fans.

3.6 Claiming messages that aren’t visible in Sprinklr columns

Sometimes you will need to pick up posts directly from Twitter/Facebook that did not get captured within Sprinklr’s listening columns.

For example, some customers may ask for help with Microsoft Advertising products or services without using the ‘@MSFTAdsSupport’ handle or search keywords, which means they don’t auto-populate in Twitter or Facebook.

Or you may be asked to pick up content on Twitter or Facebook that we need to reach out to, such as business avatar or Microsoft Advertising Fan opportunities.

For example:

“I cannot download #Microsoft #Ads editor. Why #Microsoft why?”

If you are asked to help the customer who has tweeted to us using this method, you can still claim the Tweet and offer support using the following process:

  1. Use the Sprinklr Search (top right of interface) to search for the customer’s Twitter Handle or Facebook Profile.
  2. From the Profile page, select Recent Updates.
  3. Locate the Tweet or Message that requires support and double-click the ‘white space’ of the message to load this directly in Sprinklr.
  4. In the Properties section, select the white space to load the Properties information. Choose Account Brand (In) - Microsoft Advertising.
  5. In the Queue section, edit Partner Queues and choose CSS_Commerical_Universal Inbox.
  6. Select Save Change.
  7. Select the + symbol under the Tweet and choose the Macro Self Triage – Pull Msg to Inbox.
  8. The page should auto-refresh after a minute, and the Tweet will then be in My Inbox. This will show as ‘All Microsoft Advertising Support Inbound’ in the Manager view for Sprinklr.

3.7 Business avatar program – assigning Tweets to advocates

Team Leads and/or Social Support Leads will need to own the process of identifying and assigning Tweets created by the @MSFTAdvertising handle to support advocates. 

To do this, use the following process:

  1. Monitor the Business.Avatar column in the Microsoft Advertising | Manager Dashboard.
  2. Review all posts made (or quote-retweeted) by the @MSFTAdvertising handle within the last 24 hours.
  3. Identify any posts that you feel would benefit from increased engagement. For example:
    • New features we want to like or promote.
    • Marketing events that we want to grow awareness.
    • Customer recognition that we’d like to celebrate.
  4. Use the process for Assigning posts to a Social Specialist to pass this to an advocate for processing.

Note: To prevent over-saturation, we should only create and assign a maximum of two business avatar cases per site, per day. We are more concerned with quality content than quantity of content…not all posts will require engagement. Use your judgement to decide if we can add value by engaging with a post.

3.8 Claiming posts

This process is to be performed by Social Support Specialists to take ownership of messages assigned from the Social Support Lead.

  1. Under the My Inbox column, look for posts that have been assigned to you. You will see status of Transfer – Your Name.
  2. Select the box that is hosting the customer post that has your name listed.
    • Once selected, the box background color should change, showing it has been selected.
  3. Select Macro Claim Message.
  4. Post will be moved to your Assigned to Me column.

    Result: You have officially taken ownership of the post and can begin supporting the customer. The status will show Under Review and your name. Equally, the Tweet will be pulled from the My Inbox column.

3.9 Replying to the customer's post

  1. Under the Assign to Me column, identify the customer post to reply to.
  2. Hover your mouse over the (…) more which is in the lower right-hand corner of the box and a menu will appear.
  3. Select Reply from the menu.

    Note: For Facebook select reply to reply inline to the customer's comment. Do not select the Comment on post option as this will create a new comment on the marketing post and not reply directly to the customer. This will post directly to the Facebook page and be public.

    If this is a post picked up by the listening module or is a message that you have been asked to pick up directly from a customer’s timeline, for your initial response use the Comment on post button.

    A new reply window will appear.

    As your default, Responding As Microsoft Advertising (Facebook) will be selected (this should be the only option).
  4. Input your reply text next to the customer’s name/handle.
    • When replying to Tweets, you have a 280-character limit for public replies; this includes the characters of the customer’s Twitter handle, and any characters of shortened URLS that you include.

      When replying to Facebook posts, character limits are very high, up to 63K. (After 400 characters a post may be truncated, and users will need select Read More to view the complete message. If possible, it is recommended to include major callouts and resolution points before this cut-off, though you can exceed if necessarily as the message will still be visible after selecting Read More).
  5. If applicable, in your reply, paste the ‘help URL’ that answers the customers question if available.
    • Use the URL shortener labelled msft.it (Microsoft) to shorten your URL.
    • URLs in Facebook are not truncated, but Facebook may generate a link preview widget on the message or post along with a link that is just entered into the text.
  6. From Campaign, Social Support should be selected as your default.
  7. Select Post.
  8. Apply one of the Macro’s below:
    • If you provided a solution to the customer's Tweet, apply Macro: Responded – Survey Pending.
    • If you asked for additional information to resolve the issue, apply Macro: Awaiting Reply – Msg closed.

Result: A reply is sent to the customer and your message is moved to the Awaiting Reply – Msg Closed column.

3.10 View response from customers

  1. Customers responds from their account to the social specialist’s reply.
  2. Response is visible from:
    • Under My Inbox Column from the Microsoft Advertising Support | Agent Dashboard.
    • Under All Microsoft Advertising Support Inbound from the Microsoft Advertising Support | Manager Dashboard.
  3. Response will not be auto-assigned. The Social Support Lead will assign back to original specialist by selecting the Not Assigned text.
  4. Social Support Specialist will then locate the Tweet/Comment from My Inbox, seeing that they have been reassigned the Tweet and apply Macro - Claim Message.
  5. From the Assign to Me column, a new box appears with the response from the customer.
  6. Just below the response, you will see the text Reply – Conversations:
  7. By selecting Conversations, you will see the history of engagement between you and the customer.
  8. Selecting Conversations again collapses the history leaving the most recent on top.
    • As an alternative, selecting on the customer's Twitter handle will enable you to view the entire conversation history.
  9. Status will be updated to Under Review – and Twitter Support Specialist Name as assigned.

    Result: Twitter Support Specialist is able to view the customer reply and history of the conversation. To reply to the customer, repeat the Replying to Customer Tweets process.

3.11 Invite customers to direct message (Twitter)

  1. For claimed Tweets, under the Assign to Me column, identify the customer whom you plan to send the invite to Direct Message.
  2. Hover your mouse over the (…) which is in the lower right corner of the box and a menu will appear.
  3. Select Reply from the menu.
  4. A new Reply window will appear.
  5. From Responding As dropdown, Microsoft Advertising will be selected as your default.
  6. Input your reply text next to the customer’s Twitter handle requesting the customer to respond to your Direct Message invite with information to help you better troubleshoot the issue.
    • You have a 280-character limit for public replies, this includes the characters of the customer’s Twitter handle.
  7. In the reply box, select the box to the left of Enable Private Messaging.
  8. From Campaign, Social Support should be selected as your default.
  9. Select Post.
  10. Apply Macro: Awaiting Reply – Msg closed

Result: Reply is sent to the customer and your message is moved to the Awaiting Reply – Msg Closed column.
The customer that posted the Tweet sees the response via message from their Twitter account.

Note: When refreshing the column, it could take up to 60 seconds to see the update.

3.12 Invite customers to private message (Facebook)

  4. A new Message window will appear.
  5. From the Responding As dropdown, MSFTAdsSupport will be selected as your default.
  6. Input your message text next to the customer’s Facebook name requesting information to help you better troubleshoot the issue.

  8. When the customer replies, a support lead will reassign the ticket to you. To keep the communication within PM, any further messages you send you will need to use the Reply button. (This is detailed further within the View private message section and the reply to the customer PM Section.)
  9. Once you have sent the customer a direct message then you will need to alert the customer you have done this.
    To do this:
    1. Return to the most recent message from the customer.
    2. Select (…).
    3. Select Reply to reply inline to the Comment.
    4. Within this message let the customer know that you have sent them a Private message and to check their inbox. If they cannot see the message to check their junk messages, return to then apply macro: Awaiting Reply – Msg Closed.
  • Suggested scripting: "Hi NAME. I’ve sent you a PM to ask for some additional info to troubleshoot this for you. Please check your inbox. (This may go into your filtered inbox.)"

3.15 Reply to customers direct message (Twitter)

  1. Under the Assign to Me column, identify the customer you are going to reply to.

3.23 Case management

Each new inquiry from the customer is considered a new case. Once a customer’s inquiry is detected in Sprinklr, the platform assigns a case number. All interactions associated to that customer's inquiry are associated to that case. For example, you could have interacted with the customer publicly and via private message; however, this entire conversation will be associated to a single case. This makes it easy for you to view the conversation history for an issue.

Note:

  • You can search for the customer dialogue using the case number. As a best practice, place this case number in the ASD ticket subject line.
  • If the customer has a separate issue and it should not be associated to the original case, simply hover your mouse over the ellipsis (…), select Dissociate, and create a new case.
  • If the customer Tweets something (while attempting to resolve an open issue) that is not related to the issue you are troubleshooting, simply hover your mouse over the ellipsis (…) and select Dissociate from case. You would also move the Tweet to the appropriate column using the Macro: Non Issue – Send Message Back or No Action Needed - Msg Closed.
  • You do not need to close every message you send/receive from the customer until you resolve the issue. Once you have resolved the issue and sent the survey, you can apply the macro Resolved – Case Closed to that last Direct Message/Private message or Tweet/Comment sent. This will automatically close all other messages associated to that case.

Multiple cases and advocate handling

If the customer reaches out to us for help with multiple issues (multiple cases), then the advocate who opened the first ASD Support Ticket should create additional ASD tickets for each customer issue and reply to the customer via the separate cases in Sprinklr.

This will help keep each case separate in the tool whilst ensuring Total Customer Ownership by providing them with only one support specialist for the duration of their support experience.

4. Conversation guidelines

Due to having a 280-character limitation on Tweets, we must be creative and concise in how we address issues, and maintain a level of professionalism in our responses.

We always need to consider using proper capitalization, grammar, punctuation, and allowable abbreviations. (Facebook has a Character limit of 63K however, the same applies.) After 400 characters a post may be truncated, and users will need to select Read More to view the complete message. If possible, it is recommended to include major callouts and resolution points before this cut-off, though you can exceed if necessarily as the message will still be visible after selecting Read More.

In every reply, consider the Social Service Standards (as learned in training), allowable abbreviations, and how we sign and take ownership of our Tweets.

5. Follow-up guidelines (complex inquiries only)

5.1 Follow up when there is no reply

  1. If after 24 hours the customer is unresponsive (for either a public reply or direct message), send a second response within +/- 2 hours from the 24-hour mark. If the customer is unresponsive after the second response, you will move the case to Unresolved – Case Closed.
    • Examples:
      July 19th @ 9:00 AM: The customer posts a question.
      July 19th @ 9:20 AM: Social Support Specialist responds to question.
      July 20th @ 9:20 AM: The customer doesn’t reply; Twitter Support Specialist sends second reply.
      July 21st @ 9:20 AM: Twitter Support Specialist closes conversation as the customer never responded.
  2. Code the issue as appropriate, using the guidelines found in section 3.3 of KB 3171493 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Reactive Support Ticket Handling Policy.
  3. Do not send a survey for issues closed as Insufficient Information.

  2. Code in ASD through solution provided and status Resolved.
  3. Twitter Support Specialist sends a survey.

8. Survey requirements

8.1 Survey text

Surveys will need to be sent manually from Social Support Specialist. Since we only want the customer responding to the survey, these will need to be sent via Direct Message.

Sprinklr is not a Microsoft owned tool, it is a third-party tool. This means we have to be careful what customer personal data we capture. Follow the following guidelines below:

This may also be true in event you need to contact the customer for more complex issues that may require a phone call, or screen sharing using the LogMeIn tool.
  3. DO NOT authenticate/fortify customers over Direct Message. If you need to access the customers Microsoft Advertising account to provide answers to their questions, you will need to contact the customer, asking for their phone number so you can verify their identity.

12. FAQ

Q: What do we do if the customer has an open ticket with services/support?
A: If the customer provides us with an SR relating to an existing issue, or during the course of a support case they provide their email address and you see it’s linked to an SR on this same issue, take ownership of the original ticket and continue to provide the customer with support on this issue.

Customers on Social are more engaged and want their issue fixed quickly, so we are in a better position to provide help.

If you haven’t already created a Social ticket, claim the existing Support ticket and work this from the original queue, as documented in KB 3171493 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Reactive Support Ticket Handling Policy, section ‘Taking Ownership of Existing Tickets’’.

If you already have a separate Social ticket open, (and provided there are no escalations) mark the most recent ticket as a duplicate, noting the Parent SR of the primary ticket, and take ownership of this.

Notes:

  • If you are closing a duplicate Social ticket you created, attach the transcript to this as normal.
  • If you are closing the Primary Ticket, attach the Sprinklr transcript as normal.
  • If you are unable to claim the Primary Ticket for any reason (for instance, because of open escalations in the Primary ticket.) use the following process:
    • Code your ticket as ‘Duplicate’.
    • Include an update in the Primary SR, referencing your child ticket.
    • Include the Primary SR in your duplicate ticket notes.
    • Support your customer as well as you’re able and close the ticket out as normal.
  • If you are taking ownership of an existing Support ticket, do not update the ‘Preferred Email’ field. (Adding the Twitter/Facebook handle would block MS Voice survey eligibility.)
  • If you are taking ownership of an existing Support ticket, there is no need to send the Sprinklr survey, as they will be eligible for MS Voice surveys.

Q: When assigning a post from Microsoft Advertising Inbound to Microsoft Advertising Support Inbound where does the Tweet ‘live’?
A: From Twitter and Sprinklr, the Tweet will move to: My Inbox (Microsoft Advertising Support│ Agent Dashboard) or Microsoft Advertising Support Inbound (Microsoft Advertising Support│ Manager Dashboard) and remain there unless moved back to @MSFTAdvertising.

Q: Can we send a link to the customer with an invite to direct message when responding to a post?

Q: How do you shorten links when replying in Sprinklr?
A: Copy and paste your original URL into the URL shortener labelled msft.it (Microsoft) to shorten your URL. This can then be copied and pasted back into your message.

Q: When the customer replies to a message from a Social Support Specialist, will it remain assigned to the user that originally responded?

Q: Is it ok to reply to a question and include all other customers that may have added a comment to the original question on Twitter?
A: Yes. However, if the customer that has added a comment has a unique question, that Tweet needs to be worked individually as a separate case with a new ASD ticket created.

Q: Why did the customer put a period ('.') before the @MSFTAdsSupport?
A: Because Twitter only looks at the first character of a Tweet, customers can turn a private Tweet into a public Tweet by simply adding any character in front of the “@” symbol. Most commonly, that is done by either adding words/text, or if you are already pushing 280 characters, you can simply put any punctuation at the beginning. People most often choose a “.” probably because it is small, unobtrusive, and most eyes will pass right over it.

  • Some examples of this might be:
    • @MSFTAdsSupport why did you lock me out of my account?
    • @MSFTAdsSupport no one is picking up the phone when I call!
    • @MSFTAdsSupport you guys rock at optimizing my account when I call in!

This is important for us to be aware of because it means the customer wants to “broadcast” their Tweet to all their followers, rather than just having a relatively private conversation between them and the @MSFTAdsSupport handle. When you see the period (“.”) before their Tweet, you might want to check how many followers they have, as well as the nature of their Tweet (are they angry? venting?), and determine the best way to handle this interaction (publicly or via direct message).

Q: How do I handle questions that are related to new feature requests or feature release dates?
A: Treat these as support inquiries and reply back to the customer to offer further help and/or a link to the UserVoice forum. (Applies to all Sprinklr posts we receive, regardless of which Twitter handle they were directed at.) 

Q: Do we handle .fraud cases differently in Twitter?
A: All fraud cases are to be moved from public responses to Direct Message (or email/phone if chosen by the customer). In event the account is determined a permanent closure, use the exact same permanent closure messaging from section 3.15. Once this messaging is delivered via Direct Message, cease engaging with the customer via Twitter.

For scenarios where customers are using Twitter as an outlet to express their unhappiness with Microsoft Advertising due to being flagged with a Fraudulent Penalty, which was reviewed and not overturned; continue to not engage and move these Tweets to closed in Sprinklr.

If the customer’s original Tweet is ambiguous enough to warrant collecting further details from the customer and then we find an SR with fraud upheld; engage with your Social Lead to determine if we should cease contact immediately, or seek to verify the customer in order to resend the upheld template.

For escalations, work with your Social Lead.

Q: What is TCO Policy for Twitter Support?
A: Open SRs should always be handled by the same social support specialist, wherever possible. If the customer Tweets back and the case/SR was closed within seven days, this should be assigned back to the original advocate, and the original SR reopened. If the customer Tweets back after a case/SR has been closed for eight days or more, create a new SR and assign this ticket to the next available Social Support Specialist.

The exception to the rule: If the customer replies and the Twitter Support Specialist is not on shift that day, re-assign to the next available Twitter Support Specialist. It is important we continue to follow our two-hour SLA for all incoming messages from customers during business hours.

Q: How do we handle Tweets/Direct messages in a written language other than English
A: Check that the language is supportable by the Arvato EMEA team in one of the following languages: German, Dutch, French, Italian, Spanish or Portuguese. If not, assist the customer in English as well as you are able. If so, proceed with this FAQ:

The Social Lead should create a ticket in ASD ask the customer whether they would prefer to be supported immediately in English, or if they would prefer to use their local written language during U.K. business hours. (8 AM – 6 PM, GMT).

  • If the customer responds in English and is receptive to support with that language, continue to assign the customer to one of your Twitter Support Specialists.
  • If the customer prefers their local language, ask the customer to accept a DM and provide an appropriate phone number and time to call them back during UK working hours.

After this is done, follow the steps below:

  1. Document the phone number and call back time.
  2. Create a new ticket, include the call-back request information and a transcript of the Tweets so far, and transfer this to the relevant Arvato Muenster team. Note that the new SR number you have created your original ticket’s case notes.
  3. Monitor the new SR. Once the customer is engaged with the local-language specialist, send the customer a closure message via DM to acknowledge that we have observed this.
  4. Close your original SR as Misroute > Microsoft Advertising > Customer Inquiring About Microsoft Advertising > Customer Needs Assistance Not Provided By Agent (Microsoft Advertising) > Transferred Ticket to Appropriate Support Team for Support.
  5. Do not send a survey link for these cases.

Q: I need to disassociate a Tweet to a different support team, which has been incorrectly associated with a Bing Places case. How can I do this?
A: If the customer Tweets in close succession to support teams using Sprinklr, the Sprinklr tool will automatically associate these cases together using a shared case ID. You can disassociate using the following method:

  • In My Inbox, locate the incorrectly associated Tweet and select the ellipsis (…) at the bottom-right of the Tweet.
  • Choose Dissociate and Create New.
  • A pop-up loads. Select the tick-box next to the Tweet to be disassociated and select Dissociate and Create New again.
  • A new pop-up loads in the bottom-right of Sprinklr. From here, select More (in the Status section) and update:
    • Country (In) – United States
    • Languages (In) – English
  • Select Create and refresh My Inbox. A new case number will be generated.
  • Within the Tweet Details, select the Envelope Icon to open the Properties section.
  • Locate the Account Brand (In) tagging field and select the X to remove the Microsoft Advertising branding.
  • Select Save and refresh the inbox.

Q: I’ve noticed a Tweet that is in scope for us, but which has been answered by another support team. What should I do?
A: Check the answer that’s been provided; if this is incorrect, or a better answer could be given, take ownership of the case in Sprinklr and reply to the customer. Do not use negative language or apologize for the support provided by another department. If the answer appears sufficient to solve the issue, there is no need to take further action or create a ticket for this case.
