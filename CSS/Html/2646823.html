ASMS – Handling Credit Requests and Refunds

Submit a ModTrack request to update this TSG.

Overview

This article describes how to troubleshoot and verify credit or refund requests.

Note that this document does not get into the technical details of how to generate a refund in our tools, but rather the policy details surrounding validating refund requests from our customers. To generate a customer refund in CMAT, refer to the Applying Credits and Refunds in CMAT TSG (TSG 3084129).

Applies To: All Non-Enterprise Agreement (EA) customers who are requesting credits or refunds. If you have an EA customer who needs a credit or a refund, refer to TSG 2658533 and 4094770.

The following is a (non-exhaustive) list of reasons why customers might request a refund:

  1. Technical Support Usage Charges – The customer incurred charges on their account as the result of troubleshooting or diagnostics completed by technical support personnel.
  2. Service Level Agreement (SLA), Service Interruption Event (SIE), or Live Site Event (LSI) Credit Requests – The customer was unable to access their services due to one of several types of incidents.
  3. Billing Dispute – The customer is disputing charges on their invoice (either through a billing mistake or confusion over the billing model).
  4. Microsoft Azure Discount Agreement – The customer understands that they have a special discount agreement with Microsoft and should not be charged the billed amount for services.
  5. Internal Customer Credit Request – An internal customer received charges on their personal credit card for usage incurred for Microsoft business. (This does not apply to personal use.)
  6. Proactive Customer Credits and Refunds – This is a request coming in from other Azure Product Groups that have caused an issue and wish to proactively extend a credit or a refund.
  7. Bankruptcy – A customer contacts us letting us know they can't pay because they are now bankrupt.

Policy

  • Credits or refunds are always applied at the Subscription level as opposed to the Account level.
  • Microsoft can only credit or refund the charges we invoiced them for, and only in the currency they were charged in.
  • Credit Cards: If we are providing a refund to a credit card, we can only refund the card that the customer initially charged.
    • If the original card is unavailable (due to being expired or canceled), engage the Global Financial Services (GFS) to have a check sent to your customer.
  • If the customer incurred other charges by other institutions (such as their bank or due to currency conversion), Microsoft is unable to refund these charges. Even PayOps can't refund a greater amount than was charged.
  • If the currency used on the account is not in United States dollars (USD), use the MSN Currency Converter to determine what empowerment level is needed for your credit.
  • Only GFS should collect banking information from customers. If your customer needs to modify or submit their banking information, engage the GFS team to handle that interaction with your customer. They will ensure that it is done securely.
  • Write-Off: Please follow steps in the 'Write Off (WO) process for credit card customers only' support scenario in TSG 2651536.

  • Do not suggest temporary case closure to the customer. You may receive cases where the request is not able to be fulfilled or resolved within a reasonable timeframe. The resolution may not be available until weeks or months later (ex: quota increase backlog, credit request). In these situations, you should keep the case open until the case is resolved.

    Note: Closing a case temporarily or prematurely can result in a survey being sent to the customer despite the issue being unresolved. It can also skew the data used to track case volume.

Support Scenarios

Credits for the following support scenarios can only be generated once the billing cycle that the incident occurred in closed.

If the billing cycle is still open:

  • Confirm the situation that the customer is contacting you about is supported by ASMS. If it is not, get the customer in contact with the right team as soon as possible.
  • If the situation is supported by ASMS, appraise your customer of the need to wait for the billing cycle to be closed.
    • Leave the ticket open. You will be working from the same ticket after the billing cycle has closed.
  • Use the SR Wait State of situation mitigated, pending.
  • Set a task or a reminder to contact and follow up with your customer after the billing cycle has closed.

1. Technical Support Usage Charges

Exception: If the customer reports working with EdgeCast (our content delivery network (CDN) provider) to troubleshoot an issue, we will need to credit them back for usage charges incurred during that event. Escalate to your Partner Technical Advisor or Technical Advisor (PTA or TA). They will need to work with EdgeCast to validate the test usage. After they are done, the TA or PTA will update the task, and you can then continue to work the case and give credits or refunds as needed.

Directions:

If you receive a case from a customer who requests a refund for their paid technical support subscription, determine which of the following fits the situation:

  • If the customer’s subscription is within its first 30 days and the customer has not used the subscription to access technical support, you can grant them a refund without further approval.
  • If the customer’s subscription is more than 30 days old, or the customer has used the support subscription, normal policies for credit and refund requests apply.

Note: If the customer's request is related to Azure Rapid Respond (ARR), refer to TSG 3084129 for guidance.

2. Service Level Agreement (SLA), Service Interruption Event (SIE), or Live Site Incident (LSI) Credit Requests

Summary: Your customer was unable to access their services due to one of several types of incidents.

Note: When processing a complete refund for a virtual machine (VM), the calculated amounts should include the VM Licensing charges.

Directions:

  1. Check Jarvis for SIE reports that are specific to your customer’s subscription GUID.
    1. In Jarvis, navigate to EventService > Get Events > 00 - Get Events by Subscription ID.
    2. Enter your customer’s subscription ID in the Subscription ID field and Run the operation.
  2. Check the Azure Support Center (ASC) for SIEs, LSIs, and outage information. When the ASC site is provided with your customer’s GUID, it will list events pertaining to your customer’s account that have occurred within the last 30 days.
    To do so:
    1. Navigate to, and log into the ASC website.
    2. Enter your customer’s case number in the search field.
    3. In the left pane, click Customer Details.
    4. Scroll down to Service Availability.
    5. Check for any SIE or LSI events that occurred within the last 30 days.
  3. Did the previous two steps reveal any events for your customer’s account?
    1. If Yes: Continue to Step 4.
    2. If No: Ask your customer if they’ve reported the incident to our technical team or worked with anyone to resolve the issue by raising a ticket prior.
      1. If Yes: You should contact the technical engineer associated with the previous case to gather Root Cause Analysis (RCA) materials. This will help validate if the incident was due to a Microsoft bug or a customer caused issue.
        Skip to the Scenario – Determining Customer Downtime and Possible Credit section.
      2. If No: You should dispatch a [Asset 4498193] to the respective technical support queue to help investigate or validate if any such incidents were reported. Work with the associated team to close the case.
  4. Determine: Is Microsoft proactively issuing SLA Service Credits?
    This information will be posted in a     Message Center alert. The alert will state that a credit project is being initiated for a specific SIE.
    1. If Yes:
      1. Inform the customer that Microsoft will be providing credits to customers impacted by this SIE.
      2. Let the customer know they will be receiving an email with the details regarding their credit and when it will be applied to their bill.
      3. If there are additional instructions in the Message Center post, follow them to help complete your case.
      4. Ensure you properly code and close your case. 
    2. If No: Continue to the next section (Scenario – Determining Customer Downtime and Possible Credit).

Scenario – Determining Customer Downtime and Possible Credit

  1. Review the case details.
  2. Where you assigned the case by a different team?
    1. If Yes:
      1. Verify the case has the following details. If it does not, send it back to the person who sent the case to you with a request to add the following details. (In [Asset 4497459] Case, use a Collaboration Request.)
        Note: CC the person who sent you the request on any interactions you have with the customer.
        • Contact email.
        • Live ID/Microsoft ID/Org ID/O365 Account ID of the users account.
        • Subscription GUID for each affected subscription.
        • All affected services in each subscription.
        • Amount of time each service was down (Start and End time, preferably in UTC).
      2. Once you have the above details, continue to the next step.
    2. If No:
      • Engage Azure Rapid Response (ARR) by following the guidance in TSG 2927087.
  3. Validate Start and End times, as well as which Azure services were impacted.
  4. Calculate the total time the customer was offline or suffered service downtime. The formula is as follows:
    1. Monthly Uptime % = [(Maximum Available Minutes - Downtime in Minutes)/(Maximum Available Minutes)] * (100)
    2. Example:
      1. Assume that the customer had a downtime from 7:00 AM in the morning till 3:00 PM in the afternoon.
      2. The total period of downtime would be 8 hours.
      3. If the month the outage took place in had 30 days, the total Maximum Available Minutes would be (30 days * 24 hours * 60 minutes) = 43200 minutes.
        • (Adjust the month value for the total number of days in that month.)
      4. The downtime would be (8 hours * 60 minutes) = 480 minutes.
      5. Using these values in the above formula, we get: [(43200 - 480)/(43200)] * (100) = 98.88% of Monthly Uptime for the impacted service.
  5. Review the SLA thresholds for the associated service to determine if the customer is due a 10% or 25% credit (SLA website, see the SIE calculation above.)
  6. If you are using [Asset 4497459], create a standard [Asset 4498193], and title it Research Task.
  7. Determine: Is your customer entitled to a credit based on the SLA website and your downtime calculation?
    1. If Yes: Generate a credit using the Credits and Refunds in CMAT TSG (TSG 3084129).
    2. If No:
      1. Inform the customer of your findings.
      2. Ensure you properly code and close your case. 

 

3. Billing Dispute

Summary: The customer is disputing charges on their invoice (either through a billing mistake or confusion over the billing model).

Directions:

  1. Review the bill. Is the customer misunderstanding it?
    1. If Yes: Explain the charges to your customer.
      1. If needed, you can direct your customer to the Understanding your Azure bill on Microsoft Azure online.
    2. If No: Continue to the next step.
  2. Is there a mistake on the bill?
    1. If Yes or Unsure: Follow troubleshooting steps in TSG 3034628 to help determine or resolve the charges.
    2. If No: Continue to the next step.
  3. Is the customer entitled to a credit? (Or do you wish to extend a Goodwill credit?)
    1. If Yes: Generate a credit using the Credits and Refunds in CMAT TSG (TSG 3084129).
    2. If No:
      1. Inform the customer of your findings.
      2. Ensure you properly code and close your case. 

 

4. Microsoft Azure Discount Agreement

Summary: Customers may contact ASMS stating they have a special discount arrangement with Microsoft Azure. As part of the discount program the customer will have received a Discount Agreement PDF document. Customers may then want to verify that their discount has been applied to their account.

Directions:

  1. Explain to the customer that the discounts should be applied automatically to the account upon approval.
  2. Have your customer share their Discount Agreement document with you.
  3. Is there a billing statement available?
    1. If No:
      1. Advise the customer that a billing statement will be needed to validate their discounts. (This generally means that they are within the first billing cycle, and a bill has not yet been generated.)
      2. Proactively notify the customer about the expected billing statement generation date based on Account Anniversary Date. This is so that customer may either contact us later or wait on the Service Request until the statement is available.
    2. If Yes: Compare the rates on their statement to the Discount Agreement PDF document.
      Are the rates applied properly?
      1. If Yes:
        1. Inform the customer of your findings.
        2. Ensure you properly code and close your case.
      2. If No:
        1. Send a [Asset 4498193] to the Azure Web Direct Escalations queue and attach the customer’s Discount Agreement PDF document. Request to have them verify the discount package was properly entered into the system.
        2. Continue to the next step.
  4. Based on the Azure Web Direct Escalation’s team response, is the customer entitled to a credit?
    1. If Yes: Generate a credit using the Credits and Refunds in CMAT TSG (TSG 3084129).
    2. If No:
      1. Inform the customer of your findings.
      2. Ensure you properly code and close your case. 

5. Internal Customer Credit Request

Summary: A: An internal customer received charges on their personal credit card for usage incurred for Microsoft business. (This does not apply to personal use.)

Directions: If you have an internal customer that wants a credit or a refund, on their personal card. This is not supported and against the internal use policy. They need to work with their manager/finance department to file the expenses through msexpense.

B: An internal customer requesting for a credit/refund on their internal subscription.

Directions: All internal refunds/credits have to be initially worked upon by ASMS. The ASMS Engineer will gather all the required data validating the customer's claim for the credit. Once all data/information is collected, the refund has to be calculated by downloading the usage report from the AIRS tool.

Once the calculations have been done, engage the Azure Internal offer team through a collaboration task to approve and apply the credit/refund.

 

6. Proactive Customer Credits and Refunds

Summary: This is a request coming in from other Azure Product Groups that have caused an issue and wish to proactively extend a credit or a refund.

Directions: We only cover customer escalations, and not proactive crediting. Direct the internal Microsoft group requesting a credit on the user’s behalf to instead send a [Asset 4498193]to the Azure Web Direct Escalations queue.

7. Issuing a Refund from the Technical Team

Summary: This is a request coming from the Technical Team to issue a refund for the customer after a technical issue has been resolved.

Directions: If you receive a [Asset 4497459] [Asset 4498193]from the Technical Team to make a refund, follow the steps below:

  1. Accept the task.
  2. Retrieve the following information from the technical engineer case owner.

    • Subscription GUID

    • Detailed description of the issue

    • Who is at fault: Microsoft or the customer?

    • The impact on the customer

    • Start date and time of the issue (to the minute if possible)

    • End date and time of the issue (to the minute if possible)

    • List of services impacted

    • Resource name (relevant name to identify the resource in the usage report)

    • Was the issue resolved?

    • All links related to the issue (IcM, Product Group, or any other links to support the refund request)

  3. Inform the customer since the technical issue is fixed, you will create a grace case on his behalf in order to assist the customer with the refund. Click the following link to create a grace case.

Note: Create a grace case on behalf of the customer no matter if it is Professional or Premier. 

  1. Take ownership of the grace case.
  2. Close the [Asset 4498193]and keep assisting the customer for the refund in your grace case.

 

8. Bankruptcy

Summary: A customer contacts us stating they can't pay because they are now bankrupt.

Directions:

  • Do not credit them back any charges, these accounts need to go to collections and will eventually be shut down by dunning.
  • Do not re-enable a bankrupt customer.
