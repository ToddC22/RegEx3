China  

Customers in China may advertise outside of China (outbound) or inside of China (domestic). Depending on where customers are advertising, determine the level of support Microsoft can offer as defined by the options below:

  1. China Outbound: Managed and unmanaged customers may deliver ads outside of China, internationally around the world. These are broken into Managed or Unmanaged tiers:

Managed: China customers who have an Account team in UCM are likely managed via Microsoft’s Westwin partnership (see below on how to identify and redirect these customers).

Unmanaged: China customers who do not have an Account team in UCM are self-serve and are full Tier 1 Support eligible. Note: Chinese language support for unmanaged customers does not exist. Work with the advertiser to identify the appropriate mode of communication.

  1. China Domestic: While this situation is rare, advertisers who desire ad delivery within China borders are considered Domestic. They do not advertise with Microsoft and will not have a Microsoft account or CID. This type of domestic advertising cannot be supported via our Microsoft Advertising platform and anyone requesting a domestic option should be referred to sogou.com. Note: Sogou.com does not offer support for any customer found in our Microsoft platform or UCM.

3. ﻿﻿﻿Agent Actions

Follow the steps below if an advertiser contacts your support center but needs to speak with someone else:

  • From another center (always offer to assist prior to attempting the transfer).
  • At a specific extension (always offer to assist prior to attempting the transfer).
  • In another time zone where we have a support center.
  • In a language your center does not support.
  • Skilled for a specialized service offering that you are not trained to provide.

Follow instructions for warm transfer in Ticket Handling Manual. If warm transfer is not an option, make sure to:

  1. Offer and provide any applicable assistance to the advertiser and assure him/her that you will attempt to connect him/her with the right person if you are unable to resolve the issue.
  2. Collect the advertiser’s account details and contact information and ask for a preferred day/time for the follow-up. You will either create a ticket with this information or you will include detailed comments in the existing ticket for the original owner.
  3. If the advertiser is looking for a service that has an advertiser-facing line, provide the advertiser with the other department/call center’s phone number so he/she can also contact that department/center directly.
  4. End the call.
  5. Include all details collected in the ASD ticket.
  6. When applicable, transfer the ASD ticket to the appropriate queue using the reference below.
  7. For any uncertainty or issues, escalate to an L2 for assistance.
