2.1 Return for refund out of guidelines past 30 days or non-returnable product

30 days can mean 30 days from delivery date or 30 days from the purchase or download date, times vary and can exceed 30 days depending upon the country. When following this guideline, refer to the Terms of Sale in the country where the customer purchased the product. Typically, you can locate the specifics in section 16 of the Terms of Sale. See below for examples for the US, Canada, and the United Kingdom. Note: account.microsoft.com and MST are updated to allow for returns post 30 days to accommodate the extended time. If the option is available in MST, then you can offer the customer the option to process return on their behalf.

US and Canada

 

16. Return Policy. We will accept returns and exchanges for eligible products for 30 days from the date of purchase or download, as applicable. Simply return the eligible product in like new condition and in its original packaging, along with all parts, components, instruction manuals and documentation that were initially included. This Return Policy does not affect any statutory rights that may apply to your purchase.

Packaged software and packaged games must be returned with their seal intact and must include all media and product keys. As a limited exception, software and game packages that have been opened may be returned during the return period if you are unsatisfied with the license agreement or warranty, but only if you do not make or retain any copies.

Even if you return your products, packaged software or packaged games, these Terms of Sale will still apply to the time between your purchase or download, as applicable, and return.

United Kingdom

16. Return Policy. This return policy is provided in addition to any legal guarantees or other statutory return rights that you may have under law (see Product Warranties section below). Subject to our return criteria, after purchasing an eligible product you may withdraw from the purchase contract and request a refund without giving a reason within thirty (30) calendar days. The withdrawal period will expire after thirty (30) calendar days from delivery.

If the applicable law of your country or region grants you a longer withdrawal period, that period will apply.

To exercise your right of withdrawal, you must inform us of your decision before the withdrawal period has expired. Please contact us at Sales and Support https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/p/?linkid=824761&clcid=0x0809 to notify us of your decision and to initiate the return process. You may also use this withdrawal form https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=866214&clcid=0x809, but it is not obligatory.

If you exercise your right of withdrawal, we will refund the amount you paid for the products and their delivery to you (with the exception of the supplementary costs resulting from your choice of a type of delivery other than the least expensive type of standard delivery offered by us). The refund must generally be made to the original method of payment. You must return the related products to us without undue delay (no later than 14 days from the date you initiate your return request) and at your expense.

As a condition of return, and to the extent permitted by law, the following requirements apply. All products must be returned with reasonable care having been taken (such as must not show wear or damage and must be in a condition that permits us to resell them). Packaged software and packaged games must be returned with their seal intact and must include all media and product keys.

We may extend the right of withdrawal period during holiday or other periods. If a longer period is advertised in the Store for a particular product, the advertised period will apply.

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

Is the request for a Surface or Xbox device?

Yes, inform the customer about our refund policy, and confirm the reason for the refund request with the customer.

If the device is outside the return period and/or technical troubleshooting is needed, direct the customer to contact the hardware support team to troubleshoot the issue. Sales and A&B advocates do not promise an out-of-policy refund on behalf of Surface or Xbox support.

Surface and Xbox support advocates will assist the customer with troubleshooting steps and warranty information, or market-specific options as per local consumer protection legislation, even if they are outside the warranty period.

Before redirecting the customer make sure the customer request is due to a technical issue with the device. Inform the customer are being redirected to a support line that will troubleshoot and assist with the technical issue.

If a refund is requested, restate the 30 days cooling off policy. Inform the customer we cannot provide a refund, but they can obtain assistance with a repair or replacement from a specialist.

If the customer requests a return or refund for a non-technical issue (for example, the customer does not like the product) do not redirect customer. Instead, restate the return and refund policy.

Xbox – direct the customer to http://xbox.com/help for support. Customers can request a call, chat online, and more.

Surface educate the customer on how to open a ticket at the support website. The customer can also call 1800-MICROSOFT (1-800-642-7676). The customer should follow the prompts for the issue and focus their answers on repair rather than sales or return. This will ensure they get to the correct department.

Note: There is a price difference for out of warranty (OOW) Xbox One/Xbox One S customers when setting up their repair online vs via assisted support. For details, see KB 967172 Procedure: Xbox: Global Xbox repair and fulfillment information and options.

Is the request for a Surface or Xbox accessory, or a third-party product like Dell or HP? Does the customer have a compelling reason for the return or is the customer within warranty? If yes, escalate to a customer service champion (CSC). Do not escalate for customer satisfaction or buyer’s remorse.

Example: If the customer has already contacted the OEM (manufacturer of the product) but was redirected to Microsoft, escalation to CSC is valid.

2.2 Return for exchange defective, damaged, or DOA item received

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

Ensure you are taking steps to exchange the product in MST, not Return.

KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST)

If the product is in stock, do not escalate. Initiate an exchange in MST.

If the product is out of stock:

Note: Some of these steps require CSV to be added to the customer’s account. For any case where the CSV to be added is outside the Tier 1 limit, escalate to CSC for assistance.

  • Surface: Inform the customer we cannot perform an exchange, instead, we can help them return their product for a refund and purchase an upgrade for the same price. Reference the Next Available Upgrades Table in Section 9.1 of KB 4465058 Procedure: Microsoft Store Make It Right to determine which upgrade the customer qualifies for. Initiate a return on the original product and use CSV during POBO to price-match the customer’s original purchase.
  • Xbox: Xbox devices should go through troubleshooting before exchanging a defective, DOA or damaged device. Xbox support will need to troubleshoot prior to an exchange of the device (see section 4 of KB 3055997 Procedure: Xbox: Consumer support boundaries). 

An outcome after troubleshooting may be informing the customer that we cannot perform an exchange, but instead we can help them return their product for a refund and purchase a different Xbox offer for the same price. Initiate a return on the original product and use CSV during POBO to price-match the customer’s original purchase. You can offer any bundle or hard-drive size, within the following limitations:

  • Do not upgrade the customer from a standard console to a Limited Edition console.
  • Do not upgrade the customer from an Xbox One S to an Xbox One X.
  • Do not upgrade the customer from an Xbox One S all-Digital to Xbox One S.
  • Third-party products less than $500: Inform the customer that we cannot perform an exchange. Instead, we can help them return their product for a refund and purchase an alternative for the same price. Initiate a return on the original product and use CSV during POBO to price-match the customer’s original purchase.

Note: The alternative needs to be within an appropriate price range. Price range should be calculated as 10% of the original purchase price. For example, a $300 product can be replaced with a $330 product.

  • Third-party products over $500: Escalate to Tier 2. These are sent to FT4, who will help the customer find the best solution.

CSC: Receives escalations for first-party products where the price difference is greater than Tier 1’s limit but below $500. Review the case for validity according to the steps below. If the customer is eligible for a price-match, help carry out the POBO price-match.

2.3 Return for exchange incorrect item received

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action
  1. If the ordered product is valued at more than $100, escalate to CSC.
  2. If an exchange was done already and the customer received an incorrect item the second time, a manual return may be required. If so, set the customer’s expectations on a return for refund and escalate to CSC. The customer can re-order, but we advise they order a different product.
  3. If the ordered product is valued at less than $100 and an exchange hasn’t been processed already, initiate the exchange via MST. There is no reason to escalate, as the system will perform an exchange like-for-like.
  4. If the customer is within driving distance and would like to exchange at a Microsoft Store, transfer the customer to B&M support.

ASD coding:
Customer need: Return and refunds
Customer question: I need to exchange / replace a product
Symptom/cause: Received incorrect item
  • If the ordered product is valued at more than $500, consult FT4 via TFS for investigation.

    If the ordered product is valued at less than $500, perform a cursory check for suspicious activity on the customer’s account. Look in SRs, case notes, and transaction history for activity like high velocity ordering, excessive returns, repetitive gift codes or CSV credits and notate your findings in MST. If suspicious activity is found, review KB     2991467 Procedure: Privacy: Handling social engineering, fraud, and phishing incidents for actions. If nothing is found, initiate an exchange in MST for the impacted product.
  • If an exchange was done already and a manual return is required, consult FT4 via TFS using the RMAops coding to request a return label. Once we receive the expected item at our returns warehouse, FT4 will work on manually issuing a refund. FT4 will also work on resolving the product error, but we recommend the customer order a different product in the meantime.

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

See KB 2896711 Procedure: How to handle risk rejections and engagement with Risk Ops (section 2.1) for how to identify orders stuck in Risk Review and follow the instructions there if needed.

Check the Events tab to identify potential payment issues.

If there are no Risk notes or failed authorizations, confirm the order date. All non-preorder products should ship within one business day of purchase. Pre-orders should ship within one business day of the release or launch date.

  • If a tracking number has been generated but it doesn’t show any updates, this is not considered pending fulfillment. Treat this as no movement on tracking.

2.6 Return in Singapore

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

Initiate a return in MST and set the customer’s expectations that they will receive a call within two business days to schedule a pick-up. Verify the customer’s contact phone number and notate in the SR Comments if it is different from the one on the order.

Singapore customers will not receive a label or tracking information for their returns. The warehouse automatically receives all pick-up requests and forwards details to the courier. The courier calls the customer to schedule a date and time for collection.

  • If the return was created more than two business days ago, but the customer still has not received a call from the courier, advise them to reach out to the courier directly. The customer can call +65 6845-6485 and use their RMA number as a reference for the pick-up request.
  • If calling the courier directly does not resolve the issue, treat as RMAops: Label Issue. When entering your case notes, be sure to describe why the courier was not able to help.

The courier for Singapore returns is SingPost.

3. Lost Package

3.1 Lost package (Under $500)

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

Note: This is for original shipments only. For lost returns, see section 9.2 RMAops return not processed.

 

Before providing any support, investigate the account for a history of abuse. Review SRs, case notes, and transaction history for activity like high velocity ordering, excessive returns, repetitive gift codes or CSV credits and notate your findings in MST.

  • If suspicious activity is found, see KB 2991467 Procedure: Privacy: Handling social engineering, fraud, and phishing incidents for actions.
    • If a replacement was done already and the shipment is lost for the second time, FT4 needs to review the order. Set the customer’s expectations that additional investigation needs to be done and escalate to CSC. Treat as a lost package over $500 (don’t use TracerOps coding) to ensure a complete investigation.
  • If nothing is found, proceed with the next steps.

Verify the shipment has at least one scan aside from the original label creation note. If there are no scans on the shipment, treat as no movement on tracking.

Check if there is a signature on the delivery.

  • If the signature matches the recipient name, or if the customer recognizes the name, deny the request, and provide a hard message.
  • If the customer does not recognize the signed name, continue to the next step. Shipments delivered to a workplace, businesses, or apartment block are often received in bulk and not every package is checked.

Review the situation to determine if it qualifies as a lost package:

  • If the shipment is In Transit with at least one scan but has not shown any movement for three or more calendar days.
  • The shipment shows Delivered but the customer claims to have not received it. The customer has checked around their home, with neighbors, and at the office or front desk (if applicable) but has not found it.

Note: In case the carrier has lost package, the customer may have received an email from Microsoft titled Your Microsoft package was lost in transit.

If the customer has received the email, ask them to share the email and attach it to the SR as proof of lost package.

If the issue qualifies as a lost package according to the above, escalate to CSC.

4. No movement on shipment tracking

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

First, confirm the shipment is not a lost package. Review the shipment history on the courier’s website to check if there are movement scans.

  • If the package has any transit scans where it is moving toward the customer, it cannot be considered no movement on tracking.
  • If the shipment was in transit but has no new scans within the past three calendar days, treat it as a lost package.

A no movement on tracking shipment will generally only have one scan, and it will say something like, Shipment Processed or Label Created. Non-US/UK packages may also have clearance or customs related notes, these are not considered movement.

Shipments should show movement within one business day. If there are no scans (aside from those mentioned above) and it has been more than one business day, you may escalate to CSC.

Exception: FedEx Canada can take up to three business days to show scans. This is because these shipments originate in the US and are not scanned until they enter Canada. Do not escalate these to CSC, ask the customer to wait.
  1. Review the request for validity. Because no movement on tracking can look like lost package (and their investigations are very different), it’s important to ensure the issue is correctly identified.
  2. If the request is valid, consult FT4 via TFS. FT4 will investigate with the warehouses and advise a resolution.
  3. Specify in your request whether the customer’s preferred resolution is a replacement or refund. If the shipment cannot be found, FT4 will carry out the preferred resolution after the investigation. If the preferred resolution is not specified, FT4 will issue a refund.
  • ​​​​​​​If impacted product is Office, use MST Replace Line Item or Refund Line Item – do not use ORST to issue a token.

5. Missing boxed contents

5.1 Missing boxed contents (Under $200)

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

Before providing any support, investigate for a history of abuse on the account. Look in SRs, case notes, and transaction history for activity like high velocity ordering, excessive returns, repetitive gift codes or CSV credits. and notate your findings in MST. If suspicious activity found, see KB 2991467 for actions. If nothing is found, proceed with the next steps.

Verify in MST that the items reported as missing were shipped in the same box with items the customer did receive, and the customer didn’t receive different items instead.

  • If the reported missing items were shipped separately, re-evaluate the issue type based on the shipment and delivery status.
  • If the customer has additional, unordered items instead of the reported missing items, treat as Exchange – Incorrect item received.
  • If the reported missing items were shipped in the same box as other received items, proceed to the next step.
  • If the customer received all items but is missing a piece that should have been included, consider whether we can replace it without having to exchange the product. This is not an option for third party products.
    • Xbox or Surface: Determine if we sell the item by itself. If yes, you may use CSV to replace it for the customer free of charge. This does not apply to promotional content like game codes. You are not permitted to use CSV to replace entire missing products.

Determine with the customer if the shipment appeared damaged or tampered with. If yes, inform the customer we will be starting a damaged package inspection and that they should keep all packaging and articles received with the shipment, so the courier can collect them.

  • Damage may include tearing, crushing, holes, water marks, or flattened corners.
  • Tampering may be indicated by re-taped packages, opened contents, or broken seals.

If the issue is valid according to the above, escalate to CSC. Include detailed notes on the condition of the shipment and on what specific products were missing.

Customer need: Return and refunds
Customer question: I need to exchange/replace a product
Symptom/cause: Item missing from order

5.2 Missing boxed contents (Over $200)

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

9. RMAops

9.1 RMAops label issue

Tier 1 action

Tier 2 or customer service champion action

This category is used any time the customer is eligible for a return, but something is blocking the customer from sending the product back. Examples include but are not limited to the below, all of which can be escalated to CSC.

Note: The customer cannot return their product (purchased online or at B&M) if they are no longer in the country where it was purchased. We may consider an exception return on their return to the purchase location if that will be soon. If not, we cannot support their request.

  • The customer did not receive the return shipping label. The customer has confirmed that they are checking the correct Microsoft account email mailbox. The customer has checked their junk or spam folder.
  • The customer did not receive the requried trade invoice to send their product back. Countries that require a trade invoice for a return are:
    • New Zealand
    • Puerto Rico (Over $2500)
    • Norway
    • Switzerland
    • Iceland
    • Lichtenstein
  • Unable to create RMA; the customer is unable to receive a label because the order status prevents creation of a return. Examples include: Order stuck in Pending Fulfillment, the customer already received exchange, the order Shipped Not Charged, or the order is no longer visible in MST.
  • The customer is unable to print the return label due to no computer access, no email access, or no printer.
  • The customer originally purchased from B&M and is within policy for a return, but they do not live within a reasonable driving distance to return the product.

If the customer is located in North America or EMEA, CSC is able to support most manual label scenarios. See KB Procedure: Sales: CSC only-manual return labels for Online Store using UPS CampusShip for process.

If the scenario is not supported by the above KB or if the customer is located in APGC, follow the instructions below.

  1. If the customer is still not receiving the label after resending it in MST, consult FT4 via TFS. You can ask FT4 to create a new label and send it to an alternate email address (which you need to provide in the TFS notes), or you can ask FT4 to attach the new label to the TFS work item, so you can send it to the customer directly. FT4 cannot do both.
    1. If relevant, FT4 will create a trade invoice for the customer to use so their return can pass through customs.
    2. The manual returns process may result in a refund delay of one to two days at the warehouse.
    3. If a refund is not possible due to an erroneous order state, FT4 will work with the Payment Operations team to have engineering issue a refund. The SLA is 10 business days. If the request fails, FT4 will submit a request to refund via wire transfer. FT4 will not send a paper check unless specifically requested by the customer (available only by USD) and will always try PayOps first.

  4. In this scenario, the customer is unable to select a “preferred resolution”. If the investigation is closed in the customer’s favor, FT4 will simply process the RMA and the resolution will depend on whether the RMA is for credit or exchange.
