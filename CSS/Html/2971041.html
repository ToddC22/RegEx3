What is this article about?

This article is for Commercial advocates and explains how to handle customer support scenarios with issues related to product safety for all Surface, Hub, and HoloLens models and their accessories. This article should be followed when a customer calls in about a device and mentions any of the words in section 2, RRT key words when describing their issue with the device.

1. Overview

When a customer reports a product safety concern, we follow a procedure called Rapid Response Team (RRT). This procedure is triggered by a customer stating one or more of the RRT key words, in section 2.

  • It is important for all advocates to be aware of these RRT key words, and to immediately switch to the RRT procedure when any of the key words are mentioned by the customer.
  • The RRT procedure includes a set of specific and focused interview questions. This helps gather all the pertinent details involved in the product safety concern raised by the customer.
  • You are expected to interview the customer using the RRT questions provided in section 5.
  • At the end of the interview, escalate the case to Tier 3 (T3) for evaluation and additional handling.
  • When T3 RRT receives the escalation, they evaluate the case based on the provided information, and handle accordingly.

2. RRT key words

When a customer contacts support with an issue related to product safety, they may mention one of the following RRT key words when describing their issue:

  • Property damage caused to something other than the first-party Microsoft device
  • Fire and flames seen
  • Melting
  • Leaking alkaline battery in an in warranty (IW) first-party accessory (only applies to alkaline batteries in an in warranty Microsoft accessory. Leaking from anything else is not RRT.)
  • Expanded, swollen exploded battery
    • For Surface tablets only: If the customer reports an expanded/swollen battery only in the Surface/keyboard (also described as a raised, lifted, separated, or bulging screen/keyboard) and there are no other RRT keywords mentioned, do not handle as RRT. Refer to KB 4508455 Procedure: Surface: Expanded/Swollen battery or bulging screen: Overview.
    • For Surface tablets only: If the customer states the battery has physically exploded, the case must be handled as RRT following the procedure below.

Note: If the customer reports an expanded, swollen battery in a Surface accessory or any other Microsoft device/accessory continue with the RRT procedure below.

  • Burn, burnt, burned (injury or property damage)
  • Personal injury – includes rash, blisters, skin irritation, allergic reactions, bleeding, bruises, nausea, headache, eyestrain, seizure, disorientation (HoloLens), or neck strain (HoloLens) (allegedly caused by the first-party Microsoft device or Microsoft accessory)
  • Shock - only from first-party Microsoft device or accessory while connected to AC power, or if the customer states medical attention was received
  • Smoke seen (only smelling smoke is not RRT)
  • Sparks seen
  • Loose object (on Surface tablets and HoloLens this is the power supply that plugs directly into an AC wall outlet only. On the Surface Hub, it is the Hub itself as its power supply is internal, inside the Hub.)
  • Seizure or seizure-like symptoms
  • Ingestion, swallowing, or choking on a piece or part of a Microsoft product (including batteries, figurines or toys)

3. RRT exceptions

If one of the keywords is mentioned, determine if any of these exceptions apply before continuing with RRT handling.

Exception

Advocate handling

Pinching on Surface Book hinge (Surface Only)

 
  • Pinching caused by the Surface Book hinge is an RRT if the customer reports an injury such as bleeding, blisters, or bruises in connection with the “pinch”. Move forward with the RRT procedure.
  • If a customer says that the hinge pinched them but there was no injury reported, you do not need to go through the RRT procedure. Use the following messaging to help the customer avoid future pinching:

"Microsoft regrets any discomfort or inconvenience you experienced. To avoid this in the future, we suggest being aware of the hinge location and keeping it away from your skin or fabric while opening the unit."

Excessive Heat

 
  • If the customer is reporting that their Microsoft device or accessory is too hot to touch when charging or during heavy processor use and no RRT key words are used (for example, no visible smoke, flames, melting, injury or similar is reported) this is not considered an RRT scenario.
  • Advise the customer that the device or accessory is designed to shut down automatically if it reaches pre-determined temperatures that are well below international regulatory safety thresholds. If the customer still perceives that the device is too hot, they are welcome to request an exchange.

Shock (Touch Current)

 
  • While handling the Surface device itself (not the charger) while connected to AC power, some customers may describe the following:
    • I can feel the current or a “tingling sensation.”
    • I can feel the electricity.
    • It is uncomfortable to touch or hold.
    • I can feel a static charge.
  • For this scenario, provide the following messaging:

    "Microsoft is aware of reports of sensations like pinpricks when touching their Surface while its power supply is plugged into AC wall outlets. The current involved is far less than the amount allowed by international safety standards and does not pose any risk of harm. This is not an indication of malfunction with your Surface product. All AC wall powered electronic devices with conductive metal surfaces may produce enough current that some people can feel. We regret any inconvenience or concern this may have caused you."
  • If the customer owns a Surface Pro 3 or newer Surface model with an external power supply, there is a method to reduce or remove the touch current sensation received from the AC power adapter. Walk the customer through the following steps.
    1. Direct the customer to keep the AC cord plugged into the wall outlet. It is important that the orientation of the AC plug does not change for this remedy to work.
    2. Disconnect the figure-eight style connector end of the AC cord from the PSU brick, and rotate just the figure-eight style connector end 180 degrees. Then, reconnect the rotated figure-eight style connector end back to the PSU brick.
    3. Check with the customer if symptoms have improved.
    4. If the customer experiences this same type of discomfort in the future after connecting the AC cord to a different wall outlet, recommend that they repeat the instructions in steps 1-3 to remedy the issue.
    5. If the customer insists they have been shocked after following the steps above and/or they use other RRT key words, fill out the RRT escalation form and escalate to T3.

Color transfer from a Touch or Type Cover (Surface Only)
  • If the customer is reporting that color from their Touch or Type Cover transferred onto another object (for example, sofa or coffee table), follow KB 2991451 Procedure: Surface: A Touch or Type Cover has transferred color onto an object.

4. How to handle and escalate a product safety service request (RRT)

You must report it as a potential hardware safety issue using the following RRT procedure

Steps

Notes
  1. Conduct and document the Customer RRT Interview questions in section 5.

 
  • When possible, perform the RRT interview over the phone with the customer. If the customer cannot be reached by phone, then the RRT interview can be emailed. Be sure to use the most current version of the interview located below.

DO NOT modify or change the RRT interview

  • Make sure that the RRT interview is fully filled out in as much detail as possible. If the customer refuses to answer any of the questions, have that noted and move forward.
  1. Ask the customer to email clear photo(s) of the affected items.

 

Pictures should include the following:

  • Any visible damage on the item(s) involved in the RRT incident.
    • If no visible damage, a picture of where they saw the RRT issue happen (for example, if smoke was seen from the charge port while the power supply was plugged in, a picture of the charge port and power supply charging tip should be requested).
  • The full power supply and AC cord (if it is a Surface Hub, a picture of the AC Cord only is needed).
  • The full label on the power supply brick (including the PID number in the lower right corner).
  • Any damage that the customer has reported to other items (property damage).
  • If the issue is with an expanded, swollen, or bulging screen (also described as raised, lifted, separated, or bulging screen).
    • Picture showing the expansion or lifted screen (for example, ask the customer to lay the Surface flat on a table and take a picture from the side).
  • Serial number (SN) on the back of the Surface.
    Note: If the customer has multiple Surface devices with an RRT issue, have the customer label each picture with the SN number of the device so we know what device each picture is for.
  1. Gather the customer’s contact information.

 

This should include:

  • Full name
  • Company name
  • Shipping and collection address
  • Contact phone number
  • Contact email address
  1. Following the correct RRT interview, explain the next steps to the customer.
  • Let the customer know that their case will be escalated to the appropriate team to determine next steps, which will be provided to the customer within 24-48 business hours.
  • Do not promise any resolution to the customer. T3 RRT will need to review the case and specific scenario to determine the exact steps that must be followed. If the customer is requesting a specific outcome, let the customer know that you cannot promise anything, but the request will be noted. Note the customer’s request on the escalation for T3 to review.
  • Do not create any orders for the customer. T3 RRT will create any orders that are needed to resolve the RRT matter or they will provide you with instruction on what order to create.
  1. Escalate a task to Surface Hardware Support T3 using the T3 Escalation Template.

See KB 3072561 Procedure: Surface: Repair and Exchange Collaboration and Escalation scenarios.

  1. Create a new task in the case and include all the information about the incident (for example, RRT interview, customer information).
  2. Copy the completed customer RRT interview questions to the task and note where the pictures can be located (DTM or in email for example).
  3. Ensure that the customer's information is accurate and correct. It is critical to provide T3 the correct customer information for the RRT issue. Incorrect or incomplete customer information will cause issues or delays.
  4. Escalate the task to the T3 escalations queue and let the customer know that next steps should be provided within 24-48 business hours (business hours are Monday – Friday PST).
  • Note: DO NOT escalate the SR, T3 works only with tasks.
  • Note: All updates from T3 RRT will be included on the task for you to review. For updates that involve customer contact, T3 will also update you directly with that information.
  1. Keep the original SR open until the T3 RRT escalation task has been completed.
    • DO NOT close the SR before T3 RRT has completed the task. If you need to close the SR before the task is completed, reach out to T3.

 

T3 will provide you with the necessary information for follow-up with the customer and closing the case.

T3 keeps the task updated with information important to the case. If the customer contacts you asking for updates or with concerns about the status of the RRT case, contact the T3 RRT advocate that is working on the case and request the information that the customer may be looking for.

  • If the task is already closed but further RRT help is needed, a new task needs to be created and escalated to T3 RRT. The new task needs to clearly note the issue and provide any previous SRs/Tasks so T3 RRT can properly review and assist.

Important reminders about RRT cases:

  • If any step cannot be completed as instructed above, clearly note that in the case and continue with the RRT procedure.
  • If the customer declines to go through the RRT procedure, still escalate to Tier 3 as an RRT issue with as much information as possible. Let the customer know the case will be reviewed, but we may be unable to provide a quick resolution or any resolution without all the requested information.
  • DO NOT promise any specific resolution for the RRT issue. Let the customer know that a specialist will review and provide the appropriate next steps to resolve the issue. T3 RRT cannot promise any specific resolution being requested by T1 or the customer.

5.2 HoloLens RRT interview questions

Note: Below questions 1-34 can be emailed to the customer if they cannot be contacted by phone.

IMPORTANT: Due to the nature of these questions, the customer must be informed that if answering through email that the completed interview must be returned through DTM. Provide the customer with a link to DTM so they can upload the completed form there and remind them to upload the completed questions to that link.

Questions 35-36, must NOT be emailed to the customer, those should be answered by you.

Interview questions

Notes
  1. Fully describe the incident/issue with as much detail as possible

 

Device information
  1. Serial number

 
  1. Was the HoloLens device connected to a power charger during the incident?

 
  1. What peripheral(s) were connected to the Microsoft HoloLens device at the time of the incident?

 
  1. HoloLens power supply PID (serial number).

This is located at the bottom the power supply brick, side with writing. Usually starts with a “O” or a “0”:
  1. Did you calibrate the HoloLens before using it just prior to when this event occurred?

 
  1. Is this HoloLens shared with other users? If yes, how many other users and do you know if these other users experienced similar symptoms as you?

 
  1. How long had the HoloLens been in use prior to when the incident occurred (For example, was the device new or had been used for a while)?

 
  1. Where was the HoloLens located at the time of the incident? For example, on a desk charging, or being worn on an individual’s head.
  
  1. Have any attempts to open the HoloLens been made or does the HoloLens have any physical damage in addition to the issue you’ve described?

 
  1. Do you still have the subject HoloLens and/or its accessories?
  

Incident/Symptom information
  1. On what date did the incident occur?
  
  1. Was this a single occurrence, or has it occurred multiple times?
  • Single
  • Multiple
    If multiple then complete these additional questions:
    • Did it start gradually and increase in frequency or severity over time?
    • Approximately how long ago did you first notice it occurring?
  1. Was anyone else present at the time of the incident?

 
  1. If health symptoms reported. How soon after starting a session with HoloLens do the symptoms manifest?

 
  1. If health symptoms reported. Is there a particular HoloLens usage activity that you believe triggers the symptoms?

 
  1. If health symptoms reported. Do your symptoms continue after removing HoloLens? If yes, for how long?

 
  1. If vision symptoms reported. In what environment was the HoloLens being used when you experienced the symptoms (For example, indoors, outdoors, office, home, or factory floor)?

 
  1. If vision symptoms reported. How bright was the environment lighting during the incident (For example, dim, dark, office lighting, sunlight, or very bright)?

 
  1. If vision symptoms reported. What was the brightness setting on the device (For example, maximum brightness, medium, or dimmest setting)?

 
  1. If vision symptoms reported. Was there any aspect of the display that you believe relates to your symptoms? For example, color non-uniformity, flicker, blurry images, or double images?

 
  1. Was anyone injured?
  • No
  • Yes
    If yes, did anyone receive professional medical treatment? Yes/No
  1. Was any fire or other emergency services needed?
  • No
  • Yes
    If yes then complete these additional questions:
    • Was there fire department response?
    • Confirm address where the fire occurred
    • Is there an insurance company involved with the claim? Yes/No
    • If yes, provide name of insurance company

User information
  1. If health symptoms reported. Have you tried using other HoloLens 2 devices? If yes, do your symptoms occur with all of them?

 
  1. If health symptoms reported. Have you tried using HoloLens 1 devices? If yes, do your symptoms occur with them?

 
  1. If health symptoms reported. Have you tried using virtual reality (VR) devices? If yes, do your symptoms occur with them?

 
  1. If health symptoms reported. Have you watched 3D movies, such as in a theater? If yes, do your symptoms occur with them?

 
  1. Approximately, how many hours have you used HoloLens 2?

 
  1. If there was experience with HoloLens 1. Approximately, how many hours have you used HoloLens 1?

 
  1. If there was experience with virtual reality. Approximately, how many hours have you used virtual reality devices?

 
  1. What is your age? If the customer is not willing to state their age, ask them to provide a range (13-25, 26-35, 36-45, 46-55, 56-65, 66+).
  
  1. If vision symptoms reported. Do you wear prescription glasses or contacts? 
  • No
  • Yes

    • Were you wearing your glasses or contacts while using the HoloLens?
    • Are these multi-focus lenses like progressive or bifocals?
    • Are these single focus lenses?
    • Do you wear glasses for reading?
    • Do you wear glasses for distance vision?
    • Do you wear glasses or contacts for most daily activities?
    • Is the prescription a strong or weak correction?
  1. If vision symptoms reported. Do you have any eye conditions that affect vision (For example, astigmatism, glaucoma, or macular degeneration)?

 
  1. If health symptoms reported. Do you experience vertigo? If yes, were you experiencing vertigo during the incident?
