1. What is Onboarding Consultation? 

Onboarding Consultation is a complementary, introductory service focused on educating and empowering prospective and new to Microsoft Advertising customers as they are getting started with Microsoft Advertising. The service is tailored to assist customers with setting up Microsoft Advertising accounts and campaigns.

Return to top

1.1 Program description

The Onboarding Consultation service includes a single-session phone call with the customer, potentially some tactical work conducted by the advocate after the call and an exit email with relevant resources to conclude the service engagement. There are times when a service engagement may need to be broken out across more than one session because the customer may not have time, or may not be ready, to set up a Microsoft Advertising account and campaign. In these cases, advocates can schedule a follow up call within 5 business days and leave the ticket open during that time. If the follow up call will occur after 5 business days, the advocate should close the initial ticket and open a new one.

Customers receive the Onboarding Consultation service by calling a special phone number that routes to the services team, by setting an appointment using the SMB Scheduling Tool (for new to Microsoft Advertising customers) or by receiving a phone call from a services advocate.

Important notes:

  • For all other markets, if a prospective or new to Microsoft Advertising customer calls for help getting started with Microsoft Advertising but reaches a support advocate, that support advocate should assist the customer accordingly and code the ticket under the program of  Standard Support.
  • If a prospective or new to Microsoft Advertising customer contacts standard support and asks to speak with a specific services advocate, explicitly requests the Onboarding Consultation service or the customer has an open SR where the Service Program field equals Onboarding Consultation, warm-transfer the case to the services team.
  • Attempt to transfer to the initial advocate, if possible. If that is not an option, attempt to reach an available advocate on the services team.
  • If no one on the services team is available, confirm the customer’s phone number, ask if they prefer to be contacted in the morning or afternoon, notate the service ticket with the customer’s call-back preferences, then set expectations with the customer that a services advocate has been notified and will get back to them as soon as possible.

1.3 Eligibility requirements 

Most prospective and new to Microsoft Advertising customers are eligible to receive Onboarding Consultation.

1.3.1 A customer is eligible for receiving service if:

  • Customer either:
    • Does not yet have a Microsoft Advertising account (they are a prospect).
      • NB for Support to Services transfers, make sure to check that the prospective business model is eligible.
    • Recently created a Microsoft Advertising account with valid payment method within the past 90 days (they are a New to Microsoft Advertising customer).
    • Has a Microsoft Advertising account with valid payment method and may be transitioning to the scale segment (new to scale).
  • Customer agrees to consider change recommendations made by the services advocate.
  • Customer has a live business website compliant with Microsoft Advertising’s security and editorial requirements.
  • Customer’s business model is compliant with Microsoft Advertising editorial guidelines.
  • Customer’s language and time zone are supported by Microsoft Advertising services.
 

1.3.2 A customer is not eligible for receiving service if:

  • The account is flagged with a fraud status that cannot be overturned.
  • The account’s QBR penalty or business model prohibits it from serving in the action.
  • Customer has an account manager or is assigned to another advocate (per Unified Customer Management (UCM) Advocate Dashboard).
  • Customer already completed the service and contacts advocate with a standard support issue.*
  • Customer’s language or time zone is not supported by Microsoft Advertising services.
  • Customer is not willing to provide a preferred contact name, valid email address and phone number.

When a customer contacts a services advocate on the onboarding services phone line with a support issue and is not eligible for the service, treat it as a support case and code the ticket accordingly. Be sure to remind the customer of our support center contact info so that the next time they need help, they reach the right team. This is one circumstance where a services advocate will select Microsoft Advertising in the Product field and Standard Support in the Program field of their ASD ticket.

Note: Advocates may also warm transfer the customer to Standard Support following internal warm transfer processes.

* In cases where a customer who was previously treated with Onboarding Consultation contacts you with a support issue and the account has optimization opportunities, you may deliver the Optimization Consultation service to that customer after resolving their issue. If you already know the customer’s advertising goals, you may skip the interview portion of that workflow. Be sure to create two tickets: one for the program Standard Support and one for the program Optimization Consultation.

2. Services advocate workflow

2.1 Engaging with the customer 

2.1.1 Know current promotions 

  • Get familiar with current promotions and details provided by your manager, including:
    • Coupon value in the promotion
    • Time-frame for the promotion
    • Eligibility and customer call-to-action
    • Creative assets and communication channels (such as, email or postcards)
    • Landing page URL
    • Campaign specific value propositions
  • Find more information, see KB 2643959 Informational: Microsoft Advertising: Current Promotions in Market and ASD Blog.

2.1.2 Inbound calls

Prospective customers – do not currently have a Microsoft Advertising account:

  1. When a prospective customer calls into the services team’s phone line and you hear “onboarding consultation”, prepare to deliver the Onboarding Consultation service.
  2. Introduce yourself and clarify that you are with Microsoft Advertising.
  3. Ask the caller if this is a new or existing service request.
    • If the customer has an existing SR: Follow standard procedure and either assist the customer or transfer to the advocate working the case.
    • If a new case: Create an ASD ticket and confirm if the advertiser is calling about setting up a new Microsoft Advertising account/campaign (prospective customer).
  4. Collect caller's name, phone number, and preferred email address.
  5. Accurately populate the ASD ticket with required fields, including Service Program = Onboarding Consultation.
  6. Answer the caller’s specific questions about Microsoft Advertising or the marketing promotion.
  7. Provide a quick overview to the advertiser about the program.
  8. Confirm the caller meets the eligibility requirements.
    • ­If the caller does not meet the eligibility requirements, advise them on why they are not eligible, end the call, code ASD ticket with appropriate not qualified reason, and close the SR.
  9. Use probing questions and listening skills to understand the caller’s business and their advertising goals. For a list of questions to ask, see Get to know you interview guide in section 8.
  10. Leverage the promotion being offered as a value proposition.
  11. If the advertiser is not willing to set up an account at this time:
    • Offer to email more information about Microsoft Advertising and to call back if they have more questions or are ready to set up an account.
    • Set a specific call back date/time as warranted. You can also provide the link to the language specific SMB Scheduling Tool and let them know that they can schedule an appointment when they are ready to move forward. If you are participating in the SMB Scheduling Tool program, you can provide your personalized URL.
    • End the call.
    • Customize and send the advertiser the Onboarding Consultation More Info email template (see section 8).
    • Code the ASD ticket with appropriate “not interested” reason and close the SR.
  12. If the advertiser is willing to set up an account, see section 2.2, Conducting Onboarding Consultation.
 

New to Microsoft Advertising customers – current account holder needing assistance with campaign setup: 

  1. When a new to Microsoft Advertising customer calls into the services team’s phone line and you hear “onboarding consultation”, prepare to deliver the Onboarding Consultation service.
  2. Introduce yourself and clarify that you are with Microsoft Advertising.
  3. Ask the caller if this is a new or existing service request.
    • If the customer has an existing SR: Follow standard procedure and either assist the customer or transfer to the advocate working the case.
    • If a new case: Create an ASD ticket and confirm if the customer is calling as a new account holder to get assistance with campaign setup (New to Microsoft Advertising customer).
  4. Verify the customer per standard procedures. (See KB 3178272 Microsoft Advertising: ASD Ticketing System)
  5. Accurately populate the ASD ticket with required fields, including account number and Service Program = Onboarding Consultation.
  6. Answer the caller’s specific questions about the marketing promotion.
  7. Provide a quick overview to the advertiser about the program.
  8. Confirm the caller meets the eligibility requirements.
    • If the caller does not meet the eligibility requirements, advise them on why they are not eligible, end the call, code ASD ticket with appropriate “not qualified” reason, and close the SR.
  9. Leverage the promotion being offered as a value proposition.
  10. If customer is ready to receive service, assign yourself to the customer in UCM’s MyQueue utilizing the appropriate initiative. Ask your site lead if you need more information.
  11. If the customer is not interest or if the advertiser expresses interest, but is not willing to make a commitment at this time, offer to email more information about Microsoft Advertising and end the call.
    • Offer to email more information about Microsoft Advertising and to call back if they have more questions or are ready to set up an account.
    • Set a specific call back date/time as warranted. You can also provide the link to the language specific SMB Scheduling Tool and let them know that they can schedule an appointment when they are ready to move forward. If you are participating in the SMB Scheduling Tool program, you can provide your personalized URL.
    • End the call.
    • Customize and send the advertiser the Onboarding Consultation More Info email template. (see section 8)
    • Code the ASD ticket with appropriate “not interested” reason and close the SR.
  12. Move to conducting Onboarding Consultation.

2.1.3 Outbound calls

Prospective customers – do not currently have a Microsoft Advertising account:

  1. Receive your list of outbound leads from your manager or L2.
  2. Within two days of receiving the assignment, create an ASD ticket and accurately populate required fields.
  3. Confirm that the advertiser does not already have a Microsoft Advertising account. Check Microsoft Advertising using Advertiser, Account or User’s First Name, Last Name (if you have it).
  4. Ensure the lead has contact information. If not, do contact discovery and update your lead list and ASD ticket with the phone number and email address you find.
  5. Quickly preview the advertiser’s website to confirm eligibility for the service.
    • If advertiser is not eligible per Microsoft Advertising policies , code ASD ticket with appropriate “not qualified” reason, and close the SR.
  6. Navigate to UCM and briefly review if there are any opportunities provided in the UCM Opportunities tab.
  7. Call the advertiser, introduce yourself, clarify that you are with Microsoft Bing Advertising, and pitch Microsoft Advertising (using Onboarding Consultation call guide, see section 8.3).
  8. If the advertiser asks how we received their information, use the following messaging: “Your company came up on a database of businesses that could benefit from advertising with Microsoft Advertising. Microsoft Advertising can help you increase the number of customers who see your ad just in time for the <<current season>> season.”
  9. If the advertiser is not interest or if the advertiser expresses interest, but is not willing to make a commitment at this time, offer to email more information about Microsoft Advertising and end the call.
    • Offer to email more information about Microsoft Advertising and to call back if they have more questions or are ready to set up an account.
    • Set a specific call back date/time as warranted. You can also provide the link to the language specific SMB Scheduling Tool and let them know that they can schedule an appointment when they are ready to move forward. If you are participating in the SMB Scheduling Tool program, you can provide your personalized URL.
    • End the call.
    • Customize and send the advertiser the Onboarding Consultation More Info email template. (see section 8)
    • Code the ASD ticket with appropriate “not interested” reason and close the SR.
  10. If you don’t reach the advertiser, leave a message and send the Onboarding Consultation followup2voicemessage email template (see section 8). If you have not received a response from the customer (and send follow-up email if customer doesn’t answer), call one more time within 5 business days. The advocate should attempt two total calls only. There should be four total touch points including the two email follow ups.
  11. If customer wants to set up a Microsoft Advertising account, but prefers to do it at a different time, schedule a call-back appointment (preferably within five days) and set the appointment or call back reminder on your Outlook calendar for one or two days before the appointment.
    • If the customer’s appointment is scheduled for a time more than five days out, close the ticket as not interested – no time and plan to open a new SR for that customer when you engage with them on your appointment date. Provide your personalized SMB Scheduling Tool link to allow the advertiser to schedule an appointment with you at their convenience.
  12. If the advertiser is willing to set up an account, see section 2.2, Conducting Onboarding Consultation.
 
 

New to Microsoft Advertising customers – current account holder needing assistance with campaign setup:

  1. Check MyQueue (see KB 3177673 Tool User Guide: Microsoft Advertising: UCM Agent Dashboard) in UCM for your customer assignments.
  2. Within two days of receiving the assignment, create an ASD ticket and accurately populate required fields including account number.
  3. Quickly preview the customer’s account and website, and the UCM Advocate Dashboard, to determine their business model and confirm eligibility for the service.
    • If customer is not eligible per Microsoft Advertising policies, reject customer from UCM MyQueue, code ASD ticket with appropriate “not qualified” reason and close SR.
  4. Call the customer, introduce yourself, clarify that you are with Microsoft Bing Advertising, and pitch Microsoft Advertising (using Onboarding Consultation call guide, see section 8.3).
  5. When you reach the customer, change the status in MyQueue from assigned to engaged.
  6. If the customer is not interest or if the advertiser expresses interest, but is not willing to make a commitment at this time, offer to email more information about Microsoft Advertising and end the call.
    • Offer to email more information about Microsoft Advertising and to call back if they have more questions or are ready to set up an account.
    • Set a specific call back date/time as warranted. You can also provide the link to the language specific SMB Scheduling Tool and let them know that they can schedule an appointment when they are ready to move forward. If you are participating in the SMB Scheduling Tool program, you can provide your personalized URL.
    • End the call.
    • Customize and send the advertiser the Onboarding Consultation More Info email template (see section 8).
    • Release from UCM, code the ASD ticket with appropriate “not interested” reason and close the SR.
  7. If you don’t reach the customer, leave a message and send the Onboarding Consultation followup2voicemessage email template (see section 8). If you have not received a response from the customer (and send follow-up email if customer doesn’t answer), call one more time within five business days. The advocate should attempt two total calls only. There should be 4 total touch points including the two email follow ups.
    • When leaving voicemails on a customer’s business line (not private voicemail), mention something specific about their account to incentivize a call back. Example: “You can reach more of your target audience with your products by trying a complimentary product called sitelinks. Give me a call back to learn how to get started.”
  8. If customer is interested in service but prefers the consultation at a different time, schedule a call-back appointment (preferably within a week) and set the appointment or call back reminder on your Outlook calendar for one or two days before the appointment.
    • If the customer’s appointment is scheduled for a time more than a week out, close the ticket as not interested – no time and plan to open a new SR for that customer when you engage with them on your appointment date. You may keep the assignment in MyQueue until you conclude delivering the service to that customer. Provide your personalized SMB Scheduling Tool link to allow the advertiser to schedule an appointment with you at their convenience.
  9. If customer is willing to receive service, see section 2.2. Conducting Onboarding Consultation.

2.1.4 Scheduled appointments

New to Microsoft Advertising customers – current account holder needing assistance with campaign setup:

  1. Check your Outlook calendar for Onboarding Consultation appointments created by the SMB Scheduling Tool.
  2. Review the appointment for details about the customer and any provided notes.
  3. Ensure that the appointment does not clash with an existing appointment already on your calendar. If it does, reschedule the appointment with another services advocate.
  4. Assign yourself to the customer in UCM’s MyQueue using the appropriate initiative. Ask your site lead if you need more information.
  5. An ASD ticket for the customer is automatically created when the customer schedules the appointment. Assign yourself to that ticket and accurately populate required fields including account number.
  6. One or two days before the appointment, send the customer an email reminder (use the Appointment Reminder email template, see section 8).
  7. Quickly preview the customer’s account and website, UCM Opportunities ,and the UCM Advocate Dashboard, prior to the appointment so that you are familiar with the advertiser and their account.
  8. Call the customer at the scheduled appointment time (using the Onboarding Consultation call guide, see section 8.3) and move to Conducting Onboarding Consultation in section 2.2.
  9. If the customer doesn’t show up for the appointment:
    • Customize and send the advertiser the Missed Appointment email template (see section 8).
    • Reject from UCM as Max Attempt – Contact.
    • Code the ASD ticket as Customer Not Reached – No Show for Appointment.
    • Close the SR.

2.2 Conducting Onboarding Consultation 

Set expectations with the customer that you are available to them for up to an hour based on their needs and how many questions they may have, that you will start by asking a few questions so you can learn about their business and advertising goals and that there may be some follow-up activities addressed via email after you end the call.

2.2.1 Assisting the customer with setting up a new Microsoft Advertising account

  1. Ask the customer for permission to create a Microsoft Advertising account on their behalf and set appropriate expectations with the customer about how this will work.
    • If the customer agrees to move forward with account creation.
    • Perform the account setup process. Utilize the Account Creation email template (see section 8) to confirm account setup.
    • For EMEA and LATAM markets, use the Log Me In tool for a rich and interactive engagement.
  2. If the customer has a promo code, enter the new account number into Microsoft Advertising and apply the promo code to the account on the advertiser’s behalf.
    • Explain that it could take 10 minutes for the funds to appear in the account.
    • If you need to replace the promotion code due to a system error (see KB 3170785 Informational: Microsoft Advertising: Coupon codes/promotion codes) and the account is less than 30 days old, use an acquisition coupon of the same value. If the account is over 30 days old, us a retention code of the same value.
  3. Add the account number to your ASD ticket.
  4. If the advertiser is willing to set up their first campaign, transition to assisting the customer with setting up campaigns.
  5. If the advertiser is not willing to set up campaigns at this time:
    1. End the call.
    2. Send the appropriate email template.
    3. If you scheduled a future appointment for setting up campaigns, use the Appointment Reminder email template (see section 8).
    4. If customer completed account setup but was not interested in campaign setup, craft and send a customized exit email to the customer (using Onboarding Consultation Service exit email template, see section 8).
    5. Complete your ASD ticket for Onboarding Consultation. Be sure to select Delivered service to customer and Improve Campaigns with the associated tactics in the Achieve More section of the ticket.
    6. Close the SR.

Delivered service to customer = Improved campaigns 

“Delivered service to customer” is about educating and adding value.

Therefore, if a services advocate selected “Improved Campaigns” in ASD, then the ticket should always be coded as “delivered service to customer.”

Whether a services advocate implements a “Improved Campaigns” tactic on the customer’s behalf (either requested by customer via phone call or email), witnesses that the customer implemented a tactic, or advises and educates the customer via a phone call to implement a tactic, then the ticket should be coded as “delivered service to customer.”

4. Definitions

Program terminology and definition guide:

  • BOS: Back Office Support – team of non-customer-facing advocates who execute optimization and billing tactics on behalf of AMs, Service and Support Advocates working with customers.
  • Consultation (when appended to a program title): Service typically includes one consulting session plus off-line work by advocate and a customized email with a recap and links to resources to conclude the service.
  • New to Microsoft Advertising: Customer recently set up first Microsoft Advertising account or customer recently started managing first Microsoft Advertising account.
  • New to Scale (self-serve): Customer used to have a dedicated account manager but was recently migrated to the scale segment and introduced to Microsoft Advertising Service and Support.
  • Managed Services/Strategic: Program for premium Microsoft Advertising advertisers where they are awarded a dedicated AM or team of AMs to manage their Microsoft Advertising account.
  • Pending Advertiser: Customer has a Microsoft Advertising account that does not yet have a valid payment method in place.
  • Prospect: Advertiser does not have a Microsoft Advertising account or is a Pending Advertiser
  • UCM MyQueue (KB 3177673 Tool User Guide: Microsoft Advertising: UCM Agent Dashboard): MyQueue is the individual UCM user’s view of customers that are assigned to them and what treatment they should receive. MyQueue is accessible through UCM.
  • Scale segment: Microsoft Advertising customers who do not have a dedicated Microsoft Account Manager (also known as self-serve)
  • Services advocate: Onboarding and optimization specialist dedicated to conducting a variety of planned service programs in our Microsoft Advertising Offerings portfolio via inbound and outbound channels.
  • SMB Scheduling Tool: This online tool allows new to Microsoft Advertising customers to schedule an Onboarding Consultation session with a services advocate. It is integrated into ASD to provide a seamless experience for customers and advocates.
  • Support advocate: Customer support representative skilled in troubleshooting technical issues with Microsoft Advertising and assisting customers via inbound channels.

"My name is [insert name] and I’m calling from Microsoft Bing Advertising. I am calling today to welcome you to Microsoft Advertising and offer you a free, specialized service for new advertisers. I am available to you for up to an hour based on your needs and how many questions you may have. I’d like to go over your account, help you set up or optimize your campaigns and show you some useful tools that can help you succeed with your advertising goals.

Whether you’re new to search engine marketing or already a pro, I can offer you personalized assistance with whatever you need to get your campaigns up and running successfully in no time.

  • If you don’t already have a Microsoft Advertising account, I can help you create one.
  • If you recently created a Microsoft Advertising account, I can give you a tour of the Microsoft Advertising UI and show you some exciting features that will save you time with managing your campaigns and can help to boost your ad performance.
  • If you have Google campaigns, I can help you with a quick and easy import process so that your Microsoft Advertising account has the same coverage.
  • We can cover the basics to help you understand how Microsoft Advertising works or we can jump right to the more advanced campaign management tools where I’ll review the benefits and teach you how to use them.
  • I can help get your ad campaigns set up for better performance so that you get the most from your investment with Microsoft Advertising right out of the gate.
  • I can help you setup campaign conversion tracking to help you monitor performance and adjust your campaigns later."

6. Handling objections

As you pitch the Onboarding Consultation service offering to customers, you may hear objections. This is normal. Effectively addressing objections may turn a disinterested prospect into an enthusiastic customer who is receptive to receiving your assistance getting started with Microsoft Advertising.

Here are some example objections you may hear with suggested responses to get customers more interested in your service offer:

Customer objection: I received your email and it looks good. But there must be a catch with this free trial offer.

Advocate response: “There’s no catch. We offer promotions like this so you can try Microsoft Advertising with no risk. And if Microsoft Advertising is not meeting your expectations during the free trial, I can help you tweak your campaigns so that they better align with your advertising goals.”

Customer objection: I just wanted the free trial.

Advocate response: “That’s why we offer promotions like this, so you can try it out with no risk. How did it go? What were some of the positive results shown from your free trial? What were the challenges you experienced?

If Microsoft Advertising did not meet your expectations during the free trial, I can help you tweak your campaigns so that they better align to your objectives. What are your advertising goals?”

Customer objection: I don’t want to add a payment method. I just want to use the promotion code.

Advocate response: “While we do require a payment instrument to take advantage of the promotion, you may pause your ads at any time by adjusting campaign settings. I will be happy to help you with that if you determine that this is not for you.”

Customer objection: If I do add a payment method, will my ads keep going after the promotional credit runs out and charge me? How long will it last?

Advocate response: “The credit depends on the keywords you choose and how you decide to set up your campaigns. I can help you set this up and show you how to monitor your campaign spend.”

Customer objection: It probably costs too much.

Advocate response: “You control the cost of your ad campaign. Search advertising is also known as pay per click (PPC) because you only get charged for each click that your ad generates. You set your own monthly or daily budget and are only charged when someone clicks on your ad. Also, you set the maximum amount you’ll pay for each click. No click? No charge.”

Customer objection: I already use Google, so I don’t need Bing.

Advocate response: “We recommend utilizing both Google and Bing. The Microsoft Advertising audience accounts for 1/3 of U.S. search market share — so if you only use Google, you are missing out on millions of potential customers. Also, Microsoft Advertising searchers spend more online. ComScore recently announced the latest data (comScore qSearch (custom), July 2016.) for Microsoft Advertising across Bing and Yahoo sites:

  • 31% of the U.S. search market
  • 168 million unique searchers
  • 20% more spent online than average internet searchers

Microsoft has been investing in expanding the Bing market share over the past few years. We’ve increased market share significantly and we expect that will continue to grow.”

Customer objection: Your platform is too complicated/more complicated than Google.

Advocate response: “We’ve simplified and improved Microsoft Advertising significantly over the past couple of years. You’ll find an array of features that make managing your campaigns easy and fast. I will also be there to guide you through the experience and ensure you are setup for success.

Also, if you ever have any questions on specific features, we are here 24/7 to answer any questions you have. We also have a ton of helpful information on our Help pages, including videos and walk-throughs to show you how to use many of our features and help you plan your campaign strategy.

Follow-up question: Did you use Microsoft Advertising in the past? What did you like/not like about it? I can help you make sure everything is set up and see what I can do to help with {the things they didn’t like} and guide you through set up…”

Customer objection: I get more traffic/impressions/share with Google.

Advocate response: “It’s true that the market share for Google is much larger. That is why Microsoft has been investing in expanding the Bing market share over the past few years. Through syndicated partnerships and our alliance with Yahoo and AOL, we’ve increased market share significantly. In fact, comScore announced recently that Bing has reached 30% share in the US, or 1/3 of all search queries, and we expect that will continue to grow.”

Customer objection: We used to do some online advertising, but it was too much work and I didn’t have time to manage it.

Advocate response: “Although it takes time and energy to test online advertising strategies, it is worth the investment to determine what gives you the best return on your investment. There are also ways to set up simplified campaigns that don’t take a lot of work, but that can get you back into online advertising. We can help you set your campaigns up and once you get going, we have many marketing experts and online resources to help you maintain a competitive ad campaign.”

Customer objection: I need more time to think about it; I don’t have time right now.

Advocate response: “ComScore announced recently that Microsoft Advertising has surpassed 30% market share in the U.S. — and we expect that will continue to grow.”

If customer is already using Google — “With Microsoft Advertising, you are able to import your campaigns directly from Google AdWords (and other online advertising platforms), saving you time since you don’t have to set them up from scratch.

You can tweak your campaigns to optimize your ads on Bing — and we can help you. Transferring your campaigns and optimizing them for Bing will take little time and will help you reach the 30% of the search market you are currently not reaching by only using Google.”

If customer does not have Google – “Businesses of all sizes are realizing the benefits of online advertising — your competitors may already be using online ads to target your customers! We want to help you keep up with the competition and get more customers in the door. Microsoft Advertising can help you reach the customers most interested in your products and services in a cost-efficient way, helping you increase ROI (return on investment) and boosting your sales leads.”

Customer objection: I will do this later.

Advocate response: “We know you are busy, and though this may seem time-consuming, we will be here to help you. We don’t want you to miss out – comScore announced recently that Bing and Yahoo has reached 30% market share in the U.S., and we expect that will continue to grow. That’s exactly why you should consider optimizing your campaigns now– so that when you are ready, your advertising strategy is in place and waiting to successfully compete in the auction.”

For detailed metrics about non-US markets, see the comScore kit in KB 3179894 Key Selling Points article.

Customer objection: We haven’t advertised in a while, why should we now?

Advocate response: For customers currently using Google: “ComScore announced recently that Bing has surpassed 30% market share in the U.S. — and we expect that will continue to grow. That’s exactly why you should consider signing up now — when you’re ready, your advertising strategy will be in place and waiting to successfully compete in the auction.”

For customers not doing search advertising: “Businesses of all sizes are realizing the benefits of online advertising — your competitors may already be using online ads to target your customers! We want to help you keep up with the competition and get more customers in the door. Microsoft Advertising can help you reach the customers most interested in your products and services in a cost-efficient way, helping you increase ROI (return on investment) and boosting your sales leads.

With your competitors already using search marketing, there’s no better time than now to give it a try. Your free trial will allow you to reach your customers and drive more business. This is a limited opportunity from Microsoft Advertising, so why not give it a try right now?”

Customer objection: I will sign up if you can be my account manager. Will that work?

Advocate response: “I will partner with you to set your campaigns up for success and our team of marketing experts will always be available to assist you with your account. While I can’t be your permanent account manager, I will provide you with contact information to reach an expert 24/7 in case you run into an issue. We are here to ensure a successful experience with Microsoft Advertising.”

Customer objection: I already know how to manage a Search campaign.

Advocate response: “That’s great! I can also show you some advanced tools, useful reporting options and resources like web help, forums and webinars that will help you stay up to date on the latest industry insights and Microsoft Advertising news.”

Customer objection: You don’t know my business.

Advocate response: “You are definitely the expert when it comes to your business, and I am an expert with the Microsoft Advertising platform. If you help me understand your goals for online advertising, I can help you customize your Microsoft Advertising campaigns to get the best results.”

Customer objection: I need to ask someone else at my company before I make a decision.

Advocate response: “That’s understandable; why don’t we go ahead and schedule a follow up call in a couple of days? That will give you a chance to get the input you need. I can even set up a conference call and invite your coworkers or any other decision-makers that you think may benefit from being on the call. Would you like to get that set up?”

7. Contact discovery steps

  1. Search for the Parent Company on www.bing.com.
  2. Go to the domain website.
  3. Check corporate name and address for parent company. If parent company is different, check Microsoft Advertising for parent company.

    Repeat steps above.
  4. Go to contact us/about us link on website. If there is corporate number, use that corporate number. Look for advertising or marketing contact names for roles like search manager, advertising manager, media manager. If you find specific contact names, use that!
  5. Check the privacy policy for a phone number. If there is one, try that number.
  6. If you cannot find a phone number on the website, research contact using Hoover or LinkedIn.
  7. Go to www.hoovers.com and type company name in search box.
  8. Select best option by matching address against website and click on people and contacts.
  9. Look for search manager, advertising manager, media manager, etc. and get name and phone number.
  10. If no contact info is found in Hoovers, go to www.Linkedin.com, type company name into search box and find a contact name with job title of search manager, advertising manager, or media manager.
