Procedure: Xbox downloadable content (DLC) license/entitlement issues

[Asset 4049349]

Who should use this article?

  • All Consumer

What is this article about?

This article outlines how to handle a customer calling in with issues related to downloadable content (DLC) purchased from the Microsoft Store or a third party.

Important: Any issues with entitlements must be escalated to your Ranger.

1. Symptoms

  • The customer is unable to access or play downloadable content.
  • The customer may or may not receive an error code or error message.

Use the steps below to identify the status of the license or entitlement and determine if the downloadable content is corrupted, still installing, or needs to be downloaded via the Manage Game screen.

Return to top

2. Cause

The downloadable content stored on the customer’s Xbox console might be corrupted, still installing, or need to be downloaded via the Manage Game screen. Most commonly, downloadable content issues are related to connectivity or locating where the content is on the console.

Return to top

3. Procedure

Verify that the license was correctly applied

  1. Locate the account that purchased the content.
  2. In MST, search OneStore entitlements (select OneStore, then Entitlements and User Licenses) and Xbox Live (select Xbox Live, then Entitlements and User Licenses) to find the license.
    • For more information on MST, see KB 3065764 Tool User Guide: Membership Support Tool (MST).
    • Note: Any Xbox 360 entitlements will be shown under Xbox Live and not OneStore.
  3. Locate the customer’s license/entitlement for the DLC in their account and identify if the status is active or revoked.

If the License or entitlement is showing active:

Subscriptions & billing

  1. Make sure all troubleshooting has been completed so you can confirm that the license or entitlement is not corrupted, or the content is still installing.
  2. If a transaction’s status is listed as Reversed Charge in the Transactions section of MST, we cannot provide any resolution until the investigation has been completed by the financial institution/bank. If MST shows chargeback reversal in event logs, follow KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund.
  3. ​If, after troubleshooting, the customer is still unable to launch the game or is asked to pay again, escalate to your Ranger so it can be escalated to the SPMs.
    • Note: Some DLC for titles requires the customer to complete certain conditions or discover the content in-game.
  4. Determine if the customer purchased the game via Games with Gold. You can search the news.xbox.com to find the games which have been available for free. These titles will show as a $0.00 dollar purchase.
    • Note If the customer has an Xbox 360, they should be able to play Games with Gold titles that they obtained during the availability window even after their Xbox Live subscription has lapsed. For Xbox One, customers must have an active Xbox Live Gold subscription to play games obtained via Games with Gold.

If the License or entitlement is showing revoked:

  • If the customer was not charged for their pre-order due to an issue with the payment instrument, and the customer did not update that payment method during the charging cycle prior to the release date of the title (generally 10 days), the entitlement for the pre-order may show as being in a revoked state. In this situation, the customer must attempt the purchase again.
  • If the customer has been charged and the license was revoked with no chargeback (reversed charge), engage your site Rangers or TAs.

If you determine that the customer does not have the license or entitlement applied to their account:

  • Make sure you have the customer account that made the purchase item loaded in ASD.
  • Review their purchase history to see if the content was purchased on the account that was verified.
  • If there is no purchase history and the licenses are not found in User Licenses, the customer does not own the content on that account. Inform the customer that the account does not own the content and that they should check any other accounts they own for the content. They can view their entitlements by going to https://account.xbox.com/MyGames.
  • If you do see a charge in the order history but no license/entitlement, be sure to check the source of the charge, as purchases from MicrosoftStore.com (for a disc-based game) can appear similar in MST. You can view that in the bottom left corner of the purchase section in MST and it’ll show as Microsoft Store as the partner (see below).
    [Asset 4023315]
  • Issues related to the entitlement not showing correctly should be brought to the attention of Rangers at your site to help diagnose and troubleshoot the issue

4.1 Further frontline support

If all downloadable content (DLC) troubleshooting fails after working through the solutions above, continue with these steps:

  1. Review the customers service request history and refer to the refund guideline in KB 3083551 Guideline: Microsoft Store: Return and refund.
  2. If the customer is eligible, process a refund as outlined in KB 840536 Procedure: Microsoft Store: Digital content return/refund.
  3. If the customer is not eligible for a refund, use the following suggested script:
  • "I'm sorry you are having a problem with your downloadable content, but because you have recently had an item refunded, I am unable to provide you an additional refund at this time."

Important: Do not escalate to request license removal.

5. Handling customers who pre-ordered games

  1. Navigate to OneStore Entitlements to find the entitlement for the game.
  2. Use the order ID to find the transaction the PDP to the direct offering they purchased or redeemed.
  • Example: 9p6swxks2s46 in this link: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/store/p/nba-2k20-legend-edition-pre-order/9p6swxks2s46/0010[Asset 4527362]
  • Ask the customer to log in with the Microsoft account that made the purchase and to look for Install on my devices.
    [Asset 4527363]
  • If the customer sees the above, guide them to the base game in the Microsoft Store search, or have them check their ready to install.
    • If the customer sees the below, there is an entitlement issue which needs to be escalated to your SME/TM/Ranger so it can be escalated to engineering. Do not refund the repurchase as the customer will lose all their pre-order bonuses.
      [Asset 4527364]

5.1 Why customers who pre-ordered a game will be prompted to pay again

  1. Once the game is released for general availability (GA), the pre-order is live and the offer will change. As seen above, the pre-order offer has the SKU, 9p6swxks2s46, but going to the GA page will provide a different SKU (as seen at the end of this link, https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/p/nba-2k20-legend-edition/9n8kglvsmd4n). The store indicates that the customer owns the game, but if a customer searched on the Xbox Store or web site, they will see the option to install the base version of the game only. This issue is because the customer is searching for the GA version, which will prompt them to pay when they should be seeing a message to install the base game .
    • The customer should check the Ready to install section on their console or store page to install the base game (Note: it’s not expected to have the icon match for the non-standard editions). They still need to install the base game for any DLC they can check under Ready to install, or find the content within the game itself.

5.2 Handling customers who purchased the general availability (GA) version of game (no pre-order)

  1. Go to OneStore Entitlements to find the entitlement for the game.
  2. Use the order ID to find the transaction, which show you the offer that they purchased/redeemed (screenshot shows a pre order item)
  • Example: https://www.microsoft.com/p/nba-2k20-legend-edition/9n8kglvsmd4n
    [Asset 4527367]
  • Ask the customer to log in using the Microsoft account that made the purchase, they should see the option to install on their device under Install on my devices.
    [Asset 4527368]
  • If the customer sees the above, guide them to the base game in the Microsoft Store via Ready to install (which may show a different icon).
  • If they see the below, there is an issue which needs to be escalated to your SME/TM/Ranger. Do not refund the repurchase as it will not fix the root cause.
    [Asset 4527369]
