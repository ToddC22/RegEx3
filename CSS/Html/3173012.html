What is this article about?

Microsoft Advertising has specific policies and processes in place to provide advertisers with credits and refunds. The information in this article includes guidance on when to issue credits and refunds, how to apply them, and the documentation requirements.

There are different processes for applying refunds based on how the advertiser has their payment set up (both currency and payment type).

1. Policy guidelines

1.1 Policy enforcement

Each vendor site will perform bi-weekly credit audits of all credits and refunds given for that time, and these will be reviewed with the aligned TA.

1.2 General refund discussion guidelines

  • Never proactively offer a refund or offer more than the advertiser is requesting.
  • Never set customer expectations on the amount or percentage of the refund they can expect in advance of TA approval.
  • First identify the root cause, fix the issue, and calculate the value that should be considered in the dispute based on the customer’s complaint. Document all changes within the ticket and attach any supporting documentation.
  • Only consider charges within the 60 days prior to the initial support ticket creation date. All scenarios below should be considered within the 60-day range, unless a TA grants approval.
    • For post-pay/threshold and invoice accounts, consider charges invoiced/charged 60 days prior to support contact.
    • For prepay disputes, consider clicks accrued in the 60 days prior to support contact.
  • Any Platinum advertiser (managed or unmanaged) requesting a refund over $5,000 requires additional approval by Platinum segment owners, which will be facilitated by TA’s. Do not promise advertisers any refund value prior to appropriate internal approvals.
  • Document everything within the ticket and attach any supporting documents or reports.
  • If there is a viable legal or physical threat, to either you or the customer, always raise the concern to a manager and TA for discussion, regardless of the policy. See KB 3174388 Guidelines for Unexpected Events During Client Contacts for additional actions.
  • Do not process any refunds until you have TA approval (if necessary) or the advertiser accepts the offer and agrees to make the changes (in user education scenarios).
  • Adjusted amounts do not include taxes. If taxes were charged, they will appear on the payment invoice as a separate line item.
  • Refunds will be to the original payment method, except for bank transfers and Boleto, which are processed as Offline Refunds by check or wire transfer.
  • Consult a TA if the advertiser specifically requests a refund to the payment method instead of a reduction in outstanding charges.
  • Depending on the scenario, the customer may not be eligible for a refund, eligible for only a partial refund, or, if the root cause was a technical issue, a full refund. Check the refund policy in section 1.3 below to determine whether a full or partial credit can be offered.
  • Adjustments must not be split across users or days and must be within a user's role limit (see section 1.5).

1.3 Refund policy

1.3.1 For monthly invoice accounts, open a dispute with Global Financial Services (GFS), to suspend monthly invoice collections

  • Note: Global Financial Services was also known as Worldwide Online Credit Services (WOCS)

If the dispute cannot be resolved same day, Post Collections must be informed to suspend any collections efforts while the issue is open.

  • Complete the details for Issue Category= WOCS Escalations > Issue Type =WOCS Collections Inquiry and submit an escalation Sub-Team = Regional/WOCS - Dispute/Post-Collections.
  • Notify Post Collections of the following:
    • Issue related details to suspend collections for Billing & Payments.
    • When the dispute is resolved so they can resume collection efforts.

1.3.2 Scenarios eligible for a full activity adjustment:

  • Technical issue or bug – A system issue verified by Ad Support that caused unwanted spend or significant issues for the account
  • Internal error – Error made by someone from Microsoft Advertising that caused the customer to have undesired spend.
  • Trending Now clicks for sensitive topic – Advertiser saw a sharp spike in traffic due to a trending tragic news event or natural disaster. These are invalid clicks, refundable without escalation to Network Quality Services (NQS) but should be approved by your TA. Be sure to document business justification. Refer to the Escalation Guide for Sub-Team details.
  • Customers filing for bankruptcy – See resolution steps below.
  • Customer is now deceased
    Note: Also pause the account.

1.3.3 Scenarios eligible for partial/negotiated activity adjustment:

  • User education – A customer in good standing did not understand one or more settings in Microsoft Advertising, incurring overspend as a result.

    Before applying a refund/credit, provide guidance on the functionality for their future reference, and help walk them through the necessary changes. In ‘User Education’ instances it is advised to first offer 25% (with up to 50%) of the perceived overspend for charges billed in the last 60 days prior to support contact. Do not disclose or promise a specific refund value in advance of TA approval. Instead of a specific amount, say "let me see if I can get approval to get you a refund."

    For user education scenarios, ensure the advertiser is clear that this is a one-time goodwill credit as we value his/her business, not because of a fault of Microsoft Advertising.
  • Customer claims they meant to pause their account months or years ago. In this case, we can give a refund of 50% of the billed charges for the last 60 days only once we verify that the account is paused.

1.3.4 Scenarios where it is NOT permitted to negotiate/offer a customer a credit or refund:

  • Invalid clicks – Support should not negotiate invalid click refunds with the customer. TQ must investigate and if necessary, refunds will be processed by TQ on the backend of the UI. 
  • Agency mismanagement – Customer issue with how their current or former agency was handling their account. These refunds/credits should be deferred to the agency.
  • Charges to incorrect card – It is the customer's responsibility to maintain their desired payment method in the UI.
  • Bank overdraft fees – It is the customer's responsibility to manage their own bank accounts and not overdraft.
    Note: Bank overdraft fees resulting from the SEPA duplicative charges that occurred on November 2nd,  2017 ARE eligible for adjustment.
  • No Sales/Poor ROI – Customer claims they did not receive any sales from their Microsoft Advertising campaign and wants a refund for spend.
  • Estimated sales lost – Customer went dark prematurely due to either an error with their IO or other budget depletion and wants a refund for time they were offline due to perceived sales they could have made (Exception: If budget depletion or IO error occurred due to technical issue as confirmed by Ad Support).
  • Repeat user ed requests – Retention credits are only considered for first-time errors.
  • Requested changes made on their behalf – Customer contacted support and requested advocate to make changes to their account on their behalf, then later calls disputing any charges that occurred due to the changes. If the original change instructions were followed correctly, no refund will be provided.
  • Coupon code depleted, charges accrued on pay method – Customer contacts support saying that they received spend on their specified payment method after their coupon code depleted/expired and they did not understand that this would occur. The mail that the customer receives with their coupon code clearly states they will begin to spend when their coupon depletes or expires.
  • Charges billed over 60 days prior to the support contact.
  • Trending Now clicks for topic relevant to their business model – Advertiser saw a spike in traffic due to a topic relevant for their business model trending in search traffic.

Note: Offer to opt the advertiser out of Trending Now Traffic if they are interested. This is done by escalating to Global Support.

  • Fraud refunds to online payment methods. See KB 3169386 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Fraud Status for refund eligibility. 
  • Test Campaigns - If the advertiser agrees to the changes, the traffic is legitimate. Be sure to document customer agreement of proposed changes in your ticket.

Note: Scenarios not covered in this document should be escalated to TAs for review.

1.4 Talking points

Do not over-apologize or assume the advertiser is entitled to a refund. Empathize and inform the advertiser that we won’t be able to give a refund. Address what you can offer, such as educational opportunities, assistance with preventing recurrence, or a support coupon.

  • "We apologize for any inconvenience this issue may have caused, but we do not offer credits or refunds for __________. "
  • “I will escalate this scenario for review before I can authorize a specific refund value.”
  • “I can definitely understand why you feel that way and can understand your frustration. What I can do is to help you set up ______ to meet your needs.”
  • “I realize you are unhappy with the way ______ is working. Why don’t we take a moment and walk through how this works so that you can set up your campaign/account to help maximize the ROI?”
  • “I realize that our tool works differently from other advertising tools. We set up our tools to try and provide the best experience for not only our advertisers but also for consumers. We are constantly taking feedback and using it to improve our tools.”

Advertisers filing for bankruptcy

If a customer is going through bankruptcy proceedings and needs to write off the remainder of their balance, follow the steps below:

  1. Request the proof of bankruptcy document.
  2. For invoice customers, send the request to WW Online Credit Services >GFS (also known as WOCS) Credit Services (UCMT). For non-invoice customers, send the request to your regional ad-ops team. Explain the situation and provide proof of bankruptcy.

2. How to process refund adjustments

Tier 1 Support team should only process adjustments under $10,000 USD or the market equivalent of Credit Level C. Credits. For any adjustment over $10,000 follow the Large/Aged Adjustments guidance below.

Return to top

2.1 Adjustment types summary

  • Activity adjustments modify the quantity of click charges an account has received. Debits increase activity charges while credits reduce activity charges.

On prepay accounts

On threshold accounts

On monthly invoice accounts

  • Debits will reduce the available balance
  • Credits will increase the available balance (undo prior activity)
  • Debits will increase the next swipe amount.
  • Credits may result in a reduction in next swipe amount or a refund to the payment instrument, depending on whether the charge was successfully paid.
  • Debits will increase the amount due on the next invoice.
  • Credits do not affect the invoice amount and will result in a separate credit memo (available in the UI at the end of the billing cycle).
  • Payment adjustments (prepay payment refunds) are only available in prepay accounts and generate a refund to the original payment method. Prepay balance refunds are not subject to the refund policy below – available funds must be returned to the customer if requested and will be automatically returned if the account is closed (by fraud or by manual deactivation).
    • Payment adjustments should never be processed for more than the available balance remaining on the account.
    • For online payment methods (credit/debit/SEPA/PayPal), a refund receipt will be generated, and funds returned directly to the payment method (via commerce platform).
    • For offline payment methods (check/wire/Boleto) and declined online refunds (except for .fraud ), a refund receipt will be generated, and an offline refund request must be submitted to SANOPs after the declined credit memo reflects in the UI.
    • If an offline payment was made but has not posted in the UI, contact the GFS (also known as WOCS) CashApps team to get missing offline payment posted before issuing credits.
    • Prepay customers with a refundable balance may desire reapplication of funds to an existing Microsoft Advertising account (instead of an offline refund). Requests to reapply prepay funds should be raised with a request to CashApps.
    • SEPA payments should only be adjusted after eight weeks. For SEPA payment adjustments before eight weeks, redirect the customer to their financial institution. Otherwise acquire TA approval to process the adjustment early.

Account holds & credit limitations

Prepay customer account hold status is sometimes the result of credit card payment failure. To resolve this the customer may want to use a different payment method (for example, post-pay to prepay) or payment instrument (for example, credit card to bank transfer). However, system limitations don't always make the desired changes possible.

Below are the top troubleshooting steps to resolve the customer hold. If an account has no current activity, do not attempt to credit their statement. This will only show as a pending credit memo (balance will not be reduced, and hold will not be lifted).

Assist customers by:

  1. Recommend that the customer contact their banking institution to resolve the card failure.
  2. Recommend that the customer switch to another card payment option (contact Global Support if this is an issue).
  3. If they have current activity, you may be able to credit a portion of the current activity to release the hold and allow the charge to settle and/or move to the updated payment method (rebilling all or a portion of that value to the new payment method).
  4. Escalate to your TA if there are scenarios where:
    • We are recommending that the customers create a new campaign.
    • We cannot resolve the issue to the customer’s satisfaction.

2.3 Adjustments and refunds to online payment methods

Refunds to Credit/Debit Card/PayPal are processed immediately through the Commerce Transaction Platform and should reflect in the UI within one-six hours. SEPA refunds, once initiated in the Microsoft Advertising UI, may take up to five days to appear in the customer’s account.

The status of the credit memo or refund receipt accurately reflects the success or failure of returning funds to the originating payment method. Funds should land in the customer’s account within 24 hours.

SEPA: Payment reversals for SEPA should not be processed through the UI within eight weeks of payment. Any customers requesting reimbursement of SEPA prepayment funds must be directed to their financial institution to reverse the charges. Otherwise, TA approval is required to process SEPA payment adjustments in the first eight weeks.

2.4 Adjustments and refunds to offline payment methods

  • Refund receipts for offline payment methods (including check/wire/Boleto methods) will appear in the UI immediately, but do not accurately reflect the status of the actual refund.
  • For the customer to receive their refund, CSS must ensure charges are reversed in the UI, then submit an offline refund request to the regional SANOPs Team.
  • Payments must first appear in the Microsoft Advertising UI before an offline refund can be initiated through SANOPs.
  • If the original payment is missing, submit a request to GFS (also known as WOCS) CashApps to ensure the payment is properly loaded to the prepay account. SANOPs cannot process a refund unless it is showing as declined or failed in the UI.

Note on invoice customers: Offline Refunds for Invoice Accounts (SAP), including valid or failed credit memos should be directed to Cash Apps Team alias, who will take the issue details from UCM and request the refund from their internal GFS  Refund Team (who does not use UCM). From there, GFS Refund will work with AP to generate the request. SANOPs does not process refunds for invoice customers (SAP payment method), this includes successful and failed credit memos. If the customer is using invoice (SAP) as their payment method, a refund will generate a credit memo the following month to their account, which can be applied to their account balance by contacting their regional GFS) Collections team. If the credit memo has failed, the refund is not rendering in the UI, or the customer simply desires their credit returned in the form of a refund, then CSS must escalate to GFS Collections Account Team for assistance.

UCP (Unclaimed Property or fka Uncollected Property) is an annual process of escheating unused funds on U.S. customer accounts (or accounts considered U.S. aligned by default, due to no registered ABL). If a customers account is deemed inactive due to no login activity, unused funds will be moved from Microsoft to their local states government after a determined time period.   You will know if a customers funds have been submitted to UCP or their local state government if the following apply:

  • Balance will show as $0
  • Amount paid will show with refunded value "deemed Unclaimed Property"
  • Their last statement will include note that funds went to unclaimed property and to contact ucpcorp@microsoft.om

CSS Action:

  • Authenticated customers inquiring about their funds should be redirected to the ucpcorp alias.
  • There is no need to escalate on their behalf.
  • Customers are advised to provide their last statement to the UCP Team (which includes the reference to ucpcorp@microsoft.com). This will help validate and streamline their request to the UCP Team.

Tickets should be coded accordingly:

Prepay Account Management > General Education > Unclaimed Property

For context, the complete process for UCP occurs over several months and customers are notified via email along the way. The process occurs as follows:

  1. US customers who have unspent (prepaid) funds in their account are notified via mail that their funds are with Microsoft Advertising and they have the option to use (spend) or collect their funds (contact Sales or CSS to request a refund).
  2. If the customer does not claim their funds within the specified time period, the customer will be notified via email that their unused and unclaimed funds have been moved to ucp@microsoft.com as an interim step. During this interim step the customer should reach out to the UCP team directly.
  3. Once additional time has passed (typically 30 days) the funds that are being temporarily held by UCP Team will be sent to their respective local governments, this process is called escheatment. This state is determined by the customers legal business address OR if left unspecified this will be the state of Nevada (where Microsoft Online Corp. resides). Customers can tell if their funds have been escheated by looking at the statement for references to UCP, which will direct them to contacting ucpcorp@microsoft.com for next steps. CSS should redirect any customer inquiries related to UCP or escheated funds to the ucpcorp@microsoft.com alias, there is no need to escalate on the customers behalf.

Note: As of August 2018, SANOPs does not perform an automated refund process via check. If a customer contacts CSS for a refund they have not received, SANOPs can check to see if the refund was already sent to the customer, prior to processing.

Duplicate offline refund requests: Submit an offline refund status inquiry to SANOPs to confirm whether a check/wire refund has already been processed for a specific refund billing document.

Account closure automatically triggers balance reversal in the UI. If closing an account specifically to initiate an offline refund, proceed with How to Submit Offline Refunds below.

2.5 Invoice activity adjustments

In monthly invoice accounts, adjustments to activity from a previous month will result in a credit memo, which will be generated at the close of the billing cycle along with all other billing documents.

Credit memo values will not automatically be applied to invoices. The advertiser will need to contact the customer service alias on their invoices to specify how to apply the credit memo against other invoices.

To view adjustments for invoice advertisers:

  • If the adjustment is given to a charge that has already been invoiced, you can view the activity in the online statement in the Microsoft Advertising user interface.
  • If the adjustment is given in the current balance tab (during the month that has not yet been invoiced), it will adjust the amount shown in the current amount column but will not appear as a stand-alone credit or debit.

Invoice payment status is reflected in the UI but does not indicate credit due to overpayment – this information is stored solely in SAP. If an advertiser overpays an invoice, the overage can be applied to any other invoice just like a credit memo. If a credit has been applied after paying an invoice, the credit will only show in SAP.

2.6 Redeeming credit memos

Credit memos will appear immediately in the Billing tab but will not be deducted from the balance due at the end of the next billing cycle. At the close of the next billing cycle, the advertiser will receive the invoice for the full value of activity accrued during that period as well as a separate credit memo for the credits applied. The advertiser must contact the customer service alias noted on the invoice and specify which invoice document number they would like to use the credit memo against. For example: “Please apply credit memo ID# against invoice ID#”. Then the advertiser should submit payment for any remaining amount due.

Return to top

2.7 Prepay refunds

Payment adjustments should never be processed for more than the available balance remaining on the account. If the advertiser is due back more than the balance, always adjust activity to increase the balance before adjusting a payment transaction. Otherwise, the payment adjustment will cause the account balance to go negative. If the account balance does go negative, do not apply an activity adjustment to solve for this. Instead the customer should submit a new payment.

If an activity credit is granted to a prepay customer, and the customer would like to receive a refund instead of increasing their available balance, the value of the credit must be applied twice: once to reverse the activity, and again as a payment adjustment to send the funds back to the payment method. Doing so will not double refund the advertiser.

As a best practice, do not adjust a payment transaction unless the advertiser intends to discontinue advertising or specifically requests the funds be returned to the payment method.

If an advertiser requests a refund for available funds that were submitted more than a year ago, assist the advertiser with closing their account to auto-generate the refund and proceed with the appropriate offline refund process.

3. When to submit offline refunds

See section 2.4 for related information.

Offline refunds typically occur when a reversal is made to a customer’s offline payment method, in which case the refund does not get back to the customers primary PI. Offline refund requests should be sent to SANOPs, unless it’s one of the following situations:

  1. SAP (invoice) is the customers payment method
  2. If the offline payment (check/wire) is not appearing in the UI as expected

Below are common scenarios for requesting an offline refund:

Note: See KB 3169386 for offline refund eligibility when an account is closed for some form of fraud and is inquiring about their prepay balance or post pay charges (Note: Special process must be followed to determine offline refund eligibility in fraud scenarios).

  • A balance refund was processed in the UI against an offline payment method (because of manual deactivation, fraud closure, or other payment reversal).
  • Refund receipt shows declined by an online payment method (credit/debit card) and Global Support has confirmed the funds were not sent to the customer’s financial institution.
  • For eligible .fraud accounts, request that the fraud team reviews the account before initiating an offline refund.

3.2 Process

As of July 9th, 2018, there is no need to submit the HBI form. For security and privacy purposes, offline refund requests must be submitted to your regional SANOPs team (prepay payment methods) OR GFS (also known as WOCS) (SAP payment method). Both options are sent via escalation email from ASD (KB 3170756 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Escalating to Partners using UCMT, COSMIC, and CAP). If any additional customer documentation or bank details outside of the template escalation are required, you must follow standard DTM protocol, to protect sensitive customer information.

The following fields are required in the ASD escalation: (Sent via email to SANOPs or GFS for SAP, in rare instances)

  • Declined billing document ID (if applicable)
  • Offline payment receipt ID (if applicable)
  • Refund reason
  • Refund amount
  • Currency*
  • Help needed
  • Advertiser name*
  • Account number*
  • Customer ID*
  • Payee name
  • Payee email contact
  • Email language (English is the default, if the customer has another preferred language it can be requested, and they will receive the MPC email and link in this language.)

*Items marked should match exactly with Microsoft Advertising and inability to verify may delay process.

For all offline refunds (check/wire), set customer expectations that they will receive an email from Microsoft Payment Central. The customer must complete the MPC process (including banking information for wire payments and address for check payments) before the refund can be approved and processed. Once the customer is sent a link to Microsoft Payment Central (MPC) they have 30 days to complete the required details, or the link expires. During this initial 30-day period, follow up and communication between CSS, the customer and SANOPs is important to ensure timely completion. Unless CSS requests assistance from SANOPs, they will only keep their ticket open during the first 10 days of this period. Once the customer has completed MPC details, SANOPs will proceed with processing the payment. If there is no communication between CSS and SANOPs within this 10-day period, the ticket will be closed after a 10-day reminder, assuming the customer will complete details on their own or may no longer wish to pursue the refund. 

During this 30-day period, CSS may assist with customer questions (utilizing SANOPs as an escalation point, if needed) to complete the MPC portion of the process and provide status updates. Should the customer go non-responsive, CSS should make three attempts to follow up with the customer over the course of an extended 10-day period. If the customer remains non-responsive at the time of this outreach, the ASD ticket can be closed. However, CSS should not request SANOPs close the ticket on their end within this 30-day period. Doing so will only confuse the SANOPs and AP teams, who have a 30 period in which they are expecting the customer will complete MPC details. Once customer details are completed, internal process and approvals will continue between AP and SANOPs, in the workflow outlined below. During this time, SANOPs may follow up if there's an update to the refund status which may help set appropriate customer expectations if the customer is still in contact or attempts to contact CSS for status.

  • Once the escalation details have been submitted to SANOPs and the customer has entered their personal information into MPC, SANOPs will reply with confirmation that the request has been submitted to AP and provide you with an MPC ID.
  • Set early customer expectations that offline refunds can take considerable time to process and can vary greatly depending on banking institution (wire) or customer location (check). SLA’s listed in this article are for only estimates.
  • Customer-facing guidance for setting up their MPC account can be provided to the customer by giving them the link to Microsoft Payment Central - New payee setup.
  • For more information on Payment Central, see KB 4033775 Tool User Guide: Microsoft Advertising: Microsoft Payment Central. When possible, the steps within this article may assist with basic troubleshooting of the platform.
  • If the customer needs additional help with MPC that cannot be found in the Microsoft Payment Central article, work with the regional SANOPs team to request “white glove support” directly from AP.  

For checks, set expectations about the varied time it can take to receive payment. All checks are sent from the United States and mailing times may vary depending on country location.

3.4 Refund to online payment method declined

If a credit memo is declined, the customer may be eligible for an offline refund.

  1. Confirm the decline reason. Authorized users should access CMAT and search for the account transaction details.
    • If the timestamped transaction attempt matching the declined credit memo shows the Final Decision is "bank declined" the advertiser's bank rejected the refund, possibly because the account is closed. In this scenario, the customer is eligible for an offline refund.
    • If the account is marked fraud, offline refunds for declined credit memos should not be processed unless approved by fraud. Cardholders should be redirected to dispute the charge with their bank. Otherwise, the customer may be required to submit a bank statement with the original transaction to prove eligibility for an offline refund.
  2. If there is no rejection reason, there may be a status discrepancy between the Microsoft Advertising UI and the Commerce Platform (CP) through which all billing transactions occur. Escalate to Global Support to confirm whether funds were successfully transferred back to the customer's financial institution.
  3. If Global Support is unable to retry or reconcile the UI and CP statuses, request an offline refund via Escalation to your regional SANOPs alias. Note: Reference the Global Support ticket in the request.

4. When to process rebills

Like credits/refunds, Tier 1 support team should only process rebill adjustments under $10,000 USD or the market equivalent of Credit Level C.

Credits and rebill adjustments over $10,000 should be escalated to the regional SANOPs teams after TA approval per the Large/Aged Adjustments guidance below.

A complete refund and rebill is required in several scenarios. All these scenarios are exempt from the normal 60-day refund policy and are still required even if the invoice is more than one year old or is triggering an “invalid” error upon adjustment.

  • SAP ID Changes to the bill-to or sold-to and the bills need to change immediately.
  • The incorrect currency was set as part of the account setup, and the advertiser needs to correct this to pay the bill.
  • The customer resides in a market that charges VAT and did not have their VAT ID associated with their Microsoft Advertising account, so they were overcharged.

Note: SEPA credits/rebills should not be processed within eight weeks of the payment without TA approval.

4.1 Process a refund or rebill under $10,000 (or market equivalent of Credit Level C)

SAP ID changes bill-to or sold-to or the Tax ID was not applied

Credit charges

1. Update the account settings (Bill-To/Sold-To/VAT).

2. Process a complete refund using the adjustment guidelines outlined above based on the customer’s payment type.

​Create a Rebill Campaign

3. Create a new campaign and ad group under the campaign for the rebill within the XID. Title this campaign/ad group 'Rebill'. Make sure the IO and budget will accept debit and not auto credit or pause the account for delivery. Essentially this means that the daily limit must be set high enough to cover the rebill adjustment amount.

4. Add keywords, and an ad to the ad group. As a best practice, use the word 'rebill' or 'rebills' for the keyword to quickly identify the ad and differentiate it from the other ads on the advertiser's account.

5. Set the bids to the suggestions that Bing provides. Do not set the bids at an extremely high rate if not suggested by Bing.

6. Log out of corpnet. Search on Bing using one of the keywords that you applied. Find the ad and select on it. Log back on to corpnet.

7. Return to the account in the UI. Hover over the Account number and go to Accounts > Balance.

Note: It can take up to three hours for the click to show in the Balance tab. If you don't see the click after three hours, click on the ad again.

8. Once click shows in the Balance tab. Pause the campaign created for the rebill.

Debit/Rebill Charges

9. Locate the Ad Group created for the rebill as detailed in step 3 above.

10. Select the pencil icon in the New Adjustment column, next to the listed rebill Ad Group, edit any line items necessary, making sure to include amount, adjustment type, adjustment reason and comments on the adjustment.

Note: For rebills, enter the adjustment amount in the New Adjustment Field > select Debit in Adjustment Type drop down > enter adjustment reason ‘Account Setup Incorrectly—Incorrect Currency/Discount/SAP’ and finally detail adjustment comments.

11. Select Save. This will generate a line item on the next billing statement/invoice to show the spend. Let advertisers know they will see these charges as a line item on their next statement.

Note: Any credits or rebills over $10,000 should be sent to the ART team for complete processing. Be sure to refund the clicks that resulted as a part of the rebill campaign creation (step 6).

4.2 Rebill troubleshooting

If the UI displays the error message "There are no orders on this account" this could be due to one of three things:

  1. The daily budget for the newly created campaign needs to be adjusted to the rebill amount.
  2. The account currently has no active IOs. If this is the case, a new manual IO needs to be added to complete the rebill. Escalate to BOS to do this.
  3. There is not enough revenue in the active IO. If this is the case, a new manual IO needs to be added to complete the rebill. Escalate to BOS to do this.

4.4 Suspend collections efforts or prevent a rebill from causing credit hold

Sometimes a large rebill can cause a monthly invoice advertiser to go on credit hold. To prevent adverse impact to the customer’s account while the rebill is underway, before processing the rebill, notify Post Collections of the circumstances and they can suspend all action on the account, which will allow the customer an additional two weeks to pay before resuming collections activities (including account holds). This notification should be done via a child ticket UCMT escalation:

  • Complete the details for Issue Category= WOCS Escalations > Issue Type = WOCS Collections Inquiry and submit an escalation Sub-Team = Regional/WOCS - Dispute/Post-Collections.
  • Update subject line to Post Collections to include the word "Dispute" in the description.

Notify Post Collections both when the investigation is opened/deemed legitimate, then again alerting that the issue is resolved, and collections efforts may resume.

  • If the rebill is under $10,000 (see section 4.1) for a monthly invoice account and the account is erroneously placed on hold, escalate to the GFS (also known as WOCS) team to request that they temporarily remove the hold. Let them know it was caused by a credit & rebill. They will remove the hold temporarily while you complete the credit & rebill.
  • If the rebill is over $10,000 for a monthly invoice account, your respective regional ad-ops team will escalate to GFS to temporarily remove the hold in order to complete the credit & rebill.

4.5 Rebill payment plans

This process applies for any advertiser that owes more (due to a rebill) than their payment method will allow them to pay in one swipe.

  1. Confirm with the advertiser whether they have a transaction limit on their payment method. They may need to confirm with their financial institution and should request an increase in their transaction limit, if possible.
  2. Once the transaction limit is identified & confirmed, complete and send the Rebill template to the advertiser.
    • Divide the total amount that needs to be rebilled by the advertiser’s billing threshold to estimate how many days the rebill will take.
      • Example: Rebill is $30,000. Advertiser’s daily threshold is $5,000. Rebill will take six days to complete.
  3. Initiate the rebill.
    • If the rebill is over $10,000, end the rebill to your respective regional ad-ops team to complete. Send them the daily credit card threshold the advertiser shared.
    • If the rebill is under $10,000, begin processing by applying one payment per day.
  4. Once the amount is paid in full, notify the advertiser and thank them for their patience.

4.6 Large/Aged Adjustments over $10K/one year old

(Refunds, credits/rebills, and balance reversals over $10K/one year old)

Any adjustment (including prepay payment reversals) over $10,000 or the market equivalent requires TA approval and a completed ART Form must be sent to the regional SANOPs team for processing (see KB 3170756 Process: Microsoft Advertising: Escalating to Partners using UCMT, COSMIC, and CAP). SANOPs will process the adjustments and respond back once it is complete. The SLA for this request is tiered based on the amount to be processed.

  • Two business days for $10,000-$100,0000
  • 10 business days for $100,0000 +

Set expectations with the customer that:

  • Due to the value, the SLA for this request is 2-10 business days (depending on whether a rebill must also be processed).
  • If there is a rebill, a new rebill campaign will be created (by SANOPs) to process the rebill, and that campaign will be paused after the rebill is complete.

For more information, reference the rebill talking points in section 4.3.

1. Acquire TA approval for 10K+/One yr+ adjustments

  1. Prepare an ASD child ticket for the escalation.
  2. From the child ticket draft an email and overwrite the customer email to themets@microsoft.com, summarizing the issue and why approval is needed for the large value/aged adjustment.
  3. Someone from themets alias will reply directly so the approval is logged within the ASD ticket.
  4. Proceed with the steps below for "prepare documentation for escalation".

2. Prepare documentation for escalation

Once TA approval of the refund is logged in the ticket email:

  1. Fully complete all ART form details.

    Note on Adjustment Reason Codes: Not all credits scenarios require a rebill. If your request does NOT require a rebill, do not select the credit/rebill reason code. For credit only requests without a rebill, you may use your discretion to select the most suitable reason code. For example, if the system does not support the transfer of funds or a customer tax scenario where we are simply crediting (for example, NOT a credit/rebill), you can use “system issue” instead.
  2. Encrypt the form before sending.

3. Escalate to the Regional SANOPs (aka SANOPs) Team for processing

  1. Complete the issue-specific details for Issue Category = SANOPs Escalations> Issue Type = Crediting and Adjustments.
  2. Relay the TA approval to SANOPs: "Reply All" to the approval email received from them and paste the issue-specific details into the body of the message with the ask.
  3. Overwrite the customer email with the desired regional SANOPs alias.
  4. Prepare any additional documentation (for example, attachments or screenshots). Note that SANOPS does not utilize DTM or UCM. Documents that contain sensitive customer information should be encrypted or password protected.

The following must be included in the escalation:

Hi Team,

Please assist with processing this refund over 10K USD (or adjustment >one-year-old).

  • Customer Name
  • CID
  • XID
  • Records Center Link
  • Related UCMT IDs containing refund approvals

<signature>
