What is this article about?

This article provides guidance on planned and unexpected disconnects or unresponsive customers during a chat support contact in ASD or Communication Console, as appropriate, or using a rejoin link.

Refer to KB 3064766 Procedure: Privacy: Handling Microsoft customer personal data on how personal data should be handled.

1. Recommended phone support scenarios for technical support (instead of chat)

Some technical scenarios are better suited for phone support due to their complexity and need for multiple reboot and reconnect operations. When these technical issues are identified, we should recommend that the customer leverage our phone support team using the appropriate canned text response. If the customer still prefers chat support, continue to assist the customer in a chat environment.

Recommended phone support scenarios:

  • System hangs and crashes (like Blue Screen Stop errors)
  • No boot or other boot issues
  • Unstable Internet connections (random disconnects)

2. Definitions

Type of disconnect

Definition

Planned

Technical support may determine that the next step(s) will disconnect the customer from the chat session or result in an extended delay before continuing troubleshooting, such as system reboots, downloading/installing large system updates or re-imaging.

Ghost

The customer has connected via chat, but customer has not responded to the initial greeting or provided enough information to appropriately code and create a case within 60 seconds

Only: For markets with virtual chat advocate (Global English), create a case using the taxonomy which customer provided during the conversation with virtual chat, do not treat these cases as a ghost chat.

Disconnect

The customer has connected via chat and sent a message and contact information, but disconnected unexpectedly.

Unresponsive

The customer has connected via chat and sent messages, but has not responded at least 90 seconds.

Reboot

The customer’s PC requires a reboot after troubleshooting.

3. Procedure

3.1 Ghost chat

When a customer has not communicated in any way for 60 seconds after welcome message is sent, follow a three follow up process before sending a closing message and ending the chat session. Each follow up will be sent after a 1-minute period, resulting in a total of 3 minutes for a ghost chat.

Advocate procedure:

  1. The chat is accepted and welcome message sent
  2. The customer is unresponsive for 60 seconds, send first follow up message:

Messaging: “Hello, I haven’t received anything from you. Would you like to still get assistance?”

  1. The customer is unresponsive for 1 minute (total 2 minutes), send second follow up message:

Messaging: “It’s been 2 minutes since I last heard from you. Please let me know if you still need assistance.”

  1. The customer is unresponsive for 1 minute (total 3 minutes), send closing message and end the chat session:

Messaging: “It’s been 3 minutes since I last heard from you. As a result, I’m going to close the chat session. Please feel free to contact Microsoft anytime for further assistance.”

  1. See the following guidance for coding a case as Ghost.
  • A&B: Select Ghost Contact in [Ghost / Spam] field.
  • Office and Windows advocates: KB 2967100 Procedure: Windows and Office: ASD Incident Form coding.
  • Surface advocates are not expected to create a case.

3.2 Disconnected chat

When a customer is responsive in a chat session but has disconnected unexpectedly, immediately attempt to reconnect with the customer and perform a callback as needed.

  1. Try to call the customer right away to reestablish the chat communication only.
    • A&B advocates who do not handle phone support: Engage your Tier 2 to attempt a callback. Tier 2 will continue the engagement, if successful. Original advocate to send “Disconnected Chat” email if contact is not re-established.
    • Office, Surface and Windows advocates: Do not call back customer if they disconnected after the offer of paid service.
  2. If the customer can’t be reached, add case notes that they disconnected from chat with the callback results and send an email (Subject: Disconnected Chat) when you close the case:

    “Hello,

    You seem to have been disconnected in your recent chat session, and we were not able to reach you by phone. If you still need help, please go back to support.microsoft.com to start a new chat session, and we’ll be happy to assist you.”

The chat is disconnected but you are still connected in remote sessions connected (Office, Surface and Windows advocates only), this can happen when you conduct a reboot and a reconnect with LogMeIn:

  1. Stop troubleshooting until you regain a chat connection.
  2. Use the remote access to open the chat rejoin link on behalf of the customer. Open the rejoin link URL in the customer’s web browser.

Note: If the customer is reconnected with a different advocate via the rejoin link, make sure you complete your case notes, and save the case. Let the second advocate continue to work with the customer.

3.3 Unresponsive chat

When a customer has not responded for more than 2 minutes during a chat, follow unresponsive customer process before sending a closing message and ending the chat session. Unresponsive chats are not ghost chats.

Advocate procedure:

  1. Chat is accepted, and you have been working with a customer, however, the customer became unresponsive at some point during the chat the dialog.
  2. If a customer becomes unresponsive for 2 minutes:

Messaging: “Hello, I haven’t received anything from you. Do you still need assistance?”

  1. If customer is still unresponsive for another 1 minute (3 minutes total with no response), send second follow up:

Messaging: “I haven’t heard from you yet. Please let me know if you still need assistance.”

  1. If customer is still unresponsive for additional 1 minute (4 minutes total with no response) send third follow up:

Messaging: “I haven’t heard from you in a few minutes. As a result, I’m going to close the chat session. Please feel free to contact Microsoft anytime for further assistance.”

  1. If the customer email is available, send the following email when you close the SR:

Messaging: “Hello, we stopped receiving responses from you in your recent chat session. If you still need help, please go back to support.microsoft.com and start a new chat session and we’ll be happy to assist you.”
