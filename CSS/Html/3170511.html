Process: Microsoft Advertising: Service Level - Account Shell types

[Asset 4509089]

Who should use this article?

  • Microsoft Advertising

What is this article about?

This article discusses what the different Service Level - Account Shell types are, as well as billing and other requirements.

Service Level determines what functionality is available to all accounts under a CID. Microsoft Advertising Service Level should not be confused with payment options or service program. Service Level is not a customer-facing designation and should not be used in conversation with advertisers.

An advertiser's Service Level/Shell type is listed in the Customer Details in the Microsoft Advertising UI. Service Level/Shell type is not a reference to payment option or service program (for example, a Premium shell may be unmanaged, or a self-serve shell may be managed by Yahoo!). 

Shell type can be determined by accessing the Company Information under Service Level.

Note: Agency designation is not a recognized shell type, it is a category that can be applied to the self-serve, self-serve plus, or premium shell types, and carries additional functionality and restrictions.

There are four classifications of Service Levels in Microsoft Advertising: self-serve, self-serve plus, select, and premium.

1. Self-serve

Self-serve is the default shell type upon sign up and supports both prepay and threshold payment options, Advertisers must be qualified for upgrade to self-serve plus or premium.

Return to top

2. Self-serve plus

Self-serve plus offers greater account functionality without the requirement of establishing Insertion Orders (IO)zas to keep accounts running. Advertisers must be qualified for upgrade to Self-serve plus. Self-serve plus advertisers cannot be downgraded to Self-serve. To upgrade a customer shell to Self-serve plus, the advertiser must:

  1. Have six months successful payment history (no declines or financial holds).
  2. Be approaching the Self-serve shell type limits.
  3. Adhere to editorial policies.

No exceptions will be allowed.

Return to top

3. Premium and select

Premium account shells are designed for higher spend advertisers who have passed appropriate credit checks and have proven ability to pay for larger quantities of advertising.

  • Premium Service Level is required to enable the monthly invoice billing. See KB 3175877 Process: Microsoft Advertising: How to Pay as needed.
  • Premium shell types can support both monthly invoice, threshold payment, and prepay accounts but all will require IOs for monthly invoice accounts to deliver. See KB 3175877 Process: Microsoft Advertising: How to Pay.
  • Credit card accounts within a premium shell are subject to credit hold, as per KB 3176727 Declined Payments and Credit Hold.

Return to top

3.1 Requirements for monthly invoice billing

Per the guidelines in KB 3170034 New Monthly Invoice Setup, the advertiser must provide one company contact, pass a credit check, and create an Insertion Order for monthly invoice accounts.

Once approved, the advertiser/agency will be issued a unique SAP ID (SAP is an accounting software). The SAP ID is a unique customer billing ID that is used to keep track of account payments and credit limits.

Return to top

3.2 Insertion orders

Insertion orders are required for all monthly invoice accounts in Premium and Select shells. An Insertion order is a contractual agreement to spend up to a certain value over a specified period. Advertisers can specify the terms (value and duration) by submitting an electronic Insertion Order directly through the Microsoft Advertising UI. See KB 3175371 Insertion Orders for more information.

4. System limits for service levels/Shell types

  • Service level - is not the same as System limits.
  • Service level determines things like threshold spend, but not how many objects are allowed.
  • You can have any system limit level per service level.
  • System limit levels determine how many objects you can have on the customer shell.
  • Additional system limit details can be found here (and can change without notice).
  • Direct service levels and system limit questions or concerns to Global Support.

Subject: New functionality enabled on your Microsoft Advertising account

"Dear [Advertiser’s name],

At your request, your Microsoft Advertising account has been enabled with greater functionality.

If you would like to add accounts, you can do so today by following these instructions:

  1. Sign into your Microsoft Advertising account at https://ads.microsoft.com/.

  2. Select the Accounts & Billing tab.

  3. On the Account List page, select Create account.

  4. Under Account information, enter the information for the new account.

  5. Under Payment method, verify the credit card information and account details.

  3. On the Account Details page, under Payment method, in the Payment method list, select the credit card that you want to associate with this account.

  4. A red asterisk indicates required information.

  5. Select Save.

We hope that you enjoy the new functionality available for your account. If you have any questions, contact Microsoft Advertising Support by email, or phone at (800) 518-5689, seven days a week, between 6:00 AM and 6:00 PM Pacific Time.

Sincerely,

The Microsoft Advertising Support Team"

6. Definitions

Account Shell (a.k.a. 'Customer', 'Advertiser', or 'CID'): A technical designation referring to a collection of one or more accounts created managed by a single Advertiser, Agency, or Reseller. A business may have more than one CID, but each CID must have a unique advertiser’s name and associated login. 

Service Level: A CID designation which imposes limitations on account functionality.
